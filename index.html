Legal Changes in Italy Address Femicide

The Italian parliament has unanimously passed a law creating a specific crime category for femicide, defined as the gender-motivated murder of a woman. This new legal clause allows for a life sentence to be imposed on those convicted under it. The legislation marks a significant step in addressing violence against women and could lead to stronger legal measures and protections.
