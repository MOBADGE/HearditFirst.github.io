Ukraine and US Discuss Peace Plan; Trump Sends Envoy to Moscow

Ukraine has announced that it has reached an "understanding" with the United States regarding a peace plan. President Trump stated that the original plan has been "fine-tuned" and shared that he will be sending his envoy, Steve Witkoff, to Moscow to discuss further details. This development comes amid ongoing efforts to find a lasting resolution to tensions involving Ukraine. The engagement signals potential progress, though the outcomes of the upcoming meetings remain to be seen.

Israel Receives Remains of Gaza Hostage

Israeli authorities have received a coffin reportedly containing the body of a hostage held by Hamas in Gaza. The remains will be transferred to forensic experts in Israel for identification. This event marks a significant moment in the ongoing conflict between Israel and Hamas, highlighting the human toll and the continuing challenges around hostages and casualties.

Former Brazilian Leader Ordered to Start Prison Term

In Brazil, the Supreme Court has ordered the former leader, Jair Bolsonaro, to begin serving a 27-year prison sentence related to a plot to overturn government authority. Judges ruled that Bolsonaro has exhausted all his appeals. He will start his sentence at a police station in Brasilia, signaling a major legal development in the country's efforts to address alleged coup activities.

Escalation in Dubai Custody Dispute

Zeynab Javadli, the ex-wife of a member of Dubai's royal family, has expressed fear of arrest amid a custody battle over their three daughters. Her ex-husband filed a criminal complaint with Dubai police accusing her of kidnapping. The case has heightened tensions and drawn attention to the complex legal and personal issues involved in some high-profile custody disputes.

New Arrests in Louvre Jewellery Heist Investigation

Four new individuals, two men and two women from Paris, have been arrested in connection with the ongoing investigation into the jewellery heist at the Louvre museum. Authorities continue to pursue leads as they work to resolve the case, reflecting the persistence of law enforcement in tracking down those involved in high-profile thefts.

Canceled Elections Due to Lost Encryption Key

The International Association for Cryptologic Research, an organization focused on secure communication, has canceled its elections after losing access to the encryption key needed to secure the process. The group described the incident as an "honest human mistake," underscoring the challenges and vulnerabilities that can affect even experts in cryptology and cybersecurity.
