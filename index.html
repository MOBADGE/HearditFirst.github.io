Survivor Stories from Epstein Case

Five women who say they were abused by financier Jeffrey Epstein spoke to BBC Newsnight about their experiences. They described the lasting impact of their ordeal and the trauma they endured. Their interviews shed light on the emotional and psychological effects abuse victims face, emphasizing the importance of continued support and awareness.

Danish Election Results Lead to Difficult Coalition Talks

Denmark’s Social Democrats, in power since 2019, won the most votes in the recent election but suffered their weakest performance in more than 100 years. Because they failed to secure a majority, tough negotiations with other parties will be necessary to form a government. The outcome signals potential shifts in Denmark’s political landscape and raises questions about the party’s future leadership.

Rising Oil Prices Could Threaten Global Economy

Larry Fink highlighted concerns about the oil price rising to $150 per barrel. He warned that sustained high oil prices could have “profound implications” and might trigger a global recession. This serves as a caution about how energy markets could impact economic stability worldwide, influencing inflation, consumer costs, and growth prospects.

OpenAI and Disney End Partnership as Sora App Closes

OpenAI has ended its partnership with Disney and shut down the Sora video-making app less than two years after its launch. The AI-powered app had caused significant disruption in the media industry by changing how video content could be created. The closure marks a shift in strategy for both companies, highlighting the challenges of integrating AI into creative sectors.

US Political Shift in Florida Election

Democrat Emily Gregory has won a legislative seat in Florida that includes the area around Mar-a-Lago, former US President Donald Trump’s home. This seat was previously held by a Republican who won by a large margin in 2024. Gregory’s projected victory represents a notable political shift and may reflect changing voter attitudes in that district.

Philippines’ Vice-President Faces Impeachment Hearings

Sara Duterte, the Vice-President of the Philippines, is undergoing another round of impeachment hearings. These hearings come amid growing political tensions as she prepares for a possible presidential run in 2028. The process could significantly affect her political future and the country’s leadership dynamics.

- In the Netherlands, after banning phones in schools two years ago, the government is now considering stricter limits on social media use for children under 16, evaluating its impact on student performance.
