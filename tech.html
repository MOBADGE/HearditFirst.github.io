Technology News Briefing – March 7, 2026

Pentagon’s Relations with AI Companies OpenAI and Anthropic

The U.S. Pentagon’s dealings with leading artificial intelligence companies OpenAI and Anthropic have become increasingly tense and complicated. OpenAI faces public skepticism over claims of transparency and trust in its cooperation with the Defense Department, with critics urging more openness. Meanwhile, Anthropic was officially designated by the Pentagon as a “supply chain risk,” a label that could bar the start-up from government contracts. Anthropic has responded by announcing plans to sue the Defense Department over this designation. These developments highlight the challenges AI companies face when balancing government partnerships, security concerns, and public accountability. The rivalry between these two Silicon Valley firms also reflects broader competition over Pentagon contracts and the future direction of the AI industry.

Drones and Modern Warfare in the Persian Gulf

Recent conflicts in the Persian Gulf region have seen a notable role for low-cost drones. Iran has deployed waves of Shahed drones to target neighboring countries, while the United States has responded by launching similar drone systems against Iranian targets. This exchange underscores a shift in modern warfare tactics, where relatively inexpensive, unmanned aerial vehicles can influence strategic dynamics without large-scale conventional forces. The use of such drones raises questions about escalation, defense measures, and the evolving nature of military engagements in the region.

AI Infrastructure Growth Drives Demand for Land and Power

The rapid expansion of AI services is generating increased demand for specialized data center infrastructure. Former Microsoft executive Brian Janous and his company Cloverleaf now act as intermediaries securing land and electricity packages needed to build these data centers. This trend illustrates how the AI industry’s growth affects real estate and energy markets, as locating suitable sites with sufficient power capacity becomes essential. It also reflects the behind-the-scenes logistics that enable large-scale AI operations, emphasizing the importance of infrastructure in technology development.

Securities and Exchange Commission Settles with Crypto Investor Justin Sun

The Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) reached a settlement with Justin Sun, an investor linked to cryptocurrency coins associated with the Trump family. The SEC had accused Sun of serious securities law violations, which he denied. Settling the case ends a high-profile legal battle involving digital assets and their regulation. This resolution may influence future enforcement approaches toward crypto investors and underline ongoing regulatory scrutiny in the cryptocurrency space.

AI and the Smart Home Evolution

Tech companies including Amazon and Google are investing in artificially intelligent assistants—like Alexa+ and Gemini—to improve the smart home experience. These AI systems aim to simplify the often complex setup and management of smart home devices. However, despite these efforts, fundamental challenges remain. Users continue to face issues with device compatibility, privacy concerns, and reliable automation. The sector’s progress depends on addressing these persistent problems while integrating AI technologies effectively.

AI-Generated Videos and Animal Perception

Emerging AI-generated videos that depict animals performing unusual or impressive behaviors are gaining attention. While these videos can entertain and inspire, experts caution that they may dull appreciation for the genuine qualities and natural behaviors of animals. This phenomenon raises questions about how digital content shapes public understanding of wildlife and underscores the need for critical awareness of manipulated media.
