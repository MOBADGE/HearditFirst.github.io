Meta Announces Large-Scale Layoffs as AI Becomes Central Focus

Meta, the parent company of Facebook and Instagram, revealed plans to cut about 10 percent of its workforce, equating to around 8,000 employees. In addition, it will close 6,000 open job positions. This restructuring is part of Meta's strategic shift to prioritize artificial intelligence (AI) development. The move reflects the competitive pressure in the tech industry to advance AI capabilities, but it also signals a tightening labor market at one of Silicon Valley’s largest employers. These changes could reshape Meta’s product strategies and workforce for years to come.

Legal Battle Between Elon Musk and Sam Altman Heads to Jury Trial

A high-profile legal dispute between Elon Musk and Sam Altman is set to go to trial starting Monday. Musk is seeking billions of dollars in damages from OpenAI, the company co-founded by Altman, which has been a key player in AI research. The lawsuit centers on allegations that could have significant implications for the AI landscape, including intellectual property and competitive practices. The outcome may influence how AI companies collaborate and litigate as the technology develops.

OpenAI Releases GPT-5.5 Model with Enhanced Security Transparency

OpenAI announced the release of GPT-5.5, its latest language model iteration. This update follows a trend toward improved performance and broader capabilities. Notably, OpenAI is adopting a more transparent approach to cybersecurity compared to its primary competitor, Anthropic. This difference in strategy highlights ongoing debates in the industry about model security, safety, and responsible AI deployment, which affect users and regulatory bodies alike.

Anthropic’s Mythos AI Model Raises Global Security Concerns

Anthropic, another leading AI research company, introduced its Mythos AI model, which has triggered emergency attention from central banks and intelligence agencies worldwide. The powerful model’s potential impacts have led governments and organizations to closely monitor access and use. Anthropic’s cautious approach to controlling distribution reflects broader concerns about the influence of advanced AI on financial systems, security, and societal stability.

Intel Reports Strong Quarterly Earnings Driven by AI Demand

Intel announced a 7 percent increase in revenue, reaching $13.6 billion for the latest quarter, surpassing market expectations by over $1 billion. The growth is largely attributed to rising demand for AI-related chips and hardware components. This financial performance underscores the expanding economic footprint of AI technologies across the semiconductor industry and highlights Intel’s role in supplying critical infrastructure for AI development.

New Phishing Scam Targets Users via Fake Digital Invitations

Security experts have identified a new phishing scam that involves hackers sending fake invitations through services such as Paperless Post, Evite, and Punchbowl. The scam aims to trick recipients into downloading malware that can compromise their devices. This development serves as a reminder of the evolving tactics used by cybercriminals and the importance of vigilance when interacting with unexpected or unusual online invitations.
