Elon Musk Requires Wall Street Firms to Subscribe to Grok for SpaceX IPO Advising

As SpaceX prepares for one of the largest initial public offerings in history, Elon Musk has introduced a new condition for banks interested in advising on the IPO. Wall Street firms must subscribe to Grok, Musk’s proprietary artificial intelligence chatbot, to gain access. This move ties a high-profile financial event to a technology product, arguably influencing how Wall Street interacts with Musk’s companies. The requirement could reshape underwriting practices or set a precedent for integrating AI services into investment banking workflows.

OpenAI Acquires Streaming Show TBPN to Foster A.I. Discussions

OpenAI has purchased the streaming show TBPN as part of an effort to encourage more balanced and constructive conversations about artificial intelligence. The company stated that owning TBPN allows it to create a platform for real discussions on AI’s societal and technological changes. This acquisition reflects OpenAI’s interest in shaping public discourse around AI and addressing concerns or misunderstandings about the technology’s impact.

NASA’s Artemis II Astronauts Carry iPhones into Space

NASA’s Artemis II mission astronauts were permitted to take iPhones with them on their journey. Although the devices cannot connect to the internet during the mission, their presence demonstrates the increasing integration of consumer technology into space exploration gear. This inclusion allows astronauts to use familiar tools for communication, note-taking, or photography, even in an environment disconnected from regular networks.

Europe Seeks to Reduce Online Risks for Children

The European Union and member states are intensifying efforts to create a less addictive and safer internet environment for children. Measures focus on regulating social media platforms and limiting the influence of recommendation algorithms that can foster excessive usage. This regulatory push aims to address growing concerns about the effects of online content and digital habits on young users’ well-being and development.

Gmail Now Allows Users to Change Their Email Addresses

Google has introduced a new policy enabling Gmail users to change their usernames without losing data or access to their accounts. This update addresses a long-standing issue where early Gmail adopters were stuck with outdated or embarrassing email handles. The change simplifies email personalization and may improve user experience by allowing fresh email identities while preserving the continuity of inbox and services.

Wearables Make a Comeback in Fashion-Tech

The relationship between fashion and technology is seeing renewed interest with the revival of wearables. Companies are preparing to introduce new smartglasses and other tech-integrated accessories, advancing beyond earlier attempts in the sector. This development suggests wearables remain a significant area of innovation, blending utility and style to appeal to consumers in everyday settings.
