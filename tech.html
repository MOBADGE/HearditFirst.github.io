Technology News Briefing – March 10, 2026

1. Grieving Parents Advocate for Enhanced Online Safety Amid A.I. Concerns A new movement led by parents who lost children has emerged, calling for stricter online safety regulations. These parents hold social media platforms and artificial intelligence chatbots partly responsible for harmful online interactions that contributed to their children’s deaths. The group joins earlier efforts focused on social media harms but is now highlighting risks posed by AI-driven chatbots. Their campaign underscores ongoing debates about how technology companies manage content and user safety, particularly for vulnerable groups such as minors.

2. Meta Acquires Moltbook, Social Network for A.I. Bots Meta announced the acquisition of Moltbook, a niche social network designed specifically for artificial intelligence bots to interact. Founded by Matt Schlicht, who played a prominent role in sparking Silicon Valley’s interest in AI agents, Moltbook’s integration into Meta’s Superintelligence Lab reflects the company’s continued investment in AI research and development. This move represents a strategic effort to deepen Meta’s engagement with AI-driven platforms and explore new social dynamics involving autonomous agents.

3. YouTube Launches Tool to Combat Deepfake Videos Targeting Public Figures In response to growing concerns over manipulated videos, YouTube introduced a new reporting feature enabling public figures to flag so-called deepfake videos. These videos distort or fabricate imagery to falsely portray individuals, raising issues of misinformation and reputational harm. By providing a direct channel for reporting such content, YouTube aims to improve its moderation processes and respond to regulatory and public pressure to address digital misinformation more effectively.

4. Former Meta AI Leader’s Start-Up Secures $3.5 Billion Valuation Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs, a start-up founded by Yann LeCun and other former Meta AI researchers, announced it has raised over $1 billion in funding, valuing the company at approximately $3.5 billion. The company focuses on advanced artificial intelligence development and is backed by a diverse group of investors. This funding milestone marks a significant endorsement of the firm’s vision and highlights the competitive landscape of AI innovation driven by experienced industry figures.

5. Boeing Reports Wiring Problems Causing Delays in Some 737 Max Deliveries Boeing revealed that a wiring issue will delay delivery schedules for a portion of its 737 Max aircraft orders. Despite these setbacks, the company maintains confidence that it will meet its 2026 sales target of around 500 jets. This announcement provides transparency on production challenges while reassuring customers and investors about Boeing’s ability to achieve its broader manufacturing and sales goals for the year.

6. U.S. Solar Installations Decline in 2025 Amid Political Challenges A recent report shows that although solar energy additions to the US electricity grid remain the highest among energy sources, installations fell by 14 percent in 2025. Analysts link the decline partly to political opposition from the previous administration under Donald Trump, which targeted clean energy policies including solar subsidies. The report signals lasting effects of policy shifts on renewable energy deployment despite ongoing efforts to expand clean energy infrastructure.
