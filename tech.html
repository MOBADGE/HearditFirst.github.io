New A.I. Start-Up Aims to Enhance Worker Capabilities

A new artificial intelligence company called Humans&, founded by researchers from Anthropic, Google, and xAI, recently announced its launch and is already valued at $4.48 billion. Unlike many A.I. ventures focused on automation and replacement, Humans& centers its mission on augmenting human work rather than substituting it. The company aims to develop tools that assist workers in various tasks, potentially improving productivity without eliminating jobs. This approach could influence how A.I. integrates into workplaces by prioritizing collaboration between humans and machines over full automation.

Snap Settles Social Media Addiction Lawsuit, Sidesteps Trial

Snap Inc., the parent company of Snapchat, has agreed to a settlement in a lawsuit that accused social media platforms of causing addiction and related harms. The settlement prevents the case from going to a landmark trial where plaintiffs intended to argue that inherent design features of these platforms make them liable for personal injuries. The case raised important legal questions about platform responsibility and the social impact of technology. By settling, Snap avoids a judicial decision that could have set significant precedents affecting social media companies broadly.
