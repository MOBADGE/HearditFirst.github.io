Technology News Briefing – March 10, 2026

Anthropic Sues Department of Defense Over Supply Chain Risk Label The artificial intelligence company Anthropic has filed two lawsuits against the U.S. Department of Defense (DoD). Anthropic says the DoD’s designation of the company as a “supply chain risk” is unfair and driven by ideological bias rather than factual concerns. This legal action highlights ongoing tensions between AI developers and government agencies, especially concerning national security assessments and procurement decisions. The dispute reflects broader challenges in balancing technological innovation with government oversight.

Former Coal Miner Leads Major Investment in AI Data Centers Josh Payne, CEO of Nscale, a company focused on AI data center infrastructure, has successfully raised $2 billion from investors. Notably, Sheryl Sandberg, formerly an executive at Meta (formerly Facebook), has joined Nscale’s board. Payne’s background as a coal miner turned tech entrepreneur is receiving attention in light of the expanding demand for AI computing power. The surge in funding for data center builders like Nscale underscores the increasing hardware needs that support large-scale AI models.

Ex-Meta AI Chief’s Startup Valued at $3.5 Billion Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs (AMI Labs), a new AI startup founded by Yann LeCun and other former Meta researchers, has raised over $1 billion in funding, bringing its valuation to $3.5 billion. LeCun, a well-known figure in machine learning, is aiming to pursue advanced AI research and development outside of major tech corporations. This development signals continued interest from investors in AI startups that focus on foundational AI technologies and talent departing from large platforms.

Decline in U.S. Solar Installations Despite Leading Growth A recent report indicates that although solar energy added more capacity to the U.S. grid than any other energy source in 2025, the total solar installations dropped by 14 percent compared to the previous year. Analysts attribute this decline partly to the regulatory and political climate shaped by policies during the Trump administration, which opposed clean energy expansion. The report highlights the ongoing impact of policy decisions on renewable energy growth even amid broader market interest.

Drones in the Iran Conflict Illustrate Changing Warfare Iran has been deploying waves of low-cost Shahed drones to threaten Persian Gulf nations, while the U.S. has responded with similar unmanned drone technology. This usage exemplifies a shift in modern warfare toward more widespread adoption of inexpensive drone systems. The evolution and proliferation of such drones affect regional security dynamics and suggest new considerations for military strategy and defense procurement.

Summary This week’s technology news centers on AI industry disputes with government agencies, significant investments in AI infrastructure and startups, shifts in clean energy deployment, and the evolving use of drone technology in conflict zones. These developments represent key areas where technology intersects with policy, economics, and security, shaping both industry trajectories and broader societal impacts.
