Technology News Briefing – March 26, 2026

Meta Faces Legal and Financial Challenges

Meta has been at the center of several recent developments involving legal rulings and corporate decisions. A jury found both Meta and YouTube negligent in a landmark case concerning social media addiction. The verdict determined that design choices by these companies contributed to a young user’s mental health issues due to addictive features on their platforms. In a related matter, Meta was ordered to pay $375 million following a New Mexico trial where the company was found to have misled consumers about the safety of its services, enabling sexual exploitation of minors. These cases mark significant legal setbacks for Meta around user safety and corporate responsibility.

At the same time, Meta announced layoffs affecting roughly 700 employees, as it continues to realign its business toward artificial intelligence development. Despite these cuts, the company has introduced a stock compensation program targeting top executives, highlighting a contrasting approach to workforce management amid these transitions.

Epic Games Reduces Workforce Amid Fortnite Decline

Epic Games has announced layoffs impacting over 1,000 employees, approximately 20 percent of its total staff. The decision was attributed to a slump in the performance of Fortnite, one of the company’s flagship products. The reduction in workforce signals challenges for Epic Games in maintaining its market position and revenue streams from its popular battle royale game.

Arm Holdings to Enter Chip Manufacturing

Arm Holdings, traditionally known as a designer of semiconductor architectures sold to other manufacturers, has declared plans to produce and sell its own computer chips. The initial focus will be on artificial intelligence data centers. This marks a notable shift in Arm’s business model, moving from solely licensing intellectual property to becoming a direct supplier of hardware components. The move could have implications for the semiconductor industry’s competitive landscape.

OpenAI Discontinues AI Video Generator Service

OpenAI announced the shutdown of Sora, its artificial intelligence-based video generation tool, just three months after announcing a multiyear partnership to feature Disney characters on the platform. The decision to discontinue the service so soon after its launch raises questions about the challenges and strategic priorities within AI-driven media technologies.

In Memoriam: Leon Radvinsky and Paul Brainerd

Two figures with notable impacts on technology and media passed away recently. Leon Radvinsky, aged 43, was recognized for building OnlyFans into a prominent platform in online adult entertainment by leveraging social media and influencer economics. Meanwhile, Paul Brainerd, who died at 78, was a pioneer in desktop publishing. His development of PageMaker software transformed print publishing through digital layout tools, streamlining a process that was previously manual and time-consuming.

These stories collectively highlight ongoing shifts in technology companies' responsibilities, business strategies, and the legacies of influential innovators.
