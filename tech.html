Arm Holdings to Manufacture Its Own Chips for AI Data Centers

Arm Holdings, long known for licensing chip designs to other companies, announced it will begin producing its own computer chips specifically aimed at artificial intelligence data centers. This marks a significant shift in the company’s business model from design-only to direct manufacturing. The move allows Arm to have greater control over the performance and integration of its chips in growing AI workloads. It also positions the company to compete more directly with established chipmakers in the AI hardware market.

Epic Games Cuts Staff by 20 Percent Amid Fortnite Decline

Epic Games laid off approximately 1,000 employees, or about 20 percent of its workforce, citing decreasing player engagement with its flagship game Fortnite. The layoffs reflect ongoing challenges in maintaining revenue and growth in the highly competitive video game market. This reduction may affect future development projects at Epic as the company adjusts its strategy to respond to shifts in gamer preferences and market trends.

OpenAI Ends Sora AI Video Generator After Disney Deal

OpenAI announced it is shutting down Sora, its AI-powered video generation service, just three months after signing a multiyear deal to incorporate Disney characters into the platform. The closure comes as OpenAI reassesses its AI product offerings and focuses resources elsewhere. The decision underscores the complexities and risks involved in commercializing advanced generative AI tools, especially those linked to high-profile content licensing.

Meta Ordered to Pay $375 Million Over Child Safety Issues

Meta has been ordered to pay $375 million following a court ruling in New Mexico that found the company misled users about the safety of its platforms, which allegedly enabled sexual exploitation of minors. This legal setback is one of Meta’s first major losses related to child safety concerns. The ruling highlights increased scrutiny on social media companies regarding their responsibilities to protect young users and the potential consequences of failing to do so.

State Attorneys General Challenge $14 Billion HP Tech Acquisition

A federal judge heard arguments from a group of Democratic state attorneys general opposing the Justice Department’s approval of Hewlett Packard Enterprise’s $14 billion acquisition deal. The states contend that the settlement addressing antitrust concerns was ineffective and influenced by corruption. The outcome of this case could impact how large technology mergers are reviewed and challenged in the future, with implications for competition in the tech industry.

Paul Brainerd, Pioneer of Desktop Publishing, Dies at 78

Paul Brainerd, credited with pioneering desktop publishing through his invention of PageMaker software, passed away at age 78. His work revolutionized the printing industry by allowing users to create printed layouts digitally rather than manually assembling text and images. Brainerd’s innovations paved the way for modern publishing and graphic design workflows, influencing both technology and media production.
