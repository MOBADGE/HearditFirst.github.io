**Elon Musk Requires Banks to Use Grok for SpaceX IPO Advisory**

Elon Musk is introducing an unusual condition for Wall Street firms that want to advise on SpaceX’s upcoming initial public offering, one of the largest expected in recent history. Banks interested in participating must subscribe to Grok, Musk’s own artificial intelligence chatbot. This move links the financial and technology sectors closely, as it effectively mandates the use of Musk’s AI tool for access to the IPO advisory process. The rationale behind this requirement has not been fully detailed, but it underscores Musk’s growing influence over market processes and his push to integrate AI technologies into traditional financial dealings.

**OpenAI Acquires Streaming Network TBPN to Influence AI Discourse**

OpenAI has purchased the streaming network TBPN with the goal of fostering constructive public discussion about artificial intelligence and its societal impact. The company expressed hopes that owning a media platform will allow it to present more nuanced narratives around AI developments, moving beyond common fears or hype. While details about programming changes or strategies were not disclosed, the acquisition highlights OpenAI’s interest in shaping public perception and engagement regarding AI technology.

**NASA Allows Artemis II Astronauts to Take iPhones Into Space**

For the Artemis II mission, NASA granted astronauts permission to bring Apple iPhones on board, marking a notable shift in the agency’s approach to consumer electronics in space. Although the phones cannot connect to the internet during the mission, they can be used for various onboard functions such as timing, note-taking, and photography. The inclusion of familiar devices like iPhones shows NASA’s gradual openness to incorporating widely available technology in space missions, which may influence future operational practices.

**European Union Advances Efforts to Protect Children Online**

The European Union and its member states are pushing regulatory initiatives aimed at making online environments, especially social media platforms, less addictive and safer for children. These efforts include proposals to limit algorithmic features that encourage excessive use and to enhance protections against harmful content. The move reflects growing concerns about the mental health and well-being of young users in digital spaces. If implemented, these regulations could significantly affect how tech companies design their platforms in Europe.

**Google Allows Gmail Users to Change Email Addresses Without Data Loss**

Google announced a new policy that enables Gmail users to change their email addresses without losing access to old messages or contacts. This change addresses a longstanding frustration among users stuck with outdated or embarrassing usernames created in the early days of the service. The update offers greater flexibility and control over digital identities while maintaining data continuity, which could improve user experience and reduce the need for workarounds such as creating new accounts.

**The Role of AI in Reshaping Silicon Valley Workplaces**

The tech industry is experiencing firsthand the transformative effects of AI on professional work. Employees in Silicon Valley are seeing new tools change job functions, workflows, and productivity expectations. This dynamic illustrates a broader shift in white-collar labor influenced by AI, with implications for employment patterns, skill requirements, and workplace culture. Industry leaders continue to debate how to balance AI integration with human agency in work environments.
