**Jury Verdicts Signal Legal Accountability for Social Media and Child Safety**

Recent jury decisions have held major social media companies, including Meta and YouTube, responsible for harming young users by creating addictive platforms. These rulings come amid ongoing struggles in Congress to enact comprehensive child online safety regulations. The verdicts may mark a shift toward using the courts to address perceived industry failings on protecting minors, potentially prompting changes in how social media platforms design and moderate their services.

**Snapchat Faces European Regulatory Scrutiny Over Age Verification**

European Union regulators have initiated an investigation into Snapchat’s child safety policies. Authorities in Brussels cited weaknesses in the platform’s age-verification system and expressed concern that Snapchat’s features may expose younger users to inappropriate content. This inquiry reflects increasing global attention on social media companies' responsibility to safeguard children, as regulators push for stronger protections amid widespread online risks.

**Meta Implements Job Cuts While Incentivizing Executives Amid AI Focus**

Meta announced it is laying off 700 employees as part of a restructuring effort that aligns with the company’s strategic shift toward artificial intelligence development. At the same time, Meta disclosed a new stock-based reward program for its top executives. The moves highlight how major tech companies are balancing workforce reductions with efforts to retain leadership talent during periods of significant technological transition.

**Helium Shortage Raises Concerns for AI Chip Manufacturing**

The global chip industry is facing a helium supply bottleneck that threatens production continuity. About one-third of the world’s helium supply remains offline due to disruptions linked to the ongoing conflict in Iran. Helium is critical in the manufacturing process of advanced semiconductors, including those used in AI applications. Gas suppliers are working to reassure chip makers that supply chains will remain stable despite geopolitical challenges.

**Anthropic Wins Preliminary Legal Victory Against Pentagon Risk Label**

Artificial intelligence company Anthropic secured a court injunction blocking the Department of Defense’s designation of the firm as a “supply chain risk.” The ruling is an early legal win for Anthropic in its dispute with the Pentagon, which has expressed security concerns about the company. This case underscores the increasing intersection of national security considerations with AI industry activities and the regulatory scrutiny such companies face.

**Binance Faces Criticism Over Oversight Failures in Crypto Transfers to Iran**

The cryptocurrency exchange Binance is under criticism after investigators identified accounts linked to the platform that moved approximately $1.7 billion to entities associated with Iran despite U.S. sanctions. Reports indicate that warning signs were present for over a year, suggesting gaps in Binance’s compliance and monitoring systems. This development highlights ongoing challenges in enforcing financial regulations in the crypto sector.
