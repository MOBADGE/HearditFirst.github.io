Technology News Briefing – March 28, 2026

Legal Developments in AI and Social Media

A federal judge has temporarily halted the Department of Defense’s designation of Anthropic, an artificial intelligence company, as a “supply chain risk.” This preliminary ruling marks a setback for the Pentagon in an ongoing legal dispute, allowing Anthropic to operate without the current restrictions imposed on its technology contracts. The case highlights growing tensions between AI developers and government regulators over national security concerns.

In a separate legal matter affecting tech platforms broadly, a jury found Meta and YouTube liable for creating products with addictive qualities. The verdict raises questions about the responsibility of social media companies for user engagement practices and could lead to regulatory changes in how these platforms design and promote their services. The ruling may influence future policy and corporate behavior related to digital well-being.

Global Supply Chain Challenges in Semiconductor Industry

The semiconductor sector is facing a helium shortage due to geopolitical issues, notably the ongoing conflict in Iran, which has disrupted about one-third of the world’s helium supply. Helium is critical for producing advanced semiconductor chips, essential components in AI hardware. Gas suppliers are actively seeking alternative sources and working to prevent interruptions for chip manufacturers. This shortage underscores the vulnerability of global tech supply chains to geopolitical events and commodity availability.

Cryptocurrency Exchange Under Scrutiny Over Sanctions Violations

Binance, the largest cryptocurrency exchange, is facing criticism after investigators revealed that $1.7 billion in crypto assets moved through accounts linked to Iranian entities. Analysts noted that warning signs were evident for over a year but apparently overlooked. The investigation has drawn attention to compliance oversight within crypto platforms and the challenges of enforcing economic sanctions in digital currency markets.

Military Drone Industry Sees Significant Investment

Shield AI, a startup focusing on autonomous military drone technology, announced a $2 billion funding round. The company also plans to acquire a simulation software developer to enhance its capabilities. This investment reflects increased interest and spending in next-generation defense technologies, particularly those combining AI and robotics for military applications. The developments point to a broader trend of innovation and commercialization in defense tech sectors.

Elon Musk’s Unusual Participation in Diplomatic Call

Elon Musk took part in a recent phone conversation between former U.S. President Donald Trump and Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi. Such involvement by a private citizen is uncommon in diplomatic communications and may indicate that Musk's relationship with the political leadership has improved. While the details of his role were not disclosed, the incident illustrates the growing intersection between prominent technology figures and international political affairs.
