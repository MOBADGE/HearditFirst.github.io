Elon Musk Expands Social Media Presence Amid SpaceX Plans

Elon Musk, the billionaire owner of X (formerly Twitter), has begun posting on new social media platforms, including TikTok and Instagram, from verified accounts using his own handle. This shift coincides with preparations to take his rocket company SpaceX public. The move suggests Musk is diversifying his communication channels, potentially to reach different demographics and promote his ventures beyond the traditional Twitter base. While the posts themselves have been modest, Musk’s presence on these platforms could influence public perception and investor interest as SpaceX moves toward an initial public offering.

Rising Global Competition in Military AI Technologies

The competition to develop artificial intelligence for military applications is intensifying among major powers including the United States, China, and Russia. These countries are accelerating efforts to create AI-backed weapons and defense systems, a development experts say echoes the early stages of the nuclear arms race. The buildup raises concerns about global security, arms control, and the potential for AI-driven conflicts. Governments and analysts are closely watching how this technological rivalry will affect military strategy and international stability.

Attack on OpenAI CEO Highlights Tensions Around AI

A recent attack on the home of Sam Altman, CEO of OpenAI, drew attention to the growing unease surrounding artificial intelligence advancements. A homemade Molotov cocktail was thrown at Altman’s San Francisco residence, damaging a gate but causing no reported injury. Authorities arrested a 20-year-old suspect from Texas who reportedly possessed a list of AI industry leaders, indicating a targeted act rooted in fears about AI’s impact. This incident underscores the polarizing nature of AI development and the personal risks faced by executives in the field.

Study Shows Gen Z’s Growing Frustration with AI

New research by Gallup reveals that while around half of Generation Z uses AI technologies, their attitudes toward AI have shifted toward frustration and anger. The study found a decline in hopefulness about AI’s benefits among young adults, contrasting with earlier enthusiasm. Concerns cited include job displacement, privacy, and ethical implications. This trend may influence how AI products are received in the coming years and affect policymaking around technology regulation and education tailored to younger generations.

Volkswagen Scales Back Electric Vehicle Production

Volkswagen announced it will cease electric vehicle (EV) production at its Tennessee plant, shifting focus back to gasoline-powered models. This decision follows similar actions by other automakers who are recalibrating their EV strategies in response to market conditions, supply challenges, and changing consumer demand. The move represents a setback in the automotive industry's broader push toward electrification, particularly in the United States, and may affect the pace of EV adoption.

Meta Introduces New AI Model, Muse Spark

Meta unveiled Muse Spark, a new artificial intelligence model developed by its Superintelligence Lab. Muse Spark shows improved overall performance compared to Meta’s previous AI systems but is said to lag behind competitors in coding tasks. The release marks Meta’s continued investment in AI research despite recent challenges in the tech sector. The model’s strengths and limitations will likely inform Meta’s future AI applications and products.
