Arm Holdings to Manufacture Its Own Chips for AI Data Centers

Arm Holdings, traditionally known for licensing its chip designs to other companies, announced it will begin producing its own computer chips aimed at artificial intelligence (AI) data centers. This marked a significant shift from its long-standing business model as a design firm to also becoming a chip manufacturer. The move responds to growing demand for specialized AI hardware but places Arm in direct competition with companies it previously supplied, such as Apple and Qualcomm. The change reflects evolving market dynamics where owning both design and manufacturing may provide more control over performance and supply.

Epic Games Cuts Workforce Amid Fortnite Decline

Epic Games disclosed layoffs affecting more than 1,000 employees, about 20 percent of its workforce, citing a decline in Fortnite’s popularity and revenue. Fortnite, once a top-earning battle royale game that helped drive Epic’s success, has seen reduced player engagement and lower in-game spending. The layoffs represent the largest restructuring in the company’s history and point to challenges in maintaining sustained growth in competitive gaming markets. Epic plans to focus more on new projects and emerging opportunities to offset the slump.

Meta Ordered to Pay $375 Million for Child Safety Violations

A New Mexico jury ordered Meta to pay $375 million after finding that the company misled consumers about the safety of its platforms and failed to prevent sexual exploitation of minors. This marks one of Meta’s first major legal defeats related to platform safety and content moderation. The ruling raised questions about platforms’ responsibilities toward young users and the effectiveness of existing safety measures. Meta said it would consider an appeal but acknowledged the verdict as a serious legal and reputational challenge.

OpenAI Shuts Down AI Video Generator Sora After Three Months

OpenAI announced it will discontinue Sora, its AI-powered video generation service, after just three months in operation. The move comes despite a recent multiyear agreement to feature Disney characters on the platform. Sora’s shutdown follows a period of rapid experimentation by AI companies testing new generative tools, but it also highlights the difficulties in scaling and monetizing AI video products amid technical and licensing challenges. OpenAI said it would shift focus to other AI applications.

Polymarket’s Social Media Feeds Found Filled with False Information

A recent review of social media activity linked to Polymarket, a prediction market platform, revealed hundreds of false and misleading posts across its channels. Polymarket promotes itself as dealing in factual information through betting markets but has struggled to contain disinformation on its feeds. The findings raise broader concerns about misinformation in online financial and betting communities, and the difficulties platforms face in policing content while encouraging user engagement.

Tech Workers Increasing AI Usage Despite High Costs

At multiple companies, employees are intensifying their use of AI tools, often competing on leaderboards that track individual AI usage. While this trend indicates growing acceptance and integration of AI in daily workflows, it has also led to substantially higher expenses as companies rack up millions in AI service charges. The dynamic highlights the balance organizations seek between leveraging AI to boost productivity and managing the costs associated with extensive AI deployments.
