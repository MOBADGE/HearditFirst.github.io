Parents Call for Stronger Online Safety Amid AI Concerns

A growing group of grieving parents is calling for improved protections on social media and AI platforms, blaming chatbots and other automated tools for contributing to the online harm that affected their children. These parents connect their campaigns to earlier efforts targeting social media companies, which they say did not adequately address online bullying, harassment, and harmful content. As artificial intelligence technologies become more integrated into social platforms, parents fear that current safety measures are insufficient to protect young users from emotional distress or worse consequences. The debate highlights ongoing challenges in regulating AI-driven content and maintaining user safety online.

Meta Acquires AI-Focused Social Network Moltbook

Meta has acquired Moltbook, a niche social network designed primarily for AI bots to interact. Created by Matt Schlicht, Moltbook attracted attention by showcasing the potential of AI agents to communicate independently. Schlicht is joining Meta’s Superintelligence Lab as part of the acquisition. This move reflects Meta’s continued investment in artificial intelligence and agent-based technologies, aiming to expand its capabilities in this area. While the practical implications are still unfolding, the acquisition signals Meta’s interest in developing AI ecosystems beyond traditional user interactions.

YouTube Introduces Tool to Report Deepfake Videos

In response to pressures to address manipulative content, YouTube has launched a new feature that allows public figures to report fake videos made with deepfake technology. Deepfakes use artificial intelligence to create realistic but false videos that can misrepresent real people, raising concerns about misinformation and reputational harm. By enabling individuals to flag such content, YouTube aims to improve content moderation and maintain platform integrity. This initiative is part of wider industry efforts to manage the risks posed by synthetic media.

Former Meta AI Leader’s Start-Up Hits $3.5 Billion Valuation

Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs, a start-up founded by Yann LeCun and other former Meta AI researchers, recently raised over $1 billion, reaching a valuation of $3.5 billion. The company focuses on advanced AI research and development, building on the expertise of its founders who were key figures in Meta’s AI division. The significant funding indicates strong investor interest in independent AI ventures outside major tech firms. This development points to continued innovation and competition within the AI research landscape.

Thomson Reuters Employees Challenge Use of Surveillance Tool by ICE

Employees at Thomson Reuters’ Minnesota offices have voiced opposition to the company’s provision of investigative tools used by U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). These insiders argue that the surveillance technology supports immigration enforcement policies they find problematic, and they are pressing for the company to halt such contracts. Thomson Reuters, known for media and legal information services, faces growing internal and public scrutiny over the ethical implications of supplying technology to government agencies engaged in immigration enforcement. This situation underscores broader tensions over corporate responsibility and technology's role in law enforcement.

Boeing Reports 737 Max Delivery Delays Due to Wiring Issues

Boeing announced a delay in deliveries for some of its 737 Max aircraft because of wiring problems identified during production. Despite these setbacks, the company said the delays would not affect its overall sales target of approximately 500 Max jets for 2026. The 737 Max, which has faced scrutiny and challenges since its earlier grounding, remains a critical product for Boeing’s commercial aircraft lineup. The announcement highlights ongoing supply chain and manufacturing hurdles in the aerospace industry, which can affect delivery schedules but have not, so far, altered Boeing’s broader business outlook.
