Technology News Briefing – March 24, 2026

Arm Holdings Enters Chip Manufacturing Market Arm Holdings, traditionally known for licensing its chip designs to other companies, announced it will now manufacture and sell its own computer chips aimed at artificial intelligence data centers. This marks a strategic shift from its usual business model, where it relied on partners to produce processors based on its designs. By producing chips directly, Arm aims to capture a larger share of the growing AI hardware market and exert more control over performance and integration. This move may impact existing Arm licensees and alter competitive dynamics in the chip industry.

Epic Games Cuts Workforce Amid Fortnite Revenue Decline Epic Games has laid off over 1,000 employees, about 20 percent of its total workforce, citing a sustained slump in revenue from its popular game Fortnite. The reduction reflects continued challenges in maintaining player engagement and monetization amid a competitive gaming landscape. Epic’s decision highlights the sensitivity of gaming companies to fluctuations in flagship titles’ performance and may influence how other developers approach game updates and diversification efforts.

Polymarket Social Media Feeds Filled With False Information Polymarket, a platform for prediction markets that claims to deliver honest assessments of event outcomes, has come under scrutiny after a review found hundreds of false and misleading posts on its social media feeds. The prevalence of misinformation raises questions about the moderation policies and reliability of user-generated content on such platforms. This situation underscores the difficulty in balancing openness and accuracy in online marketplaces that depend on social interactions.

Legal Challenge to Hewlett Packard Enterprise Deal Advances A judge has heard arguments from Democratic state attorneys general contesting the Justice Department’s approval of Hewlett Packard Enterprise’s $14 billion acquisition of Juniper Networks. The states argue the settlement was inadequate and tainted by misconduct, potentially undermining antitrust enforcement. The outcome of this challenge could affect future large-scale mergers in the technology sector, emphasizing the ongoing debate over regulatory oversight and market competition.

Elon Musk Found Responsible for Losses to Twitter Investors A jury has ruled that Elon Musk was responsible for some financial losses experienced by investors in Twitter (now rebranded as X). Investors had claimed Musk attempted to depress the company’s share price to secure a more favorable price for his $44 billion acquisition bid. This verdict represents a rare legal setback for Musk and highlights the scrutiny surrounding his business dealings and influence on publicly traded technology companies.

In Memoriam: Paul Brainerd, Desktop Publishing Innovator Paul Brainerd, who pioneered desktop publishing software with the creation of PageMaker, has died at age 78. His software transformed the printing industry by enabling users to design page layouts digitally rather than relying on manual paste-up techniques. Brainerd’s work helped democratize publishing and laid the foundation for modern graphic design and desktop publishing tools still in use today.
