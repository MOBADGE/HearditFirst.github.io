Technology News Briefing – March 20, 2026

1. Rising Use of A.I. Among Tech Workers Drives Higher Costs At several technology companies, employees have been encouraged to increase their use of artificial intelligence tools, sometimes competing on leaderboards that measure their A.I. engagement. While this has boosted adoption within workplaces, it has also led to significantly higher expenses as companies face mounting bills to cover extensive A.I. usage. This development highlights a growing trend of integrating A.I. more deeply into daily work activities but raises questions about cost management and sustainable deployment.

2. Polymarket’s Social Media Under Scrutiny for Misinformation Polymarket, a platform known for its prediction markets, has come under criticism after an investigation revealed that its social media accounts have spread numerous false and misleading posts. Although the company positions itself as a promoter of truthful and data-driven content, the prevalence of misinformation could undermine user trust and affect the platform’s credibility. This case illustrates ongoing challenges in managing and moderating the accuracy of information on social channels linked to technology firms.

3. Meta Scales Back Ambitions for its Metaverse Project Meta has announced changes that largely scale back its efforts to develop a fully immersive virtual reality “metaverse” under Mark Zuckerberg’s vision. The adjustments effectively place the project on life support, reflecting broader industry reassessments of virtual reality’s mass-market potential. The decision signals a shift away from extensive investment in VR-centric digital worlds, possibly influenced by user adoption rates and financial considerations.

4. Legal Action Over Export of Nvidia-Based Servers to China U.S. authorities have charged three individuals, including a co-founder of Super Micro, a Silicon Valley server manufacturer, with violating export laws by diverting servers equipped with Nvidia A.I. chips to China. The indictment exposes concerns about the unauthorized transfer of advanced technology that could have implications for national security and international trade regulations. This case underscores ongoing tensions surrounding technology exports amid geopolitical competition.

5. Jeff Bezos Plans Large-Scale Fund Focused on A.I. Investment Jeff Bezos is reportedly in discussions to create a $100 billion investment fund aimed at transforming companies through artificial intelligence. This initiative would complement his existing A.I. start-up, Project Prometheus. The move reflects continued interest from major technology figures in supporting and accelerating A.I. development across various industries, potentially influencing the competitive landscape and innovation trajectories.

6. Uses and Risks of A.I. Personal Digital Assistants New artificial intelligence bots have expanded capabilities beyond basic conversation, now able to perform tasks such as editing documents, managing emails, and booking travel arrangements. However, experts warn these digital assistants also pose risks, including privacy concerns and the potential for errors or misuse. As A.I. tools become more integrated into personal and professional tasks, understanding their limitations and safeguards remains important.
