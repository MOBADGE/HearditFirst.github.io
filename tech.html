Technology News Briefing – March 10, 2026

1. Parents Seek Stronger Online Safety Amid AI Concerns A growing group of grieving parents is calling for improved online protections, blaming advanced chatbots for worsening risks to children. These parents, who previously held social media platforms responsible for factors linked to their children’s deaths, express concern that artificial intelligence agents interacting with minors may pose new dangers. Their advocacy reflects ongoing debates about how technology companies should manage AI tools to prevent harm, especially to vulnerable users.

2. Meta Acquires Moltbook to Advance AI Bot Ecosystem Meta announced the acquisition of Moltbook, a social network designed exclusively for artificial intelligence bots. Created by Matt Schlicht, Moltbook played a role in shaping Silicon Valley’s focus on AI agents. Schlicht will join Meta’s Superintelligence Lab as part of the deal. The move highlights Meta’s interest in furthering AI-driven communications, although it raises questions about moderation and interaction between AI entities within a corporate framework.

3. YouTube Launches Deepfake Reporting Tool for Public Figures In response to growing concerns about deepfake videos—synthetic media that manipulate real images—YouTube introduced a new tool enabling public figures to report such content more effectively. This development comes as social media platforms face mounting pressure to curb the spread of deceptive videos that can damage reputations or misinform the public. The tool aims to help YouTube identify and address manipulated content while balancing free expression considerations.

4. Former Meta AI Chief’s Start-Up Valued at $3.5 Billion Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs (AMI Labs), founded by Yann LeCun and fellow former Meta researchers, secured over $1 billion in funding, valuing the company at $3.5 billion. The start-up focuses on developing new AI technologies outside of large corporate infrastructures. This substantial investment underlines continued investor confidence in independent AI research efforts and the ongoing competition in the field.

5. Boeing Reports Wiring Issue Delaying Some 737 Max Deliveries Boeing disclosed that a wiring problem would delay deliveries of certain 737 Max jets, though the company maintained it expects to meet its 2026 sales target of approximately 500 planes. The production issue adds to ongoing challenges for the manufacturer amid a recovering aviation market but does not appear to significantly impact overall business performance this year.

6. U.S. Solar Installations Decline Amid Political Opposition A recent report found that while solar power added more capacity to U.S. electric grids than any other energy source in 2025, total installations fell by 14 percent compared to the previous year. Analysts attribute part of this decline to policies and rhetoric opposing clean energy during the Trump administration. The data suggests that political factors remain influential in shaping the renewable energy sector’s growth in the United States.
