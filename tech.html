Technology Briefing – April 14, 2026

Amazon Acquires Globalstar to Boost Satellite Internet Service Amazon has agreed to buy Globalstar, a satellite communications firm, for $10.8 billion. The move is aimed at expanding Amazon’s satellite internet capabilities to better compete with existing services such as Elon Musk’s Starlink. By acquiring Globalstar’s satellite assets and expertise, Amazon seeks to enhance coverage and capacity for its internet offerings. This acquisition highlights the growing interest among major technology companies in satellite-based internet as a way to reach underserved areas and diversify connectivity options.

Attack on OpenAI CEO’s Residence Linked to AI Concerns Authorities in Texas arrested a 20-year-old man accused of throwing a homemade bomb at the gate of OpenAI CEO Sam Altman’s home. Police reported the suspect had compiled a list of prominent artificial intelligence leaders. He had also written extensively about perceived risks posed by AI technology. The incident draws attention to the social tensions unfolding around AI development and concerns about personal security for key figures in the field. It underlines the challenge of addressing public fear and misinformation amid rapid AI advances.

Elon Musk’s Verified Accounts Appear on TikTok and Instagram Verified social media accounts bearing Elon Musk’s name have recently appeared on TikTok and Instagram. This development comes as the billionaire is preparing to take his aerospace company, SpaceX, public. Although Musk primarily uses X (formerly Twitter) for communications, establishing verified profiles on other platforms may be part of a broader strategy to engage new audiences. His presence on popular visual and short-form video platforms could influence public relations and investor outreach ahead of the SpaceX offering.

Global AI Military Competition Intensifies Governments around the world, including the United States, China, and Russia, are rapidly expanding their use of AI technologies in military applications. The buildup includes AI-augmented weapons and autonomous systems, raising concerns among experts who compare the current dynamics to the early nuclear arms race. This competition has implications for global security, ethics in warfare, and international arms control. Increased AI militarization may prompt discussions on regulation and transparency at diplomatic levels.

Volkswagen Halts Electric Vehicle Production at Tennessee Plant Volkswagen announced it will stop producing electric vehicles (EVs) at its Chattanooga, Tennessee, plant, shifting focus back to gasoline-powered models. The decision reflects a broader industry trend where some automakers are reassessing EV strategies amid changing market conditions, supply chain issues, and economic considerations. Volkswagen’s move may influence US EV manufacturing plans and the pace of the transition toward electric transportation in the near term.

Meta Introduces New AI Model Muse Spark Meta has unveiled Muse Spark, a new AI model developed by its Superintelligence Lab. The model reportedly improves on Meta’s previous iterations but still trails competitors in tasks related to coding. Muse Spark represents another step in Meta’s efforts to advance AI capabilities internally. The company’s progress in AI research will impact its product offerings and competitiveness in the rapidly evolving technology landscape.
