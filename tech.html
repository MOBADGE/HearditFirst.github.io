Technology Briefing – March 31, 2026

Instagram Drops “PG-13” Label Amid Legal Pressure Instagram recently ended its use of a “PG-13” branding for its teen users after facing pressure from the Motion Picture Association (MPA). The social media platform had adopted the movie rating term to indicate a safer environment for younger audiences. However, the MPA contended that the use of their rating could be misleading and was not authorized. Instagram’s withdrawal of the label reflects ongoing challenges in how digital platforms communicate content guidelines and address youth safety.

Russian Internet Restrictions and Evasion Tactics In Russia, government efforts to control online content have intensified with increased investment in censorship technologies. Despite these measures, many Russian internet users continue to seek methods to bypass restrictions, such as virtual private networks (VPNs) and proxy services. This ongoing cycle of restrictions and evasion underscores the difficulty of regulating internet access and the persistent demand for open communication channels within restricted environments.

State-Level Actions on Artificial Intelligence Regulation Several U.S. states, including California and Utah, are moving forward with their own regulatory approaches to artificial intelligence, even as former President Donald Trump called for a halt to such efforts. California Governor Gavin Newsom recently issued an executive order requiring companies that contract with the state to implement safety and privacy protections related to AI technologies. These state initiatives highlight varying views on AI governance and indicate a patchwork regulatory landscape emerging across the country.

OpenAI Secures Additional $12 Billion Funding OpenAI announced it raised an additional $12 billion in its latest funding round, bringing the total raised to $122 billion and valuing the company at approximately $730 billion. The fresh capital is expected to support further development of AI technologies and expansion efforts. This substantial funding reflects continued investor interest in AI startups and the growing role such companies play in technology innovation.

Whoop Raises $575 Million to Expand Health Device Market Wearable health technology company Whoop has raised $575 million, pushing its valuation to $10 billion. Known for partnerships with professional athletes like LeBron James and Cristiano Ronaldo, Whoop aims to broaden its appeal to general consumers interested in health and fitness tracking. The investment signals sustained interest in health-focused wearables as consumers and investors look for data-driven personal wellness solutions.

Looking Back: An Early Apple Employee Reflects on 50 Years Chris Espinosa, one of Apple’s earliest employees, recently shared his experiences over five decades, starting as a teenager demonstrating Apple’s first computers. His reflections provide insight into the company’s growth and transformation since its modest beginnings in Steve Jobs’s family home. This retrospective sheds light on the evolution of personal computing and tech company culture over the past half-century.
