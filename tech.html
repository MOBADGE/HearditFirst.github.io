**White House and Anthropic Discuss AI Model Safety**

On April 17, the White House held a meeting with Anthropic, the AI company behind the recently launched Mythos model. U.S. officials described the talks as “productive,” focusing on addressing concerns about Mythos’s capabilities and potential security risks. The meeting underscores ongoing government efforts to engage directly with AI developers to ensure that advanced systems are deployed responsibly. Given the increasing influence of AI technologies on national security and public policy, such dialogues aim to find a balance between innovation and regulation.

**Tracking the Growth of AI Development**

A nonprofit organization, METR, has released a chart capturing the rapid expansion of large AI systems. The visualization has drawn widespread attention across the tech industry for its clear depiction of how quickly AI models have grown in scale and complexity in recent years. This measurement offers a tangible way for researchers, regulators, and stakeholders to understand the pace of AI advancement and its potential implications. As AI continues to develop, quantifying growth helps inform discussions about safety, ethics, and investment in the field.

**Cerebras Files for Public Offering Amid AI Market Activity**

Silicon Valley chip maker Cerebras Technologies has submitted paperwork to go public, joining other major AI-related companies such as SpaceX, Anthropic, and OpenAI in preparing for initial public offerings. Cerebras specializes in producing chips designed to accelerate AI computations, a critical component for the performance of modern AI systems. The move highlights increased investor interest in foundational AI hardware as the broader technology ecosystem expands. The wave of planned IPOs reflects both confidence in AI’s commercial potential and the growing attention to its infrastructure needs.

**Rise of AI-Generated Fake Avatars on Social Media**

Reports indicate a surge in AI-created fake influencer profiles on platforms including TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, and YouTube. These avatars, many showing pro-Trump sentiments, appear designed to attract conservative audiences. The use of AI to generate realistic but artificial social media personas raises concerns about manipulation and misinformation, especially during politically sensitive periods. Social media companies and regulators face challenges in identifying and managing such accounts to preserve authentic discourse.

**Pentagon Engages Automakers to Streamline Defense Production**

The U.S. Department of Defense has initiated talks with General Motors and Ford to explore their potential roles in producing certain military parts. This step responds to Pentagon concerns over the slow speed and high costs associated with current weapons manufacturing processes. Leveraging the production capabilities of established automotive companies could provide efficiencies and scale not currently available in defense supply chains. The collaboration may mark a shift toward integrating commercial manufacturing expertise into national defense efforts.

**Korean Theaters Adopt AI Translation for Global Reach**

To expand the international appeal of Korean stage productions, cultural organizations and producers are employing AI-powered glasses that provide real-time language translation for theatergoers. This technology aims to overcome language barriers that have limited the global reach of Korea’s performing arts. By enabling audiences to access performances with immediate translated subtitles, the initiative hopes to support cultural export strategies and broaden participation in Korean arts worldwide. The approach illustrates practical applications of AI in the creative sector beyond entertainment technology.
