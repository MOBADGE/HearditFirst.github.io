**Iranian Media Uses AI to Portray Confident War Narrative**

Iranian state television and social media outlets have been projecting a defiant stance on the ongoing conflict, despite reports of significant military setbacks. Some of this content incorporates artificial intelligence–generated imagery and messages. This use of AI tools aims to shape public perception domestically and internationally by presenting a more resilient image than the situation on the ground suggests. The reliance on AI in propaganda reflects growing integration of new technologies in information warfare and raises questions about the authenticity of state-controlled messaging.

**Tech Giants Commit to Funding Energy Infrastructure for AI Data Centers**

Several leading artificial intelligence companies, including Google, Microsoft, and OpenAI, have pledged to finance the development of new power plants and upgrades to the electric grid necessary to support their expanding data centers. This commitment addresses concerns about the substantial and growing energy demands of AI workloads. By investing directly in energy infrastructure, these companies aim to secure sufficient and stable power supplies while responding to criticism about the environmental footprint of AI operations. The move highlights the intersection of technology growth and energy policy challenges.

**Senators Raise Security Concerns Over Intel’s Use of Blacklisted Chinese Tools**

A bipartisan group of U.S. senators has questioned Intel over its use of manufacturing equipment sourced from a Chinese company previously blacklisted for national security reasons. Intel is partly government-owned following recent investment to boost domestic chip production. Lawmakers expressed unease that the reliance on foreign technology from a restricted supplier could expose sensitive semiconductor manufacturing processes to surveillance or interference. This issue underscores ongoing tensions in securing supply chains amid geopolitical rivalries with China.

**OpenAI Revises Pentagon Agreement to Limit Surveillance Use**

OpenAI has amended its contract with the U.S. Department of Defense to include new restrictions preventing its artificial intelligence technologies from being used for mass surveillance of American citizens. The updated agreement came in response to privacy concerns about government applications of AI. By explicitly barring surveillance activities targeting the domestic population, OpenAI seeks to balance supporting national security efforts with protecting civil liberties. The revision demonstrates growing caution as AI firms engage with military projects.

**Automakers Face Pressure as Chinese Firms Lead in Electric and Autonomous Vehicles**

Traditional car manufacturers like Ford and General Motors face increasing challenges to remain competitive as Chinese automakers and technology companies make rapid advances in electric vehicle (EV) and self-driving technologies. Experts warn that established U.S. makers risk losing market share and technological leadership if they cannot accelerate innovation. The shift toward EVs and autonomous driving represents a critical turning point for the automotive industry, with significant economic and strategic implications. The situation reflects a broader trend of technological competition between countries.

**Elon Musk Defends His Social Media Statements Amid Shareholder Lawsuit**

Elon Musk testified in a shareholder lawsuit regarding his public posts before acquiring Twitter (now rebranded as X) in 2022. He argued that he did not anticipate his statements would have a material impact on the company’s share price. The case examines the influence of executive communications on investor decisions and corporate accountability. Musk’s defense highlights ongoing scrutiny of how information disseminated by high-profile figures can affect financial markets.
