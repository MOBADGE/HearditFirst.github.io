Updated: April 15, 2026

**Amazon Acquires Globalstar for $10.8 Billion to Expand Satellite Internet** Amazon announced its acquisition of Globalstar, a satellite communications firm, for $10.8 billion. This move aims to enhance Amazon’s satellite internet service offerings, positioning the company to better compete with existing providers such as Elon Musk’s Starlink. By integrating Globalstar’s satellite infrastructure and technology, Amazon seeks to improve coverage and network capacity. This acquisition highlights the growing importance of satellite internet as a means to provide reliable broadband access, particularly in underserved areas.

**Security Incident at OpenAI Chief’s Residence Raises Concerns** Authorities detained a 20-year-old man in Texas for allegedly throwing a homemade explosive device at the home of Sam Altman, CEO of OpenAI. Reports indicate the individual maintained a list of other prominent leaders in artificial intelligence, reflecting anxieties around AI developments. The case has renewed discussions about the potential risks faced by public figures in AI and underscores the need for careful consideration of security protocols amid increased public attention to the technology.

**OpenAI Releases Limited Version of GPT-5.4-Cyber Focused on Security** OpenAI introduced GPT-5.4-Cyber, a version of its AI model intended specifically for cybersecurity applications, such as identifying software vulnerabilities. Access to this version is being restricted to a select group of trusted companies, following a pattern established by other AI developers like Anthropic. This limited release reflects ongoing efforts to balance the benefits of advanced AI tools against potential misuse or unintended consequences, particularly in sensitive domains like software security.

**The Growing Global AI Military Competition** Reports highlight an intensifying international competition among countries including the United States, China, and Russia in developing artificial intelligence technologies for military use. This buildup involves AI-backed weapons and systems, drawing comparisons to early stages of the nuclear arms race. The increasing military focus on AI raises questions about regulation, ethical implications, and global security, as the technology could significantly alter defense strategies and capabilities.

**Understanding AI ‘Jagged Intelligence’ and Interpretability** A recent analysis introduces the concept of “jagged intelligence” to describe how AI systems differ from human cognition. Unlike human intelligence, AI excels in specific, narrow tasks rather than general reasoning. This perspective may help clarify which jobs AI is likely to affect. Additionally, researchers are emphasizing the importance of “interpretability,” or understanding how AI makes decisions, which is crucial for building trustworthy systems. Efforts to demystify AI processes could influence how the technology is adopted across various sectors.

**Virtual Reality Tools Help Older Adults Combat Social Isolation** New developments in virtual reality (VR) technology are providing older adults, particularly in senior living communities, with opportunities for shared experiences and social interaction. Tailored VR programs enable users to connect, participate in group activities, and alleviate feelings of isolation. As social isolation among seniors is a significant health concern, these technology-driven solutions offer a potential avenue to improve quality of life and mental well-being without relying on physical proximity.
