Technology News Briefing – March 31, 2026

Instagram Removes ‘PG-13’ Label for Teen Users Instagram has stopped using the “PG-13” movie rating in its marketing aimed at teenage users. This change follows legal pressure from the Motion Picture Association, which objected to the social media company applying a film rating to its platform. Instagram had initially adopted this labeling to suggest a safer environment for teens but faced criticism that the rating was misleading and not officially recognized for online content. The withdrawal highlights ongoing challenges social media companies face in regulatory and public scrutiny when promoting their platforms to younger audiences.

Russian Internet Restrictions and Circumvention Efforts In Russia, government efforts to tighten internet censorship are intensifying, with increased spending on technologies to monitor and restrict access. Despite these measures, many Russian internet users continue to seek ways to bypass restrictions, using tools such as virtual private networks (VPNs) and proxy servers. The dynamic creates an ongoing cycle between government-imposed limitations and user-driven evasion methods. This situation underscores the complexities of digital information control in authoritarian contexts and the persistence of online openness despite attempts to limit it.

U.S. States Advance AI Regulation Against Federal Direction Several U.S. states, including California and Utah, are moving forward with regulations aimed at artificial intelligence technologies, even as former President Donald Trump has directed states to halt such efforts. California’s governor recently issued an executive order mandating safety and privacy protections for AI systems used by state contractors. These developments indicate a growing divergence between state and federal approaches to managing AI, reflecting concerns about potential risks associated with the technology. State-level actions may set standards that affect AI deployment more broadly across the country.

OpenAI Secures Additional $12 Billion in Funding OpenAI announced it raised another $12 billion as part of its ongoing funding round, bringing the total to $122 billion. This latest investment places the company’s valuation at approximately $730 billion. OpenAI’s ability to attract substantial capital reflects the continued interest and high expectations for its artificial intelligence technologies. The sizable funding will support research, development, and scaling efforts, but also raises questions about the concentration of financial and technical resources within a small number of AI companies.

Whoop Raises $575 Million to Expand Wearable Health Market Whoop, a company producing fitness and health tracking devices, has raised $575 million in a recent funding round, valuing the company at $10 billion. Known for attracting investments from high-profile athletes such as LeBron James and Cristiano Ronaldo, Whoop is now targeting a broader consumer base interested in health monitoring. The influx of capital will likely support product development and marketing initiatives aimed at expanding its presence beyond elite sports. This reflects ongoing interest in wearable technology as a tool for individualized health insights.

Summary Today’s technology news highlights a mix of regulatory, commercial, and societal issues: social media companies adapting to legal challenges concerning youth audiences; efforts and resistance around internet censorship in Russia; state-led action on AI governance in the U.S.; large-scale investment inflows into AI and health wearables; and shifts in how digital platforms position themselves amid public and political pressures. These stories collectively illustrate how industry, government, and users continue to navigate changing digital landscapes worldwide.
