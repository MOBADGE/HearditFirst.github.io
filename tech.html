Technology News Briefing – March 7, 2026

1. OpenAI and Anthropic’s Struggle Over Pentagon Business Two leading artificial intelligence companies, OpenAI and Anthropic, are locked in a tense rivalry involving military contracts with the U.S. Department of Defense. Both firms have been negotiating with the Pentagon, but issues around trust and security risks have complicated the process. The Pentagon recently labeled Anthropic a “supply chain risk,” which could bar the startup from government work. Anthropic has responded by threatening legal action. This dispute highlights challenges in overseeing advanced AI technologies within national security while balancing transparency and risk management.

2. Pentagon’s Supply Chain Risk Label Sparks Legal Dispute Anthropic’s designation as a supply chain risk by the Defense Department raises questions about how government agencies evaluate security threats from technology providers. The label could prevent Anthropic from participating in Pentagon contracts critical to its growth. The company plans to sue the government, arguing that the designation is unfair and damaging. This case may set precedent for how emerging AI companies are vetted and regulated in sensitive government sectors moving forward.

3. Settling with the SEC: Justin Sun and Cryptocurrency The Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) has reached a settlement with Justin Sun, an investor connected to the Trump family’s cryptocurrency ventures. Despite the serious nature of the securities violations alleged, Sun denied wrongdoing. Settling the case avoids a prolonged court battle and underscores ongoing regulatory scrutiny of crypto markets and their key investors. The outcome may influence future enforcement approaches and investor behavior in digital asset industries.

4. Rising Demand for AI Data Centers Spurs Land and Power Deals As AI computing needs grow, specialized data centers require significant electricity and land. Brian Janous, a former Microsoft executive, is leading efforts through Cloverleaf to secure and package these resources for tech companies. This trend shows how infrastructure considerations have become central to AI development at scale. Access to reliable power and real estate now shapes competitiveness and investment strategies in AI technology deployment.

5. Changing Nature of Conflict: Iran’s Use of Drones Iran has been deploying waves of inexpensive Shahed drones across the Persian Gulf. The United States has responded with its own versions as part of ongoing hostilities. This dynamic signals a shift in warfare tactics, emphasizing low-cost, unmanned aerial vehicles that can cause disruption without large conventional forces. The use of such drones illustrates evolving military strategies and raises new challenges for defense systems.

6. Developments in AI and Smart Homes While smart home technologies have struggled with adoption, companies like Amazon and Google are introducing AI-powered assistants intended to simplify setup and use. Products like Alexa+ and Gemini aim to address existing usability issues, though significant challenges remain. These efforts reflect ongoing attempts to integrate AI more seamlessly into everyday living environments, but the long-term success of smart homes depends on overcoming persistent technical and privacy concerns.
