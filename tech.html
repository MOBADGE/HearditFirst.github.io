New A.I. Start-Up Emphasizes Worker Augmentation

A new artificial intelligence company called Humans&, founded by former researchers from Anthropic, Google, and xAI, has emerged with a focus on empowering workers rather than replacing them. Valued at $4.48 billion, the start-up aims to develop AI tools designed to assist employees in their tasks, enhancing productivity without eliminating jobs. This approach contrasts with widespread concerns about AI-driven job displacement and signals a potential shift in how AI integration in workplaces might be managed.

Snap Settles Social Media Addiction Lawsuit

Snap, the parent company of Snapchat, has settled a lawsuit alleging that its social media platform causes addiction and related harms. The settlement allows Snap to avoid a significant trial that could have set a precedent by holding social media platforms liable for personal injuries attributed to their design. This outcome is important because it limits immediate legal challenges targeting platform design flaws and addiction, while the broader debate over social media's societal impact continues.

Growth of Prediction Markets

Online prediction markets such as Polymarket and Kalshi have seen a surge in activity, with billions of dollars traded as participants place bets on diverse real-world events, ranging from political outcomes to celebrity activities. These platforms offer a way to aggregate collective insights about future events, providing potentially useful data signals. Their growth reflects increased public interest in alternative forms of forecasting and digital finance.
