Meta Reduces Workforce to Focus on Artificial Intelligence

Meta announced it will lay off about 10 percent of its workforce, impacting approximately 8,000 employees. Additionally, the company plans to close 6,000 open job positions as it shifts its strategic focus toward artificial intelligence development. This move reflects Meta's prioritization of AI technologies amid growing industry competition but also signals broader challenges in managing costs and resources. Employees affected by the layoffs represent various departments, underscoring the scale of the restructuring.

Legal Battle Between Elon Musk and OpenAI Founder Begins

A significant legal dispute is entering the courtroom as Elon Musk pursues a jury trial against Sam Altman’s OpenAI, seeking billions in damages. The case centers on claims related to the competitive dynamics of artificial intelligence development. The trial may have consequences for the AI field, potentially affecting business relationships and funding strategies among leading AI companies. The court's decision will be closely watched given the influence of both parties in the AI ecosystem.

OpenAI Releases GPT-5.5 with Emphasis on Cybersecurity

OpenAI introduced its latest language model, GPT-5.5, which offers improved capabilities over previous iterations. The company highlighted a more transparent approach to cybersecurity, aiming to differentiate itself from its main competitor, Anthropic. OpenAI’s strategy involves openly sharing information about model safety and security measures, a move that could shape industry norms for AI deployment and risk management.

Controversy Surrounds Anthropic’s New Mythos AI Model

Anthropic’s release of the Mythos AI model has caused concern among global financial and intelligence agencies. The model’s capabilities have prompted emergency security reviews and led Anthropic to carefully control who gets access. Authorities worry about potential risks associated with the technology, such as misuse or destabilization. This situation raises questions about governance and oversight for powerful AI systems.

Intel Reports Strong Earnings Fueled by AI Demand

Intel’s latest quarterly report shows a 7 percent revenue increase, reaching $13.6 billion and surpassing Wall Street expectations by over $1 billion. The growth is largely attributed to increased demand for AI-related semiconductors and hardware. Intel’s performance highlights the ongoing economic impact of AI on the chip manufacturing sector, with hardware companies benefiting as AI applications expand.

Emerging Phishing Scam Exploits Digital Invitation Platforms

Security experts have identified a new phishing campaign that mimics legitimate online invitation services such as Paperless Post, Evite, and Punchbowl. Hackers use fake invitations to gain unauthorized access to users’ devices. The scam underscores the need for increased vigilance when interacting with unexpected online invites, as cybercriminals continue to exploit trusted platforms to spread malware or steal personal information. Users are advised to verify invitations carefully before opening links or attachments.
