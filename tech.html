California Wealth Tax Proposal Raises Concerns Among Billionaires

A new proposal to introduce a wealth tax in California has surfaced as a potential ballot measure for the upcoming November election. While the measure's future on the ballot remains uncertain, prominent billionaires such as Peter Thiel and Larry Page are reportedly considering relocating to avoid potential new tax liabilities. The proposal aims to tax assets of the ultra-wealthy, which could affect technology industry leaders based in California. The development matters for the state's technology sector, given the concentration of wealth and innovation there, and it may influence decisions about residency and investment.

Their bid highlights the increasing involvement of tech billionaires in the media landscape, with potential implications for the control and future strategy of one of the largest American entertainment companies. The outcome could shape media ownership and content distribution in a shifting industry.

Gmail Introduces Address Change Without Data Loss

Google announced that Gmail users will soon be able to change their email addresses while retaining all existing messages and account data. The company said this feature would initially roll out to some users before expanding availability. Importantly, previous email addresses will remain active, preventing disruption in communication. This update addresses a long-standing user complaint about the rigidity of email addresses and may simplify managing online identities.
