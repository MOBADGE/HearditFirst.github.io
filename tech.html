Technology News Briefing – March 31, 2026

Instagram Drops ‘PG-13’ Marketing for Teens Instagram has discontinued its use of the “PG-13” label in promoting its platform to teenage users. The social media company faced legal pressure from the Motion Picture Association, which objected to the rating’s application outside of its established movie context. Instagram’s initial use of the rating intended to signal a moderated experience for younger users, but the move raised questions about appropriateness and regulatory overreach. Removing this label reflects ongoing challenges tech companies face in branding and regulating content for minors in a digital environment.

States Continue AI Regulation Despite Presidential Pushback Several U.S. states, including California and Utah, are advancing laws and policies to regulate artificial intelligence systems, defying an executive order from former President Trump that discouraged such state-level action. California Governor Gavin Newsom recently issued an executive order mandating safety and privacy measures for AI companies that contract with the state government. The effort signals growing state-level involvement in defining guardrails for AI technology, highlighting the varied approaches across the country in addressing the risks and impacts of AI deployment.

OpenAI Secures Additional $12 Billion in Funding OpenAI has added $12 billion to its latest funding round, bringing the total raised in this round to $122 billion and valuing the company at approximately $730 billion. This substantial influx of capital underscores continued investor interest in AI development and application. The funding will likely support further advancements in AI capabilities and business expansion, maintaining OpenAI’s prominent position in the industry.

Whoop Raises $575 Million with Expanded Audience Focus Wearable health technology maker Whoop announced a $575 million funding round, elevating its company valuation to $10 billion. The company, which counts elite athletes such as LeBron James and Cristiano Ronaldo among its investors, is aiming to broaden its customer base to include general health enthusiasts. This growth reflects the increasing consumer interest in health monitoring and personalized data-driven wellness tools beyond professional sports.

Russian Internet Restrictions Spur Circumvention Efforts In Russia, government efforts to tighten online content controls have led internet users to seek various methods to evade censorship technologies imposed by the Kremlin. The ongoing cycle of restrictive measures and evasion tactics illustrates the persistent tension between state control over information and individual access to open internet resources. This dynamic continues to affect digital communication and information flow within the country.

California’s AI Executive Order Emphasizes Safety and Privacy Governor Gavin Newsom’s executive order requires artificial intelligence companies contracting with California to implement safety and privacy guardrails. The directive represents a focused attempt by a major state government to ensure accountability and mitigate risks associated with AI technologies used in public services. The order aligns with broader trends of increased regulatory scrutiny and the need to balance innovation with consumer protection.
