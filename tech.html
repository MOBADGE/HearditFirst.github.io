**Global Competition Intensifies Over Military Use of Artificial Intelligence**

China, the United States, Russia, and other nations are increasing efforts to develop artificial intelligence (AI) systems for military applications, including autonomous weapons and support technologies. Experts note that this build-up resembles early stages of the nuclear arms race, highlighting potential risks related to stability and security. Governments are investing heavily to avoid falling behind rivals, raising concerns among policymakers about regulation and control of AI in warfare. The rivalry underscores the need for international dialogue on AI military ethics and arms control.

**Security Incident at OpenAI CEO’s Residence**

A Molotov cocktail was thrown at the San Francisco home of Sam Altman, CEO of OpenAI, causing fire damage to the property’s exterior gate. Police reported no injuries, and Altman’s presence at the time was not confirmed. Authorities arrested a suspect in connection with the attack. The incident highlights the rising tensions and public scrutiny facing leaders in AI development, as debates about the technology’s impact continue to attract passionate, sometimes volatile, reactions.

**Volkswagen Scales Back Electric Vehicle Production in Tennessee**

Volkswagen announced it will end electric vehicle (EV) production at its Chattanooga, Tennessee plant. The company cited a shift in strategy favoring gasoline-powered models due to changing market conditions and consumer demand. This move marks a reversal from earlier commitments to expand EV manufacturing and reflects broader uncertainty in the automotive sector about the pace and scale of EV adoption. Industry watchers see this as part of an ongoing recalibration among carmakers balancing emission regulations and profitability.

**Gen Z's Mixed Views on Artificial Intelligence**

A recent Gallup study reveals that while about half of Generation Z adults use AI tools, their attitudes toward the technology have grown more negative. Many report feelings of anger and diminished hope about AI's role in society. This shift suggests growing concerns among young people about AI’s implications for jobs, privacy, and social equity. The findings point to a need for more effective communication and education on AI risks and benefits tailored to younger users’ perspectives.

**Anthropic Faces Legal Challenges Over Pentagon ‘Supply Chain Risk’ Label**

Anthropic, an AI startup, suffered a legal setback when a federal court denied its request to remove a “supply chain risk” designation imposed by the U.S. Department of Defense. The label restricts Anthropic’s involvement in certain government contracts, related to concerns about the use of AI in national security. This decision underscores ongoing tensions between innovation in AI development and government efforts to manage potential security threats associated with emerging technologies.

---

**Advances and Cautions in AI Cybersecurity Applications**

Companies such as Anthropic and OpenAI are increasingly using AI to bolster cybersecurity defenses amid rising cyberattack sophistication. Anthropic recently unveiled Mythos, an AI model aimed at preventing cyber threats, though it remains in limited release while companies evaluate its effectiveness. At the same time, hackers are incorporating AI to conduct faster and more complex attacks, creating a cycle where AI is both a tool for offense and defense in cybersecurity. The dynamic highlights the continuing challenges in securing digital infrastructure in an AI-driven environment.
