Iranian Activists Use Starlink to Counter Internet Shutdowns

In response to government efforts to cut off internet access during protests, activists in Iran have relied on Starlink satellite internet systems to maintain online communications. Over several years, they smuggled in this technology to avoid complete disconnection and reach the outside world during digital blackouts. This approach has complicated government attempts to impose information control, demonstrating how satellite internet can provide alternative connectivity in restrictive environments. The Iranian case highlights the growing role of satellite broadband in preserving communication during crises, raising questions about internet governance and state control.

Google has introduced Gemini, an artificial intelligence assistant integrated within Gmail, designed to help users manage their inboxes more efficiently.
