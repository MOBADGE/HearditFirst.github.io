**Prediction Markets Gain Popularity**

Prediction markets, platforms where users bet on the outcomes of various events, have seen a notable increase in activity and financial volume. Sites such as Polymarket and Kalshi facilitate trading on a wide range of topics, including entertainment events like the wedding date of pop star Taylor Swift and political occurrences such as election results. This growing interest highlights how prediction markets are becoming mainstream tools for individuals to speculate and gauge public sentiment. The increased liquidity and attention could influence how information is aggregated and analyzed in areas such as politics and finance.

**OpenAI Introduces Ads in ChatGPT**

OpenAI announced it will begin placing advertisements within the free version of its popular language model chatbot, ChatGPT. This rollout is set to take place gradually over the coming weeks. The introduction of ads marks a significant shift for OpenAI, which has largely relied on subscription fees and partnerships to support its product. Serving ads to users of the free tier may generate additional revenue but could also affect user experience and raise questions about data privacy and content moderation in AI-driven platforms.

**Starlink Satellites Aid Iranian Activists Amid Internet Blackouts**

In response to government efforts to restrict internet access, activists in Iran have employed the Starlink satellite internet system to maintain connectivity. Over several years, they prepared by smuggling in Starlink terminals, which provide independent access to the internet via satellites. This tactic has made it more difficult for authorities to enforce digital shutdowns during periods of unrest. The use of satellite internet underscores the ongoing challenges governments face in controlling digital communications and the evolving methods citizens use to preserve access to information.

**Energy Costs and AI Infrastructure Under Scrutiny**

The rising electricity demand from data centers supporting artificial intelligence applications has contributed to higher energy bills for consumers. This surge has prompted former President Donald Trump and various state officials to call for measures to prevent AI-related operations from inflating costs. At the same time, lawmakers and industry leaders are debating whether data centers should be subject to increased energy charges. Despite numerous proposals, no consensus has been reached on how to balance the energy needs of AI infrastructure with broader economic impacts. The issue highlights the intersection of technological growth, environmental concerns, and regulatory challenges.
