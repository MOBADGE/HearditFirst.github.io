Energy Demand and Power Outages After Winter Storms

Energy experts have explained why power outages often occur not during a winter storm itself, but in the hours or days afterward. Accumulation of snow and ice can lead to fallen trees and damaged power lines as conditions change, combined with a surge in electricity demand when people return home and heat their houses. This timing creates stress on energy infrastructure that can prompt outages after the weather has cleared. Understanding this pattern is important for consumers and utilities in preparing for and responding to winter storms, improving resilience, and managing expectations about restoration times.
