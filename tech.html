Technology Briefing – April 16, 2026

1. Rethinking Artificial Intelligence: The Concept of Jagged Intelligence A recent discussion has emerged around the idea of "jagged intelligence" as a way to better understand artificial intelligence (AI). Unlike traditional comparisons that measure AI against human intelligence as a whole, jagged intelligence views AI as specialized with uneven strengths across different tasks. This perspective helps clarify what AI can do well and where it struggles, offering insight into which jobs might be more vulnerable to automation. Understanding these nuances could guide more informed decisions about the future of work and AI integration in various industries.

2. The Enduring Role of Human Interaction in the Age of AI While AI tools continue to simplify many tasks, certain human skills—particularly those involving negotiation, persuasion, and emotional assurance—are becoming increasingly important in the workplace. A recent analysis highlights that jobs relying on interpersonal dynamics may be more resistant to automation. This shift underscores how human factors remain critical even as technology advances, suggesting that social and emotional capabilities will continue to play a key role in many professions.

3. Amazon's Acquisition of Globalstar to Expand Satellite Internet Amazon has announced its $10.8 billion acquisition of Globalstar, a satellite communications company. The move is a step toward broadening Amazon’s satellite internet ambitions to better compete in the growing space-based internet market, notably against Elon Musk’s Starlink service. With this purchase, Amazon aims to enhance its ability to provide broadband access in underserved and remote areas, which could have implications for connectivity and digital inclusion worldwide.

4. Security Concerns Surrounding AI Leadership Authorities in Texas have detained a 20-year-old man accused of attacking the home of OpenAI CEO Sam Altman using a homemade bomb. The individual reportedly maintained a list of AI executives and expressed concerns about AI risks. This incident highlights ongoing security challenges faced by prominent figures in AI development amid public anxiety about the technology’s broader implications.

5. Corporate Shifts: Allbirds’ Transition Toward AI Allbirds, known primarily for sustainable footwear, announced plans to pivot away from its original business model following a recent $39 million sale of its shoe operations. The company intends to invest in high-performance computing hardware and rebrand itself as NewBird AI. This strategic change signals how companies from various sectors are entering the AI field, reflecting shifting priorities and market opportunities.

6. Leadership Change at Ford’s Electric Vehicle Division Doug Field, who returned to Ford in 2021 to lead its electric vehicle (EV) initiatives, is leaving the company. Field brought experience from Tesla and Apple and was instrumental in developing Ford’s new EV models and software systems. His departure may influence Ford’s future direction in the competitive electric vehicle market, where leadership continuity is often critical for product development and innovation.
