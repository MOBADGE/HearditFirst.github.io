Tim Cook Steps Down as Apple CEO

Apple announced on April 21, 2026, that Tim Cook will step down as its Chief Executive Officer. Cook, who has led the company since 2011 following Steve Jobs, will be succeeded by John Ternus, the current head of hardware engineering. Ternus is an Apple veteran known primarily for overseeing the development of hardware products such as the iPhone and Mac. This leadership change marks the first new CEO appointment at Apple in 15 years, signaling a potential shift in the company’s direction. Cook’s tenure saw Apple become one of the world’s most valuable companies, and his departure comes at a time when the firm faces new challenges in innovation and competition.

Amazon’s Major Investment in Anthropic

Amazon revealed plans to invest up to $25 billion in Anthropic, an artificial intelligence company, reflecting its commitment to strengthening AI capabilities. In addition, Anthropic has agreed to spend $100 billion on Amazon technologies for building and deploying AI systems. This partnership highlights Amazon’s strategy to embed AI deeply into its infrastructure and services, enhancing offerings across cloud computing, retail, and other domains. The deal also underscores the intensifying race among tech companies to develop advanced AI, which could influence both commercial products and broader technology ecosystems.

California Accuses Amazon of Price Fixing

In a legal development, the state of California filed a lawsuit against Amazon accusing the company of pressuring major brands, including Levi’s and Hanes, to coordinate price increases with competing retailers. The complaint alleges Amazon sought to manipulate market prices, potentially harming consumers through inflated costs. This antitrust suit adds to growing scrutiny over large technology companies and their marketplace practices. The case may set a precedent for how states regulate e-commerce platforms and enforce competition laws in an era dominated by online retail.

Elon Musk’s Dispute with French Authorities

Elon Musk, CEO of the social media platform X, has refused to appear before French prosecutors investigating his company, further straining relations between Musk and European regulators. The refusal comes amid ongoing tensions over regulation and government oversight of tech firms operating in Europe. Musk’s stance may complicate legal proceedings in France and contributes to a broader pattern of resistance by some technology leaders to comply with regional rules. This dynamic raises questions about how governments will enforce regulations on multinational tech companies in the future.

Cerebras Files to Go Public Amid AI Industry Growth

Cerebras, a Silicon Valley company specializing in AI chips, has filed paperwork to go public. The move coincides with similar steps by several AI-focused firms, including SpaceX, Anthropic, and OpenAI, indicating a growing wave of initial public offerings in the sector. Cerebras produces specialized hardware designed to speed up AI computations, which are in high demand as AI applications expand. Its public offering reflects investor interest in AI infrastructure and signals increasing commercialization of the technology underpinning artificial intelligence development.
