**Rust 2 Steam Page Sparks Speculation and Ambiguity**

A partially visible Steam page for *Rust 2* surfaced recently, triggering excitement and confusion among fans of the original survival game. The page is accessible but flagged as suspicious by SteamDB, warning users to be cautious. The initial Reddit post promoting the page came from a newly created user, raising doubts about its authenticity. Shortly after, Facepunch COO Alistair McFarlane cryptically responded with "You saw nothing," further blurring the lines between tease and truth. Later, McFarlane admitted to occasionally stirring confusion on the official subreddit, leaving the reality of *Rust 2* uncertain. Although Facepunch founder Garry Newman previously mentioned *Rust 2* would be developed on a non-Unity engine, no official confirmation or details have been released. This ambiguous situation matters as it hints at a potential sequel to one of the most influential survival games, but the lack of clarity leaves fans and the industry guessing.

**Europa Universalis 5 Leaves Veteran Player Disillusioned**

A long-time fan of the *Europa Universalis* series shared a detailed reflection on their first extensive campaign in *Europa Universalis 5* (EU5), describing it as both captivating and disappointing. Unlike its predecessor EU4, EU5 features deeper economic and administrative systems with more internal micromanagement. However, the player found the lack of distinctive national traits and flavor—such as unique missions and idea groups—detracted from the experience. The gameplay felt less about broad conquest and more about managing rebellions and economies inwardly, which altered the series’ traditional appeal. Despite investing 93 hours, the player felt reluctant to continue and found their enjoyment of EU4 diminished as well. This critique highlights an important design challenge for Paradox Interactive: balancing depth with engaging, differentiated gameplay to maintain longtime fans while advancing the series.

**Castlevania: Belmont's Curse Avoids ‘Metroidvania’ Label While Embracing the Style**

Konami and developer Evil Empire provided deeper insights into *Castlevania: Belmont's Curse*, emphasizing its design as a 2D exploration action game inspired by *Castlevania: Symphony of the Night*. While the game clearly follows the exploration-focused platforming style known as "metroidvania," the team intentionally avoids using the term in official communications. Key developers repeatedly referenced exploration, intricate level design, and RPG-lite mechanics characteristic of the genre without naming it explicitly, likely to steer clear of Nintendo’s *Metroid* trademark. They also confirmed the game is not a roguelike, despite Evil Empire's past work in that genre. This approach demonstrates how legacy franchises navigate genre labels while aiming to meet fan expectations and maintain brand identity.

**Graveyard Keeper 2 Announced, Original Game Free on Steam**

Lazy Bear Games announced *Graveyard Keeper 2*, a sequel to the darkly comedic life simulation originally released in 2018. The new game maintains the macabre theme of managing a graveyard but adds tower defense elements and automation through an undead workforce. Players will engage in crafting, combat, and base-building to protect their town from zombies. In conjunction with the sequel’s reveal, the original *Graveyard Keeper* is now free to claim on Steam until April 13, offering a chance for new players to experience the series. This announcement reflects ongoing interest in niche simulation games blending humor and horror, and the free availability may help build anticipation for the sequel.

**Indie Survival Shooter Road to Vostok Secures Funding Quickly**

*Road to Vostok*, a hardcore survival shooter from solo Finnish developer Antti Leinonen, achieved notable early success after its Steam early access launch earlier this week. Leinonen reported that sales have secured the entire production budget for years of development within 24 hours. The game, praised for its realistic and unforgiving combat, currently ranks high on several regional Steam top sellers charts and holds a Very Positive user rating based on nearly 1,900 reviews. Planned updates include AI improvements, a quest system, and new maps, with a tentative full release timeline in two to four years. This rapid financial success demonstrates strong audience support for niche, intense survival experiences led by independent developers.
