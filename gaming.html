**FPS Genre Shows Signs of a Shift Toward Casual and Cooperative Play**

In 2025, the first-person shooter genre saw a marked change away from high-skill competitive games dominated by ranked modes and flashy cosmetic monetization. The success of *Battlefield 6*, which notably lacks ranked modes and emphasizes large-scale, chaotic gameplay, exemplifies this trend. Other shooters increasingly offered more casual or varied modes, such as *The Finals* introducing larger casual matches, *Rainbow Six Siege* adding respawn modes, and *Splitgate 2* reverting to classic arena shooter formats. Meanwhile, cooperative shooters like *Helldivers 2*, *Warhammer 40,000: Darktide*, and *Deep Rock Galactic* continued steady play. In addition, negative reactions to excessive and out-of-place cosmetic skins prompted developers to adjust their approaches. Overall, 2025 demonstrated that players are seeking FPS experiences that balance accessibility, variety, and quality without relying mainly on free-to-play models.
