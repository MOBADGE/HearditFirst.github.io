US Supreme Court Declines to Copyright AI-Generated Images

The US Supreme Court has refused to hear a case regarding copyright protections for AI-generated images, effectively affirming the US Copyright Office’s stance that such works cannot be copyrighted without human authorship. The case was brought by Dr. Stephen Thaler, who sought copyright for an AI-created image called *A Recent Entrance to Paradise*. The Copyright Office and lower courts rejected his claims on the basis that current law requires human creative input. This decision leaves the status quo in place, where purely AI-generated images are not protected by copyright, though works that involve human modification may still qualify. The ruling highlights ongoing complexities as AI-generated content becomes more common and raises questions on how intellectual property law will evolve.

Star Citizen Studio Discloses January Data Breach Amid Criticism

Cloud Imperium, the studio behind *Star Citizen*, revealed that it suffered a data breach on January 21 involving unauthorized access to backup systems containing users’ personal data. The affected information includes metadata, contact details, usernames, dates of birth, and names; financial data and passwords were not compromised. The studio said it acted promptly to contain the breach and refreshed security settings. However, the disclosure, which surfaced only this week via a pop-up in the game rather than direct email or social media communication, drew criticism from players who felt the announcement was too quiet and late. Concerns also remain about the vague use of “metadata” and potential risks for phishing attacks. Cloud Imperium advises affected users to change passwords and enable two-factor authentication as a precaution.

Highguard to Shut Down Following Weak Player Base

Wildlight Entertainment has announced that its free-to-play PvP shooter *Highguard* will shut down servers on March 12, little more than a month after launch. While the game initially attracted over 2 million players, concurrent user numbers quickly fell from a peak of nearly 100,000 to just a few hundred. The developer cited its inability to sustain a viable player base despite the team's effort and passion. The final update, expected soon, will add new features including a playable character and progression systems before closure. Highguard’s failure reflects the challenges new shooter titles face in a saturated market, particularly when initial momentum does not translate into long-term player retention.

Sims 4 Introduces Paid Marketplace and Creator Program

Electronic Arts has revealed a new *Sims 4 Marketplace*, launching on March 17 for PC and Mac, with console support to follow in the coming months. The Marketplace will allow players to purchase curated content packs called Maker Packs, created and sold by approved content creators. The Sims Maker Program will enable creators to earn a 30% revenue share on sales through this official platform. The in-game Marketplace uses a new virtual currency, Moola, while expansions and other standard content remain purchasable for real money. This initiative mirrors Bethesda’s Creation Club model and aims to provide a clearer and more official outlet for paid custom content, addressing longstanding issues with mod monetization. Applications for the Maker Program open on March 5.

Bungie Details First Season and Wipe System for Marathon

As *Marathon* launches on March 5, developer Bungie has shared plans for the game’s first season, “Death is the First Step,” running through June. It includes new story content, such as unlocking a derelict ship orbiting Tau Ceti, a new endgame zone, and a Ranked mode arriving in late March. Season 2 will follow from June to August with additional maps and systems. Bungie confirmed that progression wipes are mandatory every three months, resetting all player gear, contracts, faction status, and levels, with cosmetics and certain unlocks retained. This approach ensures a “clean slate” for players each season but may frustrate those who prefer to keep their progress. The wipe system contrasts with some peers by being compulsory rather than optional.

The site features a campfire, a marshmallow on a stick, and musical instruments including a banjo—a nod to one of *Outer Wilds*’ characters known for playing the banjo.
