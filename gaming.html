Banquet For Fools Offers a Unique, Note-Taking Driven RPG Experience

Banquet For Fools, released earlier this month by Hannah and Joseph Games, is an RPG that stands out for its deep emphasis on player note-taking. Unlike typical RPGs that provide quest logs and map markers, this game leaves much of the tracking and memorization to the player. It demands active engagement: players must manually record important information, such as locations and quest details, and even create custom spell icons.

The game's setting is an imaginative, harsh wilderness where survival relies on paying close attention to the environment and social cues. Combat blends real-time action with tactical pause, requiring careful observation and strategic control over a custom party. The design encourages a slow, attentive playstyle, immersing players in its world and challenges. Although this approach can lead to moments of frustration due to vague direction and demanding exploration, players who invest the effort find a rewarding, personalized experience.

Mars War: Logs — A Rough but Intriguing Sci-Fi RPG at 99 Cents

Mars War: Logs, a 2013 RPG from developer Spiders, is currently on sale for just 99 cents. While the game’s technical and narrative qualities are uneven, it presents an interesting alternative to mainstream RPGs, with a gritty sci-fi setting on a war-torn Mars. Players take on the role of Roy, a prisoner of war navigating hostile environments and factions in a resource-scarce future.

Combat is functional but limited, and voice acting is often unpolished. Nonetheless, the game’s choice-driven narrative and atmosphere provide some appeal, especially for those looking for a less polished but characterful experience. Its manageable length and affordability make it a low-risk option for RPG fans interested in rough-around-the-edges titles, particularly on the Steam Deck where it runs well.

Sand Extraction Shooter’s March Release Date Remains Unclear

Sand: Raiders of Sophie, a PvPvE extraction shooter featuring a customizable mechanical base, is slated for release this month on Steam. However, no exact launch date has been announced, causing growing uncertainty in its community. Official channels have acknowledged fans’ questions but have not provided concrete dates, explaining that final development milestones must be met before an official announcement.

Community managers have asked for patience, noting that a firm release date depends on the outcomes of the “final stretch” of work. This leaves the game in a state of ambiguity as March nears its end, with no confirmation on whether it will meet its projected launch window.

Lost Wiki: Kozlovka Blends Mystery and Database-Style Exploration

Lost Wiki: Kozlovka is a $5 mystery game that simulates investigation through a wiki-like database interface. The player uncovers a multi-layered story centered around an Eastern European town haunted by a strange tree and a history of corruption and cover-ups. Rather than challenging players with complex puzzles or distractions, it offers a straightforward, detail-oriented experience focused on careful reading and cross-referencing entries.

The game emphasizes piecing together fragmented narratives and identifying gaps in official information, mirroring real-world concerns about information control. Despite its simplicity, the game’s atmosphere and narrative structure kept the player engaged for multiple hours, making it a noteworthy entry in the investigative adventure genre.

Marathon’s Cryo Archive Map Introduces a Hybrid PvE-PvP Experience

The multiplayer game Marathon now features its fourth map, Cryo Archive, a large, intricate environment that fuses player-versus-environment and player-versus-player gameplay. Entry to Cryo Archive has specific requirements, including player level, faction unlocks, and a minimum stake of in-game credits.

The map’s design emphasizes exploration and progression through a unique security clearance system, which requires players to defeat bots and engage in team activities before encountering other human players. Unlike other competitive maps, initial encounters are PvE-focused, gradually building up to tense PvP moments. The map also contains puzzles, rare loot, and complex extraction procedures.

While praised for its innovation and depth, the map currently has technical issues such as performance drops and limited availability (weekends only), which Bungie plans to address.
