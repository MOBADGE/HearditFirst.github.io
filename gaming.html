Counter-Strike Player Receives 10-Year Ban After Onstage Assault

At the CAGGTUS Leipzig tournament, German Counter-Strike 2 player MAUschine was banned for 10 years following a physical altercation. During the awards ceremony, MAUschine unexpectedly punched his opponent Fabian “Spidergum” Salomon onstage, knocking off his glasses. The incident was captured on video and widely shared online. Reports indicate the attack stemmed from Spidergum mocking MAUschine’s own catchphrase after a match. Tournament organizer Dach CS Masters condemned the behavior and promptly handed down the ban, also reporting the incident to the Esports Integrity Commission. This significant disciplinary action underscores the industry’s low tolerance for violence and the need to maintain professionalism at live events.

RuneScape Rolls Back Update After Economy-Threatening Bug

Developer Jagex has issued an emergency rollback to before RuneScape’s recent update after a critical bug caused an excessive number of runes to flood the game’s economy. The update expanded rune pouch capacity to over two billion runes, but unintended duplication led some players to acquire trillions of runes. Jagex stated this would have caused "irreparable economic damage" and compromised the game’s economic integrity. Due to the rapid spread and severity, the rollback was seen as the only viable solution despite the setback it causes for player progression. While disappointing for those affected, the move was generally accepted by the community as necessary to protect the game’s long-term health.

Mass Effect TV Series Writer Denies Being Asked to Rewrite for Non-Gamers

Daniel Casey, writer and executive producer of the upcoming Mass Effect TV series, denied reports that Amazon MGM Studios requested rewrites to make the show more appealing to non-gaming audiences. Casey said on social media that he has not been given such notes and was surprised by the article’s claims. The series remains in early stages with few details publicly available beyond confirmation it will not adapt the original game trilogy. Casey’s statement suggests the creative team retains control over the narrative approach, which may reassure fans concerned about potential dilution of the source material.

World of Tanks: Heat Beta Offers New Fast-Paced Tank Combat

Wargaming's new spin-off title, World of Tanks: Heat, is currently in closed beta and provides a significant tonal and pace shift from the original World of Tanks. Combining hero shooter elements with detailed tank simulation mechanics, Heat puts players into fast, arena-style 5v5 matches focused on positioning and targeting critical tank components. The game features abilities and ultimates, and the combat is more immediate compared to the more methodical original. Early impressions suggest Heat offers an accessible yet tactically rich experience, though it remains to be seen how monetization and progression systems will develop. The beta remains open for a few more days for players interested in testing the game.

Crusader Kings 3 to Add Playable Popes and Merchant Republics in Upcoming Expansions

Paradox Interactive revealed plans for two major paid DLC expansions for Crusader Kings 3 scheduled for late 2026. The first, By God Alone, will overhaul Christian religion gameplay and allow players to take on roles like the Pope, managing religious rites, excommunications, and potential schisms. The second expansion, Silk & Silver, introduces a merchant republic gameplay style where players lead trading companies, control trade routes, and compete politically within a new republic government system. To prepare players, a free update released today introduces a modular story event system, age-related ailments, and other quality-of-life improvements, as well as a collaboration featuring Kingdom Come: Deliverance’s Henry as an adventurer. These expansions aim to deepen roleplaying and strategic options within the medieval setting.

Fallout: New Vegas Writer Says Bethesda Likely Lacks Means for Remaster

Chris Avellone, former Obsidian creative officer and Fallout: New Vegas writer, stated he does not expect Bethesda to produce a remaster of New Vegas. He attributed this to Bethesda’s reported lack of engineering expertise and missing complete source code needed to rebuild the game. Avellone explained that Obsidian had the opportunity to hand over all code and build data to Bethesda but chose not to claim the final milestone payment, possibly due to financial disputes. As a result, Bethesda may only have partial code and limited ability to recreate New Vegas without significant development effort.
