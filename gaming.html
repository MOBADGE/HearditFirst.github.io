Tim Cain Identifies Nine Core RPG Quest Types

Tim Cain, co-creator of Fallout, outlined nine fundamental quest categories common to RPGs: murder, kill, fetch, collect, delivery, escort, talk, puzzle, and timed quests. He distinguishes between "murder" quests targeting specific individuals and "kill" quests involving generic enemies. Delivery quests involve transporting an item, while escort quests require protecting a person, often considered more challenging. Talk quests center on convincing NPCs, and puzzle quests involve problem-solving tasks. Timed quests impose deadlines, either as modifiers or standalone challenges.

Cain notes that incorporating diverse quest types generally enhances player agency, citing Fallout‚Äôs flexible quest solutions as an example. However, he warns that with limited development resources, increasing quest variety often reduces focus elsewhere, such as game polish, potentially leading to bugs. His analysis highlights the balance developers must strike between quest variety and overall game quality.

SK Hynix Develops New Approach to PLC Flash Memory Cells

SK Hynix has made progress on penta-level cell (PLC) NAND flash technology, which stores five bits per cell and promises increased storage capacity for SSDs. Traditional PLC flash suffers from slow speeds and poor reliability due to the need to distinguish 32 voltage levels in a single cell. SK Hynix‚Äôs novel method splits each PLC cell into two halves, each requiring only six voltage levels, combining to produce 36 total levels. This reduces complexity and improves speed and durability.

While manufacturing costs and circuit complexity increase, this breakthrough could make high-capacity PLC SSDs commercially viable. Given the growing demand for storage, especially from AI workloads, this advancement may help curb rising SSD prices. However, PLC SSDs are not expected in gaming PCs in the immediate future.

Review: Sonic Racing: Crossworlds

Sonic Racing: Crossworlds, released in September 2025 by Sonic Team and published by SEGA, is a kart racing game themed around Sonic franchise characters. The game features a diverse roster, extensive vehicle customization options, and tracks with multiple routes and verticality, offering players several ways to optimize lap times.

Despite enjoyable level design and customization, the game's online functionality is currently unreliable, frequently disconnecting players mid-race. Item balancing also hampers competitive play, encouraging players to hoard powerful items and reduce skillful racing. Additionally, the game‚Äôs $70 price point is considered high given the limited modes and persistent online issues. Fans of Sonic may find appeal, but broader recommendations suggest waiting for improvements or a price drop.
