Starfield’s Terran Armada DLC Adds New Story and Features

Bethesda announced the upcoming story DLC for Starfield, called Terran Armada, releasing April 7 alongside the Free Lanes update. Terran Armada focuses on a faction that claims to be Earth's rightful heirs, backed by an army of robots. Players will encounter hostile robots at the Armada’s bases, with the option to recruit one robot companion whose morality is ambiguous — “not evil” but “definitely not good,” according to writing director Emil Pagliarulo. A separate mini-bot companion with customizable appearance and personality is also recruitable via a sidequest.

The DLC introduces “incursions,” events reflecting the Armada’s invasion progress that appear on the starmap. These incursions can disable fast travel and interrupt cruise mode, adding tactical challenges. New ships, parts, and more grounded weaponry like machine guns are included, along with additional outpost decorations and a fully built customizable outpost to deploy on any planet. The DLC costs $10 but is free for owners of the premium upgrade.

This expansion continues Starfield’s efforts to build on the base game’s systems with more structured story content and quality-of-life improvements.

Death Stranding 2 Leak Arrives Two Days Before Launch

Two days ahead of its official release on March 19, Death Stranding 2 has leaked online via a Steam pre-release build. The 113GB game was reportedly available unencrypted on Steam for a time, allowing it to be downloaded in full and shared on piracy sites. While Steam’s DRM remains in place, it is less robust than third-party options like Denuvo, which Death Stranding 2 does not use.

Pirated versions currently lack Russian voiceover support due to separate language packages on Steam. Players using pirated copies will be unable to access the game’s online features, which involve shared player infrastructure. The leak highlights ongoing challenges publishers face in protecting digital releases on major storefronts.

Subnautica 2 Early Access Set for May After Legal Disputes

After a turbulent period including leadership disputes and court rulings, Subnautica 2 is scheduled for early access release in May. Krafton, the publisher, confirmed the timeline following a milestone review and acknowledged the progress made by Unknown Worlds, the developer. Despite disagreeing with a court ruling ordering the reinstatement of former Unknown Worlds CEO Ted Gill, Krafton expressed intent to minimize disruption to development.

The statement from Unknown Worlds' current leadership suggested optimism about continuing collaboration with Gill and completing the launch successfully. The court case also extended a significant performance bonus deadline tied to the project. This marks a tentative resolution to a prolonged conflict affecting the game’s development and release schedule.

Take-Two CEO Declines to Advise New Microsoft Gaming Head

Take-Two Interactive CEO Strauss Zelnick declined to offer advice to Asha Sharma, the newly appointed CEO of Microsoft Gaming. Sharma replaced Phil Spencer after his surprise retirement and brings a background mostly outside traditional gaming roles. Zelnick described Sharma as a highly accomplished executive who will manage well on her own.

Drawing from his experience transitioning from entertainment management to gaming, Zelnick emphasized the importance of producing hit games to sustain success. He noted that Take-Two focuses on both creative and business aspects, identifying hit titles as central to an entertainment company’s performance. His remarks underscore the continued value placed on strong flagship titles in a competitive market.

Remedy Ends Support for FBC: Firebreak with Final Update

Remedy Entertainment announced that it will cease active development of its co-op shooter FBC: Firebreak following a difficult launch last year and weak sales. The latest “Open House” update, released recently, is the last content update. It adds five new maps inspired by Remedy’s Control and overhauls the perk system for greater build flexibility.

The game’s price has been permanently reduced, and a cross-platform Friend’s Pass allows owners to invite friends to play without purchasing. Remedy confirmed the servers will remain active for years and will receive maintenance patches if needed. The move reflects Remedy’s decision to shift focus away from Firebreak but maintain a base of support for remaining players.
