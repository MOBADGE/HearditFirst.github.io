Crimson Desert Launches With Mixed Reception and Technical Issues

Pearl Abyss’s open-world action RPG *Crimson Desert* launched recently and has attracted a notable player base, peaking at around 239,000 simultaneous users on Steam. Despite this initial interest, the game has received mixed reviews, largely due to its dense and complex systems, as well as its control scheme. Critics and players alike report that the game can feel overwhelming and cryptic, with some suggesting that it benefits significantly from using a controller over keyboard and mouse. The game’s many mechanics require attention and experimentation, which can be rewarding but also frustrating for some players.

Gameplay Challenges: Controls and Complexity

One of the consistent points of feedback concerns *Crimson Desert*’s control design. Players have expressed frustration with convoluted keybindings and unintuitive interactions. For example, tasks such as picking up items or locking onto enemies involve awkward button combinations and lengthy hold times, which detracts from fluid gameplay. Attempts to streamline through rebinding keys are limited, compounding the issue. These control difficulties intensify frustrations with the game's already complex and sometimes unclear systems.

Adding to complexity are intricate in-game quests and puzzles. Guides have become popular to help players navigate objectives such as the “Mystical Key” and “Polar Opposites” quests early in the game, which involve using special items and mechanics like the Axiom Force ability to unlock locations and progress the story. These quests highlight the game's reliance on discovery but also reflect how players might feel stalled or confused without external help.

Pre-Order and Deluxe Content Access Issues

Another notable problem at launch involves the inaccessibility of pre-order and deluxe edition bonus items for some players. These include special shields, horse armor, and a complete armor set. While these items’ gameplay impact is unclear, the inability to claim them has caused disappointment. Pearl Abyss has acknowledged the bug and is investigating a fix, though no timeline has been provided. This issue underscores a rough start for the title, especially for those who invested in the special editions expecting immediate rewards.

Hardware Compatibility Problems with Intel Arc GPUs

Technical difficulties extend to hardware compatibility as well. *Crimson Desert* notably does not run on Intel Arc graphics cards, with the game actively blocking launch on such hardware. This decision was not disclosed prior to release and has drawn criticism from the community. Pearl Abyss is advising affected players to seek refunds through their purchase platforms. The company has stated there are currently no plans to support Intel Arc GPUs, likely due to the small market share, but it leaves users of these cards without recourse.

This is an unusual case in modern PC gaming, where most GPUs at least allow a degraded or unoptimized experience rather than a hard block. Meanwhile, the game supports a range of other platforms including Macs and handheld PCs such as the ROG Xbox Ally, though these also experience some launch-day issues.

Updates to SteamOS and Handheld Support

Separately, Valve has released a significant update to SteamOS, its Linux-based operating system powering the Steam Deck and other handheld gaming PCs. The update includes better support for recent AMD and Intel platforms, more discrete GPU compatibility, and extended controller and firmware support for several non-Valve handhelds like the Lenovo Legion Go 2 and ASUS ROG Xbox Ally. The update also switches SteamOS to use the Wayland display protocol by default, improving display management and compatibility with modern Linux environments.

This progress is relevant for players interested in running games like *Crimson Desert* on handheld devices, potentially improving the overall experience and hardware compatibility on various systems.

Conclusion

*Crimson Desert*’s launch illustrates challenges balancing ambition with accessibility. Its expansive systems and dense gameplay offer a significant experience for dedicated players but can hinder those looking for straightforward progression. Technical issues related to control design, hardware compatibility, and content access have further complicated reception. Meanwhile, peripheral developments in platforms like SteamOS promise improved support for handheld gaming setups. How Pearl Abyss addresses the current problems will likely shape *Crimson Desert*’s long-term community and success.
