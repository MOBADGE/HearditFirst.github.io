Star Wars Action Figures Featuring Kreia and Darth Nihilus Sell Out Quickly

Hasbro launched a new Amazon-exclusive two-pack of action figures from the Star Wars Black Series, featuring iconic characters Darth Nihilus and Kreia (Darth Traya) from *Knights of the Old Republic 2: The Sith Lords*. The pack highlights Kreia, a key antagonist and complex figure dubbed the "most evil and nasty grandma" of the Star Wars universe, marking her first official action figure. Unlike Nihilus, who’s known for his eerie design and planet-consuming hunger, Kreia is portrayed with her signature three floating lightsabers and characteristic contemptuous expression. Despite strong fan interest, preorders sold out almost immediately, reflecting enduring enthusiasm for this classic RPG's characters.

**Why it matters:** This release shows the continued appeal of deep Star Wars lore outside of recent movies and TV series. Kreia’s figure acknowledges longstanding fan favorites from earlier Star Wars games and could signal further merchandise expansions for classic characters. However, availability constraints highlight how niche but passionate the audience is for such collectibles.

Late Dead or Alive Creator Tomonobu Itagaki's Unusual Life Recalled

An interview with former collaborator Kengo Aoki sheds light on some extraordinary moments from the late Tomonobu Itagaki’s life. Creator of *Dead or Alive* and the modern *Ninja Gaiden* series, Itagaki was known for his flamboyant and volatile personality. Among the anecdotes recalled is an incident where Itagaki, while intoxicated in China, caused disturbances leading to a heated fight with Aoki. Another story involves Itagaki shouting boldly at a pre-E3 party in Los Angeles, which culminated in an armed police response, with officers arriving with guns drawn. These stories emphasize the unpredictability surrounding the developer until his passing in late 2025.

**Why it matters:** These personal accounts offer insight into an influential figure in fighting games and action series development, illustrating how his personality and approach impacted both his work and relationships. The anecdotes also contribute to understanding the culture of prominent game creators beyond their titles.

Graveyard Keeper Devs Deny Using AI for Sequel Art Amid Player Concerns

Following the reveal of *Graveyard Keeper 2*, some players questioned whether promotional artwork employed generative AI, citing perceived stylistic differences from the original. The game's tech lead, Slava Cherkasov, addressed these suspicions publicly, denying any use of AI in the sequel’s art production. Cherkasov acknowledged his personal experimentation with AI tools but clarified these experiments are independent of the game's development and that the sequel's art is created by a dedicated team of artists. Player opinions remain mixed regarding AI’s role in game development, with some awaiting final release evidence.

**Why it matters:** This exchange highlights ongoing industry-wide debates about AI’s role in creative production, touching on authenticity, labor, and fan expectations. Clear communication from developers can help maintain trust, though community skepticism persists as AI tools become more prevalent.

Rockstar Games Faces Ransom Demand After Data Breach

Rockstar Games confirmed a data breach involving non-critical company information linked to its Snowflake cloud storage system. The hacker group ShinyHunters claims to have accessed Rockstar’s data via a compromised third-party analytics vendor, Anodot, and is demanding ransom with threats to leak the information by April 14, 2026. Rockstar stated that the breach has no impact on its operations or players at this time. The incident recalls Rockstar’s previous large-scale GTA6 leak in 2022 and underscores vulnerabilities inherent in third-party service integrations.

**Why it matters:** Security breaches continue to pose a risk to major studios, potentially affecting corporate data and future projects. The reliance on third-party services adds layers of risk that companies must manage proactively. This incident serves as a reminder of persistent cybersecurity challenges in the gaming sector.
