**Slay the Spire 2 Introduces Multiplayer with Party-Based Mechanics**

Slay the Spire 2, the much-anticipated sequel to the acclaimed deckbuilding roguelike, has launched a multiplayer mode allowing up to four players to team up for co-op runs. Players start by having one host create a multiplayer save, which others join through Steam friends; there is no matchmaking or local co-op yet. Each player controls their own character, maintains separate inventories, gold, and cards. Battles are fought as a party, with enemies scaled to group size and attacks that affect all players. Energy resources are individual, and players share decision-making on map paths but choose separate rewards. Multiplayer Ascension levels are unlocked independently from singleplayer, requiring all party members to have reached the same level to play at higher difficulties. This mode expands the game’s appeal by blending the established singleplayer formula with cooperative strategy and shared challenges.

**Slay the Spire 2 Sets Records as the Best-Performing Deckbuilder**

Data from Newzoo shows Slay the Spire 2 achieving record-breaking figures for deckbuilding games, with peak concurrent Steam users hitting 575,000 shortly after launch. This surpasses predecessors like the original Slay the Spire and related titles by large margins. With over three million copies sold in the first week and a daily active user count exceeding one million, it is reportedly the best-selling and most-played deckbuilder on record. Analysts attribute this success to years of player anticipation and a trusted developer reputation, as Mega Crit avoided common monetization frustrations. The game is drawing in both longtime fans and new players interested in its competitive and challenging qualities.

**Dark Outlaw Games, Led by Call of Duty Veteran, Closes Under Sony**

Sony has quietly shuttered Dark Outlaw Games, a first-party studio founded in 2025 by Jason Blundell, a notable figure behind several Call of Duty entries. The studio was reportedly still in early development stages on an unannounced project when the closure was announced, with around 50 employees affected. This marks another example of workforce reductions and studio closures within the industry despite established talent and high-profile backing. Sony cited strategic adjustments for long-term sustainability as the rationale. This follows the earlier closure of Bluepoint Games and comes amid ongoing layoffs at other major developers.

**Popular Skyrim Mod SkyUI Returns with Major Update and Source Code Release**

SkyUI, the long-standing user interface mod for Skyrim Special Edition, has received its first update in nine years. Version 6 integrates previously separate supplemental mods, improves compatibility across all Special Edition versions, and supports a wide range of monitor aspect ratios including ultrawide formats. Notably, the full source code has been released on GitHub, enabling community involvement in future updates and bug fixes. This move promises to keep the mod relevant and maintained, benefiting the modding community and players who rely on it to improve Skyrim’s interface.

**Starbreeze Announces Payday: Aces High, a VR Entry in the Franchise**

Starbreeze is developing Payday: Aces High, a VR-specific cooperative heist game scheduled for release in the third quarter of 2026. Developed by Fast Travel Games, known for VR titles like Cities: VR, the game supports up to four players and singleplayer modes. It aims to bring the Payday series’ signature chaotic heist experience to virtual reality platforms including Meta Quest and SteamVR. This endeavor comes as Starbreeze continues to face challenges with Payday 3’s reception and recent layoffs. While Payday: Aces High seeks to expand the franchise into VR, its commercial impact remains uncertain given the mixed market response to similar VR adaptations.

**Meta Develops AI Co-CEO to Streamline Executive Tasks**

Meta is building an AI agent designed to assist CEO Mark Zuckerberg by handling information retrieval and reducing the need for direct human interaction. This is part of a broader strategy to embed AI throughout the company’s operations, aiming to increase productivity and flatten organizational structures. Internal AI tools help employees manage communications and projects, reflecting Meta’s shift towards AI-native workflows. This initiative coincides with recent planned workforce reductions at Meta and a reconsideration of earlier investments in VR and the metaverse. The development highlights ongoing efforts in the tech sector to incorporate AI into leadership and business processes.
