**Banquet For Fools Offers Unconventional RPG Experience**

The recently released RPG *Banquet For Fools* by Hannah and Joseph Games presents a unique take on the genre. Unlike most RPGs, it provides no automatic quest logs, map markers, or conversation histories. Instead, players must actively take notes to navigate the complex world, remember names, and track objectives. This design choice promotes a slower, more attentive gameplay style that requires player investment in the game's society and environment. Combat combines real-time action with tactical pausing, allowing players to control a party through improvised and strategic maneuvers. Although demanding and sometimes frustrating due to vague directions and a strict day/night cycle, the game rewards persistence. This title may appeal to players seeking a personalized and immersive RPG that emphasizes exploration and careful note-taking.

**Mars War: Logs – A Gritty, Inexpensive Choice-Driven RPG**

*Mars War: Logs*, originally released in 2013 by Spiders, offers a gritty, choice-driven RPG experience set on a dystopian Mars. While not highly polished in terms of graphics or voice acting, it delivers an engaging narrative centered on a prisoner of war navigating a war-torn colony. The gameplay is functional with some unique combat mechanics such as sand-throwing to stun opponents. The title’s strengths lie in its atmosphere and story rather than technical quality. It has gained renewed attention due to a recent Steam sale pricing it at 99 cents, making it accessible for players interested in rough-around-the-edges RPGs. The game runs well on the Steam Deck, and its spiritual successor, *The Technomancer*, is also available cheaply.

**Uncertainty Surrounds Release Date of Extraction Shooter Sand**

Fans awaiting *Sand: Raiders of Sophie*, an upcoming PvPvE extraction shooter involving a customizable mechanical base, are experiencing frustration due to the lack of a specific March release date. Despite a Steam listing indicating a March 2026 release, the developers have not announced a firm date as the month nears its end. Community managers have explained that final development work is ongoing, and confirmation is pending completion of critical tasks. While the precise launch timing remains unclear, there has been no official confirmation of delay. This situation highlights the challenges developers face in balancing timely communication with development realities in the lead-up to a game's release.

**Lost Wiki: Kozlovka Uses Wikipedia-Style Interface for Mystery Solving**

*Lost Wiki: Kozlovka* is a mystery adventure game that uses a database reminiscent of Wikipedia entries to unravel a story centered on an Eastern European town and a mysterious tree known as Draken Oak. Players piece together information by reading interconnected database pages and filling in reports based on collected clues. The game emphasizes the act of investigating and connecting facts rather than complex puzzle difficulty. Though its storytelling is straightforward, the game subtly addresses themes of information control and the gaps left by censorship or power dynamics. Available on Steam for $5, it offers a focused experience for players interested in narrative-driven investigation.

**Marathon's Cryo Archive Map Blends PvE and PvP in a Complex Raid**

The newest map for the shooter *Marathon*, called Cryo Archive, introduces a distinctive weekend-only event that combines PvE and PvP elements. Players enter a large, labyrinthine spaceship where progression depends on acquiring a unique "Security Clearance" through defeating NPC bots, hacking, or taking tags from other players. Early gameplay isolates teams as they clear waves of enemies before PvP encounters escalate in a central hub called Control. The map’s design encourages exploration, puzzle-solving, and tactical combat, with loot and extraction requiring careful coordination. While praised for innovation, its limited availability and some technical issues, such as framerate drops, suggest room for improvement. Cryo Archive represents Bungie's effort to create a competitive map with layered gameplay depth and progression.
