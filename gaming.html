**Abubakar Salim on PC Gaming and Game Development**

Abubakar Salim, known for his acting in Assassin’s Creed: Origins and the TV series Raised by Wolves, shared insights into his PC gaming history and current projects. Salim grew up on classic PC strategy games like Age of Empires and Warcraft, with World of Warcraft sparking his strong interest. Transitioning from acting to game development, he founded Surgent Studios, which released the cooperative physics puzzle game FixForce. The game encourages creative construction in post-apocalyptic settings, inspired by player creativity seen in other games like Baldur’s Gate 3. Salim also discussed his eclectic gaming tastes, including titles like No Rest for the Wicked and Dave the Diver. He highlighted how games with progressive loops appeal to him and noted that despite his busy life, he maintains a cluttered desktop—so much so that even his wife avoids looking at his screen.

**Panline: An Indie Freerunning Game Inspired by Mirror’s Edge**

Indie developer Viridian Matters announced Panline, a freerunning game designed as a spiritual successor to Mirror’s Edge. The game features parkour mechanics such as wall running, increasing speed, and dynamic obstacle navigation in a bright, dystopian cityscape. Panline differentiates itself with a non-linear open city and dynamic missions accessed via an in-game PDA resembling the PlayStation Portable, a nod to early 2000s gaming aesthetics. A notable gameplay element involves high risks where falling can erase progress in current jobs, emphasizing careful navigation. Scheduled for release later this year, Panline’s development follows from the developer’s positive reception for their previous parkour title, Ikarus Parkour.

**Player Perspectives on GTA 6 Multiplayer from GTA RP Experience**

A veteran GTA Online player reflected on thousands of hours spent in both official multiplayer and FiveM’s roleplaying (RP) community, sharing expectations for the upcoming GTA 6 multiplayer. Rockstar’s acquisition of the Cfx.re team (creators of FiveM) hints at increased official RP support. The player emphasized the contrast between GTA Online’s fast-paced, wealth-driven gameplay and the RP scene’s slower, more realistic economic and social simulation, where players invest significant time to afford basic vehicles and face consequences for reckless actions. The hope expressed is for GTA 6’s multiplayer to support both high-octane action and realistic roleplay modes, catering to a broad range of player interests.

**Timberborn City-Builder Launches Version 1.0 with Automation Features**

Timberborn, the beaver-themed city-builder, has officially left early access with its 1.0 update, introducing a system of automation inspired by games like Factorio. The update adds around twenty new buildings functioning as logic nodes, enabling players to program automated behaviors such as closing floodgates, controlling production based on resource levels, and rerouting beaver pathways under certain conditions. This addition further enhances Timberborn’s gameplay depth, building on previous updates including water pumps and robotic beavers. The launch version is currently discounted 20% on Steam until March 26, reflecting confidence in the game's completed state.

**Deep Rock Galactic: Survivor Expansion Adds New Playable Class with Bulldozer Vehicle**

Funday Games revealed the “Heavy Duty” expansion for Deep Rock Galactic: Survivor, introducing the first new class in the series’ Survivor spin-off. The new Demolisher class operates from a Rock Dozer vehicle capable of flattening terrain and enemies, altering how players navigate and fight in the game. Players can choose among three class modifiers focused on mining, mobility, or machine efficiency. The expansion also includes 10 new weapons for all classes and hints at two further unannounced features. Heavy Duty is set for release on April 30, with pricing to be announced. The base game is currently 30% off on Steam until March 18.
