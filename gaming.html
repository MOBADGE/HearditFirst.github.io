Graveyard Keeper Giveaway Boosted DLC Sales and Sequel Interest

The original *Graveyard Keeper* was offered for free on Steam to promote its upcoming sequel, *Graveyard Keeper 2*, which is set for release later this year. Publisher Tinybuild’s CEO, Alex Nichiporchik, revealed that this giveaway indirectly generated nearly $250,000 in additional revenue through DLC sales for the original game, despite the base title being free. The DLC was heavily discounted at 80% off during this period, encouraging purchases.

This strategy also raised substantial interest in the sequel. *Graveyard Keeper 2* quickly accumulated 450,000 wishlists on Steam, placing it in the platform’s top 100 most wishlisted games. However, some community concerns emerged regarding the use of AI in development, given the developer’s CTO’s public pro-AI stance. The studio later clarified that AI tools were not used in the creation of *Graveyard Keeper 2*.

New Vampire RPG Director Welcomes Comparison to The Witcher 3

Konrad Tomaszkiewicz, director of the upcoming vampire-themed sandbox RPG *The Blood of Dawnwalker*, welcomed comparisons to *The Witcher 3*, a game he also contributed significantly to. Tomaszkiewicz expressed confidence in his new title, emphasizing that the game will add innovative elements such as a stronger day/night cycle impacting vampire powers, and a time management aspect that limits how many side quests players can complete per playthrough.

Founded in 2022, Tomaszkiewicz’s studio Rebel Wolves aims to create story-driven, open-world RPGs with unique twists. Despite the similarities to his past work, he wants the studio to evolve beyond former projects without becoming another large publisher like CD Projekt. The studio currently employs 160 people and intends to maintain this size to preserve communication and creative processes. *The Blood of Dawnwalker* is slated for release later this year.

Star Trek: Resurgence to Be Delisted After Three Years

*Star Trek: Resurgence*, a narrative adventure with gameplay reminiscent of former Telltale titles, is set to be removed from sale across platforms due to the expiration of its distribution license. The game launched in May 2023 and received positive feedback for capturing the tone of Star Trek storytelling with choice-driven gameplay. Existing owners will still have access, but new purchases will no longer be possible.

Announced quietly on Steam without a specific delisting date, it’s expected that the removal could occur around May 23, marking three years since release. Other Star Trek games have had similarly short lifespans, often affected by licensing issues. Developed by Dramatic Labs—a studio created by former Telltale CEO Kevin Bruner—there are currently no announcements on future projects from the developer or publisher.

Hades 2 Patch Adds Story Closure, UI Options, and Gameplay Tweaks

*Hades 2* has received a substantial update on Steam that delivers several incremental improvements. These include additional narrated conclusions for character sidequests in the Fated Prophecies system, unlimited gifting options to most characters after certain bonds are formed, and new dialogue events that expand the story.

Accessibility has been improved with a new zoom level suitable for smaller displays. Gameplay balance adjustments notably buff several Ares-related boons, making them more effective in boss encounters. Romance options have been expanded with less resistance from certain characters, such as Icarus. Players can also expect new decorative items in the Crossroads area and alternate forms for Animal Familiars.

Windrose Survival Pirate Game Faces Comparison to Sea of Thieves

After spending time with early access survival pirate game *Windrose*, some players find it lacks the authentic pirate feel that longtime fans enjoy in *Sea of Thieves*. Despite reaching nearly 70,000 concurrent players on launch day, *Windrose* leans heavily into traditional survival gameplay mechanics, such as resource gathering and crafting, with limited sailing features and no apparent wind system.

The game’s progression is slow and grinding, which contrasts with *Sea of Thieves*’ more dynamic and emergent multiplayer pirate experiences. The latter continues to evolve with new content, solo servers, and polished multiplayer interactions nearly a decade after release. Network issues and technical hiccups also affect *Windrose*, making it less appealing to those seeking a fluid pirate adventure.
