Bethesda Lowers Expectations Ahead of Starfield News

Bethesda has sought to temper enthusiasm surrounding upcoming Starfield updates after the game's lukewarm reception since its release. Earlier comments from the game's composer, Inon Zur, suggested Starfield would become "legendary" over time, citing Todd Howard as a visionary. However, Bethesda quipped that Howard's "only visionary power" was spotting running lanes in EA College Football 26, signaling a more cautious tone. The studio promises more details next week but has clarified that forthcoming updates won’t fundamentally change the experience for players who didn’t connect with the game initially. This suggests incremental improvements rather than a major overhaul.

FBI Seeks Reports of Malware Spread via Steam Games

The FBI is requesting information from users who may have been affected by malware distributed through certain Steam games. Affected titles include BlockBlasters, Chemia, Dashverse/DashFPS, Lampy, Lunara, PirateFi, and Tokenova, active primarily between May 2024 and January 2026. The bureau is collecting details such as Steam IDs, download dates, and financial losses to aid their investigation. The malware campaigns reportedly tricked players by exploiting Steam's official marketplace, lending false credibility. Previous incidents, like PirateFi’s removal in early 2025, highlight ongoing security challenges for Valve's platform and underscore the importance of vigilance when downloading games, even from trusted sources.

Resident Evil Requiem Hits Six Million Units Sold

Capcom announced that Resident Evil Requiem has sold over six million units worldwide, achieving this milestone faster than any previous title in the series. The game enjoyed a strong launch, reaching peak player counts exceeding 340,000 shortly after release and maintaining "Overwhelmingly Positive" reviews on Steam. Capcom plans ongoing support, including new content such as a photo mode and a minigame slated for May, alongside longer-term downloadable content currently in development. These additions aim to extend the game's longevity. The milestone comes as the Resident Evil franchise approaches its 30th anniversary, with planned events including collaborations and orchestral concerts.

Nvidia’s GTC Keynote to Feature Real-Time Rendering, but No Major Surprises Expected

Nvidia’s upcoming GPU Technology Conference (GTC) keynote will reportedly focus on the "future of real-time rendering," with implications for PC gaming graphics. While details remain sparse, the emphasis is expected to be on integrating AI more deeply into graphics pipelines, such as through Microsoft’s DirectX Linear Algebra API, enabling more advanced techniques like neural texture compression. However, news of new RTX GPU releases appears unlikely due to global DRAM shortages impacting hardware production. Nvidia has recently introduced features like RTX Mega Geometry and enhanced DLSS, but major hardware announcements are not anticipated. The keynote may reiterate existing advancements rather than unveiling groundbreaking technology.

Pulsar X3 CrazyLight Mini Review: Comfortable, Lightweight Ergonomic Mouse

The Pulsar X3 CrazyLight Mini gaming mouse offers a right-handed ergonomic design that suits relaxed claw grip styles well. Weighing approximately 45 grams, it balances lightness with a sturdy feel. Its contoured shape, somewhat reminiscent of the Logitech G703, supports a natural hand position with a prominent thumb rest and centered hump. The mouse features high-quality optical switches, a responsive Pulsar XS-1 sensor, and both USB-C and 2.4 GHz wireless connectivity. While wireless mode showed minor tracking inconsistencies in testing, no in-game issues were noted. The included software offers flexible, cloud-based customization. Battery life lasts a few days under heavy use. Overall, it is recommended for users with compatible grip styles seeking comfort and performance in a compact package.

Witchhunter.exe Blends Retro Adventure with Glitch Horror

Witchhunter.exe is a retro-styled adventure game combining the feel of classic Dungeons & Dragons Gold Box titles with choose-your-own-adventure mechanics. Players take on the role of a witch hunter investigating alleged witches in a Puritan setting. The game introduces meta-horror elements through pop-up appearances of the Devil, creating a glitchy computer interface atmosphere. Its combat involves typing or selecting correct words to avoid damage. While some players may find the haunted simulation elements derivative, the game's aesthetic and narrative draw inspiration from traditional horror films and themes. A beta playtest is anticipated on Steam, allowing players to follow its development.
