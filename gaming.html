GTA 6 Delay Rumor Causes Unnecessary Stir

A recent podcast comment by Bloomberg journalist Jason Schreier sparked widespread speculation about a new delay for Grand Theft Auto 6, despite no official announcements. Schreier noted that GTA 6, like Red Dead Redemption 2, might have followed a similar delay pattern, suggesting its current November 19, 2026 release date could be final after two previous delays. However, media misinterpretation turned this cautious commentary into a "third delay" rumor, generating anxious reactions across social media and even driving betting activity on prediction markets. Schreier himself criticized the overblown response as misplaced. This incident highlights how minor insider observations can be magnified in the high-stakes environment surrounding highly anticipated games.

Hooded Horse CEO Takes a Firm Stance Against Generative AI Art in Games

Tim Bender, CEO of publisher Hooded Horse, expressed strong opposition to the use of generative AI art in game development. He stated that if Hooded Horse publishes a game, AI-generated assets will not be permitted. This policy aims to prevent accidental inclusion of AI art, even as placeholders during development, which has led to issues in other games where such assets made it into final releases. Bender described generative AI art as "cancerous" and said the company actively monitors to prevent its use slipping through. This position contrasts with some other studios that cautiously incorporate AI tools in early production. The controversy around AI art underscores ongoing debates about creative integrity and ethical considerations in game development.

New Steam Workshop Version Control Eases Mod Compatibility Concerns

Valve has introduced version control features to Steam Workshop to address frequent mod compatibility problems following game updates. Mod authors can now upload multiple versions of their mods tied to specific game versions, and developers can configure Workshop settings to help users identify if their mods are incompatible with the current game build. The Steamworks API also supports automatically switching between game versions or closing the game when conflicts arise. This feature requires participation from developers and modders to configure properly but promises to reduce frustration when updates break popular mods, improving the modding experience for players.

Witcher 3 DLC Rumors Gain Momentum, Supported by Insider Report and Financial Context

Rumors of a new Witcher 3 expansion have strengthened following reports from IGN Poland stating they received reliable insider information years ago about a DLC set in the desert region of Zerrikania. The new content could potentially explore narrative developments around Ciri undergoing a witcher mutation process. Separately, financial analysis suggests CD Projekt still needs substantial revenue by the end of 2026 to meet incentive program targets, making a significant product release plausible. While the company remains officially silent and no leaks have surfaced to confirm the DLC, the alignment of insider claims and financial incentives provides a more grounded basis for the speculation.
