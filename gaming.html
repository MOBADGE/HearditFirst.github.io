Batman: Arkham Origins Receives Advanced Graphics Mod

A recently updated mod, Arkham Origins Advanced Graphics 2.0, enhances the visuals of Batman: Arkham Origins, a game that originally faced mixed reactions regarding its ambiance and combat. The mod improves snow effects by increasing particle size and count, adjusts lighting to evoke a more wintry atmosphere, and upgrades textures, including Gotham’s skyline and Batman’s default suit borrowed from Arkham Knight assets. Additionally, shadow artifacts are reduced for cleaner visuals, and reflections have been enhanced. However, cutscene resolution remains limited at 720p due to technical constraints. Installation requires at least one DLC, owing to the game’s configuration. This mod provides players a way to experience the game with visuals comparable to a remaster, potentially renewing interest in Arkham Origins years after its release.

Death Stranding 2 Surpasses Two Million Sales Following PC Launch

Death Stranding 2: On the Beach sold approximately 425,000 copies in its first week on PC, adding to the 1.6 million units sold on PlayStation 5, bringing total sales past two million. Notably, the PC release occurred just nine months after the PlayStation launch, making it one of the fastest console-to-PC transitions in recent years. The PC debut also coincided with increased PS5 sales, spurred by simultaneous discounts on the console version. This continued success highlights the benefits of releasing on multiple platforms, even as Sony reportedly considers slowing its broader PC release strategy for prestige single-player titles. The game itself continues to receive attention for its narrative and visual ambition.

Star Wars: Zero Company Director Advocates for Story and Visuals in Tactics Games

Greg Foertsch, the creative director of *Star Wars: Zero Company*, emphasizes that turn-based tactics games should not sacrifice story, graphics, or control elegance in favor of gameplay depth alone. Drawing on his experience with XCOM and Marvel’s Midnight Suns, Foertsch argues for a balance between immersive storytelling and polished visuals without compromising game mechanics. His upcoming game aims to deliver this blend, countering the stereotype that high-difficulty tactics games must have minimal production values. This perspective signals a potential shift in the genre, seeking to appeal to a broader audience through higher presentation standards while maintaining tactical complexity.

Epic Games Layoffs Impact Worker With Terminal Illness; CEO Commits to Support

Among the recent mass layoffs at Epic Games was Mike Prinke, a technical writer undergoing treatment for terminal brain cancer. His termination raised concerns due to the loss of employer-provided health and life insurance, critical support during his illness. Prinke’s wife publicly shared the situation, highlighting the broader issue of health coverage tied to employment in the U.S. Epic’s CEO, Tim Sweeney, responded by stating the company is in contact with the family and will address insurance coverage for them, acknowledging the painful circumstances and expressing regret over the handling. This case has sparked discussion on the human impact of corporate layoffs beyond financial consequences.
