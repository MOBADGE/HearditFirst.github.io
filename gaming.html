WD Introduces New HDD Technology with Flash-Like Performance

Western Digital (WD) announced two new hard drive technologies aimed at boosting HDD throughput substantially: High Bandwidth Drive Technology (HBDT) and Dual Pivot technology. These technologies enable multiple data tracks to be read or written simultaneously and incorporate a second actuator, respectively. Combined, they increase data throughput from 300 MB/s to 1.2 GB/s‚Äîa fourfold improvement. WD suggests future scaling to eight-track HBDT could reach up to 4.8 GB/s, approaching flash SSD performance levels.

This advancement is designed primarily for large, sequential data workloads typical of AI training, big data storage, and video streaming, rather than random access scenarios common in everyday PC use. By offering a cost-effective alternative to SSDs for enterprise and AI applications, WD's technology could reduce demand pressure on NAND flash memory and SSDs, potentially easing current supply bottlenecks and pricing issues in the PC storage market.

The timeline for commercial availability remains unspecified, but the innovation offers a possible partial solution to ongoing memory and storage supply challenges affecting the industry.
