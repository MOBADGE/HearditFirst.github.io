**Steam Workshop Interface Updated for Faster Mod Browsing**

Valve has introduced a redesign of the Steam Workshop interface aimed at improving user experience when browsing over 50 million mods across more than 3,000 games. The update features a wider page layout with larger mod preview images, faster filtering and sorting without full page reloads, and a new "quick view" mode allowing users to inspect and interact with mods without leaving the browsing page. The refreshed design also improves responsiveness on mobile devices, Steam Deck, and Big Picture mode. Valve plans to keep the update in beta for several weeks or months to address feedback and bugs, but it is currently accessible via a beta toggle on the Workshop pages. These changes aim to streamline mod discovery and engagement for the large Steam userbase.

**Starfield Receives Free Lanes Update and Terran Armada DLC with Keith David Narration**

Bethesda has released a major free update titled Free Lanes and a story DLC called Terran Armada for its sci-fi RPG Starfield. The Free Lanes update introduces the ability to pilot ships manually between planets, adding new encounters and exploration opportunities, alongside new resources, outpost features, and a New Game Plus mode. Terran Armada focuses on battling a faction of military deserters controlling an army of robots, offering new story content and weapons for a $10 price. Actor Keith David provides a dramatic voiceover for promotional material, drawing notable positive attention from fans for his narration. While the update has boosted player interest, it remains a smaller-scale enhancement rather than a major overhaul. The free update applies to all players while the DLC is paid content.

**PC Gamer Readers Mostly Resistant to Enabling Nvidia DLSS 5**

Following Nvidia's reveal of DLSS 5, a new AI-driven graphics feature slated for release this fall, a poll conducted among PC Gamer readers showed 71% would not enable the technology under any circumstances. Notably, 37% expressed an ethical opposition to DLSS 5 regardless of visual quality improvements. Some readers, about 10%, said they were ready to use the feature immediately, and another 9% would enable it selectively. DLSS 5 has received mixed reactions due to its reliance on AI upscaling and frame generation, which some worry may homogenize visual styles or impact performance. This poll indicates skepticism or resistance remains significant even as Nvidia advances its upscaling technologies.

**Qualcomm Snapdragon X2 Elite Chip Reviewed: Strong CPU but Limited Gaming Appeal**

New benchmark reviews for Qualcomm's Snapdragon X2 Elite chip, used in Windows-on-Arm laptops such as Asus's Zenbook A16, highlight strong CPU performance with competitive single-core and multithreaded results against Intel and AMD processors. However, gaming experiences remain mixed due to limited compatibility and optimization issues; some games fail to run properly, and frame rates vary widely. Battery life estimates range from 10 to 12 hours, depending on usage. Reviewers generally see promise in the chip's architecture but conclude that Arm-based Windows laptops still lag behind traditional x86 PCs for gaming currently. Further software and game support improvements will be needed before these chips appeal to PC gamers.

**Intel’s Upcoming Nova Lake CPUs Rumored to Outperform AMD in IPC with Higher Core Counts**

Intel’s next-generation Nova Lake processors are rumored to have higher instructions per clock (IPC) performance compared to AMD’s forthcoming Zen 6 architecture, though AMD chips may maintain a clock speed advantage. Core counts for some Nova Lake models have reportedly increased, with mid-range SKUs now expected to feature 44 cores, and top models reaching 52 cores in total. The emergence of a new, large LGA4326 socket suggests an upcoming APU variant with a powerful integrated GPU featuring up to 48 Xe3P cores. This would represent a significant boost over existing Intel graphics solutions and could target AI workloads as well as gaming. While these remain unconfirmed rumors, they point to a potentially strong Intel lineup with a focus on both CPU and GPU advancements.
