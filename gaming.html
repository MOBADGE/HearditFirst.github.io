Split Spacebars Gain Traction in Gaming Keyboards

Jacob Ridley, PC Gamer’s Managing Editor for Hardware, highlights the emerging trend of split spacebar keyboards among gamers. Unlike traditional single-piece spacebars—which are notably large at around 6.25 standard key units—split spacebars divide the key into two smaller parts. This configuration allows gamers to reassign one half of the spacebar to different functions without losing the familiar jump action typically associated with the other half. For example, Ridley programs the left side of his split spacebar to act as a delete key while retaining jump on the right.

The trend is not new to keyboard enthusiasts but is gaining more presence in mainstream gaming products like the Wooting 60HE v2. Beyond key remapping, split spacebars also produce a more uniform key sound and offer practical benefits for compact keyboards. Despite the slight increase in complexity from additional switches and stabilizers, Ridley argues the design might be a worthwhile upgrade over other features like built-in displays. As more manufacturers pay attention, split spacebars could become a standard feature in future gaming keyboards.

Animatronic Recreation of Resident Evil’s Creepy Girl

An Instagram creator named Littlejem has built a lifesize animatronic version of the eerie girl from Resident Evil: Requiem. The project was completed in nine days and involved repurposing a Halloween animatronic frame, shaping with materials like expanding foam, and adding detailed airbrushing and hair to closely match the in-game character. The animatronic features a moving jaw, enhancing its unsettling effect.

This physical representation showcases the intricate design details that often go unnoticed during gameplay, such as skin texture and facial features. The creator also made a cosplay costume of Grace, inspired by the same character, demonstrating versatility. While this project is notable mainly for its craftsmanship and dedication, it also reflects the growing interest in blending gaming culture with practical effects and cosplay.

GTA Roleplay Embraces High-Stakes Hide-and-Seek

A new pastime in the Grand Theft Auto 5 roleplay community involves high-stakes hide-and-seek matches where losers risk having their cars wrecked by winners. This mode introduces tension as players wager valuable, often hard-earned vehicles in these improvised mini-games. Unlike standard GTA Online, vehicle repairs in RP servers require interaction with other players and more elaborate processes, increasing the cost and consequence of losing.

This emerging trend highlights the creativity of RP communities in keeping long-established games fresh. It also underscores how roleplay servers often introduce unique social and economic dynamics that differ substantially from the base game, adding layers of player-driven risk and reward.

Timberborn Exits Early Access with Automation Features

The city-builder Timberborn officially launched version 1.0 with an unexpected addition: automation mechanics inspired by games like Factorio. The update introduces around twenty new buildings acting as programmable logic nodes, including switches, sensors, and weather stations, which allow players to create automated systems for tasks like floodgate control or production management.

This feature represents a significant evolution of Timberborn’s gameplay, enabling players to design complex, self-regulating beaver colonies. It adds depth and tactical options, boosting the game’s standing among city-builders. The title now holds a 95% positive rating on Steam and is currently available at a 20% discount until March 26, making it an appealing choice for fans of simulation and automation.

Project Shadowglass Deepens Its Thief Inspirations

A new trailer for the stealth game Project Shadowglass highlights its extensive inspiration from the classic Thief series. The game features many familiar mechanics such as blackjack knockouts, water arrows to douse torches, and stealthy rooftop navigation. Shadowglass combines these nostalgic elements with distinctive pixelated 3D visuals and adds its own innovations like sliding down rooftops and more dynamic swordfighting, which is intentionally "sloppy" to fit the stealthy tone.

Additionally, the game involves a mission planning phase and consequences for player actions, such as increased city security or NPC fear responses. Although still in early development with no release date, the title aims to recreate and modernize the stealth and exploration experience popularized by Looking Glass Studios.
