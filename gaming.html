**Assassin's Creed Black Flag Resynced Officially Announced for July Release**

Ubisoft has confirmed the long-rumored remake of *Assassin’s Creed Black Flag*, titled *Resynced*, set to launch on July 9, 2026, for PC, PlayStation 5, and Xbox Series X|S. This version is described as a full remake rather than a simple remaster, rebuilt from the ground up using Ubisoft’s latest Anvil Engine. The update brings modern graphical improvements including ray-traced lighting, enhanced water rendering, and weather effects. Gameplay changes include revised combat with parries and takedowns, improved stealth mechanics allowing Edward Kenway to crouch freely, and updates to tailing missions that no longer fail immediately when spotted.

The original story and open-world map will remain intact, though it adds new narrative scenes, including content involving Edward’s wife Caroline, and introduces three new recruitable ship officers with their own quests. Notably, the multiplayer modes and DLCs from the original game are omitted. Ubisoft emphasizes that *Black Flag Resynced* is an action-adventure experience like the original title, not an RPG. The remake is led by Ubisoft Singapore, the studio behind *Skull and Bones*, marking a full-circle moment as they return to the pirate-themed franchise.

**Diablo 4: Lord of Hatred Expansion Revamps Necromancer and Druid Classes**

The upcoming expansion for *Diablo 4*, *Lord of Hatred*, launching on April 27, introduces significant changes to the necromancer and druid classes. Druids now have detangled skill dependencies from specific shapeshifting forms, allowing players to choose their form for any skill, such as using storm and earth abilities as a human druid without shapeshifting. This offers more flexible build options and accommodates player preferences.

Necromancers receive a major overhaul, becoming capable of summoning and commanding up to 28 skeleton minions simultaneously with refined control mechanics. Their minions are now integrated into the skill tree and operate differently depending on type—mages use a resource called essence, while warriors arise near corpses. The characteristic *Book of the Dead* mechanic continues to allow minion sacrifice for bonuses but now coexists with the ability to keep weaker minions active as tanks or for gear effects. These updates expand the customization and strategic depth of these classes.

**Crypto-Scam Targeting Ships in Strait of Hormuz Amid Heightened Tensions**

Reports have emerged of fraudulent messages demanding cryptocurrency payments from cargo ships attempting to transit the Strait of Hormuz, a vital shipping lane that has recently seen heightened military and political tensions. The messages claim to represent Iranian authorities and request payments in Bitcoin or Tether for safe passage clearance. These scams coincide with Iran’s own trial toll system for shipping vessels, which controversially accepts cryptocurrency payments.

Maritime security firm MARISKS issued warnings after at least one ship was fired upon by Iranian forces following receipt of such a message, highlighting the risks posed by these fraudulent schemes in an already volatile region. The situation complicates safe navigation through a critical global energy chokepoint and underscores the challenges of maritime security amid geopolitical conflict and emerging cybercrime tactics.

**Vampire Crawlers: Recommended Early Upgrades and Unlocks for Progression**

For players of *Vampire Crawlers*, guidance is available on optimal upgrade paths to improve early and mid-game performance. The priority is unlocking two Crawlers—Pasqualina Belpaese (unlocked by reaching level 20 with Imelda Belpaese) and Gennaro Belpaese (defeat the Mad Forest boss). Pasqualina enhances crowd control through increased hand size and area damage, while Gennaro boosts damage output via spammable attacks and combo mechanics.

Key permanent upgrades to purchase early include Recovery (HP restoration), Reroll (reward reshuffle), Duration (buff longevity), Might, and Luck (stat boosts). Additionally, unlocking the Fortune Teller’s Arcana tent via the Polentir Relic in the Furious Forest stage introduces Arcana cards that grant passive benefits, further refining builds. Players are also encouraged to secure Relics like the Grim Grimoire and Stardust Anvil to unlock weapon evolution tracking and gem slotting, enhancing deck customization.
