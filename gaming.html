Fallout: New Vegas Post-Ending Dialogue Restored

A recent leak of a Fallout: New Vegas beta build has revealed additional post-ending dialogue that Obsidian originally planned but cut before release. This content would have allowed players to continue exploring the Mojave Wasteland with NPCs reacting dynamically to the player's ending choice, such as merchants shutting down after a Legion victory or companions voicing opinions on different factions. Modders have used this material to create the "Post-Game Dialogue Restored" mod, expanding the game's narrative closure. For fans of New Vegas, this restoration offers a deeper, more immersive experience reflecting their campaign decisions beyond the original endgame.

The Legend of Khiimori Early Access Review

The new horse courier and survival sim The Legend of Khiimori launched into early access on March 3 with a $30 price tag. The game focuses on realistic horse riding, survival management, and exploration set in a Mongolian historical context. Players plan routes to complete delivery quests while taking care of horse needs such as stamina, health, and mood. Several features like horse breeding, stable management, and combat against predators are implemented, though key elements like the main story, horse taming, multiple riding styles, and peaceful mode remain in development.

While the game offers a detailed riding experience with features such as route planning and day/night cycles, it also suffers from early access issues including bugs and visual roughness. Potential players should consider their interests: horse enthusiasts may find it worthwhile despite the rough edges, cozy gamers might prefer to wait for more polish, and survival fans may find the depth lighter than typical genre entries.

Australia Introduces Mandatory Age Verification for R18+ Online Games

Starting next Monday, Australia requires users to verify their age in order to access online games rated R18+. This regulation targets multiplayer and online-only content, affecting games such as GTA Online but exempting offline, singleplayer titles. Publishers who fail to comply risk fines up to AU$49.5 million per breach. Age verification methods will need to be robust and privacy-compliant, surpassing standard date-of-birth declarations. The requirement is part of wider age-restricted material controls aimed at safeguarding minors online.

Concerns have been raised about user privacy and data security with mandatory third-party age verification technologies. The Australian eSafety Commissioner emphasizes the need for accurate yet fair mechanisms managed by service providers, not the government. This move marks a significant step in regulating access to adult content in gaming and online platforms.

Marathon Launch Notes: Player Impressions and Loot Glitch

Bungie's extraction shooter Marathon officially launched recently, with players noting a mix of initial skepticism giving way to enjoyment of its gunplay and maps. The game remains a high-tension shoot-on-sight experience, with stealth-focused Assassin and mobility-oriented Vandal classes standing out in early play. The newly available Thief class offers unique utility and intel-gathering abilities, presenting a different playstyle from the more combat-focused classes.

Some issues have emerged, including problems with Deluxe Edition bonuses not delivering as expected. Additionally, players discovered that rare loot from the recent Marathon Server Slam event was unintentionally accessible in the full game via in-game mailboxes, allowing items to be "smuggled" across sessions. Bungie is addressing these bugs while the community adapts to the game's currency complexities and reward systems.

Diablo 4’s Upcoming Mini-Season Lets Players Become the Butcher

Before the April 28 release of Diablo 4’s Lord of Hatred expansion, Blizzard is introducing a new mini-season starting March 11 called the Season of Slaughter. Players will engage in fast-paced demon kills to accumulate currency that transforms them into the iconic demon, the Butcher. In this form, players gain unique combat abilities tied to the character.

The event takes place specifically in Helltide-infested open world regions, with an exclusive feature allowing players to confront other players transformed into the Butcher in PvP zones. The season also introduces "Bloodied" items that grant special buffs for maintaining killstreaks. The mini-season is an experimental format intended to test new gameplay ideas shortly before the expansion launch.
