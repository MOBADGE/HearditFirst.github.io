Forza Horizon 6 System Requirements Reveal Good Performance on Older Hardware

The official PC system requirements for *Forza Horizon 6* have been announced, and the news is generally positive for players with older or mid-range hardware. The game targets stable 60 fps gameplay even on relatively modest systems. Minimum specs include a CPU like Intel Core i5 8400 or AMD Ryzen 5 1600, and GPUs such as the Nvidia GTX 1650, AMD RX 6500 XT, or Intel Arc A380. Importantly, 16 GB of RAM is sufficient for 1080p gaming at these settings.

Recommended specs for 1440p include GPUs like the RTX 3060 Ti or Radeon RX 6700 XT, with 16 GB RAM also deemed adequate. To run on extreme settings at 4K resolution, players will need more powerful hardware such as an Intel Core i7 12700K CPU and GPUs like the RTX 4070 Ti or RX 7900 XT. Ray tracing options require even higher-end GPUs and more RAM.

Playground Games highlighted that *Forza Horizon 6* will perform well not only on gaming PCs but also handheld devices similar to the Steam Deck or Xbox ROG Ally. This broad hardware support aligns with the previous title’s performance characteristics and is expected to facilitate a smooth launch on May 19, or May 15 for the Premium Edition.

Fallout: London Megamod Development Faced Uncertainty but Bethesda Was Supportive

Dean “Prilladog” Carter, lead developer of the *Fallout: London* megamod, spoke about challenges during its five-year development. The team experienced moments of uncertainty about how Bethesda, the owner of the *Fallout* franchise, would react to such a large fan-made expansion using the Fallout branding.

Despite the worries, Bethesda’s response was notably calm and supportive. Carter praised Todd Howard and Bethesda’s stance, acknowledging that user-generated content helps sustain interest in established franchises beyond official releases. This approach is consistent with Bethesda’s relationship with other prominent modding projects like *Skyblivion*, which rebuilds *Oblivion* in the *Skyrim* engine and has maintained good ties with the studio.

This openness from Bethesda suggests a growing acceptance of large fan projects as complementary to official content, benefiting both the community and the longevity of their games.

Death Stranding 2 Developer Shares Wife’s Strong Reaction to Early Plot Twist

Akio Sakamoto, technical director at Kojima Productions, revealed a personal anecdote about his wife’s reaction to an early plot twist in *Death Stranding 2*. The game’s story begins with the apparent death of the protagonist Sam Porter Bridges’ adopted child Lou, which sets a tragic tone and motivates much of the narrative.

Sakamoto said his wife, while playing the game, messaged him expressing frustration and confusion, calling Kojima Productions “psychopaths” due to the emotional impact of the twist. He clarified it was meant as a compliment to the game's storytelling intensity.

The developer also noted that his wife found the game’s ending complicated, reflecting the broader mixed reception to the title’s dense and abstract narrative elements. This insight illustrates the challenging storytelling style Kojima Productions pursues and how it affects players emotionally.

MSI Launches Power Supplies with Buzzer to Prevent GPU Meltdown

MSI has introduced two new high-wattage power supplies—the MPG Ai 1600TS PCIE5 and MPG Ai1300TS PCIE5—that feature an upgraded GPU Safeguard+ technology. This system monitors power delivery to graphics cards using 12V-2x6 connectors and warns users of abnormal current levels.

The warning includes both a software alert and a hardware buzzer designed to prevent costly damage, such as the documented “Meltgate” incidents where power connector faults caused GPU damage. The safeguard can throttle power consumption instantly to protect the graphics card.

While this issue originated mainly from poor installation of power connectors, MSI’s proactive feature could provide extra security for users with high-end GPUs like the RTX 5090. No current consumer PSUs offer a similar audible alert, but MSI’s implementation might encourage industry adoption.
