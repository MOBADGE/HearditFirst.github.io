A Watch You Can Put on Your Keyboard

A unique keycap that doubles as a watch has attracted attention in the mechanical keyboard community. The Miyo Watch Keycap, which integrates a Miyota 5Y20 quartz analogue watch into a standard 1U keyboard keycap, was initially released as a limited edition of 150 units in October 2025. Its design features glow-in-the-dark hands, a sapphire glass cover, and a small crown for setting the time. Priced at $300/£227, it is seen as an unusual but well-crafted accessory for enthusiasts who value both watches and mechanical keyboards. While the current model is not waterproof, the creator has expressed interest in developing a waterproof version in the future. This crossover product highlights a niche market within gaming peripherals where form and function intersect.

AI Memory Demand Could Grow Dramatically by 2028

Dell’s CEO, Michael Dell, shared projections that the total memory demand driven by AI workloads may increase up to 625 times by 2028 compared to 2022 levels. This estimate factors in the scaling of AI accelerators’ memory capacity and deployment rates. For instance, Nvidia’s AI accelerators like the H100 currently feature 80 GB of high bandwidth memory (HBM3), with future models expected to support up to 2 TB of memory. This surge in demand raises concerns about supply constraints since only three major manufacturers—SK hynix, Samsung, and Micron—produce next-generation memory like HBM4. The ongoing shortage may impact not only AI infrastructure but also gaming and computing devices that rely on DRAM and NAND flash. The industry will need to expand production capabilities significantly to meet this expected demand.

Arc Raiders Patch Restores Unique Sound for Rocketeer Enemies

Arc Raiders recently rolled out patch 1.23.0, which fixes a bug causing Rocketeer enemies to share their alert sound with another enemy, the Vaporizer. This change had drawn significant negative feedback from the player base since distinct sounds for each enemy type are important for quick recognition during combat. The patch has reinstated the Rocketeer's original alert noise and addressed several gameplay issues, including preventing infinite swarming in the Close Scrutiny map and refining item swapping mechanics. These updates aim to enhance gameplay clarity and fairness without reducing the challenge presented by difficult encounters.

Death Stranding 2 Sees Improved Performance on Steam Deck

A recent update (patch 1.2) for Death Stranding 2 has notably improved its performance on the Steam Deck handheld console. Initially, the game suffered from low and inconsistent frame rates, but after optimization work by developer Nixxes, the game now runs between 30 to 35 fps during typical outdoor gameplay, with higher frame rates indoors. Battery life remains limited during play, but the improvements demonstrate ongoing efforts to make demanding PC games more accessible on portable devices. This also reflects a broader trend of studios adding portable presets to PC games to accommodate hardware with limited resources.

Playing Dungeons & Dragons in Space

German astronaut Rabea Rogge demonstrated that it is indeed possible to play Dungeons & Dragons in zero-gravity conditions by successfully rolling a 20-sided die while in orbit. Despite the challenges of dice floating away, Rogge found a practical solution by flicking the die with spin and catching it mid-air to read the result. This light-hearted demonstration underscores how tabletop gaming can adapt to extreme environments, and was shared during SpaceX’s privately funded Fram2 mission. Meanwhile, NASA’s Artemis 2 mission has contributed to renewed interest in space simulations such as Kerbal Space Program, which recently hit its highest player count on Steam in over a decade.

Marathon Developer Addresses Cheating Concerns in Competitive Mode

Following persistent reports of cheating in Marathon’s recently introduced Ranked competitive mode, developer Bungie has reiterated its commitment to a zero-tolerance policy on cheating. The studio confirmed it is actively banning confirmed cheaters and expanding its detection methods. Bungie is also working on improving player reporting tools and voice chat moderation to maintain competitive integrity and reduce toxicity. These ongoing updates reflect developer efforts to protect the fairness of competitive multiplayer experiences amidst community concerns. Players are encouraged to continue reporting suspicious behavior to support enforcement.
