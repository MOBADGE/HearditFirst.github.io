Wildlife Identification Prints Inspired by Video Games

Artist Holly Sweet has created a series of watercolor wildlife identification prints that reinterpret plants, fungi, and creatures from various video games in a vintage natural history style. The series covers diverse games such as *The Sims*, *Skyrim*, *Fallout*, *Red Dead Redemption*, *Minecraft*, and *Stardew Valley*. These prints transform digital game elements into subtle, museum-like art pieces suited for home decor. Beyond flora, the collection also includes entomology-themed works featuring insects from *Animal Crossing* and *Pokémon*. This approach offers gaming fans a refined way to celebrate in-game ecosystems away from typical game posters.

Banquet for Fools Leaves Early Access with Unique CRPG Features

*Banquet for Fools*, developed by a two-person team, has exited early access following a substantial update adding maps, characters, quests, and expanded character creation. The game blends elements of classic CRPGs like *Planescape: Torment* with a distinctive claymation art style and a real-time-with-pause combat system reminiscent of side-scrolling beat ‘em ups. Players investigate mysterious disappearances on a newly colonized island, with gameplay emphasizing exploration, dialogue, and faction dynamics. Notably, defeated enemies can be either killed or sent to prison colonies, affecting narrative and rewards. Priced affordably with a launch discount, it offers a fresh take on narrative-driven CRPGs.

Human Fall Flat Adds Viking-Themed Map Featuring Dave the Diver

Physics-based cooperative platformer *Human Fall Flat* has introduced a new Viking-themed map that expands the game’s quirky level collection to 32 post-launch additions. This map features Scandinavian-inspired terrain, a longship, a battering ram, and a Viking fortress to explore. Continuing the trend of playful crossovers, players can embody Dave from *Dave the Diver* within the Viking setting, adding an unusual but fitting character twist. Despite the excitement surrounding a sequel announced years ago, there has been little news on *Human Fall Flat 2*. Meanwhile, the original game remains popular and available at a discount.

Icarus Launches 'Dangerous Horizons' DLC and Free Kepler Update

The sci-fi survival game *Icarus*, from DayZ creator Dean Hall, has released a major paid expansion, *Dangerous Horizons*, alongside a substantial free update named Kepler. The DLC adds a large new map called Elysium featuring varied biomes like desert, tundra, geothermal, and volcanic areas, reminiscent of an open-world RPG. It introduces 45 new alien creatures, including a new world boss, 17 missions, outposts, NPCs, new weapons, and a fast vehicle called the Hoverframe. The free update adds an oil processing technology tier, enabling the extraction and refining of crude oil into fuel and manufacturing materials, as well as new power tools like chainsaws and jackhammers. These updates considerably expand gameplay depth and crafting potential.

Haunted Lands: Spiritual Successor to John Romero’s Dangerous Dave

*Haunted Lands*, launching fully on March 10, is a solo-developed side-scrolling shooter inspired by the 1990 8-bit game *Dangerous Dave in the Haunted Mansion* by John Romero. The game retains the retro aesthetic but notably ups the graphic violence and gameplay variety. Players can choose among six characters, each with unique abilities, and engage in fast-paced combat featuring shotguns and melee attacks against undead and fantasy enemies. The title aims to capture the spirit of early id Software platformers with modernized mechanics, presenting a more reactive and intense experience than its predecessor.

Revisiting Deus Ex: Invisible War’s Merits Amid Mixed Reception

Despite the creators' own criticism of its flaws—such as long loading times and a controversial universal ammo system—*Deus Ex: Invisible War* offers notable strengths worth reassessing. The game emphasizes factional ambiguity and choice, allowing players to navigate conflicting loyalties and alter mission outcomes in a cyberpunk setting. Its narrative explores themes of surveillance, corporate power, and consumer culture, exemplified by an AI popstar used for data gathering. When paired with fan-made mods improving performance and visuals, the game delivers a layered immersive sim experience that still holds up better than its reputation suggests.
