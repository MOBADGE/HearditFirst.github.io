Windrose Launches in Early Access

Windrose, a co-op open world pirate survival game, entered early access on April 14, 2026, following a successful demo during Next Fest in February. Players start shipwrecked on an island after a Blackbeard encounter, tasked with resource gathering, base-building, and ship repairs while fending off pirates. Key gameplay elements include ship-to-ship combat, crafting, farming, and PvE combat against animals, zombies, and pirates. Notably, the game omits PvP and complex survival mechanics like illness or temperature effects at this stage.

The ship combat system is highlighted as a strength, offering straightforward, fast-paced naval battles reminiscent of Assassin’s Creed: Black Flag. Melee combat, however, received some criticism for lacking depth, relying on a parry system that can feel repetitive. Fishing and farming features are currently limited or absent but expected to expand over time.

Windrose is planned to remain in early access for one to three years, with about half the intended content currently implemented. Despite occasional performance issues during world loading and multiplayer hosting, the game runs steadily. At $30 with an initial launch discount, it may appeal to co-op fans who appreciate naval combat and exploration but may feel basic for hardcore survival enthusiasts.

Bloodborne Animated Film in Development

An R-rated animated film adaptation of the action RPG Bloodborne is underway, co-produced by YouTube personality JackSepticEye alongside Sony Productions and Lyrical Animation. Announced at CinemaCon 2026 by Sony Pictures’ Sanford Panitch, the adaptation aims to maintain the violent tone of the original game. JackSepticEye’s involvement is notable given his large following and personal affinity for Bloodborne, but details about the film’s release date remain undisclosed.

This project forms part of Sony’s broader effort to expand its gaming franchises into film and television, joining others like The Last of Us and God of War adaptations. Bloodborne remains a PlayStation exclusive and is still not available on PC, a situation unlikely to change soon amid Sony’s recent shift away from porting exclusives. Fans continue to access the game primarily through official consoles and improved emulation.

Xbox Game Pass Pricing and Model Under Review

New Xbox head Asha Sharma has acknowledged internally that Xbox Game Pass has become "too expensive" for many players and that the subscription model requires reassessment. The memo points to a need for a "better value equation" and suggests that a more flexible subscription offering may evolve over time, although testing and adjustments will be necessary.

This comes about six months after a significant price increase, where Game Pass Ultimate rose from $19.99 to $29.99 monthly, and PC Game Pass increased from $11.99 to $16.49. The complexity of current Game Pass tiers—Ultimate, Premium, Essential—has diluted the original simplicity and value proposition. The sentiment reflects growing concern both within the industry and among players about subscription fatigue and the financial sustainability of "Netflix for games" models, which have also been criticized by some developers for undercutting retail sales.

Rockstar Hackers Release Stolen Data with Limited Impact

The hacking group ShinyHunters released stolen data taken from Rockstar Games after the company refused to pay their ransom demand. The leaked information contains mainly financial and sales data, confirming Rockstar’s dominance in microtransaction revenue, especially via GTA Online. Weekly revenues for GTA Online approach $9.6 million, vastly outpacing Red Dead Online’s $500,000.

Data also reveals that the PlayStation 5 contributes the highest number of active users and revenue, with the PC platform lagging significantly. Additionally, about 75% of GTA Online’s revenue originated from sales of Shark Cards, in-game currency bundles purchased by a small percentage of players.

Although the leak lacks meaningful details about GTA 6 or insider content, it underscores Rockstar’s heavy financial reliance on GTA Online microtransactions and hints at why official PC announcements remain scarce. The hacking effort appears to have failed in its goal of extracting a ransom or sensitive game development data.
