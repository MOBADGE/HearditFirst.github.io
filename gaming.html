Capcom Clarifies AI Use Policy in Game Development

Capcom has stated it will not use AI-generated assets in its game content but intends to apply generative AI technologies to enhance efficiency within development workflows. This clarification comes after mixed reactions to Nvidia's DLSS 5 reveal, which controversially altered characters in Capcom's Resident Evil Requiem via AI beautification. While Capcom is exploring AI applications across graphics, sound, and programming, it maintains a distinction between improving productivity and directly incorporating AI-created assets. This approach echoes practices seen elsewhere in the industry, where AI aids early concept stages but is not intended to replace final creative work. The company's position underscores ongoing debates about the appropriate scope and transparency of AI use in game production.

Elon Musk Found Guilty of Misleading Twitter Investors

A California jury has ruled that Elon Musk misled investors with tweets made during his 2022 Twitter acquisition, which impacted the social media company's stock price. Though Musk did not intend to defraud shareholders deliberately, two tweets concerning spam account estimates were deemed materially false or misleading. This resulted in investors selling shares at a lower price than Musk’s eventual $54.20 per share purchase. The class action lawsuit claims Musk attempted to renegotiate the deal by influencing the stock price via social media. Musk's legal team plans to appeal the verdict, and potential damages could reach $2.6 billion. The case highlights the financial and legal consequences of communication by high-profile figures in the tech and gaming industries.

Writing Team Explains Baldur’s Gate 3 Romance Development

Writers of Baldur’s Gate 3 have revealed that the romantic arc between party members Shadowheart and Lae'zel developed naturally because their narratives shared common themes. Both characters question their upbringing and beliefs, leading their writers to approach the romance from complementary perspectives. Early player feedback prompted revisions to make Lae'zel's character less aggressive and more relatable. The development process illustrates the challenges of creating complex relationships in RPGs that balance player choice with coherent storytelling. It also sheds light on how narrative design can evolve through collaboration and community input during early access phases.

Crimson Desert: Early Gold Bar Heist to Boost Bank Savings

Players of Crimson Desert can acquire a gold bar early in the game by stealing it from Lioncrest Manor, enabling them to open a bank account and start accumulating interest. The gold bar is hidden in a secret compartment behind a fireplace in the manor, accessible by climbing through a window. Besides theft, the game offers crafting routes for gold bars, including recipes unlocked in the Spire of Insight puzzle area and materials gathered from mining or defeating specific bosses. Bank investments carry risk and reward elements, contributing to the game's economic mechanics. Knowing how to obtain gold bars early on helps players manage in-game finances more effectively amid scarce currency availability.

Nintendo’s EU Switch 2 Updated with Replaceable Battery

Nintendo announced an upcoming European release of the Switch 2 model featuring replaceable batteries for both the console and Joy-Con controllers. This change aligns with the European Union’s new battery and repairability regulations, coming into effect in 2027, which require portable batteries to be easy to remove and recycle by consumers without specialized tools. The revised Switch 2 will comply with these standards in the EU, while other regions will continue receiving models with glued-in batteries. This move reflects increasing regulatory pressure on device repairability and sustainability. It may also prompt handheld gaming PC manufacturers to adopt similar battery designs to meet evolving consumer rights and environmental requirements.

The Witcher 3 Modders Restore Deleted Scenes Adding Villain Depth

A modding project for The Witcher 3, named Brothers in Arms, continues to restore cut and inaccessible content from the original game and its expansions. Among new recoveries is a deleted scene from the Hearts of Stone expansion that deepens the backstory of villain Olgierd von Everec. The restored scene depicts a violent confrontation with villagers that enhances the character’s dark history. While some parts of the content remain incomplete due to missing assets, the available material adds nuance to Olgierd’s character and enriches the narrative experience. This mod highlights the ongoing community effort to preserve and expand upon classic games years after release, offering players new insights into familiar stories.
