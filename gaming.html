February 2026 PC Game Releases

February will bring a moderate lineup of new PC games, featuring several notable titles despite a generally quiet season. Among the upcoming releases are *Nioh 3*, scheduled for February 6, marking the first simultaneous console and PC launch in the series. The survival simulation *Unemployment Simulator 2018* arrives on February 3, offering a candid take on anxiety and fatigue management. Other releases include *Dragon Quest 7: Reimagined* on February 5, a remake of a classic RPG, and *Mewgenics* on February 10, a roguelike from Edmund McMillen centered around peculiar cat characters. Later in the month, *Resident Evil Requiem*, blending action and survival horror, is due on February 27.

In addition to full releases, several early access titles and 1.0 launches are set for February. Notable examples include *PUBG Blindspot* and *Menace* beginning early access on February 5, and *Kletka* reaching its full launch on February 19. The month also features major updates for ongoing games such as *Rust*, which is introducing a Naval Update on February 5.

Valheim Celebrates Five Years with an Update

To mark its fifth year in early access, *Valheim* has received an anniversary update containing new content and performance improvements. The patch, numbered 0.221.10, introduces cosmetic additions like a celebratory hat and new hairstyles, alongside functional elements such as early axes and frosted sweetbread as a consumable. A new radial emote menu and two buildable garlands are also part of the update.

Performance optimization includes a key improvement for the Steam Deck, where the game now achieves 60 frames per second in Performance mode, drawing less battery and supporting native resolution. This update reflects ongoing support for *Valheim* even as it remains in early access.

No Rule of Rose Remake from Bloober Team

Bloober Team, known for the *Silent Hill 2* Remake and *The Medium*, released a teaser featuring a rose, prompting speculation about a potential remake of the cult 2006 survival horror game *Rule of Rose*. However, the studio's CEO clarified that this speculation is incorrect. The teaser does not indicate a *Rule of Rose* remake.

*Rule of Rose* retains a cult following for its unique atmosphere and narrative, despite controversial reception and challenges during its original release. Interest in a re-release or remake persists among fans, but as of now, no plans have been announced by Bloober Team.

Bethesda on Elder Scrolls TV Show Prospects

Bruce Nesmith, lead designer on *Skyrim*, shared his views on why Bethesda probably will not produce a TV show based on *The Elder Scrolls* series, despite the success of the *Fallout* TV adaptation. Nesmith argues that *The Elder Scrolls* already generates substantial revenue and would gain little financially from licensing a show.

He noted that the fantasy setting of *Skyrim*, with common elements like dragons and magic, lacks the distinctiveness that makes *Fallout*'s post-apocalyptic universe suitable for television. According to Nesmith, any *Elder Scrolls* visual adaptation would be primarily promotional rather than a major revenue source.

Adobe to Discontinue Adobe Animate

Adobe announced it will discontinue Adobe Animate, the software previously known as Flash Professional that enables Flash-style vector animations. Sales will end March 1, 2026, with support continuing until 2027 for individual users and until 2029 for enterprise clients. Adobe suggests transitioning to other apps like After Effects.
