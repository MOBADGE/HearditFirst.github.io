WCW’s Expensive Mortal Kombat-Inspired Gimmick Abandoned After Legal Threat

In the 1990s, World Championship Wrestling (WCW) invested heavily in a wrestling character named Glacier, an icy martial artist clearly modeled on Mortal Kombat’s Sub-Zero. Created to capitalize on Mortal Kombat’s popularity, the gimmick involved a $35,000 custom costume, elaborate pyrotechnics, and a $400,000 ring entrance featuring blue lasers and fake snow. The character launched amid a series of promotional videos that closely echoed the Mortal Kombat movie’s style and lines.

However, this imitation led to legal action threats from Midway, the Mortal Kombat copyright owner. WCW management, realizing they would likely face a costly defeat, quickly modified Glacier’s look and tone after only four matches. Despite this, the character never caught on and ended up in a minor storyline with a similarly derivative opponent. The episode highlights the costly risks of WCW’s sometimes misguided 1990s creative decisions, particularly during peak competition with WWE, when huge budgets often did not translate to success.

Roblox Launches “Roblox Plus” Subscription, Changing Monetization for Players and Creators

Roblox has announced Roblox Plus, a new monthly subscription service set to replace its Roblox Premium plan starting April 30. Roblox Plus offers users increasing discounts on purchases made with Robux and free access to private game servers, even those that normally charge fees. Subscribers can also send Robux without transaction fees, although only verified adults can participate in fee-free transfers.

Unlike the previous Premium, Roblox Plus will not include a monthly Robux stipend or a bonus on Robux top-ups, making the subscription potentially more expensive for those seeking regular in-game currency. To compensate for the loss of server fees, Roblox will reward creators up to 100 Robux per subscriber who spends significant time in their paid private servers. Creators can also incentivize players to subscribe via in-game prompts, earning bonuses for each converted subscriber.

This shift represents a significant change in Roblox’s economy, focusing on creator incentives to promote the new subscription while introducing features likely designed to encourage longer player engagement.

US Government Targets Gamers for Air Traffic Controller Recruitment

The US government has begun a recruitment campaign aimed at gamers to fill air traffic controller (ATC) positions. In a viral video shared on social media, Transportation Secretary Sean Duffy addressed gamers directly, emphasizing that becoming an air traffic controller is a serious career, not a game. The campaign draws on gaming imagery interspersed with real-world ATC work details and highlights the job’s average salary after three years as roughly $155,000.

This recruitment push comes amid a notable shortage of ATCs in the US, worsened by a government shutdown in late 2025 that led to many retirements. The government seeks to appeal to younger demographics familiar with fast decision-making and multitasking skills, traits associated with gamers. However, the ATC profession remains highly demanding and stressful, with mental health challenges well-documented in the industry.

Pete Hines Reflects on Leaving Bethesda Amid Concerns Over Corporate Changes

Pete Hines, a longtime Bethesda Softworks executive and public face, opened up in a recent interview about his departure from the company in 2023 after nearly 25 years. He cited frustrations with his inability to protect Bethesda’s core strengths amid organizational changes following Microsoft’s acquisition. Hines expressed concern that Bethesda was being "damaged and abused," unable to maintain the efficient game development and publishing environment it had built.

Despite these challenges, Hines praised Bethesda’s ambition and complexity, particularly under Todd Howard’s leadership. He emphasized that while Bethesda games often face criticism for bugs and jankiness, their open-world designs offer a level of freedom and systemic complexity unmatched in the industry. His comments underline ongoing tensions between Bethesda’s creative culture and its corporate ownership.

Stardew Valley’s Clint: Schedule, Gifts, and Upcoming Role as Marriage Candidate

In anticipation of the upcoming Stardew Valley 1.7 update, a detailed guide on the character Clint—the town blacksmith—has been published. Clint is accessible from the start of the game and follows a consistent daily schedule at the smithy and later the saloon, with minimal seasonal changes. The guide lists Clint’s liked and loved gifts, emphasizing gems, refined bars, and specific dishes but noting his dislike of flowers and unrefined ores.
