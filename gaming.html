**Slay the Spire 2 Adds Multiplayer with Party-Based Gameplay**

Slay the Spire 2, the deckbuilding roguelike sequel, has introduced a cooperative multiplayer mode that supports up to four players working together in a shared campaign. One player hosts the game via the main menu, and friends can join; the save file is tied to the host’s profile. Players each use their own character and have separate inventories for gold, cards, and relics, while traveling the map and making choices both together and individually. Battles feature scaling enemy stats that target the whole party, but each player has an independent energy pool and rewards. Ascension progression is separate from the single-player version, unlocking only as the lowest-ranked player in the group advances. This approach preserves the core roguelike experience while allowing shared strategic decisions and simultaneous deckbuilding, making multiplayer feel familiar yet distinct from solo runs.

**Slay the Spire 2 Sets Deckbuilder Sales and Player Records**

Data from analytics firm Newzoo shows Slay the Spire 2 is the best-performing deckbuilding game ever, by a wide margin. It peaked at 575,000 concurrent Steam players within days of its early access launch, dwarfing previous genre leaders that peaked under 60,000. Sales topped 3 million copies in the first week, yielding an estimated gross of $75 million for its $24.99 price. The game averaged over a million daily players in its first 11 days without drop-off. Analysts attribute this success to the seven years of anticipation following the original’s quiet but dedicated fanbase, along with Mega Crit’s consumer trust and a controversy-free launch. Their data suggests StS2 attracts not only genre fans but also competitive players new to deckbuilders.

**Death Stranding 2 Made Easier to Encourage More Completion**

Hideo Kojima and his team redesigned Death Stranding 2 with player retention in mind after feedback that the original game felt slow and challenging to complete. Lead level designer Hiroaki Yoshiike explained that the sequel reduced elaborate worldbuilding and offered more accessible ways to engage with the story, such as supplemental narrative elements. This made the game easier to recommend and allowed more players to reach the end, with over 62% finishing on PS4 compared to around 40% for the original. The game also grants earlier access to vehicles and tools, increasing traversal speed but reducing challenge. For players wanting higher difficulty, a “To the Wilder” mode is available, preserving some of the original’s tougher mechanics.

**Popular Skyrim Mod SkyUI Returns with Major Update and Open Source Code**

SkyUI, a widely used mod improving Skyrim’s user interface, received a surprise update after nine years of dormancy. The new version integrates previously separate mods into a single package, resolves bugs, and supports all versions of Skyrim Special Edition, including various monitor aspect ratios. Most notably, SkyUI’s source code has been made public on GitHub, enabling the community to contribute updates and bug fixes going forward. This open source move aims to keep the mod relevant and maintained for future players, ensuring its continued usability without long gaps between updates. The return has been warmly received by the community, who value SkyUI’s role in improving Skyrim’s PC experience.

**Starbreeze Announces VR Payday: Aces High for Later This Year**

Starbreeze is developing Payday: Aces High, a virtual reality cooperative heist game set for release in Q3 2026 on Meta Quest and SteamVR platforms. Developed by VR specialist Fast Travel Games, the title maintains Payday’s familiar four-player team gameplay but is built specifically for immersive VR. Single-player mode is also supported. This is Starbreeze’s second Payday VR effort following a 2018 Payday 2 VR version. Despite challenges with Payday 3 and recent layoffs, Starbreeze is pursuing VR as part of a broader licensing and expansion strategy. It remains to be seen how much impact this VR spin-off will have commercially in a VR market that has shown mixed results for high-profile adaptations.
