Overwatch Quickly Reverts Roadhog’s Chain Hook Nerf

Just one week after increasing the cooldown on Roadhog’s Chain Hook from 7 to 8 seconds—a move intended to curb the hero’s dominance—Overwatch’s developers have reverted the change, restoring the cooldown back to 7 seconds. The adjustment followed a new season rollout featuring various buffs and nerfs, but the quick reversal suggests the nerf hurt Roadhog’s viability more than expected, with a drop in the hero’s pick rate and win rate noted. Balancing Roadhog has long been challenging, as his hook ability strongly influences gameplay. The reset signifies the ongoing difficulty in tuning characters precisely without unintended side effects, especially those with high-impact tools like hooks.

Intel Reports Strong Q1 2026 Financials and Progress on 14A Chip Process

Intel released its Q1 2026 financial results reporting $13.6 billion in revenue and marked growth in AI-driven business sectors, which now represent 60% of revenue. CEO Lip-Bu Tan noted that under his leadership, the company has transitioned from survival concerns to scaling manufacturing capacity. The firm highlighted better yields and progress on its 18A chip manufacturing process despite sustained operating losses in foundry operations. More notably, Tan stated that Intel’s upcoming 14A process is already outperforming early yields of the 18A node, a positive sign for future production. Partnerships with major companies like Tesla to manufacture AI chips on 14A further underscore Intel’s focus on the expanding AI hardware market amid ongoing global CPU demand.

Blizzard Apologizes for Bug-Heavy World of Warcraft Patch 12.0.5

Following widespread player complaints about bugs, class issues, and gameplay disruptions in the World of Warcraft patch 12.0.5, Blizzard issued a public apology acknowledging the patch "was not up to our standards." The developer committed to working harder on communication and stabilizing the game after launch, citing ongoing efforts to fix major problems. Many of the issues were reported during public testing (PTR), raising concerns about Blizzard’s quality assurance and management decisions around release deadlines. The patch’s challenges highlight tensions between rapid content delivery and maintaining a stable gameplay experience, as WoW continues to expand ambitious new features amid a frequent update schedule.

Exodus Teasers Highlight Strong Influence from Mass Effect Series

The sci-fi RPG Exodus, developed by Archetype Entertainment—founded and initially led by former BioWare design director James Ohlen—has released a series of teasers that strongly evoke the aesthetics and mechanics of the Mass Effect series. Though Ohlen stepped down last year stating his work was “complete,” the game remains in development with an extended showcase expected this summer. The teasers include features like exploration, combat, and a new stealth mechanic absent from Mass Effect. While fans have noted the resemblance, the game aims to establish its own identity over time. Exodus will be published by Wizards of the Coast, offering another notable upcoming entry into the sci-fi RPG genre.

Crimson Desert Adds New Bird Taming Mechanic and Phoenix Pet

Crimson Desert’s latest update introduced bird pets and a new item called the Sotdae of Bond, essentially a bird feeder that allows players to tame various birds found in the game. Players must complete a faction quest to obtain the Sotdae of Bond. Feeding birds of different types attracts species ranging from common sparrows to legendary birds. Separately, the legendary Phoenix mount requires players to take a more involved approach: kill the Phoenix in its northern Crimson Desert location, gather its feathers, and craft a Sigil of Solidarity with a witch vendor to unlock it as a pet. This addition expands pet and companion options, enhancing player engagement with the open-world environment.

Framework’s Linux-Focused Laptop Sees Strong Demand, Outselling Windows Models

Framework, known for its modular and upgradeable laptops, reports that its new Framework 13 Pro is selling beyond expectations, with Ubuntu Linux configurations outselling Windows versions. Marketed as a "MacBook Pro for Linux users," the laptop boasts strong battery life, a quality display, and compatibility with Linux out of the box—traits that have won praise within the Linux community. Given rising hardware prices and market uncertainties, consumers may be favoring flexible, long-lasting devices. Framework’s success with Linux editions underlines growing interest in Linux-friendly laptops, especially among users wanting more control and upgradeability in their hardware.
