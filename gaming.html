US Government Busts $160 Million Nvidia GPU Smuggling Operation

The US Department of Justice has disrupted a major smuggling ring attempting to illegally export Nvidia H100 and H200 AI accelerator GPUs to China. The operation involved at least $160 million worth of these export-controlled chips, with $50 million seized in assets. Key figures, including Alan Hao Hsu and his company Hao Global LLC, pled guilty to smuggling charges. The GPUs were funneled through intermediaries using falsified paperwork and fake company labels to evade export restrictions that aim to prevent China from acquiring advanced AI technology with potential military applications.

This bust is significant because these Nvidia GPUs are currently restricted due to their role in AI development and national security concerns. Interestingly, the US administration recently announced it will lift restrictions on shipments of H200 chips to China, albeit with a 25% fee, which may affect future enforcement dynamics in this area.

Biwin Introduces SIM-Sized 2TB NVMe SSD, But Adoption Unclear

Biwin has announced the CL100, an SSD the size of a phone SIM card featuring full NVMe 1.4 support with PCIe 4.0 x2 speeds up to 3.7 GB/s read and 3.5 GB/s write. It offers performance comparable to some M.2 drives but in a much smaller form factor, making it suitable for compact devices.

However, adoption prospects are limited by its proprietary design requiring a special SIM-style tray, currently seen only in select handheld gaming PCs like the GPD Win 5. The CL100 is available only in China for now, and a broader ecosystem to support the format is unclear. While it presents an intriguing storage option for portable gaming hardware, the impact will depend on wider industry acceptance.
