Overwatch Introduces Sierra, a Hero with Controversial Ability

Blizzard has revealed Sierra, a new hero joining Overwatch, who possesses a secondary fire that marks targets with a pink dart, potentially granting an auto-aim-like effect. This ability allows her bullets to curve towards marked enemies, raising concerns within the community about fairness in competitive play. While Blizzard is expected to balance her power carefully, Sierra’s targeted shooting mechanic is drawing comparisons to previously removed or disliked auto-aim abilities in the game. Other abilities include grappling via a drone and an ultimate that carpet bombs a frontal area. Players will only be able to try Sierra starting April 14, with no early access weekend planned. Her arrival may also influence the evolving story and maps, as she is part of the same security group as existing hero Pharah.

Character Customization Mod Brings Gender Options to Crimson Desert

A modder has released a female character creator mod for Crimson Desert, enabling players to replace the default male protagonist, Kliff, with a customized female version. This mod offers several hairstyle and face presets (labeled as "beards" in the UI) and supports most armor sets resized for a feminine frame. However, some facial features remain imperfect, and animations stay the same as Kliff’s default. A male-focused version of the character creator mod is in development. The mod depends on a separate voice mod that uses AI to render Kliff’s original dialogue with a female tone. This expands customization options for a game with a protagonist otherwise considered bland, illustrating how community efforts can address limitations in official design.

Extraction Shooter The Cube, Save Us to Shut Down Shortly After Launch

Just three weeks following its release, The Cube, Save Us, a post-apocalyptic extraction shooter from XLGames, has announced it will shut down on May 8. Despite initial interest with a peak of over 5,000 concurrent players on Steam, the game's population rapidly dropped into the hundreds and it holds mostly negative reviews. The developer expressed gratitude towards players but decided to end support due to the game's underperformance. Refunds will be issued to those who made in-game purchases. This closure highlights the challenges faced by new titles in the extraction shooter space, especially in competing against more established or better-received games in the genre.

Mirror's Edge Prototype Now Playable After Extensive Reverse Engineering

Fans of the cult classic Mirror’s Edge have gained access to a prototype build of the game from about nine months before its original release. After years of reverse engineering and technical repairs, this early PlayStation 3 version can now be played via a custom emulator build. The prototype reveals notably different lighting, level design, and mechanics, including less polished animations, altered movement physics, and new combat features such as breaking enemy arms. Some weapons were cut from the final version, and there are unusual quirks like enemies standing rigidly after defeat. This achievement offers insight into the game’s development process and early design intentions, enriching the understanding of a title now nearly two decades old.

Intel Updates Arc Driver to Support AI-Focused GPU With Gaming Features

Intel’s latest Arc graphics driver update adds "Gaming Support" for its Arc Pro B70 and B65 GPUs, though these models are primarily marketed for AI workloads rather than gaming. The Arc Pro B70, based on the G31 GPU, features 32 GB of VRAM and targets AI users seeking a cost-effective alternative to higher-priced Nvidia GPUs. Despite this, the B70’s gaming performance is expected to lag behind mid-range Nvidia cards such as the RTX 5070, partly due to architectural differences and manufacturing costs. The driver’s update may improve some aspects related to gaming, but Intel appears to have shifted the G31 GPU focus away from the gaming market towards professional AI applications.

Supermicro Investigated Over Alleged Illegal AI Server Shipments to China

The employees allegedly collaborated with third-party brokers to falsify orders and supply non-working dummy servers for inspection to evade export control laws.
