Blizzard Secures Injunction Against Turtle WoW Private Server

Blizzard has won a court injunction ordering the developers behind Turtle WoW, a popular private server emulating the classic "vanilla" version of World of Warcraft, to cease all development and operations. The ruling comes after Blizzard filed a lawsuit last September citing copyright infringement. The injunction also prevents the transfer of server code to any successors, aiming to halt continuation efforts. Blizzard and Turtle WoW reportedly reached a confidential settlement following the ruling.

This legal move continues Blizzard's longstanding opposition to unauthorized private servers, which operate without official permission but have historically served communities seeking nostalgic or modified experiences not offered by Blizzard itself. Turtle WoW allowed voluntary donations for in-game rewards, blurring lines regarding monetization. The case highlights ongoing tensions between game publishers protecting intellectual property and fan projects that preserve or extend classic gameplay experiences.

GTA 5 No-Guns Run Enhanced by Melee Executions Mod

A new player-created modification for Grand Theft Auto 5's story mode introduces a variety of wrestling-inspired melee executions, significantly changing the dynamics of no-guns gameplay. The mod, developed by thalilmythos, allows players to perform moves like bodyslams, powerbombs, and brutal beatdowns on enemies, providing fresh combat options that focus on close-quarters encounters.

This mod creates a different challenge compared to traditional gunplay, encouraging players to employ melee tactics aggressively rather than relying on stealth or cover. It reinvigorates GTA 5 story mode for those interested in experimentation or increased difficulty. The mod demonstrates ongoing community engagement with a game released more than a decade ago, supported by hobbyist innovation expanding gameplay variety.

Cosplay Consent Issues Highlighted at UK Conventions

Recent conventions in Birmingham, England, saw a troubling rise in harassment incidents targeting cosplayers. Attendees reported instances of unauthorized filming, invasive questioning, and disruptions during photoshoots. The article stresses the importance of respecting personal boundaries and obtaining consent before capturing images or video of individuals in cosplay.

The phrase "cosplay is not consent" was reaffirmed as a necessary reminder, emphasizing that wearing a costume does not imply permission for unwanted attention or recording. This discussion extends to broader public spaces where filming strangers without consent can feel intrusive. Organizers and community members encourage a culture of respect to maintain conventions as safe and welcoming environments for all participants.

DayZ Creator Dean Hall Shares Gaming Habits and Preferences

Dean "Rocket" Hall, known for creating DayZ and leading the studio RocketWerkz, discussed his extensive gaming experiences and current interests. Hall revealed having over 2,000 hours logged in Kerbal Space Program, alongside nearly 5,000 hours in Stationeers, a game developed by his own studio. He also noted a deep affection for the original 1990s XCOM, which he even played while at Mount Everest base camp.

Hall shared that his studio plans a new large-scale project inspired by games like DayZ and ArmA, funded partly by recent profitability from their space survival game Icarus. His gaming preferences lean toward strategy, simulation, and deep role-playing, highlighting a longtime engagement with complex and moddable titles.

Open-World Crime Game Samson Faces Bug Issues, Developer Responds

Samson, an open-world crime game created by Liquid Swords and led by Just Cause creator Christofer Sundberg, has received mixed reviews chiefly due to bugs and performance problems. The game, which combines roguelike elements with urban crime gameplay, released with technical issues that frustrated some players.

Liquid Swords has acknowledged these problems and released patches addressing crashes and stability. The developer published a performance guide and roadmap promising further improvements focused on polish and gameplay. Despite the rough start, the game has attracted player interest, showing demand for new open-city experiences reminiscent of Grand Theft Auto.
