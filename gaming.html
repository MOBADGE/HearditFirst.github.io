Starship Troopers: Extermination Adds New Mech and Gameplay Adjustments

The cooperative shooter Starship Troopers: Extermination received a significant update introducing the Marauder M-11E Babar, a large mech suit equipped with multiple heavy weapons including a rotary cannon and howitzers. This addition represents the largest and most powerful vehicle available to players so far, although it is limited by slow movement and finite ammunition, making teamwork important to prevent being overwhelmed by hostile Arachnids. Currently obtainable only through a rare mutator during matches, developer Offworld plans to provide new methods for players to earn access to the mech.

Alongside the mech, the update added a new revolver weapon called the TW-7 Liberator, a fresh battlefield map set in a ruined Federation settlement named Hope’s Retreat, and a revamped base-building area. The patch also rebalanced enemy behaviours, such as enhancing Arachnids' ambush tactics by having them target isolated players with concentrated waves. Damage to enemy bug-holes was made more effective when using fire, explosives, and demolition. Additionally, airstrikes have been refined to better prioritize targets and reduce friendly fire incidents. These changes aim to increase the overall challenge and variety in gameplay.

Baldur’s Gate 3 Glitch Allows ‘Time Travel’ and Reveals Multiple Versions of Gale

A new glitch discovered in Baldur’s Gate 3 allows players to “time travel” by manipulating resting mechanics during Act 3, effectively returning them to earlier game areas. Normally inaccessible after progressing forward, these areas become unstable with changed states, including aggressive enemies in unexpected locations and disrupted NPC relationships. This unintended state reset reveals leftover game data that the developers intended to hide.

Unrelatedly but uncovered by the glitch, multiple “nude” versions of the character Gale appear simultaneously in the player’s camp. This occurs due to several plot and romance-related copies of Gale that are usually invisible until certain conditions are met. The glitch forces them to cluster visibly, creating an unusual and humorous sight.

While players can exploit this glitch to revisit past zones and potentially farm experience points, the impact on overall story progression and the ability to carry forward these advantages remain uncertain.

Microsoft’s Project Helix: A Console-PC Hybrid That Challenges Xbox’s Identity

Microsoft’s upcoming Project Helix is shaping up as a hybrid device capable of running both Xbox and PC games, suggesting more flexibility than the current Xbox Series X hardware. Scheduled for release in late 2027 or possibly 2028, it aims to merge aspects of traditional console gaming with the broad software ecosystem available on Windows PCs, potentially offering players the benefits of both platforms.

This approach may appeal to gamers seeking a streamlined way to access Xbox and PC libraries without owning separate devices. However, it raises questions about Xbox’s positioning as a traditional console brand, especially since most Xbox exclusives are already accessible on PC. Whereas Sony appears to be tightening exclusivity to encourage console sales, Project Helix’s PC-like nature might dilute Xbox’s distinct selling points.

Microsoft’s scale and manufacturing connections could enable competitive pricing despite ongoing memory and chip supply challenges. Still, it remains to be seen whether Project Helix will satisfy users wanting a dedicated console experience or if it signals a shift away from Xbox hardware as a standalone platform.

Sintopia Combines Dungeon Keeper and Black & White Styles with April Steam Launch

Sintopia, a management simulation game blending elements of classic god games like Dungeon Keeper and Black & White, is set to launch on Steam April 16. Published by Team 17, Sintopia tasks players with overseeing a settlement of small beings called “humus,” guiding their lives and managing complex infernal production lines in an underworld-themed punishment system.

The game emphasizes both community management and the process of torturing spirits in a Hell-themed basement environment to extract “sin” before reincarnation occurs. Early previews highlighted detailed mechanics for configuring infrastructure and dealing with crises in both the overworld and underworld layers.

As one of few recent games attempting to revisit this niche genre, Sintopia’s release may appeal to players interested in combining strategic management with morally ambiguous narrative themes.
