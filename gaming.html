Horizon Chase Turbo and Predecessor Delisted in June

Horizon Chase Turbo, a straightforward arcade racing game notable for its original tracks and lack of licensed vehicles, will be removed from sale starting June 1. Its mobile-only predecessor Horizon Chase will also be delisted. Owners of the games will retain access for redownload, but new purchases will no longer be possible. The delisting decision is puzzling given the absence of licensing costs or online components in Horizon Chase Turbo. This move closely follows recent layoffs of over 1,000 employees at Epic Games, which owns the developer Aquiris, though it remains unclear if the layoffs influenced the delisting. Horizon Chase 2 will remain available across platforms, including on Epic Games Store and PC. The changes highlight ongoing adjustments within Epic and its subsidiaries amid corporate restructuring.

Caves of Qud Ported to Nintendo Switch Against the Odds

Caves of Qud, a complex and highly systemic roguelike RPG known for its keyboard-heavy controls and demanding CPU usage, was recently released on the Nintendo Switch. The porting process was a significant technical challenge given the Switch’s hardware limitations and the game's complex controls. The developers aimed to bring all keyboard functionality to gamepad, benefiting from prior interface overhauls initially intended for Steam Deck support. The motivation included questioning the assumption that such "PC-heavy" games cannot find an audience on Nintendo’s handheld platform. Early sales data indicate the port placed in the Switch’s top 5 'Physical + Digital' charts despite being a digital-only release. The team is also exploring a mobile version, aiming to adapt controls for touch screens and portrait orientation. This project illustrates the potential for traditionally PC-focused games to expand onto diverse platforms.

Epic Games Faces Uncertainty Following Massive Layoffs

Epic Games recently laid off approximately 20% of its workforce, with over 1,000 employees affected, leaving around 4,000 staff remaining. Fortnite producer Robby Williams acknowledged the difficulty of the situation, asking for community patience as the development teams adjust. Fortnite has encountered challenges delivering consistent seasonal updates despite its continued popularity. Williams emphasized the emotional toll on the team and the uncertainty about future impacts on Fortnite through the rest of the year. Epic plans to hold a company-wide meeting to clarify the game's development roadmap, but the scale of the layoffs suggests a complex period ahead for both the studio and its flagship title.

Infamous WoW Guild Disqualified for Exploiting New Raid Boss

During the World of Warcraft Midnight raid race, the guild RAoV QA Strikes Back initially claimed a world-first kill of the new raid boss Chimaerus. However, Blizzard disqualified the team after discovering they used exploits to deal almost 300 million damage instantly while wearing low-level gear. The exploit kill was confirmed to be invalid, leading to the players' removal from Blizzard’s hall of fame. This guild has a history of exploiting raids for short-lived fame and being banned by Blizzard. Other guilds are currently still attempting to defeat Chimaerus legitimately. This incident highlights Blizzard’s ongoing challenges with raid exploit prevention and maintaining competitive integrity.

Splitgate Developer 1047 Games Announces New Project

Splitgate developer 1047 Games confirmed it is working on a new movement shooter inspired by games like Titanfall and Call of Duty: Black Ops 3. CEO Ian Proulx described the new game as a passion project for fans of fast-paced shooters, with playtesting expected to begin soon. The announcement follows difficulties with Splitgate 2, which entered beta status again after a troubled launch and staff reductions. The ongoing development of Splitgate: Arena Reloaded continues, but 1047 Games is looking to expand its portfolio. Interested players can sign up for playtesting. This move represents an effort by the studio to diversify beyond the Splitgate franchise and regain momentum.
