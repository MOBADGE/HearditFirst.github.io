Overwatch Stadium Adds Jetpack Cat and Ramattra

Overwatch Stadium, the chaotic third-person spin on Overwatch, is expanding its roster with two new characters: Jetpack Cat and Ramattra. Jetpack Cat introduces a flying mechanic, capable of carrying teammates during matches, which players anticipate will challenge existing strategies by reinforcing hitscan hero picks for counters. Ramattra arrives earlier in the season, potentially useful in dealing with airborne threats thanks to abilities like a vortex, though his full power set remains undisclosed. The additions deepen the evolving meta, though some players expressed disappointment, preferring other heroes to join the lineup. This update highlights Blizzard's continued experimentation to keep Stadium distinct from the core Overwatch experience.

NZXT's Flex Program Faces Class Action Settlement

NZXT has reached a preliminary $3.45 million settlement regarding a class action lawsuit about its Flex PC rental program. Under the proposed terms, eligible renters who joined before the end of 2023 and meet certain conditions may keep the PCs they rented rather than returning them. The suit follows criticism of the Flex program for unclear ownership terms and misrepresented hardware specifications, as exposed by a Gamers Nexus investigation. While the settlement is unopposed and could finalize by June, it underscores ongoing questions around transparency and fairness in PC rental services. The case may influence how companies advertise and structure hardware leasing in the future.

Marathon Shows Modest Player Numbers After Launch

Bungie's new extraction shooter, Marathon, has seen a drop in daily Steam player counts since its launch peak, settling at around 20,000 concurrents. Despite its polished gunplay and distinct PvP focus, Marathon remains niche compared to broader appeal titles like Arc Raiders. The game embraces high risk and complexity, including harsh penalties on death and seasonal progress resets. While it caters well to dedicated players appreciating its challenge, there is uncertainty if it meets Sony and Bungie's expectations, especially given the game's reported $200 million development cost and Sony's acquisition of Bungie. Marathon’s trajectory raises questions about support for niche AAA shooters in the current market.

Mouse: PI For Hire Blends Noir and Shooter Elements

Mouse: PI For Hire, releasing April 16, presents a noir-themed shooter set in a stylized world of anthropomorphic rodents. The game combines detective investigation with fast-paced combat, supported by a unique monochrome art style blending 3D environments and 2D hand-drawn sprites. Players take on the role of Jack Pepper, a private investigator unraveling conspiracies in a politically tense city. The title leans more on atmosphere and gameplay polish than novel storytelling but offers engaging level design, memorable characters, and tactical combat. Its distinct style and blend of genres provide a fresh take on both noir and action games.

Morbid Metal Early Access Delivers Solid Combat Foundations

Morbid Metal entered early access recently, showcasing strong fundamentals in its hack-and-slash combat and a shapeshifting mechanic allowing players to swap between three characters with distinct abilities. The game features fluid combat with dodge counters and ultimates, set against floating islands with technological overgrowth. However, some roguelike elements, like ability synergies and modifiers, feel underdeveloped or lack variety, impacting long-term engagement. As the game is still in early access, these areas are expected to improve. Priced modestly, Morbid Metal currently offers a promising action experience with potential for growth in its roguelike systems.

Minecraft's Sulfur Cube Sparks Creative Uses and Exploits

Minecraft’s new mob, the Sulfur Cube, introduced in the Chaos Cubed update, has quickly become a subject of player experimentation. This shape-shifting entity absorbs blocks and adopts their physics, such as sliding on ice or bouncing on wood. Players have found various uses for the Cube, including inventive transportation methods—one player rigged it to pull a boat at speeds surpassing traditional Minecraft vehicles like horses or minecarts. As the Cube remains in testing phases, some of these exploits may be patched, but they highlight the emergent gameplay potential added by this versatile new mob.
