Death Stranding 2 Performance Improved on Steam Deck After Patch

Following its PC release last month, Death Stranding 2 initially struggled to run well on Steam Deck, with frame rates dropping to the teens and frequent stutters. However, a recent patch (version 1.2) released by port specialists Nixxes significantly improved performance on the handheld device. Now, players experience smoother gameplay, generally reaching 30 to 35 fps in open world areas and about 50 fps indoors when using the game's default portable preset. Battery life remains limited to around 90 minutes per charge. This update showcases how targeted optimization can extend the viability of handheld platforms like Steam Deck for demanding AAA games, even as hardware ages.

Astronaut Demonstrates Playing D&D in Zero Gravity

Rabea Rogge, the first German woman in space, recently shared a video demonstrating rolling a twenty-sided die in zero gravity during a mission aboard SpaceX’s Fram2 flight. Despite challenges in keeping dice contained, Rogge showed a simple method of flicking the die to get a fair roll, concluding that role-playing games like Dungeons & Dragons can be played in orbit. This lighthearted moment highlights how traditional tabletop games can adapt to unusual environments, while space missions continue to attract broader public interest, inspiring related gaming communities.

Hunger System Mods Bring Survival Hopes to Crimson Desert

While Crimson Desert initially attracts attention with features like flying mounts and open world action, recent player engagement has focused on survival elements emerging through mods. New mods introduce hunger mechanics, food consequences like poison or nausea from eating spoiled items, and penalties for skipping meals. These modifications tap into the game’s existing but inactive systems and suggest potential for a fuller survival conversion, such as placeable bedrolls for camping or item degradation. Although official mod support from developer Pearl Abyss remains uncertain, the modding community’s interest signals a demand for deeper survival gameplay within Crimson Desert’s rich world.

King Legacy Codes Offer Free In-Game Rewards

Players of King Legacy, a popular Roblox game blending pirate themes with anime-inspired combat, can currently redeem multiple codes for rewards such as Gems, Gold Keys, and experience boosts. Notable active codes include "DinoxLive" and "Peodiz" for currency, as well as a range of codes granting double experience or special items. Some codes require group membership or game progress. These freebies support ongoing player engagement and exploration within the game’s open seas setting.

Kerbal Space Program Player Numbers Surge Amid Artemis 2 Mission

Kerbal Space Program (KSP) has reached its highest concurrent Steam player count in over ten years, coinciding with NASA’s Artemis 2 lunar mission. The game's appeal as a spaceflight simulator appears revitalized by public interest in contemporary space exploration. Discussions on KSP community forums indicate many players returned or joined inspired by the Artemis mission. Although the spike may be temporary, it underscores the impact real-world events can have on game engagement. Its sequel, Kerbal Space Program 2, has also seen a smaller increase in players recently.

Bungie Addresses Rising Cheat Reports in Marathon Ranked Mode

Player concerns over cheating in Marathon’s recently launched Ranked competitive mode have grown, with reports of cheaters affecting the experience and some streamers labeling the mode as "ruined." In response, developer Bungie confirmed its ongoing zero-tolerance policy toward cheating, applying permanent bans to verified offenders. The studio has already deployed some improved anti-cheat measures and plans further enhancements in detection and reporting tools. Additionally, Bungie intends to make it easier for players to flag cheaters and report toxic behavior, reflecting a commitment to maintaining fair play and competitive integrity in Marathon.
