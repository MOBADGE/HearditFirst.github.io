**New York Mayor Uses Mario Kart to Explain Philanthropy and Corporate Greed**

At a recent press conference discussing the Child Care Action Fund, New York Mayor Zohran Mamdani offered a unique metaphor by comparing government, philanthropy, and corporate greed to elements from the Mario Kart video game. Mamdani described the government as Yoshi, philanthropy as the golden mushroom that boosts progress, and corporate greed as Bowser, the antagonist. The Child Care Action Fund aims to provide universal child care for New Yorkers, having raised $3.5 million towards a $20 million goal. The Mayor’s Fund will focus on encouraging New York’s wealthy residents to contribute further. Mamdani, noted for his genuine engagement with gaming culture from a young age, used this playful analogy to communicate complex societal issues during the event.

**Xenosaga Series Makes PC Debut with a 2004 Mobile Game Port**

The Xenosaga series, known for its classic JRPG entries on PlayStation 2 and Nintendo DS, is making its first appearance on PC. However, the title coming to Steam is not one of the main trilogy games, but rather *Xenosaga: Pied Piper*, a 2004 mobile game originally released only in Japan for Vodafone flip phones. Published by G-Mode—the same company behind a recent Armored Core mobile port—this release offers no new language options or quality-of-life improvements. While the game’s art scales well on PC, navigating it without Japanese proficiency will be challenging. This port reflects ongoing efforts to preserve older mobile games and may be the only Xenosaga title available on PC in the near future, given Nintendo’s current ownership of Monolith Soft.

**Detailed Guides for Pragmata: Overcoming Barriers and Boss Strategies**

Several new guides provide insight for players tackling various challenges in *Pragmata*, Capcom’s recent third-person shooter.

- *Breaking Blue Crystal Barriers*: Blue crystal obstructions—called filament masses—block progress in early sectors. To clear them, players must reach Sector 2’s Lim Recycling Facility and acquire the Lim Eraser power, allowing destruction of these barriers. Players are advised to backtrack to previous areas to collect missed rewards after obtaining this ability.

- *Best Weapons Loadout*: The game features a weapon system where most guns have limited ammo and disappear when depleted. Recommended weapons include the Shockwave Gun (attack), Sticky Bombs (tactical), and Decoy Generator (defense). Players can carry one of each weapon type initially, with later upgrades allowing more. Prioritizing upgrades and managing limited ammo are key.

- *Beating Bosses*: The Sector Guard, the game’s first boss, introduces players to the hacking and Overdrive mechanics crucial for combat success. Key strategies involve hacking to expose weak spots, dodging attacks like dashes and rockets, and exploiting critical shot opportunities. The Luna Digger boss—a mechanical worm with complex attack patterns—requires players to use precise timing, hacking drones to do extra damage, and capitalizing on brief vulnerability windows.

**Comprehensive Overview of Pragmata’s Red Zones and Gate Keys**

Red Zones in *Pragmata* are challenging combat arenas that require special Red Gate Keys to enter. These keys can be found throughout the game world, often in seemingly disconnected locations such as laptop terminals or rewards from bingo-style Cabin Stamp Club boards. Players can use any Red Gate Key on any Red Zone door, offering some flexibility. Each Red Zone offers valuable loot like upgrade materials and mods that help character progression. The guide details all Red Zone and Gate Key locations across various sectors, emphasizing the importance of exploration and backtracking to access these areas.

**Actor Charlie Cox Reflects on Playing Clair Obscur: Expedition 33**

Charlie Cox, known for voicing the character Gustave in the JRPG *Clair Obscur: Expedition 33*, recently shared that he has only played a small portion of the game. He noted the contrast between this narrative-driven title and classic games from his youth, appreciating the depth and subtle pacing of story delivery. Cox admitted he has not completed the game and remains cautious about the significant time investment required, with some players spending upwards of 50-100 hours. His comments highlight the challenge actors face in balancing gaming with other commitments and underscore the demanding nature of long-form JRPGs.
