**Developer Ivy Road Shuts Down After Funding Challenges**

Ivy Road, the studio behind last year's cozy adventure game *Wanderstop*, has announced it will close its doors on March 31. The studio's first and only game featured notable collaborators, including writers from *The Stanley Parable* and *Tacoma*, as well as composer C418 from *Minecraft*. Despite positive reception, Ivy Road struggled to secure funding for its next project, *Engine Angel*. Layoffs occurred in January after attempts to find a publishing partner for the new game failed. Ivy Road expressed disappointment but acknowledged the current difficulties in raising game funds. *Wanderstop* will remain available for purchase, and Annapurna Interactive will soon share news about efforts to bring the title to new players.

**ZA/UM Chooses New Direction for Zero Parades**

The developers of *Disco Elysium* have shared insights into their latest RPG, *Zero Parades*. Initially considering another police-focused storyline, the team decided against repeating that direction due to changing public perceptions of law enforcement and a desire to explore new genres. The current game centers on espionage and political intrigue, reflecting a more cynical and urgent political climate since *Disco Elysium*'s 2019 release. The writing team aims to capture the ideological conflicts of today through a spy thriller framework, distancing themselves from their previous detective narrative.

**Frictional Games Bundle Offers Classic Horror Titles for $15**

A Humble Bundle collection now includes nearly every horror game from Frictional Games for $15. This package features the *Penumbra* trilogy, *Amnesia: The Dark Descent*, its follow-ups (*A Machine for Pigs* and *Rebirth*), *Amnesia: The Bunker*, and *SOMA*. These titles span from early influential horror to more recent survival and psychological horror experiences. The bundle provides a comprehensive look at the developer’s work and is available until April 1, 2027, with all games redeemable via Steam keys. This collection offers value for players interested in the evolution of atmospheric horror games.

**Major Path of Exile Trader Banned, Impacting Player Economy**

Jenebu, known as the leading figure behind The Forbidden Trove (TFT)—a prominent player-driven trading Discord server for *Path of Exile*—has had his account banned by Grinding Gear Games (GGG). Jenebu's stash contained many of the game's rarest items, some of which are irreplaceable, linked to a "mirror service" allowing safe item duplication among trusted players. The ban has left uncertainty over TFT's future and its impact on the in-game economy. While there is no confirmed evidence of real-money trading, the ban has drawn mixed reactions, with some community members supporting GGG’s action and others concerned about losing access to valuable items and resources.

**Deus Ex: Human Revolution’s Original Composer Passed Over**

Alexander Brandon, composer for the original *Deus Ex*, revealed that although he contributed a track for *Deus Ex: Human Revolution*, the 2011 prequel ultimately did not include his work. Eidos Montreal, wanting to establish its own direction, opted for Michael McCann as the soundtrack’s main composer. Brandon agreed that the final choice better suited the game’s tone. He also expressed disappointment at not scoring *Deus Ex: The Fall*, the mobile spinoff. Brandon remains respectful of the creative decisions but noted the challenges he has faced securing new work since then.

**Mohawk Games Prioritizes Sustainability With Small Studio Model**

Soren Johnson, co-founder of Mohawk Games, discussed the studio’s deliberate choice to remain small with approximately 15 employees. This approach aims to avoid layoffs and maintain steady revenue, supported by ongoing sales and DLC from their grand strategy titles like *Old World*. Johnson emphasized quality over speed, suggesting that longer development times can yield better games and a less stressful work environment. The studio recently announced a new *Old World* expansion, *Empires of the Indus*, launching on March 30. While interested in exploring other genres, Mohawk plans to proceed at a manageable pace to ensure sustainable growth.
