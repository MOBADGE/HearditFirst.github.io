Magic: The Gathering Skips Harry Potter Crossover for New Strixhaven Set

Magic: The Gathering’s upcoming expansion, *Secrets of Strixhaven*, returns to the game’s original wizard school setting rather than pursuing a Harry Potter crossover. This decision comes despite Hasbro’s partnership with Warner Bros. to develop Harry Potter-themed games and toys, which raised speculation about potential Magic tie-ins. *Secrets of Strixhaven* revisits the fictional university several years after the events of the original *Strixhaven: School of Mages* set, focusing on a broader curriculum and students who are young adults rather than children. The setting aims for a more self-aware take on magical school tropes, contrasting with the high school adventures of Harry Potter. The new set will be available in Arena on April 21 and in physical form on April 24.

Crimson Desert Patch Adds Hide Helmet Feature and More

Action RPG *Crimson Desert* received a new patch addressing player feedback, notably adding a highly requested option to hide helmets. Players can now choose whether headgear appears in cutscenes, combat, always, or not at all via settings. The update also includes a new armor set and helmet for feline companions, and players can expand camp storage to 1,000 slots. Additionally, a "classic" movement control option has been reintroduced for those who preferred the previous controls. These changes come amid ongoing improvements since the game’s launch, aiming to enhance user experience despite some earlier controversies regarding AI-generated content.

Leaked Footage Shows Possible Elden Ring Movie Set

A fan in the UK captured video footage that might be the first look at Alex Garland’s upcoming *Elden Ring* movie. The videos show a ruined stone church resembling the game’s iconic Churches of Marika, alongside props and construction gear from a specialist film set company near Windsor Great Park, a common filming location for major productions. While not officially confirmed as part of the movie, the set has generated speculation regarding the film’s faithful recreation of the game world. Lead actor discussions remain private, and further information from studio A24 is pending.

Datamined Elden Ring Cutscene Sheds Light on Miquella’s Story

Dataminer Lance McDonald uncovered a work-in-progress cutscene from *Elden Ring* featuring Miquella the Kind, a key antagonist from the game's DLC. The scene shows Miquella planting the sapling for the Haligtree and watering it with his blood, set in a modified version of Malenia’s boss arena. The sequence reveals hints about Miquella's original, more benevolent backstory before later DLC developments. Voice lines suggest he addresses a demigod sibling and speaks cryptically about his vision of a world where all beings flourish. This discovery offers new lore insights into the character and the overall narrative surrounding the Haligtree subplot.

Warframe Team Rejects AI-Generated Content in Games

Digital Extremes, the developer of *Warframe* and upcoming *Soulframe*, has taken a firm stance against using AI-generated art or assets in its games. Community director Megan Everett expressed frustration with AI artworks, emphasizing that all game content is human-made and insisting that "nothing in our games will be AI-generated, ever." This position is notable amid increased use and debate over generative AI in game development. While many studios incorporate AI tools, Digital Extremes highlights concerns over authenticity and quality. The stance underscores ongoing industry tensions regarding the role of AI in creative processes.

Mass Effect TV Show Script Being Reevaluated for Broader Appeal

Reports suggest Amazon MGM Studios is requesting rewrites for the upcoming *Mass Effect* TV series to make it more accessible to viewers unfamiliar with the games. Amazon’s head of global TV, Peter Friedlander, is reviewing scripts and pushing for changes to appeal to a non-gaming audience. The show remains near a series order but faces challenges balancing fidelity to the source material with broader storytelling goals. This follows other high-profile video game adaptations struggling to satisfy both fans and general viewers. Meanwhile, the next *Mass Effect* game development faces uncertainty following significant layoffs and a recent ownership change at Electronic Arts.
