Arc Raiders Addresses Cheating Concerns with Upcoming Anti-Cheat Updates

Arc Raiders, a newly popular multiplayer third-person shooter, has recently faced community concerns about cheating. Developer Embark acknowledged the issue on the official Discord server, emphasizing that they are "taking this issue very seriously" and actively responding to player feedback. In the coming weeks, Embark plans to implement significant changes to their anti-cheat systems, including updated detection rules, improved ban enforcement, and fixes for known glitches exploited by cheaters, such as the "out of map" glitch. Additionally, tools for streamers to help curb stream sniping will be introduced.

Although reports of cheating have been prominent among high-level streamers and competitive players, casual players and those engaging in low-PvP activities are less likely to encounter such issues currently. The rise in cheating visibility is partly due to Arc Raiders' Twitch popularity and the concentration of cheaters in top matchmaking ranks. The developers are also examining weapon balance, with particular attention to rapid-fire exploits involving the "Kettle" gun and others, signaling planned nerfs.

This proactive response from Embark is important in maintaining the game's integrity and long-term player trust as it grows.

Steam Workshop Introduces Version Control to Improve Mod Compatibility

Valve has introduced new version control features for the Steam Workshop designed to address a common problem: game updates breaking mods and saved games. The update allows game developers to define multiple past versions of their games and enables mod authors to upload different mod versions, each tagged with compatible game versions.

Through new Steamworks APIs, users can be warned or prevented from running incompatible mods, and in some cases, Steam can automatically switch game versions to match mods. This update requires developers and modders to actively implement version management, but when used, it promises a smoother experience for players relying on mods, preserving their setups through game updates.

This represents a meaningful improvement for the extensive Steam modding community, potentially reducing disruption and save corruption caused by game changes.
