Highguard’s Last Major Update Arrives Before Shutdown

Highguard, the cooperative first-person shooter by Wildlight Entertainment, is set to shut down on March 12, 2026. Despite this, the developers have released a substantial content update just days before the closure. The update introduces a new playable character named Koldo, a healer Warden with unique protective abilities including a "Soul Shield" and a health-regenerating dome ultimate. Additionally, the update adds new account progression features, skill trees with four specialties, and a versatile new weapon called the Switchback that functions as both shotgun and marksman rifle.

Creative director Jason McCord explained that much of this content, including the skill trees, was unfinished at launch but was completed in the final weeks as a parting gift to remaining players. While the update offers fresh gameplay elements for dedicated fans, it also underscores the bittersweet end of the game’s service. Wildlight revealed Highguard attracted over two million players with a core audience mostly on PS5, yet it could not sustain itself long-term.

Hunt: Showdown’s Extraction Points Will Soon Be Hidden

Crytek’s extraction shooter, Hunt: Showdown, will experiment with a new feature starting March 18 during the Devil’s Trail event: extraction points and loot will no longer be visible on the map at the beginning of matches. Instead, locations must be discovered in-game by finding scouting maps or visiting new scout towers. Extraction points can become visible after acquiring bounty tokens or using certain tarot cards.

This change aims to make the extraction phase more dynamic and uncertain. Currently, bounty carriers often head predictably toward a visible extraction point, making the endgame formulaic. By hiding extraction spots, players will need to explore and weigh riskier decisions about where to extract, potentially increasing tactical complexity and tension. The update also adds new tarot cards and expands environmental storytelling with persistent traces of player activity, reinforcing the game’s “hunt” theme.

US Government Considers Forcing Tencent to Divest US Game Holdings

Reports indicate the US government is debating whether to compel Chinese conglomerate Tencent to sell its holdings in various US and Finnish game companies over national security concerns. Tencent owns or holds significant stakes in multiple major studios including Riot Games, Epic Games, and Ubisoft. The concern centers on Tencent potentially accessing personal data of US citizens through these investments.

This issue has been under scrutiny since 2020 and ties into broader US-China tensions around technology and data privacy. A planned government meeting on the matter was postponed ahead of an expected Trump-Xi summit. If Tencent is forced to divest, it could disrupt current ownership structures and lead to increased private equity involvement. Such a shift has industry-wide implications given Tencent's extensive role in the global games market.

Only Two Players Passed a Challenging 1980s Adventure Game Exam

The newly created Adventure Game Aptitude Test (AGAT) challenged over 800 participants to complete the 1987 Lucasfilm graphic adventure Maniac Mansion without walkthroughs, all while being monitored online to prevent cheating. After four hours, only two players passed. The test was designed as a tough measure of puzzle-solving and game comprehension under strict conditions.

The low pass rate highlights the complexity of classic adventure games and the difficulty of playing them "blind." Organizers noted the rigid timing and proctoring helped maintain the test’s integrity but could have limited participation. The event received some unusual incidents, including a jokester offering a bribe and others submitting irrelevant screenshots. The test’s rarity and rigor may make it a unique benchmark for gaming skill.

Resident Evil Requiem Sells 5 Million Copies in Four Days

Capcom’s Resident Evil Requiem has reached over five million sales worldwide within four days of its February 27 release, marking one of the fastest-selling titles in the company’s history. The game also set a new record for concurrent players on Steam at launch for the series, topping 270,000 users. It currently holds the top spot on Steam’s paid global sales chart.

This rapid sales success places Requiem in Capcom’s all-time top 20 bestsellers, surpassing titles like Dragon’s Dogma 2 in lifetime sales. While it trails behind Monster Hunter World and Rise, which have sold over 18 million copies each, the strong start bodes well for its long-term performance. The sales figures indicate sustained player interest in high-quality survival horror experiences.
