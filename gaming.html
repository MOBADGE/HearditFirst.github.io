Starfield’s Terran Armada DLC Adds New Companions and Gameplay Features

Bethesda has announced that the upcoming Starfield story DLC, Terran Armada, will launch on April 7 alongside the Free Lanes update. The DLC introduces a new faction — the Terran Armada — who see themselves as Earth’s true heirs and use an army of robots as enforcers. Players will encounter these robots during missions at Armada bases, with one model available to recruit as a companion. Bethesda describes this character as morally ambiguous, "not evil but definitely not good."

In addition to this, players can earn a ModelG mini-bot sidekick that acts like a pet, customizable in appearance and personality. Terran Armada also features a new "incursions" gameplay element where players choose whether to respond to the Armada’s advancing attacks across the star map, with consequences such as disabled ship fast travel and cruise mode interruptions. The DLC adds new ships, ship parts including traditional machine guns, gear with a more grounded aesthetic, and enhanced outpost customization options.

Terran Armada’s blend of narrative expansion and gameplay mechanics aims to deepen Starfield’s exploration and combat, offering players new strategic choices and companion interaction.

Death Stranding 2 Steam Build Leaks Two Days Before Launch

A pre-release version of Kojima Productions’ Death Stranding 2 has leaked on piracy sites, two days before its official March 19 launch on Steam. The leak allegedly occurred due to unencrypted Steam files being accessible for some time, allowing the 113GB game to be downloaded in full. While Steam DRM is included, it is easier to bypass compared to Denuvo DRM, which Death Stranding 2 does not use.

Notably, the leaked build lacks Russian voiceovers, as Steam distributes languages as separate downloadable packages. Players using pirated copies will be unable to access the game’s connected online features that allow sharing infrastructure like roads and bridges. The leak highlights ongoing challenges with digital game distribution security ahead of major releases.

Subnautica 2 Early Access Set for May Amid Legal Developments

Following recent legal rulings concerning leadership disputes at Unknown Worlds, Subnautica 2 is now scheduled for early access release in May 2026. Krafton, the game’s publisher and parent company, released a statement confirming the milestone and indicating cooperation with former studio CEO Ted Gill, who a court has ordered to be reinstated.

Unknown Worlds’ current leadership credited the development team’s efforts for getting the game to an early access-ready state. Meanwhile, Krafton expressed disagreement with the court ruling but states its priority is minimizing disruption to the release plan and the studio. The ruling extends the deadline on a related $250 million bonus tied to Gill’s contract until at least September.

Subnautica 2’s upcoming early access launch marks a major step forward after delays and legal challenges, with the studio committed to an open development process alongside its community.

Remedy Ends Support for FBC: Firebreak with Final “Open House” Update

Remedy Entertainment will discontinue further content development for FBC: Firebreak following its underwhelming launch last year. The final update adds five new maps inspired by Remedy’s Control, a revamped perk system enabling stronger builds, and various gameplay improvements.

To support the existing player base, Remedy has permanently lowered the game’s price and introduced a Friend’s Pass allowing owners to invite friends to play for free. The studio confirmed the relay servers will remain operational and gameplay patches will continue as needed, with the intent to keep the game accessible for years despite its smaller audience.

Firebreak represents Remedy’s first foray into self-published online multiplayer, and the studio described the project as a valuable learning experience.

Take-Two CEO Offers No Advice to New Microsoft Gaming Head

In an interview, Take-Two Interactive CEO Strauss Zelnick declined to offer advice to Asha Sharma, newly appointed CEO of Microsoft Gaming. Sharma, who has a background in AI and product management rather than gaming, replaced Phil Spencer last month. Zelnick described Sharma as a highly accomplished executive capable of handling the role independently.

Zelnick reflected on his own entry into games without a prior gaming background, emphasizing the importance of balancing creative and business functions and focusing on producing hit games. Take-Two’s approach centers on delivering commercially successful titles, a strategy Zelnick sees as critical for longevity in the entertainment industry.
