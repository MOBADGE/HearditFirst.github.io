**World of Warcraft: Midnight Roadmap and Player Housing Updates**

Blizzard has shared new details about the upcoming expansion, *World of Warcraft: Midnight*, including a post-launch roadmap that continues the game's recent trend of experimentation. Among the highlighted features is the introduction of a prop hunt minigame, which leverages the extensive player housing assets now available. This addition represents a casual diversion amid the expansion‚Äôs other content.

More significant are planned ongoing single-boss raids, scattered throughout the expansion and integrated into the main questline, similar to mechanics seen in *Final Fantasy 14*. These are designed to bring narrative-driven raid encounters directly into the player's journey.

Another notable upcoming feature is "Labyrinths," large-scale dungeon variants of the smaller Delves introduced in the previous expansion. These can be tackled solo or cooperatively in short segments or entire runs. This evolution of dungeon content aims to offer more flexible and engaging experiences.

Additionally, Blizzard teased a new experimental game mode from the team that developed prior modes such as Plunderstorm and Remix. Specifics remain under wraps, but the continued willingness to try novel gameplay approaches highlights Blizzard's intent to diversify player options.

Separately, Blizzard confirmed enhancements to the player housing system. Upcoming tools will include mass select, copy/paste functionality, and import/export capabilities for creative builds. These improvements target current limitations when moving or replicating complex decorations, striving to make housing more user-friendly and expressive. Decor budgets for both inside and outside player homes will also increase, allowing for more elaborate designs. The development team considers this just the foundation of long-term housing evolution.

**VR Exercise: A Month-Long Experience**

James Bentley documented his personal experiment with exercising through virtual reality (VR) over a month, aiming to assess its viability as a consistent fitness method. Using a Meta Quest 3 headset, he set a daily minimum exercise goal based on physical exertion recorded by his smartwatch, ignoring other movement.

Beat Saber, FitXR (which focuses on boxing and HIIT), Synth Riders, and Pistol Whip formed the core of his workout rotation. He reported the VR environment effectively reduced boredom and improved exercise consistency compared to traditional workouts, despite occasional inconveniences such as sweat buildup in the headset and space constraints at home.

Significant moments included sustaining a habit beyond the initial days and engaging in high-intensity interval training that challenged his fitness levels. Bentley noted incidental positive changes in diet and motivation, partly due to wanting to support his exercise efforts.

He acknowledged that VR exercise demands focused attention, complicating multitasking or casual background activity. Still, after a month, fitness gains were apparent in daily life, such as improved stamina climbing a steep hill near his home.

**Fallout Season 1 Available Free on YouTube Temporarily**

Amazon has made the complete first season of the *Fallout* TV series available for free viewing on YouTube, via its official channel. The episodes are being released in pairs multiple times per day, with the full season expected online by January 31.

This temporary availability is a promotional lead-up to the upcoming *Fallout* season 2 finale. The free access will last until February 11, after which the episodes will be removed, and viewers will need a Prime subscription to continue watching.

For viewers undecided about the series, this provides an opportunity to watch without commitment, potentially drawing in new audiences before the second season concludes.
