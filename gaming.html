Atlas Pirate Mechanics Coming to Ark: Survival Ascended DLC

Studio Wildcard is incorporating features from its earlier survival game Atlas into the upcoming *Tides of Fortune* DLC for *Ark: Survival Ascended*. Atlas, a pirate-themed survival game launched in 2018, struggled due to its punishing difficulty and complex mechanics, such as carrying resources one log at a time and building ships plank by plank. While innovative, these aspects discouraged players, as one mistake could mean losing many hours of progress. The new DLC aims to distill Atlas's sailing and ocean physics systems into a more accessible experience for Ark players. Notably, players will be able to build large pirate ships, experience improved water physics with realistic waves and wakes, and bring dinosaurs aboard their vessels. This update could enrich Ark's gameplay by adding a naval component that builds on Wildcard’s technical expertise while avoiding the harshness that hurt Atlas.

Monster Hunter Stories 3: Guide to Obtaining Rare Monster Mutations

*Monster Hunter Stories 3* features a variety of rare monster mutations that provide gameplay benefits through boosted stats and enhanced gene abilities. These mutations are linked to the game’s ecosystem restoration system, which encourages players to hatch and release monsters to recover local populations. Certain rare subspecies, like Pink Rathian, Dreadqueen Rathian, Ivory Lagiacrus, Hellblade Glavenus, Deadeye Yian Garuga, and others become available once specific ecosystem rank thresholds are met and particular environmental conditions are satisfied, such as proximity to rival or poisonous species. Mutations typically require raising a monster’s ecosystem rank to A or S and introducing a set number of related or rival monsters. This system adds strategic depth for players who seek to expand their Monstie rosters beyond core story requirements.

Overwatch Director Reflects on 5v5 vs. 6v6 Controversy

Aaron Keller, director of *Overwatch*, acknowledged that the decision to reduce team size from six to five players in *Overwatch 2* was among the most divisive changes in the game’s history. Released with 5v5 in 2022, this shift altered gameplay and the role of tank heroes, leading to sustained community debate. Keller admitted the development team waited about a year to formally address player concerns, which he regrets, noting this delay cost them trust. After testing limited-time 6v6 modes in 2024, Blizzard reintroduced 6v6 as a permanent option, which remains popular but fragmented the player base. Keller emphasized that the team now prioritizes experimental modes and proactive communication to better align with player expectations. The insight underscores the difficulty live service games face in balancing innovation with community preferences.

Jeff Kaplan Wanted World of Warcraft as a First-Person MMO

Former Blizzard VP and game designer Jeff Kaplan revealed he strongly pushed for *World of Warcraft* to adopt a first-person perspective during its early development. Though WoW ultimately launched as a third-person game, Kaplan preferred first-person viewing inspired by his experience with *EverQuest*. He believed first-person would enhance players’ connection to their characters and the game world. Kaplan acknowledged arguments in favor of third-person visuals but noted a first-person WoW could have profoundly influenced MMO design and player experience. This alternate design approach could have led to different gameplay conventions and industry trends, though World of Warcraft’s actual impact remains significant. Kaplan’s reflections highlight how foundational design choices shape long-term game evolution.

Bungie Tests Experimental Duos Mode in Marathon

Bungie is launching an experimental duos mode in *Marathon*, its multiplayer shooter, beginning March 18. This limited-time queue will be available solely on the Perimeter map and run for approximately two weeks. The mode responds to community requests for a dedicated two-player squad option, complementing existing solo and three-player squad modes. Bungie describes the feature as a test, anticipating adjustments based on player feedback. Adding duos introduces challenges such as altered team dynamics, map flow, resource distribution, and matchmaking times. Bungie intends to use this trial to evaluate duos' impact on gameplay before considering a more permanent implementation. The introduction of duos reflects ongoing efforts to refine player grouping options.
