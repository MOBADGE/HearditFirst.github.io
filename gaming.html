Wikigacha: A Gacha Card Game Based on Wikipedia Articles

A new browser-based card game called Wikigacha has attracted attention for its unusual twist on the gacha genre. Instead of typical anime-style characters, players open packs containing cards that represent Wikipedia articles, ranging from historical events like the Gouzenko Affair to topics such as the List of Red Hot Chili Peppers band members. The game incorporates a battle system where card attack power depends on article popularity and defense on article length, allowing daily challenges against "raid bosses." Unlike many gacha games, Wikigacha limits pack openings to 10 per day, with a slow refill rate and optional ad-based top-ups, though ad support seems limited outside Japan. This approach offers a light-hearted parody of gacha mechanics while combining collectible strategy with some educational elements.

World of Warcraft: Midnight Revamps Silvermoon, Questioning MMO Expansion Models

The recent revamp of Silvermoon city in World of Warcraft: Midnight has drawn praise for its impressive redesign and thoughtful world-building. Rather than providing an entirely new continent, as is typical for MMO expansions, Blizzard has rebuilt the old Silvermoon and its surrounding zones, adding detail, new questing content, and revitalizing areas like Zul'Aman from a raid to a full zone. This approach suggests that revisiting and enhancing existing locations can deliver fresh player experiences without expanding the game's vast map further. The update raises questions about the conventional MMO strategy of releasing new continents every few years and whether focusing on older worlds might produce a more coherent and immersive game environment.

Chasm: The Rift, a Pioneering but Underappreciated 1997 Ukrainian FPS

Before the well-known Stalker: Shadow of Chernobyl, Ukrainian developer Action Forms released Chasm: The Rift, an early 3D first-person shooter notable for its custom engine and novel features. Although visually similar to Quake, Chasm blends 2.5D geometry with polygonal models but lacks full 3D vertical movement, resulting in a slower, more methodical gameplay style. The game stands out for implementing selective enemy dismemberment with tactical impact, a feature not common in shooters of that era. Chasm also integrates a narrative with voiced mission briefings and a diverse range of environments, from military bases to alien worlds. While its combat and level design have shortcomings compared to contemporaries, the game holds historical interest for its ambitious design and technical innovations.

Slay the Spire 2 Launches with Unexpected Popularity

Slay the Spire 2, the sequel to the popular roguelike deckbuilding game, launched in early access to a much larger audience than anticipated. Within days, it reached over 430,000 concurrent players on Steam and topped global sales charts, surpassing other notable releases like Bungie's new shooter Marathon. The developer, Mega Crit, had described their game as “chicken noodle soup” of videogames, implying comfort and familiarity rather than excitement, yet the strong reception indicates a solid fanbase eager for the follow-up. This success shows that well-made and approachable games can still perform very well in a competitive market.

Cities: Skylines Free Weekend and New Expansion Ahead

Paradox Interactive and Colossal Order have announced a free weekend for the original Cities: Skylines city builder, running until March 9 on Steam, coupled with a 90% discount on the base game. This comes alongside the release of a new expansion called Race Day, launching March 10, which allows players to create motor race events, tracks, and associated city infrastructure. A free update will accompany the expansion, adding features like illuminated billboards and an employment visualizer. The promotion and content update notably coincide with Cities: Skylines 2's troubled launch, potentially drawing players back to the more stable original title and extending its longevity.

Tabletop Influences Revive Modern Videogame RPGs

In a reflective analysis, the role of tabletop gaming in revitalizing modern RPGs has been highlighted. Older computer RPGs adapted mechanics and player freedom from tabletop role-playing such as D&D, but later titles shifted towards action-oriented combat and cinematic storytelling with reduced agency. Recently, the renewed popularity of live-play tabletop sessions and indie development have inspired games like Disco Elysium, Baldur’s Gate 3, and the new Esoteric Ebb to reintroduce deep roleplaying, player choice, and complex interactions reminiscent of tabletop campaigns.
