Fallout: New Vegas Receives First Comprehensive Third-Person Animation Overhaul

A notable modder known as Wombat has released a major overhaul for Fallout: New Vegas focusing on third-person movement animations. This is the first mod to extensively update player and NPC animations for actions like idle stances, walking, running, jumping, landing, and melee attacks in third-person perspective. Previous community efforts mainly focused on first-person animations, leaving this aspect untouched until now. The overhaul also fixes several animation bugs such as unnatural finger movements and character sliding issues. To use the mod, players must install prerequisite plugins like the kNVSE Animation Plugin and JIP LN NVSE Plugin. This improvement matters for fans who value the ability to switch between first- and third-person views, enhancing immersion and gameplay variety in the classic RPG.

UK Court Affirms RuneScape Gold Theft as a Legitimate Crime in Legal Ruling

A UK judge ruled that stealing virtual gold from Old School RuneScape can legally constitute theft, setting a precedent in the case against a former Jagex employee accused of hacking 68 accounts and illegally selling over $700,000 worth of in-game currency for Bitcoin. The ruling recognizes RuneScape gold as property under the Theft Act of 1968, considering its tradable value both inside and outside the game. This legal stance clarifies how virtual assets can be subject to real-world property laws, which could influence future cases involving in-game economy crimes and developer accountability measures.
