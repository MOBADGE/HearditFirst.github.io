Horizon Chase Turbo Delisting Announced for June

The arcade racing game Horizon Chase Turbo, along with its mobile-only predecessor Horizon Chase, will be removed from digital stores as of June 1. Although owners will still be able to download their copies, new purchases will no longer be possible. The delisting is unexpected given Horizon Chase Turbo’s lack of licensed vehicles and absence of online features, which typically limit licensing-related costs. The game’s developer, Aquiris, is owned by Epic Games, which recently laid off over 1,000 employees. While it is unclear if these layoffs influenced the decision, Horizon Chase 2 will remain available, including on the Epic Games Store and other platforms.

Caves of Qud Ported to Nintendo Switch Against Odds

Developers of the complex roguelike RPG Caves of Qud have managed to port the game to the Nintendo Switch, a platform not traditionally suited to such CPU-heavy and control-intensive titles. Co-creator Brian Bucklew described this effort as an "irrational decision" driven by a desire to solve difficult problems. The port became feasible after a UI overhaul in 2024 optimized the game for gamepad input, making it playable on devices like the Steam Deck. On Switch, Caves of Qud achieved a top-five ranking in sales, challenging assumptions about the market for intricate PC games on Nintendo consoles. The development team is now exploring a mobile version that adapts controls and interface for smaller, portrait-mode screens.

Stardew Valley Player Misled by AI Guidance on Vinegar Production

A Stardew Valley player inadvertently produced 136 bottles of rice juice instead of vinegar after following incorrect AI-generated advice from a Google query. The AI response mistakenly implied that unmilled rice could be used directly in a keg to make vinegar, whereas the game requires rice to be milled first. The error resulted in a waste of resources and time in-game. This incident highlights the limitations of current AI summarization tools for gaming instructions and the value of consulting verified community sources. Similar mistakes have been reported in Stardew Valley’s player base over the years.

1047 Games Developing New Titanfall-Inspired Shooter

1047 Games, known for Splitgate, confirmed it is working on a new multiplayer shooter influenced by Titanfall and Call of Duty: Black Ops 3. CEO Ian Proulx mentioned that a small part of their team has begun development on this movement-focused shooter alongside ongoing support for Splitgate. Despite a promising start, Splitgate 2 faced challenges, leading to a reversion to beta status and staff reductions. The new project appears to be an attempt to diversify the studio’s portfolio. The company plans to start playtesting soon and has invited fans of the cited games to sign up for early access.

World of Warcraft Raid Exploit Leads to Disqualification

In the ongoing race to complete the latest World of Warcraft raid, a guild named RAoV QA Strikes Back was disqualified for exploiting a bug to defeat a raid boss instantly. Their exploit caused damage totaling nearly 300 million in seconds, performed by players with low-level gear and humorous character names indicating intentional rule-breaking. Blizzard removed their names from official leaderboards shortly after the kill. This is not the first time this guild has used exploits for world-first titles, reigniting concerns about fairness in competitive raiding. Meanwhile, no legitimate guild has yet defeated the boss by intended gameplay methods.

Fortnite Developer Responds to Epic Games Layoffs

Following Epic Games’ announcement of layoffs affecting roughly 20% of its workforce, Fortnite producer Robby Williams addressed the community asking for patience. He acknowledged the difficulty developers face while restructuring and admitted uncertainty over how these cuts will affect Fortnite’s development long term. Williams expressed support for impacted colleagues and pledged to continue improving the game despite challenges. Epic CEO Tim Sweeney indicated further company updates on Fortnite’s roadmap are expected soon. The layoffs mark a significant shift for one of the industry’s largest studios.
