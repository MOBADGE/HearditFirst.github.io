UK Court Affirms RuneScape Gold Theft as Legal Property Theft

A UK court ruled that in-game gold in Old School RuneScape qualifies as property under the Theft Act of 1968, legitimizing legal action against a former Jagex developer who stole and sold over $700,000 worth of virtual currency. The court found that the time-consuming process of acquiring gold and its real-world trading value justified this classification.

This ruling sets a legal precedent establishing that virtual assets in online games can be protected as tangible property under certain circumstances. It underscores ongoing challenges game companies face with internal security and theft, as well as the evolving intersection between virtual economies and real-world law.
