Umamusume Fans Take Cosplay to the Racetrack

Fans of the anime-style horse racing game *Umamusume: Pretty Derby* have taken their enthusiasm beyond conventions, organizing real-life races in full cosplay at horse racing tracks worldwide. Originating from Japan, the game gained a significant Western following after its global launch last year, notable for its in-depth career mode where players train anthropomorphized horse girls.

While cosplay of various Umamusume characters has been popular for years, some fans have now staged actual races dressed as their favorite characters at tracks including the Royal Bangkok Sports Club in Thailand, Selangor Turf Club in Malaysia, Santa Anita Park in California, and Hipódromo de Monterrico in Peru. These fan-organized events usually take place after official horse races and are not officially sanctioned by the game’s developer, Cygames, although some locations cooperate informally. Participants race for the spirit of the game rather than prizes or formal recognition. This unique extension of cosplay culture reflects the passion and dedication of the Umamusume community.

PBA Pro Bowling 2026 Offers Realistic Bowling Challenge

*PBA Pro Bowling 2026*, a bowling simulator available on Steam, offers a detailed physics-driven experience that captures many nuances of the sport, such as oil patterns on lanes affecting ball behavior. A recent firsthand look revealed how even simple strategies, like rolling the ball straight rather than hooking it, can be surprisingly effective in lower-difficulty settings.

The game features various bowling styles including candlepin and duckpin, and includes leagues, tournaments, and a pro tour with increasingly challenging AI opponents. Despite its depth, it remains accessible for casual players and accurately models difficulties encountered by bowlers, such as risky split pin scenarios. Players can upgrade equipment and personalize their character’s style, creating a blend of simulation and progression. For enthusiasts interested in bowling mechanics, the game serves as a realistic virtual training ground.

Crimson Desert Continues Evolving With Mixed Reception

Pearl Abyss’s open-world game *Crimson Desert* has surpassed 150 hours of playtime for some players but remains difficult to categorically define. It straddles the line between action game, RPG, and MMO without fully committing to any single genre. The development team has made numerous post-launch adjustments, removing or modifying some original design choices—like removing harsh inventory limits and simplifying fast travel—to address player feedback.

The vast game world is composed of multiple regions with extensive side content, puzzles, and activities. However, the main story is considered thin and functional rather than deeply engaging. Updates continue to improve quality-of-life features, such as adding a hide helmet option and expanding storage capacity. While still imperfect, *Crimson Desert* demonstrates a shift toward a more player-friendly experience and a willingness by Pearl Abyss to adapt based on community response.

Dwarf Fortress Co-Creator Tarn Adams Shares Gaming Interests

Tarn Adams, co-creator of the influential colony simulator *Dwarf Fortress*, recently shared insights into his gaming habits and development interests. He noted a particular fondness for terraforming games like *The Planet Crafter* and *Terraformental*, which involve transforming alien planets’ environments gradually over time. Adams sees these sci-fi themed games as complementing his largely fantasy-focused work on *Dwarf Fortress*.

Among his most-played games is *Factorio*, a factory-building simulation in which he has logged over 1,400 hours across numerous expansions and mods. He also discussed the early computer games that shaped his development path, many created in BASIC programming language during the 1980s. His approach to game design balances procedural generation with handcrafted elements, reflecting decades of experience in the industry.

Intel’s Latest Processors Mark a Shift in Strategy

Intel’s refreshed Arrow Lake processors, including the Core Ultra 7 270K Plus and Core Ultra 5 250K Plus, have impressed reviewers with strong performance improvements and competitive pricing. These newer chips improve upon their disappointing 2024 predecessors by offering better power efficiency and multi-core capabilities, becoming solid contenders in the mid-range CPU market.

Intel representatives highlighted organizational changes and renewed customer focus as factors behind the improved products. Alongside Arrow Lake refreshes, Intel’s upcoming Panther Lake mobile chips show promise, though supply issues with fast memory currently limit adoption in compact devices. Intel’s recent efforts indicate a strategic redirection to produce competitively priced, capable CPUs that cater to a broad consumer
