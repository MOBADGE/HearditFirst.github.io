New City Builder City Masterplan Enters Competitive Space

Paradox Interactive’s recently announced city-building game, *City Masterplan*, aims to challenge the long-standing dominance of *Cities: Skylines* in the highly realistic metropolis simulation genre. Developed by 1:1 Studio, the game emphasizes realism and nuanced control, featuring a large 24x24km map, freeform road construction not bound by grids, and true-to-scale iconic buildings. It also offers diverse city styles, including American and Chinese designs, supported by procedural content generation.

This is notable because *Cities: Skylines* has seen limited serious competition since its release over a decade ago, and its sequel launched with performance issues and mixed reception. Whether *City Masterplan* can provide a viable alternative remains to be seen, as the developer has not announced a release date, but expectations point towards 2027.

Half-Life Mod Lets Players Rescue Scientists and Guards

A mod called *Half-Life: Lemmings*, created by TeamClown, introduces a new gameplay element allowing players to actively rescue the scientists and guards trapped within Black Mesa, a feature absent in the original *Half-Life*. Players can guide up to 45 NPCs to designated rescue points, and NPCs exhibit enhanced AI behaviors like climbing and jumping to follow the player.

The mod also upgrades guard combat capabilities and gives scientists limited self-defense by arming them with pistols. This version currently covers about half of the game’s story and is built for *Half-Life: Source*. It requires purchasing *Half-Life: Source*, which remains available despite no longer being listed directly on Steam.

WCW’s $400,000 Wrestling Gimmick Based on Mortal Kombat Ended by Legal Threat

In a detailed retrospective on a 1990s wrestling gimmick, WCW’s failed "Glacier" character was revealed as an expensive and legally precarious attempt to capitalize on Mortal Kombat’s popularity. WCW spent $35,000 on a costume akin to Sub-Zero and another $400,000 on an elaborate entrance featuring lasers and artificial snow.

Midway, the rights holder of Mortal Kombat, issued a cease-and-desist threat, which led WCW to alter the character’s appearance after only four matches. The Glacier gimmick never found success and became known as a symbol of WCW’s excessive spending and poor creative choices during its peak years.

Stardew Valley’s Clint: Blacksmith Profile and Upcoming Marriage Candidate

As part of the upcoming Stardew Valley 1.7 update, Clint, the local blacksmith of Pelican Town, will become a marriage candidate alongside another character, Sandy. Currently, players can befriend Clint to obtain recipes and participate in limited heart events.

Clint’s schedule remains consistent throughout the year, spending most days at the Smithy and evenings at the Saloon, with exceptions during specific seasonal events. His preferred gifts are primarily gemstones and smelted metal bars, while he dislikes flowers and raw ores. Players building relationships with him will receive recipe gifts and will eventually gain the option to marry him in the forthcoming update.

Roblox Introduces Roblox Plus Subscription with New Monetization Model

Roblox has revealed *Roblox Plus*, a monthly subscription launching on April 30 that will replace its previous Premium plan. Subscribers receive escalating discounts on in-game purchases, unlimited private server access (often typically paid), and fee-free Robux transfers to others aged 18 or older.

The subscription removes Premium’s Robux monthly stipend and bonus, requiring users to pay more if they want a steady Robux inflow. Additionally, Roblox Plus incentivizes creators by paying them Robux based on subscriber engagement in paid private servers and offering bonuses for convincing users to subscribe. This shift reflects Roblox’s evolving monetization strategies and focuses on increasing creator earnings tied to subscriptions.

US Government Campaign Targets Gamers for Air Traffic Controller Recruitment

The US Department of Transportation launched a campaign aimed at recruiting air traffic controllers by appealing to gamers. The campaign features a video using game footage and encourages viewers to consider air traffic control as a career rather than a game.

This effort responds to a staffing shortage worsened by a recent government shutdown that led to increased retirements. The campaign highlights the high average salary for controllers but acknowledges the job’s stresses. The government seeks to attract younger candidates who may possess skills applicable to the demanding role of managing air traffic safely.
