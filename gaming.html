Absolum's First Major Update Introduces Rule Modifiers and Corrupted Biomes

Guard Crush Games has released the first substantial update for Absolum, a roguelite beat 'em up that gained notable attention since its release last October. The "Threads of Fate" update adds several new gameplay features, most notably a mystic ordeals system offering players customizable rule modifiers, including presets like boss runs and horde modes, as well as the ability to create and share custom challenges. The game now replaces its previous rift system with randomized corrupted biomes, which appear at the start of each run and provide added difficulty and rewards. Additional changes include new skins and emotes for characters, upgraded mounts with enhanced combat capabilities, and various bug fixes that address gameplay issues. This update reinforces Absolum's position as an innovative entrant in the beat 'em up genre by expanding its replayability and customization.

Hellscreen Exits Early Access with Reduced Scope

Developer Jamie D has officially launched Hellscreen 1.0 after a prolonged early access period marked by silence since its last update two years ago. Originally planned as a three-episode title, the developer was forced to condense the game into a single episode due to budget constraints and underperformance. Hellscreen's core mechanic is a rearview mirror that enables players to see and shoot enemies behind them, making for distinctive combat encounters. Although shortened, Jamie expresses pride in the final product, drawing comparisons to smaller but well-regarded titles. To reflect the scaled-down vision, the game is priced lower and included in a discounted bundle with Vapourwave Pinball. The full original scope remains unfulfilled, but Hellscreen represents a completed experiment in innovative FPS design.

Unreal Tournament 2004 Released for Free with Community Patch

The fan community project OldUnreal, with Epic Games' consent, has made Unreal Tournament 2004 available as a free download, along with the first public patch the game has received in over two decades. The patch updates the game for modern Windows, Mac, and Linux systems, introducing bug fixes, a new renderer, and an SDL backend. The game can be downloaded via an installer that retrieves the original disc image hosted on the Internet Archive. Unreal Tournament 2004 is well-regarded for its variety of game modes, including the vehicle-based Onslaught and objective-driven Assault modes, which expanded on its predecessor's offerings. While the longevity of multiplayer activity remains uncertain, the release provides both veterans and new players with improved access to a significant title in the classic arena shooter genre.

Legacy of Kain: Ascendance Revealed as 2D Action-Platformer

The Legacy of Kain series is expanding with a new entry, Legacy of Kain: Ascendance, which departs from previous 3D action-adventure formats to become a pixel-art 2D action-platformer. The game features integrated combat and movement mechanics encouraging constant player motion and fluid transitions between fighting and platforming. Players will control three characters‚ÄîKain, Raziel (both in human and vampire forms), and a new character named Elaleth introduced in a recent prequel comic. The game employs a stamina-like system regulating abilities and movement. Notably, several original voice actors have returned for their roles. Legacy of Kain: Ascendance is set for release on March 31 across Steam, GOG, and Epic platforms. Meanwhile, Legacy of Kain: Defiance Remastered will launch on March 3.
