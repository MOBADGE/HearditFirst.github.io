Batman: Arkham Origins Gets a Graphics Mod Boost

A new graphics mod for Batman: Arkham Origins, called Arkham Origins Advanced Graphics 2.0, has been released and offers a significant visual upgrade reminiscent of a remaster. The mod enhances snow effects by increasing particle size and count, improves lighting to better convey the game’s wintry setting, and upgrades textures including Gotham’s skyline and Batman’s suit and cape using assets from later Arkham games. Shadows and reflections receive notable polish, reducing visual artifacts that were present in the original. However, cutscenes remain limited to 720p due to format constraints. Fans interested in enhancing their experience can install the mod from Nexus Mods, though it requires at least one DLC due to the game's configuration.

Death Stranding 2 Exceeds Two Million Copies Sold

Death Stranding 2: On the Beach has surpassed two million copies sold worldwide following its recent PC release. The game sold approximately 425,000 copies on PC in its first week, adding to the 1.6 million units sold on PlayStation 5 since launch less than a year ago. This marks one of the fastest transitions from console exclusivity to PC in recent years. The PC launch also coincided with a price discount on the PS5 version, boosting sales. This success comes amid reports that Sony is scaling back its strategy for porting singleplayer prestige titles to PC, signaling a possible shift in how the company approaches platform exclusivity.

Star Wars: Zero Company Director on Elevating Turn-Based Tactics

Greg Foertsch, creative director of the upcoming tactical game Star Wars: Zero Company, expressed a determination to challenge common genre conventions. He argues turn-based tactics games do not have to settle for limited storytelling, poor graphics, or clunky controls, and believes that depth and elegance can coexist. Foertsch highlights that while gameplay is vital, narrative and visual quality should also be priorities, especially in single-player games. His approach draws on previous experience, such as working on Marvel’s Midnight Suns, aiming to deliver an immersive tactical experience with compelling story elements and production values.

Epic Layoffs Spotlight Challenges of Health Insurance

Epic Games recently laid off over 1,000 employees, including Mike Prinke, a technical writer undergoing treatment for terminal brain cancer. His wife publicly explained the hardships caused by the loss of both income and vital health insurance, especially given the pre-existing condition classification which complicates obtaining new coverage. Epic CEO Tim Sweeney responded on social media, stating the company is working to resolve insurance issues for the affected family and expressed regret for not anticipating the situation. The incident underscores the precarious nature of employer-based health insurance coverage in the U.S. and the human impacts of corporate layoffs.

Crimson Desert Patch Adds Mounts and Adjusts Stealing Mechanics

The latest patch for Crimson Desert introduces five new mounts obtainable after completing specific conditions, expanding travel options for players. The update also brings numerous quality-of-life improvements including movement control refinements, new chests placed on the map, and modifications to criminal contribution mechanics. Now, players are only penalized for stealing if caught by NPCs, which affects reputation systems and regional rewards. The developers have also replaced some visual assets, addressing previous concerns about AI-generated artwork in the game’s environment. This patch reflects ongoing efforts to refine and expand Crimson Desert’s large open world.
