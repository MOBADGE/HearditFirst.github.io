Microsoft Clarifies AI PC Branding Amid Confusing Messaging

Microsoft published a beginner-friendly guide to AI PCs that highlights ongoing confusion about their definition. The company describes AI PCs as devices built to run artificial intelligence features locally, powered by specialized neural processing units (NPUs) for improved speed, battery life, and integrated AI tools like Copilot. However, previous statements have suggested all Windows 11 PCs are AI PCs regardless of dedicated hardware. The new guide distinguishes between AI PCs and higher-tier "Copilot+ PCs," which require NPUs with specific performance metrics and meet certain hardware specs. Microsoft's messaging aims to reassure users that AI is meant to assist rather than replace them, but the evolving terminology and hardware requirements make understanding what qualifies as an AI PC complex for consumers.
