No New Lovecraftian Nier Game – April Fools Clarified

Recently, fans of the Nier series were teased by an announcement about a new game titled *Nier: Cosmic Horror*, featuring unusual imagery like a robot with a tentacle for a head. This announcement led to speculation about a Lovecraftian-themed Nier sequel. However, the Nier team has confirmed that the reveal was an April Fools' joke, not an actual project in development. Although this disappointed some fans hoping for a sequel to *Nier: Automata* (released in 2017), the clarification helps set expectations. The series likely has other projects in the works, but *Cosmic Horror* is not one of them.

Nvidia’s DLSS 4.5 UI Preset Shows No Visible Improvement

Nvidia recently updated its DLSS frame generation technology to include a new preset reportedly designed to improve the visual clarity of user interface elements in games. Testing with *Dragon Age: The Veilguard* and *Hogwarts Legacy* showed no noticeable difference between the new UI-focused preset and the existing default preset. This raises questions about whether the feature is working as intended or if it may be a driver bug. Given existing issues with the new DLSS update affecting frame rate limiters, it seems likely the UI improvements have not yet materialized, though there remains potential for future fixes.

Claude Code CLI Source Leak Due to Human Error

Anthropic, the company behind the AI coding assistant Claude Code, experienced a source code leak exposing over 512,000 lines of its command line interface (CLI) code. The leak appears to have been caused by a release packaging error rather than a security breach, and the company assures that no sensitive customer data or credentials were involved. Despite this, the leak gives coding enthusiasts insight into Claude Code’s internal workings, such as its sophisticated memory system. This is Anthropic’s second reported data leak in a short period, which may affect its reputation amid a competitive AI market.

How to Obtain Arc Flex Rubber in Arc Raiders

In *Arc Raiders*’ recent Flashpoint update, players are tasked with donating Arc Flex Rubber to progress the High-Gain Antenna project. This resource drops rarely from higher-tier enemy Arc machines, including Tick, Sentinel, Leaper, and others. The new Close Scrutiny map condition increases the quantity and quality of loot from Assessor enemies, making it one of the best sources for Arc Flex Rubber and other materials needed for the project. However, this map requires good gear and skill due to its difficulty. Collecting these materials is essential to advancing the project and unlocking further stages.

Google Warns Quantum Computing Could Threaten Bitcoin Encryption by 2032

A new whitepaper from Google Quantum AI researchers predicts that quantum computers capable of breaking the cryptography behind bitcoin and similar cryptocurrencies may arrive sooner than expected—potentially by 2032 with a 10% probability. This estimate is based on advancements reducing the number of physical qubits needed for quantum attacks to under 500,000, far lower than prior assessments. While quantum attacks would compromise public-key cryptography underlying blockchain security, symmetric encryption methods would be less affected, resulting in a gradual need for stronger cryptographic standards. The findings highlight the importance of transitioning to post-quantum security measures in the coming decade.

Fortnite’s Future Uncertain After Significant Layoffs

Epic Games has laid off about 1,000 employees—around 20% of its workforce—citing a downturn in *Fortnite* player engagement and financial pressures despite generating substantial revenue. While the company aims to continue delivering new content and experiences for *Fortnite*, the reduced team size and internal comments indicate a challenging road ahead. Epic has already discontinued three peripheral game modes, likely to focus resources on core experiences. Remaining developers face significant pressure to maintain player interest and revenue with fewer staff, making *Fortnite*’s long-term trajectory less certain amid shifting fan sentiment and market conditions.
