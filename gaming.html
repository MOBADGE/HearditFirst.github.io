Ivy Road Studio Closes After Funding Challenges

The developer Ivy Road, known for the cozy tea shop adventure game *Wanderstop*, is shutting down as of March 31. Despite positive reception for *Wanderstop*, which featured notable collaborators like Davey Wrenden and composer C418, the studio struggled to secure funding for its follow-up project, *Engine Angel*. After layoffs in January due to unsuccessful funding and publisher searches, the team decided to close. Although *Wanderstop* remains available for purchase, Ivy Road hinted at a final surprise related to the game in partnership with Annapurna Interactive. The closure highlights ongoing difficulties smaller studios face in raising funds within the current gaming industry climate.

ZA/UM’s Zero Parades Moves Beyond Police Themes

ZA/UM’s new RPG *Zero Parades*, set for release this year, initially explored police-centered storylines similar to those in *Disco Elysium*, their critically acclaimed prior title. However, the studio pivoted away from this concept, partly due to shifting public attitudes towards police, and a desire to pursue a different genre. The team now focuses on espionage and thriller elements, aiming to offer fresh narrative experiences. Writer Honey Watson noted a greater political urgency influencing the game’s plot, reflecting global sociopolitical shifts. This directional change marks a notable evolution for ZA/UM as they avoid retreading familiar ground from *Disco Elysium*.

Frictional Games Bundle Offers Horror Series for $15

Humble Bundle has launched a sale featuring the complete collection of Frictional Games’ horror titles for $15. The bundle includes *Penumbra* trilogy, *Amnesia* series (*The Dark Descent*, *A Machine for Pigs*, *Rebirth*, and *The Bunker*), and *SOMA*. These games represent significant contributions to the horror genre, known for their psychological and survival elements. The offer delivers considerable value for new players interested in exploring this influential catalogue, with Steam keys available until April 1, 2027. This bundle underlines the enduring popularity of Frictional’s work and provides an accessible entry point for horror game enthusiasts.

Path of Exile’s Trading Community Faces Uncertainty After Ban

Jenebu, the leader of a major Path of Exile trading Discord server called The Forbidden Trove (TFT), has been banned by Grinding Gear Games. Jenebu’s account held over 1,500 rare and unique items used in high-trust “mirror services,” allowing duplication of valuable items through in-game mechanics. While no direct evidence of illicit real-money trading was found, the ban disrupts a key player-driven trading hub utilized by many within the PoE economy. The move has spurred debate among players about third-party trading platforms and the game's item economy. This event may accelerate a decline in the server’s influence following recent official changes making player trading more accessible.

Deus Ex: Human Revolution Passed On Original Composer’s Track

Alexander Brandon, composer for the original *Deus Ex*, shared that his submitted track for *Deus Ex: Human Revolution* was rejected as “not the right fit” by Eidos Montreal, the new team owning the franchise. Michael McCann was hired instead for the 2011 prequel, creating a distinct yet recognizable soundtrack. Brandon expressed understanding of the decision and respect for the new audio director’s vision. He also related disappointment over not contributing to *Deus Ex: The Fall*. Brandon’s experience reflects the challenges faced by veteran game composers amid franchise transitions and evolving creative directions.

PUBG: Blindspot Early Access Ends After Low Player Base

*PUBG: Blindspot*, a top-down tactical shooter from Arc Team and Krafton, is ending early access on March 30, less than two months after launch. While initially promising, the game failed to maintain a sufficient player base to sustain matchmaking and ongoing development. The closure illustrates the difficulties free-to-play games face when unable to build and retain active communities, which are critical for success and monetization. Krafton’s broader recent financial pressures, including declining profits and structural changes, may have influenced this decision. Arc Team plans to regroup with hopes for future projects.
