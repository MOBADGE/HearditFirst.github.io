Dave Oshry on His PC Gaming Favorites and Habits

Dave Oshry, CEO of New Blood Interactive, recently shared insights into his long-standing engagement with PC gaming. Oshry’s favorite genre is classic CRPGs, reflecting his fondness for titles like Baldur’s Gate and Fallout. Currently, he is playing *Aether & Iron*, an RPG noted for its narrative focus and retro-futuristic art deco style. Oshry also highlighted a modded version of *Vice City* as a game he revisits often, praising its atmospheric and well-crafted representation of 1980s Miami.

Oshry keeps some of the oldest games installed on his PC, including *Wolfenstein 3-D*—notably, the Mac version he first modded as a child. His deepest playtime, however, is logged in *Fallout 76*, with nearly 489 hours on Steam, which he enjoys primarily as a single-player experience for its detailed world and cryptid-hunting gameplay. Unsurprisingly, Oshry will never uninstall *Doom* from his PC, emphasizing its enduring appeal and the vibrant modding community around it.

Airborne Empire Launches 1.0 With New Content and Sale

The sequel to the flying city-builder *Airborne Kingdom*, titled *Airborne Empire*, has officially left early access with a substantive 1.0 update. Developed by The Wandering Band, the game expands on its predecessor’s foundation by introducing a larger world, light RPG elements, and new regions such as the snowy area Kingsfell. This new region features icy waterways and environmental challenges that add complexity to city-building and exploration.

The update also brings an expanded narrative, new quests, characters, structures, upgrades, and customization options. A notable gameplay addition is the jet engine, which enables sudden speed bursts useful in combat and tactical maneuvers. To mark the launch, *Airborne Empire* is available at a 50% discount through May 1. Additionally, the original *Airborne Kingdom* is discounted by 80%, encouraging players to experience both titles.

The Enduring Appeal of Arthur Morgan’s Journal in Red Dead Redemption 2

One aspect of *Red Dead Redemption 2* that draws attention is Arthur Morgan’s in-game journal, praised for its detailed and personalized art. As players progress, the journal fills with sketches inspired by encounters and story elements unique to each playthrough. This dynamic, player-reflective feature enhances immersion and has encouraged fans to create real-world sketches based on Arthur’s drawings.

The journal stands out among in-game art features for its authenticity and narrative integration, bringing a relatable dimension to the protagonist and the story. Digital versions of the journal are available, allowing players who do not intend to sketch themselves to experience Arthur Morgan’s recorded memories in detail.

Unearthed Cut Sewers Map Offers Insight into Dark Souls 2 Design

YouTuber Doneda has uncovered and restored a large, previously cut sewer map from *Dark Souls 2*. This map was intended to replace what ultimately became The Gutter area. While incomplete and lacking enemies, the section is interconnected and textured, revealing varied environments including temple-like spaces and a tavern.

The exploration of this cut content provides valuable insights into the design and development challenges of *Dark Souls 2*, a title known for its difficult creation process. The map contains notable spaces such as a potentially mischaracterized boss arena and recreational areas, highlighting the complexity and scale initially planned for the game.

New Create Aeronautics Mod Adds Functional Vehicles to Minecraft

A new mod for Minecraft titled Create Aeronautics has been released as an expansion of the existing Create mod. This addon provides tools and components for constructing fully functional vehicles including planes, helicopters, airships, terrestrial off-road vehicles, powered boats, and farming machinery like combine harvesters.

Create Aeronautics introduces new build mechanics such as propeller bearings for flying machines and specialized blocks for lift. The mod requires both the base Create mod and a physics pipeline mod called Sable, enabling dynamic block interactions. This extensive mod expands Minecraft’s construction possibilities significantly, allowing for more complex mechanical creations.
