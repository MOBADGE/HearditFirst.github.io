Heretic 2 Receives Fan-Made Sourceport Enhancements

A reverse-engineered sourceport named Heretic2R has been released for the 1998 third-person action-adventure Heretic 2, a sequel to the classic Raven Software FPS Heretic. Developed by programmer MaxEd, the sourceport fixes bugs, improves framerate (unlocking up to 1000 FPS), adds widescreen support with automatic HUD scaling, and introduces various cosmetic improvements to modernize the experience. Players still require a boxed copy of Heretic 2 updated to version 1.06 to use the sourceport. This development makes the somewhat forgotten sequel more accessible on modern PCs. Considering that both Heretic 2 and Hexen 2 publishers are now under Microsoft's ownership, a formal rerelease or remaster could be possible in the future, potentially revealed at upcoming events like QuakeCon.

Lawn Mowing Simulator 2 Launches Kickstarter With High-Quality Grass Tech

Skyhook Games has launched a Kickstarter campaign for Lawn Mowing Simulator 2, the sequel to its niche lawn trimming simulator. Already surpassing a £15,000 funding goal, the sequel will set players in the USA and expand gameplay with new tools such as strimmers and leaf blowers. Notably, the game features significantly improved virtual grass technology compared to the original, adding flowers, weeds, and more varied grass textures and colors. Although no firm release date has been announced, the game is targeted for release within 2026 and appears to be well into development.

Ivy Road Studio to Close Following Funding Challenges

Ivy Road, known for the cozy tea shop adventure Wanderstop, announced it will shut down on March 31, 2026. After failing to secure publishing for its new project Engine Angel, the team underwent layoffs in January and ultimately decided to close the studio. Wanderstop was well received, with creative input from notable writers and composer C418. While the closure is disappointing, the studio indicated that Wanderstop will remain available for purchase, and Annapurna Interactive will share upcoming news related to bringing the game to new players. Ivy Road expressed gratitude for the community support and hopes their work inspires future creators.

Path of Exile’s Major Item Trader Banned Amid Economy Disruption

Jenebu, the operator of The Forbidden Trove (TFT) Discord server and a key figure in Path of Exile’s player-driven item economy, has been banned from the game, reportedly for violating terms related to item trading. The account held more than 1,500 rare and irreplaceable items, including those involved in "mirror services" that enable item duplication. Jenebu's ban raises questions about the future of TFT, a server used by many for trading and game assistance. The ban has received mixed community responses, with some praising Grinding Gear Games' enforcement and others concerned about the impact on trading and community resources. The rise of official asynchronous trading features might accelerate the decline of third-party platforms like TFT.

Deus Ex: Human Revolution’s Composer Change Reflects New Creative Direction

Alexander Brandon, composer for the original Deus Ex, revealed in an interview that he was invited to contribute a track to the 2011 Deus Ex: Human Revolution soundtrack but his submission was ultimately rejected for not fitting the new creative vision. The audio director at Eidos Montreal wanted to establish a distinct sound with Michael McCann as the new composer. Brandon expressed understanding of the decision though he showed some disappointment regarding later projects like Deus Ex: The Fall, where he was not involved. McCann's work on Human Revolution is recognized for successfully differentiating the game’s audio while retaining series identity.

PUBG: Blindspot Shuts Down After Less Than Two Months of Early Access

PUBG: Blindspot, a top-down tactical shooter spin-off from the PUBG franchise, is ending its early access period on March 30, 2026, less than two months after launch. Despite some positive reception, the game struggled with low player counts, leading to matchmaking issues and inability to sustain a robust online experience. Developed by Arc Team, the closure reflects ongoing challenges for free-to-play multiplayer games to maintain active audiences. The shutdown also follows financial strains at publisher Krafton, which has faced profit decline and workforce changes despite revenue growth. The Arc Team studio stated it will take time to regroup, with hopes to develop new projects in the future.
