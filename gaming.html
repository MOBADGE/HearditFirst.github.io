Resident Evil Requiem Gets Whimsical Vehicle Mod

Modders have introduced a humorous modification for Capcom's recent action-horror title, Resident Evil Requiem. In the game, Leon Kennedy drives a Porsche Cayenne Turbo GT as part of product placement, but a modder named SinderyMonk has created the "Patty Wagon" mod. This replaces the Porsche with a drivable hamburger vehicle reminiscent of SpongeBob SquarePants’ Krabby Patty. The mod fits into a broader trend of playful modifications for the game, which include replacing characters with Shrek, a chibi figure, or even Thomas the Tank Engine for those averse to spiders. These mods require the REFramework to function. This illustrates the ongoing modding community enthusiasm and creative freedom players employ to alter games, sometimes to subvert or parody corporate placements.

Restored Elden Ring Cut Content Explores Deeper Lore

A modder named Transquility has brought back a removed NPC quest for Merchant Kalé in Elden Ring. Originally intended to provide context for one of the game's darkest endings—committing omnicide via the Frenzied Flame path—Kalé was meant to be a pivotal figure urging the player toward this conclusion. The mod restores Kalé’s more developed character arc, which was cut from the final release but hinted at through voice lines and datamining. This addition enriches the game’s narrative by providing clearer motivation behind the controversial ending, offering players a deeper understanding of the lore and moral ambiguity present in Elden Ring. The mod requires Mod Engine 2 for installation and runs separately to maintain compliance with anti-cheat systems.

Minecraft Announces New Underground Biome in Upcoming Drop

Minecraft revealed key features of its second content drop for 2026 during the Minecraft Live March 2026 event, scheduled soon after the initial update released. The highlight is the introduction of the “Sulfur Caves,” a new underground biome inspired by real-world sulfur hot springs. This biome replaces typical cave water pools with poison pools that cause a dizzying effect, making exploration more dangerous and nuanced. Alongside new blocks like cinnabar and sulfur, the update introduces a unique mob called the “Sulfur Cube," notable for its ability to absorb blocks and alter its behavior accordingly. This addition aims to refresh the experience of caving, which has grown expansive but somewhat repetitive since the 2021 Caves and Cliffs update.

Minecraft Dungeons Sequel Scheduled for 2026

Mojang announced a surprise sequel to Minecraft Dungeons during the same Minecraft Live event. The teaser trailer suggests a continuation of the voxel-based ARPG’s fantasy storyline, with the world once again threatened by dark forces. While no gameplay footage or detailed information has been provided, the announcement confirms a 2026 release planned across platforms. The original Minecraft Dungeons received moderate praise for being an accessible introduction to dungeon crawling but did not make a significant impact on the ARPG genre. The sequel signals Mojang’s ongoing investment in Minecraft’s spinoff titles despite mixed reception to Minecraft Legends, another recent spinoff.

GTA Chain Game: A Long-Running Community Endeavor

The Grand Theft Auto Chain Game, a community-run relay multiplayer project where players take turns completing missions on a shared save file, has reached new longevity milestones. Originally a casual save-passing exercise, it has evolved into an international community with hundreds of participants and structured management. The group has completed rounds across various GTA titles including GTA 3, Vice City, San Andreas, and more recently GTA 4. With GTA 6 slated for release later this year, the community anticipates incorporating the new game into the Chain Game format, though they expect to start with casual play before potentially expanding. The project underlines the dedication of fan communities in extending the life and enjoyment of older game titles through innovative multiplayer formats.
