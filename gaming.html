Elden Ring Cut Content Restored by Modder A modder named Transquility has brought back a significant but removed NPC quest from *Elden Ring* centered on the character Merchant Kalé. In the released game, Kalé plays a minor role, mostly as an early vendor and a gateway to other questlines. However, datamining after launch showed that Kalé was originally meant to be central to the hidden Frenzied Flame ending, which involves a grim narrative of omnicide. The restored quest reveals Kalé's tragic story and motivations, offering more context for this dark ending and adding depth to the game's world and moral ambiguity. The mod is available and playable thanks to a popular mod engine, providing fans access to content FromSoftware cut prior to release. This enriches understanding of *Elden Ring*’s narrative and may raise expectations for similar content in future official updates.

Minecraft Announces Sulfur Caves Biome in 2026 Content Drop Minecraft has revealed details about its second content update for 2026, known as the Chaos Cubed drop, which will introduce a new underground biome called the Sulfur caves. This biome is designed to alter the typical caving experience by presenting environmental hazards such as poison pools with subtle visual cues, alongside vibrant new block types—cinnabar and sulfur. The design takes inspiration from natural sulfur hot springs, including particulate effects over toxic pools. Accompanying the biome is a new mob, the Sulfur Cube, which absorbs blocks and gains different abilities depending on what it consumes. This addition promises to refresh one of the game’s core exploration features and extend underground adventures in *Minecraft*.

Minecraft Dungeons 2 Confirmed for Release This Year Mojang has announced a sequel to *Minecraft Dungeons*, the action-RPG spinoff inspired by Diablo. The announcement came during the Minecraft Live March 2026 event, accompanied by a teaser trailer showing the return of threats to the game's voxel fantasy world. While no gameplay footage has been shown yet, the sequel’s Steam page is now live. The original *Minecraft Dungeons* was received moderately well, noted for being an approachable dungeon crawl. The announcement indicates Mojang remains committed to its experimental titles despite mixed receptions to other spinoffs like *Minecraft Legends*. Fans of the first game and the wider *Minecraft* universe can expect a release later this year.

GTA Chain Game Community Prepares for GTA 6 The GTA Chain Game, an ongoing community-led project where players take sequential turns progressing through Grand Theft Auto titles to 100% completion, continues to thrive. It started as a local save-passing exercise and has evolved into an international event spanning multiple GTA games, maintaining player engagement years after their release. With the approaching launch of GTA 6, the community intends to incorporate the new title into their format once it’s available, though initial playthroughs will focus on casual enjoyment before formal rounds begin. The Chain Game exemplifies how dedicated players sustain interest in classic titles, and it will be interesting to see how they adapt to the complexities and save formats of GTA 6.

Deltarune Chapter 5 Nearing Final Testing, Development Team Moving On Toby Fox, creator of *Deltarune*, has shared an update on the status of the game’s fifth chapter. Professional testers have begun familiarizing themselves with the new content, with formal testing scheduled to start in April. Additionally, Japanese translation QA is projected to finish the same month. Notably, Fox mentioned that most of the development team has moved on from Chapter 5 aside from bug fixes and localization work. While no firm release date has been announced, this suggests that the chapter is nearing completion and preparation for launch is underway. Fans of the series, which has had slow episodic releases since 2018, can likely expect the next installment later this year.

Steam Credits Human Artists for Spring Sale Art Amid AI Discussions Steam has publicly credited the human artists behind its latest spring sale graphics, acknowledging Tiffany Diep and animator Thanhuki for their handcrafted contributions. This recognition stands out amid ongoing industry debates around AI-generated content and transparency. The response from the community has been positive, appreciating Valve's support for traditional artistry in contrast to some controversies other platforms have faced around AI-generated promotional material. The acknowledgment reinforces the importance of real artists in creating distinctive visual identity for digital storefront events in the current tech landscape.
