Crimson Desert Mods Spark Interest in Survival Elements

Recent player-created mods for Pearl Abyss's Crimson Desert are adding hunger mechanics and penalties for skipping meals, hinting at a possible full survival conversion by the modding community. The base game already features cooking and gathering systems, but these mods introduce consequences like poison or nausea from bad food and require regular eating to avoid penalties. This shift reframes the game’s vast open world towards survival gameplay, emphasizing camping, resource management, and environmental immersion. Although official mod support from Pearl Abyss is lacking, some players see potential for expanded survival features such as placeable bedrolls, item degradation, and improved camping options. The mods highlight an underused aspect of Crimson Desert’s mechanics that could deepen gameplay for those interested in survival challenges.

King Legacy Codes Offer Free Rewards in Roblox Game

King Legacy, a popular Roblox game inspired by anime and pirate themes, has several active codes available for players to redeem free Gems, Gold Keys, and experience boosts. Codes such as “DinoxLive” and “Peodiz” grant in-game currency, while others offer double experience for limited durations. Some codes require joining the official Sea King Games Roblox group or reaching specific progress milestones. Players can redeem codes through the in-game shop menu. These codes are periodically updated, with older ones expiring as new ones appear, supporting ongoing player engagement in the game’s exploration and combat activities.

Kerbal Space Program Sees Player Surge Linked to Artemis 2 Mission

Kerbal Space Program has reached its highest concurrent player count on Steam in over ten years, coinciding with NASA’s Artemis 2 lunar mission. Many players cite watching Artemis 2 as motivation to revisit the game and attempt similar spaceflight challenges. The recent increase, while not returning KSP to Steam’s top 100, signals renewed interest driven by real-world space exploration events. Concurrently, Kerbal Space Program 2 has seen a modest player increase, though still far from its predecessor’s peaks. The surge illustrates how major space missions can influence player engagement with space simulation games.

Bungie Responds to Cheating Concerns in Marathon’s Competitive Mode

Following the launch of Marathon’s Ranked mode, player reports of cheating have become widespread, leading to frustration among competitors and streamers. Bungie has reiterated its zero-tolerance approach to cheating, including permanent bans for confirmed cheaters. The developer is actively enhancing detection and enforcement systems and has already deployed some improvements, with more planned. Bungie is also working to simplify player reporting for cheating and toxicity, and considering in-game notifications for action taken on reports. Additional measures like voice chat moderation and stream sniping protections are under review. Bungie encourages the community to continue reporting issues to maintain competitive integrity.

Steam Workshop Redesign Improves Browsing Experience

Valve has introduced a redesign of the Steam Workshop interface to improve browsing speed and usability across platforms like mobile and Steam Deck. The update widens the browsing page, displays larger preview images, and enables faster filtering through rewritten page code that avoids full page reloads. A new “quick view” feature allows users to interact with items—such as viewing screenshots, favoriting, subscribing, or voting—without leaving the browsing screen. Developers can now tailor filters to specific item types within their Workshop. The redesign remains in beta as Valve collects feedback and fixes bugs, aiming for a more responsive and streamlined mod browsing experience.

Industry Loss: Yoshihisa Kishimoto, Creator of Double Dragon and Kunio-kun, Passes Away

Yoshihisa Kishimoto, the influential game designer behind the Double Dragon and Kunio-kun (River City) series, died on April 2, 2026, at age 64. Kishimoto began his career in the 1980s with LaserDisc games before crafting beat-em-up classics that pioneered the genre. Kunio-kun’s unique blend of brawling and RPG elements and Double Dragon’s cooperative arcade gameplay left a lasting impact on video game design. Despite commercial expectations leading him to work mainly on franchise sequels, his creations remain beloved and influential. Kishimoto continued contributing as a freelance designer and consultant into the 2010s. His family and the gaming community have expressed gratitude for his legacy.
