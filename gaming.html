**Banquet For Fools Offers a Unique, Player-Driven RPG Experience**

Released on March 5, 2026, *Banquet For Fools* by Hannah and Joseph Games is an RPG that stands out with its strong emphasis on player note-taking and exploration. Unlike most RPGs, the game omits standard quest logs or markers, requiring players to actively document clues, locations, and spells themselves. This design encourages a slower pace, demanding attention and investment in the game’s detailed, yet opaque social and geographical world. Combat blends real-time action with pause mechanics, focusing on tactical decisions and manual control over party members. While the lack of direction can lead to frustration, the game rewards persistence with a personalized sense of discovery. It also supports Steam Deck play, adding portability for players who enjoy handheld gaming.

**Mars War: Logs – A Rough but Intriguing Choice-Driven RPG at a Low Price**

Originally released in 2013 by Spiders, *Mars War: Logs* is a sci-fi RPG set on a struggling Mars colony. Despite technical limitations and uneven voice acting, the game’s gritty atmosphere and tough narrative drew renewed interest on Steam, where it’s now discounted to just 99 cents during the spring sale. Players take the role of Roy, a prisoner of war navigating a hostile environment, with gameplay combining tactical combat and branching story choices. Its relatively short length (around 15 hours) makes it an accessible indie recommendation for fans looking for a less polished but ambitious RPG, especially on devices like the Steam Deck.

**Sand: Raiders of Sophie Release Date Still Uncertain**

*Sand: Raiders of Sophie* is a PvPvE extraction shooter notable for its large mechanical bases and vehicular combat, listed with a March 2026 release date on Steam. However, as March draws to a close, the developers have yet to provide a specific launch day. Community managers acknowledge ongoing final development work, emphasizing that a firm release date depends on completing these last critical steps. While there is no official delay announced, the absence of a precise date has created some fan anxiety and speculation. The developers ask for patience as they finalize the game’s finishing touches.

**Lost Wiki: Kozlovka Blends Mystery and Database Exploration**

*Lost Wiki: Kozlovka* offers a narrative puzzle experience styled like navigating Wikipedia entries to unravel a multi-generational mystery in a fictional Eastern European town. Players spend hours piecing together family trees, timelines, and hidden secrets around a strange local tree. The gameplay centers on careful reading, hyperlink navigation, and filling in blanks on reports. While the puzzle difficulty is moderate, the game highlights themes of information control and missing context, mirroring real-world issues around knowledge and censorship. The game costs $5 on Steam and appeals to players interested in slow-burn mysteries presented through innovative interfaces.

**Marathon’s Cryo Archive Maps PvE and PvP Elements in a Complex Raid**

The weekender event map *Cryo Archive* in *Marathon* introduces a sprawling, labyrinthine environment combining PvE challenges and PvP engagements. Entry requires players to meet specific criteria, including faction unlocks and credit stakes. The map features a progressive security clearance system, gating access to new areas and fostering tactical decision-making. Teams start isolated in separate wings fighting waves of bots before converging in a contested central zone. The map avoids early chaotic player confrontations, allowing for exploration and loot collection. Although praised for its design, some issues remain, such as weekend-only availability and technical performance problems in the main PvP area.

**Capcom Clarifies AI Use in Development but Will Not Use AI-Generated Game Assets**

During a February 2026 investor session, Capcom stated it will not incorporate AI-generated assets directly into game content. However, the company plans to use generative AI tools to improve development efficiency across areas like graphics, sound, and programming. This position comes amid ongoing industry debates around ethical AI use, particularly following controversial applications like Nvidia’s DLSS 5 demo altering character appearances. Capcom's statements aim to reassure investors while leaving room for AI in support roles, such as early concept art generation and workflow automation. The company did not specify detailed boundaries between AI-assisted efficiency and content generation, reflecting the complexity of the topic.
