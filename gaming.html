Star Trek: Resurgence to Be Delisted After Three Years

Star Trek: Resurgence, a narrative-driven adventure game released in May 2023 and developed by Dramatic Labs, is set to be removed from digital storefronts due to the expiration of its distribution license. The game, noted for its storytelling style reminiscent of classic Telltale Games, remains accessible for existing owners but will no longer be available for purchase. While the exact delisting date was not specified officially, it is expected to occur around the third anniversary of its original release. This follows a pattern of limited shelf life for Star Trek video games, including titles like Star Trek: Bridge Crew and a 2013 third-person shooter that were similarly delisted after a few years. The publisher, Bruner House, has not announced any follow-up projects post-Resurgence.

Hades 2 Receives Patch with Various Gameplay and Story Enhancements

Supergiant Games released a substantial update for Hades 2 on Steam that adds new content and quality-of-life improvements. Key additions include expanded narrative closure for Fated Prophecies involving characters such as Odysseus and Arachne, allowing players to see fully narrated conclusions. Players can now gift certain characters in the Crossroads region indefinitely after establishing bonds, with fresh scenes accompanying these interactions. The patch also introduces a new zoom option for better visibility on smaller displays and adjusts some gameplay mechanics. Notably, boons related to the god Ares have been buffed, improving damage output, and interactions with the character Icarus have been made more accessible. Additional cosmetic and system tweaks round out the update.

Windrose’s Survival Pirate Gameplay Inspires Return to Sea of Thieves

PC Gamer's assessment after a few hours playing the early access pirate survival game Windrose found that, while it offers traditional survival activities such as resource gathering and combat, it falls short in delivering an authentic pirate experience. The game’s focus on crafting and grinding contrasts with the playful, emergent multiplayer encounters that define Sea of Thieves. Despite Windrose’s initial popularity and positive reviews, its gameplay felt more like a survival sim with a pirate theme rather than a true pirate adventure. The article highlights Sea of Thieves’ strengths in delivering dynamic player-driven moments and smoother online play. This comparison may influence players weighing whether to invest time in Windrose or return to the more established pirate title.

Classic Warhammer and Warhammer 40K Games Arrive on Steam

SNEG and Games Workshop have launched the Warhammer Classics label on Steam, bringing over 20 older Warhammer and Warhammer 40,000 games to modern PCs with updates to ensure compatibility and stability. Seven of these titles are making their debut on Steam for the first time. The initiative aims to preserve and reintroduce foundational games from the Warhammer franchises to new and returning players. Enhancements include widescreen support, revised audio, and Steam achievements. The collection features a range of strategy, tactical, and role-playing games spanning decades of Warhammer history. A launch sale offers discounts of 20% to 90% on these titles through April 20.

Dwarf Fortress Collaboration Brings ASCII Art to Magic: The Gathering

Wizards of the Coast announced a new Secret Lair drop featuring a collaboration with Dwarf Fortress, set to release April 27. This limited edition Magic: The Gathering set includes five cards showcasing the distinctive ASCII art style of Dwarf Fortress, representing classic game elements and creatures rendered in IBM Code Page 437 glyphs. The unusual artwork departs from typical fantasy card illustrations, paying homage to the unique visual heritage of Dwarf Fortress. The set is part of a broader "Back to School" series that also includes other themed drops like My Little Pony and anime-style cards. This collaboration highlights Wizards’ ongoing efforts to blend different gaming communities and celebrate diverse aesthetics.
