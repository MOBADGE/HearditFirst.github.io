PC Gamer Hardware Awards 2025: Top Picks and Trends

PC Gamer recently announced the winners of its Hardware Awards for 2025, highlighting notable devices tested over the past year. The awards reflect not only performance but also design, value, and innovation in PC gaming hardware. While 2025 saw the arrival of new GPU generations from AMD and Nvidia, supply issues and pricing controversies impacted their reception. However, other categories showcased impressive devices across a range of budgets.

**Best Gaming Laptop and Monitor** The Razer Blade 16 earned the title of best gaming laptop. Praised for delivering a balanced experience for both work and gaming, it combines a slim design with capable thermal management. Although it cannot run top-tier GPUs like the RTX 5090 at full speed, its overall versatility and decent battery performance stand out. The best monitor award went to the KTC H27T22C-3, a less well-known brand offering a solid 1440p gaming experience with a 210 Hz refresh rate at an affordable price just above $150. Its IPS panel has good color and viewing angles, making it a strong budget choice.

Its PCIe 5.0 interface delivers fast sequential read/write speeds (around 14,900/14,000 MB/s) while maintaining low temperatures during use. This SSD exemplifies the advancements in storage technology despite ongoing memory pricing challenges. AMD‚Äôs Ryzen 9 9950X3D was named best gaming CPU, recognized for its high clock speeds, strong gaming and content creation performance, but also noted for its niche enthusiast price tag of $699. The best motherboard went to Gigabyte‚Äôs X870 Aorus Elite Ice, balancing price and features with ample USB ports and M.2 slots, and a distinctive white design.

**Gaming Accessories** Among gaming peripherals, the quiet Be Quiet! Light Mount keyboard was awarded best gaming keyboard. It is noted for its vibrant RGB lighting combined with sound-dampened keys, making it a solid daily driver without excessive noise. The Corsair Sabre V2 Pro Ultralight was the best gaming mouse, lauded for its extremely light weight (36g) and solid performance tailored for competitive FPS games. A Personal Hardware Pick: 8BitDo Ultimate 2C Controller

A member of the PC Gamer hardware team shared their personal favorite piece of kit from 2025: the 8BitDo Ultimate 2C wireless controller. Unlike some premium controllers reviewed, this budget-friendly gamepad (about $30) stood out primarily for its compact design tailored to smaller hands, selection of Hall effect buttons to prevent stick drift, and an appealing purple color.

While it lacks some polish and premium build qualities found in pricier controllers, the 8BitDo Ultimate 2C meets the needs of many casual gamers with a good button layout and solid wireless performance. Its form factor is closer to a Nintendo Switch Pro controller, offering a more compact grip that some users prefer. The reviewer emphasized the value of having hardware that both performs well and visually fits into a personalized gaming setup.

RPG Spotlight: The Outer Worlds 2

Highlighting a notable game from 2025, The Outer Worlds 2 received praise for its complex skill and dialogue systems that provide meaningful choices and consequences based on character builds. The game offers multiple branching quest paths influenced by different skills, preventing players from just relying on a single "persuade" option, and encouraging diverse playstyles.
