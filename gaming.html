Sandisk Rebrands Popular SSDs Following Independence from Western Digital

Sandisk has announced a renaming of several popular solid-state drives (SSDs) now that it operates independently from Western Digital (WD). Previously, many of the best SSDs associated with WD were actually Sandisk products, but the branding was not clear after WD decided to spin off Sandisk to focus on hard drives. The split officially occurred in February 2025.

The new product line will fall under the "Optimus" branding with three tiers: Sandisk Optimus, Optimus GX, and Optimus GX Pro. Existing drives, such as the WD_Black SN7100 and WD Blue SN5100, will be rebranded under this scheme, for example, the SN7100 becoming the Optimus GX 7100. Sandisk also revealed a new high-speed 2230 SSD, the Optimus GX 7100M.

While this rebrand will cause Sandisk to lose the immediate recognition associated with the WD name, the hardware remains the same, continuing the collaboration between Sandisk and Kioxia. The rollout of the newly branded products will continue through the first half of 2026.

Asus Introduces 240Hz Micro-OLED Gaming Glasses with Large Virtual Display

Asus unveiled the ROG Xreal R1 AR glasses, a new gaming accessory featuring 240Hz Micro-OLED displays that project a virtual 171-inch screen approximately 4 meters away. The glasses offer a 57-degree field of view and are designed for connected gaming on PCs, consoles, and mobile devices through a control dock with HDMI, DisplayPort, and USB-C inputs.

However, the full HD (1080p) resolution on such a large virtual screen may feel insufficient in clarity, especially given the close proximity of the display to the eyes. The glasses weigh 91 grams and include features like 3D depth of field and "Anchor Mode," which pins the display in place for comfortable viewing. Launch is expected in the first half of 2026, though pricing details have yet to be announced.

Monster Hunter Wilds Struggles Despite Strong Launch Momentum in 2025

Capcom's *Monster Hunter Wilds* had a record-breaking launch with 8 million copies sold in three days, but the game's fortunes sharply declined throughout 2025. Mixed to negative community feedback, largely due to technical performance issues and microtransaction concerns, culminated in poor sales and a significant drop in Capcom's stock price.

The game's release was critical for Capcom to maintain its decade-long streak of annual profit growth, but compromises to meet launch deadlines appear to have undermined the game's long-term reception. Despite lingering optimization challenges, Capcom continues to monetize the game aggressively through extensive cosmetic microtransactions.
