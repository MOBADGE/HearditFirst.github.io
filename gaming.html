**Intel Reports Over 100 Million AI-Capable PCs Shipped**

Intel revealed it has shipped slightly more than 100 million PCs equipped with dedicated Neural Processing Units (NPUs) designed for local AI computing. Intel fellow Tom Petersen shared these figures at a briefing, noting that the total AI processing power across these devices equates to roughly four Zetta Operations Per Second (ZOPS), a significant increase from previous metrics measured in Trillions of Operations Per Second (TOPS). Despite this large installed base, Intel does not track how extensively the AI capabilities are currently used by consumers. Petersen suggested that broader utility might increase as AI integration in software, like Microsoft's Copilot features, becomes more prevalent. This announcement highlights the growing presence of AI technology in everyday computers, although current consumer purchasing decisions appear driven more by Intel's market share than AI functionalities.

**Initial Tests Show Promise for Intel Arc B390 Integrated Graphics**

In a brief benchmarking session at CES 2026, the Intel Arc B390 integrated GPU, part of the new Core Ultra Series 3 mobile chips, demonstrated noteworthy performance for an iGPU. Using a Lenovo laptop equipped with an Intel Core Ultra X9 388H processor, Cyberpunk 2077 ran at an average of 53 fps on high settings at 1200p resolution without upscaling, climbing to 74 fps with Intel's XeSS upscaling enabled. Additional tests with ray tracing and frame generation produced about 70 fps. Other titles like Borderlands 4 ran around 40-45 fps, while Shadow of the Tomb Raider delivered an average of 75 fps. These results suggest the Arc B390 is capable of playable experiences in demanding games without discrete graphics hardware, potentially broadening gaming options for thin and light laptops that lack dedicated GPUs.

**New Magic: The Gathering Expansion Features Puppet Musical Trailer**

The upcoming Magic: The Gathering expansion, Lorwyn Eclipsed, is highlighted by a unique trailer created by The Jim Henson Company featuring a puppet musical. The narrative revisits the dual planes of Lorwyn and Shadowmoor, realms with contrasting themes of light and darkness that affect characters who cross between them. The expansion introduces new gameplay mechanics including the Vivid keyword linked to color variety and Blight, which applies -1/-1 counters to opponents. Lorwyn Eclipsed will be available digitally in Magic: The Gathering Arena starting January 20, with physical releases from January 23. The trailer presents an unusual artistic approach, distinguishing it from conventional game promotional material.

**Life is Strange: Reunion Leaks via PEGI Rating**

A new Life is Strange title, tentatively called Life is Strange: Reunion, was revealed through a leak on Europe's PEGI game rating website. The plot reportedly centers on Max Caulfield and Chloe Price reuniting at Caledon University amid impending disaster. The rating details elements including a psychedelic sequence involving spiked drinks and the presence of mature themes such as violence, sexual innuendo, and references to suicide. The rating lists in-game purchases and new outfit options. Although not officially announced by Square Enix, the listing suggests the continuation of the Life is Strange series. The stated release date may be inaccurate or a placeholder, and confirmation from the publisher is pending.
