Lost Fallout: New Vegas Post-Ending Dialogue Restored

A beta version containing cut content from Fallout: New Vegas has recently leaked, revealing originally intended post-ending dialogue for the game's various endings. Obsidian had planned a system where players could continue exploring the Mojave Wasteland after the ending slideshow, with characters reacting dynamically to the player's choices. The leaked content provides detailed alternate dialogue for factions such as the Legion, NCR, independent New Vegas, and Mr. House's rule. For example, supporting the Legion results in merchants like Lupe fleeing the Strip, while NCR victory pleases companions like Cass. This content has now been partially restored through mods like "Post-Game Dialogue Restored," enhancing the game's replay value and narrative depth. This discovery is significant for fans seeking a fuller experience of one of Fallout's most praised entries.

The Legend of Khiimori: Realistic Horse Courier Sim Enters Early Access

The Legend of Khiimori, a horse courier and survival simulation game emphasizing realistic horse management, has launched in early access on March 3. Priced at $30, it aims to offer a blend of survival mechanics and detailed equine care, including riding, feeding, breeding, and managing moods and stamina. Features such as route planning, weather effects, and day-night cycles contribute to an immersive experience. However, as it is still early access, some elements remain incomplete or buggy, such as the main story quest, horse taming, and a peace mode. Visual polish and quest-related bugs have also been noted. The game currently appeals most to horse enthusiasts, offering a unique open-world riding experience; survival and casual gamers may want to await further updates before diving in.

Australia Implements Age Verification for Online R18+ Games

Starting next week, Australia will require users to verify their age before accessing online games rated R18+, including titles like GTA Online. Single-player or offline R18+ games are exempt. This measure is part of broader Age-Restricted Material Codes overseen by the eSafety Commissioner, aiming to protect minors from explicit content online. Publishers that fail to comply may face fines up to AU$49.5 million per breach. Age verification methods must be "accurate, robust, fair, and reliable," often involving government IDs or credit card checks instead of relying on self-reported dates of birth. This new regulation aligns Australia with its earlier social media restrictions for minors but raises concerns about privacy and the security of sensitive personal data required during verification. The effectiveness and impact of these measures remain to be seen.

Early Impressions of Bungie's Marathon at Launch

Marathon, Bungie’s new extraction shooter, launched recently with players reporting a high-stakes, shoot-on-sight gameplay experience. Initial impressions after about 20 hours suggest that the game requires solid gunplay and tactical movement, with popular solo classes including Assassin and Vandal. The recently introduced Thief class combines intel gathering and trickery with a unique drone and grappling hook, offering a different playstyle focused on loot and scouting. Some technical issues have surfaced, notably missing Deluxe Edition bonuses and a $10 Reward Track purchase conflict, which the developers are addressing. Marathon's competitive, fast-paced nature and steep learning curve are becoming clearer as more players engage, with further impressions expected as the community grows.

Diablo 4’s Upcoming Season Lets Players Transform into the Butcher

Blizzard has announced that the next Diablo 4 season, starting March 11, will allow players to temporarily transform into the franchise's iconic demon, the Butcher, in an experimental mini-season format. This “Season of Slaughter” involves killing monsters to collect currency called "Meaty Offerings," which can be used at a special shrine to initiate the transformation. Playing as the Butcher will grant new abilities likely centered on close-range combat. This event occurs only in open-world regions affected by enhanced Helltide monsters. Additionally, players can encounter other Butcher-transformed players in PvP zones. This season introduces new challenges and buffs tied to kill streaks and "Bloodied" items. The upcoming "Lord of Hatred" expansion arrives on April 28, making the season an interesting bridge with new gameplay elements to explore beforehand.
