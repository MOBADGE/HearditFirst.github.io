Elder Scrolls Redguard Secret Finally Revealed After 28 Years

Nearly 28 years after its release, a longtime mystery about Bethesda’s 1998 action-adventure spin-off, The Elder Scrolls Adventures: Redguard, has been solved. The question of what lies beneath the skirts of female non-player characters was uncovered thanks to the ongoing community-driven Redguard Unity project, which is porting the game to the modern Unity engine. Using a free camera mod, players discovered that the game simply displays a smiley face in such an attempt. Bethesda’s early developers appear to have left a subtle joke, acknowledging these player curiosities decades ago. The Unity project aims to modernize the game with better stability, controls, visuals, and mod support, addressing the often unstable original.

Owlcat Reveals New Warhammer 40K Companions for Rogue Trader and Dark Heresy

Owlcat Games announced two new companions for their Warhammer 40,000 RPGs. For Rogue Trader’s upcoming Infinite Museion DLC, a new tech-priest companion named Eogann Februs will join the crew. While tech-priests are not rare in the setting, the addition expands party options. More notably, the playable alpha of the upcoming Dark Heresy RPG revealed Haymar Devos, a Catachan guardsman known for jungle warfare, adding a recognizable archetype inspired by ’80s action heroes. The announcements come as both games continue refinement; Rogue Trader has improved over time through patches and expansions, and Dark Heresy is still in early release stages. Both remain listed as “coming soon” on Steam.

AOC Highlights Monitors as a Practical Upgrade Amid Rising GPU and Memory Prices

Monitor maker AOC suggested in a recent press event that upgrading monitors could be a sensible choice right now, especially given the ongoing inflation of graphics card and memory prices. Cesar Reyes Acosta, AOC’s European commercial leader, pointed to recent advances resulting in monitors offering higher specs and better value—for example, a 27-inch 1440p panel with 260Hz refresh rate at a price comparable to older, lower-spec models. AOC plans to release new OLED and high-refresh-rate models over the coming months, including 5th-generation QD-OLED and 4th-generation WOLED options priced between €899 and €999. While the message reflects AOC’s business interests, it does highlight that monitor technology and pricing have improved relative to other PC components.

Philips Launches Western Version of 5K Dual-Mode Gaming Monitor with Ambilight

Philips has introduced the Evnia 27M2D5901A, a 27-inch 5K dual-mode gaming monitor designed for the Western market following a prior release in China. The monitor can switch between 5120×2880 resolution at 165Hz and 2560×1440 resolution at 330Hz, catering both to immersive single-player and high-refresh competitive gaming. Notable features include Philips Ambilight backlighting that can sync with in-game events and smart home lighting, USB-C charging, and a clean white design. While priced at around €799, it is currently not planned for launch in the US. The dual-mode approach provides flexibility for users upgrading their rigs in stages.

Turtle WoW Classic Server to Shut Down Following Legal Injunction from Blizzard

The Turtle WoW private World of Warcraft server announced it will shut down on May 14 after Blizzard secured a court injunction against it due to copyright infringement. Turtle WoW had operated for eight years, offering a “Classic Plus” experience expanding on vanilla WoW content with new raids, zones, and races while maintaining pre-expansion era gameplay. Despite a settlement and previous requests for fan server licensing, Blizzard’s legal actions have ended its run. The closure is another example in a pattern where fan-run MMO servers face shutdowns, contrasting with a few exceptions that have gained publisher support. Turtle WoW’s community has expressed sadness as the shutdown date approaches.

Portal 2 Community Edition Launches Open Beta to Enhance Modding Capabilities

The Portal 2 Community Edition (CE) entered open beta, providing a community-maintained version of Portal 2 that extends the game’s modding potential. Built on a fork of the CS:GO engine called Strata Source, CE adds new lighting and volumetric effects, improved texture support, a revamped campaign UI, expanded scripting, and an enhanced Hammer map
