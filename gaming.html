Palia’s Upcoming Expansion Adds Realistic Breedable Horses

The life sim MMO Palia is launching its next expansion, The Royal Highlands, on May 12 as a free update. This update introduces horses as rideable and breedable mounts, a feature frequently requested by MMO players. Unlike typical mounts that function mainly as speed boosts, Palia's horses feature varying gaits such as walk, canter, and gallop, with traits that affect handling and speed. Players can raise and breed their horses at ranches on their home plots, with traits ranging from realistic coats and dispositions to more fantastical ones with visual effects and buffs. The expansion also adds a large new adventure zone, a new villager, and furniture styles. This addition marks a notable step in MMO mount design by integrating more detailed interaction and customization.

Xbox Game Pass Prices Reduced; Call of Duty Delayed on Service

Microsoft announced a reduction in Xbox Game Pass subscription prices, with Game Pass Ultimate falling from $29.99 to $22.99 per month, and the PC Game Pass from $16.49 to $13.99. These cuts partially reverse last year's major price increase while keeping costs above previous levels. However, Microsoft also revealed that future Call of Duty games will not appear on Game Pass at launch but will be added about a year later, beginning with the next series installment. This change aims to adjust the subscription's value balance, especially after reports that Game Pass availability led to significant sales losses for Black Ops 6. The move signals a shift in Xbox's strategy under new leadership, focusing more on profitability and core fans.

Framework Unveils New Wireless Touchpad Keyboard and DIY Options

Hardware maker Framework has revealed a new wireless keyboard featuring an integrated trackpad designed primarily for living room or simulator rig setups. The keyboard incorporates low-profile chiclet keys and a clickable Windows Precision Touchpad supporting multi-finger gestures for Windows and Linux. It also offers a translucent rear cover, reminiscent of older handheld devices, to showcase internal components. Importantly, Framework will provide CAD files and sell the core Bluetooth-enabled control board separately, allowing users to build custom keyboard frames or integrate the technology into other projects. The keyboard and kits are expected to release later in 2026.

Framework’s Expansion for 16-inch Laptop Supports Desktop GPUs

Alongside the new keyboard, Framework detailed an upcoming accessory for its 16-inch modular laptop that enables connecting full desktop graphics cards externally. The OCuLink Dev Kit uses an eight-lane PCIe interface via the laptop’s Expansion Bay, providing high-speed data transfer around 128 Gbps. It consists of an adapter board, a dock for the laptop’s existing mobile GPU to be housed externally, and a dock for users to plug in standard desktop GPUs supplied with their own power sources. This accessory aims to extend upgradeability and customization for portable gaming laptops, although it remains in the developer kit phase without a set release date.

Enshrouded Adds Community Adventure Sharing Feature

Survival game Enshrouded has introduced a new adventure sharing system allowing players to upload and share entire custom-built bases and worlds with the community. Accessible from the main menu, players can browse, download, and visit creations made by others. The update includes tools like a craftable camera for taking attractive screenshots of bases. While players cannot currently progress within shared worlds, future plans include expanding the feature with interactive elements, puzzles, enemy spawns, and traps. The feature aims to enhance community engagement by making player creations more accessible beyond screenshots or videos.

Troy Baker Plans to Develop His Own Video Games

Voice actor Troy Baker, known for roles in major titles such as The Last of Us and Metal Gear Solid, has announced intentions to create his own video games. Inspired by industry figures like Abubakar Salim and drawing on experience working with developers including Ken Levine and Hideo Kojima, Baker is entering the game development space with long-term plans. He emphasizes a focus on storytelling quality over rushing production. While still in early stages, Baker aims to apply lessons learned from his extensive industry career to his own projects.
