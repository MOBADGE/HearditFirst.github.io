Slay the Spire Hits New Concurrent Player Peak on Steam

Nearly seven years after its release, deckbuilding roguelike Slay the Spire achieved a new all-time high in concurrent Steam players over the holiday period. The game surged past its previous peak of 33,000 players from 2018 to over 57,000 at one point before settling around 35,000, still exceeding the older record. This resurgence likely owes to the 2025 Steam winter sale dropping the price to a historic low of $2.49, making it an accessible entry for new and returning players. Additionally, anticipation for the upcoming Slay the Spire 2 and the board game adaptation may have drawn attention back to the original. The milestone highlights the game's enduring appeal in a crowded genre and indicates strong community interest ahead of its sequel.

FPS Genre Experiences Shift Toward Casual and Cooperative Play

The first-person shooter (FPS) genre saw a notable shift in 2025 away from high skill ceilings, intense rankings, and aggressive monetization toward more accessible, cooperative, and varied experiences. Battlefield 6 led this trend by eschewing ranked modes and emphasizing large-scale, enjoyable gameplay without heavy competitive pressure. Other games, including Arc Raiders, Rainbow Six Siege, and Overwatch 2, introduced modes supporting casual or cooperative play, with relaxed rules and less focus on esports. Additionally, the community backlash against overpriced, intrusive cosmetic items led to changes in major franchises, with publishers scaling back inappropriate skins. This evolution reflects a larger recognition of player fatigue with stressful, monetized online shooters and suggests a diversification of FPS offerings to balance thrill and casual fun.
