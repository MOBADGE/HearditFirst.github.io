Monster Hunter Stories 3: Defeating Invasive Monsters

Monster Hunter Stories 3 features "Invasive Monsters," powered-up variants that occupy the dens of endangered species, acting as guardians of their rare eggs. These monsters have specialized abilities requiring tailored strategies rather than conventional combat approaches. For example, the first Invasive Monster players face is the Invasive Yian Garuga, where breaking its legs with a blunt weapon like a hammer forces it to retreat, allowing players to collect a Rathian egg. Other Invasive Monsters, such as Plesioth, Seregios, and Shogun Ceanataur, each require unique tactics like exploiting elemental weaknesses, using specific attack types, or boosting defenses to survive their most potent moves. Defeating these creatures is not always about slaying them immediately but rather driving them away to secure the eggs of endangered species for taming.

Understanding these mechanics is important because collecting these eggs unlocks some of the best monsters ("monsties") in the game. The strategies outlined help players progress through the game’s story and side quests by restoring ecosystems and raising monster ratings.

Locations of Endangered Species in Monster Hunter Stories 3

Alongside details on battling Invasive Monsters, the locations of nine endangered species in Monster Hunter Stories 3 are now clarified. These include Rathian, Lagiacrus, Astalos, Canyne, Nargacuga, Mizutsune, Zinogre, Tigrex, and Barioth. Each species can be found in distinct regions such as Azuria, Canalta Timberland, Tarukan, and Serathis. Their dens are often guarded by respective Invasive Monsters, requiring players to employ the strategies mentioned earlier to access the rare eggs.

This information matters because these endangered species provide some of the most powerful and unique creatures available for taming, directly impacting players’ capabilities and progression. Knowing precise locations and the associated battles allows for better planning and smoother gameplay.

Darktide’s New "Expeditions" Mode Introduced

The Warhammer 40,000-themed shooter Darktide is adding a new "expeditions" mode as part of the upcoming Beyond the Hive update, releasing March 17. Unlike the game’s traditionally claustrophobic, indoor missions, this mode takes players outdoors onto the planet Atoma Prime, exploring open scavenge zones under threat from radiation and pollution. The mode emphasizes extraction-style gameplay without player-versus-player elements, featuring randomized elements and roaming patrols of enemies that require tactical approaches.

Such a mode diversifies Darktide's gameplay and offers players a different kind of challenge focusing on survival and resource gathering under time pressure. Failure to leave before time runs out results in losing all progress from that run, reinforcing high-risk decision-making.

The Simpsons: Possibility of Hit & Run Revival

Matt Selman, executive producer of The Simpsons, addressed the possibility of reviving the cult-favorite 2007 game Hit & Run. Although there is no confirmed project, he stated "never say never" regarding a revival or remaster, acknowledging ongoing fan interest. Selman expressed a personal preference for the original game’s storytelling and its place among numerous Simpsons titles.

This is notable because Hit & Run remains highly regarded among licensed games, and renewed availability or a new installment could please longstanding fans. However, no concrete development is underway, so this remains speculative.

Slay the Spire 2 Sales and Early Access Success

Slay the Spire 2 has sold 3 million copies within its first week on Steam, with around 25 million runs completed by players. Developer Mega Crit reports strong Early Access reception, despite the intense workload to meet demand. The game remains close in design to its predecessor but continues to engage players widely. Planned updates include Steam Workshop support, changes to scoring systems, and accessibility improvements.

These sales figures demonstrate the ongoing popularity of roguelike deckbuilding games and suggest a healthy future for Slay the Spire's franchise with community-driven content and iterative development.
