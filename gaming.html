Asus and Hifiman Collaborate on High-End Planar Magnetic Gaming Headset

At CES 2026, Asus unveiled the ROG Kithara headset, their first gaming model to incorporate planar magnetic drivers developed by Hifiman. These drivers are known for delivering detailed and accurate audio favored by audiophiles. Asus claims enhancements that improve bass response and widen soundstage, demonstrated during a Counter-Strike 2 showcase. The headset supports multiple connection types, including balanced and single-ended jacks, aiming for broad compatibility. However, its 16 Ohm impedance raises questions about performance without dedicated amplification. Pricing is expected between $300 and $350, positioning the Kithara as a premium alternative to competitors like the wireless Audeze Maxwell 2.

Important Security Update for Asus MyAsus Users

Asus has addressed a high-severity vulnerability in its MyAsus software, which comes preinstalled on many Asus PCs, laptops, and mini PCs. The issue involves an uncontrolled DLL loading path allowing local attackers to potentially execute arbitrary code with system-level access. Affected users on 64-bit and ARM devices should update to versions 3.1.49.0/1.1.37.0 or later. The update is typically delivered automatically through the Microsoft Store but can be manually triggered. This follows other recent security advisories from Asus concerning hardware and router vulnerabilities requiring patching. Users are advised to promptly update their software to mitigate risks
