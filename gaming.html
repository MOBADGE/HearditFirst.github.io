**PC Hardware Highlights: Cooler and Monitor Standouts of 2025**

Among PC hardware releases this year, the Arctic Liquid Freezer III Pro has drawn praise for its performance and pricing. An update to a well-regarded liquid cooler, the Pro model offers improved pump control, denser fins, and faster fans. Reviewers noted it handles high-end processors efficiently while its fans maintain a balance between airflow and noise. Crucially, Arctic kept the price steady around $90, avoiding inflationary pressure common in PC components. This approach distinguishes it in a market where rising costs are the norm.

In monitors, 2025 saw strong value in 4K displays as component prices increased. The Philips Evnia 27M2N3800A was highlighted for its dual resolution options (4K at 160Hz and 1080p at 320Hz) and fast IPS panel, presenting a good price-to-performance ratio despite some compromises like limited HDR and lack of USB-C. The Alienware 27 AW2725Q QD-OLED monitor offers vibrant HDR with a snappy 240Hz refresh rate, though at a higher price point and smaller size. For budget-conscious buyers, the KTC H27T22C-3 delivers solid 1440p performance and color accuracy, making it a competitive choice where cost is important.

**Star Citizen Nearing $1 Billion in Funding, with Uncertain Release Timelines**

Star Citizen, the space simulation project by Cloud Imperium Games, is approaching a significant funding milestone of $1 billion from players in 2026, driven by a mix of crowdfunding, paid alpha access, and in-game purchases including expensive virtual ships. Despite this financial success, the project's main single-player campaign, Squadron 42, planned for release next year, faces doubts about meeting its deadline. Developers cited a focus on development over showmanship at events. The broader Star Citizen game itself is projected to reach version 1.0 in 2027 or 2028, leaving questions about when players will see a full, finished product despite the extraordinary length and expense of development.
