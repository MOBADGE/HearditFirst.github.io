Bite-sized summaries of the latest gaming trends — without the hype.

Updated: March 10, 2026

**Super Mario Galaxy Movie Reveals New Voice Cast Ahead of April Release** Nintendo held a final Direct presentation focused on The Super Mario Galaxy Movie, scheduled to hit theaters on April 1. The showcase included the release of a new trailer and key casting news. Notably, Donald Glover (aka Childish Gambino) will voice Yoshi, marking a surprising choice for the character. Other cast members revealed include Luis Guzmán as Wart, Issa Rae as the Honey Queen, and various familiar voices reprising their roles. The film draws inspiration from the Mario Galaxy games and aims to bring the franchise’s space-themed platforming to an animated audience.

**Harlem Globetrotters Return to Video Games After 20 Years** The Harlem Globetrotters will appear in a new basketball video game for the first time since the mid-2000s. Titled Super Basketball Classics Featuring the World-Famous Harlem Globetrotters, the upcoming arcade-style game from Acclaim emphasizes the team’s signature trick shots and entertainment style. The game will include season and tournament modes, co-op play, and highlights the Globetrotters’ centenary. This release looks to blend classic arcade basketball gameplay with the Globetrotters’ unique brand of on-court showmanship.

**Simpsons: Hit & Run Revival Remains Possible, Producer Comments** Fans awaiting a remaster or sequel to the 2003 action game The Simpsons: Hit & Run received a cautiously optimistic response this week. Executive producer Matt Selman, who also contributed to the game's writing, stated “never say never” when asked about a revival. The original game was inspired by Grand Theft Auto III’s open-world style but brought a Simpsons twist. While no official plans exist, Selman's remarks keep the door open for potential future development involving this well-regarded licensed title.

**EA Conducts Staff Reductions at Battlefield 6 Developer Despite Record Sales** Electronic Arts has laid off an unspecified number of employees across Battlefield Studios, the developer group behind Battlefield 6. This follows the game’s record-breaking sales and status as the top-selling U.S. game in 2025. EA described the layoffs as a “realignment” intended to refocus the team on player priorities. The four studios involved—DICE, Ripple Effect, Criterion, and Motive—are expected to remain operational. EA affirmed continued investment in the Battlefield franchise backed by community feedback.

**Fortnite Chapter 7 Season 2 Launch Set for March 19, Features Major Changes and Collaborations** The next season of Fortnite, Chapter 7 Season 2, will launch on March 19 after a two-week delay from the initial early March schedule. Epic Games began teasing the new season with live in-game events and trailers revealing key story elements, including the rescue of The Foundation character and a confrontation with the Ice King. Season 2 will include a refreshed loot pool, new team-based movement items, and a mid-game loot area. Collaborations expected in the new season range from Looney Tunes characters like Bugs Bunny to Overwatch heroes such as Tracer and D.Va. The season is planned to last about 80 days, ending with a likely live event on June 6. The delay is possibly related to ongoing technical issues from Season 1.

**Monster Hunter Stories 3 Launch Scheduled for March 13 Worldwide** Monster Hunter Stories 3: Twisted Reflection will launch globally on March 13, offering fans a fantasy creature-collecting RPG experience. The release times depend on region, with areas in East Asia, such as New Zealand, Japan, and Korea, getting early access as their clocks reach midnight. North America and Europe will have slightly later availability aligned with their local times. Console and PC players should note some differences in exact launch times. This game arrives in a year without a new mainline Pokémon title, positioning itself as a notable alternative for monster-collection fans.
