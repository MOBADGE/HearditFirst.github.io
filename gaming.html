Asus and MSI Clarify RTX 5070 Ti Has Not Been Discontinued

Asus and MSI have addressed recent reports suggesting that the Nvidia GeForce RTX 5070 Ti graphics card had reached an end-of-life (EoL) status. Asus stated that some media outlets received incomplete information from a PR representative, clarifying that neither the RTX 5070 Ti nor the RTX 5060 Ti 16 GB have been discontinued. Both companies emphasized ongoing support for these models despite current supply constraints largely due to memory shortages affecting production. While availability remains limited and prices are high, Asus and MSI insist there are no plans to cease sales. This clarification is significant because the RTX 5070 Ti occupies an important market segment; discontinuing it would leave competitors like AMD‚Äôs RX 9070 XT unchallenged.

SNK Faces Backlash Over Accusations of AI Use in Fatal Fury Trailer

A newly released trailer for Fatal Fury: City of the Wolves season 2 DLC has drawn criticism from fans alleging it contains AI-generated content. Viewers on YouTube, Reddit, and Discord expressed disappointment, pointing to irregularities in character visuals and questioning why SNK, now backed by substantial Saudi funding, would resort to generative AI for promotional material. The DLC includes six new fighters, but the focus on AI use has overshadowed excitement. No official statement from SNK has confirmed or denied these claims. The controversy follows a similarly mixed reception to the previous addition of soccer star Cristiano Ronaldo as a playable character.

Sophie Turner Revealed as Lara Croft in Upcoming Tomb Raider TV Series

Amazon released the first official image of Sophie Turner portraying Lara Croft in the forthcoming Tomb Raider television series. The costume stays faithful to the classic 1990s look with slight modifications. The casting and design have generally been well received, in contrast to some earlier controversial reveals in similar adaptations. The show is created and executive produced by Phoebe Waller-Bridge, known for Fleabag, and features a supporting cast including Sigourney Weaver and Jason Isaacs. No release date has been announced, but production is underway. This adaptation reflects continuing interest in expanding iconic game franchises into television format.
