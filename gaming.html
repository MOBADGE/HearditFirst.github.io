World of Warcraft: Midnight Introduces Premium Currency with Mixed Reactions

The recent launch of World of Warcraft: Midnight brought with it a new player housing system widely praised for its creative flexibility, including generous decoration options and clipping features that enable elaborate designs. However, Blizzard introduced a premium currency called Hearthsteel, required to purchase certain exclusive housing decor items. While Blizzard previously stated that buying Hearthsteel would be straightforward and player-friendly, the actual implementation requires players to buy currency in preset bundles ranging from 100 to 10,000 Hearthsteel, leading to leftover unused amounts. Some items are priced at awkward values (e.g., 200 or 800 Hearthsteel), forcing players to spend more than necessary.

This shift has drawn criticism because players paying real money receive only one copy of an item rather than unlocking it for repeated purchase, raising questions given that World of Warcraft already relies on subscriptions or grinding for in-game gold via WoW tokens. Although some price adjustments have been made following feedback, the premium currency model complicates what was an otherwise solid housing system and may impact player satisfaction.

Fallout: New Vegas Post-Ending Dialogue Restored by Modders

A recently leaked beta version of Fallout: New Vegas has revealed cut post-ending dialogue that expands on the game's endings. Originally planned by developer Obsidian, the idea was to let players continue exploring the Mojave Wasteland after completing the main campaign, with events reflecting their choices. While modders had previously restored some post-game content, the new restoration mod draws from the beta's additional scripted dialogue.

The dialogue varies based on the faction supported at the game's conclusion: Legion control leads to merchant closures and increased hostility; NCR victory results in characters reflecting on consequences; an independent New Vegas is described as entering a "golden age," and supporting Mr House retains access to the player's apartment. These restored lines enhance narrative depth after the main story ends and require installation of specific mods. The content provides players with a more immersive post-campaign experience closer to Obsidian’s original vision.

The Legend of Khiimori: Early Access Review of a Horse Courier Survival Sim

The Legend of Khiimori launched in early access on March 3 as a horse-focused survival and courier simulation game set in historical Mongolian steppes. It emphasizes realistic horse management, including riding mechanics with stamina and health meters, feeding, breeding with genetic traits, and delivery quests within a large open world. The game includes combat against predators and crafting minor resources.

Important to note, the main story quest is not yet implemented, and some features such as horse customization, taming wild horses, and a peaceful mode remain in development. Technical issues like a quest-blocking bug and frequent automatic saves may affect the play experience. Grafik settings range from low to ultra, with system requirements to consider.

For horse simulation enthusiasts, Khiimori offers an engaging experience focused on horses rather than broader survival mechanics. Cozy gamers may want to wait for further updates, and survival fans should temper expectations given the game's limited crafting and building complexity.

Australia's New Age Verification Law Impacts R18+ Online Games

Starting Monday, Australian authorities will require age verification for players accessing online games rated R18+, including popular titles like GTA Online. This measure is part of broader Age-Restricted Material Codes targeting online content such as explicit AI chatbots and adult websites. Publishers failing to comply risk fines up to AU$49.5 million per breach.

The verification methods accepted will have to be robust, compliant with privacy laws, and managed by the game services rather than the Australian government. Examples from other platforms include credit card checks or government ID uploads. The approach differs significantly from common self-declaration age gates.

While intended to restrict access for minors, the enforcement raises privacy concerns about the extent and handling of personal data. Australia is pioneering these regulations, and other countries may follow, prompting debates about the balance between safety and privacy in digital spaces.

Marathon: Early Impressions and Launch Issues

Bungie's new extraction shooter Marathon recently launched, with early players noting a fast-paced, shoot-on-sight gameplay style emphasizing solo play. Player classes like Assassin and Vandal stand out for their movement and stealth capabilities, while the Thief class offers a unique intel and trickery approach, featuring a pickpocket drone and grapple device.

On launch day, some users reported missing Deluxe Edition bonuses and pre-order rewards, with Bungie working on fixes and advising affected players
