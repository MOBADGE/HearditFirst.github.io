Unique Gaming PC Build with Complex Cooling System

A Reddit user known as Yam_Founder revealed a custom gaming PC build named "Seraphim," featuring an elaborate water cooling setup reminiscent of a many-winged angel. The project, which took around four years to complete, aims to provide both efficient cooling and easier maintenance by compartmentalizing the system into removable "zones." The build includes multiple radiators designed for passive cooling to minimize fan noise, with the entire system operating quietly except for pump sounds. Yam_Founder’s experience as a cryogenic welder helped ensure the cooling system is tightly sealed against leaks. The build stands out due to its visually striking design and practical modular approach, offering an alternative to standard "boxy" PC cases.

Productive Meeting Between Anthropic and White House on AI Model

Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei recently held a "productive and constructive" meeting with officials from the White House, including Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent, regarding Anthropic's new AI model, Claude Mythos. This model is notable for its ability to identify thousands of cybersecurity vulnerabilities and demonstrate exploits autonomously. Despite a previous dispute with the US Department of Defense labeling Anthropic a "supply chain risk" due to ethical safeguards, the government appears to be engaging cooperatively with the company. The discussion focused on collaboration opportunities and balancing innovation with safety, reflecting ongoing government interest in responsible AI development amid growing concerns about security risks.

Elden Ring Movie Scheduled for March 2028

Bandai Namco and A24 have announced that the Elden Ring live-action film will release on March 3, 2028. The cast includes actors such as Kit Connor, Ben Whishaw, Nick Offerman, Peter Serafinowicz, and Jonathan Pryce. Production is set to begin in spring 2026. While details about the storyline and character roles remain undisclosed, the film represents one of several upcoming video game adaptations slated for 2028, coinciding with the Call of Duty movie also scheduled that year. The announcement has drawn attention due to the franchise’s expansive yet cryptic lore, suggesting the film will aim to provide a more structured narrative for fans and newcomers alike.

Nvidia Research Reveals Over 2x Performance Boost in Path Tracing

Nvidia has published research detailing advancements in ReSTIR PT Enhanced, a technique to improve the efficiency of full path tracing, a rendering method used for realistic lighting in games. This enhanced method reportedly doubles or triples the performance compared to previous versions by optimizing sample selection and reducing visual noise and artifacts. The improvements bring path tracing closer to being practical for mainstream gaming, potentially enabling more realistic lighting effects without demanding excessive GPU power. Tests conducted on the Nvidia RTX 5880 workstation GPU showed significant speed gains, suggesting future games may utilize this technology more extensively.

Primal Repairs: A Handyman Simulator Set in a Prehistoric World

A new game called Primal Repairs is introducing a handyman simulation experience within a prehistoric setting reminiscent of The Flintstones. Players perform maintenance tasks such as repairing stone wheels and fixing dinosaur-powered equipment while exploring an open world presented in a realistic art style. The game features a velociraptor assistant that serves multiple functions and adds thematic coherence to the gameplay. Although no release date has been announced, Primal Repairs caught attention for its unusual concept and adherence to the logic of its setting, aiming to blend simulation mechanics with humor and creativity.

New HyperX Cloud Flight 2 Headset Review

The HyperX Cloud Flight 2 wireless gaming headset earned praise for its extended battery life of up to 100 hours, dependable low-latency connectivity via 2.4 GHz and Bluetooth 5.3, and solid sound quality with notable positional audio precision suited for competitive gaming. However, the build is predominantly plastic with a firm clamp and shallow ear cups, which may affect comfort during prolonged use, especially for users with larger ears. The detachable mic and companion software provide basic but functional features. Overall, the headset is recognized for delivering core essentials effectively at a mid-range price point, making it a practical choice for gamers valuing reliability and battery longevity over premium materials or advanced audio customization.
