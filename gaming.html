Updated: March 01, 2026

**Blizzard’s Stance on Generative AI in Game Development** Blizzard developers, including World of Warcraft: Midnight’s lead composer Leo Kaliski, have expressed relief at not using generative AI in their creative processes. Kaliski described the team’s work on the score as fully human-made, reflecting a broader reluctance within Blizzard to incorporate AI-generated content directly into player-facing elements. This cautious approach contrasts with the more enthusiastic AI integration efforts at Blizzard’s parent company, Microsoft. Blizzard appears to be allowing individual teams discretion over AI experimentation, rather than enforcing a company-wide AI strategy. The presence of developer unions may also influence resistance to AI adoption.

**Improved Quality of Squid Game Mods for GTA 5** Four years after the Squid Game phenomenon inspired early mods in GTA 5, fan projects have significantly improved in quality and depth. Recent mods feature better scripting, smarter AI behaviors, configurable gameplay timers, and thematic elements balancing the show’s dark tone with GTA’s style. Both standalone mods and those integrated into FiveM roleplay servers now support features like spectator cameras and scripted announcers. Players are requesting further development, including progression systems and organized tournaments. The evolution showcases the modding community’s capacity to refine popular concepts into more robust experiences.

**Narrative Designer Cara Ellison Reflects on RPG Influences and Writing Tools** Cara Ellison, former senior narrative designer on Vampire: The Masquerade – Bloodlines 2 before its developer change, shared insights into her career and creative process. She highlighted her admiration for the original Bloodlines’ atmosphere, voice acting, and animation that contributed to an immersive gothic setting. Ellison currently works on indie projects including Sleight of Hand, a deckbuilding stealth game inspired by Metal Gear Solid and Bloodlines. She also discussed the challenges of videogame narrative design, noting the lack of standardized writing tools in engines like Unreal, leading her to use Scrivener extensively for scriptwriting.

**Space Marine 2’s New $5 Voice Lines DLC Receives Backlash** The recently released voice lines DLC for Warhammer 40,000: Space Marine 2 has met strong criticism from players. Priced at $5 for a limited number of voice options and lines, the DLC is viewed by many as poor value. Complaints focus on the interrupting nature of new voices by default lines, a perceived reduction in voice line quantity and quality compared to originals, and questions about whether the advertised 450 lines include filler sounds or multiple language versions. The negative reception is considerable enough to have sparked a popular refund thread on Reddit. This backlash highlights tensions around monetization of small DLC content.

**Star Wars TIE Fighter Mod Receives Major Update, Co-op Planned** The TIE Fighter: Total Conversion mod, which ports and enhances the 1994 TIE Fighter game within 1999’s X-Wing Alliance, has released a significant update. This update introduces separate installers for Classic and Reimagined versions, stability improvements, and HD upgrades to menu environments. Development has been ongoing for about a year, with further plans including co-op multiplayer and a balance-locked version to allow future content changes. The mod is regarded as one of the most ambitious Star Wars fan projects, improving accessibility and modernizing an older title.

**Teardown’s Multiplayer Update Launching in March** Teardown’s long-awaited multiplayer component is set for release on March 12, adding cooperative play for the single-player campaign, free-roaming sandbox modes, and competitive modes such as team deathmatch. The update supports up to 12 players on PC servers, though there is no integrated voice or text chat and no crossplay plans currently. DLC missions remain single-player only. The developer, Tuxedo Labs, also indicated ongoing multiplayer content development, including a racing mode planned for later this year. The addition of multiplayer marks a substantial expansion for a game that has steadily grown since launch.
