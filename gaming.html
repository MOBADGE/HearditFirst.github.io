Five New Steam Games to Watch

Several new titles appeared on Steam this week that may have escaped notice amid the usual volume of releases. Notable among them is *Homura Hime*, a third-person action game reminiscent of classic slash-and-dodge styles popular two decades ago. Featuring a narrative about defeating powerful demon girls, it combines fast-paced combat with bullet hell elements. Another release, *Planet of Lana 2*, continues the hand-painted cinematic platformer tradition from its predecessor with expanded environments and steady-paced puzzle and stealth gameplay.

*iRacing Arcade* offers a lighter, more casual take on the serious iRacing sim, incorporating cartoon-style visuals and management elements to broaden its appeal. For fans of cozy simulation, *Shinehill* mixes farming, cooking, and dungeon crawling with a storyline about an alien spy integrating into a small town. Lastly, *Angel Engine* adapts a vertical video horror series into a surgical-themed adventure game with point-and-click mechanics, marked by a gritty aesthetic. These games highlight a mix of genres and approaches for diverse player interests.

Ultima 8 Retrospective Released by CRPG Docuseries

Majuular, a YouTube creator well-known for deep dives into classic computer RPGs, has published an extensive three-hour retrospective on *Ultima 8*, part of the influential Ultima series that shaped the CRPG genre. The series blends historical context, narrative analysis, and development stories, offering insight into the evolution of role-playing games across decades. Though *Ultima 8* itself is not the most celebrated entry, it remains a significant piece of CRPG history.

This retrospective serves as useful background for understanding modern role-playing games, as many mechanics and design philosophies trace back to the Ultima series. For those interested in RPGs’ roots or game design history, the documentary provides detailed examination and critique, enhancing appreciation for the genre's development.

Hytale Encourages Modding, Hiring from the Community

Hypixel Studios, developers of the voxel sandbox game *Hytale*, recently announced a substantial modding contest with a six-figure prize pool that will reward 65 winners. CEO Simon Collins-Laflamme emphasized that fostering modding is part of a broader studio strategy to remain closely connected to the passionate community. Notably, Hypixel actively recruits talented modders from the player base to join their development team.

This approach differs from typical support of modding by directly integrating the creative community into the studio workforce. By building an agile, hands-on team of creators, Hypixel aims to sustain ongoing innovation and engagement with *Hytale*. The modding ecosystem already features thousands of mods, indicating a strong foundation for extended longevity and evolving gameplay shaped by players.

Crazy Taxi’s Dreamcast Cityscapes Made Browser-Explorable

A programmer named Will has completed a project to recreate the city maps of the Dreamcast-era arcade game *Crazy Taxi* in a format playable within web browsers. These maps are part of noclip.website, a digital museum presenting video game levels rendered using original techniques. Unlike typical gameplay that focuses on the racing aspect, this project allows for leisurely exploration, revealing new perspectives on the urban design and landmark placement of the classic game.

This effort highlights both a technical achievement in decoding and replicating game data without existing community tools, and an opportunity to study and appreciate game level design. Given that a fully featured *Crazy Taxi* sequel remains in early development, this project offers the community an alternative way to revisit the game's iconic environments.

Slay the Spire 2 Clarifies Marathon Joke Amid Record Sales

*Slay the Spire 2* developers at Mega Crit recently issued a sarcastic congratulatory message to the team behind the indie game *Marathon* at its launch. The joke was meant as good-natured banter but was later acknowledged as “a bit meaner” than intended, especially since *Slay the Spire 2* has achieved unusually high concurrent player counts, surpassing those of *Marathon* on Steam by a large margin.

The exchange reflects the shift in context caused by *Slay the Spire 2*’s unexpected success. Initially considered a modest indie release, the game now ranks as one of the most played titles on the platform. Mega Crit expressed surprise at their own rapid growth, underscoring the unpredictable nature of game popularity in today’s market.
