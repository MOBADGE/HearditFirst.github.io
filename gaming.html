Dawn of War 4 Playtesters Enjoy Slower Combat to Watch Sync-Kills

King Art Games, developer of *Dawn of War 4*, shared insights from their recent closed alpha playtests. Senior game designer Elliott Verbiest revealed that many players asked for longer combat sequences because they enjoyed watching the game's advanced synchronized melee animations, which go beyond the original series’ sync-kills. This cinematic approach adds a visual layer to the gameplay that players find compelling.

The developers also discussed balancing gameplay mechanics for multiplayer. For example, the Necron faction’s resurrection orb, which can revive dead units, was reworked to avoid frustrating opponents. Instead of immediate resurrections, the game will implement a building mechanic called the resurrection gallery that allows delayed unit revival and opens opportunities for counterplay. The game plans to include special campaign moments, such as playing as the Dark Angels' primarch Lion El'Jonson, with such characters reserved for singleplayer. *Dawn of War 4* targets release in 2026.

Jeff Kaplan Reflects on His Departure from Activision Blizzard

Jeff Kaplan, co-creator of *Overwatch* and former public face of the franchise, spoke candidly about his exit from Activision Blizzard in a recent interview. He cited the heavy pressure from the company and investors primarily focused on monetizing the Overwatch League esports initiative. Kaplan described how resources shifted away from developing new game content toward meeting the demands of the league’s business model, which was heavily commercialized with high expectations that ultimately were unmet.

Kaplan recounted a meeting where he was given revenue targets for *Overwatch* tied to large-scale layoffs if unmet, calling it the "biggest f**k you moment" in his career. He indicated that the studio's focus on quick revenue over game quality contributed to his decision to leave. Since his departure in 2021, the game—relaunched as *Overwatch* in 2026—has undergone changes, including scaling back on features like PvE modes initially promised for *Overwatch 2*.

New Warhammer Blood Bowl Update as Separate Game Free for Current Owners

Cyanide Studio announced a new update for *Blood Bowl* will not be a patch but a distinct game titled *Warhammer Blood Bowl*, incorporating the latest tabletop rules. Players who already own *Blood Bowl 3* will receive it for free, retaining all unlocked content. The new title adds two teams—Tomb Kings and Bretonnians—bringing the launch lineup to 26 teams.

Additionally, a new "Rumble mode" offers seven-player teams on smaller pitches for shorter, faster matches, available as a free Steam demo from March 18 to 25. The update, planned for spring 2026, will also introduce updated rosters, new competitions, tutorials, and an evolving competitive framework culminating in an annual World Championship.

Valve Responds to New York Loot Box Lawsuit, Dismisses Violence Claims

After New York filed a lawsuit accusing Valve of enabling illegal gambling via loot boxes, the company issued a detailed rebuttal. Valve argued that loot boxes resemble widely accepted collectibles such as baseball cards and that all items obtained are cosmetic, posing no financial disadvantage to players who do not spend money.

Valve emphasized efforts to combat fraudulent gambling outside Steam, including account lockdowns and restrictions on trading to prevent misuse. The company also criticized proposals from New York authorities that would limit digital item transferability, calling such measures detrimental to user rights and industry innovation.

Notably, Valve dismissed the lawsuit’s unrelated claims linking violent videogames to real-world violence as outdated and unsupported by research. Valve stated numerous studies show no causal relationship between game content and violence, calling these arguments "a distraction and a mischaracterization."

Diablo 4: Lord of Hatred Expansion Introduces Enhanced Crafting System

The upcoming *Lord of Hatred* expansion for *Diablo 4* will introduce a new crafting system inspired by *Diablo 2*'s Horadric Cube. Players will be able to combine items and upgrade lower-tier gear into legendary-quality equipment through various recipes. Notably, even common items can drop with powerful affixes, enabling them to be crafted into valuable upgrades, broadening the scope of potential loot.
