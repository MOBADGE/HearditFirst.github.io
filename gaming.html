Arc Raiders Adjusts Blueprint Drop Rates Following Player Efficiency

The developers behind the third-person shooter Arc Raiders have released a patch reducing the drop rate of rare blueprints found in First Wave Caches. This change comes after player feedback and data indicated that many players were quickly locating and collecting an excessive number of these blueprints. While this is not the first time blueprint availability has been scaled back, the current adjustment aims to balance gameplay by limiting how many blueprints players can reliably obtain per session. To offset the reduction, the drop rates for higher-tier crafting materials within these caches have been increased. This should help players craft better gear more easily, especially in light of recent crafting cost increases for Wolfpacks. Other fixes include resolving quest progression bugs, reaction glitches with snap hooks, and exploits allowing weapons to be placed in safe pockets without the proper augment.

**Why it matters:** This update reflects ongoing tuning efforts to maintain a balanced item economy in Arc Raiders. By adjusting drop rates, the developers seek to preserve the acquisition challenge for rare blueprints without overly frustrating players who depend on crafting materials. It also addresses exploits and bugs that could affect gameplay fairness and performance.

Ambrosia Sky Cuts Planned Acts, Will Conclude Narrative in One Free Update

Soft Rains, the developer behind the narrative sci-fi power-washing game Ambrosia Sky, has revised its content release plans. Originally intending to launch two more paid acts following November 2025’s Act One, the studio now plans to combine these into a single, free Act Two update later this year. This change is driven largely by positive player responses who connected strongly with the protagonist Dalia’s emotional journey and the sapphic relationship central to the story. The streamlined approach aims to deliver a polished and cohesive conclusion without diluting narrative momentum. Existing Act One owners will receive Act Two free of charge, while the game’s price will increase for new players once the final act is published.

**Why it matters:** Shifting from multiple paid acts to one free final update underscores how player feedback influences episodic game development. It enables Soft Rains to focus on the most appreciated story elements and gameplay mechanics, potentially improving overall player satisfaction. This approach also impacts how indie studios might manage narrative scope and episodic content monetization going forward.

Sony Retracts PC Release Plans for Some PlayStation Exclusives

According to a Bloomberg report, Sony is reversing its strategy of releasing PlayStation exclusive games on PC. Titles such as Sucker Punch’s Ghost of Yotei and Housemarque’s upcoming Saros are reportedly no longer slated for PC release. However, live-service games like Bungie's Marathon and Marvel Tokon will continue to come to PC. Notably, recent and upcoming games like Kena: Scars of Kosmora and Death Stranding 2 remain on track for PC launch. The pullback suggests Sony is prioritizing its console exclusivity to strengthen its brand and hardware sales, although insiders acknowledge the possibility of future strategy changes.

**Why it matters:** This policy shift may limit PC gamers' access to high-profile PlayStation singleplayer games they had expected after years of Sony porting efforts. It signals a potential reevaluation of platform exclusivity priorities at one of the industry's key players, which could affect market dynamics between consoles and PC.

Marathon’s Roadmap Details Seasons and Mandatory Progression Resets

Bungie has outlined the content roadmap for its extraction shooter Marathon through the first three seasons. The initial season, titled First Step, begins in March and brings new maps, ranked mode, events, guns, implants, and balance patches after the game’s launch. The subsequent seasons, Nightfall and the yet-to-be-named Season 3, will add night variants of maps, new enemies, upgrades, and weapon mods. A notable feature is mandatory seasonal server wipes that reset player gear and progression at each season’s end, leaving only cosmetics, titles, and Codex advances intact.

**Why it matters:** The roadmap reveals Bungie’s commitment to a seasonal live-service format with regular content injections and progression resets. These wipes are intended to balance gameplay and introduce flexibility in gameplay loops but may divide player opinion due to the loss of gear and progress.
