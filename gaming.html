MMO Dungeon Groups: When to Call It Quits

A recent MMO-focused column explores the common experience of joining random dungeon or raid groups in MMORPGs and facing underperformance or poor teamwork. Such pickup groups (PUGs) are often formed via group finders to tackle daily content, but occasionally players encounter tanks who delay pulls excessively, healers who struggle to keep the party alive, or DPS whose damage output is low. The article reflects on how players decide when to leave these difficult groups, especially when conflict erupts in chat or wipes accumulate.

This discussion highlights a social challenge in MMOs: balancing patience with frustration when cooperating with strangers. It also touches on the emotional toll, with some players admitting to quietly leaving or using the Alt+F4 shortcut to exit during poor runs. Understanding this dynamic matters because group content is central to many MMO experiences, and improving these multiplayer interactions can affect player enjoyment and retention.

Eternity Egg: Early Access Launch of Unique 3D Platformer

After approximately five years of solo development, "Eternity Egg," a 3D platformer inspired by 1990s computer-generated imagery, has entered early access on Steam. The game features distinct visuals reminiscent of older CG software and focuses on advanced movement mechanics like slide jumps, wall bounces, and pogo hops. The developer has warned players that the current version contains bugs and incomplete content.

This launch is notable as an example of a small-scale, artistically ambitious project reaching the market after a lengthy development period. Players interested in platformers with experimental visuals and motion may find it worth exploring, though patience is required given its unfinished state. Early reception has been generally positive despite technical issues, and a full release is expected next year.

Remembering Voice Actor Jim Ward

Jim Ward, a prolific voice actor known for roles in many classic games including Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic, Fallout: New Vegas, and Grim Fandango, has passed away at age 66. His contributions span a range of memorable characters such as Captain Qwark in Ratchet & Clank and Jack Krauser in Resident Evil 4. Ward was diagnosed with Alzheimer's disease, and complications from this illness led to his death.

His passing is significant to the gaming community given his extensive work shaping notable characters in RPGs and other genres, particularly during a period when full voice acting became common. Fans and colleagues have shared tributes acknowledging his impact on gaming narratives and character performances.
