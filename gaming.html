Game Developers Adjust Randomness to Favor Players

Adhoc, the studio behind the superhero comedy game Dispatch, has revealed that it uses a system similar to Firaxis’s XCOM series to adjust random success percentages in the player’s favor. In Dispatch’s mini-game where players assign heroes to tasks, any action with more than a 76% success chance is automatically successful up to three times in a row before true random odds resume. Additionally, low chances between 1% and 14% are bumped up to 15% to reduce frustration. These adjustments are disabled in the game’s final episode to increase challenge. This approach reflects a broader industry tendency to balance randomness so games feel fair without relying on true chance alone.

s&box Likely to Have Similar Pricing to Garry’s Mod

Garry Newman, creator of Garry’s Mod, discussed the upcoming game creation platform s&box and indicated that it will probably be priced between $10 and $20, close to Garry’s Mod’s original $10 price tag. Although the exact price is not finalized, Newman and Facepunch Studios COO Alistair McFarlane agree on that range. s&box is set to launch in April and continues Newman’s legacy of providing accessible game sandbox environments with modding and creation at their core.

Crabmeat Blends Crab Catching and Horror in a New Game

Crabmeat is a short point-and-click horror game released on Steam that takes place on a boat caught in an ocean swarming with unnerving crabs. Players work as Penal Colony Workers in a bleak Antarctic setting, tasked with catching Southern King Crabs to meet quotas under threat of lethal consequences. The game mixes survival elements with horror as players manage traps, navigate ice floes, and face monstrous sea creatures. Its unique premise and atmospheric design offer a different take on survival horror, currently available with a launch discount and a free demo.

Kickstarted Roguelite Monster Collector Surpasses $800K

Wonderful Neoran Valley, a roguelite monster-collection game inspired by Pokémon and roguelite card games like Slay the Spire, quickly surpassed its Kickstarter goal within 10 minutes and has raised over $800,000 so far. The game features unique creatures recruited through dialogue rather than capturing mechanics and combines RPG dialogue trees with run-based gameplay. Its rapid crowdfunding success is attributed in part to the popularity of its creative director Cydonia, an Italian Pokémon streamer. Stretch goals include expanded content and a Switch 2 port.

Brendan Greene Critiques AI Content’s Impact on the Internet

Brendan Greene, known as PlayerUnknown, shared concerns that large language models (LLMs) and AI content are creating a feedback loop undermining the quality of online information. He explained that AI systems scan and generate text based on existing content, including low-quality material, which then becomes accepted as truth, leading to a "race to the middle" of mediocre or false information. Greene also questioned the current reliance on massive data centers and cloud computing to advance AI, suggesting that intelligence won’t simply emerge from scaling existing models and emphasizing the importance of localized computing solutions.

Greedfall: The Dying World Released After Two Years in Early Access

Spiders’ RPG Greedfall: The Dying World has left early access with a full 1.0 release after two years but still feels incomplete in some areas. The game features a new real-time with pause combat system but often suffers from lackluster AI and mechanical depth, making many fights tedious or easy. While the story explores themes of colonization and chemical warfare with branching choices, many characters fail to engage, and bugs persist including crashes and progression issues. Despite problems, its atmospheric soundtrack and detailed environments present moments of quality in a largely mixed experience.
