Minecraft Updates Baby Mobs with Unique Models and Sounds

Minecraft has revealed a feature planned for its first update of 2026 that redesigns baby mobs in the game. Unlike previous versions where baby creatures were just smaller models with oversized heads, the update will introduce unique textures and new models for each baby mob, such as a small yellow blocky chick instead of a miniature chicken. This change applies to all passive mobs players can breed, including ocelots, rabbits, farm animals, and wolf pups. The redesign is accompanied by new, age-appropriate sounds to better match the baby versions of these mobs. This update aims to enhance immersion and could influence player behavior away from routine mob farming by making interactions with young animals feel more engaging. While specifics such as a release date or update name have not been announced, it is expected around March 2026, based on previous update timelines.
