Organized Inside Offers Relaxed Puzzle Gameplay with Cat Companion

A new cozy puzzle game, Organized Inside, has been released as a follow-up to titles like Unpacking and A Little to the Left. Developed by Meox Studio, the game centers on following a stray black cat through various locations, tidying and organizing objects in settings such as a pet store, dorm, kitchen, and train compartment. Unlike some puzzle games that have fixed solutions, Organized Inside allows players flexibility in how they arrange each level’s items, as long as the final space looks tidy. This design choice reduces frustration by permitting multiple valid outcomes, encouraging replayability and personal organization styles.

While the game does not break new ground in storytelling or mechanics, its appeal lies in delivering a calming, satisfying experience enhanced by the presence of the cat. A free demo is available on Steam, allowing players to sample the gameplay before purchase.

Crimson Desert Updates: Patch Introduces Improvements and Boss Nerfs, Recognizes Control Issues

Pearl Abyss released a patch for the recently launched action RPG Crimson Desert addressing several quality-of-life concerns. The update adds private storage chests at base camps to alleviate inventory management frustrations, introduces additional fast travel points, and improves user interface responsiveness. Several bosses and enemies have been nerfed to balance difficulty, and stamina consumption for blocking attacks has been adjusted. Additionally, parrying attacks now more effectively increases a boss's stun gauge, and horses better respond when called.

The developers acknowledged player complaints regarding the game's control scheme, especially for keyboard and mouse users, and committed to preparing further improvements. The patch signals Pearl Abyss’s intent to refine gameplay post-launch in response to community feedback.

Guides Provide Clarity on Completing Crimson Desert Quests and Boss Fights

Several detailed how-to guides have surfaced for Crimson Desert players seeking help with challenging quests and bosses. One explanation covers completing the "Close Threat" quest, the final stage of the Extinguishing the Last Flames storyline, where players gather evidence and name the guild traitor. The guide includes the correct sequence of evidence selections to identify the culprit, leading to a final confrontation.

Another guide offers strategies for defeating the Crimson Nightmare boss located in Fort Perwin. It advises players to acquire a gas mask from nearby enemies to counteract the boss’s damaging gas attack and recommends using specific abilities to stun and damage it effectively. Clearing the surrounding bandits before engaging the boss is also suggested to simplify the fight. These resources aim to reduce player confusion around complex tasks in the game.

AI Use in Games Remains Controversial Following Crimson Desert Artwork Incident

The presence and use of artificial intelligence tools in game development has become a contentious topic again after players discovered AI-generated artwork in the final release of Crimson Desert. Pearl Abyss issued an apology explaining that the AI-created assets were meant to be placeholders during early development but were mistakenly left in the shipped product. This incident follows earlier cases where other studios unintentionally included AI assets in commercial releases.

In response, former Blizzard president Mike Ybarra publicly argued that AI will inevitably be part of every video game going forward and criticized developers for apologizing to a subset of players resistant to this change. Meanwhile, industry surveys indicate that most game developers favor greater transparency about AI use in games. The debate highlights ongoing tensions between production practices, player expectations, and disclosure norms around AI technology in the gaming industry.

Marvel Heroes Returns with Player-Run Servers After Eight Years Offline

The MMO action RPG Marvel Heroes, which was shut down in late 2017, has been revived through player-operated servers. A server emulator recently reached version 1.0, enabling fans to host and join servers independently of the original developer and publisher. The first public player-run server launched in early 2026, allowing users to play online again after nearly a decade offline. A single-player option is also available for those preferring a lag-free experience.

This resurgence was facilitated by the community-driven Project Tahiti, which provides infrastructure and instructions for setting up the emulator and connecting to servers. The revival allows fans to revisit Marvel Heroes’ blend of Diablo-style gameplay with Marvel characters, extending the game’s lifespan beyond its official closure.
