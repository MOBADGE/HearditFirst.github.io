Marathon’s Cryo Archive: Understanding Security Clearance

Marathon’s Cryo Archive map presents a complex system called Security Clearance, which players need to increase in order to access restricted areas, complete puzzles, and ultimately extract from the map. Unlike other maps, Cryo Archive requires raising a Security Clearance level through three main actions: killing UESC enemies to collect red Security Tags, defeating other players and looting their tags, and hacking terminals that grant points toward clearance. Clearance points collected from tags are lost if the carrier dies, so distributing tags among teammates is advisable. Achieving Security Clearance level 3 is essential for activating Exfil Stations and completing an extraction successfully. Higher levels grant access to vaults with better loot and secret rewards, making understanding and managing this system critical for successful runs.

How Extraction Works in Cryo Archive

Extraction from Cryo Archive differs substantially from other Marathon maps. Players must first reach Security Clearance level 3 to interact with the hidden Exfil Stations scattered across the map. Using scanners in many rooms helps locate these stations. Once an Exfil Station is activated, it sets a Secret Exfil point for the team, typically appearing across the map. Players then have about three and a half minutes to reach this extraction point before the countdown expires. Aside from this timed method, a final extract occurs at the end of the match, but it’s unmarked and unlikely to be found accidentally. Players who defeat the boss, Compiler, gain access to a unique extraction. This extraction process requires careful team coordination and time management, emphasizing the map’s increased difficulty compared to typical extraction gameplay.

Sand Delayed Amidst Ongoing Challenges for Ukrainian Developer

The PvPvE extraction shooter Sand: Raiders of Sophie, previously planned for a March release, has been delayed due to ongoing difficulties faced by its Ukrainian developer, Hologryph. The team cited severe power outages and air alert disruptions over recent months as causes for delays in finalizing key features such as improvements to PvE enemies, server physics, anti-cheat systems, and weapons. While no new release date has been revealed, the developers plan to provide further updates and community engagement events in the interim. The delay reflects the challenging environment impacting development, underscoring how external factors continue to affect game release schedules.

Baldur’s Gate 3 Hotfix Addresses Issues from Previous Patch

Larian Studios released Hotfix 36 for Baldur’s Gate 3 to resolve crashes and performance problems introduced by their earlier patch addressing corrupted save files. Although the February update successfully fixed some save corruption issues, players reported new crashes and framerate drops linked to that patch. The latest hotfix, under 100MB, aims to fix these new issues, along with additional bugs affecting Xbox tooltips and Mac progress blockers in Honour Mode. Early player response indicates relief at the patch’s improvements, though some continue to raise unrelated concerns. This hotfix highlights the challenges of balancing fixes without unintended side effects in live-service RPGs.

Stalker 2 Reveals First Major Story Expansion “Cost of Hope”

GSC Game World announced “Cost of Hope,” the first major story expansion for Stalker 2: Heart of Chornobyl, targeting a summer release. The expansion introduces two new regions, including the long-sealed Chornobyl Nuclear Power Plant, and focuses on escalating tensions between the opposing factions Duty and Freedom. Players take on the role of Skif, navigating new threats, mutants, anomalies, and equipment while influencing the Zone’s fate. Promised as a nonlinear experience offering dozens of hours of gameplay, Cost of Hope is part of the game’s second trilogy of content, with a third story DLC planned for the future. This expansion aims to deepen the Stalker 2 narrative and environmental experience.

Serious Sam Transforms into a Roguelite Multiplayer Shooter

Devolver Digital revealed Serious Sam: Shatterverse, a new multiplayer run-based roguelite FPS planned for later this year. Unlike previous games in the series, Shatterverse features up to five players, each playing as different versions of Sam from alternate dimensions, traversing procedurally altered arenas filled with hazards and enemies. The game emphasizes quick runs with escalating risks and rewards, permanent character upgrades, and a mix of new and classic weapons. Developed by Behaviour Interactive rather than Croteam, it aims to respect the franchise’s legacy while offering fresh multiplayer dynamics. The game is currently available for wishlist on Steam though no firm release date
