Asus Reveals 240 Hz Micro-OLED Gaming Glasses with Virtual 171-Inch Screen

Asus introduced the ROG Xreal R1 AR gaming glasses featuring a 240 Hz Micro-OLED display that projects a virtual 171-inch screen at roughly 4 meters distance. The device covers 95% of the human focus area with a 57-degree field of view. Despite this, the glasses have a full HD (1080p) resolution, which some may find low for such a large virtual screen close to the eyes. The glasses connect via a control dock with DisplayPort, HDMI, and USB-C inputs, compatible with PCs, consoles, phones, and Asus handhelds. Launch is expected in the first half of 2026. While innovative, questions remain about how well the glasses balance resolution, comfort, and usability.

In a typically quiet January for large game launches, Ball x Pit, a roguelike indie game released last October, is gaining attention as a compelling alternative. It features a creative mix of ball types and passive items, 16 unique characters with distinct traits, and map-specific challenges. Despite its simple premise, Ball x Pit has proven engaging and inventive, making it a good choice for players seeking a smaller-scale but addictive experience during a slow release period.
