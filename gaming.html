Windrose: Comprehensive Guide to Discoveries Across Its Three Main Biomes

Windrose, the pirate-themed survival crafting game currently in early access, features three playable biomes: the Coastal Jungle, the Foothills, and the Cursed Swamps. Each area has a set of unique items and resources to discover, some of which can be difficult to find. A detailed list of these discoveries has been compiled, organized by region, with notes on quest and boss requirements that unlock further exploration and crafting.

- **Coastal Jungle**: This starter zone introduces core quests such as "I Need a Bigger Boat" and culminates in a boss fight against Thomas Richards, which unlocks the Foothills region. Noteworthy items include the elusive Crab Shell—obtained from harmless red crabs—and rare Palm and Ficus Seeds, which drop after persistent tree harvesting. Key resources like Copper Ore and Rough Hide enable crafting of early-game tools and armor.

- **Foothills**: This region features more hostile wildlife and enemies, demanding upgraded equipment like the Iron Pickaxe and Axe. The main questline revolves around the boss Israel Hands, whose defeat grants access to the Cursed Swamps. Major discoveries here include Fertile Soil for farming, Iron Ore for weapon crafting, and rare plants like Aloe and Coffee Beans. The area also introduces tougher enemies dropping materials such as Wolf Fangs and Bezoar for elixirs.

- **Cursed Swamps**: The most challenging current biome, the Cursed Swamps are filled with dangerous foes and environmental hazards such as plague-infested pink forests. Progression includes the "Forgotten Relics" questline and a boss fight with the High Priestess. Rare materials such as Ancient Scraps and Crocodile Hide Pieces underpin advanced crafting, while unique resources like Tar and Plague Wood enable specialized recipes. Players must be cautious due to status effects like the plague in this zone.

This compilation aids players by providing clear guidance on where to find specific items, what quests and bosses need to be completed to access new crafting options, and tips to overcome tricky drops. Understanding these intricacies is valuable in navigating Windrose's survival mechanics and crafting systems.

Slay the Spire 2 Development Prioritizes Quality Over Deadlines

Since its early access launch in March, Slay the Spire 2 has attracted a strong player base and continues to receive updates. Developer Mega Crit recently shared a roadmap of upcoming content, including alternate versions of Acts 2 and 3, Steam Workshop support, new experimental game modes, an additional character, and an array of new cards, events, relics, and potions. Importantly, the developers have explicitly rejected setting release deadlines.

Mega Crit co-founder Casey Yano explained that the small team prefers flexible, healthy pacing over strict schedules, which they believe can lead to rushed and uninspired results. The approach allows room for experimental content such as unique dialogues and offbeat game modes. Players who wish to engage with new features early can opt into the beta branch and provide feedback.

This commitment to a quality-first approach highlights Mega Crit’s desire to maintain the creative integrity of Slay the Spire 2, even if that means a longer development process. For fans, this suggests continued iterative refinement rather than premature feature releases.

Clarifying the Location of the People of Tortuga’s Buyer in Windrose

Players navigating Windrose’s trading systems may encounter confusion when attempting to sell provisions and medicine to the People of Tortuga faction. Although their bounty agent and provisioner are located on the island of Tortuga, the faction’s buyer is actually found on a different nearby island.

To locate this buyer, players should progress through the "Glorious Hunters" and "Buccaneers" questlines. Completing these quests will reveal markers on the map pointing to the buyer’s island outside of Tortuga’s waters. Delivering gunpowder to Henri, the Buccaneers' head, unlocks the next task to deliver tanned hides to Charlie Sharpe, who represents the buyer.

Knowing this location helps players complete trading activities and raises faction reputation. Additionally, a cook named Black Axel can be hired on the buyer’s island to enhance potion crafting at the player’s base.
