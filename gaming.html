Marvel Rivals Introduces Victim Compensation Protocol to Address Match Throwing

Marvel Rivals is implementing a new system called the "Victim Compensation Protocol" to restore points lost by players affected by a recent surge in match throwing. This exploit, known as "incentivised throwing," involved a website and Discord channel that offered bounties encouraging players to deliberately lose games for profit. NetEase, the developer, has responded by strengthening penalties for confirmed match throwing and using a new detection system combining data analysis and manual review. The compensation system will automatically credit points back to players who lost matches due to a teammate’s malicious behavior. This move aims to preserve game integrity following community backlash and could significantly improve the player experience.

Bungie Aims for Inclusive Progression with Marathon’s Evergreen Content

Bungie has revealed that its upcoming live-service shooter, Marathon, will avoid the content vaulting practice seen in Destiny 2. Unlike Destiny 2, where large portions of story content have been removed over time, Marathon’s story and priority contracts are designed to remain accessible and evergreen regardless of when players join. Creative director Julia Nardin explained that players will always be able to complete established quests and earn achievements, with ongoing content added based on feedback. However, Marathon’s mandatory seasonal resets every three months wipe faction and gear progress while retaining some collection unlocks, raising questions on how the growing content will be managed season to season. Bungie's approach seeks to create a stable and accessible experience while maintaining the challenge of seasonal renewals.

PS5 Slim Runs Linux and Steam, Transforming Into a Budget Gaming PC

A software engineer named Andy Nguyen has successfully ported Linux (Ubuntu) to the PlayStation 5 Slim, effectively turning the console into a Steam Machine capable of playing PC games like GTA 5 Enhanced. While Sony’s PlayStation OS is based on Linux, the modding required bypassing strict firmware security restrictions using a known exploit that only applies to older versions. Nguyen adapted the open-source Mesa graphics drivers for the PS5’s custom AMD GPU. Despite thermal limitations and some hardware recognition quirks, the system runs well enough to serve as a functional gaming PC. This achievement demonstrates that with effort, modern consoles can be repurposed beyond their intended use, though such modifications remain technically complex and unavailable to most users.

Master Chief Voice Actor Condemns White House’s Use of His Voice in War Video

Steve Downes, the voice of Master Chief from the Halo series, has publicly demanded the White House remove his voice from a propaganda video posted on its official X account supporting the ongoing US and Israel conflict with Iran. The video controversially uses clips of popular fictional characters including Master Chief alongside footage of bombings in Iran. Downes clarified he was not consulted and does not endorse the video’s message, labeling it “disgusting and juvenile war porn.” The White House’s use of copyrighted characters and celebrity voices in political messaging raises questions about consent and appropriation. The video remains online, continuing a contentious pattern of politicized use of pop culture.

Counter-Strike 2’s X-Ray Scanner Loot System Expands to Germany

Responding to regulatory scrutiny, Valve is rolling out an “X-ray scanner” for loot containers to Counter-Strike 2 players in Germany starting March 16. This feature allows players to preview a container’s contents before purchase but comes with a strict restriction: once scanned, players must buy that item before scanning another container. This prevents players from scanning multiple cases to cherry-pick desirable items, aiming to comply with anti-gambling legislation. The system has been in place in France since 2019 and is now expanding due to similar legal pressures. While the change does not affect loot odds or container costs, it alters how players experience loot boxes under tightening global regulation of virtual gambling mechanics.

Konami’s Master of Epic MMO Still Active 21 Years Later

Master of Epic (MoE), Konami’s Japanese-only fantasy MMO launched in 2005, remains active with regular updates and a small but dedicated player base. Its unique features include time-traveling zones, a complex skill system without rigid classes, and survival elements like hunger and stamina. MoE eschews modern MMO conveniences such as quest markers and teleportation, encouraging exploration and player immersion. Despite its niche status, the game’s enduring community and content updates make it a rare example of an early MMORPG that has continued steady operation for over two decades
