Dispatch Follows XCOM’s Example in Favoring Players with Adjusted Probabilities

The indie superhero comedy Dispatch uses a system similar to Firaxis's XCOM series that subtly adjusts random chances to benefit players. Game directors Nick Herman and Dennis Lenart revealed in a recent talk that any dispatch success chance above 76% is made to automatically succeed, reducing frustrating failures on what should be reliable actions. However, after three consecutive boosted wins, the system returns to true odds until the player fails above 76%, then the boosts resume to avoid strings of bad luck. Lower probabilities between 1% and 14% are raised to 15%. This approach aims to create a perception of fairness by limiting unlucky streaks, a common frustration in randomness-based games. The system is disabled in the game’s final episode to increase challenge. This transparency highlights the balancing act developers undertake to ensure randomness feels fair without simply relying on pure chance.

Garry Newman Indicates s&box Will Cost Around the Same as Garry’s Mod

With its launch approaching in April, Facepunch Studios’ new game creation platform s&box’s pricing remains unofficially set around $10 to $20, similar to its predecessor Garry’s Mod. Creator Garry Newman and COO Alistair McFarlane discussed the price range in a recent interview but have not finalized an exact figure. Garry’s Mod was priced at $10 on release 20 years ago and remains popular, making it a reasonable benchmark. The new platform builds on Garry’s Mod’s legacy but targets a modern audience with updated tools. The tentative pricing suggests Facepunch aims to remain accessible while reflecting improvements since the original physics sandbox debuted.

Wonderful Neoran Valley Kickstarter Surpasses $800,000, Fueled by Popular Pokémon Streamer

Wonderful Neoran Valley, a roguelite monster-collector inspired by Pokémon with added JRPG and horror elements, quickly met and exceeded its Kickstarter goal thanks in part to the involvement of popular Italian Pokémon streamer Cydonia. The campaign raised over $800,000—more than seven times its initial target—unlocking stretch goals such as additional creatures, questlines, an endless mode, and a planned Switch 2 port. The game differentiates itself by requiring players to recruit monsters through dialogue rather than capture mechanics. Its blend of styles and strong community backing positions it as an interesting entrant into the monster-collecting genre currently dominated by established franchises.

Brendan Greene Critiques AI Content’s Impact on the Internet and Calls for Local Computing

PlayerUnknown creator Brendan Greene expressed concerns over AI-generated online content perpetuating misinformation in a feedback loop, describing it as a “race to the middle of [bad content].” In a PC Gamer interview, Greene said many large language models reproduce low-quality data, which then becomes accepted as truth. He also criticized the environmental and societal costs of expanding massive data centers to fuel AI, doubting that scaling computing automatically leads to true intelligence. Greene advocates for more localized computing solutions rather than centralized, resource-heavy data centers. He also gave a nuanced view of AI, supporting its use in domain-specific applications while cautioning against overreliance on non-deterministic models.

Greedfall: The Dying World Launches After Two Years in Early Access, but Feels Unfinished

The sequel and prequel Greedfall: The Dying World has officially released 1.0 after two years in early access. Developed by Spiders, it shifts from live third-person combat to a real-time with pause system. Reviews and extensive playtesting reveal that while the game has ambitious narrative and world-building elements, its combat is easy and repetitive. Bugs, crashes, and awkward quest mechanics were also reported. Dialogue choices and moral decisions remain a highlight, though many found the characters and story less engaging than expected. The game’s blend of eurojank-style RPG design and new mechanics results in an experience that feels unfinished despite its lengthy development. Nonetheless, it maintains a niche appeal for players interested in slower-paced, story-driven RPGs.
