Nvidia Reflects on 25 Years of the GeForce 3 Graphics Card

Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang recently marked the 25th anniversary of the GeForce 3, a graphics card launched in 2001 that Nvidia considers a major transition point for the company. Huang highlighted that GeForce 3 introduced the first successful mainstream graphics accelerator architecture that integrated the entire graphics pipeline stage—including memory traffic and texture processing—into hardware. This allowed game developers greater creative freedom through pixel shaders, moving away from pre-coded graphics. Huang also noted the evolution in technology since, comparing the GeForce 3’s 30 million transistors and 35-watt power draw to modern GPUs with hundreds of billions of transistors requiring hundreds of watts. The GeForce 3 powered classics like Max Payne and Civilization III, illustrating early 2000s PC gaming before moving to today's graphically advanced titles.

Jeff Kaplan Recalls Overwatch’s Difficult Birth After Titan’s Cancellation

Ex-Overwatch director Jeff Kaplan shared new insights on the rapid pivot from Blizzard’s canceled MMO, Titan, to what would become Overwatch. In an interview, Kaplan described a stressful six-week period during which a small, temporary team was tasked with pitching new game concepts. The criteria were tight: the game had to ship within two years and have the potential for revenue comparable to World of Warcraft, though Kaplan personally downplayed the latter. Three pitches were developed—Overwatch, a StarCraft MMO, and "Crossworlds," a sci-fi universe concept. Overwatch was chosen partly for practicality and because it best fit the reduced team and timeline. Elements of Titan’s character concepts inspired heroes like Tracer. Despite the pressure, the team succeeded in creating a new game that launched successfully.

Arc Raiders Slows Down to Find Its Identity in Extraction Shooter Pivot

Development director Caio Braga discussed a major redesign of Arc Raiders, notable for slowing the game’s movement speed by roughly 60% during a pivotal development phase. Earlier versions of Arc Raiders featured fast-paced gameplay with abundant mobility tools, but the speed made many core elements feel like background noise. Slowing down allowed the team to better showcase sound design, map layouts, and the underpinning "extraction shooter" mechanics. Braga described how the team stopped forcing the original game concepts and instead embraced what was working, which shaped the current gameplay style emphasizing stamina management and deliberate movement.

Microsoft Quietly Ends “This is an Xbox” Marketing Campaign

Microsoft has begun removing all references to its "This is an Xbox" ad campaign, which launched in late 2024 to promote Xbox’s cloud gaming ecosystem and device versatility. The campaign presented Xbox as a gaming platform accessible on many devices beyond consoles, including phones, smart TVs, and even car seat screens. The original campaign announcement and related website pages have been taken offline, signaling a shift in marketing strategy possibly tied to the upcoming “Project Helix” Xbox console-PC hybrid. The move reflects Microsoft’s evolving positioning from a traditional console maker toward a hybrid gaming platform integrated more closely with Windows and PC gaming.

Key Copper Foil Supplier Raises Prices, Impacting PC Component Costs

Mitsui Kinzoku, a leading supplier of copper foil used in printed circuit boards (PCBs), announced a 12% price increase effective April 2026. The rise is attributed to surging copper raw material prices, labor costs, and market volatility. Copper prices reached all-time highs early this year amid supply challenges and geopolitical factors, despite record smelter output in China. The increase contributes to broader hardware pricing pressures affecting PC components beyond memory and storage. Additionally, Mitsubishi Gas Chemical announced a 30% price rise on resin-coated copper foil. The strain on supply chains and rising material costs suggest that PC hardware prices may continue to climb in the near term.

Fortnite Community Reacts Negatively to Increased V-Bucks Costs

Epic Games announced that the in-game currency V-Bucks will become more expensive due to increased operational costs for Fortnite. This adjustment reduces the value of monthly Fortnite Crew subscriptions and sparked immediate backlash from players, including calls for boycotts and subscription cancellations. Fortnite developers responded publicly to address player concerns, emphasizing ongoing commitment to gameplay and content development. However, the controversy highlights ongoing tensions between game monetization strategies and player expectations. Previous similar boycotts in the industry have generally had limited success, leaving the community divided on the effectiveness of the current pushback.
