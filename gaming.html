Asus Begins Internal Review Over Ryzen 7 9800X3D Failures on 800-Series Motherboards

Asus has announced an immediate internal review following reports that some Ryzen 7 9800X3D CPUs are failing when installed on its AMD 800-series motherboards. This comes after similar issues were documented with ASRock motherboards, where BIOS configurations may have allowed excessive current draw causing CPU damage. AMD attributed the problem to some motherboard BIOSes not adhering to its recommended electrical specifications.

Asus is collaborating with AMD to identify the causes and ensure product stability, recommending users update their BIOS via Asus EZ Flash or BIOS Flashback to improve compatibility. Customers who have experienced problems are advised to contact Asus support for assistance. The issue raises broader concerns about motherboard and CPU compatibility with high-performance Ryzen chips, hinting that microcode adjustments might be necessary to prevent hardware failures.

Intel Confirms Nova Lake Launch for Late 2026 Amid Supply Constraints

Intel's next-generation Core processors, known as Nova Lake, are scheduled for release at the end of 2026, according to CEO Lip-Bu Tan. Nova Lake is expected to offer performance and cost improvements over the current Arrow Lake series, including features such as a large last-level cache akin to AMD's 3D V-Cache technology.

Despite the upcoming launch, Intel acknowledges supply constraints due to ongoing memory shortages. The company is prioritizing mid- and high-end client processors alongside datacenter demands rather than low-end models. Intel's CFO clarified that the company aims to allocate its limited capacity primarily to important customers in both datacenter and client markets.

These constraints may explain the lack of an Arrow Lake refresh this year, as Intel shifts focus to Nova Lake. However, Intel's upcoming Panther Lake chips show promise for thin-and-light laptops and handheld devices, although Q4 revenue declined compared to the previous year.
