Magic: The Gathering Avoids Harry Potter Crossover, Returns to Strixhaven

Magic: The Gathering will not feature a Harry Potter crossover set despite the recent partnership between its owner Hasbro and Warner Bros for Harry Potter licensed products. Instead, the new set "Secrets of Strixhaven" revisits Magic’s original wizarding school setting, first introduced with "Strixhaven: School of Mages" five years ago. Unlike the Hogwarts-inspired narrative, Strixhaven depicts a university with a variety of magical and non-magical subjects and older students, allowing for more nuanced storytelling and less reliance on life-threatening student adventures. This focus on its own setting, which also satirizes magical school tropes, has been welcomed by fans who appreciate Magic’s distinct identity apart from more mainstream fantasies.

Crimson Desert Updates with Hide Helmet Feature and More

The RPG Crimson Desert has received a notable update adding a player-friendly "hide helmet" option—a highly requested feature. Players can now customize helmet visibility in cutscenes, combat, or disable it entirely. Alongside this, the patch introduces a new armor set and helmet for the in-game cats, increased camp storage capacity to 1,000 slots, and a "classic" control scheme for movement, catering to players preferring the original style. The developers, Pearl Abyss, continue to refine the game post-launch, addressing player feedback to improve experience amid mixed initial reception and some controversy over the use of AI art assets.

Leaked Footage Offers Possible First Look at Elden Ring Movie Set

Leaked videos from a fan in the UK show a detailed outdoor set featuring a ruined stone church that closely resembles the Church of Marika from Elden Ring, raising speculation it is connected to Alex Garland’s upcoming Elden Ring movie. The set includes props such as wagons and barrels and is constructed by a company specializing in film scenery. While it is unclear if the set directly relates to the film or another promotional project, its location near a popular filming area known for large productions adds credibility. Apart from cast rumors and Garland’s strong attachment to the game, minimal official information about the movie’s plot or production has been released.

Datamined Elden Ring Cutscene Reveals More About DLC Villain Miquella

Dataminer Lance McDonald uncovered an unreleased cutscene from Elden Ring featuring the DLC antagonist Miquella the Kind. The scene depicts Miquella planting the Haligtree sapling and watering it with his own blood, linking to the DLC’s endgame location. Voice lines suggest Miquella speaks both to his twin (implied to be Malenia) and the player, referencing a vision of a new world where various forces—both "graceful and malign"—will flourish. This discovery sheds light on early story elements of Miquella’s character, emphasizing a once more benevolent and idealistic figure before the darker turns seen in the DLC narrative.

Warframe Developer Commits to No AI-Generated Content

Warframe's community director Megan Everett stated unequivocally that no content within Warframe or its spiritual successor Soulframe will ever be generated using AI. The developer Digital Extremes rejects AI-generated art, emphasizing human creation throughout their projects. Everett expressed frustration with the prevalence of AI art that can be difficult to distinguish from human work, highlighting concerns about artistic integrity. This stance contrasts with the wider industry trend where AI tools are increasingly common despite growing debate over their ethical use and transparency.

Amazon TV Orders Mass Effect Show Rewrite for Broader Appeal

Reports indicate Amazon MGM Studios is requesting rewrites on the in-development Mass Effect TV series to make it "more appealing to non-gamers." The series, nearing a green light for production, faces the challenge of satisfying both dedicated fans and general audiences unfamiliar with the games. This reflects a common tension in video game adaptations, where balancing fidelity to source material against broader storytelling poses difficulties. Meanwhile, development on the next Mass Effect game continues despite recent layoffs and corporate changes at BioWare, the game's developer.
