Sekiro Mod Transforms Game into Skateboarding Simulator

A modder named Ionian-MikiriyAKATotallyNotShinobi has released "Jet Set Sekiro," a skateboarding mod for Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice. The mod replaces regular game movement with skateboarding mechanics set in the familiar Fountainhead Palace environment. Players collect spray paint canisters to tag spots around the map, blending the action style of Sekiro with the navigational flow reminiscent of early 2000s skateboarding games. This mod is notable for putting skateboarding gameplay inside a FromSoft title, a reversal of past fan projects that brought FromSoft themes into skateboarding games. The mod also highlights a unique cultural link between Japanese action games and skateboarding games. Separately, the skating-themed game Skate Story is currently discounted to $16 on GOG, expanding access to this niche genre blend.

Allegations Surface Over MMO Kickstarter Fund Misuse

Serious accusations have emerged regarding the handling of Kickstarter funds for the now-defunct MMO Ashes of Creation by Intrepid Studios. Investor Jason Caramanis and YouTuber NefasQS allege that founder Steven Sharif misappropriated millions intended for the game's development, spending on personal items including private chefs, antiques, cigars, and trading card websites. These claims are based on leaked financial ledgers spanning 2015 to 2026, ostensibly corroborated by interviews with former employees. Sharif has denied wrongdoing, attributing the project's collapse to board decisions. The controversy adds complexity to a previously troubled development history marked by layoffs and project cancellation. The situation remains unresolved due to confidential settlement terms and ongoing public debate about funding accountability in crowdfunded game development.

Blizzard Secures Legal Victory Against WoW Private Server

Blizzard Entertainment has obtained a court injunction against Turtle WoW, a popular private server emulating classic World of Warcraft with additional features. A US district court ordered the server’s developers to cease all development, support, and operation activities, including prohibiting code transfer to successors. This marks a further legal crackdown following a 2025 lawsuit alleging copyright infringement. Despite community appeals for an official fan server license model, Blizzard remains firm in protecting its intellectual property. The private server’s audience expressed frustration, noting a lack of comparable official offerings. This case reflects ongoing tensions between rights holders and fan-run servers seeking to preserve or expand legacy MMO experiences.

Dean 'Rocket' Hall Discusses Gaming Habits and Projects

Dean Hall, creator of DayZ and founder of RocketWerkz, shared insights into his gaming preferences and work. Currently engaged with RimWorld and its expansion Odyssey, he has logged many hours into the game and released popular mods enhancing gameplay. Hall also maintains interest in niche titles like Ostranauts and the open-source remake OpenXcom, which he played at Mount Everest base camp. His highest Steam playtime is on Stationeers, developed by RocketWerkz, and he has invested over 2,000 hours into Kerbal Space Program. Hall noted an upcoming Linux PC for better community support. In development, RocketWerkz is focusing on refining existing projects and planning a significant new game blending survival and tactical elements.

Life is Strange Soundtrack’s Lasting Impact

The soundtrack of the original 2015 Life is Strange continues to be recognized for its influential use of licensed indie music to enhance the game’s emotional and narrative depth. Its music selection helped establish an intimate mood aligning with the story's coming-of-age themes and teenage drama. The soundtrack bridged the episodic game format with television-style music curation, helping to set tone and atmosphere with carefully chosen tracks. Songs like Foals’ "Spanish Sahara" remain iconic, reinforcing key narrative moments. Subsequent Life is Strange titles have maintained strong soundtracks, but the first installment’s music curation is considered especially well-crafted and integral to the game’s experience even after more than a decade.
