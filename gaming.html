Tap Tap Loot: A More Involved Clicker Game Featuring Violent Bongo Cat

A new clicker game called **Tap Tap Loot** has launched a demo as part of the Steam Next Fest. The game expands on the concept of the popular Bongo Cat meme, placing a sword-wielding kitty character into battle against endless waves of enemies. Players kill monsters to earn experience points, level up, and acquire upgraded weapons and equipment automatically. Distinct from simpler clickers, Tap Tap Loot incorporates RPG elements such as an upgradeable inventory, multiple biomes, and co-op multiplayer for up to four players. While the current demo lacks some features like a stats page and has interface scaling quirks, the full game promises over 300 unique items and a deeper clicker experience. This game matters as it offers fans of clickers an option that adds more depth and strategy without losing the charm of the original Bongo Cat.

Discord Delays Global Age Verification and Ends Partnership with Controversial Vendor

Following widespread backlash over its age verification plan, **Discord** has announced a delay to its global age assurance rollout until the second half of 2026. The company acknowledged missteps and will revise its approach, including adding more age verification options and providing greater transparency about the vendors involved. Discord also severed ties with the identity verification provider Persona, whose connections to Peter Thiel raised privacy concerns among users and security researchers. Discord promises to publish technical details about its automated age determination process and include age assurance statistics in future transparency reports. This development shows the challenges tech platforms face in balancing regulatory compliance, user privacy, and trust in implementing mandatory age checks.
