Senator Bernie Sanders Proposes Moratorium on US AI Data Center Construction

US Senator Bernie Sanders, alongside Representative Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez, introduced the AI Data Center Moratorium Act aiming to halt new data center builds across the United States. The bill intends to provide lawmakers time to create regulations addressing the environmental impact, energy consumption, and socio-economic effects related to the rapid expansion of AI infrastructure. Sanders emphasized concerns over misinformation, privacy, and potential job losses as key reasons for legislative pause. If passed, the bill would take immediate effect, including a ban on exporting AI computing hardware to countries without similar safeguards. Despite bipartisan legislative challenges and strong industry momentum in AI investments, Sanders stresses the need for democratic oversight to avoid unchecked decisions by Big Tech.

Global PC Sales Forecast to Decline in 2026 Due to High Memory Prices

Counterpoint Research projects a 5% drop in global PC shipments in 2026, reversing recent growth trends. While gaming PCs have shown resilience, the overall market faces pressure from sustained high memory prices, which increase manufacturing costs and retail prices. This price rise dampens consumer demand, notably in mainstream and budget segments. Major brands like Dell and HP are expected to experience less severe declines compared to second-tier manufacturers. Apple is forecasted to grow its PC sales, partly due to new hardware releases. The report highlights the disproportionate effect on mid-range devices, where memory costs represent a larger share of total price, while high-end gaming PCs are less affected by the price increase.

Star Wars Zero Company Blends Turn-Based Tactics with Third-Person Action

Developer Bit Reactor’s upcoming Star Wars Zero Company offers a blend of turn-based tactical gameplay and third-person exploration, set during the Clone Wars era. Drawing heavily on XCOM’s gameplay and storytelling influences similar to Mass Effect, the game features permanent character consequences including permadeath (except for the player’s main character). The narrative includes a diverse cast with complex interpersonal dynamics, supported by tactical combat that incorporates advantage points and class-based abilities. Operations outside combat involve intelligence gathering and decision-making impacting enemy difficulty. The game is built with close collaboration with Lucasfilm and aims to appeal both to Star Wars fans and strategy enthusiasts. It is scheduled for release later this year.

Arc Raiders Faces Player Criticism Ahead of Flashpoint Update

Embark Studios’ Arc Raiders is preparing to launch the Flashpoint update on March 31, introducing new map conditions and player projects. A recent teaser revealed the Shredder enemy will escape its original map, Stella Montis, expanding its threat to other areas. Some players express concern that this move signals a lack of fresh content and are wary that Shredders may be replacing established enemy threats rather than offering new gameplay innovations. While Embark has a history of adding features and balancing updates, a vocal portion of the community is disappointed, hoping for substantial new additions and improvements in future patches such as the upcoming Riven Tides update.

Intel Releases Arc Pro B70 GPU Targeted at AI Applications, Not Gaming

Intel launched the Arc Pro B70 graphics card featuring the Big Battlemage GPU with 32 Xe cores and 32 GB of VRAM. Priced at $949, this model is designed primarily for AI inference workloads rather than gaming. The card offers substantial VRAM capacity compared to Nvidia’s competitors at a lower price point, which could appeal to users running large local AI models. Despite its gaming GPU origins, Intel has positioned the Arc Pro B70 for professional markets and not optimized it for game drivers. Alongside the B70, Intel also introduced the Arc Pro B65 with similar VRAM but fewer cores. The launch dampens expectations for a forthcoming cut-price gaming GPU based on this architecture.

Asus Responds to FCC Router Ban While Patching Critical Security Flaw

Following the FCC’s designation of foreign-made routers as a security risk, effectively banning their sale in the US without special approval, Asus issued a statement reaffirming confidence in its supply chain and product security. On the same day, Asus released a significant security update addressing CVE-2025-15101, a high-severity vulnerability allowing potential execution of system commands by authenticated users. While routine security patches demonstrate Asus’ ongoing efforts to secure its devices, the timing highlights challenges manufacturers face in maintaining router security. Users are reminded to keep their networking hardware updated to mitigate risks.
