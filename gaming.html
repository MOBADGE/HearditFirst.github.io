Red Dead Redemption 2 Gets a Mod That Randomly Kills the Player

A new mod titled "Random Heart Attacks" for Red Dead Redemption 2 introduces a random mechanic where every five minutes there is a 1 in 500 chance for the player character to have a heart attack and die instantly. The mod's creator offers no explanation or motivation for adding this feature. While the player's character Arthur Morgan is historically plausible to risk heart failure being in his late 30s in 1899, the mod essentially forces a quick death randomly during gameplay. This unusual addition exemplifies the creative and sometimes bizarre directions modders take, as recent mods have radically altered Red Dead Redemption 2's gameplay in unexpected ways, including transforming it into titles inspired by Katamari Damacy and Super Monkey Ball.

Larian Confirms No WASD Keyboard Movement for Divinity RPG

Larian Studios has provided a definitive answer regarding the control scheme of its upcoming RPG, Divinity: the game will not support WASD keyboard movement and will remain mouse-driven on PC. The decision, clarified during a recent AMA, contrasts with popular player mods for Baldur's Gate 3, which introduced WASD movement. Larian's gameplay lead Artem Titov gave a simple "No" to the keyboard control question. While this may disappoint some PC players favoring keyboard movement, it reflects the developer's intention to maintain a certain design philosophy. Controller movement will be supported on both PC and consoles, but the PC experience will not include this particular movement style natively. Mods will likely address this once the game releases.

Memory Crisis Drives Significant Rise in SSD Prices

SSD prices have sharply increased in recent months due to ongoing shortages in memory components. The 1TB Lexar NM790 NVMe SSD, for instance, has nearly doubled in price from about $66 in October 2025 to approximately $118 in early 2026. Higher-capacity drives such as the 4TB and 8TB WD_Black SN850X have seen even more dramatic price surges, with some listings exceeding $1,000. The root cause lies in a memory supply crisis tied to rising demand from AI data centers and manufacturing limitations. Industry insiders have indicated that memory manufacturers are sold out through 2026. For consumers, this suggests that SSD prices may remain elevated for some time, complicating PC upgrades and purchases.
