Ruiner 2 Shifts Toward an Action RPG Model

Developer Reikon Games has announced **Ruiner 2**, a sequel to their 2017 top-down shooter Ruiner. The original game was noted for its stylish, fast-paced cyberpunk action, but the sequel aims to add more depth by evolving into a systems-driven action role-playing game. Players can switch between three different "combat bodies" called Shells during fights, each offering unique playstyles, special abilities, and progression trees. Players unlock new shells by defeating bosses rather than menu selection, which aims to encourage experimentation and synergies between builds. The game supports both singleplayer and three-player co-op. While no release date is set, Ruiner 2 is now available for wishlisting on Steam. This shift reflects an effort to expand the original’s gameplay while maintaining its core combat intensity.

Big Tech's White House Energy Cost Pledge for AI

Several major technology companies, including Google, Microsoft, Meta, and Amazon, signed a **Ratepayer Protection Pledge** at the White House to cover the increased energy costs associated with AI data centers. This pledge commits these companies to fund new or expanded energy capacity and infrastructure upgrades to prevent increased electricity bills for consumers caused by rapidly expanding data center demand. Despite the announcement, the pledge's legal enforceability remains unclear, as the presidential proclamation invoking national authority does not cite specific statutes. Meta confirmed its ongoing commitment to pay full costs and support energy infrastructure projects. The initiative addresses concerns in the US about AI's impact on energy usage but may also serve political purposes ahead of upcoming elections.

Slay the Spire 2 Launches in Early Access Today

After a delay from late 2025, **Slay the Spire 2** officially launches in early access on March 5, 2026, at 10 a.m. Pacific Time on Steam. This sequel to the popular deck-building roguelike returns with new and returning characters, a large collection of cards, and introduces a four-player cooperative mode. Given the success of the original, this launch may see high server traffic. The global release occurs simultaneously worldwide, with timed availability adjusted per region. Fans of the original can now access the sequel's new content and experimentation opportunities as development continues.

Lightweight Gaming Mouse Without Shell Holes: G-Wolves Lycan 8K

G-Wolves is preparing to launch the **Lycan 8K** gaming mouse, which weighs approximately 28.8 grams without relying on perforated or honeycomb shell designs to reduce weight. This is relatively light for a solid-shell mouse and lighter than some competitors like the 36-gram Corsair Sabre V2 Pro. The Lycan 8K uses a Pixart PMW-3950 sensor with up to 30,000 DPI, features SPDT/SR-Latch switches designed to reduce input latency, and offers 8K polling rate. It maintains a standard size and includes no apparent sacrifices in features for weight. Preorders are open at $129, with a planned release around March 15. This offers gamers a lightweight option that prioritizes solid construction.

Vib-OS: An AI-Coded Operating System with Practical Issues

An experimental operating system called **Vib-OS**, developed through AI coding (specifically Claude), has been demonstrated by YouTuber tirimid. Designed from scratch as a Unix-like system supporting Arm64 and x86_64, it includes a GUI and some basic apps like a text editor and a Snake game, but in practice, has many broken or incomplete features. The system struggles with installation issues, lack of internet connectivity, dysfunctional file management, and absence of expected programming language support. Interestingly, function keys change the wallpaper, showing a quirky design choice. While not currently usable as a main OS, Vib-OS serves as an example of early-stage AI software development and highlights the challenges of relying fully on AI-generated code for complex systems.

Arc Raiders Dam Battlegrounds Key Locations Guide

Players of **Arc Raiders** can improve their success on the Dam Battlegrounds map by learning keycard locations and usage. There are six locked doors requiring keycards of varying rarity—uncommon, rare, or epic—or special items like the Power Rod. Players are advised to keep keys in a safe inventory slot to avoid loss on death, and to bring Door Blockers or traps to secure valuable loot rooms after unlocking doors.
