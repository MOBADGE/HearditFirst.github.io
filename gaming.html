**Steam Next Fest Highlights Diverse Game Demos**

The recent Steam Next Fest showcased a wide variety of game demos attracting significant player interest. Leading the charts was the multiplayer shooter *Marathon Server Slam*, notable for its fast-paced combat involving an electric teal spacegun. Following this, *Burglin' Gnomes* secured the second spot, offering cooperative play as gnome burglars who face dangers from homeowners and pets. Third on the list was *Windrose*, an open-world pirate survival game combining base building with naval combat; its demo gathered over 5,400 mostly positive reviews. Other notable demos included *Vampire Crawlers*, a deck-building dungeon crawler inspired by *Vampire Survivors*, and *Far Far West*, a co-op FPS featuring robot cowboys with magical abilities. These demos reflect the variety and creativity showcased during the festival, offering players a chance to experience early versions of diverse games across genres.

**Space Marine 2 Introduces Techmarine Class in Free Update**

Almost 18 months after release, *Warhammer 40,000: Space Marine 2* has added a new playable class called the Techmarine through a free update. This engineer-type character can deploy and maintain an automated sentry turret, blending support abilities with frontline combat capability using a large servo-actuated axe and a mounted gun. The update also includes a new PvE mission featuring factional enemies and new cosmetic options inspired by Warhammer chapters like the Raven Guard and Carcharodons. In PvP mode, players using the Techmarine on the Chaos side will instead control a Warpsmith character. This addition expands gameplay options and caters to fans interested in the Warhammer universe's lore and tactical combat.

**Muri: Wildwoods Demo Delivers Satisfying Cleaning Adventure**

The demo for *Muri: Wildwoods* offers an adventure centered around cleaning corruption from a mysterious island. Players control Pelle, a mouse-like creature who uses a water gun to cleanse both the environment and afflicted wildlife, bringing life back to the world. Unlike other cleaning simulators, the game visually represents progress as flora and fauna return with each cleaned area. The demo presents a gentle challenge of exploration and restoration, accompanied by light narrative elements and interactions with a team that supports Pelle remotely. The game's atmosphere and mechanics provide a calming yet engaging experience, suggesting promise for the full release.

**Phanteks XT V3 Case Review: Compact and Affordable**

The Phanteks XT V3 is a compact Micro-ATX PC case praised for its efficient design and affordability, priced around $66/¬£60. Despite its small size, it supports robust hardware configurations including seven 120mm fans, a 360mm radiator, and large graphics cards up to 430mm. The build experience is straightforward with accessible glass panels and included RGB fans. Storage options are limited to two 2.5-inch drives and one 3.5-inch HDD, reflecting the trade-offs in compact cases. Some materials, such as plastic elements, feel less premium, but these are considered acceptable given the cost. Airflow is effective, aided by well-placed ventilation and fan support. Overall, the XT V3 offers a balance between size, function, and price, appealing to users seeking a small yet capable case.

**Direct3D Update Adds Features Beneficial for Graphics Developers**

Microsoft has officially updated the Direct3D 12 graphics API with Shader Model 6.9 and new features such as opacity micromaps and shader execution reordering (SER). These additions aim to improve ray tracing performance by optimizing how GPUs handle complex scenes, particularly those with translucent objects and varied shader workloads.
