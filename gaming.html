The Blood of Dawnwalker Draws Comparisons to The Witcher 3

Konrad Tomaszkiewicz, director of the upcoming vampire RPG *The Blood of Dawnwalker*, has expressed confidence about the frequent comparisons between his new game and *The Witcher 3*, which he also helped direct. Set in a plague-ridden 14th century Europe, the game features a half-vampire protagonist, Coen, whose character bears similarities to Geralt of Rivia—both are morally complex figures operating in brutal worlds.

Tomaszkiewicz's new studio, Rebel Wolves, aims to build on the open-world, story-driven approach popularized by *The Witcher* series but with new twists. These include a day/night cycle affecting vampire powers and a more selective sidequest system, which introduces time constraints on what players can complete in a single playthrough. Tomaszkiewicz emphasized the importance of growth and innovation, stating the company wants to stay relatively small to maintain efficient development practices. *The Blood of Dawnwalker* is set for release later this year.

Star Trek: Resurgence to Be Delisted After Three Years

*Star Trek: Resurgence*, a narrative adventure inspired by older Telltale Games titles, will soon be removed from sale due to the expiration of its distribution license. The game, which launched in 2023 and was praised for capturing Star Trek storytelling within a decision-driven gameplay framework, remains accessible to current owners but will no longer be available to new buyers.

The exact date of the delisting is unspecified but is likely to coincide with its third anniversary on May 23. The Xbox version has reportedly already been pulled. This move aligns with the relatively short lifespan typical for Star Trek games, as several previous titles have also been delisted after a few years due to licensing complexities.

Hades 2 Receives Detailed Patch with Story and Gameplay Tweaks

Post-launch support for *Hades 2* continues with a new patch on Steam that introduces narrative and gameplay refinements. Notable additions include expanded closure scenes for character sidequests, notably updates to Fated Prophecies narrated by Homer, and the ability to gift characters repeatedly after relationship thresholds are met.

Accessibility improvements bring an additional level of zoom designed for smaller displays. Gameplay balancing focuses on bolstering certain boons, especially those associated with the god Ares. The update also enhances romance options and adds decorative content to the Crossroads area. These incremental changes aim to refine player experience and deepen immersion without altering the core gameplay loop.

Windrose Survival Game Compared Unfavorably to Sea of Thieves

After spending several hours playing *Windrose*, a new pirate-themed survival crafting game, some players and critics find it lacking compared to *Sea of Thieves*. Despite promising pirate activities like sailing and island exploration, *Windrose* focuses heavily on resource gathering and survival mechanics that can feel repetitive and slow-paced.

Notably, *Windrose* does not simulate wind dynamics in sailing and leans more toward survival game conventions than pirate roleplay. In contrast, *Sea of Thieves* offers a more lively and emergent multiplayer pirate experience with player interactions central to its appeal. Technical issues with *Windrose*'s multiplayer connectivity also detract from its enjoyment. While *Windrose* had a strong launch in player numbers, the depth and quality of pirate gameplay remain points of debate.

Warhammer Classics Brings Over 20 Games to Steam, Including Seven New to the Platform

A new initiative called Warhammer Classics has launched on Steam, delivering enhanced versions of more than 20 classic Warhammer and Warhammer 40,000 games. Seven titles are making their Steam debut, such as *Warhammer 40,000: Chaos Gate* (1998) and *Warhammer: Mark of Chaos*. The games have been updated for modern Windows compatibility, widescreen resolutions, and feature Steam achievements.

SNEG and Games Workshop aim to preserve and reintroduce these foundational games to contemporary audiences in a "future-proofed" form, acknowledging that some content may not reflect the current Warhammer canon. The collection spans strategy, RPG, and tactical genres from across both Warhammer universes and is currently featured in a sale with discounts lasting until April 20.
