**World of Warcraft Ends Era of Interface Addons**

Blizzard has officially disabled many user interface (UI) mods in World of Warcraft (WoW) with the release of the Midnight expansion, requiring all addons to update to a new infrastructure. Ion Hazzikostas, WoW's game director, acknowledged that the company had allowed addon reliance to go too far, impacting player experience‚Äîespecially for newcomers in group content such as Mythic+. The change aims to replace popular addon functionalities with official Blizzard systems to level the playing field and simplify gameplay. However, this transition has required extensive redesign of specializations and game mechanics that previously depended on addons. Blizzard continues working to ensure key features, including those affecting accessibility, are supported natively. Hazzikostas noted that while earlier action might have softened the transition, the decision to start now was necessary.

**Epic and Larian Debate Over Free Games and Store Viability**

Epic Games CEO Tim Sweeney reiterated that increased competition between digital storefronts benefits gamers and developers by offering more options and deals. Speaking in response to claims that Epic's frequent free game giveaways boost sales on rival Steam, Sweeney emphasized that both platforms succeed on different transactions. However, Michael Douse, publishing director at Larian Studios, offered a critical perspective. He pointed out that high-profile exclusives like Alan Wake 2 struggled financially during Epic Store exclusivity, implying some developers face challenges under this model. Douse also questioned the sustainability of relying on free giveaways to convert casual players into premium buyers, suggesting that such tactics inflate user numbers without creating a viable platform for selling premium games. The exchange reflects ongoing tension over digital storefront strategies in the PC gaming market.
