Goblin Sushi: A New Cooking Simulator from Old Cake Factory

Goblin Sushi is an upcoming cooking simulation game where players take on the role of a goblin chef serving sushi to other goblins. Developed by indie studio Old Cake Factory, the game centers around preparing dishes from various ingredients like rice, seaweed, and fish, while managing an ongoing line of hungry customers. The gameplay involves quickly combining items and serving them before customers grow impatient, with some quirky elements such as customers who enjoy unconventional dishes like "poo."

The game emphasizes simple but tense mechanics reminiscent of titles like Cook, Serve, Delicious but with mouse controls. It also features a playful and somewhat gross aesthetic with unique goblin characters and scenarios. Goblin Sushi will launch in early access on February 9th, with a demo currently available on Steam.

**Why it matters:** Goblin Sushi may appeal to players looking for a casual yet challenging cooking sim with a novel fantasy twist. Its focus on quick reaction and management could attract fans of the genre, contributing to the diversity of indie cooking games.

Fallout-themed Magic: The Gathering Cards Incorporate TV Characters and New Additions

Magic: The Gathering is continuing its collaboration with the Fallout franchise through a series of new Secret Lair card drops releasing later this month. After an initial Fallout-themed set two years ago, these new cards introduce characters from the Fallout TV show alongside familiar game figures from Fallout: New Vegas. Highlights include cards based on Lucy, Maximus, The Ghoul, Benny, and items like The Platinum Chip and Sunset Sarsaparilla machines.

Additional themed sets feature recurring fan-favorite character Dogmeat in new forms, plus a "Rad" Superdrop focusing on mutant-themed cards. These new releases reuse existing card mechanics but add fresh visual designs and thematic elements aligned with Fallout's lore.

**Why it matters:** This extension of Fallout into Magic: The Gathering keeps both fanbases engaged and broadens crossover appeal. The addition of TV show characters reflects ongoing media expansion for the Fallout universe.

Hytale Receives Dinosaur Update Days After Early Access Launch

Hytale, a Minecraft-style sandbox adventure game, launched in early access recently and received a major update within four days, adding dinosaurs as new wildlife. **Why it matters:** Quick updates and new content like dinosaurs demonstrate developer commitment to improving Hytale and maintaining player interest. This bodes well for its growth as a sandbox survival title.
