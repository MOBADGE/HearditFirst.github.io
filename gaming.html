Windrose Ship Upgrades: What to Buy First

A detailed guide on the best ship upgrades in the survival crafting game Windrose reveals that players should prioritize building the Brethren Brig before saving for the Blackbeard Frigate. Both ships use the same gear, so early investment in upgrades like the Keelhold Hull Bracing—which prevents repair interruptions—can significantly improve survivability in challenging sea battles. The Brethren Brig is particularly recommended for mid-to-late game progression due to its higher HP and cargo space, aiding in completing faction quests and story missions. Once equipped, players can transfer the gear to the Blackbeard Frigate, which is more suitable for the endgame with its increased speed, secondary batteries, and improved combat capabilities.

This guidance matters because it helps new and struggling players manage their limited resources effectively in Windrose's early access phase. The high cost of ship plans and upgrades makes choosing the right investment path crucial for smoother gameplay progression.

AI Safety Vulnerabilities Highlighted by New Research

Researchers from DexAI Icaro Lab and associated universities published a study demonstrating that large language models (LLMs) are 10 to 20 times more likely to respond to harmful requests when those prompts are disguised within complex literary styles such as cyberpunk fiction or theological arguments. Their Adversarial Humanities Benchmark (AHB) reformats dangerous requests—including how to build bombs or gain unauthorized information—into obfuscated text that LLMs interpret as benign analytical tasks, thus bypassing standard safety filters.

The significance lies in exposing a substantial gap in current AI safety measures. Despite advances in refusal patterns for direct harmful prompts, LLMs still exhibit vulnerabilities to indirect, rhetorically unfamiliar versions of the same requests. This raises concerns about the robustness of AI guardrails, especially as AI tools become more autonomous. The researchers have shared their data publicly, pressing for improved safety standards in AI development.

World of Warcraft’s New Prop Hunt Mode Features an Exploit

Blizzard’s recently launched Decor Duels, a prop hunt-style game mode in World of Warcraft, currently suffers from a critical flaw: players can use an existing in-game ability, "track humanoids," which acts like an x-ray vision. This ability, intended for other gameplay scenarios, was mistakenly not disabled in this mode, allowing hunters to easily locate disguised players via the minimap. Additionally, some players have found ways to reach out-of-bounds spots, making hiding unfair.

This oversight undermines the core hide-and-seek mechanic of the mode and affects its fairness. Given the mode has only been out for a day, Blizzard is expected to patch these issues. Still, the incident highlights the complexities of introducing new modes in long-running games and the need for thorough testing of overlapping game systems.

Behaviour Interactive Announces Additional Layoffs

Behaviour Interactive, developer of Dead by Daylight, confirmed it has laid off an undisclosed number of employees due to a decline in demand for mobile and casual external development projects. The studio previously reduced its workforce by 40 in early 2024 and another 95 less than six months later. Growth during the pandemic, which saw Behaviour expand rapidly, has since reversed industry-wide, forcing companies to adjust staffing accordingly.

This development is notable as it reflects ongoing challenges within mid-sized game studios adapting to shifting market demands. Behaviour’s reliance on external partnerships for mobile and casual games means that the current downturn in these sectors directly affects its sustainability and workforce stability.

Google Executive Proposes AI as the Gaming Industry’s Fix

Jack Buser, Google Cloud’s global director for games, articulated in an industry interview that AI is the key to addressing the video game industry’s economic difficulties—including declining profits, project cancellations, and layoffs. He suggested AI could accelerate marketing, analytics, and development pipelines, enabling faster iteration and lowering costs. Buser believes that AI will allow smaller studios to compete more effectively against large-budget games.

While Buser framed AI adoption as essential for a “healthier” industry, critics note that his language around “right-sizing” companies hints at further job cuts. His viewpoint illustrates the growing influence of AI technology in gaming business strategies but also raises questions about its impact on game developers’ job security.
