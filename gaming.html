Classic Space Combat Sim Star Quest Updated for Modern Systems and Released Free

Thirty years after its 1995 debut, *Star Quest 1 in the 27th Century*, a lesser-known space combat simulator from the '90s, has been updated to run on modern Windows PCs and is now available as a free download. Originally developed by Virtual Adventures Inc, the game distinguished itself by allowing seamless flight transitions from space to planetary atmospheres, a rare feature for its time. The updated "30th Anniversary Edition," prepared by the original developer Paul Lauzon, also adds support for modern input devices including HOTAS joysticks, game controllers, and refreshed keyboard and mouse controls. Though it never achieved the fame of contemporaries like *Star Wars: X-Wing* or *Freespace 2*, its re-release offers a glimpse into early space sim design, featuring over 60 spacecraft, 3D mapped stars, Gouraud-shaded planets, and multiple mission types ranging from dogfights to tactical squad engagements. The update preserves the original game's ambiance but makes it more accessible for current hardware and players interested in gaming history.

Barony Dungeon Crawler Receives Major Two-Part Update After Over a Decade

*Barony*, a cooperative roguelike dungeon crawler inspired by *Ultima Underworld*, has been steadily growing in popularity since its 2015 launch. Developer Turning Wheel now delivers one of the game's largest updates to date with "Instruments of Destruction," rolled out in two phases. The first part, available now, revamps the magic system, consolidating the previous disparate skills into three new comprehensive skill trees‚ÄîSorcery, Thaumaturgy, and Mysticism‚Äîeach governing different magical abilities. This update also introduces improvements to dungeon generation, including loot-rich treasure rooms and expanded biomes like crystal caves and mystic citadels with enhanced level variety and complexity. A second update will follow, promising broader level design changes and additional mechanical tweaks. These changes aim to refresh gameplay and engage returning players while supporting the game's ongoing player base.

On-Together Offers a Virtual Co-Working Space With Social and Productivity Features

The new app *On-Together* combines gaming elements with productivity tools to create a virtual co-working environment designed to address the challenges of working or studying from home alone. The program provides users with a shared virtual space where participants can see each other's avatars, communicate via chat, and display focus tasks through customizable animations, such as cleaning or writing. It features standard productivity aids like pomodoro timers, task lists, and journals, but emphasizes the social aspect by encouraging users to work "alongside" friends or strangers in a low-pressure setting. Break periods can be spent exploring mini-games and activities in the virtual world. The platform's design aims to replicate the benefits of collaborative presence and social accountability observed in multiplayer games and streamed study sessions, potentially helping users maintain focus and reduce isolation. *On-Together* is currently available on Steam.

Cities: Skylines 2 Updates Planned by New Developer Iceflake Studios

Following the departure of original developer Colossal Order, *Cities: Skylines 2* development has been taken over by Iceflake Studios, known for *Surviving the Aftermath*. The studio shared its initial plans via a new developer diary called "City Corner," detailing an upcoming patch focused mainly on user interface and visual improvements. Key changes include a new asset recoloring system, inspired by a popular mod, allowing players to customize building, vehicle, and prop colors directly within the game. Additionally, the interface will be streamlined, with redesigned toolbars and icons to improve usability and reduce confusion, particularly for new players. Visual improvements to lighting, skyboxes, and weather effects (such as snow accumulation) are also slated to enhance the game's atmosphere. Iceflake plans further updates addressing gameplay mechanics and bug fixes. This transition is notable as it represents a significant shift in stewardship for a franchise that has been a cornerstone of city-building games for over a decade.
