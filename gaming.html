New Quiz Challenges Players to Recall Video Game Movie Adaptations

A recent quiz published by PC Gamer invites readers to name every English-language theatrical release adaptation of video games. The quiz challenges participants to recall widely released films such as the Resident Evil series while excluding movies that are not direct adaptations, like documentaries or films about gaming culture. The quiz is timed, giving players eight minutes to list as many titles as possible, with clues including release year, budget, box office earnings, and critic scores. This quiz highlights the mixed history of video game movies, many of which have received poor critical reception despite some commercial success. It serves as a reflection on the ongoing challenge of translating video games into films that resonate with audiences.

Ambitious Mod Converts Duke Nukem: Zero Hour Into a First-Person Shooter on PC

A dedicated modding team has released Duke Nukem: Zero Hour Overclocked, a mod pack that transforms the original third-person Nintendo 64 game into a "janky '90s style" first-person shooter for PC. This collection of modular tweaks allows players to enable new control schemes, including classic FPS mouselook, alternative firing modes, and revised jumping mechanics. It also adapts movement and aiming to suit a first-person perspective, adding elements like viewmodel recoil and crouch toggling. The mod has drawn positive attention, including praise from 3D Realms founder Scott Miller. While Zero Hour received mixed reviews on initial release, this mod aims to reintroduce the game to modern players with a fresh gameplay experience. To use Overclocked, players need a copy of the game's N64 ROM.

Razer Viper V4 Pro Review: Emphasis on Build Quality Over Novel Features

The Razer Viper V4 Pro gaming mouse has recently been reviewed as a product that prioritizes solid build quality over revolutionary technologies. While other competitive mice like the ultralight Corsair Sabre V2 Pro or the analog-switch-equipped Logitech G Pro X2 Superstrike offer standout features such as featherweight design or adjustable click latency, the Viper V4 Pro focuses on refinement. It balances decent weight (around 49-50 grams), responsive optical switches, and a high-performance sensor with a durable, well-crafted shell. The mouse also benefits from Razer’s web-based Synapse software for customization. Though it lacks headline-grabbing specs, the Viper V4 Pro is praised for its overall polish, ergonomics, and consistent performance, making it a strong contender for competitive gamers who value reliability and comfort.

Data Miner Uncovers Possible Protagonist Model from Cancelled Rockstar Game Agent

Data mining efforts on the leaked Grand Theft Auto 5 source code have revealed character models that may originate from Rockstar's cancelled spy game Agent. A notable discovery involves a model labeled "player" in a folder named 'Jimmy,' the latter being Agent’s codename. This character model is dated June 2009 and was created at Rockstar North, with similarities to Grand Theft Auto 4’s protagonist Niko Bellic in its map layout. Online sleuths have linked this model to Agent’s protagonist based on these factors and images found in a former Rockstar artist’s portfolio. This finding adds a new layer of understanding to Rockstar's unrealized projects and provides rare insight into a game effort that never progressed beyond concept stages before its quiet cancellation.

City Masterplan Poised to Offer Competition to Cities: Skylines

A new city-builder called City Masterplan, developed by 1:1 Studio and published by Paradox Interactive, promises a realistic, large-scale simulation experience in the vein of Cities: Skylines. The game offers a 24x24 km environment with freeform road-building tools and detailed zoning capabilities, aiming to blend realism with user-friendly controls. The visual style shows high attention to environmental details like weather and lighting, and the game will include a substantial architectural library with procedural content generation. While no release date is set, expectations are that it will arrive in 2027 or later. Given that Cities: Skylines is now over a decade old and its sequel has received mixed reviews, City Masterplan could attract players looking for a fresh approach to the modern city-builder genre.
