**Hytale Introduces Memories System for Creature Progression**

Hytale's early access version features a progression mechanic called Memories, essentially a bestiary that tracks creatures players encounter throughout exploration. However, the Memories system is initially locked and requires players to locate the Forgotten Temple near their spawn point. Inside the temple, players defeat or bypass an earthen golem to access a portal leading to the Edge of the Echo, a tranquil area where they can unlock Memories via an interactable totem.

Once unlocked, players gradually discover creature entries by coming near new mobs; these discoveries trigger notifications but are not retroactive. Currently, there are 240 entries to collect. Players must return periodically to the Forgotten Temple to record recent discoveries officially. Reaching milestone numbers of Memories restored‚Äî10, 25, 50, 100, and 200‚Äîgrants rewards like decorative chests, seeds, teleporters, morph potions, and backpack upgrades, providing incentives to engage with the exploration system.

**Expanding Inventory in Hytale with Backpack Upgrades**

Inventory management is an early challenge in Hytale, especially for players who collect numerous items. The backpack upgrade system increases inventory capacity and can be crafted at the Workbench's tinkering section. The initial upgrade requires eight iron ingots and 16 medium leather pieces. Subsequently, a larger upgrade (Backpack Upgrade 1) needs cindercloth scraps, heavy leather, and cobalt ingots once Workbench Tier 3 is reached. A further upgrade unlocks after restoring 100 Memories.

Notably, crafting the backpack does not grant immediate inventory expansion; it produces a consumable item that players must activate manually. Once used, a backpack icon appears next to the character in the inventory, opening a secondary, smaller stash area with nine slots. The backpack offers additional storage space but is slightly slower to access, so it's best used for less frequently needed items.

**Obtaining Types of Leather in Hytale**

Leather is a versatile crafting resource in Hytale, available in three types: light, medium, and heavy. Each requires hunting specific animals to collect raw hide, which then must be processed at a Tanning Rack to convert into usable leather. Light leather comes from small animals such as boars, cows, and sheep; medium leather drops from predators like wolves and hyenas; heavy leather is sourced from large or armored creatures including bears and mosshorns.

Crafting a Tanning Rack requires wood, stones, and light hides. Processing hide takes roughly 30 seconds, and the basic rack holds two types of hide or leather at a time. Besides hunting, players might find leather types in chests within ruined structures scattered around the game.

**UK Police Apologize Over AI-Generated False Intelligence Report**

West Midlands Police in the UK have issued an apology to parliament after admitting a controversial policy decision banning Israeli football fans relied on an AI-generated report containing false information. The erroneous intelligence included a fabricated match between Maccabi Tel Aviv and West Ham that never occurred, which influenced the decision to classify a game as high-risk and ban fans.

Initially denying AI usage, police acknowledged that Microsoft Copilot contributed to assembling the flawed report. This revelation has caused significant concern in government circles, raising questions about the competence and reliability of AI in sensitive decision-making. Calls have been made for the police chief to resign, and for an independent investigation to oversee the force's use of AI-generated intelligence.
