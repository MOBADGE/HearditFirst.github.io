Zero Parades Shows Promising Moments Despite High Expectations

ZA/UM, the developer behind the acclaimed Disco Elysium, has released a demo of its follow-up, Zero Parades. The game takes on a spy thriller theme that contrasts with the original’s amnesiac detective story but maintains the verbose narrative style fans expect. A playthrough at the Game Developers Conference highlighted moments where the writing combined unexpected humor, sincere character development, and dramatic skill checks. For example, interactions with an intercom system and attempts to crack a locked door blend comedy with meaningful player choices, evoking Disco Elysium’s tendency to make failure engaging rather than punitive. While the tone feels lighter and more sarcastic than its predecessor’s solemnity, these scenes raise hope that Zero Parades can deliver unique brilliance while stepping away from direct comparisons.

Society Simulation MMO Seed Develops Persistent Character and Player-Driven Worlds

Klang Games, led by former EVE Online developers, presented new updates on Seed, an MMO focused on society simulation. Unlike most games where only the world persists, Seed’s key feature is that player characters—called Seedlings—continue acting and evolving even when their owners are offline. The game encourages players to collaborate within societies that manage laws, economies, and resources. Examples from closed beta include player governments responding to an in-game bread market dispute. Klang aims to blend deep simulation elements inspired by RimWorld and Dwarf Fortress with more accessible features appealing to players who enjoy nurturing individual characters, such as home decoration. Seed is still in closed beta with plans to invite more players but no firm launch date yet.

Shadowstone Launches Public Playtest Ahead of Early Access

Shadowstone, developed by Secret Door and Dreamhaven, began a surprise public playtest accessible through Steam. The tactics roguelike is set in the same universe as Secret Door’s tabletop RPG Sunderfolk but takes a more streamlined, mouse-and-keyboard approach rather than smartphone controls. Players can try solo or up to four-player matches that focus on escaping randomized ruins with evolving skill cards and equipment. Progress made during the playtest will not carry over to early access, which is planned for sometime in 2026 but without a specific date. Testers will be selected based on various factors including signup timing. The open nature of this testing phase allows streaming and sharing of gameplay experiences.

Rob Pardo Urges Industry to Value Development Teams Over Layoffs

At this year’s Game Developers Conference, veteran game designer Rob Pardo delivered a keynote urging industry executives to reconsider frequent layoffs. Drawing on his experience launching landmark games like Warcraft 3 and Starcraft: Brood War, Pardo emphasized that the teams behind successful games are invaluable assets. He noted that game development involves unpredictable challenges and learning experiences that only an established team can navigate well. Contrary to recent trends of firing developers shortly after successful releases, he argued that nurturing and retaining these teams offers better chances to sustain success. Pardo’s message comes at a time when some studios have laid off staff despite strong game sales.

Path of Exile’s Mirage League Adjusts Economy and Gameplay

Grinding Gear Games released the Mirage league update for Path of Exile, introducing significant changes to currency item drop rates and an innovative new "Mirage" mechanic. Currency items now account for a much larger portion of loot, allowing players more freedom to craft without heavy reliance on trading. The Mirage mechanic lets players revisit portions of levels with selectable bonuses, effectively doubling rewards from league events or map runs. Additionally, the league features an overhauled atlas map system replacing specific map drops with tiered tokens and map modifiers called astrolabes. These changes aim to improve endgame progression and player agency. The update also includes a new ascendancy class and various challenge rewards.

Computing Pioneer Tony Hoare Passes Away at 92

Charles Anthony Richard Hoare, a seminal figure in computer science, died at age 92. He is best known for inventing the Quicksort algorithm, reportedly created after a bet in 1959, which remains a fundamental sorting method. Hoare also contributed to formal software verification through Hoare logic and co-designed ALGOL W, influencing programming languages like Pascal. His work on Communicating Sequential Processes has had lasting effects on concurrent programming. Hoare was recognized with numerous awards including the Turing Award and was knighted for his contributions.
