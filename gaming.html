Star Wars Zero Company: Realtime-With-Pause Combat Still Relevant

Lead designer James Brawley of the upcoming turn-based tactics game *Star Wars Zero Company* emphasized that realtime-with-pause (RTWP) combat is not obsolete. Despite a current resurgence in turn-based games, Brawley believes RTWP will see innovative renewals in time. He explained that turn-based tactics have become more accessible due to advancements in pacing, camera work, and immersive presentation, citing Firaxis' *XCOM* series as a notable example of revitalizing traditionally slower genres. Brawley highlighted that older games of similar styles required patience and were often slower, limiting their audience. His comments suggest the tactical game space remains diverse in combat styles, and RTWP could regain popularity with fresh design approaches.

RPG Maker to Adopt 2.5D Visual Style Inspired by Octopath Traveler

RPG Maker, long known for its characteristic 16-bit style, is preparing its first major visual overhaul by integrating a 2.5D aesthetic reminiscent of *Octopath Traveler* and other modern Square Enix RPGs. This update, teased by Gotcha Gotcha Games, introduces models operating on an invisible grid with enhanced shadowing and lighting effects, differing slightly from the freer movement in *Octopath Traveler*. While release details remain unconfirmed, this change marks a significant evolution for RPG Maker and could broaden the appeal and creative possibilities available to its users by moving beyond the engine’s traditional look.

Dimension 20 Partners with White Wolf for Official Vampire: The Masquerade Series

The actual-play show Dimension 20 will collaborate with White Wolf to produce *City Council of Darkness*, an official *Vampire: The Masquerade*-based campaign. This marks Dimension 20’s first season using an official third-party tabletop RPG ruleset and setting, moving beyond their usual *Dungeons & Dragons* play. The story follows outcast vampires sent to oversee the small town of Purpee, Oregon, focusing on the challenges of undead life outside the typical urban environments. The series, starting April 8 on Dropout, features Dimension 20’s cast and highlights an intriguing exploration of vampire lore in a rural setting.

World of Warcraft Pro’s Subscription Lapse Causes Mid-Fight Disconnect During World First Race

During a broadcast of a world-first raid attempt in *World of Warcraft*’s *Midnight* expansion, a professional player disconnected mid-fight due to an expired game subscription. The player had purchased a WoW Token for renewal but forgot to activate it, leading to an unexpected logout captured on Twitch. This incident, while embarrassing, is reportedly not uncommon among top-level guilds who manage multiple accounts during competitive races. The team quickly resumed the fight, downplaying the disruption. This situation underscores the complexities and challenges professional players face beyond gameplay, such as account maintenance in high-pressure environments.

Former Valve Writer Criticizes Epic Games’ Mass Layoffs Amid Profits

Chet Faliszek, former Valve writer known for major titles such as *Half-Life 2* and *Portal*, publicly criticized Epic Games and CEO Tim Sweeney following the company’s layoffs of over 1,000 employees despite ongoing profitability. Faliszek questioned morale and agency for remaining staff when layoffs occur without financial necessity. He contrasted this with Valve’s culture during his tenure, where employees felt a strong sense of ownership and pride in their work. Faliszek’s remarks reflect broader industry concerns about studio stability, creative commitment, and the impact of corporate decisions on developer motivation. Epic has acknowledged the layoffs but did not respond directly to his specific comments.

Neuralink Patient Successfully Controls World of Warcraft via Brain Implant After 100 Days

In a notable advancement of brain-computer interface technology, a Neuralink participant with paralysis has demonstrated effective control of *World of Warcraft* using only thought inputs, 100 days after receiving the implant. British Army veteran Jon Noble described minimal surgical recovery and rapid adaptation to mind-controlled computing, including complex in-game actions such as raiding and combat. This progress illustrates Neuralink’s potential not just for gaming, but for improving autonomy and interaction with digital devices among users with severe disabilities. While still in trial phases, developments like this represent important steps toward assistive technology integration.
