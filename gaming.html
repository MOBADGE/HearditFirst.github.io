Yoshiki Okamoto Invests Heavily in Gacha Games to Understand High-Spending Players

Yoshiki Okamoto, known for producing classics like Street Fighter 2 and Final Fight, has transitioned to the mobile gacha game market. After significant financial troubles following the collapse of his studio's US publisher, Okamoto rebuilt his career producing successful gacha titles like Monster Strike, now boasting over 65 million players. Interestingly, he spends roughly $515,000 of his own money on each personal game account. Okamoto explains this substantial expense is to genuinely understand the experience and feelings of high-spending users, ensuring that the top spenders in his games remain satisfied. His approach underlines the continuing importance of empathetic insight in managing monetization in free-to-play games.
