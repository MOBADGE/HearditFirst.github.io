Highlights From the Weekend: Handheld Gaming and Hidden Gems

A writer spent their weekend immersed in two recently released games—**Slay the Spire 2** and **Esoteric Ebb**—on the handheld gaming PC ROG Ally. Both titles captured their attention for distraction-free play on a portable device, with Slay the Spire 2 offering an accessible and engaging deckbuilder experience and Esoteric Ebb showcasing a rich narrative RPG with a distinctive art style that translated well to the small screen. The combination of these genres and the handheld form factor provided a refreshing break, underscoring the growing appeal of powerful, portable PC gaming.

Legal Challenges Mount Against Valve Over Loot Boxes

Valve is now facing a second class-action lawsuit related to loot boxes in games such as **Counter-Strike 2**, **Team Fortress 2**, and **Dota 2**. This lawsuit echoes an earlier complaint from New York state accusing Valve of facilitating illegal gambling, especially affecting minors. The core allegation is that Valve’s loot boxes function much like slot machines, using psychological tactics to encourage spending. Legal scrutiny centres on whether these digital loot items, which can be sold or traded for real-world money, constitute gambling under certain state laws. Valve has recently begun adding features like "X-ray scanners" to comply with specific regulations, but court outcomes remain to be seen.

Nagoshi Studio Loses NetEase Funding

After just three months following its debut trailer at the 2025 Game Awards, Nagoshi Studio, led by Yakuza creator Toshihiro Nagoshi, has announced that its major funder, NetEase, will stop financing the studio as of May. The project, **Gang of Dragon**, remains largely mysterious aside from a brief cinematic and vague Steam page description. Bloomberg reports that NetEase’s decision was driven by the need for an additional $44 million to complete the game and difficulty in securing alternate investors. While the studio may continue development independently, it faces significant financial uncertainty.

Bungie’s Marathon Includes Many Callbacks to Halo

Bungie’s new extraction shooter **Marathon** subtly and overtly references the studio’s Halo series, especially in its weapon design. Several guns resemble iconic Halo firearms such as the CE Tactical Sidearm (Halo pistol), Magnum MC (Halo Magnum), and the BR33 Volley Rifle (Battle Rifle). Some weapons include attachments that further cement these ties, like turning the Volt Thrower into a weapon reminiscent of Halo’s Needler. The game also retains traditional Bungie mechanics, such as weighty movement and grenade physics familiar to Halo players.

Diablo 4’s Expansion Offers New Difficulty and Meaning to Damage

Blizzard plans to address one criticism of **Diablo 4**—that gear upgrades often feel meaningless due to quick monster kills—by expanding the game's Torment difficulty tiers from four to twelve in the upcoming **Lord of Hatred** expansion. This change aims to provide more granular and challenging difficulty levels that better reward player progression and encourage use of diverse builds. The highest tiers are intended to be very challenging and optional, preserving player choice in difficulty. Additionally, the expansion will introduce a loot filter and a crafting system to improve item management amid increased loot drops.

Steam’s 2025 Data Shows Massive Download Volumes and User Growth

Valve released its 2025 Steam Year in Review revealing continued user growth, hitting a peak of 42 million concurrent users, up from 25 million five years prior. Steam delivered around 100 exabytes of data in 2025—up from 80 exabytes in 2024—with an average daily install and update volume of 274 petabytes. These figures highlight the vast scale of digital distribution on the platform and Valve’s role as a major force in PC gaming.

Free and Discounted Daedalic Point-and-Click Adventures on Steam

As part of a Steam publisher sale, the first **Deponia** game is currently free until March 16, offering players a chance to try a well-regarded point-and-click adventure. Other classic Daedalic titles like **Night of the Rabbit**, **Edna & Harvey: Harvey’s New Eyes**, and **The Dark Eye: Memoria** are deeply discounted, making it a good opportunity for fans of narrative-driven adventure games to explore these older titles at reduced prices.
