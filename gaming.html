Bungie Retains Key Halo Developers Amid Studio Identity Debate

Recently, Elliott Gray, a UI designer known for his work on Bungie's Marathon series, responded to claims that Bungie no longer retained many of the original Halo developers. Gray listed approximately 40 former and current Bungie employees who worked on Halo and remain with the studio. This counters the narrative that Bungie has lost its foundational talent and questions about the authenticity of the studio calling itself the "creator of Halo." Former Destiny VP Mark Noseworthy supported Gray’s statement by emphasizing the cultural and technological legacies passed down within the studio. This discussion highlights ongoing tensions around studio branding and legacy, relevant as Bungie continues developing new projects under the Halo banner.

Intel Discloses Multiple UEFI Vulnerabilities, Recommends Patching

Intel has published a security advisory detailing several vulnerabilities found in the Unified Extensible Firmware Interface (UEFI) component across multiple products. The most severe vulnerabilities, rated with a CVSS score of 8.7, could potentially allow local code execution through improper input validation. However, these issues require local access to the machine and do not involve remote exploitation or user interaction. Intel is rolling out software updates to mitigate these risks, with motherboard manufacturers expected to distribute the fixes over time. While the vulnerabilities are serious in theory, the necessity of local access means that for most users, the risk is limited pending patch deployment. Still, this disclosure serves as a reminder of maintaining regular system updates.

Blizzard Faces Criticism Over World of Warcraft Abundance Event Balancing

Blizzard's recent balance changes to the Abundance world event in World of Warcraft: Midnight have drawn mixed reactions from players. The event involves collecting a crafting currency called Unalloyed Abundance through repeated runs of a cave area. Players optimized this by forming large raid groups, which led to lag and forced Blizzard to curtail how the currency could be shared among group members. This reduction makes it harder to reach weekly currency caps, frustrating players who rely on professions for progression. Early access participants gained a head start, raising concerns about fairness. The situation illustrates the challenge of designing events that maintain fun while supporting vital in-game economies and progression systems, especially for dedicated grinders and crafters.

Windows 11 Bug Locks Samsung PC Users Out of Their Boot Drives

An issue affecting Samsung devices running Windows 11 versions 24H2 and 25H2 has been causing users to lose access to their primary C: drives, with an "Access denied" error appearing when attempting to open files or run applications. The problem is linked to the Samsung Galaxy Connect app, which Microsoft and Samsung have temporarily removed or rolled back to older versions while investigating fixes. Impacted users face additional difficulties such as inability to elevate privileges or uninstall updates. Recovery options remain limited at this time. Although affecting only a subset of Windows 11 users, this bug highlights the complexities of OEM software interactions with operating systems and underscores risks inherent to pre-installed apps interfering with core system functions.

FBI Seeks Information From Victims of Malware-Infected Steam Games

The FBI has called on gamers who may have been victims of malware distributed through Steam titles to come forward. The agency identified several games involved in the scams, including BlockBlasters, Chemia, Dashverse, Lampy, Lunara, PirateFi, and Tokenova, active mainly between May 2024 and January 2026. These games contained malicious code used to steal personal information and cryptocurrency from players. The FBI is collecting data via email and online forms covering details such as affected accounts, losses, and timelines. The Steam-based malware incidents reflect ongoing challenges in protecting users from threats even on official digital storefronts, emphasizing the importance of vigilance when downloading and interacting with lesser-known games.

Capcom Reports Fastest Sales Milestone for Resident Evil Requiem

Capcom announced that Resident Evil Requiem has surpassed six million units sold worldwide, marking the fastest sales milestone in the series' history. The game has seen significant player engagement, with a peak concurrent player count over 340,000 on Steam and "Overwhelmingly positive" user reviews. Capcom plans ongoing support including additional content such as a photo mode and new minigames expected later in 2026.
