Microsoft Excel’s 1900 Leap Year Bug Remains Unfixed

Microsoft has confirmed that its Excel spreadsheet software continues to treat the year 1900 as a leap year, despite the fact that it was not one. This issue originates from Lotus 1-2-3, an early spreadsheet program that included the bug—either as a simplification or error—which Excel adopted for compatibility. Fixing this bug now would cause major disruptions: all existing dates before March 1, 1900, would shift by one day, some date functions would return different values, and breaking compatibility with other programs relying on the same system. Given Excel's vast usage—estimated between 750 million and a billion users—Microsoft has decided the drawbacks of fixing this historic inaccuracy outweigh the benefits. The problem mainly affects rare uses of dates before 1900.

GTA 6’s User-Generated Content Likely to Create New Income Opportunities

YouTuber HipHopGamer suggested that Grand Theft Auto 6 will not only be a commercial success for Rockstar and Take-Two but also generate significant wealth for users creating content within the game. Based on Rockstar’s recent acquisition of the FiveM modding team and plans to relaunch modding platforms with paid marketplaces, GTA 6 is expected to feature a robust user-generated content ecosystem. This could enable players and creators to monetize their mods, maps, and game modes, potentially producing millionaires. The game launches for consoles on November 6, 2026, although the PC release date has yet to be announced.

Songs of Glimmerwick Demo Provides a Fresh Take on Wizard School Life Sims

Eastshade Studios released a playable demo of Songs of Glimmerwick, a life simulation game set at a magical university. The game emphasizes ethical storytelling and inclusive magic, portraying a society recovering from a repressive regime that banned music and magic. Players engage in gardening, potion-making, and learning music-based spells. The demo encompasses the first several in-game days, highlighting daily routines and social events with a distinct voice-acted and story-driven approach. Though some mechanics feel slightly unpolished, the game stands out for its narrative depth and hopeful tone. A full release is planned for later this year, with demo progress carrying over.

Invincible VS Director Encourages Players to Try Fighting Games for Fun

Dave Hall, the director of the upcoming fighting game Invincible VS, encourages players to try a broad range of fighting games. He notes that, beyond competitive challenges, fighting games provide straightforward enjoyment through their core mechanics. Invincible VS features tag team battles inspired by the superhero series and aims to balance complexity with approachability. The development team actively incorporates community feedback from testing phases. The game’s single-player mode, reportedly involving writers from the series, remains in development. Invincible VS is part of a wave of new fighting games expected in 2026.

Overwatch’s New Hero Sierra May Face Ban Due to Auto-Aim Ability

Blizzard revealed a new Overwatch hero, Sierra, who employs a distinctive ability that attaches a pink dart to enemies, causing her shots to curve toward them. This auto-aim or homing mechanic is unusual in Overwatch and could lead to controversy or frequent bans in competitive play, similar to the previously contentious hero Jetpack Cat. Besides this ability, Sierra can grapple using her drone and deploy an area-of-effect ultimate. She also provides enemy reveal effects. Sierra will join Overwatch’s roster on April 14, 2026, without an early-access weekend before her release.

Extraction Shooter The Cube, Save Us to Shut Down After Three Weeks

XLGames’ free-to-play extraction shooter The Cube, Save Us, has announced it will cease operations on May 8, just three weeks after its March 18 launch. Despite an initial peak of over 5,000 concurrent players on Steam, numbers quickly fell, accompanied by a largely negative player reception. The developer expressed gratitude to players but cited insufficient ongoing support as the reason for closure. Refunds will be offered to those who spent money in the game. The shutdown highlights the challenges even established developers face in the crowded extraction shooter market.
