James Bond Rights Hold Up James Pond Trademark in UK

The owners of the James Bond trademark have opposed a UK trademark application for the classic 1990s video game character James Pond. Gameware and System 3, who co-own James Pond's rights, filed the trademark covering categories like computer games and clothing. However, Danjaq LLC, which holds James Bond intellectual property, filed the opposition. This is not their first challenge—Danjaq previously blocked a similar European trademark attempt in 2012. UK trademark law does not protect parodies as US law does, making System 3’s attempt seem unlikely to succeed. Meanwhile, System 3 plans to release a new James Pond sequel titled *James Pond and the Rogue AI*, though its original creator has criticized the approach, especially the use of AI in marketing.

Infocom’s Failed Business Software Now Runs on Modern PCs

Infocom, known for seminal text adventures like *Zork*, once tried branching into business software with the 1985 database program Cornerstone. Unlike its games, Cornerstone ran on a custom virtual machine which made it slow and poorly suited for the IBM-compatible PCs dominating the market. This business misstep contributed to Infocom’s decline and eventual acquisition by Activision. Currently, a developer named TaradinoC has created Linchpin, an interpreter to run Cornerstone on modern machines. This effort has revived interest in Infocom’s lesser-known software side and ties in with ongoing preservation of their games, such as the 2025 open-sourcing of *Zork*. Though unsuccessful at the time, these projects offer a way for enthusiasts to revisit Infocom’s full catalog.

Nexon Restructures Neople as Development Winds Down on *The First Berserker: Khazan*

Nexon has confirmed team reorganization at Neople, the studio behind *The First Berserker: Khazan*. Reportedly prompted by disappointing sales despite strong early reception, Nexon clarified the move is a strategic realignment rather than layoffs. The game, a solo single-player prequel to *Dungeon & Fighter*, is entering its final development stages. Resources are being shifted to other projects related to the *Dungeon & Fighter* franchise for global markets. While this suggests limited prospects for additional content or expansions for *Khazan*, it reflects Nexon’s intent to support live service games and future initiatives with retained talent.

*Darkstone* Action RPG Remastered by Original Creator

*Darkstone*, a lesser-known action RPG from 1999 notable for features like managing hunger and aging characters, is receiving a remaster called *Darkstone Restoration*. The project is led by original creator Paul Cuisset and aims to fix stability issues, improve controls, and enhance compatibility for current systems. The remaster may also restore multiplayer and introduce a new quest editor, although these are tentative pending early access feedback. Set for release on Steam without a firm date, the early access version promises a playable experience rather than an unfinished prototype. This update revives interest in a niche title that historically struggled with performance on modern hardware.

Google Removes *Doki Doki Literature Club* from Android over Content Concerns

Google has removed the visual novel *Doki Doki Literature Club* from the Play Store citing its "depiction of sensitive themes," primarily related to suicide and mental health. The game, originally released in 2017 and later ported to various platforms, subverts the typical high school visual novel format with psychological horror elements addressing depression and suicide. Its creators and publisher highlighted the game’s positive impact on mental health awareness and expressed efforts to restore availability on Google Play or alternative Android outlets. Google's policy bars apps promoting self-harm or suicide, though *Doki Doki Literature Club* does not promote these but rather explores them in a narrative context. The removal raises ongoing debate about content moderation in games addressing delicate subjects.

*Dune: Awakening* Makes PvP Fully Optional

Developer Funcom announced major changes to *Dune: Awakening*’s PvP system by disabling PvP zones in the Hagga Basin and providing separate PvP and PvE instances for endgame players. Previously, PvP was compulsory in the Deep Desert area for high-level progression, which garnered persistent complaints from players preferring PvE.
