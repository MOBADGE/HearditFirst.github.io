Blizzard Hiring for Unreal Engine Open-World Shooter

Blizzard has posted a job listing seeking a "Lead Designer, Innovation" to work on an unnamed AAA open-world shooter built using Unreal Engine. This represents a departure from Blizzard's usual use of in-house engines. The position demands extensive experience, suggesting Blizzard is aiming to hire a seasoned designer, potentially a former veteran. Industry speculation links this project to a rumored Starcraft third-person shooter, a concept previously reported but not formally announced. Blizzard will hold a BlizzCon event in September, which could be the platform for revealing this game. However, considering Blizzard’s recent cancellation of other projects and ongoing industry layoffs, it's uncertain whether this title will reach release. If it does, it would be the first new Starcraft game in over a decade, marking a notable shift toward new genres for the franchise.

Valve Launches Steam Storefront Refresh in Beta

Valve is testing a refreshed Steam store homepage for users in the beta client, aimed at improving discoverability and overall layout. Key changes include more detailed information in the “Featured and Recommended” section, larger artwork in Discounts and Events, and new wishlist and DLC sections with infinite scroll. The update also uses higher-resolution game art and adjusts to wider displays for improved responsiveness. Although subtle, the redesign represents a refined and more cohesive store experience. Valve has not announced when the update will be rolled out to all users. Interested users can access the beta by opting into Steam’s client beta program.

Diablo 4’s April Fowl’s Day Event Introduces Giant Chicken Boss

For April Fools' Day, Diablo 4 has added an in-game event featuring a giant chicken world boss named Chik’han, The Fowl Beast. Unusually for such events, this boss drops entirely unique, themed loot like the "Cluckonomicon" staff and the "Eggcecutioner" scythe, which apply unconventional effects such as causing enemies to drop eggs. The achievement for defeating the boss implies the event may recur annually. The event deviates from typical Diablo 4 events that focus on XP boosts and crafting materials, offering instead a humorous and unusual challenge. The addition has been received positively as a rare creative April Fools' feature in recent years.

USPTO Issues Nonfinal Rejection of Nintendo’s Pokémon Battling Patent

The United States Patent and Trademark Office recently issued a nonfinal rejection of all claims within Nintendo’s controversial US patent 12,403,397, which described a system for battling with summoned characters. The patent had earlier been criticized as overly broad and questionable in scope. The USPTO found the patent’s claims to be obvious in light of prior gaming patents and existing mechanics, including earlier patent applications by Nintendo and other companies. This rejection marks the start of a reexamination process, with Nintendo having the opportunity to respond and appeal. This ruling currently applies only within the US and does not affect Nintendo’s ongoing patent litigation in Japan.

Garry’s Mod Successor s&box Set for April 28 Release on Steam

Facepunch Studios has quietly updated its Steam page to confirm that s&box, the successor to Garry's Mod, will launch on Steam on April 28. The announcement follows news that Facepunch signed a licensing agreement with Valve allowing s&box creators to export and sell standalone games on Steam without revenue sharing. The technical arrangement is still being finalized, but the launch date signals that s&box is nearing release. The game’s price has not been confirmed but is expected to be in the $10-$20 range. Facepunch CEO Garry Newman emphasized that the studio aims to support creators broadly rather than focus on direct revenue from game sales.

Shinji Mikami’s New Studio Unbound Acquired by Shift Up

Industry veteran Shinji Mikami, known for pioneering survival horror games, has had his new studio Unbound acquired by Korean publisher Shift Up. The move brings Mikami and a team of about 50 staff under Shift Up's umbrella, with promises of creatively focused development. A recent video teasing Unbound’s first project includes gothic, Egyptian, and horror motifs, suggesting a possible return to Mikami’s signature horror roots. Mikami emphasized the collaborative studio atmosphere and his intention to be fully involved in a larger-scale game development. The acquisition aligns with Shift Up’s intentions to strengthen global development competitiveness through this partnership.
