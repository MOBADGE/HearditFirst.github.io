Banquet for Fools Exits Early Access with Unique CRPG Features

Banquet for Fools, a claymation-styled CRPG developed by a two-person team, officially launched its 1.0 version this week following a major final update. The game combines narrative depth reminiscent of Planescape: Torment with roleplaying elements from Disco Elysium and Dungeons & Dragons. Players investigate mysterious disappearances on an island recently colonized by the Vollings, using exploration and dialogue without quest markers to progress. Combat merges real-time pause with side-scrolling beat ’em up mechanics, allowing party switching and combo moves. A notable gameplay mechanic permits players to stun defeated enemies for imprisonment, rewarding gold but carrying risks if misused. This update expands maps, characters, quests, and character creation options and is available at a launch discount. This release highlights ongoing innovation in the CRPG genre through unusual aesthetics and gameplay systems.

Human Fall Flat Adds Viking-Themed Map with Rare Crossover

Human Fall Flat has introduced its latest free map featuring a Viking theme. Players can navigate rugged Scandinavian settings, pilot a longship, and breach a Viking fortress with a battering ram. This new stage is the 32nd post-launch map, continuing the game’s tradition of whimsical and varied level designs following last year’s Candyland and steampunk additions. Notably, it also incorporates a crossover character—Dave the Diver—adding a peculiar twist by placing this character within the new Viking world. Despite the anticipation for Human Fall Flat 2, which was announced years ago, there have been no recent updates on its development. The original game is currently discounted by 70%, making it accessible for newcomers and returning players.

Icarus Expands with Dangerous Horizons DLC and Free Kepler Update

Icarus, the sci-fi survival game from DayZ creator Dean Hall, released a substantial DLC titled Dangerous Horizons alongside a free update called Kepler. The DLC introduces a new 8x8 km open world map, Elysium, featuring diverse biomes like desert, tundra, volcanic zones, and alien wildlife, evoking comparisons to a space-themed Skyrim. It adds 17 missions, hostile soldier outposts, radioactive zones for uranium harvesting, NPC interactions, laser weaponry, and a new vehicle dubbed the Hoverframe. Priced at $30 with a launch discount, the expansion significantly broadens gameplay options. The free Kepler update adds an oil refining tech tier with pumpjacks and processing facilities, allowing crafting of plastics and fueling new power tools such as chainsaws and jackhammers. Additional features include new base defenses, building materials, missions, armor sets, and tool attachments. This reflects RocketWerkz’s continued commitment to evolving Icarus nearly four years post-launch.

Haunted Lands: Spiritual Successor to id Software’s Dangerous Dave Arrives

Haunted Lands, developed by solo creator Alevgor, is launching next week as a spiritual successor to id Software’s early 1990s side-scrolling shooter Dangerous Dave in the Haunted Mansion. The game replicates the style of its inspiration but adds considerable depth with multiple playable characters, including a sorceress and a werewolf, each with distinct combat abilities. Gameplay involves using shotguns and other weapons to fight undead enemies with detailed dismemberment effects. Early demos highlight intense, gory combat and a progression system featuring artifacts that alter character skills. Haunted Lands seeks to revive a lesser-known chapter in id Software’s history by offering a more visceral and varied side-scrolling shooting experience.

Revisiting Deus Ex: Invisible War’s Strengths Amid Its Criticisms

Deus Ex: Invisible War remains a divisive entry in the Deus Ex series, often criticized for its loading times, universal ammo system, and console-driven design choices. Despite this, recent reflections argue that the game offers notable freedom of choice and factional complexity uncommon even in modern RPGs. Players navigate shifting alliances between the WTO’s surveillance state and a global religious order, with missions allowing nuanced responses beyond simple acceptance or rejection. Side quests on smaller scales include corporate rivalries that parallel larger political conflicts. Modern community mods improve loading and display, enhancing playability. While its flaws are recognized, Invisible War's thematic and gameplay depth continue to merit reconsideration.
