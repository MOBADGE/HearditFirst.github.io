Ivy Road Studio Shutting Down After Funding Challenges

Ivy Road, the developer behind last year’s cozy adventure game *Wanderstop*, announced it will close its doors on March 31. *Wanderstop* was the studio’s first and only title, notable for its collaboration with writers Davey Wrenden (*The Stanley Parable*) and Karla Zimonja (*Tacoma*), as well as composer C418 (*Minecraft*). Despite positive reception, Ivy Road struggled to secure funding for their next project, *Engine Angel*, leading to layoffs earlier this year and ultimately the studio's closure. The developers expressed disappointment at not bringing *Engine Angel* to life but remain committed to *Wanderstop*, which will continue to be available for purchase. Annapurna Interactive will soon share more information on efforts to reach new players with the game. The closure highlights ongoing difficulties smaller studios face in raising funds amid a challenging industry climate.

ZA/UM’s Zero Parades Avoids Police Themes in Follow-up to Disco Elysium

The developers of ZA/UM’s new RPG *Zero Parades* deliberately moved away from police-focused storytelling—a major theme in their acclaimed 2019 game *Disco Elysium*. Principal writer Siim Sinamäe explained that the shifting cultural perceptions of police, combined with a desire to explore new genres, motivated the team to settle on a spy thriller narrative instead. The previous title’s focus on an amnesiac detective was rooted in a different political context, and the studio wanted to respond to the current atmosphere, marked by increased political urgency worldwide. Writer Honey Watson noted that spies in their story seek to influence ideology behind the scenes, offering fresh narrative challenges. This shift reflects the evolving political sensitivities in gaming narratives and the developer’s intent to innovate beyond their prior success.

Frictional Games Bundle Offers Classic Horror Titles for $15

A new Humble Bundle makes available the complete collection of Frictional Games’ horror titles for $15. This package includes the *Penumbra* trilogy, *Amnesia: The Dark Descent*, *Amnesia: A Machine for Pigs*, *Amnesia: Rebirth*, *Amnesia: The Bunker*, and *SOMA*, delivered as Steam keys redeemable until April 1, 2027. While some older titles may feel dated, *Amnesia: The Dark Descent* remains a key influence on modern horror, and *SOMA* is praised for its unsettling story and atmosphere. The bundle provides access to a broad spectrum of the developer’s work, covering survival horror to more narrative-driven experiences, at a cost-effective price.

Path of Exile’s Major Item Trading Hub Faces Uncertainty After Ban

Jenebu, leader of *Path of Exile*’s prominent Discord trading community The Forbidden Trove (TFT), had his account banned recently, spotlighting the complexities of the game’s unofficial item economy. Jenebu’s account held thousands of rare items used in “mirror services,” where players duplicated valuable gear using a rare in-game item. The ban—though details remain unclear—removes access to many items and calls the future of TFT, which served around 700,000 players, into question. Some community members welcome enforcement actions due to concerns over real-money trading linked to third-party platforms, while others are uncertain about how the ban will affect trading access. Meanwhile, official updates on the issue from Grinding Gear Games are awaited.

Deus Ex: Human Revolution Soundtrack Rejects Original Composer’s Track

Alexander Brandon, composer for the original *Deus Ex* (2000), revealed that a track he composed for the 2011 prequel *Deus Ex: Human Revolution* was ultimately rejected by Eidos Montreal for not fitting the new game’s style. Although invited to contribute, Brandon’s remake of the classic theme was not used, a decision he agrees with in retrospect. The new soundtrack, composed by Michael McCann, gave the game a distinct musical identity while respecting the franchise’s roots. Brandon also expressed disappointment over not being involved in *Deus Ex: The Fall* (2013). He has since faced challenges in the industry, reporting limited job opportunities despite his legacy. The story illustrates how studios prioritize new creative directions while maintaining franchise continuity.
