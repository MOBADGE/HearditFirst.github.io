High on Life 2 Review: A Different Tone Without Justin Roiland

*High on Life 2*, released February 13, continues the single-player FPS formula with a focus on dark humor and bold level design. This sequel notably moves away from some of the harsh nihilism associated with the previous game and co-founder Justin Roiland, who left following controversy. The game centers on the Outlaw bounty hunter facing off against a galactic pharmaceutical cartel. It stands out for its inventive level moments, such as a cruise-ship murder mystery segment, and strong animation work on its talking guns. However, combat and movement mechanics are less engaging, and technical issues, including bugs and performance dips, affect the experience at launch. Overall, it provides a quirky narrative-driven FPS with uneven execution.
