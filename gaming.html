Goblin Sushi: A New Cooking Simulator with a Goblin Twist

Goblin Sushi is an upcoming cooking simulation game where players take on the role of a sushi-chef goblin serving a variety of sushi-loving goblins in a dungeon setting. The gameplay revolves around combining ingredients like rice, seaweed, and fish to prepare sushi dishes under time pressure, managing a conveyor belt system, and cleaning plates to keep up with an endless queue of customers. A notable gameplay challenge is the need to pay rent to a demanding goblin landlord, adding extra pressure to deliver quickly. The game features quirky characters including multi-headed goblins and anthropomorphic dog-like creatures. Developed by indie studio Old Cake Factory, Goblin Sushi will enter early access on February 9, with a demo currently available on Steam. While its mechanics are simple and reminiscent of other cooking sims, its unique aesthetic and humor could appeal to fans of the genre.

Fallout and Magic: The Gathering Collaborate Again

Wizards of the Coast will release several new Fallout-themed Magic: The Gathering card sets this month, revisiting the previous successful crossover from two years ago. This time, the releases expand to include characters from the Fallout: New Vegas game and the recent Fallout TV series. Cards feature notable figures and items like Benny, Joshua Graham, and the Platinum Chip, as well as TV show characters such as The Ghoul, Maximus, and Lucy in various thematic appearances. These cards mostly reuse existing Magic mechanics but with new skins and themes relevant to the Fallout universe. The sets also include a playful "Dogmeat" Sol Ring variant and mutation-themed cards called the "Rad Superdrop." This continuing partnership highlights the ongoing appeal and cross-promotional potential between established gaming franchises.
