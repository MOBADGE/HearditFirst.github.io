Graveyard Keeper 2 AI Art Accusations and Developer Response

Following the announcement of *Graveyard Keeper 2*, some players expressed concern that the promotional art resembled generative AI output, sparking debates especially due to the involvement of Lazy Bear’s CTO, Slava Cherkasov, known for public AI experiments. Cherkasov addressed the issue on Reddit, affirming that *Graveyard Keeper 2* does not use AI in its art or development. He clarified that his AI experiments are personal tech explorations unrelated to the game, which involves a full team of artists. Player reactions vary from relief to cautious skepticism, reflecting ongoing community tensions around AI usage in game development.

This matters as it highlights the growing scrutiny AI tools face in creative industries, with developers needing to clearly communicate their use or non-use of such technologies to maintain trust with their audiences.

Rockstar Games Targeted by Hackers Demanding Ransom

Rockstar Games has confirmed a security incident after a hacking group called ShinyHunters accessed a limited amount of company data via a breach in third-party analytics provider Anodot. The hackers are demanding a ransom, threatening to leak stolen data by April 14 if their demands are not met. Rockstar assures the breach involved non-material information and has no impact on players or core operations.

This situation echoes Rockstar’s 2022 hack when *Grand Theft Auto 6* details leaked, underscoring persistent security challenges within major studios. The breach highlights vulnerabilities caused by third-party services and the ongoing risks of sensitive data exposure in the games industry.

Proton Experimental Improves Compatibility for Classic Capcom Games on Steam Deck

A recent update to Proton Experimental has enhanced compatibility for several older Capcom titles on Steam Deck and Linux, including *Resident Evil* (1996), *Resident Evil 2* (1998), and the *Dino Crisis* series. These games, newly released on Steam this year, have been promoted to "playable" status with improvements such as better performance and bug fixes, although some aspects like full FMV functionality in *Dino Crisis 2* still require community patches.

This update is significant as it facilitates access to classic survival horror titles on portable hardware, expanding preservation and play options for longtime fans and new players on non-Windows platforms.

BioWare’s Struggle and the Future of Its RPG Legacy

Reflecting on BioWare’s recent years, the studio faces major challenges after underperforming RPGs and the ill-fated *Anthem* venture. *Dragon Age: The Veilguard*, despite positive reviews, sold below expectations and saw limited post-launch support, leading to layoffs and restructuring. The author argues that BioWare’s identity tied to franchises like *Mass Effect* and *Dragon Age* may no longer be sustainable, recommending the studio pursue new directions rather than rely on past successes.

This analysis matters because BioWare’s trajectory reflects broader industry tensions between creative freedom and publisher pressures, raising questions about how legacy studios adapt or fade in a changing market environment.

Final Patch Update on Redfall Reveals What Could Have Been

The last major update (1.4) for *Redfall* added community engagement systems such as "Community Standing," encouraging exploration and interaction beyond main story missions. While these improvements help showcase the setting's atmosphere and history, critical gameplay elements like shooting mechanics and AI remain problematic. The game's design, which frames characters mostly as outsiders to the community, limits narrative depth and player immersion.

This postmortem serves as a case study on how ambitious projects with troubled launches may partially redeem themselves, but also how foundational issues can limit ultimate success despite later efforts.
