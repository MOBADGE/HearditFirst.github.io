Okami Marks 20 Years with Distinctive Action-Adventure Appeal

Today, April 20, 2026, marks the 20th anniversary of Okami, a game celebrated for its unique artistic style and gameplay that draws clear inspiration from the Zelda series. Although it follows the familiar action-adventure structure involving exploration, dungeon clearing, and ability progression, Okami distinguishes itself through its cultural art direction and the innovative "Celestial Brush" mechanic that rejuvenates the game's world. This mechanic allows players to restore color and life to the environment, offering a creative interaction uncommon in similar games. Okami’s continued recognition highlights the enduring appeal of games that blend traditional formulas with distinctive artistic identities.

Take-Two CEO Addresses AI Use and Industry Impact

Strauss Zelnick, CEO of Take-Two Interactive, recently shared his perspective on artificial intelligence during Semafor’s World Economy 2026 event. He cautioned against alarmist narratives around AI’s potential dangers but acknowledged that, like any technology, it can be misused. Zelnick emphasized that AI is becoming a valuable tool for developers to handle routine tasks and improve productivity, rather than a threat to creative employment. He cited Elon Musk’s continued involvement in AI development as evidence that the industry can adapt. His comments suggest a balanced industry outlook on AI's role going forward, focusing on integration rather than disruption.

Xbox Game Pass Announces Multiple Upcoming Day-One Releases

Microsoft revealed a second wave of new titles arriving on Xbox Game Pass this April and May. Notable day-one additions include Double Fine’s Kiln, a game featuring competitive pottery battles; Vampire Crawlers, a first-person spin-off from Vampire Survivors; and Aphelion, set for release at the end of April. Meanwhile, several games such as Citizen Sleeper and Dragon Ball Xenoverse 2 are set to leave the service by month's end. The continued influx of new content reflects Xbox’s strategy to enhance Game Pass’ appeal, providing subscribers with a diverse library covering various genres and play styles.

Elden Ring Movie Cast Details and Production Plans

A24 and Bandai Namco confirmed key cast members and date for the live-action Elden Ring film, scheduled for theatrical release on March 3, 2028. Directed and written by Alex Garland, known for Annihilation, the production will be filmed with IMAX technology. Actors Kit Connor and Cailee Spaeny, both collaborators with Garland, have been confirmed as part of the cast, though their specific roles remain undisclosed. The film is described as A24’s most ambitious project to date, indicating significant investment and expectations. This adaptation follows a growing trend of translating popular game properties into cinematic formats.

Nintendo's Super Mario Bros. and Galaxy Films Surpass $2 Billion Globally

Nintendo's recent return to film with The Super Mario Bros. Movie (2023) and the newer Super Mario Galaxy Movie generated over $2 billion combined at the global box office. The Galaxy film has reached nearly $750 million, ranking as the third highest-grossing video game movie, with the possibility to overtake the Minecraft Movie currently at $960 million. These financial successes have also positively affected related game sales, although Nintendo has not formally confirmed sales data linked to the Galaxy film. This marks a notable turnaround from the company’s early, unsuccessful film efforts in the 1990s.

Bandai Namco Confirms Dragon Ball Xenoverse 3 for 2027

Bandai Namco officially announced Dragon Ball Xenoverse 3 during the Dragon Ball Games Battle Hour 2026 event, confirming earlier rumors. Scheduled for release on PC, PlayStation 5, and Xbox Series X|S in 2027, the game takes place far in the future with players joining the Great Saiyan Squad to protect West City. The story, developed with input from series creator Akira Toriyama, introduces a new Dragon Ball timeline separate from previous entries. The announcement coincided with news of a final DLC for Dragon Ball Xenoverse 2, set for summer 2026, closing that chapter of the series.
