New 5th-Generation OLED Gaming Monitors from Philips and AOC

Philips and AOC are launching new 5th-generation QD-OLED gaming monitors in the third quarter of 2026. These panels address one of the main previous OLED drawbacks: font fringing, thanks to a V-RGB pixel substructure. Both monitors deliver improved HDR performance (HDR 500 TB), higher brightness peaks of 1300 nits, and full brightness at 300 nits. The AOC AGP346UCSD features a 34-inch WQHD display with a 360 Hz refresh rate, HDMI 2.1, DP 2.1, and a 15 W USB Type-C port, oriented towards high-end gamers. The Philips Evnia 34M2C8600P, also 34-inch but with HDMI 2.0 and DP 1.4, offers a 65 W USB Type-C port, a white shell, and built-in Ambiglow RGB lighting targeting a more casual audience. Pricing starts at approximately €899 for Philips and €999 for AOC. These monitors highlight the growing availability and refinement of OLED technology for gaming, offering better visuals and competitiveness with IPS panels.

Byte Magazine Archive Available for Free on the Internet Archive

Over 200 issues of Byte Magazine, a seminal personal computing publication running from 1975 to 1998, are now freely accessible via the Internet Archive. Byte chronicled the evolution of personal computing alongside industry developments, providing technical insights and cultural context. The collection includes many iconic cover artworks and articles reflecting the optimistic atmosphere surrounding early computing. The archive serves as both a historical resource and a reminder of a different era in technology journalism. Although some issues and the final 1998 issue are missing, the collection remains substantial. This preservation effort also complements similar archival projects, including PC Gamer’s demo discs and other digital gaming history collections.

PC Gamer Demo Discs Preserved and Playable on the Internet Archive

The Internet Archive has cataloged and preserved 758 PC Gamer demo discs spanning multiple decades, making them available for download and use. These discs were once a primary method for gamers to sample upcoming titles and included exclusive content such as maps, trailers, full episodes, and sometimes unique bonuses unavailable elsewhere. Examples range from 1997’s Quake-O-Rama disc featuring speedrun demos to 2008’s disc containing a full episode of Sam & Max. The collection offers both nostalgia for older gamers and a tangible window into the history of PC gaming marketing and culture. This archive supplements other digital preservation initiatives and supports appreciation of gaming’s evolving distribution methods.

Windrose Surpasses 1 Million Copies Sold with 200,000 Concurrent Players

The pirate-themed survival crafting game Windrose has sold over 1 million copies within six days of launch and peaked at 200,000 simultaneous players on Steam. The development team at Kraken Express expressed gratitude to players while working on ongoing fixes, particularly for online co-op connectivity issues. Windrose combines resource management and crafting loops with ship-to-ship combat reminiscent of titles like Assassin’s Creed: Black Flag. Its rapid growth in player base reflects strong interest in survival games with naval combat themes, despite the genre’s competitive market. The early access launch and active developer engagement point to a continuing community-driven development.

2026’s Noted RPG Esoteric Ebb Features an Overpowered Cleric Protagonist

Developer Christoffer Bodegård explained that the protagonist in Esoteric Ebb, a top RPG of 2026 using adapted D&D 5th Edition rules, is a Cleric primarily because the class is versatile and “overpowered.” The Cleric’s ability to heal, cast spells, and handle melee combat offers a wide gameplay range. Beyond mechanics, the narrative connection between the Cleric and their god Urth provides thematic depth exploring religion and masculinity. The game also allows rebellion against this role with alternative class choices. Bodegård contrasted the Cleric with other classes like Warlocks and Paladins, noting the narrative significance and broad utility of the Cleric class shaped the game’s design.
