Windrose Traveler’s Camp Puzzle Solved

Players of the cooperative pirate survival game *Windrose* encountered a common early-game puzzle involving Traveler’s Camps, which show two treasure chests on the map but only reveal one chest openly near tents and campfires. After some investigation, it was discovered that the second chest is typically buried at the base of a nearby tree marked with a red scarf, a clue not always explicitly provided by in-game notes. This method applies to multiple camps, clarifying a frustrating gameplay element for explorers seeking full completion of these locations. The discovery helps players efficiently clear these areas and progress in the game’s XP system.

Krafton’s Name Removed from Subnautica 2 Steam Page

*Krafton* is no longer listed as the publisher of *Subnautica 2* on its Steam page, with the developer Unknown Worlds now shown as both developer and publisher. Since Krafton acquired Unknown Worlds in 2021, the relationship has been complicated, especially after the firing and subsequent reinstatement of Unknown Worlds’ CEO due to legal disputes surrounding a $250 million performance bonus tied to the game’s launch success. Despite Krafton’s name removal sparking speculation about a split, Krafton confirmed it continues to support *Subnautica 2*’s early access launch. The release date remains uncertain but has a judge-approved extension to September 15, allowing for completion of the game to qualify for the bonus.

Marvel Rivals Introduces Overpowered Black Cat

*Marvel Rivals* prepares to add Black Cat as a new duelist in its upcoming Season 7.5 update. Black Cat’s abilities revolve around a Fortune resource system, which she can accumulate and spend on attacks or purchasing rare artifacts that grant powerful effects such as invisibility, portals through walls, area damage and stuns, and crowd control immunity. Early observations suggest Black Cat’s kit may be disproportionately strong, continuing a trend of overpowered hero releases that require balancing in subsequent seasons. The addition may impact competitive play until any necessary nerfs are applied.

Open-Source Nvidia GPU Driver Approved for Macs

Apple has officially approved an open-source driver allowing Nvidia GPUs, including GeForce RTX 50-series cards, to operate on Macs via external GPU (eGPU) enclosures connected over USB4 or Thunderbolt. This development, created by tiny corp through their TinyGPU driver, enables Macs to once again utilize Nvidia hardware, primarily targeting AI workloads rather than gaming at this stage. Although initial tests show performance limitations and incomplete utilization of the GPUs’ advanced features, the approval indicates a significant step towards broader compatibility. Gaming support is not yet available and would require further software development.

Allbirds Transitions from Shoe Brand to AI Infrastructure Provider

The shoe company Allbirds announced a major strategic pivot toward GPU-powered AI infrastructure, backed by a $50 million investment from an institutional investor. The company plans to rebrand as "NewBird AI" and aims to provide GPU-as-a-Service (GPUaaS) through cloud offerings targeted at AI training and related workloads. While the AI market remains expensive and competitive, Allbirds intends to lease high-performance GPU assets to clients. The announcement caused the company’s stock price to increase substantially. This move reflects the growing interest in AI computing but raises questions about profitability and resource competition in the sector.

Crimson Desert Surpasses Five Million Sales Amid Ongoing Improvements

Pearl Abyss’s action RPG *Crimson Desert* has reached over five million sales worldwide within its first month. The game launched to mixed reviews due to its complexity and difficulty but has received frequent patches and quality-of-life updates that address player feedback, including difficulty options, inventory management improvements, and better teleport mechanics. These continuous updates have improved player sentiment and attracted new users, helping the game recover from a rocky start. Despite the evolving nature of the game leading to some uncertainty about its core identity, the developer’s responsiveness appears to support sustained interest in the title.
