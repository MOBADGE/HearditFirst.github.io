Gaming News Briefing — April 10, 2026

Intel's Foundry Investments Show Signs of Paying Off Intel has reached its highest market capitalization in over 25 years, surpassing $300 billion following recent product releases and strategic partnerships. The company's move into manufacture-for-others, especially through a collaboration on Elon Musk’s Terafab project that may involve producing AI chips for SpaceX and Tesla, marks a notable pivot away from its historical model of making chips solely for itself. Additionally, a multi-year chip deal with Google supports Intel's foundry momentum. The renewed investor confidence highlights Intel's ongoing efforts to stabilize its business, especially amid competition from companies like AMD and Nvidia, which have larger market caps but lack Intel’s own semiconductor fabrication facilities.

Jeff Kaplan Addresses Tracer’s Design Claims from Overwatch Era Jeff Kaplan, former director of Overwatch, has confirmed that Blizzard never altered Tracer’s character model to reduce the size of her butt, a matter that sparked controversy around the game's 2016 launch. The debate stemmed from a change to one of Tracer’s poses rather than any model adjustments. Despite the relative triviality, the topic has lingered in the Overwatch community for a decade. Kaplan’s recent comments coincided with demonstrations of his new game, The Legend of California, and serve as a final clarification on this longstanding fan question.

Microsoft Scales Back Copilot Branding in Windows Apps Following user feedback, Microsoft is removing Copilot branding from several Windows 11 built-in apps including Notepad and Snipping Tool, though AI-powered features remain accessible. This action aligns with Microsoft's earlier commitment to reduce “unnecessary Copilot entry points” and create a more streamlined user experience. The change reflects a cautious response to concerns about pervasive AI integration within the operating system. However, the core functionality of Copilot-enabled tools will still be integrated, just under less prominently branded interfaces.

Asus Unveils ROG Equalizer Cable to Prevent GPU Power Connector Failures Asus has announced the ROG Equalizer, a new power cable designed to address a common issue where GPU power connectors overheat or melt under heavy loads, notably for high-power graphics cards like the RTX 5090. The cable balances current delivery across its pins and features higher current ratings than standard cables. It also integrates with Asus PSU software for real-time current monitoring. The ROG Equalizer works with any compatible PSU using the 12V-2x6 connector, offering broader usability beyond Asus power supplies.

Crimson Desert Developers Respond to Player Feedback with Upcoming Updates Pearl Abyss has outlined a series of updates planned between April and June for Crimson Desert, focusing on addressing criticisms raised by players since the game’s recent launch. Key changes include “re-blockading” to replenish enemies in cleared areas, boss rematches, and the addition of selectable difficulty levels. The update also improves character abilities for side characters and overhauls item storage with dedicated categories. UI and control refinements, new pets, mounts, and mounts' armor are also on the list. These efforts aim to enhance gameplay variety and quality of life.

CPUID’s Download Page Compromised, Leading to Malware Distribution Security researchers have identified that CPUID’s official download page for popular tools CPU-Z and HWMonitor has been hacked, resulting in malware being distributed in place of legitimate software. The compromised files use deceptive naming and installation wrappers, triggering antivirus alerts. This breach is linked to a sophisticated threat group known for similar attacks on other well-known software. Users are advised to avoid downloads from the CPUID site for now and to rely on updated security measures and trusted alternative sources for PC diagnostic tools.
