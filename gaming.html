Trump Appoints Tech CEOs to Science and Technology Advisory Council

President Trump has reestablished the President's Council of Advisors on Science and Technology (PCAST) and appointed 13 members, prominently including leaders from major technology firms. Among the new members are Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang, AMD CEO Lisa Su, and Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg. This panel, which includes business executives alongside some academics, aims to guide national policy on critical technologies such as artificial intelligence, quantum computing, and biotechnology. The shift toward industry leaders rather than predominantly academics marks a change in how scientific advice may be integrated into government policy. The council’s influence extends into areas like national security and economic innovation, highlighting the administration’s focus on maintaining American competitiveness in emerging technologies.

PC Gamer’s New Issue Features Star Wars Zero Company

PC Gamer magazine’s latest issue, now available, includes a detailed cover feature on *Star Wars Zero Company*, a turn-based tactical squad game inspired by XCOM. The magazine provides exclusive coverage, including developer interviews and extensive gameplay impressions of this sci-fi strategy title set in the Star Wars universe. Beyond the cover story, the issue offers previews of several upcoming games such as *Warhammer 40,000: Dark Heresy*, and reviews of recent releases like *Resident Evil Requiem*. Hardware coverage includes a review of AMD’s new Ryzen 7 9850X3D CPU alongside a group test of PC cases. The issue is purchasable in print and digital formats and offers a broad look at current and upcoming PC gaming content.

World of Warcraft Tank Class Faces Unexpected In-Game Cost

A player in *World of Warcraft* discovered that using the Protection Warrior’s primary mitigation ability, Shield Block, results in higher in-game repair costs. The ability increases the frequency of blocked attacks, which damages the warrior’s shield, requiring more silver to maintain. Calculations show that Shield Block use adds about 25 gold in repair costs per dungeon run compared to other shield users, amounting to significant extra spending over time. While the absolute cost is not burdensome for most players, the finding highlights a rarely discussed economic downside to this popular tank spec. It underscores the complexity of long-standing MMO mechanics and how they can impact player experience beyond combat performance.

Horizon Chase Turbo Scheduled for Delisting

The arcade racing game *Horizon Chase Turbo* and its predecessor will be removed from digital storefronts on June 1. Despite lacking licensed vehicles or online components, these titles will no longer be available for new purchases, though existing owners can still download them. The decision follows recent layoffs at Epic Games, which acquired the developer Aquiris. Notably, *Horizon Chase 2* will remain on sale, including on the Epic Games Store and other platforms. The removal is unusual given the game’s content, suggesting internal strategic shifts related to Epic’s broader business decisions. This delisting may affect players interested in owning the earlier Horizon Chase games digitally.

Caves of Qud Developed for Nintendo Switch Despite Technical Challenges

The roguelike RPG *Caves of Qud*, known for its complex gameplay and heavy reliance on keyboard controls, has been successfully ported to the Nintendo Switch. Co-creator Brian Bucklew explained that the project was motivated by the challenge of adapting such a systemic PC game to a gamepad-based console. The development involved a UI overhaul for gamepad-friendly controls, initially improving playability on the Steam Deck. Contrary to industry assumptions about audience demand, the Switch version of *Caves of Qud* performed well in sales, peaking within the top five digital and physical releases at one point. The team is now exploring adapting the game further to mobile devices. This port highlights how niche, complex PC titles can find new audiences on portable platforms with appropriate design adjustments.
