Fallen Aces Episode 2 Delayed with New Gameplay Details

The second episode of Fallen Aces, a neo-noir immersive sim FPS, will not be released in 2025 as initially expected. However, a recent Steam update revealed that the upcoming episode will feature a first-person car chase where the player controls driving with one foot on the steering wheel and fires a tommy gun with both hands. Additional new content shown includes environment updates, narrative comics, and a controllable NPC companion named Delia who responds to player commands. Fallen Aces remains in early access, joining a pattern of extended development seen in other New Blood studio FPS titles, such as Gloomwood and Ultrakill. The delay means fans will have to wait longer but can anticipate expanded gameplay mechanics and story elements when the episode becomes available.

Rainbow Six Siege Servers Restore Normal Operation After Hack

Rainbow Six Siege experienced a significant security breach over the weekend, during which hackers artificially doled out billions of in-game credits, rare and developer-only skins, and randomly issued bans and unbans to players. The incident caused temporary server shutdown by Ubisoft. Official statements clarified that no legitimate players would be punished based on the chaotic bans or credited currency, as all transactions during the period are being rolled back. Ubisoft has since restored servers, though the in-game marketplace remains disabled. Multiple hacker groups claimed responsibility, but the full scope of the breach remains unclear. The episode underscores ongoing challenges in safeguarding multiplayer games against unauthorized access and maintaining player trust.

GOG Separates From CD Projekt Red with New Ownership and Future Plans

GOG, the digital storefront known for DRM-free classic games, has been acquired by Michal Kicinski, one of its original co-founders, separating it from current owner CD Projekt Red. Kicinski emphasized maintaining the platform's commitment to player freedom and control over game ownership. Looking ahead, GOG plans to undertake "ambitious rescue missions" in 2026 and 2027 aimed at preserving and expanding its library of classic games. Though the specifics are yet to be detailed, these efforts may build upon the existing in-house Windows compatibility projects designed to keep older titles running on modern systems. The separation reassures users that GOG's existing services and game libraries will remain intact while potentially increasing its focus on classic and retro games, as well as niche titles that face rejection elsewhere.
