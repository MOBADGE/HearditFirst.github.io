Restored Post-Ending Dialogue Discovered for Fallout: New Vegas

A recently leaked beta containing cut content for Fallout: New Vegas has revealed post-ending dialogue that was originally planned by Obsidian but removed before release. The restored lines reflect different endings—from siding with the Legion or NCR to backing New Vegas independence or Mr House—providing additional interactions with key characters that respond to the player's choices. Modders have begun integrating this content into the game via the ongoing “Post-Game Dialogue Restored” mod, which requires the “Functional Post Game Ending” mod to operate. This discovery offers fans a closer look at Obsidian’s original vision for extending gameplay beyond the final slideshow, allowing for more narrative depth in the game’s conclusion.

The Legend of Khiimori Early Access: A Realistic Horse Courier Sim

The horse courier and survival simulation The Legend of Khiimori has launched into early access with a $30 price tag and promises about a year of development before full release. The game combines survival elements such as managing horse health and stamina with route planning and exploration on a Mongolian steppe setting. It includes features like horse care, breeding with genetic traits, and combat involving bows and repellants. However, some key features such as a full main story, horse customization, taming, and peaceful mode remain under development. Early access players have reported some progress-blocking bugs and occasional visual awkwardness. The game is aimed primarily at horse enthusiasts seeking a realistic and detailed experience, though others may wish to wait for further polish and content before purchasing.

Australia Implements Age Verification for R18+ Online Games Including GTA Online

Starting next Monday, Australia requires users to verify their age before accessing online games rated R18+, affecting games like GTA Online but not offline singleplayer titles. This regulatory move is part of new Age-Restricted Material Codes, aiming to prevent minors from accessing explicit content online, and is accompanied by potential fines of up to AU$49.5 million for publishers who fail to comply. The measures also extend to pornographic content and explicit AI chatbots. Australia is adopting stringent age assurance methods beyond simple birthdate input, such as requiring government ID or facial age estimation, mirroring recent practices on platforms like YouTube, Instagram, and TikTok. The regulations have raised privacy concerns due to third-party involvement in age verification, with watchdogs and researchers urging careful implementation.

Marathon’s Launch Observations: Diverse Classes, Deluxe Edition Issues, and Server Slam Loot

Marathon, Bungie’s new extraction shooter, officially launched recently after a high-activity playtest event called Server Slam. Early impressions highlight a shoot-on-sight gameplay environment with popular solo-friendly classes being Assassin and Vandal, favored for their stealth and movement abilities. The newly playable Thief shell adds elements of intel-gathering and tricky grappling to the mix. Some players report problems with Deluxe Edition bonuses and preorder rewards not appearing, with Bungie acknowledging the issue and promising fixes. Additionally, a bug from Server Slam unintentionally allowed some rare borrowed loot to be retained in players’ inboxes upon the full release, creating a minor inventory anomaly. Overall, the launch is stable with no major server issues reported so far.

Diablo 4’s Upcoming Season Introduces Butcher Transformation Event

Diablo 4’s next mini season, "Season of Slaughter," begins March 11 and introduces a new mechanic where players can transform into the Butcher, the iconic demon from the series. Players build killstreaks by slaying demons, collecting “Meaty Offerings” currency to access the Butcher form via a Shrine of Slaughter. The transformation grants new abilities themed around the Butcher’s signature fighting style. This event will take place in areas affected by enhanced Helltide monsters and also feature PvP zones where players can face off against other Butcher forms. The season will run ahead of the release of the Lord of Hatred expansion on April 28 and includes a free week where players can try the Paladin class without pre-ordering the expansion. This experimental season aims to offer new gameplay variety shortly before the major expansion launch.
