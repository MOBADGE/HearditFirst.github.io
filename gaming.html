Arc Raiders Player Base and Potential PvE Mode Discussion

Arc Raiders has seen notable success, with Nexon reporting nearly 1 million concurrent players in January and around 6 million weekly active users across platforms. These figures place the extraction shooter alongside some of the most popular MMORPGs by player count, underlining its strong position in the live service genre. The game combines PvP and PvE elements, but recent discussions have focused on whether a dedicated PvE mode should be added. The current PvPvE design uses Arc machines to create hazards encouraging PvP engagement, making pure PvE encounters feel less engaging and potentially harmful to the core loot economy and matchmaking balance.

The developer Embark initially pivoted away from a fully PvE focus due to such challenges. While a standalone PvE raid mode involving waves of AI enemies and bosses could enrich the gameplay and better utilize high-end gear, maintaining a fresh and sustainable PvE experience poses significant difficulties, especially for a live service model. Introducing strict PvE matchmaking risks weakening the dynamic tension and economic loop that define Arc Raiders. Therefore, despite player interest, a PvE mode remains unlikely without careful design to avoid undermining the current structure.

SteelSeries Arctis Nova Elite Headset Review

SteelSeries released the Arctis Nova Elite, a high-end gaming headset priced at $600. The headset features an extensive list of audio and connectivity features, including active noise cancellation, beamforming microphones, and swappable batteries with a dedicated charging hub. It uses 40mm carbon fiber drivers designed for detailed and aggressive sound reproduction, capable of delivering pronounced audio cues in games.

However, the headset has some drawbacks. Its fit is described as somewhat tight and heavy, which may affect long-term comfort. The microphone quality is adequate but not exceptional. Although the audio drivers provide impressive detail and impact, the sound can become fatiguing during long sessions, and the overall sonic character lacks the sweetness expected from more expensive audiophile gear. Given its high price, the Nova Elite‚Äôs value is mixed, with better options available for less elsewhere, especially for those prioritizing comfort or a balanced sound profile.

Delisting of ‚Äô90s-Era Jurassic Park Classic Games

The Jurassic Park Classic Games Collection, featuring seven early-1990s Jurassic Park titles, will be delisted from Steam and other digital storefronts on March 31, 2026. The collection has been available for just over two years and is currently priced at $30. After delisting, players who already own the collection will retain access, but it will no longer be available for new purchases.

Limited Run Games, the publisher, emphasized providing advance notice due to previous criticism over short delisting windows. The relatively short sales period and current price point may dissuade some potential buyers. The move reflects the challenges of maintaining licensing agreements with older film-based games and the business realities of preserving vintage game collections digitally.
