A Fan's Wedding to Like a Dragon's Kazuma Kiryu at Sega Anniversary Event

At a recent event in Tokyo celebrating the 20th anniversary of Sega's Like a Dragon series, a female fan staged a mock wedding to a cardboard cutout of Kazuma Kiryu, the series' iconic protagonist. The event, titled "Weddings, Funerals, and Other Ceremonies Exhibition," is aimed at symbolically reflecting the themes of life and drama found in the Yakuza games. Although the marriage was not legally binding, the fan expressed her affection publicly, sharing photos online and receiving mock marriage and divorce certificates at the exhibition. The event runs through late December in Tokyo and will have another occurrence in Osaka in February 2026. This fan-led tribute highlights the emotional connections players form with game characters beyond the virtual realm.

Codex Mortis: A Rogue-lite Developed Entirely Using AI Tools

A new roguelite game, Codex Mortis, has emerged claiming to be the "world's first fully playable game created 100% through AI." Developed by Crunchfest3 over three months, the game was built without a traditional game engine, using programming languages like TypeScript and open source libraries for rendering and system architecture. Generative AI systems like Claude Code and ChatGPT contributed to coding, art, and animation. Currently available as a demo on Steam, Codex Mortis resembles bullet-hell games such as Vampire Survivors but features indistinct AI-generated visuals. While the claim of being the first fully AI-developed game is difficult to verify due to previous AI-assisted projects, Codex Mortis represents an experimental use of AI within game development pipelines.

Diffusion: A Decade-Long Half-Life Total Conversion Now Available

Diffusion, a total conversion mod for Half-Life developed over ten years, has recently released with a significant scope and ambition. Built on the Xash3D engine, it features new environments ranging from underground labs to alien cities and a cyberpunk metropolis, along with upgraded visuals and new weapons. The game offers gameplay reminiscent of Half-Life and Black Mesa but expands with unique vehicle segments and dimension-hopping narratives. While the current version uses AI-generated voices that have drawn mixed reactions, the developer plans to replace these with human voice actors soon. Diffusion stands as a detailed fan project that showcases long-term dedication to reviving and expanding classic FPS gameplay.
