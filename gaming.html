**Batman: Arkham Origins Graphics Mod Revitalizes Visuals**

A graphics mod for Batman: Arkham Origins, recently updated to version 2.0, significantly improves the game's visual presentation. This mod enhances snow effects by increasing particle size and quantity, refines lighting for a more wintry ambiance, and replaces several textures with higher-resolution alternatives including Gotham’s skyline and Batman's default suit. Shadow quality is also improved, reducing banding artifacts, and reflections are upgraded. However, cutscenes remain at their original 720p resolution due to technical limitations. Installation requires downloading the mod from Nexus Mods and having at least one DLC installed. This mod may attract players interested in experiencing Arkham Origins with visuals closer to what was hinted at before release.

**Death Stranding 2 Exceeds Two Million Sales Following PC Port**

Death Stranding 2: On the Beach has surpassed two million copies sold after releasing on PC. The game sold 425,000 copies in its first week on PC, adding to the 1.6 million units sold on PlayStation 5 since its initial launch less than a year ago. This rapid PC port marks one of the fastest transitions from console exclusivity to PC for a singleplayer-focused title. Additionally, the PC release correlated with increased sales on PS5, especially during a concurrent discount period. The game's unique narrative exploring themes of grief and connection has maintained discussion and interest across platforms. This milestone is notable given recent reports that Sony may be scaling back its PC release strategy for prestige singleplayer games.

**Star Wars: Zero Company Director Advocates for Story and Visuals in Turn-Based Tactics**

Greg Foertsch, creative director of the upcoming turn-based tactics game Star Wars: Zero Company, emphasized the importance of integrating strong storytelling and quality visuals within the genre. Drawing on his experience with XCOM and other titles, Foertsch argued against the notion that tactics games must sacrifice narrative or graphical polish in favor of gameplay depth. He highlighted that depth and elegance can coexist and that immersive production values are valuable in single-player strategy titles. His approach aims to broaden the appeal of tactics games beyond puzzle-like mechanics, suggesting that good story and presentation are achievable without compromising gameplay.

**Epic Games Layoffs Affect Terminally Ill Programmer; Company Responds**

Epic Games' recent layoffs included Mike Prinke, a technical writer who has been undergoing treatment for terminal brain cancer. Prinke’s termination raised concerns as losing his employer-provided health insurance also meant loss of life insurance coverage, and his condition was classified as a pre-existing one, complicating replacement coverage options. His wife publicly shared their situation, highlighting the challenges facing workers caught in layoffs while managing severe health issues. Epic CEO Tim Sweeney responded on social media, stating that the company is in contact with the family and is working to address insurance matters. He acknowledged that the medical condition was confidential and not considered during the layoff decisions and expressed regret for not foreseeing the hardship. This case underscores the vulnerabilities of healthcare tied to employment in the U.S. games industry workforce.
