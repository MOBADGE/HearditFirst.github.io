Life is Strange’s Soundtrack Remains Impactful After 11 Years With the recent conclusion of Life is Strange: Reunion, reflections turn to the original 2015 Life is Strange and its notable use of licensed music. The game’s soundtrack, heavily featuring indie rock and emotive songs, played a significant role in shaping the player’s experience. Unlike many games that use licensed tracks solely for immersion or surface-level coolness, Life is Strange curated its music akin to a TV show, using songs to amplify emotional and narrative beats. Tracks like Syd Matters’ “To All of You American Girls” and Foals’ “Spanish Sahara” remain memorable, with the latter being emblematic of the game’s tone and even reused in the Reunion installment. This thoughtful soundtrack curation helped solidify Life is Strange’s identity and emotional impact, highlighting the value of licensed music beyond mere background ambiance.

Blizzard Secures Injunction Against Turtle WoW Private Server Blizzard has won a legal injunction to halt the operation of Turtle WoW, a popular private server that offered a modified version of the original World of Warcraft experience, blending classic gameplay with new content. The court ordered Turtle WoW’s developers to cease all development and prevent passing along their code to prevent a successor server’s rise. The injunction reflects Blizzard’s ongoing efforts to protect its intellectual property and control over its game environments, a stance contrasting with other companies who have embraced fan-run servers under official frameworks. Turtle WoW had previously sought dialogue with Blizzard for a fan server licensing system but was met with lawsuits instead. The decision underscores tensions between game companies and fan projects in maintaining proprietary control over game worlds.

Open-World Crime Game Samson Faces Bug Issues Post-Launch Liquid Swords’ Samson, an open-world crime game with roguelike elements inspired by Grand Theft Auto, has launched to mixed reviews on Steam largely due to bugs and technical problems. The developer acknowledges releasing a product with flaws and has already issued a hotfix addressing performance and crash issues. Further patches focusing on stability, gameplay polish, and player feedback are scheduled, with a roadmap detailing updates planned through late April. Despite its troubled launch, Samson has attracted a player base interested in its open-city premise, a genre area that has seen limited new entries since GTA 5. The developer’s commitment to long-term support suggests efforts to improve the game’s quality and player experience going forward.

Dean 'Rocket' Hall Shares Gaming Habits and Development Insights Dean Hall, creator of DayZ and founder of RocketWerkz, recently shared extensive insights into his gaming preferences and ongoing projects. He is currently invested heavily in RimWorld, logging hundreds of hours and creating popular mods. Hall also highlighted his enduring affection for classic titles like the original XCOM, which he played even while at Mount Everest’s base camp. His highest-played game on Steam is Stationeers, a title developed by his studio, with nearly 5,000 hours logged. Hall is known for a clean, cloud-based desktop setup due to security and development needs and mentioned plans to support Linux users more robustly. His reflections provide a glimpse into the balance between developer duties and personal gaming pursuits.

The Planet Crafter 2.0 Update Brings New Biome and Visual Improvements The Planet Crafter, a terraforming-focused survival game released in 2024, has received a substantial 2.0 update alongside a temporary 50% price reduction on Steam. This update introduces a new, undisclosed biome on the main world, enhancements to existing biomes, and new decorative and functional items like arcade machines and vending machines. Visual improvements include upgrades to skyboxes, terrains, vegetation, and water shaders, aiming to make the world more immersive. A notable feature is the dynamic evolution of terraformed bodies within the solar system visible in real-time. Additionally, mobility improvements like a jetpack chip for the player’s rover facilitate exploration of difficult terrain. The update addresses both aesthetic and gameplay enhancements to sustain player interest and broaden appeal.
