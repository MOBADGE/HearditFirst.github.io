Magic: The Gathering Returns to Strixhaven Setting, Avoiding Harry Potter Crossover

Wizards of the Coast confirmed that their next Magic: The Gathering set, *Secrets of Strixhaven*, will revisit the original Strixhaven setting rather than featuring a Harry Potter crossover. This decision follows a period of experimental crossovers ranging from Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles to The Office characters. The Strixhaven setting, which debuted five years ago with *Strixhaven: School of Mages*, offers a university-style magical school with broader academic subjects and mature student characters, differentiating itself from the Hogwarts-style wizard school trope. The set will be available in Magic Arena on April 21 and tabletop from April 24. This approach aligns with the company’s ongoing strategy to develop its own lore-rich settings despite partnering with Warner Bros on Harry Potter-related toys and games.

Crimson Desert Patch Adds Hide Helmet Feature and Other Improvements

Pearl Abyss released a new patch for *Crimson Desert* focusing on gameplay quality-of-life improvements, including the highly requested ability to hide helmets. Players can now toggle helmet visibility in cutscenes, combat, or entirely, enhancing character customization and immersion. The update also introduces new armor sets for in-game cats, expands camp storage to 1,000 slots, and adds a “classic” control scheme option for movement. Since its launch, *Crimson Desert* has been undergoing frequent updates addressing player feedback. The patch reflects developer commitment to refining the single-player RPG experience amid mixed critical reception and some controversies related to AI-generated assets.

Possible First Footage Surfaces from Elden Ring Movie Set in the UK

A TikTok video by a UK-based fan offers a potential first look at the set for Alex Garland’s upcoming *Elden Ring* movie adaptation. The footage shows a ruined stone church closely resembling in-game structures known as the Churches of Marika, set in a field akin to the game’s Limgrave area. Nearby construction equipment and props bear logos linked to a known film scenery company near Windsor Great Park, a popular filming location. While the clip has stirred community speculation, it remains unconfirmed if this is linked to the movie, an upcoming game edition, or a separate project. The film’s casting and detailed production plans have largely remained under wraps since the initial announcement.

Datamined Elden Ring Cutscene Reveals Early Story Elements for DLC Villain Miquella

Dataminer Lance McDonald uncovered a work-in-progress cutscene from *Elden Ring* featuring the DLC antagonist Miquella the Kind. The scene shows Miquella planting the sapling of the Haligtree—central to the DLC’s narrative—watering it with his own blood in a modified boss arena. The voice lines suggest Miquella’s ideals about growth and a new world, potentially addressing another character and the player. This cut content hints at a more utopian and benevolent interpretation of Miquella’s character before the eventual darker depiction in the DLC. The discovery offers additional lore context to one of FromSoftware’s most mysterious figures.

Warframe's Community Director States Firm Opposition to AI-Generated Content

Digital Extremes' community director Megan Everett stated unequivocally that no AI-generated content will ever be used in *Warframe* or *Soulframe*. Everett expressed frustration with AI art and asset usage, emphasizing the studio’s commitment to purely human-made content. This stance contrasts with growing industry adoption of generative AI in development processes, highlighting ongoing debates about authenticity and labor in game creation. Although AI use is widespread in some studios, Digital Extremes opts to maintain traditional content creation methods, reinforcing a clear position amid the evolving technological landscape.

Amazon TV Head Requests Mass Effect Show Rewrite to Appeal to Non-Gamers

Reports indicate that Peter Friedlander, head of global TV at Amazon MGM Studios, has requested a rewrite of the upcoming *Mass Effect* television series to make it more accessible to viewers unfamiliar with the games. The series is nearing a series order but is now facing creative adjustments aimed at broadening its appeal beyond the core gaming audience. This reflects ongoing challenges in adapting video game properties for television, balancing fan expectations against mainstream storytelling needs. While some fans may worry about dilution of the source material, others note the risks of overly insular adaptations. The series remains in early development, with no confirmed release date.
