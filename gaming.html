Resident Evil Requiem PC Performance Tested Amid Driver Issues

PC gamers have reported significant performance drops playing Capcom’s Resident Evil Requiem using certain Nvidia drivers, particularly the GeForce Game Ready Driver 591.86. Some users with RTX 40-series GPUs claimed large FPS declines and reduced GPU power draw when ray tracing was enabled. Rolling back to older drivers was suggested as a workaround.

PC Gamer conducted extensive tests on powerful rigs using multiple Nvidia GPUs and driver versions but could not reproduce the reported severe performance loss. Differences in FPS between newer and older drivers were marginal and within expected testing variance. The test hardware featured a high-end AMD Ryzen 7 9800X3D and up to RTX 5090 graphics, which could mask the issue.

This inconsistency suggests the problem is not universal and may depend on hardware configurations or other system factors. Meanwhile, Nvidia’s recent driver releases have faced criticism following issues like fan outages and clock speed instability, contributing to community frustration. The ongoing "driver drama" highlights challenges in delivering stable PC experiences, especially with demanding technologies like path tracing.

New OS Age Verification Laws Stir Debate Among Open Source Projects

California’s new legislation requiring operating systems to ask user age/date of birth during setup, effective January 2027, has provoked concern among open source developers. The law mandates age-based app availability controls and real-time data sharing with developers, raising privacy and implementation questions.

Fedora’s project lead suggested a local age file or D-Bus service as a straightforward means for apps to check age without telemetry. Security-focused distros like Kicksecure and Whonix are exploring ways to comply without compromising user privacy. However, enforcement practicality is unclear. Some projects, like MidnightBSD, have humorously opted to exclude California residents to avoid compliance.

Colorado is considering a similar bill effective 2028. Resistance also came from unexpected quarters, including open source calculator DB48X, which stated it will not implement age verification and barred California and Colorado users accordingly. The debate underscores tensions between regulatory intent and technical feasibility in privacy-conscious open source communities.

Samsung Confirms Sharp DRAM Price Increase Amid Memory Shortage

Samsung Electronics has reportedly finalized a DRAM price increase exceeding 100% for the first quarter of 2026, worsening an already significant cost surge that started at 70% in January. This price hike applies to many customers including memory module suppliers and GPU partners.

Quarterly and possibly monthly contract negotiations are replacing annual deals to adjust quickly to soaring demand driven by AI and server expansion, while supply remains constrained. Higher DRAM prices contribute to rising costs for PC components and consumer electronics broadly, with GPU prices already elevated and SSDs also increasing.

The trend indicates tighter memory markets and less price relief in the near term. Consumers and manufacturers alike face challenges from inflated prices for essential hardware. This situation affects affordability and availability of gaming PCs and devices reliant on DRAM.

Acer Nitro V 16 AI Review: Balanced Entry-Level Gaming Laptop

The Acer Nitro V 16 AI offers a largely solid gaming experience with few major flaws, standing out in a market crowded with confusing SKUs and inconsistent specs. The reviewed model includes an AMD Ryzen 7 260, Nvidia RTX 5070 (95 W), 32 GB DDR5 RAM, and a 2560x1600 100 Hz IPS display.

Performance benchmarks show it outperforms comparable laptops like the Gigabyte Aero X16 with the same GPU but lower power limits. Battery life is strong at 140 minutes under gaming workloads, surpassing rivals. The design embraces a typical gamer aesthetic, which may not appeal in professional settings.

Connectivity options are abundant. Despite marketing emphasis on AI features, the laptop delivers reliable and cost-effective gameplay when extraneous software is removed. Buyers should note the variety of Nitro V 16 AI configurations available to select the best fit for their needs.
