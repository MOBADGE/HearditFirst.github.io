**Rainbow Six Siege Servers Restored After Hacking Incident**

Rainbow Six Siege experienced a significant security breach over the weekend of December 27, during which hackers issued random bans and unbans, distributed rare skins, and granted players billions of in-game credits. Ubisoft acknowledged the incident, temporarily taking servers offline and rolling back all transactions to prevent exploitation effects. The ban ticker was used for unauthorized messaging, including jokes and references irrelevant to the game. As of December 28, servers are restored, though the in-game marketplace remains offline. The full impact on user accounts is still being assessed, and an unknown hacker group has claimed responsibility.

**GOG Acquired by Original Co-Founder with New Plans for Classic Games**

GOG, the DRM-free digital storefront formerly owned by CD Projekt Red, has been acquired by its original co-founder, Michal Kicinski. The transition maintains existing libraries and platform operations unchanged. Kicinski emphasized a renewed focus on preserving classic games, celebrating standout titles, and supporting the development of new games with retro appeal. Plans for ambitious "rescue missions" to bring more classic games into the library and improve compatibility are set to be detailed in 2026. The acquisition also suggests GOG will continue to serve as a platform for games that may face restrictions on other digital stores, reinforcing its position as a haven for diverse and classic titles
