Japanese Indie Studio Pursues Legal Action Over Missing Crowdfunding Funds

Skeleton Crew Studio, the developer behind the upcoming spiritual successor to the detective visual novel *428: Shibuya Scramble*, is seeking legal recourse after receiving only half of its crowdfunding money. The game’s executive producer, Jiro Ishii, raised 55 million yen (approximately $340,000) via a campaign on the Japanese platform Ubgoe. However, the studio has received only 27.75 million yen to date. Ubgoe’s CEO reportedly admitted the funds were mistakenly wired to another client and has failed to fully reimburse the developer, providing insufficient evidence to verify the transaction. Although the project has secured alternate funding to continue development, the case highlights risks developers face in crowdfunding and the unclear financial liabilities within platform terms, where project owners remain responsible to backers even if funds are mishandled.

Bungie Nerfs Marathon’s Biotoxic Disinjector Weapon

In Bungie’s multiplayer shooter *Marathon*, the powerful endgame weapon Biotoxic Disinjector has been reduced in damage by 35%. Known for its lethal laser beam and grenade launcher alt-fire that bounces projectiles and sticks to enemy players, the weapon was viewed as giving an unfair advantage, with players often using it to quickly eliminate teams. While the damage nerf aims to reduce its dominance, some players suggest the change is insufficient. The update follows a recent change increasing access to the weapon by making key boss encounters more frequent. Bungie’s actions reveal the challenge of balancing high-reward weapons in competitive games where accessibility and power must be carefully managed to maintain fairness without diminishing player incentives.

Star Wars Zero Company Studio Founder Commends Lucasfilm and Respawn

Greg Foertsch, founder of Bit Reactor and developer of the upcoming *Star Wars Zero Company* tactics game, praised Lucasfilm and Respawn for supporting a turn-based strategy project in a franchise typically associated with action titles. Foertsch noted the rarity of such boldness in the industry given market risks, especially for a new studio venturing into a genre outside the usual Star Wars offerings. He expressed gratitude for their confidence despite the genre’s niche appeal, referencing the commercial failure of other well-reviewed tactical games. This partnership indicates a willingness within major IP holders to diversify game types under their brand, though the genre’s market challenges persist.

Narrative Director Highlights Intended Team Conflict in Star Wars Zero Company

Aaron Contreras, narrative director for *Star Wars Zero Company*, outlined the game’s emphasis on team dynamics and moral ambiguity during the Clone Wars era, diverging from typical heroic RPG tropes. The game avoids the "chosen one" fantasy, focusing instead on leadership, strategy, and managing diverse operators with conflicting worldviews. Contreras described a relationship system where squadmates develop bonds that affect gameplay but also create setbacks reflecting real interpersonal friction. This approach aims to provide a more layered narrative experience in the Star Wars setting, underscoring the complexities of war and leadership rather than simple good-versus-evil storytelling.

Robot Cache Storefront Reportedly Shutting Down, Locking Users Out

Reports have surfaced that Robot Cache, a blockchain-based PC game storefront launched in 2018, is closing down and will soon render games purchased on its platform unplayable. Customers received emails stating the store will cease operations within 30 days, after which access to games and the platform’s digital currency, “iron,” will be lost due to the shutdown of the verification system. The platform had struggled with low user engagement and financial difficulties, with low revenue reported in 2024 and key staff departures. The shutdown highlights ongoing challenges and risks associated with blockchain-powered game distribution, including concerns over game ownership and digital rights management tied to platform operations.
