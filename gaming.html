Crimson Desert: How to Solve the Arboria Castle Puzzle

Crimson Desert players tackling the Arboria Castle puzzle, which involves repairing an elixir factory, should note that completion requires the "Restarting the Elixir Factory" quest, unlocked after defeating a spider bounty and completing the "Queen of the Silkspun Cradle" quest. The puzzle has multiple stages including activating the cultivation room, ingredient dispenser, crusher, compounder, heater, and bottling machine. Successfully finishing it yields a Fertilizer Sprayer. Understanding these steps is important because the puzzle can be completed without the quest but yields no reward. For those progressing in the game, following this structured approach ensures proper completion and unlocking of rewards.

Razer Viper V4 Pro: A Competitive Gaming Mouse Contender

Razer has introduced the Viper V4 Pro, a lightweight competitive gaming mouse aimed at esports players and enthusiasts. Compared to its predecessor, the V3 Pro, the V4 Pro is lighter by around 5 grams, offers extended battery life (up to 180 hours at 1 kHz polling), improved sensor performance, and an optical scroll wheel. It also features Razer's new FrameSync technology, which synchronizes mouse polling with PC scanning akin to variable refresh rates used in monitors. While it lacks Logitech’s G Pro X2 Superstrike’s haptic-inductive clicks, the Viper V4 Pro’s shape and performance improvements position it as a viable alternative for competitive gamers. Priced at $160, it also offers a slight cost advantage over the $180 Superstrike.

Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang on DLSS 5 Backlash

Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang addressed recent criticism of DLSS 5, Nvidia’s AI-driven image enhancement technology, in an interview on the Lex Fridman podcast. Huang expressed empathy for gamers concerned about AI-generated content's homogenization but clarified that DLSS 5 aims to enhance, not alter, the game's artistic geometry and textures as defined by artists. The technology is 3D-structure and artist-guided, and Huang emphasized that developers retain artistic control and can opt out of using DLSS 5. However, public demonstrations of DLSS 5 have shown stylistic changes that some interpret as changes rather than enhancements, illuminating ongoing confusion about the tool's exact impact. Nvidia continues to develop the technology with input from artists and developers.

Masters of Albion Closed Beta Opens Ahead of Early Access

22cans is running a closed beta for its upcoming god game Masters of Albion, which enters early access on April 22. Interested players must register via the official Discord by March 25. The beta aims to gather player feedback to refine the game’s complex systems before launch. Critics highlight that some gameplay systems could be optional and streamlined, potentially reducing depth compared to classic inspirations like Fable and Black and White. Nevertheless, the beta offers an opportunity for players to sample the blend of city-building and god game elements, informing final adjustments ahead of early access.

Former Windows Lead Reveals Past Plans to Cut Windows 11 Memory and Storage Use

Mikhail Parakhin, a former Windows development lead, revealed that he previously worked on a project aiming to reduce Windows 11's idle memory and storage footprint by 20%. Though the project was never completed, current Microsoft leadership is renewing focus on Windows' performance fundamentals. Recent Windows Insider updates confirm ambitions to improve memory efficiency and reduce baseline system overhead, which aligns with Parakhin’s goals. Given rising memory and storage costs, reducing Windows’ resource requirements could positively impact PC users, though delivery timelines remain uncertain.

Princeton Review Names Top Game Design Programs for 2026

The Princeton Review published its 2026 rankings of the best undergraduate and graduate game design programs in the US, considering graduate employment, salaries, faculty experience, and curriculum relevance. New York University (NYU) ranks first for both levels, followed by the University of Southern California and the University of Central Florida. The list highlights leading programs that equip students for careers in areas such as AI programming, map design, and creative accessibility. The report also discusses industry challenges and practical design exercises, offering valuable insight into educational paths for aspiring game developers.
