Survival-Crafting Shooter Faces Competition as Windrose Surges on Steam

Capcom’s sci-fi shooter *Pragmata* was expected to dominate this week’s sales charts following its release, but it encountered strong competition from the early access pirate-survival game *Windrose*. Launched on April 14, *Windrose* quickly sold over one million copies in six days and peaked at around 220,000 concurrent Steam players. This figure surpasses the highest concurrent player count of Bungie’s *Marathon*, another recent live service title. At the time of writing, *Windrose* ranks as the second best-selling game on Steam by revenue, just behind *Counter-Strike 2*, with *Pragmata* in third place.

*Windrose*’s success indicates sustained player interest in pirate-themed survival games, a genre where few titles have found widespread consensus. The game features simple but engaging ship combat and survival mechanics, which seem to resonate well with players. Meanwhile, *Pragmata* also had a strong presence with nearly 69,000 peak concurrent players and a $59.99 launch price. Both games target different player bases but show that survival and sci-fi shooters continue to perform well.

Magic: The Gathering’s New Set Deepens Faction Mechanics

Wizards of the Coast are releasing a new *Magic: The Gathering* expansion titled *Secrets of Strixhaven*, which focuses on five magical colleges each associated with unique mechanics and color pairs. The set aims to build on the existing Strixhaven lore by emphasizing the roles and themes of these universities.

Each college introduces a signature gameplay mechanic triggered by specific in-game actions: - *Witherbloom* uses **Infusion**, triggered by life gain. - *Prismari* has **Opus**, linked to casting instants and sorceries, with more power for higher-cost spells. - *Quandrix* features **Increment**, which relates to casting spells with mana costs above a creature’s stats. - *Lorehold* brings back the older **Flashback** keyword, allowing casting spells from the graveyard. - *Silverquill* uses **Repartee**, triggered by instant or sorcery spells targeting creatures.

The set is set to arrive on *Magic Arena* on April 21 and will release for tabletop play on April 24. It places renewed focus on instants and sorceries, aiming to diversify strategic options across the factions.

New 5th-Gen OLED Gaming Monitors from Philips and AOC

Philips and AOC are launching new 34-inch 5th-generation QD-OLED gaming monitors in the third quarter of 2026, addressing previous OLED drawbacks like font fringing. These monitors feature high peak brightness (up to 1300 nits), a 1800R curve, and improved HDR performance (HDR 500 TB).

The AOC AGP346UCSD model offers a refresh rate of 360 Hz, HDMI 2.1 and DisplayPort 2.1 support, though its USB Type-C port is limited to 15W due to design constraints. The Philips Evnia 34M2C8600P model reduces connectivity to HDMI 2.0 and DP 1.4 but offers 65W USB Type-C charging, a white shell, and Ambiglow RGB lighting compatible with Philips' smart lighting ecosystem.

Priced around €999 ($1,176) and €899 ($1,050) respectively, these monitors target high-end and casual gamers. They compete with other recent OLED options like MSI’s similar 34-inch display. Both aim to broaden the market and reduce reliance on single manufacturers for OLED gaming panels.
