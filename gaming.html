Monster Hunter Stories 3: Invasive Monsters and Endangered Species Locations

Recent guides have detailed how to tackle the nine Invasive Monsters in *Monster Hunter Stories 3*. These monsters are enhanced variants that guard the dens of rare, endangered species. Defeating them or forcing their retreat is necessary to claim valuable monster eggs, which unlock some of the best monsties in the game.

Each Invasive Monster requires specific strategies. For example, the Invasive Yian Garuga’s legs must be broken to make it flee, whereas defeating the Invasive Plesioth involves breaking its head using fire weapons. Other tactics include defense-boosting against the Shogun Ceanataur’s claw attacks, timing gunlance counters against the invisible Invasive Nerscylla, or using hammer abilities to target the burrowing Invasive Diablos.

Additionally, a companion summary lists the locations of all nine endangered species across the game’s regions: Azuria, Canalta Timberland, Tarkuan, and Serathis. Each species’ den is usually guarded by one of these challenging Invasive Monsters. Understanding their locations and guardians helps players plan their exploration and hunting routes.

This information matters to players aiming to collect rare monsties and progress through the storyline since these endangered species and their eggs are key progression elements and offer unique companions.

Darktide Introduces New Extraction-Like “Expeditions” Mode

The Warhammer 40,000 first-person shooter *Darktide* is adding a new gameplay mode called "expeditions" as part of the upcoming Beyond the Hive update, releasing March 17. This mode shifts gameplay from the usual tight indoor corridors to more open, sunlit environments on Atoma Prime.

Players explore dangerous scavenge zones, collecting valuable Tech-Remnants before extraction. The mode incorporates survival elements like radiation and pollution, forcing players to either complete objectives quickly or call an early extraction. Dying after time expires results in losing all rewards.

The expeditions also include roaming enemy patrols, encouraging stealth and tactical approaches rather than typical close-quarters combat. While not fully procedurally generated, the randomized elements aim to provide a fresh experience each run.

This update offers a new dimension to *Darktide*’s gameplay, expanding the environments and challenges while maintaining the game’s core grim tone and tactical difficulty.

The Simpsons Executive Producer Comments on Hit & Run Revival Possibility

Matt Selman, executive producer of *The Simpsons*, discussed the possibility of reviving the cult-favorite 2003 game *Hit & Run* in a recent interview. While nothing has been confirmed, Selman acknowledged that those involved are aware of fan interest in a remaster or revival, saying "never say never."

Selman expressed a particular fondness for *Hit & Run*, which gained a strong following for its exploration of Springfield and engaging gameplay. He also noted his preference for the 2007 game released after the Simpsons movie due to its storytelling.

The comments are notable as they represent one of the few indications from official sources that a return to this well-regarded but long-dormant game might be possible, though no concrete plans have emerged.

Just Cause Creator’s New Open-City Game “Samson” Faces Skepticism Ahead of Release

*Samson*, an upcoming open-city action game from Liquid Swords, founded by *Just Cause* creator Cristofer Sundberg, is set to release on April 8. The game centers on a protagonist trying to pay off a large debt through various criminal activities in the industrial city of Tyndalston.

*Samson* aims for a tighter gameplay loop than blockbuster GTA titles, introducing daily cash quotas and limited mission points to encourage quick, strategic play.

Though the concept and setting show promise, a recently released gameplay trailer has drawn skepticism due to its underwhelming combat animations and sparse city population. Driving mechanics appear solid, but melee fights lack polish and variety.

How *Samson* performs post-launch will determine whether it can fill the void left by the lack of arcade-style open-world games seen in the 360 era.

Total Chaos Remake Delivers Atmospheric Horror but Struggles with Combat

*Total Chaos*, a remake of a celebrated Doom 2 mod by Sam Prebble, launched on Steam last November. The game blends survival horror with maze-like level design, set in the bleak industrial town of Fort Oasis.
