## PC Gamer Hardware Awards 2025: Highlights and Top Picks

PC Gamer has released its Hardware Awards for 2025, presenting a detailed review of the year's best and worst PC gaming hardware. While 2025 did not mark a standout year for gaming tech innovations, it offered several notable devices across categories such as laptops, monitors, SSDs, and peripherals.

Best Gaming Hardware of 2025

The Razer Blade 16 was crowned best gaming laptop, praised for its slim design and ability to deliver strong performance both plugged in and on battery power. It represents a shift from previous bulkier models towards more versatile, all-purpose machines.

KTC‚Äôs H27T22C-3 took the prize for best gaming monitor, standing out as a budget-friendly 1440p option with a 210Hz refresh rate and a 1ms response time. Its combination of affordability and performance marks progress in accessible gaming displays.

Among handheld gaming PCs, the Lenovo Legion Go S SteamOS won recognition. This device delivers solid performance running SteamOS, trading an often bloated Windows experience for a more console-like, streamlined approach. The Asus ROG Xbox Ally X was highlighted as an honorable competitor with superior raw power but a significantly higher price tag.

The WD Black SN8100 2TB NVMe SSD emerged as the best SSD, showing fast sustained read/write speeds exceeding 14,000 MB/s and efficient temperature management. This highlights the ongoing improvements in PCIe 5.0 storage technology despite rising memory prices.

AMD‚Äôs Ryzen 9 9950X3D took best CPU, combining gaming and content creation power at a premium price. Alongside, the Gigabyte X870 Aorus Elite Ice was awarded best motherboard, offering a balance of features and affordability with future-proof AM5 socket support.

AMD‚Äôs Radeon RX 9070 XT won best graphics card, noted for competitive pricing and solid performance compared to Nvidia's offerings. The card‚Äôs availability at reasonable MSRPs following initial price gouging makes it a meaningful choice in the high-end GPU market.

Notable Peripherals

Be Quiet!‚Äôs Light Mount keyboard was recognized as the quietest gaming keyboard tested, favored for its sound-dampened switches and robust build, making it a daily-use favorite even among reviewers.

The Corsair Sabre V2 Pro Ultralight claimed best gaming mouse status due to its ultralightweight design (36g) and reliable sensor performance, targeting competitive FPS gamers who prioritize speed and precision.

Razer‚Äôs BlackShark V3 headset was chosen as best gaming headset for delivering quality wireless audio with a low-latency mode suited to esports, while the Amazon Basics USB condenser mic won best gaming microphone for its unexpectedly good sound quality at a very low price.

The wildcard award went to Razer‚Äôs Wolverine V3 Pro 8K PC controller, which offers an exceptionally high 8,000Hz polling rate and Hall effect triggers at a premium price, tailored for professional-level gaming.

Top Rated Hardware Reviews and Price Considerations

In 2025, 12% of 289 hardware reviews scored 90% or higher, yet most highly rated products remain costly, reflecting ongoing affordability challenges in PC gaming. Notably, only one top-rated item cost less than $100: the Arctic Freezer 36 CPU cooler, praised for its quiet operation and value.

Graphics cards, despite the significant GPU launches in 2025, were generally absent from the highest review scores due to inflated pricing and availability issues earlier in the year. This underscores the tension between hardware advancements and market realities.

Personal Hardware Pick: 8BitDo Ultimate 2C Wireless Controller

A member of the PC Gamer hardware team shared a personal highlight: the 8BitDo Ultimate 2C wireless controller. Chosen for its compact design suited to smaller hands and its lilac color matching personal gear, it also offers Hall effect buttons and remappable controls at an accessible price around $30.

Despite its plastic build and less premium feel under stress, the controller balances cost, performance, and aesthetics in a way that appeals to casual gamers. Its design evokes the Nintendo Switch Pro controller but at a fraction of the cost, providing an alternative for players who prioritize comfort and style over pro-level specs.
