Betty Boop, Blondie, and Early Mickey Mouse Enter Public Domain

Several classic animations and comics have entered the public domain as of 2026. While this opens the door for new creative works featuring these characters, it also comes with legal complexities regarding trademarks and later character designs. The public domain status could potentially inspire new adaptations or games, though experience suggests a mix of serious and low-quality reinterpretations. Creators interested in using these characters will need to navigate the nuances of copyright and trademark law, particularly regarding design elements developed after the original works.
