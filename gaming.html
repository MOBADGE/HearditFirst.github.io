Marathon South Maintenance Terminal Location Guide

A new contract in Bungie's online first-person extraction shooter *Marathon* tasks players with finding the data download terminal inside the south Maintenance area. This contract, part of the Order/Chaos mission granted by the MIDA faction, becomes available after completing an earlier task to destroy 15 windows. While the mission appears straightforward, the final objective—locating the terminal amidst player combat—can be challenging. A detailed guide has been published to help players navigate to the exact terminal location to progress in the game. This information is helpful for players looking to efficiently complete faction contracts and advance within *Marathon*.

Pokémon Pokopia: Waking Mosslax and Food Effects

In *Pokémon Pokopia*, players encounter a sleeping Mosslax whose tendency to remain asleep creates challenges in the game’s story. To wake it, players need to use light in the Bleak Beach area, accessible after leveling up sufficiently in the Withering Wastelands. Once awake and with the Pokémon Center issues addressed, players can feed Mosslax various foods like berries and crops. Different foods grant Mosslax buffs that indirectly benefit the player, such as increasing the chance to find rare items. However, the exact effects of each food can be unclear, so new guides clarify both the steps to awaken Mosslax and the benefits tied to its diet. This helps players make strategic choices in managing their Mosslax for improved gameplay rewards.

Humble Boomer Shooter Bundle Released

Humble Bundle has launched the "Best of Boomer Shooters Penta Boom 5," which features up to seven fast-paced first-person shooters reminiscent of classic titles like *Duke Nukem*, *Quake*, and *Doom*. Purchasers can pay what they want with tiered rewards—the more paid, the more games included—and proceeds support the ocean conservation charity Oceana. Notable in the bundle is *Sulfur*, a roguelite shooter where players take on the role of a gunslinger fighting goblins using various weapons. The bundle offers value for fans of the shooter subgenre by providing multiple games at a reduced price while supporting charity efforts.

Refunds and Lawsuit Planned for Chained Echoes Physical Edition Delays

*Chained Echoes*, a 16-bit style RPG well-regarded by critics and players, is facing issues fulfilling its Kickstarter promise to deliver physical editions of the game. While digital releases and some physical tiers have shipped, many backers who purchased physical copies through publisher First Press Games remain unpaid after years of delay. The order page for preorders remains active despite the lack of shipments. Frustrated by the situation, creator Matthias Linda announced plans to offer refunds to affected backers and to file a lawsuit against First Press Games. This highlights ongoing concerns about Kickstarter campaign fulfillment, particularly surrounding third-party physical edition publishers.

Intel Announces New Gaming CPUs

Intel has introduced two new CPUs in its Arrow Lake family aimed at gaming PCs: the Core Ultra 5 250K Plus and the Core Ultra 7 270K Plus. The Core Ultra 5 250K Plus features 18 cores (8 performance, 12 efficiency) with a boost clock of 5.3GHz, while the Core Ultra 7 270K Plus features 24 total cores (8 performance, 16 efficiency) with a boost clock of 5.5GHz. Notably, the Core Ultra 7 270K Plus matches the performance of Intel’s previous flagship model but at a more affordable price point. These releases represent Intel’s effort to compete with AMD in gaming CPU performance through competitive specs and pricing, which may influence hardware choices for gaming builds.

Microsoft to Celebrate Xbox 25th Anniversary with Classic Games

Microsoft revealed plans to commemorate the 25th anniversary of the Xbox by offering "new ways to play" classic titles from the system’s history. The announcement was part of a focus on game preservation, with Microsoft’s dedicated game preservation team working to keep backward compatibility spanning four generations of Xbox alive. Specific details were not provided, but the timing aligns with upcoming releases like the *Halo* remaster and a new *Gears of War* title. This initiative underscores Microsoft's ongoing commitment to supporting legacy games for both new and returning players.
