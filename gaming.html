**Crimson Desert Sales Success Amid Control Complaints and Intel GPU Issues**

Pearl Abyss announced that their open-world action game *Crimson Desert* has sold over 2 million copies worldwide within 24 hours of release. This strong commercial performance comes despite the game's mixed reception, particularly concerning its complex control scheme and restrictive inventory system. Players have expressed frustration over the convoluted and unintuitive controls, which complicate basic interactions such as fishing, menu navigation, and combat targeting. The developer acknowledged this feedback publicly, promising to listen closely and work on improvements to enhance player experience. A day-one patch addressing minor tweaks and performance was already deployed, but significant changes like control remapping and UI updates are expected to take more time.

Adding to the launch challenges, *Crimson Desert* does not support Intel Arc graphics cards. The developer’s official FAQ now advises players with Intel GPUs to request refunds due to the game not booting on such hardware. This lack of support was not disclosed prior to release and has drawn criticism as a rare instance where a GPU brand is actively blocked. While the Intel Arc market share remains small, the decision leaves those users without options unless they use cloud gaming services like GeForce Now.

**New Black Library App Offers Eight Free Warhammer Books and Audiobooks**

Games Workshop is retiring the Black Library website in favor of a new mobile app for accessing their Warhammer novels and audiobooks. While the app only allows reading and listening within its own interface, removing the option for DRM-free downloads, the company is encouraging adoption by offering eight free books and audiobooks if the app is downloaded by April 20. The free selection spans multiple Warhammer universes—40,000, Age of Sigmar, Horus Heresy, and Fantasy—and includes notable works such as *Realmslayer*, an audio drama featuring actor Brian Blessed as Gotrek, and a collection of *Ciaphas Cain* stories. Existing Black Library users can link accounts to transfer prior purchases easily. The new app represents a shift towards a more controlled content delivery platform, balancing convenience against DRM constraints.

**Smaller Gaming Mouse Manufacturers Deliver Streamlined Software Experience**

Recent analysis highlights that smaller gaming mouse brands tend to offer software that better fits user needs compared to larger manufacturers’ bulky and over-complicated apps. Brands like Pulsar and Mchose provide both web- and locally-based applications that are responsive, easy to navigate, and consume less system memory. These apps focus primarily on essential features like DPI settings, polling rates, button mapping, and firmware updates, with additional advanced options such as sensor rotation and lift-off distance. This contrasts with some large-brand software that includes unnecessary elements like device ecosystems or ads. Users benefit from software that doesn’t run constantly and allows setting profiles to be saved directly to the mouse’s onboard memory. The trend signals a potential path toward less intrusive, more effective peripheral management tools.

**Indie Developers Collaborate After Accidental Games Name Clash**

Two independent studios, Neon Polygons and Gamkat, coincidentally released games named *Piece by Piece* within days of each other—a puzzle platformer and a cozy repair shop simulator respectively. Rather than conflict, the developers connected amicably and decided to embrace the coincidence by creating a joint Steam bundle offering both games at a discount. This friendly collaboration was welcomed by fans and seen as a positive example of cooperation in a crowded indie market where over 19,000 new Steam games appeared last year. Both teams expressed mutual respect and appreciation for how the situation resolved.

**Space Marine 2 Chapter Voice Pack DLC Made Free Following Player Backlash**

Saber Interactive has made the *Space Marine 2* Chapter Voice Pack 1 DLC free following widespread negative feedback. Originally priced at $5, the DLC added new voice lines and facial options but suffered criticism due to poor implementation, such as default voices overriding key story dialogue. Developers apologized for not meeting player expectations, committed to delivering quality content regardless of cost, and are processing refunds for purchasers. Whether similar voice packs will be released in the future remains unclear, as the DLC will no longer be monetized.
