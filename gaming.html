Slay the Spire Hits New Steam Player Peak Nearly Seven Years After Launch

Almost seven years after its initial release, the deckbuilding roguelike Slay the Spire has set a new record for concurrent players on Steam. Over the 2025 holiday season, the game surpassed its previous peak of around 33,000 players from early access in 2018, reaching over 57,000 concurrent users briefly before settling near 35,000. This surge is likely driven by the game's historic discount to $2.49 during the Steam winter sale, making it highly accessible, alongside anticipation for the forthcoming Slay the Spire 2. The original title continues to garner attention for its innovative gameplay and enduring appeal within the deckbuilder genre.

FPS Genre Experiences a Shift Toward Casual and Cooperative Play in 2025

The first-person shooter genre showed a noticeable change in player preferences throughout 2025. The emphasis on high-skill competitive play and elaborate ranked systems has lessened, replaced by more accessible, fun-focused experiences. Battlefield 6's success without ranked modes represents this shift, appealing to players desiring large-scale battles with less pressure. Additionally, games like Arc Raiders introduced cooperative elements that encourage teamwork over competition. There was notable backlash against aggressive monetization and poorly matched cosmetics, prompting publishers like Activision and EA to adjust their approaches. Popular shooters are adding modes that lower skill barriers, and co-op shooters like Helldivers 2 and Warhammer 40,000: Darktide continue to retain dedicated audiences. Overall, 2025 highlighted evolving player desires for FPS games that balance fun with lower emotional investment.
