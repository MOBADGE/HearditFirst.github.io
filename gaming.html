Marathon Updates Pinwheel Loot Spot, Raises Difficulty and Rewards

Bungie has updated the Outpost map in Marathon with patch 1.0.5.2, introducing significant changes to the popular loot area known as Pinwheel. The previously disabled Destroyed Wing entrance is back, but accessing the locked room now requires three red security cards instead of two reds and a yellow, and players face waves of Ghost Commanders when entering. Additionally, strongboxes inside move between runs, and the final extraction points are randomized, increasing the challenge for players.

To compensate for the increased difficulty, Bungie improved the quality of loot rewarded at Pinwheel. Players report higher-value items such as Cryo Archive keys, Prestige-tier implants, and advanced weapons. However, solo players have expressed frustration, noting that the tougher access and enemy waves make Pinwheel runs more punishing without a squad. Overall, the update balances greater risk with richer rewards, but may create uneven experiences for solo versus group players.

Cozy Games April Lineup Focuses on Relaxation, No Farm Sims Included

The cozy games scheduled to release in April emphasize relaxed, low-pressure gameplay, with a range of hidden object puzzles, diorama builders, and emotional adventures. Notably absent this month are any farm or life simulation games, which have typically been staples in cozy game genres.

Highlights include the co-op off-the-grid base builder Outbound launching on April 23, currently among Steam’s top wishlisted titles. Other releases offer players a chance to unwind at their own pace without fail states or timed objectives. The lineup aims to provide a calm gaming experience perfect for extended periods of indoor downtime.

Stormgate Ends Online Multiplayer Due to Server Partner Sale

Frost Giant Studios announced that Stormgate, the StarCraft-like RTS that reached full release in 2025, will end its online multiplayer support at the end of April. This is due to Hathora, the company providing Stormgate’s server infrastructure, being acquired by an AI firm and discontinuing game hosting services. Although Frost Giant hopes to restore online play with a future partner, no timeline exists for that.

Despite an initially strong Kickstarter and investor funding, Stormgate struggled with low player counts and mixed reviews since launch. The loss of online modes leaves only offline campaign and solo content playable going forward. For a multiplayer-focused RTS, this change significantly restricts its core appeal.

Intel’s Upcoming Low-Power Core 300 Laptop Chips May Drop Regular E-Cores

Leaked specifications for Intel’s next generation of mobile processors—codenamed Wildcat Lake—suggest that these Core 300 series chips will feature only performance cores and low-power LPE cores, omitting the previous generation’s regular E-cores. The lineup ranges from Core 3 to Core 7 models, all with a 35W turbo TDP and two performance cores plus four LPE cores.

While designed for thinner, lower-cost laptops rather than direct Panther Lake successors, the chips maintain decent clock speeds around 4.4 to 4.8 GHz. Graphics capabilities are modest, with up to two Xe cores, indicating limited gaming potential. The shift reflects Intel’s evolving approach to balancing efficiency and performance in budget mobile devices.

Apple MacBook Neo Gains Significant Performance Boost with Custom Water Cooling

Tech enthusiasts have demonstrated that the Apple MacBook Neo, powered by the six-core A18 Pro chip, can achieve notably higher performance with a custom water cooling loop. Currently, the Neo runs its GPU at around 105 °C under load, limiting thermal headroom.

YouTubers Jakkuh and Zip Tie Tech replaced the stock thermal pad with thicker materials, then developed a complex water cooling system involving a copper plate, acrylic water block, tubing connected to an automotive power steering cooler, and a modified laptop chassis. This setup improved multi-core and GPU benchmark scores by up to 20%, setting unofficial performance records for the A18 Pro GPU.

Though the mod adds weight and bulk and remains impractical for most users, it highlights the inherent thermal limits of the Neo’s design and the performance potential of Apple’s mobile silicon under better cooling.
