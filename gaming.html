Windrose: Comprehensive Discovery Guide and Tortuga Buyer Location

Windrose, the pirate-themed survival crafting game currently in early access, features three main biomes—Coastal Jungle, Foothills, and Cursed Swamps—each with unique items and challenges. A recent detailed guide compiles all the items players might miss in each region, along with the quests and bosses necessary to progress. Notable early obstacles include tricky-to-find items like Crab Shells and certain seeds in the Coastal Jungle, or Corn in the Foothills.

Advancement in Windrose requires completing key quests such as defeating bosses Thomas Richards and Israel Hands, which unlock new crafting recipes and exploration areas. The Cursed Swamps offer the toughest survival challenge at present, with attention needed to avoid plague zones. Additionally, players seeking to sell goods to the People of Tortuga faction should note that their buyer is not located on the island of Tortuga itself but on a nearby island accessible after completing specific quests. This clarification helps players avoid wasting time searching the wrong location.

This detailed breakdown helps newcomers navigate the game's procedurally generated world and understand progression requirements, improving the overall survival and crafting experience.

Slay the Spire 2 Prioritizes Quality Over Deadlines

Slay the Spire 2, which launched in early access in March and has seen significant player engagement, continues development without fixed deadlines. Developer Mega Crit announced upcoming features including new alternate acts, a new character, Steam Workshop support, experimental game modes, additional cards, events, relics, and potions, plus an eventual true victory condition.

Co-founder Casey Yano explained that the small team values maintaining a healthy development pace and creative flexibility over adhering to firm deadlines. This approach aims to avoid rushed, subpar work that could diminish the game’s quality, a philosophy summed up by their rejection of “Sloppy Spire 2.” Players can try beta features early to provide feedback on experimental updates. While this means no specific release dates are set, it reflects a development style focused on polish and player experience rather than speed.

EU Parliament Hearing Shows Support for Stop Killing Games Initiative

The Stop Killing Games campaign, which opposes the shutdown of server-dependent games once they cease profitability, recently had a hearing before the European Parliament’s Internal Market and Consumer Protection committees. The founders and consultants presented arguments emphasizing that game shutdowns are usually business decisions rather than technical necessities.

The hearing received positive responses from members across political groups, with several affirming support for legislation addressing the issue. The campaign stressed that maintaining servers post-launch is financially manageable and beneficial for preserving game access. Though the hearing marks only an initial step in the legislative process, attendees viewed the results as encouraging momentum towards greater protections for online games and players. Stop Killing Games also plans further advocacy and NGO formation in both the EU and US.

US Bill Proposes Mandatory Age Verification at OS Level, Raising Privacy Concerns

A new bill named the "Parents Decide Act" has been introduced to the US House, proposing mandatory age verification for all users of operating systems (Windows, Linux, MacOS) during setup and use. The legislation would require OS providers to verify users’ birthdates and share this information with app developers as necessary for app-level age checks.

Key compliance details, including the verification methods and data protection standards, are to be determined post-passage by the House Committee on Energy and Commerce. The bill’s broad language raises concerns about user privacy, data security, and potential impacts on open-source OS distributions that may lack infrastructure to handle such verification securely.

Critics warn this could erode digital privacy and complicate everyday computing, as well as possibly requiring online access for basic OS functions. The proposal follows similar laws in California and continues the trend toward greater regulation of digital age controls.

Clair Obscur: Expedition 33 Wins Best Game at 2026 BAFTA Games Awards

French RPG Clair Obscur: Expedition 33 won the Best Game award at the 2026 BAFTA Games Awards, marking continued recognition following its previous success at other major shows. However, unlike earlier events where it dominated, Clair Obscur took home three awards in total: Best Game, Best Debut Game, and Best Performer in a Leading Role for Jennifer English.

Other notable winners included Dispatch with three awards, and Death Stranding 2 taking Artistic Achievement. The variety of winners contrasts with Clair Obscur's prior award sweeps, reflecting strong competition
