**Developer Ivy Road to Close After Funding Challenges**

Ivy Road, the studio behind the 2025 cozy tea shop adventure game *Wanderstop*, announced it will officially shut down on March 31, 2026. The team cited difficulties in securing funding for their new project, *Engine Angel*, which led to layoffs earlier this year and ultimately the decision to close. Despite efforts to find a publishing partner for *Engine Angel*, the studio was unable to land a deal amid a challenging market for raising game funds. *Wanderstop*, written by notable creators Davey Wrenden and Karla Zimonja with music by C418, will remain available for purchase. Ivy Road also hinted at a forthcoming announcement related to expanding the reach of *Wanderstop*, in collaboration with Annapurna Interactive. The closure highlights ongoing funding difficulties facing indie developers in the current game industry climate.

**ZA/UM Avoids Police Themes in Zero Parades Development**

The makers of *Disco Elysium* have shared insights on the creative process behind their new RPG, *Zero Parades*. The team initially considered a plot involving a fictional equivalent of the International Money Fund, which risked aligning the story with police or detective narratives similar to *Disco Elysium*. However, given shifts in public perception of law enforcement since *Disco Elysium*'s 2019 release, the developers chose to pursue a different genre focus, ultimately settling on a spy thriller. Principal writer Siim Sinamäe noted a more cynical outlook towards authority figures influenced the decision, while writer Honey Watson emphasized the political urgency and ideological themes shaping the new game's espionage story. This marks a deliberate departure to explore fresh narrative territory while maintaining the studio's strength in rich writing.

**Frictional Games Bundle Offers Six Horror Titles for $15**

Humble Bundle is offering a package including every major horror game from Frictional Games—for $15 until April 1, 2027. The collection includes all three *Penumbra* titles, *Amnesia: The Dark Descent*, its two sequels (*A Machine for Pigs* and *Rebirth*), *Amnesia: The Bunker*, and *SOMA*. This deal presents an opportunity to experience the evolution of Frictional’s atmospheric horror, from the older *Penumbra* series through the modern *Amnesia* games, to the science fiction horror of *SOMA*. The bundle is available via Steam keys and is noted for providing significant value for fans of the genre or newcomers interested in landmark horror games.

**Path of Exile Trading Community Hit by Ban of Major Account**

The leading member of The Forbidden Trove (TFT), a prominent Path of Exile player trading Discord server, has been banned from the game by Grinding Gear Games. Known as Jenebu, this player held an extensive stash with over 1,500 rare items, many of which cannot be replicated. TFT operated significant mirror services that allowed item duplication using the rare Mirror of Kalandra, reliant on community trust. Jenebu’s ban has caused uncertainty about the future of TFT and has generated debate among players, with some supporting the ban due to concerns over third-party trading platforms linked to real-money trading. While no evidence of illicit activity has been publicly presented, this action disrupts a key part of PoE’s player-driven economy and may accelerate changes brought by official in-game trading updates.

**Deus Ex: Human Revolution’s Original Composer Passed Over for Soundtrack Contribution**

Alexander Brandon, composer of the original *Deus Ex* soundtrack, revealed that although he was invited to contribute a track for *Deus Ex: Human Revolution* (2011), his work was ultimately rejected for not fitting the project’s vision. Eidos Montreal, taking ownership of the series, wanted to establish a distinct audio identity and hired Michael McCann for the soundtrack. Brandon composed a remake of the original theme for possible inclusion in the end credits but agreed with the decision to not use it in retrospect. He also expressed disappointment at not being involved in the mobile spinoff *Deus Ex: The Fall*. Despite this, Brandon respects the creative direction and recognizes McCann’s soundtrack as well-suited to the game’s tone. He also shared challenges he faces in the current industry job market.
