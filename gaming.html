Diablo 4 Celebrates April Fools' Day with a Giant Chicken Boss

Blizzard marked April Fools' Day in Diablo 4 by introducing "Chik'han, The Fowl Beast," a large chicken-themed world boss. Unlike typical events focused on crafting materials or standard legendaries, this boss drops a range of uniquely themed items such as the "Cluckonomicon" staff and the "Eggcecutioner" scythe. These weapons come with unusual effects, including a buff called "Henlightenment" and the ability to make slain enemies drop eggs. Players can earn an achievement titled "April Fowl's Day 2026" for defeating Chik'han, suggesting that this boss may return in future events. The event provides a rare, humorous diversion in Diablo 4’s currently modest event schedule, offering players a fresh reason to log in.

Nintendo’s Pokémon Battling Patent Faces Revocation by the US Patent Office

The United States Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO) has issued a nonfinal decision rejecting all claims in Nintendo’s controversial patent (US patent 12,403,397) related to a system for battling summoned characters. This patent, which had attracted significant criticism from industry experts and players alike, described a highly specific battle mechanic involving summoned sub-characters with automated combat sequences. The USPTO’s examiner concluded that the patent’s claims were too similar to prior art and therefore not patentably non-obvious. Nintendo now has an opportunity to respond before a final ruling, and could still appeal the decision. This ongoing legal scrutiny reflects growing concern over the scope and enforcement of videogame patents.

Garry’s Mod Successor s&box to Launch on Steam April 28

Facepunch Studios’ sandbox game s&box, designed as a successor to the influential Garry’s Mod, will launch on Steam on April 28. This launch date became known quietly through an update to the Steam page and SteamDB, without a formal announcement. A notable aspect of s&box is its licensing deal with Valve, allowing creators to develop and release standalone games using its tools on Steam without sharing revenue with Facepunch. Pricing details remain tentative, but the game is expected to cost between $10 and $20. This move aims to expand the range of Source 2 engine games and support user-generated content in a low-barrier environment.

Black Ops 7 Introduces Smallest Map in Franchise History as April Fools’ Joke

Treyarch created an actual playable version of an April Fools’ joke for Black Ops 7 with a new map codenamed "GRWM," a diminutive setting roughly the size of a walk-in closet. The map supports an 8-player free-for-all mode, compressing typical Call of Duty gameplay into an extremely confined space with little room for tactical movement. Despite initial skepticism, the map offers fast-paced combat and is efficient for XP grinding, allowing rapid progression within short matches. It is likely that this map will be available only briefly as part of the April Fools’ festivities.

Marathon’s Popular Loot Area Modified, Increasing Difficulty and Rewards

Bungie updated the popular Pinwheel loot location in Marathon’s Outpost map, making it significantly harder to access. Changes include reinstating the previously disabled Destroyed Wing entrance with additional entry requirements, increasing the number of red security cards needed, and adding waves of enemies guarding the entrance. Loot placement now varies per run, and exfil points spawn randomly, further complicating escapes. In exchange for the heightened challenge, loot quality has increased substantially, with reports of high-tier items regularly found. The update has drawn mixed reactions from the player base, especially among solo players who find the new difficulty a barrier, while groups appear more capable of managing the changes.
