AMD Market Cap Hits Record High Amid Strong Financial Performance

AMD’s market capitalization recently surpassed $450 billion, the highest in its 54-year history since going public in 1972. This rise follows Intel’s market cap crossing $300 billion for the first time since 2000, reflecting renewed investor interest in chip manufacturers. AMD reported strong financial results for 2025, with a 34% increase in revenue to $34.6 billion and a 27% rise in operating income. Much of AMD’s appeal to investors stems from its growing role in supplying accelerators for AI data centers—a sector currently dominated by Nvidia but with opportunities for AMD and Intel to expand. The company’s focus on AI-optimized products suggests confidence in sustaining this momentum.

Valve’s Proton 11 Beta Expands Linux Game Compatibility Including Deadly Premonition

Valve released the first beta version of Proton 11, the Windows-to-Linux compatibility layer integral to running many Steam games on Linux systems and Steam Deck. This update integrates Wine 11 and introduces an experimental Arm64 build designed for Valve’s upcoming Steam Frame VR headset. Proton 11 significantly expands the list of playable titles on Linux, most notably adding support for the cult classic Deadly Premonition, which previously suffered from a problematic PC port. The update also resolves compatibility issues with several EA games, addresses various game-specific bugs, and improves features like Steam overlay and controller support. This release strengthens Valve’s ongoing efforts to make Linux a more viable platform for gaming.

Skyrim Mod Adds Unique Sitting Animations to Jarls, Enhancing Character Distinction

A Skyrim mod titled "Unique Jarl Throne Sitting Animation," created by modder daubuoi, enhances the behavior of the game’s jarls—the rulers of Skyrim’s nine holds—by giving each a distinct throne-sitting animation. The vanilla game uses a single generic sitting pose for all jarls, but this mod assigns unique animations that reflect their individual personality and status. All except one jarl retain a new pose tailored to their character. This change contributes to the immersion and depth of Skyrim’s world by adding subtle but meaningful differentiation among important NPCs. It is part of a broader trend in the modding community to refine small details for a richer player experience.

Marathon’s New Mercy Kit Sparks Unintended PvP Exploits

Bungie’s FPS Marathon recently received a mid-season update introducing the Mercy Kit, an item intended to promote player cooperation by allowing revival of downed enemies. However, players have found ways to misuse the Mercy Kit to revive opponents only to kill them again immediately, effectively enabling double kills and griefing. This exploit has led to criticism of the Mercy Kit as a "troll-friendly" tool that encourages unsportsmanlike behavior rather than fostering cooperation. While some cooperative matches have occurred, the prevailing player approach remains competitive and aggressive. This raises questions about how game mechanics designed to encourage teamwork might be subverted in PvP environments.

Intel’s New Drivers Improve Crimson Desert Performance for Some Arc GPUs

Intel released graphics driver version 32.0.101.8724, which addresses flickering and corruption issues in Crimson Desert for users with Battlemage (B-series) Arc GPUs and equivalent integrated GPUs. Previously, Intel’s Arc graphics cards had limited support for the game, and players using these cards experienced visual glitches or were unable to run the game properly. Although the update improves stability and visual fidelity for newer Arc GPUs, first-generation Arc cards like the A770 are still affected by these problems. Intel and developer Pearl Abyss continue to work towards full Arc GPU support, but a complete fix dependent on game patches is still pending.

Among Us Surpasses One Billion Downloads on Google Play Store

In a notable milestone, the social deduction game Among Us has exceeded one billion downloads on the Google Play Store. This makes it more widely downloaded on the platform than popular titles such as Minecraft, Stardew Valley, and Terraria. The developer acknowledged the achievement with a lighthearted message, noting the scale of the player base. While some downloads may be repeat installs or shared devices, the figure underlines the game’s enduring popularity since its initial release over half a decade ago. Among Us helped bring social deduction gameplay into mainstream awareness and remains influential in the genre.
