Slay the Spire 2 Responds to Steam Backlash and Prepares New Game Modes

Mega Crit co-founder Casey Yano addressed the intense negative response on Steam following a controversial beta balance patch for *Slay the Spire 2*. Despite over 9,000 negative reviews in 24 hours, mostly from players in China, Yano was unbothered by the volume and tone of feedback. He explained that players often resort to Steam reviews because alternative feedback channels like forums or Discord are restricted or inaccessible in some regions, notably China. Yano emphasized the importance of improving communication methods to better hear and incorporate player feedback globally.

Additionally, Yano revealed that Mega Crit is exploring three new game modes in development. These modes aim to cater to different player interests: one focused on competitive play, another offering a condensed experience for those short on time, and a third enabling new forms of social or multiplayer interaction based on current systems. Yano cautioned that not all ideas make it to release but highlighted the ongoing efforts to expand how players engage with the game during its early access phase.

Games Workshop’s Warhammer 40,000 Musical: An Elaborate April Fool’s Joke

Games Workshop released *The Emperor Protects: A Warhammer 40,000 Musical* as a detailed and committed April Fool’s prank, which was still notable a day after April 1. The musical features fully costumed Space Marines and other characters performing original songs that reflect the Warhammer universe. A behind-the-scenes video presents the production as a real theatrical project, complete with rehearsals and producer commentary.

Despite being a joke, the elaborate presentation and high production value have convinced some in the community that a genuine event or continuation might happen next year. The prank has sparked enthusiasm among fans, many expressing interest in attending if it ever becomes an actual show.

Revisiting Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2019

*Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2019* is seeing renewed attention following a significant Steam sale. The game, praised at launch for its realistic approach to shooter mechanics, detailed environments, and sound design, still holds up well in 2026. Its more constrained weapon customization system encourages distinct gunplay styles, offering a contrast to recent entries that lean heavily on extensive attachments with few downsides.

The article reflects on how Modern Warfare 2019 marked a turning point before the series shifted towards integrating the Warzone battle royale and embracing a larger focus on cosmetics and crossovers. For many, it remains the last "pure" Call of Duty experience before these changes, showing the challenges the franchise faces in balancing tradition with evolving player demands.

Shift in Direction for Warren Spector’s Thick as Thieves

Otherside Entertainment announced a change in *Thick as Thieves*, Warren Spector’s multiplayer spiritual successor to the *Thief* series. Originally planned as a four-player PvPvE game mixing human and AI opponents, development is now focusing on singleplayer and two-player cooperative modes. The shift aims to strengthen the stealth gameplay and deliver a better overall experience.

While some fans of the initial concept expressed disappointment, the studio indicated that PvPvE may return later. The new focus is seen as a practical decision, given the niche appeal of immersive sims and the complexities of implementing a live service multiplayer design. A gameplay reveal is scheduled for April 9 at the Triple-I Initiative showcase.

MechWarrior Studio Piranha Games Lays Off 30% of Staff

Piranha Games, developer of *MechWarrior 5: Mercenaries*, confirmed layoffs affecting approximately 30% of its workforce. CEO Russ Bullock acknowledged the difficult decision and confirmed ongoing work on upcoming DLC scheduled for May, as well as another MechWarrior product planned for later in the year. Narrative director Chris Lowrey commented on the challenges in sustaining long-term support for the single-purchase game but affirmed the team’s commitment to the franchise for as long as possible.

These layoffs follow a previous round in early 2025 tied to unmet sales expectations. The situation underscores the volatility in the mid-sized developer space, particularly for long-running but niche franchises.
