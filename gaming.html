Scottish Tribunal Denies Interim Pay for Fired Rockstar Developers

A Scottish employment tribunal has rejected an interim relief request from over 30 former Rockstar employees who were dismissed in October 2025. The fired workers and their union, the Independent Workers of Great Britain (IWGB), allege unlawful dismissals linked to union activities and organizing efforts. Rockstar initially cited "gross misconduct" and leaks of confidential information as reasons for the terminations.

The tribunal ruled that the former employees did not sufficiently prove the likelihood of unfair dismissal at this stage. However, the decision does not preclude a finding in favor of the employees during the full hearing. The firings sparked protests and drew attention from the UK government, with some officials expressing concern and calling for better protections for workers. The IWGB remains optimistic about their case moving forward, emphasizing that interim relief has a high legal threshold.

Arc Raiders‚Äô Aim Assist Strengthens with Higher Frame Rates

Players of the third-person shooter Arc Raiders have noticed that controller aim assist becomes significantly stronger at higher frame rates. Tests confirmed that at around 300 fps, the aim assist locks onto targets far more aggressively and maintains tracking longer than at standard frame rates like 30 or 60 fps. This behavior gives players with high-performance PCs and controller setups an advantage in aiming.

The aim assist sometimes causes the reticle to move without player input, and breaking its lock requires substantial thumbstick movement. At lower frame rates, aim assist is weaker and less consistent. For players with capable hardware, reducing graphics settings to increase frame rates can result in markedly improved aim assistance. The findings highlight how performance can impact game mechanics in subtle but meaningful ways.
