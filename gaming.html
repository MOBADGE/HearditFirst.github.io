Arc Raiders Achievement Made Easier for Players with Acrophobia

Players of the third-person shooter *Arc Raiders* have found a simpler method to earn one of the game‚Äôs toughest achievements, Death From Above. Normally, this achievement requires dealing at least 50 damage to an enemy while standing atop a live Rocketeer enemy, a flying robot that typically forces players into difficult and risky maneuvers. However, Reddit user WoWAltoholic revealed that the achievement can also be earned by standing on the remains of a *dead* Rocketeer and dealing damage from there, bypassing the need to climb a live one.

Bandcamp Implements Ban on AI-Generated Music Content

Music marketplace Bandcamp has introduced a firm policy banning music and audio that are generated fully or substantially by AI, as well as any AI use that impersonates other artists or styles. This policy was announced amid growing concerns about the influx of AI-created music flooding the market, following notable cases like the AI-generated band The Velvet Sundown, which amassed large listener counts despite lacking genuine human creators.

Bandcamp's move contrasts with services like Spotify, which are adopting more lenient approaches focused on AI disclosure rather than outright bans. The new policy on Bandcamp aims to preserve human artistry and prevent deceptive or low-quality AI content. The decision also underscores ongoing debates in creative industries over the role of AI and the importance of transparency regarding its use.

Framework Increases Prices for Desktops and Mainboards Amid Rising Memory Costs

Framework, known for its modular laptops, has announced price increases on its Desktop and Mainboard products due to rapidly rising memory costs from suppliers. Earlier, the company raised laptop memory prices but had avoided raising prices on desktop components until now.

The most affected configurations are those using 128 GB of RAM, which have seen price hikes exceeding $400. Framework states it will honor existing pre-order prices and that these increases are necessary to cover higher component costs. The company also warns that memory prices are unlikely to decrease soon, forecasting a challenging market for consumers throughout 2026 and possibly beyond.

Steam Removes Community Clown Award and Changes Points System

Valve has updated Steam‚Äôs community awards system, removing the infamous clown (jester) award and eliminating Steam points gained from giving or receiving awards. The clown award had been widely criticized for encouraging negative or trolling behavior, as users frequently awarded it to content they found offensive or foolish.

The new system reduces the number of awards available to twelve, all intended to represent positive or neutral reactions, and no longer provides points, removing previous incentives for attention-seeking posts. While this change may not transform community behavior entirely, it aims to decrease toxic interactions and lower the reward for low-quality content in Steam forums, guides, and reviews.
