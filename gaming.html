Renegade X Reaches Version 1.0 After Nearly Two Decades

The fan-made spiritual successor to Command & Conquer: Renegade, Renegade X, has formally reached version 1.0 after about 20 years of development. Initially released as a playable beta in 2014, the full version now includes refined bug fixes, balance adjustments, and a new map called CnC-City, completing the adaptation of the original game's locations.

Renegade X offers large-scale, team-based FPS combat with vehicles and superweapons, supporting both online multiplayer and bot play. It also features a single-player campaign named Black Dawn.

The developer Totem Arts is continuing work on another project, Firestorm, a reimagining of Command & Conquer: Tiberian Sun, which promises similarly ambitious multiplayer modes and large-scale combat.
