**Menace Offers a Fresh Take on Turn-Based Tactics**

Menace, now in early access, aims to revitalize the turn-based tactics genre. Developed by Overhype Studios, the team behind Battle Brothers, it combines campaign strategy with tactical platoon combat. Players command a crew of space marines on a politically sensitive mission to a lawless planetary system. Sprinkled with elements from board wargames, Menace focuses on suppression, morale, and squad management rather than overwhelming numbers. Each soldier reacts dynamically in combat, and squad members have distinct personalities and skill trees. Supply constraints limit how many troops and equipment can be deployed per mission, encouraging meaningful tactical decisions. Though still early in development, with roughly one-third of its content available, Menace has received praise for accessible yet deep gameplay systems and continuous developer support.

**Airframe Ultra: Racing Meets Competitive Brawls**

The creators of Rain World have introduced Airframe Ultra, a multiplayer cyberpunk hoverbike racer now playable in the Steam Next Fest demo. The game mixes high-speed racing with on-foot combat sections where players dismount to fight enemies in arena-style battles before remounting their bikes. This blend aims to provide competitive multiplayer without reliance on ranked play, battle passes, or engagement hooks. Developers emphasized quick entry into gameplay and have designed complex movement mechanics requiring skill. Though chaotic, the game features a variety of weapons and power-ups, and allows exploratory roaming on foot. Airframe Ultra does not yet have a release date but presents a different take on racing games that could appeal to competitive players seeking variety beyond pure racing.

**Diablo 2 Developers Reflect on Legacy While Building New ARPG**

Original Diablo 2 developers at Moon Beast Productions shared perspectives on the ongoing popularity of Diablo 2, which recently saw a remaster release on Steam with new content. While proud that the 26-year-old game still attracts players, they acknowledge the challenge of competing with their own classic as they develop Darkhaven, a new ARPG with sandbox and survival elements. Darkhaven includes features like deformable terrain, building, mod support, and both solo and multiplayer modes. After four years of development and venture funding, the team released a playable demo alongside a Kickstarter campaign aiming to raise $500,000. The developers emphasize that Darkhaven is not simply another Diablo-like game but seeks to blend traditional ARPG mechanics with dynamic, responsive worlds.
