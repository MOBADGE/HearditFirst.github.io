Microsoft Will Provide FBI Access to BitLocker Encryption Keys Upon Warrant

Microsoft has confirmed it will hand over BitLocker encryption keys to law enforcement agencies such as the FBI when served with a valid warrant. This policy came to light following a case involving three laptops in Guam tied to alleged Covid-related fraud. The affected data was encrypted using BitLocker with keys stored on Microsoft‚Äôs cloud servers‚ÄîMicrosoft complied by providing the keys.

BitLocker, a full-disk encryption tool, typically stores keys either locally or in the cloud by default. Microsoft stresses that users can manage their key storage, but if keys are stored on its servers, the company can be compelled to share them under legal orders. Experts warn that any system where keys are accessible to the provider risks eventual law enforcement access.

This stance contrasts with companies like Apple, which have resisted FBI requests to bypass device encryption, emphasizing user privacy. The ongoing case underscores the privacy implications of cloud-based key storage and suggests that privacy-conscious users seeking to avoid such access may need to consider alternative encryption solutions.

Nightdive CEO Explains Why Immersive Sims Remain Rare

Stephen Kick, CEO of Nightdive Studios, which recently released the System Shock Remake, highlighted the challenges that make immersive sims a rare game genre today. In an interview, he explained that immersive sims have limited financial appeal due to a small, niche audience and complex development cycles.

Kick noted these games often "come together at the 12th hour," meaning the gameplay experience only fully crystallizes late in development, which increases risk for larger publishers. Despite these risks, Nightdive continues to develop such games out of passion and a desire to preserve what it considers an important art form.

This insight sheds light on why immersive sims, beloved by many players for their depth and player choice, remain outliers in a market driven largely by broad appeal and predictable returns.
