**Resident Evil Classics Transformed by BioRand Mod**

A community-driven randomizer mod called BioRand has significantly altered the original Resident Evil trilogy (prior to the remakes), offering players a chaotic and often humorous new experience. Beyond simply reshuffling item and enemy locations, BioRand allows extensive customization such as cross-wiring doors, mixing character models and voice lines, and filling safe zones with enemies. It includes a large roster of alternative characters and enemies across the series, with unique voices and even randomized soundtracks, creating a surreal take on these survival horror staples. Available through its website with options including unofficial HD remasters, the mod is gaining attention for how it subverts expectations and encourages creative play. Versions are also in development for the remakes of Resident Evil 2 and 4, with early testing sign-ups open.

**Major Retro Game ROM Site Myrient to Shut Down Due to Rising Costs**

Myrient, a prominent retro game preservation and ROM distribution site established in 2022, announced it will cease operations on March 31. The primary reasons cited include unsustainable hosting costs surpassing $6,000 per month and insufficient donation revenue. A notable factor is the surge in RAM prices, driven by high demand for AI datacenters, which increased site hosting expenses. Additionally, misuse of the site through third-party download managers has contributed to the closure decision. This shutdown highlights the financial challenges faced by preservation-focused but donation-reliant platforms, independent of legal pressures that often threaten similar services.
