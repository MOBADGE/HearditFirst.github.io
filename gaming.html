Monster Hunter Wilds Patch Shows Tangible Performance Gains

Capcom's latest performance patch for *Monster Hunter Wilds* has received positive feedback from players, marking a notable improvement after nearly a year of technical issues. The update, focused on PC, brought CPU and GPU optimizations alongside new graphics options. Player reports highlight smoother gameplay and consistent frame rates, reversing previous trends where patches often worsened performance.

Early user benchmarks and Steam reviews reflect this positive turn, with some even achieving playable performance on lower-end hardware like the Steam Deck. Although hardware limitations remain a factor, the patch represents a significant step toward resolving longstanding performance problems.

Fatal Frame 2 Remake Approaches with New Demo and Features

Koei Tecmo and Team Ninja are preparing to release a remake of the horror title *Fatal Frame 2: Crimson Butterfly* on March 12, with a demo available March 5 via Steam. The remake introduces new gameplay elements, including alternate endings, additional story content, and new locations such as the Umbral Mound and Eikado Temple.

Significant gameplay updates focus on camera mechanics, with new upgrade paths and special shots using filters that affect ghost combat. The remake also includes a photo mode for capturing in-game moments. Post-launch, a collaboration with *Silent Hill f* brings themed costumes as downloadable content.

Code Vein 2 Review Highlights Fast-Paced Combat and Performance Issues

The recently released *Code Vein 2*, an action RPG with time-travel elements, offers a more accessible combat system compared to traditional "soulslike" games. The title emphasizes quick, hack-and-slash gameplay with AI companions assisting players throughout. Its varied environments and character customization receive praise.

However, the game's narrative and character development are seen as generic and underdeveloped. Additionally, the open-world areas suffer from performance inconsistencies even on above-minimum-spec hardware, with frequent frame rate drops and texture loading delays disrupting the experience.

Out of Action FPS Offers Classic Multiplayer Features and Movement Mechanics

*Out of Action*, a new multiplayer FPS by solo developer Doku Games, entered early access recently. The game features 12-player matches, real server browsers with dedicated servers, loadout customization, and objective-based modes. Priced at $20 without microtransactions, it aims to reintroduce classic FPS elements.

A key aspect of gameplay is its complex movement system including dodges, dives, rolls, wall runs, and double jumps. Weapon handling demands precision with no aim assist and small hitboxes. Though in early development with limited maps and modes, the game has garnered attention for its skill-based combat and community-focused features.
