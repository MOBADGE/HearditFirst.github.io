FBI Reports $21 Billion Lost to Cybercrime in 2025, Highlighting Crypto and AI Scams

The FBI has released new data indicating that U.S. victims lost nearly $21 billion to cybercrime last year, with complaint numbers reaching over one million—an increase of nearly 20% from 2024. Cryptocurrency-related scams accounted for the largest share of losses, with more than 181,000 complaints and about $11 billion stolen. These crimes are partly enabled by the relatively unregulated and complex nature of crypto wallets.

Artificial intelligence (AI) scams, while fewer in number with around 22,000 complaints, caused significant financial damage, tallying close to $893 million in losses. The FBI noted the rise of sophisticated AI-led social engineering attacks, including deepfakes and voice cloning, which scammers use to gain trust and deploy pressure tactics. Experts warn these AI-related cybercrimes are likely to worsen without stronger defenses.

Crimson Desert’s Low Enemy Density Causes Concerns Near Endgame

Players of Pearl Abyss’s open-world action RPG Crimson Desert have raised issues about low enemy density as they near the game’s end. While early gameplay features sparse hostile encounters, players report that combat opportunities dwindle significantly later on, with long stretches of exploration free of enemies.

This scarcity creates challenges for completing combat-focused objectives and farming Abyss Artifacts, which are essential for unlocking character skills and upgrading gear. Some gamers liken this problem to older RPGs where enemy camps or dungeons respawn, offering continuous combat. Fans and critics hope the developer will address this to maintain engagement for endgame players and improve long-term enjoyment.

New Guide Clarifies Samsung QD-OLED Monitor Panel Generations

TFT Central has released updated cheat sheets to help consumers understand the evolving Samsung QD-OLED panel technologies used in gaming monitors. Samsung previously classified panels by generation numbers but has shifted to using the year of release for newer models, adding to confusion alongside differing marketing terms from monitor brands.

Key improvements in the latest panels include the introduction of the third generation of Samsung’s electroluminescent material, HyperEfficient EL 3.0, which reportedly doubles durability with each iteration. This translates to about four times the durability compared to first-generation QD-OLEDs. The guide serves as a useful reference for shoppers concerned about factors like brightness and burn-in over the lifespan of OLED monitors.

South Korea Moves to Address Rising PC Costs with Free PC Distribution

In response to soaring prices for gaming PCs and laptops—driven largely by a global memory supply shortage—the South Korean government has unveiled a plan to provide free computers to low-income students. By the end of 2025, authorities already distributed 4.4 million units and aim to continue expanding support in education.

Alongside subsidies and market regulation efforts, the government is promoting recycling of surplus state agency PCs to reduce waste and improve access. They also intend to guarantee minimum internet speeds for those exceeding their data limits, benefiting millions, especially senior citizens. This initiative reflects a broader view of digital access as a fundamental right in an increasingly AI-driven and online world.

Xbox Controllers Shipped Without Batteries, Microsoft Sends Rechargeable Kits

Microsoft has acknowledged that since December last year, a limited number of Xbox controllers were shipped missing the standard AA batteries. To address this, the company is now compensating affected customers by providing free rechargeable battery packs with USB-C cables.

The rechargeable kits typically retail for about $25 and offer a more sustainable alternative compared to disposable batteries, which have traditionally filled the compartments due to a marketing partnership with Duracell. Microsoft confirms that future Xbox controllers will include batteries by default to ensure players can start gaming immediately out of the box.
