Framework Increases Memory Module Prices Amid Ongoing Market Volatility

Modular laptop maker Framework announced a 50% price increase on its DDR5 memory modules due to sharply rising costs from distributors and suppliers. The company emphasized that existing pre-orders and systems with pre-installed memory will not be impacted immediately. Framework anticipates further price updates across various memory types including DDR5, LPDDR5X, and GDDR, in light of the sustained demand driven largely by AI data center expansion. Framework pointed out that AI servers consume vast amounts of memory, contributing significantly to the constrained supply and cost inflation in the PC hardware market. This follows warnings from other PC manufacturers like Lenovo, HP, and Dell about impending price hikes. The rising prices add pressure on consumers looking to upgrade or build new PCs in the near term.
