**Epic Games Lays Off Over 1,000 Employees Amid Financial Challenges**

Epic Games announced the layoff of more than 1,000 employees as part of significant budget cuts, including slashing over $500 million in contracting and marketing expenses. CEO Tim Sweeney attributed this move to ongoing financial pressures, noting that the company continues "spending significantly more than [it’s] making," despite owning Fortnite and the widely used Unreal Engine. Contributing factors include a downturn in Fortnite engagement and weaker console sales compared to the previous generation. Sweeney stressed that layoffs were not performance-related but a necessary business decision, highlighting that Epic has maintained high hiring standards. The affected staff includes key personnel such as producers, lead artists, engineers, and marketing managers, which raises questions about the company’s capacity to continue innovating in Fortnite and Unreal Engine development.

**Ayaneo Suspends Pre-Orders for Next 2 Handheld Due to Rising Storage Costs**

Ayaneo has temporarily halted pre-orders for its powerful Next 2 handheld gaming PC, citing storage costs that have risen to nearly double the original projections. The company initially launched pre-orders despite already high memory and storage prices, hoping these costs would stabilize. However, post-Chinese New Year price surges have made continued sales financially unsustainable at current pricing. Existing pre-orders will still be fulfilled, but new orders are paused until storage prices return to more reasonable levels, which industry forecasts suggest may not happen until 2027 or later. This suspension reflects broader supply chain issues affecting the electronics market and poses challenges for high-spec handheld gaming devices.

**Guide: How to Dispatch the Howling Hill Expansion in Crimson Desert**

The latest update for the open-world action RPG Crimson Desert includes the Howling Hill camp expansion, which can be unlocked via dispatch missions. Players must have at least 100 camp food and 250 camp funds to initiate the expansion. To dispatch the mission, navigate the map, inspect Howling Hill, select the mission from the tab, assign Freeswords characters, and send them out. The expansion unlocks additional structures like ranches and farms and is key to furthering the Greymane questline. The dispatch system is also accessible through an NPC later in the game, making it a core part of managing resources and camp development.

**Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2 Director Defends Nvidia’s DLSS 5 Technology**

Daniel Vávra, director of Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2, weighed in on Nvidia’s controversial DLSS 5 upscaling technology, dismissing criticism and asserting the technology will continue despite detractors. Vávra suggested future developments could tailor DLSS for specific art styles and replace more expensive rendering techniques like ray tracing. However, skepticism remains among hardware and gaming communities due to concerns about DLSS 5’s impact on visual fidelity and difficulty for developers to fine-tune the technology. Critics argue that it may compromise artistic intent and be used primarily as a cost-cutting measure, although Nvidia promotes it as a performance enhancer.

**Geekbench Flags Intel’s BOT Tool Impact on Arrow Lake Plus CPU Benchmarks**

Geekbench’s developer, Primate Labs, will begin attaching warnings to benchmark results from Intel’s latest Arrow Lake Plus CPUs due to the influence of Intel’s Binary Optimization Tool (BOT). This tool can increase Geekbench 6 scores by up to 8%, with individual test improvements as high as 40%. Because BOT modifies benchmarks in ways that are not transparent, results using it are deemed incomparable to standard runs. Primate Labs cannot currently detect whether BOT was active during a test, so all new Intel CPU results will carry a validity warning. While BOT optimizes instruction ordering for better utilization of CPU pipelines, it does not alter computation, but this practice raises questions about the comparability and fairness of benchmark scores.

**Overview of Slay the Spire 2 Multiplayer Mode**

Slay the Spire 2 introduces a multiplayer co-op mode allowing up to four players to climb the tower together. One player hosts a game that others join through Steam friends; there is no matchmaking or local co-op available. Players maintain separate inventories, gold, and energy pools, but share turns during battles. Enemy difficulty scales with party size, and loot including relics and cards is distributed individually. Co-op save files are tied to the host’s profile, and all players must participate from the start of a campaign, with no mid-game joining or player substitution
