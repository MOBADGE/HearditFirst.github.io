Death Stranding 2 Exceeds Two Million Sales After PC Launch

The PC release of *Death Stranding 2: On the Beach* has significantly boosted the game's overall sales, pushing it past two million copies sold. According to Alinea Analytics, the PC version sold 425,000 copies in its first week, adding to the 1.6 million sales on PlayStation 5. This marks a notable milestone less than a year after the game's initial console launch and sets a record for the fastest PlayStation exclusive to reach PC—coming just nine months post-launch.

This porting strategy contrasts with Sony's shifting approach, reportedly pulling back from releasing single-player exclusives like *Ghosts of Yotei* and Insomniac’s upcoming *Wolverine* game on PC. Interestingly, the PC launch also led to a spike in PS5 sales, likely fueled by simultaneous discounts and renewed media attention highlighting the game’s unique narrative and visual style.

Star Wars: Zero Company Director Challenges Tactics Game Norms

Greg Foertsch, creative director of the upcoming turn-based tactics game *Star Wars: Zero Company*, has expressed a desire to broaden the genre’s appeal beyond its traditional gameplay focus. Foertsch argues that turn-based tactics games don’t have to compromise on story depth, graphics quality, or control fluidity. He contends that narrative and elegant design can coexist without sacrificing gameplay complexity.

Drawing from his previous work on *XCOM* and *Marvel's Midnight Suns*, Foertsch aims to deliver a game that feels both engaging and visually appealing. His comments address a common perception among genre enthusiasts that graphics and story are less important than mechanics, and he hopes *Zero Company* will help overturn this notion.

Epic Games Layoff Highlights Challenges with Employer-Tied Healthcare

Epic Games' recent layoff of over 1,000 employees has drawn attention to the difficulties faced by workers in the U.S. healthcare system, especially those reliant on employer-provided insurance. Among those affected was Mike Prinke, a technical writer undergoing treatment for terminal brain cancer. His termination meant losing both income and vital health and life insurance coverage.

Epic CEO Tim Sweeney acknowledged the situation, stating the company is in contact with Prinke’s family and intends to resolve the insurance issues. This case highlights the human impact behind large corporate layoffs and the precarious nature of employment-based healthcare in the United States.

Crimson Desert Patch Introduces New Mounts and Stealth Theft Mechanics

The latest update to *Crimson Desert* adds five new mounts, expanding player mobility and customization options. The mounts become available after completing specific in-game conditions, adding fresh content for existing players.

Additionally, the patch introduces several quality-of-life improvements, including visuals replacing previously controversial AI-generated art assets, refined movement controls, extra chests, and relocation of certain storage features. A notable gameplay tweak means players only suffer reputation penalties (called contribution) for theft if caught by NPCs, which could influence stealth and theft strategies in the game’s open world.

Tales of Kenzera: Zau Director Reflects on Adapting Indie Game Development Strategies

Abubakar Salim, director of *Tales of Kenzera: Zau* and founder of Surgent Studios, shared insights about the studio’s shift from large-scale projects toward smaller, rapid-turnaround games. After commercial challenges with *Zau*, Surgent pivoted to releasing two diverse games in quick succession, emphasizing adaptability and sustainability.

Salim credits his experience working with filmmaker Ridley Scott on *Raised by Wolves* for inspiring this approach, noting the importance of strong collaboration to accelerate project development. Surgent’s latest game, *FixForce*, reflects this mindset with a focus on cooperative multiplayer and creative gameplay mechanics.
