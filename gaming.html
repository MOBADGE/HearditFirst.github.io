**Starfield: Terran Armada DLC and Free Lanes Update Incoming**

Bethesda has announced the upcoming Terran Armada story DLC for Starfield, releasing on April 7 alongside the Free Lanes update. The DLC introduces a conflict with the Terran Armada faction claiming Earth's legacy, featuring robotic enemies that can attack from pods during exploration. Players will have the option to recruit one of these robots into their crew; this companion is described as morally ambiguous, neither evil nor good. Additionally, completing a sidequest in New Atlantis allows recruiting a customizable ModelG mini-bot with pet-like characteristics.

Terran Armada adds incursions, representing the Armada's territorial advances in settled systems. These encounters disable ship abilities like grav-jump and fast travel until resolved and can interrupt cruise mode navigation between planets. The DLC also brings new ships, parts, more grounded gear such as machine guns, extra outpost decorations, and a fully built customizable outpost for placement on any planet. It will cost $10 but is included with the previously released premium upgrade.

This DLC and update collectively aim to enhance exploration, combat, and player freedom while expanding Starfield’s story.

**Death Stranding 2 Steam Build Leaks Before Official Launch**

Two days before its scheduled release on March 19, a pre-release build of Kojima Productions’ Death Stranding 2 leaked via Steam. The leak reportedly included an unencrypted version of the 113GB game, allowing early access to piracy channels. Although Steam’s DRM is present, it lacks the more robust protection of alternatives like Denuvo, facilitating unauthorized distribution.

The leaked build lacks certain localizations, notably Russian voiceovers, possibly because Steam distributes language packages separately. Importantly, players using pirated versions will not access the game’s online features, which are a significant part of its experience, allowing shared infrastructure between players.

The official launch remains imminent. This incident highlights ongoing challenges in digital distribution security for high-profile PC releases.

**Subnautica 2 Early Access Scheduled for May Amid Legal Developments**

After a prolonged and contentious development period, including legal disputes over studio leadership, Krafton and Unknown Worlds have set a May early access launch target for Subnautica 2. Krafton’s statement acknowledges progress following a recent milestone review and underlines a commitment to community-driven open development.

The announcement also signals Krafton’s acceptance of a court order to reinstate former Unknown Worlds CEO Ted Gill, indicating a willingness to ensure leadership continuity ahead of release. Krafton expressed disagreement with the ruling but aims to minimize disruption to the team and players.

This concession could smooth the path to launch, while a $250 million performance bonus tied to the game’s success remains pending.

**Take-Two CEO Offers No Advice to New Microsoft Gaming Head**

Strauss Zelnick, CEO of Take-Two Interactive, declined to offer advice to Asha Sharma, the recently appointed CEO of Microsoft Gaming. Sharma's promotion has drawn attention due to her background outside traditional gaming, focusing on AI and tech sectors rather than game development.

Zelnick emphasized Sharma’s accomplishments and independence, stating she does well without external input. Reflecting on his own non-gamer background when taking over Take-Two, Zelnick reiterated the importance of balancing creative and business leadership and focusing on producing successful, hit games.

Take-Two’s experience underscores a belief in strong business and creative collaboration as central to industry success.

**Remedy Ends Active Development of FBC: Firebreak With Final Update**

Remedy Entertainment is ceasing active development on FBC: Firebreak, its co-op shooter that launched in mid-2025 with disappointing sales. The “Open House” update marks the final content release, adding five new maps, a revamped perk system, and various gameplay adjustments.

The game’s price has been permanently reduced, and a cross-platform Friend’s Pass has been introduced to facilitate multiplayer access. Remedy confirmed that while no new content will follow, maintenance updates will continue, and server support is engineered to sustain the game for years despite a small player base.

This represents Remedy’s shift away from the title while maintaining support for the existing community.
