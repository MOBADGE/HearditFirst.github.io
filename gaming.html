Windrose Reviewed: A Survival Game Dressed as a Pirate Adventure After spending four hours with *Windrose*, a new pirate-themed survival game from Pocketpair, the experience fell short of delivering the expected feel of pirating. The gameplay focuses heavily on gathering resources, crafting, and survival elements, often with frustrating deaths and slow progression through tech trees. The reviewer notes that *Windrose* does not evoke the "pirate life" as effectively as older games like *Sea of Thieves*, which emphasize player interaction, unpredictable encounters, and playful hijinks over grinding mechanics. Despite a strong launch with nearly 70,000 concurrent players and positive early reviews, *Windrose*’s lack of immediate engagement with classic pirate activities may make it less appealing to those seeking a pirate adventure rather than a survival sim. Network issues and clunky combat further detract from the experience.

Windrose Tips: How to Fast Travel and Find Clay For players venturing into *Windrose*, fast travel is available but requires crafting specific items. Once copper ingots are smelted, players can craft a Fast Travel Bell and subsequently build Fast Travel Points on coastlines to teleport between locations. Fast travel by ship is more flexible, allowing movement between points while sailing, but co-op gameplay can sometimes separate players during fast travel. Additionally, clay is a vital resource used in potions and crafting stations, found in distinctive muddy rock deposits scattered outdoors. A pickaxe is required to harvest clay nodes that respawn periodically. These guides offer practical help for navigating the early crafting and exploration hurdles in *Windrose*.

Warhammer Classics Arrive on Steam with Enhanced Compatibility More than 20 classic *Warhammer* and *Warhammer 40,000* games have been reintroduced to Steam under the new “Warhammer Classics” label by SNEG and Games Workshop. Seven of these titles, including *Warhammer 40,000: Chaos Gate* and *Warhammer: Dark Omen*, are appearing on Steam for the first time. The re-releases include updates for modern Windows compatibility, widescreen resolutions, adjusted audio, and Steam achievements. This initiative aims to preserve and celebrate the legacy of foundational *Warhammer* games for current and future audiences. The new label also launched with a sale running until April 20, offering discounts between 20% and 90%. While some titles may feel dated, the collection provides access to strategic and fantasy classics that have shaped the *Warhammer* gaming universe.

Raccoin: Understanding Special Coins and Recommended Seeds The roguelike coin-pusher game *Raccoin* features over 150 special coins with varying effects, including 21 negative "bad coins" that impose debuffs. Understanding these coins is essential for strategy, as positive coins can significantly boost scores by granting bonuses, spawning extra coins, or triggering effects, while bad coins can hinder progress by disabling features or reducing coin value. Alongside this, a list of the best seed codes for *Raccoin* has been compiled to help players access advantageous runs with strong item shops and easier scoring conditions. Players can input these seeds when setting up a run to tailor their experience and improve chances of success in this procedurally generated title.

Magic: The Gathering and Dwarf Fortress Cross Over with ASCII Art Cards Wizards of the Coast announced a new *Magic: The Gathering* Secret Lair set featuring *Dwarf Fortress*, notable for using the latter’s iconic ASCII art style on the cards. This collaboration marks the first MTG release to employ text glyph artwork, depicting classic *Dwarf Fortress* elements such as dwarves, dragons, and fortress scenes in a minimalist, symbolic manner. The set is part of the "Back to School" Secret Lair drop and will be available from April 27. This artistic choice pays homage to *Dwarf Fortress*’ unique aesthetic and will appeal to fans of both franchises.
