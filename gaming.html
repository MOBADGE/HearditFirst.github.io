OpenAI Revises Controversial Military Deal After Backlash

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman announced an amendment to the company’s recently unveiled agreement with the US Department of War to provide AI tools for military use. The original deal, struck hastily last Friday, drew significant criticism over concerns about the potential use of AI in domestic mass surveillance and autonomous weapons. OpenAI’s updated terms now explicitly prohibit the AI system’s intentional use for domestic surveillance of US persons, referencing protections under the Fourth Amendment and the National Security Act of 1947. The Department of War has also confirmed no use of OpenAI services by intelligence agencies like the NSA. Despite the clarifications, the deal has led to a surge in ChatGPT uninstalls and damaged OpenAI’s public image. Altman acknowledged that rushing the announcement was a mistake and called it a “good learning experience” as OpenAI navigates complex ethical challenges around AI deployment in defense.

Esoteric Ebb Delivers a New Take on the Disco Elysium Formula

Esoteric Ebb, released today, is receiving praise as a worthy successor to Disco Elysium, blending narrative-driven roleplaying with tabletop-inspired mechanics. Developed by Christoffer Bodegård, the CRPG places players in a time-sensitive investigation with surreal, darkly humorous events and a flexible character system that supports diverse playstyles. Unlike Disco Elysium’s focus solely on investigation, Esoteric Ebb combines detective work with exploration, combat, and diplomacy in a fantasy setting heavily influenced by Dungeons & Dragons. The game’s combat is turn-based and text-driven, encouraging players to engage with their character’s skills as active participants in each encounter. Early feedback highlights the game’s depth, ambition, and its successful adaptation of tabletop roleplay dynamics into a narrative RPG format.

Voice Actor Nick Apostolides on AI and Human Performance in Games

Nick Apostolides, the voice of Leon Kennedy in the new Resident Evil Requiem, shared his views on the growing role of AI in game voice acting. While acknowledging AI’s increasing presence, Apostolides emphasized that AI cannot replicate the human nuance vital to storytelling and character immersion. He argued that for major games capable of selling millions of copies, the cost of employing professional actors remains reasonable. Apostolides described his approach to portraying an older, more experienced Leon Kennedy, including creating a mood board to deeply understand the character’s mindset. His comments contribute to ongoing debates in the industry about the limits and ethics of AI voice synthesis versus traditional acting.

Classic Baldur’s Gate Titles Receive New Patch and Bundle Offer

Beamdog released a surprise 2.7 beta patch for the enhanced editions of Baldur’s Gate 1 and 2, adding support for Apple Silicon and new languages. This update, arriving 14 years after the original enhanced editions, signals ongoing support for these classic CRPGs. Alongside this, an RPG Humble Bundle featuring over 500 hours worth of games from Beamdog, Owlcat, and others is available for $23. The bundle includes Baldur’s Gate 1 & 2, Neverwinter Nights Enhanced, Planescape: Torment, Icewind Dale, and Pathfinder titles. This package offers a compelling entry point for players interested in classic narrative-rich RPGs.

Heart of the Machine Review: A Cyberpunk Strategy RPG with AI Themes

Heart of the Machine, launching March 6, is a turn-based strategy RPG that puts players in the role of a sentient artificial general intelligence navigating a corporate-controlled near-future city. The game features branching narratives with multiple endings, strategic combat involving mechs and androids, and a progression system that emphasizes upgrading intelligence to transcend humanity. A unique mechanic allows players to manage multiple timelines, each facing eventual challenges and “dooms,” requiring careful resource and population management. While praised for its writing and innovation, some players may find the growing complexity and interface less accessible in longer play sessions. Its approach to emergent storytelling and AI themes adds a distinct voice to the strategy RPG genre.
