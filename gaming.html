Windows 11 Memory and Storage Reduction Plans Revisited

Former Windows development lead Mikhail Parakhin has revealed that Microsoft had previously planned a project—dubbed "20/20"—aimed at reducing Windows 11’s idle memory usage and disk footprint by 20%. Although the initiative was never completed, this ambition aligns with current efforts announced by Microsoft’s Windows and Devices VP, Pavan Davuluri, to improve Windows “fundamentals.” These improvements focus on enhanced memory efficiency and a smaller baseline memory footprint, which could benefit all users by freeing more resources for applications. This renewed focus is significant in the context of rising memory and storage costs, making leaner operating systems particularly relevant. While these changes have not been formally confirmed as a priority, better UI responsiveness and reduced interaction latency are also included in Microsoft's quality improvement goals.

Princeton Review Ranks Top Game Design Programs for 2026

The Princeton Review has published its annual ranking of the best game design programs at both undergraduate and graduate levels. The rankings consider factors like graduate employment rates, early-career salaries, faculty experience, and curriculum relevance. Leading the undergraduate list is New York University, followed by the University of Southern California and the University of Central Florida. Graduate rankings mirror some of these institutions, with NYU also topping the list. The report highlights the importance of practical skills such as programming believable AI characters, map design, and creative accessibility in game development careers. For those interested in the industry, this guide offers a comprehensive overview of where to pursue education and what skills to develop.

EverQuest Legends Targets Busy Players with Classic Gameplay and Buffed Characters

Daybreak Game Company announced EverQuest Legends, launching this July as a return to the original EverQuest experience from 1999, but designed for players who no longer have the time for traditional MMO grind. The game preserves the original’s challenges, visuals, and sounds but enhances player power by allowing characters to start with two classes and eventually add a third. This multiclass system grants more skills and abilities simultaneously, making solo or small-group play more viable. Additional content from later expansions is included, such as the beastmaster class and new mechanics. While this adapts the classic for modern playstyles, some players may miss the original’s emphasis on teamwork and group challenges.

Lian Li Lancool 217 Case Emphasizes Features but Faces Pricing Challenges

Lian Li’s Lancool 217 PC case offers an extensive feature set aimed at first-time builders and those who prioritize airflow and build flexibility. Priced at $120, it supports various motherboard sizes, multiple fan configurations, vertical GPU mounting, and includes five high-airflow pre-installed fans. The case also has a unique wood-accented aesthetic, blending modern and traditional design elements. While the quality and features are commendable, the price places it in direct competition with premium alternatives such as the NZXT H7 Flow RGB, which may offer more value. The Lancool 217 excels in ease of building and cooling efficiency but may need a price adjustment to stand out in a crowded market segment.

Intel’s Binary Optimization Tool Shows Potential but Limited Gamer Impact

Intel’s new Binary Optimization Tool (BOT), introduced alongside its Arrow Lake Plus processors, aims to improve CPU efficiency for specific games by optimizing how threads are processed. Testing shows that for some titles, such as Shadow of the Tomb Raider and Marvel’s Spider-Man Remastered, BOT can boost CPU-related performance by up to 35%, with smaller overall frame rate improvements. However, many popular titles see negligible gains, especially when GPU performance is the bottleneck. Currently limited to relatively few modern CPUs and just a dozen supported games, BOT’s practical benefits for most PC gamers are limited. Additionally, competitive games are unlikely to support BOT soon due to anti-cheat concerns. Intel continues development, and broader adoption may come with future hardware releases.
