DRAM and SSD Prices Stabilize Amid AI Demand

After steep increases in memory (DRAM) and storage component prices driven largely by AI development demands, recent market analyses show signs of leveling off or slight decreases in some sectors. Reports from 3DCenter indicate that DDR5 memory prices have remained steady over the past month, though still significantly higher than mid-2025 levels. DDR4 prices have seen a mild uptick between January and February but far less than previous months. A report from IT Home notes a modest 10-20% correction in DDR4 prices. Experts caution this may be temporary, framing the current state as a "calm before the storm," since AI's voracious need for high-speed memory and storage continues, and supply limitations are expected to persist into and beyond 2026. Consumers and tech manufacturers should remain prepared for ongoing price pressures.

The Pimax Crystal Super VR headset, available with multiple optical engine options, has been tested with particular focus on its Micro-OLED display variant. Compared to the QLED alternatives, the Micro-OLED offers slightly lower vertical resolution but benefits from higher pixel density and superior contrast due to its self-emissive OLED technology.
