**Memory Shortage Risks Consumer Electronics Industry Stability**

Phison CEO Pua Khein-Seng has warned that the global shortage of memory components, driven largely by demand for AI technologies, could force many consumer electronics manufacturers to go bankrupt or abandon product lines by the end of 2026. The current imbalance between supply and demand for DRAM and NAND flash memory is unprecedented, with prepayments stretching as far as three years to secure stock. This shortage is expected to continue through 2030 or longer, complicating the production of consumer devices including mobile phones, PCs, and TVs. Furthermore, the demand for AI-focused hardware, such as Nvidia's upcoming Rubin GPUs requiring vast amounts of SSD storage, exacerbates the pressure on memory supplies. The shortage may not only reduce output volume but shift manufacturing focus towards commercial AI and server hardware instead of consumer gadgets, reshaping the electronics market landscape over the next several years.
