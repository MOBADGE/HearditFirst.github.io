Bungie Buffs Marathon's Recon Shell with Echo Pulse and Tracker Drone Improvements

Bungie has announced upcoming changes to the Recon Shell in its extraction shooter Marathon, addressing that character’s relatively weak status in the game. The planned update, set for April 14, will notably enhance Recon’s Echo Pulse ability and make its tracker drone more effective. Echo Pulse will now distinguish between AI-controlled enemies and human players on the HUD and become less visible to foes, making it harder for enemies to avoid detection. However, enemies equipped with a Signal Jammer can disguise themselves as AI to counter this advantage. Additionally, the tracker drone will be able to switch targets if it cannot reach its current one, reducing frustrating moments where it gets stuck on objects. These adjustments aim to bring Recon closer in balance to other playable Shells.

Take-Two Lays Off AI Staff Including Head of Artificial Intelligence

Take-Two Interactive has laid off its Head of Artificial Intelligence, Luke Dicken, along with an unspecified portion of its AI team. Dicken had only held the role since early 2025 after coming from Zynga, where he led generative AI initiatives. This move comes despite CEO Strauss Zelnick’s statements last year about "actively embracing generative AI." The precise reasons and scale of the layoffs remain unclear, but they highlight some uncertainty in Take-Two's approach to AI technology development. The broader industry context includes recent layoffs and setbacks at other AI-focused companies, suggesting potential challenges in the sector. Take-Two has not clarified how this shift will affect ongoing projects like Grand Theft Auto 6.

Former GameStop Exec Reflects on Impulse, the Forgotten Steam Competitor

Larry Kuperman, a former GameStop executive and co-founder of Nightdive Studios, reflected during GDC 2026 on GameStop’s early efforts to build a Steam-like digital distribution platform called Impulse. Originally launched by Stardock and acquired by GameStop in 2011, Impulse was intended as a competitor to Valve’s Steam but was discontinued in 2014. Kuperman cited GameStop’s failure to fully embrace digital distribution, treating it as a temporary trend and betting on brick-and-mortar retail’s resurgence instead. In hindsight, this decision limited GameStop’s ability to adapt to the industry’s digital shift. Kuperman also highlighted Steam’s success in building a strong community and social features, which were key differentiators compared to Impulse.

Mega Crit Addresses Steam Review Backlash on Slay the Spire 2 Beta Patch

Mega Crit co-founder Casey Yano discussed the large wave of negative Steam reviews that followed the first beta balance patch for Slay the Spire 2. While the volume and harshness of the feedback unsettled some team members, Yano expressed understanding of why players resorted to review bombing as a protest mechanism. He noted that players in China were largely restricted from using other feedback channels due to internet limitations, leaving Steam reviews as their primary outlet. Mega Crit aims to improve communication and feedback options for players in all regions. Yano described Steam reviews as an imperfect, coarse method that simplifies complex player feelings into a star rating, but said they intend to listen and respond to player input constructively.

Otherside Entertainment Shifts Thick as Thieves Focus to Singleplayer and Two-Player Co-op

Otherside Entertainment announced a change in direction for Thick as Thieves, its upcoming first-person stealth game and intended successor to the Thief series. The game will now focus on singleplayer and two-player cooperative play, dropping its original plan for a four-player PvPvE multiplayer mode. The developers cited more enjoyment found in solo and co-op gameplay during development and believe this focus enhances the dynamic stealth experience. The decision also addresses concerns about the challenges in creating a multiplayer live service game that appeals broadly. PvPvE multiplayer may return after launch, but the initial release will prioritize these streamlined modes. A new gameplay trailer and further details are expected at the Triple-I Initiative showcase on April 9.

Revisiting Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2019 Highlights Its Lasting Strengths

Call of Duty: Modern Warfare (2019) has returned to public attention following a deep discount on Steam, prompting reflections on why it remains one of the franchise’s strongest entries. The game’s grounded, realistic presentation and immersive audio design stand out, with detailed environments and weapon sounds that still outperform more recent titles.
