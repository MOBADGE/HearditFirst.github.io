Highlights from PC Gaming Hardware in 2025

Among the hardware developments in 2025, advancements in peripherals for competitive first-person shooters have stood out. A notable innovation was the launch of the Corsair Sabre V2 Pro wireless mouse, notable for its light weight at 36 grams.

These enhancements address the needs of competitive FPS gamers who require precision and comfort over extended sessions.

Linux Gaming Progresses but Faces Challenges in 2025

Linux made substantial strides in gaming during 2025, largely thanks to Valve‚Äôs SteamOS operating system and its Proton compatibility layer, which allows many Windows games to run on Linux systems. SteamOS has become more accessible on devices beyond the Steam Deck, such as the Lenovo Legion Go S handheld. This has made Linux a more viable platform for gaming, with improved performance and usability in many cases.

Despite progress, some obstacles remain. The use of kernel-level anti-cheat software in many popular multiplayer games restricts compatibility with Linux, as these anti-cheat systems do not support Linux's open and modifiable kernel environment. Additionally, driver and hardware support issues occasionally cause instability. Consequently, while Linux gaming is maturing, mainstream gamer adoption as a full replacement for Windows remains limited.
