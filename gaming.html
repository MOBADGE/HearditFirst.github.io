BAFTA Pulls Trailer for Trauma-Themed Game “The Quiet Things”

Silver Script Games founder Alyx Jones revealed that BAFTA withdrew the planned trailer debut for her game *The Quiet Things* during last night’s BAFTA awards. The trailer, which deals with childhood trauma and abuse themes, had already been revised to remove flagged imagery of potential weapons and violence. Despite these changes, BAFTA stated the trailer was pulled to protect attendees from potentially triggering content without sufficient prior warning.

This decision highlights ongoing tensions around presenting difficult subject matter in gaming spaces while balancing audience sensitivity. Jones expressed disappointment as the trailer represented a personal story and a significant exposure opportunity for her indie studio. The incident raises questions about content policies at major industry events and how they affect games tackling heavy themes.

Modders Bring 2003’s *Manhunt* Setting to *GTA: San Andreas* with Total Conversion Mod

A new ambitious mod called *GTA: Carcer City* lets players explore the gritty city from Rockstar’s 2003 game *Manhunt* within *Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas*. The mod replaces San Andreas’ map with the Carcer City environment, featuring districts inspired by Rust Belt cities such as Detroit and Pittsburgh. It includes all the original *Manhunt* locations and offers a unique dark storyline set in 2001, diverging from the usual satirical *GTA* tone.

This total conversion demonstrates the ongoing dedication and creativity of modding communities to expand established games in unexpected ways. Although currently available only as a demo, the project serves as a reminder of how modders keep classic games fresh while Rockstar’s official *GTA 6* release remains pending.

Fans Meticulously Catalog Wildlife in *GTA 6* Trailer Footage

An online community of enthusiasts is carefully examining every detail of *GTA 6*’s trailers and images to document all animal species featured in the game’s environment. Starting from the initial trailer, contributors on GTAForums have identified creatures ranging from sea turtles and birds to panthers through detailed screenshots and real-world references.

This level of fan engagement reflects how players build deeper connections to game worlds by appreciating environmental details. It also illustrates how modern game trailers are designed with layers that fuel speculation and discussion, contributing to extended interest during long development cycles.

CCP to Add Dedicated Non-PvP Starter Zone “Exordium” to *EVE Online*

After 23 years of *EVE Online*’s PvP-centric universe, CCP announced the addition of Exordium—a new region designed as a non-PvP zone specifically for new players. Unlike the current scattered starter areas, Exordium will centralize rookies in a safe space where PvP is systemically disabled, aiming to reduce anxiety for newcomers and encourage cooperative exploration and learning.

This shift marks a notable change in the game’s approach to onboarding, reflecting broader industry trends favoring more approachable multiplayer experiences. CCP believes easing players into PvP gradually will ultimately benefit the entire *EVE* community by fostering growth and retention.

Multiplayer Shooter *Last Flag*, Developed by Imagine Dragons’ Lead Singer, Faces Player Shortage

*Last Flag*, a team-based 5v5 multiplayer shooter from Night Street Games—founded by Imagine Dragons’ lead singer Dan Reynolds and his brother—has struggled to attract a substantial player base since its April 14 release. Despite positive reviews and extensive social media marketing, peak concurrent player counts have remained below 600, with reports of bot-filled matches.

This difficulty underscores ongoing challenges even for well-funded multiplayer shooters to gain traction in a crowded market. Industry observers note the game’s art style may contribute, as its Fortnite-like aesthetic seems less broadly appealing. The developer aims to nurture the community over time, but raising funds and sustaining momentum remains tough for new shooters in 2026.
