Mega Crit Addresses Backlash Over Slay the Spire 2 Balance Changes

Developer Mega Crit responded to strong negative reactions following the first balance update patch for *Slay the Spire 2*. The changes notably altered the card "Prepared," reducing its utility and provoking thousands of negative Steam reviews, particularly from players in China where platform restrictions may have amplified discontent.

Mega Crit explained that this balance pass is the initial step in an ongoing adjustment process expected to last 1-2 years. They emphasized that no change in the beta balance branch is permanent and that player feedback gathered directly through the game's in-built reporting system is most valuable. The studio’s approach mixes player input, collected metrics, and their design goals. This response was aimed at reassuring players that the game is still evolving and that experimental changes are being tested on the beta branch before final decisions are made.

Geoff "Zag" Keene on Game Development and Gaming Habits

Geoff "Zag" Keene, design director of *Abiotic Factor*, shared insight into his gaming background and development philosophy in a recent interview. Keene has extensive experience, including 1,160 hours in *PUBG*, and highlighted how cooperative multiplayer games have influenced his work. He praised *Arc Raiders* for its immersive world and shared that he often plays games with friends and family, emphasizing the social aspect of gaming.

Keene noted he revisits classic titles such as *Warcraft 2*, which he considers his oldest and most cherished game, using it as a form of relaxation under stress. He also spoke about his preference for games that retain a sense of human touch and imperfection rather than overly polished experiences.

Dragon Quest X to Introduce an AI-Powered Companion

Square Enix is preparing to add an AI-driven companion named "Chatty Slimey" to the Japan-exclusive MMO *Dragon Quest X*. Developed in partnership with Google, this AI slime will engage players in voice conversations triggered by in-game events, such as defeating enemies or acquiring rare items. A beta test for this new feature is underway, with participant applications open until the end of March.

The initiative aims to reduce player loneliness during gameplay, offering a semblance of interaction in the often solitary MMO environment. However, incorporation of AI chatbots in games remains controversial due to concerns about reliability, manipulation, and the nature of player attachment to these systems.

Crimson Desert Responds to Player Concerns About AI-Generated Art

Following player reports identifying what appeared to be AI-generated art assets in *Crimson Desert*, developer Pearl Abyss issued a statement clarifying the use of AI during early production phases. They confirmed some 2D props were created using AI generation tools as placeholders to explore tone and atmosphere, but these were intended to be replaced with final hand-crafted assets before release.

Pearl Abyss apologized for the lack of transparency and pledged to review all in-game assets, replacing any undesired AI-generated content in future patches. This incident fits into broader industry discussions about disclosure and responsible use of AI tools in game development.

Half Sword Developer Addresses Player Concerns Over Early Access Version

The makers of the medieval dueling simulator *Half Sword* responded to mixed player feedback following the game's early access release. Fans of the original tech demo have noted significant differences in combat feel and mechanics in early access. The developer explained that substantial changes and additions to animation, control, and combat systems have been implemented since the demo, aiming to mature the game.

They also clarified that features shown in the early access trailer may not yet be available in the release, as the trailer was meant to hint at future content. The development team reaffirmed commitment to ongoing balance and optimization efforts, acknowledging early access as a phase of iteration and learning.

Peglin Adds Fourth Act and Final Boss Nearly Two Years After Launch

*Peglin*, a pachinko-inspired roguelike, received a significant update introducing a fourth act and a final boss, expanding content well after its initial 1.0 release in late 2024. The update also introduces new classes, relics, and optional challenges to enhance progression and gameplay variety.

Developer Red Nexus Games aimed to increase consistency in class unlocking and refresh the experience for players seeking extended content. Performance improvements and new music tracks accompany the content additions. This update signals ongoing developer support for an indie title that blends casual gameplay with roguelike elements.
