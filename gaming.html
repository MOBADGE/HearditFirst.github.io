Banquet For Fools: A Demanding and Immersive RPG Experience

Banquet For Fools, released on March 5, 2026, by Hannah and Joseph Games, offers an unconventional role-playing game experience focused heavily on player-driven note-taking and observation. The game eschews typical RPG conveniences such as quest logs, conversation histories, or map markers. Instead, it requires players to actively record information themselves and engage deeply with the game's complex world and social structures.

This design choice encourages a slower, more contemplative approach to exploration and survival, creating tension in travel and combat. Battles are tactical and real-time with pausing elements, requiring attention to enemy behavior and manual control over party members. Success depends on understanding the environment, managing scarce resources, and piecing together subtle clues. While this can be frustrating, many players may find the challenge rewarding, as progression involves personal investment and discovery rather than handholding.

The game is playable on Steam Deck and available for $20, appealing to players seeking a unique, personalized RPG experience that values player agency and detail over accessibility.

Mars War: Logs – A Gritty, Choice-Driven RPG at a Budget Price

Mars War: Logs, an older game from 2013 by Spiders, is currently available on Steam for 99 cents as part of a sale. Though not technically polished or highly rated during its initial release, the game maintains an interesting setting and narrative. Set in a harsh Martian prison colony torn by war and environmental hazards, it combines a gritty atmosphere with a choice-driven role-playing framework reminiscent of early BioWare titles.

Combat remains functional but limited, with some tactical elements like sand throwing to stun enemies. The game runs well on Steam Deck and offers a roughly 15-hour story experience. For players interested in a rough but soulful RPG with a unique setting and narrative ambition, Mars War: Logs presents a low-risk purchase.

Sand: Raiders of Sophie Release Date Remains Unspecified

Fans of Sand: Raiders of Sophie, a PvPvE extraction shooter featuring customizable mechanical bases, are awaiting a precise March 2026 release date. Despite the Steam page listing March as the launch window, no firm date has yet been announced, causing uncertainty among the community.

Community managers have acknowledged the lack of specific information, attributing the delay to ongoing final development work. They emphasize that a concrete release date depends on these last stages being successfully completed to avoid disappointing fans with postponed launches. While March is not over, players are advised to remain patient as the developers finalize requirements. This situation highlights common challenges in contemporary game release scheduling, where transparency must be balanced with development realities.

Lost Wiki: Kozlovka – A $5 Mystery Game Emulating Wikipedia Research

Lost Wiki: Kozlovka is a $5 mystery game that involves solving puzzles by navigating a database of Wikipedia-like entries. Players investigate the secrets of the fictional Eastern European town Kozlovka and uncover a complex story involving generational deceit and a sinister tree known as Draken Oak.

The gameplay centers on reading interconnected reports and filling in missing details such as people’s names and event timelines. While the mystery is not overly difficult, the clear design and tight narrative encourage focused exploration. The game doubles as a reflection on information control, illustrating how public knowledge can be shaped by those in power.

Its retro ‘90s computer interface and atmospheric music support an immersive experience. Lost Wiki: Kozlovka is suited for players interested in narrative-driven investigative games rather than traditional puzzle complexity.

Crimson Desert Players Request Permanent Exotic Mounts

Since its release last week, Crimson Desert has drawn attention for its variety of mounts, including bears, wolves, dodo-like birds, dinosaurs, and dragons. However, players have voiced frustration that only horses can be permanently domesticated. Exotic mounts such as bears or the dragon are temporarily rideable following combat or quest events but revert to hostile behavior afterwards and cannot be stabled.

The player base has expressed disappointment and requested that developers Pearl Abyss allow these mounts to become permanent companions. Because Crimson Desert is a single-player game, many argue that balance concerns common to multiplayer titles do not apply here. The community’s feedback points to a desire for more freedom in enjoying the game’s unique creature designs and mounting options.
