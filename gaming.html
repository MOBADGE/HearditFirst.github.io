**Larian Studios Responds to Fan Backlash Over AI Usage**

Larian Studios, developer of Baldur's Gate 3, has addressed recent criticism regarding its use of generative AI in game development. CEO Swen Vincke clarified that the studio uses AI tools only for early prototyping tasks like creating rough mock levels and exploring ideas. He emphasized that AI is not used to produce final in-game art, writing, or voices, and that the studio continues to hire and support creative professionals. The controversy reignited after a Bloomberg interview described Larian as "pushing hard" on generative AI, a characterization Vincke and publishing director Michael Douse disputed. Fans expressed concern about potential AI-generated content appearing in upcoming titles like Divinity, but Larian stressed its commitment to original creative work and fair treatment of employees amid industry-wide layoffs.

**Fallout Shelter Updates With New Vault Tied to Fallout Season 2**

In conjunction with the launch of Fallout Season 2 on Prime Video, Bethesda released a free update for its free-to-play game Fallout Shelter. The update introduces a new "Viva New Vegas" seasonal Vault located near Novac, Nevada, featuring characters from the TV show such as Lucy, Maximus, and The Ghoul. Players can recruit these allies, complete quests, and unlock themed outfits and weapons tied to the series. The update also adds a new "Lucky Spins" mechanic where players earn Poker Chips from quests to gain additional rewards. This seasonal content provides Fallout fans a way to engage with the franchise ahead of Fallout 5, which remains years away.

**Dead Island 3 Development Progressing, Targeting 2028 Release**

Developer Dambuster Studios revealed in financial documents that Dead Island 3 is now officially in early production, with feature, character, world, and story design underway. After the October 2024 release of Dead Island 2 Ultimate Edition, the studio's entire team including QA is focusing on the sequel. The game is currently planned for release in the first half of 2028. This marks a significantly shorter development cycle compared to Dead Island 2, which took over a decade to complete and changed hands between multiple studios during that time. Although 2028 is still some years away, this update suggests the franchise remains active and progressing steadily.

**Sapphire Calls on AMD to Allow More Design Freedom for Graphics Cards**

Edward Crisler, PR representative for graphics card maker Sapphire, openly criticized AMD‚Äôs restrictive approach to third-party Radeon card design. Speaking on the Hardware Unboxed podcast, Crisler expressed a desire for AMD to "get out of the way" and let partners experiment with aspects like VRAM amounts and overclocking to create more differentiated products. He noted that the actual performance difference between the lowest and highest-end versions of the same model is often negligible due to strict limitations set by AMD. As a result, manufacturers instead compete on cooler design, noise levels, and customer support. While AMD enforces these constraints to ensure product consistency and reliability, Crisler argued that the market currently offers little variety beyond such incremental tweaks.
