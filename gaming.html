**Crimson Desert: How to Defeat the Crimson Nightmare Boss**

Players tackling the House Roberts faction quest in *Crimson Desert* face the Crimson Nightmare, a boss encountered in the Bleed Bandit-infested Fort Perwin. This foe is a sentient cloud of red gas that damages players who get too close. Success against this boss hinges on key strategies: acquiring a Scarlet Blades Gas Mask to gain immunity to the toxic gas; using the Axiom Force ability to stun and temporarily incapacitate the boss; clearing surrounding bandits before the fight to reduce distractions; ensuring sufficient health and stamina for both the fort clearing and the boss battle; and refining weapons to increase damage output. These preparations simplify the encounter significantly despite the hazardous environment and the boss’s damaging aura.

**New Blood’s Renewed Support for GOG**

Dave Oshry, CEO of New Blood Interactive, has reaffirmed his company’s commitment to supporting the GOG storefront after previously raising concerns about its viability. Despite GOG’s challenges competing with Steam, including a difficult financial year in 2021 and controversies around DRM policies, New Blood plans to maintain parity between Steam and GOG releases. This includes simultaneous release dates, demos on GOG, and offering one-click mod installations similar to Steam Workshop functionality. GOG, now independent from CD Projekt and supported by a patron program, continues focusing on game preservation and classic titles. New Blood’s stance may encourage other developers to support DRM-free platforms, helping maintain GOG’s relevance in a competitive market.

**Resident Evil Requiem Now Playable in VR Through Modding**

A mod allowing *Resident Evil Requiem* to be played in virtual reality has recently become available. The mod places players in the perspective of protagonist Grace Ashcroft, offering an immersive experience of the game’s survival horror elements. While certain puzzles require temporarily switching back to a traditional screen, most gameplay can be experienced fully in VR. The mod requires additional software such as ViGEmBus and REFramework. This development adds a new way to experience a game that balances first-person suspense and third-person action, although VR use may not appeal to all players given the game’s tense atmosphere and jumpscare elements.

**Marvel Heroes Revived with Player-Run Servers**

Nearly eight years after its closure in November 2017, the MMO action RPG *Marvel Heroes* has returned through player-run servers enabled by an open-source server emulator reaching version 1.0. The first such server, Project Tahiti, is active with thousands of players queuing to join. This revival gives fans access once again to a game known for its "Diablo-style" gameplay combined with Marvel characters like Scarlet Witch. Players can also set up their own servers or play a singleplayer version. The resurrection preserves a cult favorite and shows the potential for community efforts to extend the lifespan of shuttered online games.

**Five Steam Releases Worth Noting**

Several noteworthy games have launched recently on Steam:

- *Bonnie Bear Saves Frogtime* is a tactical RPG with a whimsical aesthetic where players collect frogs and engage in grid-based battles.

- *Lost Wiki: Kozlovka* is a narrative-driven investigatory game where players as a journalist sift through in-game “Wikipedia”-style records to unravel a mysterious, supernatural event.

- *Deadline Delivery* offers physics-based comedy and fast-paced cooperative delivery missions with unstable vehicles, supporting up to eight players online.

- *Heroes of Science and Fiction* is a turn-based strategy reminiscent of *Heroes of Might and Magic*, featuring multiple factions and campaigns.

- *The Coin Game* presents an unusual open-world arcade experience centered on realistic physics in various retro-style amusements within a large ‘90s shopping mall, including claw machines and rollercoasters, along with a survival mode.
