The Killing Stone Delays Full Release, Launching in Early Access

Card-based deckbuilder **The Killing Stone**, inspired by Inscryption, postponed its full launch originally scheduled for February 18. Instead, it will enter early access on that date. The developer, Jordan Thomas, cited increased narrative depth and player interest in story-driven content as reasons for the delay. The early access version will feature the game's three story arcs, optional missions, and key demon lord opponents but will exclude the final endgame content called "The Reckoning," which will come with the full release later.

The delay shifts expectations but allows the developer to incorporate player feedback on the story and voice-acted segments. The game will remain available on Steam with a demo for players to try beforehand.

Valve Details Steam Machine Performance and Delays Due to Hardware Shortages

Valve has addressed questions about its upcoming Steam Machine and Steam Frame hardware amid ongoing memory and storage shortages affecting pricing and release dates. The company confirmed that most Steam games run well at 4K resolution and 60 frames per second using FidelityFX Super Resolution (FSR) on the Steam Machine, though some titles require more upscaling or are better played at lower internal resolutions.

While initial plans aimed for a first-half 2026 release, exact pricing and dates remain unconfirmed due to these supply constraints. Both devices will have upgradable SSD and RAM, and Valve encourages third-parties to create custom faceplates by sharing relevant manufacturing specs. The company's commitment to the 2026 release window continues despite these challenges.

Turkey Proposes Restrictive Legislation Targeting Gaming Platforms

The Turkish government has proposed legislation that would impose strict regulations on digital gaming platforms such as Steam and Epic Games Store. The draft law requires platforms to maintain a local office or legal representative and to provide detailed company information, including algorithms and data processing methods, to Turkish authorities. Failure to comply could lead to severe penalties including throttling platform bandwidth by up to 90%.

Additionally, the legislation mandates age ratings for games, with unrated titles subject to removal. It also grants the government power to demand content modifications. Although the stated purpose is protecting children online, critics raise concerns about censorship and privacy infringements. This proposal reflects a broader international trend toward increased online regulation under child safety justifications.
