Nioh 3 Boss Guides and Gameplay Mechanics

A series of detailed guides for *Nioh 3* bosses and systems have been published, offering players strategies and tips to navigate its fast-paced combat and complex mechanics.

**Defeating Yamagata Masakage**

The game‚Äôs prologue boss, Yamagata Masakage, serves as an introduction to key mechanics such as Burst Break‚Äîa timed counterattack dealing significant Ki damage to stagger enemies. The guide recommends using Ninja Mode for its mobility and ranged attacks to counter Masakage's spear combos and grab attacks. Recognizing attack cues is essential to successfully execute Burst Break and avoid damage.

**Jakotsu-baba Boss Fight**

Jakotsu-baba is the first major post-prologue boss encountered in a challenging area called the Crucible. Players are advised to prepare by acquiring specific skills (Supreme Parry for healing on Burst Break, Enduring Destruction for increased damage), leveling up, and using certain summonable Guardian Spirits. The boss exhibits a limited set of attacks but requires precision due to health penalties from damage taken in the Crucible.

**Spirit Veins Access**

Spirit Veins are environmental features that allow traversal abilities like wall-running. They are initially inaccessible and require unlocking specific Guardian Spirits by progressing the story, such as Enko the fire tiger. Players need only have unlocked the relevant Guardian Spirit, not necessarily equip it, to utilize these Spirit Veins.

**Changing Appearance**

To change character appearance, players must first complete the Jakotsu-baba boss fight to unlock the Eternal Rift hub. Within this area, a mirror allows customization of both Samurai and Ninja forms separately, including hair, body, and facial features. Additionally, a character code system lets players import and share custom character designs.

**Skill Capacity and Respec**

Skill capacity, limiting active slottable skills, can be increased incrementally by collecting texts and memorandums through exploration, trading with NPCs (Sudama), and completing activities. Respeccing both stats and skills is available at any time after reaching the open-world portion of the game, allowing players to adjust their build and skill investments without permanent commitment.
