Witchhunter.exe Blends Retro Adventure with Glitch Horror

A new indie game, *Witchhunter.exe*, offers a retro-style adventure combining choose-your-own-adventure elements with a Gold Box D&D interface. Players take on the role of a Salem-era witch hunter tasked with interrogating a group of women to find a devil’s accomplice. A unique feature is the presence of the Devil as pop-up glitches disrupting the interface, adding a ghost-in-the-machine horror aspect. Combat involves typing or selecting the correct word to avoid damage. While the glitch concept recalls other haunted simulation games, this entry stands out for its nostalgic aesthetic inspired by classic horror movies. A beta playtest is soon expected on Steam, where development updates are available.

Five Smaller Steam Releases Worth Noting

A selection of five recent Steam games has drawn attention this week:

- *Mama’s Sleeping Angels* merges chaotic dreamscapes with cooperative gameplay reminiscent of *Lethal Company*, letting players kill threats and collect cursed objects. - *Lucid Blocks* offers an open-ended voxel world with procedural generation, combining surreal art inspired by liminal spaces and survival challenges. - *The State of Nowhere* echoes *Papers, Please* by making players ration scarce food in a totalitarian regime, with multiple endings and modes. - *Thysiastery* is a pixel-art roguelike dungeon crawler where players navigate a shapeshifting labyrinth to escape. - *Pluto* blends roguelike and deckbuilding mechanics in a dungeon setting involving an imprisoned wizard seeking freedom.

These games showcase a range of indie creativity across genres like survival, strategy, and roguelike dungeon exploration.

Tomb Raider Remaster Patch Sparks Community Backlash

The *Tomb Raider I-III Remastered* collection received a recent free update introducing a new challenge mode and ten new outfits. However, player response has been largely negative, citing poor visual quality, broken audio and enemy AI, and save data losses. The original remaster team was reportedly not involved in this patch's development. Suspicion of unauthorized AI use in asset creation has surfaced, following past controversies in the franchise’s voice lines. This update has drawn criticism for damaging the game’s reputation and disappointing longtime fans who expected higher-quality post-launch support.

Abubakar Salim Shares His PC Gaming Habits and Preferences

Actor and developer Abubakar Salim, known for roles like Bayek in *Assassin’s Creed: Origins* and founding Surgent Studios, gave a detailed look at his PC gaming background and setup. He credits classic strategy games and early Windows titles like Solitaire for sparking his interest. Currently, he plays a range of games, including the indie titles *No Rest for the Wicked* and *Dave the Diver*, appreciating progression systems and immersive loops. His oldest installed game is *Warcraft 3*, a favorite for its online multiplayer experience. Salim also revealed his cluttered desktop filled with shortcuts and highlighted a non-gaming essential: Final Draft scriptwriting software. His insights reflect a mix of nostalgia and contemporary indie interests.

New Mirror’s Edge-Inspired Freerunning Game in Development

Indie developer Viridian Matters is working on *Panline*, a freerunning game heavily inspired by *Mirror’s Edge*. The game features a clean, colorful open city and emphasizes parkour movement like wall-running and speed-building. It includes an in-game PDA reminiscent of a PlayStation Portable, used for navigating dynamically generated jobs. Notably, failing a run erases progress for that session, adding risk to gameplay. Slated for release this year, the game aims to capture the original’s fluid movement while offering an open-world experience, following the developer’s previous parkour title with positive Steam reviews.

Beaver City-Builder Timberborn Adds Automation in 1.0 Release

*Timberborn*, a city-builder centered on engineering beaver colonies, has officially exited early access with a 1.0 release that introduces Factorio-style automation. The update adds 20 new buildings acting as logic nodes, such as levers, sensors, and relays, enabling automated control of city functions. This lets players program responses to environmental changes, resource levels, and more, enhancing management depth. Since its alpha, *Timberborn* has grown through additions like water pumps and robotic beavers. It maintains a high Steam rating and is currently discounted, making it accessible for new players
