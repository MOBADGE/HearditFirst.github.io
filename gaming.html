Realtime-with-pause still has a place, says Star Wars Zero Company lead designer

James Brawley, lead designer for the turn-based tactics game *Star Wars Zero Company*, shared his views on realtime-with-pause (RTWP) combat in a recent interview. Contrary to some opinions that RTWP is outdated, Brawley believes the format will make a comeback when a developer innovates on its pacing and presentation. He noted that turn-based tactics games have recently resurged due to improvements in camera work and game immersion, citing Firaxis’s work on *XCOM* as a major influence.

Brawley pointed out that older tactical games could be slow and clunky, requiring patient players to endure long play sessions. His team rethought these elements to make tactical combat more dynamic and accessible, but he remains optimistic about the future of RTWP games. This discussion is timely as *Zero Company* itself blends turn-based combat with RPG elements and immersive squad management, offering an updated approach to the genre.

RPG Maker to adopt 2.5D visual style inspired by Octopath Traveler

RPG Maker, a widely used game development tool known for its 16-bit aesthetic, is set to receive its first major visual overhaul. The update will introduce 2.5D graphics reminiscent of the style made popular by *Octopath Traveler* and other modern Square Enix RPGs. Early trailers suggest characters will move on a fixed grid rather than free roam, but lighting and shadows show notable improvements.

This shift could help RPG Maker games stand out in a crowded marketplace where many titles rely on default assets. More details on release timing and whether this will be an expansion or a new product edition are still pending. This update represents a potential milestone in the evolution of RPG Maker’s visual capabilities, possibly attracting creators aiming for a more modern look while retaining the platform’s accessibility.

Dimension 20 to produce official Vampire: The Masquerade actual-play series

Dimension 20, a popular tabletop RPG actual-play series traditionally focused on Dungeons & Dragons, announced its first collaboration with an external publisher. The new show, *City Council of Darkness*, will be based on *Vampire: The Masquerade* and produced in partnership with White Wolf. The campaign features outcast vampires navigating the political and supernatural challenges of a small town called Purpee, Oregon.

The campaign is led by Brennan Lee Mulligan and includes the core Dimension 20 cast. It is set to premiere on April 8 as a 14-episode run on the subscription service Dropout. This marks a notable expansion for Dimension 20 into licensed settings, potentially broadening the audience for *Vampire: The Masquerade* and showcasing new narrative possibilities for actual-play shows.

Pro World of Warcraft player disconnected mid-world first raid due to subscription lapse

During a highly competitive world first race for the new *World of Warcraft: Midnight* expansion, a professional player unexpectedly disconnected after forgetting to renew his game subscription. The lapse occurred mid-boss fight and was visible to thousands on a live Twitch raid stream. The player had actually purchased an in-game WoW token but neglected to activate it, leading to the sudden disconnection.

While embarrassing, this incident reflected the multitiered complexity of managing multiple raid accounts simultaneously—common in world first races. The guild quickly resumed the fight and attributed the oversight to human error. Such moments highlight behind-the-scenes challenges even for top-tier esports competitors.

Valve veteran criticizes Epic Games CEO amid massive layoffs

Chet Faliszek, former Valve writer and developer, posted a strong critique of Epic Games CEO Tim Sweeney following Epic’s recent layoffs of over 1,000 employees. Faliszek questioned the company culture at Epic, contrasting it with Valve’s approach to employee ownership and creative agency. He expressed concern that layoffs undermine developers’ motivation and the industry’s overall health.

Faliszek lamented the loss of passionate long-term employees at Epic and suggested the company's financial focus has overshadowed its commitment to creative work. While Epic highlighted the necessity of restructuring, Faliszek’s comments underscore ongoing tensions in the games industry regarding workforce stability and creative leadership.
