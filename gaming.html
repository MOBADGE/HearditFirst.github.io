New Sodium-Ion Battery Breakthrough Could Improve Device Charging Safety

Researchers at Tokyo University of Science have achieved a promising breakthrough with sodium-ion batteries, which could eventually replace lithium-ion batteries in consumer devices. Sodium-ion batteries use more abundant materials and are less prone to overheating or catching fire, addressing safety concerns with current lithium-ion tech. The breakthrough involves combining hard carbon with aluminium oxide to improve ion flow, making sodium-ion batteries more practical. If commercialized, this could lead to safer external chargers allowed on flights and more sustainable battery production. The development is in early stages but offers a potential alternative to current battery limitations.
