**Marathon Introduces Free Kit Frenzy Mode for Experimental Testing**

Marathon is launching a new experimental playlist called the "Sponsored Queue," starting April 15. Unlike existing Ranked and Cryo Archive playlists that require players to have high-value loadouts, this mode lets all players start with a free sponsored kit—essentially a zero-credit buy-in. Set exclusively on the Dire Marsh map, players will need to scavenge and upgrade their gear during matches. The test will run for two weeks and aims to help the developers understand gameplay dynamics under low fixed gear conditions and observe how equal starting points influence playstyles and balance. This mode coincides with an upcoming balancing patch where the Recon shell weapon will see a buff.

This approach may offer a purer skill-based experience by leveling the gear field, reducing early match disparities. Bungie's exploration of different ecosystem factors could lead to new permanent modes or balancing insights.

**91-Year-Old Gamer Engrossed in Play Sparks Police Welfare Check**

In Westlake, Ohio, local police conducted a welfare check on a 91-year-old woman after she missed her daily "Are You Okay?" call, part of a community safety program. Upon entry, officers discovered that she was safe and healthy but deeply immersed in playing video games, attempting to beat her record level. This lighthearted incident illustrates the growing diversity of gaming demographics, highlighting that video games are not limited by age.

While this story was widely shared for its unexpected twist and happy ending, it also raises considerations about how society views older gamers—often treating them as novelties rather than recognizing their genuine participation in gaming culture. The specific game she was playing remains unknown.

**Pickmon Rebrands to Pickmos Amid Controversy Over Imitation**

Pickmon, an upcoming creature-collecting survival game criticized for closely copying designs from Pokémon, Final Fantasy, Zelda, Overwatch, and Palworld, has officially changed its title to Pickmos. The single-letter change was justified by the publisher NetworkGo as aligning better with the game’s “brand identity and lore,” though it follows scrutiny over potential legal risks and brand confusion.

The creatures retain the "Pickmon" name, but the rebranding attempts to distance the title from Pokémon. Given Nintendo's history of legal protection over its IP and ongoing lawsuits related to similar imitators, this change could be a strategic move to reduce infringement claims. However, critics remain skeptical of the superficial nature of the change.

**Fable Studio Confirms Autumn 2026 Release Despite GTA 6 Delay Rumors**

Playground Games and Microsoft have reaffirmed that the Fable reboot is still scheduled for autumn 2026, despite rumors suggesting an internal delay to avoid coinciding with the November release of Grand Theft Auto 6. Industry insider Jeff Grubb had speculated on a possible delay motivated by concerns over launching alongside GTA 6.

While such strategic delays are common in the industry, Playground has reiterated their commitment to the original timeline. The challenge of scheduling releases amid competition remains complex, as delays to avoid one title may simply lead to clashes with others later.

**Resident Evil: Requiem Mod Makes Enemies Tiny for a Less Scary Experience**

For players finding Resident Evil: Requiem’s enemies overly intimidating, modder lucablight83 has released the Enemies Scale Modifier mod on NexusMods. This mod allows users to shrink enemies down to as little as 10% of their original size, reducing the fear factor while maintaining challenge, as smaller enemies can be harder to target.

The mod requires the RE Framework to operate and offers real-time toggling of enemy sizes. While it adds a humorous element to the game, some visual glitches and physics quirks are present when enemies are scaled down. This mod is an example of the flexibility community mods provide in adjusting game experiences to player preferences.
