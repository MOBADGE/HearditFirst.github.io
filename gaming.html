A Keycap That Doubles as a Watch

A unique Miyo Watch Keycap, created by Sneakbox, integrates a functioning analogue watch into a single 1U keyboard keycap. It features a Miyota 5Y20 quartz movement, glow-in-the-dark dual-tone dial and hands, and sapphire glass with anti-reflective coating. Originally released in limited quantities in October 2025 at $300 each, it remains a niche item for collectors. While the current model is not waterproof, the creator expressed interest in developing a waterproof version in the future. This fusion of watchmaking and keyboard customization highlights ongoing innovation in personalizing gaming setups, although its high cost limits it to enthusiasts.

Dell CEO Projects Massive Growth in AI Memory Demand

Dell’s CEO Michael Dell projected that total memory demand driven by AI infrastructure could grow by about 625 times from 2022 to 2028. This estimate is based on expectations that AI accelerators will increase from 80 GB of High Bandwidth Memory (HBM) per unit in 2022 to around 2 TB by 2028, alongside a 25-fold growth in accelerator deployment in data centers. However, this rapid increase poses challenges, since only a few manufacturers currently produce advanced HBM4 memory, and supply already struggles to meet current needs. This forecast signals potential and ongoing difficulties in scaling hardware capacity to meet the expanding requirements of AI workloads, which could impact the broader tech and gaming industries reliant on these technologies.

Arc Raiders Fixes Rocketeer Sound Bug After Community Backlash

The recent Arc Raiders 1.23.0 update addressed a bug where the Rocketeer enemy emitted the same alert sound as the Vaporizer enemy. This issue had sparked community petitions demanding the original Rocketeer sound be restored to maintain clear audio cues during gameplay. The patch reinstates the unique Rocketeer alert noise, resolving player confusion. The update also tackles performance problems on the Close Scrutiny map, including despawning idle summoned enemies and adjusting enemy movement for more consistent behavior. Additionally, it fixes item swapping mechanics and corrects damage exploits with Trigger Grenades. These fixes aim to improve game clarity and balance without easing the challenging aspects of the map.

Death Stranding 2 Performance Improved on Steam Deck After Patch

Following its PC release last month, Death Stranding 2 initially performed poorly on the Steam Deck, with frequent frame rate drops and stutter. A recent 1.2 patch from porting specialists Nixxes improved performance significantly, allowing the game to run consistently between 30 and 35 fps during open world sections and up to 50 fps indoors using the default portable preset. While battery life remains limited to about 90 minutes per charge, the smoother experience demonstrates ongoing optimization efforts for handheld PCs. This highlights how continued support can enhance playability of complex PC titles on portable devices, expanding access to demanding games outside traditional setups.

Playing Dungeons & Dragons in Space Is Possible

German astronaut Rabea Rogge demonstrated in a video how to roll a twenty-sided die (D20) in zero gravity aboard a space mission. She managed the dice roll by flicking the die with a spin and catching it in her fist, confirming that role-playing games like Dungeons & Dragons can be played in microgravity environments. This small experiment illustrates how even traditional tabletop gaming activities can adapt to unique conditions in space. Rogge’s flight was part of a private SpaceX mission, underscoring interest in leisure activities beyond Earth and the broad appeal of gaming culture.

Kerbal Space Program Sees Player Surge Linked to Artemis 2 Mission

Kerbal Space Program has recorded its highest concurrent player count on Steam in more than ten years, coinciding with NASA’s Artemis 2 lunar mission. Players and fans cite the high-profile spaceflight as their inspiration to revisit or try the game. Community discussions on Reddit reflect enthusiasm about recreating Artemis-style missions within Kerbal’s simulation environment. While the player increase may decline after the mission concludes, this resurgence highlights how real-world space events can revive interest in related simulation games and educational software. Kerbal Space Program 2 also noted a smaller player increase, though it has struggled with retention since launch.
