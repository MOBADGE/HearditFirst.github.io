Gunzilla Faces Widespread Payment Issues

Game studio Gunzilla, known for *Off The Grid* and reviving *Game Informer* magazine, has been reported by multiple sources to have ongoing payment problems with contractors and partners. While CEO Vlad Korolev says salaries for full-time employees have only been delayed briefly, many contractors—especially in the Ukraine office—have reportedly gone unpaid since 2025. Notably, Gunzilla owes over $100,000 to a marketing company contracted in 2024, with no payments made despite formal commitments.

Legal troubles are also mounting. Gunzilla’s UK division is currently subject to a winding-up petition from asset development studio The Vertex Guild over unpaid debts. Another petition backed by the UK tax authority and a publisher was recently withdrawn after Gunzilla settled those dues. Contractors have gone public with their experiences of extended payment delays, citing stress and broken trust. Attempts to obtain comment from Gunzilla did not succeed before publication.

The situation highlights risks in the game industry’s reliance on contractors and raises questions about Gunzilla’s financial stability and management transparency.

Fallout: New Vegas Remaster Unlikely, Says Former Developer

Chris Avellone, writer and former Obsidian creative officer for *Fallout: New Vegas*, expressed skepticism about the possibility of a remaster similar to *Oblivion’s*. He claims Bethesda may lack both the full source code and the necessary engineering expertise to rebuild the game effectively. Avellone noted that the final milestone involving source code delivery to Bethesda was never fulfilled by Obsidian, possibly due to unresolved financial disputes.

While Bethesda owns both the *Fallout* and *Obsidian* studios under Microsoft, Avellone doubts collaboration will be smooth. He suggests that any future remaster projects might rely on complex engine integrations like those used in the *Oblivion* remaster, but given the technical hurdles, a *New Vegas* remaster is not assured in the near term.

Fans hoping for an updated version of this popular RPG should temper expectations as these underlying challenges persist.

Reddit User Shares Complex Water-Cooled PC Build

A Reddit user known as Yam_Founder shared details of a custom gaming PC build named “Seraphim,” featuring an elaborate water-cooling system and extensive fan array inspired by angelic imagery. The project reportedly took four years to complete, designed to improve maintenance accessibility despite its complexity.

Yam_Founder, experienced in welding and cryogenics, explained that the cooling system is divided into isolated zones for ease of servicing. The large radiator surface area allows for quieter, more efficient passive cooling. While visually striking, the build also serves practical purposes, especially for users prioritizing long-term maintenance and cooling performance. Custom cases may be available upon request from the creator.

This build reflects the growing interest in highly personalized and functional PC hardware setups beyond conventional designs.

Anthropic Holds Meeting with White House Following AI Model Preview

Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei met with senior White House officials last week after releasing an early version of Claude Mythos, an AI model capable of identifying thousands of cybersecurity vulnerabilities and writing its own exploits. The White House described the meeting as “productive and constructive,” focusing on opportunities for collaboration and balancing innovation with safety.

This marks a shift from Anthropic’s recent tensions with the US Department of Defense, which blacklisted the company over its refusal to remove AI safeguards related to autonomous weapons and surveillance. Despite previous harsh remarks from former President Trump labeling Anthropic as a problematic company, the new talks suggest a more measured government approach toward managing powerful AI technologies with security implications.

The conversation underscores the challenges governments face in regulating advanced AI while engaging with the developers responsible for them.

Elden Ring Movie Scheduled for March 2028, Cast Revealed

The film adapts the widely acclaimed game known for its complex, mysterious lore.
