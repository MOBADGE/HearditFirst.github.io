Fallout: New Vegas Could Have Been Set in New Orleans

Chris Avellone, writer for Fallout: New Vegas and former Obsidian creative officer, revealed in a recent interview that New Orleans was a serious contender for the game’s setting. Inspired by the unique post-apocalyptic vibe featured in the comic "Grendel: Four Devils, One Hell," Avellone felt the city’s flavor could have offered an interesting twist on the Fallout universe. He described New Orleans as “so cool” and “so sweet” for a Fallout game. While this setting wasn’t pursued, Avellone shared insights into Fallout’s future, including hints Bethesda once discouraged the use of San Francisco and possibly removed related content, suggesting Fallout 5 might explore that area. He also mentioned Bethesda’s lack of source code and engineering knowledge for New Vegas, which complicates prospects for a remaster.

New Laptops with 128GB RAM Coming to the International Space Station

NASA is updating the computing equipment aboard the International Space Station (ISS) with custom HP ZBook G9 Fury laptops. These machines come equipped with Intel Core Ultra 9 processors, Nvidia RTX Pro Blackwell GPUs, and a substantial 128 GB of DDR5 ECC (Error-Correcting Code) RAM. The ECC memory is critical for maintaining data integrity in the harsh space environment where radiation can cause errors. HP subjected these laptops to rigorous tests, including neutron radiation bombardment and battery assessments, to ensure reliability in zero gravity. The first batch launched in October 2025, with activation expected soon. While comparable hardware is more difficult to obtain and afford on Earth currently, the ISS benefits from this top-tier technology to support essential scientific missions.

Backdoors Found in 31 WordPress Plugins After Ownership Change

Security researchers have discovered that 31 WordPress plugins, previously owned by a company called Essential Plugin, were sold on Flippa and shortly after had backdoors inserted by the new owner. The backdoors were activated in early April 2026, allowing malicious code distribution to potentially thousands of websites using these plugins. WordPress.org does not currently flag or review plugin ownership changes, meaning users had no prior warning before the malicious update. This incident highlights ongoing risks in software supply chain security, as similar attacks have occurred previously with other popular plugins. WordPress users are advised to update carefully and monitor plugin sources more diligently.

Alternatives to 2026’s Big AAA Games for Budget-Conscious Players

Facing rising costs for AAA releases and console hardware, PC Gamer compiled a selection of overlooked or indie games as budget-friendly alternatives to major 2026 titles. For example, "Samson" and "The Precinct" offer different takes on crime sandbox games as alternatives to Grand Theft Auto 6. "Fear the Spotlight" and "Crow Country" provide survival horror experiences similar to Resident Evil Requiem, while "Ooblets" and "Before the Green Moon" appeal to fans of Pokémon Pokopia with farming and creature collection mechanics. Racing fans might enjoy "XF Extreme Formula" or "Wreckreation" instead of Forza Horizon 6. For those seeking sci-fi RPG action akin to Death Stranding 2, "The Alters" and "Echo" represent cheaper options. The list emphasizes diverse experiences without the high price tags or hardware commitments of blockbuster games.

Kiki Wolfkill Departs Microsoft After Nearly 30 Years

Kiki Wolfkill, a long-time Microsoft veteran and executive producer known for her work on the Halo franchise and Xbox Film and TV, announced her departure from the company on April 17, 2026. Over nearly three decades, Wolfkill contributed to various franchises such as Monster Truck Madness 2, Fable, Mass Effect, Forza Motorsport, and played a key role in transmedia projects including the Halo TV series. In her farewell message, she expressed gratitude for her career at Microsoft and excitement for new opportunities outside the company. Wolfkill’s exit marks the end of an era for one of Xbox’s most influential creative figures.
