Japanese Typeface Maker's Font Licensing Fees Surge, Linked to AI Investments

Game developers in Japan have recently faced a dramatic increase in licensing fees for Japanese fonts supplied by Fontworks, now under the ownership of US-based Monotype Imaging. The company terminated its affordable LETS license plan, which cost around $380 annually, replacing it with a steep fee of approximately $20,500 per year and imposing a 25,000-user cap. Due to backlash, Monotype has temporarily allowed developers to renew under the old terms.

This hefty price hike reportedly stems from Monotype‚Äôs significant but unsuccessful investment in AI-driven typography projects, which led to layoffs and efforts to recoup losses. This situation is particularly challenging for Japanese developers, as Monotype owns several other font providers, limiting affordable alternatives. The episode highlights how risky ventures in AI and private equity ownership can indirectly impact game development costs.

Dune: Awakening Chapter 3 Update Adds New Systems and Maps

Funcom announced a substantial Chapter 3 update for their survival MMO, Dune: Awakening. The update features a completely overhauled Landsraad system, introducing a specialization experience system spanning five mission types: combat, sabotage, crafting, harvesting, and exploration. Players earn XP to unlock traits in five specialization trees, offering more character customization.

Additionally, eight new small overland maps provide focused missions like timed races. The update also brings an augmentation system for crafting mods, new multi-phase bosses, and five repeatable testing stations with escalating difficulty. This marks the largest update yet, delivering more endgame content and gameplay depth, aiming to extend player engagement.
