Witcher 3 Multiplayer Mod Released

A notable modder known as rejuvenate has created a multiplayer mod for *The Witcher 3*, turning the acclaimed single-player RPG into a multiplayer experience. Dubbed *Witcher Online*, the mod allows players to connect on servers and interact through emotes and character customization. However, major gameplay elements remain single-player focused: enemies, horses, and boats are not synchronized between players, preventing cooperative combat or shared exploration.

While the mod cannot deliver full MMO-style gameplay or co-op combat, it offers a way for players to socialize and roleplay together within the world of the Continent. The mod requires several other mods to function and is available for download on Nexus Mods. This development is significant for fans as it introduces a new social dimension to a game historically known for its solo experience.

Arknights: Endfield Gameplay Guides and Tier List

Several new articles focus on *Arknights: Endfield*, a tactical RPG with factory-building and combat elements. Detailed guides explain early gameplay mechanics such as how to transmit power to the Protocol Resonance Tower and how to unlock access authorization for Protocol Dataloggers. These steps are essential for advancing in the game's factory automation and technology research systems.

Additionally, a comprehensive tier list evaluates the best characters in the game based on their roles, elemental affinities, and synergy with team compositions. The list categorizes characters from S-tier to C-tier, discussing their combat abilities and ideal teammates. This information can help players decide which characters to invest in as they build their teams.

Also, four active redeemable codes for *Arknights: Endfield* have been shared, awarding players with various in-game resources necessary for progression. The article includes instructions on how to redeem these codes once specific story milestones are reached.

World of Warcraft Add-on Changes Continue

*World of Warcraft* is undergoing major changes to its add-on system following the recent pre-patch for its upcoming expansion, *Midnight*. The changes restrict the information add-ons can access during combat to reduce reliance on third-party tools that simplify complex raid mechanics. This redesign responds to player feedback about add-on complexity but has resulted in many popular add-ons and accessibility tools breaking when the update launched.

Blizzard is working closely with add-on developers to adjust APIs and update the core user interface to restore functionality while maintaining the restrictions intended to preserve game balance. Senior game director Ion Hazzikostas notes that further patches will refine these changes, attempting to balance competitive integrity with user customization.

Ubisoft Implements Return-to-Office Policy Amidst Restructuring

Ubisoft has announced a full return-to-office policy requiring all employees to work onsite five days a week. This decision accompanies a broader company restructuring involving studio closures, project cancellations, and layoffs. Ubisoft claims the move aims to enhance collaboration and creativity but faces significant backlash from employees and unions concerned about work-life balance and job security.

Industry observers note that such mandates can prompt voluntary resignations, effectively serving as indirect layoffs. The union Solidaires Informatique has called for strike action in response to the policy. Ubisoft simultaneously reiterated its investment in generative AI technology, suggesting a shift in company focus despite workforce reductions.

Enshrouded Roadmap and Release Window Updated

The survival crafting game *Enshrouded* has updated its roadmap, setting a 1.0 release window for fall 2026. The developers at Keen Games detailed plans for new features, including an Adventure Sharing system, world expansions, combat improvements, and performance optimizations targeting lower-end systems.

Although the full release is months away, the team intends to continue supporting and updating the game for years, including post-launch paid expansions. The update reflects a continued commitment to evolving the early access title based on player feedback and lengthy development progress.
