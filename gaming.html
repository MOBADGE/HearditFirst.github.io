Cassette Boy Revives 2D/3D Puzzle Hybrid Gameplay

The recently released puzzle game *Cassette Boy* offers a fresh take on the 2D/3D hybrid puzzle format popularized by 2012's *Fez*. Players navigate a world that appears two-dimensional but can be rotated in three dimensions to discover hidden secrets. Unlike *Fez*, *Cassette Boy* starts as a Zelda-like adventure before introducing a rotational mechanic that transforms how puzzles and exploration are approached. The game challenges players to think spatially and intuitively, with puzzles that provide little guidance but reward careful observation and experimentation.

This gameplay style, which blends 2D visuals with 3D manipulation, has been rare since *Fez*. *Cassette Boy* stands out for pushing the concept further with intricate puzzles involving perspective tricks and the interplay between visible and hidden elements. It has received praise for its clever design, though some players may find its orientation-based navigation demanding. Overall, *Cassette Boy* marks a notable return of a distinctive puzzle genre seldom seen in recent years.

Dune: Awakening Introduces Character Transfers After Months Without

Survival MMO *Dune: Awakening* has added a highly requested feature in its 1.2.40.0 update: character transfers between servers. For the first time since the game's June 2025 launch, players can move their characters, along with inventories, bases, vehicles, and in-game currency, to different private or official servers. Transfers require a token system allowing one transfer per week.

This feature addresses community concerns over players being stuck on inactive or overly dominated servers and allows for greater flexibility in finding active player bases and factions. Transfers must be initiated in a specific in-game region, and players need to use backup tools for bases and vehicles to avoid losing assets. Modder Demonstrates Prototype for Minecraft and Hytale Crossplay

A modder named Sadat Sahib has demonstrated a working prototype that enables crossplay between *Minecraft* and *Hytale*, two block-based sandbox games. The mod synchronizes player movements, block placements, and other game elements between the two games running simultaneously, with *Hytale* serving as the host. Additional features like cross-game chat, animation sync, and combat have also been partially implemented.

While still a work-in-progress and not publicly released, this prototype shows promise for bridging two popular but separate gaming communities. Challenges remain including consistent world generation across games. If developed further, it could influence future modding projects and player experiences in sandbox games
