Pragmata Surpasses One Million Sales Shortly After Launch

Capcom’s new sci-fi action game Pragmata has sold over one million copies within just two days of its release on April 17, 2026. The game, which follows a father-daughter pair in a strange lunar setting, experienced a lengthy development cycle marked by three delays and a four-year span since its announcement in June 2020. Despite its unconventional narrative and gameplay that combines shooting and hacking mechanics, Pragmata has received generally positive reviews, including an 87 score from PC Gamer. Its early demo attracted over two million downloads, indicating strong interest ahead of launch. The solid initial sales suggest Pragmata has carved out a notable place in Capcom’s portfolio and could potentially lead to future projects set in the same universe.

Fishing Arrives Tomorrow in RuneScape: Dragonwilds

RuneScape: Dragonwilds will finally introduce fishing, a staple skill traditionally associated with the series, starting April 21. The skill has been notably missing since the game’s launch, disappointing many long-time players. The upcoming update lets players fish with rods and nets, gather around various water bodies, and even create mounted trophies displayed in their bases. Magic-based fishing is also hinted at, offering an alternative to traditional methods. Fishing, like other Dragonwilds skills, will have a level cap of 99 and reward players with a themed cape upon mastery. This addition fills an important gap in Dragonwilds’ gameplay, aligning it closer with player expectations for RuneScape’s core activities.

Steam Week in Review: Windrose Outperforms Pragmata on PC

In a competitive week for new game releases on Steam, the pirate-themed survival game Windrose has outpaced Capcom’s Pragmata in terms of concurrent player counts on PC. Windrose, which launched on April 14 in early access, sold one million copies in six days and achieved peak simultaneous players above 220,000. Pragmata, with a higher launch price and availability on consoles, saw a peak of roughly 68,000 concurrent players on Steam. Although Pragmata’s sales are strong and it is available across platforms, the numbers underline a substantial demand for pirate-survival games on PC. Windrose’s success highlights player interest in that genre compared to the more narrative-driven, single-player sci-fi shooter experience offered by Pragmata.

Magic: The Gathering’s New Set Focuses on College Mechanics

Wizards of the Coast’s upcoming Magic: The Gathering set, Secrets of Strixhaven, emphasizes distinct gameplay mechanics tied to five magical colleges, each representing a combination of colors and unique in-game abilities. The set arrives in Arena on April 21 and in physical form on April 24. Key mechanics include Infusion (Witherbloom), Opus (Prismari), Increment (Quandrix), Flashback (Lorehold), and Repartee (Silverquill). These mechanics aim to differentiate the colleges more clearly than in the first Strixhaven set, affecting how players build decks and strategize with instant and sorcery spells. This design approach suggests an effort to deepen thematic and mechanical diversity within the game’s existing framework.

Upcoming 5th-Generation OLED Gaming Monitors from Philips and AOC

Philips and AOC are preparing to launch new 5th-generation QD-OLED gaming monitors in the third quarter of 2026. These 34-inch curved displays aim to address prior OLED issues such as font fringing while offering high peak brightness (up to 1300 nits) and improved HDR performance. The AOC AGP346UCSD has a higher refresh rate of 360 Hz and supports the latest HDMI and DisplayPort standards, targeting high-end gamers with a price near €999. The Philips Evnia 34M2C8600P offers a 175 Hz refresh rate, additional USB Type-C power delivery, embedded RGB lighting, and a white design, priced around €899. Both monitors feature an 1800R curve for immersive viewing. These releases represent continued advancement in OLED technology for PC gaming displays, providing more options for consumers seeking high image quality and performance.
