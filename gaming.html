Overwatch to Revise Hero Ban System and Launch Season 2: Summit

Blizzard is planning improvements to Overwatch’s hero ban system, addressing player complaints about limited ban choices and uncoordinated team picks. Currently, up to four heroes can be banned per match, split evenly between teams, with a voting system determining the bans. Associate game director Alec Dawson indicated the team is exploring ways to provide players more detailed information on teammates’ hero preferences during the ban phase to encourage better-coordinated bans. Moreover, a potential fifth "Lobby Ban" is under consideration, which would not be restricted by the current role-limit rules and would factor in votes from both teams for a final ban decision. If implemented, this could help target commonly banned heroes more effectively and improve team composition flexibility.

Coinciding with these system updates, Overwatch Season 2: Summit will launch on April 14, 2026, at midnight Pacific Time. The new season brings a fresh DPS hero named Sierra, who uses an automatic rifle with tracking and deployable drones for offense and scouting. The update also revamps several existing hero abilities, reintroduces a post-match accolades system with voice chat access, and overhauls the map voting process to prioritize maps with majority votes and introduce a random map option. The ability to lose less competitive progress on new or reworked maps is another notable change.

These changes aim to refine gameplay experience and offer players a wider strategic range in hero selection and match flow.

Epic Games Developing Disney-Themed Extraction Shooter Amid Mixed Internal Feedback

Following significant layoffs last month and a noted decline in Fortnite engagement, Epic Games is reportedly advancing a Disney-branded extraction shooter scheduled for release in November 2026. This new game represents the first major output following Disney’s $1.5 billion investment in Epic. The collaboration seeks to build a new entertainment universe within Epic's gaming ecosystem.

Reports indicate internal mixed reactions within Epic toward the game's originality and mechanics. While some staff have expressed lukewarm views, Epic’s senior communications director emphasized that these opinions do not reflect the company’s ambitions for the project. In addition to this first shooter, Epic is developing at least two more Disney-related games, although details on those remain sparse and have also received cautious internal reactions.

The extraction shooter genre has seen substantial entries in recent years, and Epic is looking to leverage Disney’s IP to regain momentum and diversify its portfolio amid its business challenges.

Microsoft Expands FAT32 Partition Limit in Windows 11 Beta

In the latest Beta Channel build of Windows 11 (build 26220.8165), Microsoft has raised the maximum FAT32 partition size allowed via command line from 32 GB to 2 TB. This change aligns with a feature previously available in more experimental Windows Insider builds and is expected to roll out to public releases soon.

While FAT32 still limits individual file sizes to 4 GB, a higher partition cap will assist users needing to format large drives or USB sticks with FAT32 — a common necessity for legacy hardware compatibility or certain firmware updates. Until now, users had to rely on third-party tools to create FAT32 partitions larger than 32 GB. This update improves native Windows support for these use cases.

The change is minor in scope but resolves a longstanding inconvenience for some users dealing with older systems or specific technical requirements.

Bethesda’s Former Marketing VP Credits Skyrim with Elevating Studio’s Industry Status

Pete Hines, former marketing vice president at Bethesda, reflected on the company’s rise in stature during an interview, pinpointing The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim as the critical game that made Bethesda a notable industry force. According to Hines, earlier titles like Morrowind, Oblivion, and Fallout 3 laid groundwork, but Skyrim was the “Holy shit” moment that commanded attention from other studios and awarded Bethesda a lasting reputation.

He noted that while Fallout 3 was important, Skyrim succeeded in attracting a broad audience, including many who had not previously engaged with RPGs. Oblivion was recognized as Bethesda’s initial step toward mainstream credibility, but Skyrim’s unprecedented popularity established the studio as a serious contender for year-end awards and market dominance.

This perspective highlights the impact Skyrim had in shaping Bethesda’s brand and shaping player expectations for its future projects.
