Skyrim Mod Adds Realistic Wind Effects to Weather

A new mod for Skyrim, called Particle Wind, developed by RavenKZP, enhances the atmospheric experience by adding wind effects that influence various environmental elements. Unlike the vanilla game, this mod causes bushes, fire sources like torches and candles, smoke, steam, and waterfall sprays to visibly react to wind, making weather conditions feel more dynamic and immersive. To use Particle Wind, players need to have SKSE and Address Library for SKSE Plugins installed. The mod is configurable, allowing users to adjust the strength of the wind effects through an .ini file.

The significance of this mod lies in its subtle but effective addition to Skyrim’s environment, improving immersion without altering core gameplay. RavenKZP is known for similar mods that enhance Skyrim's realism by addressing small details often overlooked.

Controversy Surrounds Former Ashes of Creation Studio Founder

New allegations have emerged against Steven Sharif, founder of the now-defunct MMO studio Intrepid Studios, which was developing Ashes of Creation before its cancellation earlier this year. Investor Jason Caramanis accused Sharif of misappropriating millions of dollars from Kickstarter funds for personal expenses including private chefs, cigars, antiques, and trading card shops.

A detailed video by YouTuber NefasQS presents ledger data and employee testimonies suggesting financially questionable spending patterns, though these claims have not been independently verified. Sharif has denied wrongdoing, blaming board members for the project's failure. The dispute highlights ongoing concerns about accountability and management transparency in crowdfunded game development projects.

Sekiro Mod Transforms Action Game into Skateboarding Adventure

Modder Ionian-MikiriyAKATotallyNotShinobi has released "Jet Set Sekiro," a mod for Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice that reimagines the game as a skateboarding sim. Using Sekiro’s animations and the Fountainhead Palace map, the mod replaces combat with skate-style movement and graffiti tagging challenges.

This mod represents an unusual genre crossover that plays on nostalgia for early 2000s skateboarding games. It adds a playful new way to experience a traditionally serious action title, showcasing the creativity and technical skill of the modding community in repurposing existing game assets.
