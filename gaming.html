Computer History Museum Launches Digital Portal Showcasing Vintage Hardware

The Computer History Museum near Mountain View, California, has opened a digital portal granting public access to its extensive collection of computing artifacts remotely. The online platform categorizes items into curator picks, stories from the collection, and a "discovery wall" featuring a wide range of historical hardware. Notable exhibits include early multi-button computer mice and the "world's first Lisp processor megachip." The portal offers high-resolution images, allowing detailed examination of devices from earlier computing eras. This development provides researchers, enthusiasts, and casual viewers a convenient way to engage with computing history without traveling to the museum, preserving and promoting awareness of technological evolution.
