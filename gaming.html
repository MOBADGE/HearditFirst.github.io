Solasta 2 Early Access Improves on Its Predecessor

Solasta 2 has entered early access with several refinements over the original game. Improvements include enhanced voice acting with recognized actors, updated writing that feels less clichéd, and adoption of D&D’s 2024 rules making combat choices more meaningful. The game focuses on tactical battles rather than plot, presenting a new overworld map on a hex grid filled with permanent and temporary encounters, offering increased exploration freedom compared to the first installment’s linear paths. Character creation has been adjusted for better visual consistency, although variety remains somewhat limited.

While the game’s story progresses slowly, players are tasked with searching for a missing sister across Neokos wilderness. Combat scenarios take advantage of terrain and strategic positioning, though some AI pathfinding issues persist. The early access roadmap includes plans for additional classes, co-op, crafting, and deeper faction interactions. Overall, Solasta 2’s early access showcases solid enhancements that are likely to appeal to fans willing to revisit the initial act during ongoing development.

Dead by Daylight Shifts Update Strategy with K-pop Chapter

Dead by Daylight’s latest chapter, "All-Kill: Comeback," released without a new killer character for the first time in years. Instead, the update centers on a killer rework, a new survivor, and a new map. This change comes amid the developers’ adjustment of their update cadence—reducing new killer additions to three per year instead of four—and focusing more on quality of life improvements and system changes. Developers aim to make the game more accessible, addressing complexities that have accumulated over its decade-long life.

Senior creative director Dave Richard explained this approach as a trial to determine if releasing chapters without new killers but with substantial content is viable. This strategy potentially frees resources for other improvements, such as customizable UI elements and new game modes. While some players may be disappointed by fewer killers, Behaviour Interactive remains committed to maintaining a steady release pace, experimenting with varied content types in future updates.

Crimson Desert Launches Worldwide with Preload Available

Crimson Desert, the action role-playing game from Pearl Abyss, officially launched worldwide on March 19, 2026, with launch times staggered by region. Players can prepare for the release by preloading the game on Steam now, with the download size exceeding 92 GB and a minimum 150 GB free storage requirement. The game is notable for its vast open world, complex systems, and a blend of MMO-like mechanics with single-player narrative elements.

The developer’s focus on exploration, combat variety, and large-scale battles creates a diverse experience. Despite some criticisms related to story pacing and system overload, Crimson Desert’s launch marks an ambitious entry in the RPG genre. The simultaneous global release ensures players across different time zones can start playing together without delay.

Thomas the Tank Engine Game Features Unexpected Physics Glitches

Thomas & Friends: Wonders of Sodor, newly released on Steam, has attracted attention for its unconventional physics behaviors. Built on Unreal Engine 5, the game sometimes causes the titular trains, including Thomas, to be ejected violently off the tracks in unpredictable ways. These physics glitches have become a notable aspect among players sharing viral videos online.

Despite this, initial player feedback on Steam is largely positive. The game casts players in a simulation of duties on the North Western Railway, though the unpredictable physics add an unexpected twist to the experience. The launch illustrates how physics engines can significantly affect gameplay and player perception, even when glitches are unintentional.

Tomb Raider Remastered Studio Rebuts AI Usage Claims Amid Bug Fix Promises

Following the recent Challenge Mode update to Tomb Raider 1-3 Remastered, Aspyr Studios has responded to negative user reviews and accusations that new in-game outfits were created using generative AI. The studio denied these claims, affirming that all new outfits were artist-created and that no AI-generated assets were used.

Aspyr acknowledged texture problems and technical bugs introduced by the update and said fixing these issues is their top priority. They invited players to report bugs and promised forthcoming patches across platforms. While complaints about outfit design persist, the studio has not indicated if aesthetic concerns will be addressed in upcoming fixes.
