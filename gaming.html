**Marathon's First Three Seasons Revealed**

Bungie has shared the roadmap for the first three seasons of its extraction shooter, Marathon. Launching alongside the game, Season 1 (March-June) introduces a new map called Cryo Archive, ranked mode, a special event called C.A.R.R.I., a new gun, and new implants. Season 2 (June-August) will bring a nighttime map variant, a new Sentinel Shell, an upgrade/build system called "The Cradle," new guns, mods, and enemies. Details for Season 3 (August-November) remain undisclosed but are expected to include new events, weapons, and possibly new maps or playable classes.

A notable aspect is the mandatory seasonal server wipes, which reset player progression and vault items, retaining only cosmetics, titles, and Codex progress. This approach aims to prevent gear hoarding and refresh the gameplay loop regularly, maintaining player engagement over time.

**Control Resonant Integrates Deep RPG Mechanics**

Remedy Entertainment's upcoming Control Resonant departs from its predecessors by focusing more heavily on RPG elements. The game features extensive talent trees, customizable weapon forms, and build-crafting systems reminiscent of action RPGs like Diablo. Players can modify weapon attack styles, abilities, and combat enhancements within a mental space called The Gap.

Combat revolves around chaining weapon attacks to charge supernatural abilities that stun enemies, allowing for executions that boost melee damage. This cycle encourages specialization in combat builds rather than generalist playstyles. While the gameplay footage indicates diverse playstyles—from nimble assassins to powerful juggernauts—the final feel remains to be seen upon hands-on experience.

**Overwatch Announces Nier: Automata Collaboration Skins**

Blizzard announced a new collaboration event bringing skins inspired by Nier: Automata to Overwatch, launching March 10. The lineup features characters like Kiriko as 2B, Vendetta as A2, and Wuyang as 9S, along with Mercy and Lifeweaver adopting designs from Nier's androids and human characters.

This marks another support-heavy set of skins, following a previous Sanrio collaboration that also favored support heroes. While the collaboration has been met with some mixed opinions regarding character selections and missed opportunities to include fan-favorite characters like Emil or Pascal, it continues Blizzard’s trend of themed cosmetic drops that target specific hero roles.

**Crimson Desert Preview Highlights Chaotic Combat and Vast Scope**

After six hours with Pearl Abyss’s Crimson Desert, the game emerges as a complex and sometimes overwhelming action RPG. It mixes high-fantasy and absurd elements, with a combat system that offers numerous playstyles via skill trees focused on archery, unarmed combat, swordplay, and grappling mechanics. The game's tone swings from grim fantasy tropes to almost surreal and humorous moments, such as NPCs praising Kliff’s combat prowess after being defeated.

Beyond combat, Crimson Desert features expansive activities including crafting, bounty hunting, faction reputation, stronghold management, and multiple minigames. The world includes mounts like dragons and a flying mech, suggesting a large and varied map. While its breadth may risk appearing cluttered or unfocused, the game's unrestrained design and variety of systems stand out as its defining characteristics.

**Nvidia Driver Issues Reported with Resident Evil Requiem Are Inconsistent**

Some PC players have reported significant performance drops with Nvidia's GeForce Game Ready Driver 591.86 when playing Resident Evil Requiem with path tracing enabled. Complaints include reduced frame rates and lower GPU power draw, with users recommending rolling back drivers to mitigate issues. Attempts by PC Gamer’s hardware team to replicate these drops on a high-end test bench resulted in no significant performance variation across driver versions.

This suggests the problem may not affect all setups and could depend on specific hardware or software configurations. The driver situation continues to be complicated by recent Nvidia driver releases and retractions due to stability concerns. Resident Evil Requiem’s use of path tracing, a demanding GPU feature, adds to the technical challenge for both hardware vendors and players.
