Crimson Desert GPU Driver Issue on ROG Xbox Ally X

Pearl Abyss has acknowledged a compatibility problem affecting Crimson Desert on Asus ROG Xbox Ally X handheld PCs. The game fails to launch when using the latest GPU driver versions for this device. Until a fix is released, players are advised to roll back their graphics drivers to the previous version (specifically driver V32.0.21025.27003, available on Asus’s support page). Asus has confirmed this guidance but noted some issues with their direct download link. This driver rollback provides a temporary solution to enable game launch on the device.

This matters because Crimson Desert’s hardware support has been problematic since release, including previous Intel Arc GPU issues. The developer and hardware partners are working on resolving these, aiming to restore full compatibility. Rolling back drivers is inconvenient, but the move highlights ongoing efforts to stabilize the game on diverse hardware platforms.

Trail of Cthulhu Mega Bundle Available Until April 6

Tabletop RPG fans interested in Lovecraftian horror can currently access a large digital bundle centered on the game Trail of Cthulhu through Bundle of Holding. The mega bundle includes the core game plus 13 additional books covering adventures, supplements, and alternate campaign settings. The pricing offers two tiers: a starter pack at $17.95 and a full collection needing to beat the average price (~$36.85 at the time of writing).

Although this bundle excludes some popular expansions like Bookhounds of London and upcoming 2nd edition core rules (due July 2026), it represents a significant collection for those looking to run or play investigative horror campaigns. PDFs are downloadable and can be re-accessed any time. The sale ends April 6, so interested players have limited time to purchase this discounted set.

Disney Dreamlight Valley Adds Pocahontas Next Month

The upcoming free update for Disney Dreamlight Valley, titled Whispers of the Wind, will release on April 8. It introduces a new Dream Castle area and adds the character Pocahontas to the game’s roster. Unlike previous updates, details and trailers were withheld at announcement. The update will feature quests involving nature-themed activities leading to unlocking Pocahontas as a friend.

This addition expands the game’s content and character lineup, though players currently have limited information on quest specifics or additional features. Updates typically also include new companion pets and collectibles, but these remain unconfirmed for this release.

Industry Views on Open World Games and Game Length

Nick Lives, co-founder of Night Signal Entertainment, shared in an interview that the appeal of massive open world games is decreasing among players. He suggests many now prefer concise, one-off gaming experiences that deliver focused quality over lengthy, expansive titles full of repetitive content. This aligns with consumer fatigue over “forever” games demanding extensive time investment.

Lives cites his own development of the short horror game Home Safety Hotline as profitable despite its brief two-hour runtime, challenging the idea that larger games are necessary for commercial success. Other developers echo this sentiment, encouraging studios to focus on doing one thing exceptionally well rather than pursuing open worlds by default. This conversation reflects ongoing debates in gaming about balancing content scope, quality, and player time.

Jack Black Interested in Live-Action Yakuza Role

Actor Jack Black has expressed interest in participating in a potential second live-action Yakuza movie. Known for voicing Bowser in recent Super Mario Galaxy projects, Black joked about playing a “portly American” character if Sega were to offer roles in the Yakuza franchise’s film adaptations. Although Yakuza’s primary setting is Japan, the series occasionally features American characters, giving some scope for such a role.

While previous adaptations like Amazon’s Yakuza series have had mixed reception, Black’s enthusiasm suggests continued crossover between video game franchises and mainstream actors. His involvement could bring renewed attention to Yakuza adaptations should new projects materialize.
