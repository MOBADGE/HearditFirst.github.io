Blippo+: An Experimental FMV Game with Mixed Results

Blippo+ offers a unique experience by presenting a series of short live-action and animated broadcasts from an alien world styled like 1980s camp TV. Players mainly channel-surf through these transmissions, piecing together a larger narrative about an interdimensional broadcast from Planet Blip. The game features quirky shows including rants, quiz shows, and teen talk segments, creating an intriguing, surreal atmosphere. Although the concept is interesting, the game struggles with repetitiveness and a lack of satisfying story resolution. Additionally, the original version’s timed content unlock system was altered for PC to unlock by watching percentage thresholds, exposing more repetitive content and diminishing pacing. Despite its experimental nature, Blippo+ ultimately feels more like a nostalgic curiosity than a fully successful game. It is currently available on Steam and itch.io.

Deus Ex: Invisible War’s Audio Director Reflects on the Sequel

Alexander Brandon, the audio director for Deus Ex: Invisible War and a contributor to the original Deus Ex, shared his thoughts on the sequel’s audio design in a recent interview. While acknowledging room for improvement in his first audio director role, Brandon remains proud of much of the game's musical work, especially its main theme and the incorporation of diverse faction soundscapes to support the game's darker and more immersive tone. He noted that while the game simplified some systems compared to the original, its soundtrack successfully enhanced the storytelling and atmosphere. However, Brandon admitted he would make changes if given another chance. The discussion underscores how even well-regarded games can have elements their creators wish to refine.

Resident Evil Requiem Adds Photo Mode and Tweaks Expressions

A new patch for Resident Evil Requiem introduces a photo mode that allows players to capture scenes featuring the protagonists Leon and Grace with various poses, stickers, filters, and limited camera movement. While photo mode options remain somewhat constrained—such as a lack of adjustable light sources—it provides a new way for players to engage visually with the game’s content. The update also includes vague bug fixes and adjustments to character expressions in some cutscenes intended to better convey emotions, though these changes go largely unnoticed during gameplay. The patch enhances the game’s replay value by inviting players to explore its scenes creatively.

Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous Boasts Extensive Subclass Options and Mount Mechanics

Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous continues to appeal to players who enjoy deep character customization, now offering 232 subclasses, including 26 base classes and 13 prestige classes. This extensive variety caters to build-focused players, although not all subclasses fit every campaign context equally well. Notably, the “Sable Company Marine” Ranger subclass, added in the game's final expansion, offers a highly powerful mount: a rideable griffon-like creature from level one, which excels in mounted combat and aerial attacks. This mount-and-rider dynamic provides tactical advantages, reflecting the game’s complexity and challenge, especially at higher difficulties. The game remains popular among players who appreciate detailed buildcrafting and challenging gameplay, with its technical depth encouraging repeated playthroughs and experimentation.

Star Wars Zero Company’s Director Discusses the Return of Classic PC Strategy Genres

Greg Foertsch, creative director of Star Wars Zero Company, weighed in on the resurgence of traditional PC gaming genres such as RTS and turn-based tactics. He attributes earlier genre decline in the 2000s to challenges in adapting complex PC games to consoles, especially due to UI and camera limitations. With improvements in user experience and controller support, these genres have become more accessible across platforms, leading to renewed interest. Foertsch highlights that many modern tactics games blend classic elements with new innovations, pushing the genre forward while maintaining player engagement. His observations reflect broader trends in gaming where genres once considered niche have regained popularity thanks to technological advances and design evolution.
