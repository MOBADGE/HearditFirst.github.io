UK Tribunal Allows $900 Million Lawsuit Against Valve to Proceed

Valve will face a major legal challenge in the UK after the Competition Appeal Tribunal permitted a collective action lawsuit accusing the company of abusing its dominant position with the Steam platform. Filed in 2024, the suit alleges that Valve enforces price parity obligations restricting game makers from offering lower prices elsewhere, requires all add-on content to be purchased through Steam, and imposes excessive commissions that inflate prices. Valve argued technical grounds to halt the claim but the tribunal disagreed, allowing it to move forward on an opt-out basis representing around 14 million UK consumers. This development is a setback for Valve and parallels similar ongoing litigation in the US. The case could impact how digital store pricing and exclusivity are regulated.
