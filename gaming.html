Timberborn Sets March 5 Release Date with New Intro Trailer

Timberborn, the beaver-themed city builder from developer Mechanistry, will officially leave early access on March 5, 2026. The game has been in early access since 2021, gradually expanding with updates adding features like water pumps, robotic beavers, hazardous polluted water, and new transportation methods such as ziplines and water tubes. The current build includes 16 playable maps, faction-specific wonders, and full mod support via Steam Workshop.

Alongside the release date announcement, Mechanistry shared a new intro cinematic dubbed a "lore trailer," featuring beavers crossing a desert to found a city. While simple in narrative, the trailer showcases animation with British actor Shaun Dooley providing narration. The upcoming 1.0 update will add various natural phenomena, environmental hazards, spiral staircases, and city banners. Skyrim Mod Adds Stealth Bonus for Hiding in Bushes

A new mod for The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim Special Edition introduces the ability for players to be harder to detect when crouching in bushes and near trees. Titled "Hide in Bushes," the mod makes players 50% harder to detect when hiding in bushes, with smaller shrubs providing lower bonuses and trees/stumps providing a 10% stealth bonus. Grass is not included due to game limitations.

This mod relies on the "Object Impact Framework," which expands Skyrim‚Äôs recognition of environmental objects beyond typical interactions, enabling new mechanics such as setting fire to objects or extinguishing torches. The addition of stealth benefits for foliage marks a fresh layer of tactical gameplay, reinforcing the enduring popularity of stealth archer builds despite past calls for variety.

Veteran Indie Developer Dan Marshall Shares Gaming Habits

Dan Marshall, director of British indie studio Size Five Games, recently shared insights into his gaming history and current play habits. With over 218 hours in XCOM 2, Marshall identifies it as his most played game on Steam and appreciates its layered strategic gameplay. His current focus is on Atomfall, a first-person shooter set in a British village that balances mystery and satisfying combat mechanics.

Marshall also highlighted older games still installed on his PC, including the original Fallout and X-Wing, reflecting a broad appreciation for game history. When asked about non-gaming software he values, he emphasized daily use of the Windows Snipping Tool for capturing screenshots.

Thomas & Friends is Getting a Train Simulator Game

Dovetail Games, creators of the Train Sim World series, announced a new game titled "Thomas & Friends: Wonders of Sodor." The game allows players to control classic characters like Thomas, Percy, Gordon, Emily, and Diesel on a fully explorable island. It offers various modes including story-driven character missions, mini-games, a timetable mode for running trains according to schedules, and free roam for casual exploration.

Powered by the Train Sim World engine and Unreal Engine technologies, the game blends simulation realism with accessible controls tailored for fans of all ages. The announcement has generated enthusiasm on social media, particularly among longtime fans of the Thomas the Tank Engine franchise.
