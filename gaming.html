Updated Steam AI Disclosure Clarifies Focus on Player-Consumed Content Valve has revised its AI disclosure form for games on Steam to clarify that developers only need to report AI-generated content when it appears in final assets that players encounter, such as art, sound, or writing. The update excludes internal AI-assisted tools used for enhancing efficiency in development, like code helpers or concept art not present in the released game. This distinction aims to ease confusion for developers about when disclosure is necessary. Additionally, Steam has added functionality allowing users to report illegal content generated by live AI in games, addressing concerns over unintended outputs from generative AI systems.
