Marvel Rivals’ Latest Character Highlights Significant Power Creep

Marvel Rivals is introducing Black Cat as the newest playable Duelist in its upcoming Season 7.5 update. While Black Cat has some straightforward abilities like Claw Strike and wall climbing, her unique "Fortune" system stands out. During a match, she accumulates Fortune points by damaging enemies or using her grappling hook to steal it from others. Players can spend Fortune points to activate various powerful abilities, including creating portals, stunning enemies, turning invisible, and launching damaging projectiles with wall sight—abilities that combine offensive and utility effects.

This new kit has been widely described as overpowered compared to earlier heroes, demonstrating a notable increase in "power creep" within the game. Historically, Marvel Rivals has introduced strong characters that later receive nerfs, and Black Cat’s release may follow this trend. The character’s capabilities blend aspects previously seen separately in heroes like Thanos, Doctor Strange, and others, raising concerns about maintaining balanced gameplay.

Apple Approves Open-Source Nvidia GPU Driver for Macs, Gaming Support Limited

Apple has officially approved an open-source driver that enables the use of Nvidia GPUs on Macs via external GPU (eGPU) setups. A new app and driver developed by tiny corp allow recent Nvidia graphics cards, including some RTX 50-series models, to function on Apple Silicon Macs through Thunderbolt or USB4 connections.

Although the technical breakthrough allows Nvidia GPUs to run on Macs once more, this development currently targets AI computation rather than gaming performance. Benchmarks reveal that, while faster than Apple’s built-in GPU for AI workloads, significant performance limitations remain. The driver and runtimes are open source, potentially allowing community efforts to expand support, but gaming capabilities are not yet practical. The move signals Apple's openness to external GPU technology but also highlights the still-nascent state of such integrations for graphics-intensive tasks.

Allbirds Exits Footwear to Pursue AI Infrastructure with $50 Million Investment

Footwear company Allbirds announced a strategic pivot to become an AI-focused cloud infrastructure provider, renaming itself NewBird AI. The firm secured a $50 million convertible financing facility to support its ambition to offer GPU-as-a-Service (GPUaaS) and AI-native cloud solutions markets. This transition would position NewBird AI as a rental service for high-performance GPUs, intended for clients that need scalable AI compute without owning physical hardware.

While AI infrastructure commands substantial demand and profitability today, questions remain about whether $50 million is sufficient to build a competitive operation in a crowded and capital-intensive market. The move caused Allbirds’ stock price to spike, reflecting investor enthusiasm despite uncertainties about the company’s experience in a vastly different industry.

Crimson Desert Passes 5 Million Sales Mark Amid Active Post-Launch Development

Crimson Desert has reached over 5 million sales worldwide within its first month after launch. Initially facing mixed reviews due to complex mechanics and accessibility challenges, the developer Pearl Abyss has released numerous patches addressing gameplay difficulty, inventory management, and quality-of-life issues. These ongoing improvements appear to be successfully attracting and retaining players.

Nevertheless, the frequent updates also raise questions about the game's original direction and identity. Critics suggest that Crimson Desert is evolving rapidly as the studio adapts to player feedback, which improves the experience but may dilute the game’s distinctiveness. The developer’s continued commitment to refinement shows responsiveness but also points to challenges in defining the intended player experience.

Book of Travels Development Ends After Five Years; Offline Mode Added

Might and Delight announced the end of development for the MMORPG Book of Travels after five years and ongoing difficulties sustaining the project. Servers will shut down on July 31, though an offline single-player mode has been introduced to allow players to continue exploring the game’s world alone. The studio also plans to support extensive modding and has reduced the game’s price to $5.

The developer acknowledged overestimating their capacity to deliver the original vision, citing fundamental technical challenges that could not be resolved. Book of Travels will shed its early access status with this final update. The transition aims to offer a lasting experience for fans despite the cessation of online features.
