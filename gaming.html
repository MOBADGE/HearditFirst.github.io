Horizon Chase Turbo to be Delisted in June

The arcade racing game Horizon Chase Turbo and its mobile precursor will be removed from sale starting June 1. This means new buyers will no longer be able to purchase the titles, though existing owners can still download them. Horizon Chase Turbo is notable for lacking licensed cars or online features, featuring original tracks instead. The delisting decision is unusual given these factors, but may be connected to recent layoffs at Epic Games, which owns the development studio Aquiris. Horizon Chase 2, released more recently and also connected to Epic Games, will remain available. This removal reflects ongoing changes at Epic and how they affect their acquired studios.

Stardew Valley Player Misled by AI, Ends Up Brewing Hundreds of Worthless Rice Juice

A Stardew Valley player shared a cautionary tale after following incorrect AI-generated guidance from Google on how to make vinegar in the game. The AI response mistakenly suggested putting unmilled rice directly into a keg produces vinegar, when in fact, the rice must be milled first. The player ended up producing 136 bottles of rice juice—a low-value item yielding little profit or benefit—because of the misinformation. This incident highlights the potential pitfalls of relying on AI-generated gaming advice without verification and serves as a reminder to consult established sources or community knowledge for game mechanics.

Caves of Qud Ported to Nintendo Switch to Explore Unusual Tech Challenges

The complex roguelike RPG Caves of Qud has been ported to the Nintendo Switch, a move its developers admit was not entirely rational but driven by a desire to solve difficult technical problems. The game, known for its keyboard-heavy controls and demanding procedural systems, was adapted to gamepad input and optimized to run on the Switch’s older hardware. Early work on controller-friendly UI helped the game become playable on the Steam Deck, paving the way for the Switch version. The developers also plan to bring the game to mobile devices, continuing to test unconventional platforms for traditionally PC-focused games. This port challenges assumptions about market demand and technical feasibility for niche, systemic RPGs on consoles and mobile.

1047 Games, Makers of Splitgate, Begin Work on New Titanfall-Inspired Shooter

The studio behind Splitgate, 1047 Games, has officially announced development on a new multiplayer shooter influenced by games like Titanfall and Call of Duty: Black Ops 3. CEO Ian Proulx indicated that a small team is working on this project alongside ongoing Splitgate support, aiming to recapture the fast-paced movement and gameplay fans of those series appreciate. Playtesting is expected to start soon, with fans invited to sign up for early access. This marks a shift for 1047 as they attempt to broaden their portfolio after Splitgate 2 struggled post-launch and was reverted to beta status last year.
