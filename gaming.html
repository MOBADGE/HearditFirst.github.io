**Realtime-with-Pause Combat Still Relevant, According to Star Wars Zero Company Lead Designer**

James Brawley, lead designer of the upcoming turn-based tactics game *Star Wars Zero Company*, has stated that realtime-with-pause (RTWP) combat remains a valid and promising style despite the recent resurgence of pure turn-based tactics games. Speaking to PC Gamer, Brawley noted that game genres tend to oscillate in popularity over time and that innovations in presentation have made turn-based games more accessible recently. However, he believes RTWP is not obsolete and anticipates a future title will successfully bring it back into the spotlight. Brawley highlighted that earlier turn-based games often felt slow and cumbersome but modern approaches like *XCOM* have shown the potential for a dynamism that keeps players engaged. His comments suggest that the debate between realtime and turn-based tactics combat styles is ongoing, and developers continue to explore both formats.

**RPG Maker to Introduce 2.5D Visuals in Style of Octopath Traveler**

RPG Maker, a long-standing game development tool known for 16-bit style role-playing games, is preparing its first major visual overhaul. The upcoming update will enable developers to create games with 2.5D graphics reminiscent of *Octopath Traveler* and other recent Square Enix titles using HD-2D style visuals. While movement remains grid-based, the update showcases improved lighting and shadows that enhance aesthetic depth. This shift represents a significant change in RPG Maker's visual capabilities and could help refresh the distinctly familiar but often criticized “RPG Maker look.” No release date or further details about whether this is an expansion or new product have been announced.

**Dimension 20 Partners with White Wolf for Vampire: The Masquerade Actual-Play Series**

The actual-play series Dimension 20 is collaborating with White Wolf to produce an official *Vampire: The Masquerade* campaign titled *City Council of Darkness*. This marks Dimension 20’s first season using an externally licensed tabletop role-playing game system. The story places vampire characters in Purpee, Oregon, a small town of 18,000, shifting the typical vampire setting from urban centers to a more intimate, peculiar rural environment. Brennan Lee Mulligan will return as the game master, with the regular Dimension 20 cast participating. The series will premiere on April 8 on the subscription platform Dropout, running 14 episodes. This partnership indicates growing mainstream interest in officially licensed actual-play productions beyond *Dungeons & Dragons*.

**Pro World of Warcraft Player Disconnected Mid-Raid Due to Expired Subscription**

During the world-first race for the *Midnight* expansion's new raid in *World of Warcraft*, a professional player was abruptly disconnected mid-boss fight due to an expired game subscription. Although the player had purchased a WoW token to renew the subscription, he had forgotten to redeem it in time. The incident was captured live on Twitch, with teammates reacting humorously and quickly adapting to continue the encounter. This episode highlights the logistical challenges faced by competitive raiding teams who often manage multiple active accounts simultaneously. Despite its humor, it underscores the importance of subscription management for esports practitioners.

**Former Valve Writer Criticizes Epic Games' Layoffs and Company Culture**

Chet Faliszek, a former Valve writer and key contributor to titles such as *Half-Life 2* and *Portal*, publicly criticized Epic Games and CEO Tim Sweeney following the recent layoffs of over 1,000 employees. Faliszek questioned the morale and motivation of current Epic staff amid such cuts, contrasting Epic's approach with Valve's company culture where employees reportedly felt a strong sense of ownership and agency in their work. He argued that focusing solely on profitability without nurturing developer passion risks damaging the industry’s creative environment. Epic has not responded directly to these remarks but referenced their official statement regarding the layoffs. The situation reflects ongoing tensions in the games industry around business decisions and employee treatment.
