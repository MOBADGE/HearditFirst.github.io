Dispatch Uses RNG Favoring Players Like XCOM

The developer behind the superhero comedy Dispatch has openly confirmed that its in-game random percentage chances for success are adjusted to benefit players, similar to how Firaxis’s XCOM series handles randomness. According to Adhoc’s directors, any task with over a 76% success chance automatically succeeds initially, but after three boosts, true odds return until a failure occurs. This approach aims to reduce player frustration from unlucky outcomes while preserving a sense of fairness. Players are informed transparency about these mechanics matters because understanding how randomness is managed can affect enjoyment and perceptions of game fairness.

s&box Pricing Likely to Match Garry’s Mod

Garry Newman, creator of Garry’s Mod, has suggested that the upcoming game creation platform s&box will be priced similarly to Garry’s Mod’s original $10 cost, possibly around $10 to $20. While the exact price remains undecided, Newman and his studio Facepunch indicated that they plan to charge for it initially. s&box is set for an April release and represents Newman’s next step in user-generated content platforms, following the long lifespan and success of Garry’s Mod. Pricing close to the previous title reflects a strategy to remain accessible yet sustainable.

Crabmeat Combines Horror and Crab Catching

Crabmeat is a new horror-themed point-and-click game centered on catching Southern King Crabs while stranded in an Antarctic ocean setting. You play as a penal colony worker under threat of lethal enforcement if quotas aren’t met. The game uses mouse controls for a survival horror atmosphere involving navigating ice floes, operating crab traps, and avoiding threats from sea creatures. Available now on Steam with a launch discount and demo, Crabmeat targets players with an interest in survival horror scenarios and unique themes, extending the range of indie horror titles.

Pokémon Streamer’s Roguelite Kickstarter Surges Past Goal

Italian Pokémon streamer Cydonia’s project, Wonderful Neoran Valley—a roguelite monster-collector with RPG dialogue and run-based progression—hit its Kickstarter funding target in 10 minutes and has surpassed $800,000. The game eschews traditional monster-catching mechanics like capturing with balls, favoring interaction through conversation. Stretch goals unlocked include additional content and platform ports, including Switch 2. The game's momentum highlights how established community personalities can drive strong crowdfunding results for niche genres blending popular gameplay elements.

Brendan Greene on AI Content and the Internet's State

Game developer Brendan Greene (PlayerUnknown) commented on the impact of AI-generated content on the internet, describing it as a "loop" where AI models scrape low-quality, AI-generated data that then feeds back as a distorted truth. He expressed skepticism toward large language models (LLMs) as non-deterministic and prone to hallucination, doubting that true artificial general intelligence (AGI) is imminent. Greene highlighted concerns over the environmental and social effects of data centers supporting AI computation and advocated for more localized computing approaches.

Greedfall: The Dying World Launches After Early Access but Feels Unfinished

The RPG Greedfall: The Dying World, a prequel developed by Spiders, officially launched after two years in early access but still exhibits significant issues such as bugs, crashes, and uneven gameplay. The game shifts to a real-time with pause combat system criticized for lacking challenge and depth at normal difficulty, leading many players to rely on stealth. Despite strong narrative ambitions and a detailed setting inspired by 17th-century European colonization themes, the world feels static and underdeveloped. The title's polish and engagement shortcomings may limit its appeal beyond dedicated fans of “eurojank” RPGs.

Bungie Responds to Marathon Battle Pass Feedback

Following player criticism of Marathon’s season one battle pass—which costs $10 and was seen as offering limited character skins—Bungie announced an upcoming addition of three new shell skins to the premium track and one to the free track in mid-April. Additional Arachne-themed shell skins will also be unlockable through in-game challenges. Bungie aims to improve value perception for the pass but has not addressed all feedback, including the absence of premium currency returns. This update shows Bungie’s responsiveness to community input while continuing to refine monetization strategies for Marathon.
