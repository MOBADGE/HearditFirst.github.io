**The Importance of Headbob in RPGs**

A recent firsthand look at The Outer Worlds 2 has brought attention to a small but significant design choice: the absence of headbob, the subtle camera movement that mimics natural body motion while walking or running in first-person games. The lack of this effect in The Outer Worlds 2 makes the player feel more like a hovering camera than a character within the world, disrupting immersion. This contrasts with games like Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2, Fallout 4, and Cyberpunk 2077, where headbob is present and contributes to a more natural viewing experience. The omission appears to be a stylistic choice by Obsidian rather than an accessibility feature, and the impact suggests that even minor visual cues like headbob play a vital role in maintaining player engagement in RPGs.
