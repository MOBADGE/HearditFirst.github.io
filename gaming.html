Witchhunter.exe Blends Retro Adventure and Glitch Horror

Witchhunter.exe is an upcoming retro-style adventure game that mixes choose-your-own-adventure elements with the aesthetic of classic Gold Box D&D games. Players take on the role of a witch hunter in a Salem-like setting, interrogating women suspected of consorting with the Devil. Uniquely, the Devil manifests through unsettling pop-up windows, blending narrative and glitch-horror mechanics.

The combat system requires players to type or select the right words to survive attacks, offering an interactive challenge. While the game leans heavily into stylistic influences from vintage horror films, its meta-narrative twist may divide players who prefer more straightforward storytelling. A beta playtest is scheduled, and development updates are available on Steam. This title stands out by combining nostalgic gameplay with psychological horror motifs.

Five New Steam Releases to Explore

This week, several notable new games have launched on Steam, offering a variety of experiences across genres:

- **Mama's Sleeping Angels**: A multiplayer dream-horror shooter with cursed objects granting unique powers, reminiscent of Lethal Company with a cyber-surreal twist.

- **Lucid Blocks**: A procedurally generated voxel sandbox built around surreal and liminal environments with infinite vertical landscapes, allowing extensive crafting and survival.

- **The State of Nowhere**: A Papers, Please-inspired narrative game about rationing food in a dystopian state, featuring 46 endings and multiple game modes that examine moral dilemmas.

- **Thysiastery**: A pixel-art, turn-based roguelike dungeon crawler with a shapeshifting labyrinth and a variety of skills and party members.

- **Pluto**: A roguelike deckbuilder dungeon crawler starring an imprisoned wizard escaping a dungeon, offering card-based combat with unusual elemental combinations.

These diverse releases demonstrate ongoing innovation in indie game design, from narrative-driven moral simulators to hybrid roguelike dungeon crawlers.

Tomb Raider Remaster Patch Draws Criticism for Visuals and Bugs

The remastered collection of Tomb Raider I-III recently received a free update that added a new challenge mode and 10 new character outfits. However, the patch has been poorly received due to low-quality visual assets and technical problems including broken audio, deteriorated AI, and lost save files.

Some players suspect AI-assisted creation tools were involved, raising concerns about quality and artistic direction. Notably, the original remaster's lead artist distanced himself from the update, confirming that none of the original development team worked on it. This follows an earlier incident where unauthorized AI-generated voice lines were removed from the series. The patch controversy highlights ongoing challenges in remastering classic games while meeting modern expectations.

Abubakar Salim Shares Insights on His Gaming Setup and Habits

Actor and developer Abubakar Salim, known for his role in Assassin's Creed: Origins and founder of Surgent Studios, recently offered a detailed look at his PC gaming preferences. Salim's earliest exposure to PC games included classic titles like Solitaire and Minesweeper, progressing to strategy staples such as WarCraft and Age of Empires.

Currently, he is playing "No Rest for the Wicked" and "Dave the Diver," appreciating their progression systems and immersive loops. His longest installed game is WarCraft 3, and the title with the most hours logged is Garry’s Mod, reflecting his fondness for social and creative multiplayer experiences. Salim candidly revealed his cluttered desktop and noted Final Draft as an essential non-gaming software for his scriptwriting work. This glimpse into his setup offers perspective on the varied influences and modern games preferred by industry figures.

Timberborn Emerges from Early Access with Automation Features

City-builder Timberborn has exited early access with a full 1.0 release that notably includes automation mechanics reminiscent of Factorio. The update introduces over twenty logic-based buildings—such as levers, sensors, and relays—that enable players to program complex behaviors for structures and processes.

Examples include setting floodgates to automatically close against contamination or pausing production when resources run low. This addition expands the depth and complexity of managing beaver colonies in a post-apocalyptic setting. Timberborn’s automation system has been presented in explanatory materials and reinforces the game’s reputation, boasting a 95% positive rating on Steam. To mark its release, the game is available at a 20% discount until March 26.
