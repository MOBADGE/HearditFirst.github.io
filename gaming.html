Dead by Daylight Opens Community Voting for Next Chapter Characters

Dead by Daylight has begun allowing players to vote on the character designs for its upcoming chapter. The community can choose from four survivor options and three killer designs on the game's official website. The survivor concepts include a spiritual scientist and divine investigator from a collapsed world, each with distinct physical traits like heterochromia or a blind eye. The killer options lean into a dark angel motif with variations on winged, punished figures. This marks one of the few times Dead by Daylight will feature original characters rather than crossovers from popular franchises. Stop Killing Games Petition Surpasses Verification Threshold in EU

The volunteer group behind the Stop Killing Games petition announced that the European Union has verified approximately 1.3 million of the over 1.4 million signatures collected. This surpasses the one million signatures required for the initiative to be formally presented to the European Commission. The petition calls for legislation requiring game publishers to establish end-of-life support plans, safeguarding players‚Äô continued access to live service and network-dependent games even after official support ends. The initiative has encountered some industry resistance but remains popular among gamers. If accepted, it could influence regulations around digital game preservation and consumer rights within the EU.

Doom Ported to PineBuds Pro Earbuds with Remote Play Capability

Programmer Arin Sarkisian has managed to port the original Doom to run on PineBuds Pro earbuds, which feature open-source firmware and a small but capable CPU. Running Doom on the earbuds involves performance downgrades, including about 18 frames per second, and fits a compressed version of the game within limited memory. Interested players can experience Doom on these earbuds remotely via a browser interface connected to the developer‚Äôs device, where gameplay is streamed through Twitch. The project is a technical novelty demonstrating creativity in software porting and the enduring appeal of Doom in unconventional environments.

GTA Roleplay Sees Surge in Realistic Police Role Applications

In several Grand Theft Auto (GTA) roleplay communities, particularly on the FiveM and RageMP platforms, there is an increased interest in joining virtual police forces like the Los Santos Police Department (LSPD). Unlike casual gameplay, these roleplay servers have formal application processes including character backstories, voice interviews, and police academy training. The training covers radio codes, patrol procedures, and report writing. Players progress through probationary periods before gaining full officer status. This detailed approach adds structure and realism to in-game law enforcement, marking an evolution in the complexity and commitment expected of GTA roleplayers. It highlights how roleplay communities continue expanding immersive experiences.

PC Port of Original Banjo-Kazooie Released by Modders

A group of modders led by Mr-Wiseguy has released Banjo: Recompiled, a native PC port of the original Banjo-Kazooie game. The port supports Windows, Linux, and macOS, and offers enhancements such as ultrawide support, improved controls including dual analog camera, and higher framerates. It also includes a feature from Rare Replay allowing players to retain collectibles upon leaving levels or dying. The release is available on GitHub and requires original game files from the user. The port supports popular mods and quality-of-life improvements, providing a refined way to play Banjo-Kazooie outside its original platforms. This project exemplifies efforts to preserve and modernize classic games independent of official releases.
