PlayStation 1 Mod Project Adds Modern Features

YouTuber Elliot Coll of The Retro Future has created a significant hardware mod for the original PlayStation (PS1) console. The project updates a 32-year-old PlayStation SCPH-5502 model by adding HDMI output, USB-C power, and wireless controller support compatible with PS5’s DualSense controller. The mod uses components like the PicoStation ZeroWire, which incorporates a Raspberry Pi Pico to simplify loading games from an SD card, replacing the original CD-ROM mechanisms for game access.

This work involves substantial soldering, including connecting nearly 50 points for the HDMI mod, disconnecting pins from the CD-ROM controller, and installing a custom PCB for wireless controllers. The project revives the original PlayStation with capabilities expected from modern devices while maintaining the console’s classic form factor, appealing to retro hardware enthusiasts looking for updated functionality.

World of Warcraft Played Using Hot Dog Controllers

A player known as Addison2k demonstrated an unusual way to play WoW by using custom-made controllers built into hot dogs for a mythic keystone run. The hot dog controllers consist of 3D-printed plastic holding four hot dogs wired for touch input, with one controlling movement and another managing abilities. Despite limited functionality—such as inability to move the camera or use some key shortcuts without keyboard assistance—the run was completed successfully.

While not practical, this experiment highlights the community’s creative approaches to input devices in gaming. The run also sparked humorous interactions, with teammates joking about using his tongue to operate the controllers. Although this is more a novelty, it continues the trend of players experimenting with unconventional hardware.

Arc Raiders Sees Balanced Interest in PvE and PvP Modes

Embark Studios revealed that approximately 30% of Arc Raiders players focus exclusively on PvE content, with another 30% focused on PvP, and the remaining 40% enjoying both modes. This balanced engagement surprised the developers, who had not anticipated such a strong PvE-only base in an action shooter.

In-game proximity chat sees over 95% adoption, facilitating cooperation, especially notable when players unite quickly to tackle tougher enemies like the Matriarch boss. The game’s post-apocalyptic setting, featuring signs of ecological recovery, aims to convey a hopeful tone, which the developers see as influencing the positive player interactions.

Euro Truck Simulator 2 to Add Türkiye in Upcoming DLC

SCS Software announced a new DLC expansion called Soul of Anatolia for Euro Truck Simulator 2. This update will add the country of Türkiye, extending players' routes from Istanbul eastwards to locations like Aksaray, with cities such as Ankara, Antalya, and Alanya included. The expansion features landmarks like the 1915 Çanakkale Bridge, the world’s longest suspension bridge.

No release date has been confirmed yet. This expansion continues to support the long lifecycle of Euro Truck Simulator 2, which remains a popular and relaxing simulator after 14 years of updates and content additions.

PC Gaming Revenue Increasingly Driven by Titles Outside Top 20

Newzoo’s PC & Console Gaming Report 2026 notes a shift in PC gaming revenue distribution. In Western markets, games ranked beyond the top 20 now generate 56% of total PC gaming revenue, up from 48% in 2024. Player time spent on these titles also rose by 44%, while playtime on top 20 games remained stable or slightly declined.

Notable enduring titles contributing to this trend include Cyberpunk 2077, Elden Ring, Skyrim, and survival games like Rust and DayZ. The pattern contrasts with console trends, where PlayStation players focus mostly on top exclusives and annual sports games, and Xbox’s platform is dominated by Game Pass content. This suggests a diverse and sustained interest in a broader range of PC games beyond immediate hits.
