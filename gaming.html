Death Stranding 2 Adds "To the Wilder" Difficulty Mode on PC

Hideo Kojima's Death Stranding 2 launched on PC with a new challenging difficulty mode called "To the Wilder," designed to respond to player feedback that the existing "Brutal" difficulty was not hard enough. Lead level designer Hiroaki Yoshiike explained that this mode increases the impact of the game's Timefall mechanic, which rapidly degrades items, making resource management and strategic planning of tools essential. The developers played through the mode fully and found that some items and weapons that were previously underused become critical. Players are encouraged to strengthen online social connections, known as the "Social Strand System," to better cope with the harsher conditions. This difficulty update reflects Kojima Productions’ intent to offer a more realistic and demanding experience for seasoned players.

Crimson Desert's Controls Receive Defense Amidst Player Complaints

The recently released open-world game Crimson Desert has faced widespread criticism for its complex and unintuitive control scheme, with some players describing it as convoluted and frustrating, especially on keyboard and mouse. Pearl Abyss's PR director Will Powers responded by likening learning the controls to riding a bike: awkward at first, but natural with practice. He acknowledged the control scheme’s challenges but suggested they diminish after some time. Industry voices like Arkane’s co-creative director also expressed some appreciation for mastering unconventional controls, citing it as a form of rewarding mental exercise. Despite control issues contributing to a mixed player reception, Crimson Desert has seen strong sales, moving over two million copies in under 24 hours. Pearl Abyss has pledged to listen to community feedback and improve the game, with control adjustments likely to be part of future updates.

Kirsten Dunst Cast as Alex in Minecraft Movie Sequel

Actress Kirsten Dunst has been confirmed to play Alex, a default female player avatar in Minecraft, in the upcoming Minecraft Movie sequel scheduled for release in 2027. Dunst, known for roles ranging from Spider-Man to Marie Antoinette, reportedly joined the project partly because of its financial security. The original Minecraft Movie earned nearly $1 billion worldwide, making it a safe bet. The sequel will see the return of several original cast members, including Jack Black and Jennifer Coolidge, and will be directed again by Jared Hess. While the film’s appeal will primarily be to younger audiences familiar with the game, Dunst's involvement highlights the franchise’s continued crossover from gaming into mainstream entertainment.

OpenTTD Assures Community Amid Atari’s Transport Tycoon Deluxe Relaunch

OpenTTD, the open-source remake of the classic Transport Tycoon Deluxe, has been removed from Steam and GOG due to the official re-release of the original 1995 game by Atari. The OpenTTD development team clarified that the project retains full independence and continues to be freely available through its official website. Atari supported a compromise to prevent conflicts between their commercial interests and the free availability of OpenTTD. The re-released Transport Tycoon Deluxe currently holds a low player count and mostly negative user reviews on Steam. OpenTTD developers urge the community to engage respectfully with these changes, emphasizing the longevity and ongoing development of the free version.

Subnautica 2 Early Access Release Announcement Disputes Continue

Subnautica 2’s planned early access release in May has sparked internal disagreement between Krafton, the parent company, and Unknown Worlds, the developer studio. After a Delaware court ruling reinstated former Unknown Worlds CEO Ted Gill, Krafton announced the May release date before Gill was officially restored. Gill and his legal team argue that this unauthorized announcement has disrupted marketing plans and community coordination, potentially harming the game's reception and development. Krafton maintains that the announcement was made based on previously set plans and that Gill, once reinstated, can adjust the date if necessary. The ongoing dispute reflects tensions over Kreafon’s management of Unknown Worlds and the pressure to meet the conditions of a $250 million earnout tied to the game’s success.
