Dispatch’s Success Rooted in Arrogance and Stubbornness

Creative directors Nick Hermand and Dennis Lenart of AdHoc Studio revealed in a recent GDC talk that their narrative superhero game Dispatch, which launched in late 2025 and sold over 3 million copies with overwhelmingly positive reception, was developed against industry expectations. After leaving Telltale Games in 2018, the team believed singleplayer choice-driven narratives like those Telltale popularized were considered a "dead" genre and faced skepticism from investors focused on live service games. Despite this, AdHoc chose to focus on quality and their strengths, avoiding features they couldn’t excel at. Their gamble paid off, as Dispatch proved commercially and critically successful, showing there remains a market for well-made narrative-driven singleplayer games even in a live service dominant era.

Mega Man Voice Actor Steps Away over Union Dispute

Ben Diskin, voice of Mega Man in the 2018 game Mega Man 11, announced he will not return for the upcoming Mega Man: Dual Override due to Capcom’s refusal to work under a union contract. Diskin’s withdrawal follows a "Do Not Work" order issued by the SAG-AFTRA union because Capcom failed to initiate the signatory process allowing union voice actors to participate. Diskin cited concerns over AI protections promised by Capcom, but without enforceable union agreements, these safeguards remain uncertain. His departure highlights ongoing tensions between voice actors seeking union protections, especially around emerging AI technologies, and game companies hesitant to accept union terms. This situation could impact the talent involved in future Capcom projects.

Tim Cain Got His Start by Knowing THAC0

Industry veteran Tim Cain recounted how his knowledge of the old Dungeons & Dragons rule system THAC0 helped him secure his first programming contract at Interplay. When competing for a job, Cain impressed by accurately reciting THAC0 values for multiple character classes, demonstrating his depth of understanding beyond pure coding skills. This entry helped him contribute to classic RPGs like Fallout and Arcanum. Cain also explained how his multiplayer tabletop game nights influenced Interplay’s choice to license GURPS for a game project. His story illustrates the value of niche knowledge and passion in career opportunities within game development, especially in RPG design.

WoW’s Personal Key to the Arcantina Item Nerfed — Indirect Loop Exploited

World of Warcraft’s recently introduced Personal Key to the Arcantina, meant as a short-cooldown teleport to a social hub, was hotfixed by Blizzard after players exploited it as a fast global travel device. Originally teleported users to Silvermoon City every 1.5 minutes, the key’s ability was first limited so players immediately return to their original location upon leaving the hub. Players soon uncovered a loophole by queuing for dungeons, teleporting, then exiting to appear near the dungeon entrance in any zone, enabling fast map travel. Blizzard’s final fix now returns players to the Silvermoon inn with a 15-minute cooldown, roughly balancing usability and preventing abuse. The episode reflects ongoing challenges in balancing convenience and game integrity in MMORPGs.

Slay the Spire 2 Feels More Like a Remake Than a True Sequel

Early Access impressions of Slay the Spire 2 describe the game as familiar and enjoyable but lacking significant evolution compared to its acclaimed predecessor. Returning characters, cards, relics, and even playstyles strongly resemble those from the original 2017 game, leading to a strong sense of déjà vu. While the sequel introduces new content and a co-op multiplayer mode, the latter is noted as functional but limited, with awkward UI and minimal interaction between players. Some critics feel the game is more of a polished "greatest hits" album or expansion rather than a fresh sequel. However, being in Early Access, there is potential for future updates to add innovation and differentiation over time.
