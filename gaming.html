**Lana Del Rey Releases Theme Song for 007 First Light**

Lana Del Rey has unexpectedly released "First Light," the theme song for the upcoming James Bond game 007 First Light by IO Interactive. The track follows the franchise tradition of featuring a notable artist but has received mixed reactions. Critics describe the song as derivative and lacking the emotional resonance typical of classic Bond themes. While Del Rey is generally well-regarded, this particular piece is seen as forgettable compared to iconic songs like Adele's "Skyfall" or Carly Simon's "Nobody Does It Better." 007 First Light is due for release on May 27.

**Metro 2039 Reveals Darker Story and Voiced Protagonist**

Metro 2039, the next installment in the post-apocalyptic Metro series by 4A Games, was showcased at the Xbox First Look event. This marks the first new non-VR Metro game since 2019’s Metro Exodus. Set in a dystopian Moscow Metro now dominated by a Nazi faction called the Novoreich, the game introduces a new voiced protagonist known only as "The Stranger," replacing previous leads like Artyom. The story reportedly takes a darker direction, reflecting recent real-world conflicts, with themes of authoritarianism, propaganda, and resistance. The game is planned for release this winter.

**Fortnite Festival Adds MIDI Drum Kit Support**

Epic Games has expanded the musical features of Fortnite Festival Season 14 by introducing compatibility with MIDI drum kits. This allows players to connect older peripherals, such as Rock Band 4 drum kits, or any compatible MIDI device to enhance rhythm gameplay within the event's music-themed activities. Alongside this, the update supports microphone input for vocals and adds a Quick Mix interface for more flexible music mixing. These additions follow Epic’s acquisition of Harmonix, known for music games, and build on Fortnite's ongoing blend of gaming and music interaction.

**Intel-VLSI $3 Billion Patent Lawsuit Reignited**

A long-running legal battle involving Intel and semiconductor patent holder VLSI has reignited following a US appeals court decision. The court has allowed VLSI to pursue a patent infringement case concerning technology related to multi-core processor task allocation, originally filed in 2009. This continues a nearly decade-long dispute that has included previous lawsuits, reversals, and multi-billion-dollar claims. Intel's processors are alleged to infringe on patents acquired by VLSI from other chipmakers. With Intel focusing heavily on new processor technologies, the ongoing lawsuit represents a significant legal and financial challenge.

**Battlefield 6 Announces Large Map Additions and Feature Plans for 2026**

DICE and EA have released a detailed roadmap for Battlefield 6 covering the remainder of 2026, addressing community feedback about map sizes and gameplay variety. Key updates include seven new maps, with a focus on larger environments and the return of naval warfare. Notable maps like Railway to Golmud and classic Wake Island will arrive in upcoming seasons, alongside new game modes, weapons, and custom lobby features. Additionally, a revised server browser with persistent servers, spectator mode, proximity chat, and clan support are planned. These changes aim to improve player experience after criticism of Battlefield 6's launch content and matchmaking.

**Street Fighter Movie Trailer Released**

The first trailer for the new Street Fighter movie has been unveiled, featuring key characters like Ryu, Ken, and Chun-Li in a storyline centered around a brutal tournament with a hidden conspiracy. The film embraces its video game roots with over-the-top fight scenes and character moments. However, Jason Momoa, cast as Blanka and a major draw for the film, appears briefly and obscured in the trailer. The movie is slated for release on October 16, with expectations tempered by comparisons to previous video game adaptations.
