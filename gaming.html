YouTuber Completes Difficult Titanium Liquid-Cooled Gaming PC Build

YouTuber Jakkuh has documented a challenging two-month project building a high-end gaming PC cooled using custom-bent titanium tubing. Equipped with an AMD Ryzen 9 9950X3D processor, Nvidia RTX 5090 GPU, 96 GB of DDR5 RAM, and extensive storage, the build aimed to test the feasibility of titanium liquid cooling, inspired by automotive exhaust components. Due to the complexity of bending titanium tubing and component fit discrepancies, Jakkuh faced multiple setbacks, dubbing it "the worst build" of their life. Despite concerns over galvanic corrosion between titanium tubing and copper components, the rig functions without leaks and maintains reasonable temperatures under load. While the build is not practical or recommended for most users, it serves as an experimental showcase of custom cooling solutions at the enthusiast level.
