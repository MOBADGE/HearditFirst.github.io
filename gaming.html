Resident Evil Requiem: Hidden Details and Strong Sales

A recent discovery using out-of-bounds camera footage in Resident Evil Requiem revealed that the character Grace Ashcroft is depicted as a Reddit user. During a flashback scene, Grace is seen browsing a fictional subreddit named r/amateurjournalists, where posts discuss zombie outbreaks and experiments connected to the game's lore. This detail adds a subtle layer of world-building by linking her character to online communities within the game's narrative. Additionally, Resident Evil Requiem has reached over five million sales within its first four days, making it one of Capcom’s top 20 best-selling titles ever. The game also set a new series record for Steam launch concurrency, indicating strong player interest despite being a single-player focused game.

Copyright Battle Frees Obscure Japanese Game Cookie’s Bustle

The Video Game History Foundation (VGHF) recently succeeded in preserving and documenting the obscure 1999 Japanese point-and-click adventure Cookie’s Bustle after years of suppressive copyright claims by a company named Graceware. The game had been largely removed from online platforms due to DMCA takedowns, including playthrough videos and fan content. VGHF's efforts, including archiving a physical CD-ROM and producing a longplay video, forced Graceware to back down after failing to provide valid proof of copyright ownership. Cookie’s Bustle is now accessible for study and appreciation, marking an important case in game preservation against aggressive copyright trolling.

Highguard Adds Content Despite Imminent Shutdown

Just eight days before its scheduled shutdown on March 12, the free-to-play shooter Highguard received a substantial final update. It includes a new playable healer character, Koldo, who offers unique defensive abilities, new skill trees, and player progression systems. The update also added a versatile new weapon, the Switchback, which can switch between shotgun and marksman rifle modes. Creative director Jason McCord credited the small development team for completing and shipping these additions, despite the game's closure. Highguard attracted over two million players, mainly on PS5, but ultimately did not sustain a viable long-term player base.

Hunt: Showdown Experiments with Hidden Extraction Mechanics

Crytek’s Hunt: Showdown is introducing a new event called Devil’s Trail starting March 18 that changes how players locate extraction and supply points. Traditionally visible on the map, these points will now be hidden at the start of matches and must be discovered by players through exploration or by finding scouting maps. This change aims to make endgame strategy more dynamic by removing certainty about extraction locations. Additional features such as Scout Towers providing useful items and expanded environmental tracking will increase immersion and tactical depth. This approach continues Hunt’s pattern of testing gameplay changes through limited events before deciding on permanent implementation.

US Government Considers Forcing Tencent to Divest Game Holdings

Reports indicate that the US government is debating whether to compel Chinese conglomerate Tencent to divest its stakes in major Western game companies due to national security concerns. Tencent owns or has significant stakes in Riot Games, Epic Games, Ubisoft, and others. The concerns focus on potential access to US citizen data and intellectual property. This discussion was expected to be part of a recent high-level US-China meeting, though that was postponed. Previous administrations have scrutinized Tencent’s holdings without forcing sales, but this renewed focus adds uncertainty to the industry. Forced divestiture could disrupt the market and potentially lead to private equity takeovers, which carry risks for company stability.

Rare Pass Rate in Standardized 1980s Adventure Game Test

More than 800 players attempted the inaugural Adventure Game Aptitude Test (AGAT), designed to assess the ability to complete the 1987 graphic adventure Maniac Mansion without using walkthroughs or external help. The test was strictly proctored with webcam and microphone monitoring. Only two participants successfully passed within the four-hour limit, yielding a pass rate of 0.24%. The test aimed to recreate the challenge and frustration of classic adventure games under controlled conditions. Some participants submitted irrelevant proof or attempted to cheat, but the outcome highlighted how demanding older adventure games can be, even for experienced players.
