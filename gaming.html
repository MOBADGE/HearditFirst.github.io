Challenges in PC Gaming Hardware Pricing and the Impact of the Memory Crisis

The PC gaming market currently faces a pricing issue exacerbated by an ongoing memory (RAM and storage) supply crisis. Affordable gaming PCs traditionally start around $1,000, which remains a considerable barrier. Recent spikes in DRAM and SSD prices, driven largely by increased demand from AI data centers and a limited number of key manufacturers, threaten to further increase hardware costs. Components such as 32 GB DDR5 RAM kits have tripled in price or become difficult to find, while entry-level GPUs hover around $300-$500, providing just barely adequate performance for modern games.

Although GPU prices have stabilised near MSRP after previous markups, the aggregate effect of rising memory, storage, and graphics card costs presents a growing obstacle for newcomers and upgrade-minded players. This pricing pressure risks limiting access to PC gaming, potentially shrinking the community over time by deterring budget-conscious buyers. The article points out that record profits among tech giants contrast sharply with these affordability issues, underscoring an industry-wide challenge that may continue into 2026.
