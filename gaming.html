AI Startup Tesana Aims to Expand Game Development Accessibility

Tesana, a new AI-focused company, is working on a platform that allows users to create videogames by simply typing descriptive prompts. Its system converts those text inputs into game code, assisted by third-party AI tools for assets and narrative elements. While current outputs resemble rough, glitchy versions of popular games, the startup has already attracted about 10,000 paying users within weeks of launch. The founder, Johannes Vermandois, envisions Tesana enabling up to 100 million new people to make games, potentially fostering fresh game styles and innovations. He acknowledges many AI-generated games will be low quality but hopes the ease of creation will raise overall standards by increasing volume and diversity. This development highlights ongoing efforts to simplify game creation, although some skepticism remains regarding the depth of creative control such AI tools offer.

Slay the Spire 2 Introduces End-of-Run Badges in Beta Update

The card-based roguelike Slay the Spire 2 has added badges that appear at the end of each run to summarize unique accomplishments or events, such as defeating bosses without damage or discovering easter eggs. These badges aim to make runs feel distinct and improve scoring feedback. Additional changes in the recent beta patch include reworked map generation, card balance adjustments, new visual effects, and UI improvements. This follows a previous controversial balance update that triggered a significant number of negative player reviews. While this patch also faces mixed reception, it is less severe. Badges are intended as small reminders of player achievements and will eventually appear in run histories, enhancing the game's replay value by providing more detailed performance insight.

Fake AI-Generated Interview with One Piece Actor Sparks Criticism

Esquire Singapore published a feature presenting an "interview" with Mackenyu Arata, the live-action One Piece actor; however, the responses were created by feeding his past interview content through AI language models rather than conducting a real interview. The piece included AI-generated answers on personal topics but lacked genuine engagement. The article has drawn backlash from journalists and readers for misleading presentation and ethical concerns about fabricating interviews using AI. This incident adds to broader discussions about the use of AI to replicate or simulate public figures in media without clear disclosure, raising questions about journalistic standards and authenticity in the AI era.

Homeworld Remastered Collection and Other RTS Titles Offered in $8 Bundle

A new 2K Tactics and Tycoons Humble Bundle features the Homeworld Remastered Collection and its prequel, Deserts of Kharak, along with eight other real-time strategy games for $8. Although Homeworld Remastered and Deserts of Kharak are individually on steep sale on Steam, this bundle offers a good value by including additional classic RTS games like Railroad Tycoon 2 Platinum and Freedom Force titles. While some included games are dated, they remain respected within their genre. This bundle presents an accessible way for strategy fans to build a sizable game library affordably, especially for those interested in the Homeworld series and its science fiction setting.

Skyblivion Lead Expresses Optimism Despite Possible Delays

Rebelzize, the lead developer of Skyblivion—the fan-made mod rebuilding The Elder Scrolls 4: Oblivion in Skyrim Special Edition’s engine—addressed concerns about delay following a recent public call for more development help. Though originally planned for 2025, the mod was postponed to 2026, and the recent recruitment suggests substantial work remains. Rebelzize confirmed that all quest locations are complete and Imperial City progress has improved, with ongoing bug fixes. While acknowledging potential setbacks could still occur, he expressed optimism about meeting release goals. He also urged the community to appreciate the team's efforts and avoid undue criticism, emphasizing a commitment to delivering a polished, functional mod.

Echo Point Nova Indie FPS to Receive New Game+ and New Movement Modes

Echo Point Nova, an indie open-world FPS known for fast-paced gunplay and mobility, will see a significant update later this month. This update will introduce New Game+ features, allowing players to carry over select unlocks, randomize aspects like enemy spawns and weapon pickups, and customize their replays. Additionally, new movement mechanics will be added, including a rope grapple akin to Spider-Man’s web-swinging, sprinting with wallrunning reminiscent of Titanfall, and a jetpack option.
