Updated: April 11, 2026

**Graveyard Keeper 2 AI Concerns Addressed by Developers** Following the recent announcement of *Graveyard Keeper 2*, some players suspected that the game's promotional art might have been generated using AI, prompting backlash and scrutiny on social media. Lazy Bear CTO Slava Cherkasov publicly responded, clarifying that no AI was used in the production of *Graveyard Keeper 2*. He acknowledged experimenting with various AI models personally but emphasized that these experiments are separate from the game’s development, which involves a dedicated team of artists. This response aims to address fan concerns about AI usage and reassure the community about the creative process behind the sequel.

**Rockstar Faces Ransom Demand After Data Breach** Rockstar Games confirmed a security incident after the hacking group ShinyHunters claimed to have accessed data stored in Rockstar's cloud system, Snowflake. The breach reportedly resulted from exploiting tokens linked to a third-party analytics service, Anodot, which previously suffered its own compromise. ShinyHunters has issued a ransom demand, threatening to leak the data by April 14 if Rockstar does not comply. Rockstar stated that the compromised information is limited and non-material, asserting there is no impact on the company or its players. This event echoes a prior GTA 6 leak in 2022, highlighting ongoing security challenges associated with high-profile game developers.

**Proton Experimental Update Improves Classic Capcom Games on Steam Deck and Linux** Proton Experimental's latest build includes significant compatibility fixes for several older Capcom titles on Steam Deck and Linux platforms. Notably, classic survival horror games such as *Resident Evil* (1996), *Resident Evil 2* (1998), *Dino Crisis*, and *Dino Crisis 2* have been moved to a playable state, improving their accessibility outside of Windows PCs. While these enhancements require opting into the experimental version of Proton and some issues—such as FMV playback in *Dino Crisis 2*—remain unresolved without community fixes, the update marks a positive step toward preserving and enabling classic games on modern portable hardware.

**BioWare's Challenges: Reflecting on Its Past and Questioning Its Future** An in-depth analysis of BioWare's recent trajectory highlights the studio's struggle to replicate the success of its earlier RPGs. While *Dragon Age: The Veilguard* received generally positive reviews at launch, it underperformed commercially relative to expectations and saw limited post-release support. The piece argues that BioWare’s traditional identity has been eroded by frequent staff changes and external pressures, particularly from parent company Electronic Arts (EA), leading to overreliance on established franchises like *Mass Effect* and *Dragon Age*. It suggests that BioWare's best chance at revitalization would involve stepping away from legacy titles and focusing on smaller-scale, innovative projects—though it also notes skepticism about EA allowing such a pivot, given its focus on large launches.

**Revisiting Redfall Reveals Unfulfilled Potential After Final Update** *Redfall*, Arkane Austin's multiplayer vampire shooter, had a troubled launch in 2023 and faced criticism for its bugs, AI issues, and uninspired combat. Its final major patch, version 1.4, introduced a Community Standing system designed to encourage exploration and engagement with the game's environment through side missions and upgrades. While these additions improved the connection to the game world and offered some meaningful bonuses, core gameplay problems, particularly with enemy AI and shooting mechanics, remain largely unaddressed. The update underscores what the game might have achieved had the studio remained operational, but it does not fully redeem *Redfall* in its current state.

**City Masterplan Emerges as Potential Competitor to Cities: Skylines** A new city-building game, *City Masterplan* by 1:1 Studio, aims to enter the market dominated by *Cities: Skylines* and its sequel. Offering a large-scale simulation with freeform road construction, realistic zoning, and detailed procedural content, *City Masterplan* emphasizes balance between simulation depth and user-friendly control. Built on Unreal Engine, it promises substantial environments and architectural variety. While no release date has been announced, early footage indicates it could present a meaningful alternative to *Cities: Skylines*, especially as its sequel has received mixed feedback. The game could appeal to players seeking a fresh and realistic metropolis-building experience.
