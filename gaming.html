Magic: The Gathering Returns to Strixhaven Setting Without a Harry Potter Crossover Magic: The Gathering announced its new set, *Secrets of Strixhaven*, a sequel to the *Strixhaven: School of Mages* set from five years ago. Despite speculation about a potential Harry Potter crossover following Hasbro’s partnership with Warner Bros, Wizards of the Coast is sticking to its original Strixhaven setting. Unlike Hogwarts, Strixhaven is a university offering a variety of subjects beyond magic, such as art and zoology, with self-chosen colleges rather than assigned houses. This choice reflects a more mature and self-aware take on magical school themes. The set will be available on Magic: The Gathering Arena from April 21 and in physical form from April 24.

Crimson Desert Patch Introduces Feature to Hide Helmets The open-world RPG *Crimson Desert* received an update adding a small but requested feature: a hide helmet option. Players can now decide when their helmets show during gameplay, including options for always, never, only in cutscenes, or only in combat. The patch also adds a new armor set and helmet for cats and expands camp storage capacity to 1,000 slots. Additionally, a "classic" control scheme for movement has been reintroduced alongside the newer updates. The patch comes as the game continues to evolve post-launch, addressing player requests and feedback amid a mixed reception.

Leaked Footage May Show First Look at Elden Ring Movie Set A fan’s TikTok videos from the UK show footage that appears to be a faithful recreation of the Church of Marika from *Elden Ring*, suggesting it might be part of Alex Garland’s upcoming film adaptation of the game. The set includes period-appropriate props and construction materials linked to a professional film scenery company near Windsor Great Park, a known filming location. While this does not confirm the connection definitively, it has sparked discussion about the film’s content and fidelity to the game. The project, announced by A24, remains largely under wraps aside from casting rumors and Garland’s known enthusiasm for the source material.

Datamined Elden Ring Cutscene Reveals More of Villain Miquella’s Story Dataminer Lance McDonald uncovered an unused *Elden Ring* cutscene featuring DLC antagonist Miquella the Kind. The scene shows Miquella planting and watering the Haligtree sapling with his blood, hinting at key lore concerning the origins of this important location. Voice lines suggest a mix of speeches directed at another character and the player, reflecting Miquella’s vision for a new world. This scene appears connected to early story beats involving the Dectus Medallion and further demonstrates the complexity behind Miquella’s character, contrasting with his harsher portrayal in the DLC’s final story.

Warframe Developer Declares No Use of AI-Generated Assets in Games Digital Extremes’ community director Megan Everett stated firmly that *Warframe* and upcoming *Soulframe* will not include AI-generated content. She expressed concern about distinguishing genuine human-made art from AI-generated ones, underlining the studio’s commitment to traditional creative processes. This stance contrasts with many developers who have integrated AI tools despite increasing skepticism within the industry. The topic follows recent controversies over AI-generated assets appearing in games like *Crimson Desert* and *Anno 117*, highlighting ongoing debates over transparency and ethics in game development.

Amazon Orders Rewrite of Mass Effect Series to Appeal More to Non-Gamers Reports indicate that Amazon’s global TV head, Peter Friedlander, has requested rewrites for the *Mass Effect* TV adaptation to make it "more appealing to non-gamers." The series is reportedly close to receiving a series order but faces the challenge of balancing fan expectations with broader audience accessibility. This development reflects wider difficulties in adapting video games for TV, where creators must negotiate the interests of long-time fans and first-time viewers. The *Mass Effect* show remains in early stages, with few details released about casting or plot.
