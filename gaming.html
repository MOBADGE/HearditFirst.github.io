Baldur's Gate Mod Manager Receives Major Update

The EET Mod Forge, a web-based tool designed to manage mods for the original Baldur's Gate games, has been significantly updated to version 2.0. This tool helps players organize and preview mod load orders and warns of potential conflicts that could break the game. It features over 640 mods and now includes the ability to preview NPC portrait art when multiple mods compete for the same assets. The update also tracks in-game caps like kits to prevent exceeding limits before installation. A new preset called "Mod Forge Ultimate" includes 475 mods, carefully reviewed to resolve component-level conflicts. This overhaul aims to make modding classic Baldur's Gate games more accessible and less prone to save file corruption.

Bethesda Reflects on DLC Notification Improvements

Bethesda has acknowledged past missteps with DLC notifications in older titles, particularly Fallout 3's Game of the Year Edition, where constant pop-up messages about new content negatively affected player experience. Istvan Pely, art director on Starfield, shared that lessons learned from those earlier experiences have influenced how DLC is integrated in newer games. The upcoming Starfield Terran Armada story DLC will be introduced more organically in-game, allowing players to discover content naturally without intrusive notifications. This approach contrasts with Bethesda's earlier method of overwhelming players with DLC prompts, aiming for smoother immersion.

Resident Evil Requiem Mod Adds Drivable Hamburger Vehicle

A modder has released a mod for Resident Evil Requiem that replaces the in-game product-placed Porsche Cayenne Turbo GT with a drivable hamburger vehicle inspired by SpongeBob SquarePants. The mod is part of a broader wave of humorous and playful mods for the game that swap various characters and elements for unexpected replacements, including Shrek, a chibi character from Umamusume, and even Thomas the Tank Engine replacing spiders. These mods require the REFramework tool to install and demonstrate the active modding community's creativity in altering the latest Resident Evil title's experience.

Elden Ring Cut Content Restored by Modder

A modder has restored a cut NPC questline related to Merchant Kalé in Elden Ring. Originally intended to deepen the narrative behind the game's Frenzied Flame ending—a path involving omnicide—this quest was removed before release. The restored content gives the character greater depth, portraying his despair and rage at the tragedies inflicted upon his people. It provides additional context for hidden story elements and offers new motivations regarding the game's endings. The mod uses the Mod Engine 2 utility, allowing players to experience this part of Elden Ring's story which was previously only accessible through datamined voice lines.

Minecraft Unveils New Underground Biome: Sulfur Caves

Minecraft is set to introduce a new underground biome called Sulfur Caves as part of its second feature drop in 2026. This biome differentiates itself by including poisonous pools that cause a dizziness effect, presenting new environmental hazards for players exploring caves. The area is visually distinct with bright red and yellow colors inspired by real sulfur hot springs and includes new blocks such as cinnabar and sulfur. Alongside the biome, a new mob called the Sulfur Cube has been revealed, capable of absorbing blocks and changing its attributes accordingly. This addition aims to refresh and expand the caving experience in Minecraft.

Minecraft Dungeons Sequel Announced

Mojang has announced a sequel to Minecraft Dungeons, their action-RPG spin-off. Revealed during the recent Minecraft Live 2026 event, the teaser trailer offers a glimpse of the game's fantasy world under threat once again, continuing the lighthearted dungeon-crawling gameplay. No gameplay details have been shared yet, and the Steam store page currently only has cinematic screenshots. Minecraft Dungeons had a modest reception but built a fanbase, and the sequel is planned for release within 2026. This announcement underscores Mojang’s ongoing interest in expanding the Minecraft universe beyond the core sandbox experience.
