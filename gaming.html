Gaming News Briefing — March 24, 2026

1. Nvidia CEO Highlights Doom’s Influential Legacy Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang spoke about the impact of classic games during an appearance on the Lex Fridman podcast. He named Doom as the most influential game ever made, emphasizing its role in transforming the PC from an office tool to a gaming platform embraced by families and gamers. Huang noted that while flight simulators preceded Doom, they did not have similar cultural impact. He also mentioned Virtua Fighter as impactful from a technology viewpoint. Huang’s comments underline Doom’s enduring place in gaming culture and its wider influence beyond just technology.

2. Solo Victory Against Marathon’s Compiler Boss In the game Marathon, the Cryo Archive map offers a challenging boss fight against Compiler designed for three players. Surprisingly, a player known as TheSnazzzyRock managed to defeat Compiler solo within a week of the map’s release after eight attempts. This feat is notable given the difficulty of the fight, which requires collecting rare keys and subroutine items, with some spawning issues recently fixed. TheSnazzzyRock shared his loadout and tactics, which focus on long-range damage and careful use of smokescreens and healing. This achievement showcases the evolving skill ceiling and player dedication in multiplayer FPS challenges.

3. Sony Details Its PSSR Upscaling Technology in PlayStation 5 Pro Sony PlayStation lead architect Mark Cerny revealed details about the company’s new PSSR upscaling technology used in the PlayStation 5 Pro. The technology shares a core algorithm with AMD’s FSR Redstone upscaling used on PCs, but with differences in implementation due to hardware variations. Notably, PSSR employs 8-bit integer math, whereas FSR uses 8-bit floating point. Although related, these differences mean it’s unlikely older AMD graphics cards will directly benefit from PSSR upgrades despite similarities to leaked FSR 4 INT8 code. The PS5 Pro’s specialized INT8 hardware gives it a significant performance advantage, situating Sony’s approach as tailored rather than broadly transferable to older PC GPUs.

4. Epic Games Announces Layoffs Amid Financial Challenges Epic Games has laid off over 1,000 employees amid ongoing financial challenges despite Fortnite’s continued success. CEO Tim Sweeney cited a slump in player engagement starting in 2025 and high spending as main reasons for the cuts, which follow a similar round of layoffs in 2023. The company continues to invest in seasonal content and live events for Fortnite and hinted at a next-generation initiative later this year. Sweeney clarified that these layoffs are unrelated to AI developments and framed the moves as necessary adjustments in a difficult industry environment, with hopes of strengthening Epic’s long-term position.

5. New DLC Announced for Hello Kitty Island Adventure Hello Kitty Island Adventure will receive its second DLC, titled City Town, on April 16. The expansion introduces a new area where players rebuild and customize a town featuring new shops like an arcade and a cafe. It also adds a new Sanrio character, U*SA*HA*NA, along with over 90 quests to progress through 35 friendship levels. The DLC expands gameplay options and narrative content, offering players fresh activities beyond the original Friendship Island.

6. Reviews: Asus Releases Two Gaming Peripherals Asus released the ROG Strix Morph 96 Wireless keyboard and ROG Cetra Open Wireless earbuds. - The ROG Strix Morph 96 Wireless features a compact 96 percent layout with hotswappable switches, dual wireless connectivity (Bluetooth and 2.4 GHz), and customizable RGB lighting. It shares many traits with the older Strix Scope II 96 Wireless but uses ABS keycaps rather than PBT, which may affect long-term durability. The keyboard offers a balanced enthusiast-grade option but faces competition from similarly priced alternatives. - The ROG Cetra Open Wireless are open-back earbuds designed to rest on the ear rather than inside it, catering to those seeking comfort without traditional in-ear buds. They support 2.4 GHz and Bluetooth connections, provide good battery life, and deliver a sound profile tailored primarily for gaming with clear directional audio and solid bass. Their unique form factor, however, may not suit everyone, and they are priced higher than many conventional wireless earbuds.
