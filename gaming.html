Tarn Adams Shares Insights on Gaming Habits and Preferences

Tarn Adams, co-creator of the cult classic Dwarf Fortress, revealed in a recent interview that he has logged over 1,400 hours playing Factorio. Adams expressed his current fascination with terraforming games, noting that while Dwarf Fortress is deeply rooted in fantasy, it does not fully accommodate a science fiction theme. He highlighted several terraforming titles he enjoys, including The Planet Crafter, Terraformental, and Terraforming Titans. Adams emphasizes how these games allow him to explore concepts and settings that diverge from his work on Dwarf Fortress. His remarks shed light on how even veteran developers engage extensively with other games, which can influence their own creative processes.

Crimson Desert’s Complex Identity and Ongoing Evolution

After accumulating more than 150 hours in Crimson Desert, PC Gamer’s Sean Martin described the game as difficult to categorize. Despite its scope rivaling many MMOs, the developers at Pearl Abyss have insisted it is neither a traditional RPG nor an MMO. The game has undergone significant post-launch changes, softening initially uncompromising design choices like lack of fast travel or limited inventory space. These adjustments have made Crimson Desert more accessible and are helping cultivate a steady player base. Despite some control and bug issues, the game offers a large, detailed open world with numerous systems and side activities. The experience emphasizes exploration over linear storytelling, making it a challenging but captivating title for some players.

Intel’s Arrow Lake Refresh Signals Broader Strategic Shift

Hardware writer Nick Evanson reviewed Intel’s Core Ultra 200S Arrow Lake Refresh processors, praising their improved performance and competitive pricing compared to previous generations. Evanson notes that the refresh marks a significant turnaround from the initial Arrow Lake launch, which had underwhelming performance and less confidence from Intel’s staff. The refreshed chips demonstrate Intel’s renewed focus on consumer needs, balancing power efficiency, gaming capability, and affordability. The company is also taking longer-term approaches such as committing to more durable CPU sockets. While AMD remains the leader in some market segments, Intel’s latest releases are gaining ground, indicating a shift in the competitive landscape in desktop CPU offerings.

Magic: The Gathering Opts Against a Harry Potter Crossover

Magic: The Gathering’s upcoming sequel set, Secrets of Strixhaven, is returning to its own original magical university setting rather than crossing over with the Harry Potter universe, as some had speculated. The Strixhaven setting differentiates itself by focusing on a university with a broad range of magical and mundane subjects, featuring adult students rather than schoolchildren. The design intentionally calls back to various fantasy magical schools without relying on Harry Potter tropes. This decision maintains Magic’s established tone and creative independence, steering clear of the crowded wizard-school genre and avoiding potential controversies associated with the Harry Potter franchise.

Crimson Desert Patch Adds Hide Helmet Option and Other Features

A recent patch for Crimson Desert introduced a popular quality-of-life update: an option to hide helmets during gameplay and cutscenes. The feature can be customized to hide helmets always, only in combat, only in cutscenes, or never. Along with this, the patch expands player camp storage and offers a “classic” control scheme option in response to community feedback on movement controls. The update also includes new armor sets for pets within the game. These changes demonstrate Pearl Abyss’s ongoing efforts to refine Crimson Desert and respond to player preferences, helping improve the overall user experience.

Potential First Look at Elden Ring Movie Set Emerges

Footage from a filming location in the UK shows a ruined church set resembling the churches of Marika as seen in Elden Ring’s game world. A fan-captured video highlights construction details consistent with film production, including props and construction company markings. While it has not been officially confirmed as part of Alex Garland’s Elden Ring film adaptation, the set’s details align well with in-game architecture and iconography. This could offer an early hint at the movie’s visual direction. The film remains largely mysterious aside from casting rumors and Garland’s known enthusiasm for the source material.
