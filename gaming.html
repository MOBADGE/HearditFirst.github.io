Nvidia’s DLSS 5 Receives Mixed Reactions Over Character Visuals

Nvidia recently revealed its latest upscaling technology, DLSS 5, which aims to enhance visual realism in games. Early demonstrations, however, have sparked mixed reactions from the gaming community. Several examples showcase noticeably altered character faces—such as Grace Ashcroft from *Resident Evil Requiem*—where the AI-driven filter appears to smooth and modify features in ways that many feel disrupt the original artistic style. Players have humorously nicknamed the effect the "Yassify Filter" due to its pronounced beautification.

While the images circulating aren’t confirmed to be direct outputs of DLSS 5, the response highlights a debate on the balance between graphical enhancement and preserving original design intent. Despite social media skepticism, major publishers like Capcom, Bethesda, and Ubisoft plan to support DLSS 5 in upcoming titles scheduled for release this fall.

Marathon to Test Duos Mode in Upcoming Experiment

Bungie’s shooter *Marathon* is experimenting with a new duos gameplay mode, allowing players to team up in pairs rather than the current trio-based squads. Game director Joe Ziegler announced the limited-time test, which will activate on the Perimeter map beginning March 18 at 10 AM PT. The aim is to gather data and player feedback on how the game performs with dual-player crews in a broader context.

This change represents an adjustment to the existing multiplayer format that could influence how players strategize and engage in the game, potentially attracting different playstyles or preferences within *Marathon*’s community.

Subnautica 2 Publisher Must Reinstate Fired CEO Following Legal Ruling

A Delaware court has ruled against Krafton, the parent company of Unknown Worlds—the developer behind *Subnautica 2*—ordering the reinstatement of CEO Ted Gill after a wrongful termination lawsuit. The suit, filed last year by Unknown Worlds’ leadership, accused Krafton of attempting to bypass a substantial performance bonus through questionable legal strategies inspired by ChatGPT.

The ruling criticized Krafton CEO Changhan Kim’s reliance on AI-generated legal advice, which the company’s own legal team had warned against. Krafton had also restricted Unknown Worlds’ ability to release *Subnautica 2* on Steam, actions the judge noted pressured the developer’s founders into disputed negotiations. This case highlights challenges in corporate governance and the role of AI in legal decision-making within the industry.

Atlus Commits to Salary Increases and Reducing Overtime Amid Industry Layoffs

In contrast to ongoing mass layoffs hitting several major game publishers globally, Atlus announced plans to improve its working conditions. Starting in April, the company will raise starting monthly salaries for new hires by 10%, increasing from 300,000 yen to 330,000 yen. Existing employees and contractors are set for roughly a 15% annual salary increase.

Atlus will also reduce mandatory fixed monthly overtime from 30 to 20 hours, addressing long-standing concerns over harsh working hours in Japanese corporate culture and the broader game industry’s notorious crunch practices. This move indicates Atlus’ intention to foster a better workplace environment as many peers continue staff reductions.

What’s Next for Animal Crossing? A Look Back and Forward

As *Animal Crossing* reaches its 25th anniversary with multiple entries across Nintendo platforms, a retrospective explores the series' evolution from its humble beginnings on the GameCube to the expansive customization and multiplayer experiences of *New Horizons* and its recent updates.

Nintendo’s continual enhancements include expanding player creativity through town and home customization, increasing multiplayer capacity, and refining controls—including mouse support—especially in the newer Switch 2 editions. Past spin-offs focusing on interior design have also influenced mainline titles. Looking ahead, the next installment is expected to build upon these foundations, possibly integrating even more cooperative multiplayer and customization options underscored by the latest content such as Slumber Islands.
