Star Wars Zero Company: A New Tactical RPG from Former XCOM Talent

Bit Reactor’s Star Wars Zero Company is a turn-based tactics game set during the Clone Wars that merges familiar XCOM-style combat with deeper RPG elements. Players lead a mercenary squad composed of former Separatist and Republic operatives investigating a new Dark Side faction. Unlike traditional tactical games, Zero Company allows third-person exploration and interaction at the home base called the Den, blending squad management with story-driven character interactions reminiscent of Mass Effect.

The game features a custom protagonist, Hawks, and a diverse roster of story and custom squadmates, each with unique skills and permadeath consequences. Classes include Assault, Heavy, Sharpshooter, and others, and players select alien species for their mercenaries. Combat emphasizes action points shared between movement and attacks and introduces a communal "advantage" meter for powerful abilities. Missions vary from hostage rescues to control point battles, demanding tactical planning and adaptability.

Zero Company’s meta-game involves mission cycles on a galaxy map and non-combat operations that impact squad morale and resources. The game is expected to launch sometime in 2026, though no exact date is set. Its combination of immersive base exploration and thoughtful tactical combat represents a notable evolution in the genre.

Stranger than Heaven: Yakuza-Style Action Across Five Eras and Cities

The Ryu Ga Gotoku Studio (RGG), known for the Yakuza series, revealed Stranger than Heaven, a narrative-driven action game spanning five time periods (1915 to 1965) and multiple cities, including the familiar district of Kamurocho. This setting marks the earliest depiction of Kamurocho seen in the franchise, promising a historical view of the area.

The game focuses on RGG's signature close-quarters combat but appears to take a more serious tone than recent Like a Dragon titles. Stranger than Heaven does not include returning characters from previous Yakuza games, aligning with its historical timeline. Details on gameplay remain limited, with a fuller presentation expected in May. This project continues RGG’s interest in blending action and story across new periods and settings within its established style.

V Rising Developer Shifts Focus to a New Vampire Game

Stunlock Studios announced that V Rising, their vampire survival game, will no longer receive content updates after nearly two years. The studio views the current game as a complete journey to Dracula and is now working on a new, more ambitious project set in the same vampire universe. This upcoming game aims to improve on the limitations encountered in V Rising by exploring new gameplay ideas with greater depth and complexity.

While no release timeline or further details have been shared, Stunlock emphasized ongoing support for bug fixes and balancing in V Rising. The developer also confirmed that official modding support will not be provided for V Rising due to structural constraints but noted that lessons learned may influence their next title.

Hades 2 to Receive New Story Scene and Updates with Console Launch

Supergiant Games will release an update for Hades 2 alongside its console debut on April 14. The update includes bonus content, quality-of-life improvements, and a newly teased narrative scene featuring the character Heracles in a hot spring setting. While the new scene may expand certain character interactions, Supergiant clarified it is unlikely to significantly alter the game's main story.

This update continues Supergiant’s approach of post-launch refinements to enrich the experience without rewriting core endings. Patch notes will be released with the update, leaving room for players to discover all additions upon launch.

Two Jesus Simulation Games Launch Days Apart

Two unrelated Jesus simulation games are launching within one week of each other, both with very different approaches. "Jesus Simulator," released today, is an interactive fiction visual novel offering perspectives from various biblical figures, focusing on story exploration rather than action gameplay. The game uses AI-generated images.

On April 2, "I Am Jesus Christ" will launch, presenting a more action-oriented experience that includes miracles, telekinesis, and puzzles. This title also used AI tools for voice acting to manage development costs for its small team.

Additionally, a third Jesus-themed game, "Jesus Take The Wheel," a physics-based co-op racing game, recently launched and takes a non-religious, gameplay-focused angle on the theme.
