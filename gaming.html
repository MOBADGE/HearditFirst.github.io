**Gaming Randomness and Fairness Explored in Dispatch and XCOM**

A recent talk by Adhoc, the developer of the superhero comedy game Dispatch, revealed that their game’s success chance mechanics secretly favor the player, following a practice popularized by Firaxis in the XCOM series. In Dispatch’s dispatching minigame, any chance of success over 76% is treated as an automatic success initially, until the player has benefited three times, after which true odds resume until a failure occurs, triggering another cycle of protections. Low percentage chances are also slightly boosted. These invisible adjustments are intended to reduce frustrating experiences common with pure randomness, similar to XCOM’s concealed odds boosts. However, for the game’s final episode, Adhoc disables these “training wheels” to provide a harder, less forgiving challenge. This approach highlights ongoing efforts in game design to manage player perception of randomness and fairness.

**s&box Pricing Likely Close to Garry’s Mod**

Garry Newman, creator of Garry’s Mod, recently discussed the anticipated pricing for his new game creation platform, s&box. Though the final price is not yet officially decided, Newman and Facepunch Studios COO Alistair McFarlane indicated a likely price between $10 and $20, aligning closely with the longstanding $10 cost of Garry’s Mod. With s&box’s planned release in April, this pricing information offers an early indication of how Facepunch intends to position the new platform in the market, balancing accessibility with the value of the enhanced game creation tools it aims to provide.

**Crabmeat: A Horror Experience Centered on Crab Catching**

Crabmeat is a newly released point-and-click horror game themed around catching crabs—not as a lighthearted fishing trip, but a tense survival scenario set in the Antarctic. Players take on the role of a penal colony worker tasked with trapping Southern King Crabs under threat of lethal enforcement. The game blends survival horror elements with resource management and uses mouse controls to create an unsettling atmosphere, capitalizing on the natural creepiness attributed to crabs. Available now on Steam with a launch discount and demo, Crabmeat offers a brief but atmospheric experience that may appeal to fans of underwater and survival horror titles.

**Kickstarter for Roguelite Monster-Collector Surpasses $800K Quickly**

Wonderful Neoran Valley, a roguelite monster-collector game blending JRPG dialogue-driven creature recruitment with roguelite mechanics, has hit its Kickstarter funding goal within 10 minutes and has since exceeded $800,000. Led by popular Italian Pokémon streamer Cydonia, the project distances itself from traditional monster catches by requiring players to convince creatures to join via conversations rather than capturing them. The funding success has unlocked stretch goals such as an endless mode and a Switch 2 port. The Kickstarter campaign remains active with over two weeks to go, showing strong community interest fueled in part by the streamer's influence.

**Brendan Greene Comments on AI Content and Internet Quality**

Brendan Greene, known as PlayerUnknown, expressed concerns in a recent interview about the impact of AI-generated content on the quality of information available online. He described a feedback loop where large language models (LLMs) consume AI-created content, which becomes accepted as truth, leading to homogenized and degraded information. Greene questioned the reliability of non-deterministic AI models and highlighted environmental and social concerns related to extensive data center use. While recognizing domain-specific AI applications, he remains skeptical about the near-term arrival of true artificial general intelligence (AGI) and advocates for more localized computing solutions.

**Greedfall: The Dying World Launches After Early Access but Feels Unfinished**

After two years in early access, Greedfall: The Dying World has reached its full release, serving as a prequel to the 2019 original. The game introduces a real-time with pause combat system replacing the prior title’s live action combat but suffers from pacing issues, lackluster enemy AI, and frequent bugs, including crashes and soft locks. The story focuses on a colonized islander protagonist resisting exploitative forces. Despite some strengths, such as a competent soundtrack and evocative world design, the gameplay and technical problems make the experience feel incomplete. The game may primarily interest fans of “eurojank” RPGs familiar with similar niche titles, but its overall reception is mixed.
