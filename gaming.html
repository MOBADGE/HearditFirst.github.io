**Echo Point Nova Expands with New Game+ and Movement Mechanics**

Greylock Studio's indie open-world FPS *Echo Point Nova* is set to receive a substantial update later this month. The "2007-core" shooter, praised for its fluid movement and kinetic gunplay, will introduce a New Game+ mode allowing players to carry over selected unlocks into subsequent playthroughs. The update also adds options to randomize weapon pickups and enemy spawns, increasing replayability. Additionally, new movement modes are being implemented, including a jetpack, wallrunning-enabled sprinting reminiscent of *Titanfall*, and a rope grapple mechanic akin to Spider-Man’s web-swinging. These changes aim to deepen player interaction with the game’s momentum and environment, enhancing the core experience of fast-paced combat and traversal.

**State of Decay 3 Alpha Testing Announced for May**

After a long development period since its announcement in 2020, *State of Decay 3* is entering a new phase with an alpha test planned for May. Developed by Undead Labs, the upcoming zombie survival shooter will feature four-player co-op, new base-building dynamics, and updated resource management systems. The announcement invites players to register on the official website for a chance to participate in early testing, marking a shift from prolonged silence to active community engagement. This milestone signals tangible progress for the game, which previously only released cinematic teasers and has no confirmed release date yet.

**Rust Introduces Water Wheels for Hydro Power and New Base Defense Options**

The latest *Rust* update, called Spring Clean, adds water wheels as a new renewable energy source. Players can harness power from rivers or oceans through these wheels, supplementing prior additions like wind turbines and solar panels. Interestingly, water wheels can also be powered manually by having players or even handcuffed opponents run inside them, adding a unique gameplay interaction centered on player cooperation or coercion. The update also includes an armoured ladder hatch to better secure vertical base access, colored buttons for easier control, an overfishing mechanic to prevent constant reliance on fishing, and improvements to technology unlocking systems. Seasonal Easter content, including an egg hunt and themed cosmetic items, rounds out the patch.

**Petit Planet Beta Signup Opens for Cozy Galactic Life Sim**

HoYoVerse is accepting signups for a closed beta test of *Petit Planet*, a life simulation game focusing on community and creativity within a cosmic setting. The beta, starting on April 21, will let selected participants engage with new characters, Starsea exploration features, and expanded activities at the Galactic Bazaar. Players manage their own planet, fostering relationships and developing interconnected environments through in-game vitality called Luca. While no release date is set, earlier reports indicate the game is approaching its final development stages. The test will provide a chance for players to experience the game's relaxing and social systems firsthand.

**Raven Software Co-founder Brian Raffel Retires After 36 Years**

Brian Raffel, co-founder and longtime studio head of Raven Software, announced his retirement following a 36-year career. Raffel helped establish Raven as an FPS developer with titles like *Heretic*, *Hexen*, and the *Jedi Knight* series before guiding the studio through its acquisition by Activision in 1997. In recent years, Raven primarily contributed to the *Call of Duty* franchise, including campaigns and *Warzone* design. Raffel’s departure marks the end of a significant era for the studio, which has been influential in shaping fantasy and shooter games since the early 1990s.

**Bungie Details Upcoming Recon Buff in Marathon Update**

Bungie revealed details about an upcoming balance update for the Recon character in *Marathon*, scheduled for April 14. Recon, previously considered one of the weaker characters, will get improvements to its Echo Pulse ability, enabling it to distinguish between AI-controlled enemies and human players while making the ability less detectable to opponents. Additionally, its tracker drone will be enhanced to select new targets if blocked, addressing prior issues with inefficient targeting and unintended self-damage. These changes aim to make Recon a more viable option in Bungie’s extraction shooter, with other character updates expected soon.

**Take-Two Lays Off AI Team Including Head of Artificial Intelligence**

Take-Two Interactive has laid off an unspecified portion of its artificial intelligence team, including Head of AI Luke Dicken, just months after public statements about embracing generative AI technology.
