The Last Caretaker Update Adds Laser-Equipped Sharks and New Companions

The survival crafting game *The Last Caretaker* released its third major update, "Curiosity Wakes," introducing sharks with laser beams attached to their heads, alongside two other aggressive shark types: ram sharks and archangels. This addition underscores the game’s unpredictable tone, mixing serious sci-fi elements with quirky, almost comedic design choices.

Beyond hostile sea creatures, the update adds deployable NPC companions—a turret named "Sweetheart" and swarms of spider-like bots—that assist players in combat situations. Other features include new robot customization options, additional quests and locations, and performance improvements. Players can also now interact remotely with vatgrown humans launched into space via the in-game Council of Humanity, though the specific gameplay effects of this remain unspecified.

Since its alpha launch in November 2025, *The Last Caretaker* has steadily introduced features such as jet skis, new biomes, a forklift, and a compost system, maintaining steady development. An upgraded demo exploring more starting areas was recently released on Steam, offering players a broader initial experience before purchasing.

Blight: Survival Developer Explains Long Delay, Focuses on Rebuilding Core Systems

The medieval hack-and-slash *Blight: Survival* has reached over 1.5 million Steam wishlists but remains without a release date. Its developer, Haenir Studio, revealed that significant delays are due to a decision to rebuild the game’s core combat systems from the ground up, aiming to deliver a more physical and impactful combat experience.

Partnered with Behaviour Interactive, known for *Dead by Daylight*, the studio prioritized refining melee combat and adding immersive finishers. Recent updates also teased new environments like marshlands and special zombie encounters, keeping community interest alive.

While the release date is not set, the promise of deeper combat mechanics places *Blight* among notable medieval swordfighting games, alongside titles like *Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2*, *Half-Sword*, and *Chivalry 2*.

Death Stranding 2 PC Launch Introduces Tougher "To the Wilder" Difficulty Mode

With the PC launch of *Death Stranding 2*, Kojima Productions added a new difficulty level named "To the Wilder," designed in response to player feedback that existing difficulty settings were too easy. This mode increases environmental hazards, particularly intensifying effects like Timefall, the in-game rain that ages and degrades equipment.

Lead level designer Hiroaki Yoshiike noted that this mode demands careful mission planning and strategic use of a wider range of equipment, making the game more challenging even for experienced players. The developers have tested this difficulty thoroughly and emphasize the importance of social features like the Strand Agreements system to help players navigate tougher conditions.

This update aims to provide a more realistic and demanding gameplay experience, extending appeal to players seeking greater challenge beyond the standard campaign.

Crimson Desert's Controls Draw Criticism; Developer Compares Learning Curve to Riding a Bike

*Crimson Desert* has seen mixed user reviews on Steam, with frequent complaints about its complex and unintuitive control scheme. Critics and players report frustration with the controls, describing them as convoluted and difficult to master, particularly when using keyboard and mouse.

Pearl Abyss’s PR and marketing director, Will Powers, responded by suggesting that the controls feel natural once learned, comparing it to riding a bike. He acknowledged the controls’ complexity but encouraged players to persist through the initial learning curve.

The game sold over two million copies within 24 hours but struggles with user ratings impacted partly by the control issues. Pearl Abyss has stated it will consider community feedback for upcoming improvements.

Minecraft Movie Sequel Casts Kirsten Dunst as Alex

The upcoming *Minecraft Movie* sequel has cast Kirsten Dunst as the player avatar Alex, aligning with the character’s red hair and female identity. The film, scheduled for release in 2027 by Warner Bros. and Legendary, will also bring back several actors from the first movie, including Jack Black and Jennifer Coolidge.

Dunst expressed interest in joining the project because of her children’s appreciation for the game and the financial stability the film offers. The first movie grossed over $1 billion worldwide despite mixed critical reception, making this sequel a financially safe bet for the actress.
