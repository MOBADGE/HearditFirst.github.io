**Masters of Albion: A Return to Classic Peter Molyneux Style**

Peter Molyneux and 22cans are developing *Masters of Albion*, a game drawing heavily from Molyneux’s earlier works like *Fable*, *Black & White*, and *Dungeon Keeper*. Launching in Steam Early Access on April 22, the game combines city-building, hero exploration, and defense mechanics. Players act as a god-like "sky hand," managing towns, crafting goods, and controlling a hero to expand influence. A notable feature is the design minigame, allowing players to customize recipes and equipment. However, early impressions suggest that while it captures the feel of Molyneux’s older games, none of its parts fully match the impact or quality of the originals. The modular design approach aims to let players engage at their chosen depth, but this may limit the game’s depth and cohesion.

**Intel Core Ultra 7 270K Plus: A Significant Arrow Lake Refresh**

Intel has introduced the Core Ultra 7 270K Plus, an upgraded processor in its Arrow Lake Refresh line. Unlike minor speed bumps of past refreshes, this chip features eight performance cores and sixteen efficiency cores, matching the flagship Ultra 9 285K in core count but with slightly higher clock speeds. Internal clock speeds for inter-tile communication and memory support have been increased, allowing DDR5-7200 RAM without overclocking. Benchmarks show the 270K Plus offers improved gaming and content creation performance compared to older Arrow Lake chips and competes well against AMD’s mid-to-high-end Ryzen offerings. However, it consumes more power and runs hotter under load, possibly requiring stronger cooling solutions. At a $299 price point, it presents a strong option for users valuing balanced performance, though the upcoming change in Intel sockets may affect upgrade paths.

**Intel Core Ultra 5 250K Plus: Affordable Performance Boost**

Alongside the 270K Plus, Intel released the Core Ultra 5 250K Plus, priced at $199. This chip brings additional efficiency cores and several internal clock improvements over its predecessor, the 245K. It supports DDR5-7200 memory and benefits from architectural refinements resulting in better multithreaded and single-core performance. Gaming benchmarks place its performance close to the Core Ultra 7 265K, and it surpasses comparable AMD processors at this price range, especially in content creation tasks. Power consumption is modest, and a basic air cooler suffices for thermal management. Its competitive pricing and good all-around performance make it attractive for budget-conscious builders, though as with the 270K Plus, future socket compatibility may be limited.

**Cat Parents Reaches 100,000 Wishlists in Three Days**

Indie game *Cat Parents*, a cozy simulator where players care for and manage stray cats, has unexpectedly gained rapid popularity, amassing over 100,000 Steam wishlists within three days of announcement. Developed by Gaze In Games, the title focuses on feeding, cleaning, and customizing a home for a growing collection of cats. The strong early interest is notable for a small indie title and demonstrates a continued player appetite for relaxed, nurturing simulations. While release details remain unconfirmed, the game's success offers encouragement for the developer and highlights the market potential of cozy, animal-centered games.

**Intel VP Hints at Longer Socket Lifespans**

Intel’s Enthusiast Channel VP, Robert Hallock, has indicated that future Intel CPU sockets may support more generations than in the past. Traditionally, Intel has introduced new sockets with nearly every CPU generation, requiring new motherboards for upgrades. Hallock’s comments suggest a shift toward longer-lasting platforms, aligning more with AMD’s strategy of multi-generation socket support. This change could affect upcoming platforms like Intel's LGA 1954 socket for Nova Lake CPUs. Should this approach be realized, it may improve upgrade flexibility for Intel users and impact the competitive landscape for PC building.
