Phoenix Point Updates Original Game Inspired by Fan-Made Mod

Snapshot Games has released a significant patch for Phoenix Point, a tactical strategy game originally created by Julian Gollop. This update, named the Firebird update, incorporates numerous changes inspired by the popular fan-made "Terror from the Void" mod. The mod is known for overhauling the game's story, progression, and mechanics, adding features such as delirium infections and altered enemy evolution. The patch addresses pacing issues in the original by reducing research and construction times, easing ammo production, and making base defense missions less punishing. Notably, it also removes the controversial mechanic where the game's aquatic enemies, Pandorans, gain power by losing battles, a long-standing source of player frustration. The update is fully compatible with Terror from the Void, allowing both new and ongoing campaigns to benefit. To mark the game's fifth anniversary and the patch release, Phoenix Point is discounted by 60% through December 18.

Developer Promises Fate of the Old Republic Release Before 2030

Casey Hudson, co-founder of Arcanaut Studios and director of the upcoming Star Wars RPG Fate of the Old Republic (FotOR), has shared on Twitter that the game will be released within the next five years, pushing back against skepticism regarding the timeline. The studio was only recently formed in 2025, leading some industry observers to consider a 2030 release date optimistic. Hudson highlighted that he is motivated to complete the game in a relatively short development cycle, adding a personal note about age reflecting his desire to see the project completed. While this is an ambitious timeline for a large-scale RPG project, it offers an updated perspective on the game's progress. FotOR is highly anticipated as a follow-up to the classic Knights of the Old Republic.

Early Access Launch for 1990s Style 3D Platformer Eternity Egg

Eternity Egg, a 3D platformer inspired by 1990s computer graphics, has entered early access following five years of solitary development by its creator known as Userlands. The game features an abstract dreamscape aesthetic and emphasizes high-mobility platforming mechanics such as slide jumps, wall bounces, and pogo hops. While still in development and containing bugs, the game has attracted positive attention for its unusual art direction and technical movement systems. It is available on Steam with a temporary discount through December 24, ahead of a full release planned for 2026.
