Starsand Island Pulled from Steam over Unauthorized Visual Content

The cozy farming simulation *Starsand Island*, praised by PC Gamer as a "can't-miss cozy game of 2026," has been temporarily removed from Steam. Developer Seed Sparkle Lab announced that some core visual elements used in a mini-game section were sourced from a classic title without proper authorization. While the studio did not name the title, the community suspects elements resembling Tetris. The game has been delisted while a revised version without the offending content awaits approval. Once reinstated, existing owners can update without loss of progress and will receive a compensation outfit. This follows earlier unusual activity where the game was targeted with fake positive reviews after its launch.

Esoteric Ebb Creator Embraces Players Missing Half the Content

Christoffer Bodegård, creator of *Esoteric Ebb*, an RPG contender for 2026’s top honors, shared insights indicating many players likely only experience about 40–60% of the game’s content on a single playthrough. Bodegård considers this design intentional, to foster a sense of a larger, richer world that encourages exploration and discussion among players. Players can 100% complete the game in one run, but given the extensive content and subtle secrets, missing parts lends the game depth and replayability. This approach contrasts with strictly linear storytelling, valuing player discovery over exhaustive coverage.

Amazon Luna Ends Support for Third-Party Game Purchases and Streaming

Amazon announced that its Luna cloud gaming service will cease allowing users to purchase games from third-party stores such as EA, Ubisoft, and GOG. Additionally, subscriptions like Ubisoft+ and Jackbox Games sold via Luna will no longer be available. Streaming access to previously purchased third-party titles will end on June 10, 2026. Users can still access these games through their original stores. The "Bring Your Own Library" feature enabling streaming of third-party games will close on June 3. Amazon cites player feedback preferring easier access to popular games and social experiences, though the change reduces Luna’s catalog. This signals a contraction of Amazon's game service offerings and a move toward subscription-based libraries like Luna Premium.

PC RAM Prices Show Slight Decline after Massive Spike

Spot prices for RAM modules have fallen about 5% recently, following a steep increase of over 2000% in the past year caused largely by AI industry demand for memory and storage. For instance, 16 GB DDR4 sticks that cost around $3.20 in March 2025 reached almost $74 in the current market. DDR5 memory prices have also dropped but remain significantly elevated compared to previous years, with no expected return to affordable levels until at least 2028. Manufacturers are expanding production, but the supply-demand imbalance persists. This ongoing memory price crisis impacts PC builders and gamers, increasing upgrade costs and slowing typical build cycles.

16 GB of System Memory Remains Adequate for Most PC Gaming in 2026

A recent PC Gamer analysis tested several modern, demanding games to assess performance differences between 16 GB and 32 GB of system memory. Using high-end GPUs such as the RTX 5090 and a more mainstream RTX 5070, results showed most games run similarly well on 16 GB, with only a few exceptions. Games like *Cyberpunk 2077*, *Black Myth: Wukong*, and *Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2* showed negligible differences. Larger open-world titles streaming substantial data, such as *Stalker 2* and *Escape from Tarkov*, demonstrated some benefit from 32 GB, especially in frame rate stability. Notably, heavy titles like *Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024* and *Star Citizen* may require more memory or benefit from tuning settings. The main caveat: running other applications alongside games (e.g., streaming or background programs) may make 16 GB insufficient. Given steep RAM prices, a balanced approach focusing on sufficient (not excessive) memory while prioritizing GPU power is advisable.
