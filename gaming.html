Bite-sized summaries of the latest gaming trends — without the hype.

Updated: March 16, 2026

**Schedule 1 Beta Branch Now Available for Early Update Access** The developers of the drug-dealing simulator Schedule 1, TVGS, have made a beta branch available on Steam for players eager to try upcoming updates before their public release. Accessing this beta build requires navigating to the game's properties in Steam and selecting "beta" from the game versions dropdown. Players are advised to back up their save files using the game's export feature before switching to the beta, as test branches can be unstable. This early access option allows avid fans to preview new features and changes ahead of the main release but requires coordination among players to ensure matching game versions for multiplayer.

**Resident Evil Requiem Actor Shares Challenging Audition Experience** Angela Sant'Albano, who voices Grace Ashcroft in Resident Evil Requiem, recently described the demanding nature of her audition process. She had to perform a prolonged, two-to-three-minute "slow burn" death scene with real-time direction changes from the casting director, such as simulating a zombie bite during her demise. This intense vocal performance highlights the unique demands placed on voice actors, especially in motion-capture-heavy games like Requiem. The game itself continues to succeed, having sold six million copies faster than any previous title in the Resident Evil series.

**Nvidia Reveals DLSS 5 at GTC, Emphasizes AI-Driven Rendering** At Nvidia's GPU Technology Conference keynote, CEO Jen-Hsun Huang unveiled DLSS 5, the next iteration of the company's AI-based upscaling technology, scheduled for release this fall. Early demonstrations show DLSS 5 functioning more like an AI-powered lighting and material enhancement filter rather than a traditional performance booster. The system uses neural rendering to infuse photorealistic lighting effects into games such as Resident Evil Requiem and Oblivion Remastered. While DLSS 5’s hardware demands appear significant in current demos, the final product is expected to be less demanding. The adoption of DLSS 5 by major publishers and games suggests it will gain traction, although its transformative impact on game visuals and performance remains to be seen upon release.

**MSI Plans Significant Price Increases Amid Memory Market Challenges** MSI, a prominent PC hardware manufacturer, has announced plans to raise prices of its gaming products by 15-30% due to ongoing supply shortages and rising costs in the memory market, which have affected the broader PC industry. The company's leadership describes 2026 as its most challenging year since its founding. MSI intends to focus more on high-end products, introduce DDR5 motherboards compatible with DDR4 memory to ease supply issues, and increase revenue from AI server markets. These efforts echo similar strategies from competitors like Asus. Consumers may see higher prices and shifts towards more premium PC components as manufacturers adjust to current market pressures.

**Legal Ruling Reinstates Unknown Worlds CEO Amid Subnautica 2 Dispute** A judge has ruled that Krafton must reinstate Ted Gill as CEO of Unknown Worlds, the studio behind Subnautica 2, and restore his control over the game's early access release schedule. This decision comes nine months after Krafton terminated key executives following disputes over compensation tied to the game's sales targets. The court found Krafton had breached contract terms by acting without valid cause, interpreting the terminations as an attempt to avoid paying a $250 million earnout. The ruling extends the earnout period and mandates that Krafton cannot interfere with Gill’s authority. Krafton has expressed disagreement with the ruling and is considering next steps, but the legal battle remains ongoing.

**Digg Faces Shutdown and Layoffs Shortly After Relaunch** Digg, the once-popular social news aggregator, has announced significant layoffs and a shutdown just two months after its January relaunch under original founder Kevin Rose and Reddit co-founder Alexis Ohanian. CEO Justin Mezzell attributed the failure to challenges from AI-generated content flooding the internet, making it hard for Digg to maintain quality and relevance. Additionally, strong user loyalty to existing platforms has hindered user migration. Despite these setbacks, a small team will continue working to rebuild Digg from a new perspective, though specific plans or timelines remain unspecified. The relaunch and subsequent shutdown reflect difficulties veteran platforms face adapting to rapidly evolving internet and content dynamics.
