Intel Unveils Core Ultra Series 3 Mobile Chips with Improved Integrated Graphics

Intel officially launched its Panther Lake-based Core Ultra Series 3 mobile processors, highlighting up to a 77% increase in integrated GPU (iGPU) gaming performance compared to the previous Lunar Lake generation. Key models such as the Core Ultra X9 388H feature the new Intel Arc B390 GPU with 12 Xe3 cores and built-in ray tracing. Intel also addressed past criticisms about its GPU driver support by overhauling its software stack, expanding testing to hundreds of games, and collaborating closely with developers from early stages. While the new chips promise stronger performance for slim, power-efficient laptops and handhelds, Intel‚Äôs ability to sustain reliable driver support will significantly impact whether gamers adopt these new processors as primary gaming devices.

Nvidia Details DLSS 4.5 and Multi Frame Generation Enhancements

Nvidia announced upgrades to its DLSS technology with version 4.5, promising support for 4K gaming at 240 Hz with path tracing enabled, utilizing an improved transformer AI model. Central to this is an expanded Multi Frame Generation (MFG) feature capable of generating up to six intermediary frames between rendered ones to deliver smoother visuals. Nvidia claims the new AI model reduces artifacts such as ghosting and flickering and that DLSS 4.5 will be available to all RTX GPUs, not just the latest RTX 50-series. However, achieving smooth gameplay still requires substantial GPU power and low latency. Nvidia is also introducing dynamic MFG control via its app, adjusting frame generation in real time to balance performance and responsiveness depending on user settings and hardware capabilities.

Alienware Expands Laptop Range and Adds AMD Ryzen to Area-51 Desktop

Alienware introduced several new hardware offerings at CES 2026. Among these is a new ultra-slim gaming laptop design around 17mm thick, aimed at users wanting a discreet yet capable gaming machine. However, details such as GPU options and thermal design power remain unconfirmed. In addition, Alienware unveiled entry-level gaming laptops intended to broaden its market reach by offering more affordable options without compromising on core features like build quality and thermals. Another significant update is the addition of AMD Ryzen X3D processors to the high-end Area-51 gaming desktop lineup, addressing previous criticism that the desktop was Intel-only. Models including the Ryzen 7 9850X3D now provide customers with AMD choices alongside Nvidia GPU configurations.

AMD Adds New Chips to Ryzen AI Max Lineup

AMD extended its Ryzen AI Max range with two new processors: the Ryzen AI Max+ 392 and Max+ 388. The Max+ 388, featuring a single core chiplet and a full complement of 40 RDNA 3.5 compute units, stands out as a strong candidate for high-end handheld gaming PCs, offering better compatibility with games that can have issues on dual chiplet designs. These chips continue to target devices needing a balance of CPU and GPU performance without a discrete graphics card. Additionally, AMD refreshed its Ryzen 7 series with the 9850X3D, a modestly faster variant of the 9800X3D, featuring an 8% higher boost clock. While incremental, these updates give AMD options across different segments, especially in portable and handheld gaming hardware.
