Crimson Desert Plans Major Updates to Address Player Feedback

Pearl Abyss is rolling out several updates for its recently launched open-world action RPG *Crimson Desert*. Responding to player complaints about enemy scarcity in late-game zones, the developer will implement "re-blockading," where previously cleared enemy camps are reclaimed, ensuring a steady supply of combat challenges. Players will also gain the ability to rematch bosses, although without new rewards, focusing on skill improvement.

Importantly, the game will introduce difficulty settings (easy, normal, hard) to let players tailor combat challenge. Additionally, the other playable characters, Damaine and Oongka, will receive ability upgrades to better match the main character’s combat capabilities. Improvements extend to UI and item management with new storage categories for food, gear, gatherables, and collections.

This comprehensive patch series, releasing gradually from April to June, aims to strengthen core gameplay and usability. It reflects Pearl Abyss's quick response to feedback, helping stabilize player engagement after a mixed reception at launch.

CPUID’s Website Compromised, Malware Distributed via Popular Tools

The official download page for CPUID’s widely used system monitoring tools, CPU-Z and HWMonitor, has been hacked to distribute malware. Users attempting to download these programs from the official site receive malicious files disguised with misleading names and Russian-language installation screens. Antivirus programs immediately flag these files, which are not authentic.

Security researchers identified the malware as a sophisticated trojan that operates primarily in memory and employs advanced evasion techniques. The same hacking group previously compromised FileZilla’s site, suggesting an ongoing campaign targeting popular PC utilities.

Users are advised to avoid downloading CPUID software directly from the compromised site for now and rely on reputable antivirus solutions. Verifying file signatures by comparing downloads from multiple sources is also recommended until the issue is resolved.

James Bond Rights Holders Oppose James Pond Trademark Application

Danjaq LLC, the rights holder of *James Bond*, has filed an opposition against a recent UK trademark application by Gameware and System 3 for *James Pond*, a 1990s platformer parody series. The trademark sought to cover various categories, including games and clothing, but Danjaq previously blocked a similar European trademark attempt in 2012.

Since UK trademark law does not protect parody as U.S. law does, experts view this opposition as likely successful. Gameware and System 3 plan to relaunch the *James Pond* series with a new game, *James Pond and the Rogue AI*, though the original creator has publicly criticized the current direction and promotion of the sequel.

This legal dispute illustrates ongoing tensions between intellectual property holders and those seeking to revive or leverage legacy game properties.

Nexon Reorganizes Neople Team as *The First Berserker: Khazan* Development Winds Down

Nexon confirmed an internal reorganization at Neople, the studio behind *The First Berserker: Khazan*, which was reported to be driven by disappointing sales. However, Nexon clarified the move reflects a strategic reallocation of staff to other projects rather than any layoffs or dissolution of the development team.

*The First Berserker: Khazan*, a single-player action RPG and part of the *Dungeon & Fighter* franchise, launched strongly and holds a "very positive" Steam rating. With development entering its final phase, Neople is shifting resources toward ongoing and future *Dungeon & Fighter* titles aimed at global markets.

While this suggests reduced post-launch support for *Khazan*, it maintains Nexon's commitment to the franchise and its expansion.

*Darkstone* Action RPG Remastered by Original Creator

Paul Cuisset, creator of the 1999 action RPG *Darkstone*, is leading a remaster titled *Darkstone Restoration*. The original game is noted for its dual-character system, randomized quests, and mechanics like aging and hunger management.

The remaster will focus on enhancing stability, compatibility, UI readability, and controls, with potential additions such as improved networking and a quest editor planned depending on Early Access reception. *Darkstone Restoration* is set to launch in Early Access, though an exact release date is not yet announced.

This revival seeks to make the classic more accessible to modern players while preserving its unique gameplay features.
