Look Outside Receives Balance Update

The indie cosmic horror RPG *Look Outside* has released version 2.3, bringing numerous balance changes and bug fixes. Notably, the "Rat Child’s Copy Dad ability" no longer copies gun skills for free, impacting gameplay strategy. Other tweaks include adjustments to enemy abilities such as Col Squeakums' bites and Monty’s pyromania skill, which now deals less damage to player characters. The update also improves various enemy types and introduces minor refinements throughout the game.

This continuous support is notable given *Look Outside*’s indie status and niche genre, reflecting developer commitment to refining gameplay. The game blends elements from classics like *Yume Nikki* and *World of Horror* and remains available on Steam and GOG, maintaining interest among fans of experimental RPGs.

Cave Story+ Updated with Co-op and Mod Support After 22 Years

The beloved indie metroidvania *Cave Story+* received a major update on PC, aligning it with the Nintendo Switch version released nine years ago. The long-awaited update adds local two-player co-op, widescreen support, modding capabilities, visual improvements including animated dialogue portraits, and a new challenge level called Sand Pit.

While co-op in a precise platformer like *Cave Story* is an unconventional addition, early player feedback has been positive. Mod support is currently limited to cosmetic changes such as graphics packs and sound modifications, without enabling user-created levels.

This patch is significant for preserving the legacy of *Cave Story*, a foundational title in indie gaming that demonstrated the potential for solo developers to make impactful games. PC players now have access to features that have been exclusive to other platforms for nearly a decade.

Windrose Developers Seek ISP Expertise Amid Connectivity Problems

Following the early access release of *Windrose*, a PvE-focused co-op survival game, the developer Kraken Express has asked players for assistance in diagnosing online co-op connectivity issues. Despite a smooth launch in terms of player numbers and positive reviews, users have reported lag spikes and difficulty connecting.

The developers specifically requested introductions to contacts at major ISPs in Europe and North America to help investigate what might be causing network disruptions. Part of the confusion arises from the dedicated server infrastructure requesting registration from a Russian endpoint, which has raised concern among some players given the developer is based in Uzbekistan.

Kraken Express acknowledges ongoing efforts to resolve these problems and plans multiple hotfixes. However, the lack of traditional admin commands and the unusual server behaviors suggest areas where the dedicated server setup could be improved for a more stable co-op experience.

Take-Two Share Price Rises After Rockstar Data Leak, But Context Matters

Take-Two Interactive's stock saw a modest increase following the public release of stolen Rockstar Games data after hackers' failed ransom demand. While the leak confirmed Rockstar’s financial strength—particularly its substantial ongoing revenue from GTA Online—it did not include significant information about the anticipated *GTA 6*.

The stock climbed from around $201 to over $214 within two days. Nevertheless, the gain should be understood in context: Take-Two’s share price remains down more than 16% since the start of the year and the recent rise was influenced by multiple factors.

Investors await the company’s upcoming quarterly financial report in early May, which will offer more insight into Take-Two’s performance. The long-term impact of the data leak appears limited, and further stock movement is likely to depend on earnings and broader market conditions rather than the hack.

Marvel Rivals Introduces Black Cat with Controversial Abilities

The upcoming Season 7.5 update for the hero shooter *Marvel Rivals* will add Black Cat, a new Duelist with a diverse and potent ability set. Alongside standard features like a melee attack and double jump, Black Cat utilizes a "Fortune" resource accrued by dealing damage or stealing it from opponents. This resource can be spent on activating abilities or purchasing powerful artifacts granting effects such as invisibility, portal creation, area damage, and temporary immunity to crowd control.

Community commentary highlights concerns over Black Cat’s kit, with comparisons to overpowered characters like Thanos due to her range of mobility, stealth, and control options. Players familiar with *Marvel Rivals*’ history of challenging balance will likely watch for adjustments in future updates, as previous heroes with similarly strong kits have experienced delayed nerfs.
