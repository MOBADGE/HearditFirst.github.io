Nvidia Denies Acquisition Talks Amid Market Speculation

Nvidia has refuted recent reports claiming it is negotiating to acquire a major PC-oriented company. This followed a paywalled report by SemiAccurate suggesting such a purchase could "fundamentally change the computing landscape." Despite Nvidia’s clear denial, share prices for PC manufacturers Dell, HP, Lenovo, and Asus rose by up to 4% on April 13, driven by speculation over a potential deal. The market has since cooled down.

Nvidia’s spokesperson told Bloomberg that the report was false and no talks were underway. Still, Nvidia’s broader strategy shows its intention to expand its footprint in the datacenter and client computing markets. The company is actively developing the Arm-based N1X chip, intended for PCs, and has partnered with Intel on future CPU-GPU products. These moves suggest Nvidia may prefer partnerships to outright acquisitions in the near term.

Windrose Launches in Early Access as a Co-op Pirate Survival Game

Windrose, a cooperative open-world pirate survival game, officially launched in early access today after gaining popularity through its demo at February’s Next Fest. The game involves resource gathering, ship repair, base building, and ship-to-ship combat, with an emphasis on cooperative play against AI enemies like pirates, zombies, and animals.

Key gameplay systems include crafting, base-building, and customizable difficulty settings. There is no PvP; multiplayer consists solely of online co-op. While survival mechanics like hunger provide attribute buffs, there are no severe penalties for starvation or temperature, and illness/injury mechanics are minimal. Some features such as fishing are not fully implemented yet.

Ship combat is highlighted as a fast, accessible experience reminiscent of Assassin’s Creed: Black Flag, while melee combat is more traditional and slower-paced. The game offers solid content despite some performance hiccups. Windrose plans to remain in early access for one to three years, and current pricing of $30 is reasonable given the content available. It may appeal most to groups of friends seeking cooperative naval battles and exploration.

Bloodborne Receives R-Rated Animated Film Adaptation with JackSepticEye

Sony announced an R-rated animated film adaptation of the game Bloodborne, to be co-produced by popular YouTuber JackSepticEye. The news came from Sony Pictures Entertainment’s president Sanford Panitch at CinemaCon in Las Vegas. The film aims to retain the intense, violent tone of the game.

JackSepticEye, who has over 30 million subscribers and is known for a popular Bloodborne playthrough, will collaborate with Sony Productions and animation studio Lyrical Animation on the project. The film forms part of Sony’s wider strategy to adapt its game properties into movies and television, alongside titles like The Last of Us, God of War, and The Legend of Zelda.

No release date was provided. The announcement coincides with continued uncertainty over a PC version of Bloodborne, as Sony appears to have moved away from porting key exclusives to other platforms.

Xbox Game Pass Could See Changes Following Price Concerns

The new head of Xbox, Asha Sharma, reportedly described Xbox Game Pass as "too expensive" in an internal memo leaked to the media. She indicated that the current subscription model needs rethinking to offer better value to players. Sharma suggested that while Game Pass is central to Xbox’s gaming ecosystem, it will be evolved into a more flexible system over time.

This comes six months after a significant price increase, which saw the Ultimate tier grow by 50% to $29.99 monthly and the PC tier rise as well. The current tiered structure has complicated what content is available and at what price, leading to criticism from both players and developers about diminished value and revenue impact.

Xbox appears poised to reassess Game Pass’s pricing and structure as part of a broader shift to focus again on console hardware and next-generation projects.
