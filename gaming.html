Bauhutte Unveils Electric Gaming Bed 2 for Relaxed Play

Japanese gaming furniture company Bauhutte has released the Electric Gaming Bed 2, an upgraded version of its specialized gaming bed designed for playing while lying down. Featuring motorized sections to independently elevate the upper body and legs, the bed transitions users quickly from rest to gaming mode with the push of a button. A notable addition is the option to split the bed into three parts for easier transport, along with a retention bar to prevent the mattress from slipping during adjustments.

While such beds may offer mobility benefits, Bauhutte’s marketing targets gamers seeking comfort and convenience in dedicated gaming setups. The product is priced at around $450 in Japan, with an optional matching gaming mattress sold separately. International availability remains uncertain. This product illustrates niche hardware development focused on enhancing gaming comfort in non-traditional ways.

Composer Inon Zur Defends Starfield, Praises Todd Howard

In a recent interview, Inon Zur, composer for Bethesda’s Starfield and previous Fallout titles, spoke highly of Todd Howard’s creative vision behind the game. Zur described Howard as a strong visionary with ideas that people may only appreciate years after release. Zur expressed confidence that Starfield will eventually be regarded as a "legendary" title, despite its mixed reception since its 2023 launch.

The game has faced varied opinions, with some players and critics finding it uninspiring compared to Bethesda’s earlier successes. Zur also detailed his approach to the Starfield soundtrack, aiming to convey both the vastness of space and the significance of the individual player. This interview offers insight into the developer perspective amid ongoing debates about Starfield’s standing in Bethesda’s catalog.

CD Projekt Clarifies No Planned Cyberpunk 2077 DLC Amid Witcher 3 Expansion Rumors

Amid circulating rumors about new downloadable content, CD Projekt has officially stated it has no plans for additional DLCs or expansions for Cyberpunk 2077. This declaration was made in response to an inquiry on social media, with the company adding that any future changes will be communicated publicly.

The statement comes as speculation intensifies regarding possible secret development of Witcher 3 expansions, though CD Projekt has not confirmed such projects. The company’s reply has fueled further fan theories that while Cyberpunk 2077 content is unlikely, Witcher 3 might still see new updates. CD Projekt’s next earnings report, due shortly, may provide further clarity on its ongoing projects.

Arc Raiders Considers Vehicles but Offers No Concrete Timeline

Embark Studios’ extraction shooter Arc Raiders may include vehicles in the future, according to production director Caio Braga, though no development is currently underway. Braga mentioned that vehicle prototypes dating back seven years exist but have not been revisited recently. The team remains open to adding new features that align with the game’s lore and experience.

This flexibility reflects Embark’s long-term plans to support Arc Raiders for many years. Introducing vehicles could distinguish the game in a genre traditionally focused on on-foot engagements. However, vehicles have generally become less common in recent multiplayer shooters, and any addition would need to fit gameplay style and balance.

Palworld’s John Buckley Flooded with Messages About Pokémon-Like Pickmon

John "Bucky" Buckley, Palworld’s Head of Publishing & Communications, revealed that upon landing after a flight to GDC 2026, he was met with approximately 8,000 Discord messages from fans alerting him to a new creature-collecting game called Pickmon. This title, currently little known outside of its debut trailer, has drawn attention for its resemblance to Pokémon and Palworld.

Buckley admitted he had not had a chance to investigate Pickmon thoroughly but acknowledged the community’s vigilance in keeping him informed. The game’s developer and background remain unclear, and Nintendo has yet to comment despite its history of protecting its Pokémon IP. This situation underscores fan engagement and the challenges of differentiating games within popular genres.

Initially not designed as a live service, the title evolved over time with added features like in-game stores and battle passes to sustain player interest.
