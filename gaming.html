Ashes of Creation Early Access Launches December 11 on Steam

The long-anticipated fantasy MMORPG *Ashes of Creation* will begin its early access phase at 10 a.m. PST on Thursday, December 11, 2025. This marks the release of its alpha build on Steam, replacing the previous standalone launcher. The early access launch is global and available simultaneously across time zones with no staggered schedule. Notably, there is no preload option, so players need to download the game once servers go live.

Players should temper expectations as this is an unfinished product; the game is still in its second alpha stage and plans two beta phases before reaching version 1.0. Progression may not be permanent, with possible wipes occurring during the development stages. Returning players must link their Intrepid accounts to Steam to continue playing, but all previous launchers are being retired.

This early access launch is an important milestone for *Ashes of Creation*, which has been in development and anticipation since its Kickstarter success in 2017. Its community-driven civilization mechanics offer an old-school PvX MMORPG experience.

John Romero Confirms Survival and Redesign of Cancelled Shooter Project

Legendary developer John Romero announced that his studio, Romero Games, survived the cancellation of a large, multi-year shooter project that employed about 110 people. Despite losing publisher funding, presumably from Microsoft, the team retooled the project into a smaller, redesigned game.

Romero described the new game as innovative within the shooter genre, comparing its novelty to the experience of playing *Elden Ring*. While maintaining certain assets from the original project, the new game will be developed by a smaller team taking a more hands-on approach.

This development highlights the challenges mid-sized studios face with large-scale funding and the flexibility required to pivot toward sustainable projects.

The Temple of Elemental Evil Re-Released on Steam With Integrated Fan Fixes

SNEG has released *The Temple of Elemental Evil* on Steam, notable for including over a thousand fan-created patches and quality-of-life improvements integrated into the base game.
