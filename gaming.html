Low-Poly Oil Pastel Art Brings Video Game Characters to Life

Artist Lasher, based in the UK, has gained attention for creating low-poly style paintings of video game characters using oil pastels. Unlike the predominantly digital low-poly art trend, Lasher's work involves meticulous blending and manipulation of pastels to replicate the geometric simplicity of classic 3D models while adding tactile texture. Highlighted characters include the Chao from "Sonic Adventure," Katamari cats, and the Katamari Prince. The approach offers a unique, physical representation of beloved gaming icons, renewing interest in traditional media such as oil pastels and demonstrating creativity in adapting digital aesthetics to physical art forms.

Fractal Design Scape Headset Blends Practicality with Refined Design

The Fractal Design Scape wireless gaming headset stands out in the 2025 hardware landscape for its combination of thoughtful design and user-friendly features. Sporting a minimalist aesthetic with matte black finishes and subtle RGB lighting, the Scape incorporates a removable, flip-to-mute microphone and breathable earcup fabrics. Its Qi wireless charging stand magnetically secures the headset, encouraging consistent charging without the inconvenience of cables or docks. Audio quality is balanced and detailed, with adjustable equalizer settings. Some drawbacks include the lack of active noise cancellation and a premium price point of around $200. Overall, the Scape offers an elegant and convenient option for gamers prioritizing both form and function.
