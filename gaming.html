**Remaster of 1995 3DO Shooter PO’ed Highlights Early Console FPS Attempts**

Nightdive Studios has released a remastered version of PO’ed, a little-known 1995 first-person shooter originally for the 3DO console. PO’ed follows an alien-infested spaceship setting and includes quirky weapons like frying pans and a jetpack mechanic with six degrees of freedom. Despite its ambitious ideas and humor, the game was flawed by poor controls, confusing level design, and awkward audio-visual presentation. The remaster also brings PO’ed to PC for the first time, offering a rare look at early console FPS efforts before Halo: Combat Evolved broke PC dominance in the genre. While few will find the gameplay enjoyable today, the preservation effort reveals the stumbles in adapting first-person shooters from PC to consoles in the mid-1990s.

**Indie Pass Subscription Service Receives Mixed to Negative Feedback**

A new subscription service called Indie Pass will launch on April 13, offering access to 70 indie games for $6.99 per month. The service is backed by publisher indie.io and shares developer revenue based on the amount of time subscribers spend playing each game. While the concept is a cheaper, curated alternative to larger platforms like Xbox Game Pass, the reception from players and developers has been largely cautious or negative. Critics argue that revenue tied to engagement time may disincentivize games designed for shorter or more varied play sessions and question the value of subscription over direct purchases for already inexpensive indie titles. Indie Pass also requires users to install a new launcher, another point of concern. Developer reactions range from skepticism to dismissive jokes, indicating potential challenges for Indie Pass in gaining traction.

**ScummVM Expands Support for Obscure and Quirky Older Games**

The popular compatibility tool ScummVM, known for running classic adventure titles on modern systems, has added support for two full-motion video shooters—Crime Patrol and Crime Patrol 2: Drug Wars—as well as the Lovecraftian point-and-click adventure Necronomicon: The Dawning of Darkness. These titles, dating from the 1990s and early 2000s, are notable for their campy or low-quality production values and limited commercial success. Support through ScummVM enables players to revisit or discover these obscure games on contemporary platforms. While the gameplay and narrative in these newly supported titles have received generally poor marks, such as a YouTuber’s 2/10 rating for Necronomicon, their preservation meets demand for experiencing gaming history broadly, including its less polished corners.

**Skyblivion Lead Remains Hopeful Amid Potential Delays**

Skyblivion, a fan-made total conversion mod that aims to remake The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion using Skyrim’s engine, faces ongoing development challenges. Originally targeting a 2025 release, it was delayed to 2026, with recent calls for additional modders to help polish the project raising concerns about further postponements. Project lead Rebelzize expresses cautious optimism, noting good momentum, completion of quest locations, and significant progress on the previously problematic Imperial City area. He acknowledges risks of setbacks but emphasizes the team's commitment to delivering a functional game. Rebelzize also appeals for patience and respect toward the development team, emphasizing the unpaid labor and passion driving the project.

**Slay the Spire 2 Introduces End-of-Run Badges in Beta Update**

The latest beta patch for Slay the Spire 2 adds a new feature: badges that appear at the end of each run to highlight unique accomplishments such as defeating bosses without damage or completing runs quickly. These badges aim to provide clearer feedback about notable in-run achievements and will later integrate into the run history and stats screens. The patch also includes various card and enemy balance changes, visual updates, and bug fixes. This update follows a previous balance patch that sparked substantial negative feedback, though current community reaction appears more balanced. The patch is available on the game’s beta branch on Steam for players who opt in.
