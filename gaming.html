John Carmack Reflects on Early id Software Hardware

To mark id Software‚Äôs 35th anniversary, co-founder John Carmack shared reflections on the modest hardware used during the development of Wolfenstein 3D and early Doom games. He recalled working with 16 MHz CPUs, 640 KB of RAM, floppy disks, and 320x200 graphics resolution, emphasizing how constraints required close coordination between programming, art, and design.

Carmack noted moving to NeXT workstations for Doom development as a significant upgrade. These early technical limitations have become iconic milestones in gaming history, with Doom holding a special place as a popular benchmark for hardware. Carmack currently focuses on AI research through his company Keen and has stepped back from direct game development roles.

Be Quiet! Dark Perk Ergo Mouse Reviewed

Be Quiet! ventured into gaming peripherals with the Dark Perk Ergo, a lightweight (55g) wireless mouse targeted at competitive players. Featuring a PixArt PAW3950 sensor, up to 32,000 DPI, and an 8000 Hz polling rate, it offers solid performance without RGB lighting. The ergonomic design is suited for right-handed users preferring a claw grip due to its small size and shape.

Although well-built and quiet in operation, the mouse lacks a standout feature to differentiate it from competitors and is priced at $110, which places it against strong alternatives like the Corsair Sabre V2 Pro and Razer DeathAdder V4 Pro, both offering comparable or superior specs for less or more justified costs. The Dark Perk Ergo could appeal to users seeking an alternative to the major brands but might struggle to capture a significant market share.
