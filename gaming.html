Slay the Spire 2 Introduces Multiplayer Co-op Mode

Slay the Spire 2, already noted as a top-performing deckbuilder, has launched a cooperative multiplayer mode that allows up to four players to tackle its roguelite tower together. One player hosts the game and others join via Steam friends, with no matchmaking or local co-op options currently available. Each player picks a character, and campaign saves are tied to the host’s profile. Players must all start together; no substitutions are allowed mid-run.

Multiplayer keeps the core deckbuilding and roguelite systems intact but adapts several mechanics for co-op play. Enemy stats scale with party size and attacks hit the entire group. However, energy and loot are individual, with players earning their own rewards. Players share map control and decisions but maintain separate inventories and gold. Ascension progression is independent of singleplayer, requiring each member to unlock levels before running at higher difficulties as a team.

The addition of multiplayer extends the game’s appeal and offers a new way to experience its challenging card-based combat with friends.

Analysts Confirm Slay the Spire 2’s Unmatched Market Success

Data from analytics firm Newzoo confirms that Slay the Spire 2 is the most successful deckbuilding game to date. It recorded a peak of 575,000 concurrent Steam players shortly after its early access release, substantially surpassing previous records by games like Balatro and the original Slay the Spire. The game sold 3 million copies in its first week, generating around $75 million before distribution fees.

This strong performance is attributed to years of player anticipation and Mega Crit’s solid reputation. Unlike many contemporary games, Slay the Spire 2 eschewed monetization gimmicks, offering a straightforward paid experience. The launch has also expanded the audience for challenging roguelike card games, attracting players interested in competitive elements and high difficulty.

Kojima Productions Designed Death Stranding 2 for Higher Completion Rates

Hideo Kojima directed Death Stranding 2 with a focus on encouraging more players to complete the game. Feedback from the first title indicated it was perceived as slow and intricate, causing many to drop out before finishing. For the sequel, the developers simplified some worldbuilding elements and added more accessible story aids, making it easier for players to follow the narrative.

This design shift paid off: data shows over 62% of players completed Death Stranding 2’s final chapter, compared to under 50% for the original. Improvements like earlier access to vehicles and tools also helped reduce travel frustration. While some players feel the game is easier, a harder difficulty mode remains available for those seeking more challenge.

OpenAI Shuts Down Sora Video Generation App, Disney Withdraws $1 Billion Deal

OpenAI announced the discontinuation of its Sora video generation app less than six months after the launch of its second version. The app had become popular but raised copyright issues by generating videos featuring copyrighted content without permission. OpenAI is also ending video features in its ChatGPT product to focus on productivity tools like its new desktop superapp.

Following this move, Disney has pulled out of a previously announced $1 billion investment deal with OpenAI that included licensing its characters for Sora-generated content. Disney expressed appreciation for the collaboration but respects OpenAI’s strategic pivot away from video generation.

Prominent Skyrim Mod SkyUI Returns After Nine Years With Significant Update

SkyUI, one of the most popular mods improving Skyrim’s user interface, has released a major update for the first time since 2017. The new SkyUI 6 version integrates several commonly used supplemental mods, improves compatibility across all Skyrim Special Edition versions, and adjusts automatically to different screen aspect ratios.

Importantly, the mod’s source code has been published on GitHub, opening it up for community contributions and ensuring ongoing updates and maintenance. This release will benefit players who rely on SkyUI for better UI functionality and reduce compatibility issues with other mods.
