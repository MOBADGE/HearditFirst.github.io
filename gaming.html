Homebrew VR Headset Using Mini CRTs Demonstrates Alternative Display Solutions

YouTuber dooglehead has created a functional, homemade VR headset utilizing small CRT screens sourced from vintage devices like Sony Watchman units. Rather than modern high-resolution LCD or OLED panels, the CRTs eliminate screen door effect through analog display characteristics, resulting in smooth, flicker-free images despite 640x480 resolution. The headset uses an FPGA to convert digital video signals into analog for the CRTs, along with off-the-shelf infrared laser tracking for head movement. Weighing just over 500 grams, the setup offers unique visuals but lacks color and contrast, with a 60Hz refresh rate limiting smoothness. While not practical for long-term gaming, the project showcases inventive uses of older technology and open-source VR components, underscoring creative potential outside mainstream hardware.
