**Humble Bundle Offers Nine Classic and Modern CRPGs for Around $23** The latest Humble Bundle, titled "Beamdog & Owlcat RPG Masters," collects nine computer role-playing games (CRPGs) alongside three expansions for approximately $23. Notable classics include Baldur's Gate (1 and 2), Planescape: Torment, and Icewind Dale, all presented in their enhanced editions. The bundle also features modern titles like Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous and Warhammer 40,000: Rogue Trader. For fans of traditional narrative-driven RPGs, the package presents a significant value with hundreds of hours of gameplay. The inclusion of Mythforce—a first-person shooter roguelite by Beamdog—adds variety to the roster. Buyers may select various tiers starting at $5, making it accessible for those interested in specific titles.

**New Blood Bowl Update Launches as Separate Free Upgrade for Owners** Cyanide Studio announced that the upcoming update to Blood Bowl, based on the latest tabletop rules, will arrive as a distinct game titled Warhammer Blood Bowl. This new version will be free for existing Blood Bowl 3 owners and will retain all their progress and purchases. It introduces two new teams—Tomb Kings and Bretonnians—expanding the total to 26 teams available at launch. Additionally, a new Rumble mode offers faster-paced 7-on-7 matches on a narrowed pitch, with a free Steam demo planned from March 18 to 25. The update also promises refreshed rosters, new competitions, and improved tutorials, aiming to provide a comprehensive update aligned with the tabletop experience.

**Valve Responds to New York Lawsuit, Criticizes Violent Game Claims** Valve issued a public statement responding to New York's lawsuit accusing it of enabling illegal gambling through loot boxes. The company rejects the claim that its loot boxes constitute gambling under state law and stressed efforts to combat unauthorized third-party gambling using Steam accounts. Valve criticized New York's ancillary comments linking violent videogames to real-world gun violence as an unfounded distraction. The company referenced numerous studies finding no connection between media violence and real violence. Valve also expressed concern over regulatory proposals that could affect user privacy and limit digital item transferability. This marks a rare direct communication from Valve on legal challenges concerning its marketplace and virtual items.

**Diablo 4’s Lord of Hatred Expansion Introduces Overhauled Crafting System** The upcoming Lord of Hatred expansion for Diablo 4 will bring a new crafting system inspired by Diablo 2’s Horadric Cube. Players will be able to combine and upgrade items, including turning common and magic items into legendary ones with powerful affixes. This marks a shift from the current loot system where low-tier items become irrelevant at higher levels. The update also includes a loot filter to tailor visibility of items based on desired stats, reducing the need to salvage unwanted gear. Additionally, a “transfiguration” mechanic similar to last season’s sanctification will allow players to gamble for bonus effects on items. These changes aim to reinvigorate endgame progression and expand loot value.

**Microsoft Details Project Helix Xbox-PC Hybrid, Release Years Away** At GDC, Microsoft provided new information on Project Helix, the code name for its upcoming hybrid Xbox-console and PC. The device will support running both Xbox console and PC games and feature next-generation AMD hardware with advanced ray tracing, machine learning upscaling, and texture compression technologies. Alpha units are expected to be sent to developers in 2027, positioning a potential retail release in 2028. Microsoft emphasized the diminishing divide between console and PC gaming, aiming to unify experiences across platforms. The timeline indicates the current Xbox generation will be nearly eight years old before the new hardware launches.

**Minecraft’s Shift Away from Large Yearly Updates Explained** Mojang’s general manager Ryan Cooper discussed why Minecraft moved away from its tradition of annual large-scale updates.
