Marvel Rivals Focuses on UI, Not Original Heroes

NetEase’s producer Danny Koo confirmed that Marvel Rivals will not introduce any original superheroes soon, despite having approval to do so. The game’s roster is already filled with many well-known Marvel characters planned through at least season 8. The development team is prioritizing improving the game’s user interface (UI) over creating new heroes. Balancing the growing roster remains a challenge, with player complaints about perceived unfair nerfs and confusing patch notes. Additionally, NetEase has had to address issues like incentivized game-throwing, implementing compensation to affected players. The focus on UI optimization aims to simplify hero selection and make the game more accessible to players.

LG Unveils Energy-Efficient 1-120 Hz Laptop Display

LG Display announced a new LCD technology called "Oxide 1 Hz," which automatically adjusts the refresh rate from 1 Hz to 120 Hz depending on screen activity. LG claims this can extend battery life by up to 48% compared to existing laptop panels. The efficiency gain is attributed to reduced image processing when static images are shown, though the exact implementation details remain unspecified. Dell’s 2026 XPS 14 and 16 laptops will be among the first to feature this technology, boasting up to 31 hours of battery life. LG is also preparing to mass-produce a similar 1 Hz OLED panel by 2027, promising efficiency improvements for OLED laptop displays.

Baldur’s Gate 3 Romance System Could Have Been Larger

An interview with Kevin VanOrd, a writer for Baldur’s Gate 3, revealed that the game’s romance system was originally planned to include a platonic “friendship system” and companion-to-companion romances independent of the player character. However, these ideas were cut due to time constraints. Such features could have added complexity and depth to character relationships. Despite this, VanOrd expressed satisfaction with the current romance mechanics, noting the strong community interest and creative fan engagement with the characters. The insight highlights the trade-offs developers face between ambitious content and production schedules.

UK Regulators Outline Expectations for Age Verification

Two UK regulatory bodies, the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) and Ofcom, published guidance clarifying what constitutes "highly effective" age assurance under the UK’s Online Safety Act. The statement emphasizes that systems must be technically accurate, robust, reliable, and fair while considering user accessibility and interoperability. Examples of acceptable methods include credit card checks, photo ID matching, and mobile network operator verification. Self-declaration and simple contractual restrictions are insufficient. Services that allow pornographic or harmful content accessible to children must implement these high standards. The guidance adopts a flexible, technology-neutral stance, focusing on effectiveness and compliance with data protection laws rather than mandating specific technologies.

Tekken 8 Faces Backlash After Recent Patch

Following a troubled 2025, Tekken 8 has continued to draw negative community response after its latest Season 3 balance patch. Players on Reddit and Steam express dissatisfaction with unresolved balance issues and perceived developer missteps. Steam reviews have turned “Mostly Negative” in recent weeks, reflecting widespread frustration. Bandai Namco acknowledged these concerns in an official statement, apologizing for the experience and promising incremental improvements focused on balancing offense and defense with the Heat system mechanic. However, significant adjustments are not expected until mid-April or later, which some players fear may be too late to restore confidence. The situation underscores ongoing challenges in managing competitive game balance and community expectations.

Forza Horizon 6 System Requirements Confirmed

Playground Games released the system requirements for Forza Horizon 6, revealing that it is accessible to a broad range of PC hardware, including older CPUs and GPUs. The minimum specification calls for an Intel Core i5 8400 or Ryzen 5 1600, along with entry-level GPUs like Nvidia GTX 1650 or AMD RX 6500 XT. Recommended specs suggest mid-range modern components, while extreme settings with ray tracing require high-end GPUs like the RTX 5070 Ti or Radeon RX 9070 XT and up to 32 GB of RAM. The game supports 1080p to 4K resolutions and ultrawide monitors. These requirements indicate the game’s optimization for diverse hardware, including compatibility with PC handhelds such as the Steam Deck. Forza Horizon 6 is scheduled to launch on May 19, 2026.
