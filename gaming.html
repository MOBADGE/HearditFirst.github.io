DIY DDR5 Memory as a Response to the Ongoing PC Memory Shortage

A Russian modder known as VIK-on has developed a method to create their own 32 GB DDR5 memory stick by combining two 16 GB laptop SODIMMs on a custom PCB. The project reportedly cost $218, which is about a third of the usual price for similar capacity in Russia. This mod requires advanced soldering skills and handling of memory DIMM firmware, making it accessible only to very experienced enthusiasts. While not a practical solution for most consumers, this DIY approach highlights the severity and distortion in the current memory market caused by supply shortages and rising prices. If the crisis continues, there could be commercial interest in repurposing memory modules in this way, although it is unlikely to significantly lower RAM prices.

Power Supplies and CPU Coolers Likely to See Price Increases Due to Raw Material Costs

A leaked letter from electronics supplier Guangzhou Xinhongzheng Electronic Technology suggests imminent price hikes for power supplies and CPU coolers, ranging from 6% to 10%. The increases are attributed to rising costs of raw materials such as copper, silver, and tin, influenced by factors like mine disruptions and import tariffs. Unlike memory shortages driven by AI, this price pressure derives from basic commodity inflation and supply chain issues. While the letter's authenticity is not independently verified, commodity price trends support the possibility of higher costs. For consumers, this may mean modestly higher prices on budget cooling and power supply components, adding to the overall cost of PC upgrades in 2026.

Noctua Pushes Back Release of Fan-Equipped Mouse and Desk Fan but On Schedule for Liquid Cooler

Noctua updated its product roadmap, delaying the release of its Pulsar Feinmann fan-equipped gaming mouse and 140 mm desk fan from Q1 to Q2 and Q3, respectively. The delay contrasts with the company's all-in-one liquid cooler, still planned for launch in Q2 2026. The upcoming liquid cooler, produced in collaboration with Asetek, promises a quieter pump design and will be available in multiple radiator sizes. Noctua underlines that its roadmap is subject to change and serves as a transparency measure rather than firm release commitments. The delays likely reflect Noctua's cautious approach to product quality and timing, indicating the cooler remains a priority.
