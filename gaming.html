French Government Moves to Linux for Digital Sovereignty

The French government announced plans to replace Windows with Linux across its agencies in an effort to reduce reliance on foreign software providers, specifically those from outside Europe. This initiative aims to enhance "digital sovereignty" by adopting open-source software that can be customized and controlled within the country. The transition will begin with the National Health Insurance Fund's 80,000 agents and is expected to complete by the end of 2026. The move reflects growing concerns about data control, infrastructure security, and geopolitical issues related to dependence on U.S.-based technology.

**Why it matters:** This shift sets a precedent for governmental adoption of open-source software motivated by security and autonomy concerns rather than purely technical factors. It could influence other governments and large organizations to consider similar strategies amid increasing global tensions around digital infrastructure.

Treasure Beach: A Beach Scavenging and Bartering Game

Treasure Beach, showcased during Steam’s Hidden Objects fest, offers players an experience of scavenging items hidden in the sand, restoring them, and selling them to cautious shoppers. The game emphasizes not just collecting, but also strategic bartering to maximize profits and afford upgrades. Players find diverse items ranging from mundane objects to luxury watches and must prepare them properly before selling. Early gameplay reveals the challenge of negotiating with buyers, who often offer low prices.

**Why it matters:** Treasure Beach combines simple scavenging mechanics with economic negotiation, providing a distinct gameplay loop focused on resource management and player choice, which could appeal to fans of casual and simulation games looking for unique selling mechanics.

Upgrading MacBook Neo Storage Is Possible but Technical

A YouTuber known as dosdude1 demonstrated how to upgrade the Apple MacBook Neo’s storage from 256GB to 1TB by physically replacing the soldered NAND flash chip. Despite the MacBook Neo’s compact and integrated design making the process challenging, it was successfully executed with the right tools and expertise. This workaround is particularly relevant given Apple's charging premium price for increased storage.

**Why it matters:** This highlights the potential for advanced users to modify and upgrade devices typically seen as fixed, opening the door to more affordable hardware modifications. However, it remains out of reach for most consumers due to the technical difficulty involved.

Slow DDR5 Memory Has Minimal Impact on Gaming Performance

Tests comparing slower DDR5-4800 memory modules to faster DDR5-6000 versions across several modern games show that most titles do not experience meaningful performance drops with slower RAM. Games heavily dependent on GPUs, like Cyberpunk 2077 or Black Myth: Wukong, ran at nearly identical frame rates on both memory types. CPU-intensive titles and competitive shooters showed minor differences, primarily at low resolutions and settings. Even the system-intensive games benefited little from faster memory in practical terms.

**Why it matters:** With DDR5 RAM prices notably high in 2026, these findings suggest gamers can save costs by opting for slower, more affordable RAM without significant performance sacrifice. This informs PC builders and upgraders about balancing component costs smartly.

16GB System Memory Still Adequate for Most PC Games in 2026

Despite a trend toward recommending 32GB of RAM, detailed testing reveals that 16GB remains sufficient for most of the current generation’s demanding games, especially when paired with a high-end GPU like the RTX 5090. Tests on titles such as Cyberpunk 2077, Black Myth: Wukong, and Stalker 2 showed negligible differences between 16GB and 32GB in typical gameplay situations. Exceptions include very memory-heavy games like Star Citizen and Microsoft Flight Simulator, which benefit somewhat from increased RAM. Multitasking scenarios, such as streaming or running multiple applications alongside gaming, may expose limitations of 16GB setups.

**Why it matters:** This challenges the notion that 32GB is the necessary minimum today, potentially saving gamers money on memory. However, it also highlights that users who multitask heavily or play the most demanding titles should consider more RAM.
