Krafton Remains Involved with Subnautica 2 Despite Steam Page Change

The Steam page for Subnautica 2 no longer lists Krafton as the publisher; instead, Unknown Worlds—the developer acquired by Krafton in 2021—now appears as both developer and publisher. This follows a legal dispute in which Krafton fired Unknown Worlds' leadership to avoid a $250 million bonus, leading to a lawsuit where a judge ordered Krafton to reinstate the studio's CEO, Ted Gill. Krafton still owns Unknown Worlds and has stated it remains focused on supporting Subnautica 2's early access launch. However, the release date remains uncertain, with a court-approved extension allowing launch eligibility for the bonus until September 15. This shift on the Steam page may be cosmetic rather than signaling a breakup between the companies.

Marvel Rivals Introduces Black Cat with Powerful and Complex Abilities

The online shooter Marvel Rivals is adding a new character, Black Cat, in its upcoming Season 7.5 update. Black Cat’s abilities include basic moves like melee strikes and double jumps, but her defining feature is a “Fortune” system where she earns a currency in matches to activate abilities or purchase powerful artifacts. Her kit includes portals, invisibility, area-of-effect stuns, and wall detection. Though these offer creative gameplay, community members and streamers have noted that Black Cat appears significantly overpowered, continuing a pattern of powerful hero releases in the game that require later nerfs. It remains unclear when balance adjustments might arrive.

Apple Approves Open Source Driver for Nvidia GPUs on Macs, Focused on AI Use

Apple has officially approved an open-source driver that enables the use of Nvidia GPUs, including the GeForce RTX 50 series, on Macs via external GPU docks connected over USB4 or Thunderbolt. The driver, created by tiny corp and demonstrated by Alex Ziskind, allows Macs to run Nvidia graphics hardware primarily for AI applications rather than gaming. Although performance is still limited by software, this marks a return to Nvidia GPU compatibility on Apple devices without requiring unofficial hacks. The open-source nature of the driver may eventually prompt further developments, potentially including gaming support in the future.

American Shoe Company Allbirds Shifts Focus to AI Infrastructure with $50 Million Investment

Allbirds, known for sustainable footwear, is pivoting entirely to AI infrastructure and cloud services, rebranding as NewBird AI. The company has secured a $50 million investment to establish a facility aimed at providing GPU-as-a-Service (GPUaaS) offerings for AI compute needs such as training and rendering. This move places it in competition with established GPU cloud providers during a period of high demand and expensive hardware shortages. While stock prices have increased due to the pivot announcement, it remains uncertain whether NewBird AI will be able to compete effectively in this rapidly growing but capital-intensive market.

Crimson Desert Reaches Five Million Sales Amid Ongoing Improvements

Pearl Abyss’s action RPG Crimson Desert has sold over five million copies worldwide less than a month after release. Despite a difficult launch marked by mixed reviews and player complaints about complexity and design choices, the developer has implemented numerous patches addressing quality-of-life issues, difficulty balancing, and usability. These continuous updates have improved player reception and likely contributed to steady sales growth. However, discussions continue about the game’s overall identity and the impact of such extensive post-launch changes on the development team and the game’s long-term direction.

Development Ends for Book of Travels After Five Years

Might and Delight has announced the end of active development for its online RPG Book of Travels, which launched into early access in 2021. Following staff layoffs and ongoing struggles to meet the game's goals, the studio cited an unsustainable foundation and limited capacity as reasons for halting development. Servers will shut down on July 31, but an offline singleplayer mode allowing continued play as a “lone wanderer” has been introduced. Alongside this full release marking the 1.0 version, the game’s price will drop significantly. The developers have also opened modding options and expressed hope the game will remain accessible and enjoyable for its community going forward.
