Hitman and MindsEye Crossover Cancelled as Publishing Deal Ends

Build a Rocket Boy, the developer behind the troubled game *MindsEye*, announced the termination of its publishing deal with IO Interactive under the IOI Partnership program. This officially cancels the previously announced *Hitman* crossover mission slated for 2025. Despite the setback, Build a Rocket Boy said development on the planned *MindsEye* expansion and a multiplayer mode continues, with details expected within the coming months. The split confirms earlier rumors and follows *MindsEye*’s poor launch and ongoing development challenges. IO Interactive suggested that *MindsEye*’s failure may end its willingness to publish similar third-party projects.

Slay the Spire 2 Escapes Unity Engine After Dev Fee Controversy

*Slay the Spire 2* has sold over three million copies in its first week of early access, marking it as one of this year's major successes. The developer, Mega Crit, switched the game’s engine from Unity to the open-source Godot engine after the 2023 Unity developer fee controversy. Unity’s introduction of retroactive per-install fees led many developers to abandon the engine. Mega Crit’s move, initiated despite two years of work in Unity, highlights the impact of Unity’s missteps on its relationships with indie studios. In contrast, Godot’s MIT license and lack of fees make it an appealing alternative. Mega Crit’s choice underscores ongoing shifts in game development tools triggered by industry disputes.

Marathon Patch Prepares for Cryo Archive Map with Weapon Nerfs and Audio Changes

Bungie’s *Marathon* received update 1.0.5, introducing the Cryo Archive map, currently inaccessible until players solve community puzzles to unlock it. Entry requirements include reaching runner level 25, unlocking all six factions, and maintaining a loadout value of at least 5,000 credits per run — a high bar encouraging risk with valuable gear. The update also nerfs the popular WSTR shotgun by reducing its effective range and ammo exploit potential, aiming to balance PvP play. Sound adjustments revert recent changes that made combat more chaotic by lowering the audible range of other players in fights. These tweaks set the stage for the new content and aim to improve gameplay balance.

Arc Raiders Studio Embark Parts Ways with Co-founder Amid Streamer-Related Allegations

Embark Studios, developer of *Arc Raiders*, has separated from co-founder and chief content officer Rob Runesson after allegations surfaced involving inappropriate behavior with a streamer. The studio’s external investigation found no evidence to substantiate the claims but deemed the situation "unsustainable" and mutually agreed to the split. This follows a recent pause on *The Finals* esports activities due to multiple player misconduct complaints. The developments suggest ongoing challenges within Embark related to workplace conduct and community management, leading to structural changes in key leadership roles.

Nvidia DLSS 5 Reveal Sparks Mixed Reactions Over AI-Driven Visuals

Nvidia unveiled DLSS 5 at GTC 2026, showcasing AI-based lighting enhancements intended to improve visual fidelity without excessive computational cost. However, the reveal drew significant criticism for the demonstration’s use of AI filters that alter character models in ways some found unsettling and inconsistent with original artistic intent. Critics argued that the technology risks homogenizing game visuals and imposing unrealistic beauty standards. Supporters, including Epic Games’ lead producer Jean Pierre Kellams, praised the shader and lighting improvements, emphasizing developer control over effects and potential performance benefits. Nvidia is still refining DLSS 5 for broader GPU compatibility. The debate highlights tensions between emerging AI tools and preserving creative vision in game development.

Krafton Expands Into Physical AI and Defense with Hanwha Aerospace Partnership

Krafton, known for *PUBG*, announced a strategic alliance with Korean aerospace firm Hanwha Aerospace focused on developing physical AI technologies in defense and other sectors. The deal includes up to $1 billion in investment, leveraging Krafton’s expertise in large-scale game data and virtual physics to support AI applications in robotics, autonomous systems, and military hardware. "Physical AI" refers to AI that can perceive and interact with the physical world, such as autonomous vehicles and defense equipment. Krafton CEO Kim Chang-han envisions the partnership positioning the company as a global defense technology player. This marks a notable diversification for Krafton beyond gaming into AI-driven defense systems.
