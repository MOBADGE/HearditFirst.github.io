Ustwo Shifts Focus to PC-First Development After Netflix Pulls Monument Valley 3

Ustwo, the developer behind the acclaimed Monument Valley series, has announced a strategic pivot from mobile-first to PC-first game development. This change follows Netflix's removal of Monument Valley 3 from its streaming service just six months after the game's December 2024 launch. According to Ustwo CEO Maria Sayans, creating games primarily for mobile platforms imposes limitations in design and market strategy, and the perception of being a mobile-first studio caps their potential on PC and console. Moving forward, Ustwo plans to launch its games multiplatform with PC as the initial platform to foster more direct engagement with players. This marks a shift away from reliance on subscription platforms like Netflix and Apple Arcade, seeking greater stability and stronger community ties.

Tim Cain on Increasing Hostility in Gaming Communities

Tim Cain, co-creator of the original Fallout, spoke publicly about the growing intensity and hostility of arguments within gaming communities. He noted that disagreements over game features and design have always existed, but modern discussions are more aggressive, often involving people talking past one another and dismissing opposing views. Cain attributes this trend to the rapid growth and diversification of the gaming audience, alongside industry consolidation that pressures developers to appeal to broad player bases. This diversity of expectations leads to conflicting feedback and heated debates online. Cain suggests that gamers might have more impact by voting with their wallets rather than engaging in unproductive arguments on social media.
