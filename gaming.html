Moves of the Diamond Hand Launches in Early Access

Moves of the Diamond Hand, an RPG by developer Cosmo D, has entered early access on Steam as of April 13. The game is set in the surreal noir city of Off-Peak where clones, secret societies, and political intrigue intermingle. Players take on the role of a circus hopeful navigating a mayoral election and various skill-based quests involving cooking, music, deception, and more. The game’s distinctive style combines dice-based tabletop mechanics with exploration and story-driven gameplay.

While some players and reviewers praise its ambitious scope, rich content, and unique dice-rolling system, the early access state means noticeable unfinished sections and occasional bugs. Despite these limitations, it is viewed as a promising upcoming RPG, with a full release planned for spring 2027.

Pickmos Removed from Steam After Controversy and Publisher Intervention

The Palworld-inspired game Pickmos, formerly Pickmon, has been removed from Steam following intervention from its publisher, NetworkGo. The game faced heavy criticism for closely imitating art and gameplay elements from Pokémon, Zelda, Overwatch, and Palworld itself. After attempts to address issues by changing its title slightly, the publisher took over supervision of the developer, PocketGame, citing the need to improve the game and ensure a controversy-free release.

This situation highlights ongoing challenges in the industry with knockoff games and intellectual property concerns. Whether Pickmos will return under a revised version remains unclear.

Alan Wake Crossover Event Coming to Phasmophobia in May

Phasmophobia will host a limited-time crossover event with Alan Wake starting May 12 through June 2. The event introduces a new "presence" called the Mimic, linked to the Alan Wake universe’s Dark Place, bringing new challenges to ghost-hunting gameplay. The collaboration promises themed rewards and content, though specific in-game details remain limited.

This marks Phasmophobia’s first major collaboration event, reflecting its sustained popularity—having sold over 28 million copies worldwide—and ongoing updates two years after its initial release.

Call of Duty Movie Scheduled for June 2028 Release

The Call of Duty movie, announced in 2025 by Activision and Paramount, is confirmed to release on June 30, 2028. This was shared on the official Call of Duty Movie social media account. The film involves high-profile writer Taylor Sheridan and director Peter Berg, though plot details remain sparse.

Given the lengthy lead time, the film remains in early production stages with more information expected closer to release.

Lana Del Rey’s Theme Song for 007 First Light Released, Met With Mixed Reception

Lana Del Rey released the official theme song “First Light” for the upcoming Bond game 007 First Light, launching May 27. While Bond theme songs are often high-profile works, the reception to “First Light” has been lukewarm. Critics describe it as lacking the depth and distinctiveness of classic Bond themes, characterizing it as a derivative performance that fails to stand out.

The release sparks discussion about the musical direction of Bond themes in contemporary media.

Metro 2039 Revealed with Darker Story and New Protagonist

Metro 2039, the next installment in the Metro series by 4A Games, was unveiled at the Xbox First Look event. Set in a dystopian Moscow Metro under Nazi control, the game introduces a voiced protagonist known as "The Stranger." The narrative focuses on the consequences of war, propaganda, and authoritarianism, reflecting the real-world context of the Ukrainian conflict.

The game is described as having a much darker tone than its predecessors and promises a story-driven single-player experience. It is scheduled for release this winter.
