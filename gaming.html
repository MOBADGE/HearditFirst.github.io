Counter-Strike: Global Offensive Returns to Steam

Nearly three years after being integrated into Counter-Strike 2, Counter-Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO) has reappeared on Steam as a standalone unlisted title. While online matchmaking remains disabled, players can access traditional matchmaking via Steam's server browser by joining community servers. The move has surprised the community and quickly put CS:GO back into Steam’s top 20 most-played games with over 61,000 concurrent players. This re-release suggests Valve may see continued value in maintaining both titles separately, acknowledging CS:GO's enduring player base and its significance to long-time fans.

Deus Ex: Invisible War's Development Challenges

A retrospective from Edge magazine has shed light on the troubled development of Deus Ex: Invisible War. Developers revealed that a strategic decision to build the game on a modified engine primarily designed for Thief: Deadly Shadows hindered the project. The engine struggled with Invisible War’s larger environments, forcing compromises such as frequent loading screens and reduced environmental detail to maintain performance. Additionally, pressure from publisher Eidos to release on consoles, particularly Xbox, imposed hardware limitations that further constrained the game. Lead designer Ricardo Bare described the engine decision as "a super-boneheaded call" that "really tanked development," and director Harvey Smith expressed regret over design decisions, including the controversial shared ammo system. Smith and other key staff left Ion Storm Austin soon after, later contributing to successful immersive sims like Dishonored.

World of Warcraft: Midnight's Hidden Gold Puzzle Solved

In World of Warcraft: Midnight, players encountered a rare in-game painting priced at the maximum gold limit of 9,999,999 gold. This prompted a secret-hunting effort among the community. A player named Christa discovered that collecting and holding all 13 "bad fortune" items dropped by a fortune teller NPC in the same area triggered a price drop for the painting to 999 gold. These fortunes can be gathered daily, traded among players, and shared between characters to unlock the discount. The puzzle demonstrates Blizzard’s ongoing use of hidden in-game challenges. Some players apparently paid the full price before the secret was discovered, highlighting the difficulty of uncovering hidden content without community collaboration.

The Legend of Khiimori: A New Approach to Horse and Survival Games

The Legend of Khiimori, currently in early access, blends open-world survival mechanics with a focus on historically influenced horse riding set in 13th century Mongolia. Players manage their horse’s stamina, health, and mood while making deliveries across the rugged terrain, with detailed systems for weight balancing and route planning. The game prioritizes the horse’s survival over the player's, and features breeding and training systems for mounts. Despite some bugs and unfinished story content, Khiimori aims to balance authentic horse simulation with engaging gameplay. The developer, Aesir Interactive, has prior experience in horse games and is advised by a respected horse game critic. The game presents an interesting niche but still needs to overcome early access shortcomings and concerns about consistent developer support.

Ubisoft Confirms Assassin's Creed Black Flag Remake

Ubisoft officially confirmed the long-rumored remake of Assassin's Creed 4: Black Flag, revealing its official title as Assassin's Creed Black Flag Resynced. The announcement came alongside updates on the series’ future, including the winding down of support for Assassin’s Creed Shadows and plans for a "darker, narrative-driven" project codenamed Hexe. Notably, Ubisoft dropped the "4" from the remake’s title, which may indicate plans to position Black Flag as a potential sub-series within the franchise. The original Black Flag was well-received for its open-world sailing mechanics and remains one of the series’ standout entries. The remake will likely revisit and update this fan-favorite experience under fresh leadership with the original director, Jean Guesden, involved in the ongoing mainline Assassin's Creed development.
