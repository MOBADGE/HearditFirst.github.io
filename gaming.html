Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous Offers Extensive Build Options with Unique Mounted Combat

Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous presents an expansive character customization system, featuring 232 subclasses across 26 base classes and additional prestige classes. This depth supports a variety of unique playstyles, appealing especially to players who enjoy complex buildcrafting. Among these, the Sable Company Marine subclass for Rangers stands out by granting a rideable griffon companion from level one. Mounted combat becomes highly strategic at higher difficulties, with mounts serving as tanks and riders acting as damage dealers. The game remains challenging and sometimes punishing, with fans frequently debating optimal builds and rerolling characters to find the ideal combination. The enhancement of mounts as significant tactical assets differentiates it from other CRPGs.

Star Wars Zero Company Director Reflects on Revival of Classic PC Gaming Genres

Greg Foertsch, creative director of the upcoming Star Wars Zero Company, discussed the resurgence of traditional PC gaming genres like real-time strategy (RTS) and turn-based tactics. He pointed to the early 2000s as a period when many complex PC games failed to transition well to consoles, partly due to technical limitations and less flexible camera systems. Today, innovations in user experience and camera technology have made these genres more accessible on consoles, with games like Age of Empires and Company of Heroes successfully adapting to controller support. Foertsch noted that modern turn-based tactics games are innovating while respecting genre conventions and emphasized the ongoing challenge of designing these deceptively complex games.

Owlcat Addresses Use of Generative AI in The Expanse RPG Development

Owlcat Studios clarified its approach to generative AI in developing The Expanse: Osiris Reborn. The studio affirmed that all final game assets—including writing, voice acting, and art—are created by humans. AI is employed solely for internal prototyping, vision coordination, and concept inspiration during early development phases. This use is intended to accelerate iteration without replacing creative work. Despite these reassurances, some players expressed skepticism and concern over AI involvement in game creation, reflecting broader industry debates. Owlcat's stance aligns with other studios' recent disclosures about limited, controlled AI use focused on preliminary or placeholder content.

Mechabellum Delivers Strategic Interaction Through Dynamic Player Responses

The new season of Mechabellum, a 1v1 and occasional multiplayer autobattler, highlights the game's focus on strategy as a form of player interaction rather than rote execution. Players deploy various mech units and adapt their tactics in response to opponents' moves, creating a back-and-forth dynamic described as a "conversation." This emphasis on reactive play rather than established metas maintains engagement and encourages on-the-fly thinking. Matches are relatively short and clear in cause and effect, allowing players to learn from failures without frustration. Mechabellum’s design resists the trend toward optimization-heavy competitive play, offering a more interactive strategic experience.

Elder Scrolls: Blades Mobile Game to Shut Down After Eight Years

The Elder Scrolls: Blades, Bethesda's mobile RPG spinoff, is scheduled to shut down on June 30, ending an eight-year run. Ahead of closure, in-game store items are now available at minimal cost, and players are being granted free resources to experience remaining content. The shutdown follows the discontinuation of another Elder Scrolls spin-off, Legends, in early 2025. Blades will not receive an offline mode or preservation effort, marking a definitive end to its service. This reflects challenges mobile games face in longevity and ongoing player support compared to traditional PC or console Elder Scrolls titles.

Baldur’s Gate 3 Glitch Reveals Time Travel Exploit and Unexpected NPC Bug

A new exploit discovered by Baldur’s Gate 3 players allows time travel between acts by manipulating resting mechanics to bypass intended cutscenes. This glitch enables returning to earlier game areas with reset world states, causing NPC placement and behaviors to become inconsistent. Notably, it reveals multiple invisible or dormant versions of the character Gale grouped together and visibly naked in the player’s camp. This happens due to the game retaining multiple scripted versions of Gale for different story events or romances, which normally remain hidden. Players can use this glitch to farm experience points from respawned enemies, although its impact on main story progression remains unclear. The exploit highlights the complexity of the game’s state management across its acts.
