**Fallout: New Vegas Nearly Set in New Orleans**

Chris Avellone, writer for *Fallout: New Vegas* and former Obsidian creative officer, revealed in a recent interview that the game could have been set in New Orleans. Inspired by a post-apocalyptic comic titled *Grendel: Four Devils, One Hell*, the vibrant atmosphere and unique flavor of New Orleans attracted the team early in the development stage. Avellone expressed enthusiasm for the idea but noted it was ultimately not pursued. He also shared insights into Bethesda's relationship with Obsidian, mentioning that Bethesda showed dissatisfaction by making the studio sit through a presentation detailing Obsidian’s alleged shortcomings. This strained collaboration partly explains why Obsidian was not involved in further Fallout projects. Avellone also hinted at the possibility of *Fallout 5* being set in San Francisco, aligning with Bethesda’s previous restrictions on the franchise's setting.

**Astronauts on the ISS Receive High-End Laptops**

The International Space Station (ISS) crew is set to upgrade to new HP ZBook G9 Fury laptops, equipped with Intel Core Ultra 9 processors and Nvidia RTX Pro Blackwell GPUs, alongside a substantial 128 GB of DDR5 ECC RAM and multiple NVMe SSDs. These machines are tailored for the demanding conditions of space, including radiation and battery testing, emphasizing durability and reliability. The use of ECC memory ensures data integrity in the high-risk environment of space, where errors could have significant consequences. While the ISS hardware surpasses what most Earth gamers contend with amid global RAM shortages, the upgrade reflects NASA’s commitment to maintaining cutting-edge technology for ongoing missions.

**Security Risk Found in WordPress Plugins**

Austin Ginder, founder of Anchor Hosting, uncovered a supply chain security threat affecting 31 WordPress plugins. After the original creators sold Essential Plugin’s portfolio on a marketplace due to declining revenue, the new owner reportedly inserted backdoors into all the plugins within a month. These malicious modifications remained dormant until recently when some began pushing harmful code. WordPress responded by taking the affected plugins offline, but concerns linger about the lack of oversight during ownership transfers. This incident underscores ongoing risks in plugin ecosystems, where changes in control may not trigger code reviews or user notifications, potentially exposing thousands of websites to attack before issues are detected.

**PC Gamer Readers Share PC Placement Habits**

In a recent survey, only 37% of PC Gamer readers reported keeping their gaming PCs "beside me, always with me, on my desk," the quiz's correct answer regarding optimal PC placement. Nearly as many (34%) said their PCs reside on the floor, a less ideal location for hardware maintenance and airflow. Other placements included 12% using a stage attached to the desk, 7% next to a TV, and 7% hooked under the desk. Notably, 1% claimed to have PCs bolted to the wall. These results reflect diverse habits among PC gamers, impacting both performance and user experience.
