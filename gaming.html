Open-Source Project Enables Building Your Own Drawing Tablet

Project Patchouli is an open-source initiative aiming to allow makers to build their own electromagnetic resonance (EMR) drawing tablets from scratch. Designed by Yukidama, the project offers detailed hardware blueprints, firmware, and explanations of EMR technology that drives tablet-pen interactions.

While building such a tablet requires technical skills and access to circuit board manufacturing, the project aims to provide compatibility with various pen vendors and achieve low-latency input. It serves both as a learning tool and a potential foundation for more customizable or alternative drawing tablet designs. Although it does not yet match commercial tablets in features or size, the project may interest hobbyists and developers exploring digital art hardware.
