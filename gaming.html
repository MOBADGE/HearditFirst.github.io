Prime Monster Turns Political Conflict into a Monster-Filled Card Game

Cavalier Game Studios has released a demo for **Prime Monster**, a card-based roguelike set in a fantasy parliament populated by creatures like vampires, orcs, and witches. Players use cards representing insults, smears, and literal attacks to navigate political scandals and volatile alliances in a dystopian legislature. The game blends fantasy elements with realistic political tactics, including blackmail and manipulation. It offers a turn-based strategy experience where players compete for votes through underhanded means yet face limits enforced by a ghoul overseer who monitors excessive violence. This unique approach reframes the often grim theme of politics with imaginative, monster-filled gameplay. Prime Monster is slated to launch later this year, and the demo is currently available on Steam.

Thrifty Business Offers Relaxed Shopkeeping with Emphasis on Item Arrangement

The cozy shop simulator **Thrifty Business** from Spellgarden Games now has a demo available for players interested in laid-back management games. Drawing inspiration from titles like *Potion Craft* and *Tiny Bookshop*, this game focuses on the visual and tactile joy of organizing various items on shelves. Players buy mystery boxes of used goods, sort them by categories such as style, function, and color, and arrange them to maximize store charm rather than rush for profit. The demo lets players experiment within a few in-game days and participate in events like grand openings or local community nights. While the release date is not finalized, Thrifty Business aims for a 2026 launch with future updates planned to include character customization and photo modes.

Arc Raiders Community Shows Moments of Cooperation Amidst Competitive Play

**Arc Raiders**, the multiplayer third-person shooter by Embark Studios, continues to receive positive attention for its cooperative player behavior, even as it remains competitive. A recent story highlighted a player escorting a downed teammate to safety over a 15-minute span without communication, demonstrating the game's capacity for unspoken teamwork. This contrasts with other reports of betrayals, a common occurrence in such games, but the supportive moments stand out and contribute to the community's reputation as friendly. The game's unique "Expeditions" account-reset system remains an area Embark plans to rework to balance the difficulty and rewards more thoughtfully.

Discord Age Verification Raises Privacy Concerns Following Persona Exposure

Security researchers have revealed worrying details about **Persona**, the age verification provider used by Discord during a UK trial. Persona reportedly conducts as many as 269 individual verification checks covering categories from identity to potential security risks like terrorism and espionage. A frontend related to Persona was found exposed on a government-authorized server, revealing extensive source code and data-processing methods, including facial recognition linked to watchlist comparisons. This level of scrutiny has sparked privacy concerns among users and advocates given the sensitive data involved. Discord has stated that the Persona trial concluded, but the findings have intensified debate on the risks associated with digital age verification platforms.

Skate Locks New Map Area Behind Paywall Contrary to Initial Promises

The free-to-play skateboarding game **Skate** by EA has announced that the upcoming Isle of Grom map expansion will be gated behind a paid "Skate Pass." This contradicts prior statements from developers who promised no map areas would require additional payment. Initially, access will be exclusive to pass holders, followed by a limited free access period, and then a paid rental option via in-game currency after April. Though EA framed this as a necessary change to support the game's long-term development, players have expressed frustration over the departure from earlier commitments. The move fuels ongoing criticism over the game's microtransaction-heavy design despite its large player base.

Resident Evil games generally benefit from minimal prior exposure to their storylines, and Capcom aims to protect that element.
