Battlefield 6 Javelin Anticheat System Explained

Battlefield Studios recently provided clarity on how their kernel-level anticheat system, EA Javelin, detects and manages cheating in Battlefield 6. Although some players questioned the accuracy of the match infection rate (MIR) metric, developers explained that MIR includes both confirmed and suspected cheaters, based on evolving detection signals. This approach accounts for elusive cheaters using stealth methods. The MIR rose from 2.28% to 3.09% in January due to improvements in detection and new ban acceleration methods. Javelin has blocked over 580,000 cheating attempts recently and monitors 224 cheat-related programs, most of which are now offline or impaired. This update underscores ongoing efforts to maintain fair play, despite the transparency around the system still relying on player trust.

Multiplayer Mod Progress for Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2

Independent modders have made early strides in adding multiplayer functionality to Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2, a game originally designed as a single-player RPG. The current implementation lets players see each other as ghostly NPCs with synchronized movements, armor, and weapons. While still in an experimental phase, the mod is steadily improving with smoother animations and more features. The mod team plans to include multiplayer dice games, such as two-player Farkle, to enrich social interactions. Although it is uncertain whether the mod will reach a fully polished multiplayer experience, it represents an ambitious community effort to add online elements to a single-player title.

Story Developments in World of Warcraft: Midnight

In the ongoing story campaign of World of Warcraft: Midnight, new character interactions hint at a possible future antagonist. The Amani troll Zul’jan expresses frustration and ambition after a failed plan involving lightwood weapons, signaling potential conflict ahead. These story moments add depth to the game’s narrative, showing complex relationships such as the alliance and tension between Zul’jan and his sister Zul’jarra. Players have noted this as credible foreshadowing of upcoming content, reflecting Blizzard’s gradual improvement in storytelling within the WoW universe.

Gartner Predicts Decline of Sub-$500 PCs by 2028

Market research firm Gartner has released projections highlighting a significant shift in the global PC market. Due to rising memory costs—expected to surge 130% by the end of 2026—PC prices will increase by approximately 17%, leading to a 10.4% drop in worldwide PC shipments this year. One notable prediction is the disappearance of the sub-$500 entry-level PC segment by 2028, as manufacturers find it unprofitable to produce low-margin budget machines amid surging component costs. This trend may make affordable PCs scarce for everyday users, potentially affecting the accessibility of computing for casual or budget-conscious buyers.

Minecraft Adds Golden Dandelion to Slow Baby Animal Growth

In the upcoming Minecraft 2026 update, players will find a new craftable item: the Golden Dandelion. This flower can be fed to baby animals to pause their aging process for about 20 minutes, allowing players to enjoy their juvenile forms longer. However, not all baby mobs can consume the item—undead creatures, piglins, and baby villagers are excluded. Crafting a Golden Dandelion requires eight Gold Nuggets, making it a resource-intensive option for players interested in customizing the growth of their animals. The update, currently available in testing builds, expands gameplay options related to the new baby mob designs.

Resident Evil Requiem Actor Details Filming Approach

Angela Sant’Albano, who plays Grace Ashcroft in Resident Evil Requiem, shared insights into the production’s efforts to capture the horror atmosphere. New to the series and horror games, Sant’Albano studied previous Resident Evil titles and adapted by performing motion capture scenes in near-darkness, using flashlights to enhance authenticity. The approach aimed to emulate the tension and immersion players experience in survival horror games without literal danger. Sant’Albano also noted her engagement with the series, particularly enjoying Resident Evil 4’s immersive qualities. This reflects Capcom’s intent to return the franchise to its survival horror roots with Requiem.
