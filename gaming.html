Bungie Defends Its Halo Legacy Amid Criticism

Elliott Gray, a UI designer on Bungie’s Marathon series, responded to ongoing discussions claiming Bungie is no longer the studio behind Halo. Addressing player concerns, Gray listed around 40 developers from the original Halo days who are still working at Bungie. This challenges the narrative that the studio’s core team has completely moved on while affirming the continuity between Bungie’s past and present. Mark Noseworthy, former Destiny vice president, also supported this view, emphasizing that Bungie’s culture and technology legacy have been passed on to newer employees, maintaining the feel of Bungie games over the years. This defense matters as it reassures fans about the studio’s identity and its ongoing connection to the Halo franchise.

Intel Highlights UEFI Security Flaws, Mitigations Incoming

Intel disclosed several UEFI (Unified Extensible Firmware Interface) vulnerabilities affecting many of its products, with severity ratings up to 8.7 on the CVSS scale. These flaws could potentially allow local attackers to escalate privileges on affected devices. While mitigations are being developed and rolled out to manufacturers, so far updates are limited and the fixes are not widespread. The vulnerabilities require local access, reducing the immediacy of risk but still warranting awareness and prompt updating when patches become available. This report underscores the importance of updating system firmware and staying vigilant against security threats that could compromise foundational system components.

Blizzard Faces Challenges Balancing WoW: Midnight’s Abundance Event

World of Warcraft: Midnight’s recent Abundance world event, which tasks players with farming resources under time pressure, is facing criticism following changes that reduced rewards, especially for players using raid groups. The changes appear intended to reduce lag caused by large groups, but have made it harder for profession-focused players to accumulate the critical Unalloyed Abundance currency needed for progression. Players also highlight the weekly run cap, feeling pressured to complete all runs to keep up, exacerbating stress rather than enjoyment. Blizzard's efforts to balance gameplay and rewards remain delicate, illustrating ongoing tensions in meeting both casual and hardcore player expectations.

Samsung PCs Running Windows 11 Face Boot Drive Access Issue

Certain Samsung PCs running Windows 11 versions 24H2 and 25H2 are affected by a serious issue that locks users out of their own C: drive, showing an “Access denied” error. The problem is linked to the Samsung Galaxy Connect app and impacts file access, launching applications, and even system utility functions. Microsoft and Samsung have taken steps to mitigate the problem by removing the app from the Microsoft Store and rolling back to a previous stable version. No full fix is yet available, and recovery options for affected users are limited. While the issue affects a specific hardware and software combination, its severity highlights the challenges of complex OS-software interactions on modern PCs.

FBI Seeks Information from Victims of Malware Hidden in Steam Games

The FBI has issued a public call for victims who downloaded malware disguised in several Steam games, including BlockBlasters, Chemia, and PirateFi, to come forward. These games operated mainly between May 2024 and January 2026, and the malware reportedly allowed attackers to steal money and personal information. The FBI requests details via email or an online form to link incidents and identify perpetrators. Previous cases involved thousands of players and significant financial theft, notably targeting streamers and players enticed by free-to-play or investigatory gameplay. Valve’s official platform being exploited in this way raises concerns about digital storefront security and the importance of cautious behavior with unfamiliar online content.

Capcom Reports Fastest Sales Milestone for Resident Evil Requiem

Capcom announced that Resident Evil Requiem has sold over six million units worldwide, reaching this milestone faster than any other title in the Resident Evil series. The game has been well received, achieving an “Overwhelmingly positive” rating on Steam and setting peak concurrent player records for the franchise. Capcom plans ongoing support, including new content such as photo modes and minigames planned for release in the coming months. Additionally, the 30th anniversary of the Resident Evil series will feature collaborations and orchestral concerts. The strong sales performance indicates robust fan engagement and ongoing lifecycle support for the title.
