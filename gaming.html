Highlights and Lowlights in PC Game Development Performance of 2025

In a review of 13 notable PC game releases this year, three titles stood out for their technical execution: "Battlefield 6," "Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2," and "Doom: The Dark Ages." "Battlefield 6" notably achieved high frame rates at 1440p with maximum quality settings, avoiding ray tracing to prioritize stability and performance across a range of hardware. "Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2" showcased steady frame rates and effective use of voxel-based software ray tracing, while "Doom: The Dark Ages" integrated hardware ray tracing with acceptable performance on supported GPUs.

"Monster Hunter Wilds" struggled with low frame rates even on mid-range hardware and visual compromises, while "Borderlands 4" demanded high-end systems yet delivered inconsistent performance. "Gears of War: Reloaded," a remaster of an older title, had difficulties maintaining smooth frame rates on handheld PCs. These disparities underscore challenges in optimization and hardware demands within contemporary game development.
