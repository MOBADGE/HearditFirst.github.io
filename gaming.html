FPS Genre Experiences a Shift Toward Casual Play and Quality Over Microtransactions

In 2025, the first-person shooter genre showed signs of transitioning away from the previously dominant focus on extreme skill-based competition and pervasive microtransaction-driven content. The success of Battlefield 6, which notably lacked ranked modes, reflects a growing appetite for more casual, accessible shooters with an emphasis on fun and low-pressure gameplay. Similarly, games like Arc Raiders have gained traction by fostering cooperative and social interactions rather than pure PvP intensity. The backlash against overpriced and thematically out-of-place cosmetics in major shooters influenced developers like EA and Activision to reconsider their approaches. Additionally, several traditionally competitive shooters introduced more relaxed modes to accommodate a broader audience. This trend also highlights that players remain willing to pay upfront for complete and polished shooter experiences rather than purely free-to-play models.
