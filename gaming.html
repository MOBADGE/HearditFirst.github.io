**Five New Steam Games to Note**

Several smaller or niche titles have appeared recently on Steam, offering a range of gameplay styles. *Homura Hime* is a third-person action slasher with anime aesthetics that recalls early 2000s games like *Devil May Cry*. *Planet of Lana 2* continues as a hand-painted cinematic platformer with expanded environments and a blend of puzzles and stealth. Racing sim fans have *iRacing Arcade*, a more casual and accessible spin-off involving management elements. For those who enjoy cozy farming and social simulators, *Shinehill* features an alien spy blending into a small town alongside activities like fishing and dungeon crawling. Finally, the horror-themed *Angel Engine* adapts an analog horror web series into a point-and-click adventure format with surgical gameplay. These releases reflect varied indie and mid-tier game offerings to explore beyond major studio launches.

**Ultima 8 Explored in Extensive CRPG Retrospective**

A detailed, three-hour documentary covering the classic CRPG *Ultima 8* has been released by YouTuber Majuular. Part of a larger series on the *Ultima* franchise, the video examines the game's development, story, and roleplaying elements in historical context, including its creator Richard Garriott’s influence. Though *Ultima 8* is not widely considered the series’ high point, the retrospective highlights its enduring impact on computer roleplaying games and its place in the evolution of the genre. For those interested in game history and CRPG design, this documentary provides substantial insight into a foundational series that shaped subsequent titles.

**Hytale Prioritizes Modding, Recruiting From Community Creators**

Hypixel’s development team for *Hytale* has publicly emphasized the importance of modding within the game’s ecosystem. The studio is currently running a large modding contest with a six-figure prize pool, reflecting their commitment to fostering creativity. Simon Collins-Laflamme, Hypixel’s founder, explained that many recent hires come directly from the modding community, ensuring the team includes active creators who contribute hands-on. This approach aims to keep development agile and closely connected to player interests. The focus on mod support indicates that *Hytale*’s longevity and evolution may be driven significantly by user-generated content, while also providing a pathway for talented community members to join the official team.

**Recreating Crazy Taxi’s Dreamcast Cityscapes in Browser**

An independent coder, known as Will, has recreated the iconic city environments from the Dreamcast-era arcade game *Crazy Taxi* for exploration within web browsers. Using original rendering methods, this project allows users to freely fly through detailed level geometry without the gameplay pressure of the original taxi-driving mechanics. This browser-accessible model has been added to noclip, an online video game level museum. It provides a fresh appreciation of *Crazy Taxi*’s design and landmarks, revealing previously unnoticed route possibilities. This project supports the preservation and new ways of experiencing classic game worlds ahead of the upcoming official *Crazy Taxi* sequel, which remains in development.

**Banquet for Fools Launches After Significant Update**

*Banquet for Fools*, an unusual claymation-style CRPG developed by a two-person team, has reached a full release following a large update. The game features a dark narrative where players investigate disappearances on a newly colonized island. Combat merges real-time and beat ’em up elements, with party members controlled automatically except for the character under direct player control. The game also allows alternative approaches to defeating enemies, such as imprisoning them rather than killing, which adds strategic and narrative depth. Priced affordably around $20 and recently discounted, this title offers a different twist on CRPG mechanics and artistic presentation from mainstream offerings.
