World of Warcraft Puzzle Advances with 10th Orb Unlocked

A year after players solved the original Felcycle puzzle in World of Warcraft, a new update connected to the freshly released Midnight expansion has revealed a 10th orb in the longstanding puzzle. Originally, nine orbs collected around Azeroth unlocked a motorcycle mount, but the discovery of this additional orb suggests the mystery is not yet complete. Players accidentally activated the 10th orb while exploring a different puzzle involving a secret group called the Mind-Seekers. This fresh development has reignited community efforts to decipher the next steps, highlighting Blizzard’s ongoing commitment to multi-expansion secrets that engage the dedicated player base.

Capcom Credits PC Market for Continued Growth in 2025

Capcom has reported its 12th consecutive year of growth, attributing much of this success to the PC gaming market. According to the company’s 2025 annual report, PC platforms now account for about half of Capcom's game sales and are expected to comprise an even larger share moving forward. The publisher has improved technical support for its PC titles, evidenced by recent patches addressing performance issues in games like Monster Hunter World. Additionally, Capcom plans to increase investment in movies to broaden its IP reach, building on successes such as the Resident Evil film franchise and the Devil May Cry Netflix series. This strategic focus underscores the growing importance of PC gaming and multimedia integration for major publishers.

Crystal Dynamics Conducts Fourth Layoff Round Amidst Tomb Raider Development

Crystal Dynamics has carried out its fourth round of layoffs within the past year, letting go of 20 employees across both development and operational roles. These cuts come despite the studio working on two new Tomb Raider titles, including a re-imagining and a new adventure game set to be published by Amazon Game Studios. The company stated this restructuring aligns team size with evolving development phases and long-term goals. Meanwhile, Amazon continues scaling back its gaming division, having recently canceled various projects and publishing deals. The repeated layoffs and corporate shifts raise concerns about the stability of the Tomb Raider franchise’s future development despite official commitments.

Counter-Strike 2 Introduces Significant Reloading Mechanic Change

Valve’s Counter-Strike 2 has implemented a major gameplay alteration: when players reload, any remaining ammunition in the current magazine is now lost rather than carried over. This move is intended to make the choice to reload carry more weight, adding a layer of tactical consideration previously absent. The update also adjusts ammo capacities for various weapons, with some rifles receiving more bullets and others, like the AWP and M4A1-S, having their magazine size reduced. The change has met widespread criticism from the player community, causing a notable dip in Steam review scores. Concurrently, Valve introduced new optional map guides and simplified custom game joining, which have seen a more positive reception.

Marathon’s Complex Competitive Mode Explained Through Interactive Maths Test

Bungie has released detailed documentation on Marathon’s competitive Ranked mode, which involves nuanced scoring and ranking mechanics uncommon in extraction shooters. To aid player understanding, Bungie created a "ranked calculation worksheet" featuring example problems that clarify concepts like score targets, penalties for failing to exit, and potential rank gains. The system factors in starting gear, loot collected, and player eliminations, balancing risk and reward. This educational approach aims to help players adapt to the mode's complexity and fully engage with its strategic depth, indicating Bungie's investment in competitive integrity and player clarity.

GPU VRAM Study: 8GB Still Usable but 16GB Models Offer Noticeably Better Performance

Recent real-world testing of budget GPUs shows that 8 GB graphics cards remain capable of running modern games in 2026, though 16 GB variants consistently deliver higher frame rates and smoother performance. Tests were conducted across several recent titles including Cyberpunk 2077, Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2, Arc Raiders, Resident Evil Requiem, and The Last of Us Part 1. The 16 GB cards achieved significantly better 1% lows and average frame rates, especially at higher resolutions and settings, illustrating the advantage of additional video memory. AMD's GPUs tend to use more VRAM than Nvidia's, which can further impact the performance differences between VRAM capacities.
