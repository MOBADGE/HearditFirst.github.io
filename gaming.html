**Peak Developers Respond to Player Demands on Updates**

The developers behind the surprise hit co-op climbing game *Peak* have addressed player complaints regarding the frequency and extent of updates. After a rapid rise in popularity last year, the game has seen three major updates, over 30 patches, and multiple hotfixes despite being developed by two small indie studios, Landfall and Aggro Crab. The studios emphasized that neither identifies as live service developers, and that updates should be viewed as a bonus rather than an entitlement. They also noted that while full customization is a large request, they remain open to specific player suggestions. The decision to wind down development this year reflects the small teams' limited resources and workload over the last busy period. This exchange highlights ongoing tensions between player expectations and the realities faced by indie developers in maintaining post-launch support.

**Modders Uncover Removed Food Consequence System in Crimson Desert**

Modding community members have revealed that Pearl Abyss' action RPG *Crimson Desert* contains a fully designed but inactive food consequence system. The system includes around 50 food skills affecting aspects such as temperature effects, elemental resistances, combat buffs, and immunities. Consuming more powerful food items allegedly had risks like drunkenness or poisoning. The complexity resembles mechanics seen in games like *Monster Hunter*. Developers seemingly cut this system, likely due to concerns about overwhelming the player with complexity on top of already intricate controls and inventory management. The mod includes difficulty presets that increase these effects. This discovery illustrates the decisions studios make to balance gameplay depth against accessibility and player experience.

**Guide to Completing Furtive Meetings Quest in Arc Raiders**

A new challenging quest called *Furtive Meetings* in the third-person shooter *Arc Raiders* requires players to visit three specific secret meeting spots within the Stella Montis map and extract with a required item. Locations include the southwest corner of Medical Research near a staircase, a ventilation shaft near the Robotic Sandbox B area, and the Cultural Archives staircase connection. The quest demands completing all objectives in a single round and safely extracting the quest item, with advice provided for using minimal loadouts to avoid risking loss. Detailed walkthroughs and map markers help players navigate the sprawling map. This addition signals the game’s ongoing content development and increased quest complexity.

**Linux Gaming Uptick and Steam Hardware Survey Insights**

The latest Steam Hardware Survey for March shows a notable rise in Linux users, increasing from about 2.1% to 5.3%, now outpacing macOS users. Arch Linux and Linux Mint continue to be popular distributions, with SteamOS (used on the Steam Deck) contributing to growth. Meanwhile, Windows 11 usage increased by 10% as Windows 10 dropped by 15%. In GPU rankings, the suspicious surge of Nvidia’s RTX 5070 in February reverted in March back to more typical figures, placing it fifth behind stable leaders like the RTX 3060. AMD’s newest RDNA 4 GPUs remain scarcely represented. Although Steam survey data has known inaccuracies, the trends indicate gradual shifts in PC gaming platform choices and hardware adoption.

**Dell XPS 14 Laptop Delivers Exceptional Battery Life**

Testing by Hardware Canucks reveals the new Dell XPS 14 laptop with an Intel Panther Lake Core Ultra 7 355 chip achieves an outstanding 43+ hours of battery life during web browsing tests. This performance substantially surpasses the latest 15-inch MacBook Air, which managed about 15 hours in similar tests. The key factors include the laptop’s variable refresh rate display, a high-capacity 70 Wh battery, and efficient Intel silicon. Video playback tests also favored the Dell. However, in gaming scenarios, the MacBook Air performed better, lasting 4 hours 10 minutes versus the Dell’s 2 hours 38 minutes, partly due to differences in integrated graphics. These results highlight significant progress in laptop power efficiency, important for portable gaming and productivity.
