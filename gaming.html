**Peglin Expands with New Act and Final Boss**

Nearly two years after Peglin’s official release, developer Red Nexus Games has delivered a substantial update dubbed Peglin 2.0. The new content adds a fourth act to the fantasy pachinko roguelike, introducing fresh enemies and challenging players with a final boss encounter. The update also expands progression options by adding an alternate path for unlocking playable classes, aiming for a steadier advancement system. Additionally, the patch includes new items linked to limited-time challenges exclusive to Act 4, alongside fresh music, achievements, relics, and technical improvements such as faster load times and better battle framerates. Peglin is currently available at a 40% discount until March 26.

**Half Sword Developer Addresses Early Access Reception**

Half Sword Games has responded to mixed player feedback following the early access release of their medieval duelling simulator Half Sword. The developers explained that the early access version’s combat system differs significantly from the popular tech demo showcased during last year's Steam Next Fest. The demo was based on an earlier build with simpler mechanics, while the current version incorporates more complex procedural animations and ongoing balancing, which can alter the feel of combat between updates. The team clarified the tech demo was never intended to represent the final game and reiterated that combat and controls will continue to evolve as development progresses. They also noted their focus on performance optimization and addressed concerns about their early access trailer showing features not present at launch, stating it was meant to tease future content.

**Ranger’s Path: National Park Simulator Offers Low-Key Gameplay**

Ranger’s Path: National Park Simulator, an early access title by Astragon Entertainment, puts players in the role of a park ranger managing maintenance tasks and visitor regulation within a large open world. Gameplay involves repairing damaged benches and signs, clearing fallen trees, and responding to radio calls about missing hikers or wildlife sightings. The game features a simple system of minigames simulating tasks like hammering and sawing, combined with a "Ranger Sense" mechanic that highlights nearby issues. A notable aspect is the ability to enforce park rules by checking permits and expelling visitors without proper authorization, giving players a level of control that players found satisfying. While repetitive at times, the game’s expansive map and relaxed consequences for inaction create a low-pressure experience.

**The Wheel of Time Franchise Expands with New Media Projects**

Iwot Studios, the owner of The Wheel of Time license, announced several new projects including a PC and mobile game, an animated series, and an animated feature film based on Robert Jordan's fantasy novels. These initiatives are separate from a previously revealed open-world, AAA action RPG currently in development, led by Craig Alexander, former president of Warner Bros Games. The studio aims to broaden The Wheel of Time universe across multiple platforms and media formats. Past adaptations, including the Amazon TV series, faced criticism and eventually cancellation after three seasons due to fan dissatisfaction with source material changes. The timeline for the new projects remains unclear, but developers emphasize that none constitute the final product yet.

**Screamer Review: Distinct Racing Experience with Mixed Driving Feel**

Milestone’s new arcade racer Screamer introduces a futuristic racing tournament infused with anime-inspired storytelling and diverse, internationally voiced characters. The narrative focuses on complex team dynamics and offers a stylistic presentation uncommon in racing games. The gameplay features a unique dual-stick control scheme for drifting, which aims to emulate its 1995 predecessor but may feel awkward and disorienting for some players. An added resource management system involving "Sync" and "Entropy" allows tactical use of speed boosts, shields, and attacks, shifting victory conditions towards resource mastery over traditional driving skill. While the game excels in character design, worldbuilding, and variety of race modes, its driving mechanics may not appeal to all players.

**Baldur’s Gate Mod Management Improved with Major Tool Update**

The tool now allows users to preview NPC portraits when mods overlap, tracks game engine limits for various modules to prevent conflicts, and includes a comprehensive preset featuring 475 mods with conflict resolution.
