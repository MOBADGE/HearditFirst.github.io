Crimson Desert Players Request Permanent Exotic Mounts

Since its launch last week, players of Pearl Abyss’s Crimson Desert have expressed disappointment that many of the game’s exotic mounts—such as bears, dodo-like kuku birds, and dragons—cannot be kept permanently. While earlier marketing trailers suggested these creatures could be domesticated as mounts, in practice, they can only be ridden temporarily after being defeated in combat or summoned briefly through quests. Only horses are accepted as permanent mounts in the stables. This design choice has drawn criticism from the community, who argue that, as a single-player experience, allowing permanent use of these mounts would not unbalance gameplay. The developers may revisit this system in response to player feedback, but currently, these mounts remain limited to short-term use.

Capcom Clarifies Use of AI in Game Development

During a February 2026 investor session summarized today, Capcom stated that it will not include AI-generated assets directly in its game content. However, the company plans to employ generative AI tools to improve efficiency and productivity in broader development processes like graphics, sound, and programming. This stance comes amid industry debates over AI’s role in creative work, especially after Nvidia’s DLSS 5 demo altered character appearances in Capcom’s Resident Evil Requiem, drawing criticism. Capcom did not specify precisely where it draws the line between AI-assisted development and final asset usage, reflecting ongoing challenges studios face in integrating these technologies while maintaining creative control.

Elon Musk Found Guilty of Misleading Twitter Investors

A California jury has ruled that Elon Musk made materially false or misleading statements in tweets during his 2022 acquisition of Twitter, which affected the company’s stock price. Musk’s tweets suggesting uncertainty about Twitter’s spam account figures led some investors to sell shares below the final purchase price of $54.20 per share. While the jury did not find Musk intended to defraud shareholders, the judge determined the tweets caused financial losses to investors who sold prematurely. Musk’s legal team plans to appeal. The case highlights the influence of public statements on stock markets and the regulatory scrutiny of social media figures involved in large transactions.

Baldur’s Gate 3 Romance Writing Balances Natural Interaction and Dialogue Limits

Writers for Baldur’s Gate 3 explained that the romantic relationship between characters Shadowheart and Lae’zel developed naturally because their narratives reflected similar arcs from differing perspectives. Creating believable romances within the RPG’s complex systems requires balancing player freedom with narrative coherence. To manage this, Larian Studios uses a reputation system influencing NPC reactions to player actions, limiting total breakdown in character relationships. At certain points, dialogue outcomes depend on dice rolls to maintain some unpredictability in romances. This approach helps manage the scope of writing required without overly restricting player choice or character consistency.

Crimson Desert Gold Bar Location Enables Early Banking and Investment

Players seeking to open a bank account early in Crimson Desert can steal a gold bar from Lioncrest Manor, located northwest of Hernand, near the city. This gold bar acts as the deposit required for bank registration, allowing players to earn interest and generate income through investments. Gaining entry to the manor involves climbing through a back window and retrieving the bar from a hidden compartment in the fireplace. Additional gold bars can be crafted later via a recipe found in the Spire of Insight puzzle. This system offers players an alternative money-making route beyond combat and theft, addressing early-game currency scarcity.

Nintendo to Release EU Version of Switch 2 with Replaceable Battery

Nintendo is launching a version of the Switch 2 in the European Union equipped with easily replaceable batteries for both the console and Joy-Con controllers. This move responds to new EU repairability regulations taking effect in 2027, which require portable electronics to support easy battery replacement by consumers without specialized tools. The current model with glued-in batteries will remain on sale outside the EU, where Nintendo does not recommend user battery replacement. This change demonstrates how regional repair laws influence hardware design and may pressure other handheld gaming PC manufacturers to adopt similar user-serviceable battery designs.
