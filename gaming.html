Skyrim Mod Adds Realistic Wind Effects to Weather and Environment

A new mod called Particle Wind by developer RavenKZP enhances the way weather and environmental elements behave in Skyrim. It makes fires, smoke, steam, waterfalls, and foliage respond realistically to wind, improving immersion during gameplay. For example, embers from alchemy burners swirl appropriately in a breeze, and lanterns tilt in strong gusts. The mod requires the Skyrim Script Extender (SKSE) and the Address Library for SKSE Plugins to function. Users can adjust the intensity of the wind effects via a configuration file. Part of a series of subtle yet effective mods by RavenKZP, Particle Wind highlights how small environmental details can enrich game atmospheres. This mod may appeal to players seeking a more atmospheric and dynamic Skyrim experience without drastic gameplay changes.

Allegations Surface Over Kickstarter Fund Misuse at Ashes of Creation Studio

Controversy surrounds the now-defunct MMO Ashes of Creation and its developer, Intrepid Studios. Investor Jason Caramanis alleged that founder Steven Sharif misappropriated millions in Kickstarter funds, spending on items such as private chefs, expensive cigars, antiques, and trading card websites. A YouTuber named NefasQS released a detailed video analysis based on financial records reportedly verified through multiple sources and former employees. The video highlights discrepancies including payments to the former mansion's owners and a private chef who allegedly catered only to the studio heads. Sharif denies misconduct, blaming the board for the project's collapse. The situation remains complicated, with ongoing debates about financial transparency in crowdfunded game development.

Modder Transforms Sekiro Into a Skateboarding Game

A new mod titled Jet Set Sekiro converts FromSoftware’s Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice into a skateboarding simulation inspired by the Tony Hawk series. Created by modder Ionian-MikiriyAKATotallyNotShinobi, it retools Sekiro’s animations and movement mechanics to allow skating around the Fountainhead Palace setting. Players collect spray paint cans to tag locations while performing tricks. This unusual crossover repurposes Sekiro’s action-focused gameplay into a leisurely, creative reinterpretation. It recalls past examples of combining skateboarding with other game universes. The mod exemplifies how community creations continue to expand and reshape popular titles in unexpected ways.
