Mozilla Criticizes Microsoft’s AI Integration, Highlights Firefox’s Control Options

Mozilla recently criticized Microsoft for aggressively embedding AI features—specifically the Copilot assistant—into Windows 11 and Microsoft 365 apps without clear user consent. In a blog post, Mozilla accused Microsoft of "going too far," noting issues such as the auto-installation of Copilot and the introduction of a physical Copilot key that is difficult to remap. The company also condemned Microsoft’s distribution tactics for its Edge browser, describing deliberate difficulties in changing the default browser as deceptive. This criticism echoes a prior antitrust complaint from Opera regarding the same concerns.

In contrast, Mozilla emphasized Firefox’s new AI Controls panel, introduced in Firefox 148, which allows users to enable or block AI enhancements explicitly. A ‘Block AI Enhancements’ switch remembers user preferences across updates, setting Firefox apart in terms of user control over integrated AI features. While Mozilla positions itself as cautious and user-focused with AI, the move also raises questions about how genuinely distinct its approach is from other big tech firms, given its own AI initiatives.

Overwatch Introduces Sierra, a Recon Damage Hero with Tracking and Drone Abilities

Blizzard is adding Sierra as the 51st hero in Overwatch with the upcoming Season 2: Summit update, releasing on April 14. Sierra is a Recon damage hero whose kit combines traits from previous characters like Sojourn, Widowmaker, and Freyja. She wields an automatic Helix Rifle that improves in accuracy with sustained fire and can mark enemies with a Tracking Shot, causing bullets to auto-track targets.

Sierra’s Anchor Drone offers mobility by enabling a grapple-like movement, and her Tremor Charge ability creates an area shockwave. Her ultimate, Trailblazer, deploys a bombing drone, and a passive ability allows her to detect enemies below half health after damaging them. Players can choose from minor and major perks to enhance her capabilities, such as faster bullet spread control and increased attack speed when using Tracking Shot. Sierra’s introduction adds a fast-paced, technically demanding option likely to impact Overwatch’s evolving meta and narrative focus.

Keychron Releases Open Hardware Files for Keyboards and Mice

Keychron has published comprehensive hardware design source files for its keyboard series (K, L, P, Q, and V) and gaming mice on GitHub, allowing users with technical skills to customize or create replacement parts such as cases, plates, and keycaps. The repository includes detailed guides on working with CAD software and encourages community engagement via an official Discord server.

This open approach supports repairability and customization, making it easier for users to modify or fix their devices without creating designs entirely from scratch. While printed circuit board (PCB) files are not included, users can still 3D print many components. Additionally, Keychron clarifies that accessories made from these designs may be sold commercially, but manufacturing and selling complete modified keyboards or mice is prohibited under their license. This move aligns with growing industry efforts to enhance hardware longevity and user control.

World of Warcraft’s Upcoming Patch Adds World Content and New Gearing Options

World of Warcraft’s next update, scheduled for April 21, will introduce several open-world activities and a new gearing system linked to a mysterious ally. The patch adds void assaults—weekly rotated open-world events attacking zones like Eversong and Zul’aman—and ritual sites featuring scalable difficulty where small groups combat naga and Twilight’s blade forces for rewards.

A spearfishing mini-game called “Abyss Anglers” also debuts, providing a more relaxed activity for gear upgrades and cosmetics. Additionally, Decimus’ Voidforge, a gearing mechanic tied to the ongoing Midnight storyline, becomes fully operational. Players can spend Nebulous Voidcores, collected through high-difficulty raids and dungeons, to receive random loot appropriate to their specialization. The patch thus expands world content for solo and group players and broadens endgame gearing choices, continuing WoW’s trend of linking gameplay systems to narrative elements.
