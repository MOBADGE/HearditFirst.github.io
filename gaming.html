Star Citizen Studio Discloses January Data Breach, Faces Criticism for Delayed Notice

Cloud Imperium Games, developer of Star Citizen, revealed this week that it experienced a data breach on January 21, 2026. According to a statement posted on the Roberts Space Industries website, a “systematic and sophisticated attack” gained unauthorized, read-only access to some backup systems containing limited user personal data such as metadata, contact details, usernames, names, and dates of birth. The studio emphasized that no passwords, financial, or payment information were accessed, and no data alteration occurred.

Despite these reassurances, the breach notification was quietly presented to players only as an in-game pop-up, without email alerts or social media announcements. This led to player frustration over the six-week delay and the low-profile disclosure. Some community members raised concerns about the unspecified “metadata” and the potential risk of phishing using the exposed information, urging caution and recommending security measures like changing passwords and enabling two-factor authentication.

World of Warcraft: Midnight Easter Egg References Outer Wilds

Players exploring the World of Warcraft: Midnight expansion discovered an abandoned campsite in the Voidstorm zone that appears to be a tribute to the 2019 space exploration puzzle game Outer Wilds. Positioned at coordinates (51,83), the campsite features a campfire, a chair, musical instruments including a banjo, and a marshmallow roasting stick—elements reminiscent of a notable scene from Outer Wilds where the player repeatedly awakens at a campfire during time loops.

This subtle reference connects two games centered on exploration and mystery and showcases Blizzard’s ongoing tradition of hidden nods to other games and pop culture. While accessible only near the end of the Midnight campaign, it may encourage players unfamiliar with Outer Wilds to explore the acclaimed title.

Highguard PvP Shooter to Shut Down Next Week Due to Low Player Base

Wildlight Entertainment announced the closure of their team-based PvP shooter Highguard, with servers scheduled to go offline on March 12, 2026, barely over a month after its launch. Despite attracting over two million players initially, current player numbers have dropped dramatically from a peak of nearly 100,000 concurrent users to around 200, making the game financially unsustainable.

The developer expressed gratitude for the community’s support but cited an inability to maintain a long-term player base. In an ironic note, Highguard’s final update will include new content such as a playable character, weapon, account progression, and skill trees—features that the studio had planned for the future. Wildlight had previously laid off most staff just over two weeks after launch, and with mixed reviews and declining interest, the shutdown marks an unfortunate end for the title.

The Sims 4 Launches Official Marketplace and Creator Program for Paid Custom Content

Electronic Arts revealed the upcoming Sims 4 Marketplace, launching March 17 on PC and Mac and later on consoles, which will enable players to browse and buy new content packs created by approved content creators called Makers. The Marketplace functions as an in-game store that uses a new virtual currency called Moola. Creators set prices for their "Maker Packs" and receive 30% of sales revenue.

The Sims Maker Program, opening applications on March 5, allows creators meeting specified criteria to publish their work directly through EA, providing a new official route for monetizing custom content. This move echoes Bethesda’s Creation Club but arrives amid a different modding landscape. While free mods can still be distributed elsewhere, only content approved through the Maker Program can be sold on the Marketplace. The system aims to clarify guidelines around custom content monetization and expand access to official user-created content, including for console players.

Bungie Details Marathon’s First Seasonal Content and Confirms Regular Progression Resets

With the launch of the sci-fi shooter Marathon imminent on March 5, Bungie shared plans for its first season, titled "Death is the First Step," running through June. Season 2, scheduled for June to August, will add new maps, weapons, and gameplay systems.
