Solasta 2 Early Access Launches with Improvements Over Original

Solasta 2 entered early access recently, offering a range of small but meaningful improvements compared to the original game. Highlights include enhanced voice acting, updated writing, and integration of the 2024 Dungeons & Dragons rules, which add more tactical depth to weapon choices. The game features a party of four adopted siblings of varied ancestries, with more coming in future updates. Its open-world exploration uses a hex grid with permanent and temporary encounters, increasing freedom compared to the linear map of the first title.

Combat remains a core focus, with battles emphasizing tactical positions, terrain use, and status effects. Players can switch weapon loadouts freely to accommodate spellcasting and combat strategy. While the character creator offers more balanced options, some limitations remain, such as difficulty creating distinct age appearances. Roadmap plans include adding more classes, co-op multiplayer, crafting, leveling beyond four, and a faction system. Currently, Solasta 2 mainly appeals to dedicated CRPG fans willing to replay early content as development continues.

Dead by Daylight’s K-pop Chapter Focuses on Quality of Life Over New Killer

Dead by Daylight’s latest chapter, All-Kill: Comeback, diverges from usual patterns by not introducing a new killer. Instead, it brings a survivor, a map for an existing killer, and major reworks, marking a shift in developer Behaviour Interactive's approach. This change is part of a trial focusing on quality of life improvements to address the game’s growing complexity after 10 years.

Senior Creative Director Dave Richard explained that the reworked content aims to make the game more accessible without sacrificing update frequency. Examples include customizable Aura colors, a frequently requested feature. The team remains open to experimenting with chapter content, possibly adding new modes or events instead of strictly new killers. While some players may be disappointed by fewer new killers, this approach could allocate resources toward a healthier long-term game experience.

Crimson Desert Launch Schedule and Preload Details

Crimson Desert, the action RPG from Pearl Abyss, launches worldwide on March 19, 2026, with release times staggered by region to synchronize its global availability. For example, players on the U.S. West Coast can begin playing at 3 pm PDT, while Australian players will access it the following morning.

The game is available for preload on Steam, requiring over 92 GB initially and 150 GB total disk space at launch. Crimson Desert offers a large game world and diverse activities, from combat and exploration to crafting and trading. Preloading allows players to be ready for launch without delay.

Thomas the Tank Engine Game Encounters Unpredictable Physics Glitches

Thomas & Friends: Wonders of Sodor, released on Steam, has drawn attention for unusual physics glitches that cause the familiar trains to be unexpectedly launched into the air or violently thrown off tracks. These incidents are due to the game’s use of Unreal Engine 5 physics, resulting in unpredictable and often humorous outcomes.

Despite the erratic physics behavior, the game has received mostly positive early reviews. Its viral presence on social media is largely due to players sharing clips of the trains’ unexpected ejections. The game allows players to operate trains and complete tasks on the North Western Railway, presenting a mix of simulation and surreal moments.

Tomb Raider Remastered Developer Addresses Update Criticism and AI Claims

Aspyr Studios responded to criticism following the recent Challenge Mode update for Tomb Raider 1-3 Remastered, which introduced new character outfits but was met with negative user reviews citing poor quality and technical issues. The studio emphasized that fixing bugs and texture problems is their top priority and denied using generative AI to create the new outfits, countering widespread speculation.

While no timeline for the fixes has been provided, Aspyr committed to more updates addressing technical issues across platforms. The developer’s denial of AI involvement has not alleviated all skepticism among players.
