Global CPU Shortage Deepens as Industry Awaits Intel’s 18A Chips

A recent report highlights an escalating global shortage of CPUs, which is currently more severe than the ongoing memory chip shortage. Key models from both Intel and AMD are becoming increasingly difficult to obtain regardless of price. This scarcity is impacting PC manufacturers, especially those assembling enterprise-grade systems reliant on Intel processors. The situation has persisted since 2025, with hopes pinned on Intel’s new 18A process node chips to alleviate the bottleneck.

However, the 18A node has faced production challenges, including low yields and profitability concerns. Recent improvements in manufacturing have prompted Intel’s CEO to consider offering these chips to external customers, potentially increasing supply. Despite this, analysts predict that high component costs might dampen consumer demand for PCs in 2026. The surge in AI server requirements continues to strain processor availability, suggesting the shortage may persist for some time.

Pragmata Sells Over One Million Copies After Four Years in Development

Capcom’s newly released third-person shooter, Pragmata, has reached over one million sales in just two days post-launch. The game faced multiple delays during its four-year development cycle, with challenges in integrating its unique combat and hacking mechanics. Directed primarily by younger developers within Capcom, Pragmata showcases a dystopian sci-fi setting and a complex gameplay system. Early reception includes an 87 rating from PC Gamer, which described it as “a cult classic in the making.”

Pragmata's demo previously achieved more than two million downloads, helping to build anticipation despite the prolonged wait. The strong sales performance indicates that the game has successfully connected with an audience, although future plans, including potential sequels, remain unconfirmed.

RuneScape: Dragonwilds Introduces Fishing Skill Tomorrow

Fishing, a traditional and highly popular skill in RuneScape, will finally be added to the survival crafting game RuneScape: Dragonwilds on April 21. The absence of fishing had been noted as a significant omission at the game's initial release. The upcoming update will allow players to fish using rods and nets around water bodies and even employ magic-based fishing methods.

A new fishing skill in Dragonwilds features a level cap of 99 and rewards dedicated players with a unique cape. Additionally, players can create mounted trophies of prized catches to display in their bases. The inclusion of fishing aligns Dragonwilds more closely with core RuneScape gameplay elements and is likely to enhance player engagement.

Steam Week in Review: Windrose Outperforms Pragmata in Player Numbers

Despite Pragmata’s strong launch, the survival pirate game Windrose has outperformed it in terms of concurrent players and sales revenue on Steam. Having released on April 14, Windrose sold one million copies in six days and reached a peak of over 220,000 concurrent players, compared to Pragmata’s roughly 69,000. Windrose’s success shows a notable interest in pirate-themed survival games, which often have a polarizing reception.

Additional new releases such as Mouse: P.I. For Hire and Road to Vostok have seen modest success, while pre-orders for Forza Horizon 6 exceed 500,000. The data reflects a diverse gaming landscape with varied player interests across genres.

Magic: The Gathering’s New Set ‘Secrets of Strixhaven’ Focuses on College Mechanics

The upcoming Magic: The Gathering set, Secrets of Strixhaven, emphasizes unique mechanics tied to five magical colleges. Each college introduces a distinct gameplay mechanic triggered under specific conditions—such as life gain or casting certain spells. For example, the Witherbloom college features "Infusion," which activates upon gaining life, while Prismari’s "Opus" triggers on instant or sorcery spells.

This design aims to deepen strategic differentiation between factions and encourages players to explore new deck-building possibilities. The set will be available in Magic Arena on April 21 and in physical formats on April 24.

Archive Offers Free Access to Historic Computing Magazines and PC Gamer Demos

For those interested in computing history, the Internet Archive now hosts over 200 issues of Byte magazine, a seminal publication covering the rise of personal computing from 1975 through the late 1990s. Byte’s archives reveal technological optimism and provide context for today’s computing landscape.

Additionally, the archive includes 758 PC Gamer demo discs, preserving decades of game demos and extras on formats ranging from floppy disks to CDs.
