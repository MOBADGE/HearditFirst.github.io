Anthropic CEO Criticizes Nvidia and US Government Over AI Chip Sales to China

At the World Economic Forum in Davos, Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei openly criticized Nvidia and the US government for permitting the sale of advanced AI chips, specifically Nvidia's H200 superchips, to Chinese companies. Amodei described this decision as "crazy," comparing it to selling nuclear weapons to North Korea. His primary concern stems from national security implications, given the rapid advancement of AI capabilities and the risk of ceding technological advantages. Anthropic, a prominent AI company backed by Nvidia and Microsoft investments, stresses that the US currently has a significant lead in chip manufacturing. This development fuels ongoing debates about balancing commercial interests with geopolitical risks amid the global AI race.

Kioxia Reports Fully Committed NAND Flash Production for 2026

Japanese memory manufacturer Kioxia announced that its entire NAND flash production capacity for 2026 is already sold out due to strong demand driven largely by the AI industry. Shunsuke Nakato, managing director of the memory unit, noted that many companies continue heavy investment in AI to remain competitive, prolonging supply shortages at least into 2027. Although Kioxia honors existing contracts rather than prioritizing buyers by price or timing, customers face price increases averaging 30% year-over-year. The company also expressed reservations about QLC flash technology's durability and performance, favoring newer eighth-generation NAND flash instead. Despite plans to increase efficiency and open a new factory later this year, supply tightness is expected to persist.
