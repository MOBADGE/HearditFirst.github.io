Lort Developers Adjust Difficulty Following Player Complaints

The recent launch of *Lort*, a fantasy roguelike inspired by *Risk of Rain 2*, has stirred discussions on game difficulty. Developed by Big Distraction, *Lort* was intended as a challenging single-player experience, with a design philosophy centered on learning through repeated failure. However, this approach led to about 28% negative reviews on Steam, citing the game as too difficult. In response, the developers released an update tweaking certain aspects to ease the difficulty curve, such as making early enemies like Goblin Lobbers less lethal and more predictable, and introducing tutorial prompts to guide players in character progression. Despite these changes, the team emphasized that *Lort* remains a demanding roguelike primarily balanced around solo play, and multiplayer modes likely ease the challenge. The update also addressed ongoing issues with multiplayer connectivity. These adjustments aim to make the game more accessible without compromising its core design.

Out of Action Brings Agile Cyborg Combat to Early Access

*Out of Action*, a new PvP multiplayer shooter by Doku Games, entered early access this week. The game features cybernetic ninjas engaging in fast-paced combat reminiscent of *Titanfall* and *Max Payne*. Players can perform advanced maneuvers like slide-dodging, wall-running, and first-person somersaults, creating fluid and acrobatic gameplay. Notably, the title introduces an "advanced multiplayer bullet-time" mechanic influenced by player positioning and combat performance, which applies selectively within players' line of sight. The current version has garnered mostly positive reviews, recognizing its core gameplay while noting the need for additional content. The developers plan to expand the game throughout its expected 12 to 18 months in early access with more weapons, game modes, and improved environments. This release comes amid an environment where mainstream multiplayer shooters face criticism, highlighting indie developers' growing presence in the space.

Star Birds Adds Food Production and Alien Life in New Update

The factory management sim *Star Birds* from Toukana Interactive has released its Flora & Fauna update in early access. This expansion introduces organic resource production including alien plants and food crafting, diversifying the previously synthetic-focused gameplay. Players can explore the new green star system Paradeira, cultivating crops and cooking meals in establishments like bakeries and the Bird Restaurant. Some new plants, like Volt Shrooms, serve as alternative energy sources, while alien creatures with tentacles add strategic obstacles by occupying construction space. Players can use food to lure these creatures away. The update also features three new longer, more complex levels and user-generated level creation. The developers are testing further additions, with the game currently available at a 25% discount until early February.
