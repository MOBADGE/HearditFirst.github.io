Old-School MMO *Project: Gorgon* Officially Launches Version 1.0

*Project: Gorgon*, a low-fi, old-school style MMO, has exited Steam Early Access with a 1.0 update. The key addition is Statehelm, a capital city described by the developers as their largest and most complex map, offering 200 quests and expanding character progression up to level 100. The update also introduces new character customization options, changes to combat experience gains, crafting improvements, and economy adjustments.

Known for its open-ended skill system and minimal handholding, *Project: Gorgon* appeals to players seeking a more traditional MMORPG experience with unique systems like NPC relationships and environmental interactions. The launch price includes a 25% discount until February 4, and a free demo allows progression up to level 15.

This release signifies the continuing niche appeal and viability of classical MMO designs amidst a market dominated by more modern, streamlined titles.

Google Introduces Project Genie AI, Raising Industry Questions

Google unveiled Project Genie 3, an AI model that generates interactive 3D worlds dynamically rather than relying on static pre-built environments. Available as part of a $250 per month subscription, Project Genie simulates physics and real-time interactions, enabling users to create and explore virtual spaces featuring consistent states and plausible physical behavior.

Media and investors responded with mixed reactions; some video game stocks declined possibly due to perceived disruption. However, early examples produced by The Verge were criticized as crude and unfinished, replicating rough Nintendo-like environments but lacking polish and narrative coherence. The AI cannot yet match handcrafted games in quality and depth.
