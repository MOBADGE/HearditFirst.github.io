The Simpsons Hit & Run Revival Remains Possible

Matt Selman, executive producer of The Simpsons, addressed the longstanding cult appeal of the 2007 game *The Simpsons: Hit & Run* in a recent interview. While Selman prefers this 2007 title for its strong storytelling, he acknowledged that the game’s unexpected success has kept interest alive. When asked about a potential remaster or revival, Selman responded with a cautious "never say never," indicating the developers are aware of fan demand but have no concrete plans at present. This leaves open the possibility for fans to access or revisit one of the best-reviewed Simpsons games, though nothing official is announced.

*Why it matters:* The original *Hit & Run* remains a fan-favorite game for its blend of open-world adventure and humor. Having it reintroduced or remastered could preserve a unique adaptation of the franchise and satisfy longtime followers.

Samson: Open-City Crime Game Faces Questions Ahead of Release

Samson, an open-city game from Liquid Swords—led by *Just Cause* creator Cristofer Sundberg—is set to launch on April 8. The gameplay focuses on managing a debt in a gritty city through missions involving driving and brawling. While the concept combines action points and cash quotas to encourage quick choices, recent trailers have drawn mixed reactions due to underwhelming combat animations and concerns over a sparse city population. The visual style and driving mechanics appear promising, reminiscent of Sundberg’s past projects, but the combat and mission variety raise doubts about whether the game will deliver a fully rounded experience.

*Why it matters:* Samson aims to revive the open-city genre popular in the Xbox 360 era but scaled down from blockbuster titles like *Grand Theft Auto*. How well it performs could influence the viability of smaller-scale open-world crime games.

Total Chaos Remake Revisits Notorious Doom Mod with Mixed Results

*Total Chaos* is a full remake of a well-known *Doom 2* mod by Sam Prebble, launched recently on Steam using Unreal Engine 5. The game blends survival horror and FPS elements with a bleak atmosphere set in the industrial town of Fort Oasis. It offers maze-like levels, environmental puzzles, and psychological horror themes. While the remake impresses with its atmospheric world and level design, its combat—especially melee—feels lacking in impact and variety. Inventory management and resource abundance also reduce tension. A “Survivalist” mode and revamped new game plus provide a tougher challenge, but the core experience struggles to match the original mod’s legacy.

*Why it matters:* This project highlights the challenges of transforming ambitious mods into standalone games. It also showcases Prebble’s unique style, though combat and resource balance issues limit its broader appeal.

Embark Studios Responds to AI Voice Criticism in Arc Raiders

Embark Studios CEO Patrick Söderlund spoke publicly about the studio’s use of AI-generated voices in *Arc Raiders*, a third-person extraction shooter. Following player complaints about the artificial quality of AI voices, Embark has re-recorded many lines with professional actors post-launch. Söderlund emphasized that real actors still provide better quality and that AI is primarily used as a tool during development for internal testing and iteration rather than to replace humans entirely. The studio plans to continue using AI where it saves production time but appears committed to maintaining a human touch in final voice acting.

*Why it matters:* This reflects ongoing industry debates over AI’s role in game development, balancing efficiency gains against concerns about quality and the value of human performance.

Slay the Spire 2 Hits 3 Million Sales in First Week

Mega Crit’s *Slay the Spire 2*, following the success of the original deckbuilding roguelike, has sold 3 million copies within its first week of Early Access. The game has already seen 25 million runs played, indicating strong player engagement. Developers thanked their team despite the intense workload and have plans for future updates, including Steam Workshop support and accessibility features. Additionally, the physical board game expansion *Downfall* is scheduled for release on March 24.

*Why it matters:* Strong early sales and active playtime show sustained interest in the *Slay the Spire* franchise and reinforce the popularity of deckbuilder roguelikes. Ongoing support suggests the game will evolve with community involvement.
