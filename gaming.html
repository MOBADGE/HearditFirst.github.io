Marvel Rivals Expands Beyond Shooter Genre

NetEase's creative director Guangyun Chen revealed plans for Marvel Rivals to evolve from a typical 6v6 PvP hero shooter into what he described as "a comprehensive Marvel 'moving anime' experience." While the phrase remains vague, more concrete changes include the introduction of new PvE modes and a tie-in event called Path to Doomsday, connected to the upcoming Avengers: Doomsday movie. This event will bring five updates inspired by the Infinity Saga. Previously, the game experimented with PvE during last year's Marvel Zombies Halloween event, though player reception to enemy toughness varied. The shift signals NetEase’s intention to diversify gameplay and keep the title thematically connected to current Marvel releases.

Wizards of the Coast Uploads Classic Dungeons & Dragons Cartoon

Wizards of the Coast has started sharing episodes of the 1980s Dungeons & Dragons animated series on YouTube, releasing two to three episodes every Friday. The series, co-produced by Marvel Productions and animated by Toei, features voice talents like Peter Cullen and Frank Welker. It is notable for its period-specific style and storytelling, reflecting the era's cultural sensibilities, some of which are now seen as outdated or controversial. The upload serves both as nostalgic content for longtime D&D fans and a cultural artifact illustrating the era’s approach to children's animation and fantasy.

Fighting Game YouTuber Offers $10,000 Bounty for Marvel vs. Capcom 3 Netcode Upgrade

YouTuber and fighting game streamer Maximilian Christiansen, known as Maximilian Dood, announced a $10,000 bounty for modders who can implement rollback netcode in Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3. The game currently relies on delay-based netcode, which is less responsive and can lead to frustrating online experiences. Rollback netcode predicts player inputs to reduce perceived lag and has become a standard in newer fighting games. Christiansen, who has previously supported mods with substantial funding, aims to improve the game’s long-term online viability beyond existing third-party solutions like Parsec. This effort highlights the ongoing community demand for technical improvements in older competitive games.

Developers Address AI Art Concerns for Graveyard Keeper 2

Following criticism from fans who felt that the promotional art for Graveyard Keeper 2 appeared to be generated by AI, Lazy Bear's CTO Slava Cherkasov responded publicly. Cherkasov clarified that the studio is not using AI to create art or assets for the game. While he personally experiments with AI tools outside the project, the official art production is handled by a team of about eight artists without AI assistance. Fan reactions remain mixed, with some expressing relief and others remaining skeptical until the final game is released. The situation reflects wider industry and community debates about the role of AI in creative processes.

Rockstar Games Faces Ransom Demand After Data Breach

Rockstar Games confirmed a security breach involving access to a limited amount of non-material company information hosted on its outsourced Snowflake cloud storage system. The hacking group ShinyHunters claims to have exploited credentials from a prior breach of a third-party analytics service, Anodot, to enter Rockstar’s systems without directly breaking Snowflake’s security. The group has demanded a ransom, threatening to leak the data by April 14 if their demands are not met. Rockstar stated the breach does not impact players or its organization. This incident recalls the major GTA6 leak in 2022 and underscores ongoing risks associated with third-party vendor security in the gaming industry.

Proton Experimental Build Improves Capcom Classics on Steam Deck and Linux

A recent update to Proton Experimental has enhanced compatibility for a number of older Capcom games on Steam Deck and Linux platforms. Titles including the original Resident Evil, Resident Evil 2, Dino Crisis, and Dino Crisis 2 have been promoted to “playable” status, making it easier for players to enjoy these classic survival horror games on portable hardware. Although some issues remain, such as FMV problems in Dino Crisis 2, the update represents progress in preserving and supporting access to retro games on modern systems. This aligns with ongoing community and developer efforts to keep older games playable outside their original hardware environments.
