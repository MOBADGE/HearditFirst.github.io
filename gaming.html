GTA Chain Game: An Evolving International Community

The GTA Chain Game, a unique community-driven multiplayer relay through classic Grand Theft Auto games, continues to thrive in 2026. Originating as a "save file passing" exercise, it now involves hundreds of players worldwide who take turns progressing through missions until games reach full completion. According to organizer GTAKid, the project faces challenges due to the aging nature of the games but has welcomed new and casual players alike. The format’s low-commitment style allows participants to join for individual missions rather than entire playthroughs, fostering inclusive participation.

The community recently embraced GTA 4 and its expansions, highlighting the diversity of gameplay experiences across the series. With GTA 6 anticipated later this year, the Chain Game group plans to extend their relay format to the newest title, although they first intend to explore it casually before deciding how to structure a GTA 6 Chain Game. The continued dedication underscores a sustained, collaborative engagement with the franchise's legacy titles and the growing anticipation surrounding the upcoming release.

Deltarune Chapter 5 Nears Final Testing Phase

Toby Fox, creator of the RPG Deltarune, announced that professional testing for Chapter 5 has begun, with formal testing scheduled to start on April 1. The Japanese translation quality assurance is expected to finish in April as well. Most of the development team has moved on from active production of Chapter 5, focusing now on bug fixes and translation work. Fox indicated the testing and approval process for console releases will take an indefinite amount of time, with no further updates expected until release plans solidify.

This update signals that Chapter 5 is approaching completion and release, which fans anticipate following long waits for prior chapters. Fox previously predicted a 2026 release, which would mark a shorter gap compared to earlier chapters. The ongoing episodic rollout means players should expect continued incremental releases rather than a complete series drop.

Steam Spring Sale Highlights Human Artists

Valve’s recent Steam Spring Sale has been well-received for its use of human artists behind its seasonal sale graphics. The campaign artwork, including animation and banner designs, was created by Tiffany Diep and Thanhuki, whose contributions were explicitly credited by Steam on social media. This transparency stands out amid increasing industry discussions about AI-generated content and its disclosure.

The acknowledgment of human artists has drawn positive community reaction, praising Steam for supporting traditional artwork over generative AI methods. Given controversies around AI use in digital storefronts, this move demonstrates Valve’s emphasis on craftsmanship in visual presentation for sales events.

World of Warcraft’s Midnight Expansion and Class Balance Concerns

World of Warcraft’s recent Midnight expansion implemented reductions to combat add-ons and a streamlining of class rotations intended to simplify gameplay. However, this approach has led to an uneven damage output experience, particularly noticeable outside raid environments. Unlike Final Fantasy 14’s tightly synchronized two-minute burst windows, WoW classes now exhibit bursty damage phases followed by extended low-output periods, which can feel unsatisfying during open-world content.

This pattern repeats an ongoing MMO design challenge where burst-focused abilities complicate balance outside coordinated group play. With WoW featuring significant solo and world activities, the current state risks frustrating players during less structured gameplay. Blizzard may need to revisit balance adjustments to provide smoother performance across all content types.

154 GB ‘Biblically Accurate Skyrim’ Mod Pack Tested on Linux

A recent test installing Curseadelica, an extensive 154 GB mod pack for Skyrim, demonstrated the limits of modding capacity, especially on Linux systems. Curseadelica dramatically alters Skyrim’s visuals, characters, quests, and environments, creating an intense and surreal experience. While installation succeeded and the mod ran briefly, it frequently crashed and ultimately stopped launching.

The experiment highlights both the potential and challenges of massive modding projects on non-Windows platforms. Skyrim’s enduring moddability remains a strong point for the game, but compatibility and stability issues persist with highly ambitious mods such as Curseadelica.

Heroes of Science and Fiction Marks Sci-Fi Spin on HoMM

Players eager for new Heroes of Might and Magic content can try "Heroes of Science and Fiction," a sci-fi-themed turn-based strategy game now fully released on Steam following 18 months in early access. The game replaces traditional fantasy factions with futuristic alternatives like cybernetic moles and religious insects. It features multiple campaigns and introduces multiplayer support, allowing online, LAN, hot-seat, or remote play.
