Leon Kennedy’s Marital Status Confirmed by Capcom

Capcom has officially confirmed that Resident Evil’s protagonist Leon S. Kennedy is married, as revealed in several cutscenes and concept art from *Resident Evil Requiem*. While the identity of Leon’s spouse remains undisclosed, speculation centers around long-time associates Ada Wong or Claire Redfield. Director Koshi Nakanishi hinted that the ring symbolizes Leon having “a place to go home to” after his intense battles. This confirmation also establishes Requiem’s “good” ending as canon. The revelation matters to fans invested in Leon’s character development and signals that future story expansions could shed more light on his personal life. Additionally, *Resident Evil Requiem* recently hit a milestone of over six million copies sold, marking it as the fastest-selling title in the series.

Indie Developer’s Emotional Success After Years of Effort

An indie solo developer known as Cakez shared an emotional moment after discovering his game *Tangy HD* earned $250,000 in its first week on Steam. Having worked on the title for four years, Cakez streamed the moment he saw the sales report, expressing disbelief and gratitude. The game has received positive reviews, with 89% of players giving it favorable feedback. This story highlights the challenges indie developers face and how perseverance can lead to significant, if rare, financial success. It also underscores the impact of community support in the indie gaming sector.

Take-Two CEO Opposes Ads in Paid Games

Take-Two Interactive’s CEO Strauss Zelnick stated he is against introducing advertisements into premium, full-priced games, describing such a move as “unfair” to consumers. While acknowledging the presence of ads in free-to-play titles and their fit in sports games like NBA 2K (which simulates real-life stadium advertising), Zelnick dismissed the idea of interstitial ads in games players purchase at a premium. This stance contrasts with predictions from analysts that in-game advertising would become more widespread as publishers look for revenue streams. Zelnick’s position is notable given Take-Two’s influence in the industry and signals a reluctance to compromise the experience of paid titles with intrusive monetization.

Meta to Shut Down VR Horizon Worlds, Focus on Mobile

Meta announced it will shut down the VR version of *Horizon Worlds* on June 15, 2026, removing it from the Quest Store on March 31. The mobile app version of Horizon Worlds will continue to operate independently. This decision follows Meta’s shift in strategy to focus more narrowly on mobile platforms while still investing in VR through support for third-party developers. The closure excludes several official VR experiences and removes benefits tied to Meta Horizon Plus subscriptions. Meta’s move is part of broader reshuffling within the company, following recent VR studio closures and layoffs, reflecting the challenges of sustaining VR social platforms.

PlayerUnknown Productions Hosts In-Studio Modding Event

PlayerUnknown Productions recently held a two-day in-person modding jam involving its developers working on mods for their game *Prologue: Go Wayback*. The event encouraged teams to experiment with new mechanics, environments, and total conversions, including projects that transformed the game’s biome from woodland to desert or turned it into a horror experience. Devs created gameplay innovations like new vehicles, environmental hazards, and unique challenges such as a black hole entity that influences player movement. The modjam serves as both a creative outlet for devs and a testbed for ideas that might influence future updates, reflecting a commitment to community-oriented development and fostering a modifiable game ecosystem.

Starfinder: Afterlight Shows Promise but Needs Work

Early impressions from a closed beta playtest of *Starfinder: Afterlight* reveal a game with strong potential, especially for fans of the Pathfinder 2e tabletop system on which it is based. The game features appealing hand-drawn visuals and voice acting from notable actors. Combat mechanics align well with the source material, emphasizing tactical movement and action economy. However, the current build lacks several key actions and tutorials critical for players unfamiliar with the system. User interface and feature polish remain work in progress, and the game will need improvement before its planned public demo this summer. Despite these shortcomings, *Afterlight* could become a significant addition to the CRPG genre if the developers address these issues.
