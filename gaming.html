GameStop’s Failed Attempt at a Steam Competitor

Larry Kuperman, former GameStop executive and key figure behind Impulse, GameStop’s now-defunct Steam competitor, reflected on why the platform never succeeded. Originating from Stardock’s early digital distribution efforts, Impulse launched in 2008 and was acquired by GameStop in 2011. However, GameStop’s management at the time underestimated the shift to digital, treating it as a temporary trend and placing continued faith in physical sales. This strategic misstep contributed to Impulse’s shutdown in 2014. Kuperman noted that Steam’s success largely came from fostering community features and offering ease of access for developers, enabling a thriving indie ecosystem. While Impulse lacked the scale and vision Steam had, Kuperman credits platforms like GOG also for preserving and supporting games outside of mainstream distribution channels.

Mega Crit Responds to Slay the Spire 2’s Steam Review Backlash

Mega Crit’s co-founder Casey Yano addressed the recent wave of negative Steam reviews following a beta balance patch for Slay the Spire 2. Despite over 9,000 negative reviews within 24 hours, Yano was not personally upset by the backlash. He explained that players, especially those in China, often find Steam reviews their only means of providing direct feedback due to regional internet restrictions that limit access to discussion forums or official channels. Yano acknowledged the frustrations players have about not being heard directly by developers and expressed a desire to improve communication with international audiences. He also highlighted the limitations of Steam’s review system in capturing nuanced opinions, emphasizing the complexity behind player feedback.

Games Workshop’s Warhammer 40,000 Musical April Fools’ Joke

Games Workshop released "The Emperor Protects: A Warhammer 40,000 Musical," an elaborate and well-produced April Fools’ joke showcasing live-action performances of Warhammer characters singing thematic songs. The musical video features costumes, rehearsals, and production values that suggest a genuine stage show, albeit one tied to the Warhammer universe’s grimdark setting. While clearly a joke, the dedication to the concept has impressed fans, sparking speculation about a possible real production in future years. The project reflects the company’s willingness to experiment with community engagement and creative content beyond traditional tabletop and gaming formats.

Revisiting Call of Duty: Modern Warfare (2019)

A retrospective look at Call of Duty: Modern Warfare (2019) highlights why it is still regarded as the series’ last truly great installment by some players. Praised for its realistic presentation, detailed environments, and impactful sound design, it represents a high point before subsequent entries moved toward more fantastical or cosmetic-heavy approaches. The game’s streamlined Create-a-Class system enforced meaningful trade-offs in weapon customization, contrasting with the more open-ended setups of later titles. The launch also preceded the wide integration of Warzone, which entangled multiplayer modes and complicated the franchise’s progression paths. Despite its age, Modern Warfare 2019 remains popular on Steam, especially following a recent price drop aimed at drawing players.

Linux Gaming Adoption Shows Mixed Interest

A recent PC Gamer poll asked readers about switching to Linux for gaming. Results indicated that 29% of respondents have already transitioned to Linux gaming or plan to within the year, while over a quarter do not mind staying on Windows. Interest in Linux has grown alongside better support through Valve’s Steam Deck and Proton compatibility layers. However, limitations remain, especially around multiplayer and anti-cheat systems that rely on Windows kernel-level support. Other respondents cited waiting for unavailable hardware like Steam Machines or general reluctance to change platforms. The poll sample was 2,333 participants, so while indicative of some growing enthusiasm, Linux gaming remains a niche within the PC community.

MechWarrior Studio Piranha Games Announces Layoffs

Piranha Games, developer of MechWarrior 5: Mercenaries, confirmed layoffs affecting about 30% of its staff. The cuts were described as part of an economic restructuring response to industry challenges and underperformance in reaching new audiences.
