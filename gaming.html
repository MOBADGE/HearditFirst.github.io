**Zohran Mamdani Uses Mario Kart Metaphor to Discuss Philanthropy and Corporate Greed**

New York Mayor Zohran Mamdani recently explained the role of government and philanthropy in tackling social issues using a Mario Kart analogy. Speaking at a press conference about the Child Care Action Fund—a campaign aimed at providing universal child care for New Yorkers—Mamdani described the government as Yoshi and philanthropy as the golden mushroom that provides an extra boost to overcome challenges. In his metaphor, Bowser represents corporate greed. The fund has so far raised $3.5 million toward its $20 million goal. The conference also announced the new chair of the Mayor’s Fund, tasked with encouraging wealthy New Yorkers to increase their donations. Mamdani’s analogy offers a relatable way to understand the interplay between public efforts and private generosity, highlighting ongoing tensions around tax increases aimed at funding social programs.

**Xenosaga Makes PC Debut via 2004 Japanese Mobile Game Port**

The Xenosaga series, a well-known part of the broader Xeno universe, is appearing on PC for the first time—but not in the form some fans might expect. Instead of the original PlayStation 2 trilogy, Japanese publisher G-Mode is bringing Xenosaga: Pied Piper, a 2004 mobile game originally released only in Japan for Vodafone flip phones, to Steam. The port retains its original form with no English localization or quality-of-life updates. While it may attract completists and preservationists, it is unlikely to capture the broader audience familiar with the console titles. This release underscores the complexities of bringing classic JRPG series to modern platforms, especially when the intellectual property belongs to Nintendo and the main entries remain console exclusives.

**Pragmata Gameplay: Breaking Barriers and Best Weapons Guide**

Pragmata players have encountered blue and red crystal barriers—called filament masses and dead filament respectively—that block progress in different sectors. To break blue crystal barriers found in early areas such as the Solar Power Plants and Mass Production Array, players must acquire the Lim Eraser power inside the Lim Recycling Facility in Sector 2. Red crystal barriers appear in later sectors and require the Cleanse ability, unlocked in Sector 5, which also functions to cleanse corrupted enemies.

Regarding combat, Pragmata’s weapon system includes four types: primary (infinite ammo, eg. the Grip pistol), attack (high damage, limited ammo, eg. Shockwave Gun), tactical (crowd control), and defensive (utility). Recommended weapons include the Shockwave Gun and Charge Piercer (attack), Sticky Bombs and Stasis Net (tactical), and Decoy Generator (defensive). Players are encouraged to upgrade weapons and balance their loadouts carefully due to resource limitations.

**Boss Strategies: Sector Guard and Luna Digger Encounters**

Pragmata’s boss fights emphasize strategic hacking combined with shooting. The first boss, Sector Guard, introduces players to the Overdrive mechanic, where exploiting hacked weak spots enables critical damage windows. Players should dodge charge attacks and use the Stasis Net and Shockwave Gun to manage the encounter.

The Luna Digger, a mechanical worm boss encountered later, is more challenging. It requires players to attack brief vulnerability points like its mouth and use hacking skills to stagger it. Once its health drops below half, it exposes more vulnerable areas and introduces missile attacks and drone minions. Managing these drones by hacking and using powerful weapons such as the Charge Piercer and Homing Missiles is critical to victory.

**Pragmata Red Zones and Red Gate Keys Explained**

Red Zones in Pragmata are combat arenas that yield valuable rewards but require Red Gate Keys to access. These keys are found scattered throughout various sectors and through completing bingo challenges in the in-game Cabin Stamp Club. Importantly, any Red Gate Key can open any Red Zone, providing some flexibility.

Red Zones span multiple sectors, including the Mass Production Array, Terra Dome, Lunum Mines, and Central Port. Each offers different rewards like Pure Lunum (used for upgrades), mods, and Storage Expanders for the hacking gauge. Some Red Gate Keys are obtained by exploring labs or completing levels, while others come from completing Cabin Stamp Club boards unlocked after certain story progress. Players will benefit from planning key collection alongside advancing the story to efficiently tackle these arenas.
