Darktide Adds New Extraction-Style Mode with Environmental Hazards

The cooperative Warhammer 40,000 shooter Darktide is expanding with a new mode called "Expeditions," launching March 17 as part of the Beyond the Hive update. Unlike its usual claustrophobic missions inside Hive Tertium, this mode takes players into the bright but dangerous outdoor environment of Atoma Prime’s dunes. Here, squads collect Tech-Remnants by scavenging across randomized zones, echoing extraction shooters but without player-versus-player combat. Players must also manage radiation exposure and limited time; dying when the timer runs out means losing all progress and rewards. This mode introduces new tactical challenges as enemies patrol wider areas and the game dynamically adjusts difficulty based on player aggression. The update offers a fresh experience for fans seeking risk-reward gameplay outside Darktide’s typical close-quarters combat.

The Simpsons Hit & Run Revival Remains Possible, Says Executive Producer

Matt Selman, executive producer of The Simpsons TV show, addressed the possibility of reviving the popular 2003 game Hit & Run in an interview with People Magazine. Though no concrete plans exist, Selman advised fans to "never say never" regarding a remaster or revival. He expressed fondness for Hit & Run, highlighting its movie-level storytelling and acknowledging its cult status among the many Simpsons games. This comes amid ongoing fan demand to see the title return to digital platforms. While this does not confirm new developments, Selman’s comments suggest that those behind the scenes are aware of player interest, leaving the door open for future updates or re-releases.

Just Cause Creator’s New Open-City Game Samson Shows Promise but Raises Concerns

Samson, an open-city action game from Liquid Swords founded by Just Cause creator Cristofer Sundberg, is set to release April 8. It focuses on tight gameplay loops involving debt repayment through missions limited by in-game days and "action points," encouraging players to prioritize choices carefully. The game features a worn industrial city and mix of car chases and brawling combat. However, a recent gameplay trailer drew mixed reactions, with criticism focusing on repetitive enemy animations and combat lacking depth. The city also appears somewhat sparse, raising questions about mission variety and world immersion. While the game offers potential as a smaller-scale alternative to big-budget open worlds, it remains to be seen if it can fully deliver on its arcade-style ambitions.

Total Chaos Remake Revisits a Noted Doom 2 Mod with Mixed Results

Total Chaos, a full remake of one of Doom 2’s ambitious mods, launched on Steam in November, expanding its survival horror and complex melee combat elements. Created by developer Sam Prebble – known for 2023’s acclaimed Turbo Overkill – the game blends maze-like levels with psychological horror themes and narrative hints about mental deterioration. Visually, the remake impresses with atmospheric, bleak environments, alternating between industrial urban and surreal, fleshy corridors. However, the combat has been criticized for lacking impact and variety, and the generous resource availability reduces tension. A survivalist mode and new game plus option offer tougher challenges but with inconvenient save restrictions. While not reaching the innovation heights of its mod predecessor, Total Chaos remains a tense exploration experience for fans of early 1990s shooters and horror.

Embark CEO Notes Shift Away from AI Voices in Arc Raiders

Embark Studios has started re-recording some of the voice work in its shooter Arc Raiders with professional actors, moving away from the AI-generated audio used at launch. CEO Patrick Söderlund told GamesIndustry.biz that real human performances provide higher quality and expressiveness than AI voices. He emphasized that AI is still valuable as a production tool for internal testing and generating multiple dialogue drafts quickly, not as a wholesale replacement for actors. This partial shift comes after player criticism regarding the blandness of the game's AI-voiced characters. While the studio intends to maintain AI's role in development efficiency, it acknowledges that professional voice acting remains important for delivering a better player experience.
