PC Gamer Hardware Awards 2025: Top Hardware Highlights and Honorable Mentions

PC Gamer has published its annual Hardware Awards, spotlighting the best and worst gaming tech from 2025. Among 289 reviews, 34 products earned scores of 90% or more, reflecting a strong year for PC gaming gear‚Äîeven as some categories faced challenges, notably in graphics cards and pricing.

**Top Six Highest Rated Hardware**

1. **Arctic Freezer 36** Topping the list is the Arctic Freezer 36 air cooler, praised for its quiet operation, excellent performance for under $50, and inclusion of a contact frame. Its affordability stands out in an increasingly expensive PC hardware market.

2. **Thermal Grizzly Der8enchtable** This open-platform bench table suits hardware testers and overclockers, featuring extensive fan control and storage options. While pricey, it serves a niche audience well.

3. **WD Black SN8100 2 TB NVMe SSD** The fastest PCIe 5.0 SSD tested, it delivers sequential read speeds around 14,700 MB/s and operates at relatively cool temperatures, making it a premium yet practical upgrade for gamers and professionals.

4. **Razer Wolverine V3 Pro 8K PC Controller** Offering an 8,000 Hz polling rate and Hall effect triggers, this premium controller is designed for competitive PC gaming despite a high price.

5. **Be Quiet! Light Mount Keyboard** Noted for being exceptionally quiet with vibrant per-key RGB lighting, the Light Mount balances performance and comfort, praised by PC Gamer reviewers who use it daily.

6. **Soundcore Liberty 5 Earbuds** Despite being earbuds rather than a headset, they impress with long battery life, gaming-mode audio, and active noise cancellation, presenting an alternative for PC gamers.

Graphics cards notably did not feature among the highest-rated hardware, largely due to inflated prices throughout 2025. However, the AMD Radeon RX 9070 XT was awarded PC Gamer's Best Graphics Card of the Year, praised for competitive performance and pricing improvements after an initially difficult launch.

**Dishonorable Mentions**

Some hardware failed to meet expectations, with the lowest scores hovering around 40-57%. Notable disappointments include:

- **Corsair Xeneon Edge Monitor (57%)**: Although innovative as a second screen, it suffers from limited usefulness and screen tearing issues. - **Razer Joro & Basilisk Mobile (55%)**: Premium-priced peripherals with design and functional compromises, including controversial AI buttons. - **Colorful iGame RTX 5070 Ti Vulcan OC GPU (53%)**: Performance inconsistency and excessive pricing mar its appeal. - **OneXPlayer X1 Air handheld (51%)**: Attempts to blend laptop, tablet, and handheld features but struggles with performance and form factor flaws. - **Nacon RIG M2 StreamStar Microphone (47%)**: Good audio capture hampered by difficult setup and lack of software support. - **Logitech G PowerPlay 2 Mouse Pad (40%)**: Disappointing design, limited compatibility, and flimsy construction undermine the concept of a charging mouse pad.

Personal Hardware Pick: 8BitDo Ultimate 2C Wireless Controller

Among the pool of reviewed gear, PC Gamer's hardware editor personally chose the 8BitDo Ultimate 2C as their favorite purchase of the year. Priced at around $30 and available in a preferred purple color, this budget wireless controller offers Hall effect sticks and triggers, enhancing durability and effectively limiting stick drift.

The controller's compact design favors smaller hands, differing from the more standard GameSir Nova Lite controller. While both have plastic constructions susceptible to flexing under pressure, the Ultimate 2C balances affordability, aesthetics, and functionality for casual gaming effectively.

This choice highlights how consumer preferences, including comfort and visual appeal, can influence hardware selection beyond raw specifications.
