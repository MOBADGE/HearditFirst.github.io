Windrose Survival Tips: How to Craft Gunpowder and Find Hidden Treasure

In the cooperative pirate survival game Windrose, players have recently shared useful information on crafting gunpowder and locating elusive treasure chests. Learning to make gunpowder is essential because, without it, firearms won't work—even if you have ammunition like Stone Bullets. To craft gunpowder, you need to combine sulfur and ash at a Millstone. However, this crafting option only becomes available after progressing through specific main quests and defeating the boss Thomas Richards to unlock the Foothills area and the Millstone blueprint. Sulfur requires an Iron Pickaxe to mine and can be found in Coastal Jungle and Foothills biomes, though it sometimes camouflages as normal stone. Ash can be obtained from burning wood or grinding charcoal but should be used sparingly since charcoal is needed elsewhere.

Separately, explorers encountering Traveler’s Camps often find that a map icon indicates multiple chests to find, but only one is visible nearby. The mystery of missing second chests was solved by remembering a clue from another camp: look for a tree marked with a red scarf and dig there. This solution turns out to be consistent across many camps, even when no note is provided. This kind of environmental puzzle encourages players to pay attention to subtle visual hints when scavenging for loot.

Krafton and Unknown Worlds: Continued Support for Subnautica 2 Amid Corporate Changes

Subnautica 2 is facing uncertainty as Krafton, the publisher that acquired developer Unknown Worlds, is no longer listed on the game’s Steam page. Instead, Unknown Worlds appears as both developer and publisher. This change comes amid ongoing legal disputes between Krafton and Unknown Worlds, including a lawsuit over the firing and reinstatement of the studio’s CEO related to a substantial performance bonus. Despite the public separation of names, Krafton says it is still supporting the early access launch of Subnautica 2. The lack of a fixed early access release date and past miscommunication suggests tension, but the game remains highly anticipated, currently the most wishlisted title on Steam.

Marvel Rivals Introduces Black Cat, Raising Concerns About Power Balance

Marvel Rivals will soon add Black Cat as a new character with a unique "Fortune" system. Her abilities include combat moves, mobility skills like double jump and wall climb, and an economy mechanic where she collects Fortune points by dealing damage or grappling from opponents. Fortune can be spent on offensive moves or purchasing powerful artifacts that grant abilities such as invisibility, portals through walls, and area-effect traps. This diverse and strong arsenal has sparked concerns about power creep and balance, echoing past issues the game has had with other characters like Deadpool and Gambit. Players and analysts note Black Cat’s kit could overshadow others, which may require significant future tuning.

Apple Approves Open Source Driver Enabling Nvidia GPUs on Macs, Though Gaming Support Lags

Apple has approved an open-source driver, developed by tiny corp, that enables Nvidia GPUs—including the latest RTX 50-series cards—to run on Apple Silicon Macs via external GPU docks connected over USB4 or Thunderbolt. This restoration of Nvidia support was demonstrated on a Mac mini, although the current focus remains on AI computing rather than gaming. Performance is preliminary and does not fully utilize the GPUs’ capabilities, but this step opens possibilities for accelerated AI workloads on Mac hardware. The open-source nature of the driver could lead to broader applications, including future game support, though technical challenges remain.

Shoe Company Allbirds Rebrands to NewBird AI and Shifts Focus to GPU-Based AI Infrastructure

Allbirds, previously known for its environmentally focused footwear, announced a major pivot to becoming an AI-native cloud solutions provider called NewBird AI. The company secured a $50 million investment to establish a GPU-as-a-Service business, renting out high-performance graphics cards for AI compute tasks such as training and simulations. While the AI infrastructure market is competitive and capital-intensive, and the company faces challenges like memory shortages affecting pricing, this strategic change has significantly increased its stock price. The shift exemplifies broader trends of companies moving into AI technology sectors, though the long-term viability remains uncertain.
