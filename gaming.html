Vaunted: A Tactical RPG with Shifting Perspectives and Combat Modes

Lost Lake Games has revealed Vaunted, a sci-fi tactical RPG blending turn-based and real-time combat. Players lead a group of thieves and mercenaries forced to serve a criminal syndicate after a failed expedition. The game allows switching between traditional turn-based planning—where abilities and maneuvers are mapped out—and real-time third-person action to move characters dynamically during battles. This dual approach adds strategic depth but also raises questions about balance.

Narratively, Vaunted explores the story from multiple character perspectives: mercenary Genril, thief Dyse, and warlock Kyvaath. When a character dies, players can rewrite the unfolding events from another hero’s viewpoint. This creates branching storylines within each run and incorporates roguelite progression, where completing story arcs grants "credibility" used for stat upgrades. With James Phinney—veteran designer from StarCraft and Guild Wars—leading development, Vaunted aims to innovate within tactical RPG conventions. It is slated for release later this year.

Artificial Detective: Action-Adventure with Futuristic Noir Setting

Artificial Detective, from new indie studio Vivix (composed of ex-developers from Dead Space and Control), shows promise as a futuristic action-adventure game starring a robot detective in a city where humans have vanished. Players will explore a dystopian metropolis, unraveling the mystery of humanity's disappearance while confronting infected fauna and robotic factions.

Early footage reveals a mix of exploration, combat, and light puzzles, including moments where the protagonist uses a large drill as a weapon. The game features nonlinear progression across 10 city districts, companion-driven exploration with support from a child and robotic dog, and sandbox-style encounters offering shooting, stealth, or environmental tactics. Although the detective elements are not clearly prominent yet, Artificial Detective’s blend of action and narrative may appeal to fans of immersive adventure games. It is expected to launch next year.

Lizard State: Indie Stealth Game Filling a Long Void Since Splinter Cell

Lizard State is a solo-developed indie stealth game that aims to capture the mood and gameplay style of classic titles like Splinter Cell. Players control a stealth operative on a time-limited mission to rescue a hostage from an island, moving through a focused campaign filled with tactical options.

The game’s visual style harks back to the PS2 era, using angular character models and stark shadows. Stealth gameplay involves light manipulation, choking out guards, and various ranged options. Unique mechanics include "mimic cameras" that transform from static surveillance devices into mobile threat detectors, and spider-like gadgets usable by the player to disable enemies electrically. Though still in early alpha, Lizard State shows potential to provide depth and tactility for stealth fans seeking an alternative to Ubisoft’s stalled Splinter Cell series. No release date has yet been announced.

Resident Evil Requiem Patch Adds Photo Mode and Expression Tweaks

Capcom has released a new patch for Resident Evil Requiem that introduces a photo mode, allowing players to adjust camera angles, apply filters, and capture character poses. While limited in camera movement and lighting control, the feature lets users experiment with the game’s visual details.

The update also includes bug fixes addressing progress-blocking issues and crashes, though official patch notes remain vague. Additionally, character expressions in some cutscenes have been adjusted to better convey emotions, enhancing narrative moments. The photo mode can be accessed via the pause menu, but camera control is restricted during pre-animated sequences. This update adds a new way to engage with the game’s atmosphere post-completion.

Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous Boasts 232 Subclasses and Strong Mount Combat

Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous offers an extensive character customization system, featuring 26 base classes, 193 subclasses, plus 13 prestige classes, totaling 232 options. This variety caters to players who enjoy buildcrafting and experimenting with complex role-playing builds.

A notable addition is the "Sable Company Marine" Ranger subclass introduced in the game's final expansion, granting a rideable griffon-like mount from level one. Mounted combat is emphasized at higher difficulty levels, providing increased mobility and tactical advantages. While riding a griffon indoors or in confined spaces may feel odd, the subclass equips players with powerful aerial attacks capable of quickly dispatching enemies. The game remains a deep and challenging CRPG for dedicated fans
