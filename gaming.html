Majority of PC Gamer Readers Unlikely to Use Nvidia’s DLSS 5

Nvidia recently revealed DLSS 5, a new AI-driven upscaling technology set to launch this fall. Despite the potential of DLSS 5 to enhance game visuals and performance, a recent PC Gamer poll indicates significant skepticism among readers. Approximately 71% said they could not be convinced to enable DLSS 5, with 37% stating that no matter how good it looked, they are ethically opposed to it.

This reflects a broader hesitation about AI-based graphics enhancements, with some readers concerned about the impact on game art and originality. However, around 10% are ready to use DLSS 5 immediately, while another 9% would enable it selectively depending on the game. Some respondents also expressed hope that the technology could improve beyond its current AI-generated look, which at reveal appeared somewhat limited.

The poll suggests Nvidia will face an uphill battle convincing gamers to adopt DLSS 5 widely. How Nvidia and developers implement the technology, and whether it can retain game visual identity without excessive resource use, will likely influence player acceptance.

Qualcomm Snapdragon X2 Elite Shows Strong CPU Performance But Mixed Gaming Results

Qualcomm’s new Snapdragon X2 Elite chip, designed for Windows-on-Arm devices, has been reviewed by outlets including Tom’s Hardware and Hardware Canucks. Early benchmarks indicate the CPU performs competitively with Intel and AMD mobile chips, particularly in single-threaded workloads. For example, in Cinebench and Geekbench tests, it outpaces many rivals.

However, gaming performance proves more uneven. While Cyberpunk 2077 ran at around 60 FPS on medium settings, some games like Resident Evil Requiem and Arc Raiders encountered launch issues or poor performance due to software emulation challenges on Arm architecture. Variable frame rates and inconsistent gameplay in titles like Dota 2 highlight ongoing optimization issues.

Battery life is reported between 10 and 12 hours but varies with workload and reviewer experience. Overall, while Snapdragon X2 Elite shows promise as a powerful mobile chip, current software support and game compatibility limit its attractiveness for PC gamers for now.

Rumors Suggest Intel’s Nova Lake May Outperform AMD Zen 6 in IPC But Clock Speeds Differ

Intel’s upcoming Nova Lake CPUs are generating interest due to leaked claims that their performance cores (codenamed Coyote Cove) possess higher instructions per clock (IPC) than AMD’s next-generation Zen 6 cores. However, Zen 6 is said to have a clock speed advantage, making overall performance comparisons uncertain until official benchmarks.

Additional rumors indicate increased core counts for some Nova Lake models; for example, a mid-range variant may feature 44 cores rather than the previously suggested 42. There are also references to a large APU (code-named Nova Lake AX or Razer Lake AX) featuring a massive integrated GPU with potentially 48 Xe3P cores—a substantial leap over current Intel integrated graphics.

While these rumors are not confirmed, they paint a picture of Intel preparing a highly competitive chip lineup with improved per-core efficiency and high core counts. The rumored APU could rival some discrete GPUs, adding a new dimension to Intel’s portfolio.

Crimson Desert Players Discover Detailed Space Model Beyond Expectations

Players of Pearl Abyss’s open-world action game Crimson Desert have used a stamina mod to ascend into the sky and explore space within the game world. Remarkably, the game appears to model large portions of outer space, including a curved planetary surface, atmospheric layers, and a faint Milky Way backdrop.

Although Crimson Desert is not a space simulation, the level of detail discovered suggests an extensive environmental model in the game’s Blackspace Engine. Some speculate this could hint at future space-themed content, but it may simply reflect the developers’ attention to detail in world-building.

This discovery adds an unexpected layer to the game’s experience, illustrating how mod communities can explore and reveal unused facets of game worlds.

SteelSeries Updates Aerox 3 Wireless Gen 2 Mouse with Improved Sensor and Battery Life

SteelSeries has released the Aerox 3 Wireless Gen 2, an update to its lightweight gaming mouse aimed at the mid-range market. The new model features the TrueMove sensor with up to 26,000 DPI, a wireless polling rate boosted to 4000 Hz, and significantly improved battery life—up to 120 hours on 2.4 GHz wireless and 200 hours on Bluetooth.
