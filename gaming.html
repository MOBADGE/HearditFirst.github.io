17-Year-Old Excel Vulnerability Currently Exploited

The U.S. Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) has flagged an active exploitation of a 17-year-old vulnerability in Microsoft Excel. This security flaw, first recorded in 2009 and updated last in 2018, enables remote code execution through specially crafted Excel documents, often resulting in Trojan malware installation. Although Microsoft initially patched it, CISA has now categorized the exploit as active and has urged renewed patching within two weeks. The severity score assigned is 8.8, indicating potential for significant damage despite uncertain prevalence. Additionally, CISA highlighted a newer exploit involving Microsoft SharePoint with a lower severity, noting that automated attacks using AI could amplify its impact. This development underscores ongoing cybersecurity risks from longstanding vulnerabilities, emphasizing the importance of timely software updates.

Bungie's Marathon Tests Battle Royale Mode with Positive Player Feedback

Bungie is experimenting with a new "free kit frenzy" mode in its game Marathon, effectively introducing a battle royale-style experience where players start with a basic equipment kit and scavenge during matches. Running until April 29, this mode is intended to evaluate player interaction with the game's early gear system. Player response has been notably favorable, praising the concentrated combat and reduced reliance on previously unpopular weapons like snipers and shotguns. Bungie recently also released a mid-season patch boosting certain classes and adding new weapons. This experiment suggests a potential broadening of Marathon’s gameplay styles, balancing its established hardcore extraction shooter approach with more accessible play options.

TSMC Reports Strong Revenue Growth Driven by AI Demand

Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC) announced first-quarter revenue of $35.9 billion, exceeding its earlier estimates and representing 40% growth year-on-year. This marks the eighth straight quarter of double-digit gains, largely attributed to increased demand from the AI sector, which has exacerbated global memory shortages. Shipments of wafers grew 28% compared to last year, with advanced 3-nanometre wafers accounting for 25% of wafer revenue. TSMC has also adjusted Q2 revenue projections upward to $39.0-$40.2 billion. Despite expansions, the company’s production capacity remains fully booked through 2028, signaling continued chip shortages and sustained high prices for consumers. These trends reflect the broader impacts of AI on hardware manufacturing and supply chains.

AMD May Release Special Ryzen 7 5800X3D for AM4 Socket Anniversary

AMD is reportedly preparing a special edition of the Ryzen 7 5800X3D processor to commemorate the 10th anniversary of its AM4 CPU socket this September. Although the chip’s specifications appear identical to the original 2022 model—eight cores, 16 threads, and 3D V-Cache technology—the release may make this previously discontinued CPU more accessible. Details about packaging, pricing, and availability remain unclear. The 5800X3D is noted for its strong gaming performance due to its large L3 cache. This release would be symbolic of AMD’s support for the AM4 platform, which has seen extensive use over the past decade.

Blizzard’s Cinematic Team Assists Upcoming Fable Game

Xbox chief content officer Matt Booty confirmed that Blizzard Entertainment’s cinematic team is contributing to the production of the upcoming Fable game under Xbox Game Studios. This collaboration is part of broader resource sharing across Xbox-owned studios, including Unreal Engine expertise from The Coalition and motion capture support for Compulsion Games. Fable’s development has faced delays but remains on track for an autumn 2026 release. Blizzard’s renowned cinematic work is expected to enhance the game’s visual storytelling. Such cross-studio cooperation reflects Xbox’s strategy to leverage internal talent in elevating its game projects.

Pragmata Launching Worldwide April 17

Capcom’s Pragmata, a narrative-driven third-person game set on the Moon, is launching in various regions starting from midnight EDT (April 17) with earlier times in other time zones such as 9 pm PDT on April 16 for the U.S. West Coast. The game features pre-loading for early access, allowing players who pre-ordered to download in advance. Pragmata received positive early impressions highlighting its atmospheric gameplay and sense of momentum. This staggered release schedule will enable global audiences to experience the game shortly and aligns with modern digital launch practices. Further guidance and tips for new players will follow after launch.
