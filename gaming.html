Marathon’s Outpost Map Updated with Tougher Access for Rewards, Solo Players Concerned

Bungie has updated the Outpost map in *Marathon* with patch 1.0.5.2, making the popular loot area called Pinwheel significantly harder to access. The Destroyed Wing entrance was previously disabled but has now returned with extra steps to enter. Players must collect three red security cards (up from the previous two reds and a yellow) and face a wave of Ghost Commanders when opening the locked room. Strongboxes inside now move around each run, and the extraction point appears randomly, complicating quick escapes.

This increase in difficulty is balanced by improved loot quality. Players report consistently finding high-tier items such as Cryo Archive keys, Prestige implants, cores, and weapons, making the risk worthwhile if successful. However, solo players have expressed frustration, citing the tougher requirements and tougher enemy waves as barriers, while trios still manage the challenge more easily.

The updated design aims to create a risk-reward balance. Nevertheless, some highlight that solo players might be at a disadvantage, possibly encouraging more team play or piggybacking on others’ efforts. Bungie's change has sparked debate on how to best accommodate different player styles without diluting the challenge and rewards.

Cozy Games in April Focus on Relaxation, with No Farm Sims in Sight

April’s lineup of cozy games is dominated by mellow, no-pressure experiences without any farm or life simulators this month. The selection includes hidden object games, diorama builders, emotional story adventures, and low-stress puzzle platformers. These titles allow players to enjoy gaming at a relaxed pace, without time constraints or fail states.

Notable upcoming releases include the co-op base-builder *Outbound*, which is highly anticipated and currently among Steam’s top wishlisted games. Other examples are *Fishbowl*, an emotional story game; *Belle Citique*, a castle-building diorama creator; and *Moomintroll: Winter's Warmth*, an adventure inspired by the popular franchise.

This focus on chilled gameplay fits the mood of lingering winter in many regions and offers players a chance to enjoy laid-back gaming before the busier spring season arrives.

Stormgate to Lose Online Multiplayer Support as Server Partner Changes Ownership

*Stormgate*, a StarCraft-inspired RTS that launched last summer, will cease online multiplayer support at the end of April. The game’s server provider, Hathora, was recently acquired by an AI company, which is winding down the services that support *Stormgate*. Developer Frost Giant Studios plans to release a patch enabling offline play but has no immediate plans to restore online multiplayer until a new partner is secured.

Despite strong initial backing, including a Kickstarter campaign that raised $2.8 million and additional private funding, *Stormgate* struggled post-launch. Its Steam user reviews remain mixed, and player counts have been low, making the transition to offline play a cost-driven decision.

The game will remain playable in solo campaign and other offline modes, which should allow existing players to continue enjoying content without online functionality. Restoring multiplayer hinges on future partnerships.

Intel’s Upcoming Core 300 Laptop Chips Reportedly Omit Regular E-Cores

Leaked specifications suggest that Intel’s forthcoming Core 300 series laptop processors will exclude the standard efficiency cores (E-cores) found in recent generations, instead featuring low-power variants called LPE-cores paired with two performance cores (P-cores). The chips, codenamed Wildcat Lake, target low-power applications in thin and affordable laptops with a fixed 35W turbo power ceiling.

All models in the lineup share similar configurations, offering two P-cores and either four or fewer LPE-cores, along with up to two Xe3 graphical cores. This design contrasts with previous generations that included distinct E-cores aimed at balancing workload efficiency.

The move could simplify Intel’s offerings in the lower power segment, but it raises questions about gaming and heavy multitasking performance compared to chips with standard E-cores and more potent integrated graphics.

Billy the Pickpocket: An Unfortunate Character in Crimson Desert

In *Crimson Desert*, the character Billy the pickpocket has become notable for his persistent bad luck. Players frequently encounter him in the town of Hernand, where he tries to steal from passersby only to be quickly caught. This recurring scenario serves as an introduction to the game’s bounty hunting mechanics.
