**Fallout: New Vegas Remaster Rumors Inspired by Iron Galaxy Tease**

Iron Galaxy Studios sparked speculation over a possible Fallout: New Vegas remaster after posting a screenshot featuring an iconic New Vegas loading screen during a company meeting. While the studio did not explicitly confirm working on the project, the image caught attention due to Iron Galaxy’s history of working on Bethesda-related games such as Fallout 76 and VR versions of Fallout and Skyrim. Fans have long hoped for a New Vegas remaster, especially following Bethesda’s success with the Oblivion remaster. Despite previous false alarms, this subtle hint raises cautious optimism about a potential remake or remaster, though no official announcement has been made.

**Tim Cain Highlights Risks of Overambition in Game Design**

Tim Cain, co-creator of Fallout and veteran RPG designer now back at Obsidian, discussed common pitfalls in game design, emphasizing the importance of knowing when to stop adding features. Cain warns against feature creep and spreading plots too thin, which can dilute a game's identity and focus. He cited examples where adding mechanics or lore without clear purpose leads to lack of cohesion. Cain advocates for clear design pillars that serve the core experience rather than including elements just because they are popular. His insights reflect ongoing challenges in modern game development around scope and clarity.

**Arma 3 Footage Mistaken for Real Combat Again Amid Middle East Conflict**

A video showing aerial combat was shared on social media as authentic footage of the US attack on Iran, but the clip actually originates from the military simulation game Arma 3. Despite community fact-checks and clarifications, the video spread widely, including by a Canadian political commentator. This incident echoes previous cases where Arma 3 and War Thunder gameplay footage have been misused as real combat videos in different conflicts. Bohemia Interactive, the developer, has shared tips on distinguishing game footage from real world video to help prevent this recurring misinformation issue, which can exacerbate confusion during sensitive geopolitical events.

**Bungie Reviews Player Feedback Following Marathon Server Slam**

Following the recent server slam playtest for the shooter Marathon, Bungie released a summary of feedback and acknowledged various community concerns. Among the topics under consideration are adding a dedicated duos mode, adjusting time-to-kill (TTK), improving consumables availability, and refining user interface elements. Bungie also noted ongoing investigations into reported PC performance issues. While the team did not commit to specific changes, the recap suggests they are monitoring early player experiences closely and are open to adjustments before the full release scheduled for March 5.

**Nacon Postpones Nacon Connect Showcase Amid Insolvency Proceedings**

Nacon, publisher and peripheral maker, has delayed its Nacon Connect event originally planned for March 4. The postponement comes shortly after the company filed for insolvency, citing challenging economic conditions. Nacon stated it will focus its resources on polishing current projects and upcoming releases, with plans to reschedule the showcase possibly in May. Financial difficulties largely stem from issues faced by its majority owner, Bigben Interactive, affecting Nacon’s ability to meet debt obligations. The delay affects announcements and updates for titles under Nacon’s umbrella, including niche RPGs, stealth games, and racing franchises.

**New God of War Franchise Leak Surfaces via LinkedIn Profile**

A LinkedIn profile for a senior Sony writer briefly listed work on “a new franchise within the God of War universe,” sparking discussion and confirmation from industry journalist Jason Schreier that the project aligns with Sony’s strategy to expand and diversify its established IPs. Details on the game remain scarce, though leaks suggest a combat-focused entry possibly centered on the character Faye. This follows multiple recent developments related to the God of War series, including remakes of the original trilogy and a TV adaptation. The leak highlights ongoing efforts by Sony to extend the franchise beyond Kratos-centered stories.
