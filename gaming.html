Stardew Valley 1.7 Update to Add More Engaging Children and New Marriage Candidates

Eric Barone, creator of *Stardew Valley*, revealed in a recent interview that the upcoming 1.7 update will address a frequent player request: making in-game children more interesting. Currently, children in the game have minimal interaction and no growth, which many fans find underwhelming. While specifics weren't given, Barone confirmed changes aimed at enriching this aspect. Additionally, the update will introduce two new marriage candidates. Barone also mentioned the possibility of a farm map editor, potentially enabling players to create and share custom farm layouts, expanding replayability. Like previous updates, 1.7 is expected to introduce a new farm type and additional social content, though full details will emerge closer to release.

Pahdo Labs Shuts Down Despite $17.5 Million in Funding

Pahdo Labs, the studio behind the anime action RPG *Starlight Re:Volver*, has announced it is closing after failing to achieve commercial success. Despite raising $17.5 million, and following a brief early access period beginning last November, the game did not sustain a healthy player base. The studio had attempted to develop a new demo titled *Edge of Divinity* as a fresh pitch to investors, but was unable to secure further funding. CEO Dan Zou explained that much of the funding supported salaries and content creation, with projects including *Halcyon Zero* preceding their latest efforts. The closure highlights ongoing challenges in launching paid games in a market dominated by free-to-play anime-style RPGs.

Fallout Series Countdown Ends with No Game Remaster Announcement

Amazon's *Fallout* website hosted a countdown timer that sparked fan speculation about possible remasters of *Fallout 3* or *Fallout: New Vegas*. The countdown expired alongside the release of Episode 8 of *Fallout* Season 2 on Prime Video, but instead of a game announcement, it revealed an interactive 3D walkthrough of the Lucky 38 penthouse from the show, offering behind-the-scenes content rather than any new game news. This response may disappoint fans hoping for remastered versions of classic *Fallout* games.

Epic Games Store Plans Forums and Major Launcher Overhaul

Epic Games Store's General Manager Steve Allison announced upcoming improvements focused on player needs, including testing forums for top games and adding social features like player profiles, avatars, messaging, and voice chat. This marks a shift from Epic's earlier stance against internal forums and user reviews, aiming to foster more community engagement while maintaining caution against issues like review bombing. Additionally, a technical overhaul of the Epic Games Launcher is planned for mid-2026 to address performance and loading speed issues, aiming for a notably smoother user experience.
