Nvidia’s Investment in OpenAI Reduced to $30 Billion

At a recent Morgan Stanley conference, Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang announced that the company’s planned investment in OpenAI has been scaled back from $100 billion to $30 billion. This adjustment comes amid recent tensions between OpenAI and Nvidia regarding GPU usage. Huang explained that with OpenAI preparing to go public, Nvidia expects to benefit from ongoing revenues rather than a larger upfront investment. The company also indicated that its $10 billion investment in Anthropic is likely its last major AI funding commitment. This move reflects Nvidia’s confidence that supplying AI computing power will continue to drive long-term growth without needing to invest heavily upfront.

Marvel Rivals Returns Chrono Rush Event with Unlimited Points

Marvel Rivals has reintroduced the Chrono Rush event for two weeks, allowing players to earn unlimited Chrono Tokens with no cap on points. These tokens can be used to unlock battle pass tiers and cosmetics. Even players who have completed the battle pass can exchange surplus tokens for unity, although there is a cap on how many units can be stored. The event runs until March 20, offering an opportunity for both new and existing players to gather rewards. The latest patch also addresses some minor bugs involving character abilities but does not feature balance changes.

World of Warcraft: Midnight’s Housing System Impacted by Premium Currency

World of Warcraft: Midnight’s player housing system, praised for its creative flexibility, has come under criticism due to the introduction of a new premium currency, Hearthsteel. Despite earlier assurances from Blizzard that the currency would be sold in player-friendly increments, current bundles require players to purchase set amounts, forcing them to manage leftover currency inefficiently. Many decorative items are priced such that buying them individually often leads to excess Hearthsteel that cannot be spent efficiently. This monetization approach, combined with the necessity to pay a subscription or grind gold, has left some players frustrated with the housing system despite its otherwise solid design.

Lost Fallout: New Vegas Post-Ending Dialogue Discovered and Restored

Modders have uncovered and restored previously cut post-ending dialogue from Fallout: New Vegas, offering players the ability to continue exploring a world that reflects their choices after the main campaign’s conclusion. The recovered content provides additional character interactions depending on the player’s chosen faction at the game's end. For example, new dialogue covers the fate of the Mojave Wasteland under different regimes, such as the Legion, NCR, independent New Vegas, or Mr. House’s rule. These lines are accessible through mods requiring installation alongside a functional post-game ending mod, enabling fans to experience more of the game’s narrative depth beyond the original ending.

The Legend of Khiimori Launches in Early Access as a Horse Courier Sim

The Legend of Khiimori, a realistic horse courier and survival simulator, became available in early access on March 3. The game emphasizes managing your horse’s survival needs while completing delivery quests across an open world inspired by historical Mongolian settings. Features include horse care, breeding, riding simulation, and a detailed route planning system. However, some key elements such as the full main story, horse customization, taming, and peaceful mode are still in development. The game’s early access state has some bugs and visual imperfections, and the price is on the higher side for an early-access title. It may appeal most to horse enthusiasts and those interested in survival games with a unique focus.

Australia to Enforce Age Verification for R18+ Online Games Starting Next Week

From next week, Australian players will need to verify their age to access online games rated R18+, including titles like GTA Online. The requirement is part of new Age-Restricted Material Codes aimed at safeguarding minors from adult content online. Publishers must comply with the age assurance protocols or face fines up to nearly AU$50 million per breach. The verification process involves more than just self-declared birthdates, potentially requiring government-issued ID or other robust measures. Australia's approach is notable internationally but raises privacy concerns due to the increased sharing of personal data with third-party verification services. The regulation applies only to online multiplayer content, excluding offline singleplayer games.
