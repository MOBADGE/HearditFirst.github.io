Defunct MMO Studio Founder Accused of Misusing Kickstarter Funds

Recent allegations have emerged surrounding Steven Sharif, founder of the now-defunct Intrepid Studios, which was developing the MMO Ashes of Creation before laying off its entire team earlier this year. Investor Jason Caramanis accused Sharif of spending millions of Kickstarter funds on personal expenses rather than development. A YouTuber known as NefasQS claimed to have analyzed Intrepid’s financial ledgers from 2015 to 2026, pointing to expenditures including a private chef for Sharif and his partner, expensive antiques, trading card websites, and dubious payments such as $80,000 linked to a mansion purchase. Former employees reportedly never saw the chef at the workplace despite promises he would cater for the whole team. While Sharif denies wrongdoing and attributes the project's failure to the board of directors, these allegations further complicate the controversy surrounding Ashes of Creation’s shutdown. Verification of the full extent of these claims remains pending.

Skate Story Available with Discount; Developer Permits Piracy

Skate Story, an indie demon-themed skateboarding game praised for its unique style and gameplay, has recently launched on GOG with a 20% discount, priced at $16. Creator Sam Eng openly stated on social media that if the price remains a barrier, players might as well pirate the game, emphasizing his desire for wide accessibility over profit. The game, originally released in 2025, is also available on Steam without a current discount. Its distinct blend of skateboarding mechanics and narrative tone earned it critical acclaim. Eng’s candid stance reflects ongoing discussions about game pricing and accessibility in indie development.

Blizzard Wins Legal Injunction Against Vanilla WoW Private Server

Blizzard has secured a U.S. district court injunction against Turtle WoW, a popular private server recreating vanilla World of Warcraft with added content. The court order requires Turtle WoW developers to cease operations and prohibits transferring their server code to unauthorized successors. This follows Blizzard’s 2025 lawsuit over copyright infringement related to the server, which allowed players to donate in exchange for in-game rewards. The companies have reached a confidential settlement tied to actions expected in the coming weeks. Turtle WoW’s community voiced disappointment, citing the lack of official classic-era WoW servers with custom features that meet their preferences. Blizzard’s crackdown underscores ongoing tensions around private servers and intellectual property in MMORPG communities.

Life Is Strange Soundtrack Remains Impactful After 11 Years

Eleven years after its 2015 release, Life is Strange’s soundtrack continues to resonate with players. The episodic narrative adventure used licensed indie rock tracks to create an emotional connection, elevating moments of teenage drama and supernatural storytelling. Its music selection, including songs like Foals’ “Spanish Sahara,” complemented the game’s tone and has become an essential part of its legacy. The integration of licensed music in episodic games during the mid-2010s shifted from mere ambiance to narrative tools, similar to television scoring. Subsequent Life is Strange entries maintain strong musical choices, but the original’s soundtrack remains widely regarded as a benchmark in game music curation.

DayZ Creator Dean Hall Shares Gaming Habits and Influences

Dean “Rocket” Hall, creator of DayZ and founder of RocketWerkz, recently shared insights into his gaming preferences and habits. Hall has logged over 2,000 hours in Kerbal Space Program and is currently engrossed in RimWorld, accumulating over 1,400 hours. He also played the original XCOM game extensively, including during a mountaineering expedition at Everest base camp, highlighting its enduring appeal. Hall described his preference for using a text-based programming environment, Rider, as his team develops their own game framework rather than relying on popular engines like Unity or Unreal. This interview offers a glimpse into the personal gaming experiences of a prominent developer and his ongoing involvement with modding communities.
