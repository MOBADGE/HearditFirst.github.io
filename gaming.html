New Blood Doubles Down on Supporting GOG

Dave Oshry, CEO of New Blood Interactive, has reaffirmed his commitment to GOG after previously expressing concerns about the storefront's future. In discussions, Oshry noted that GOG struggles to offer players enough incentive to choose it over Steam. Despite appreciating GOG’s preservation efforts and DRM-free philosophy, he worried about its long-term viability if it doesn't attract enough active users.

In response, New Blood plan to release games like *Dungeons of Dusk* and *Tenebrous Somnia* simultaneously on both Steam and GOG, include demos on GOG, and make their *Dusk HD* remaster available with one-click mod installation on the platform. This comes amid GOG's recent independence from CD Projekt and renewed focus on classic games and preservation, supported through a patrons program. These moves signal a mutual interest in bolstering GOG's place in the market facing strong competition from Steam.

Resident Evil Requiem Now Playable in VR via Mod

Fans of *Resident Evil Requiem* can now experience the game in virtual reality through a fan-developed mod called RE9VR. This mod lets players immerse themselves fully as Grace Ashcroft or Leon S. Kennedy, switching perspectives between first-person and third-person views. While some puzzles require briefly leaving VR for practical reasons, most gameplay can be done entirely through the headset.

The VR adaptation offers a new way to experience the game’s horror elements, though it is an unofficial modification that requires additional tools and setup steps. It caters to more dedicated VR users and those seeking a notably different take on the survival horror experience.

These diverse offerings reflect the wide range of genres and styles in Steam’s constant influx of new games.

Marvel Heroes Relaunches With Player-Run Servers

Nearly nine years after the closure of *Marvel Heroes,* player-run servers are now online following the release of a server emulator at version 1.0. The first server, Project Tahiti, supports thousands of players, although occasional restarts occur as the community builds infrastructure. A single-player alternative is also available for those who prefer offline play.

This revival gives fans the chance to revisit a game that combined Diablo-like mechanics with a Marvel superhero setting. Community efforts to maintain the title highlight the enduring interest in online RPGs even after official support ends.

Man Pleads Guilty to AI-Generated Music Streaming Fraud

Michael Smith, a man from North Carolina, has pleaded guilty to using artificial intelligence to generate thousands of fake music tracks and then streaming them billions of times via bots. This fraud scheme accrued over $8 million in royalties by exploiting streaming platforms’ payment structures. The case underscores growing concerns around abuse involving AI-generated content.

Smith faces up to five years in prison and has agreed to forfeit his illicit earnings. The incident highlights not just ethical questions about AI in creative industries, but also how automated systems can be manipulated for financial gain. Authorities continue to evaluate responses as AI becomes more prevalent in media production.
