Gaming News Briefing – April 19, 2026

1. Philips and AOC Launching New 5th-Gen OLED Gaming Monitors Philips and AOC are preparing to release 5th-generation QD-OLED gaming monitors in the third quarter of 2026. These new 34-inch monitors, the AOC AGP346UCSD and Philips Evnia 34M2C8600P, feature improved image quality by addressing previous issues like font fringing. Both panels peak at 1300 nits brightness and support HDR 500 TB, with differences in connectivity and refresh rates aligning with their target markets: AOC for high-end setups and Philips for more casual users. Prices are set at approximately €999 and €899 respectively. The introduction of these monitors adds more variety to the OLED gaming market, offering gamers alternatives to IPS displays with enhanced contrast and brightness.

2. Internet Archive Preserves Historic Computer and Gaming Media The Internet Archive has made over 200 issues of Byte Magazine, a key publication in personal computing history, freely available online. Byte, which ran from 1975 to 1998, documented the evolution of computing technology and culture. Additionally, the Archive has preserved 758 PC Gamer demo discs from various years, containing game demos, exclusive content, and extras not seen in full releases. These resources provide both historical insight into computing and gaming and nostalgic value for enthusiasts wishing to explore past media.

3. Windrose Hits One Million Copies Sold with Strong Player Count Windrose, a pirate-themed survival crafting game, reached the milestone of one million copies sold within six days of launch on Steam. It also achieved a peak concurrent player count of 200,000. Developed by Kraken Express, the game includes mechanics like resource gathering and naval combat reminiscent of Assassin’s Creed: Black Flag. While the online multiplayer is experiencing some technical challenges, developers are actively engaging the community to diagnose issues. Windrose’s commercial success signals ongoing interest in survival crafting games with distinctive settings.

4. Esoteric Ebb Highlights the Strength of the Cleric Class in RPGs Christoffer Bodegård, developer of the RPG Esoteric Ebb, discussed the choice of a Cleric protagonist in his game. The Cleric’s versatility—combining spellcasting, healing, and melee combat—is cited as a major factor in its power and narrative suitability. Bodegård also noted the thematic richness of the Cleric class concerning religion and godly conflict, central to the game’s story. The depiction contrasts with more common player preferences for classes like Rogues or Paladins and adds depth to the role-playing experience.

5. Airborne Empire Sequel Launches 1.0 with New Content and Discount The sequel to the flying city-builder Airborne Kingdom, titled Airborne Empire, has officially launched version 1.0 following over a year in early access. The major update introduces a new snowy region called Kingsfell, expanded narrative quests, additional city-building options, and a new jet engine feature to assist in combat and navigation. The game is currently offered at a 50% discount until the end of April, while the original Airborne Kingdom is also on sale with an 80% price reduction. This release broadens the niche of city-building games with unique fantasy settings and gameplay elements.

6. Dave Oshry, New Blood Interactive CEO, Shares Gaming Preferences In a recent interview, Dave Oshry revealed insights into his gaming habits and development philosophy. Despite being known for publishing boomer shooters, he stated his favorite genre is classic RPGs such as Baldur’s Gate and Fallout. Oshry also expressed a strong personal attachment to games like Doom, which he will never uninstall, and Fallout 76, in which he logged over 488 hours playing mostly single-player. Additionally, he recommended the "Everything" software for efficient PC file searching, critiquing Windows’ default feature. His reflections provide perspective on the interests of a prominent figure in the retro and indie gaming scene.
