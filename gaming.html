**Amazon Lays Off Lost Ark Staff Amid Organizational Restructuring**

Amazon Game Studios recently conducted layoffs affecting several key members of the Lost Ark team, stirring concern among fans about the game's future. Despite the game's solid player base on Steam, notable personnel departures, including community managers Roxanne Sabo and Sean Hamilton, and producer Henry Stelter, have been confirmed. Amazon attributes these departures to "broader organizational decisions" and maintains that development will continue with upcoming updates like the recent "Ends of the Abyss" content. The layoffs come after Amazon shifted focus away from large-scale MMORPG projects following the announcement that New World will shut down in early 2027. This pattern suggests uncertainty about the long-term viability of Lost Ark under Amazon's stewardship, though the original South Korean developer Smilegate continues core development.

**Death Stranding 2 PC Release Approaches With Scheduled Unlock Times**

Death Stranding 2: On The Beach is set for a PC launch on March 19 for most regions, with some areas such as Australia unlocking it in the early hours of March 20. The game is now available for preloading on Steam. The sequel arrives less than a year after its PS5 release, continuing the practice of bringing prominent PlayStation exclusives to PC. System requirements suggest that the game will run well on a broad range of current hardware. This release stands out as a potential last major PC launch before Sony reportedly winds down its PC port initiatives, making it noteworthy for fans awaiting new PC content from traditionally console-focused publishers.

**Crimson Desert Garners Significant Attention Ahead of Launch**

Pearl Abyss’s upcoming title Crimson Desert has attracted notable interest as it nears its release this Thursday. Originally planned as a Black Desert Online prequel, the game evolved into a standalone open-world action-adventure with multiple gameplay systems, including crafting, bounty hunting, faction reputation, and various minigames. Steam wishlist rankings indicate strong anticipation. The game's ambitious scope comes amid a relative lull in large-scale open-world games on PC. Players and critics are watching closely to see if Crimson Desert meets expectations, with some commentators cautioning on waiting for patches before purchase. The game’s reception will provide insight into current market appetite for expansive single-player experiences.

**Resident Evil Requiem Confirms Leon S. Kennedy is Married**

Capcom has confirmed through Resident Evil Requiem’s in-game details that Leon S. Kennedy is married, signified by a wedding ring visible in cutscenes and official concept art. Director Koshi Nakanishi emphasized that the narrative intent is to show Leon has a "place to go home to" and a peaceful life after his ordeals, without revealing the spouse’s identity. This confirmation aligns with one canonical ending of Requiem and adds a new dimension to the character’s story. While fan speculation continues over who Leon's partner might be, Capcom plans additional story content for Requiem, though a release timeline has not been specified.

**Indie Developer Emotionally Reacts to $250,000 Revenue From Tangy TD**

Solo developer Cakez, who spent four years creating the tower defense game Tangy TD, publicly expressed an emotional reaction upon learning the game earned around $250,000 in its first week on Steam. The developer shared the milestone during a live stream, highlighting gratitude for community support. Tangy TD holds an 89% positive rating on Steam, and player feedback praises the game's quality and the grassroots nature of its development. This case illustrates the potential for smaller developers to achieve significant commercial success with sustained effort and community engagement.

**Take-Two CEO Opposes Ads in Premium Games**

Take-Two Interactive CEO Strauss Zelnick stated in an interview that he is not interested in including advertising in premium, full-priced games. While the company includes contextual ads in sports games like NBA 2K to reflect real-life environments, Zelnick described interstitial ads in paid titles as unfair. His comments come amidst industry discussions about increasing in-game advertising to offset waning player engagement and diversify revenue. Take-Two’s stance may influence how publishers balance monetization with player experience, particularly as the release of Grand Theft Auto 6 approaches.
