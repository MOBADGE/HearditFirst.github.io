Low-Cost Multiplayer Games Defined 2025

The year 2025 saw a noticeable rise in affordable cooperative games, often costing less than a typical coffee, providing an accessible way for friends to play together amidst economic challenges. A term briefly popularized as "friendslop" marked this trend, highlighting games designed for casual group play without heavy financial commitment. Titles like *REPO*, a horror extraction game with comedic elements, and *Peak*, a climbing-focused game with strong physics mechanics and tension, stood out as representative successes. Other games such as *RV There Yet*, *Guilty as Sock*, and *Mage Arena* contributed to this landscape, fostering social gaming at a low cost. This trend is significant as it offers players ongoing social engagement in tight budgets, reflecting economic realities while encouraging community through gaming.

Linux Adoption Gains Momentum Among PC Gamers

Linux-based operating systems have become increasingly user-friendly for gaming in 2025, prompting more users to consider switching from Windows. Steam's hardware surveys noted a peak in Linux usage at 3.2%, surpassing macOS users, indicating growing acceptance. Distributions tailored for gaming, like Bazzite, have simplified setup and compatibility, even supporting Nvidia GPUs effectively. Despite some remaining challenges such as inconsistent HDR support and issues with live-service games due to anticheat incompatibilities, improvements in compatibility layers like Valve's Proton suggest a brightening future for Linux gaming. This shift matters because it offers gamers greater control over their systems, addressing concerns over software bloat and corporate overreach in traditional platforms.

Highlights from PC Gamer Hardware Awards 2025

The PC Gamer Hardware Awards for 2025 recognized leading gaming technology across several categories. The *Razer Blade 16* won Best Gaming Laptop for blending portability, design, and performance, while the *KTC H27T22C-3* was named Best Gaming Monitor, noted for its high refresh rate and affordability. For handheld gaming PCs, the *Lenovo Legion Go S SteamOS* received top honors, praised for performance and comfort despite some Linux compatibility issues. In storage, the *WD Black SN8100 2 TB NVMe SSD* distinguished itself with exceptional PCIe 5.0 speeds. AMD's *Ryzen 9 9950X3D* was selected Best CPU, offering a broad balance of gaming and content creation performance. The *Gigabyte X870 Aorus Elite Ice* motherboard and the *Corsair Sabre V2 Pro* gaming mouse also earned recognition. Notably, the *Razer BlackShark V3* headset and *Amazon Basics USB condenser microphone* were awarded for audio equipment. The *AMD Radeon RX 9070 XT* took Best Graphics Card amid a year of fluctuating GPU pricing and availability.

The Return of Desktop Pet and Partial-Screen Idler Games

A surprising cozy gaming trend in 2025 was the resurgence of desktop pets and partial-screen idler games. Modern takes have evolved beyond simple animated companions to fully-fledged idle games that occupy small portions of a screen, providing low-effort entertainment. Titles like *Ropuka's Idle Island*, *My Little Life*, and *Your Big, Cute Monster Farm* exemplify this, combining casual gameplay with an ever-present visual presence on players' desktops. This trend aligns with the need for unobtrusive entertainment during work or routine tasks, reflecting shifts in how gamers integrate light gaming with daily digital life.
