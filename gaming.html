Industry Reflection at Game Developers Choice Awards

During the recent Game Developers Choice Awards, composer Austin Wintory performed an unreleased piece from a canceled project that he described as his "dream job of all time." This poignant moment underscored ongoing challenges in the industry marked by multiple studio closures and game cancellations, including projects linked to former BioWare boss Casey Hudson's studio Humanoid Origin. Although the game did not come to fruition, Wintory’s shared music offers a rare glimpse into what might have been. The awards also paid tribute to late industry figures such as Rebecca Heineman, Julian Lefay, and Vince Zampella, reflecting on the legacy and human side of game development.

Zero Parades Offers Humorous Moments in a Spy RPG

ZA/UM’s upcoming role-playing game Zero Parades, a follow-up to Disco Elysium, showcased moments from its Steam demo and GDC build that hint at flashes of the predecessor’s wit. Players step into the role of Hershel, a troubled spy, navigating both dramatic and comedic situations. Notable interactions include a conversation through a hastily installed intercom and the process of trying to unlock a door with a keypad—a failure of which triggers entertaining dialogue permutations. These sequences reveal ZA/UM’s attempt to balance new narrative tones while retaining the distinctive, sometimes goofy, style that endeared Disco Elysium to fans.

Seed: A Persistent Society Simulation MMO

Klang Games, founded by former EVE Online developers, revealed updates on Seed, an MMO where player characters continue their lives even when offline. With influences from The Sims, RimWorld, and Dwarf Fortress, Seed emphasizes a deep simulation of societies governed by player-made laws and economies. Players can have multiple characters living in different societies varying from sprawling cities to small villages. Seed is currently in closed beta with plans for gradual player inclusion and a companion mobile app for managing characters remotely. The game highlights a niche for players who enjoy in-depth social simulation combined with MMO persistence.

Shadowstone Begins Public Playtest Ahead of Early Access

Secret Door and Dreamhaven launched a surprise public playtest for Shadowstone, a roguelike tactics game linked to the Sunderfolk universe. The playtest accommodates solo and four-player matches, offering players a chance to experience the game prior to its early access release scheduled for later this year. Shadowstone simplifies some tabletop RPG elements of Sunderfolk in favor of a run-based experience focusing on strategic use of skill cards, equipment, and powerups as players attempt to escape randomized ruins. Players can sign up on Steam for a chance to join the testing pool.

Rob Pardo’s GDC Keynote Urges Retention of Development Teams

At this year’s Game Developers Conference, Rob Pardo, former Blizzard chief creative officer, emphasized the importance of preserving development teams after successful game launches. Reflecting on his own career, including work on Starcraft and Warcraft, Pardo cautioned against frequent layoffs following hits, noting that the proven ability of a development team to navigate the unpredictable nature of game creation is more valuable than any single game. His message comes amid ongoing industry debates about layoffs even after commercial successes, advocating instead for nurturing and retaining talent.

Path of Exile’s Mirage League Adjusts Economy and Gameplay

Grinding Gear Games released the Mirage league update for Path of Exile, significantly increasing currency item drop rates to make in-game economies more balanced and rewarding. The league introduces "mirages," optional repeat runs of areas that provide additional rewards and strategic opportunities, such as doubling up on league mechanics like Ritual or Harvest. An overhaul of the atlas map system replaces specific map drops with tiered tokens, streamlining progression and enhancing the game’s endgame variety. These adjustments may improve player experience in farming rare items, including the elusive Mirror of Kalandra.
