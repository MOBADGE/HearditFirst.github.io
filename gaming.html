**Slay the Spire 2 Sees Unexpectedly Strong Launch**

Slay the Spire 2 launched in early access on March 5, drawing far more attention than its developers anticipated. The game rapidly peaked at over 430,000 concurrent Steam players, securing the top spot on global sales charts. Mega Crit co-founder Casey Yano had previously described the sequel as the "chicken noodle soup of videogames," implying a reliable but unexciting experience. The strong player turnout suggests the sequel’s appeal is significantly broader and more enthusiastic than expected. This performance also eclipses Bungie's new extraction shooter, Marathon, which launched the same week amid tough competition. The success underscores the enduring popularity of deckbuilding roguelikes and reflects strong support for smaller indie projects.

**Resident Evil Requiem Release Sparks Franchise Quiz**

Capcom recently released Resident Evil Requiem, adding another installment to the long-running horror series. To mark the launch, PC Gamer has created a timed quiz challenging players to name all 30 Resident Evil games from the franchise’s history. The quiz includes mainline titles as well as lesser-known spin-offs and related games, reflecting the series’ extensive and sometimes convoluted catalog. This offers fans a way to engage with Resident Evil’s legacy, highlighting the depth and complexity of the series. According to reviews, Requiem builds on the foundation laid by previous entries, marking it as a noteworthy continuation of the franchise.

**Tabletop RPG Mechanics Influence Modern Videogames**

An analysis of recent RPG trends points to a resurgence of tabletop role-playing influences in video games. Whereas earlier RPGs from the '80s and '90s closely adapted mechanics from games like Dungeons & Dragons, later developments shifted toward real-time action combat and cinematic narratives, often prioritizing spectacle over player agency. Today’s standout RPGs such as Disco Elysium, Esoteric Ebb, and Baldur’s Gate 3 are revisiting tabletop-inspired designs. They emphasize player choice, wide-ranging interactions, and complex skill checks to create more reactive and personalized stories. Combat systems are also evolving, with some games moving away from traditional fight mechanics to more creative and strategic encounters. This revival offers a path for smaller studios to produce rich RPG experiences without requiring blockbuster budgets.

**Cities: Skylines Free Weekend and New Expansion Incoming**

Paradox Interactive is spotlighting the original Cities: Skylines with a free weekend on Steam from now through March 9, along with a 90% discount on the base game until March 12. This comes amid ongoing issues surrounding the recently released Cities: Skylines 2, which has faced delays, developer changes, and technical problems. Alongside the free weekend, the first expansion for Cities: Skylines in two years, titled Race Day, is set to launch on March 10. The expansion allows players to build and host motor races, adding new roads, spectator areas, and event management features. A free update will also add visual enhancements and an employment visualizer. This renewed support for the original game gives new and returning players reasons to revisit it while its sequel navigates a troubled launch.

**Abyssus Update Adds Speargun and New Features**

The roguelike FPS Abyssus, noted for its underwater setting and combination of fast shooting with roguelike elements, has received its 1.2 update. The patch introduces a long-missing speargun, which acts as a slow-firing but powerful weapon that boosts combo points for special attacks. Additionally, a new ability called Smiting Spear allows players to throw spectral polearms for area damage and crowd control. The update also includes new cosmetic items and additional boss attack patterns. Alongside the content update, the game’s price has been reduced by 30% until March 12. Abyssus continues to stand out among FPS games for its effective blending of gameplay styles, even as it remains a niche title.
