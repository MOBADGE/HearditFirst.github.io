Windrose Outperforms Pragmata in Early Sales and Player Numbers

This week on Steam, the pirate-themed survival crafting game *Windrose* significantly outpaced the sci-fi shooter *Pragmata* in both sales and concurrent player counts. Released in early access on April 14, *Windrose* sold over one million copies within six days and peaked at more than 220,000 concurrent players. By comparison, *Pragmata*, priced at nearly double *Windrose’s* launch price, reached a peak of just under 69,000 players on PC. *Windrose’s* strong performance highlights a continuing demand for pirate-themed games, especially after years of mixed reception for similar titles like *Sea of Thieves* and *Skull & Bones*. Although *Pragmata* also launched on consoles and early reception isn’t negative, *Windrose*’s success so far suggests it has captured a notably larger share of the PC audience.

New 5th-Generation QD-OLED Gaming Monitors from Philips and AOC

Philips and AOC announced plans to release their 5th-generation QD-OLED gaming monitors in the third quarter of 2026, with prices starting around $1,050 and $1,175 respectively. These monitors address previous OLED shortcomings such as font fringing and offer improved HDR and brightness levels—peak brightness reaches 1300 nits. The AOC AGP346UCSD targets high-end gamers with a 34-inch WQHD display and a 360Hz refresh rate, while the Philips Evnia 34M2C8600P offers features like a 65W USB Type-C port and synchronized Ambiglow lighting for a more casual audience. Both monitors feature a subtle 1800R curve to enhance immersion without overwhelming the viewer. These releases add more options in a growing market segment competing with similar products like MSI’s QD-OLED gaming monitor, potentially benefiting consumers by increasing competition and innovation.

Byte Magazine Archive Offers a Look Back at Computing’s Early Days

More than 200 issues of *Byte Magazine*, a foundational publication chronicling the early years of personal computing, are now freely accessible on the Internet Archive. Spanning from 1975 through the late 1990s, the collection provides insight into the development and culture of computing during a period when computers were still novel and widely seen as symbols of progress. The magazine’s distinctive, whimsical cover art by Robert Tinney captures the spirit of the era. While the archive is missing some issues—such as the final July 1998 edition—it represents a valuable resource for those interested in technology history and the cultural context that shaped today’s computing and gaming environments.

Internet Archive Preserves 758 Old PC Gamer Demo Discs

In a related archival effort, the Internet Archive has preserved 758 demo discs from *PC Gamer* magazines dating back to the 1990s and 2000s. These discs often included playable demos, exclusive content, and early looks at upcoming games, serving as significant artifacts of PC gaming history. Highlights include early *Quake* maps and full episodes of episodic games like *Sam & Max*. This collection can serve both as a nostalgic resource and a reference point for the evolution of PC gaming. The preservation work is attributed in part to archivist Jason Scott and collaborators, helping ensure that this aspect of gaming culture remains available as games and media formats continue to change.

Esoteric Ebb’s Cleric Protagonist Reflects Game Design and Narrative Choices

Developer Christoffer Bodegård of *Esoteric Ebb*, a 2026 RPG inspired by Dungeons & Dragons 5th Edition, explained why the game’s protagonist is a Cleric, traditionally considered less appealing to many players. The Cleric’s versatility in combat, spells, and healing makes it mechanically strong—“overpowered,” as Bodegård put it—and narratively suitable due to its connection to themes of religion and masculinity. This choice aligns with the game’s mature storytelling focus, contrasting with other popular classes like Paladins or Warlocks. Players have the option to diverge from this archetype, but the Cleric remains central to the game’s thematic and gameplay design.
