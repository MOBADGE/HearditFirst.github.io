Two Out of Over 800 Gamers Complete Classic Adventure Game Without Cheats

Last weekend, Woe Industries held the first Adventure Game Aptitude Test (AGAT), challenging more than 800 participants to finish the 1987 graphic adventure Maniac Mansion without consulting any walkthroughs. Contestants were monitored via webcam and microphone to prevent cheating. The results were striking: only two players passed within the allotted four hours, yielding a pass rate of 0.24%. This low success rate underscores how challenging older adventure games can be under strict, monitored conditions.

The fixed test time on a Saturday afternoon likely limited participation and contributed to many dropouts. Woe Industries intends to consider a more accessible schedule if running the test again. Some participants submitted unrelated screenshots or attempted joking bribes, but the two successful players completed the game quickly, hinting at strong prior familiarity or exceptional memory skills. This event highlights the enduring difficulty of classic adventure games when played without modern aids or spoilers.

Resident Evil Requiem Hits Five Million Sales in Four Days

Capcom’s Resident Evil Requiem, released February 27, has quickly become one of the publisher's biggest hits. Selling over five million copies in its first four days, it joined Capcom’s all-time top 20 bestsellers list, positioned between the original 1998 Resident Evil 2 and its 2014 HD remake. The game also set a new series record for launch-day concurrent players on Steam, surpassing Resident Evil 4 Remake by about 100,000 players.

Requiem's strong launch sales reflect its blend of action and survival horror elements, appealing to longtime fans and newcomers alike. While it falls short of Monster Hunter Wilds’ recent eight million copies sold in three days, many industry observers believe Requiem may enjoy a steadier sales pace over time. Its success reinforces Resident Evil’s continuing commercial viability and Capcom’s established place in the survival horror genre.

Counter-Strike: Global Offensive Returns to Steam After Being Folded into CS2

Three years after being merged into Counter-Strike 2, CS:GO has surprisingly reappeared on Steam as a standalone, albeit unlisted, title. Although online matchmaking is still disabled, players can manually join community servers using the Steam server browser. Since the reintroduction, CS:GO has surged back onto Steam's most-played games chart, reaching over 61,000 concurrent players and cracking the top 20.

The reasons behind Valve’s decision remain unclear, but the revival is widely welcomed by the CS community. Many players appreciate CS:GO's unique gameplay and long-standing player base, suggesting there is room for both it and CS2 in the competitive shooter landscape. This move may indicate Valve's recognition of CS:GO's cultural significance and enduring appeal even years after its formal replacement.

Developer of Build a Rocket Boy's MindsEye Alleges Espionage Amid Layoffs

Build a Rocket Boy, the studio behind the game MindsEye, has accused unnamed parties of orchestrated espionage and corporate sabotage that negatively impacted the game’s launch. CEO Mark Gerhard claims the studio possesses overwhelming evidence and is pursuing legal action, though no details have been disclosed publicly due to ongoing prosecution. Alongside these allegations, the studio announced another round of layoffs.

These accusations follow previous claims of sabotage, such as coordinated negative campaigns involving bot farms. However, former developers and the game's publisher have attributed MindsEye’s troubled launch to internal issues including mismanagement, crunch culture, and lack of direction. The studio’s admission of difficulties combined with layoffs underscores the challenges smaller developers face, regardless of whether external interference played a role.

Ubisoft Officially Confirms Assassin’s Creed Black Flag Remake

Ubisoft confirmed the long-rumored remake of Assassin’s Creed 4: Black Flag, officially titled Assassin's Creed Black Flag Resynced. The announcement accompanies a teaser image of protagonist Edward Kenway aboard the Jackdaw. The confirmation comes amid other series news such as the end of support for Assassin’s Creed Shadows and plans for a future co-op AC game.

Interestingly, Ubisoft has dropped the "4" from the remake’s title, a move that may signal intent to treat Black Flag as a standalone sub-series.
