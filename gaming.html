Marvel Rivals Plans Expanded Content Inspired by MCU's Infinity Saga

NetEase’s Marvel Rivals is looking to evolve beyond its original 6v6 hero shooter format. Guangyun Chen, the game's creative director, described upcoming developments as becoming a “comprehensive Marvel ‘moving anime’ experience.” While the phrase is unclear, more concrete details indicate expansions beyond PvP to include new PvE modes. A notable event titled *Path to Doomsday* will tie in with the upcoming *Avengers: Doomsday* movie, offering themed content and game modes inspired by the Infinity Saga. This shift aims to diversify gameplay and engage players with story-connected material, moving Marvel Rivals closer to narrative-driven experiences.

Dungeons & Dragons 1980s Cartoon Episodes Now Streaming

Wizards of the Coast has begun uploading the full 1980s *Dungeons & Dragons* animated series to YouTube, releasing two or three episodes weekly. The show, a relic co-produced by Marvel and animated by Toei, features voice actors famous for other animated classics like *Transformers*. Known for its dated animation and storytelling, the series remains a nostalgic piece for longtime RPG fans and those interested in 1980s pop culture. The episodes include appearances of iconic D&D monsters and occasionally contain storylines reflective of the era’s unusual approaches to children's programming.

Fighting Game YouTuber Offers $10,000 to Upgrade Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3 Netcode

Popular fighting game streamer Maximilian Christiansen, known as Maximilian Dood, has put up a $10,000 bounty to support development of rollback netcode for *Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3*. Currently, the game uses delay-based netcode, which often results in laggy online matches. Rollback netcode, favored in many modern fighting games, predicts player inputs to reduce latency and improve responsiveness. Christiansen expressed a desire to enhance the game’s online experience beyond current stopgap solutions like Parsec streaming, reflecting ongoing community interest in preserving and improving classic fighting game infrastructure.

Rockstar Games Hit by Data Breach; Hackers Demand Ransom

Rockstar Games confirmed a security breach involving their outsourced Snowflake cloud storage system, exploited via a third-party analytics tool recently compromised. A hacker group known as ShinyHunters claimed responsibility and is demanding a ransom, threatening to leak company data by April 14 if unpaid. Rockstar stated the breach involved “a limited amount of non-material company information” and assured no player-related data or organizational operations had been affected. This incident follows high-profile leaks of Rockstar’s *GTA 6* material in 2022, underscoring ongoing challenges around data security in the industry and reliance on third-party services.

1000xResist Developer Revisits FMV for New Narrative Game

The development team behind cyberpunk adventure *1000xResist* revealed that a video sequence included in their game was initially added “out of desperation” using existing documentary footage. Encouraged by this experience, the studio Sunset Visitor plans to embrace full-motion video (FMV) in their upcoming title *Prove You’re Human*. This new game centers on a digital copy of a person interacting in a virtual environment, with narrative sequences depicted through live-action video. Despite FMV's mixed reputation due to past low-budget titles, several indie developers are exploring the format as a storytelling tool, aiming for new uses within narrative-driven games.

BioWare’s Future and Challenges: A Closer Look

Reflecting on BioWare’s recent struggles, an analysis outlines the difficulties facing the studio historically known for iconic RPGs like *Mass Effect* and *Dragon Age*. While their last release, *Dragon Age: The Veilguard*, received mixed reviews and underperformed commercially, BioWare’s challenges are attributed to shifting market demands and pressures from parent company EA. The studio has lost much of its institutional knowledge and faces hurdles balancing innovation with legacy franchises. The critique suggests that BioWare’s potential comeback might require shelving plans for new *Mass Effect* entries and instead focusing on regaining creative space to explore new ideas and rebuild its identity. However, current pressures from EA for commercial hits create significant obstacles to such a reset.
