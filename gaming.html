The Importance of Headbob in First-Person RPGs

A recent experience playing *The Outer Worlds 2* has brought attention to the role of headbob in first-person RPGs. Headbob is the subtle camera movement that mimics the natural bobbing of a person's head while walking or running. Unlike many modern RPGs such as *Skyrim* or *Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2*, *The Outer Worlds 2* notably lacks headbob except during sprinting. This absence has led to a less immersive feeling, making the player feel like a detached, stabilized camera rather than a character moving through the world. The game's developer, Obsidian, may have chosen this design as a stylistic preference or for aiming stability in combat, but the lack of headbob reduces the sense of embodiment for players. This highlights how minor design choices, often unnoticed, can significantly impact player experience in RPGs.
