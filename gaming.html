Five New Steam Games Worth Noting

This week on Steam, several new releases may have escaped widespread attention amid the steady flow of launches. Among these is **Homura Hime**, a third-person action game reminiscent of early 2000s slashers like Devil May Cry, delivering straightforward combat against demon girls with anime-inspired visuals. For those seeking a more relaxed experience, **Planet of Lana 2** continues the hand-painted cinematic platforming style of its predecessor, featuring diverse environments such as frozen peaks and deep oceans. Meanwhile, **iRacing Arcade** offers a more casual, cartoonish alternative to the serious racing sim iRacing, including some management elements, accessible to a broader audience. Cozy game fans can explore **Shinehill**, a post-Stardew-like farming and community game with a sci-fi twist, while **Angel Engine** transforms a vertical-video analog horror series into a grim surgical simulator with a ’90s point-and-click aesthetic.

These releases highlight the variety available on Steam, catering to different tastes, from action and racing to cozy farming and narrative horror, demonstrating that niche and experimental games continue to find space alongside bigger titles.

Ultima 8 Gains In-Depth Coverage in Noted CRPG Docuseries

YouTuber Majuular, known for his extensive retrospectives on computer role-playing games, has released a three-hour documentary focused on **Ultima 8**. This installment delves into the game’s development history, narrative ambitions, and its place within the Ultima series and the CRPG genre. Although Ultima 8 is often considered one of the weaker entries in the series, its influence remains noteworthy.

This video is part of the broader Ultima retrospective series that explores how these early games helped shape modern role-playing games. Its detailed analysis is valuable for anyone interested in the evolution of RPG design and history, providing context for contemporary CRPG successes and design philosophies.

Hytale Emphasizes Modding and Community Hiring

Hypixel Studios, developers of the voxel sandbox game **Hytale**, have announced a significant push towards supporting modding, underscored by a large modding contest featuring a six-figure prize pool. Studio founder Simon Collins-Laflamme explained on social media that their motivation goes beyond community engagement—they actively recruit talented modders into their development team.

This approach emphasizes maintaining a smaller, agile development team closely connected to the game's active player base. By fostering modding, Hytale aims to stay responsive to player creativity and crowdsource ideas that can influence the game’s future development, blending community talent with professional development.

Browser-Based Exploration of Crazy Taxi’s Dreamcast Maps

Programmer Will has recreated the cityscapes from the Dreamcast-era arcade classic **Crazy Taxi** for free exploration in web browsers through the noclip project, a digital video game level museum. Using an open-source approach to replicate original rendering techniques, these recreations allow players to explore the game’s distinctive urban environments without time pressure or gameplay constraints.

This project offers a fresh perspective on a cult classic’s world design and urban layout, illustrating the enduring appeal of early 3D arcade games beyond their gameplay. It also serves as an example of the increasing trend of interactive preservation and exploration of game worlds in alternative formats.

Slay the Spire 2 Developer Reflects on Competitive Steam Launch

Mega Crit, developers of **Slay the Spire 2**, addressed a recent social media comment that humorously suggested supporting smaller indie projects like **Marathon**, which was perceived by some as dismissive. The developers clarified that the remark was meant as light sarcasm, expressing surprise at Slay the Spire 2’s overwhelming success, which saw it surpass Marathon’s concurrent player count significantly.

Slay the Spire 2’s strong launch demonstrates the challenges smaller indie projects face competing for attention alongside breakout hits. Mega Crit’s response indicates a recognition of this dynamic alongside healthy competition within the Steam marketplace.
