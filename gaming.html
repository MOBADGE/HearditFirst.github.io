Obsidian Shares Challenges in Balancing RPG Difficulty in The Outer Worlds 2

At this year's Game Developers Conference, Obsidian’s senior systems designer Robert Donovan discussed the difficulty curve design for The Outer Worlds 2. The team aimed to maintain a balance similar to the first game’s opening area, where weak enemies felt weak and strong enemies strong. They targeted a more modest character power progression, avoiding exponential gains typical in RPGs. Instead, they sought meaningful upgrades giving around 10% boosts to player stats to ensure choices felt impactful.

A notable approach was their "hits to kill" system, calibrating enemy strength based on the number of shots needed to defeat them. Despite extensive testing and tuning, player feedback varied widely, with equal numbers finding the game too hard, too easy, or just right. Donovan acknowledged it's difficult to satisfy all players, especially given diverse playstyles, from casual to build-focused. The Outer Worlds 2 reflects this compromise, offering varied builds while aiming to make normal difficulty appealing to most players.

Disco Elysium Creators Reflect on Impact and Welcome Successors

The creators of Disco Elysium, a 2019 narrative RPG known for its complex writing and unique style, expressed surprise and appreciation at the game's cultural impact, particularly considering its modest beginnings in a small flat in Estonia. During GDC, ZA/UM writers Honey Watson and Siim Sinamäe noted how the game has inspired successors in the genre, such as Esoteric Ebb and Tangerine Antarctic, which share Disco Elysium’s emphasis on rich storytelling and player choice.

While acknowledging their success, the team emphasized the need to keep innovating and not rest on past achievements. They welcomed new entries in the niche RPG market, seeing them as positive competition that encourages creative growth. Early impressions of ZA/UM’s upcoming game Zero Parades indicate it retains the charm and narrative depth fans expect.

Bauhutte Unveils Electric Gaming Bed 2 Aimed at Specialized Use

Japanese furniture company Bauhutte introduced the Electric Gaming Bed 2, a motorized bed designed to allow users to game while lying down. It features adjustable sections for the upper body and legs, with new folding capabilities aimed at easier transport. The product is marketed for users seeking a highly indulgent gaming experience, combining comfort with functionality.

Though such adjustable beds can benefit people with mobility issues, Bauhutte’s promotion focuses on luxury and lifestyle appeal. The bed costs about $450 in Japan, excluding the separately sold gaming mattress. Availability outside Japan is currently unclear.

Starfield’s Composer Comments on Todd Howard’s Vision and Game Reception

Composer Inon Zur, responsible for the music of Starfield and several Fallout titles, spoke about Starfield's development and its creator Todd Howard in an RPG Site interview. Zur described Howard as a visionary whose ideas might take time for players to fully appreciate. He believes Starfield will eventually be regarded as a "legendary" game despite its mixed reception since its 2023 launch.

Zur discussed his approach to the game’s score, highlighting the contrast between the vastness of space and the individual’s significance, which shaped the music's atmospheric qualities. Although some players found Starfield underwhelming or too ambitious, Zur credited Howard’s unwavering commitment to his vision.

CD Projekt Clarifies No New Cyberpunk 2077 DLC Amid Witcher 3 Expansion Speculation

CD Projekt has explicitly stated that there are currently no plans for additional DLCs or expansions for Cyberpunk 2077. This response came amid rumors and speculation about new content for The Witcher 3, with fans and analysts fueling expectations for future expansions.

While the company’s statement only applies to Cyberpunk 2077, some interpret the silence on Witcher 3 updates alongside this as suggestive, though nothing official has been confirmed. CD Projekt’s next earnings report on March 19 may provide further insights.

Arc Raiders Developer Keeps Vehicle Inclusion Open but Uncertain

Arc Raiders production director Caio Braga spoke at GDC about the possibility of adding vehicles to the game in the future. Though vehicle prototypes exist from seven years ago, there are no current plans to develop them further. The developers remain open to ideas that fit the game's lore and experience.

Braga noted the project’s long-term support plans, intending to evolve the game over many years. Adding vehicles could help distinguish Arc Raiders in the extraction shooter market, which typically emphasizes on-foot combat
