Size Five Games Director Shares His PC Gaming Habits

Dan Marshall, director of British indie studio Size Five Games, provided insight into the games he currently plays and keeps installed on his PC. Despite usually moving on after finishing a game, Marshall is still playing *Atomfall*, a first-person shooter set in a British village he initially underestimated. He also draws inspiration from *Into the Restless Ruins*, a dungeon-building game, and keeps older classics like *Fallout* and *X-Wing* installed for nostalgia and historical appreciation. His most played game by far is *XCOM 2*, with nearly 219 hours logged, a title he says he will never uninstall. The interview highlights the diversity of games inspiring indie developers and the lasting impact of some classics on modern creators.

Final Fantasy 14 Community Revives Blitzball with a Detailed Roleplay System

Within *Final Fantasy 14*, a player-created community has built an extensive roleplay league centered around Blitzball, the fictional sport from *Final Fantasy 10*. Despite the official game not including Blitzball, fans developed a comprehensive 140-slide rulebook and TTRPG-like mechanics to host fast-paced in-game matches using macros, chat commands, and raid markers for timing and interaction. The Etheirys Blitzball League has grown from previous smaller organizations and now hosts seasonal tournaments, creating a social hub where players develop characters and narratives over time. The community also uses its platform for charity work and brings together players across multiple servers. This highlights how player-driven creativity can extend game experiences beyond official content.

Tingus Goose: A New Idle Clicker with a Dark Twist on Human-Goose Relations

*Tingus Goose* is a new idle clicker game on Steam noted for its disturbing aesthetic. Developed by internet artist MasterTingus, the game envisions a world where humans reproduce geese in a semi-parasitic relationship. Players grow a "goose-tree" made of goose-branches that interact with hatchling "tingis" in ways both grotesque and mechanically intricate. The game combines clicker progression with strategic placement and evolution of tingis to maximize income while featuring unsettling animations and themes. Despite its unsettling nature, *Tingus Goose* offers a surprisingly deep gameplay loop appealing to fans of unique and challenging idle games.
