Arc Raiders Community Creates Bounty System to Address Player Betrayal

Players of the third-person shooter *Arc Raiders* have developed a community-run bounty system called Speranza Bounties, aimed at penalizing unsportsmanlike behavior such as ambushes during extractions. Using a website platform, players can mark offenders, vote on the most wanted targets, and submit proof of their elimination in-game. Rewards are planned for hunters who take down top offenders but have not yet been detailed.

The streamer TheBurntPeanut currently holds the top spot on the bounty list, despite already being "eliminated" multiple times, due to reported actions like "voice chat snake" and "hate speech." Other well-known streamers also appear on the list, suggesting the system may be used as much for targeting popular players as for controlling toxic play. While some players appreciate this method of community accountability, others caution that such systems can lead to witch hunts and overreactions, echoing similar issues in games like *Marvel Rivals*.

Steam Survey Data Shows Limited Shift from Windows to Linux in Gaming

Despite improvements in Linux gaming support and growing interest in alternatives to Windows, historical Steam Hardware & Software Survey data indicates that most PC gamers remain on Windows. As of March 2026, over 92% of Steam users still use some version of Windows, though there was a small decline of 3.77% from the previous year.

Linux usage among gamers rose by 3% to 5.33%, while macOS users increased by 0.77%. This growth suggests some gradual migration, particularly among users with older hardware unable to meet Windows 11 requirements. However, the majority of the gaming population has yet to make a significant switch. Notably, a substantial portion of Windows users still run Windows 10, which no longer receives general support, highlighting the challenges some face when upgrading.

Graveyard Keeper Giveaway Boosted DLC Sales by Nearly $250,000

Publisher TinyBuild revealed that offering the original *Graveyard Keeper* free for a limited time on Steam has generated nearly $250,000 in DLC sales revenue. This move was designed to increase awareness and interest ahead of the upcoming sequel, *Graveyard Keeper 2*, which has already entered Steam’s top 100 most wishlisted games, with over 450,000 wishlists.

While the base game giveaway has ended, DLC remains heavily discounted. The sequel is anticipated later this year, though some community concerns have arisen about the developer’s stance on AI-generated content, with the studio clarifying that AI is not being used in the new game.

The Blood of Dawnwalker’s Director Welcomes Witcher 3 Comparisons

Konrad Tomaszkiewicz, director of the upcoming sandbox vampire RPG *The Blood of Dawnwalker* and former director on *The Witcher 3*, expressed confidence in his new game despite frequent comparisons to his previous work. Set in 14th-century Europe, the game features a half-vampire protagonist and blends familiar dark fantasy elements with unique mechanics such as a day-night cycle that affects vampire abilities and a time-limited quest system.

Tomaszkiewicz aims to push AAA RPG boundaries with new immersive features while maintaining a mid-sized studio structure, hoping to avoid the communication issues that come with rapid growth. The game is planned for release later this year and is currently available for wishlist on Steam.

Star Trek: Resurgence to Be Delisted After Three Years

*Star Trek: Resurgence*, a narrative adventure released in 2023 reminiscent of Telltale Games’ style, is scheduled to be removed from sale due to an expiring distribution license. Current owners will retain access, but new purchases will soon be unavailable. The exact date of delisting has not been specified but is expected around the game’s third anniversary in late May.

The delisting follows similar patterns with other Star Trek titles, many of which have had short market lifespans due to licensing complications. The game was developed by Dramatic Labs, founded by former Telltale Studios CEO Kevin Bruner, with no announced new projects after *Resurgence*.
