LATEST Gaming

Bite-sized summaries of the latest gaming trends — without the hype.

Updated: March 18, 2026

**Publisher Finji Overwhelmed by AI-Driven Game Submissions** Finji, the indie studio behind games like *Tunic* and *Night in the Woods*, reports a significant influx of submissions featuring AI-generated content. CEO Rebekah Saltsman expressed fatigue with the volume of AI-made games, pitches, and resumes filling their inbox. Finji remains cautious about AI involvement, emphasizing that they do not want uncredited AI assets in their projects. Their updated contracts allow terminating partnerships if undisclosed AI use is detected, due to concerns over copyright and quality. This stance highlights ongoing industry tensions over generative AI's role in game development and publishing.

**Inside PlayerUnknown Productions’ 2-Day Modjam Experiment** PlayerUnknown Productions recently held an in-studio modjam where developers spent two days creating game mods for their own title *Prologue: Go Wayback*. Mod projects ranged from converting a temperate forest biome to a desert, to adding new mechanics like sandstorms and vehicles, to creating a horror-themed total conversion mod. The event served as both a creative outlet for the team and a way to explore community modding possibilities, something community feedback has called for. Studio founder Brendan Greene envisions modding as central to building an open-source metaverse, emphasizing the importance of human collaboration beyond online interactions.

**Starfinder: Afterlight Shows Promise but Needs Polish Before Demo** The newly formed studio Epictellers’ first game, *Starfinder: Afterlight*, received a positive early impression during a closed beta, particularly for its faithful adaptation of the Starfinder 2e tabletop RPG mechanics and art style. However, the game is still missing important tactical combat actions and lacks a comprehensive tutorial, potentially limiting broader player understanding. Voice acting and visual design have drawn praise, but UI and gameplay depth require refinement ahead of the planned public demo this summer. The game’s progress illustrates the challenges new studios face in adapting complex RPG systems to video games.

**V-color Launches DDR5 Memory Kits with Dummy Modules** Hardware manufacturer V-color introduced “1+1” DDR5 memory kits that pair a functional DIMM with a dummy DIMM to mimic dual-channel memory setups while physically providing single-channel performance. This unconventional approach addresses the current high prices and scarcity of DDR5 chips, giving cost-conscious PC builders the aesthetic and upgrade path benefits of dual-channel kits without the immediate expense. The kits aim to allow users to start with one module and add a second later. The success of this offering will depend on final pricing and consumer acceptance, given the performance trade-offs.

**Former Sims 4 Developer Discusses Limitations in Sim Planning AI** David “Rez” Graham, former lead AI programmer on *The Sims 4*, shared insights on why Sims do not autonomously plan multi-step actions. He explains that Sims operate by continually reassessing immediate needs rather than long-term plans, partly due to the challenge of telegraphing intent to players. Fully autonomous Sim planning could reduce player interaction and story creation, turning the game into a mere simulation to observe. While Graham personally wanted more planning in The Sims, the balance between believable AI and player agency has kept such features out.

**China Approves Nvidia H200 GPU Imports Amid US Export Limits** Chinese authorities have approved imports of Nvidia’s H200 AI GPU, a significant development following earlier US restrictions on AI chip exports to China. Nvidia has resumed production of the H200, which is part of their Hopper line but predates the latest Blackwell generation. Despite this, export to China remains subject to caps imposed by the US government, limiting sales to individual Chinese firms. This reflects ongoing tensions between US efforts to limit China’s AI hardware access and China’s desire both to leverage imported technology and foster domestic AI chip development.

**Actor Charlie Cox to Star in Another Video Game Following Success in Clair Obscur** Charlie Cox, known for his role as Daredevil in the Marvel TV series, has announced participation in another video game project following his voice role as Gustave in *Clair Obscur: Expedition 33*. Cox acknowledged that his experience with *Expedition 33*, a game that won multiple awards last year, broadened his appreciation for the medium and its fanbase. The new project promises a more involved role for Cox, signaling growing crossover between traditional acting and video game performance.
