Vince Zampella, Influential Game Developer, Dies in Car Crash

Vince Zampella, co-founder of Infinity Ward and head of Respawn and Ripple Effect studios at Electronic Arts, tragically died in a car accident in the San Gabriel Mountains near Los Angeles on Sunday. The crash also claimed the life of another passenger. Zampella was 55.

Zampella was a prominent figure in the video game industry, best known for co-creating the Call of Duty franchise and later leading Respawn's successful releases including Titanfall, Apex Legends, and Star Wars Jedi games. After a high-profile departure from Activision, he founded Respawn to maintain creative control over projects. In recent years, Zampella took charge of the Battlefield series. His death has been widely mourned, with Electronic Arts and Respawn acknowledging his profound impact on the industry and gaming communities.
