Mchose Ace 68 Turbo Review: World's First 16 kHz Polling Keyboard

The Mchose Ace 68 Turbo has been released, claiming the title of the world's first keyboard with a 16 kHz polling rate enabled by a 512 MHz dual-core microcontroller. This polling rate doubles that of existing high-end gaming keyboards, offering theoretical latency reductions that may be noticeable only to a narrow subset of users.

The keyboard is a compact 68-key design with Mount Tai GT HE magnetic switches that utilize the Hall effect for keypress detection, providing smooth, quiet operation and presumably longer durability without physical contact wear. The unit features aluminum construction making it noticeably heavy at 1.36 kg. It connects via detachable USB Type-C and supports RGB backlighting controllable through software.

While the hardware and software provide extensive customization options, including adjustable trigger points and profiles optimized for different game genres, the benchmarking impact on gaming performance is expected to be minimal due to other latency factors inherent in online play. The keyboard is priced at around $139, positioning it as a premium model aimed primarily at gamers who prioritize responsiveness.
