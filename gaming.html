Roleplay Police Academies Rise in GTA V RP

Grand Theft Auto V roleplay communities have seen a surge of players applying for roles in the in-game Los Santos Police Department (LSPD), treating the experience almost like real employment. Servers such as Eclipse RP have organized detailed application processes, including written character backstories, voice interviews, training academies, and probation periods. This structured approach maps closely to actual police recruitment, with realism stressed through training on radio codes, report writing, and controlled in-character behavior. The trend highlights the evolving complexity and seriousness of GTA RP, where players commit significant time and effort to immersive roleplay, blending online simulation with social interaction.

Banjo-Kazooie Receives Native PC Port with Enhancements

A modding group led by developer Wiseguy has released Banjo: Recompiled, a native port of the original Nintendo 64 Banjo-Kazooie for Windows, Linux, and Mac systems. The port requires original game files but improves upon emulation by supporting higher frame rates, widescreen and ultrawide displays, and customizable controls including dual analog camera options. It also includes quality-of-life features like retaining collectible notes between deaths or level changes, borrowed from the Rare Replay edition. A built-in mod menu supports popular mods and an HD texture pack, making this port a comprehensive way for fans to experience the game with modern updates. The release continues a recent pattern of decompilation-based ports revitalizing classic N64 titles on PC.

Players can build police headquarters with up to 50 officers who patrol and respond to incidents employing more realistic AI behavior. The system uses real-world crime statistics and research as a basis, underlining the developer‚Äôs commitment to realism. Fantasy Roguelike Lort Adjusts Difficulty Following Player Feedback

Shortly after launching, Lort, a fantasy roguelike inspired by Risk of Rain 2, has made adjustments to its difficulty in response to player concerns. The developer, Big Distraction, emphasized that the game is designed to be challenging, encouraging players to die and learn from encounters. To improve accessibility without easing core difficulty, the update has made early enemies like Goblin Lobbers less lethal and their attacks easier to anticipate. A new tutorial prompt highlights character attributes to guide player progression more effectively. While 28% of Steam reviews were negative at launch, the team is also working to improve multiplayer connectivity, aiming to enhance the overall player experience.
