Blippo+ Offers a Campy FMV Experience but Falls Short in Engagement

Blippo+ is a Full Motion Video (FMV) game that simulates receiving transmissions from an alien world named Planet Blip, which resembles an eccentric 1980s television landscape. Players mainly cycle through short live-action and animated shows, unlocking more content by watching a set percentage of daily broadcasts. The game features quirky segments like a quiz show without dice rolls and real teens discussing topical issues. Despite its creative premise and nostalgic aesthetic, Blippo+ struggles with repetitive content and lack of narrative payoff. The broadcast-based unlocking system can feel tedious, as many segments repeat multiple times before new ones become accessible. Ultimately, its attempt to blend television and gaming is intriguing but not fully satisfying. It is available on Steam and itch.io.

Audio Director Reflects on Deus Ex: Invisible War’s Soundtrack

Alexander Brandon, composer of the original Deus Ex and audio director for its sequel, Deus Ex: Invisible War, shared insights on the game’s audio design in a recent interview. Brandon acknowledged that the sequel’s audio had "room for improvement," but expressed pride in elements like the main theme and faction-specific soundscapes. The soundtrack aimed to support a darker, more immersive tone than its predecessor. Notably, music shifted to reflect different game locations and factions dynamically, enhancing gameplay atmosphere. Brandon also highlighted contributions from the band Kidneythieves and praised the thematic freedom he exercised despite certain constraints. While the game remains divisive among fans, its audio direction represents a thoughtful effort within its development context.

Resident Evil Requiem Patch Introduces Photo Mode and Subtle Visual Tweaks

A new patch for Resident Evil Requiem adds a photo mode allowing players to capture and tweak screenshots of characters and scenes. Although the camera movement range is limited, users can apply filters and adjust lighting to create varied shots. The patch also addressed bugs, including fixes for crashes and progress issues. Additionally, some character expressions in cutscenes have been adjusted to better convey emotions, though these changes are subtle. The photo mode is accessed through the pause menu or shortcut keys, but camera movement is restricted during scripted animations. This update offers players more ways to engage with the game's visuals post-completion.

Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous Boasts Extensive Subclass Options and Powerful Mounted Combat

Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous stands out among CRPGs for its extensive character customization, featuring 232 subclasses across 26 base classes and additional prestige classes. This breadth supports complex buildcrafting, appealing especially to players who enjoy optimizing character builds. The game also emphasizes mounted combat as a strategic advantage in higher difficulties. A recently added Ranger subclass from the expansion "A Dance of Masks" allows players to ride a griffon mount from level one, granting mobility and a unique aerial attack. While some subclasses may be niche or less effective depending on the campaign, this diversity encourages experimentation and replayability. The game is available with a sale until early April on Steam and GOG.

Star Wars Zero Company’s Director Discusses the Revival of Classic PC Strategy Genres

Greg Foertsch, creative director of the upcoming Star Wars Zero Company, provided his perspective on the resurgence of traditional PC gaming genres like real-time strategy (RTS), turn-based tactics, and grand strategy. He noted that in the early 2000s, many such games struggled to transition to consoles due to technical and design limitations, including insufficient camera use and content delivery methods. Recent advancements in user experience and controller support have made these genres more accessible on consoles and couch gaming setups. Foertsch also pointed out the continuing innovation within turn-based tactics games since 2012, driven by developers exploring new mechanics and narratives. His comments suggest a broader evolution in the genre’s appeal and design.

Owlcat Clarifies Its Use of AI in The Expanse RPG Development

Developer Owlcat confirmed that its upcoming RPG The Expanse: Osiris Reborn will be fully human-created in terms of final game content, but that generative AI has been used during early prototyping and concept development phases. The studio emphasized that no AI-generated assets or voice acting will appear in the finished product. Owlcat’s stance aligns with a broader industry trend where AI serves as a tool for inspiration, vision coordination, and speeding up internal workflows rather than final asset creation.
