Slay the Spire 2 Introduces Multiplayer Co-op Mode

Slay the Spire 2, the sequel to the popular deckbuilding roguelite, has added a multiplayer co-op mode, allowing up to four players to climb the tower together. One player hosts a save file from their profile, and friends can join via Steam friends list—there are no matchmaking queues or passwords. Each player selects their character and retains an individual inventory of gold, cards, and relics, while the party navigates a shared map and battles enemies scaled to party size.

In combat, energy pools remain individual, but enemy attacks hit the entire party. Players revive after battles if the team wins, and rewards like loot and cards are not shared but individualized. Multiplayer Ascension progression is separate from singleplayer and requires all players to have unlocked corresponding levels to access them. The co-op mode aims to preserve the core solo gameplay while enabling shared strategy and camaraderie.

This multiplayer addition may broaden the appeal of Slay the Spire 2 by introducing cooperative elements without sacrificing the game's detailed deckbuilding and roguelike challenges.

Slay the Spire 2 Sets New Records Among Deckbuilders

According to data from Newzoo, Slay the Spire 2 has become the best-performing deckbuilding game ever at launch. Its early access release saw a peak of 575,000 concurrent Steam players, far exceeding the peaks of predecessors like Slay the Spire 1 (57,000) and Monster Train 2 (18,500). Sales reached three million copies within the first week, generating substantial revenue.

The game has averaged over one million daily users in its opening days, with no apparent drop-off, indicating strong player engagement. Newzoo attributes this success to years of anticipation following the first game, a lack of competing sequels, and consumer trust in Mega Crit as a developer. The addition of multiplayer may also be attracting broader audiences, including those interested in competitive and cooperative gameplay challenges.

These metrics indicate a significant milestone for the deckbuilder genre and highlight Slay the Spire 2’s potential for sustained popularity.

Kojima Productions Adjusted Death Stranding 2 to Improve Completion Rates

Hideo Kojima and his development team designed Death Stranding 2 with the explicit goal of having more players complete the game compared to its predecessor. Feedback on the original Death Stranding noted the game felt slow, which influenced design choices to streamline worldbuilding and make the sequel more accessible.

The team retained depth for completionists but ensured the main story was easier to follow through supplemental elements. Early data shows that over 62% of players have reached the final chapter of Death Stranding 2, a rise compared to fewer than half finishing the first game across various platforms. Improvements include earlier access to vehicles and tools that ease traversal, though the game still offers a harder difficulty mode for players seeking challenge.

These changes reflect Kojima Productions’ response to player input, balancing narrative integrity with pacing to enhance overall completion.

OpenAI Ends Sora Video App Amid Copyright Concerns; Disney Withdraws Investment

OpenAI has announced it will discontinue Sora, its AI-powered video generation app, less than six months after the release of Sora 2. The decision comes amid ongoing copyright issues raised by multiple Japanese studios over unauthorized use of their content. Along with Sora’s shutdown, a developer version and video features in ChatGPT will also be discontinued.

Disney, which had planned a $1 billion investment in OpenAI linked to Sora’s use of Disney characters, has also pulled out of the deal. Disney cited respect for OpenAI’s strategic shift away from video generation while expressing intent to pursue AI opportunities responsibly.

This move signifies a retreat by OpenAI from AI video generation in favor of focusing on productivity tools, reflecting mounting legal and corporate pressures in the evolving AI landscape.
