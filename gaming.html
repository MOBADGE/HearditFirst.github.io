MMO Difficulty Remains a Persistent Design Challenge

Current MMORPG developers face ongoing difficulties balancing game challenge to satisfy a diverse player base. According to insights shared by industry insiders and the PC Gamer column *Terminally Online*, MMOs need to cater simultaneously to casual, midcore, and hardcore players, each with varying expectations for difficulty and engagement. Recent efforts in games like *World of Warcraft* and *Final Fantasy XIV* include scalable difficulty options and modular content such as optional hard encounters, aiming to provide tailored experiences. However, this approach increases the workload on studios, complicates tutorialization, and risks fragmenting player progression. Despite these hurdles, adapting difficulty remains essential for MMOs to retain players and sustain long-term engagement amid the live service model.
