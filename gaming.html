**AI-Generated Music Floods Deezer’s Uploads**

Deezer reports receiving nearly 75,000 fully AI-generated music tracks daily, making up 44% of all new uploads. While the platform employs AI detection measures that demonetize about 85% of such content as fraudulent, these tunes see very little actual user consumption—only 1-3%. A recent Deezer-commissioned survey found that 97% of participants could not distinguish AI-created tracks from human-made ones in blind tests. This raises concerns about the value and authenticity of music in the streaming era, as many listeners cannot reliably tell the difference. Most respondents believe AI music should be labeled clearly, and over half feel pure AI tracks should not compete on music charts alongside human-created songs. Deezer is now licensing its AI-detection technology industry-wide to mitigate the issue, citing studies that suggest creators’ revenues could decline by up to $4 billion by 2028 due to AI-generated fraudulent streams.

**Diablo 4: Lord of Hatred Expansion Reviewed**

Blizzard’s upcoming expansion for Diablo 4, *Lord of Hatred*, introduces two new classes, a fresh campaign set on the Skovos Isles, and significant gameplay and systems updates. Early reviews highlight a more flexible skill tree, greater build customization, and a stronger focus on fundamental action RPG gameplay. The campaign centers on the demon Mephisto, offering a story with a sense of finality uncommon in previous Diablo titles. The new region provides a mix of brighter and more surreal environments, including decaying forests and hellish dungeons reminiscent of Diablo 3. The expansion refines buildcrafting with robust skill trees and an updated Horadric Cube crafting system, allowing players to better tailor gear and abilities. While endgame activity updates have room to grow, the expansion is regarded as a successful evolution of Diablo 4’s core strengths.

**Razer Unveils World’s Thinnest Glass Mouse Pad**

Razer has announced the Atlas Pro, which it claims to be the world’s thinnest glass gaming mouse pad at just 1.9mm thick. This new mouse pad aims to improve wrist comfort by reducing thickness roughly by half compared to popular existing glass pads, which are around 4mm thick. The Atlas Pro features tempered glass, rounded edges, and a minimalist all-black or white aesthetic designed to blend seamlessly with modern setups. While durability is emphasized, the most critical factor remains surface glide and texture, which can impact gaming performance. Razer’s design head notes this product challenges longstanding norms in glass mouse pad design, aiming for refinement and comfort.

**Windows 11 April Update Causes Startup Issues on Some PCs**

The latest Windows 11 update (KB5083769) released in April has caused problems for some HP and Dell users, resulting in blue screen errors and boot loops after installation. Affected machines reportedly show graphical glitches on boot before entering a failure loop. In certain cases, recovery environments were inaccessible, complicating fixes. Some users succeeded in resolving the issue by uninstalling the update or pausing future updates, though some report the system attempts to reinstall the problematic patch automatically. Microsoft acknowledges the issue and continues work to improve Windows 11’s reliability and user experience amid a broader effort to manage update rollout and AI feature integration.

**Playdate Makers Ban Games Using Generative AI**

Creators of the Playdate handheld console with a crank have implemented a new policy disallowing games developed using generative AI for art, audio, music, text, or dialogue from being accepted in the Playdate Catalog store. The developer, Panic Inc., requires explicit disclosure of AI use and reserves acceptance for games created without generative AI, though traditional AI functionalities like enemy behaviors are permitted. Existing games that used AI will remain available but be flagged and documented accordingly. This move contrasts with some trends in the wider gaming industry and reflects a cautious stance on the use of AI in independent game development, particularly for niche platforms with constrained resources.
