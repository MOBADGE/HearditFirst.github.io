Cloudflare Reports Increasingly Complex Cyber Threats

Cloudflare's latest yearly threat report highlights a concerning shift in cybersecurity risks. Threat actors have become more sophisticated and varied, moving away from brute force attacks toward exploiting trusted tools and networks. The rise of AI-driven techniques is notable, with attackers using AI for real-time network reconnaissance, exploit development, and realistic deepfake-based scams. These AI-powered phishing attempts have proven significantly more effective than traditional methods. Additionally, state-sponsored actors continue to target telecommunications, while hackers increasingly misuse legitimate cloud services and APIs to bypass security measures. The report also warns of more frequent large-scale, short-lived distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks. For gamers and the wider public, the key takeaway remains cautious interaction with unfamiliar accounts and links.

A Dark Turn in Birds Watching’s Survival Narrative

The indie game Birds Watching subverts expectations of a peaceful nature simulator with survival horror elements. Set after an apocalyptic event where the protagonist is the presumed last human alive, the game begins with calming birdwatching gameplay but quickly grows tense. Players must earn trust from talking birds by transforming physically and psychologically to fit in. This narrative development is accompanied by a looming, monstrous bird-man entity that stalks the player, creating a persistent sense of threat. The game blends exploration, questing, and unsettling encounters, culminating in multiple possible endings influenced by player choices. Birds Watching illustrates how a meditative genre can incorporate horror to deliver a unique story experience.

Graphics Card Sales Decline as Nvidia Dominates Market

A recent Jon Peddie Research report reveals a decline in discrete graphics card sales during the fourth quarter of 2025, with shipments falling 4.4% from the previous quarter. Despite the overall drop, Nvidia’s market share grew by 1.6%, now representing approximately 94% of the market compared to AMD’s 5%. This shift follows the Nvidia RTX 50-series launch, contrasting with AMD’s Radeon 9000-series release. The report attributes the sales decrease in part to high hardware prices, driven by persistent memory and storage shortages linked to AI industry demand. Additionally, fewer new PCs shipped with discrete graphics units, as integrated GPUs gain traction. Industry forecasts anticipate a continued market contraction through 2028, suggesting consumers and manufacturers are adjusting to these economic pressures.

KOTOR Remake Project Confirms It Is Still Under Development

The long-delayed Knights of the Old Republic (KOTOR) remake remains in development, according to Saber Interactive’s chief creative officer Tim Willits. In a recent interview, Willits confirmed the project is ongoing but provided no further updates regarding progress or release timing. This marks the project's yearly status check since its troubled development history, including the original developer Aspyr’s removal from the project and minimal communication since. Previously, reports suggested that Saber is also considering a remake of KOTOR 2 in the future, but no official plans have been revealed. Fans anticipating the remake should expect ongoing silence until substantial progress is announced.
