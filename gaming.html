Crimson Desert Update Adds Hide Helmet Option

Crimson Desert has released a new patch that includes a highly requested feature: the ability to hide helmets. Players can choose from several options to display or conceal headgear during cutscenes, combat, or all the time via the settings menu. Additionally, the update introduces a new armor set and helmet for cats, increases camp storage capacity to 1,000 slots, and offers a "classic" movement control mode for those preferring the original setup. This patch reflects ongoing efforts by developer Pearl Abyss to refine the single-player RPG post-launch, addressing player feedback and improving gameplay flexibility.

Possible Elden Ring Movie Set Spotted in the UK

A fan in the UK captured video footage of a set resembling a ruined Church of Marika, a recognizable location from Elden Ring’s world, sparking speculation that it may be part of Alex Garland’s upcoming Elden Ring film. The set includes detailed props and construction materials typical of professional film production and is near a known filming site used for major productions. While unconfirmed by studio A24, the footage offers a rare look at the film’s potential visual style. Little is known about the movie’s plot or casting beyond earlier reports of actors in talks, leaving much to be revealed.

Datamine Reveals Unused Elden Ring Cutscene Featuring Miquella

Dataminer Lance McDonald uncovered a previously unseen base-game cutscene from Elden Ring involving DLC villain Miquella. The scene shows Miquella planting and nurturing the Haligtree sapling with his blood, linking closely to key story elements in the Shadow of the Erdtree expansion. Voice lines suggest Miquella’s vision of abundance and a new world, though the exact narrative context is unclear. This discovery sheds light on earlier iterations of Miquella’s character and storyline, highlighting content cut or reworked before the final DLC release.

Warframe Developer Rejects AI-Generated Content in Games

Digital Extremes’ community director Megan Everett stated unequivocally that Warframe and upcoming Soulframe content will never include AI-generated assets. Everett expressed frustration with distinguishing AI art from human work and rejects the use of generative AI in their games, citing a strong commitment to human creativity. This stance contrasts with an industry trend where many developers incorporate AI tools, sometimes controversially. The comment adds to ongoing debates about AI’s role and transparency in game development and content creation.

Amazon TV Orders Rewrite of Mass Effect Show for Broader Audience

Reports indicate that Amazon MGM Studios’ head of global TV, Peter Friedlander, has requested rewrites for the Mass Effect TV adaptation to make the show "more appealing to non-gamers." This suggests a focus on accessibility beyond the existing fanbase, a common challenge faced by video game adaptations balancing source material fidelity with broader audience engagement. The show remains in development, with casting talks ongoing and details about the series still sparse. The rewrite may influence how the narrative and characters are presented to television viewers less familiar with the games.

Fallout: New Vegas PS3 Edition Added as Mod for PC

A modder has ported Fallout: New Vegas’ PS3 “Greatest Hits” edition into the PC version as a usable in-game item. This meta-mod adds a detailed physical representation of the PS3 release’s box art that can be wielded as a weapon, complete with unique effects tied to the Wild Wasteland perk. The mod is notable for its humor and nostalgia, highlighting the lasting community interest in Fallout: New Vegas and creative cross-platform experimentation within modding circles.

Reflections on Consoles: One Writer’s Move Away From PS6

A PC Gamer hardware writer shared personal reflections on why they won’t be purchasing a PlayStation 6, despite previously owning a PS5 at launch. Increasing console prices, supply constraints linked to AI-driven memory demand, and a shift in gaming preferences toward shorter, self-contained experiences inform their decision. The writer also notes Sony’s renewed console exclusivity and contrasts PlayStation’s trajectory with Nintendo’s ongoing appeal. This perspective illustrates evolving consumer attitudes amid economic and industry changes shaping hardware choices.
