Lost Fallout: New Vegas Post-Ending Dialogue Restored

Recently discovered beta content for Fallout: New Vegas revealed additional post-ending dialogue that was originally cut due to time constraints. Obsidian had planned for players to continue exploring a Mojave Wasteland reflecting their campaign choices after the ending slideshow. The new lines provide more depth to the game’s conclusion — for example, merchants closing shop under Legion rule, companion remarks varying by faction allegiance, and new reactions from characters when Mr. House or Yes Man assumes control. This content has been partially restored by modders through a work-in-progress mod requiring another functional post-game mod. This restoration offers players a richer, more immersive post-campaign experience that was initially missing from the title.

The Legend of Khiimori Early Access Review

The Legend of Khiimori, a realistic horse courier and survival simulator, entered early access on March 3, 2026. Priced at $30, the game combines horse management, route planning, and survival mechanics in a historical Mongolian setting. Players manage horse needs such as food, stamina, and mood while navigating environmental hazards and delivering quests. Features like horse breeding, crafting, and combat with predators are included, though several planned elements—such as riding styles, taming, and the main story—are still in development. The game shows promise, particularly with its immersive route planning system, but currently suffers from bugs, incomplete narrative content, and some graphical roughness. It may appeal most to dedicated horse simulation fans willing to engage with an early-stage product.

Age Verification Required for R18+ Online Games in Australia

Starting next week, Australia will require users to verify their age when accessing online games rated R18+, including popular titles like GTA Online. This move is part of the new Age-Restricted Material Codes aiming to extend online protections comparable to those in physical retail and real-world venues. Publishers that fail to comply could face fines up to AU$49.5 million per breach. The requirement applies only to online multiplayer or connected components, exempting offline singleplayer games. Various age assurance methods are under consideration, ranging from government ID checks to facial recognition technologies used by social platforms. The policy has raised privacy concerns, especially regarding the involvement of third-party verification companies, and marks a significant step in Australia's effort to regulate adult content in interactive media.

Marathon Launch Day and Early Player Experience

Bungie’s extraction shooter Marathon officially launched this week, following a recent server test weekend. Initial player feedback notes that the game maintains a shoot-on-sight environment with heavy PvP focus. Popular character classes for solo play include Assassin, valued for its temporary invisibility, and Vandal, praised for mobility. A new character class, Thief, emphasizes stealth and utility with tools like a pickpocket drone and grapple hook. Some Deluxe Edition bonuses and pre-order rewards are not yet accessible, prompting the developer to advise against purchasing the Reward Track until issues are resolved. Additionally, some players discovered they unintentionally carried over rare loot from the server slam event into the full game via their in-game inboxes, a quirk stemming from how borrowed items are handled. Marathon appears to be off to a stable start with ongoing fixes expected.

Diablo 4 Experimental Mini-Season Features Player Transformation

Ahead of its upcoming expansion "Lord of Hatred" scheduled for April 28, Diablo 4 will host a new experimental mini-season titled the Season of Slaughter beginning March 11. This event introduces a novel mechanic allowing players to transform into the franchise’s notorious demon, the Butcher, by maintaining kill streaks and collecting specific currency dropped by monsters. The Butcher form grants new abilities, though details on these skills remain limited. The event focuses on combat against enhanced enemies in designated regions and includes PvP opportunities involving transformed players. The season also features unique buffs triggered by special items that increase challenge and rewards. Blizzard’s approach tests fresh ideas within the seasonal format shortly before the expansion’s release.

Upcoming Pragmata Release Date Moved Forward

Capcom announced that its sci-fi action game Pragmata will release on April 17, 2026, a week earlier than its previously planned April 24 date. No specific reasons were given for the change, but the move positions Pragmata prominently in a month with relatively few major releases beyond the Diablo 4 expansion. The game has been in development since 2022 and its trailer showcases a clean, corridor-based aesthetic
