Dawn of War 4 Playtesters Request Longer Combat to Enjoy Sync Animations

Developer King Art Games revealed that early playtesters of *Dawn of War 4* have expressed a desire for longer combat sequences. This feedback came as a surprise but reflects players’ appreciation for the game’s advanced sync-kill animations, which coordinate melee actions in a visually striking way. Senior game designer Elliott Verbiest noted that these animations elevate the spectacle of combat, making it worth extending battles to fully experience these moments.

The game adapts tabletop elements to the video game format with adjustments to multiplayer balance. For example, the Necron faction’s resurrection mechanic has been redesigned: instead of instant revival, players can build a structure that brings specific units back over time, allowing opponents a chance to counter it. *Dawn of War 4* is scheduled for release in 2026 and aims to innovate on both lore and gameplay, including a campaign focused on the Dark Angels with their primarch Lion El’Jonson.

Overwatch Co-Creator Jeff Kaplan Details Difficult Exit from Activision-Blizzard

Jeff Kaplan, known for his role as Overwatch’s public face until his departure in 2021, shared insights into his exit from Activision-Blizzard during a recent interview. Kaplan pointed to the overambitious expectations set on the Overwatch League esports venture as a primary source of tension. The league, launched in 2017, was hyped as potentially surpassing major sports leagues, leading to promises to investors that strained development resources.

Kaplan explained that commitments to the league interfered with core game development, with resources shifted away from new content toward features serving the league and its sponsors. The pressure escalated to the point where corporate demands tied Overwatch’s financial targets to the threat of large-scale layoffs. Kaplan described this as a “biggest f**k you moment” of his career, signifying a turning point that led him to leave the company. Since then, Overwatch 2 has undergone changes, including recently dropping the “2” and returning to the original branding.

New Blood Bowl Update Launches as Separate Free Upgrade for Current Owners

Cyanide Studio announced an upcoming update for *Blood Bowl 3* that will launch as a standalone game titled *Warhammer Blood Bowl*. This new edition will integrate the latest tabletop rules and add two teams, the Tomb Kings and Bretonnians, increasing the roster to 26 teams. Existing owners of *Blood Bowl 3* will receive the update free of charge, including all previously purchased content.

A new "Rumble" mode will also debut, featuring 7-player teams on a narrower pitch for faster matches. A playable demo runs from March 18 to 25 on Steam. The update promises refreshed rosters, new competitions, tutorials, and a competitive ecosystem culminating in an annual World Championship. The release is planned for spring 2026, reflecting ongoing support for this strategic fantasy sports adaptation.

Diablo 4 Expansion Introduces Advanced Crafting and Revamps Loot System

The *Lord of Hatred* expansion for *Diablo 4*, arriving next month, will introduce a significant overhaul to item crafting inspired by *Diablo 2*’s Horadric Cube. Players will be able to combine items and recipes to upgrade or transform gear in ways not possible before, including the ability to convert common or lower-tier loot into powerful legendary items.

In support of this, Blizzard is reintroducing lower-tier item drops enhanced with "greater affixes," ensuring even basic loot can be valuable for crafting. Additionally, a crafting mechanic called "transfiguration," similar to sanctification seen in season 11, will allow players to gamble for bonus effects on items. This system aims to diversify endgame progression by encouraging experimentation and reducing the repetitiveness of gear grinding.

Valve Responds to New York Loot Box Lawsuit, Rebuts Violence Claims

Valve issued a public statement responding to a New York Attorney General lawsuit accusing the company of facilitating illegal gambling through loot boxes on Steam. Valve disputes that its mystery boxes legally constitute gambling and highlights efforts to combat fraud, theft, and unauthorized gambling sites using its platform. The company emphasized that all items from its loot boxes are cosmetic, with no gameplay advantage, and that no player is disadvantaged for not spending money.
