**Fallout 3 Remastered Toy Listing Suggests Possible Release Window**

A toy listing on a third-party retailer, Bro Depot, has appeared featuring a "Fallout 3 Remastered" branded figure, specifically a 7-inch Nuka-Cola T-45B power armour from McFarlane Toys. The pre-order deadline is listed as March 15, with a release date marked for August 31, 2026. Although this is not an official announcement from Bethesda and the dates could be placeholders, the listing adds to longstanding speculation that Bethesda plans to remaster Fallout 3. This potential release would come nearly a year after Oblivion Remastered, indicating a continued focus on revisiting classic Bethesda titles. The accuracy of such retailer leaks is often reliable, but official confirmation remains pending.

**Phison CEO Highlights Continuing NAND Memory Shortage and Price Surges**

Phison Electronics has reported that NAND flash memory prices surged by 50% overnight due to strong demand, particularly from AI sector clients, and persistent supply constraints. Despite signing long-term agreements with multiple NAND and DRAM suppliers and increasing inventory from NT$35.6 billion to NT$50 billion in recent months, Phison's CEO expressed concern that current financial and inventory resources remain insufficient. The company has approved a syndicated loan of $400-500 million to support inventory growth and R&D activities. The memory shortage is affecting costs, with reports of DRAM prices doubling and memory comprising 35% of PC costs in some cases. Phison is also promoting its aiDAPTIV+ technology to improve AI processing efficiency amid limited hardware resources.

**Corsair Sabre V2 Pro Wireless Carbon Fiber Mouse Reviewed**

Corsair's Sabre V2 Pro Wireless Carbon Fiber edition is a sturdier but heavier variant of their popular ultralight gaming mouse, priced at $200—double the cost of the original model. The mouse weighs 55 grams, 19 grams more than the standard version, due to a carbon fiber shell that provides extra durability. While the sensor and wireless performance match the excellent original model, the review notes the added weight and high price may not justify the upgrade for most users. The mouse offers longer battery life and Bluetooth connectivity but retains mechanical switches instead of more durable optical options. The software setup is web-based only, which may limit offline customization. Overall, it is a competent but expensive choice favoring durability over weight savings.

**Amazon Listing Misrepresents Laptop Storage Capacity**

A listing by a third-party seller on Amazon advertises an HP laptop with "1.1 TB" of storage, but 1 TB of that storage is cloud-based via a one-year Microsoft OneDrive subscription, leaving only 128 GB of local storage on the device. After the free subscription expires, users would need to pay to retain cloud storage access or manage with limited local space. The laptop features an Intel N100 CPU, 32 GB of RAM, and comes bundled with budget wireless earphones. This marketing tactic may mislead consumers who expect the full storage to be physically present on the laptop. The issue is exacerbated by confusing pricing deals where larger memory models are occasionally cheaper. Consumers are advised to carefully check specifications and consider better alternatives within the price range.

**World of Warcraft: Midnight's 'Prey' Mechanic Offers Novelty but Lacks Challenge**

The new "Prey" system in World of Warcraft: Midnight, wherein players can be ambushed by specific target enemies in open world zones, has been integrated with three difficulty settings: normal, hard, and nightmare. Current impressions indicate that the normal and hard modes offer limited challenge, with prey creatures demonstrating only basic attack patterns and low mechanical complexity. This limits the intended feeling of being hunted by a unique and threatening nemesis. Nightmare difficulty, releasing on March 17, promises additional mechanics such as ambushes occurring even when inactive, potentially increasing tension. The system is recognized as promising but in need of deeper strategy and differentiation at lower difficulties to fully engage players.
