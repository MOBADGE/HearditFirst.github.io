Mirror’s Edge Review Reflects on 2009 Release

A 2009 review of Mirror’s Edge highlights its innovative first-person free-running mechanics set in a strikingly clean, dystopian city. The game puts players in the role of Faith, a Runner delivering messages across rooftops while evading a repressive regime. The review praises the environmental design and the immersive physicality of movement but notes that frequent enemy encounters interrupt the core enjoyment of parkour. Combat, though deliberately simplified to discourage traditional shooting, often hampers the flow of the game. Despite some frustrations with pacing and difficulty, the game’s short six-hour campaign, augmented by speed and time trial modes, is acknowledged for its unique approach to first-person platforming.

Create Aeronautics Mod Expands Minecraft’s Vehicle Building

A new mod for Minecraft called Create Aeronautics builds on the existing Create mod by adding tools and components for fully functional vehicles. Players can now construct a variety of machines including planes, helicopters, powered boats, combine harvesters, and airships. The mod introduces new mechanics like propeller bearings, burners for hot air balloons, and suspension systems for offroad vehicles with more realistic wheels. The inclusion of physics-based interactions through compatibility with the Sable physics mod enhances vehicle realism. This mod expands Minecraft’s creative potential by enabling complex mechanical builds beyond standard crafting limitations.

Marathon’s Popular WSTR Shotgun Receives Further Nerf

Bungie announced a second nerf to the WSTR shotgun in their shooter Marathon, aiming to reduce its dominance in close combat. The weapon, already limited in range by a previous update, will no longer down enemy players equipped with stronger “blue” shields in just two shots, requiring an additional reload in such scenarios. This adjustment is intended to promote diversity in weapon choice and improve counterplay during engagements. However, the shotgun will remain effective against opponents with weaker “green” shields common in early game areas. Bungie continues to tweak Marathon’s meta with experiments like free loadout modes and new mercy kits that have surprised players with their tactical applications.

Turtle WoW Classic Server to Shut Down After Legal Injunction

Following a court injunction in favor of Blizzard, the private World of Warcraft server Turtle WoW announced it will shut down by mid-May. The server offered an enhanced vanilla WoW experience with additional raids, zones, and races, operating in the vein of “Classic Plus.” Despite an 8-year run and a dedicated fanbase, Blizzard’s copyright infringement lawsuit led to a cease and desist order and a settlement forcing the project’s closure. The shutdown reflects ongoing challenges faced by fan-run servers, contrasting with a few officially supported projects like EverQuest’s Project 1999. Turtle WoW’s closure marks another instance of fan content being curtailed by rights enforcement.

Portal 2 Community Edition Enters Open Beta with Modding Enhancements

Portal 2 Community Edition launched into open beta, offering a community-maintained version of the game designed to expand its modding capabilities. Built on a fork of the CS:GO engine called Strata Source, it adds support for advanced lighting, volumetrics, higher-resolution textures, and improved scripting tools. Additionally, it features custom campaign support with integrated UI and a revamped Hammer map editor. These improvements aim to facilitate more ambitious custom maps and mods than the original game allowed. While not altering Portal 2’s core campaign, this edition empowers creators to push the game’s puzzle design and aesthetics further.

Monitor Manufacturer AOC Highlights Monitors as Cost-Effective Upgrade

AOC presented new gaming monitors and suggested that upgrading a monitor is currently a more sensible investment than purchasing new graphics cards or memory, which remain expensive due to ongoing supply issues. The company showcased models offering higher refresh rates, better HDR support, and OLED panels at competitive price points similar to monitors released five years ago. AOC’s argument centers on improving visual experience and performance longevity without the high cost inflation seen in GPU and memory markets. While some may view the claim skeptically given the source, the broader idea reflects the relative affordability and rapid technological advancement in displays compared to other PC components.
