**Micron Highlights Upcoming RAM Demand from Driverless Cars and Humanoid Robots**

Micron’s CEO Sanjay Mehrotra shared during a recent earnings call that the next wave of technology, beyond PCs and large language models, will be autonomous vehicles and humanoid robots requiring massive memory capacities. Current cars with basic advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) use about 16 GB of RAM, but fully autonomous vehicles at level 4 autonomy will need over 300 GB of DRAM. Similarly, humanoid robots equipped with AI will require comparable memory resources to these autonomous cars.

Micron is preparing for this shift by developing automotive-grade LPDDR5 DRAM to meet the memory demands of these new markets. However, supply constraints are expected in 2026 despite a planned 20% growth in overall RAM supply. This indicates that component markets may remain tight for the foreseeable future as demand expands into AI-powered devices outside traditional data centers.

**Clair Obscur’s Lead Writer Rejects AI for Creative Storytelling**

Jennifer Svedberg-Yen, narrative lead for the RPG *Clair Obscur*, spoke at GDC 2026 about her views on generative AI in creative writing. While acknowledging AI’s technical sophistication, Svedberg-Yen said that AI does not contribute meaningfully to her writing process, which she described as a painful but vital personal expression.

She emphasized that writing involves nuance, emotional understanding, and conveying individual viewpoints—elements she feels AI cannot replicate. Though *Clair Obscur* faced criticism for AI-assisted production assets, Svedberg-Yen affirmed she personally avoids using AI for narrative work. Her perspective highlights the ongoing tension between creative professionals and AI tools in game development.

**Asus ROG Strix XG27JCG Review: A 5K 27-Inch Gaming Monitor**

The Asus ROG Strix XG27JCG is a 27-inch IPS monitor offering a native 5K resolution (5120x2880 pixels) with a 180 Hz refresh rate. It also supports a secondary 1440p mode with up to 330 Hz, achieved by pixel-doubling the 5K panel. This configuration results in exceptionally sharp visuals and a high pixel density of 215 ppi, surpassing common 4K displays.

The monitor features HDR 600 certification but uses edge-lit local dimming with only 10 zones, resulting in limited HDR performance. Response times are fast for an IPS panel at 0.3 ms. Connectivity includes HDMI 2.1, DisplayPort 1.4, and USB-C with 15W power delivery.

While the 5K resolution offers impressive clarity, gaming at native 5K demands powerful GPUs but is made more accessible by modern upscaling technologies like Nvidia DLSS. However, the alternate 1440p mode shows softness due to interpolation, making it less ideal for competitive gaming. At around $849, the monitor competes with 4K OLED options that may offer better contrast and value, positioning this Asus model as a niche choice for users prioritizing pixel density.

**Arc Raiders Devs Refocus Game as an Adventure Experience Over Pure Shooter**

Embark Studios’ production director Caio Braga explained that *Arc Raiders* underwent a significant change in design approach, moving away from being strictly a shooter towards embracing more adventure and exploration elements. The shift occurred after several conflicting internal visions, which previously made the game difficult to define.

The team now emphasizes player choice and freedom, allowing playstyles that include PvP, PvE, or role-playing. This sandbox-style design encourages non-combat activities such as questing and exploration, differentiating *Arc Raiders* within the extraction shooter genre. This flexibility has contributed to the game’s appeal as an accessible alternative in a crowded market.
