## Five New Steam Games to Note This Week

Several new titles have quietly launched on Steam, offering a diverse mix of genres and styles. Among them, *Homura Hime* delivers classic third-person action reminiscent of older slash-and-dodge games, with players tasked to defeat powerful demon girls. *Planet of Lana 2* follows up its hand-painted cinematic platformer predecessor with new environments such as frozen peaks and deep oceans, maintaining its focus on thoughtful puzzles and stealth.

*iRacing Arcade* provides a more casual, approachable alternative to its simulation counterpart, blending lightweight racing with some management elements. For fans of cozy simulators, *Shinehill* puts you in the role of an alien spy farming land and integrating with a local community, including fishing and dungeon crawling. Lastly, *Angel Engine* offers an atmospheric horror surgery simulator based on a vertical video series, presented in a point-and-click adventure style.

These releases may not be headliners but add fresh variety to Steam’s catalog and highlight ongoing trends like nostalgia-driven gameplay and community-focused experiences.

## Ultima 8 Retrospective Adds Context to CRPG History

YouTuber Majuular has released a comprehensive three-hour retrospective on *Ultima 8*, part of an ongoing docuseries exploring the Ultima franchise’s influence on the CRPG genre. The series examines each game's development, narrative, and genre impact, serving as an informative resource for both fans and newcomers.

*Ultima*’s legacy is significant, having shaped online worlds, influenced major developers, and introduced innovations in roleplaying. While *Ultima 8* is not considered the best in the series, understanding its design and place in the timeline helps contextualize the evolution of RPGs. The docuseries underscores the importance of these early games for today’s CRPG resurgence.

## Hytale Developer Emphasizes Modding and Community Hiring

Hypixel Studios has outlined a strategic focus on modding for *Hytale*, highlighting how integral the modding community is to the game's future. The developer is running a large modding contest with over 60 winners receiving a share of a six-figure prize pool.

Beyond encouraging creativity, Hypixel founder Simon Collins-Laflamme revealed the studio hires contributors directly from the modding community. The team values agility and hands-on development and believes close ties with modders pay off long-term. This approach suggests *Hytale* will continue evolving heavily through user-generated content.

## Browser-Based Exploration of Crazy Taxi’s Dreamcast Cityscapes

A developer known as Will has recreated the city environments from the Dreamcast-era *Crazy Taxi* for browser exploration via the noclip project, which hosts digital game level museums. Unlike the original arcade experience, this version allows players to freely navigate the detailed cityscapes without gameplay pressures.

This effort required reverse engineering undocumented file formats and rendering methods, representing both a technical challenge and an appreciation of the original game’s urban design. The recreation offers fans a new way to experience a classic title’s setting ahead of the upcoming *Crazy Taxi* sequel.

## Slay the Spire 2’s Unexpected Steam Success and a Lighthearted Joke

*Slay the Spire 2* recently outsold and outplayed several competitors on Steam, including the indie game *Marathon*. The developers made a joking post referencing *Marathon* as a “small indie passion project,” which sparked discussion after *Slay the Spire 2*’s rapid rise complicated the context.

Mega Crit clarified the comment was meant sarcastically and not to belittle other developers. The unexpected size of *Slay the Spire 2*’s launch caught the team off guard. Currently, it ranks among the most-played games on Steam, indicating strong player interest and a significant moment for the roguelike deckbuilder.
