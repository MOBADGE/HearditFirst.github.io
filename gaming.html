**Dreamhaven Prepares Run-Based Spin-Off Shadowstone**

Dreamhaven, founded by Blizzard co-founder Mike Morhaime, revealed *Shadowstone*, a tactics roguelike set in the world of the 2025 tactics RPG *Sunderfolk*. Designed as a standalone experience with no prerequisite knowledge needed, *Shadowstone* focuses on escaping randomized shadowstone-infested ruins via run-based gameplay that emphasizes adaptive strategy. Unlike *Sunderfolk*, which supports phone controls, *Shadowstone* will launch initially without mobile support, focusing on mouse and keyboard. Meanwhile, *Sunderfolk* is getting a 2.0 update adding mouse and keyboard controls as well as online multiplayer support. *Shadowstone* is expected in early access later in 2026.

**Skyrim Lead Designer on $100 Game Prices**

Bruce Nesmith, lead designer of *Skyrim*, discussed the ongoing debate about rising video game prices, particularly the possibility of games costing $100 or more. He noted that game prices held steady at around $60 for 15 years without inflation adjustments, making price increases understandable. However, he questioned whether players always consider value beyond the immediate price impact. Nesmith also highlighted the potential for subscription models similar to those in software like Microsoft Word. Ultimately, he suggested developers will price games according to market acceptance, and that gamers tend to pay for games they strongly desire, despite economic pressures.
