Five New Steam Games Released This Week

Several new titles launched on Steam this week, adding to the already large daily influx of games. Notable releases include:

- **Stiff Neck**: A visual novel blending psychological horror and drama, set in a surreal world with a mysterious cursed doll plague. Players follow Noele, a small-town cop with a neck ailment and complicated relationships. This game caters to players interested in story-heavy, text-driven experiences.

- **Which Sausage, Mate?**: A life simulation about a Polish miner turned convenience store cashier facing financial and moral challenges. Players must balance expenses like food, rent, and medicine while interacting with various characters. Different endings provide replayability.

- **Wasteland Bites**: Combining cooking and combat, players run a food truck in a post-apocalyptic world. Alongside battling mutants, players prepare unusual dishes while traveling toward a safe, non-irradiated beach.

- **It Has My Face**: Formerly known as DoubleWe, this roguelike pits players in a procedurally generated, hostile environment where they hunt their doppelganger, either solo or in an eight-player online mode.

- **Grime 2**: A sequel to the 2021 metroidvania-style game focused on combat and exploration. Players can use molds from defeated enemies for various attacks, encouraging a tactical playstyle involving parries and boss fights.

These releases offer a variety of genres for different tastes and demonstrate the continued diversity of indie games available on Steam.

Pillars of Eternity Adds Turn-Based Combat Mode

Eleven years after its initial release, Obsidian has added a full turn-based combat mode to the original *Pillars of Eternity*. Previously available as a beta, this update introduces a system where players have one action per turn but can gain extra turns based on a recovery mechanic tied to equipment. This contrasts with *Pillars of Eternity 2: Deadfire*, whose turn-based mode had a fixed initiative order limiting such flexibility.

The new mode changes combat dynamics, making movement generous but decisions impactful. Builds that relied on lighter armor and quicker actions gain new advantages, and crowd control remains vital for success. Tests suggest the mode offers a gentler onboarding for newcomers and a fresh tactical approach for veterans.

Given the popularity of both real-time-with-pause and turn-based systems, this update gives players the option to choose their preferred combat style for a classic RPG experience.

Reflection on the First Video Game TV Adaptation: Pac-Man

A retrospective look at the very first TV show based on a video game, Hanna-Barbera’s *Pac-Man* (1982-1983), highlights how far game adaptations have come. The cartoon featured Pac-Man, Ms. Pac-Man (renamed Pepper), and their child in a world with origins explained by whimsical and sparse worldbuilding, such as Power Pellet Trees in Power Pellet Forest.

The show’s plots often revolved around ghosts trying to catch Pac-Man, with the villain Mezmaron sending them on various doomed missions. Despite the attempt, the series is generally considered low-effort and lacking in storytelling or charm, even for a children’s show.

This contrast reinforces how contemporary adaptations, despite occasional criticism, invest much more in worldbuilding and production quality, setting modern standards for translating games to television.

OpenAI’s Reported Proposal to Pit World Leaders Against Each Other

A feature in The New Yorker has brought to light a controversial proposal reportedly discussed within OpenAI, where the company might have tried to leverage its AI technology to incite competition among world powers like China and Russia. The idea, attributed to OpenAI president Greg Brockman, involved encouraging an AI arms race to benefit the company financially by playing nations against each other.

OpenAI denies that this plan was seriously considered, stating talks were more about encouraging cooperation. However, former employees say the plan was real and caused internal dissent. The proposal highlights ethical concerns around AI development amid powerful global interests.

The article provides broader context on OpenAI CEO Sam Altman’s reputation and the company’s evolution, raising questions about AI governance and corporate accountability.
