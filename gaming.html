Reflections on the PlayStation 5 and Hesitations Toward the PS6

Jess Kinghorn, a hardware writer and former PlayStation Magazine UK contributor, shared her thoughts on owning a PS5 since its 2020 launch and why she does not plan to purchase a PS6. Despite PlayStation exclusives no longer coming to PC, Kinghorn questions the value of investing in a new console given the rising costs. The base PS5 model's price has increased multiple times, now retailing around $650/£570, which is unusual for aging hardware. Contributing factors include a global memory shortage tied to AI demand impacting console production costs.

Kinghorn also notes a personal shift toward PC gaming, where there is a broad library of games including many affordable indie titles. While the PS5 remains useful for exclusive titles such as Resident Evil Requiem, the growing price and overall cost of gaming on consoles weigh heavily on her decision. She contrasts Sony’s approach with Nintendo, whose more affordable Switch 2 and its compelling exclusive games still draw her attention. This perspective highlights ongoing tensions in the console market between hardware cost, exclusive content, and player preferences.

MindsEye: A Flawed Yet Engaging Experience

The game MindsEye, developed by Build a Rocket Boy, has been described as a deeply flawed, glitchy experience that paradoxically remains compelling. Despite widespread criticism about its technical issues and incoherent narrative, one analyst found themselves enjoying the chaotic pace and unexpected events throughout a roughly 20-hour playthrough.

MindsEye places players in a large, dystopian tech company environment with minimal consequences for violent or disruptive actions, creating an experience comparable to a lawless open-world shooter devoid of in-game law enforcement. The story attempts to tackle themes like government surveillance and corporate overreach but ends with an underwhelming climax that leaves many questions unanswered. Though the $60 price on Steam is considered steep given the game's issues, the unpredictable and energetic nature of MindsEye keeps some players engaged. The developer claims external sabotage hampered the game's launch, and updates are ongoing.

Final Fantasy 14's Variant Dungeon Praised, Rewards Critiqued

Final Fantasy 14’s newest variant dungeon, The Merchant’s Tale, has received praise for its aesthetics, boss design, and mid-core Advanced difficulty level. The visually striking dungeon features inventive fights and diverse challenges, making it a highlight among recent MMO content updates. The Advanced mode offers a balanced challenge that appeals to players seeking more than casual encounters but less intense than the hardest raids.

However, the game’s reward system has drawn criticism. The dungeon provides cosmetic items and consumables rather than meaningful gear upgrades, limiting its appeal for players focused on character progression. This issue reflects a broader challenge in FFXIV’s design where significant gear rewards are locked to time-gated raid content, leaving a large portion of players without appealing incentive to engage repeatedly with these dungeons. Square Enix is reportedly reviewing the reward structure for future expansions.

Classic 3DO FPS Remastered but Largely Unplayable

Nightdive Studios recently remastered PO’ed, a first-person shooter originally released for the 3DO console in 1995. PO’ed attempted to break the PC’s FPS dominance with unique features, including a chef protagonist, a jetpack with six degrees of freedom, and a quirky arsenal. Despite these ambitions, modern reviewers find the game’s level design, controls, and aesthetics to be highly problematic.

The remaster preserves all the qualities that made PO’ed difficult to enjoy, including minimal floor textures, erratic enemy variety (such as projectile-firing disembodied rear ends), and a problematic control scheme. The game struggles with navigation and combat responsiveness. Nightdive has chosen to maintain the original presentation in the name of preservation rather than overhaul its gameplay. PO’ed serves as a historical example of early console FPS efforts that ultimately failed to match PC standards before Halo's rise.

Indie Pass Subscription Service Faces Skepticism

A new indie-focused subscription service, Indie Pass, will launch on April 13, offering around 70 curated indie games for $6.99 per month. The service, operated by publisher indie.io, pays developers based on how long players engage with their games. It aims to improve discoverability for indies in a crowded market.

However, the reception from both developers and players has been largely critical. Concerns include the compensation model incentivizing "endless loop" gameplay
