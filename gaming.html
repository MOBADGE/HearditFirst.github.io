Professor Loses Two Years of Academic Work Due to ChatGPT Setting Change

Marcel Bucher, a professor at the University of Cologne, has lost two years of academic work stored within ChatGPT after he disabled a data consent setting in the AI tool. Bucher used ChatGPT Plus to assist with writing, grant applications, and data analysis, relying on its continuous chat memory. However, when he turned off data sharing in August 2025, all of his chats and project folders were irreversibly deleted without warning or recovery options. OpenAI support confirmed that the data could not be restored. Bucher‚Äôs experience illustrates a significant concern about the reliability and professional readiness of generative AI tools for critical academic tasks, highlighting that these platforms prioritize privacy but may lack safeguards to protect valuable user content.

NAND Memory Chip Prices Expected to Double

Reports indicate that major NAND flash memory manufacturers Samsung, SK Hynix, and Sandisk plan to roughly double their chip prices this year amid supply constraints and increased demand from AI applications. Digitimes cites planned price increases in the first quarter of 2026, with Samsung also reporting strong recent financial results. NAND chips are key components in storage devices like SSDs, which have already seen price surges. The extent to which these chip price hikes will further affect consumer SSD prices remains uncertain. However, given the already elevated costs, further increases could continue to impact PC component affordability and gaming hardware budgets.

Darkest Dungeon Board Game Kickstarter Fails to Deliver

More than five years after its highly successful Kickstarter campaign raising $5.7 million, the *Darkest Dungeon* board game project has collapsed. Publisher Mythic Games, financially strained and officially liquidated in December 2025, is no longer able to manufacture or deliver the game. Red Hook Studios, the original video game developer, stated that they lack the resources to fulfill orders themselves and have instead provided backers with all game files, including rules and 3D models. The failure follows prior controversies, including Mythic requesting additional funds in 2022 to cover rising production costs. For backers, the release of digital assets offers some closure but cannot replace receiving the physical game.
