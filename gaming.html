Resident Evil Requiem Aims to Recapture Classic Horror Atmosphere

Angela Sant’Albano, who plays Grace Ashcroft in the new game *Resident Evil Requiem*, shared insights into the development approach Capcom took to revive the series’ horror roots. Unlike veteran voice actor Nick Apostolides returning as Leon Kennedy, Sant’Albano is new to horror gaming. To help immerse actors into the tense, dark scenarios typical of the early *Resident Evil* games, filming was sometimes done in total darkness with only flashlights or headlamps to simulate the eerie atmosphere. Sant’Albano also played earlier titles like *Resident Evil 4* to better capture the slower, creeping tone the developers aimed for. This reflects Capcom’s deliberate decision to return to survival horror elements fans appreciated in earlier games, distinguishing *Requiem* from more action-oriented recent entries.

Why Act 1 of Diablo 2 Still Resonates

In a reflective piece, writer Jody praises Act 1 of *Diablo 2* for its distinct atmosphere and level design that provides a direct sequel feel to the original *Diablo*. The act’s open yet linear environment contrasts with the more confusing and segmented later acts. The varied spawns and enemy behaviors encourage tactical combat, while the return to a ruined Tristram adds emotional weight, highlighting the game’s heavy metal horror theme. With its atmospheric locales and fresh enemy mechanics, Act 1 stands out as a core part of what made *Diablo 2* memorable. This analysis underscores the longevity and impact of *Diablo 2*’s earliest stages on RPG design and fan appreciation.

Five Noteworthy New Steam Releases

Several new games arrived on Steam recently that might have flown under the radar:

- *Water Womb World*: A free, cryptic adventure set in a murky underwater world, involving exploration and object study for a mysterious purpose. Its unusual, atmospheric style is both reflective and eerie. - *New Heights*: A climbing simulator with a focus on manual limb control and realistic physics, offering 280 routes and workshop support. It presents a more simulation-driven experience than similar titles. - *Fear Effect 2: Retro Helix*: This long-awaited PC release updates the 2001 PS1 cult classic with quality-of-life improvements such as manual saves and widescreen support, though its style may feel dated to newcomers. - *Tales of Berseria Remastered*: A re-release including all DLCs and minor improvements but no major enhancements. It’s priced lower than the original, making it an accessible option for newcomers. - *Emberbane*: A metroidvania emphasizing precise platforming and challenging one-hit kill spikes, following an orphan protagonist. Its fluid moveset marks it as a notable entry for fans of the genre.

These releases reflect a range of styles and player interests, offering fresh options across adventure, simulation, and RPG genres.

Rollin' Rascal: A New Sonic-Inspired Speedrunning Platformer Adds Ranked Multiplayer

*Rollin' Rascal* is an upcoming 3D platformer inspired by early Sonic games, emphasizing high-speed momentum and expansive levels. Developed by Gabriel Gonzales, who worked on the Sonic fangame *Sonic GT*, it offers mechanics like air dashing and grinding, capturing the feel of classic Sonic Adventure titles but without licensed characters. Recently added ranked multiplayer races introduce competitive online play with item boxes similar to Mario Kart. While still in a demo state with minor bugs and some absent content, the game has drawn attention for its tight movement and level design, aiming to appeal to fans of speedrunning and fast-paced platforming.

Rare Tsukihime Visual Novel Prototype Destroyed in Transit

A highly rare pre-release floppy disk copy of the visual novel *Tsukihime*—one of only 50 ever made—was reportedly destroyed during shipment, likely by US Customs or during a customs search. The buyer shared images showing the disk physically pried apart despite careful packaging. This loss is significant for game preservation, as prototypes like this represent unique pieces of gaming history that are already scarce. The incident highlights ongoing risks to physical game media that can be damaged or destroyed due to inspections, raising awareness about the fragility of preserving vintage and rare gaming artifacts.
