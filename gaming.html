**Marathon Updates: New Map, Gameplay Insights, and Community ARGs**

Since its recent launch, Bungie’s extraction shooter *Marathon* is gaining attention for its intricate gameplay and active community. The newly unlocked third map, Outpost, offers a compact but highly strategic play area enclosed by walls and patrolled by AI. Its unique environmental hazard, a phosphorus-like barrage damaging players exposed to the sky, adds a fresh dynamic. The map’s vertical design encourages use of character abilities such as the double jump, enhancing tactical options for players.

Players also note thoughtful design elements like door mechanics that create sound cues when opened, offering tactical advantage. This subtle interaction encourages players to close doors behind them to detect approaching enemies. In combat, the double-barrel shotgun stands out for its high damage potential at close range, making it a key weapon despite its rarity and costly ammunition.

On the narrative front, *Marathon* has engaged players with an alternate reality game (ARG) embedded in the game world. Recently activated terminals in the Perimeter map reveal cryptic clues and audio logs featuring Durandal, the AI antagonist from the original *Marathon* series. These secrets hint at the upcoming fourth map, Cryo Archive, and deepen player engagement by introducing investigative objectives alongside loot runs.

**Artist Credit Controversy Resolved for Marathon**

Earlier reports revealed that Bungie used unauthorized artwork from the artist Antireal in *Marathon*, continuing a pattern from past titles like *Destiny 2*. Bungie attributed the issue to a now-ex-employee and committed to a thorough asset review. Recently, Antireal confirmed that the matter has been settled to her satisfaction. Notably, she is now credited as a freelance visual design consultant in *Marathon*’s official credits.

While the resolution appears mostly positive for the artist, who gains formal recognition, lingering concerns remain about further unauthorized uses of her art, including its appearance on licensed Xbox controllers. This incident underscores ongoing issues around intellectual property and artist rights within the industry.

**Stardew Valley’s New NPC ‘Sandy’: Romance, Schedule, and Gifts**

Ahead of the 1.7 update that will add Sandy as a new romance candidate in *Stardew Valley*, players are getting a preview of her character and to-do’s for building friendship. Sandy runs the Oasis shop in the Calico Desert and has a consistent daily schedule at the shop counter, with occasional appearances during events like the Desert Festival.

Players aiming to befriend or eventually marry Sandy should note her birthday on Fall 15 and her preferences: she loves flowers such as Crocus, Daffodil, and Sweet Pea, and dislikes gifts like Holly and Red Mushrooms. Befriending her grants access to her Tom Kha Soup recipe, useful for cooking and crafting. Although not yet available for marriage, Sandy’s introduction expands NPC interactions and variety in upcoming updates.

**Valve’s Steam Machines Face Potential Delays Amid Component Shortages**

Valve’s anticipated 2026 hardware releases—including the Steam Machine console, Steam Controller, and Steam Frame VR headset—may face delays into 2027 due to ongoing industry shortages and inflated prices of memory and storage components. Valve noted these challenges publicly, explaining the need to revisit pricing and launch schedules accordingly.

The delay risks affecting consumer expectations as Valve’s new Steam Machine aims to be a streamlined living room PC gaming device without the subsidized pricing models used by console manufacturers. Market factors outside Valve’s control, driven largely by AI-related demand for PC components, could push the project beyond initial timelines.

**Diablo 2’s ‘Souls Dance’ Easter Egg Gains New Relevance With Reign of the Warlock Expansion**

A decades-old Diablo 2 easter egg known as the “souls dance” has resurfaced as a powerful tool for farming rare loot in the Reign of the Warlock expansion. By leaving four wisp enemies alive to perform a lightning-letter lightshow, players activate a long-lasting hidden magic find buff. This buff significantly increases the drop rates of valuable items, especially the new “herald” enemies introduced for terrorized zones.

This mechanic offers more control and efficiency for players in endgame farming, reducing reliance on equipment-based magic find stacks. While powerful, the original game’s diminishing returns on magic find help maintain balance. The discovery adds depth to Diablo 2’s ongoing player-driven metagame.
