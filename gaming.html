Military Survival Sandbox Altera Features Unexpected Penguin Soldiers

Altera, a new military survival sandbox game, combines realistic, unforgiving gameplay with an unusual twist: penguin soldiers equipped with reflex sights fixed to their heads. Developed by Vulkanic, the game's setting resembles a central European landscape where players engage in base-building, crafting, and combat against NPC enemies. Though it appears grounded, the presence of armed penguins introduces an odd sci-fi element stemming from the developer’s original vision of humans, animals, and robots fighting alongside each other. Altera is currently preparing for a playtest with over 2,000 sign-ups and plans to launch in early access via Steam, though no firm release date is announced. The inclusion of penguin soldiers adds a unique and curious character to what is otherwise a conventional military survival experience.

Vaunted Blends Tactical RPG with Variable Narrative Perspectives

Vaunted is a newly revealed tactical RPG that merges turn-based strategy with real-time third-person action. Players command a team of thieves and mercenaries who must navigate missions for a criminal syndicate after a failed expedition. The game’s narrative is notable for featuring multiple protagonists—Genril, Dyse, and Kyvaath—each with their own perspective on events. If a character dies, the story continues from another's viewpoint, effectively allowing players to rewrite history through these different lenses. Vaunted also incorporates roguelite elements wherein character progression influences future runs. Developed by Lost Lake Games and led by veteran designer James Phinney, Vaunted is expected to release later this year. Its hybrid gameplay and dynamic narrative approach offer an innovative take on familiar tactical RPG mechanics.

Artificial Detective Combines Action Adventure with a Futuristic Detective Theme

Artificial Detective, from studio Vivix, presents an action adventure set in a future metropolis devoid of humans and ruled by robots and nanotech-infected animals. Players assume the role of a robotic detective investigating humanity’s disappearance. Although primarily following traditional exploration, combat, and puzzle-solving gameplay, the game’s reveal trailer highlights moments of inventive action, such as attacking robots with a large pneumatic drill. The design includes open or nonlinear exploration across ten city districts, with companionship from a robotic dog and a child aiding progress. While the detective focus is less clear from early footage, Artificial Detective aims for a richly atmospheric experience and is slated for release next year.

Indie Stealth Game Lizard State Echoes Splinter Cell Atmosphere

Lizard State is an indie stealth game developed by solo dev Benjamin Rose that evokes the feel of classic Splinter Cell titles. The story centers on a covert operative tasked with rescuing a hostage on an island during a tense single night. Its gameplay emphasizes tactical stealth including shooting out lights, choking guards, and using gadgets like "mimic cameras"—surveillance devices that become spider-like enemies once disabled. The game’s graphics bring back PS2-era art styles with angular characters and heavy shadows. Though still in early alpha, early footage shows promising fluid execution of tactical maneuvers and melee takedowns. There is no confirmed release date yet, but interested players can request access to an upcoming playtest on Steam.

Resident Evil Requiem Adds Photo Mode and Minor Cutscene Tweaks

A recent patch for Resident Evil Requiem introduces a photo mode allowing players to capture in-game moments with adjustable camera angles, filters, and stickers. Although camera movement is somewhat limited, the feature provides new ways to engage with the game’s visuals. Alongside photo mode, the patch addresses unspecified bugs related to progression and crashes and notes adjustments to character expressions in some cutscenes to better convey emotion. This addition follows similar trends in modern games aiming to enhance player creativity and immersion. The photo mode can be accessed via the pause menu or shortcut keys during gameplay, except during fixed animations.

Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous Highlights Extensive Build Variety and Powerful Mounted Combat

Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous stands out for its extensive depth in character customization, offering 232 subclasses across 26 base classes and additional prestige classes. This complexity appeals to players keen on detailed buildcrafting but can also be daunting to newcomers.
