Zero Parades Shows Promising Moments as Disco Elysium Successor

ZA/UM's upcoming RPG, *Zero Parades*, aims to follow up on the critical success of *Disco Elysium* by exploring a new spy thriller setting. At this year’s Game Developers Conference (GDC), a longer demo was previewed which reveals that the game retains the signature verbose style and complex choice-driven dialogue that defined its predecessor. Players take on the role of Hershel, a disgraced spy, navigating a city filled with mystery and quirky interactions. Notably, the game introduces creative dialogue options and skill checks that influence character states, such as anxiety, and turns failures into humorous or meaningful moments rather than dead ends. While the game’s tone feels lighter and more sarcastic compared to *Disco Elysium*, its writing shows potential to deliver an engaging spycraft RPG experience.

Seed MMO Offers Persistent Character Simulation Led by Ex-EVE Developers

Klang Games, founded by former EVE Online developers, showcased *Seed*, an ambitious MMO emphasizing persistent, society-driven simulation. Players create "Seedlings" whose lives continue evolving even when offline, embodying a blend of world persistence and deep character simulation. The game’s core is player-run societies, with governance, resource management, and social dynamics that can reflect everything from small player groups to massive urban centers. Recent closed beta testing highlighted emergent player-driven political drama and economic disputes resembling those seen in EVE Online. A mobile app companion will allow players to interact with their Seedlings remotely, though AI-driven character conversations remain an open question in terms of engagement. *Seed* remains in gradual closed beta with no broad release date announced yet.

Shadowstone Begins Public Playtest Before Early Access Launch

*Shadowstone*, a tactics roguelike from Secret Door and Dreamhaven, has launched a surprise public playtest concurrent with the Future Games Show Spring Showcase. The game, a spin-off of the tabletop-inspired RPG *Sunderfolk*, offers both solo and four-player multiplayer runs through randomized ruins. Unlike *Sunderfolk*, which relied on smartphone controllers, *Shadowstone* uses a traditional mouse-and-keyboard interface and focuses on run-based gameplay utilizing dynamic skill cards, equipment, and power-ups. Progress made during this playtest will not carry over to early access, and participation requires signing up on Steam. An early access release is planned for later in 2026, but a specific date has not yet been announced.

Industry Veteran Rob Pardo Advises Against Layoffs in GDC Keynote

At GDC 2026, former Blizzard creative chief Rob Pardo urged game industry executives to reconsider layoffs, emphasizing the long-term value of keeping experienced development teams intact. Drawing on his career working on landmark titles like *Starcraft* and *Warcraft 3*, Pardo stressed that successful games are the product of teams who have navigated unpredictable development challenges together. He warned that replacing these teams after a successful launch endangers the ability to replicate that success. Pardo’s comments come amid ongoing industry trends of layoffs following major releases, a practice he suggests undermines future creative achievement and player care.

Path of Exile’s Mirage League Introduces Major Economy and Gameplay Changes

Grinding Gear Games’ new *Path of Exile* Mirage league brings significant updates including increased currency drop rates and a revamped atlas system. Players can enter “mirages,” replaying portions of areas with beneficial wishes to boost rewards, allowing double runs of favorable league mechanics. The atlas no longer drops specific maps but tiered tokens usable on any layout, promoting more intentional endgame progression. Additionally, socketing currency-enhancing astrolabes modifies map areas with impactful effects. These changes together alter gameplay flow, currency acquisition, and farming strategies, potentially marking one of the most substantial economic updates since 2019. The update also adds new rewards and a class ascendancy, expanding player options.
