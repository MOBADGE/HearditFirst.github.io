Infocom's Failed Business Software Now Runs on Modern PCs

Infocom, known for its classic text adventures like the Zork series, released a business database program called Cornerstone in 1985 in an attempt to diversify beyond games. Unlike their adventure titles, Cornerstone ran on its own virtual machine, making it slow and ill-suited for the business market dominated by IBM-compatibles. This misstep contributed to financial difficulties that eventually led to Infocom's merger with Activision and closure in 1989.

Recently, developer TaradinoC created an interpreter named Linchpin that allows Cornerstone to run on modern PCs, reviving interest in this obscure piece of software history. While mainly of archival interest, this project highlights how Infocom's diversion of resources to Cornerstone hampered innovation in their games division during a critical period.

Nexon Clarifies Neople's Team Reorganization Amid Development Wind-Down

Nexon confirmed a reallocation of staff at Neople, the developer of the action RPG The First Berserker: Khazan, but denied reports suggesting layoffs due to poor sales. Instead, the company said the game’s development is naturally concluding, and teams are being reassigned to other projects, including further entries in the Dungeon & Fighter franchise.

Despite positive critical reception and Steam user reviews, The First Berserker: Khazan did not meet sales expectations, leading to the winding down of its development. Nexon’s statement underscores their intention to focus on sustaining live service operations and upcoming global market entries for DNF-related titles.

Darkstone Action RPG Remaster Announced by Original Creator

Darkstone, a 1999 action RPG known for its unique features such as a second playable companion and a player hunger and aging system, is receiving a remaster led by its original creator Paul Cuisset. The original game, although available digitally, suffers from technical issues on modern systems.

The remaster, titled Darkstone Restoration, aims to improve stability, compatibility, controls, and readability. Potential future features include modernized multiplayer and a quest editor, dependent on early access feedback. The remaster will launch into early access without a set release date, offering fans a chance to experience one of the lesser-known Diablo-style RPGs from the late 1990s in a more polished form.

Doki Doki Literature Club Removed from Google Play Over Sensitive Themes

Google has pulled the visual novel Doki Doki Literature Club from its Android store, citing violations related to sensitive content policies. The game began as a seemingly typical high school romance visual novel before shifting into psychological horror exploring themes of depression and suicide.

Developer Dan Salvato and publisher Serenity Forge issued a statement emphasizing the game’s intent to responsibly portray mental health issues and its ongoing availability on other platforms. They are seeking ways to restore the game on Google Play or find alternative Android distribution methods. The removal draws attention to challenges faced by games addressing serious topics within app store content guidelines.

Dune: Awakening Makes PvP Fully Optional in Endgame Zones

Funcom is restructuring the PvP system in Dune: Awakening's Deep Desert area by removing mandatory PvP zones and introducing separate PvP and PvE instances for endgame players. This change follows ongoing complaints since the game's launch about forced PvP in a primarily PvE-oriented player base.

The update will disable PvP zones in the Hagga Basin and offer a PvE instance without player combat alongside a PvP instance with higher resource rewards to balance the increased risk. Funcom also announced upcoming self-hosted servers allowing for customizable private worlds, though these require technical setup. The adjustments reflect Funcom’s effort to better accommodate player preferences and improve the game’s accessibility.

Blizzard Adds Prop Hunt Mode to World of Warcraft Player Housing

Blizzard announced Decor Duels, a prop hunt-style PvP mode within World of Warcraft’s player housing system, arriving with patch 12.0.5 on April 21. Taking place in Silvermoon city, the mode divides players into teams of props and seekers, adding a playful twist to the housing tools.

This marks Blizzard’s first use of housing assets for competitive gameplay beyond decorating. Decor Duels will offer rewards like mounts and toys and be accessible via the group finder. The mode demonstrates Blizzard’s commitment to expanding the possibilities of WoW’s player housing system, moving beyond static construction toward interactive and social experiences.
