Security Flaw Exposes Thousands of Robot Vacuums Globally

Software engineer Sammy Azdoufal discovered a significant security flaw while attempting to control his DJI Romo robot vacuum with a PlayStation 5 controller. Instead of linking only to his own device, he inadvertently gained control over approximately 7,000 robots worldwide across about two dozen countries.

- Through the flawed security token system, Azdoufal accessed live camera feeds, microphones, floor plans, and status data of these devices. - DJI released a patch shortly after being notified to address this vulnerability but admitted some issues remain under review. - This incident highlights ongoing security challenges with internet-connected household devices and raises concerns over privacy, particularly since such vacuums include microphones, an unusual feature given their purpose.

The discovery underscores the importance of robust security in consumer IoT devices and careful scrutiny from both manufacturers and users.
