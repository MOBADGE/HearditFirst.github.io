GameStop’s Failed Steam Competitor: A Lesson in Digital Distribution

Larry Kuperman, a veteran of the games industry and former GameStop executive, reflected on the retailer’s early attempts at building a Steam rival through its acquisition of Stardock’s Impulse platform. Launched in 2008 and acquired by GameStop in 2011, Impulse aimed to be a digital storefront for PC games but was ultimately discontinued in 2014. Kuperman expressed surprise that GameStop’s management regarded digital distribution as merely a passing trend, betting heavily on brick-and-mortar sales to rebound. In hindsight, this misjudgment stalled their adaptation to shifting market demands and let Valve’s Steam dominate by focusing on community features and broad third-party game support. Kuperman also highlighted the significance of other platforms like GOG, which helped preserve classic games and sustain smaller developers.

Slay the Spire 2 Faces Steam Backlash but Looks to Improve Communication

Mega Crit co-founder Casey Yano addressed the recent wave of over 9,000 negative Steam reviews aimed at Slay the Spire 2 after a contentious beta balance patch. Yano explained that much of the backlash, particularly from players in China, stemmed from limited avenues for feedback due to internet restrictions, making Steam reviews the only accessible channel for many. While some Western players criticized the review bombing for bypassing official forums and Discord, Yano expressed understanding for the players’ perspective and emphasized the importance of better communication. Mega Crit plans to explore varied ways for players to interact with the game, including competitive, accelerated, and social modes, while acknowledging that not all ideas may come to fruition.

Games Workshop’s Warhammer 40,000 Musical: A Persistent April Fools’ Project

Games Workshop released an April Fools’ musical video titled *The Emperor Protects: A Warhammer 40,000 Musical*, featuring fully costumed characters performing original songs inspired by the franchise. Though clearly a joke, the company provided an elaborate behind-the-scenes video suggesting a full stage production might be planned for April 1, 2027. Despite the comedic nature, many Warhammer fans responded positively, expressing interest in seeing the musical become a real event. Games Workshop’s commitment to the concept could indicate future follow-up projects tied to April Fools’ events or more.

Revisiting Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2019’s Lasting Appeal

A retrospective look at *Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2019* highlights why it remains a standout entry years after its release. Praised for its grounded realism, detailed visuals, and impactful sound design, the game redefined the series with a focused multiplayer experience and a campaign that avoided some controversial pitfalls. The simpler Create-a-Class system encouraged diverse gunplay with meaningful trade-offs between weapon attachments, contrasting with later entries’ more homogenized loadouts. The article notes that MW2019 was also the last major Call of Duty before the franchise became heavily intertwined with the Warzone battle royale and an increasing number of external brand collaborations, which some fans view as a decline.

Growing Interest in Linux for PC Gaming

A recent PC Gamer reader poll reveals that 43% of respondents have already switched or plan to switch to Linux for gaming in 2026, reflecting growing interest following improvements like Valve’s Proton compatibility layer and Steam Deck’s success. Surprisingly, 26% said they don’t mind using Windows, suggesting a diverse range of preferences among PC gamers. However, challenges remain for Linux’s multiplayer game support due to issues like kernel-level anti-cheat systems. The poll collected over 2,300 responses and highlights an ongoing debate about the viability of Linux as a primary gaming platform.

Piranha Games Lays Off 30% of Staff Amid Uncertain Future for MechWarrior

MechWarrior developer Piranha Games confirmed layoffs affecting approximately 30% of its employees as part of an economic restructuring. CEO Russ Bullock acknowledged the cuts but said development continues on upcoming DLC for *MechWarrior 5: Mercenaries*, scheduled for release in May, and another MechWarrior project planned for the fall.
