Windrose: How Fast Travel and Lamp Oil Work

In the pirate-themed co-op survival game *Windrose*, players navigating its extensive oceanic map may wonder how fast travel functions. Fast travel is available, but not from the start. It unlocks after smelting your first copper ingot and crafting a *Fast Travel Bell*, which requires copper ingots and ropes, alongside building a *Fast Travel Point* from wood and the bell. These points must be placed near water on coastlines, such as docks, and at least two are needed to fast travel on foot.

While sailing your ship, fast travel becomes more flexible—you can activate it from anywhere on the sea map provided you’re not in combat, although current co-op mechanics mean only the ship captain will instantly teleport, requiring re-summoning the vessel.

Additionally, players seeking to make caves less perilous by using a lantern need Lamp Oil to keep it lit. Lamp Oil is crafted by refilling an empty lantern with animal fat at a workbench. Animal fat, crucial for multiple recipes, is primarily obtained by hunting wild boars on islands. Carrying extra lanterns is advisable due to fuel consumption in larger cave systems.

These insights clarify key gameplay mechanics that address exploration challenges in *Windrose* and underline resource management’s role in survival.

Skull Horde: A Necromantic Hybrid Roguelike

*Skull Horde*, newly available on Steam, blends gameplay elements from roguelikes like *Vampire Survivors* and auto-battlers like *Teamfight Tactics*. The protagonist is a detached skull leading an army of skeletal minions against its own headless body. Players recruit and upgrade diverse skeleton units, balancing offensive and defensive strategies under a limit of six unit types.

Combat efficiency is time-sensitive; lingering attracts more enemies. Though players cannot direct minions precisely, commands to regroup and limited spells aid troop management. Damage to the skull affects all minions collectively, introducing tactical risk-reward decisions.

While multiple characters and skill trees exist, currently only one is unlocked, emphasizing tank-style builds. This limits variety early on, potentially funneling players toward similar strategies despite hints at diverse playstyles involving magic or mass minion sacrifice.

The game’s combination of strategic army building with real-time action presents a fresh take within the roguelike genre, though its long-term appeal may depend on expanding character options.

Escape from Tarkov Creator Addresses New Sci-Fi Shooter

Nikita Buyanov, creator of the widely known hardcore shooter *Escape from Tarkov*, clarified his involvement in the upcoming sci-fi FPS *Fragmentary Order*. Developed by a separate studio founded by Buyanov, the new game is distinct from *Tarkov* and its developer Battlestate Games. Buyanov reaffirmed ongoing dedication to *Escape from Tarkov* alongside his new project.

Though *Fragmentary Order* shows some similarities to *Tarkov*, it is not a follow-up or related IP. Buyanov aims to explore a "hard sci-fi" universe differently but acknowledged the complexities of managing both projects simultaneously. The gameplay style, including whether it will feature extraction shooter mechanics like *Tarkov*, remains unconfirmed.

This update helps alleviate player concerns about *Tarkov* losing focus and positions *Fragmentary Order* as a parallel creative venture rather than a replacement.

Metro 2039 Promises Increased Narrative Darkness

Dmitry Glukhovsky, author of the *Metro* novels and involved with the game series, confirmed *Metro 2039* as the next entry in the post-apocalyptic franchise. While details remain sparse, Glukhovsky stated this installment would be "darker than anything you've seen before."

The nature of this darkness—whether narrative, atmospheric, or gameplay-related—is unspecified, but the remark suggests a continuation of the series’ grim tone. The official reveal is scheduled for April 16.

Glukhovsky remains engaged with the games, providing writing contributions beyond the original novel adaptations, contrasting with some authors who distance themselves from licensed game projects. This involvement may influence the game’s fidelity to the source material.
