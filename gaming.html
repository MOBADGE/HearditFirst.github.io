Magic: The Gathering Returns to Strixhaven Setting, Avoids Harry Potter Crossover

Magic: The Gathering has announced its new set, *Secrets of Strixhaven*, which revisits the original Strixhaven university setting rather than pursuing a crossover with the Harry Potter universe. This decision follows Wizards of the Coast's recent history of incorporating diverse crossovers, from Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles to The Office. The choice to stick with Strixhaven offers a university-level magical setting that includes varied academic subjects beyond magic, and enables more mature storytelling with young adult characters.

This matters because it preserves Magic’s unique fantasy lore while avoiding potential creative pitfalls tied to more mainstream franchises. The original Strixhaven setting is noted for its self-aware play on magical school tropes, contrasting with the more rigid and sometimes criticized depictions in Harry Potter. *Secrets of Strixhaven* will be available in Magic: Arena on April 21 and on physical tabletop formats from April 24.

Crimson Desert Patch Adds Player-Requested Hide Helmet Feature

The single-player RPG *Crimson Desert* has received a new patch introducing a "hide helmet" option, a feature highly requested by players since the game’s launch. The update allows players to customize when their equipped headgear is visible—in cutscenes, combat, or never. Additionally, the patch adds a new armor set and helmet for the game's feline companions, expands camp storage to 1,000 slots, and offers a "classic" movement control scheme for players preferring the original setup.

This update is notable as it signals the developers’ responsiveness to user feedback and willingness to refine gameplay experience post-launch. Despite *Crimson Desert*’s mixed critical reception and some earlier controversies, such patches contribute to improving player satisfaction and highlight an evolving single-player RPG.

Leaked Footage Offers Early Look at Alex Garland’s Elden Ring Movie Set

A fan-filmed video from the UK has potentially revealed the first glimpse of the upcoming *Elden Ring* movie directed by Alex Garland. The footage features a detailed set replicating a ruined church from the game, complete with props and signage from a known scenic construction company specializing in film sets. The location is suspected to be Windsor Great Park, a frequent filming spot for major productions.

While the video does not officially confirm the film's production, it has sparked speculation among fans about the movie’s focus and setting. Garland, who developed a lengthy script to secure the project, is expected to faithfully adapt the game’s atmosphere, but details remain scarce. The precise role of the depicted set in the movie's narrative and casting updates are yet to be clarified.

Elden Ring Datamine Sheds Light on DLC Villain Miquella’s Origin Story

Dataminer Lance McDonald has uncovered a base game cutscene for *Elden Ring* featuring the DLC antagonist Miquella, providing fresh insight into the character's backstory. The scene shows Miquella planting the Haligtree sapling and watering it with his blood, suggesting a deeper narrative connection to the known boss fight locations and the wider lore.

This cutscene implies early conceptualizations of Miquella’s motivations centered on growth and abundance, showing a slightly different, more benevolent portrayal than the final DLC characterization. For fans of the game’s lore, this discovery offers additional context that enriches understanding of the character and the evolution of the game's story.

Warframe Developer Commits to No AI-Generated Content in Games

Digital Extremes’ community director Megan Everett has publicly stated a firm stance against the use of generative AI in the development of *Warframe* and the upcoming *Soulframe*. Everett emphasized that all creative content in their games is handcrafted by humans and expressed frustration with the difficulty in distinguishing AI-generated art from human-created work.

This position contrasts with a growing trend in the industry where AI tools are increasingly utilized in game development. Digital Extremes’ commitment highlights ongoing debates about the role of AI in creative industries, intellectual property concerns, and player expectations for authenticity in game assets.
