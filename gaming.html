DIY DRAM Production in a Garden Shed YouTuber Dr. Semiconductor has advanced their backyard chip fabrication setup by successfully creating functional DRAM cells in a home-built cleanroom. Starting with pre-made silicon wafers, the process involved intricate steps like wafer cutting, cleaning, high-temperature oxidation, photolithography, and doping using homemade solutions. Despite the complexities and limitations—such as faster capacitor charge loss requiring more frequent refreshes—the attempt marks a rare achievement of producing working DRAM outside industrial environments. Dr. Semiconductor plans to scale this by linking cells into larger arrays and potentially connecting them to a PC, which could offer interesting insights into semiconductor manufacturing and DIY hardware. While not a practical solution for the broader "RAMpocalypse," it highlights individual innovation amid high memory prices.

Positive Early Impressions of the Framework 13 Pro Laptop for Linux Gaming The newly launched Framework 13 Pro laptop, aimed at Linux users and promoted as a "MacBook Pro for Linux," has received favorable early reviews from notable Linux gaming experts. GloriousEggRoll, known for Proton GE (a popular compatibility layer enhancer for Steam games on Linux), reports excellent performance running the Nobara Linux distribution and praises the machine’s unexpectedly long battery life. Similarly, the CachyOS team, from another gaming-oriented Linux distro, claims the laptop can last up to two days on typical workloads like programming and browsing with optimizations. This positive feedback is noteworthy as battery life is a critical concern for Linux gamers and portable users. The laptop’s combination of modularity, repairability, and strong battery endurance could make it an appealing choice for Linux gaming enthusiasts once more extensive performance tests are conducted.

Cloudflare Discusses Challenges of an AI-Driven Web Cloudflare has published a blog post outlining the challenges posed by an increasingly AI-driven internet, where distinguishing between human users and AI bots becomes difficult and eventually meaningless. They argue that the traditional web infrastructure is not equipped to verify legitimate, acceptable connections in an environment where automated agents perform actions that humans would traditionally do manually. Cloudflare suggests a new approach focusing on verifying "behavior without proving identity" through active, privacy-preserving client-side validation methods rather than intrusive tracking. One proposed technology is Privacy Pass, which allows anonymous proof of legitimacy without revealing personal data or linking sessions. The post warns that failing to address this could result in a more restrictive and costly web experience with fewer free-access articles and greater reliance on walled gardens, raising concerns about openness and privacy online.

How to Tame the Abyss Heuklang Cat in Crimson Desert The recent Crimson Desert patch has made the Abyss Heuklang, a black cat creature from the game's abyssal area, available as a pet companion. Players can find the cat near the entrance to the Axiom Archives in the Abyss. If trust isn’t already maxed, players gain it by petting and feeding the cat with milk or bird meat purchasable in Hernand city. Once trust is sufficient, players can adopt the Abyss Heuklang like any other pet. This addition expands the range of companions and adds a unique atmospheric element for players exploring the Abyss, enhancing the game's personalization options without altering gameplay balance.

Leaked Elden Ring Movie Footage Features the Loathsome Dung Eater Leaked 37-second behind-the-scenes footage from the upcoming Elden Ring movie directed by Alex Garland centers on the Loathsome Dung Eater, a notorious character from the game known for his grim backstory. The clip shows the Dung Eater hanging in stocks and being pelted with rotten food by townspeople—a scene mirroring the game's introductory cutscene at Leyndell. The character's inclusion suggests fidelity to the source material and raises expectations about the film’s narrative approach. Fans have reacted with a mix of amusement and excitement, noting the potential for the film to capture Elden Ring’s dark and chaotic tone. This footage gives an early glimpse into the film’s production design and character representation without revealing broader plot details.
