Nightdive’s Larry Kuperman Retires After 25 Years in Games

Larry Kuperman, vice president of business development at Nightdive Studios, has retired from full-time work in the gaming industry after a 25-year career. His journey spanned roles at publisher Stardock and retailer GameStop before joining Nightdive, a studio known for preserving classic games. Kuperman specialized in navigating complicated intellectual property rights, a challenging task exemplified by efforts to re-release titles like *No One Lives Forever* and *System Shock*. He noted that games are better viewed as art to be preserved rather than mere products.

Before retiring, Kuperman ensured Nightdive completed key projects including the *Sin* remaster and the *System Shock 2* remaster, fulfilling long-standing promises despite financial sacrifices. Reflecting on losses within the industry, he emphasized the importance of capturing the history and experiences of veteran developers. Although stepping away from full-time work, Kuperman plans to stay active with the International Game Developers Association to support newcomers.

French Consumer Group Sues Ubisoft Over *The Crew* Shutdown

French consumer association UFC-Que Choisir, supported by the Stop Killing Games campaign, has launched a lawsuit against Ubisoft regarding the 2024 closure of *The Crew*. The lawsuit claims Ubisoft violated consumer rights by shutting down servers and revoking licenses without adequately informing players that their access to the game was temporary. Following the shutdown, Ubisoft faced widespread criticism for stripping licenses, including efforts to prevent fan-run servers.

The Stop Killing Games initiative, which emerged from this controversy, gathered over 1.3 million signatures on an EU petition aimed at regulating how publishers manage online games. The UFC-Que Choisir’s legal action seeks to clarify publishers’ obligations and ban harmful contractual clauses allowing sudden game shutdowns without recourse. Meanwhile, dedicated modders have revived *The Crew* through unofficial means, highlighting ongoing player demand for ways to preserve online games.

*World of Warcraft* Players Complete Year-Long Puzzle With Synchronized Dance

A group of *World of Warcraft* players recently solved a complex year-old puzzle related to the Felcycle motorcycle mount by performing a coordinated emote dance involving 40 participants. The puzzle, spread across the Karazhan raid and the Midnight expansion, required lighting 12 orbs scattered in secret locations. The dance sequence necessitated precise in-game synchronization, a nod to *WoW*’s earlier era of large-scale raids.

The final steps also included a stealthy quest involving evading enemies while carrying a “bad orb,” recalling the challenge of other difficult escort missions. Completing the puzzle rewards players with a themed outfit matching the demonic Felcycle mount. This lengthy community effort underscores player dedication to uncovering hidden content and collecting rare cosmetic items.

Bungie Patches *Marathon* to Remove Slide Cancel Movement Exploit

Bungie has addressed a movement exploit in its shooter *Marathon* that allowed players to slide cancel into ability animations, generating unintended high momentum. The latest patch removed this exploit to maintain competitive balance and preserve the game's intended pace. Bungie emphasized that aggressive repositioning should come with clear costs, such as ability cooldowns or increased risk, and stated it will continue to monitor for similar movement abuses.

While some highlight-reel players may lament the change, the update aims to keep gameplay fair and accessible by preventing “unbounded” movement. The grappling ability remains usable for limited boosts but now incurs ability cooldowns when used, aligning with the studio's design philosophy.

New Drone “Vaporizer” Brings Increased Challenge in *Arc Raiders*

The recent “Close Scrutiny” update for *Arc Raiders* introduces the Vaporizer, a new aggressive ARC drone enemy that poses a significant threat to player squads. Equipped with powerful laser attacks and strong defensive shields, Vaporizers can swiftly eliminate groups and require coordinated fire and ample healing to overcome. Their fast, unpredictable movement marks a departure from previous drone behavior.

This added difficulty reflects feedback from dedicated players seeking more challenging enemies. Despite the Vaporizer’s toughness, some veterans have already begun developing strategies to counter it. The update also reduces advantages from free loadouts, promoting a more balanced late-game experience.
