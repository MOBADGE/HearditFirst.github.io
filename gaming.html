Ashes of Creation Early Access Launches on Steam

After a lengthy development period dating back to its successful 2017 Kickstarter campaign, the fantasy MMORPG Ashes of Creation is entering early access on Steam. The early access launch, featuring its alpha build, is scheduled for 10 a.m. PST on Thursday, December 11, 2025. This is a global release with simultaneous server availability worldwide. Players should expect an unfinished game, as it remains in its second alpha phase with two betas planned before the full 1.0 release. Returning players will need to uninstall the previous Intrepid launcher and link their accounts to Steam since the standalone launcher is being retired. Notably, there is no preload option, so downloading and registration must happen once servers are live. This release marks an important step for Ashes of Creation, opening the game to a wider audience via Steam but underlining that progress and stability are still in development.

The Temple of Elemental Evil Gets Steam Re-Release with Community Fixes

The 2003 D&D CRPG The Temple of Elemental Evil has been re-released on Steam, now including over 1,000 community-created patches, fixes, and quality-of-life improvements integrated into the game by default. Known previously for technical issues like poor pathfinding and bugs, this version aims to present the game as originally intended, with restored content and improved gameplay. The re-release retains the game's classic early 2000s aesthetic and complex D&D 3.5 ruleset in the Greyhawk setting. This update offers both longtime fans and newcomers an opportunity to experience a historically unique and tactically rich CRPG without the frustrations of its past technical problems.
