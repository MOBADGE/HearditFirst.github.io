Framework Champions Upgradeable PCs Amid Tech Industry Trends

Framework, a laptop maker known for its focus on upgradability and user ownership, introduced its new Framework 13 Pro laptop with a redesigned chassis. Rather than pushing customers to replace entire devices, Framework encourages continuing use of older components by offering compatibility with parts from laptops up to six years old. Users can also purchase just the chassis or upgrade certain components like the battery or mainboard, promoting sustainability and long device lifespans.

Founder Nirav Patel contrasted Framework’s approach with prevalent trends in the tech industry, where companies increasingly prioritize cloud computing, AI integration, and hardware leasing over personal device ownership. Framework’s dedication to modular designs and support for Linux sets it apart amid a landscape where many firms favor sealed or soldered hardware, reducing repairability.

This approach addresses concerns about growing electronic waste and loss of consumer control, offering a practical alternative for users seeking longevity and customization in their PCs.

Meta Plans Extensive Employee PC Monitoring for AI Training

Meta has announced plans to implement keylogging software on US-based employee computers to capture mouse movements, clicks, keystrokes, and screenshots of activity on work-related applications. The goal is to collect data for training AI models to better automate common computer tasks.

The company stated it will exclude sensitive content and will not use the data for performance evaluations. However, this monitoring raises privacy concerns, especially as Meta is simultaneously preparing significant layoffs, suggesting automation may replace some human roles.

Sources report low morale and paranoia within Meta, with internal fears exacerbated by such invasive surveillance. The initiative is limited to US employees, likely due to stricter data privacy laws in Europe.

Masters of Albion Early Access Launch Details

The god game *Masters of Albion* by renowned designer Peter Molyneux launches into early access on April 22, 2026. The release time is scheduled for 1 pm EDT (6 pm BST), with regional launch times spanning from 10 am PDT in Los Angeles to early hours of April 23 in Sydney and Auckland.

No official preloading option appears available, though the modest 20 GB install size suggests downloads won’t be overly lengthy. Players interested in classic god game mechanics, involving town-building and supernatural powers, can expect to start exploring soon.

11 Bit Studios Announces Full Remake of *This War of Mine*

11 Bit Studios, developer of narrative-driven survival games, revealed plans for a full remake of their critically acclaimed anti-war title *This War of Mine*, originally released in 2014. The remake aims to modernize the game with fresh designs and a multi-year support plan.

This announcement coincides with 11 Bit’s confirmation of new unannounced projects and a new *Frostpunk* game expanding the series into a different genre. The original *This War of Mine* remains relevant today due to ongoing global conflicts impacting civilians, and the studio intends to maintain its emotionally impactful storytelling.

No release window was given, but 2026 will see additional DLC for *Frostpunk* and *The Alters*, along with the full release of *Moonlighter 2*.

Palia Introduces Breedable and Rideable Horses in Upcoming Expansion

The life simulation MMO *Palia* will add breedable and rideable horses in its next expansion, *The Royal Highlands*, set to launch as a free update on May 12, 2026. The horses feature multiple breeds with realistic gaits and individual traits that affect handling and abilities.

Players can care for their horses at ranches, and a breeding system allows the creation of mounts with desirable traits. Unlike typical MMO mounts that mainly increase movement speed, *Palia*’s horses include nuanced gameplay features such as varied gaits and status effect buffs.

The expansion will also bring a new explorable zone, a new villager, and additional furniture styles.

Extraction Shooter *Marauders* Resumes Development After Hiatus

After more than 20 months without updates, extraction shooter *Marauders* has announced a significant resurgence in development. The studio shared a production update explaining that initial fixes expanded into a broader overhaul of core systems and mechanics.

Previously thought to be abandoned, this renewed commitment has drawn positive attention from the community, although the game’s crowded genre and the end of its stated 2023 roadmap introduce uncertainty about its future trajectory.

The update signifies ongoing interest in refining and supporting the title despite a lengthy development silence.
