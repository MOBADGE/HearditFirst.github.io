Atlas Pirates Mechanics Coming to Ark: Survival Ascended DLC

Studio Wildcard is incorporating elements from its earlier pirate survival game Atlas into Ark: Survival Ascended's upcoming DLC, *Tides of Fortune*. Atlas, released in 2018, faced criticism for its punishing gameplay and technical issues, which hindered its success despite innovative mechanics such as massive oceanic worlds and detailed shipbuilding. Ark's co-founders Jeremy Stieglitz and Jesse Rapczak explained that the new DLC will adopt some of Atlas's sailing and ocean physics, but in a more accessible form. This includes building and customizing large pirate ships that sail realistic waves, a marked upgrade from Ark’s traditionally flat water. The integration is meant to revive Atlas’s sophisticated naval mechanics with easier ship maintenance and management, while retaining the dinosaur-based gameplay Ark players expect. This move addresses Ark’s longstanding criticism regarding its ocean systems and aims to expand player options without overwhelming them.

How to Obtain All Mutations in Monster Hunter Stories 3

Monster Hunter Stories 3 features a mutation mechanic that allows players to obtain rare subspecies of monsters, known as Monsties, by restoring local ecosystems. These mutated Monsties are not essential for story completion but provide enhanced stats and genetic benefits for gameplay. To trigger mutations, players need to raise the ecosystem ranks by hatching and releasing certain monsters under specific environmental conditions. Examples include Pink Rathian, Dreadqueen Rathian, Ivory Lagiacrus, and several notable variants of Zinogre and Tigrex. Some mutations require the presence of rival or complementary monsters, and certain ecosystem ranks must be achieved (typically A or S rank). The article provides detailed requirements for each notable mutation, highlighting the strategic aspect of populating and balancing the ecosystems to unlock powerful variants.

Overwatch Director Reflects on 5v5 Versus 6v6 Debate and Player Trust

Overwatch’s move from 6v6 to 5v5 players per team with the release of Overwatch 2 in 2022 sparked extensive player debate and dissatisfaction. Aaron Keller, the game director, acknowledged at the 2026 Game Developers Conference that delaying engagement with community concerns over this change “cost us trust.” Blizzard initially waited over a year before addressing the format publicly and testing limited-time 6v6 modes in response to player demand. These tests confirmed substantial interest, leading to a permanent 6v6 mode addition. Keller reflected that the team initially underestimated the desire for rapid evolution and transparent communication from the player base. While experimentation with game modes continues, Blizzard still weighs whether multiple core gameplay identities are sustainable long-term. Keller noted that a shift toward more responsive development and listening to the community has redefined their approach going forward.

Jeff Kaplan on WoW’s Perspective and Game Criticism Culture

Jeff Kaplan, former Blizzard vice president and lead designer on World of Warcraft and Overwatch, revealed during a 10-hour stream for his new game *The Legend of California* that he initially wanted WoW to be a first-person MMORPG rather than the official third-person perspective. He cited EverQuest’s first-person view as a formative influence on his appreciation for character art and social interaction. Kaplan also expressed frustration with the prevalent online game criticism culture, urging players not to loudly denounce games they have no intention of playing. He characterized much of the outrage as unproductive “nerd baby rage” and encouraged constructive engagement instead. Kaplan’s candid remarks reflect his current independent status and willingness to speak openly about design and community dynamics.

Bungie Tests Experimental Duos Mode in Marathon

Bungie announced an experimental duos mode for its battle royale game Marathon, launching on March 18, 2026, and running for approximately two weeks. This mode will be limited to one map, Perimeter, and is intended to gauge player response and gather data on how reducing squad size from three to two affects gameplay and matchmaking. Bungie game director Joe Ziegler noted this initial implementation is a prototype and may change before a broader rollout. The test aims to understand impacts on map flow, player behavior, loot distribution, and queue times. The duos option responds to community feedback requesting more flexible team sizes, but Bungie recognizes the complexities involved in balancing and supporting an additional queue type.
