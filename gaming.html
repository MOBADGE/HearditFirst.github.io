Fallout: New Vegas Available for 99 Cents in Steam Spring Sale

The Steam Spring Sale has started, featuring thousands of discounted games. Among the deals, Fallout: New Vegas stands out with a price of just 99 cents. Developed by Obsidian rather than Bethesda, this RPG is often regarded as one of the series’ best due to its mix of humor, atmosphere, and impactful player choices. The availability of the Ultimate Edition, which bundles all DLC, is also discounted to around five pounds. This sale might attract new players, especially with the recent reference to New Vegas in the second season of the Fallout TV show, stirring discussions about the game's canon within the community. For players interested in improving their experience, guides and mods are readily available.

Robert Garriott on Excess PC Games Back in 1989

In a recent archival release, Robert Garriott, co-founder of Origin Systems and brother to Ultima creator Richard Garriott, spoke at the 1989 Computer Game Developers Conference about challenges the PC gaming industry faced more than three decades ago. Garriott noted that although PC game sales were growing annually, the increasing number of new titles was diluting sales per individual game. This “product proliferation” led to consumer confusion and dissatisfaction about game quality. He also addressed the impact of the Nintendo Entertainment System on arcade and entry-level games. His advice—to focus on unique, high-quality, in-depth games that consoles couldn’t replicate—remains relevant given today’s even larger market and volume of game releases.

Hoarder: A Darkwood Co-Creator’s Cleaning Simulator with a Dark Twist

One of the original developers of the horror game Darkwood has announced a new project called Hoarder, described as a cleaning simulator with a dark and eerie atmosphere. Unlike traditional cleaning or power-washing simulators, Hoarder tasks players with decluttering an abandoned hoarder’s house, uncovering secrets in a mysterious basement that defies normal geometry. The game gradually strips away tutorial assists, leaving players alone to explore and manage an unsettling environment. Although Hoarder does not yet have a release date, it is available to wishlist on Steam and may appeal to players interested in atmospheric and slow-building horror with mechanical gameplay elements.

Seven Deadly Sins: Origin Codes Offer Free In-Game Items

Netmarble’s gacha RPG Seven Deadly Sins: Origin is offering several redeemable codes that grant free in-game items, including hero draw tickets, enhancement stones, cube keys, and gold. The codes are accessed after completing initial tutorial quests, and players must claim their rewards from an in-game mailbox within seven days after redemption. Codes like ORIGINSTEAM expire at the end of April, while others have no fixed expiration yet. These promotions aid players in progressing and obtaining desirable characters or equipment without purchase. Instructions for redeeming codes are detailed both in-game and via Netmarble’s official website.

Monster Hunter Stories 3: Strategies for Defeating Elder Dragons

Monster Hunter Stories 3 features four challenging Elder Dragons—Namielle, Yama Tsukami, Velkhana, and the combined Wind Serpent Ibushi and Thunder Serpent Narwa—which appear under certain conditions and offer valuable rewards upon defeat. Each dragon has specific elemental weaknesses and requires tailored strategies, including selecting optimal Monsties with suitable resistances and attack types. For example, Namielle is weak to fire, dragon, and ice attacks and resistant to water and thunder, while the combined Ibushi and Narwa encounter is considered the hardest due to their deadly attacks and synergy. Players must carefully manage buffs, debuffs, and abilities to succeed, with recommended levels typically ranging from 60 to 85 depending on the dragon.

Retro Rewind Simulates Running a '90s Video Rental Store

Retro Rewind is a recent simulation game that captures the experience of managing a video rental store in the 1990s. Players handle stocking shelves with VHS tapes, processing rentals and returns, managing cash transactions, and even promoting the store to customers. The game includes period-authentic details such as adult films, which were rented directly at the counter in that era. Unlike many job sims that start slowly, Retro Rewind quickly immerses players into a busy daily routine. Its gameplay is noted for being engaging and detailed, evoking the social and operational aspects of running a retail business at a time when video rental stores were common. The game is currently available on Steam.
