LATEST Gaming

Bite-sized summaries of the latest gaming trends — without the hype.

Updated: April 16, 2026

**Phasmophobia and Alan Wake 2 Collaborate in May Event** Phasmophobia, the cooperative ghost-hunting game developed by Kinetic Games, will host its first crossover event with Alan Wake 2 starting May 12 and running through June 2. Titled "Phasmophobia by Alan Wake," the limited-time event introduces a new antagonist described as a professional Mimic linked to Alan Wake’s "Dark Place" universe. Players can expect themed in-game rewards such as character cosmetics and trophies, though specific details remain undisclosed. This collaboration is a notable first for Phasmophobia and aligns with its growing presence in gaming culture, having surpassed 28 million copies sold across platforms. Kinetic Games has no current plans to rerun the event but remains open to future collaborations.

**Call of Duty Movie Release Set for 2028** Activision and Paramount have announced the release date for the Call of Duty film, scheduled for June 30, 2028. While the project was first revealed in 2025, details remain limited. Taylor Sheridan, known for television works like Yellowstone, is writing the script, with Peter Berg attached as co-writer and director. The film is expected to be an action-oriented narrative likely resembling previous military-themed projects from the studio. A dedicated Call of Duty Movie social media account has been created for updates leading up to the release.

**Metro 2039 Revealed with Darker Tone and New Protagonist** The upcoming Metro 2039, unveiled at the Xbox First Look event, marks the next single-player chapter in the Metro series. This installment introduces a voiced protagonist, "The Stranger," a recluse haunted by traumatic nightmares, diverging from the series' previous hero, Artyom. The game’s setting reflects a harsher post-apocalyptic Moscow Metro, now under control of the authoritarian Novoreich faction led by the character known as "the Fuhrer." Developed by Ukrainian studio 4A Games, Metro 2039 incorporates contemporary geopolitical context, notably Russia’s invasion of Ukraine, shaping its narrative around themes of tyranny and resistance. The game is slated for a winter release.

**Overwatch Season 2 Launches with Notable Hero Adjustments** Overwatch’s Season 2, named Summit, introduces a new damage-per-second hero, Sierra, whose abilities have quickly stirred discussion. Sierra’s Tremor Charge grenade deals significant damage and knockback, while her ultimate ability has drawn attention for its high effectiveness against tank characters, considered by some as overpowered. The season includes several balance changes: Soldier: 76 can now reload while sprinting; Roadhog’s chainhook has been nerfed; health reductions for some tank characters in 6v6 play; and cooldown adjustments to abilities of heroes like Ana and Mercy. The update aims to refine gameplay balance across the roster.

**Hello Kitty Island Adventure Expands with City Town DLC** The latest DLC for Hello Kitty Island Adventure, titled City Town, builds on the game’s Friendship Island setting by offering a new area to explore and a fresh character, Usahana the bunny. Unlike the previous Wheatflour Wonderland expansion, City Town initially presents a quiet, inactive community with locked shops, gradually revitalized through a series of paced quests and friendship tasks. The DLC encourages daily engagement over extended play sessions and expands the world-building of the original game. City Town has been positively received for adding meaningful content and pacing improvements to the cozy life simulation.

**Asus Unveils ZenMouse MD202 with Durable 'Ceraluminum' Finish** Asus has announced the ZenMouse MD202, a wireless mouse featuring a "Ceraluminum" top coat designed for enhanced durability against wear and scratches. Although not marketed specifically as a gaming mouse, it includes features favorable to gamers, such as gaming-grade switches rated for 50 million clicks and adjustable sensitivity from 800 to 4200 DPI. The mouse offers both Bluetooth and 2.4 GHz wireless connections, fast USB-C charging, and an impressive battery life claimed to last up to seven months on a full charge. No release date or price has been provided yet.
