Valve Releases Proton 11 Beta with ARM64 Version for Steam Frame

Valve has quietly released the Proton version 11.0 beta, including a new experimental version designed for ARM64 processors. Proton is a compatibility layer allowing Windows games to run on Linux-based systems like SteamOS. This ARM64-specific build supports Valve's upcoming Steam Frame VR headset, which uses a Qualcomm Snapdragon 8 Gen 3 ARM chip.

The ARM64 Proton beta is still very early in development, with mixed results reported from initial testers. This version leverages a translation layer called FEX to enable Windows x86 code on ARM hardware, though this currently results in performance penalties. The release hints at Valve's preparation for Steam Frame's retail launch, offering better native support for Windows games on ARM-based VR devices.

This development is noteworthy because it marks a step toward broader ARM support in gaming, reflecting evolving hardware trends. Proton's ARM64 version could extend Valve’s ecosystem beyond traditional x86 PCs, potentially reducing reliance on bulky gaming setups for VR gaming.

Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2 Director Comments on LGBT Nomination and "Woke" Label

The creative director of Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2 (KCD2), Daniel Vávra, responded to the game’s nomination for the Gayming Awards, particularly for its depiction of a gay romance option. Vávra expressed pride in the portrayal of the relationship between protagonist Henry and the character Hans, emphasizing that it was handled naturally and historically.

However, Vávra also stressed that including this content does not make the game "woke." He highlighted that the gay romance was a choice for players, integrated without forcing perspectives or overt political messaging. Vávra has a history of vocal opinions on culture wars and gaming narratives, which adds context to his comments.

The nomination and discussion illustrate ongoing debates within gaming about representation and inclusivity. While some applaud KCD2 for its approach to LGBTQ+ content, the conversation around "wokeness" in media continues to provoke diverse reactions from different audiences.

Among Us Surpasses One Billion Downloads on Google Play Store

In an announcement, the developers of Among Us revealed the game has exceeded one billion downloads on the Google Play Store. This milestone positions Among Us ahead of other popular games like Minecraft and Stardew Valley in terms of mobile downloads.

Among Us' sustained popularity, even years after its initial release, underscores the enduring appeal of social deduction games. While the exact number of unique players isn’t clear—downloads can include repeat installs—it reflects strong global engagement.

This achievement is significant as it demonstrates the long-term viability of simple multiplayer experiences and their ability to become part of gaming culture, influencing language and social interaction well beyond peak trends.

Review: AMD Radeon RX 9060 XT 8 GB Graphics Card

AMD's Radeon RX 9060 XT 8 GB graphics card is positioned as an affordable option within the current generation of GPUs. Testing shows it performs well at 1080p in many games but struggles in VRAM-heavy and ray-tracing scenarios compared to Nvidia’s RTX 5060 Ti 8 GB. The RTX card typically maintains higher and more stable frame rates under these conditions.

The RX 9060 XT 8 GB excels in thermals and runs quietly, aided by an oversized cooler in the tested Asus Prime variant. It also benefits from AMD's Adrenaline software suite for driver updates and tuning options.

Price-wise, the card is attractive if budget constraints are strict, but with current market prices around $350 (above MSRP), it's close enough to Nvidia's more capable card to favor the latter for most buyers. Overall, the RX 9060 XT 8 GB offers decent performance but is less future-proof due to VRAM limitations.

MSI Updates Raider and Crosshair Gaming Laptops to Reduce Fan Noise

MSI unveiled refreshed versions of its Raider and Crosshair gaming laptops with a focus on improved thermal design and quieter fans. The company redesigned the motherboard layout to create space for larger fans in the Raider model, allowing for effective cooling at lower fan speeds and keeping noise under 50 dBA during gaming.

The updated motherboard design also contributes to slimmer chassis profiles, enhancing portability without sacrificing performance. MSI claims that fan curves have been tuned to avoid rapid speed changes, reducing sudden noise spikes during lighter workloads.

This approach targets a common complaint about gaming laptops: fan noise. Improvements in noise and portability could make these models more attractive to gamers
