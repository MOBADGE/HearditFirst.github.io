Team Fortress 2 Classified Launches on Steam with Strong Player Interest

*Team Fortress 2 Classified*, formerly known as TF2 Classic, has officially launched on Steam as a free mod, attracting nearly 10,000 concurrent players shortly after release. This version of the 19-year-old shooter simplifies the game to its pre-hats era, reintroduces some pre-release weapons, adds a new class called the Civilian, and includes fresh game modes such as VIP and Multi-Team maps.

Running on Valve‚Äôs official TF2 SDK, Classified also offers enhanced modding tools, improved visuals, and quality-of-life changes like a redesigned loadout screen. The Civilian class supports teammates but is weaponless, mainly designed for VIP mode. New weapons bring fresh tactical options, and the absence of microtransactions contrasts with modern live service models, making the mod appealing for both returning and new players. Server overcrowding at launch indicates strong community interest, although it may affect immediate accessibility.

Sword Hero Demo Impresses with Melee Combat and Systemic RPG Elements

The demo for *Sword Hero*, an upcoming systemic RPG set on a sci-fi ring world, has garnered praise for delivering some of the best melee combat outside FromSoftware games. Mixing methodical swordplay with a first-person perspective, the combat emphasizes cooldown-managed directional attacks and parrying, requiring strategic timing and positioning.

The demo features a ranked tournament mode with 35 fights and roguelike progression on randomized arenas, unlocking better gear as players advance. Additionally, emergent gameplay elements like environmental hazards, explosive traps, and dismemberment add depth to encounters. While melee combat shines, other playstyles such as magic and archery appear less compelling so far but remain under development. The Kickstarter campaign behind the game has exceeded funding goals, with a full alpha expected by April 2026 and a planned release in late 2027.

Roblox Adjusts Avatar Face Changes Amid User Backlash

Roblox Corporation announced plans to replace all "Classic" avatar faces and heads with new Dynamic Heads starting February 2026, aiming to unify the avatar system and enable new features. This change removes classic face options from the marketplace, sparking criticism from players unhappy with the new appearances or concerned about losing previously purchased items.

In response to user feedback, Roblox introduced some concessions: users will be able to disable facial animations on Dynamic Faces, and creators will have tools to produce more visually similar heads to the classic ones. Limited Heads with transaction histories will remain unchanged. Despite these measures, some community members feel the changes do not fully preserve the look and feel of the classic faces, leaving debate about the impact ongoing.
