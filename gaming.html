Game Developers Use Hidden Systems to Balance Randomness

A recent talk at GDC revealed that the superhero comedy game Dispatch employs behind-the-scenes adjustments to randomness, a method familiar to XCOM players. The developers at Adhoc shared that any success chance over 76% is guaranteed, and low chances (1-14%) are boosted to 15%. This is done to prevent frustrating "bad luck" moments that players often perceive as unfair. However, after a player benefits three times from these boosts, the game temporarily returns to true odds to maintain challenge. These invisible aids are disabled during the game’s final episode to increase difficulty. This approach reflects ongoing efforts by developers to make probability feel more just to players, illustrating how game design often balances randomness with player experience.

s&box Pricing Expected Around Garry's Mod’s Range

Garry Newman, creator of Garry's Mod, disclosed that his upcoming game creation platform s&box will likely be priced similarly to Garry’s Mod, which launched at $10 in 2006. Although the Facepunch Studios team hasn’t finalized a price, estimates suggest it will fall between $10 and $20. s&box, set for an April release, aims to carry forward the legacy of Garry’s Mod by offering a physics sandbox with creative game-making tools. The pricing approach indicates an intent to keep the platform accessible to a broad user base while reflecting its expanded capabilities.

Crabmeat Merges Point-and-Click and Horror in a Sea Setting

Crabmeat is a new horror point-and-click game where players catch Southern King Crabs while serving a sentence as a penal colony worker. Set in an Antarctic ocean filled with crabs and dangerous creatures, players must meet catch quotas or face fatal consequences. The game combines survival horror with fishing mechanics and uses mouse controls innovatively. It is available now on Steam with a launch discount and includes a demo. Crabmeat offers a different take on horror by leveraging the unsettling qualities of sea creatures in an atmospheric, survival-based experience.

Pokémon Streamer’s Roguelite Monster Game Kickstarter Surpasses Expectations

Wonderful Neoran Valley, a roguelite monster-collecting game with influences like Slay the Spire and Inscryption, has quickly exceeded its Kickstarter goal, raising over $800,000 with 18 days remaining. Unlike traditional monster games, it requires players to convince creatures to join their party through dialogue rather than capture mechanics. Developed by Nara Studio, the campaign’s success is partially attributed to the involvement of popular Italian Pokémon streamer Cydonia as creative director. Stretch goals unlocked include new game modes, a Switch 2 port, and expanded content, supporting the studio’s aim to remain independent.

Brendan Greene on AI Content’s Impact and the Future of Computing

PlayerUnknown’s Brendan Greene expressed concerns about AI-generated content degrading internet quality. He criticized large language models (LLMs) for creating a feedback loop where AI scans low-quality AI content, which then becomes accepted as truth. Greene argued that this cycle is problematic and that scaling data centers is not a solution to achieving true artificial intelligence. Instead, he advocates for more local computing power to reduce reliance on centralized servers. Greene distinguishes between useful, domain-specific machine learning applications and less reliable generative AI, warning against overestimating current technology's capabilities.

Marathon's Battle Pass to Add More Skins Following Player Feedback

Bungie announced plans to expand the selection of character shell skins in Marathon’s first season battle pass after players expressed dissatisfaction. The battle pass currently costs $10 and includes mostly gun skins, with only one character skin. Starting mid-April, three new premium shell skins and additional free track rewards will be added, along with new Arachne-themed skins unlockable through challenges. Bungie also increased the amount of in-game currency included in a related bundle. While this addresses some player concerns, the lack of premium currency rewards in the battle pass is yet to be resolved.
