**League of Legends MMO Development Advances with New Leadership**

Riot Games has made a notable addition to its long-anticipated League of Legends MMO project by hiring Raymond Bartos, a former lead producer on World of Warcraft. The MMO has faced a lengthy and uncertain development period since its announcement over five years ago, including leadership changes and a major development reset two years ago. Bartos expressed enthusiasm about joining Riot and working alongside colleagues with WoW experience, signaling a renewed commitment to the project. However, an official release date remains uncertain, with the most recent estimate humorously described as "before we go to Mars." Riot is also focusing on a reported major overhaul of League of Legends itself, planned for 2027, to improve accessibility for new players.

**XGIMI MoGo 4 Laser Projector Balances Portability and Versatility**

The XGIMI MoGo 4 Laser is a portable projector that combines projection, audio, and ambient lighting features in a compact unit priced below $800. It offers 1080p resolution with 550 ISO Lumens brightness and includes magnetic filters for light shows, a rotatable body to switch between projector and audio modes, and Harman Kardon speakers. Battery life varies between 2.5 hours for projection and nearly 6 hours for audio-only use without lighting effects. While it supports gaming with around 20 ms latency, it is best suited for darker environments due to brightness limitations. The projector's adaptability and relatively affordable price position it as a flexible option for users seeking mobility and multifunctionality, though it is less ideal for bright rooms or professional gaming.

The game combines jumping, air-dashing, sliding, grappling with a tongue, and a climbing system reminiscent of Breath of the Wild, encouraging momentum-based gameplay. Players can equip trinkets that modify abilities and engage with simple puzzles and exploration.
