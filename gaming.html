**Fallout Co-Creator Discusses Non-Linear Game Design Philosophy** Tim Cain, co-creator of the Fallout series, shared his views on non-linear game design in a recent video. Cain emphasized the importance of not assuming a fixed order for player actions, illustrating this with an example involving an NPC guard who players might bypass or eliminate rather than interact with as intended. His core guideline urges level designers to provide open, flexible playgrounds for players rather than enforcing strict sequences. Cain also reflected on the value of understanding the purpose behind game mechanics and story elements to avoid aimless design. His insights reinforce the evolving mindset in game development that prioritizes player agency and organic exploration.

**Fallout: New Vegas Setting Could Have Been New Orleans** Chris Avellone, writer of Fallout: New Vegas and former creative officer at Obsidian, revealed in an interview that New Orleans was once considered as a potential setting for the game. Avellone cited the city's unique vibe and referenced a post-apocalyptic comic set there as inspiring factors. Though Bethesda reportedly restricted certain locations during Fallout pitches, including San Francisco, Avellone found New Orleans' flavor appealing and would have liked to explore that idea further. He also commented on Bethesda’s use of critical reception in decisions against continuing work with Obsidian on Fallout projects. This sheds light on development dynamics and the creative directions considered behind one of the series' most acclaimed entries.

**International Space Station Upgrades with High-End Laptops** The astronauts aboard the International Space Station are receiving new custom laptops featuring impressive specifications uncommon in typical consumer hardware. These HP ZBook G9 Fury machines include Intel Core Ultra 9 processors, Nvidia RTX Pro Blackwell GPUs, and 128 GB of ECC DDR5 RAM designed for data integrity in demanding environments. They have undergone extensive radiation and durability testing suitable for space conditions. The upgrade reflects the high-performance computing needs in space missions, contrasting with terrestrial users currently facing challenges such as high RAM prices. The enhanced hardware supports essential scientific and operational tasks aboard the ISS.

**Security Concerns as Backdoors Found in WordPress Plugins** A security issue emerged involving 31 WordPress plugins bought by a new owner who inserted backdoors into each. The discovery followed unusual behavior from the widely used Countdown Timer Ultimate plugin. The WordPress plugins team removed the affected plugins to limit the impact. The incident highlights risks associated with plugin ownership transfers, as there are currently no automated mechanisms for notifying users or performing in-depth reviews after such changes. Experts warn this type of supply chain attack is not isolated, emphasizing the need for vigilance in plugin management for website security.

**PC Gamer Survey Reveals Where Gamers Keep Their PCs** A recent PC Gamer survey asked readers where their gaming PCs are located. Only 37% kept their PC on their desk beside them, the "correct" and most practical answer. A substantial 34% reported placing theirs on the floor, exposing hardware to dust and potential damage. Other placements included attached stages on desks, next to TVs, or tucked away in cupboards. Interestingly, 1% reported having their PC bolted to the wall. The survey reflects a variety of setups among gamers and underlines differing priorities or constraints in PC placement related to space, aesthetics, or convenience.

**Discussion Around Pragmata’s Path Tracing Versus Ray Tracing Graphics** Following the release of Capcom’s sci-fi title Pragmata, some gamers debated the visual differences and performance trade-offs between the game’s path tracing and ray tracing options. Path tracing offers more realistic lighting and shadows but demands significantly more GPU power. The discussion includes speculative claims about potential motives behind emphasizing path tracing. Firsthand comparisons indicate that path tracing produces sharper shadows and better depth cues, though ray tracing still delivers impressive visuals at a higher framerate. The debate underscores ongoing tensions between graphical fidelity and performance in modern gaming.

**Additional Note: Exit of Kiki Wolfkill from Microsoft** Kiki Wolfkill, a veteran of nearly 30 years at Microsoft and influential figure behind many Xbox and Halo projects as well as Xbox Film and TV, has departed from the company. Wolfkill expressed gratitude for her time and hinted at future endeavors beyond Microsoft. Her departure marks the end of a significant era at the intersection of gaming and entertainment within Xbox’s operations.
