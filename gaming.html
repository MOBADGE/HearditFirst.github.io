**Windrose Developers Seek ISP Expertise to Address Co-op Connectivity Issues**

Windrose, a PvE co-op survival game currently in early access, has reached nearly 100,000 peak concurrent players on Steam but is experiencing notable online connectivity problems, particularly with co-op multiplayer. Developer Kraken Express posted in the game’s Discord asking if any players have contacts at major ISPs in Europe or North America to help diagnose and resolve these issues. Players have reported multi-second lag spikes and hangs even over short distances, and the dedicated server tools lack typical features such as specific port forwarding options. Additionally, there is some player concern over server requests routing through a Russian IP address, though Kraken Express clarified they are investigating backend service restrictions or ISP blocklisting causing connectivity failures. The developer is actively working on hotfixes but appears to be seeking external technical assistance to understand the full complexity of the problem. This ongoing situation highlights the challenges independent studios can face when handling large-scale online infrastructure.

**Take-Two’s Share Price Rise Following Rockstar Data Leak Not Indicative of Sustained Growth**

After data stolen from Rockstar Games was leaked earlier this month, Take-Two Interactive’s share price rose from around $201 to over $214 within a few days. While this might appear as a positive market reaction, analysts caution it should not be overinterpreted. The leaked data revealed limited confidential information and no substantial details about the much-anticipated GTA 6. Year-to-date, Take-Two’s stock remains down more than 16% from its January highs. The temporary bump may reflect investor reminders of Rockstar’s lucrative GTA Online revenue, but broader factors, such as competition, market trends, and upcoming earnings reports expected in May, remain key drivers of the stock’s performance. Take-Two’s financial results will be closely watched as they could influence the longer-term investor outlook on the company and its major franchises.

**Four Upcoming Indie Mecha Games to Watch on Steam**

With no immediate FromSoft mecha game sequel in sight after 2023’s Armored Core 6, several independent developers are working on Armored Core-inspired titles aimed at mecha enthusiasts. Among those gaining attention are: - *Ironwing Valiant: Record of Astera*, which combines classic mech combat with vibrant environments and party-based mechanics reminiscent of Valkyria Chronicles. - *Code Rapid*, featuring fast-paced, stylized mecha action with fluid combat movements and character action elements. - *Rig Riot*, a roguelike action game blending traditional mecha gameplay with procedurally generated runs and a modular customization system, aiming for a May 2026 release. - *Omega Phenex Commenced Project Six*, emphasizing high-speed mech combat inspired by Armored Core: For Answer, targeting release in 2027.

These games offer alternative options for fans while awaiting new major studio releases and showcase the diverse approaches in indie mecha game development.

**Windrose Dedicated Servers Lack Admin Commands but Allow Basic Configuration**

While Windrose players seeking custom servers for persistent pirate adventures would expect robust admin controls, the current early access dedicated server implementation lacks in-game admin commands entirely. Hosting your own server on Windows is possible, but users have reported crashes and high resource usage. Server configuration is limited to editing JSON files, where hosts can set server name, password protection, max player count, and world difficulty settings such as enemy health and damage multipliers. The absence of typical server management features like player kicking or live restarts constrains the multiplayer experience. Developers are likely to expand these tools over time, but the present limitations underscore challenges in providing smooth, player-run multiplayer environments during early access.

**Krafton Still Supporting Subnautica 2 Early Access Despite Publisher Credit Removal**

Subnautica 2’s Steam page no longer lists PUBG publisher Krafton, instead showing Unknown Worlds as both developer and publisher. The two companies maintain a complex relationship after Krafton’s 2021 acquisition of Unknown Worlds and a recent legal dispute involving the firing and reinstatement of Unknown Worlds’ CEO, Ted Gill. Although Krafton has removed its name from storefront listings—spurring speculation of a split—a Krafton representative confirmed ongoing support for Subnautica 2’s early access launch. The game’s release date remains unclear amidst these corporate tensions, with a court-ordered deadline for September 15 tied to contractual bonuses. Subnautica 2 remains highly anticipated, holding the top spot on Steam wishlists.
