Baldur's Gate Mod Manager Receives Major Update

The EET Mod Forge, a web-based mod management tool for the original Baldur's Gate games, has undergone a significant 2.0 update. It helps users manage mod compatibility and prevent game-breaking conflicts by providing warnings and detailed install orders. This tool supports 640 mods and now includes a preview feature for NPC portraits when multiple mods compete for the same image, as well as tracking in-engine limits such as kits. The update also introduces a new large preset mod list called "Mod Forge Ultimate," which combines 475 mods with detailed conflict resolution. This overhaul aims to simplify the modding experience for newcomers and veterans alike, highlighting the ongoing vitality of the Baldur’s Gate modding community.

Bethesda Reflects on DLC Pop-up Notifications in Classic RPGs

Bethesda veteran Istvan Pely spoke about lessons learned from DLC notification systems in older Bethesda RPGs, such as Fallout 3’s Game of the Year Edition. Originally, players were greeted with numerous pop-up messages about DLC as soon as they loaded their save, which was often overwhelming when replaying the game in its expanded form. Pely noted that Bethesda has improved this approach in recent years. Regarding Starfield’s upcoming Terran Armada story DLC, Bethesda plans a more seamless integration where players discover the new content naturally without intrusive notifications. The DLC will launch alongside the Free Lanes update on April 7. This change reflects Bethesda’s effort to improve user experience by reducing intrusive messaging.

Resident Evil Requiem Mod Lets You Drive a Hamburger Instead of a Porsche

A new mod for Resident Evil Requiem replaces the in-game product-placement Porsche Cayenne Turbo GT with a drivable hamburger vehicle inspired by SpongeBob SquarePants. Created by modder SinderyMonk, the "Patty Wagon" mod adds a humorous alternative to the original SUV, changing the visual tone of the game’s driving sections. This mod is part of a broader trend of playful and meme-inspired mods released soon after the game's launch, including character swaps and enemy replacements. To use these, players must install the REFramework tool first. This variety of mods illustrates the community’s creativity in adding both practical and comedic content to the game.

Elden Ring Cut NPC Quest Restored by Modder

A modder named Transquility has restored the cut NPC quest of Merchant Kalé from Elden Ring. The quest, removed from the final game, tied Kalé closely to the game’s hidden Frenzied Flame ending, providing emotional context to this darker narrative path. Datamined voice lines revealed Kalé’s transformation from a friendly merchant to a figure consumed by rage and despair over the fate of his people, encouraging players to pursue the Frenzied Flame ending with greater narrative justification. The mod brings the quest back into a playable state, allowing players to experience this expanded storyline. This restoration adds depth to one of Elden Ring’s lesser-known narrative threads, potentially enriching player understanding of the game’s complex lore.

Minecraft Announces New Underground Biome and Dungeons Sequel

Mojang revealed details of Minecraft’s second update drop for 2026, introducing a new underground biome called the Sulfur Caves. This biome features poisonous pools that cause a dizzying effect, along with new blocks such as cinnabar and sulfur, offering fresh visual and gameplay experiences for cave explorers. Inspired by real-world sulfur hot springs, the biome adds environmental hazards and vibrant colors to the underground. The update also includes a new mob, the Sulfur Cube, which can absorb blocks and change its properties dynamically depending on what it consumes. Separately, a sequel to Minecraft Dungeons was announced during the same event, featuring a cinematic teaser but no gameplay footage yet. Scheduled for release later this year, it represents continued investment in Minecraft’s action-RPG spinoff despite a mixed reception for related Minecraft titles.
