Nvidia Reportedly Ends GPU Pricing Incentive, Signaling Rising Graphics Card Costs

According to hardware YouTuber Der8auer, this move comes amid a global memory shortage and looming tariff threats. Without the scheme, Nvidia cards' prices are expected to rise significantly, with MSRP effectively becoming irrelevant.

The suspension also follows Nvidia ending its bundle system last November, which involved AIBs buying GPU dies together with memory chips. This change, combined with dwindling supply of certain mid-range cards like the RTX 5070 Ti in favor of pricier models like the RTX 5080, hints at tighter market conditions. Estimates suggest some GPU prices, particularly the RTX 5080, could increase by 40-50%. This development is important as it points to harder times ahead for consumers seeking GPUs at reasonable prices.

Intel Secures Memory Supply for Lunar Lake Chips Amid Ongoing Shortage

Intel reports it has adequately stocked up on necessary memory components for manufacturing its Lunar Lake processors, despite the ongoing global memory shortage that has disrupted the supply chain and increased costs industry-wide. The company's CFO credited an aggressive early procurement strategy for this preparedness.

Lunar Lake uniquely integrates LPDDR5X memory chips directly on-package, a design that improves performance but raises production complexity and costs. Intel had confirmed this approach is a one-time occurrence, moving future chip generations like Panther Lake to traditional off-package memory configurations. This memory management is crucial as shortages continue impacting PC hardware availability and pricing.
