Valheim Devs Release New Co-op Dungeon-Crawler Demo

Three developers from Valheim have created a new first-person co-op dungeon crawler called Begraved, currently available as a free demo on Steam. This project is being developed independently by a newly formed studio, Grip Jaw, with the team working on it during evenings and weekends.

Begraved focuses on exploring procedurally generated crypts filled with traps and supernatural threats, supporting up to five players with proximity voice chat. It emphasizes careful item management and a tense atmosphere where players must balance looting with survival. While still early in development, the game adopts a medieval dark fantasy setting and promises a focused, multiplayer dungeon experience. This side project highlights ongoing creative efforts by veteran developers beyond their main titles.

Discord Age Verification Changes Coming in March

Discord will begin enforcing a new age verification process worldwide starting March 2026, reclassifying all users as "teen accounts" by default until verification is completed. This new system imposes restrictions on mature content, access to age-restricted servers, and communication settings, affecting both public and private chats.

To lift these limits, users must verify their age by either submitting facial scans for on-device age estimation or uploading government-issued ID alongside a matching selfie. The move aims to create a safer environment but has raised privacy concerns due to the sensitive nature of the data involved and recent breaches at third-party verification providers. Users unwilling or unable to verify will be limited in content and interactions. Discord also plans to use behavioral analysis to infer user age in some cases. This represents a significant change in how the platform manages user access and safety features.
