Owlcat’s Romance Writing Challenges in Pathfinder RPGs

Owlcat Games, known for its CRPGs like Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous, recently shared insights into their approach to writing romance options in their games. The studio revealed an unusual creative challenge early in development: all three of their female romanceable characters were cannibals. This led to adjustments in character portrayals to balance the unusual traits with player expectations. For example, Arueshalae, a succubus character, was given new dialogue to highlight her atypical distaste for human flesh, softening her edge somewhat. Owlcat also noted the complexity of polyamorous romance options, which they have experimented with before but found too demanding to include in future titles. This discussion sheds light on the narrative complexities and development considerations behind companion romances in role-playing games.

Australian Government Asks Valve About Extremist Communities on Steam

The Australian eSafety Commissioner has issued a transparency notice to Valve, demanding information on how the company is addressing the presence of extreme-right communities on Steam. Valve faces penalties of up to AU$825,000 daily if it does not respond. This notice comes amid broader scrutiny of several gaming platforms—including Roblox, Microsoft (Minecraft), and Epic Games (Fortnite)—regarding grooming, radicalization, and extremist content within their communities. The Commissioner cited reports of games and content that glorify terrorist themes and violent events. Valve’s handling of such issues has been under criticism for years, with prior US governmental inquiries highlighting concerns about Steam’s safety for younger users. The Australian government’s move reflects ongoing tensions about platform responsibilities for community safety and content moderation.

Framework Laptop Emphasizes User Ownership and Upgradability

Framework, a laptop manufacturer focused on modular and upgradeable PCs, introduced its new Laptop 13 Pro chassis that remains compatible with previous components. Founder Nirav Patel criticized industry trends favoring cloud computing and leased hardware, instead highlighting the value of owning personal hardware that users can maintain and upgrade themselves. Despite the growing tech industry focus on AI and cloud services, Framework’s approach supports long-term usability and reduction of electronic waste through forward- and backward-compatible parts. The company's emphasis on flexibility and support for Linux also stands out amid increasing software and hardware consolidation. Framework’s philosophy offers a counterpoint to dominant industry narratives prioritizing cloud dependency and sealed, non-upgradeable devices.

Masters of Albion Early Access Launch Details

Peter Molyneux’s new god game, Masters of Albion, is launching in early access on April 22 at 1 pm EDT / 6 pm BST. The launch aims to provide players worldwide access times adjusted by region, from morning in the U.S. West Coast to early morning hours in Australia and New Zealand the following day. Unlike many large releases, no preloading appears to be offered, but the game’s modest install size of about 20 GB should keep download times reasonable. As a return to Molyneux’s signature genre, the launch is a significant moment for fans interested in the evolution of god games and the developer's latest vision.

Meta to Monitor Employee PC Activity for AI Training

Meta has announced plans to monitor the keystrokes, mouse movements, clicks, and screenshots on U.S.-based employee work computers to collect data for AI training. The initiative aims to gather real-world examples of user interactions to help automate tasks but excludes sensitive content and is not intended for performance evaluation. This move comes as part of Meta’s larger AI expansion, including the recent introduction of its Muse Spark AI model, and precedes significant layoffs planned in May. The surveillance plan has drawn concern over employee privacy and workplace morale, amid reports of dissatisfaction within the company. The initiative currently targets U.S. employees due to stricter European privacy laws.

Revival of Extraction Shooter "Marauders" After Extended Silence

Marauders, an extraction shooter released in early access in 2022, has unexpectedly announced a major overhaul after more than 20 months without updates. Small Impact Games, the developer, detailed that the team has been working on significant system reworks rather than smaller patches, aiming to bring the game “where it should be.” This update contrasts past statements from the publisher Team17 suggesting that development had ceased.
