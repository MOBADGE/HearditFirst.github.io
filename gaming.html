GTA Chain Game: An Ongoing Global Community Anticipates GTA 6

The GTA Chain Game, a community-driven relay where players sequentially progress through a single save file to complete each Grand Theft Auto title 100%, continues to thrive in 2026. Originally a simple save-passing exercise, it has grown into an international initiative involving hundreds of players who share the experience across multiple classic GTA games. GTAKid, the lead coordinator, highlights that the project now functions as a close-knit community that hosts online events and supports players of varying skill levels.

The group recently shifted focus from GTA 3 to GTA 4 and its Episodes, enjoying the increased complexity and variety those titles offer. With GTA 6's release planned for later this year, the Chain Game community is preparing for its biggest challenge yet by considering new formats for the sequel. The core principle remains the same: players take turns without heavy commitment, allowing casual and veteran gamers alike to engage. Managing save file compatibility amid game updates remains a technical hurdle, but solutions exist thanks to dedicated community members.

This enduring project illustrates how fan communities can sustain interest in aging games while building lasting social connections. The GTA Chain Game’s official hub on GTAForums offers details and invites new participants.

Deltarune Chapter 5 Nears Testing Phase as Team Moves On

Toby Fox provided an update on the development of Deltarune’s fifth chapter, noting that professional testers are scheduled to start formal testing on April 1st, with Japanese translation quality assurance expected to finish the same month. Beyond fixing bugs and polishing translation, most of the development team has already moved on from Chapter 5.

This marks a significant step toward the chapter’s eventual release, though a firm date is not yet set. Fox stated that testing will last an indefinite period and additional preparations, including console approvals, remain before launch. Given the lengthy intervals between prior chapters, fans might still wait several months or more.

Once released, Chapter 5 will continue the episodic rollout of Deltarune, whose earlier chapters helped it reach strong sales and enduring popularity since 2018. The number of eventual chapters remains uncertain, with seven often speculated. Players can currently try the first two chapters free on Steam.

Steam Sale Highlights Human Artist Contributions Amid AI Concerns

Steam has received praise for prominently crediting human artists behind its latest spring sale graphics. The artwork, created by Tiffany Diep and animator Thanhuki, contrasts with recent industry debates on AI-generated content. Steam’s public shout-out has been welcomed by the community as a reaffirmation of traditional artistry in digital storefront presentations.

Steam confirmed that Diep oversees all 2026 seasonal sales banners and point shop assets, reinforcing the personal craftsmanship behind the sale visuals. This has sparked positive reactions on social media, where users expressed relief at the presence of handcrafted art amid widespread AI usage in gaming.

The acknowledgment serves as a subtle statement given competitors like GOG recently faced criticism over AI-generated sale banners, illustrating the sensitivity surrounding AI ethics in creative content.

World of Warcraft’s New Class Balancing Echoes Final Fantasy 14 Challenges

World of Warcraft’s recent class rebalancing for its Midnight expansion, which simplified rotation complexity, is drawing parallels to longstanding issues in Final Fantasy 14’s combat design. The two-minute “burst window” meta, where all jobs have synchronized damage spikes, has led to periods of inactivity outside these bursts.

In WoW, players have noted that several classes now feel weak for extended stretches between large cooldowns, especially during solo world content, which contrasts with the brief, intense bursts of damage. This pattern can reduce gameplay variety and complicate encounter design across MMO content.

While Blizzard appears more responsive to player feedback than Square Enix, finding a balance between maintaining interesting combat and accommodating multiple player types remains challenging. The discussion highlights ongoing tensions in MMO combat design regarding class differentiation and engagement outside of coordinated raid scenarios.
