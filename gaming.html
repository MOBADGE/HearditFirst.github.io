Rockstar Hackers Release Stolen Data, Offering Limited Insights

The hacker group ShinyHunters, who breached Rockstar Games last week, has publicly released the stolen data a day before their ransom deadline. Examination of the data shows it mainly consists of Rockstar’s sales and financial information, rather than significant spoilers or details about the highly anticipated Grand Theft Auto 6. Notably, the data reveals GTA Online’s massive revenue — nearly $500 million annually — vastly outperforming Red Dead Online, which pulls in about $26 million yearly. Platform-wise, PlayStation 5 leads in activity and earnings, with PC trailing far behind. The hackers’ attempt to extort $200,000 appears to have failed, leaving them exposed without payment or sensitive content. Rockstar has declined to comment further.

Xbox Game Pass Subscription Seen as Too Expensive by New Leadership

In an internal memo leaked to the press, Xbox boss Asha Sharma acknowledged that the current Game Pass subscription pricing is considered “too expensive” by players. The memo hinted at reworking the subscription model to offer better value. Since the latest price hikes six months ago, the Ultimate tier now costs $29.99 monthly, up 50 percent from $19.99, while the PC tier jumped to $16.49 from $11.99. The growing complexity of tier options and what games are accessible at launch has made the service less straightforward. Game Pass’s financial impact has also drawn criticism from developers, who say the platform reduces retail sales and can discourage paid purchases. Under Sharma’s leadership, Xbox intends to focus more strongly on its hardware business alongside evolving subscription offerings.

Nels Anderson Perseveres After City Builder’s Struggling Early Access Launch

Nels Anderson, known for Firewatch and Mark of the Ninja, spent seven years developing his solarpunk city builder Generation Exile. Despite thousands of wishlists and positive early press, the game sold fewer than 300 copies after its early access debut. Anderson attributes the disappointing sales partly to early access fatigue and the game’s unique but hard-to-classify nature. Despite this, his studio Sonderlust continued development, releasing two final story chapters and improving game systems. Anderson reflects on the industry’s increasing pressure for fast success, warning that focusing on short-term growth can undermine long-term creativity and sustainability—key themes of Generation Exile itself, which challenges players to restore balance to a failing generation ship.

Marathon Introduces “Free Kit Frenzy” Experimental Mode

The multiplayer shooter Marathon is launching a two-week experimental playlist called “Free Kit Frenzy” starting April 15. In this mode, all players begin with a free sponsored kit instead of purchasing loadouts, creating a “zero to hero” experience that tests how matches unfold with equal low-level gear. The test is limited to the Dire Marsh map and aims to gather data on gameplay balance and potential technical issues. This approach contrasts Marathon’s usual ranked and cryo archive modes, which require a minimum gear value. The experimental mode coincides with an upcoming game balance patch targeting underpowered weapons.

91-Year-Old Gamer Mistakenly Checks In Missing, Police Find Her Playing Video Games

Police in Westlake, Ohio, responded to a welfare check after a 91-year-old woman missed her daily “Are You Okay?” phone call. Upon entering her house, they discovered she was safe and engrossed in playing video games, trying to beat her record level. The incident ended on a light note, with officers and the community amused. The story sheds light on the growing diversity of gaming demographics and challenges stereotypes about older adults and gaming habits. While the report omitted which game she was playing, it highlights how video games continue to engage players across all ages.

Pickmon Rebrands to Pickmos Amid Controversy Over Knockoff Designs

The creature-collecting survival game initially known as Pickmon has changed its name to Pickmos. The rebrand involves only a single letter but is said to better reflect the game’s lore and brand identity. Pickmon attracted attention for its strikingly derivative art and designs resembling Pokémon, Final Fantasy, Zelda, Overwatch, and Palworld characters. The name change may also be a preemptive effort to avoid legal issues given Nintendo’s active protection of its intellectual property. Public reaction to the rebrand has been largely skeptical, with many questioning the originality of the game’s designs. The publisher has promised to review community concerns over art similarities.
