Wordless Forest: A Unique FMV Survival Game Born from Real Wilderness Experience

Solo developer Dogus Cagrici has created *Wordless Forest*, a full-motion video (FMV) survival game that stands out for its authenticity. Unlike typical survival games with fast crafting systems, this title features footage recorded by Cagrici himself during 40 days of hiking alone through remote forests in Turkey. The game is entirely dialogue-free, relying on visual storytelling to immerse players in survival scenarios such as drinking from puddles, eating raw fish, and facing life-threatening choices. The difficulty includes realistic dangers like falls and drowning, and the absence of dialogue emphasizes observation and decision-making. Scheduled for release on Steam in August, *Wordless Forest* may be among the first FMV games focused purely on survival rather than horror, offering a different take on the genre.

New Turkish Legislation Imposes Stricter Controls on Gaming Platforms

Turkey’s parliament has approved a bill tightening government oversight of gaming platforms such as Steam and Epic Games Store. Platforms with over 100,000 daily users must appoint local representatives who respond promptly to government data and content removal requests. Non-compliance can result in substantial fines and restricted access speeds. Although the law stops short of banning platforms outright, it puts pressure on them to conform to new regulatory demands within six months of enactment. The legislation is part of a broader clampdown focused mainly on social media companies, which face harsher restrictions including bans on services for users under 15 and obligations to remove content related to national security within one hour. Critics note the law's broad definitions could lead to censorship under the guise of protecting children.

Assassin’s Creed Black Flag Resynced: A Remake by Ubisoft Singapore

Ubisoft has officially announced *Assassin’s Creed Black Flag Resynced*, a full remake of the 2013 pirate adventure slated for July 9, 2026, on PC, PS5, and Xbox Series X|S. Developed by Ubisoft Singapore—the same studio behind the multiplayer pirate game *Skull and Bones*—the remake rebuilds the game from the ground up using the latest Ubisoft Anvil Engine. Improvements include updated combat and stealth mechanics, new story scenes, additional recruitable crew officers, and enhanced modern-day segments. Notably, the remake will omit multiplayer modes and DLC content from the original. The project reunites many original developers and aims to provide a refreshed action-adventure experience while maintaining the core narrative and world map.

Diablo 4: Lord of Hatred Expansion Enhances Necromancer and Druid Classes

Blizzard’s upcoming *Diablo 4: Lord of Hatred* expansion, releasing April 27 (or April 28 in some regions), brings significant skill tree updates to all seven classes, with the most notable changes affecting Necromancers and Druids. Druids now have improved flexibility, allowing players to decouple skills from specific shapeshifting forms and customize transformations freely. Necromancers receive expanded minion control, capable of commanding up to 28 skeletons with new skill mechanics that manage summons more strategically. These changes simplify combat and open up new build possibilities by reducing the need for constant minion resummoning. The expansion’s updates aim to enrich gameplay variety and tactical depth for players favoring these classes.

Little Tree Kingdom: A Roguelike with a Deceptively Cute Facade

*Little Tree Kingdom* initially presents itself as a charming city builder where players help gnomes develop a tree-based town through card-driven mechanics. However, the game evolves into a challenging roguelike with resource scarcity and hazards such as ghost attacks. Players must carefully manage limited card uses while balancing community welfare metrics like happiness and security. Defending the town involves building tactical defenses with restricted effective zones, requiring thoughtful layout planning. The game offers a unique twist by inverting typical city builder progression; it challenges players to survive with diminishing resources rather than expand endlessly. A free demo is available on Steam for interested players to explore this blend of genres.
