Obsidian's Josh Sawyer on RPG Difficulty Design

Obsidian studio design director Josh Sawyer recently discussed his approach to game difficulty in a video. Instead of focusing on fixed challenge levels, Sawyer considers three RPG player archetypes based on what players want from the game:

- **Explorer:** Players with limited time who prioritize story and a smooth experience without excessive retries. - **Adventurer:** Typical RPG players who seek moderate challenge and engagement without diving into complex mechanics. - **Survivalist:** Hardcore players who appreciate deep, immersive, and simulative gameplay systems.

Sawyer noted that while granular difficulty customization is desirable, development practicalities often limit its implementation. He advocates starting with complex mechanics and simplifying them for easier modes, giving players control over what to engage with. This design philosophy shapes Obsidian‚Äôs RPGs, aiming to address diverse player motivations.

Shareholders Sue Hasbro Over Magic: The Gathering Card Printing Practices

Hasbro faces a shareholder lawsuit alleging that executives, including CEO Chris Cocks, breached their fiduciary duties by overproducing Magic: The Gathering cards, thereby devaluing the brand. The suit claims Hasbro misled investors about print volume and demand to maintain short-term revenue.

Bank of America highlighted concerns in 2022 that overprinting was inflating Hasbro's financial results. The lawsuit also accuses Hasbro of manipulating perceived demand for high-profile products like the $1000 30th Anniversary Edition, which sales were briefly paused despite unsold inventory.

This legal challenge underscores ongoing scrutiny of collectible card game management and brand sustainability.
