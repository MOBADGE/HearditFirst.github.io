Man Pleads Guilty to AI-Generated Music Streaming Fraud

Michael Smith, a man from North Carolina, has pleaded guilty to conspiracy to commit wire fraud after generating thousands of AI-created songs and using bots to stream them billions of times. This scheme reportedly netted him more than $8 million in fraudulent royalties. The U.S. attorney’s office described how hundreds of thousands of fake songs were streamed repeatedly by bot accounts, turning tiny royalty payments into millions. Smith’s sentencing is scheduled for July, and he has agreed to forfeit the illicit earnings. This case highlights emerging issues with AI in creative industries, including potential for fraud and ethical concerns around AI-generated content.

Slay the Spire 2 Developer Addresses Backlash Over Early Balance Patch

Mega Crit has responded to strong negative player reactions to the first balance pass for *Slay the Spire 2*. The patch notably nerfed a card called Prepared, changing it from a zero-energy discard-and-draw card to a more costly and complex version. This change sparked thousands of negative Steam reviews, especially from Chinese players. Mega Crit explained on social media that this patch is just the beginning of ongoing balance updates expected over the next one to two years, with no changes being permanent. They encouraged players to share feedback through the in-game reporter on the beta branch, where experimental changes are being tested. The situation underscores the challenges developers face in adjusting popular games post-launch.

Abiotic Factor’s Design Director Shares Gaming Habits and Inspirations

Geoff "Zag" Keene, design director of the co-op survival game *Abiotic Factor*, shared insights into his gaming preferences and background in a recent interview. He revealed he has logged over 1,160 hours in *PUBG*, a game that influenced the extraction shooter genre and helped bond his development team. Keene also highlighted his appreciation for the atmosphere in games like *Arc Raiders*, valuing “vibe” over mere mechanics. Interestingly, he uses the classic *Warcraft 2* as a way to de-stress, likening it to a form of informal therapy thanks to its nostalgic comfort. Keene emphasized the social aspect of gaming, often playing co-op games with friends and enjoying watching others play.

Dragon Quest X MMO Introduces AI-Powered Slime Companion

Square Enix is collaborating with Google to introduce an AI-powered companion named Chatty Slimey to its largely Japan-exclusive MMO, *Dragon Quest X*. This conversational AI will engage with players using voice-generated chat and can initiate interactions based on in-game events such as defeating powerful enemies or acquiring rare items. A beta test is currently open for applications. Developers hope this feature will help new players feel less isolated in the game world. However, the use of AI chatbots in MMOs remains controversial due to past issues with manipulation and appropriateness of AI interaction in roleplaying contexts.

Pearl Abyss Apologizes for Use of AI-Generated Art in Crimson Desert

Developers of *Crimson Desert*, Pearl Abyss, have issued a public apology after players discovered AI-generated art assets within the game. The company explained that some 2D visual props were created using generative AI tools during early development stages as placeholders to explore style and atmosphere. They stated their original plan was to replace these assets with hand-crafted art aligning with quality standards before release. Acknowledging the lack of transparency, Pearl Abyss committed to auditing all in-game assets and replacing any remaining AI-generated content in future updates. This incident reflects ongoing debates about AI use and disclosure in game art production.

Peglin Adds New Content Nearly Two Years After Launch

*Peglin*, a unique blend of pachinko-style gameplay and roguelike elements, received a significant update adding a fourth act and a final boss encounter. Released in early access in 2022 and reaching version 1.0 in 2024, the game has maintained positive player reception. The new act introduces fresh enemies and optional challenges that last for just the new act, along with additional relics, achievements, and improvements to load times and framerates. The update aims to extend the game's longevity and deepen player progression. A temporary 40% discount on Steam accompanies the launch of this content.
