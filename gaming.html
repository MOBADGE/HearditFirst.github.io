Crimson Desert Puzzle Guides: Trembling Woods, Spire of Insight, and Duskwood Hill Ruins

Several detailed puzzle solutions have been shared for *Crimson Desert*, tackling some of the game's more challenging Mysterious Energy trials. The **Trembling Woods puzzle** requires aligning a mural and its rings using three turnstiles, each controlling different puzzle layers. Notably, the leftmost turnstile lacks handles, so players must use a stabbing attack to move it. Successfully completing this puzzle opens a hidden door granting an Abyss Artifact.

The **Spire of Insight puzzle** involves entering the spire by burning thorny roots with the Blinding Flash skill, then solving three riddles. Each riddle requires collecting specific items and placing them in a stone basin to unlock elevators to higher floors. Completing all three riddles leads to a portal opening to the Abyss, a new area.

Lastly, the **Duskwood Hill Ruins puzzle** has players raising five pillars to the same height by manipulating a dial via stabbing attacks to create handles. Each dial position affects different pillars, and aligning them all opens a prize containing an Abyss Artifact. This one's noted as particularly finicky, often requiring reloading and retrying.

These guides matter for players seeking to progress efficiently, as these puzzles guard valuable rewards and new areas in *Crimson Desert*.

Nintendo Switch 2 Re-release in EU Adds Replaceable Battery

Nintendo will re-release a version of the **Nintendo Switch 2 in the European Union** featuring a replaceable battery. This change is in response to new EU right-to-repair legislation, which mandates that portable electronics be easier to repair by consumers without specialized tools.

The updated Switch 2 model will have removable batteries for the console and Joy-Con controllers. However, this change applies only within the EU; other regions continue with the glued-in battery design. Nintendo recommends users outside the EU avoid self-replacement to prevent damage.

This adaptation signals a broader industry shift prompted by EU policies aiming to reduce electronic waste, prolong device lifespan, and enhance consumer repair options. It may also influence other handheld gaming PC manufacturers to consider similar designs for forthcoming models.

Minecraft Announces Chaos Cubed Update Featuring New Underground Biome and Mob

Mojang has revealed the **Chaos Cubed update** for *Minecraft*, set to introduce a new underground biome called the Sulfur Caves. This biome includes new blocks such as cinnabar and sulfur, adding visual diversity to cave exploration.

Players will encounter pools with a unique gas animation that causes disorienting effects when underwater. The update also introduces a new mob, the Sulfur Cube, which can absorb blocks placed near it and adopt their properties, like sliding on ice or rolling as a wooden ball.

There is no confirmed release date, but a mid-2026 summer launch is expected based on past update schedules. This update expands subterranean gameplay, enriching the survival and exploration experiences players have come to enjoy.

Masters of Albion Offers Nostalgic Gameplay but Raises Questions on Depth

*Masters of Albion*, developed by 22cans and Peter Molyneux, is launching into early access on April 22. The game combines elements from classic Molyneux titles like *Fable*, *Black and White*, and *Dungeon Keeper* into one experience where players manage town-building, city defense, and direct hero control.

The economic system involves designing goods production through combining ingredients with effects on outputs, aiming for depth in crafting. Combat and exploration mimic *Fable*'s style, and city defense includes turret placement and resource management.

However, early observations note that no single system currently matches the depth of its inspirations, and some mechanics feel optional or lightweight. The design aims for player flexibility but might sacrifice the complexity fans expect.

This early look highlights the challenge of blending multiple legacy gameplay systems without diluting their appeal.
