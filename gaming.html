Thermaltake View 380 WS ARGB PC Case Review

Thermaltake's View 380 WS ARGB is a mid-tower PC case that combines high-end aesthetics with a more affordable price point of around $120/£90. It features tempered glass panels with wooden trim and includes four ARGB fans, three of which are reverse fans designed for neat intake airflow without showing their rear sides. Its dual-chamber layout separates storage and power supply components from the main build, supporting motherboards from Mini-ITX to ATX sizes.

The case offers good space for components, including room for radiators up to 360mm on the top or side. Noise levels are low with fans running below 1000 rpm, though they can reach 60 dB at maximum speed, which may be loud. Temperatures during gaming tests remained within standard limits.

However, there are some compromises. The expansion slot covers are not reusable and difficult to remove, which might be a concern for users frequently upgrading hardware. The case also lacks dust filters, possibly leading to more frequent cleaning. Cable management features exist but are less refined compared to other modern cases, requiring extra effort to keep cables tidy.

Overall, the View 380 WS combines style and functionality at a budget-friendly price, though enthusiasts wanting better airflow or easier maintenance might look elsewhere.

Arc Raiders Flashpoint Update Adds New Enemies, Weapons, and Crafting Improvements

The upcoming Flashpoint update for Arc Raiders launches on March 31 and introduces several gameplay changes. The update adds a new aerial enemy, the Vaporizer, armed with a laser that hunts players, as well as Shredders appearing in additional maps beyond their original location.

A new map condition called Close Scrutiny presents high-risk engagements focused around the Arc Assessor, a platform carrying rare loot and part of the new High-Gain Antenna project. Players will need to defend or attack while facing elite enemies.

Two new weapons come with the update: the Dolabra energy shotgun, useful against Arc armor, and the Canto sub-machine gun for close to mid-range combat. Flashpoint also streamlines crafting by allowing missing items to be fulfilled directly via suggested actions in the menu, eliminating the previous need to switch interfaces.

Additionally, the player’s pet, Scrappy, can now be "fed" to boost what resources it brings back, adding depth to resource gathering strategies. The update includes new quests and cosmetic bundles as well.

Doom Running Entirely from DNS Text Records via PowerShell Script

A novel technical feat has been achieved by developer Adam Rice, who constructed a version of the 1993 shareware Doom game that runs entirely from DNS text records accessed through a PowerShell script. The game’s files were divided into 1,964 DNS TXT records hosted on a Cloudflare zone.

The script performs DNS queries to retrieve these records, reconstructs the game data entirely in memory without writing to disk, and launches Doom. Audio was removed to reduce the file size, and the game code was modified to run from memory streams.

This project showcases the flexibility and unconventional uses of the DNS protocol, which was originally designed simply to resolve hostnames to IP addresses. Though running a game like Doom over DNS is impractical for normal play, it highlights the underutilized potential and risks associated with legacy internet protocols.

Crimson Desert Puzzle and Mount Guides

Guides for Crimson Desert were published covering several in-game challenges and rewards:

- The Sanctum of Absolution puzzle involves defeating the Antumbra's Sword boss and placing cores and batteries with the Axiom Force at specific points. Completion allows cleansing the sanctum and accessing a hidden chest with special gear.

- The White Bear mount can be obtained by locating and defeating the bear south of the White Wastes Sanctuary, skins it to collect items, then crafting and using a Sigil of Solidarity from a vendor to unlock the mount, which serves as a combat-focused ride.

- The Silver Fang wolf mount requires significant progress through the Hernand faction quests, including the 'Legendary Wolf' quest. After defeating Silver Fang and collecting its fang and helm, players craft a Sigil of Solidarity to unlock the mount.
