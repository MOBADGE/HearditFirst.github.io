**Gaming on Linux: Mixed Adoption Among PC Gamers**

A recent poll involving over 2,300 PC Gamer readers explored attitudes toward gaming on Linux. Results show that 29% of respondents have already switched to Linux for gaming, with an additional 14% planning to do so within the year, totaling 43% considering or using Linux. Notably, 26% of participants said they do not mind using Windows, a figure somewhat higher than expected given the push for Linux alternatives. However, concerns remain over multiplayer game support on Linux, especially due to restricted access imposed by kernel-level anti-cheat systems. This poll reflects the ongoing debate about Linux as a gaming platform, highlighting moderate but cautious interest among PC gamers in transitioning away from Windows.

**Insights from a Former GameStop Digital Store Architect on Steam’s Success**

Larry Kuperman, a veteran who worked on GameStop’s now-defunct Impulse storefront, offered his perspective on why Valve’s Steam outpaced competitors like EA and Microsoft. He credits Steam’s early adoption of third-party game sales, which was unconventional at the time, and Valve’s focus on building community features such as friends lists, messaging, and playtime notifications to keep users engaged. Kuperman also noted Steam’s accessible platform for indie developers, allowing smaller studios to publish games without needing retailer backing, which helped sustain many during tough market conditions. He praised Good Old Games (GOG) as well, recognizing its role in game preservation and independent developer support. His reflections emphasize that Steam’s combination of retail innovation and community-building contributed to its long-term dominance in PC digital distribution.

**Thick as Thieves: Multiplayer Thief Successor Shifts Focus**

Otherside Entertainment announced a significant change to Thick as Thieves, a multiplayer first-person stealth game led by industry veterans Warren Spector and Paul Neurath. Originally designed as a four-player competitive PvPvE experience, the game will now center on two-player co-op and singleplayer modes. The studio cited that solo and cooperative play were proving more enjoyable during development and aligned better with the core stealth gameplay. While some fans expressed disappointment over the removal of PvPvE at launch, developers indicated it might be introduced later. A new gameplay trailer and additional updates will be showcased at the Triple-I Initiative event on April 9. This shift suggests a move toward a more focused and traditional immersive sim experience, potentially increasing the game’s appeal to fans of the stealth genre.

**Samson’s Development Challenges Shape Its Unique Open-World Crime Structure**

Open-world crime game Samson, launching on April 8, underwent substantial changes mid-development due to financial constraints and team layoffs at Liquid Swords. Originally planned as a larger action RPG with gunplay, the game pivoted to focus on debt management mechanics and hand-to-hand combat, removing guns entirely. This new structure involves players managing daily financial goals under pressure, blending roguelike elements with open-world exploration in the urban setting of Tyndalston. Designer Christofer Sundberg highlighted how these constraints led to a more unique gameplay experience distinct from other titles in the genre. Despite comparisons to Grand Theft Auto, Samson emphasizes strategic evasion and melee combat. The team is launching without crunch, aiming for a stable release while acknowledging that not all envisioned features made it into the final game. Samson represents a story of adaptation within a volatile industry environment.

**Piranha Games Lays Off 30% of Staff Amid Uncertain MechWarrior Future**

Piranha Games, developer of MechWarrior 5: Mercenaries, confirmed laying off approximately 30% of its workforce, totaling 17 employees. This follows earlier layoffs in January 2025 after MechWarrior 5: Clans failed to meet sales expectations despite positive reviews. CEO Russ Bullock stated the company continues to develop upcoming DLC scheduled for May and another MechWarrior product planned for later in the year. However, narrative director Chris Lowrey expressed uncertainty about the franchise’s future, noting that extended post-launch support has been rare for single-purchase games. These layoffs underscore ongoing challenges within the MechWarrior series and the broader difficulties faced by mid-sized studios in maintaining long-term viability in a competitive market.
