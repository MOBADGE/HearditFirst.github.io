AI Startup Tesana Aims to Democratize Game Development

Tesana, a new AI-driven game engine, offers users the ability to create videogames using natural language prompts. By integrating its own engine with third-party AI tools like Claude and asset generators, Tesana can convert text descriptions of game elements into playable code. Early examples display rough, often glitchy versions of familiar game types, but Tesana’s co-founder Johannes Vermandois envisions the tool helping millions prototype and eventually complete full games. He believes this could expand the indie game scene significantly by lowering barriers to game creation. However, some skepticism remains about whether AI-generated games can match the depth and unique vision often found in traditional development. For now, Tesana sees itself as fostering experimentation and rapid iteration among creators.

Slay the Spire 2 Beta Update Introduces End-of-Run Badges and Balancing

Mega Crit’s Slay the Spire 2 beta patch adds end-of-run badges that summarize notable accomplishments or events from each playthrough. These badges aim to highlight what made a run distinct, enhancing player engagement and potentially refining scoring systems. Alongside badges, the update delivers balance changes to cards, enemies, and events, with adjustments to Ascension 6 modifiers and reworks of key cards like Blade of Ink and Borrowed Time. Visual improvements are included, such as new card art and enemy effects. While previous balance changes provoked substantial negative backlash on Steam, this patch has so far received mixed but less severe reactions. The patch remains in beta and can be accessed by opting into the public beta branch on Steam.

Esquire Singapore Publishes AI-Generated ‘Interview’ with One Piece Actor

Esquire Singapore released a feature on Mackenyu Arata, actor in the live-action One Piece series, containing what it calls "an unprecedented interview." However, the piece was generated by AI using previous interviews and artificial dialogue from programs like Claude and Copilot, rather than direct interaction with the actor. Readers and some journalists criticized the article as misleading and inappropriate, given it presents AI-generated responses as an interview without the actor’s involvement. The incident highlights ongoing ethical and transparency challenges surrounding AI content generation in journalism and media.

Homeworld Remastered and Additional RTS Titles Offered in $8 Bundle

The 2K Tactics and Tycoons Humble Bundle currently includes the Homeworld Remastered Collection, its prequel Deserts of Kharak, and eight other real-time strategy games for $8. While Homeworld Remastered and Deserts of Kharak are available at deep discounts individually on Steam, the bundle adds value by including multiple legacy RTS titles such as Railroad Tycoon 2 Platinum and Freedom Force vs. The Third Reich. Though some games in the package are dated, their strong reputation among strategy fans makes the deal a noteworthy opportunity for newcomers and collectors alike. The bundle is available until April 24.

Skyblivion Project Lead Expresses Optimism Amid Development Challenges

The team behind Skyblivion, the ambitious mod recreating The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion within Skyrim’s engine, issued a recent call for additional developers to help with final polishing. Despite concerns fueled by earlier delays and staffing issues, project lead Rebelzize remains cautiously optimistic about meeting the revised 2026 release timeframe. He noted that major content such as quest locations and the Imperial City are complete and undergoing quality assurance. The team acknowledges risks of unforeseen setbacks but emphasizes ongoing progress and encourages patience from the community, highlighting the volunteer nature of the mod’s long development cycle.
