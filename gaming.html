**Infocom’s Failed 1985 Database Software Now Playable on Modern PCs** Infocom, famed for classics like Zork and The Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy, once expanded into business software with Cornerstone, a database program released in 1985. Designed to run on a virtual machine similar to their games’ Z-machine, Cornerstone was slow and struggled to compete as IBM-compatible PCs dominated the business market. This venture diverted resources from game development and contributed to Infocom’s decline, leading to its 1989 closure. Recently, developer TaradinoC released Linchpin, an interpreter allowing Cornerstone to run on modern PCs. While of limited practical use, this revival preserves a little-known piece of Infocom’s history and offers insight into how business missteps impacted one of gaming’s early pioneers.

**Nexon Confirms Team Reorganization at The First Berserker: Khazan Developer** Nexon has confirmed a reorganization of Neople, the team behind The First Berserker: Khazan. Contrary to reports attributing the reshuffle to poor sales, Nexon states the move was a strategic reallocation of talent as development winds down on the standalone single-player game. The First Berserker: Khazan launched well and maintains a "very positive" Steam rating, but sales did not meet expectations. Employees have not been laid off and are being reassigned to other projects, including upcoming titles in the Dungeon & Fighter franchise. This marks the winding down of support for Khazan, though future sequels or related releases remain possible under Nexon’s broader strategy.

**Darkstone Action RPG Being Remastered by Original Creator** Darkstone, a 1999 action RPG with unique features such as a hunger mechanic and character aging, is receiving a remaster titled Darkstone Restoration. The original creator, Paul Cuisset, is leading the project to improve stability, compatibility, controls, and overall quality. The game was notable for its sidekick system, music, and atmospheric presentation but suffers from technical issues on modern systems. The remaster will launch in early access with potential additions like updated network features and a quest editor, depending on player feedback. This revival aims to restore a lesser-known but distinctive title from the late 1990s for current audiences.

**Doki Doki Literature Club Pulled from Google Play Due to Sensitive Themes** Google has removed the nine-year-old visual novel Doki Doki Literature Club from the Play Store for violating content policies related to its depiction of sensitive topics such as suicide and mental health. The game, which unexpectedly shifts from a seemingly typical dating sim into a psychological horror exploring depression and suicide, was praised for handling these themes with care. Despite its removal from Android, the developers and publisher Serenity Forge said they are exploring ways to return the game to Google Play or distribute it through alternative methods. Doki Doki remains available on other platforms, and its removal highlights ongoing challenges for games that address difficult subject matter within platform policy frameworks.

**Dune: Awakening Makes PvP Fully Optional in Endgame Zones** Funcom announced changes to Dune: Awakening’s PvP system following ongoing player complaints. Previously, endgame zones like the Deep Desert required participation in PvP, alienating a majority of players who preferred PvE. The update will disable all PvP zones in the Hagga Basin and introduce separate PvE and PvP instances for high-level content. PvP instances will offer increased rewards to match the risk. Additionally, self-hosted private servers will be introduced, allowing players to customize game rules and settings. These changes reflect Funcom’s shift toward emphasizing PvE experiences while still supporting PvP players, aiming for better player satisfaction and flexibility.

**Blizzard Adds Prop Hunt Mode to World of Warcraft Player Housing** World of Warcraft’s player housing system will soon include a new PvP-inspired mode called Decor Duels, launching with patch 12.0.5 on April 21. The mode is a hide-and-seek style game set in Silvermoon City where one team disguises as household objects, and the other searches for unusual items. This addition uses the extensive furniture options available in the housing system and provides rewards such as mounts and toys. Blizzard has signaled plans to expand the player housing tools beyond mere decoration into more diverse gameplay experiences.
