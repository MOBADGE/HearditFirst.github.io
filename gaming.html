Mystery in Elder Scrolls Spin-off Redguard Finally Revealed

Nearly 28 years after its release, a long-standing curiosity about Bethesda's 1998 action-adventure game *The Elder Scrolls Adventures: Redguard* has been answered. The Ultimate Elder Scrolls Portal, aided by the community-led Redguard Unity project—which is porting the game to the Unity engine—discovered that attempting to view beneath female NPCs’ skirts through the game’s free camera reveals only a simple smiley face. This playful Easter egg shows Bethesda anticipated players' curiosity, choosing humor rather than hidden content.

The Redguard Unity project aims to modernize *Redguard* by improving stability, updating controls, enhancing visuals, and adding mod support. The original game was known for crashes and a dated control scheme, so this project could make the game more accessible to modern audiences and preserve it as a part of Elder Scrolls history.

Owlcat Unveils New Warhammer 40K Companions for Rogue Trader and Dark Heresy

Owlcat Games has shared new information about companions in their Warhammer 40,000 RPGs. For *Rogue Trader*, the forthcoming DLC *The Infinite Museion* will introduce Eogann Februs, a new tech-priest character. While somewhat less exotic than anticipated—given the diverse Warhammer 40K universe—the addition expands the companion roster.

In parallel, Owlcat revealed a new companion, Haymar Devos, in the playable alpha of *Dark Heresy*. Devos is a guardsman from the hostile jungle world of Catachan, known in 40K lore for its tough soldiers reminiscent of '80s action heroes. This character aligns well with the grimdark setting of the game.

Both titles are still in development stages, with *Rogue Trader*’s DLC and *Dark Heresy* themselves labeled as "coming soon" on Steam. The reveals continue to build expectation around Owlcat's Warhammer 40K adaptations as they near release.

AOC Advocates Monitors as a More Practical Upgrade Than GPUs or Memory

At a recent press event in Croatia, Cesar Reyes Acosta, European commercial leader for monitor manufacturer AOC, argued that upgrading monitors currently offers more value compared to graphics cards or memory upgrades. Due to ongoing price inflation in memory and GPU markets, Acosta suggested that investing in OLED or high-refresh monitors might deliver a more noticeable improvement to gaming experience.

He highlighted advancements in monitor technology that deliver better specs for similar prices compared to five years ago, noting that some 27-inch 1440p monitors with high refresh rates now cost as much as lower-spec 24-inch models from earlier years. While the claim that "monitors are anti-inflation" was partly tongue-in-cheek, it reflects real trends where monitor pricing and features have improved relative to other PC components.

Philips Launches Western Version of 5K Dual-Mode Gaming Monitor

Philips has announced the western release of its 27M2D5901A gaming monitor, a follow-up to its China-only Evnia 27M2G5800. The all-white 27-inch IPS panel supports dual-mode operation, switching between 5K resolution at 165 Hz and 1440p at 330 Hz. This allows users to prioritize resolution or high refresh rates depending on their gaming needs.

Distinctive features include Philips Ambilight backlighting with AI integration and Matter compatibility, enabling smart home lighting synchronization with in-game events. The monitor offers USB Type-C with 65W power delivery for laptop charging. Priced at €799, availability is planned for July. Currently, there are no confirmed plans for a U.S. release.

Turtle WoW Classic Server to Shut Down Following Blizzard Injunction

Private WoW server Turtle WoW will shut down on May 14 following a legal injunction won by Blizzard in a copyright infringement case. Turtle WoW was an 8-year-running fan project offering a "Classic Plus" experience of vanilla WoW with additional raids, zones, and races, but no expansion content.

Despite Turtle WoW’s appeal to Blizzard for fan server licensing, the developer community received no accommodation, aligning the shutdown with past closures like Nostalrius. The server team expressed gratitude to its player base, and all related forums and social channels will close by mid-October.
