Project Zomboid Removes Malicious Mods from Steam Workshop

Developer The Indie Stone has banned and removed 14 mods from Project Zomboid’s Steam Workshop after identifying malicious behavior. The mods contained "heavily obfuscated code" that created files outside the game directory, posing potential security risks. The affected mods, many themed around soundtracks of various games and shows, had been installed on hundreds to over two thousand machines each. The issue impacted players using the game's Build 42 'unstable' branch; those on Build 41 were unaffected. The developer urges users who installed the mods to take thorough security measures beyond merely uninstalling them. This incident highlights ongoing risks posed by unofficial mods and underscores the importance of vigilance with community-created content.

Creative Player Rebuilds Counter-Strike’s Dust 2 Map in World of Warcraft Housing

A World of Warcraft player named Edengonedark has recreated the iconic Dust 2 map from Counter-Strike using WoW’s player housing tools. The build impresses with clever use of in-game items like wooden shelves, doormats, and fruit plates to mimic map details such as crates and vehicles. Despite World of Warcraft’s fantasy setting and limited building mechanics, the recreated map is visually recognizable and has drawn attention, even from Blizzard's official WoW account. The player notes challenges with tool limitations but encourages others to visit the map, which is accessible in-game through a hidden passage inside their WoW house on European servers. This project reflects the creativity of players using existing game features to build ambitious, cross-genre tributes.

Hazelight Studios Celebrates 50 Million Sales Milestone Across Three Titles

Hazelight Studios announced that their three co-op action-adventure games––A Way Out, It Takes Two, and Split Fiction––have collectively sold over 50 million copies worldwide. It Takes Two remains the studio’s best-seller with 30 million units sold since its 2021 release, followed by A Way Out with 13 million and Split Fiction reaching 7 million in about a year. The sales figures emphasize Hazelight’s strength in delivering well-received cooperative experiences. The studio confirmed it is currently developing a fourth game, though details remain under wraps. This milestone underscores Hazelight’s continued impact in a niche genre that blends storytelling and multiplayer gameplay.

Songs of Glimmerwick Offers Ethical and Charming Wizard School Experience

Eastshade Studios released a playable demo of their upcoming life simulation game, Songs of Glimmerwick, which places players in a magical university setting centered around music-based magic. Players tend gardens, learn musical spells via a rhythm minigame, and interact with a socially conscious narrative in a world recovering from decades of magical repression. The game emphasizes inclusivity and challenges typical fantasy tropes by presenting an egalitarian system where anyone can learn magic through practice rather than heritage. Though still early in development, with some fiddly controls, the demo has been praised for its charming art style, voice acting, and thoughtful storytelling. The studio plans a full release later this year, with demo saves carrying over.

Overwatch’s New Hero Sierra Features Potentially Controversial Auto-Aim Ability

Blizzard has revealed Sierra as the latest hero coming to Overwatch. Early gameplay footage suggests her secondary fire marks enemies with darts that cause subsequent shots to curve and hit the target, effectively offering an auto-aim mechanic. This ability could make her highly effective but also contentious among players, as auto-aim features often generate debate about fairness. Other confirmed abilities include aerial mobility via grappling a drone and an area-of-effect ultimate. Sierra is affiliated with the game's security force narrative and will arrive on April 14, without an early playtest period. Player reactions to her abilities will likely be closely watched to assess balance and impact on competitive play.

Fighting Game Director Encourages Broad Exploration of the Genre

Dave Hall, director of the upcoming Invincible VS fighting game, advocates for players to try a variety of fighting games, noting their accessibility and fun beyond competitive complexity. Hall, with a background in Killer Instinct development, highlights the appeal of tag team mechanics and community feedback-driven design in Invincible VS. The game aims to balance approachability with depth, integrating iconic characters from the Invincible series into layered combat systems. Its release is part of a busy year for fighting games competing with established franchises. Hall’s comments reflect ongoing challenges and opportunities in expanding fighting games’ appeal while catering to dedicated fans.
