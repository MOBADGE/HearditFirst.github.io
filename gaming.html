Larian Studios revealed its upcoming RPG, *Divinity*, described as the studio's biggest and most ambitious project yet. The Game Awards 2025 marked the game's initial reveal, accompanied by an interview where Larian shared several key details:

- **Turn-Based Combat with New Ruleset:** Contrary to speculation, *Divinity* maintains turn-based combat but does not simply reuse the system from *Divinity: Original Sin 2*. Studio founder Swen Vincke emphasized that the combat features a new ruleset developed from lessons learned across all previous Larian games. The system promises to be accessible yet challenging, allowing players to combine abilities and create varied strategies.

- **Increased Player Agency:** The game aims to deliver a higher level of agency than *Baldur‚Äôs Gate 3*, in terms of both narrative and systems. Decisions will lead to deeper, longer sequences of consequences, with interconnected storylines and reactive gameplay that evolve dynamically with player choices.

- **Technical and Production Advances:** Larian has updated its game engine to support greater efficiency and experimentation during development, including a streamlined motion capture pipeline and improved analytics for playtesting. The studio is well past pre-production and is actively recording actor performances, having built multiple on-site recording studios to support continuous voice work.

- **Tone and Content:** *Divinity* is said to be the darkest entry in the series yet, featuring explicit and intimate scenes of horror and violence. However, the tone is not relentlessly grim; it offers moments of light and hope and encourages players to shape the world as beacons of hope or as agents of chaos.

- **Uncertain Release and Early Access Plans:** While no release date or window has been shared, Larian has not ruled out launching *Divinity* in early access, mirroring strategies used previously with their other RPGs. The team plans ongoing iteration and recording, suggesting a lengthy and evolving development process.

PC Gamer‚Äôs Game of the Year Awards 2025

The annual PC Gamer Game of the Year Awards highlighted the best PC gaming experiences of 2025. Rather than fixed categories, editors nominated games freely, resulting in a diverse selection:

- **Game of the Year:** *Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2* was chosen for its detailed simulation of medieval life, immersive worldbuilding, and committed design that appeals to players seeking a deep, systems-driven RPG experience.

- **Other Category Winners:** - Best Adventure Game: *Old Skies*, noted for its compelling time-travel narrative and emotional character work. - Best Immersive Sim: *Skin Deep*, praised for its fluid movement, engaging stealth mechanics, and unique humor. - Best Horror: *Labyrinth of the Demon King*, recognized for its tense atmosphere, unsettling design, and challenging combat. - Best Cozy Game: *Promise Mascot Agency*, a narrative-driven, absurdist small-town drama with rich characters. - Best Multiplayer: *Arc Raiders*, standout for innovating within the extraction shooter genre by encouraging cooperation and featuring solid performance and design.

Other categories included Best Comedy (*Baby Steps*), Best Roguelike (*Deep Rock Galactic: Survivor*), Best Action (*Hollow Knight: Silksong*), Best Co-op (*Abiotic Factor*), Best Story (*Clair Obscur: Expedition 33*), and Best Design (*Blue Prince*).

Fallout Season 2 Review: Focus on New Vegas

The second season of the *Fallout* TV series continues the narrative established in the first, with a tighter focus on the Mojave Wasteland and the legacy of *Fallout: New Vegas*. Highlights include:

- Flashbacks remain integral, deepening character stories rather than becoming background filler. - The season explores the Brotherhood of Steel in a critical and often satirical manner, portraying them as misguided rather than heroic. - Returning landmarks and factions from *New Vegas* add nostalgia and continuity for fans. - The show balances black comedy with morally complex storytelling, including subplots like drug addiction handled with humor and depth. - While some narrative moments are heavy-handed, the season generally rewards attentive viewing and continues to build on its distinct tone.
