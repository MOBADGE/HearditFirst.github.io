Banquet for Fools Review: An RPG That Demands Player Involvement

Banquet for Fools is an RPG released in early March 2026 by Hannah and Joseph Games. It differentiates itself by emphasizing active player note-taking and engagement rather than providing traditional quest logs or waypoints. The game drops players into a complex world with little hand-holding, requiring them to manually document clues, map locations, and spell designs to progress. This approach encourages players to slow down and immerse themselves deeply in the game's social structures and environments.

Combat blends real-time action with tactical pause moments, focusing on positioning, timing, and manual control of party members. The lack of enemy respawns means that careful management and survival are central. While this design offers a rewarding sense of personal investment, the game can sometimes feel frustrating due to its cryptic directions and reliance on player memory. Banquet for Fools stands out for its demanding playstyle and immersive world-building, though it may not appeal to those wanting more guided experiences.

Mars War: Logs Offers a Gritty, Affordable RPG for Steam Deck Users

Originally released in 2013 by Spiders, Mars War: Logs is a low-budget, story-driven RPG set on a post-disaster Mars. Despite dated graphics, awkward combat, and uneven voice acting, the game’s atmospheric setting and choice-driven narrative provide a unique appeal. Players take on the role of Roy, a prisoner in a harsh Martian prison colony embroiled in a resource war.

The game's rough edges and simple combat are balanced by intriguing story elements and tactical options such as sand-throwing to stun enemies. With a playtime around 15 hours, Mars War: Logs remains an accessible, if niche, title—especially with its current price of 99 cents during the Steam Spring sale. It runs well on the Steam Deck, making it a suitable pick for portable gaming with an interest in lesser-known RPGs.

Sand: Raiders of Sophie Release Date Still Uncertain

The upcoming PvPvE extraction shooter Sand: Raiders of Sophie, scheduled for March 2026, has yet to announce a specific release date despite the month nearing its end. The game involves building and piloting a large mechanical base across a hazardous alien planet in multiplayer matches. Fans have expressed concern on official Discord channels about the lack of details and possible delay.

Community managers from publisher tinyBuild have acknowledged these concerns but have explained that final development elements must align before confirming a date. They promised to share information once work reaches a sufficient stage. At this point, the exact launch date remains unconfirmed, but the game has not been officially delayed. This uncertainty highlights the challenges of coordinating communication during late development.

Lost Wiki: Kozlovka – A Mystery Game Built on Database Exploration

Lost Wiki: Kozlovka is a $5 mystery game that uses a Wikipedia-style interface to unravel the secrets of a small Eastern European town and a mysterious tree called Draken Oak. Players investigate through linked database entries, piecing together family trees, timelines, and hidden connections. While the puzzles are relatively straightforward, the game rewards thorough reading and attention to detail.

The narrative emerges gradually, revealing corruption, deceit, and historical events through accessible but carefully crafted entries. The game also comments on the nature of controlled information and the gaps in public knowledge. Its focused storytelling and engaging interface captivated the reviewer for over two hours, illustrating how an unconventional format can deliver a compelling mystery experience without complexity overload.

Marathon’s Cryo Archive Map Blends PvE and PvP in a Unique Endgame Setting

Bungie's Marathon shooter recently introduced its fourth map, Cryo Archive, offering a labyrinthine spaceship setting that mixes puzzle-solving, PvE enemy waves, and competitive multiplayer. Unlike the other maps, newcomers start isolated in separate wings where they combat AI-controlled enemies before meeting in a central Control area for PvP encounters.

Progress through the map requires earning Security Clearance by hacking or looting, which unlocks new zones and extraction opportunities. The map's complexity and size provide varied strategic choices, with loot distributed throughout and multiple gameplay layers including locked puzzles and a final boss encounter. While its weekend-only availability limits access and some technical issues remain, Cryo Archive represents a novel and ambitious design for shooter maps.
