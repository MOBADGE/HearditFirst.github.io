Frictional Games Horror Bundle Offers All Major Titles for $15

Frictional Games’ horror catalog—including classics like *Penumbra*, *Amnesia*, *SOMA*, and its recent survival horror *Amnesia: The Bunker*—is now available in a bundle on Humble Bundle for $15. This collection spans the studio's horror evolution from the older *Penumbra* trilogy and the seminal *Amnesia: The Dark Descent* to the more narrative-driven *SOMA*. The bundle includes *Amnesia* sequels *A Machine for Pigs* and *Rebirth*, along with the latest *The Bunker*. The offer lasts until April 1, 2026, with Steam keys redeemable until 2027. This provides a comprehensive way to experience one of the most influential horror studios at a low cost, important for both newcomers and genre enthusiasts.

Path of Exile Trading Server Leader Banned Amid Controversy

Jenebu, who ran The Forbidden Trove (TFT), a major Discord server central to *Path of Exile*'s rare item trading, has been permanently banned by Grinding Gear Games. Jenebu’s account reportedly held over 1,500 unique items, including some irreplaceable ones, tied to a "mirror service" that allowed item duplication—a practice requiring high trust within the community. The ban revived past rumors of illicit real-money trading, though no evidence has surfaced publicly. This move disrupts TFT’s online economy and community, raising questions about third-party trading platforms in online games. While asynchronous in-game trading has decreased reliance on Discord servers like TFT, Jenebu’s absence may hasten the server’s decline.

Deus Ex: Human Revolution’s Soundtrack Took a New Direction

Composer Alexander Brandon revealed in a recent interview why his original *Deus Ex* theme was rejected for *Human Revolution*’s soundtrack. Eidos Montreal, the new studio behind *Human Revolution*, aimed to make the project distinct and chose Michael McCann to compose the music instead. Brandon had the opportunity to submit a remake of the classic theme for the end credits but it was deemed "not the right fit." In hindsight, Brandon agrees with that choice. He also expressed disappointment at not being involved in the mobile spinoff *Deus Ex: The Fall*, as rights decisions centralized with Eidos Montreal. The shift marked a new musical identity for the franchise as it moved forward under a different creative team.

Mohawk Games Prioritizes Size and Quality Over Speed

Soren Johnson, co-founder of Mohawk Games, discussed the studio’s careful growth strategy amid the gaming industry’s broader challenges. With 15 employees, Mohawk remains deliberately small to avoid layoffs and financial risk. Johnson emphasized that longer development times often lead to better games and a healthier creative process. Mohawk, known for *Old World* and *Offworld Trading Company,* is releasing new expansion content soon but prioritizes sustainability over rapid growth. Johnson also shared interest in branching into tactical RPGs and narrative games in the future, underscoring a measured approach to studio and game development.

PUBG: Blindspot Early Access Ends After Less Than Two Months

Krafton announced the early access closure of *PUBG: Blindspot*, a top-down tactical shooter that launched in February 2026. Despite initial promise, the game struggled to build a large player base, leading to long matchmaking queues and dwindling concurrent users. As a free-to-play title dependent on active players for in-game purchases, sustainability became untenable. Krafton’s recent financial and organizational challenges, including profit declines and workforce reduction efforts, may have influenced the decision. The developer Arc Team plans to regroup but offered no immediate plans for the game post-closure.

Overwatch Season 1 Event Reveals Player Alignment Breakdown

Following the conclusion of Overwatch’s season 1 Conquest event, developer Blizzard disclosed that only 32% of participants stayed loyal to the Overwatch faction led by Winston throughout the event. Meanwhile, roughly 30% sided with the antagonist group Talon, and 38% fluctuated between the two, labeled “sneaky mercenaries.” Players who switched sides could earn rewards from both factions, offering motivation for the middle ground. The event saw over 250 million challenges completed over nine weeks. These figures highlight diverse community engagement and player faction loyalty within Overwatch’s ongoing seasonal structure.
