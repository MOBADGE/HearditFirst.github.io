Tomb Raider Remastered Patch Sparks Backlash Over New Outfits and Bugs

Last week’s update to the remastered collection of the first three Tomb Raider games introduced a new challenge mode and 10 additional outfits for Lara Croft. However, many players have criticized the new outfits for their poor visual quality, describing them as rough and unappealing. Feedback on Steam has been predominantly negative, with reports that the patch also broke audio, visuals, enemy AI, and deleted save files.

The original remaster’s lead artist confirmed that the development team behind this update was separate from the original Saber Interactive group, fueling concerns about quality control. While it remains unconfirmed whether AI was involved in asset creation, the patch follows a previous incident where unauthorized AI-generated voice lines were removed from the remasters. This ongoing controversy highlights challenges in maintaining classic games with modern updates and the scrutiny developers face from original fans.

Abubakar Salim Shares Insight Into His PC Gaming History and Setup

Actor and developer Abubakar Salim, known for his role as Bayek in Assassin’s Creed: Origins and as founder of Surgent Studios, gave a detailed look at his gaming background and current PC setup. Salim’s early gaming came through classic Windows titles and strategy games like Age of Empires and Warcraft. His highest-playtime game is Garry’s Mod, used extensively in his youth for roleplaying with friends.

Currently, Salim enjoys titles like No Rest for the Wicked and Dave the Diver, appreciating their progression loops and gameplay depth. He mentioned that the oldest installed game on his PC is Warcraft 3, emphasizing the importance of multiplayer and social experiences in his gaming past. Salim also shared that his desktop is cluttered, joking that even his wife refuses to look at it—a reflection of a busy creative mind juggling acting and game development projects.

Panline: New Indie Freerunning Game Evokes Mirror’s Edge

Indie developer Viridian Matters announced Panline, a freerunning game reminiscent of Mirror’s Edge, featuring a clean, dystopian cityscape. The game’s open city design allows for dynamic job-based navigation, with players unlocking movement abilities and upgrading an in-game PDA styled after a PlayStation Portable.

Panline’s gameplay includes parkour elements like wall-runs and speed-building, with a risk-reward system where lethal falls cause loss of progress on specific jobs. With an anticipated release later this year, the developer’s previous parkour game received a positive reception, suggesting that Panline aims to capture the same core appeal while introducing new mechanics.

GTA RP’s High-Stakes Hide-and-Seek Highlights Differing Multiplayer Experiences

GTA Online's roleplay community has taken to a new hide-and-seek mode where players risk their vehicles in tense matches, with winners allowed to damage the loser’s car. This mode exemplifies the slower, more immersive economy and gameplay style of GTA RP, contrasting with the mainstream GTA Online’s abundant wealth and chaotic play.

The writer recounts spending months earning in-game money only to have a prized, uninsured vehicle destroyed, illustrating how RP servers emphasize realistic consequences and long-term investment. Rockstar’s acquisition of the FiveM modding team indicates a potential official expansion of RP features in GTA 6. The hope is that future GTA multiplayer offerings will balance both high-action chaos and grounded roleplay systems.

Timberborn Exits Early Access with Automation Features

City-builder Timberborn officially launched its 1.0 version this month, adding significant automation mechanics inspired by games like Factorio. The update introduces 20 new logic-based buildings, including sensors, relays, and controllers, allowing players to program their beaver settlements to operate with minimal manual input.

Players can automate tasks such as closing floodgates during hazardous tides, managing resource production based on supply levels, or controlling beaver movement schedules. This automation broadens the game’s strategic depth and complexity. Timberborn, which has earned a high player rating on Steam throughout early access, is currently discounted until late March.
