**Linux on the Desktop: A Turning Point for PC Gamers**

An article reflecting on the state of Linux for gaming argues that 2026 could be the year more users seriously consider switching from Windows to Linux for their gaming PCs. The author shares personal experience of using Bazzite, a Linux distribution focused on gaming, and how running various games felt as easy or easier than on Windows, even with Nvidia GPUs. Increased user adoption is reflected in the Steam hardware survey, showing Linux usage hitting 3.2%, surpassing Mac users.

The piece points to growing dissatisfaction with Windows due to frequent promotions and perceived lack of control over personal computers and notes the advantages Linux offers being fully open-source and allowing users to choose precisely what software runs on their systems. While issues remain, such as imperfect HDR support and compatibility with anti-cheat software, improvements‚Äîbolstered by Valve‚Äôs push with SteamOS‚Äîsuggest Linux is a viable platform for gaming now.
