Turtle WoW Classic Server to Shut Down Following Blizzard Injunction

After a legal dispute spanning several months, Turtle WoW, a popular World of Warcraft private server known for its "Classic Plus" experience, announced it will shut down. This follows a court injunction in favor of Blizzard Entertainment due to a copyright infringement lawsuit filed last September. Turtle WoW's development team revealed in a forum post that all servers will close on May 14, with social channels going offline by October 16. The server provided an expanded version of WoW's pre-expansion content with new raids and zones, distinct from official Blizzard servers. The shutdown highlights ongoing tensions between fan-run private servers and game publishers, underscoring the challenges these community projects face despite their dedicated player bases.

Fallout Designer Credits U.S. Navy Officers for Introducing Him to D&D

Tim Cain, known for designing the Fallout series, recently shared his introduction to Dungeons & Dragons, which he credits as a formative experience shaping his career in game development. In a detailed video, Cain explained how, back in 1979 during the Carter administration, naval officers who worked with his mother invited him to play D&D. This encounter was his first experience with the tabletop RPG, which inspired many of his later works. Cain's early exposure to D&D’s complex rules and open-ended gameplay helped him grasp fundamental design elements, eventually contributing to his success in creating narrative-driven role-playing games. His story sheds light on the often personal and unexpected origins behind influential game designers.

Portal 2: Community Edition Launches Open Beta to Enhance Modding

A new community-driven version of Portal 2, known as Portal 2: Community Edition (CE), has entered open beta. This version is not a remake but an enhanced build focusing on expanding the game's modding capabilities. Improvements include updated lighting and texture support, enhanced scripting, custom campaign features with integrated UI, and an upgraded level editor. P2:CE is built on Strata Source, a fork of the CS:GO engine, enabling more advanced creations. Although still early in its lifecycle, modders have already begun releasing custom maps and assets, with the developers planning an original campaign. This initiative offers modders greater tools to extend Portal 2’s lifespan with fresh content.

New Doom Mod Introduces 28 Pilotable Mechs After Two Years of Development

A total conversion mod for Doom titled MechDoomer: Rebooted has been released after two years of work. This mod introduces 28 pilotable mechs spanning light to assault classes, including well-known models like the Atlas. It changes gameplay by adding mech-style movement and customizable weapons, providing a new way to experience the classic FPS. The mod supports exiting mechs on demand and is designed to be compatible with many existing Doom mods, including the Halo Doom mod. The addition of giant mechs offers a distinctive gameplay twist, demonstrating the continued creativity and extensibility of the Doom modding community.

AOC Responds to Complaints With New On-Screen Display Software for Monitors

Monitor manufacturer AOC addressed longstanding user complaints about its on-screen display (OSD) software, announcing a redesigned interface for all its latest and upcoming gaming monitors. The new OSD simplifies access to common settings such as game mode and brightness, while also offering a second level of detailed controls. The updated interface adapts color schemes to different AOC monitor lines, aiming to improve usability and consistency. This change comes as AOC expands its lineup of dual-mode monitors, which can switch resolutions and refresh rates on the fly—a feature that benefits from efficient software navigation. The update reflects AOC’s efforts to improve the user experience in response to customer feedback.

BAFTA Withdraws Trailer for Indie Game Citing Potentially Triggering Content

BAFTA decided not to show the trailer for Silver Script Games’ upcoming title, The Quiet Things, at its recent awards ceremony due to concerns over sensitive content. The developer, Alyx Jones, worked to revise the trailer after initial feedback, removing imagery flagged as potentially violent. Despite this, BAFTA ultimately pulled the trailer, citing insufficient time to provide appropriate content warnings for attendees. The studio expressed disappointment, noting the game tackles difficult themes around trauma and survival. BAFTA stated its aim was to prioritize the wellbeing of all guests during the event. This incident reflects ongoing debates about content sensitivity and support for challenging narratives within the games industry.
