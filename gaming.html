GTA RP Hide-and-Seek Becomes a Costly New Trend

In Grand Theft Auto 5’s roleplay community, a new hide-and-seek game mode has gained traction where players risk damaging their vehicles upon losing. Unlike traditional GTA Online play, the repair process in RP servers involves real players towing and fixing vehicles at high costs, making the stakes notably higher. This has added a fresh layer of tension and strategy to the gameplay, as players must weigh the potential loss of hard-earned and uninsured vehicles—such as a “Beater tow truck” affectionately called Rusty—against the thrill of winning hide-and-seek matches.

This trend highlights how community-driven innovations continue to enrich GTA RP, helping keep the game mode dynamic even years after the original game’s release. The added economic consequences also encourage players to prepare better, such as purchasing in-game car insurance, before engaging in these challenges.

Timberborn Exits Early Access With New Automation Features

The city-builder Timberborn has officially left early access, releasing version 1.0 with several new additions, most notably automation features inspired by games like Factorio. Developer Mechanistry introduced logic components such as levers, relays, and sensors that allow players to program their beaver settlements to operate autonomously. This includes practical applications like automatically closing floodgates or halting production when resources run low.

Alongside these logic tools, Timberborn’s launch expanded the game with twenty new buildings and additional environment objects. The implementation of automation marks a significant evolution, enhancing the depth and complexity of the game’s management systems. As a result, Timberborn maintains its place as a highly rated city-builder on Steam, with overwhelmingly positive reviews from nearly 39,000 players. A launch discount is currently available until March 26.

Project Shadowglass Trailer Reveals Strong Inspiration from Thief

A new trailer for Project Shadowglass, a fantasy stealth heist simulator, has revealed a heavier influence from the classic stealth game Thief than previously known. Developed by Starhelm Studios, the game melds nostalgic pixelated 3D graphics with gameplay elements like blackjack knockouts, water arrows to douse torches, and stealth approaches involving rooftop navigation and distraction.

The trailer also showcases unique mechanics such as sliding down rooftops, firework detonators, and a more “sloppy” swordfighting style that fits the stealth focus. The game promises long-term consequences for player actions, jail escape sequences if caught, and a research-based mission planning stage. While still in early development with no release date, a playable demo is expected later this year.

Deep Rock Galactic: Survivor Expands with New Class and Weapons

Deep Rock Galactic: Survivor is set to receive a new expansion titled Heavy Duty on April 30, introducing the series' first new playable class: the Demolisher. This class operates a weaponized bulldozer called the Rock Dozer, capable of flattening terrain and enemies to forge new paths. Players can select one of three class modifications emphasizing mining output, mobility, or machine efficiency.

The DLC will also add ten new weapons usable across all classes, including elemental armaments and automated drones. Additionally, two major yet-unannounced features are planned to be revealed later. The base game is currently discounted by 30% until March 18, offering a lower entry price ahead of the expansion.

Monster Hunter Stories 3 Guides: Invasive Monsters and Endangered Species Locations

Monster Hunter Stories 3 features powerful Invasive Monsters that guard the dens of rare Endangered Species, requiring special strategies to repel them. Detailed guides have now emerged explaining how to defeat all nine Invasive Monsters, each demanding tailored tactics such as breaking specific monster parts, using elemental weapons, or timing counterattacks to special moves.

Complementing this, another guide lists the precise locations of all nine endangered monster species in the game’s three regions: Azuria, Canalta Timberland, and Tarukan. Knowing these locations and how to handle their invasive guardians is crucial for players aiming to collect and raise the best monsties. These resources support players in navigating one of the more complex aspects of Monster Hunter Stories 3’s post-launch content.
