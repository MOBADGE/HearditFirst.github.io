Five New Steam Games to Watch

A recent overview highlights five lesser-known but notable new releases on Steam that might interest players looking for fresh experiences. Among these:

- **Cult Vacui** is a short point-and-click adventure set in 19th-century Scotland revolving around a janitor caught in a deadly cult ritual. It emphasizes story-driven stealth and puzzle-solving. - **Subversive Memories** offers a Brazilian survival horror experience inspired by historical military dictatorship themes, featuring fixed camera angles and 3D models. It includes puzzles and combat across a labyrinthine base. - **KuloNiku: Bowl Up!** combines cozy restaurant simulation and life simulation with a quirky anime-inspired aesthetic. Players manage a noodle shop and engage with eccentric characters. - **ChainStaff** is an action platformer noted for its unusual and divisive art style. Gameplay incorporates shooting and grappling hook mechanics, along with a choice-driven upgrade system. - **Agony Increment** follows the style of surreal, disorienting shooters with complex combat and movement mechanics, though story details remain sparse.

These games illustrate the diversity of indie offerings on Steam, appealing to various tastes from narrative adventure to experimental action.

AI-Generated Warhammer 40,000 Fan Fiction Sparks Concerns

A recently published AI-generated story titled *Taken by the Tyranid: A Romance in the Far Future* on Amazon has drawn attention for combining Warhammer 40,000 themes with speculative romance. The cover features AI-generated artwork with visual artifacts, and the story is thought likely to be AI-written, echoing a broader issue of low-quality AI content flooding online marketplaces. Such content often mimics popular tropes—in this case, dinosaur-erotica—and raises questions about the impact on established fan communities and the integrity of creative works. Official fan content remains abundant and free on platforms like Archive of Our Own, providing an alternative for enthusiasts.

Sekiro Mod Transforms Combat Into Skateboarding

Modder Ionian-MikiriyAKATotallyNotShinobi has released *Jet Set Sekiro*, a mod that reimagines *Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice* as a Tony Hawk-style skateboarding game. Using Sekiro’s existing animations repurposed for skating moves, players navigate the Fountainhead Palace level while collecting spray paint cans to tag graffiti spots. This unusual crossover blends action platforming with skateboarding mechanics, reflecting the ongoing creativity in modding communities. The mod also connects nostalgically to previous skateboarding-themed mods that bridged different game universes.

Ashes of Creation Studio Founder Denies Misuse of Kickstarter Funds

Following the shutdown of the MMO *Ashes of Creation* and layoffs at Intrepid Studios, investor Jason Caramanis accused founder Steven Sharif of misappropriating over $12 million in Kickstarter funds. Allegations include expenditures on private chefs, antiques, and trading card purchases. These claims were presented in a detailed video analysis supported by purported internal financial records. Sharif has denied wrongdoing, attributing the project's failure to the studio’s board of directors. The situation remains complex, with ongoing disputes over accountability and transparency for crowdfunded game development.

Blizzard Secures Injunction Against Vanilla WoW Private Server

Blizzard Entertainment has won a court injunction against Turtle WoW, a prominent vanilla World of Warcraft private server. The ruling demands that the server’s operators cease development, maintenance, and distribution of the server software and prohibits transferring server code to any successor projects. The game studio has settled confidentially with the defendants, signaling the shutdown of this fan-operated version of classic WoW. Turtle WoW had petitioned Blizzard for an official fan server license framework after facing legal challenges. The decision highlights ongoing tensions between copyright enforcement and community-driven preservation of legacy MMO experiences.

Skate Story on Sale With Developer’s Unusual Piracy Comment

*Skate Story*, a demon-themed skateboarding game released in 2025 and noted for its distinctive style, is currently available on GOG at a discounted price of $16. Developer Sam Eng acknowledged affordability issues by openly suggesting that players who find the cost prohibitive could consider pirating the game, though this is an informal comment rather than a call to action. The sale provides an accessible opportunity for players interested in alternative skateboarding games with unique artistic sensibilities.
