HP Launches Gaming Laptop Subscription Service

HP has introduced a subscription service for its gaming laptops, allowing customers to pay a monthly fee to use a gaming rig without the option to own it permanently. Plans start at $50 per month for models like the HP Victus 15-inch with Ryzen 7 and RTX 4050, and go up to $130 per month for high-end machines like the HP Omen Max 16 with an Intel Core Ultra 9 and RTX 5080. While renters can upgrade their laptops annually and access enhanced support and next-day replacements, the subscription does not build any ownership equity. After roughly 16 to 19 months of payments, customers will have paid amounts near the laptop‚Äôs retail price but still will not own the device. Early cancellation fees are significant, though canceling after one year is free. This service targets gamers who want access to up-to-date hardware without a large upfront cost, although financing options on outright purchases remain competitive.

Itch.io Offers $10 Bundle Supporting Immigrant Rights

Itch.io has launched the "No ICE in Minnesota" bundle, a collection of over 1,400 items including more than 200 games, TTRPGs, books, soundtracks, and game assets for $10. Proceeds go to the Immigrant Law Center of Minnesota, providing free legal aid and policy advocacy for low-income immigrants and refugees in that region. Notable games in the bundle include critically praised titles such as Baba Is You, A Short Hike, Coffee Talk, and Hypnospace Outlaw. The bundle aims to raise $100,000 and will remain available for a month. This initiative combines affordable access to a wide range of indie games with support for a social cause.

Fallout TV Show Stars Abstain from Playing the Games

Actors Walton Goggins and Aaron Moten, who star in the Fallout TV series, have both chosen not to play the game series on which the show is based. Goggins has openly stated disinterest in playing the games, while Moten has "forbidden" himself from playing to maintain a sense of authenticity in portraying his character without the influence of the game's existing lore and mechanics. Moten has instead watched streams of others playing the games. In contrast, fellow lead Ella Purnell is the only main cast member reported to have played Fallout 4 and participated actively as a player. This approach reflects the actors‚Äô decisions on how to approach adapting interactive media into scripted performances.

Helldivers 2 Voting Overview

In Helldivers 2, players can vote on the position of the Democracy Space Station (DSS) to affect tactical advantages during ongoing galactic campaigns. Voting takes place on the Galactic War map using the voting interface, allowing players to decide where to move the DSS among planets. The DSS provides community-wide buffs such as Eagle Airstrikes, Orbital Blockades, and Heavy Ordnance Distribution that influence mission conditions and campaign progress based on collective resource donations. Choosing the correct location strategically is important to support current campaign efforts. The voting system integrates community participation in tactical choices for multiplayer coordination.

This hands-on, analogue method helps designers assess the form, proportion, and feel of the mouse in real scale, which digital tools alone reportedly cannot fully replicate. The approach highlights the balance between technology and manual design in hardware development.
