**Arc Raiders Faces Duplication Glitch Exploited for Valuable Rubber Ducks**

Players of the third-person shooter Arc Raiders have discovered a duplication glitch allowing them to hoard large quantities of rare rubber ducks, some worth millions in the game's currency. These "Familiar Ducks" have a high in-game value, and several instances have appeared on social media where players extracted raids with millions of coins worth of these items. This exploit extends beyond just rubber ducks; players have also duplicated valuable blueprints like the Tempest. While the developer Embark has not formally commented, it has previously dealt with cheaters by using inventive in-game punishments. Using this duplication glitch risks adding strikes to player records, potentially resulting in bans. Despite the seemingly large payoff, the consequences suggest exploiting this bug is not advisable.

**Microsoft Shifts Windows 11 Focus Toward Performance and Stability**

Microsoft's Windows and Devices president, Pavan Davuluri, announced that Windows 11 development will prioritize core improvements in system performance, reliability, and the overall user experience during 2026. This change reportedly responds to widespread user dissatisfaction stemming from numerous bugs and issues affecting Windows 11 since its release, including problems with Task Manager, peripherals in recovery environments, File Explorer, and update-related failures. The company intends to adopt a "swarming" approach, mobilizing engineers to address pressing concerns efficiently. This shift moves away from an emphasis on new AI features, which have been a contentious topic among users, to focus on foundational system quality. The move is seen as addressing frustration from users compelled to upgrade from Windows 10, many of whom have considered alternative operating systems such as Linux.

Mozilla has outlined plans to form a "rebel alliance" comprising developers, activists, researchers, and investors to promote a human-centered and trustworthy approach to AI development.
