Among Us Surpasses 1 Billion Downloads on Google Play

In a recent announcement, the developers of *Among Us* revealed that the popular social deduction game has exceeded 1 billion downloads on the Google Play Store. This milestone places *Among Us* ahead of other major titles such as *Minecraft*, *Stardew Valley*, and *Terraria* in terms of download numbers. The game originally gained widespread attention during the COVID-19 pandemic and has maintained a strong presence over the past eight years. While some downloads may be repeat installs, the figure underscores the game's significant cultural impact and continued popularity. This achievement highlights *Among Us*' role in bringing social deduction games into mainstream gaming vernacular.

The Adventures of Sir Kicksalot: An Action Sandbox Inspired by Dark Messiah

Solo developer Stéphane Le Roy is releasing *The Adventures of Sir Kicksalot*, an action sandbox game inspired by *Dark Messiah of Might and Magic*. The game's demo showcases a variety of combat and stealth mechanics, including directional melee attacks, disarming enemies, and creating environmental traps like banana spikes. A notable feature is the magic system that enables elemental attacks, weapon infusions, and mind control over enemies. The sandbox nature allows creative enemy takedowns, such as kicking foes off cliffs or throwing objects. The full game is scheduled for release on May 15, offering an immersive sim experience with a strong emphasis on player creativity and humor.

John Carmack Reflects on Quake Arena as His Favorite id Software Game

A 2008 interview with *id Software* co-founder John Carmack resurfaced, in which he identified *Quake III Arena* as his favorite game from the studio's portfolio. Carmack described *Quake Arena* as a "pure game," emphasizing its focus on gameplay over technical spectacle. He noted that while *Quake* wasn't the most commercially successful, it has stood the test of time due to its refined mechanics. At the time, *Quake Live*, a browser-based remake of *Quake III Arena*, was also discussed as an attempt to leverage the ubiquity of PCs. Carmack's insights provide historical perspective on the evolution of competitive multiplayer games and PC gaming's unique qualities.

Meta Increases Prices of Quest 3 and Quest 3S VR Headsets Citing Memory Costs

Meta announced price increases for its Quest 3 and Quest 3S virtual reality headsets, attributing the hikes to rising costs for components, especially memory chips. The price adjustments will affect both new and refurbished units across key regions starting April 19, 2026. In the US, the Quest 3's price will rise by $100, while Quest 3S variants will increase by $50. The surge in memory prices is linked to high demand driven in part by AI infrastructure investments, including Meta's significant planned spending on data centers. Despite these changes, Meta confirmed its commitment to VR development, citing a "long-term roadmap" for the platform, though recent signs suggest a strategic shift towards AI as the metaverse vision faces challenges.

Intel Launches Wildcat Lake Core Series 3 Processors for Budget Laptops

Intel introduced its new Wildcat Lake Core Series 3 processors targeting entry-level and budget laptops. These chips shrink the design of the earlier Panther Lake architecture to fewer cores, with top models featuring two performance cores and four efficiency cores. Unlike the higher-end Panther Lake Ultra series, Wildcat Lake processors include a smaller integrated GPU, making them less suited for gaming or graphics-intensive tasks. Intel positions these chips as offering improved battery life and everyday productivity for business or casual use. With ongoing supply constraints and increased memory costs, these new processors aim to balance performance and efficiency for affordable devices.

Review: AMD Radeon RX 9060 XT 8 GB (Asus Prime Edition)

The AMD Radeon RX 9060 XT 8 GB, especially the Asus Prime OC Edition, is a budget-friendly graphics card based on AMD's RDNA 4 architecture. While it offers solid performance at its MSRP of $299, it struggles in certain VRAM-intensive games compared to its main rival, the Nvidia RTX 5060 Ti 8 GB, which maintains better frame rates under constrained memory conditions.
