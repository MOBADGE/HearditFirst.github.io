Real-time with Pause Still Relevant, Says Star Wars Zero Company Designer

James Brawley, lead designer of the upcoming turn-based tactics game *Star Wars Zero Company*, emphasized that the "real-time with pause" (RTWP) style of combat is not obsolete. While many modern tactical games have shifted toward purely turn-based systems, Brawley sees RTWP as a genre that cycles in and out of popularity rather than being permanently replaced. He referenced innovations in pacing, camera work, and immersion that have made turn-based tactics games more accessible and dynamic, helping revive interest in the genre. However, Brawley expressed hope that RTWP games will see fresh innovation and a potential resurgence, pointing out that the genre still has untapped potential.

RPG Maker to Support Octopath Traveler-Style 2.5D Visuals

RPG Maker is preparing its first major visual overhaul to introduce a 2.5D graphical style inspired by games like *Octopath Traveler* and *Dragon Quest 3 HD-2D*. The update, teased by developer Gotcha Gotcha Games, moves away from the traditional 16-bit aesthetic commonly associated with RPG Maker titles. The new style uses high-quality lighting and shadows combined with a grid-based movement system. This visual shift aims to diversify the look of RPG Maker projects, which often carry a familiar stock appearance. Although the release timing and detailed scope are not yet clarified, the change could help creators appeal to players who favor more modern retro-styled games.

Dimension 20 Partners with White Wolf for Vampire: The Masquerade Series

The popular tabletop RPG actual-play group Dimension 20 is launching a new season using the *Vampire: The Masquerade* setting, in official partnership with White Wolf. Called *City Council of Darkness*, it will follow a cast of outcast vampires managing a small town, Purpee, Oregon. The campaign will explore vampires outside their usual urban context with humor and a fish-out-of-water premise. Brennan Lee Mulligan returns as the game master, with the core Dimension 20 cast participating. The season premieres April 8 on the subscription platform Dropout, marking Dimension 20’s first use of a licensed setting and an expansion of their usual *Dungeons & Dragons* focus.

Pro World of Warcraft Player Disconnected Mid-Fight Due to Expired Subscription

During a high-profile world first race in Blizzard’s *World of Warcraft* “Midnight” expansion, a professional player was unexpectedly disconnected mid-boss fight because their game subscription had lapsed. The incident, visible on a live Twitch stream, revealed the player had purchased a WoW Token to renew but forgot to activate it in time. Despite the setback, the raid team maintained composure and continued the fight. This underscores the challenges of managing multiple high-level accounts and the kind of logistical details that can affect even top-tier esports competitors.

Valve Veteran Criticizes Epic Games’ Layoffs and Leadership

Chet Faliszek, former Valve writer and developer, publicly criticized Epic Games CEO Tim Sweeney and the company’s recent layoffs of over 1,000 employees. Faliszek questioned the direction of Epic, which has prioritized Fortnite and profitability over broader game development, contrasting the current culture with Valve’s approach, where workers had more ownership and agency. He argued that mass layoffs harm employee morale and the industry by eroding passion and commitment. Faliszek’s comments come amid reports of internal disruption at Epic and reflect ongoing industry concerns about studio management and the impact of corporate decisions on development teams.

Neuralink Patient Uses Brain Implant to Play World of Warcraft After 100 Days

Jon Noble, a British Army veteran with paralysis, has managed to play *World of Warcraft* using a Neuralink brain implant just 100 days after surgery. The implant interprets neural signals from his motor cortex, enabling him to control the game entirely by thought, without mouse or keyboard. Noble described the experience as “pure magic” and showed fluid gameplay during raids and exploration. While still in trial phases and limited to a small number of participants, Neuralink’s progress demonstrates potential for greater autonomy for people with severe disabilities, beyond gaming, by providing new ways to interact with computers and devices.
