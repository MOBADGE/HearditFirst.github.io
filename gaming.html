James Bond Rights Owners Oppose James Pond Trademark Application

The rights holders behind the James Bond franchise, Danjaq LLC, have filed an opposition against a UK trademark application for "James Pond," a 1990s platformer series known for its parody of James Bond. Gameware and System 3, co-owners of James Pond, sought to trademark the name last year to support a planned sequel titled *James Pond and the Rogue AI*. However, Danjaq has opposed this move citing previous successful objections in Europe based on trademark laws, which in the UK do not protect parodies as US law does. The original creator of James Pond has also publicly criticized the upcoming sequel for relying on AI-generated content. This opposition could delay or prevent the sequel from fully launching under that name and highlights ongoing tensions between parody works and established trademarks.

Infocom’s Failed Business Software Now Runs on Modern PCs

Infocom, famed for its classic text adventure games like *Zork* and *The Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy*, released a business database program called Cornerstone in 1985. Unlike their games, this software was critically panned and contributed to the company’s downfall. Cornerstone was built on a virtual machine that made it slow and unpopular at a time when IBM-compatible PCs dominated the business market. Recently, an enthusiast named TaradinoC developed a modern interpreter called Linchpin that allows Cornerstone to run on current systems. This revival serves as a digital preservation effort and offers insight into Infocom's less successful ventures. It also underlines how pivoting away from core competencies can jeopardize even established studios.

Nexon Clarifies Team Changes at The First Berserker: Khazan Developer

Nexon has confirmed a reorganization within Neople, the studio behind *The First Berserker: Khazan*. While reports claimed the changes were due to disappointing sales of the game, Nexon stated the shift is part of a strategic talent reallocation as development winds down. *Khazan*, a standalone singleplayer title connected to the larger *Dungeon & Fighter* franchise, had a solid launch and maintains positive user reviews. Nexon emphasized no layoffs occurred and that the reallocation supports live operations and future projects within the franchise. This suggests the studio is preparing for upcoming titles rather than ending the franchise, though it may signal reduced near-term support for *Khazan* itself.

Darkstone Remaster Led by Original Creator

The 1999 action RPG *Darkstone*, known for its now-obscure but distinctive gameplay features such as a solo sidekick system and aging characters, is receiving a remaster. Original creator Paul Cuisset is leading the development of *Darkstone Restoration*, aiming to improve stability, compatibility, controls, and quality-of-life features. The remaster may also reinstate network functionality and add a quest editor depending on early access reception. The decision to release the remaster via early access is somewhat unusual but aims to provide a playable experience while gathering player feedback. While *Darkstone* is a niche title, this effort can bring renewed accessibility to a game that has suffered from technical issues on modern platforms.

Doki Doki Literature Club Removed from Google Play for Content Concerns

The cult visual novel *Doki Doki Literature Club* has been pulled from the Google Play Store over its depiction of sensitive themes including mental health and suicide. Despite its popularity since 2017 and positive reception for handling these topics with nuance, Google cited violations of content policies prohibiting apps that promote self-harm. The game's creators and publisher have expressed disappointment and are seeking reinstatement or alternate distribution methods on Android. The removal highlights ongoing challenges around content moderation and the balance between artistic expression and platform guidelines, particularly for titles that address difficult psychological themes.

Dune: Awakening Makes PvP Fully Optional

Funcom announced significant changes to PvP in *Dune: Awakening*, making previously mandatory PvP zones in the Deep Desert and Shipwreck areas optional. This follows longstanding player complaints about forced PvP interactions in endgame content. The update introduces separate PvP and PvE instances, allowing players to choose an experience suited to their preferences. PvP players will receive higher rewards to balance the risk. Additionally, early testing for self-hosted private servers is planned, enabling more customizable gameplay settings. These changes reflect a shift towards accommodating different player styles and underline the importance of flexibility in multiplayer MMORPG design.
