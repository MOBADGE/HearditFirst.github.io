Fallout Remasters Rumors Stirred by Steam Glitch, but No Confirmation Yet

Some Fallout fans were briefly excited by a Steam glitch that prevented them from leaving reviews for *Fallout 3* and *Fallout: New Vegas*, which led to speculation about possible remasters of these titles. Steam displayed a message saying that the games were not yet released, which is incorrect given both have been available for many years.

This error, combined with the ongoing *Fallout* TV series and fan interest following the *Oblivion* remaster, fueled hopes that remastered versions might be imminent. However, investigations suggest the glitch is unrelated to any remaster announcements. Similar issues affect other unrelated games, making the case for a software bug rather than a marketing tease. Additionally, Steam showing 12 Fallout games when only 10 are listed likely relates to regional versions of *New Vegas*, not new releases.

Microsoft Fixes 112 Vulnerabilities in January Update, a Decline from Last Year

Microsoft has addressed 112 security vulnerabilities in January 2026 across its Windows 11 operating system and related services. This marks a decrease of 68 fewer bugs fixed compared to the same period last year. Of these vulnerabilities, most are considered unlikely to be exploited, though eight are rated as "more likely" with severity scores of 7.8 out of 10.

Two of the highest severity cases (scored 8.8) involve remote code execution via the Routing and Remote Access Service and SharePoint Server. While such scores indicate potential risks, Microsoft emphasizes that severity scores reflect exploit complexity and impact rather than immediate threats. The company encourages users to keep their systems updated to maintain protection.
