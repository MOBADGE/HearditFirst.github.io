MIT Researchers Pioneer New Method to Increase Chip Transistor Density

A research collaboration between MIT, the University of Waterloo, and Samsung Electronics has developed a technique to fabricate an additional layer of transistors on the backside of finished semiconductor dies. Traditional CMOS chips face challenges in increasing transistor density due to costly smaller process nodes and material sensitivities to heat during manufacturing. The new approach applies a thin 2 nm layer of amorphous indium oxide transistors at the chip's back end, avoiding heat damage to the front-end transistor layer. Additionally, ferroelectric hafnium-zirconium oxide enables memory cell creation on these layers. While still experimental, this method could lead to significantly denser chips when combined with existing stacking technologies. This progress suggests Moore's Law's continuation despite recent concerns.

*Why it matters*: Higher transistor densities enable more powerful and efficient processors, which influence gaming hardware capabilities such as GPUs and CPUs. Innovations like this could impact future gaming performance and device designs.

KOTOR 2 Remake Revealed Through Court Filings; KOTOR 1 Remake Progress Hinted

Recent court documents from a lawsuit involving Aspyr have disclosed the existence of "Project Juliet," a full remake of *Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic 2* (KOTOR 2) planned by Aspyr and Lucasfilm. The remake would include modern art, updated gameplay, and restored cut content previously available only via a fan mod (TSLRCM). Although the project is "technically on the roadmap," focus remains on the *KOTOR 1* remake first. The *KOTOR 1* remake development has shifted studios from Aspyr to Mad Head Games (a Saber Interactive-owned studio) and has seen limited public updates since its 2021 announcement. Aspyr's efforts to officially release TSLRCM on Nintendo Switch faced legal and financial barriers, including objections from Disney's legal team over modder crediting.

*Why it matters*: KOTOR 2 is widely regarded as needing a remake to fulfill its original vision, and confirmation of its development may interest RPG fans. The legal complexities highlight challenges in modding communities and official game preservation efforts.

Total War: Warhammer 40k Officially Announced, Set for Console and PC Release

Creative Assembly confirmed the next *Total War* title centered on the *Warhammer 40,000* universe, titled *Total War: Warhammer 40k*. The game lacks a firm release date but is anticipated for 2027 or later, with more details expected in December 2026. It will launch on PC, PS5, and Xbox Series X/S, marking the first *Total War* game releasing on consoles. At launch, four factions will be available: Space Marines, Orks, Aeldari (Eldar), and Astra Militarum. Other iconic factions like Chaos Space Marines are planned post-launch. Features include army customization, Exterminatus (planetary destruction), orbital bombardments, and terrain-altering combat mechanics. The setting is the Era Indomitus, characterized by galaxy-spanning warfare and chaos following a major warp storm.
