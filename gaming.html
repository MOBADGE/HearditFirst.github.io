Intel Launches Core Ultra Series 3 Mobile Chips with Increased Gaming iGPU Performance

Intel unveiled its new Panther Lake-based Core Ultra Series 3 mobile processors at CES 2026, boasting up to a 77% improvement in integrated graphics (iGPU) gaming performance compared to the previous Lunar Lake generation. The top-tier processors, such as the Core Ultra X9 388H, are equipped with the Intel Arc B390 iGPU featuring 12 Xe3 cores and built-in ray tracing support. Intel also promises improvements in GPU drivers that could enhance gaming performance on slim and light laptops relying on integrated graphics.

The new lineup includes 14 chips ranging from high-end 16-core models to entry-level 8-core processors, some featuring advanced integrated GPUs (Arc B390 or B370) and others with more modest Intel Graphics iGPUs. This expanded range is designed to offer options for different laptop configurations, balancing PCIe lanes, clock speeds, memory speed, and power consumption. Intel emphasizes the architectural enhancements and power efficiency enabled by its RibbonFET switches and PowerVia backside power delivery, aiming for longer battery life alongside better performance.

This release matters as it continues to push the viability of integrated graphics in gaming laptops, potentially expanding options for gamers who prefer thin-and-light systems without discrete GPUs.

Nvidia Reveals DLSS 4.5 with Up to 6x Multi Frame Generation for 4K 240 Hz Gaming

Nvidia introduced DLSS 4.5 at CES 2026, highlighting its potential to enable 4K gaming at 240 Hz with path tracing, powered by an improved AI upscaler and a new Multi Frame Generation (MFG) capability. MFG, a frame interpolation method using AI to generate extra frames between rendered ones, now supports inserting up to five additional synthetic frames per real frame, multiplying the frame rate up to six times.

This advancement relies on a second-generation transformer model with enhanced training datasets and compute power, aiming to reduce visual artifacts like ghosting and improve smoothness. The feature is exclusive to RTX 50-series GPUs, with the potential to dynamically adjust frame generation levels based on display refresh rates or user presets through the Nvidia App.

While Nvidia's claims promise smoother gameplay in graphically demanding scenarios, the technology still requires robust hardware to maintain input latency and frame delivery. This incremental update could improve accessibility to high-refresh, high-resolution gaming experiences, although actual adoption will depend on users‚Äô acceptance of AI-generated frames and potential visual trade-offs.
