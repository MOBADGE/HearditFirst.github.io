**Ark: Survival Ascended DLC Revives and Simplifies Atlas’ Ocean Sailing**

Developers from Studio Wildcard are incorporating elements from their earlier game, Atlas, into Ark: Survival Ascended’s upcoming DLC, *Tides of Fortune*. Atlas, a pirate-themed survival game released in 2018, struggled with a punishing gameplay loop and technical issues, despite its innovative networked ocean world and ship-building mechanics. Studio Wildcard’s founders Jeremy Stieglitz and Jesse Rapczak acknowledged that while Atlas offered “crazy cool tech,” it was daunting for players due to its difficulty and labor-intensive systems, such as hauling individual logs and rebuilding ships plank by plank.

The *Tides of Fortune* DLC aims to salvage the satisfying parts of Atlas—the sailing mechanics and ocean physics—but distill them into a more accessible experience for Ark players. The new ocean will feature improved realistic waves, foamy wakes, and dynamic lighting effects, a notable upgrade over Ark’s previously flat water. Players will be able to construct customizable pirate ships more easily, with cannons and structural attachments, and even bring dinosaurs aboard. This shift represents an effort to leverage existing technology while avoiding the pitfalls that hampered Atlas’ reception.

**Monster Hunter Stories 3: Complete Guide to Mutations and How to Obtain Them**

Monster Hunter Stories 3 includes a mutation system that allows players to obtain rarer and stronger subspecies of Monsties by influencing ecosystem conditions. These mutations are tied to mechanics in the game’s environment restoration system: by restoring monster populations and meeting specific ecosystem criteria, players can trigger subspecies with enhanced stats and improved genes.

The article details all known mutations, such as Pink Rathian, Dreadqueen Rathian, Ivory Lagiacrus, Hellblade Glavenus, and others. Each mutation requires particular conditions, like raising an ecosystem rank to a certain level or introducing specific monsters into the habitat. For example, to unlock Dreadqueen Rathian, the player must have a high-rank Rathian variant in an environment populated by multiple poisonous monster species. The system encourages strategic release and management of monsters, providing an optional but advantageous challenge for players seeking to strengthen their team.

**Overwatch Director Reflects on Delayed Response to 5v5 vs. 6v6 Debate**

Aaron Keller, game director for Overwatch, addressed the controversial switch from 6v6 to 5v5 gameplay introduced with Overwatch 2 in 2022. The decision eliminated one tank role and changed team dynamics significantly, leading to player backlash and demands to revert to the original team size. Keller admitted at the 2026 Game Developers Conference that the development team waited over a year before seriously addressing community concerns, a delay that “cost us trust” among players.

Blizzard eventually introduced limited-time 6v6 modes in 2024, which led to the addition of a permanent 6v6 option, now the game’s second most popular mode. Keller noted that multiple competing main formats might not be healthy for the game’s long-term direction. He emphasized the importance of listening to player feedback and maintaining regular communication, framing these as key lessons for ongoing Overwatch development.

**Jeff Kaplan’s Early Vision for World of Warcraft: A First-Person MMO**

Former Blizzard vice president Jeff Kaplan revealed that he initially wanted World of Warcraft (WoW) to be a first-person game rather than the third-person perspective the game ultimately adopted. Kaplan said during a recent stream that he argued strongly for first-person to foster a closer connection with other players and appreciation for character art, drawing on his positive experiences with first-person MMORPG EverQuest.

While acknowledging the merits of third-person viewpoints for gameplay clarity, Kaplan’s reflections suggest that WoW might have shaped the industry differently had his vision prevailed. The first-person perspective could have affected game design, player interaction, and the broader evolution of MMO features. This insight offers a rare glimpse into early design debates on one of the most influential games in the industry.
