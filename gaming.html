Hytale Launches Early Access After Revival, Receives Warm Initial Reception

After nearly a decade of development and a cancellation by Riot Games last summer, Hytale has launched in early access, surprising many by making a comeback rather than disappearing entirely. The voxel-based adventure sandbox, reminiscent of Minecraft and created by former Minecraft modders at Hypixel Studios, attracted a large player base on launch day and became the most-watched game on Twitch with over 420,000 viewers. The early access version is functional but incomplete, with features like vendors and NPC villages still marked as works-in-progress. Gameplay involves familiar mechanics such as mining, crafting, exploring various biomes, and combat against creatures ranging from spiders to yetis. Hypixel's CEO had cautioned that the game "isn't good yet," indicating more development is planned. Nonetheless, the early access release secured enough preorders to fund two more years of development, marking a notable achievement for a game that once seemed doomed.

Ubisoft Announces Second Layoff Wave of 2026 at Swedish Studios

Ubisoft has announced layoffs affecting around 55 roles at Massive Entertainment and Ubisoft Stockholm, marking the company's second round of staff reductions this year. Massive is known for The Division series, Star Wars Outlaws, and Avatar: Frontiers of Pandora, while Ubisoft Stockholm is working on an unannounced title. The layoffs follow a voluntary career transition program launched in late 2025 but signal continued operational restructuring as Ubisoft seeks to optimize staffing according to new long-term roadmaps. Earlier in January, Ubisoft had closed its Halifax studio shortly after it unionized, though the company stated the closure was unrelated to the union vote. Ubisoft's strategy includes the formation of a new Tencent-backed company, Vanguard Studios, which will oversee major franchises Assassin's Creed, Far Cry, and Rainbow Six.
