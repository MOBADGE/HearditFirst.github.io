Overclocker Destroys $5,000 MSI RTX 5090 Lightning Z GPU Using Extreme Overclocking BIOS

Top overclocker Alva Jonathan showcased the destruction of an MSI RTX 5090 Lightning Z graphics card, valued at $5,000, due to running an extreme overclocking BIOS designed for 2,500 watts of power. While the card normally operates at a 575 W power limit (with options for 800 W and 1,000 W modes), the aggressive BIOS used a default voltage of 1.2 volts, suitable only for subzero LN2 cooling, applied here at ambient room temperature. This caused thermal shock and physical damage to the GPU die, cracking it. The card was a free MSI sample intended for extreme testing, illustrating the risks of pushing hardware beyond safe operating conditions, regardless of onboard safeguards or cooling systems.
