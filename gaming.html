Dragon Age 2’s Development Challenges Revealed by Creator

David Gaider, creator of the Dragon Age series, has discussed the difficult development process behind *Dragon Age 2*. The game was developed in just 16 months, a very short time for a role-playing game, forcing the team to drastically cut content. Gaider said BioWare initially planned for a much larger game, but upon learning about the limited schedule, he had to remove half of the quests. This hurried approach resulted in a “raw” writing style that was less refined than previous titles. Despite these constraints, Gaider praised the writing team for their dedication and ability to deliver under pressure. This insight sheds light on why *Dragon Age 2* remains a divisive entry in the series and helps explain some of its more noticeable design shortcuts.

On-Together Adds New Content to Virtual Co-Working Experience

*On-Together*, a virtual co-working game that helps players focus on tasks while socializing, has released its first content update of 2026. This update brings 14 new facial hair customization options, which were previously limited, and enhancements to focus environments. A notable addition is a decorated secret cave area with a campfire and seating where players can work together. The update also introduces a new minigame involving fishing for keys to unlock a buried treasure chest. These additions aim to enrich the social and immersive qualities of the game without undermining its productivity focus, offering more ways for users to personalize and engage with the co-working space.

Discord Fixes iOS Launch Delay Bug

Discord has resolved a bug affecting its iOS app that caused the program to take over two minutes to launch after a device restart. The issue occurred because the app’s background queue was overloaded with asset requests triggered during phone boot-up. Discord acknowledged the problem last week and implemented a fix. The delay mainly impacted mobile users and was separate from other performance concerns some players reported in competitive games when Discord was running. While Discord postponed the worldwide rollout of its age verification system in response to criticism, the company continues to address technical issues to maintain user experience on its platform.

Nintendo and Lenovo Seek Refunds Following Tariff Ruling

Nintendo, Lenovo, and several other companies are pursuing refunds for tariffs paid under former President Trump’s use of the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA), recently ruled unlawful by the US Supreme Court. Nintendo’s legal filing requests repayment with interest for duties imposed, citing significant financial and operational disruptions, including delays in announcing the Nintendo Switch 2’s US pricing. Lenovo has submitted similar claims seeking refunds and legal costs. The process to refund hundreds of billions in tariffs may be slow due to the complexity and volume of claims involved. The outcome could set a precedent for other affected companies and raise questions about past price increases that consumers absorbed while tariffs were in effect.

3D Printed Laptop ‘Suitcase’ Offers New eGPU Solution

A 3D printed external GPU (eGPU) enclosure, designed like a suitcase, has been created to address the scarcity of external graphics cards suited to laptops. MakerWorld user MPKVR adapted an Ikea pegboard to house a laptop on one side and a full-size desktop GPU, power supply, and controller holder on the other. The design prioritizes portability around a workspace rather than long-distance travel, as components are held by friction rather than locks. This approach allows gamers to upgrade laptops with more powerful desktop cards, overcoming the power and performance limits of mobile GPUs. It’s a practical DIY solution for improving laptop gaming performance without relying on limited commercial eGPU units.

Slay the Spire 2’s Golden Compass Explained

In *Slay the Spire 2*, the Golden Compass is a unique starting bonus offered by the floor guardian Tezcatara at the beginning of Act 2. When selected, it replaces the usual Act 2 map with a single fixed path that players must follow to reach the boss. This path includes a predetermined sequence of battles, encounters, campfires, shops, and elite fights. While the route’s exact configuration may vary, it maintains a balance of challenge and rest opportunities. Although it limits player choice compared to the regular branching map, the Golden Compass provides a fresh variation for players who want a different gameplay experience without the need for strategic route planning.
