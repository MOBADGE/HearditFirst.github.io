Dean Hall Reflects on Gaming Habits and Development Plans

Dean "Rocket" Hall, creator of DayZ and founder of RocketWerkz, shared insights into his extensive gaming history and current projects in a recent interview. Hall revealed a personal attachment to games like RimWorld, where he has logged over 1,400 hours, and Kerbal Space Program, with more than 2,000 hours played. Notably, he also still plays the original XCOM extensively, including during a Mount Everest expedition. Hall is currently focused on supporting RocketWerkz's survival game Icarus, which has begun turning a profit after prolonged updates. He indicated plans for a future large-scale project resembling DayZ or ArmA. This reflection highlights how deep player engagement can shape developers’ visions and ongoing game support strategies.

Cosplay and Consent: Community Concerns at UK Conventions

Recent conventions in Birmingham, UK—including DreamHack UK and MegaCon—have sparked discussions about harassment and consent within the cosplay community. Multiple reports surfaced of cosplayers being photographed or filmed without permission and subjected to disruptive behavior. The phrase "Cosplay is not consent" was emphasized as a reminder that dressing in costume does not grant others the right to invade personal space or capture images without explicit approval. Attendees and creators advocate for more respectful interactions, encouraging people to ask before taking photos or videos to maintain a safe and welcoming environment. This highlights ongoing social issues within fan communities and the importance of consent in public spaces.

Samson Addresses Bugs Following Mixed Reception

Samson, an open-world crime game developed by Liquid Swords, has received a mixed response since its recent launch. The game’s reception has been affected by numerous bugs and performance issues, resulting in a 53% rating on Steam. Developer Christofer Sundberg acknowledged these shortcomings, citing production difficulties including layoffs and reworking the project to a smaller scale. Liquid Swords released a hotfix addressing stability problems and committed to further updates focused on polishing gameplay. Despite the challenges, Samson’s moderate launch success suggests ongoing player interest in open-city games, while the developer’s transparency and roadmap may help regain user trust over time.

Far Cry 2 Reviewed: A Look Back at a Pioneering FPS

A retrospective review of Far Cry 2 (2008) highlights its unique approach to open-world first-person shooters. The game is praised for its dynamic narrative system, realistic combat mechanics, and immersive African setting, offering players a high degree of freedom. Far Cry 2’s adaptive story changes based on player choices, fostering emergent storytelling. Its commitment to a first-person perspective throughout and realistic healing mechanics contributes to player immersion. However, criticisms include underdeveloped characters, repetitive missions, and vehicle combat limitations. Overall, the game remains notable for advancing the open-world FPS genre with a focus on narrative variability and atmosphere.

Anno 117 Expands with Volcano-Featured DLC

Ubisoft announced the first DLC for Anno 117: Pax Romana, titled Prophecies of Ash, releasing on April 23. The expansion introduces the largest island in the series—the volcanic island of Cinis—which adds new resources such as Obsidian and challenges like eruptions and volcanic winters. This integration of natural disasters into city-building gameplay introduces new strategic considerations. The DLC also includes a temple to Vulcan, enhancing production and fire safety. Anno 117 has faced mixed reviews due to perceived incompleteness and prior controversies, yet it achieved strong sales. This DLC seeks to enrich the game’s world and mechanics, addressing some player expectations.

The Planet Crafter 2.0 Update and Discount

The Planet Crafter, a terraforming-focused survival game, received a comprehensive 2.0 update aimed at improving visuals, gameplay balance, and content. The update adds a new secret biome on the main planet, enhances existing biomes, and introduces new craftable items and traversal options such as a rover jetpack chip. A notable feature lets players observe terraforming progress on moons orbiting the main planet. To coincide with the update, the game is available at half price on Steam until April 20. These improvements reflect the developer’s intention to refine the experience for both new and returning players, maintaining the game’s positive reputation in the survival genre.
