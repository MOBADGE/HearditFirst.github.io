Witchhunter.exe Offers Retro Adventure with a Twist

A new game called Witchhunter.exe has appeared in early development. This retro-style adventure casts players as a witch hunter in a Salem-inspired setting, interrogating a group of women to uncover who is allied with the Devil. What sets it apart is the incorporation of supernatural "glitch-horror" where the Devil intrudes via computer pop-ups, blending a choose-your-own-adventure format with an old-school Gold Box D&D user interface. Combat involves timed word-typing or selection before attacks land. While the haunted simulation concept has been done before, this game seeks to capture the mood of classic witch-hunting horror films. A beta test is planned soon on Steam, allowing players to follow its development.

Five New Steam Games to Explore

Five lesser-known games recently launched on Steam:

- *Mama's Sleeping Angels*: A multiplayer dream-world shooter with "cursed objects," mixing surreal elements and Y2K aesthetics. - *Lucid Blocks*: A Minecraft-like voxel game with infinite procedural worlds that stretch vertically, featuring odd art styles and survival against strange creatures. - *The State of Nowhere*: A Papers, Please-style game focused on rationing food in a totalitarian regime, with 46 endings and various play modes. - *Thysiastery*: A pixel-art, turn-based dungeon crawler roguelike set in a shifting labyrinth, featuring numerous skills and party-focused gameplay. - *Pluto*: A roguelike deckbuilder dungeon crawler with unusual visuals reminiscent of Eye of the Beholder and quirky characters, including mutant sloths.

These games highlight a range of indie innovations in familiar genres, offering fresh experiences for players seeking variety.

Tomb Raider Remastered Patch Sparks Controversy

The remastered collection of Tomb Raider I-III received a free update that added a new challenge mode and ten new character outfits. However, fan response has been largely negative due to the low quality of these new assets. Players reported bugs affecting audio, visuals, enemy AI, and lost save files. Concerns arose about potential use of generative AI in creating the outfits, though this has not been confirmed. The original lead artist of the remaster distanced himself from the patch and publicly criticized its quality. This update follows earlier issues where unauthorized AI-generated voice lines were removed, highlighting ongoing tensions around AI’s role in game development.

Abubakar Salim Opens Up About His Gaming Habits and PC Setup

Actor and game developer Abubakar Salim shared insights into his gaming history and current habits. Known for roles such as Bayek in Assassin’s Creed: Origins and founding Surgent Studios, Salim recalled early experiences with classic PC games like Solitaire, Warcraft, and World of Warcraft. Currently, he enjoys games like *No Rest for the Wicked* and *Dave the Diver*, appreciating their progression loops. Salim revealed that his oldest installed game is Warcraft 3 and the game with the most hours logged is Garry’s Mod, which he played extensively during school years. He also discussed his notably cluttered desktop and the importance of scriptwriting software to his creative process. His commentary offers a personal look at how game developers and actors engage with games both professionally and as players.

Mirror’s Edge-Inspired Freerunning Game in Development

An indie developer, Viridian Matters, announced *Panline*, a new freerunning game inspired by Mirror’s Edge. It features parkour mechanics including wall-running and speed-building within an open, non-linear city. Instead of Mirror’s Edge’s signature red highlights, Panline uses a varied color palette and includes an in-game PDA resembling a PlayStation Portable. Jobs appear dynamically to guide exploration and challenge progression. A unique feature is that falling to death wipes progress for the current task, adding risk to traversal. The game is aiming for release in 2026, building on the developer’s previous parkour title, which received positive reviews.

GTA Roleplay Hide-and-Seek Emerges as a New Trend

A new hide-and-seek variant in GTA 5 roleplay servers has gained popularity, involving players risking their vehicles in tense matches. The winner gains the right to damage the loser’s car, adding real consequence to the game. Unlike regular GTA Online, where car repair is automated and covered by insurance, GTA RP requires arranging repairs through other players, often at great expense.
