OpenAI Robotics Leader Resigns Over Ethical Concerns

Caitlin Kalinowski, a hardware leader at OpenAI, has resigned citing serious concerns about the company's recent involvement with the U.S. Department of War. Kalinowski expressed discomfort with the prospect of AI being used for surveillance of Americans without judicial oversight and the deployment of lethal autonomous weapons without human authorization. The resignation follows OpenAI’s controversial deal to supply AI tools for military use after another AI firm, Anthropic, declined such involvement. OpenAI CEO Sam Altman acknowledged the rush and promise to implement safeguards, but Kalinowski criticized the lack of thorough deliberation before making such agreements. Her departure highlights ongoing ethical tensions around AI's military applications and government collaborations.

Thermal Grizzly Exposed to €40,000 Metal Scam

Thermal Grizzly, a PC cooling hardware company, recently uncovered a significant scam in its supply of copper and aluminum metal sheets. After sourcing materials from Chinese suppliers to offset rising copper prices, the company discovered the metals were not pure but counterfeit. Testing revealed copper-plated steel sheets instead of genuine copper, and aluminum pallets were partially filled with empty space and steel layers masked by genuine metal on top. This fraud led to a loss estimated at €40,000 for Thermal Grizzly. While some scrap value can be recovered, the company faces limited recourse due to the suppliers being overseas. The incident underscores risks businesses face when sourcing critical raw materials amid volatile markets.

Big Walk Offers Cooperative Puzzle Experience Before Launch

The upcoming co-op puzzle game Big Walk, from House House (known for Untitled Goose Game), has been previewed by select players. Set in a quirky open world, the game focuses on cooperative puzzle solving requiring teamwork and communication. Players control blob-like characters navigating various puzzles that scale with the number of participants. Beyond puzzles, the game features social spaces for players to interact, such as a dance party by the ocean or collaborative character customization. A demo will be available starting March 16 but only at the publisher Panic's Portland, Oregon office, requiring an in-person visit. The hands-on demo setup uses proximity chat to emphasize social collaboration. Though not widely accessible, the preview indicates an engaging cooperative experience that emphasizes teamwork without combat or stress.

Dragon Age 2 Development Revealed as a Rush Job

David Gaider, creator of the Dragon Age series, has shared details about the rushed development of Dragon Age 2. The game was developed under a tight 16-month schedule, forcing significant cuts to content including half of the planned quests. Gaider explained that BioWare initially planned DA2 as a large game but lacked experience making smaller-scale titles, leading to a "butcher's job" in cutting down the scope. Despite these constraints, the veteran writing team worked efficiently but produced a "raw" narrative with less refinement than usual. This pressure-filled process provides context for the sometimes divisive reception of Dragon Age 2, which remains a "cult classic" within the franchise. The story underscores challenges studios face balancing ambition and deadlines.

Discord Fixes Long Launch Times on iOS

Discord has resolved a bug causing the iOS app to take up to two minutes to launch after a device restart. The slowdown resulted from an asset request mistakenly placed on a background queue that became overwhelmed during phone boot-up. The company acknowledged the issue in recent patch notes and confirmed the fix has been applied. Some users had also reported frame rate stutters in competitive games while Discord was running, but no resolution for that has been detailed. Meanwhile, Discord’s ongoing global rollout of age verification checks has been delayed until late 2026. Addressing stability and performance issues remains a priority as Discord navigates user safety and platform policies.

Nintendo and Lenovo Seek Tariff Refunds After Supreme Court Ruling

Nintendo and Lenovo are pursuing refunds for tariffs imposed under former President Trump's use of the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA), recently ruled unlawful by the U.S. Supreme Court. Both companies have filed complaints seeking repayment with interest for duties paid, following a ruling that challenges the legality of tariff enforcement. Nintendo’s hardware pricing and launch plans, including the Switch 2, were impacted by the tariffs last year. While the legal actions could set precedents for other companies, Customs and Border Protection has indicated refund processing will not start until at least April, citing the complexity of the massive task. The outcomes could influence future trade policy and pricing strategies in the gaming hardware sector
