Blizzard's World of Warcraft Team Opts Out of Using Generative AI in Development

Blizzard's approach to generative AI (gen AI) remains cautious, with key developers emphasizing minimal or no use of this technology in their game development. Leo Kaliski, lead composer for the upcoming *World of Warcraft: Midnight* expansion, stated in an interview that their music score is entirely human-made and that the team feels "very lucky and happy" not to rely on gen AI. This aligns with previous statements from Blizzard staff, including former WoW franchise director John Hight, who confirmed no gen AI was used in the MMO, and Overwatch director Aaron Keller, who expressed discomfort with releasing AI-generated content.

While Blizzard's president, Johanna Faries, mentioned the company has a centralized AI governance team to explore the potential role of AI, individual teams appear to have freedom to decide if and how to experiment with it. This measured stance contrasts with other companies openly embracing AI. Given Blizzard's parent company Microsoft’s known interest in AI integration, the future use of generative AI in Blizzard games remains uncertain, though internal union efforts may influence this direction.

GTA 5’s Squid Game Mods Improve Significantly After Several Years

Squid Game-themed mods for *Grand Theft Auto 5* have seen dramatic improvement over the past four years. Initially, these mods suffered from poor scripting, lackluster assets, and unconvincing AI, failing to capture the intensity and atmosphere of the original Netflix series. However, recent fan projects have introduced more polished mechanics, including reliable detection logic, configurable timers, and defined elimination rules, especially for popular minigames like "Red Light-Green Light."

Mod developers have incorporated thematic NPC behavior that balances the deadly aspects of Squid Game with GTA’s more irreverent tone. Some mods feature custom maps with spectator cameras and announcers, adding to player immersion. Community feedback suggests a desire for further features such as player progression and tournaments, indicating a growing, sustainable modding scene that has moved beyond its original rough state.

Space Marine 2’s $5 Voice Lines DLC Faces Player Backlash

The recent release of a $5 voice lines DLC for *Space Marine 2* has drawn significant criticism from players. The pack offers additional voice options and effects but lacks quantity and quality compared to original in-game voices. Users report that default mission voices override the new lines, breaking immersion, and some suspect that the advertised number of voice lines includes filler grunts and multiple languages, not unique content.

Feedback on Steam and Reddit highlights disappointment over the perceived value and execution. Many feel the add-on provides an inferior audio experience at a notable cost, fueling accusations of poor business practices. This marks a rare but notable backlash against DLC offerings in the game’s early post-launch period.

Major Update to Star Wars TIE Fighter Mod Adds Features and Prepares for Co-op

The fan-made mod *TIE Fighter: Total Conversion*, which ports and enhances the 1994 game *TIE Fighter* using the 1999 *X-Wing Alliance* engine, has received a substantial update. The latest patch separates the mod into "Classic" and "Reimagined" versions with differing levels of modernization, improves stability through background processes, and refreshes menu concourses with new HD art and animations.

Mod developer Angel described the update as nearly a year in the making, laying groundwork for future improvements. Planned features include cooperative play and a third game version solidifying current gameplay balance. The effort exemplifies ambitious fan dedication to preserving and evolving classic Star Wars space combat games.

Upcoming Teardown Multiplayer Update Expands Gameplay With Co-op and Competitive Modes

*Teardown* is set to launch its long-anticipated multiplayer update on March 12. The update introduces cooperative play for the full single-player campaign (excluding DLC missions), free-roaming sandbox modes, and competitive game types like team deathmatch. The multiplayer supports up to 12 players on PC, with no integrated voice or text chat but open to external communication solutions.

Developer Tuxedo Labs also plans to add new multiplayer modes later, including a racing experience. This move marks a major expansion for the physics-based destruction sim, potentially increasing its longevity and community engagement following recent additions like creative and third-person modes.
