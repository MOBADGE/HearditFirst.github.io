Batman: Arkham Origins Receives Advanced Graphics Mod

A graphics mod for *Batman: Arkham Origins*, called Arkham Origins Advanced Graphics, has recently been updated to version 2.0, significantly enhancing the game's visual presentation. The mod increases snow particle effects and deformation, adjusts lighting for a more immersive wintry atmosphere, and upgrades textures by incorporating higher-resolution assets from *Arkham Knight* and multiplayer suits. Shadow quality improvements reduce visual artifacts, and reflections are notably enhanced. However, cutscenes remain unchanged due to technical limitations.

This mod is relevant for players seeking a visual refresh that approaches a remaster without an official re-release. Installation requires downloading from Nexus Mods and having at least one DLC installed owing to the game's configuration.

Death Stranding 2 Surpasses Two Million Copies After PC Launch

*Death Stranding 2: On the Beach* has sold over two million copies worldwide following its PC release, which accounted for 425,000 sales in its first week. Combined with 1.6 million sales on PlayStation 5, the milestone comes less than a year after the initial console launch. The game's PC debut marked one of the fastest arrivals of a PlayStation exclusive onto PC, taking nine months—faster than other recent ports.

In addition to boosting total sales, the PC launch coincided with increased PlayStation console sales after price reductions. This suggests renewed interest and visibility for the title. The sequel continues to explore themes of grief and connection beneath its sci-fi setting.

Star Wars Zero Company Director Aims to Evolve Turn-Based Tactics Genre

Greg Foertsch, creative director of *Star Wars: Zero Company*, has expressed intentions to challenge long-standing assumptions about turn-based tactics games. He argues that these games need not sacrifice story, graphics, or elegant controls for depth. Foertsch contends that narrative and visual immersion enhance player engagement rather than detract from gameplay.

The game aims to balance complex mechanics with polished production values, providing a single-player experience rich in both strategy and emotional content. This perspective follows Foertsch’s experience on *Marvel’s Midnight Suns*, a tactics game praised for marrying quality storytelling with deep gameplay.

Epic Games Layoffs Affect Terminally Ill Programmer; CEO Responds

Among the recent layoffs at Epic Games affecting over a thousand employees was Mike Prinke, a technical writer undergoing treatment for terminal brain cancer. Prinke lost both his job and employer-provided health coverage, leading to concerns expressed publicly by his wife regarding medical expenses and insurance.

Epic CEO Tim Sweeney responded, indicating the studio is in contact with the family to resolve insurance coverage. He stated that Prinke’s medical condition did not influence the layoff decision and apologized for the lack of proactive handling. This situation highlights the challenges of employer-dependent healthcare systems, especially during mass layoffs.
