Gloomwood Adds Controller Support, Improving Steam Deck Compatibility

After years of waiting, the stealth-focused immersive sim *Gloomwood* has finally received full controller support. This update significantly improves playability on handheld PCs such as the Steam Deck, eliminating the need for community-generated controller layouts and adding in-game gamepad tutorials and customizable bindings. Previously, while the game was playable on Steam Deck, controls were cumbersome and lacked proper input prompts, which hindered the experience of this Thief-like first-person stealth game.

The game also received a new in-game shop in the market plaza as part of today's update. Originally launched on Steam in early access in 2022, *Gloomwood* continues to attract positive player feedback and frequent updates, including a major new vertical map released in January. With the new controller support and a discount currently running on Steam, the update marks a notable improvement for fans and potential new players.

Look Outside RPG Receives Balance Updates in Latest Patch

The indie cosmic horror RPG *Look Outside* has received a new patch (version 2.3) focusing on balance and gameplay tweaks. Notable changes include alterations to character abilities, such as the rat child's "Copy Dad" skill no longer replicating gun skills without cost, as well as adjustments to enemy attacks and behaviors to better balance encounters.

*Look Outside* is recognized for its unique blend of Earthbound-style menu combat and horror elements, with a development pedigree linked to *Barkley, Shut Up and Jam: Gaiden*. Despite a surprise release last year, the game continues to receive active updates that refine its complex systems and content. The ongoing support underscores the developers’ commitment to an evolving experience for a niche but dedicated audience.

Cave Story+ Gains Co-op and Mod Support in Major 22-Year Update

*Cave Story+*, a landmark indie metroidvania originally released on PC in 2004, has received a significant update on Steam, aligning it with features long offered on Nintendo Switch. The update introduces two-player local co-op play, various visual enhancements such as animated dialogue portraits and improved water effects, widescreen support, and a challenging new Sand Pit level. Additionally, mod support now allows players to apply cosmetic changes like new graphics, fonts, and sound packs.

While *Cave Story+’s* gameplay and difficulty remain somewhat dated compared to modern peers, the update preserves the game’s historic significance and enduring appeal. Given its influence on later indie platformers and the rare nature of such a substantial update after more than two decades, this release serves as an important moment for fans and new players alike.

Windrose Developers Seek ISP Assistance to Address Co-op Connectivity Issues

The co-op survival game *Windrose* has found early success with high player counts and positive reviews but is experiencing ongoing online connectivity problems. Developer Kraken Express has publicly requested help from players with contacts at major EU or North American internet service providers to diagnose and resolve these issues. Some players report severe lag and multi-second spikes even when playing geographically close to each other.

Additionally, users attempting to run dedicated servers have noted limited configuration options and unusual network behavior, notably involving server endpoints routed through Russia. Although the Uzbekistan-based developer has stated that multiple backend services operate worldwide and the issue may stem from ISP restrictions or blocklists, clarity remains lacking. Kraken Express is working on hotfixes but acknowledges the need for external expertise to fully identify and resolve these network difficulties.

Take-Two Share Price Rises Modestly Despite Rockstar Hack Data Leak

Following a recent data breach of Rockstar Games, where hackers released some stolen information after Rockstar refused to pay a ransom, Take-Two Interactive’s share price experienced a short-term increase. The leak, however, revealed only a limited amount of non-critical data, with no significant details about the highly anticipated *Grand Theft Auto 6*.

Stock analysts suggest the share price bump reflects market recognition of Take-Two’s strong ongoing revenue from *GTA Online*, rather than any material impact from the hack. Despite recent volatility, Take-Two’s overall year-to-date stock performance remains down substantially from early 2026 levels. Investors now await Take-Two’s upcoming quarterly financial report in May for clearer insight into the company’s fiscal health and any updates on major titles.
