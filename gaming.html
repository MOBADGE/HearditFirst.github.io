Roguelike 'Pathogenic' Taps Into Lost Potential of Spore's Cell Stage

A new roguelike twin-stick shooter called *Pathogenic* draws inspiration from the "cell stage" of the 2008 game *Spore*. Unlike *Spore*'s single-celled organism, *Pathogenic* has players controlling a microscopic parasite invading a human body. The game emphasizes evolution and customization, allowing players to modify their organism‚Äôs "organelles" to influence combat abilities and movement.

Gameplay combines responsive twin-stick shooting with strategic customization, where damage buffs and synergies encourage thoughtful combat rather than indiscriminate firing. Players unlock new parts by defeating enemies, and can evolve their parasite into new forms with unique stats. The game integrates roguelike elements smoothly, including a clear UI and convenient fast travel between cleared rooms.

*Pathogenic* is still in development but features a polished demo currently available. Its success in realizing some of *Spore*'s overlooked potential highlights renewed interest in revisiting the concept of biological evolution in games.

Google Fast Pair Bluetooth Audio Devices Found Vulnerable to Remote Hacking

The flaw could potentially allow attackers to join connections without detection, enabling microphone eavesdropping or real-time location tracking through Google‚Äôs Find Hub network.
