Games Using Subtle RNG Bias to Improve Player Experience

The developers of the superhero comedy game *Dispatch* revealed that, like Firaxis's *XCOM* series, they adjust random success probabilities behind the scenes to favor players. In *Dispatch*’s dispatching minigame, any task with a calculated success chance above 76% is automatically marked as successful—up to three times in a row—to prevent players from experiencing frustrating streaks of bad luck. Conversely, very low chances are slightly boosted. These "invisible helpers" are disabled in the game's challenging final episode to increase difficulty. This approach reflects a broader industry understanding that purely random outcomes often feel unfair, and subtle adjustments can create a more satisfying player experience.

Garry Newman Discusses Pricing for s&box Game Platform

Garry Newman, creator of *Garry’s Mod*, spoke about pricing plans for *s&box*, Facepunch Studios’ upcoming game creation platform. While no final price has been confirmed, Newman indicated it will likely be in the range of $10 to $20—similar to the original *Garry’s Mod*’s $10 price point from 20 years ago. *s&box* is expected to launch in April, offering a modern toolset that enables players to create and share custom multiplayer game modes. The pricing strategy balances accessibility with funding Facepunch’s ongoing development.

*Crabmeat* Combines Horror and Point-and-Click Mechanics

A new indie horror title, *Crabmeat*, released on Steam with a 10% launch discount, puts players in the role of a penal colony worker harvesting crabs in an Antarctic ocean filled with creepy crustaceans. The game blends point-and-click exploration with survival horror elements, using mouse controls to navigate underwater environments and manage crab traps while avoiding monstrous threats. *Crabmeat* builds tension from the unsettling nature of crabs and isolation, offering a short, atmospheric experience for fans of both genres.

Roguelite Monster-Collection Game by Pokémon Streamer Raises $800K on Kickstarter

The Kickstarter campaign for *Wonderful Neoran Valley*, a roguelite monster-collector game inspired by titles like *Slay the Spire* and *Inscryption*, exceeded its $100,000 goal in just 10 minutes, currently surpassing $800,000 with 18 days remaining. Uniquely, it replaces traditional monster-catching mechanics with dialogue-based recruitment, where players convince creatures to join through conversations. The game's creative director is Italian Pokémon streamer Cydonia, whose popularity has helped fuel the campaign’s rapid success. Stretch goals include an endless mode and a Switch 2 port.

Brendan Greene Comments on AI Impact and Challenges

Brendan Greene, creator of *PlayerUnknown’s Battlegrounds*, gave an interview discussing concerns about artificial intelligence and large language models (LLMs). He criticized the rise of AI-generated content on the internet, warning that LLMs often recycle low-quality data which then becomes accepted as truth, creating a feedback loop detrimental to information quality. Greene expressed skepticism about achieving true artificial general intelligence anytime soon, describing current AI as statistical models that "hallucinate" and require fact-checking. He advocates for more localized computing solutions rather than scaling data centers, noting environmental and societal impacts.

*Greedfall: The Dying World* Shows Ambition but Feels Rough After Early Access

After two years in early access, *Greedfall: The Dying World* has officially released its 1.0 version. The prequel to *Greedfall* changes its combat system to real-time with pause but suffers from easy, unengaging battles and numerous bugs, including crashes and progression soft locks. While the game explores colonial themes with a new protagonist and offers branching storylines, its world lacks reactive depth, and stealth mechanics can be exploited. The game’s polish issues and design choices make it a mixed experience, appealing mainly to players with a tolerance for "eurojank" RPGs. Its soundtrack and setting stand out as positive aspects.

Bungie Responds to *Marathon* Battle Pass Feedback with More Skins

Following player criticism of *Marathon*’s season one battle pass for offering limited character customization and few premium rewards despite a $10 price, Bungie announced plans to add more cosmetics. Three new premium shell skins and one free shell skin will be added mid-April
