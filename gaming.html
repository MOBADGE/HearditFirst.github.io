**Guild Wars 1 Introduces New 'Ironman' Mode After 21 Years** Guild Wars 1, a unique MMO blending deckbuilding and action RPG elements, has received a significant update named Melandru's Accord. Launched on January 28, this mode draws inspiration from community-driven challenges, imposing restrictions such as forbidding player trading and the use of mercenaries. These changes encourage players to acquire all skills and items through self-found means, similar to hardcore modes found in titles like World of Warcraft: Classic Hardcore and Old School RuneScape. Besides the new mode, the patch also improves Steam Deck touch support, refines the user interface, and increases progression speed in the original Prophecies campaign by reducing skill costs and boosting mission experience and rewards. This update offers both new and returning players a reason to revisit the original Guild Wars experience, which remains distinctive amid the MMO landscape.

**Fallout Series Available at Discounted Prices on Steam** Valve's Steam platform is currently hosting a sale offering the entire Fallout series, including DLCs and spin-offs, bundled for $77. This price represents a substantial reduction and provides an opportunity for newcomers to explore the franchise. The series spans top-down, turn-based games like the original Fallout and Fallout 2, as well as open-world RPGs developed under Bethesda, such as Fallout 3, New Vegas, and Fallout 4. The sale also includes the online survival-oriented Fallout 76. Each title in the series offers different gameplay experiences and narrative styles, from the tactical depth of early games to the more action-focused and base-building elements in later entries. The sale runs until February 6, offering a chance for players to acquire these well-regarded games at a favorable price.

**Rust Implements New Anti-Cheat Measures Involving Hardware Security** The developer of Rust, Facepunch Studios, announced plans to enhance the game's anti-cheat system beginning in March by enabling server owners to restrict access to players whose systems have Secure Boot and TPM 2.0 enabled. Secure Boot and TPM are hardware and firmware security features increasingly used to prevent cheating in competitive multiplayer games. Initially optional, these requirements may become mandatory in the future. The move reflects a broader industry trend toward more invasive anti-cheat techniques operating at the kernel level. Responses from the Rust community have been mixed, with some supporting efforts to reduce cheating, while others express concerns about hardware costs and privacy implications. **Classic Space Combat Simulator 'Star Quest 1 in the 27th Century' Updated and Released for Free** Thirty years after its original 1995 release, the space combat simulator Star Quest 1 in the 27th Century has been updated to run on modern Windows systems without requiring DOS emulators. Developer Paul Lauzon also added support for game controllers and joystick setups. The game, which was notable for seamless transitions between open space and planetary atmospheres, features several mission types including dogfighting, racing, and surface attacks, along with over 60 different spaceships. It includes 3D graphics features that were advanced for its time, such as Gouraud shading and texture mapping. The updated version is now available as a free download on itch.io, with optional contributions welcomed. This release revives a lesser-known title from the 1990s space sim genre for contemporary audiences.

**Barony Dungeon Crawler Receives Major Update Overhauling Magic and Environments** Barony, a cooperative dungeon crawler inspired by Ultima Underworld and active since 2015, has launched the first part of a two-stage update called Instruments of Destruction. The update introduces a new magic system dividing abilities into three skill trees: Sorcery for direct offensive and defensive spells, Thaumaturgy for buffs and dealing with spirits, and Mysticism for manipulation and transformation tactics. Additionally, the update enhances dungeon generation, adding new room types such as treasure chambers, traps, and puzzles, and expanding area variety in crystal caves and mystic citadels with larger and more diverse interiors. These changes aim to refresh the gameplay experience and encourage player engagement. Part two of the update will bring further level design improvements.
