Slay the Spire 2 Introduces Multiplayer Mode and Breaks Records

Slay the Spire 2, the sequel to the influential deckbuilding roguelite, has added multiplayer co-op functionality for up to four players. One player hosts a multiplayer save and others join via the main menu using Steam friends. There is no matchmaking or local co-op, and all players must join from the start to maintain a consistent save. While the multiplayer experience retains many familiar elements from the singleplayer mode, some mechanics differ: enemy difficulty scales with party size, energy pools are individual, and loot including relics and cards is not shared but given individually. Players move as a unit on the map but make independent inventory and event choices. Ascension progression in multiplayer is separate and depends on the lowest unlocked level among players.

This new multiplayer mode extends the game's appeal beyond solo runs and encourages cooperative deckbuilding strategy. Meanwhile, Slay the Spire 2’s launch success has been historic. Data from analytics firm Newzoo shows it as the best-performing deckbuilder of all time based on peak concurrent players and sales. It reached a peak of 575,000 concurrent users shortly after early access launch, vastly surpassing the peaks of popular deckbuilders like Balatro and the first Slay the Spire. Over the first 11 days, the game averaged over a million daily active users on Steam alone, with roughly 3 million copies sold within the first week. Analysts suggest that years of anticipation and player trust in developer Mega Crit fueled this major launch performance.

Marathon Review Highlights Bungie’s Return to Extraction Shooters

Bungie's new game, Marathon, is a first-person extraction shooter that revives the studio’s early FPS legacy. Set on the planet Tau Ceti IV, players take on digital “shells” to complete contracts in a sci-fi world rich with lore. Marathon combines intense PvP gunfights with loot-driven progression and unique map designs. The game’s emphasis on short matches and accessible gunplay distinguishes it from other extraction shooters, making tense combat encounters repeatable and satisfying.

The environments are visually distinct with a blend of art and nature, reflecting Bungie’s signature style. Weapon design is varied, with 28 guns at launch that each fulfill a role in combat. The game’s non-linear storytelling integrates logs and audio chats to deepen narrative context beyond the shooting. While some features, such as the UI and initial battle pass, show room for improvement, Marathon has been praised for successfully merging gunplay, lore, and competitive elements. The upcoming seasonal wipe and limited number of maps remain open questions for long-term player retention.

City Builder Nova Roma Features Jealous Roman Gods Alongside Demanding Citizens

Nova Roma, a new city builder entering early access, adds a twist on the traditional management sim by including Roman gods as demanding, jealous entities affecting gameplay. As players grow their civilization on a picturesque island, they must build temples to various gods, each offering specific bonuses tied to city production or resource yields. However, gods require continual offerings and building progression in return, and neglected gods manifest as imposing apparitions that threaten disasters like floods, fires, or invasions.

This layer of divine interaction creates a dynamic where players balance pleasantries toward both citizens and gods. Gods provide valuable bonuses and tech progress but come with quests and demands that can disrupt city operations. The game’s pace is quicker than many city builders, maintaining constant player engagement. Early impressions note the god system’s strategic depth as the main distinguishing feature.

OpenAI Ends Video Generation App Sora, Impacting Disney Investment Plans

OpenAI announced it is discontinuing its AI video generation app Sora less than six months after the launch of Sora 2 caused concerns within the entertainment industry. The decision includes stopping support for developer APIs related to video and removing video features from ChatGPT. OpenAI plans to refocus on productivity tools like its combined desktop app integrating ChatGPT and coding/browser functions.

Sora was initially popular but faced copyright issues due to generating videos featuring protected intellectual property without permission. Japanese studios had demanded OpenAI cease such uses, adding legal pressure. Meanwhile, Disney withdrew from a previously announced $1 billion investment deal with OpenAI tied to Sora, citing respect for OpenAI’s shift away from video generation. The move represents a broader recalibration of how AI applications intersect with media and licensing concerns.
