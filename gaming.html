AMD Announces FSR Diamond for Future Xbox Consoles

AMD has revealed its next-generation AI-driven upscaling technology, FSR Diamond, designed specifically for upcoming Xbox hardware under Project Helix—a new collaboration between AMD and Microsoft. This platform promises improvements in neural rendering, machine learning-based multi-frame generation, and enhanced ray tracing capabilities. While FSR Diamond is currently targeted at Xbox, the closer alignment between Xbox and PC platforms raises questions about potential PC support. Early indications suggest that FSR Diamond may require the dedicated AI hardware expected in AMD’s next-generation RDNA 5 GPUs, possibly excluding current RDNA 4 models. This could mean a wait until 2027 for PC gamers to access this upscaling technology, aligning with the projected release of both Project Helix hardware and RDNA 5 GPUs.

**Why it matters:** AMD's announcement highlights ongoing efforts to close the performance and quality gap with Nvidia’s DLSS technology, particularly using AI and machine learning. However, the probable hardware restrictions may limit immediate benefits for current PC users. The development is key to understanding how future Xbox and PC graphics technology will evolve together.

Microsoft Prepares Windows 11 Xbox Mode for April

Microsoft plans to introduce an “Xbox mode” for Windows 11 devices this April, offering a full-screen, controller-optimized gaming experience on laptops, desktops, and tablets. This mode will simplify accessing game libraries, launching titles, and managing apps through the Xbox ecosystem. Initially showcased on the Asus ROG Xbox Ally X handheld device, this Xbox mode aims to streamline gaming on Windows devices. Details about how the mode will integrate—whether via a separate app or existing Xbox Game Bar functionality—are still pending.

**Why it matters:** This move signals Microsoft's ongoing integration of Xbox and PC gaming under Project Helix. By providing a dedicated Xbox gaming environment on Windows 11, Microsoft is likely preparing for a future where the distinction between Xbox consoles and PCs continues to blur, potentially enhancing the gaming experience across devices.

PlayerUnknown Wants to Reclaim the Term “Metaverse”

Brendan Greene, known as PlayerUnknown, expressed interest in reclaiming the word “metaverse,” which he feels has been misappropriated by certain corporate interests. Greene envisions a decentralized and open metaverse, where users create and own content in a connected multiverse of digital spaces, diverging from mainstream corporate interpretations. He draws a parallel to his continued use of Twitter (now X) despite disliking its current management, emphasizing a desire to hold on to terms before they become dominated by others.

**Why it matters:** Greene’s perspective reflects ongoing discussions about the future of virtual worlds and user agency versus commercial control. His approach contrasts sharply with some corporate uses of the term, suggesting a push for more community-driven virtual environments.

Jeff Kaplan Details Overwatch Departure Amid Development Challenges

Jeff Kaplan, co-creator of Overwatch, shared new insights about his exit from Activision Blizzard during a recent podcast interview. He cited pressures from the heavily overhyped Overwatch League esports venture, which diverted resources away from game development and placed unrealistic financial expectations on the team. Kaplan described a pivotal moment when he was told by the CFO that failure to meet revenue targets would lead to massive layoffs, a directive he referred to as “the biggest f**k you moment” of his career. These strains contributed to his decision to leave Blizzard in 2021.

**Why it matters:** Kaplan’s comments provide a candid look at the internal conflicts between live-service ambitions, esports expectations, and game development realities. This context helps explain some of the challenges Overwatch faced during and after his tenure.

Warhammer Blood Bowl Update To Launch as New Free Game for Existing Players

Cyanide Studio announced that the forthcoming update to Blood Bowl will be released as a new game, Warhammer Blood Bowl, based on the latest tabletop rules. Players who own Blood Bowl 3 will receive this update for free, retaining all previous content and purchases. The update introduces new teams, including Tomb Kings and Bretonnians, bringing the roster to 26 teams. A new fast-paced “Rumble mode” featuring smaller teams and pitch is planned, with a free demo available on Steam from March 18–25. The full launch is scheduled for spring 2026.

**Why it matters:** Instead of a conventional patch, this approach ensures a comprehensive update aligned with tabletop rules while preserving players’ existing progress
