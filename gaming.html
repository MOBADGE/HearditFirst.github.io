Godot Game Engine Struggles with AI-Generated Code Contributions

The open-source game engine Godot is facing significant challenges due to an overwhelming influx of low-quality, AI-generated code submissions, often referred to as "AI slop." R√©mi Verschelde, a key maintainer of Godot, has expressed concern about how draining and demoralizing it has become for the team to evaluate these pull requests. Many contributors use large language models (LLMs) to generate code or verbose descriptions without fully understanding or testing their changes. This situation forces maintainers to repeatedly second-guess contributions and deal with unclear authorship and trustworthiness.

Godot aims to remain welcoming to new contributors, but the surge in AI-sourced code threatens the project's health by increasing maintenance overhead. The team is considering possible solutions, such as automated AI detection tools and potentially moving the repository to alternative platforms, though both options carry drawbacks. With no clear technical fix yet, Verschelde highlights increased funding as the best way to hire more maintainers and manage the workload.

The wider open-source community is grappling with similar issues. GitHub, the platform hosting Godot's repository, recently introduced settings to limit pull requests to collaborators or disable them, partly to address the rise of low-quality AI contributions. However, given Microsoft's ownership of GitHub and its promotion of AI technology, the effectiveness and motivation behind these measures remain debated.

New Heroes from World of Warcraft Classes Join Fellowship in Pre-Season 2

Fellowship, an indie co-op dungeon-crawling RPG inspired by World of Warcraft (WoW), is releasing a pre-season update featuring two new heroes, Xavian and Aeona. Both characters are designed as distilled versions of popular WoW classes, focusing on key playstyles rather than the complexity of the original game.

Xavian resembles a paladin, wielding a sword to cleave enemies and using a resource system to support teammates with quick healing, easing the burden on dedicated healers. Aeona channels aspects of WoW's discipline priest, turning received direct damage into a damage-over-time effect while providing healing through her offensive abilities. Her signature skill temporarily freezes health bars, allowing her to build up healing power before releasing it.

Peter Molyneux Candid About Mistakes Ahead of Masters of Albion Launch

Peter Molyneux has openly acknowledged that his upcoming game, Masters of Albion, which he calls his final project, will likely be marked by significant missteps. In an interview, he described the development and publishing team as "incredibly inexperienced" with the business and community management aspects, which Molyneux finds daunting. The game will launch in early access on April 22, allowing iterative refinement based on player feedback.

Masters of Albion combines multiple gameplay systems requiring balance and polish. Molyneux stressed the importance of learning from inevitable mistakes during early access. This approach is a departure from his previous track record of bold promises; here, he aims for a more tempered and transparent tone.

Although Molyneux hopes for the best, skepticism exists. Some industry observers have noted that recent previews suggest the game struggles to coherently integrate its various elements. The early access phase will be key in determining whether it fulfils its ambitions.
