**Baldur’s Gate Mod Management Tool Overhauled**

A web-based mod management tool for the original Baldur’s Gate games, EET Mod Forge, has received a major update. The tool helps players avoid conflicts and glitches when modifying these classic RPGs, supporting over 640 mods. The recent 2.0 version adds features like NPC portrait previews and tracking mod limits for game mechanics. It also introduces a comprehensive preset with 475 mods tailored to minimize conflicts. This update highlights ongoing community support that makes modding these older titles more accessible and stable for modern players.

**Bethesda Reflects on Improved DLC Integration**

Bethesda veteran artist Istvan Pely has acknowledged past shortcomings in how Bethesda RPGs handled downloadable content (DLC) notifications, particularly in Fallout 3’s Game of the Year Edition, which was overwhelmed with pop-ups. Pely notes the developer has since improved the integration of DLC into games, aiming for more organic story inclusion. The upcoming Starfield Terran Armada DLC, due next month, is designed to blend naturally into gameplay without intrusive alerts. This shift aims to reduce player frustration and improve immersion in Bethesda’s expanding RPG portfolio.

**Resident Evil Requiem Porsche Replaced by Modded Hamburger**

Modders have introduced a humorous customization for Resident Evil Requiem by replacing Leon Kennedy’s product placement Porsche Cayenne Turbo GT with a drivable hamburger vehicle, named the Patty Wagon mod. This is part of a wider set of playful mods available shortly after the game’s release, including character swaps with popular meme figures and added difficulty options like infectious zombie bites. These community creations offer players alternative experiences ranging from comedic to challenging, showing the vibrant modding activity around this recent Capcom release.

**Elden Ring Cut Quest Restored by Modder**

A modder named Transquility has restored a substantial cut quest involving Merchant Kalé in Elden Ring. Originally intended to deepen the narrative around the Frenzied Flame ending, Kalé’s quest was removed before release. The restored content gives context to some of the game’s darker story elements and humanizes Kalé’s motivations. The mod uses existing datamined voice work, allowing players to experience this story thread directly for the first time. This restoration offers fans a fuller understanding of Elden Ring’s lore and the moral complexity behind one of its endings.

**Minecraft Announces New Sulfur Cave Biome in Upcoming Update**

Minecraft’s second content drop of 2026, showcased during Minecraft Live March 2026, will introduce a new underground biome called Sulfur caves. Inspired by real-world sulfur hot springs, the biome will feature distinctive bright colors, poisonous pools causing status effects, and two new blocks: cinnabar and sulfur. The update also brings a new mob, the Sulfur Cube, which can absorb blocks and change behavior based on what it consumes. This addition aims to diversify underground exploration, adding environmental hazards and new materials to the established caving experience.

---

**Minecraft Dungeons Sequel Confirmed for 2026 Release**

Mojang has announced a surprise sequel to Minecraft Dungeons, the franchise’s action-RPG spin-off with Diablo-style gameplay. Revealed during the same Minecraft Live event, the teaser hints at a story continuation involving renewed threats to the Minecraft universe. Gameplay details remain limited, and the Steam page currently only shows cinematic images. Scheduled for release within 2026, the sequel marks Mojang’s commitment to expanding Minecraft’s spin-off projects despite mixed receptions for related titles like Minecraft Legends. Fans of the original Dungeons can expect new content later this year.
