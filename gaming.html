**New Blood’s CEO Recommits to Supporting GOG**

Dave Oshry, CEO of New Blood Interactive, has reaffirmed his company’s support for the digital game storefront GOG after initially expressing concerns about its future viability. Despite previous criticisms that GOG was not keeping pace with Steam in offering players compelling reasons to choose it, Oshry has committed to expanding New Blood's engagement with the platform. This includes running sales concurrent with Steam, releasing demo versions of upcoming titles like *Dungeons of Dusk* and *Tenebrous Somnia* on GOG, and ensuring day-and-date launches on both stores. Additionally, New Blood plans to bring mod support with one-click installation, as demonstrated by the *Dusk* HD remaster on GOG. This renewed commitment comes as GOG, now independent from CD Projekt, seeks to emphasize its DRM-free philosophy and game preservation mission.

**Resident Evil Requiem Now Playable in VR via Mod**

A fan-made mod now allows players to experience *Resident Evil Requiem* in virtual reality. The mod lets players inhabit the character Grace Ashcroft in a VR first-person perspective, while Leon S. Kennedy’s segment remains in third-person. Although some puzzle segments still require exiting VR for practical reasons, the mod aims to immerse players in the game’s horror atmosphere through VR technology. Installation requires additional files and some technical steps. While the VR adaptation is unofficial and may appeal to a niche audience, it highlights ongoing community efforts to reimagine established games with emerging technologies.

**Five New Steam Games to Watch**

Recent releases on Steam include a variety of titles across genres:

- *Bonnie Bear Saves Frogtime*: A tactical RPG combining grid-based combat with point-and-click adventure elements, centered around collecting and battling with frogs. - *Lost Wiki: Kozlovka*: An investigative game employing a retro-style database interface for uncovering a supernatural mystery. - *Deadline Delivery*: A multiplayer physics-comedy game focusing on fast, risky parcel deliveries in unstable vehicles. - *Heroes of Science and Fiction*: A turn-based strategy game inspired by classic *Heroes of Might and Magic* titles, featuring multiple factions and campaigns. - *The Coin Game*: A sandbox experience set in a '90s-style mall populated by arcade-style mini-games and attractions, including claw machines and go-karts.

These games represent a mix of new ideas and genre homages, offering players a range of options outside mainstream releases.

**Player-Run Servers Revive Marvel Heroes MMO**

*Marvel Heroes*, an action MMO shut down in 2017, has returned through community-led efforts. A server emulator has reached a stable 1.0 release, enabling player-run servers such as "Tahiti." Players can join via Steam or set up their own solo servers using available tools on GitHub. Although initial stability issues remain, this revival provides fans a way to revisit the game’s Diablo-like gameplay with Marvel characters. The return underscores how dedicated communities can sustain online games beyond their official lifespan when developers cease support.

**Developer Mega Crit Addresses Slay the Spire 2 Balance Changes**

Following a wave of negative Steam reviews triggered by the first balance patch for *Slay the Spire 2*, developer Mega Crit has clarified their approach to ongoing game adjustments. The update notably altered the card "Prepared," changing it from a zero-cost flexible option into a more costly and situational card called "Prepare." This led to backlash, particularly among beta testers. Mega Crit explained that this patch is only the initial step in a series of iterative changes planned over one to two years during Early Access. They emphasized that no balance change is definitive and encouraged players to provide feedback through in-game reporting tools. The developer’s statement highlights the challenges of modifying popular games while balancing player expectations during extended development periods.
