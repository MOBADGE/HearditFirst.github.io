**Alan Wake Remastered Receives HDR Support and Gameplay Improvements**

*Alan Wake Remastered* received an unanticipated 1.33 patch introducing High Dynamic Range (HDR) support. This enhancement improves the game's color vibrancy and contrasts for compatible displays. Besides HDR, the patch offers an optional modern camera style mode, an intro skip command tailored to speedrunners and frequent players, and adjustments to weapon swapping timings for smoother gameplay. The frame rate cap has been increased from 200 to 240 FPS, and improvements were made to DLSS, foliage rendering, and compatibility with ultrawide monitors.

These updates enhance visual fidelity and user experience, reflecting ongoing support for remastered titles and catering to both new and returning players.

**Challenges and Funding Cut Impact Highguard‚Äôs Development**

The free-to-play multiplayer shooter *Highguard*, developed by Wildlight Entertainment and published by Tencent, has encountered severe setbacks. A Bloomberg report based on former employee interviews indicates that the team was caught off guard when Tencent abruptly ended funding on February 11, leading to widespread layoffs. Staff expected runway funding to last several months, but the sudden cutoff left the studio with limited options.

After launching on January 26 with almost 100,000 players, the game quickly saw its player base shrink by 90% within weeks, undermining its revenue potential. Despite these challenges, Wildlight recently released a new 5v5 mode called "Raid Rush," temporarily disabling traditional 3v3 raids. The situation underscores difficulties smaller studios face when reliant on large publishers and highlights how critical sustained player engagement is for free-to-play titles.
