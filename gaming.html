Star Wars Zero Company: A Risky Tactical Game Endorsed by Lucasfilm and Respawn

Greg Foertsch, founder of Bit Reactor and developer of the upcoming Star Wars Zero Company, recently praised Lucasfilm and Respawn for their willingness to invest in a turn-based tactics game—a relatively niche genre for a major Star Wars title. Foertsch highlighted that both companies showed uncommon "courage and conviction" in supporting a new studio to explore something outside their usual offerings. Despite historical concerns—such as Marvel’s Midnight Suns struggling commercially despite familiar characters—Zero Company’s blend of Star Wars lore with tactical gameplay may find a dedicated audience due to this backing. This move is notable in an industry that often favors safer, more derivative approaches amid fierce competition for players’ attention.

Narrative Direction Emphasizes Team Dynamics and Moral Ambiguity

Aaron Contreras, narrative director for Zero Company, explained the game's approach to storytelling aims for more realistic team dynamics rather than typical RPG tropes. The game avoids the usual "chosen one" archetype, instead positioning the player character, Hawks, as a leader managing diverse personalities with conflicting viewpoints. Set during the morally gray Clone Wars era, the narrative explores ethical complexity rather than straightforward good-versus-evil themes familiar from later Star Wars media. The in-game bond system encourages thoughtful management of team relations, including potential romantic connections or tensions, making interpersonal friction a deliberate gameplay element that reinforces the theme of leadership challenges.

Robot Cache Blockchain Storefront Shutting Down, Affecting Users’ Game Access

Robot Cache, a blockchain-powered digital game storefront launched in 2018, appears to be shutting down, according to user reports and leaked emails. The closure will deactivate access to games purchased through the platform—even those fully downloaded—due to the "verification system" relying on the now-defunct service. Customers have expressed concern over losing their game libraries and the platform’s digital currency ("iron") becoming unusable. Founded by Brian Fargo and once backed by an AMD partnership, Robot Cache struggled to gain traction financially, reportedly earning only $532 in game sales during 2024 and accumulating over $30 million in losses. Attempts to reach the company for confirmation have so far been unresolved.

Dwarf Fortress Creator Tarn Adams Shares Gaming Insights and Current Interests

Tarn Adams, co-creator of the influential colony simulator Dwarf Fortress, recently shared details about his gaming habits and inspirations. He revealed a deep engagement with Factorio, clocking over 1,400 hours, and an interest in terraforming games like The Planet Crafter and Terraformental. Adams discussed his programming background, early BASIC experiments, and his preference for fantasy settings, explaining that Dwarf Fortress does not accommodate full sci-fi scenarios. He also noted how gaming helped him cope with personal loss. Adams highlighted his use of music software Ableton for composition and described his meticulous but evolving desktop organization.

Crimson Desert’s Ambiguous Identity and Vast Scope After Extensive Playtime

After over 150 hours with Pearl Abyss’s open-world game Crimson Desert, a senior PC Gamer writer reflected on the game’s challenging-to-define nature. It blends elements of RPG and MMO genres without fully fitting either category. The game underwent significant changes post-launch, softening some of its original unconventional design choices, such as inventory and fast travel restrictions. The vast game world with multiple regions and complex systems encourages slow-paced exploration rather than rushing the main story. Despite technical issues and narrative shortcomings, the reviewer found the game compelling due to its scale, variety of activities, and unique protagonist.

Umamusume Fans Take Cosplay Beyond Conventions with Real-World Races

Fans of the anime and game Umamusume: Pretty Derby have organized real-life cosplay races at horse tracks around the world, including venues in Thailand, Malaysia, the United States, and Peru. These events see participants dressing as their favorite horse-girl characters and racing on foot, emulating the in-game racing experience. While not officially sponsored by Cygames, these grassroots activities demonstrate notable fan dedication and creativity. They add a physical and social dimension to the fandom, extending engagement beyond digital gameplay and traditional cosplay gatherings.
