Amazon Reports 100 Million Views for Fallout Series

Amazon announced that its Fallout TV series on Prime Video has now been watched by 100 million viewers in total, combining seasons one and two. Season one initially attracted 65 million viewers, while the second season surpassed that with 83 million viewers, making both seasons among Amazon’s top four launches ever. It’s important to note that these figures represent the number of viewers who pressed play rather than completion rates, so engagement levels may vary. Season two’s success boosted interest in the first season as well. Production for a third season is set to begin this summer, with plans to explore a new location not yet featured in the games. This milestone demonstrates ongoing interest in adapting popular game franchises for television.

Cozy Games Highlighted in Steam’s House and Home Sale

Steam is hosting its House and Home Fest sale through April 6, focusing on cozy, home-themed games. A recent feature shared five recommended titles that emphasize building, organizing, or renovating virtual spaces. Highlights include *Creature Kitchen*, a short and charming cooking puzzle game; *PowerWash Simulator 2*, a sequel with new cleaning tools and improved co-op; and *Hozy*, a fixer-upper sim featuring granular tasks like painting and cleaning. Other mentions were the unpacking puzzle game *Unpacking* and *My Little Life*, an idle game about tending small virtual people. The sale provides opportunities to explore relaxing, low-pressure games, with some offering demos, catering to players interested in domestic and creative gameplay.

Super Mario Galaxy Movie Receives Negative Reviews

The newly released *Super Mario Galaxy* movie has been met with overwhelmingly negative critical reception. Reviews from several major outlets describe the film as poorly crafted and lacking depth. Critics from The Guardian and The New York Times characterized it as bland, unengaging, and lacking a coherent center. The Times even described it as uncomfortable to watch. While a few reviewers noted that the film may hold some appeal as light entertainment due to its pace and references, most conclude it fails to live up to expectations for game-based adaptations. The movie appears targeted more at generating revenue than providing substantive storytelling.

Dark Souls Run Completed Under Extreme Restrictions

A YouTuber named Zeldasouls has completed an exceptionally difficult Dark Souls run with a series of stringent conditions: playing at level 1, no hits taken, no dodge rolls, no ranged weapons or spell projectiles, and no quitting out to reset enemies. This challenge required precise strategy and slow, careful progression over approximately two years. The run excludes common defensive and offensive tactics that most players rely on, making even early game sections notably challenging. This accomplishment highlights the enduring dedication of the Dark Souls community to pushing the limits of the game’s difficulty and mechanics. The full playthrough lasts about three and a half hours and is recommended for fans interested in high-skill runs.

Lawsuit Filed Against Ubisoft Over The Crew Shutdown

French consumer organization UFC-Que Choisir, supported by the Stop Killing Games campaign, has filed a lawsuit against Ubisoft concerning the 2024 shutdown of *The Crew* servers. The lawsuit claims Ubisoft violated consumer rights by shutting down the game and revoking licenses without proper notice, effectively denying players fair access to products they purchased. *The Crew*, which launched in 2014, became unplayable after the server closure, and Ubisoft’s subsequent removal of licenses sparked controversy. This case forms part of a wider debate over digital ownership and game preservation. The Stop Killing Games initiative has gained significant momentum in advocating for clearer regulations. Ubisoft has pledged to avoid similar shutdowns in the future, but the lawsuit seeks legal clarification to prevent such practices.

World of Warcraft Players Solve Year-Old Puzzle Requiring Synchronized 40-Player Dance

World of Warcraft players have completed the final steps of a complex, year-long puzzle tied to unlocking the Felcycle mount and related cosmetic rewards. The penultimate puzzle step required a synchronized emote dance involving 40 players, a nod to the larger raid sizes of WoW’s past. Coordinating this many players in the current game environment, which supports smaller raids, involved special items and mods to synchronize emotes precisely. The last step involves a challenging orb-carrying quest across hostile areas in the game world. The puzzle’s complexity and required cooperation highlight the dedication of WoW’s secret-finder community and the continued appeal of in-game events that encourage large group collaboration for unique cosmetic items.
