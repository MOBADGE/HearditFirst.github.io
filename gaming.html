Five Steam Games Worth Noting This Week

A selection of lesser-known Steam releases from early April offers a variety of experiences, from point-and-click adventures to unconventional shooters. Highlights include *Cult Vacui*, a roughly two-hour point-and-click game set on a 19th-century Scottish island where a janitor must evade a deadly ritual. *Subversive Memories* is a Brazilian survival horror title inspired by historical events and classic fixed-perspective gameplay. Meanwhile, *KuloNiku: Bowl Up!* combines food preparation with life simulation in a cozy anime style, earning positive user reviews. The action platformer *ChainStaff* features a distinctive, somewhat unsettling art style along with grappling hook mechanics and a unique upgrade system. Lastly, *Agony Increment* offers a post-*Cruelty Squad* FPS experience with disorienting visuals and a complex combat system. These games may be overshadowed by bigger releases but provide diverse gameplay for curious players.

AI-Generated Warhammer 40K Erotica Raises Concerns

A recent Amazon listing, *Taken by the Tyranid: A Romance in the Far Future*, uses AI-generated cover art and is suspected to be AI-written as well. This story is part of a troubling trend of AI-derived fan erotica flooding platforms, often with questionable quality and originality. The narrative blends elements of Warhammer 40,000 lore with a controversial romance between a human warrior and an alien creature, echoing the popularity of dinosaur-erotica tropes. While fan fiction related to Warhammer 40K already exists extensively online, the use of AI tools for such material raises questions about quality control and intellectual property. The presence of these AI products illustrates ongoing challenges in managing AI-generated content within niche fandoms.

Modder Transforms Sekiro Into Skateboarding Game

A mod titled *Jet Set Sekiro* reimagines *Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice* as a Tony Hawk-inspired skateboarding game. Developed by Ionian-MikiriyAKATotallyNotShinobi, the mod repurposes existing animations and environments from Sekiro’s Fountainhead Palace map, introducing mechanics like graffiti tagging and skating tricks. This crossover showcases creative modding that blends distinct gameplay styles, highlighting the flexibility of game engines and community enthusiasm. Such mods bring fresh experiences to established titles and demonstrate how player creativity can significantly alter a game’s tone and interaction.

Controversy Over Kickstarter Fund Usage in MMO Project

The defunct MMO project *Ashes of Creation* faces renewed scrutiny due to allegations against its studio founder, Steven Sharif. A YouTuber analyzing Intrepid Studios’ financial records claims millions of dollars intended for game development were diverted towards personal expenses, including private chefs, luxury items, and unrelated purchases. Some former employees corroborate these claims, though Sharif denies wrongdoing, placing blame on the studio’s board. The complexity and confidentiality of the case mean few definitive conclusions can be drawn at present. This situation highlights risks of transparency and accountability in crowdfunded game development.

Skate Story Discount and Developer’s Candid Piracy Comment

*Skate Story*, praised for its unique demon-skating gameplay and stylish aesthetics, is currently discounted to $16 on GOG. Creator Sam Eng openly suggested that if players find the price too steep, pirating the game is an option, reflecting an unusual and frank stance on game access. The game initially gained attention several years ago and continues to attract a dedicated audience. Its availability at a reduced price may broaden its reach, while the developer’s position sparks discussion about pricing, accessibility, and game preservation in the digital age.

Blizzard’s Legal Victory Against World of Warcraft Private Server

Blizzard has won an injunction against the operators of Turtle WoW, a popular vanilla World of Warcraft private server. A US district court ordered the team to cease the server’s development and prevent the creation of successor projects. Turtle WoW had operated without official licensing and offered players a version of WoW classic with additional content, funded partially through donations. Blizzard’s legal action and settlement reinforce its control over intellectual property and indicate ongoing tensions between rights holders and private server communities. The ruling may impact how fan-made servers operate and negotiate space in the gaming ecosystem.
