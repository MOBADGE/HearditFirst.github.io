Ivy Road Shuts Down After Funding Challenges

Indie developer Ivy Road, known for the cozy tea shop adventure game *Wanderstop*, announced it will close its doors on March 31. *Wanderstop*, released last year and developed by a team including writers from *The Stanley Parable* and *Tacoma* plus music by Minecraft’s C418, was the studio’s first and only game. Despite positive reception, the studio struggled financially, particularly after failing to secure funding or a publishing deal for their new project, *Engine Angel*. Layoffs began in January, and the studio cited the current tough climate for raising game funds as a key factor in their closure. *Wanderstop* will remain available for purchase, and there may be future developments with Annapurna Interactive to help the game reach new audiences.

ZA/UM Takes a Different Approach with *Zero Parades*

ZA/UM, the studio behind the acclaimed RPG *Disco Elysium*, shared insights into their new game *Zero Parades*. Early development considered focusing on police stories similar to *Disco Elysium*’s detective narrative. However, the team decided against this due to changing public perceptions of police and a desire to explore new genres. *Zero Parades* shifted to a spy thriller theme, allowing the writers to address current political urgency and ideological clashes in a fresher context. The developers emphasized wanting to challenge themselves with new styles of writing rather than repeating previous successes, reflecting evolving cultural and political attitudes since 2019.

Frictional Games Horror Bundle Available at $15

Frictional Games, known for horror titles like *Amnesia* and *SOMA*, is offering a bundle including nearly all of its games for $15 through Humble Bundle. The package contains the *Penumbra* trilogy, *Amnesia: The Dark Descent*, *A Machine for Pigs*, *Amnesia: Rebirth*, *Amnesia: The Bunker*, and *SOMA*. This collection spans from Frictional’s early work to its latest releases, offering a comprehensive look at the studio’s evolution in horror gaming. The bundle is available until April 1, 2026, with Steam keys redeemable until 2027, presenting an accessible opportunity for players to experience a significant portion of the studio’s portfolio.

Path of Exile’s Prominent Trader Banned, Raising Community Concerns

Jenebu, known as the richest player and leader of *Path of Exile*’s major item trading Discord server The Forbidden Trove (TFT), was recently banned from the game. This account held more than 1,500 rare and unique items, many irreplaceable, linked to a mirror service that allowed item duplication via the rare Mirror of Kalandra. Jenebu pleaded with Grinding Gear Games (GGG) for account restoration, emphasizing the impact on about 700,000 community members using the server. While no direct evidence of real-money trading has been found, the ban disrupts one of the largest third-party trading hubs, sparking debate about the role of such communities in *Path of Exile*’s player economy and the game's ongoing adjustments to trading mechanics.

PUBG: Blindspot to End Early Access After Low Player Engagement

*PUBG: Blindspot*, a top-down tactical shooter spin-off, will end its early access period on March 30 after less than two months. Despite initial promise and some positive feedback, the game struggled to maintain a sufficient player base, resulting in long matchmaking times that further deterred users. Developed by Arc Team and published under Krafton, the closure reflects challenges faced by free-to-play multiplayer titles dependent on a critical mass of players. Krafton itself has experienced organizational strains recently, including profit declines and employee buyouts, which may have influenced support for experimental projects like *Blindspot*. The developer plans to regroup but has not announced future projects.
