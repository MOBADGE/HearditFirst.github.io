Earth Must Die Launches with Notable British Comedy Cast

Size Five Games is releasing *Earth Must Die*, a comedy adventure game inspired by 1990s point-and-click adventures, on January 27. The player takes on the role of Vvalek Lizardtongue, an alien ruler whose kingdom is under attack, prompting him to direct his wrath toward Earth. The game features a modern animation style and a voice cast drawn from British comedy and gaming talent, including Joel Fry (*Game of Thrones*), Ben Starr (*Final Fantasy 16*), and Alex Horne (*Taskmaster*). This casting follows a recent trend in games leveraging strong vocal performances to boost appeal, as seen in titles like *Dispatch*. A demo is currently available on Steam for those interested in an early look.

Fortnite Bans Paid Prize Wheels Following Controversy Over Gambling Mechanics

Epic Games has issued a new rule banning in-island transactions that influence prize wheels in Fortnite Creative mode, effective January 20. This move comes days after the popular Fortnite game *Steal the Brainrot* introduced paid prize wheel spins for in-game rewards, which critics described as gambling targeting younger players. The updated rules prohibit sales of spins, luck boosts, or related items that affect prize wheel outcomes. Epic emphasized ongoing reviews of developer practices to maintain acceptable monetization. This response highlights the challenges of balancing monetization freedom with player protection, particularly in user-generated content platforms.
