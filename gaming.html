Fighting Game YouTuber Pledges $10,000 for Rollback Netcode in Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3

Popular fighting game content creator Maximilian Christiansen, known as Maximilian Dood, has offered a $10,000 bounty to developers who can implement rollback netcode in Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3. The game currently uses delay-based netcode, which can lead to lag and less responsive online play. Rollback netcode, by contrast, predicts and corrects inputs to provide smoother experiences, and has become the preferred standard for modern fighting games. Christiansen has a track record of financially supporting fan mods, notably putting in nearly $30,000 for a Marvel vs. Capcom Infinite mod. His offer signals ongoing demand from players for improved online play in classic fighting games.

Star Wars’ Kreia and Darth Nihilus Action Figure Sells Out Quickly

Hasbro released a two-pack of action figures in its Black Series line featuring characters from the Star Wars RPG Knights of the Old Republic 2: The Sith Lords. The pack includes Darth Nihilus, a memorable antagonist known for his planet-devouring power, and critically, Kreia (a.k.a Darth Traya), an important and philosophically complex villain in the game. This figure marks the first official Kreia collectible. Despite detailed sculpting and accessories like floating lightsabers for Kreia, preorders sold out rapidly on Amazon, reflecting strong fan interest in KOTOR lore. While currently unavailable, additional production might follow if demand remains high.

Rockstar Games Faces Ransomware Threat Following Data Breach

Rockstar Games confirmed a limited data breach involving its third-party cloud services provider, Snowflake, accessed through a recently compromised analytics partner, Anodot. Hacker group ShinyHunters claims to have gained unauthorized access and is demanding a ransom to prevent data leaks. Rockstar stated the breach affected non-critical company information and does not impact players or the company’s operations. This incident echoes previous major leaks Rockstar suffered around GTA6 development. The event highlights ongoing cybersecurity risks when third-party services are integrated into large game studios’ infrastructure.

Graveyard Keeper 2 Devs Deny Using AI for Promotional Art Amid Fan Concerns

Developers of the upcoming Graveyard Keeper sequel addressed player speculation that generative AI was used to create the game’s key art. Fans pointed to perceived AI-like features and the studio’s CTO’s visible interest in AI tools. Lazy Bear CTO Slava Cherkasov clarified that while he experiments with AI personally, the game’s art is produced by a team of about eight human artists and AI was not used in official assets. The debate reflects wider industry scrutiny over AI’s role in creative processes and player concerns about authenticity and quality.

Proton Experimental Update Improves Compatibility for Classic Capcom Titles on Steam Deck and Linux

The latest Proton Experimental build for Steam Deck and Linux has enhanced support for several older Capcom games. Titles now rated as "playable" include the original Resident Evil (1996), Resident Evil 2 (1998), Dino Crisis, and Dino Crisis 2, among others. While these improvements are not final, and some issues like FMV playback in Dino Crisis 2 remain unresolved, the update makes it easier for players to experience these survival horror classics on portable Linux devices. This continues efforts to preserve older games’ accessibility on modern platforms.

New Mod Transforms N64’s Duke Nukem: Zero Hour Into First-Person Shooter on PC

A fan-made modpack called Duke Nukem: Zero Hour Overclocked allows players to convert the original third-person Nintendo 64 shooter into a first-person experience on PC. The mod offers customizable modules, including FPS-style controls with mouse look, altered animations, new alt-fire options for weapons, and camera adjustments for the first-person perspective. The project aims to evoke a nostalgic ’90s FPS feel and has received praise from 3D Realms founder Scott Miller. Players interested in this experience will need the original Zero Hour ROM to use the mod legally.
