Bungie's Marathon UI Design and Player Feedback

Bungie’s recent server slam for their new FPS, *Marathon*, drew mixed reactions, particularly about its user interface. Some players and commentators described the UI as visually overwhelming and confusing, coining the term “fontslop” to express their discomfort with the menu design. Elliott Gray, a UI designer on the project, embraced this feedback with humor and defended the UI’s unique style, saying the team will improve usability but won’t sacrifice the distinctive “sauce” that defines its look. This stance suggests Bungie values the aesthetic identity of *Marathon*’s UI even as it plans refinements based on player input. The discussion highlights an ongoing balance in modern game design between usability and visual distinctiveness.

Marathon Server Slam: From Skepticism to Support

Initial responses to *Marathon* during its server slam period were cautious, with some players finding issues like a punishing item economy and unfamiliar PvP dynamics challenging. However, after around 21 hours of play, impressions improved significantly. One player detailed how embracing *Marathon*’s slower pacing and tactical gunplay—comparing it to games like *Halo* and *Hunt: Showdown*—deepened their enjoyment. The player also noted the game’s upgrades and weapon mods provided meaningful progression despite the risk of losing gear typical in extraction shooters. This shift from “meh” to appreciation highlights the game’s underlying design strengths, particularly its PvP combat and progression systems, and suggests that *Marathon* may find a solid audience with time and experience.

Bungie Considering Changes Post-Server Slam

Following community feedback during the *Marathon* server slam, Bungie acknowledged several key areas under review. These include adding a dedicated duos mode for matchmaking, adjusting the time-to-kill to balance combat pacing, and reworking the health and ammo economy to ease resource scarcity. The developer also committed to ongoing UI improvements and addressing technical performance complaints, such as high CPU usage and frame rate issues on PC. While no changes are confirmed, Bungie’s openness to responding to player concerns indicates a willingness to refine the game before its full release on March 5.

Halfbrick Studios Announces Layoffs Amid Industry Challenges

Halfbrick Studios, known for mobile hits like *Fruit Ninja* and *Jetpack Joyride*, has announced organizational restructuring including layoffs in its Australian office and the end of some international contracts. The studio cited a challenging environment in the games industry and strategic priorities as reasons for the changes, emphasizing that the decisions were unrelated to employee performance. Halfbrick reassured that the affected staff are talented and recommended them to potential employers. The news reflects broader industry pressures that even established studios face, despite recent releases like *Bluey’s Quest for the Gold Pen*.

PC Game Highlights for March 2026

- *Marathon* launches on March 5, marking Bungie’s return to a sci-fi extraction shooter format. - *Slay the Spire 2* enters early access on March 6 with a new four-player co-op mode. - The *Fatal Frame 2: Crimson Butterfly Remake* releases on March 12, expanding horror options for PC players. - *Crimson Desert* and *Death Stranding 2* also launch mid-month, bringing action RPG and narrative-driven experiences to the platform. - Major events like the Game Developers Conference, Future Games Show, SXSW gaming panels, and PAX East occur throughout the month. - Steam sales and thematic events provide additional opportunities for players to explore new titles and discounts.

This lineup illustrates a varied and active month for PC gaming across genres and formats.

Fallout: New Vegas Remaster Rumors Surface

Iron Galaxy Studios sparked speculation about a potential *Fallout: New Vegas* remaster after sharing a company meeting update that included a familiar *New Vegas* loading screen image. While the studio did not confirm the project, the inclusion of such a distinctive asset has fueled fan anticipation. Iron Galaxy is known for work on ports and remasters, which adds to the plausibility of the rumor. Previous attempts at a remaster have seen false starts, but the renewed hints bring attention to the continued interest in revitalizing the popular RPG for modern platforms. Bethesda has yet to comment on any such project.
