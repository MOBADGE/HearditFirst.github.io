Researchers Explore Terahertz Waves to Examine Electronic Components in Action

A team of engineers from Australia, Germany, and the USA have developed a method using terahertz waves to observe the behavior of electronic parts like transistors and diodes while they are operating. Unlike X-rays, terahertz waves are less invasive and can examine internal structures without shutting down devices. The researchers used homodyne detection to reveal component activity that standard terahertz methods could not capture. This technique might help monitor electronics in systems that cannot be easily powered down, such as power delivery networks. They are also investigating whether terahertz waves could potentially be used to read encrypted data in chips, which raises security questions. However, since terahertz waves only penetrate non-metallic materials and modern chips have complex, copper-filled layers, practical data snooping is difficult for now.

Find Your Words Offers a Unique Communication Experience in Adventure Game

The adventure game *Find Your Words* introduces players to a novel way of communicating without spoken dialogue. Players assume the role of a non-speaking camper who uses a symbol-based Communication Binder to interact with other campers at Camp Pals. The binder contains phrases, locations, items, and characters that players combine to form messages. The game emphasizes the challenge and creativity involved in non-verbal communication and slowly expands its vocabulary as relationships deepen. This approach abandons traditional dialogue trees in favor of symbol manipulation, highlighting the effort and patience required to build connections through unconventional means. The game features various minigames like trivia and hide-and-seek, further integrating the communication system into gameplay.

Nvidia Denies Acquisition Talks Despite Market Reaction

A report claimed Nvidia was in talks to acquire a large PC-focused company, suggesting a substantial shift in the computing industry. Following this, shares of PC manufacturers Dell, HP, Lenovo, and Asus rose by up to 4%. The GPU maker quickly denied these talks, calling the report false. Although Nvidia has not confirmed any acquisition plans, the company is expanding its reach in both datacenter and client computing sectors. It has developed the powerful N1X chip for PCs and has invested in Intel, aiming for collaboration on x86 and AI server technologies. The denied rumor does not diminish Nvidia’s clear intentions to increase its presence in overall computing hardware markets through partnerships and innovation rather than outright purchases at this time.

Windrose Launches Early Access as a Co-op Pirate Survival Game

*Windrose*, a cooperative pirate survival game, has entered early access following a successful demo at the recent Next Fest. The game features ship-to-ship combat, open-world exploration, resource gathering, base building, and questing. Players start shipwrecked after an encounter with Blackbeard and must rebuild their pirate crew and empire. Key gameplay elements include hunting animals, fighting enemy pirates, and managing hunger without fatal consequences. PvP is not available, focusing instead on co-op PvE play with friends. Combat features simple ship controls with broadside attacks and a parry-based melee system. The current early access version lacks fishing, though farming is limited but present. Performance issues exist with world loading and multiplayer hosting but the game remains stable. Priced at $30, it offers a solid foundation with expectations for further content over one to three years in development.

Bloodborne to Receive R-Rated Animated Film Co-Produced by JackSepticEye

Sony announced an upcoming R-rated animated film adaptation of *Bloodborne*, co-produced by popular YouTuber JackSepticEye (Seán McLoughlin). The film aims to maintain the dark and violent tone of the original game. JackSepticEye’s involvement stems from his large fanbase and his affinity for the game, which he has extensively played on his channel. The adaptation joins Sony’s growing slate of game-to-film/TV projects, including *The Last of Us* series and a live-action *Legend of Zelda* film. No release date has been confirmed yet. Given that *Bloodborne* remains a PlayStation exclusive and is unlikely to come to PC, the film adaptation could introduce the franchise’s story and lore to a broader audience.
