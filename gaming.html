Be Quiet! Enters Gaming Mouse Market with Two New Models

Be Quiet!, known for quiet PC components, has announced its entry into the gaming mouse market with two new models: Dark Perk Ergo and Dark Perk Sym. Both mice offer wireless functionality, featuring a 110-hour battery life at 1000 Hz, 55 grams weight, an 8000 Hz polling rate, and up to 32,000 DPI. The Ergo model is designed with an ergonomic shape, while the Sym version is symmetrical. They use PixArt PAW3950 sensors and optical Omron switches, and can be customized via a web-based app compatible across multiple platforms, including Windows, iOS, and Linux.

The mice will be available from February 3, priced at $110 each. While Be Quiet! has a solid reputation in PC accessories, these mice face stiff competition as other established gaming mice offer comparable or better specifications at similar or lower prices. Their success may largely depend on real-world usage and handling rather than specs alone.

Ubisoft Cancels Prince of Persia Remake Amid Major Restructuring

Ubisoft has cancelled the long-delayed *Prince of Persia: The Sands of Time Remake* as part of a broad internal restructuring to create focused "Creative Houses" for key franchise management. Six projects, including this remake, were cancelled, while seven other titles faced delays. The new structure consists of five distinct units focusing on genres such as shooter games, live service titles, fantasy RPGs, and casual games.

Epic Games recently vaulted shockwave grenades from *Fortnite*, citing concerns that their ease of use encouraged combat fleeing, which disrupted engagements. Shockwaves allowed players to quickly escape hostile situations, often resetting fights and extending gameplay duration. While some community members and players see the removal as limiting mobility options and making gameplay slower and less dynamic, others agree with Epic that the item could undermine skilled play.

Shockwaves were widely regarded as a fun and signature movement item, especially in Zero Build mode, helping both casual and experienced players with mobility. Epic has indicated that shockwaves will return in a "special February update," suggesting a temporary vault rather than permanent removal.

World of Warcraft Adjusts to Removal of Combat UI Addons

With the launch of *World of Warcraft: Midnight* pre-patch, Blizzard has enforced restrictions on combat-related UI addons to prevent competitive imbalances. Players can still use non-combat addons, but major combat addons have been disabled. Blizzard introduced new in-game tools like the Cooldown Manager and a Personal Resource display to compensate, although some limitations remain, such as restricted audio alert customization.

Early player feedback, including from long-time addon users, has been mostly positive regarding the cleaner and quieter UI experience, despite some functionality gaps. The changes aim to level the competitive playing field and reduce reliance on third-party tools while maintaining core gameplay quality.

Nintendo Considers Switch 2 Pricing Amid Rising Memory Costs

Research firm Niko Partners predicts that the Nintendo Switch 2 may experience a price increase in 2026 due to rising RAM and storage prices, tariffs, and other macroeconomic factors. Nintendo President Shuntaro Furukawa has acknowledged the volatility in the memory market but has not confirmed any price changes. He noted that tariffs are generally treated as costs passed on to prices globally, though there is no immediate impact on earnings.

The Switch 2 launched in June 2025 at a higher price point than the original Switch, which may make further increases challenging from a consumer perspective. Nintendo is reportedly considering removing cheaper console-only bundles, potentially raising the purchase cost for standalone consoles but not bundled packages.
