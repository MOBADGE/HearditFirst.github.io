OneXPlayer X1 Air Review: Powerful but Flawed Gaming Tablet

The OneXPlayer X1 Air is a versatile Intel-based gaming tablet with strong gaming performance driven by its Intel Core Ultra 7 258V processor and Arc 140V GPU, paired with a vibrant 11-inch 2560x1600 display at 120 Hz refresh rate. It can function as a PC tablet, a laptop-like device via a magnetic keyboard and touchpad, or a handheld gaming PC with clip-on controllers.

Despite its solid hardware, the device struggles with usability and reliability issues. Users report various bugs, flaky software updates, poor battery life (just over 100 minutes of gaming), and awkward ergonomics due to its thickness and controller design. The keyboard stand is improved over the previous model but still limits lap use, and the tablet lacks auto-rotate functionality in Windows 11.

While gaming performance is impressive for the form factor, the overall experience is marred by cumbersome handling, unreliable boot and charging behavior, and fragile controller attachments. At a price point starting around $1,280, the X1 Air faces strong competition from lighter gaming handhelds and dedicated laptops, making it difficult to recommend except for users willing to accept its compromises.

Australia Implements Ban on Social Media Accounts for Under-16s

Starting today, Australian law prohibits children and teenagers under 16 from having accounts on ten major social media platforms including Twitch, TikTok, Facebook, Instagram, Reddit, Snapchat, Threads, X, and YouTube. Platforms must take reasonable steps to enforce this or face fines up to AU$49.5 million.

The ban aims to protect young users from harmful content and addictive design elements. It excludes online gaming and standalone messaging services like Roblox and Steam, despite ongoing concerns about child exploitation in parts of those environments.
