Marvel Rivals Expanding Beyond Shooter Formula

NetEase's creative director Guangyun Chen has indicated that Marvel Rivals will evolve beyond its original six-versus-six player versus player (PvP) shooter format. The game plans to introduce a broader "Marvel moving anime" experience that includes new player versus environment (PvE) modes and a tie-in event called Path to Doomsday, inspired by the upcoming MCU movie Avengers: Doomsday. This initiative aims to diversify gameplay, potentially including co-op boss fights and other content themed around the Infinity Saga. This shift could affect how players engage with the title, moving it closer to narrative or anime-styled experiences rather than pure PvP competition.

Wizards of the Coast Uploading 1980s Dungeons & Dragons Cartoon

Wizards of the Coast has begun uploading episodes of the 1980s Dungeons & Dragons cartoon to YouTube, releasing multiple episodes weekly. The series, co-produced by Marvel Productions and animated by Toei, features notable voice actors such as Peter Cullen and Frank Welker. While the show reflects the animation and storytelling style of its era, it remains an interesting cultural artifact for fans of D&D and 1980s animation. The release serves both nostalgic purposes and as a low-key way to engage older fans as the franchise continues to expand.

Fighting Game YouTuber Offers $10,000 Bounty for Improved Marvel vs. Capcom 3 Netcode

Popular fighting game content creator Maximilian Christiansen has announced a $10,000 bounty for developers or modders who can implement rollback netcode for Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3, currently using less responsive delay-based netcode. Rollback netcode predicts player inputs to reduce latency and improve online gameplay experience. Christiansen’s offer underscores ongoing community efforts to improve online play for older fighting games that were not originally designed with modern netcode standards. Enhancing netcode could significantly extend the active player base and enjoyment of this decade-old title.

Rockstar Games Faces Ransom Demand After Data Breach

Rockstar Games acknowledged a security breach involving its outsourced Snowflake cloud data storage, exploited via a recent breach of a third-party analytics tool, Anodot. The hacking group ShinyHunters claims to have accessed Rockstar's data and is demanding a ransom to avoid leaking it. Rockstar stated that only a limited amount of non-material company information was accessed and affirmed no impact on players or operations. Given Rockstar's history with leaks, including the extensive GTA 6 leak in 2022, this incident highlights ongoing cybersecurity vulnerabilities stemming from third-party vendors and raises concerns about the protection of sensitive data in major game studios.

BioWare’s Challenges and Potential Path Forward

A recent analysis reflects on BioWare’s current state following mixed reception to recent releases like Dragon Age: The Veilguard and the departure from the studio’s earlier critical and commercial highs. While The Veilguard garnered some positive reviews and robust sales initially, it ultimately underperformed against EA’s expectations and lacked significant post-launch support. The article suggests that BioWare faces institutional pressures under EA that prioritize big-brand sequels and live service models, limiting creative freedom. It argues that BioWare’s revival would require breaking from reliance on established franchises such as Mass Effect and Dragon Age, finding new identities and development approaches, possibly including smaller-scale projects and experimentation. However, given EA’s influence and market pressures, a true turnaround appears challenging.

Graveyard Keeper 2 Developers Address AI Art Usage Concerns

Developers of Graveyard Keeper 2 have responded to community accusations that promotional art for the upcoming sequel was generative AI-created. The studio clarified that despite the CTO’s personal experimentation with AI technologies, the game’s official art was produced by a team of approximately eight artists without the use of AI. This exchange reflects broader industry and player sensitivity regarding AI involvement in game development and content creation. The developers’ transparency aims to reassure fans and maintain trust ahead of the game’s release.
