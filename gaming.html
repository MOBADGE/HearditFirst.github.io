Esoteric Ebb: A New Reactive RPG with a D&D Twist

Esoteric Ebb, released on March 3, 2026, is receiving praise as a standout RPG that blends the narrative depth of Disco Elysium with the mechanics of Dungeons & Dragons. Developed by Christoffer Bodegård and published by Raw Fury, the game centers on a cleric protagonist investigating a mystery in a unique fantasy world. Unlike traditional RPGs focused on combat, Esoteric Ebb emphasizes exploration, character attributes that voice their own opinions, and dialogue-driven combat encounters. The simplified skill system uses core attributes for checks, offering a fresh take on character interaction. Players can customize their approaches, from spellcasting to rogue-like stealth, and choices significantly alter story outcomes. The game’s humor and rich writing stand out, delivering a layered experience with surprising reactivity and memorable world-building. A few UI and polish issues remain but do not detract from its strengths as one of 2026’s best RPGs, especially on PC and Steam Deck.

Highguard Shutdown Highlights Risks in Live Service Games

The 3v3 shooter Highguard is shutting down its servers less than two months after launch, underscoring longstanding problems in the live service market. Despite a core group of players and competent gameplay, the game failed to build a sustainable audience quickly enough to satisfy its investor Tencent, which cut funding abruptly. This incident exemplifies a broader industry trend where even experienced developers with successful prior live service games struggle to replicate hits. Similar struggles at Riot Games and Valve illustrate that quality and expertise do not guarantee commercial success in this volatile genre. The fallout includes multiple cancellations and layoffs across the industry, as companies reassess risky investments and scale back large projects. The situation raises questions about sustainability and business models for live service games moving forward.

Dice A Million: A Dice-Rolling Roguelite with Challenging Bosses

Dice A Million, launched last week and currently on sale on Steam, is a roguelite that combines dice-rolling mechanics with rapidly escalating challenges. Players roll various dice types to meet score targets, with extra points allowing the purchase of new dice and power-ups. The game stands out for its inventive dice designs, including dice that mimic other dice and those with enchantment abilities. However, tough bosses create significant obstacles by imposing unique conditions, such as only counting odd rolls or disabling dice effects intermittently. The game's difficulty rises steeply, especially due to an expensive in-game economy after bosses. Despite this, the gameplay loop of assembling a versatile dice set and experimenting with magical combinations offers engaging variety.

World of Warcraft: Midnight Demonstrates Single-Button Healing Build

A player known as Dvalin Gaming demonstrated a successful single-button healing build for the holy paladin class in World of Warcraft: Midnight. The game features a single-button assistant feature that cycles through abilities automatically, meant primarily for casual or accessibility use. While traditionally considered weak for healing roles, Dvalin’s build leverages multiple talents and abilities that provide passive area healing and shields. This approach enables effective group healing in normal dungeons with minimal input. The example challenges assumptions about the single-button system’s limitations and may encourage further experimentation with build design to simplify gameplay while maintaining functionality.

Capcom Reports Majority of Sales Coming from PC

Capcom’s latest quarterly report reveals that approximately 50% of the publisher’s game sales now occur on PC, surpassing combined sales on PlayStation 5, Nintendo Switch, Xbox, and smartphones. This marks a significant shift reflecting Capcom’s increasing focus on PC development. The report notes ongoing efforts to address technical issues in Monster Hunter Wilds, which have impacted Steam user reviews. Capcom plans to apply lessons from these challenges to future projects. Historically slower to launch PC versions, Capcom has accelerated its support for the platform, with PC already accounting for 60% of digital sales in 2024. This trend indicates a strategic pivot emphasizing PC as a key market for the company's growth.
