Dune: Awakening Makes PvP Optional in Endgame Zones

Funcom announced a significant change to the PvP system in their MMO Dune: Awakening. After nearly a year of trying to balance PvP and PvE interactions, the developer will disable all PvP zones in the Hagga Basin area and add separate instances for PvP and PvE players in the Deep Desert endgame zone. Previously, players needed to engage in high-risk PvP to access the best resources, which sparked ongoing complaints.

This update emphasizes the game’s PvE focus, with over 80% of players engaging exclusively in PvE content. PvE players will be able to explore and survive without player combat, while PvP players can enjoy heightened risks and rewards, including a 2.5 times increase in resource yields. Additionally, Funcom plans to introduce self-hosted servers allowing players to customize gameplay and run private worlds, though the initial setup requires some technical know-how and capable hardware.

This change matters as it reshapes player progression choices, making PvP strictly optional and potentially broadening the game’s appeal to those steering clear of forced PvP encounters.

Sony Confirms Metal Gear Solid Movie Nearly 20 Years After Initial Announcement

Sony has confirmed it is moving forward with a Metal Gear Solid movie, essentially reviving a project first announced by series creator Hideo Kojima around 2006. The film will be produced by director duo Zach Lipovsky and Adam B. Stein, who have various projects lined up but are now attached to this adaptation.

The announcement ends two decades of uncertainty, with previous attempts at the movie failing to materialize despite director and casting rumors. While no details on casting or production timelines were provided, Sony’s involvement indicates renewed momentum for what remains a high-profile video game adaptation.

This development is important because it shows persistence in adapting popular game properties in Hollywood, though whether this iteration will reach screens remains to be seen given the project's history of delays.

World of Warcraft Adds Prop Hunt-Style PvP with Decor Duels

Blizzard will introduce a prop hunt-inspired PvP mode called Decor Duels in World of Warcraft with patch 12.0.5, releasing April 21. The hide-and-seek style game takes place in Silvermoon City, where one team disguises themselves as everyday objects and the other hunts for those out-of-place items.

This mode makes creative use of WoW’s player housing decor assets and expands the functionality beyond traditional housing. Participants can queue via the PvP group finder and earn in-game rewards such as mounts, toys, and housing decorations.

Decor Duels adds a fresh, social PvP experience that leverages existing game features in new ways, contributing to the evolving World of Warcraft endgame and community interaction.

Rust 2 Steam Page Appears Amidst Confusion and Developer Teasing

An incomplete Steam page for Rust 2 surfaced recently, sparking speculation about a sequel to the popular survival game. The listing was flagged as suspicious by SteamDB, and the Reddit account that shared it was newly created, raising doubts about authenticity.

Facepunch’s COO Alistair McFarlane responded cryptically, adding to the uncertainty with messages implying something is underway but not confirming anything directly. The developer has previously mentioned plans for Rust 2, though no formal announcement has been made.

This incident highlights how ambiguous marketing or leaks can stir conversation in the gaming community. Whether Rust 2 is officially imminent remains unconfirmed but suggests development interest from Facepunch.

Europa Universalis 5’s First Campaign Divides a Veteran Player

A longtime Europa Universalis 4 player shared frustration after spending 93 hours on a first Europa Universalis 5 campaign. While appreciating the deeper simulation and complexity, they criticized the lack of distinct national identities and flavor that characterized EU4’s gameplay.

The new installment demands more micromanagement and economic focus, with rebellions and internal politics overshadowing aggressive expansion. Veteran fans may find the experience less rewarding because each faction plays similarly and lacks the individual narrative elements and unique missions that made previous titles engaging.

This critique matters as it reflects the challenge Paradox Interactive faces in evolving a series without losing key elements that defined its success.
