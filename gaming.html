Moves of the Diamond Hand Early Access Launch and Demo Update

The sequel to the lesser-known 2022 RPG *Betrayal at Club Low*, titled *Moves of the Diamond Hand*, will enter early access on April 13. Developed by Cosmo D, the game expands on the surreal world of Off-Peak City with a more immersive first-person perspective and a multi-act structure. A significant update to its demo, which covers the full first act, is now available on Steam for players to try ahead of release.

This sequel builds on its predecessor’s mix of narrative-driven RPG gameplay and exploration. Players take on the role of a secret agent undercover as a pizza delivery person, weaving through branching story paths and character interactions reminiscent of *Disco Elysium*. The demo received praise for balancing absurd humor with noir storytelling and offering a highly reactive experience. The forthcoming early access aims to continue this reactivity across multiple sandbox-style acts.

The early access release and demo update provide an opportunity for players to engage with this unique RPG world while the developer refines the experience based on player feedback.

Diablo 4 Director on Ongoing System Overhauls in Action RPGs

Zaven Haroutunian, game director of *Diablo 4*, discussed the ongoing major overhauls that have occurred since the game’s launch three years ago. He described these “transitions” as normal for action RPGs, where unforeseen issues and evolving player needs necessitate repeated system changes over tens of thousands of gameplay hours.

*Diablo 4* has seen substantial reworks to its loot system and max level cap, which can give the impression of uncertainty about the game’s direction. Haroutunian emphasized the challenge of balancing frequent updates with player retention, noting that while some systems may feel obsolete or awkward over time, developers must address problems to keep the game functional and engaging.

This approach mirrors long-running support seen in other ARPGs such as *Diablo 3* and *Path of Exile*. The director’s comments underscore the iterative nature of live-service action RPGs and the continuous evolution based on player feedback and gameplay data.

Crimson Desert Guides: Pets, Contribution, and Gameplay Mechanics

Several new guides provide details on gameplay elements in the open-world action RPG *Crimson Desert*.

**Pets System:** Players can befriend cats and dogs by building trust, increased through petting (up to five times daily) and feeding (three times daily with varying food quality). Once trust reaches 100 points, the animal can be adopted as a pet, following the player and even looting items while summoned. Pets can be managed via the inventory, with a maximum of 30 allowed.

**Contribution Currency:** Contribution is a regional currency earned primarily through quests, requests, and good deeds. It can be spent at region-specific shops—such as the Contribution Shop in Hernand Castle—to purchase special gear. Contribution points can be lost by committing crimes like stealing. The system encourages a balance of earning and spending within the local economy.

**Stealing Items:** To steal in *Crimson Desert*, players must equip a mask, which enables the steal option at relevant objects. Masks can be bought early in Hernand or earned via quests and enemy drops. Stealing introduces a hide mechanic to avoid detection and penalizes players by reducing their Contribution points.

Additionally, there are guides addressing specific tasks such as chopping down trees and mining ore for early-game requests, using the *Blinding Flash* skill to unlock doors and stun enemies, solving the Hernand Castle Strongbox puzzle, and locating keys to open generic locks in the game world.

These practical tips aim to help players navigate *Crimson Desert*’s sometimes unclear controls and systems, enhancing the experience without relying on external assistance.
