The Planet Crafter 2.0 Update Launches with New Content and Visual Enhancements

The Planet Crafter, a survival game centered on terraforming an alien world, has received a substantial 2.0 update from developer Miju Games. This update introduces a new biome on the main planet Prime, alongside improved existing biomes. To assist player navigation, new portals and decorations have been added. The game’s visuals also received a makeover, featuring enhanced skyboxes, terrain, vegetation, and water shaders to lift the overall presentation. An interesting addition is the dynamic evolution of terraformed moons and other celestial bodies visible in the sky, reflecting the player’s broader influence.

Gameplay improvements include a jetpack chip for the player’s rover, making traversal of difficult terrain easier, and several new craftable items such as lockers, arcade machines, vending machines, showers, and toilets. The update also brings a variety of balance tweaks and bug fixes. To coincide with the update, the game is currently half-priced on Steam until April 20, making it a timely opportunity for new or returning players to explore the enhanced version.

Soulmask Exits Early Access with Free Egypt-Themed DLC

Tribal survival game Soulmask has officially left early access and launched version 1.0 with a comprehensive overhaul. Developer CampFire Studio simultaneously released a new DLC titled Shifting Sands, which introduces a large Egypt-themed map that replaces the original Mayan jungle setting. This DLC adds 10 new ruins and dungeons, six bosses, and 325 new talents players can develop for their tribes. Players can also build new vessel types, including wooden boats and airships, expanding the scope of mobile bases available.

CampFire is offering this Egypt-themed DLC for free for one month to all owners of the base game, making it accessible to new and existing players alike. Alongside the DLC, the launch update reorganizes the game into three modes: survival, tribe-focused civilization building, and a combat-centric warrior mode that minimizes survival elements. A training ground mechanic has been added allowing talent inheritance among tribe members, and a new onboarding system helps ease players into the game’s many features. Soulmask is currently available at a 10% discount until April 23.

Marvel Rivals Expands Beyond PvP, Plans New PvE Content

Marvel Rivals creative director Guangyun Chen discussed plans for the game to evolve beyond its original six-versus-six player versus player format. The studio intends to develop the game into a broader "Marvel moving anime experience," a phrase left somewhat open to interpretation. More concretely, Marvel Rivals will introduce new PvE modes and a tie-in event named "Path to Doomsday," which corresponds to the upcoming Avengers: Doomsday movie set for December 18 release.

The new event will add themed content and five Infinity Saga-inspired updates, expanding the gameplay variety. Marvel Rivals previously experimented with PvE through a Marvel Zombies mode in a Halloween event. The developer’s goal appears to be diversifying the game’s offerings beyond standard PvP to attract a wider audience through fresh content and narrative integration.

Rockstar Games Hit by Data Breach, Hackers Demand Ransom

Rockstar Games confirmed a security breach involving their outsourced Snowflake cloud storage service. The hacking group ShinyHunters claims to have gained unauthorized access by exploiting a third-party analytics provider, Anodot, and is demanding a ransom while threatening to leak sensitive data if their demands are unmet. Rockstar stated that the breached information is limited and non-material to company operations or player data.

This incident echoes a major 2022 leak that revealed Grand Theft Auto 6 details early, renewing concerns over developer security practices. The hackers have targeted several large companies in recent years using similar methods, emphasizing the risks posed by vulnerabilities in third-party service providers. The situation remains ongoing as Rockstar deals with the threat and assesses the full scope of the breach.

Graveyard Keeper Developers Address AI Use Claims

Following the announcement of Graveyard Keeper 2, some community members expressed suspicion about the promotional art, suggesting it might have been generated using AI tools. The development team at Lazy Bear Games responded, clarifying that AI technology was not used in the creation of the sequel’s artwork. Slava Cherkasov, Lazy Bear’s CTO, acknowledged experimenting individually with different AI models but assured that these experiments do not influence Graveyard Keeper’s official content.
