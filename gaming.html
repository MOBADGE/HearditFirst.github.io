**Retro-Inspired Psychedelic Shooter ChainStaff Offers a Fresh Take**

The indie developer Mommy’s Best Games has released ChainStaff, a new psychedelic alien shooter that stands out among retro-inspired games by drawing from less commonly tapped influences. Unlike many modern retro games that echo popular classics like Zelda or Metroid, ChainStaff evokes the vivid, surreal art styles reminiscent of the 1990s Psygnosis titles and early computer run-and-guns such as Turrican. Its gameplay centers around using a versatile grappling weapon while navigating bizarre, brightly colored environments filled with oversized insects and strange creatures. This approach highlights the potential for innovative design within retro aesthetics, encouraging a broader exploration of gaming history beyond well-worn templates.

**Microsoft Excel’s 1900 Leap Year Bug Will Remain Unchanged**

Microsoft has acknowledged that Excel’s date system incorrectly treats 1900 as a leap year, a long-standing issue inherited from Lotus 1-2-3 software introduced in the 1980s. Although this is historically inaccurate—1900 was not a leap year—the compatibility challenge it introduces makes correcting the error infeasible. Fixing it would shift all existing dates before March 1, 1900, cause formula errors, and break interoperability with other programs. Given Excel’s extensive user base, currently estimated between 750 million and one billion, the risk of widespread disruption outweighs the benefits of accuracy. The bug persists primarily affecting outcomes in legacy date calculations, but has limited impact since most users do not work with dates predating 1900.

**GTA 6’s User-Generated Content Could Create New Millionaires**

Known GTA community figure HipHopGamer has suggested that Grand Theft Auto 6 will foster a significant user-generated content (UGC) ecosystem, potentially enabling creators to earn substantial income. Building on Rockstar’s recent acquisition of the modding platforms behind FiveM and RedM, GTA 6 is expected to integrate official mod support and marketplaces where players can create and monetize content. This reflects a broader industry trend toward games as platforms hosting player-driven economies. While GTA 6 will launch on consoles later this year, PC players remain without a confirmed release date. The development of a vibrant UGC marketplace could have considerable implications for how content and revenue streams are shared in the game.

**Songs of Glimmerwick: A New Ethical Wizard School Sim Launches Playable Demo**

Eastshade Studios has released a playable demo for Songs of Glimmerwick, a life simulation game set in a magic school environment that emphasizes inclusive and conscientious storytelling. The game combines gardening, music-based spellcasting, and community interactions within a softly stylized 2D world. It focuses on themes of social recovery from political oppression, portraying a society healing after a period during which magic and music were suppressed. With full voice acting and a save carryover planned for its upcoming full release this year, Songs of Glimmerwick aims to offer players an engaging narrative and an alternative to other wizard school-themed games fraught with controversy.

**Invincible VS Director Encourages Trying Every Fighting Game**

Dave Hall, director of the upcoming tag-team fighting game Invincible VS, encourages players to explore multiple fighting games, highlighting their straightforward fun and competitive depth. Drawing from his own experience starting with Street Fighter 4 and working on Killer Instinct, Hall notes that Invincible VS’s superhero roster and tag mechanics fit well together. The game is part of a wave of fighters launching in 2026, including Riot’s 2XKO and Arc System Works’ Marvel Tōkon. Invincible VS aims to be accessible yet complex, incorporating teammate assists into combos, and will feature single-player content developed with the show’s writers. The team actively engages with community feedback to refine gameplay ahead of release.

**Overwatch’s New Hero Sierra May Stir Competitive Debate**

Blizzard’s upcoming Overwatch hero Sierra has been revealed with an ability that marks enemies with darts, causing her subsequent shots to curve and home in on targets. This auto-aim feature could make her a polarizing pick in the competitive scene, potentially rivaling the controversial Jetpack Cat for the most banned hero. Sierra also has mobility options via grappling and an area-effect ultimate attack. Her abilities appear designed to offer strong tactical options but may provoke balance discussions due to the aim-assist element. Players will be able to try her starting April 14, with initial impressions likely to shape how she fits into ranked play.
