Marathon Update Increases Sound Range, Impacting Gameplay Dynamics

A recent patch in the competitive FPS *Marathon* increased the range at which gunfire and explosions can be heard in-game. While the update also included several fixes and balance changes, it is this sound adjustment that has caught the community’s attention. Players report that the enhanced audio range leads to more frequent third-party engagements, where teams or solo players quickly converge on ongoing fights. This has made matches feel more intense and chaotic, especially in smaller maps, reducing opportunities for stealth or isolated play.

The developer did not provide a detailed rationale for the sound change, leading players to speculate that it was intended to increase PvP encounters. However, reception is mixed; some find the change disrupts the game’s previous balance, particularly for solo players who feel punished for making any audible noise. The community is hoping for a possible reversion or fine-tuning in future patches.

Jeff Kaplan Shares Insights on Reinhardt’s Design Origins

Jeff Kaplan, former director of *Overwatch*, recently spoke about his personal connection to Reinhardt, one of the game’s original tank heroes. Kaplan revealed that Reinhardt was his main and praised the hero’s straightforward yet effective design. The iconic shield mechanic encourages a “protector” playstyle, which has since influenced many other games.

Notably, Kaplan explained that Reinhardt’s charge ability was inspired by a special infected called The Charger from *Left 4 Dead 2*. The charge is a high-risk, high-reward move that distinguishes skilled players and adds tactical depth in battles, especially when matched against another Reinhardt. Kaplan values simplicity in hero design, citing Reinhardt and Cassidy as examples of characters with intuitive, engaging mechanics that foster meaningful team interactions without complexity.

Dave the Diver’s “In the Jungle” DLC Set for June Release

The fishing RPG *Dave the Diver* is expanding with its upcoming DLC, *In the Jungle*, scheduled to release on June 18. The new content shifts the setting to Utara, a jungle village by a freshwater lake, and introduces a real-time day-night cycle, adding time management and relationship-building elements reminiscent of games like *Stardew Valley*.

Players will balance diving, fishing, combat, and new social activities, with a new open-layout restaurant hub serving as a central point of interaction. The DLC also adds approximately 10 hours of gameplay and introduces the Jungle Gun, a versatile weapon transforming between several forms for underwater encounters. Originally planned for 2025, the expansion’s slight delay has not diminished expectations for its deeper gameplay systems.

Vin Diesel’s Ark 2 Character Was Rewritten to Fit His Persona

Studio Wildcard revealed that the character Santiago, voiced and portrayed by Vin Diesel in *Ark 2*, was originally written as a "nerd." However, during collaboration with Diesel, the team adjusted the character to better align with the actor’s physical presence and personality, making Santiago a "buff, awesome nerd," blending intelligence with strength.

Diesel is also credited as “President of Creative Convergence” at the studio, reflecting his deep personal involvement backed by extensive playtime in the original *Ark: Survival Evolved*. Voice work from Diesel has been sporadic so far, featuring in teasers and minor roles related to the game and its associated animated series. *Ark 2*’s release remains slated for 2028 after multiple delays.

Bravely Default Flying Fairy HD Remaster Launches on Steam Without Prior Announcement

Square Enix silently released *Bravely Default Flying Fairy HD Remaster* on Steam, bringing a high-definition update of the 2012 Silicon Studio classic to PC. The remaster includes updated graphics, a redesigned user interface, fast-forward options for cutscenes, and new minigames while retaining the original’s turn-based "Brave and Default" battle system.

This release extends the franchise’s availability beyond Nintendo platforms, now verified for Steam Deck with a launch discount until March 26. A digital art album is also offered as part of the package. The sudden arrival on PC broadens access to the game for fans and newcomers alike.
