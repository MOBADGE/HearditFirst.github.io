AMD's Next-Gen Zen 6 CPUs Adopt Intel's FRED Interrupt Handling

AMD has revealed technical details of its upcoming Zen 6 CPU architecture, notably adopting Intel's Flexible Return and Event Delivery (FRED) interrupt handling system. FRED replaces the decades-old Interrupt Descriptor Table (IDT), streamlining how CPUs manage system events like inputs and network packets. This change suggests Zen 6 may represent a major architectural revision rather than a minor update, contrary to AMD's usual pattern of significant redesigns every other generation.

FRED aims to improve CPU efficiency by reducing the complexity of interrupt handling, which could lead to better performance and stability. Both AMD and Intel CPUs will benefit, as FRED is ratified by the x86 Ecosystem Advisory Board. Linux creator Linus Torvalds praised FRED's approach compared to legacy systems, noting AMD previously developed its own workaround for IDT issues. AMD plans to launch Zen 6 CPUs later this year, which could mark a meaningful step forward in processor design.
