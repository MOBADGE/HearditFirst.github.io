ZA/UM’s Zero Parades Moves Away from Police Drama

The developers behind the acclaimed RPG Disco Elysium have shared insights on their new game, Zero Parades. Initially, the story concept leaned toward police-related themes similar to Disco Elysium’s detective narrative. However, the team moved away from this direction due to changing perceptions of police in society and a desire to explore new creative territory. Instead, Zero Parades became an espionage thriller, aiming to deliver a fresh genre experience while maintaining some narrative depth. This shift reflects how current political and social climates influence game storytelling. For players and fans of ZA/UM, it indicates a change in tone and themes from their previous work.

Frictional Games Bundle Offers Classic Horror Titles for $15

Frictional Games’ horror repertoire, including Penumbra, Amnesia, and SOMA, is now available as part of a $15 Humble Bundle. This collection features all major releases from the studio, showcasing the evolution of their horror design from the early Penumbra trilogy through the well-known Amnesia series and the more story-driven SOMA, up to their latest, Amnesia: The Bunker. The bundle provides both newcomers and long-time fans access to influential titles at a significant discount. It emphasizes the lasting impact of these games on the horror genre and offers a comprehensive look into Frictional’s horror legacy.

Path of Exile Trading Hub Leader Banned, Raising Community Concerns

Jenebu, the leader of The Forbidden Trove (TFT)—a major Discord-based trading community for Path of Exile—has had his main PoE account banned. Known within the community for managing rare item "mirror services" involving the duplication of valuable in-game items, his ban has caused disruption in the player-driven economy. Jenebu appealed for restoration of his items, including over 1,500 rare ones, some irreplaceable. The ban aligns with Grinding Gear Games’ ongoing efforts to combat real-money trading and unauthorized item duplication. The incident highlights tensions between community-driven trading platforms and official game policies, impacting players reliant on TFT for trading and game resources.

Deus Ex: Human Revolution’s Composer Alexander Brandon Reflects on Missed Opportunity

Alexander Brandon, the original Deus Ex composer, discussed his involvement with Deus Ex: Human Revolution and its soundtrack. Although he composed a remake of the original theme intended for the prequel’s end credits, the track was ultimately rejected for not fitting the game's style. The project’s audio direction was steered by Michael McCann, whose distinct soundtrack gave Human Revolution its unique sound identity separate from the original. Brandon expressed understanding of this decision but noted disappointment around other spin-offs where he was not involved. His reflections offer insight into creative transitions and team dynamics in franchise continuations.

Mohawk Games Maintains Small Studio Approach for Sustainable Development

Soren Johnson, co-founder of Mohawk Games, emphasized the value of keeping the studio small and focused. With around 15 employees, Mohawk avoids over-expansion to maintain financial stability and reduce layoffs. This approach means slower development cycles but aims to improve creative quality and workplace conditions. Their upcoming Old World DLC, Empires of the Indus, arriving March 30, continues the grand strategy game’s expansion. Johnson also mentioned interest in exploring other genres like tactical RPGs but prioritizes a sustainable pace and long-term studio health over rapid growth.

PUBG: Blindspot Shuts Down Shortly After Early Access Launch

PUBG: Blindspot, a top-down tactical shooter by Arc Team published under Krafton, is ending its early access less than two months after launch. Despite initial promise, low player counts led to matchmaking difficulties, which further suppressed the player base in a feedback loop common to multiplayer games. This shutdown reflects the challenges free-to-play titles face in achieving and maintaining concurrent player numbers necessary for sustainability. Krafton’s broader financial and corporate issues may have influenced the decision. Arc Team has not announced future plans but intends to regroup.
