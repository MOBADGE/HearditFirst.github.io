**Slay the Spire 2 Multiplayer Explained**

Slay the Spire 2 recently introduced a multiplayer mode, allowing up to four players to cooperate in the deckbuilding roguelike. To start a co-op game, one player hosts a multiplayer save that friends join via Steam; however, there is currently no local co-op or matchmaking, and players must be friends from the beginning of the campaign. Each player maintains their own inventory, energy pool, gold, and relics, while enemies scale to the party size and attacks affect all members. Battles require all players to end their turns before progressing, and fallen players are revived if the team wins. Ascension levels in multiplayer are separate from single-player, requiring all players to have unlocked the desired level to play together. This mode retains the core roguelike experience while enabling cooperative strategies. Its arrival marks a significant expansion of the game’s appeal following its record-breaking launch.

**Slay the Spire 2: Best-Performing Deckbuilder on Record**

Industry analysts from Newzoo report that Slay the Spire 2 has become the best-performing deckbuilder ever, based on metrics such as peak concurrent Steam users and early active player counts. The game reached a peak of 575,000 concurrent Steam players shortly after launch—far surpassing competitors like Balatro and the original Slay the Spire. It also sold around three million copies in its first week. Newzoo attributes this success to several factors, including seven years of player anticipation and the developer’s reputation for transparency and lack of monetization friction. The strong launch suggests it has attracted a broader spectrum of players, including those typically less drawn to roguelike deckbuilders. The impressive early engagement supports its sustained multiplayer and solo appeal.

**Marathon Review: Bungie’s Extraction Shooter**

Released earlier this month, Marathon is Bungie’s return to its original FPS series with a focus on extraction shooter mechanics. The game blends intense PvP encounters with a loot-driven progression set on the colony world Tau Ceti IV. Match durations are short, encouraging multiple plays and frequent player interaction across smaller, dense maps. Marathon combines signature Bungie gunplay with strategic loadouts and a richly developed backstory told through optional codex entries, audio logs, and non-linear narrative elements. Players assume control of digital consciousnesses inhabiting disposable shells to complete contracts. Despite some interface and balancing issues, the game’s unique mix of lore, gun handling, and map design has been well received. The first major seasonal reset is planned for June, placing importance on future content updates.

**Nova Roma City Builder Introduces Demanding Gods**

Nova Roma, a new city builder in early access on Steam, challenges players with managing not only the needs of their citizens but also the demands of multiple Roman gods. Players build temples and must balance worship by completing quests and providing offerings to various deities who provide bonuses. However, neglected gods become antagonistic, causing natural disasters and other setbacks. This system adds a layer of complexity to traditional city-building mechanics by requiring players to manage their relationships carefully. The game offers faster pacing than typical city builders and emphasizes continuous engagement. Nova Roma is notable for introducing this mythological tension alongside standard urban management tasks.

**OpenAI Discontinues Sora Video App; Disney Withdraws Investment**

OpenAI has announced it will discontinue its video generation app Sora less than six months after launching version 2. The decision is part of a strategic shift away from video generation products to focus on productivity tools like an integrated desktop app combining ChatGPT and coding assistants. Sora’s launch brought copyright concerns due to its ability to generate videos featuring unlicensed intellectual properties, leading to legal worries particularly from Japanese studios. Meanwhile, Disney has withdrawn from a $1 billion investment deal with OpenAI tied to Sora, citing the company’s pivot away from video generation. This turn of events underscores the challenges of deploying AI video tools amid copyright issues and shifting corporate priorities.

**Dark Outlaw Games, New Studio from Call of Duty Veteran, Closed by Sony**

Sony has closed Dark Outlaw Games, a first-party studio founded in 2024 by former Call of Duty director Jason Blundell. The studio was reportedly in early development on an unannounced project and had about 50 employees laid off as part of Sony’s recent strategic adjustments and workforce reductions. This closure follows Sony’s shuttering of another notable studio, Bluepoint Games, reflecting ongoing industry pressures
