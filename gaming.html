Nvidia’s DLSS 5 Draws Mixed Reactions and Memes

At Nvidia’s recent GTC keynote, CEO Jensen Huang highlighted the company's ongoing commitment to gaming and AI-powered graphics, revealing the upcoming DLSS 5 technology. Designed to improve game visuals via AI upscaling and enhanced lighting, DLSS 5 debuted with games like Resident Evil Requiem. However, the technology’s effect on character appearances, particularly facial features, has sparked widespread reactions. Some players feel that DLSS 5 imposes an AI-driven beauty filter on characters, creating uncanny or dramatically altered looks. This sparked a wave of memes and jokes across social platforms, with examples exaggerating the AI’s effect dubbed the “yassification” of characters.

Despite the humor, it is important to note that Nvidia stated game developers retain artistic control over the final visuals. DLSS 5 also brings tangible improvements in environmental lighting and may extend the life of older hardware, which is relevant given ongoing PC component supply issues. Its reception remains mixed, and further refinement is expected in coming months.

Native PC Port of GameCube’s Animal Crossing Emerges

A significant development for fans of cozy gaming is the arrival of a native PC port of the original Animal Crossing from the GameCube, created through an ongoing decompilation project. This allows players to run the classic title on PCs, though it requires ownership of the original game disc as the port does not include game assets.

While still in early stages, with minor audio and graphical issues being addressed, the project promises to bring the nostalgic experience of managing a village of animal neighbors to a new platform without the need for outdated hardware adapters. This is noteworthy as Animal Crossing has long been coveted on PC, and the port may encourage renewed interest in the title or inspire future emulation projects.

Nvidia’s Vera CPU Showcases Strong Single-Core Performance but Aims at AI Servers

Amid the focus on DLSS 5, Nvidia revealed further details about its Vera CPU, designed primarily for AI servers rather than consumer PCs. Featuring 88 custom Arm-based “Olympus” cores, Vera boasts high single-threaded performance and energy efficiency, with innovations such as a 10-wide instruction fetch and a neural branch predictor. The chip supports 176 simultaneous threads and offers a large memory bandwidth thanks to a wide LPDDR5X interface.

Currently, Vera is targeted at AI factories and agentic AI workloads. There is speculation that Nvidia’s future PC processors may eventually adopt these custom cores, replacing the off-the-shelf Arm designs used in the current generation. However, no plans have been announced for Vera or Olympus cores to appear in gaming PCs soon, making this a development to watch rather than expect imminently.

Arc Raiders Nerfs the Il Toro Shotgun Following Player Feedback

In its latest update (1.20.0), Arc Raiders has significantly nerfed the Il Toro shotgun weapon, a change driven by community requests to reduce its dominance, especially in close-quarter areas like the Stella Montis map. Modifications include lowered pellet damage, reduced fire rate, increased dispersion, and notably slower reload times, all aimed at making the weapon less effective at range and less overpowering in PvP.

Additionally, the update lowers the sell price of Energy Clips to curb a crafting exploit that allowed players to generate easy in-game currency. Embark, the developer, has committed to monitoring the impact of these changes and adjusting balance further if needed.

Studio Wildcard Adapts Elements of Atlas for Ark: Survival Ascended DLC

Studio Wildcard has announced that some mechanics and features from Atlas, its pirate-themed survival game that launched in 2018 to mixed reception, will be reworked into Ark: Survival Ascended's upcoming "Tides of Fortune" DLC. Atlas was known for its detailed ship mechanics and vast open-world ocean but was criticized for being punishing and having a steep, unforgiving gameplay loop.

"Tides of Fortune" aims to incorporate ocean sailing and ship customization in a streamlined, more accessible form. The updated water physics and visuals represent an improvement over Ark’s traditional flat ocean surfaces. Players will be able to build pirate ships, equip attachments, and bring dinosaurs aboard, maintaining Ark’s trademark elements while exploring pirate-themed gameplay.
