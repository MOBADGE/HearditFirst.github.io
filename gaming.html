Atlas Mechanics Adapted for Ark: Survival Ascended DLC

Studio Wildcard is incorporating certain elements from their earlier pirate survival game Atlas into the upcoming Tides of Fortune expansion for Ark: Survival Ascended. Atlas, which launched in 2018, was criticized for its punishing survival mechanics, including laborious resource gathering and rebuilding after defeat, which hindered its success. However, Ark’s co-founders recognize the strengths in Atlas’s sailing and water physics systems. The new DLC will feature large pirate ships sailing across a more realistic ocean with simulated physics and improved water visuals. This reuse of Atlas technology aims to offer Ark players a more accessible nautical experience without the excessive difficulty that hampered Atlas. Modders may also benefit from these enhanced physics systems.

Monster Hunter Stories 3: Guide to Rare Monster Mutations

Monster Hunter Stories 3 offers players the chance to acquire rare Monsties through a mutation system linked to ecosystem restoration. Mutations produce subspecies with boosted stats and enhanced abilities. The process involves raising ecosystem ranks by hatching and releasing monsters, sometimes under specific environmental conditions or in the presence of particular other species. Notable mutations include Pink Rathian, Ivory Lagiacrus, and various exalted forms of Zinogre and Tigrex. These mutations, while not required to finish the story, provide strategic advantages in combat. The article outlines detailed conditions to trigger each mutation, serving as a resource for players aiming to optimize their monster collections.

Overwatch Director Reflects on Delayed 6v6 Response

Aaron Keller, Overwatch’s game director, addressed the controversy around the shift from six to five players per team in Overwatch 2 at this year's Game Developers Conference. The change significantly altered gameplay and alienated many players who preferred the original 6v6 format. Keller acknowledged the development team’s delay—waiting over a year—to respond meaningfully to community feedback, which impacted player trust. Blizzard eventually reintroduced a 6v6 mode following testing that confirmed demand for it, but the team remains uncertain about maintaining multiple core game modes indefinitely. Keller emphasized the importance of consistent communication and iteration moving forward, recognizing that protecting the franchise’s core identity is vital.

Jeff Kaplan Reveals Early WoW First-Person Ambition

Former Blizzard vice president Jeff Kaplan shared insights into World of Warcraft’s development, revealing that he initially pushed for a first-person perspective instead of the third-person view the game is known for. Drawing from his experience with EverQuest, which was first-person, Kaplan believed this viewpoint would deepen players' personal connection to their characters and others. Although the team ultimately chose third-person for practical reasons, Kaplan’s reflections highlight an alternate design path for WoW that might have changed the MMO genre’s evolution. This perspective offers context on past design decisions shaping modern MMORPG conventions.

Bungie Tests Experimental Duos Mode in Marathon

Bungie announced plans to introduce an experimental duos matchmaking mode for Marathon starting March 18. The limited-time test will take place on a single map, Perimeter, and run for about two weeks, subject to player feedback and potential adjustment. Currently, Marathon supports solo and three-player squads, and duos mode represents a new team size that could alter gameplay dynamics such as map flow and resource distribution. Bungie plans to use this trial to understand the impact on matchmaking and game balance before further development. Players interested in duos mode can participate in the upcoming test to help shape its future.

Nvidia’s DLSS 5 and AI-Driven Visual Changes Raise Questions

Nvidia unveiled DLSS 5, an AI-powered upscaling technology that significantly alters in-game visuals using "3D-guided neural rendering." Unlike previous versions that enhanced performance while preserving original graphics, DLSS 5 applies real-time changes that can modify character appearances. Nvidia stresses that developers retain artistic control over these effects, noting endorsements from studios like Bethesda. Still, observations reveal that the AI introduces features reflecting contemporary beauty standards, altering facial proportions and details beyond lighting improvements. This dynamic raises broader discussions about creative control, authenticity, and the influence of AI on game art. DLSS 5 is slated for release in the fall with initial support in several upcoming titles.
