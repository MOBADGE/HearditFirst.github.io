**Ubisoft Proposes Voluntary Departure Plan to Cut 200 Paris HQ Jobs**

Following a major restructuring announcement last week, Ubisoft has opened discussions about a voluntary mutual termination agreement known as a Rupture Conventionnelle Collective to reduce approximately 200 positions at its Paris headquarters. This move aims to avoid conventional layoffs by offering severance and career assistance to employees who agree to leave voluntarily. Ubisoft currently employs around 1,100 staff at this location. The plan is subject to agreement with employee representatives and approval by French authorities. However, labor representatives criticize the approach, arguing it pressures workers following recent announcements about degradation in working conditions. The proposal applies solely to Ubisoft International employees under French contracts and does not affect staff at other Ubisoft locations.
