**Linux Revives Windows 8's Metro Interface with Win8DE Shell**

A developer named er-bharat has created Win8DE, a Linux shell that recreates the Windows 8 tile-based Metro interface on Wayland window managers such as Labwc and Hyprland. Windows 8 originally introduced this blocky, tile-focused Metro UI in 2012, primarily designed for touchscreens, but it was widely criticized for feeling cluttered on traditional PC monitors and was dropped in later Windows versions. Win8DE includes features like a crowded start menu, app lists, wallpaper tools, and on-screen volume and brightness controls, though it excludes the Windows 8 Charms bar, which the developer found unnecessary. While this shell may not appeal to all users, especially gamers or corporate users, it might find interest among those looking to repurpose old tablets or enjoy a nostalgic, retro aesthetic.

**Amazon GPU Purchase Scam Highlights Risks in High-Value Orders**

A Reddit user reported receiving an Asus RTX 5060 Ti graphics card instead of the RTX 5080 they ordered via Amazon Prime. Due to the similar design of the Asus Prime series cards, including near-identical triple-fan cooling solutions and 2.5-slot form factors, the substitute card was disguised by swapping product stickers. This type of "switcheroo" scam involves ordering a high-end GPU, replacing it with a cheaper model, and returning the fake for a refund, potentially passing undetected by warehouse staff. The incident raises caution about the potential for such fraud when purchasing expensive hardware through online marketplaces, highlighting the importance of verifying products immediately upon receipt and considering recording unboxing as evidence.
