Super Battle Golf Hits 100,000 Copies Sold in Two Days

The multiplayer golf game *Super Battle Golf* has sold 100,000 copies within just 48 hours of release, according to developer Brimstone. The game was developed in 4.5 months and offers a fast-paced twist on golf by blending traditional gameplay with action elements such as orbital lasers and rockets to disrupt opponents. Its competitive, inebriation-friendly multiplayer mode and low price point seem to resonate with players looking for accessible and chaotic fun. This rapid success highlights how creative spins on classic sports can attract players by breaking away from conventional gameplay.

Deckbuilding Roguelike Handmancers Delays Launch to Avoid Clashing with Slay the Spire 2

58BLADES, the developer behind *Handmancers*, a rock-paper-scissors themed deckbuilding roguelike, announced a delay of its planned release originally set for March 9. The postponement is meant to avoid launching simultaneously with the highly anticipated *Slay the Spire 2*, which enters early access on March 6. This cautious move reflects a wider trend among indie developers to steer clear of release windows crowded with big titles to maximize visibility and player engagement. The delay will also allow extra time for polish, balance adjustments, and adding new content to improve the final product.

Project: Gorgon Revives Old-School MMO Experience

*Project: Gorgon*, an indie MMORPG, has recently left early access and captured the attention of MMO enthusiasts with its unconventional, open-ended gameplay. The game abandons modern trends like streamlined tutorials and linear progression in favor of discovery, experimentation, and player-driven narrative through an intricate skills system. Players can develop unusual abilities, engage in quirky quests, and face unpredictable dangers in a world that encourages exploration and social interaction. Its success demonstrates there remains a niche appetite for old-school MMO designs grounded in challenge and surprise rather than polished simplicity.
