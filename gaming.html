Lawn Mowing Simulator 2 Launches Kickstarter with Advanced Grass Technology

Developer Skyhook Games has announced a Kickstarter campaign for *Lawn Mowing Simulator 2*, succeeding the original virtual lawn trimming game. The new installment expands gameplay with additional gardening tools such as strimmers and leaf blowers and moves the setting to the USA. A key highlight is its significantly enhanced grass rendering technology; the grass now features varied textures, colors based on sunlight exposure, and includes flowers and weeds for more realistic lawns. The Kickstarter has quickly surpassed its modest £15,000 goal, suggesting healthy community interest. While no firm release date is set, a 2026 launch is anticipated. This sequel matters in terms of pushing visual fidelity in simulation games and catering to niche audiences seeking detailed, relaxing gameplay.

Ivy Road Studio Shuts Down After Funding Struggles

The developer behind the cozy tea shop adventure *Wanderstop*, Ivy Road, announced it will close by March 31. The closure follows layoffs in January and failure to secure funding for a new project called *Engine Angel*. Despite attempts to find a publishing partner, the studio could not continue amid a challenging financial landscape for independent developers. *Wanderstop* remains available for purchase, and there may be upcoming news related to Annapurna Interactive’s involvement to extend its reach. Ivy Road’s closure highlights ongoing difficulties in raising funds for indie games, especially after a positive reception to their debut title, emphasizing the volatility facing small studios despite creative success.

Zero Parades Developers Opt for a Spy Thriller Over a Police Story

ZA/UM’s upcoming RPG *Zero Parades* initially considered a plot focusing on a fictional equivalent of the International Money Fund, involving police stories similar to their acclaimed *Disco Elysium*. The team chose to pivot away from cop narratives, citing shifts in public perception of police and a desire for creative differentiation. Instead, they selected a spy thriller genre to explore political intrigue and ideological conflicts, reflecting contemporary global tensions. This genre shift shows ZA/UM’s intent to innovate narratively while respecting the changing social context since 2019’s *Disco Elysium*. It may influence player expectations and how political themes are handled in future RPGs.

Complete Frictional Horror Game Bundle Available for $15

Humble Bundle has released a package offering all of Frictional Games’ major horror titles, including *Penumbra*, *Amnesia* trilogy, *SOMA*, and *Amnesia: The Bunker*, for $15. This bundle provides a comprehensive look at the evolution of narrative-driven survival horror games spanning over a decade. The inclusion of both early and more recent entries offers players an accessible way to experience a series known for its unsettling atmosphere and innovative mechanics. Players interested in horror or game history may find value in this collection, which is available until April 1, 2027, with Steam key redemption required before then.

Path of Exile Item Trading Leader Banned, Causing Community Uncertainty

Jenebu, the leading figure behind *Path of Exile*’s major player trading Discord server, The Forbidden Trove (TFT), was banned by Grinding Gear Games (GGG) amid old allegations regarding real-money trading. Jenebu’s account held over 1,500 rare items used for a "mirror service" that allowed players to duplicate very rare items via an in-game mechanism. The ban disrupts a long-standing economy hub for PoE players, causing uncertainty about the server’s future and many valuable items’ fate. While no concrete proof of wrongdoing by Jenebu has emerged, GGG’s enforcement reflects a stricter stance on player trading and third-party marketplaces. This incident underscores challenges in balancing community-driven economies with developer policies.

PUBG: Blindspot Early Access Ends After Less Than Two Months

Krafton has announced the closure of *PUBG: Blindspot* early access on March 30, just under two months after its February launch. The top-down tactical shooter struggled with low player counts, resulting in long matchmaking times that discouraged new users. Although the game saw some positive reception for accessible tactical gameplay, it was unable to maintain a sustainable player base necessary for free-to-play monetization. Krafton’s financial constraints, including profit declines and recent workforce reductions, likely influenced the decision. The shutdown reflects risks involved in launching multiplayer-focused live-service games without robust initial audiences.
