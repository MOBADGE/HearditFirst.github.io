Rockstar Employees Denied Interim Pay in Union Dispute

A Scottish employment tribunal has rejected an interim relief request from over 30 former Rockstar developers fired in October 2025, who claimed their dismissals were due to union membership or activities. Rockstar originally cited gross misconduct and alleged confidential leaks on a public Discord channel. The Independent Workers of Great Britain (IWGB) union disputes this, labeling the firings as union busting and has initiated legal action.

The tribunal ruled that the employees did not sufficiently prove a likelihood that union activity motivated their firing, citing some employees were not union members and noting the quick terminations lacked formal disciplinary processes. However, the tribunal emphasized that this interim decision does not determine the final case outcome. The IWGB expressed disappointment but remains hopeful for a favorable ruling at trial. This case highlights ongoing tensions around unionization efforts in the games industry.
