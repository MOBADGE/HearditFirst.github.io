**Blippo+ Delivers a Quirky but Repetitive FMV Experience**

Blippo+ is a full-motion video (FMV) game that simulates receiving TV broadcasts from an alien world resembling a campy 1980s aesthetic. Players scroll through short live-action and animated shows that build a loose narrative about an alien planet reaching out to humanity via a spacetime bend. While the game's concept and some of its individual broadcasts, such as quiz shows and teen discussions, offer an intriguing and humorous experience, it struggles with repetitive content and minimal player interaction. Unlocking new episodes depends on watching a quota of shows, which often repeat, making the progression tedious. Ultimately, the overarching story remains unresolved, leaving Blippo+ as an interesting but flawed experiment in storytelling. It is available on Steam and itch.io.

**Deus Ex: Invisible War’s Audio Director Reflects on the Soundtrack**

Alexander Brandon, who served as the audio director for Deus Ex: Invisible War, recently discussed the game’s music and sound design in an interview. He acknowledged that there was room for improvement, citing this as his first time directing audio on a project. Nonetheless, he expressed pride in the thematic and melodic elements, especially the main theme, which featured vocals by his then-wife. The soundtrack was designed to adapt to locations and factions, contributing to the game's immersive tone despite its simpler gameplay systems. Although the game's sliced-level design had its flaws, Brandon felt the music was a strong component, with notable contributions from artists like Kidneythieves. He remains positive overall despite wishing for some changes.

**Resident Evil Requiem Patch Adds Photo Mode and Improves Cutscene Expressions**

Capcom released a patch for Resident Evil Requiem that introduces a photo mode, allowing players to adjust camera angles, apply filters, and tweak lighting for character snapshots. Although the camera movement is limited and does not enable changing light sources, the feature offers a new way to engage with character poses and scenes. The patch also addresses unspecified bug fixes that prevented progress or caused crashes and adjusts character expressions in some cutscenes to better convey emotions. This update adds content without changing core gameplay and offers players more tools for visual creativity within the game.

**Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous Offers Extensive Build Options and Unique Mounted Combat**

Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous stands out for its large variety of classes and subclasses, boasting 232 in total, which appeals to players who enjoy character customization and build experimentation. While some subclasses may feel niche or less useful within the campaign’s context, others, like the "Demon Slayer" Ranger, are notably powerful. The game emphasizes mounted combat, where mounts serve as effective tanks and enhance mobility and damage output, especially at higher difficulty settings. A recent expansion added a Ranger subclass that starts with a griffon mount, providing significant tactical advantage and flair. This depth and complexity continue to attract players who appreciate detailed role-playing systems.

**Star Wars Zero Company Director Discusses the Return of Classic PC Strategy Genres**

Greg Foertsch, creative director of the upcoming Star Wars Zero Company, shared insights on the resurgence of traditional PC gaming genres such as real-time strategy (RTS), turn-based tactics, and CRPGs. He noted that in the early 2000s, many such games struggled to transition to consoles effectively, partly due to technical and design limitations, including camera usage that didn't adapt well to television screens. Modern improvements in user experience and controller support have helped these genres regain popularity on multiple platforms. Foertsch cited the growing innovation within turn-based tactics games since 2012 and expressed hope that games will be evaluated simply on their quality regardless of genre labels.

**Owlcat Clarifies Use of AI in Expanse RPG Development Amid Player Concerns**

Owlcat, developer of The Expanse: Osiris Reborn, addressed player concerns about the use of generative AI in the game's production. The studio confirmed that while AI is used for internal tasks such as prototyping, placeholders, and vision coordination, all final game assets, including writing and voice acting, will be fully human-made. This position aligns with industry patterns where developers use AI tools to streamline early creativity phases but replace AI-generated content before release. Despite these clarifications, some players have responded negatively, expressing apprehension about AI’s role in creative work. Owlcat maintains that their approach facilitates iteration speed without affecting the final artistic integrity.
