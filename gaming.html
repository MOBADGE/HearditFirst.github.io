Five Notable New Releases on Steam

Several new games have recently launched on Steam, offering a range of experiences across genres. Among these is *Homura Hime*, a third-person action game reminiscent of classic titles like *Devil May Cry*, featuring a protagonist tasked with defeating powerful demon girls. Another is *Planet of Lana 2*, a sequel to the hand-painted cinematic platformer, expanding the world with new environments such as frozen peaks and deep oceans, maintaining a focus on platforming and puzzle-solving.

*iRacing Arcade* offers a more casual take on its serious racing simulation predecessor, with simplified mechanics and a cartoon-like style, developed by the makers of *Circuit Superstars*. *Shinehill* follows in the footsteps of games like *Stardew Valley*, casting players as an alien spy who must farm and integrate into a community with wider gameplay elements like fishing and dungeon crawling. Finally, *Angel Engine* brings an analog horror series to the gaming medium as a point-and-click horror surgery simulator, drawing on its source's unsettling aesthetic.

These games add diversity to Steam's large catalogue, providing options ranging from narrative-driven platformers to casual racing and horror simulators. Their releases reflect the ongoing breadth of indie and smaller studio output within PC gaming.

Extensive Ultima 8 Retrospective Available

A comprehensive three-hour video retrospective on *Ultima 8* has been published by YouTuber Majuular, continuing his in-depth docuseries on the *Ultima* franchise, foundational to the CRPG genre. The series breaks down the historical context, narrative, and design of *Ultima 8*, and explores the eccentricities of its creator, Richard Garriott.

*Ultima*'s influence extends to many modern RPGs; for example, Larian Studios’ founder cites *Ultima 8* as a key inspiration, and the series shaped the development of MMORPGs and first-person RPGs. While *Ultima 8* itself is not regarded as the best in the series, this retrospective provides significant context for the evolution of CRPGs and the genre’s current state.

For fans and scholars of RPG history, the video offers detailed insights into one of the genre's formative titles, contributing to the understanding of how contemporary role-playing games emerged.

Hytale Emphasizes Modding and Community Hiring

The development team behind *Hytale* has highlighted the importance of community modding in their ongoing plans for the game. Developer Hypixel recently announced a modding contest with 65 winners sharing a six-figure prize pool, encouraging creative contributions from players.

Simon Collins-Laflamme, founder of Hypixel, noted that many new hires to the growing development team have come directly from the modding community, emphasizing a work culture focused on hands-on creation. This approach keeps the studio agile and closely connected to engaged players, with the modding community seen as a foundation for *Hytale*’s future direction.

This strategy showcases how mod support can strengthen a game’s ecosystem and provide development pipelines that involve engaged creators, an increasingly common but still noteworthy approach in game production.

Exploring Crazy Taxi’s Cityscapes in Browser

A recent personal project by programmer Will has recreated levels from the Dreamcast game *Crazy Taxi* for exploration within web browsers. Hosted on the noclip website, this project allows users to freely navigate the game's city environments rather than racing through them in the original arcade format.

This recreation uses an open-source method to emulate the original rendering processes, overcoming challenges such as undocumented file formats. The project offers a new perspective on the design of *Crazy Taxi*’s urban landscapes, highlighting the detailed layout and visual landmarks beyond the game’s racing objectives.

For fans and preservationists interested in video game environments, this project provides a unique means of experiencing a classic title’s worlds in a different context while waiting for the still-developing *Crazy Taxi* sequel.

Slay the Spire 2 Developer Reflects on Unexpected Success

Mega Crit, developer of *Slay the Spire 2*, addressed a recent sarcastic comment they posted regarding the concurrent player count of the game *Marathon*, another indie title. The comment was intended jokingly but was later described as coming across as "a bit meaner than expected" after *Slay the Spire 2* surpassed *Marathon* in player numbers unexpectedly.
