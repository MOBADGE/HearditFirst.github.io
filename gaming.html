Moomintroll: Winter's Warmth Demo Provides a Glimpse into a Cozy Adventure

The recently released demo for *Moomintroll: Winter's Warmth* offers players a small slice of what the full game will deliver. The demo begins with little introduction and drops players directly among familiar Moomin characters, requiring minimal prior knowledge of the series to enjoy. Players are tasked with rescuing a lost dog in a snowy landscape, initiating a short but touching journey that highlights the game's focus on companionship and atmosphere. The hand-painted art style and gentle pacing encourage players to appreciate the detailed, storybook-like world rather than stress over challenges or complex mechanics. Although the demo ends quickly and leaves many story elements unexplored, it sets a tone that fans of previous Moomin games will likely find appealing. No release date has been announced yet.

King of Meat Studio Faces Layoffs After Underwhelming Launch

Glowmade Games, the developer behind the co-op platformer *King of Meat*, is reportedly planning layoffs following the game's poor performance since its release. Expectations set by the publisher Amazon apparently aimed for concurrent player peaks as high as 100,000, but Steam numbers only peaked at 320 during a free weekend, rapidly dropping to a mere dozen active players. While the game is also available on PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series consoles, even generous estimates suggest it has fallen far short of the anticipated player base. Despite significant marketing efforts, including a Gamescom 2024 announcement and a Mr. Beast special, the game failed to gain traction. These developments come amid recent cutbacks at Amazon Games, including the halting of other projects, signaling a difficult path ahead for *King of Meat* and Glowmade.

Razer Revives the Boomslang Gaming Mouse with a Limited Edition Model

Marking 20 years since the original release, Razer has introduced the Boomslang 20th Anniversary Edition, a modern wireless iteration of its early gaming mouse. The new model upgrades the tracking to 45,000 DPI and adds features like 8,000 Hz polling rate, optical switches rated for 100 million clicks, and a wireless dock with what Razer calls "next-gen" charging. It retains the ambidextrous shape of the original but adds PU leather-wrapped buttons for improved feel and grip. The mouse features customizable Nine-Zone Razer Chroma RGB lighting and eight programmable buttons. Razer is limiting production to 1,337 units globally, targeting collectors. There is some historical ambiguity in celebrating the 20th anniversary because the original Boomslang dates back to 1999 and included mechanical ball tracking. The new edition reflects the 2005 company re-founding as an American-Singaporean entity.
