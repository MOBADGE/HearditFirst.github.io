Vampire Crawlers Launching This April

Poncle has announced that Vampire Crawlers, a fast-paced card game spinning off from Vampire Survivors, is set to release on April 21 for multiple platforms including PC, PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X/S, and Nintendo Switch. Priced at $10, this game blends deckbuilding and roguelike dungeon crawling. Early impressions highlight its addictive nature despite a simpler strategy system, suggesting it may demand significant player time. The game’s demo remains available on Steam for those interested in assessing it ahead of the full launch. This release adds another option for players seeking quick, engaging roguelike experiences across platforms.

Counter-Strike 2 Reload Mechanic Overhaul Meets Mixed Reaction

Valve introduced a significant gameplay change in a recent Counter-Strike 2 update: when players reload, all unused bullets in the magazine are discarded instead of being preserved. This adjustment makes reload decisions riskier, aiming to add strategic depth. Accompanying the change are tweaks to ammo counts across various weapons and a new HUD element displaying ammo and magazine status. The update also added optional competitive map guides and simplified friend invitations in custom games.

The community reaction has been largely negative, with many players frustrated by the departure from a core mechanic established over 27 years. Some appreciate the added skill requirement to manage ammo carefully. Valve’s willingness to experiment with fundamental gameplay elements is notable, though the long-term adoption of this change remains uncertain, especially given the vocal backlash.

Marathon’s Competitive Mode Explained Through a Math Test

Bungie has provided a detailed explanation of the complex Ranked mode in Marathon, their extraction shooter, by creating a math worksheet to help players understand its scoring and ranking system. Marathon’s competitive mode diverges from traditional formats by considering factors such as starting gear value, score targets based on equipment, penalties for failing to extract, and points gained for looting and player kills.

This approach is meant to balance risk and reward in a genre where victory conditions are less clear-cut than standard shooters. By providing a hands-on method for players to grasp the scoring mechanics, Bungie is addressing the inherent complexity of competitive extraction gameplay. This may help players engage more deeply with the mode and appreciate its strategic nuances.

Steam Spring Sale 2026 Now Live

Valve’s 2026 Steam Spring Sale has started, running until March 26 and offering discounts on thousands of games across all genres. This is only the fourth official Spring Sale in Steam’s history, now joining the traditional seasonal sales in summer, fall, and winter. Highlights include steep discounts on well-known titles like The Long Dark, Fallout: New Vegas, The Division 2, Civilization 5, and Star Wars: Jedi Survivor. The sale features a “deep discount” section aimed at offering significant bargains on popular games.

The event provides an opportunity for players to expand their libraries at reduced prices, continuing Steam’s role as a major sales platform for PC gaming.

Analysis of 8 GB vs 16 GB Graphics Cards in 2026 Gaming

Recent real-world testing compares 8 GB and 16 GB VRAM budget graphics cards, specifically the Nvidia RTX 5060 Ti and AMD RX 9060 XT variants, across several modern titles including Cyberpunk 2077, Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2, Arc Raiders, Resident Evil Requiem, and The Last of Us Part 1.

Findings show that while 8 GB cards remain capable of delivering playable frame rates at 1080p and 1440p, the 16 GB models consistently achieve higher average and 1% low frame rates, leading to smoother gameplay. AMD cards generally utilize more VRAM than Nvidia equivalents, which contributes to the performance gap seen in 8 GB variants. Lowering graphics settings significantly improves performance for 8 GB cards, making them a viable choice for budget-conscious gamers who adjust detail levels.

This testing underscores that 8 GB VRAM is still adequate for many current games but suggests that having 16 GB provides noticeable benefits, especially as game demands increase and frame rate targets rise.
