**Custom Plushies Renew Interest in Webkinz**

Fans of the early-2000s collectible plush-toy-meets-browser-game Webkinz are seeing renewed engagement through handmade custom plushies. Some Webkinz pets, such as the Cotton Candy Sheep and Magical Retriever, were originally virtual-only and never had physical counterparts. Creative community members like Instagram's Factory_of_Hearts craft detailed plush versions of these virtual pets, replicating original design details. This hands-on approach rekindles enthusiasm among nostalgic collectors and offers a way to complete collections despite the end of official plush production in 2019.

**Xalavier Nelson Jr Shares Gaming Insights**

Xalavier Nelson Jr, the creative director behind Strange Scaffold and Space Baby Warlord Trading Simulator, discussed his ongoing relationship with various games. He highlighted current engagement with the tactical shooter Cultic’s second chapter and revisiting Outriders, which he praised for its ambitious but somewhat flawed design and expressed regret over the cancellation of its planned sequel. Nelson Jr also noted his enduring attachment to Warframe, a live-service game he won’t uninstall due to its evolving content and respectful approach to player time. He shared that the classic 1997 title Blood remains the oldest game on his PC and recalled deep engagement with Worms: Crazy Golf from childhood.

**Zoo Life Simulator’s Challenge Lies in Animal Management**

Zoo Life Simulator, an early access zoo management sim, presents a mix of challenges and humor. Starting with a minimal budget and only a few animals like chickens and rabbits, the player quickly learns that managing an animal population requires careful planning. Issues such as constant feeding needs, animal behavior, guest satisfaction, and trash management emerge. The game’s physics and UI quirks add to the complexity, while the purchase of large numbers of chickens illustrates the game's balance between profitability and increasing maintenance demands. The simulation’s current state suggests it benefits from co-op play but still requires further development before wide appeal.

**Altera Combines Military Simulation with Unusual Elements**

Altera is a military survival sandbox aiming for realism in combat and base building within a large, central European-inspired environment. Despite this serious tone, the game incorporates an unusual feature: penguin soldiers equipped with reflex sights and capable of firing weapons and grenades. This quirk traces back to Altera’s original sci-fi conception, where animals fought alongside humans. The developer has shifted toward a more grounded setting but kept this eccentric element. Altera is currently recruiting players for playtesting via Steam, with no set release date as it prepares for early access.

**Vaunted Offers Tactical RPG with Narrative Shifts**

Vaunted is a sci-fi tactical RPG from developer Lost Lake Games, blending turn-based combat and real-time action elements. Players lead a crew of mercenaries entangled with a criminal syndicate, navigating a story told from multiple character perspectives. A notable mechanic allows rewriting story events from different viewpoints upon character death, adding variety to successive playthroughs. The game includes roguelite features where characters earn “credibility” to unlock upgrades for future runs. Led by James Phinney, known for work on StarCraft and Guild Wars, Vaunted has no confirmed release date but is anticipated later this year.

**Artificial Detective Mixes Action Adventure with Sci-Fi Noir**

Artificial Detective, from new developer Vivix, is a futuristic action-adventure game featuring a robotic detective probing a city after humanity’s disappearance. The game promises exploration, combat, and light puzzles across an open or nonlinear cityscape composed of ten districts accessible via flying streetcar. Gameplay includes sandbox encounters allowing multiple approaches like shooting, sneaking, or environmental interaction. Companion characters, such as a child and a robotic dog, assist during exploration. The reveal trailer highlights stylish combat sequences, including a scene where the detective attacks a robot with a large drill. Artificial Detective is expected to launch in 2027.

**Lizard State Seeks to Fill the Stealth Action Void**

In the absence of new Splinter Cell games, indie developer Benjamin Rose’s Lizard State aims to provide a stealth-action experience reminiscent of classic titles. The game features a single-night mission on an island with stealth mechanics including disabling cameras and non-lethal takedowns. Innovative gadgets like "mimic cameras" that transform into spider-like sentries and a player-controlled electric spider device offer fresh twists on surveillance and stealth gameplay. The developer is preparing a playtest phase; no release date has been announced.
