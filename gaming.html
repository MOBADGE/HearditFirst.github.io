Vampire Cathedral PC Mod Turns Custom Builds into 3D Printed Art

Small custom PC builder Zombie Tech Gaming, run by Zoë Baye and Brandon Ayala, has attracted attention with their unique 3D printed case mods inspired by gothic cathedrals and the game Bloodborne. What started as a bespoke build for a friend's vampire-themed PC evolved into a viral product: a magnetic cathedral kit for popular Hyte PC cases, blending architecture elements from vampire lore and stained glass aesthetics.

The couple handles all design and production themselves, with Brandon focusing on 3D modeling while Zoë builds the PCs. The cathedral kits require extensive 3D printing time, leading them to expand to nine printers to meet demand. Currently, the kits only fit certain Hyte cases, but they are working on compatibility for other brands. This project highlights a niche market for highly personalized, artistic PC customization beyond standard RGB lighting.

Far Cry Director Calls for More Acceptance of Asset Reuse in Games

Alex Hutchinson, director of Far Cry 4, spoke at GDC about the necessity and value of asset reuse in game development. He recalled the controversy when Far Cry Primal reused the terrain layout from Far Cry 4, which frustrated some players but was not officially explained by Ubisoft. Hutchinson noted that large studios inevitably reuse elements like animations or environments to build sequels efficiently.

He observed a cultural shift, pointing out that popular Japanese studios like FromSoftware openly reuse assets as a creative choice rather than a necessity, which fans now accept or even appreciate. Hutchinson argued that Western studios often waste resources by duplicating work, such as re-recording nearly identical gun sounds for each shooter game. With budgets and funding constraining projects, embracing asset reuse could help studios allocate resources better. However, Hutchinson remains skeptical that AI will significantly ease this challenge in the near term.

EA Discounts Dead Space Series but Future Sequels Appear Unlikely

EA has launched a sale offering significant discounts—up to 85% off—on the Dead Space franchise, including the 2023 remake by Motive and the original trilogy by Visceral Games. The remake is available for $9, offering graphical and mechanical updates like improved combat and Zero-G segments. While Dead Space 2 and 3 are also on sale at lower prices, there are no announced plans for further sequels or remakes.

Despite fan interest, EA reportedly rejected a proposal for Dead Space 4 in 2024. The publisher seems increasingly focused on sports titles and franchises with ongoing live services, leading to layoffs at some studios working on other series. For now, fans can revisit the franchise affordably, but new Dead Space entries appear unlikely.

Xenonauts 2 Exits Early Access in April with Ongoing Support Planned

Goldhawk Interactive’s Xenonauts 2, a tactical strategy game inspired by classic X-COM, will leave early access and officially launch April 2. Since entering Steam Early Access in mid-2023, the developer has released seven major updates adding new aliens, UFOs, story missions, and increasing the number of battle maps to over 180.

Although reaching 1.0 status, Goldhawk emphasizes the game will continue to receive patches and improvements post-launch, including the upcoming release of modding tools. Xenonauts 2 aims to provide a faithful but expanded take on turn-based tactical combat and base management, filling a niche for fans of classic tactical games now that XCOM 3 is not yet available.

Classic Half-Life Mod Zombie Panic Arrives on Steam After 22 Years

Zombie Panic!, a mod for the original Half-Life released in 2004, has finally launched on Steam. This cooperative survival mod pits groups of survivors against player-controlled zombies, where fallen survivors turn into additional zombies, gradually overwhelming the team. The new Steam release features a complete code rewrite to improve compatibility following Half-Life’s 25th anniversary updates.

The update includes new player models, weapons, reworked maps, a fatigue system, zombie vision mechanics, and integrates a hardcore mode from the mod’s Half-Life 2 sequel. While it requires ownership of Half-Life to play, the availability on Steam and workshop support may introduce this pioneering mod to a wider audience after more than two decades.
