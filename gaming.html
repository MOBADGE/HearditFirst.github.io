**Appreciating the Art of FPS Skyboxes**

A recent feature highlights the often overlooked but important element of skyboxes in first-person shooter (FPS) games. Skyboxes are the distant backgrounds—such as skies, mountains, and cities—that help create a sense of immersion. While modern games often pursue photorealistic environments with complex weather effects, some older or stylized skyboxes remain visually striking and contribute more effectively to the game's atmosphere. The article reviews exemplary skyboxes from various games including the Halo series, Warhammer 40,000: Boltgun, Straftat, Doom Eternal, Battlefield 6, Marathon, classic Source engine games, and Overwatch. It notes that good skyboxes can tell stories, establish mood, and enrich the player's experience without necessarily relying on realism.

**PUBG: Blindspot Ends Early Access After Low Player Engagement**

PUBG: Blindspot, a top-down tactical shooter released by Krafton in early access this February, will shut down its service on March 30, less than two months after launch. Despite initial positive impressions and promising gameplay blending tactical shooter elements with a top-down perspective, the game struggled with low player counts. This led to long matchmaking times that discouraged new players, creating a downward spiral common among free-to-play games reliant on active communities. Krafton's recent financial challenges, including profit declines despite revenue growth, a voluntary resignation program, and a hiring freeze, may have influenced the decision to discontinue the game. The developer team behind Blindspot will take time to regroup but no specific future plans have been announced yet.

**D&D Beyond Launches Simplified Character Creator**

D&D Beyond, an official digital toolset for Dungeons & Dragons, has introduced a new Quickbuilder feature to streamline character creation. This tool allows users, especially newcomers, to create playable characters in seconds by selecting class, species, background, and portrait through an interface inspired by videogames. While it automates the assignment of stats, equipment, and skills, users can still customize details manually as desired. The Quickbuilder seeks to lower barriers for new players who find traditional character sheets complex and intimidating. A design blog indicates future updates will add more detailed customization options and improved visuals. This update may assist in onboarding and retaining players unfamiliar with tabletop role-playing games.

**Overwatch Player Loyalty Statistics Revealed After Conquest Event**

Postmortem data from Overwatch’s season 1 Conquest event shows only 32% of players remained loyal to the Overwatch faction until the event’s end. Approximately 30% consistently supported the opposing Talon faction, while 38% switched sides weekly, categorized as "sneaky mercenaries." The event involved collecting in-game items and completing challenges over about nine weeks, resulting in over 250 million challenges finished. The analysis suggests player allegiance was influenced more by the appeal of new or featured heroes than by narrative factors. The data offers insights into player behavior in community-wide multiplayer events.

**Linux Kernel Maintainer Notes AI’s Increasing Usefulness for Development**

Greg Kroah-Hartman, a lead Linux kernel maintainer, reported a sudden improvement in the usefulness of AI tools for developers, particularly in generating security reports and assisting in code reviews. While earlier AI-generated outputs were unreliable ("AI slop"), recent developments have produced more accurate and actionable reports. The reasons behind this rapid progress are unclear but may relate to improvements in AI tools or their wider adoption. Kroah-Hartman shared an example where an AI system identified multiple coding issues, with most suggested patches being correct or helpful. The Linux kernel team has integrated an AI code review tool named Sashiko into its workflow. This reflects a growing acceptance of AI assistance in complex software development tasks.

**Concerns Raised Over Video Game Industry Stability**

Industry veterans Brenda and John Romero expressed concern about the current state of the video game industry, comparing it unfavorably to the 1980s crash. They cite widespread layoffs, project cancellations, financial pressures, and uncertainty about AI integration as key issues contributing to a difficult environment for developers. The Romeros have personally faced funding loss for a new shooter project but have managed to continue working at a smaller scale. The trend of large studios laying off significant staff despite successful game launches continues, leaving the future uncertain. Additionally, high hardware prices and component shortages exacerbate challenges for consumers and developers alike. Despite this, the Romeros reaffirm their commitment to creating games.
