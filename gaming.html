Resident Evil Requiem Adds Photo Mode and Improves Cutscene Expressions

Capcom has released a new patch for Resident Evil Requiem that introduces a photo mode, allowing players to capture and customize in-game screenshots. The camera movement is somewhat limited, and the lighting options are basic, but players can apply filters and adjust brightness. This addition follows other games like Mass Effect Legendary Edition that featured similar modes.

Alongside photo mode, the patch fixes bugs—including progress-stopping issues and crashes—and adjusts character expressions in some cutscenes to better convey emotion. While the changes to expressions may be subtle, the photo mode offers players a new way to engage with the game world and characters, potentially increasing replay value.

Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous Stands Out with 232 Subclasses and Mount Mechanics

Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous continues to impress with its extensive character customization, offering 232 subclasses spread across 26 base classes and 13 prestige classes. This level of detail appeals particularly to players who enjoy experimenting with builds. The diversity includes unique options like the halfling-exclusive “Order of the Paw” Cavalier who rides a hound, and the “Separatist” Cleric with distinct religious powers.

A notable addition is the “Sable Company Marine,” a Ranger subclass from the latest expansion—which allows players to ride a griffon (hippogriff) from level one. Mounted combat proves valuable at higher difficulties, providing tactical advantages by directing enemy attention toward mounts, allowing riders to act more aggressively. While the game has a reputation for difficulty, these systems encourage strategic planning and experimentation, with ongoing discussions among players about optimal builds.

Star Wars Zero Company Director Reflects on Rise of Classic PC Genres

Greg Foertsch, creative director of the upcoming Star Wars Zero Company, shared insights on the resurgence of traditional PC gaming genres such as real-time strategy (RTS) and turn-based tactics. He attributes a decline in these genres during the early 2000s partly to the industry's focus on consoles, which made translating PC-centric mechanics and interfaces difficult.

Foertsch notes that many early tactics games did not fully embrace camera control or the user experience required for console play. Recent advances in UI and control schemes have made these traditionally complex genres more accessible on consoles, contributing to their revival. He also highlighted that the explosion of turn-based tactics games since 2012 reflects both a respect for genre conventions and ongoing innovation.

Owlcat Confirms Human-Made Assets for The Expanse RPG Despite AI Use in Development

Owlcat Games clarified that while generative AI is used during early development stages of their upcoming RPG The Expanse: Osiris Reborn, all final game assets will be created by humans. The studio explains that AI assists mainly with prototyping, vision coordination, and inspiration, and no AI-generated content will appear in the final release.

This statement follows industry controversies around AI usage, with players expressing skepticism about claims that games are fully “human-made” if AI was involved at any stage. Owlcat emphasized that AI tools help speed up iteration but denied using AI for writing or voice acting, aiming to reassure fans concerned about content originality.

Mechabellum’s New Season Highlights Strategy as Player Interaction

The latest season of Mechabellum, a largely under-the-radar 1v1 (and occasional 2v2 or free-for-all) autobattler, underscores the game’s focus on strategic interaction over rote execution. Players deploy different mechs and respond dynamically to opponents’ moves, creating a tactical “conversation” rather than a pure puzzle to memorize.

This approach slows down the common trend in competitive games toward optimized, predetermined strategies. Instead, Mechabellum rewards real-time adaptation and understanding of opponent intent. Matches remain concise, making the game approachable for casual sessions, while offering depth for those who wish to engage more seriously.

Elder Scrolls: Blades Mobile Spinoff Shutting Down This Summer

After an eight-year run, Bethesda is officially shutting down The Elder Scrolls: Blades on June 30, 2026. The mobile-focused dungeon crawler and town-building game never arrived on PC, and its closure ends official support. Until shutdown, all in-game store items are available at minimal cost, and players receive free in-game currency bundles.
