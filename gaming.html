AOC Highlights Monitors as Practical Upgrades Amid Rising GPU and Memory Costs

At a recent press event, AOC emphasized that upgrading gaming monitors offers more value right now compared to graphics cards or memory, which remain expensive due to ongoing supply issues. Cesar Reyes Acosta, AOC’s European commercial leader, illustrated this by comparing current monitors with those from five years ago, noting significant performance improvements at similar price points. For example, a newer 27-inch 1440p monitor with 260Hz refresh rate and HDR support matches the price of an older 24-inch 1080p 165Hz monitor, reflecting better specs and competitive pricing.

AOC is launching new OLED models, including a 34-inch QD-OLED monitor priced at €999 and a 27-inch W-OLED model with a 540Hz refresh rate for €899. These options showcase the company’s push into advanced panel technologies despite higher costs. While it is understandable that AOC promotes monitors amid GPU and memory price inflation, the message underlines how monitors have become a practical, enduring upgrade for PC gamers.

Philips Introduces Western Version of Dual-Mode 5K Gaming Monitor with Ambilight

Philips announced that its Evnia 27M2D5901A 5K gaming monitor, previously available only in China, will launch in Western markets in July at €799. The 27-inch IPS panel supports dual-mode resolution switching between native 5120×2880 at 165Hz and 2560×1440 at 330Hz. This feature allows players to alternate between high resolution for immersive single-player games and high refresh rate for competitive play.

Unique to Philips’ model is the inclusion of Ambilight lighting with AI and Matter smart-home integration, which can sync the monitor’s back lighting with game events to enhance ambience. The monitor also offers 65W USB-C power delivery, adding convenience for laptop users. While the price is high for an IPS monitor, Philips positions it alongside similar products from Asus, targeting users interested in cutting-edge display technology and versatility.

Turtle WoW Private Server to Shut Down Following Legal Action from Blizzard

Turtle WoW, a popular private server for the original World of Warcraft, announced it will close its servers on May 14 after Blizzard secured a legal injunction against its operation. The server team reached a settlement but must comply with terms that include shutting down. Turtle WoW offered a "Classic Plus" experience with new raids and races but stayed within the scope of vanilla-era content.

The shutdown reflects ongoing challenges for fan-run servers operating without official licenses. Despite a plea from the Turtle WoW team for fan server frameworks, Blizzard has not relaxed enforcement. The closure echoes previous private server shutdowns like Nostalrius and highlights legal and community tensions surrounding emulated MMO experiences.

Portal 2: Community Edition Enters Open Beta, Enhancing Modding Tools

Portal 2: Community Edition (P2:CE), a community-maintained fork of the original game’s engine, launched its open beta to expand modding capabilities. Built on a modified CS:GO engine, it introduces improved lighting, higher-resolution textures, more flexible scripting, and an overhauled Hammer map editor.

The edition is not a remake but a platform to create more elaborate custom campaigns and mods, with early projects including a faithful Portal 1 remake within a single map. The developers plan to release an original campaign built on this framework. P2:CE aims to overcome limitations in the original game's modding environment and foster richer community content.

Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas Mod Lets Players Explore Manhunt’s Carcer City

A new total conversion mod for GTA: San Andreas, titled GTA: Carcer City, recreates the fictional city from 2003’s Manhunt within the GTA engine. The mod replaces San Andreas’ open world with a single urban setting inspired by Rust Belt cities such as Detroit and Cleveland, featuring districts like downtown, shipping port, airport, and old town.

Carcer City includes all locations from Manhunt and introduces a new storyline set in 2001, aiming for a darker, more immersive tone than typical GTA games. Currently available as a demo, the mod offers full city exploration and a storyline intro, with no announced completion date. It illustrates continued fan interest in expansive, creatively ambitious GTA mods.
