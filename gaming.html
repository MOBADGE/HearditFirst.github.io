Slay the Spire 2 Faces Review Backlash Over Upcoming Card Nerfs

Slay the Spire 2’s recent beta patch, which includes substantial balance changes to cards, artifacts, and enemies, has prompted a wave of negative reviews on Steam—even though these changes are not yet live on the main branch. Most criticism centers on the Silent character’s Prepared card being reworked into Prepare, which now costs energy and alters discard and draw mechanics. Players feel these changes reduce viable options in the game’s challenging runs.

An unusual aspect of this backlash is the significant number of negative reviews coming from Simplified Chinese users. Due to restrictions on Steam’s community features and Discord access in China, players there have limited avenues to provide feedback. This situation may have contributed to the review bombing as Steam reviews become one of the few visible channels to express dissatisfaction. Despite the negative spike, the overall English-language reviews remain overwhelmingly positive.

This incident highlights how regional platform restrictions can influence player communication and feedback, potentially leading to disproportionate public reactions on global storefronts.

OpenTTD Clarifies Independence Amid Atari’s Transport Tycoon Deluxe Re-release

OpenTTD, the open-source remake of the classic Transport Tycoon Deluxe (TTD), has requested players to stop blaming Atari for its removal from Steam and GOG. This removal accompanies Atari’s commercial re-release of the original 1995 TTD.

The OpenTTD team explains that they collaborated with Atari, who respect the project’s independence and allow OpenTTD to continue being available for free through its own website. To accommodate Atari's commercial interests, OpenTTD is no longer freely available on major storefronts, but existing Steam and GOG ownership remain unaffected.

Atari has also contributed financially to help maintain OpenTTD's server infrastructure. While some community members remain critical of Atari’s role, the developers ask for respectful discourse and emphasize the ongoing vitality of both projects.

Subnautica 2 CEO Raises Concerns Over Krafton’s May Release Announcement

Ted Gill, recently reinstated CEO of Unknown Worlds (developer of Subnautica 2), has expressed serious concerns about Krafton publicizing a May early access release date without his approval. His legal team argued that the announcement was unauthorized and harms coordinated marketing efforts.

This tension arises amid ongoing legal disputes over bonuses tied to the game’s milestones. Gill contends that development has been overseen by inexperienced leadership and that the announced timeline could pressure the team into a premature launch, risking quality.

Krafton maintains the release date was decided before Gill’s reinstatement and that he retains authority to adjust it. The disagreement underscores continuing friction between Krafton and Unknown Worlds leadership during this critical phase for Subnautica 2’s development.

Industry Survey Finds Strong Support Among Developers for AI Usage Disclosure on Steam

A recent survey of over 800 games industry workers found that nearly 9 out of 10 believe Steam should require developers to more fully disclose the use of generative AI in their games. This includes not only AI-generated content consumed by players but also tools used behind the scenes.

Nearly half of respondents disagreed with Steam’s current policy, which limits disclosure requirements to AI content directly experienced by players. Most developers support detailed disclosure methods, like checklists specifying AI use cases.

Notably, most surveyed studios report minimal current use of AI for asset generation, with more common applications including brainstorming, code generation, and prototyping. The results indicate ongoing desire for transparency about AI integration in game development.

Crimson Desert Sells Over 2 Million Copies Despite Mixed Reviews and Player Frustrations

Pearl Abyss announced that Crimson Desert sold more than 2 million copies worldwide within the first 24 hours of launch. This strong commercial performance contrasts with mixed player feedback due to the game’s complex controls, restrictive inventory system, and other design issues.

The developer acknowledged the community’s feedback and promised to address problems promptly to improve the player experience. Minor patches are already in place, while larger control and quality-of-life adjustments are expected later.

Additionally, Pearl Abyss confirmed the game does not support Intel Arc GPUs at launch, advising affected users to seek refunds when applicable.
