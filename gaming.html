Bungie to Buff Marathon’s Recon Shell with Improved Abilities

Bungie announced upcoming buffs to the Recon Shell in their extraction shooter Marathon, addressing player concerns about its relative weakness. The mid-season update, arriving April 14, will enhance Recon’s Echo Pulse ability, which now can differentiate between AI-controlled UESC robots and human players on the HUD. Additionally, Echo Pulse will become less visible to enemies, making it harder for them to detect when they are scanned. Players using Signal Jammer can appear as UESC to confuse Recon users. Bungie also revealed a change to Recon’s tracker drone to be “more aggressive” by allowing it to switch targets if blocked, reducing instances where it gets stuck or self-destructs prematurely. These tweaks aim to bring Recon more in line with other available Shells.

Take-Two Lays Off AI Team Including Head of Artificial Intelligence

Take-Two Interactive has laid off an unspecified number of employees from its AI team, including Head of Artificial Intelligence Luke Dicken, who announced his departure on LinkedIn. Dicken’s role, which began in early 2025 after joining from Zynga, involved guiding the company’s generative AI initiatives and keeping executives informed about AI opportunities and risks. This move contrasts with CEO Strauss Zelnick’s statements earlier this year emphasizing the company’s active embrace of generative AI. Reasons for the layoffs have not been disclosed. This development adds to recent industry-wide AI setbacks, such as OpenAI discontinuing a video generation app and Oracle's large-scale layoffs.

GameStop’s Digital Distribution Bet on Impulse Didn’t Pay Off

Larry Kuperman, former head of electronic distribution at GameStop, shared insights into Impulse, GameStop’s failed attempt to challenge Steam. Originally developed by Stardock from 2004-2008, Impulse was acquired by GameStop in 2011. Kuperman believed digital distribution would be his lasting career focus, but GameStop’s management considered digital sales a passing trend and continued to bet on traditional retail stores. This outlook hindered Impulse’s growth, and the service was eventually discontinued in 2014. Kuperman highlighted that Steam outperformed rivals by fostering a strong community with social features and accessibility, changes that Impulse and similar services failed to fully implement. The episode is seen as an example of missed opportunity during the early digital era.

Mega Crit Responds to Steam Review Backlash on Slay the Spire 2

Mega Crit co-founder Casey Yano addressed the backlash from Slay the Spire 2’s beta balance patch, which prompted over 9,000 negative Steam reviews within 24 hours. Yano acknowledged the difficulties players face feeling heard by developers, especially in China, where restrictions limit access to forums and official feedback channels, leaving Steam reviews as the only accessible outlet. While some criticism was aimed at the tone and volume of the reviews, Yano expressed understanding rather than frustration, emphasizing Mega Crit’s intent to improve communication methods globally. He also noted that the feedback from China generally reflected concerns similar to those from English-speaking players, just expressed differently due to platform restrictions.

Otherside Entertainment Shifts Thick as Thieves Focus to Singleplayer and Co-op

Otherside Entertainment announced a new development direction for Thick as Thieves, the Warren Spector-led stealth game initially planned as a four-player PvPvE multiplayer experience. The team now intends to focus on two-player cooperative and solo play, citing that these modes better highlight the game's dynamic stealth mechanics. While some fans expressed disappointment at the reduction in multiplayer scope, the change reflects a refinement based on internal testing feedback. PvPvE may still be considered post-launch, but the upcoming release will prioritize singleplayer and co-op modes. A new gameplay trailer and further details are expected at the Triple-I Initiative showcase on April 9.
