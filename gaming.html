**Death Stranding 2 Adds Hardest Difficulty for PC Players**

With the PC launch of *Death Stranding 2*, developer Kojima Productions introduced a new difficulty mode called "To the Wilder." This mode was designed in response to player feedback that previous difficulty settings, including the Brutal mode, were too easy. Lead level designer Hiroaki Yoshiike described this new option as pushing the challenge to "the upper limit" while maintaining enjoyment. In To the Wilder, environmental hazards like the Timefall rain have a stronger impact, making resource management and careful planning essential for missions. Players are encouraged to explore the full range of in-game items and leverage social features to navigate this tougher version of the game's world.

**Crimson Desert’s Controls Criticized, Developer Suggests Patience**

*Crimson Desert* has drawn criticism for its complex and sometimes frustrating control scheme since launch. Reviews and player discussions have labeled the controls as "convoluted" and clunky, with even controller users experiencing issues, such as accidentally jumping instead of interacting. Will Powers, PR and marketing director at Pearl Abyss, responded by comparing mastering the controls to learning to ride a bike, saying it becomes intuitive after some time. Despite the criticism, the game has been commercially successful, selling over two million copies shortly after release. Pearl Abyss acknowledged the feedback and promised to work on improvements, though no specific changes to controls have been announced yet.

**Kirsten Dunst Cast as Alex in Minecraft Movie Sequel**

Actress Kirsten Dunst is set to play the character Alex, a default female player avatar with red hair, in the upcoming *Minecraft* movie sequel scheduled for 2027. The sequel, produced by Warner Bros. and Legendary, builds on the commercial success of the first film, which grossed nearly one billion dollars despite mixed reviews. Returning cast members include Jack Black and Jennifer Coolidge, among others. Dunst has expressed interest in the role partly because her children enjoyed the first film and she considered it a financially safe choice. Jared Hess will return as director, with a script by Chris Galletta.

**Silksong Player Uncovers Cut Alternate Boss Fight**

A player-modder uncovered a hidden, unfinished version of the Act 1 boss fight with The Last Judge in *Silksong*. This alternate "voided" variant, intended for the secret third act, features dark-themed attacks and increased health. However, upon testing the fight through mods, the player found the encounter underdeveloped and somewhat unbalanced, with some attacks ineffective or oddly positioned. This discovery helps explain why Team Cherry likely removed this version from the final game, as it did not offer a significantly more challenging or polished experience compared to existing content.

**Slay the Spire 2 Faces Review Bombing Over Upcoming Card Nerf**

*Slay the Spire 2* has received over 9,000 negative Steam reviews within 24 hours following a beta patch introducing significant balance changes, particularly a controversial nerf to the Silent character’s "Prepared" card. Although the patch has not yet been applied to the main game, the reaction reflects player dissatisfaction with increased difficulty and reduced player options. Analysis suggests many of the negative reviews come from Chinese players, who, due to restrictions on Steam’s community and Discord access in China, may use review bombing as a primary means of feedback. The overall rating remains positive among English-speaking users but is notably lower among Simplified Chinese reviews.

**OpenTTD Clarifies Situation After Removal from Steam**

The open-source transportation simulation *OpenTTD* was recently removed from Steam and GOG to accommodate the commercial re-release of the original *Transport Tycoon Deluxe* by Atari. The OpenTTD development team emphasized that the project remains fully independent and free to download from their official website. Atari agreed to support the open-source game’s server infrastructure as part of the cooperative arrangement. Some community members have expressed frustration with Atari’s decision, but the developers asked for respectful discourse, pointing out that OpenTTD still offers a free alternative without restrictions on other platforms.
