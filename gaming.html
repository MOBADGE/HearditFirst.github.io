CyberPowerPC Launches MA-01 Case with Manual LED Controls

At CES 2026, CyberPowerPC introduced the MA-01, a mid-tower PC case that combines clean aesthetics with physical controls for RGB lighting. Notably, the case features three side-mounted knobs dedicated to adjusting LED color, brightness, and lighting modes, allowing users to fine-tune their setup without software. This approach is somewhat unusual in a market dominated by software-based RGB control and may appeal to users who prefer tactile adjustments.

The case supports ATX and micro-ATX motherboards, GPUs up to 420mm, CPU coolers up to 185mm, and PSUs up to 200mm. It offers practical front connectivity with two USB 3.0 ports, one USB 3.2 Gen 2 Type-C port, and a 3.5mm jack. Available in multiple finishes including dark silver, dark steel grey, off-white, and a pricier Chrome Steel version, the MA-01 is priced at $150 for standard models and $250 for the Chrome Steel variant, launching in Q2 2026.

This case stands out for merging elegant design with manual LED control, providing an alternative for users who find software control cumbersome or limiting. Its mid-range price positions it as a practical yet stylish option for both office and gaming setups.

Lego Introduces Smart Brick with Integrated Sound and Sensors

Lego announced a hardware innovation called the Smart Brick, a standard 2x4 Lego brick embedded with a custom ASIC, speaker, synthesizer, and multiple sensors including accelerometers and light sensors. Developed by an expert team spanning video gaming, computing, and sound engineering, the brick is designed to provide real-time audio responses and interact dynamically with other Lego Smart components, including Smart Tags and Smart Minifigures.

The speaker features an onboard synthesizer capable of generating a wide variety of sounds rather than relying on recorded audio. This enables nuanced audio effects that change based on movement and interactions, offering a fresh layer to physical Lego play without involving screens or external devices. The Smart Play system debuts with Lego Star Wars sets scheduled for March 2026, including an X-Wing, TIE Fighter, and a Throne Room Duel.

By focusing on integrated physical interactivity and in-brick audio synthesis, Lego is expanding play possibilities while avoiding reliance on screen-based augmented reality. This innovation could influence how traditional toys blend with digital features in the future.

Arc Raiders Plans Nerf for Trigger Nades Amid Player Concerns

The developers of Arc Raiders acknowledged community feedback on the power of Trigger Nades, grenades that can be detonated remotely and have become a disruptive element in multiplayer matches. The official Discord confirmed that the team is looking into nerfs affecting Trigger Nades and possibly other items, though details regarding the extent or nature of these adjustments are yet to be clarified.

The main point of uncertainty is whether the nerf will impact damage against players only or if it will also reduce effectiveness against the enemy Arcs. Since Trigger Nades serve a strategic role in combating AI enemies efficiently, a universal nerf could influence gameplay balance. The developers have not announced a timeline for the update.

This response reflects ongoing efforts to balance multiplayer experiences by addressing overpowered mechanics without compromising the game's cooperative elements.
