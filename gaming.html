**Star Wars Action Figures Featuring Iconic Knights of the Old Republic 2 Characters Sell Out Quickly**

Hasbro recently opened preorders for a Black Series action figure two-pack exclusive to Amazon, featuring characters from *Knights of the Old Republic 2: The Sith Lords*. The set includes Darth Nihilus, known for his planet-destroying presence in the game, and Kreia (Darth Traya), the game’s complex antagonist. This marks Kreia's first official action figure. The figures combine cloth and plastic elements, with Kreia’s set including her trademark three floating lightsabers. Despite interest, preorders sold out rapidly, suggesting strong demand from fans of the classic Star Wars RPG. Hasbro might consider additional production if interest persists. This release highlights enduring enthusiasm for *KOTOR 2* characters in gaming culture.

**Dead or Alive Creator’s Unusual Encounter with LAPD Revealed**

Tomonobu Itagaki, the late creator known for *Dead or Alive* and modern *Ninja Gaiden* games, was reportedly once held at gunpoint by Los Angeles police officers. The story emerged through an interview with a former collaborator, Kengo Aoki. Itagaki, known for his outspoken personality and controversial behavior, apparently attracted significant police attention during a chaotic incident at a hotel party before E3. The incident ended with Itagaki hospitalized for an extended period. This anecdote sheds light on the turbulent and eccentric life behind a developer who left a memorable mark on the fighting game genre and action titles in general.

**Graveyard Keeper Developers Deny Using AI for Sequel Artwork Amid Controversy**

Following criticism from players suspecting that AI was used in the promotional art of *Graveyard Keeper 2*, Lazy Bear Games’ CTO Slava Cherkasov publicly denied these claims. Although Cherkasov experiments with AI technology personally and posts AI-generated images online, he confirmed that the development team did not employ AI in creating the new game’s art or sound. The clarification aims to address concerns about AI use in creative processes amid broader industry debates. Player reactions remain mixed, with some accepting the explanation and others remaining skeptical until the final product is released.

**Rockstar Games Faces Ransom Threat After Security Breach**

Rockstar Games disclosed a data breach involving a limited amount of non-sensitive company information accessed via a third-party cloud service. The hacker group ShinyHunters claims to have gained access through an exploited analytics vendor and has demanded a ransom from Rockstar, threatening to leak data if unpaid. The studio stated the breach does not affect players or core organizational systems. This incident follows a history of security issues for Rockstar, including a major GTA6 leak in 2022. The situation underscores ongoing challenges companies face with third-party software vulnerabilities in protecting sensitive data.

**Proton Experimental Update Enhances Compatibility for Classic Capcom Games on Steam Deck and Linux**

Valve’s Proton experimental build received an update that improves the playability of several older Capcom titles on Steam Deck and Linux platforms. Notably, classic survival horror games such as *Resident Evil* (1996), *Resident Evil 2*, and the *Dino Crisis* series have been moved to "playable" status within Proton Experimental. While some issues persist—like FMV cutscene problems in *Dino Crisis 2*—this advancement makes it easier for players to enjoy these titles on portable hardware. The update demonstrates ongoing support for retro games in modern gaming ecosystems, catering to nostalgia and preservation.

**Analysis: BioWare’s Struggle and the Challenge of a Comeback**

A recent in-depth commentary examines BioWare’s current state, noting that the studio’s heyday producing landmark RPGs like *Baldur’s Gate* and the original *Mass Effect* trilogy has passed. The author argues BioWare’s recent projects, including *Dragon Age: The Veilguard*, have underperformed commercially and critically, reflecting broader difficulties with corporate oversight from publisher Electronic Arts. The piece suggests BioWare’s future lies not in continuing established franchises like *Mass Effect*, but in finding new creative directions and smaller-scale projects. However, it expresses skepticism that EA’s risk-averse approach will allow the necessary freedom for such a shift, implying that BioWare’s potential revival is constrained by corporate priorities.
