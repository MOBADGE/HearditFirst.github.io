Witchhunter.exe Blends Retro Adventure and Glitch Horror

Witchhunter.exe is an upcoming retro-style game that combines elements of choose-your-own-adventure storytelling with a combat system reminiscent of classic Gold Box Dungeons & Dragons games. Players take the role of a witch hunter in a setting inspired by the Salem witch trials, tasked with interrogating suspects for allegiance to the Devil. The Devil itself disrupts gameplay via pop-up windows that simulate glitches or system intrusions, adding a ghost-in-the-machine horror layer.

The game’s combat challenges players to select or type the correct words to defend themselves before being attacked. While the blend of retro aesthetics and story-driven mechanics may appeal to fans of atmospheric horror and seventies-style witch hunter cinema, the use of metafictional “haunted simulation” elements has been met with some reservation due to genre saturation. Witchhunter.exe is currently in development with a beta playtest forthcoming, and interested players can follow it on Steam.

Five Notable New Steam Releases

A selection of five recently launched Steam games highlights a variety of indie and niche experiences:

- **Mama’s Sleeping Angels** offers a multiplayer dream-survival shooter with surreal powers and a mysterious narrative, drawing comparisons to games like Cruelty Squad but featuring Y2K-inspired visuals.

- **Lucid Blocks** is a procedurally generated voxel world combining liminal and misty aesthetics, with infinite vertical exploration and crafting, and survival against strange creatures.

- **The State of Nowhere** channels Papers, Please-style mechanics by having players control food distribution within a totalitarian regime, balancing scarce resources and moral dilemmas across 46 endings.

- **Thysiastery** is a pixel art roguelike dungeon crawler focusing on turn-based combat and puzzle solving inside a shape-shifting labyrinth.

- **Pluto** mixes roguelike deckbuilding with dungeon exploration, where the player is an imprisoned wizard trying to escape for a personal reason involving family. It features elemental synergies and unexpected creatures.

These titles illustrate the continuing diversity in indie game development, spanning genres from tactical moral simulators to challenging pixel-art roguelikes.

Tomb Raider Remasters Receive Critical Patch Featuring Controversial Outfits

The remastered collection of Tomb Raider I, II, and III was recently updated with a free patch that added a new challenge mode and ten new character outfits. However, the quality of the new cosmetic assets has sparked significant backlash from players and fans. The outfits have been described as having low visual fidelity, and the update reportedly introduced bugs affecting audio, enemy AI, and save data.

Notably, the original development team at Saber Interactive was not involved in the creation of this patch. This has raised concerns and speculation about the use of generative AI in the design of new assets, a topic of ongoing debate given previous controversies related to AI-generated content in the Tomb Raider remasters. The patch has drawn mixed responses, underlining challenges in managing legacy game updates while meeting community expectations.

GTA Roleplay’s High-Stakes Hide-and-Seek and GTA 6 Multiplayer Expectations

GTA Online’s roleplaying community has embraced a new form of entertainment: hide-and-seek where the loser’s vehicle is at risk of being destroyed. This twist adds tension to the gameplay by imposing real consequences, such as high repair costs and the need for cooperation with other players for vehicle recovery. This in-game economy resembles more realistic financial constraints compared to the main GTA Online mode where money is easier to accumulate.

Reflecting on this experience, players are expressing hopes for the upcoming GTA 6 multiplayer. Given Rockstar’s acquisition of Cfx.re (the developer behind FiveM, a popular GTA V roleplay mod), there is speculation that GTA 6 will integrate more official support for roleplay features, including a stricter economy and deeper consequences for player actions. This points to a potential parallel multiplayer experience—one chaotic and expansive like current GTA Online, the other more grounded and roleplay-focused.

Timberborn City-Builder Launches with Factorio-Style Automation

Timberborn, a city-builder focused on managing a colony of beavers, has officially exited early access, introducing a significant new gameplay addition: automation. Inspired somewhat by Factorio's logic systems, Timberborn’s 1.0 release includes 20 new buildings that act as programmable logic nodes, allowing players to create automated responses for their colony’s infrastructure.
