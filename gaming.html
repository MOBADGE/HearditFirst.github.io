Supervive MOBA Shuts Down After Seven Months

Supervive, a MOBA-style battle royale developed by former Riot Games veterans, will shut down in February 2026, roughly seven months after its full release. Developer Theorycraft Games cited insufficient player retention as the key reason, with active users dropping to just over 200 players.

Despite a sizable initial player base and investment, the game struggled to maintain engagement, leading to the decision to end active development and server support. The studio indicated its next project will differ significantly from Supervive in scope and style, signaling a strategic shift.

This closure adds to a trend of challenges faced by new MOBAs breaking into a competitive market dominated by established titles.
