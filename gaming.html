Horizon Chase Turbo Delisting in June

The arcade racing game Horizon Chase Turbo, along with its mobile precursor Horizon Chase, will be removed from sale on June 1. Although no longer purchasable, owners will retain access to downloads. This decision is puzzling since the game lacks licensed vehicles and online features, which often cause licensing or operational complications. The studio behind the game, Aquiris, was recently acquired by Epic Games, which itself announced a major layoff affecting over 1,000 employees. While it's unclear if these corporate changes influenced the delisting, Horizon Chase 2 will remain available, including on the Epic Games Store and PC. The removal restricts horizon chase turbo's availability mostly to current owners, signaling a possible shift in focus or resources within the owning company.

Caves of Qud Based on Solving Technical Challenges

Caves of Qud, a complex roguelike RPG known for its deep systems, has been ported to the Nintendo Switch, despite the system's limited hardware capabilities and the game's traditionally heavy keyboard controls. The developers undertook extensive UI redesigns and performance optimizations to enable play on gamepads and the older Switch hardware. The motivation behind this effort lies in challenging perceived limits and exploring market potential for intricate PC-style games on consoles usually considered less suitable for such titles. Sales peaked impressively in the Switch digital rankings, suggesting demand for niche systemic games on the platform. The team is now considering a mobile release, aiming to adapt the gameplay to touchscreen and portrait orientations, continuing their experimental approach to platform adaptation.

Splitgate Studio Ventures into New Multiplayer Shooter

1047 Games, best known for the arena shooter Splitgate, has announced work on a new project inspired by games like Titanfall and Black Ops 3. This upcoming title will be a movement-focused shooter, continuing the studio's emphasis on fast-paced multiplayer gameplay. The reveal came alongside continued support updates for Splitgate: Arena Reloaded, which itself struggled to regain its initial player base after relaunching. The studio recently downsized following funding and market challenges. Interested players can sign up for upcoming playtests, though detailed information on the new game's features and release timeline remains limited. This project appears to be a strategic move to diversify the studio's portfolio amid an evolving competitive landscape.

Epic Games Faces Uncertainty After Large Layoffs

Epic Games has laid off more than 1,000 employees, roughly 20% of its workforce, citing financial challenges despite Fortnite's continued popularity. Fortnite producer Robby Williams has publicly addressed the situation, requesting patience from the community as the remaining team "picks up the pieces." The full impact on Fortnite's development and update schedule remains unclear. Epic CEO Tim Sweeney has promised a company roadmap update soon, but Williams acknowledged the difficulty of forecasting the game's future amidst the staffing cuts. These layoffs highlight ongoing pressures within major game publishers and may affect Fortnite's content delivery and support throughout the year.

World of Warcraft Guild Disqualified for Exploiting New Raid

In the ongoing race to complete World of Warcraft’s new Dreamrift raid, a guild named RAoV QA Strikes Back was disqualified after using exploits to defeat the raid boss Chimaerus instantly, dealing nearly 300 million damage in seconds. Blizzard removed their names from the official leaderboard and invalidated the kill. This group has a history of similar exploitative behavior in previous expansions and appears focused on gaining notoriety rather than legitimate competition. Meanwhile, other guilds continue working on the raid through normal gameplay, although many are prioritizing the main six-boss Voidspire raid released simultaneously. This incident underscores persistent challenges in enforcing fairness in competitive MMO environments.

Companions Revealed for Owlcat’s Third-Person RPG The Expanse: Osiris Reborn
