Amazon’s Fallout Series Hits 100 Million Viewers Across Two Seasons

Amazon has announced that its TV adaptation of the Fallout video game series has now been watched by over 100 million viewers globally, combining partial viewing figures from both its first and second seasons. Season one initially drew 65 million viewers, while the second season attracted 83 million, making both seasons rank among Amazon Prime Video’s top four launches ever. These numbers reflect the initial engagement rather than full episode completions, so actual viewership depth may vary. The series' success has prompted Amazon to greenlight a third season, which is set to begin production this summer and will explore new locations not seen in the games.

*Why it matters:* This milestone demonstrates the continuing appeal of adapting popular game franchises into television content and highlights Amazon’s investment in extending the Fallout universe through streaming. The series contributes to the broader trend of video game properties being leveraged as multimedia franchises.

Steam’s House and Home Fest Showcases Cozy Games for Players Seeking Relaxation

Steam’s ongoing House and Home Fest, running through April 6, offers a curated selection of cozy, home-themed games with discounts and demos available. A recent feature highlighted five notable titles:

- *Creature Kitchen*: A short, puzzle-driven cooking sim featuring quirky creatures. - *PowerWash Simulator 2*: The sequel to the cleaning sim with enhanced gameplay and co-op features. - *Hozy*: A fixer-upper simulation focused on restoring old buildings room by room. - *Unpacking*: A 2D home decor sim centered on arranging belongings in new spaces. - *My Little Life*: An idle game where players care for tiny, demanding virtual people.

The sale encourages players to explore these gentler, slower-paced experiences, often centered around building, organization, and light puzzle elements.

*Why it matters:* These cozy games fill a niche for players seeking relaxing gameplay that contrasts with more action-oriented titles. Steam’s themed sale supports discoverability of smaller indie games that emphasize comfort and creativity.

Reviews for Super Mario Galaxy Movie Marked by Broad Criticism

The recently released Super Mario Galaxy Movie has received predominantly negative reviews from major outlets. Critics describe the film as lacking depth, coherence, and compelling character development. The Guardian called it “execrable churn” likening the production to low-effort content created for maximum global marketability. Reviews from The New York Times, Variety, and The Times similarly cite its “flat empty nothingness” and “torturous” viewing experience. While a few positive remarks note the film’s pacing and numerous references to the games, overall reception is poor.

*Why it matters:* The movie’s reception highlights continued challenges in translating popular video game franchises into successful films. It may influence audience interest and impact future adaptations of Nintendo properties or similar high-profile games.

PlayStation Player Completes Dark Souls Run Without Dodge or Hits at Level 1

YouTuber Zeldasouls has completed a highly challenging Dark Souls playthrough under a strict set of conditions: no level ups, no dodge rolls or any evasive moves, no ranged weapons or magic, and no taking damage from enemies. The run, which took over two years to prepare and execute, showcases a feat of skill and patience in one of gaming's toughest titles. This playthrough is noted as potentially the hardest hitless run in the entire Soulsborne series.

*Why it matters:* This accomplishment continues the tradition of challenging speedruns and no-hit runs in difficult games, illustrating player dedication and innovation. Such feats sustain community engagement around older titles and demonstrate the depth of player mastery even years after release.

Consumer Group in France Sues Ubisoft Over The Crew’s Server Shutdown

A French consumer association, UFC-Que Choisir, supported by the Stop Killing Games campaign, has filed a lawsuit against Ubisoft concerning the 2024 server shutdown of The Crew. The suit alleges that Ubisoft violated consumer rights by closing the game without adequately informing players about the temporary nature of their access and by revoking previously purchased licenses, effectively preventing offline play or private servers. The case seeks to establish clearer obligations for publishers regarding digital game ownership and service continuity.

*Why it matters:* This legal action is part of wider industry scrutiny about digital rights and the permanence of purchased games. It may influence future policies and consumer protections related to online-dependent games and digital ownership.
