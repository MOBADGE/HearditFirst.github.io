**World of Warcraft Revisits Nostalgia with Silvermoon Revamp** Blizzard has introduced a significant overhaul of Silvermoon and its surrounding zones in the upcoming *World of Warcraft: Midnight* update, moving away from the typical MMO formula of adding a brand-new continent every few years. Instead, the team opted to rebuild an old area with fresh art and quest content, earlier seen only in the Cataclysm overhaul. The redesigned Silvermoon City features expanded districts like Murder Row and a scenic Sunwell bridge, offering players a richer and more connected experience. This approach addresses an issue often faced by longstanding MMOs—an overabundance of isolated zones. Rather than continuously expanding the world, Blizzard emphasizes deepening existing areas, which may offer a more coherent and nostalgic journey for players.

**Chasm: The Rift – An Early Ukrainian FPS Experiment** Ahead of the popular *Stalker* series, an obscure Ukrainian first-person shooter called *Chasm: The Rift* quietly pushed boundaries in the late 1990s. Although it resembles *Quake* in style, *Chasm* uses a custom engine with a blend of 2.5D and 3D elements, offering mostly horizontal level designs. Combat pacing emphasizes careful progression rather than twitch action common in other shooters of the era. Notably, it introduced selective enemy dismemberment with tactical implications, a feature rare at the time. While its gameplay may feel dated today, *Chasm* stands as an interesting point in FPS history, showing early experimentation with narrative briefings and varied thematic settings, from military bases to medieval castles and alien worlds.

**Slay the Spire 2 Sees Unexpected Success at Launch** The deckbuilding roguelike *Slay the Spire 2* launched in early access to a much stronger reception than developer Mega Crit anticipated. Within its initial days, the game peaked at over 430,000 concurrent Steam players and reached the number one spot on the global top sellers list. Prior to release, the studio had modest expectations, likening their offering to “chicken noodle soup” — something familiar and comforting but not flashy. The game's strong launch indicates a sustained demand for well-crafted, turn-based roguelikes. Meanwhile, Bungie's new PvP shooter *Marathon* launched in the same period but has not matched *Slay the Spire 2*’s player numbers. Mega Crit jokes that if this growth continues, the next sequel could reach astronomical player counts.

**Cities: Skylines Free Weekend and New Expansion** Paradox Interactive is offering a free weekend for *Cities: Skylines* on Steam until March 9, alongside a 90% discount on the base game until March 12, priced at approximately £2.49. This comes ahead of the March 10 launch of *Race Day*, the first expansion in two years developed by Tantalus Media. The expansion centers on adding major motor racing events to the city builder, letting players design racetracks, build spectator areas, and manage event logistics that affect traffic and citizen movement. A substantial free update will accompany this release, including new billboards, fences, and an employment visualizer tool. This renewed activity supports the original *Cities: Skylines* as its sequel continues facing challenges after a troubled launch.

**Tainted Grail: The Fall of Avalon Receives Major Update** The RPG *Tainted Grail: The Fall of Avalon* recently received its 1.20 patch, addressing criticisms of its weaker third act. The update adds a new explorable area, two minibosses with distinct designs, and a narrative quest involving a disfigured mystic. Several existing locations have been reworked for better exploration and aesthetics, including a customizable home for players. The patch also adjusts late-game balance by introducing diminishing returns on stats beyond a certain point and providing nerfed weapon variants as optional duplicates. Developer Questline plans a second expansion later this month to continue enhancing the game.
