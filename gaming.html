Death Stranding 2 Now Runs Well on Steam Deck After Patch

After its PC release last month, *Death Stranding 2* initially performed poorly on Valve’s Steam Deck, struggling with low frame rates and stutters. However, patch 1.2 from Nixxes last week focused on performance improvements, including optimizations specifically targeting the Steam Deck. With this update, players now report steady frame rates averaging 30-35 fps in open-world scenes and up to 50 fps indoors while using the game's portable preset. Battery life remains limited at around 90 minutes per charge, but the patch demonstrates that with proper optimization, demanding AAA titles can run satisfactorily on handheld hardware. This is an encouraging sign for Steam Deck users and illustrates the potential for future ports with tailored settings.

Astronaut Demonstrates Playing D&D in Zero Gravity

German astronaut Rabea Rogge recently showed that tabletop role-playing games like Dungeons & Dragons are playable in microgravity aboard a spacecraft. In a video, she rolls a twenty-sided die by flicking it upward and catching it to read the result, confirming that dice rolling is manageable without gravity. Rogge was part of SpaceX’s Fram2 polar orbit mission funded by a private entrepreneur. While complex dice rolls remain challenging, this playful demonstration highlights the adaptability of in-person gaming even in low Earth orbit. It also coincides with renewed interest in space-themed games, seen in increased player activity for titles like *Kerbal Space Program*, which recently experienced a surge in concurrent players.

Hunger and Survival Mods Show Promise for Crimson Desert

Some players of *Crimson Desert* have started installing mods that add hunger and food consequence systems to the game, sparking hope for a fuller survival experience. While the core game already features activities such as fishing, gathering, and cooking, these community mods deepen the mechanics by introducing penalties like nausea and debuffs for bad or skipped meals. Players are also calling for features like placeable bedrolls, camping, and item degradation to enhance immersion and survival challenges. Since the developer Pearl Abyss currently does not officially support modding, these community efforts remain limited but indicate strong interest in expanding the game's depth through survival elements.

Bungie Addresses Cheating Concerns in Marathon’s Ranked Mode

Competitive mode in *Marathon*, which launched recently, has faced significant issues with cheating, leading to frustration among the player base and streamers. Bungie responded publicly, reaffirming its zero-tolerance policy against cheating and confirming that confirmed cheaters are being permanently banned. The studio said it is actively improving detection and telemetry tools and is also working to make reporting easier and more transparent for players. Additional moderation efforts are planned to reduce toxicity and prevent stream sniping. Bungie's efforts aim to safeguard the integrity of competition, though challenges with cheating remain a common issue in ranked multiplayer environments.

Steam Workshop Redesign Improves Browsing Experience

Valve has begun rolling out an update to the Steam Workshop interface, aimed at improving browsing speed and usability on both desktop and handheld devices like the Steam Deck. The redesigned UI widens the browsing page to display larger preview images, allows faster filtering without full-page reloads, and introduces a "Quick View" option to inspect items without leaving the page. Developers can also configure filters more precisely based on item types. The update is still in beta but reflects Valve’s attempts to modernize the Workshop experience, accommodating its vast library of over 50 million mods and making community content easier to navigate, particularly on mobile and handheld platforms.

Kerbal Space Program Sees Player Surge Linked to Artemis 2 Mission

*Kerbal Space Program* has reached its highest concurrent Steam player count since its 2015 full release, boosted by interest coinciding with NASA’s Artemis 2 lunar mission. Posts on the game’s subreddit indicate that the real-world spaceflight inspired players to revisit the simulation. While the game’s player base traditionally remains steady, this recent surge is notable and suggests a renewed cultural interest in space exploration. Although the follow-up *Kerbal Space Program 2* also saw a smaller increase in players, it remains less popular than the original. This uptick may be temporary but highlights the impact that real-world events can have on gaming trends.
