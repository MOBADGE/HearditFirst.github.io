**Dark Souls Hitless Level 1 Playthrough Completes After Two Years**

YouTuber Zeldasouls has achieved a remarkable Dark Souls feat by completing the original game at soul level 1 without taking a single hit. The run, which took over two years to perfect, also bans dodge rolling, ranged weapons, and ranged magic, forcing a slow and cautious playstyle. These restrictions mean no ranged attacks or mobility to evade enemies traditionally, making encounters like the Asylum Demon notoriously difficult. Zeldasouls detailed the full conditions, including no leveling, no ranged items or spells, no rolling or backsteps, no enemy hits or staggers, and no deaths caused by enemies aside from a scripted event. The end result is considered one of the hardest flawless runs in the Souls series, showcasing the dedication of the player and the enduring challenge of Dark Souls.

**Little Nemo and the Guardians of Slumberland Offers a Lighter Metroidvania Experience**

The recently released Little Nemo and the Guardians of Slumberland is a 2D hand-drawn metroidvania inspired by the original 1989 animated film and comics. Unlike the movie’s dark tone, the game embraces a bright, whimsical world where the young protagonist Nemo explores dreamlike levels threatened by void-like disintegration. Kickstarter-funded with only $80,000, the game impresses with its colorful visuals and joyful soundtrack reminiscent of early HD-era platformers like Wario Land: Shake It! and Cuphead. Gameplay emphasizes slower exploration and light platforming, making it an accessible alternative to more intense metroidvanias such as Hollow Knight: Silksong. Its design incorporates small animation flourishes that enhance feel without demanding punishing difficulty, offering players a relaxing change of pace in the genre.

**Aether & Iron Combines Tactical Vehicle Combat with Visual Novel Storytelling**

Aether & Iron is a steampunk-flavored RPG blending turn-based tactical combat with visual novel-style exploration and storytelling. Set in an alternate 1930s New York transformed by aether technology into floating boroughs ruled by powerful barons, players take on the role of a smuggler caught in a revolutionary plot. The game features fully voiced dialogue, skill checks reminiscent of tabletop RPG mechanics, and a story that balances linear progression with some side quests and choices. Combat involves managing vehicle positioning and weaponry on neon-lit highways, starting simply but ramping up in challenge. The game avoids typical isometric RPG navigation by using 2D landscape scenes with clickable points. Overall, it delivers a focused narrative experience with tactical depth in combat and noir atmosphere.

**Call of Duty’s Ongoing Fan Divide Reflects Studio Differences**

Call of Duty remains divided along the lines of its two primary development studios, Infinity Ward and Treyarch, leading to contrasting game styles and fan loyalties. Infinity Ward’s Modern Warfare series is lauded for polished visuals, grounded and mature storytelling, and detailed gunplay, often seen as more technically refined. Treyarch’s Black Ops series appeals more to hardcore multiplayer fans with faster movement, skill-based gameplay, and fan-favorite modes like Zombies and party play. The dual-studio setup results in alternating creative directions and uneven development cycles, fueling debates among players about which studio better represents Call of Duty’s future. Additions from other developers like Sledgehammer and Raven complicate the output, while Activision’s relentless annual schedule contributes to franchise fatigue. This schism shapes expectations for the 2026 Call of Duty release, the first major Infinity Ward-led title since 2022.

**Nightdive’s Larry Kuperman Retires After 25 Years in Games Preservation**

Larry Kuperman, Nightdive’s Vice President of Business Development, has announced his retirement after a 25-year career across various roles in the games industry. Kuperman was instrumental in Nightdive’s efforts to preserve classic PC games like System Shock and Sin, often navigating complicated intellectual property issues to bring remasters and re-releases to modern platforms. He emphasized that Nightdive regards games as art to be preserved rather than products to be sold and discarded. His retirement coincides with several losses in the industry and a sense of responsibility to protect gaming history. Though stepping back from full-time work, Kuperman plans to remain active in supporting new developers through the International Game Developers Association.
