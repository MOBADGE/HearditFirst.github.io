Resident Evil Requiem's Final Puzzle Solved

After several days of community effort, players have finally solved the complex Final Puzzle in Resident Evil Requiem. This intricate challenge involves obtaining a Marie doll, performing specific actions like waiting by the basement grinder to let zombies be disposed of properly, and flushing a toilet eight times in a hospital bathroom. Completing these steps enables progression to the final puzzle box, which when solved, rewards players with 20,000 CP — the in-game currency for skins and bonuses. While the reward is moderate, the puzzle's real significance lies in the collaborative and detailed deciphering experience it provided, engaging the community with a demanding alternate reality game (ARG) style task.

World of Warcraft's Haranir Allied Race Faces Gear Clipping Problems

Blizzard's newly introduced allied race in World of Warcraft's Midnight expansion, the Haranir, has landed on live servers with notable visual issues. Many gear items clip through the Haranir models due to their slightly bulkier physique—issues range from leg armor intersecting thighs to helmets clipping into collarbones. This problem has been reported on the PTR for months but remains unresolved at launch. The ongoing clipping glitches raise questions about Blizzard's quality assurance process, especially amid recent studio layoffs and a QA team unionization that should, in theory, protect against such oversights. The issue impacts player experience by detracting from the otherwise high-quality character models and customization.

Disney Dreamlight Valley Spicy Spaghetti Recipe Clarified

Players working on the Paw-fect Romance Star Path quest in Disney Dreamlight Valley have faced confusion regarding the "Cook a Spicy Spaghetti" task. It turns out that the recipe called Spicy Spaghetti actually refers to "Spaghetti Arrabiata" in the game. The dish requires three ingredients: tomato, wheat, and chili pepper. While the cooking process is straightforward, growing the necessary ingredients can be time-consuming, with chili peppers taking up to 45 minutes to grow. Players with access to Wall-E's Garden may find expedience, though crop availability depends on chance. This clarification helps players complete the quest more efficiently without unnecessary frustration.

Marathon Server Slam Reveals Heavy Player vs Player Combat

Bungie's Marathon Server Slam event concluded after four days with revealing gameplay statistics. Players were responsible for over 55% of all kills among 16.5 million total deaths, with the rest caused by AI-controlled UESC robots. This highlights a highly competitive PvP environment where player encounters dominate lethality. Additionally, valuable in-game items like Premium Tick Milk and Drinkable Cheeseburgers were heavily contested. The event also showed low exfiltration rates, particularly on the Dire Marsh map with only about 36% extraction success, emphasizing the challenge of survival and loot retention. These insights underscore Marathon's design focus on intense player conflict and high-stakes extraction.

Marathon’s UI Designer Embraces Criticism, Pledges Improvements

In response to player feedback criticizing Marathon’s user interface, the game’s UI designer Elliott Gray has acknowledged issues but affirmed that the game’s distinctive visual style will remain intact. While conceding that inventory management, navigation, and information density can be improved, Gray rejected calls to simplify the UI’s artistic flair, affectionately calling himself the "fontslop merchant" and promising to retain the “sauce” that defines the game’s look. This stance indicates Bungie’s commitment to refining usability without sacrificing the unique aesthetic that sets Marathon apart in a crowded shooter market.

Fallout: New Vegas Remaster Speculation Grows After Iron Galaxy Tease

Rumors of a Fallout: New Vegas remaster resurfaced following a social media post by Iron Galaxy Studios that included an image of the original New Vegas loading screen. While the post was ambiguous, speculation arises from Iron Galaxy’s history with Bethesda projects, including Fallout 76 and VR ports. Fans have long awaited a remaster, and the image has reignited hopes. However, no official confirmation has been made, and previous hints have led to false alarms. Nonetheless, the possibility remains plausible given the commercial success of past remasters and Bethesda’s recent approach to revisiting classic titles.
