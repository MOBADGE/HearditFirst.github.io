AI-Created Roguelite Codex Mortis Launches Demo on Steam

A new roguelite game called *Codex Mortis* claims to be the first fully playable game created entirely using AI. Developed by Crunchfest3 over three months, the game was built from scratch without a traditional game engine, using AI tools powered by Claude Code for coding and ChatGPT for art generation. The game is a bullet-hell style roguelite resembling *Vampire Survivors*, though its graphics are noted as indistinct and AI-generated. Currently, only a demo is available on Steam.

This project highlights how AI has entered game development beyond isolated tools and into entire game creation, but it also raises questions about quality and originality. While *Codex Mortis* may not be a polished commercial product, its release demonstrates the expanding possibilities and the limitations of using AI in game development workflows.

Fate of the Old Republic Director Commits to Release Before 2030

Casey Hudson, co-founder of Arcanaut Studios and director of *Star Wars: Fate of the Old Republic* (FotOR), has publicly promised that the game will launch within the next five years, countering skepticism about a potential 2030 release date. Hudson noted the studio was only formed recently in 2025, yet insists they are aiming for a four and a half-year development cycle.

Given the scale of FotOR as a high-profile RPG, this timeline is ambitious. The game is likely still in pre-production and the studio is in early stages of staffing and development. If achieved, FotOR's release before 2030 would demonstrate an expedited development cycle for a major AAA title, though it depends on the studio‚Äôs ability to scale production efficiently.

French President Emmanuel Macron Praises Clair Obscur: Expedition 33

French President Emmanuel Macron has again publicly congratulated French developer Sandfall Interactive for their RPG *Clair Obscur: Expedition 33*, which recently won nine awards at The Game Awards, including Game of the Year. Macron called the achievement historic and a source of national pride for France and the city of Montpellier.

This governmental recognition underlines the increasing cultural significance of video games as artistic and creative works. Acknowledgement from a head of state is notable in demonstrating how games are gaining legitimacy alongside other cultural industries in France and beyond.
