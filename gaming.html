Classic Point-and-Click Adventure Restored for Modern PCs

A notably unusual point-and-click adventure game from the 1990s, originally called *The Dark Eye*, is being re-released on Steam under the new title *Edgar Allan Poe's Interactive Horror: 1995 Edition*. Published by Inscape, the game adapts three Edgar Allan Poe stories with an unsettling mix of FMV claymation and early 3D environments. It also features narration by writer William S. Burroughs and a soundtrack by 1980s musician Thomas Dolby. The re-release retains the original content and aspect ratio with minimal changes, primarily renaming the title to avoid confusion with a German tabletop franchise. This restoration makes the game accessible and playable on current systems for the first time in decades, offering players a chance to experience an obscure but distinctive piece of gaming history.

Baldur's Gate 3 Mod Recovers Removed Dialogue and Content

A mod for *Baldur's Gate 3* has restored over 100 conversations and more than 1,000 lines of voiced dialogue removed after the game's early access phase. Created by modder HyperspaceTowel, this restoration particularly enhances companion character interactions for Astarion, Shadowheart, Gale, and Lae'zel, though some dialogue for newer characters was recovered via audio splicing. The mod reinstates cut scenes, such as encounters with brainwashed NPCs during the tutorial, which were omitted in the final release. Players are advised to install the mod before starting a new playthrough and to keep it active throughout, as it integrates new characters and camp events. This effort gives fans access to content that adds depth and variety to the narrative experience that was lost in the transition from early access to release.

Multiple Survival Games Are Centered Around Trains in 2026

There is a noticeable trend this year within the survival genre: several upcoming titles are focusing on train-based mobile bases. At least six survival base-building games set on trains are scheduled for release in 2026. These games often feature frozen or post-apocalyptic worlds where trains serve as moving homes and supply hubs. Examples include *Frostrail*, a survival FPS with engine management; *EverRail*, which incorporates passenger respawning mechanics; and *Enginefall*, which emphasizes progression through fighting and expanding a mega train. Other titles like *Frostliner* and *Steel Ark* offer variations combining city building, survival, and combat elements on trains. This clustered focus on trains introduces a new setting and gameplay dynamic within the survival genre.
