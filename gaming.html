Monster Hunter Stories 3: How to Beat All Invasive Monsters

Monster Hunter Stories 3 features a set of unique Invasive Monsters that occupy the dens of endangered species. These monsters have enhanced abilities requiring specific tactics. Players need to drive them away rather than slay them immediately to claim rare monster eggs. For example:

- **Invasive Yian Garuga** can be repelled by breaking its legs with blunt weapons like hammers. - **Invasive Plesioth** requires fire weapons to break its head and prevent a devastating attack. - **Invasive Seregios** demands a hammer with an area-of-effect ability and careful response to its colored roars. - **Invasive Shogun Ceanataur** can be driven off by boosting defense to withstand its claw attacks.

Each Invasive Monster's location and weakness are key to successfully reclaiming endangered eggs. This system adds a puzzle-like layer to combat, emphasizing strategy over brute force.

Locations of Endangered Species in Monster Hunter Stories 3

The game also emphasizes finding rare endangered species, each guarded by one of the Invasive Monsters. Notable locations include:

- **Rathian** in Azuria’s Sunpetal Plains, guarded by Invasive Yian Garuga. - **Lagiacrus** in Mirror Lake’s hidden cave, under the protection of Invasive Plesioth. - **Astalos** at the northwest of Mirror Lake, protected by Invasive Seregios. - **Canyne**, found in Canalta Timberland, guarded by Invasive Arzuros. - **Barioth** in Serathis, behind an Invasive Khezu.

Successfully repelling the guardians allows players to collect powerful monster eggs, essential for building strong teams and completing sidequests. The detailed mapping of species locations supports exploration in line with the game's environmental themes.

Darktide Adds New "Expedition" Mode With Outdoor Zones and Radiation Mechanics

Warhammer 40,000 shooter Darktide will introduce a new mode called "expeditions" in its Beyond the Hive update releasing March 17. Unlike previous missions confined indoors, this mode offers open, sunlit environments on Atoma Prime's dunes. Players must scavenge valuable Tech-Remnants while avoiding hazards like radiation poisoning.

Key points about the mode:

- Missions are time-limited; dying when time runs out results in losing rewards. - The world features randomized elements and patrols, changing each run’s dynamic. - Hostile enemies appear more frequently if players engage excessively, encouraging tactical play.

This new mode diversifies Darktide's gameplay by blending extraction-style mechanics with survival elements, a notable shift from its usual claustrophobic settings.

The Simpsons Executive Producer Open to Hit & Run Revival

Matt Selman, executive producer of The Simpsons, addressed the cult success of the 2003 action-adventure game *Hit & Run*. While he personally favors the original title, he acknowledged fan interest in a revival or remaster by saying "never say never." Though there are no firm plans, the comment indicates the possibility remains open.

The original game combined open-world exploration with humor and racing gameplay, standing out among Simpsons adaptations. Its enthusiastic fanbase and Selman's remarks keep hopes alive for its return on modern platforms.

Slay the Spire 2 Reaches 3 Million Sales Within First Week

The deckbuilding roguelike *Slay the Spire 2* has sold 3 million copies within its first week of early access. Developer Mega Crit also confirmed over 25 million runs played, signaling strong engagement beyond initial sales.

The sequel maintains much of the original gameplay while introducing new content. Planned updates include Steam Workshop support, a revamped scoring system, and an accessibility mode. Additionally, a board game expansion titled *Downfall* is scheduled for release on March 24.

This early success suggests *Slay the Spire 2* continues to resonate strongly with fans of the genre.
