Security Flaw Exposes Thousands of Robot Vacuums Worldwide

A software engineer unintentionally gained control over 7,000 DJI Romo robot vacuums across multiple countries while attempting to operate his own with a PS5 controller. The flaw was due to a backend permission validation issue allowing the engineer to access live video, audio, and spatial data from all devices globally. DJI has since issued a patch for affected vacuums, acknowledging the potential for unauthorized video access. This incident highlights broader concerns about security in internet-connected home devices and follows previous cases involving hacked robot vacuums. The engineer eventually regained control of his vacuum with the gamepad as intended, but the episode raises questions about privacy and device security.
