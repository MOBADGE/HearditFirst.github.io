Resident Evil Requiem Adds Photo Mode and Subtle Cutscene Improvements

Capcom released a patch for Resident Evil Requiem that introduces a new photo mode, allowing players to capture in-game moments with various poses, stickers, and filters. While camera movement is somewhat limited and lacks advanced lighting controls, the mode provides a fresh way to engage with the game’s characters and environments. The update also notes vague bug fixes, including resolving issues that blocked progress and reduced crashes. Additionally, some character expressions in cutscenes have been fine-tuned to better convey emotions, likely enhancing narrative immersion. This patch offers players a new way to appreciate the game’s atmosphere and encourages creativity in capturing scenes.

Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous Brings a Massive Subclass Roster and New Mount Mechanics

Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous continues to stand out for its complex character-building system, boasting an extensive pool of 232 subclasses across 26 base classes and numerous prestige classes. This depth caters to players who enjoy deep customization and strategic experimentation, though it can be overwhelming, leading many to restart their playthroughs multiple times seeking the ideal build. Of particular interest is the addition of the "Sable Company Marine" Ranger subclass introduced in the final expansion, which grants a rideable griffon (or hippogriff) companion from the start—offering a powerful mount that supports unique aerial attack strategies. The game’s emphasis on mounted combat and diversity of builds adds a distinct tactical layer uncommon in many CRPGs. The title remains accessible via Steam and GOG, with a current discount running through early April.

Star Wars Zero Company Director Reflects on the Revival of Classic PC Strategy and Tactics Genres

Greg Foertsch, creative director of the upcoming Star Wars Zero Company, offered insights into why traditional PC genres such as RTS, turn-based tactics, and CRPGs have experienced renewed interest after a period of decline. He explained that during the early 2000s, many complex PC game types struggled to transition to consoles due to technological and user interface challenges, particularly with camera management and input adaptation. Recent advancements in UX and cross-platform design now allow these genres to function well with controllers, making them more accessible on consoles. Foertsch points out that this has led to an uptick in new titles reinvigorating these classic genres, often introducing innovative mechanics and challenging player expectations. This ongoing evolution suggests diverse audiences can now enjoy these traditionally PC-centered experiences across multiple platforms.

Owlcat Clarifies Use of AI in The Expanse: Osiris Reborn Development Amid Player Concerns

Owlcat has addressed concerns about generative AI involvement in their upcoming RPG, The Expanse: Osiris Reborn. The studio confirmed that while AI tools are employed during early prototyping phases and for concept development tasks such as vision coordination and inspiration, no AI-generated assets or writing will appear in the final game. All final content is guaranteed to be created by human team members. This statement follows similar industry discussions about the role of AI in game development, with some players expressing unease about potential overreliance on such technologies. Owlcat’s clarification aims to reassure fans about the quality and originality of the game's final assets while acknowledging productivity benefits during initial design stages.

Mechabellum Season Launch Highlights Strategic Interaction Over Execution

The strategy autobattler Mechabellum has launched a new season, prompting a look at what distinguishes it in the strategy genre. Unlike many competitive games that emphasize memorizing optimal builds and precise execution, Mechabellum focuses on dynamic interaction between players. Matches unfold as a tactical conversation, with each participant responding and adapting to the other's moves rather than following rigid scripts. This design fosters deeper engagement and learning, as players interpret their opponent’s choices in real time and adjust accordingly. The game’s clear visual feedback and relatively short matches also make it approachable while retaining strategic depth. Its emphasis on player interaction rather than meta optimization offers a distinctive experience within competitive strategy games.

Elder Scrolls: Blades Mobile Game to Shut Down After Eight Years

Bethesda has announced the closure of The Elder Scrolls: Blades, its cross-platform mobile and Nintendo Switch spinoff, effective June 30. The game launched eight years ago offering dungeon crawling and town-building features but struggled to gain lasting traction, receiving mixed reactions due to its look and monetization model.
