Scott Pilgrim EX Released with Modernized Beat ‘Em Up Design

Tribute Games has launched *Scott Pilgrim EX*, a side-scrolling beat ‘em up inspired by the popular comic and a follow-up to their previous action games. Released on March 3, 2026, the game combines traditional brawling mechanics with RPG-like stat upgrades, offering about five hours of campaign play. Players can choose from seven characters, each representing different fighting archetypes, and explore a sizable pixel-art city filled with secrets and varied enemies.

The game was praised for smooth combat and the unique movesets of its roster, allowing for accessible but varied gameplay. However, reviewers noted that the progression system can reduce the challenge by making players too powerful too quickly, diminishing the need for strategic play in the late game. Despite its nostalgic references and strong soundtrack, some felt the game missed opportunities to deepen its mechanics or offer greater post-campaign challenges. Overall, it provides a fun but familiar beat ‘em up experience that leans heavily on nostalgia.

The Sims 4 Introduces Paid Content Marketplace and Creator Program

Electronic Arts announced a new in-game Marketplace for *The Sims 4*, launching March 17 for PC and Mac, with consoles to follow later. This Marketplace will allow players to buy curated content packs created by community Makers using a new virtual currency called Moola. Approved creators can sell their custom-created items directly to players and earn 30% of the sales revenue.

This official system marks a notable step toward monetizing custom content in *The Sims* ecosystem more transparently. It resembles Bethesda’s Creation Club but may succeed due to the Sims community’s willingness to pay for content. Importantly, free content can still be shared outside the Marketplace under existing policies, and traditional EA expansions will remain available through standard channels. The program opens applications on March 5 for creators meeting certain criteria. The Marketplace also brings official custom content availability to console players for the first time.

Bruce Campbell Shares Cancer Diagnosis, Plans Reduced Public Appearances

Bruce Campbell, known for his role as Ash in *Evil Dead* and as the voice narrator in the *Spider-Man 2* video game, revealed he has been diagnosed with a cancer that is “treatable but not curable.” The actor announced via social media that this diagnosis will force him to cancel several public appearances and cons planned for 2026 while undergoing treatment.

Campbell conveyed a determined mindset and reassured fans that he expects to remain active, including touring with his latest film *Ernie & Emma* later this year. His announcement aims to manage expectations and reduce misinformation. Campbell’s work over the years has made him an iconic figure in genre entertainment and video games alike.

Bungie Details Marathon’s First Season and Confirms Quarterly Loot Resets

With *Marathon* launching on March 5, Bungie has detailed its plan for the game’s first seasonal update, running through June. Season 1, titled *Death is the First Step*, will unlock a new endgame zone called Cryo Archive and add ranked modes, new weapons, and events. Season 2 plans include additional maps, weapons, and systems.

Significantly, Bungie confirmed that every three months all player loot, progress, and character levels will be wiped. Only cosmetic items, titles, and certain progression elements will persist. This design choice aims to maintain a balanced meta and ensure new and returning players start on equal footing. While common in the genre, this commitment to mandatory wipes may alienate players who wish to keep accumulated gear longer.

Tripwire Interactive Lays Off 23 Employees Amid Industry Shifts

Tripwire Interactive, developer of *Killing Floor* and *Rising Storm*, announced layoffs affecting 23 staff members due to evolving business conditions in the games industry. The studio described the decision as difficult but necessary to align with long-term strategies.

Employees expressed surprise at the cuts, which come amid a wider pattern of industry layoffs starting from 2023 and continuing into 2026. Several studios recently reduced staff or restructured, reflecting ongoing volatility and uncertainty in game development sectors.
