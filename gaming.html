Metro 2039 Reveals a Darker Story with a Voiced Protagonist

At the Xbox First Look event, Ukrainian developer 4A Games unveiled **Metro 2039**, the newest installment in the post-apocalyptic Metro series. This title marks the first non-VR game since 2019’s *Metro Exodus*. Set in the devastated Moscow Metro, the story presents a bleak future where the Novoreich, a Nazi faction formerly led by the Spartan Hunter, have taken full control of the Metro, imposing an authoritarian regime on the survivors.

Unlike previous games, Metro 2039 introduces a voiced protagonist known only as “The Stranger,” a recluse haunted by nightmares who is forced to return to the Metro. The reveal trailer emphasizes a "much darker tone," depicting brutal scenes like children being indoctrinated into the Novoreich. The game draws clear parallels to current real-world events, with executive producer Jon Bloch and creative director Andriy Shevchenko highlighting themes of tyranny, war, and freedom shaped by the ongoing conflict in Ukraine. The game is due for release this winter.

Fortnite Festival Adds MIDI Drum Kit and Microphone Support

Epic Games’ **Fortnite Festival Season 14** has introduced a new way for players to engage with its music features by enabling compatibility with MIDI drum kits and Fortnite-compatible microphones. This allows players to plug in real instruments or microphones and perform in-game, extending the music mashup experience first seen in last year’s festival update.

The season’s content is headlined by Icelandic-Chinese jazz-pop singer Laufey, with themed outfits and music unlockables. The integration of MIDI drum kits follows Epic’s acquisition of Harmonix, the studio behind *Rock Band* and *Fuser*, suggesting a long-term plan to deepen Fortnite's musical gameplay elements. These features aim to enhance player interaction in the game's Jam Ring mode and provide more flexibility with a new Quick Mix UI.

US Appeals Court Revives $3 Billion Patent Suit Against Intel

A US appeals court has revived a longstanding patent infringement case between chipmaker Intel and VLSI Technology. VLSI alleges that Intel’s processors infringe on multiple patents related to processor architecture and power management, including patent 8,566,836, which involves selecting processor cores for tasks.

This legal battle, ongoing since 2017, includes previous large judgments overturned and contested by both sides. The revived proceedings indicate the case will continue with further examination, not a ruling of Intel’s infringement. The resolution holds significance given Intel’s strategic investments in AI and new processor lines, highlighting the financial and operational implications of intellectual property disputes in the semiconductor industry.

Street Fighter Movie Trailer Released, Offers Glimpse of Cast and Style

The first trailer for the upcoming **Street Fighter** movie debuted, showcasing a story centered around a fractured friendship between Ryu and Ken and the involvement of Chun-Li in recruiting fighters for the 1993 World Warrior Tournament. The trailer highlights signature moves and characters faithful to the original game, including a notable suplex from Zangief and some action sequences involving Ken.

David Dastmalchian appears as M. Bison, while Jason Momoa’s role as Blanka is briefly glimpsed near the trailer’s end but remains mostly obscured. The film maintains a playful tone mixing nostalgia and action, with a release scheduled for October 16, 2026.

Battlefield 6 Maps and Features Roadmap Expands Gameplay in 2026

DICE has outlined an expanded roadmap for **Battlefield 6** through the rest of 2026, focusing on responding to player feedback about map size and vehicle availability. The game will see seven new maps added across Seasons 3 to 5, including large-scale returns like *Railway to Golmud* from Battlefield 4, naval combat on the oversized *Tsuru Reef*, and the fan-favorite *Wake Island* with functional aircraft carriers.

Additionally, several quality-of-life features are planned, such as an improved server browser, spectator mode, proximity chat, and clan support. The studio also intends to revamp existing maps Blackwell Fields and New Sobek City to address concerns about cramped spaces. While no firm dates are set, Season 2 is expected to conclude in mid-May.
