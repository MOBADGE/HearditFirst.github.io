WCW's Costly Mortal Kombat Rip-Off Gimmick

In the mid-1990s, World Championship Wrestling (WCW), known for extravagant spending under billionaire Ted Turner, invested heavily in a wrestling character named Glacier. This gimmick was a clear imitation of Mortal Kombat’s Sub-Zero, featuring a similarly styled costume and elaborate pyrotechnic entrance costing around $400,000 per setup. The character was introduced with video promos directly borrowing lines and imagery from the Mortal Kombat movie.

Despite the large investment, Glacier failed to resonate with audiences, especially as WCW’s focus shifted to the more realistic New World Order (NWO) storyline. After only four matches and escalating legal pressure from Midway, Mortal Kombat’s rights holder, WCW quickly altered the character’s look and abandoned the original concept. The episode highlights WCW's wasteful expenditures and questionable creative choices during its 1990s heyday, illustrating how litigation risks and poor management can derail promotional strategies in entertainment.

Stardew Valley’s Clint: New Friendship and Romance Details

Stardew Valley players can now start building relationships with Clint, the town’s blacksmith, ahead of the upcoming 1.7 update that will add him as a marriage candidate alongside Sandy. Clint is typically found at his Smithy during the day and occasionally visits the Saloon in the evening. His birthday is Winter 26.

The guide details Clint’s gift preferences, with a focus on gems, smelted bars like gold and iridium, and Artisan recipes rather than flowers or raw ores. Clint has a limited number of heart events leading to recipe rewards. The upcoming update will expand his role, giving players the ability to pursue marriage and offering new content centered on his character.

This information helps players plan their in-game interactions and gift-giving strategies to deepen friendships and prepare for fishing, mining, and crafting activities tied to Clint’s role.

Roblox Introduces Roblox Plus Subscription with Creator Incentives

Roblox will replace its Premium subscription with Roblox Plus starting April 30. The new plan offers subscribers greater discounts on Robux purchases, access to private servers without fees, and no-fee Robux transfers (for age-verified users 18 and older). However, it removes the previous Premium’s monthly Robux stipend and bonus on Robux purchases, instead offering these as optional add-ons for an extra cost.

Creators benefit through a new system that pays them based on the time Roblox Plus subscribers spend in their game's paid private servers, and for each subscriber they successfully convince to sign up via in-game prompts. This model incentivizes creators to engage players and encourages longer play sessions.

The new subscription appears aimed at increasing monetization and creator income but introduces trade-offs for subscribers who previously received a straightforward Robux allowance.

US Government Targets Gamers for Air Traffic Controller Recruitment

The US Department of Transportation has launched a recruitment campaign targeting gamers to become air traffic controllers. Through social media videos featuring popular game clips, the campaign highlights the potential career benefits of air traffic control, including an average salary of $155,000 after three years.

This push responds to a significant staffing shortage worsened by government shutdown-related retirements. The job is described as high-pressure but rewarding, with an expedited hiring process now in place. The campaign aims to attract younger adults with relevant skills acquired through gaming, reflecting evolving approaches to public sector recruitment.

Pete Hines Reflects on Departure from Bethesda

Former Bethesda Softworks executive Pete Hines recently discussed his 2023 retirement after nearly 25 years at the company. Hines explained he left because he felt powerless to protect Bethesda from internal changes and perceived mistreatment under new management, likely alluding to Microsoft’s ownership.

He expressed respect for longtime colleague Todd Howard and credited him for support during his final period at the studio. Hines also advocated for greater appreciation of Bethesda’s ambitious, complex open-world games, which he argued offer unparalleled player freedom despite bugs and technical flaws that often attract criticism.

Overwatch Season 2 Adjusts Map Voting to Favor Majority Choice

Blizzard announced that starting with Overwatch’s upcoming season 2 on April 14, the map voting system will change so that the map with the majority of votes automatically wins. This reduces the chances of less popular maps being selected. To offset this, the pool of map options will favor maps individual players have previously voted for, increasing the likelihood of seeing preferred maps.
