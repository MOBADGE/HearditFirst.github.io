**AOC Updates Gaming Monitor Software After User Criticism**

AOC has acknowledged longstanding user complaints about the usability of its gaming monitors’ on-screen display (OSD) and announced a redesigned interface for current and future models. Presented at a press event in Croatia, the updated OSD aims to simplify adjustments by providing key game settings and quick-access modes on the first screen, with deeper customization available in a secondary menu. The new UI also distinguishes different AOC monitor lines with color-coded themes. This improvement is particularly relevant as AOC expands its lineup of dual-mode monitors, which allow on-the-fly switching between resolutions and refresh rates—a feature that benefits from a clearer OSD experience.

**BAFTA Withdraws Trailers Over Sensitive Themes Despite Developer’s Revisions**

Silver Script Games’ founder Alyx Jones revealed that BAFTA pulled the trailer for her upcoming game *The Quiet Things* hours before the awards event due to concerns over potentially triggering content. The trailer, which explores themes like childhood trauma and abuse, had already been revised at BAFTA’s request to remove imagery flagged as suggestive of weapons and violence. Jones offered to make further edits, but BAFTA declined, citing insufficient time to add audience warnings. BAFTA stated the decision was made solely out of caution for the wellbeing of the attendees, not as a judgment of the game itself. The incident highlights ongoing tensions around presenting mature themes in public gaming showcases.

**Ambitious San Andreas Mod Brings Manhunt’s Carcer City to Life**

A fan-made total conversion mod for *Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas* called *GTA: Carcer City* has been released in demo form. The mod replaces the original expansive map with a single city inspired by the gritty setting of 2003’s *Manhunt*, a related title in Rockstar’s fictional universe. Set in 2001, Carcer City offers a new storyline with a darker tone, contrasting with the satirical style typical of *GTA*. Players can explore districts modeled after Rust Belt cities featuring downtown skyscrapers, ports, and airports. The project has been in development for over a decade and allows players to experience *Manhunt*’s locations in an open-world format. There is no confirmed completion date, but the demo showcases significant progress.

**GTA 6 Community Catalogues Wildlife in Upcoming Game**

Dedicated fans have been closely examining every frame of *Grand Theft Auto 6*’s trailers and artwork to identify the species of animals featured in the game. Led by a community member known as PauloJz on GTAForums, this informal “fauna analysis” organizes observed wildlife such as sea turtles, birds, and dogs, matching them to real-world counterparts and noting their appearances across various trailer timestamps. The aim is to document the game’s environmental detail, contributing to a collective understanding ahead of the game’s November release. Such community efforts exemplify the increasing attention to environmental immersion in modern open-world games.

**CCP to Introduce Non-PvP Starter Zone in EVE Online**

EVE Online will soon add a dedicated non-player-versus-player (PvP) region called Exordium, marking a significant change after more than 23 years of the game’s established full-PvP universe. Designed to improve the new player experience, Exordium will centralize rookie systems in one region where PvP is disabled rather than just discouraged. This move aims to reduce the anxiety new players commonly face and provide a safer space to learn EVE’s mechanics while engaging in varied activities, albeit with lower rewards. CCP anticipates that this change will help sustain the game’s long-term community by making it more accessible for newcomers. The expansion is slated for partial release before EVE Fanfest in May, with full rollout expected over the summer.
