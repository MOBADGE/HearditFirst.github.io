Samsung to End SATA SSD Production Amid Memory Shortages

Multiple industry sources report Samsung is expected to announce the cessation of SATA SSD production early next year, a move tied to ongoing memory supply constraints driven by AI industry demands. SATA SSDs, a budget-friendly storage option limited in speed compared to NVMe SSDs, may become scarcer and costlier as production shifts towards more profitable and performant components.

This development could exacerbate the current "memory pricing apocalypse," affecting consumers and gamers reliant on affordable storage. Similar exits from the consumer memory market by companies like Micron signal broader industry realignment favoring AI and data center segments.

However, some analysts foresee potential price relief by late 2026, contingent on AI's consumer market adoption.
