Stop Killing Games Petition Surpasses EU Signature Threshold for Consideration

The Stop Killing Games campaign has officially reached over 1.29 million verified signatures from its 1.44 million total, exceeding the one million needed for formal consideration by the European Commission. The initiative calls on game publishers to establish clear plans ensuring ongoing access to online games after official support ends, addressing the issue of live service closures leaving players unable to play purchased titles. Verified signatures from across EU countries demonstrate broad support. While the campaign has faced industry resistance, this milestone moves it into the next stage of EU legislative discussion, potentially influencing future regulation on video game preservation and consumer rights.

GTA Roleplay Sees Growing Interest in Structured Police Roleplay Careers

GTA roleplay communities have witnessed an increased number of players applying to in-game police forces, treating the experience much like real law enforcement careers. Servers such as Eclipse RP require applicants to complete written applications, background checks, voice interviews, and attend virtual police academies covering radio codes, arrests, and traffic stops. New recruits then enter probationary periods before gaining full police authority. This structured approach contrasts with earlier, more casual roleplay and reflects the scene's maturation over the past decade. The complexity adds realism to the experience but also involves significant time commitment and adherence to roleplay standards.

Modders Release Native PC Port of Original Banjo-Kazooie With Added Features

A group of modders has released Banjo: Recompiled, a native PC port of the original Banjo-Kazooie game, long absent from PC platforms despite Microsoft's acquisition of Rare. This port supports ultrawide resolutions, improved controls, higher framerates, and includes quality-of-life features like retaining collected notes after level exits. The release is available for Windows, Linux, and Mac, requiring users to supply their own original game files. It also features a built-in mod menu compatible with popular mods and HD texture packs. This port improves upon emulation by offering lower input lag and visual clarity, contributing to preservation and accessibility of classic games outside official channels.
