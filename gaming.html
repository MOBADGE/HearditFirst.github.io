Hades 2 Delivers More of the Same in Its Sequel

Supergiant's **Hades 2** has been released to mixed recognition, missing the official "Game of the Year" and "Best Roguelike" awards, yet it continues to hold a special place for many players and critics as a personal favorite. The sequel sticks closely to the formula established by the original Hades, preserving the roguelike mechanics, narrative style, and thematic focus on familial conflict. Improvements include expanded content, new spell mechanics such as charged Omega versions, and additional progression features. While some see the sequel as lacking innovation, others appreciate its familiarity and polish, reinforcing the idea that sequels do not always need to reinvent the wheel to be worthwhile.

Fallout Season 2 Episode 1 Explores New and Old Wasteland Locations

The first episode of **Fallout Season 2** expands on the game's universe by featuring several familiar and new locations, including the previously unseen **Vault 24**, which was a mystery since its planned inclusion in *Fallout: New Vegas*. The episode depicts Vault 24 as a vault where questionable brainwashing experiments took place, shedding light on its lore. It also shows the **Starlight Drive-In**, a location originally exclusive to *Fallout 4*, now reimagined as a franchise location, which struck a nostalgic chord with fans. The episode deepens the storyline around mind-control technology and its implications in the Fallout world, setting up significant themes for the season ahead.

Completing Quests in Arc Raiders: Deciphering the Data and Groundbreaking

The game **Arc Raiders** offers multiple quests that need careful navigation. The **Deciphering the Data** quest, introduced in the Cold Snap update, involves interacting with terminals using the Magnetic Decryptor in two main locations: the Fuel Control Building and the Arrival Building. Players may find the second terminal's location less obvious, but guidance is available to assist with completion.

Similarly, the **Groundbreaking** quest requires players to enter a locked room in Pilgrim's Peak with a particular key, locate a whiteboard with a building sketch, and then identify and photograph the corresponding building in the Abandoned Housing Project. Both quests emphasize exploration and specific key usage, underscoring the game's focus on detailed, environment-based objectives.

Hardware Scam Highlights Risks in the Memory Market

A recent hardware scam involved a buyer purchasing **DDR5 RAM** from Amazon only to find **DDR2 sticks** inside the sealed box. The fraudulent packages included fake DDR5 heatsink stickers and metal plates to mimic genuine weight. This appears to be a case of return fraud, where genuine kits were replaced with older, irrelevant components and resold. This incident highlights ongoing challenges buyers face in the current volatile PC hardware market amid supply shortages and inflated prices, stressing the importance of thorough unboxing documentation and vigilance when purchasing expensive parts.

Steam Replay: Players Spend Consistent Time on New Releases

Valve's **Steam Replay for 2025** reveals that gamers spent about 14% of their playtime on games released within the same year, consistent with trends from recent years. This equates to roughly half an hour of new games per four hours of total gaming time for the average player and corresponds to playing approximately 0.56 new games annually at the median. The data suggests players maintain a steady interest in fresh titles despite market fluctuations including live-service trends and industry challenges. This insight offers a perspective on player engagement and game discovery patterns on one of the largest PC gaming platforms.
