Lost Fallout: New Vegas Post-Ending Dialogue Restored

A recently unearthed beta build of Fallout: New Vegas has revealed extensive post-ending dialogue that Obsidian originally planned but cut due to time constraints. This content reflects player choices in the main campaign, adding unique scenes depending on which faction prevailed—Legion, NCR, New Vegas independence, or Mr. House. For example, characters react differently based on the ending, such as Caesar’s Legion taking over the Strip or the Brotherhood controlling Helios One. Modders have begun restoring these missing dialogues through the "Post-Game Dialogue Restored" mod, which works alongside the "Functional Post Game Ending" mod. This restoration enriches the post-game experience by better reflecting player decisions and provides fans a glimpse into the developers’ original vision.

The Legend of Khiimori Early Access Preview

The Legend of Khiimori, a realistic horse courier and survival simulation game, launched in early access recently with a $30 price tag. The game emphasizes horse management, survival aspects like feeding and stamina, and route planning across an open world inspired by a Mongolian setting. Key features include various horse breeds, breeding mechanics, day-night cycles, and combat with bows and repellents. However, the main story and several gameplay elements like taming and peaceful mode remain under development. The game’s early access state includes some bugs, notably a progression-blocking quest issue, and visual roughness during lighting changes. Player reception suggests the game is promising for horse enthusiasts and cozy sim fans but advises cautious buying due to its unfinished state and technical requirements.

Australia Implements Age Verification for R18+ Online Games

Starting next week, Australia will require users to verify their age to access online games rated R18+, including multiplayer titles like GTA Online. The legislation targets online-only games and excludes single-player offline experiences. Non-compliance by publishers could result in fines reaching AU$49.5 million per breach. This move is part of broader Age-Restricted Material Codes enforced by Australia’s eSafety Commissioner, covering explicit content across online platforms. The age verification methods mandated must be robust, accurate, and privacy-compliant, surpassing simple self-declaration. Some platforms employ government ID checks or biometric estimation techniques. This development places Australia at the forefront of stricter online age assurance, raising discussions around privacy implications and data security for players.

Marathon Launches with Mixed Early Reports

Bungie's new extraction shooter, Marathon, has recently launched and sparked a variety of player experiences. Early impressions note engaging gunplay and map design, with solo play favoring the Assassin and Vandal classes due to their abilities. The new Thief class introduces unique mechanics centered on intel gathering and sabotage with gadgets like a pickpocket drone and grapple device. However, some players have reported issues such as missing Deluxe Edition content and pre-order bonuses, which Bungie is actively addressing. Also notable is a gameplay quirk where rare loot borrowed during the prior server test event unintentionally carried over into the full game via the in-game mailbox system. Overall server stability appears solid at launch, but the title is still settling in with the community.

Diablo 4's Upcoming Season Lets Players Transform Into the Butcher

Blizzard revealed that Diablo 4’s upcoming Season of Slaughter, starting March 11, will let players temporarily become the series’ iconic demon, the Butcher. By maintaining a killstreak in certain open-world areas afflicted by enhanced monsters, players can collect a seasonal currency to trigger this transformation. Playing as the Butcher grants new abilities for dispatching enemies more effectively, fitting the season’s focus on sustained demon slaying. The season also introduces “Bloodied” items that activate special buffs. While the Butcher form is restricted to specific open zones, it will be available as a player-versus-player challenge in PvP areas. This experimental seasonal content arrives ahead of the Lord of Hatred expansion due April 28 and offers fresh gameplay options during the interim.
