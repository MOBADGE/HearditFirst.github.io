Striden Shuts Down After Struggling to Sustain Player Base

Multiplayer shooter *Striden*, known for its unique blend of radioactive attack bears and moose-drawn artillery, will be closing its servers by the end of April. Developer 5 Fortress announced that less than a year after its early access debut in July 2025, the game couldn't attract enough players to sustain ongoing development. Despite initial interest from several major publishers, the low player counts dissuaded them from investing in funding or marketing, creating a "Catch-22" that hindered growth.

The studio also faced unexpected challenges, such as addressing cheating issues and recovering from a cyberattack, which diverted resources away from adding new content or fixes. Attempts to introduce solo-play survival modes failed to reverse the trend, leading to the difficult decision to lay off all staff and end the project.

This closure reflects broader difficulties in the current games market, where launching a successful online shooter remains challenging amid ongoing industry contractions and numerous studio shutdowns.

Avalanche Reveals Details About Cancelled Fantasy RPG AionGuard

Avalanche Studios co-founder Christofer Sundberg shared insights into *AionGuard*, a cancelled ambitious fantasy RPG from the late 2000s that he says "would have been Crimson Desert". The game centered on a sorcerer-knight capable of riding dragons and transforming into a giant golem, engaged in large-scale conquest missions.

Initially backed by a prominent publisher rumored to be Disney Interactive, *AionGuard* was cancelled when the publisher shifted focus to existing intellectual properties. According to Sundberg, the partnership ended abruptly via a text message, leaving Avalanche to buy back the rights and attempt to secure new funding.

The project was publicly announced without a publisher and failed to gain traction due to its early reveal. The team later moved to develop another game, *Arcadia Rising*, which also faced cancellation following its publisher's financial troubles. These experiences highlight the volatility that can affect ambitious game projects even at well-established studios.

State of Decay 3 Reveal Trailer Created Without a Game or Team

Undead Labs has explained that the 2020 reveal trailer for *State of Decay 3* was made before the game or development team truly existed. According to studio member Philip Holt, the game was at concept stage described only in a Word document when the trailer was produced, with help from external visual effects artists.

The trailer featured a zombified deer, which led to fan interest in undead wildlife, but Holt clarified these elements were speculative concepts and will not feature in the final game. The reveal was prompted by Microsoft's request for Xbox Series X launch content, despite the studio feeling unprepared to announce a real project.

More concrete progress is now being made, with *State of Decay 3* preparing for alpha playtests. The game will offer four-player co-op and new base-building mechanics, moving beyond the conceptual phase it was in six years ago.

Intel Partners with Elon Musk’s Terafab Project to Advance AI Chip Production

Intel announced a partnership with Elon Musk’s Terafab project, aiming to overhaul silicon fabrication technology to supply AI compute power for SpaceX, Tesla, and Musk’s xAI venture. Details of Intel’s role remain unclear, but it will likely leverage its foundries and packaging facilities.

Terafab intends to deliver one terawatt (TW) of compute annually, an unprecedented scale driven by AI demand. Musk’s Tesla foundry in Austin is set to be an initial manufacturing site, and how Intel’s capacity fits into this remains to be seen.

The collaboration may have broader market impacts, especially given industry concerns that prioritizing AI chip production could further tighten supply and raise costs for consumer PC hardware, which is already facing component shortages and price inflation.

Creative Player Builds Cyberpunk Underground City in Pokopia After 260 Hours

A Pokopia community member has dedicated 260 hours over 22 days to build an elaborate underground Cyberpunk-style city using the game's building and environmental tools. The creation features neon signage, detailed architecture, and immersive atmosphere, impressing fellow players.

This level of dedication showcases the creative potential within Pokopia, where players can build expansive custom worlds. Other users have constructed recognizable locations from popular media, illustrating the game's growing appeal as a platform for player-driven design.
