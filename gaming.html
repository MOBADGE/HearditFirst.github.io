Pulsar X3 CrazyLight Mini Review: A Comfortable Lightweight Gaming Mouse for Relaxed Claw Grips

Pulsar's new X3 CrazyLight Mini wireless gaming mouse has received attention for its ergonomic design tailored to palm and relaxed claw grip users. Unlike the previous ambidextrous X2H CrazyLight Mini, the X3 features a right-handed ergonomic shape with a centrally aligned but width-lopsided hump and a thumb curve, similar to the Logitech G703 but smaller and lighter. Weighing approximately 45 grams, it balances lightness with a sturdy, premium feel.

Reviewers highlight its comfortable grip for medium-sized hands and the quality of its optical switches, scroll wheel, and PTFE skates, though the wireless connection shows some inconsistency without impacting in-game performance noticeably. Battery life lasts several days under heavy use. Pulsar also introduced improved browser-based software, offering more customization options including sensor rotation. The mouse, priced around $156/£130, is recommended mainly for those using relaxed claw grips, while fingertip grippers may find it unsuitable.

Witchhunter.exe Blends Retro Visual Novel with Glitch Horror Themes

Witchhunter.exe is an upcoming retro-styled adventure game combining choose-your-own-adventure mechanics with an old-school Dungeons & Dragons gold box interface. Set in a witch-hunting Salem-era environment, players interrogate women to identify witches while a devilish entity intermittently appears through computer pop-ups, adding a glitch-horror element.

The combat involves quickly typing or selecting correct words to dodge attacks. While the ghost-in-the-machine concept has been explored in titles like Pony Island, the stylistic homage to classic horror films and Salem folklore sets it apart. Witchhunter.exe is currently in development with a beta playtest anticipated on Steam, positioning itself as a niche title for fans of retro narrative and horror games.

New Steam Releases Highlight Varied Indie and Dungeon Crawler Titles

Five notable recent Steam game releases encompass a range of genres, including:

- *Mama's Sleeping Angels*: A multiplayer dream-world shooter with "cursed objects" affecting gameplay, blending Lethal Company and Cruelty Squad influences. - *Lucid Blocks*: Minecraft-inspired voxel survival with infinite vertical procedural generation in surreal environments. - *The State of Nowhere*: A moral decision-making game about rationing food in a totalitarian state, offering multiple endings. - *Thysiastery*: A pixel-art turn-based dungeon crawler focusing on escaping a labyrinth with party-based skills. - *Pluto*: A roguelike deckbuilder dungeon crawler featuring multiple characters and elemental synergies.

These releases offer varied gameplay experiences from cooperative shooters to narrative-heavy and strategy-driven games, appealing to indie and dungeon-crawling enthusiasts.

Controversy Over New Patch for Tomb Raider I-III Remastered Collection

The remastered collection of the first three Tomb Raider games recently received a free update that introduced a new challenge mode and 10 new character outfits. These new visual assets have sparked backlash from the community for low quality and unsatisfactory design, with some users reporting the patch caused technical issues including broken audio, enemy AI problems, and the loss of saved data.

The original lead artist of the remaster distanced himself from the update, noting the original development team was not involved. There are ongoing concerns about the possible use of AI in creating the new assets, following previous unauthorized AI voice additions that were removed last year. The patch has drawn criticism for not meeting fans’ expectations and for degrading the game’s polish.

GTA RP’s Hide-and-Seek Craze Adds Realism to Roleplay Experience

A new trend in Grand Theft Auto 5 roleplay servers is a high-stakes version of hide-and-seek where players wager their vehicles. Unlike standard GTA Online, where damaged cars can be easily repaired via insurance, RP servers require complex player-driven towing and repair processes, making vehicle loss more consequential.

This gameplay loop has resonated with players seeking immersive, realistic roleplay that involves economic struggle, such as saving for a dilapidated truck over months only to risk losing it in a game. The craze highlights how player creativity continues to evolve within long-standing multiplayer spaces, and has influenced expectations for GTA 6’s multiplayer features to support both high-octane and grounded roleplay styles. Rockstar’s recent acquisition of the FiveM mod platform hints at official support for deeper RP integration in future GTA titles.
