Sony Increases PlayStation 5 and PS5 Pro Prices Globally

Sony has announced a significant price increase for its PlayStation 5 lineup and the PlayStation Portal remote player. Beginning April 2, 2026, the standard PS5 will cost $650 (up from $550), the PS5 Digital Edition $600 (up from $500), and the PS5 Pro $900 (up from $750). The PlayStation Portal will increase to $250 (from $200). Sony attributes this adjustment to ongoing global economic pressures and rising costs, particularly the scarcity and price of memory components essential for console production. This marks the latest in a series of gradual price hikes since the console's 2020 launch, reflecting sustained challenges in component supply chains that also affect competitors like Valve and Nintendo.

Google's TurboQuant Algorithm May Ease Memory Demand but Impact RAM Manufacturers

Google introduced TurboQuant, a new algorithm that compresses AI model data to reduce memory usage by approximately six times without loss of accuracy. TurboQuant changes the way data vectors are processed, replacing traditional vector calculations with a polar coordinate system to minimize memory overhead. This technical advance has led to a drop in memory manufacturers’ stock values, as it may reduce the demand for high-capacity RAM in AI data centers. If memory demands from AI companies decrease, this could increase supply availability and potentially lower prices for consumer electronics like gaming PCs. However, industry analysts caution that high demand is expected to persist for the foreseeable future, and any freed capacity might be absorbed by larger or more complex AI models.

Warhammer 40,000 Launches 11th Edition with Nostalgic Starter Set

Games Workshop revealed the 11th edition of Warhammer 40,000 at Adepticon 2026, starting with a starter set that revisits the classic Blood Angels versus Orks battle on the planet Armageddon, echoing the game’s 2nd edition from over 30 years ago. The new edition brings incremental rule changes, such as more flexible army building, terrain-based mission objectives, and revised combat and terrain mechanics. This approach is intended to maintain compatibility with existing game materials while refreshing gameplay. Additionally, the publisher announced new miniatures and expansions across related game lines including Kill Team, Age of Sigmar, and The Horus Heresy, blending nostalgia with modest rule refinements.

New Sim Racing Pedal Stand Offers Improved Stability and Convenience

SR Rigs introduced the SR Pedal Stand Pro, a specialized mounting bracket designed for sim racers to secure their pedals and chair more effectively. The stand raises pedal height for ergonomic comfort and locks chair casters in place with straps to prevent movement during heavy braking. Compatible with popular pedals from brands like Fanatec, Thrustmaster, and Logitech, it supports up to 60kg of force and is adjustable to user height. The product addresses a common issue for sim racers who often resorted to makeshift solutions, providing a more stable and compact alternative to full racing seats or bulky foldable stands. Initial availability is limited, with a waitlist open at the manufacturer’s website.

Arc Raiders Developers Address Player Concerns About Balance and Endgame Content

The development team behind the third-person shooter Arc Raiders, which achieved over 12 million players since its launch, has acknowledged ongoing community concerns regarding weapon and arc balancing issues, rampant cheating, and limited endgame content. Production director Caio Braga stated that balancing remains a priority as the team adapts to live service demands and player feedback. The game’s hybrid PvE/PvP design complicates balance, as PvP players seek nerfs to competitive elements, while PvE players prefer more empowered arcs for cooperative play. The developers also recognize the need to expand challenging endgame activities for veteran players and are committed to gradually enhancing this aspect without compromising accessibility.
