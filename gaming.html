**Ashes of Creation Early Access Launches on Steam**

Ashes of Creation, the fantasy MMORPG known for its player-driven world, is launching its early access alpha build on Steam at 10 a.m. PST on December 11, 2025. This release marks a key step in the game's long development process, which began with a successful Kickstarter campaign in 2017. Importantly, this is an early alpha version; the game remains unfinished, with two beta phases planned before the full version arrives. Players should expect incomplete content and potential wipes of their progress typical of early access MMOs.

Steam will be the sole platform for accessing the game moving forward, meaning previous launchers and versions will be retired. There will be no preload option, so players must download the game when servers go live. Existing account holders will need to link their Intrepid accounts to Steam ahead of launch to ensure a smooth transition.

This release matters as it brings the long-awaited Ashes of Creation experience to a wider audience, though with the typical caveats of early access software. Players interested in contributing to the game's ongoing development and testing can now participate but should be prepared for an evolving and incomplete product.

**Call of Duty Announces Changes After Black Ops 7 Reception**

Following a lukewarm reception to Black Ops 7, Activision has publicly committed to ending its practice of annual Call of Duty releases within the same subseries. The company stated its intention to provide more unique and meaningful innovations year-to-year rather than incremental updates.

This announcement comes after years of yearly CoD titles, which have frequently drawn criticism for repetitiveness and rushed development cycles. Black Ops 7, released less than a month ago, has seen players skipping it in favor of previous entries or competitors. Activision's statement acknowledges that back-to-back releases contributed to player fatigue and signals a shift in strategy, though detailed plans are yet to be shared.

The change reflects broader concerns about franchise burnout and the difficulty of sustaining quality under a yearly cycle. It may also respond to successes from competitors who take longer between releases. How this affects future CoD development and player engagement remains to be seen.

**Romero Games Moves Forward After Shooter Project Cancellation**

John Romero, co-founder of Romero Games, confirmed that the studio avoided closure despite losing funding for a major shooter project earlier this year. The original project, which employed around 110 people and was reportedly funded by a major publisher, was canceled, but the studio has pivoted to a smaller-scale game.

The new shooter, substantially redesigned, incorporates elements from the canceled project to build an indie game experience. Romero describes it as something new among shooters, comparing its novelty to the experience of playing Elden Ring. The reduced scope also allows the developers more hands-on involvement rather than purely managerial roles.

This development illustrates challenges indie studios face with publisher dependencies but also shows resilience and adaptability in recovering from setbacks to continue game development.
