Philips Launches 5K Dual-Mode Gaming Monitor in the West

Philips has announced the western release of its 27M2D5901A gaming monitor, a 27-inch 5K dual-mode display initially introduced in China as the Evnia 27M2G5800. This monitor can switch between a 5120 x 2880 resolution at 165 Hz for immersive gaming and 2560 x 1440 at 330 Hz for competitive play. Its standout feature is the ability to swap display modes quickly, catering to different gaming needs on the same screen. The monitor also includes Philips Ambilight lighting synced with gameplay and compatibility with smart home systems for enhanced room illumination effects. It supports 65W USB Type-C charging and features a clean white design. Priced at €799 (~$940), the monitor will be available in Europe from July, though there are currently no plans for a US release. This addition offers gamers a high-resolution, high-refresh-rate option with unique lighting features, though 5K gaming remains a niche needing powerful hardware.

Turtle WoW Classic Server to Shut Down Following Blizzard Injunction

Following a legal ruling in favor of Blizzard Entertainment, the popular private server Turtle WoW, which provided a modded "Classic Plus" World of Warcraft experience, has announced it will shut down entirely by May 14. The decision comes after Blizzard secured an injunction over copyright infringement claims related to the server’s operation. Turtle WoW aimed to expand upon the original WoW Classic with new raids and zones while preserving the pre-expansion era gameplay, catering to fans desiring fresh content in the vanilla WoW style. Despite an eight-year run and community support, the server’s team acknowledged the end of their project and the closure of associated forums and social media later this year. This development echoes previous industry conflict where fan-run servers face legal challenges, illustrating the ongoing tension between game publishers and fan communities regarding unofficial content.

Fallout Designer Credits Navy Officers for Introducing Him to Dungeons & Dragons

Tim Cain, the designer behind Fallout, shared a story about how his early exposure to Dungeons & Dragons (D&D) influenced his game development career. Cain recalls learning to play first-edition Advanced D&D in 1979 at the home of several US Navy officers who were his mother’s colleagues. Unlike computer-based RPGs, this face-to-face experience with D&D allowed him to absorb the complexities of the tabletop system in detail. His initial character was a multi-class elf, reflecting his enthusiasm for the game's flexibility. Cain credits this introduction for shaping his understanding of role-playing mechanics, which later contributed to his work on Fallout and other RPG titles. His story highlights the roots of many digital RPGs in traditional tabletop gaming and the significance of these early personal experiences in game development history.

Portal 2: Community Edition Enters Open Beta to Enhance Modding

A new community-driven version of Portal 2, called Portal 2: Community Edition (CE), has launched its open beta. Unlike a remake or new campaign, CE is a forked version of the game engine that enables advanced modding features not possible in the original version. It introduces new lighting effects, higher resolution textures, enhanced scripting capabilities, and improved map editing tools. This update is built on the Strata Source engine, previously used for ambitious Portal mods. The community edition also supports custom campaigns with integrated UI and already hosts various mods, including Half-Life 2 maps and new puzzles. Although the selection of mods is still growing, developers plan to create original campaign content. This release aims to provide modders with more creative freedom and technical tools, potentially extending Portal 2’s lifespan as a mod-friendly platform.

Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas Mod Transforms Setting to Manhunt’s Carcer City

A total conversion mod for Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas named GTA: Carcer City recreates the urban setting from 2003’s stealth horror game Manhunt. This ambitious mod replaces the original expansive map with a new city inspired by Rust Belt areas such as Detroit and Cleveland. The mod features locations directly adapted from Manhunt’s levels and introduces a new storyline set in 2001 with a darker tone than typical GTA games. Available as a demo, the mod allows players to explore the entire city and sample the narrative.
