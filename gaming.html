Diablo 4 Paladin Free Access Ahead of New Season

Blizzard is offering free access to the Paladin class in Diablo 4 from March 11 to March 18, coinciding with the start of the game's next season, the Season of Slaughter. Players can level a Paladin to 25 without needing to pre-order the upcoming Lord of Hatred expansion or own the base game. The Paladin is currently considered one of the strongest classes, though some overpowered bugs have been fixed ahead of the new season. This free trial aims to attract players to experience the holy warrior class, which suits the season's focus on kill streaks and hordes of enemies. Notably, the expansion also introduces a second new class, the Warlock, arriving with the expansion’s release on April 28.

Microsoft Unveils Next Xbox: Project Helix

Microsoft revealed that the codename for their next-generation Xbox console is Project Helix. The new console aims to "lead in performance" and will support playing both Xbox and PC games. This represents a strategic shift towards a more versatile gaming device, closer in nature to a living room PC. Project Helix is expected to launch in 2027, based on recent statements from AMD. The announcement comes amid leadership changes at Microsoft Gaming, with Asha Sharma recently named as the new head. Sharma’s comments foreshadow further details at the upcoming Game Developers Conference (GDC) starting March 9.

Fallout TV Series Boosts Game Sales Across the Franchise

The Fallout TV series on Amazon has significantly increased sales and player numbers for several Fallout games, including classic titles from the 1990s. Data from reseller G2A.com indicates sustained growth since the series' debut in April 2024, with spikes in sales occurring even in the month before Season 2's premiere. Sales of complete editions with additional content have increased alongside demand for the lore-rich experience. The theme of each season influenced game interest; for example, Fallout: New Vegas saw a market share increase during the series’ second season. This boost underscores the continued relevance of Bethesda’s post-apocalyptic RPGs, highlighting demand for a new Fallout installment.

Diablo 4 Warlock Preview Highlights Skill Customization

Ahead of Lord of Hatred’s release, a recent hands-on with Diablo 4’s new Warlock class showcased a complex and customizable skill tree system. Unlike the previous “skill twig” design, the Warlock’s skills offer numerous upgrade paths that significantly alter their function rather than just increasing damage numbers. This reflects a broader design shift for the upcoming expansion, which includes reworks of existing classes to emphasize skill-based buildcrafting over reliance on gear. The Warlock specializes in summoning demons and wielding shadow magic, clearly differentiated from the Necromancer class. This approach aims to provide more meaningful player choices in character development.

Let It Die Transitioning to Offline Version with One-Time Purchase

Grasshopper Manufacture announced that their free-to-play roguelike Let It Die will shut down its online servers on August 31, 2026. To keep the game playable, an offline version requiring a one-time purchase will be launched on Steam. The offline edition will remove live-service elements such as microtransactions and convert online-only features into offline equivalents. Base-raiding PvP content will shift to AI-controlled bases, and functionalities tied to premium currency will become earnable through gameplay. The move aims to preserve gameplay while addressing challenges related to monetization and the end of online support.

Marathon’s Faction Reputation Grind Can Be Eased With More Actions

In the recent launch of Bungie’s Marathon, players faced a slow faction reputation progression, partly due to the game limiting them to one active contract per run. However, reputation gains are also awarded through faction-specific actions beyond contracts, such as looting particular items or engaging in faction events. Players who focus on these additional reputation activities alongside contracts can accelerate their faction rank increases. This offers a practical approach to mitigating the otherwise grind-heavy faction system and enhances the game's progression dynamics.
