Doki Doki Literature Club Removed from Google Play Store

Google has pulled the cult visual novel *Doki Doki Literature Club* from its Android store, citing violations of its Terms of Service due to the game's treatment of sensitive themes. Originally released on PC in 2017 and ported to various platforms including Nintendo Switch, PlayStation, and iOS, the game had been available on Google Play since December. Known for subverting its cheerful visual novel facade to explore psychological horror elements such as depression and suicide, the game’s removal highlights ongoing challenges in regulating mature content in app stores. The developers and publisher emphasized the game's positive impact on players dealing with mental health issues and stated that they are seeking ways to have the game reinstated, as well as exploring alternate Android distribution methods.

Dune: Awakening PvP Zones Become Optional

Funcom has announced a major change to *Dune: Awakening*'s PvP system, responding to player feedback about mandatory PvP in the Deep Desert endgame area. Starting with the upcoming 1.3.20.0 patch, all PvP zones in Hagga Basin will be disabled on official servers. Players will be able to choose between separate PvE and PvP instances for endgame content, allowing those who prefer player versus environment gameplay to avoid PvP encounters entirely. The PvP instance will retain traditional open-world conflict but with increased resource rewards. Additionally, Funcom revealed plans for self-hosted servers, giving players the ability to customize gameplay settings, though the feature is intended for technically capable users and will enter testing soon. This change underlines Funcom’s intention to focus on a PvE-first experience while keeping PvP available as an optional, incentivized feature.

Metal Gear Solid Film Announcement Revisited by Sony

Sony has entered a deal to develop a *Metal Gear Solid* film, reviving a project first announced by franchise creator Hideo Kojima two decades ago. Directors Zach Lipovsky and Adam B. Stein, known for *Final Destination: Bloodlines*, are attached to the project alongside other Sony productions. Despite numerous previous attempts and rumors since 2006, this development marks a tangible step forward, though no production timeline or casting details have been disclosed yet. The longevity of this adaptation effort reflects the challenges of translating complex video game narratives to film, but Sony’s involvement signals renewed interest in bringing Kojima’s work to the big screen.

World of Warcraft Introduces Prop Hunt Style PvP Mode in Player Housing

Blizzard is expanding player housing in *World of Warcraft* beyond building and decorating by adding a new PvP mode called Decor Duels. Launching with patch 12.0.5 on April 21, this prop hunt-style mode takes place in Silvermoon city where players hide as objects and others seek them. The mode utilizes Blizzard’s extensive collection of household decor and will reward participants with mounts, toys, and housing items. This marks a notable addition to the player housing system, showing Blizzard’s commitment to diversifying gameplay experiences associated with housing beyond static customization and into interactive game modes.

Rust 2 Steam Page Sparks Fan Speculation Amid Developer Teasing

A Steam page for *Rust 2* briefly appeared, stirring excitement and skepticism among fans. The listing was flagged as suspicious by SteamDB, and the Reddit post announcing it originated from a newly created account, increasing doubts about its legitimacy. Shortly after, Facepunch Studios’ COO Alistair McFarlane cryptically responded to the post saying, “You saw nothing,” fueling speculation about whether the page was a deliberate tease or an unauthorized leak. While *Rust 2* has been referenced by Facepunch before, no official confirmation about its release or details has been given. This incident highlights a mix of anticipation and cautiousness surrounding potential announcements from the studio.

Europa Universalis 5 Review Finds Depth but Lacks Distinctiveness

A veteran player of the *Europa Universalis* series shared a mixed reflection on *Europa Universalis 5* following a 93-hour first campaign. The game offers greater complexity and simulation depth compared to its predecessor, but the reviewer expressed frustration with the lack of differentiation among factions and the absence of unique national flavor found in *Europa Universalis 4*.
