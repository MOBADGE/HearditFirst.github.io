Rollin' Rascal Update Adds Ranked Multiplayer Races

Rollin' Rascal, a fan-made 3D platformer inspired by early Sonic the Hedgehog Dreamcast games, has received a significant update during the Steam Next Fest. Developed by Gabriel Gonzales, who previously worked on the Sonic fangame Sonic GT, Rollin' Rascal expands on the momentum-based platforming and fast pace of Sonic Adventure titles. The recent update introduces ranked multiplayer races featuring lengthy courses and item boxes reminiscent of Mario Kart. While the online player base appears small currently, the addition broadens the game’s appeal to competitive speedrunners and multiplayer enthusiasts. Despite some minor bugs and camera issues, the update shows promising progress toward a full release.

Rare Tsukihime Prototype Disk Destroyed in Shipping Incident

A rare prototype floppy disk of the 1999 Japanese visual novel Tsukihime was found destroyed upon arrival to its new owner. Only 50 copies of this pre-release trial version were ever produced, making it a valuable collector’s item and piece of gaming history. The owner reported that US Customs had removed protective packaging and dismantled the disk, apparently during an inspection. The incident highlights vulnerabilities of physical game preservation against damage and mishandling during transit. The owner has filed a formal inquiry to seek answers and raise awareness about the importance of careful handling of such historical media.

WoW Players Criticize Loud Sunwell Ambient Sound

Players of World of Warcraft: Midnight are expressing frustration over the persistent and loud crackling sound coming from the Sunwell, a magical beam visible in Silvermoon City and the surrounding areas. The ambient noise is intended to represent the Sunwell’s power in resisting the Void but currently plays at high volume over a large area, making it difficult for players to hear NPCs or communicate. Some players have found a workaround by disabling ambient sounds in settings, though this also cuts other environment sounds. The consistent complaints suggest Blizzard may need to adjust the audio’s range or volume to improve player experience in this iconic zone.

Blizzard’s WoW: Midnight Composer Rejects Generative AI Use

Leo Kaliski, lead composer for World of Warcraft: Midnight, stated in a recent interview that the game’s music was created without the use of generative AI. Blizzard developers have generally expressed caution about incorporating generative AI in their content, with previous comments from Overwatch and WoW directors emphasizing their reluctance to deploy AI-generated assets directly to players. While Microsoft’s ownership of Blizzard encourages AI experimentation, Blizzard appears to maintain a cautious and measured approach, allowing individual teams autonomy but so far keeping generative AI out of core game development. This stance reflects ongoing debates about AI’s role in creative industries.

GTA 5’s Squid Game Mods Show Substantial Improvement

Modders have revived and improved Squid Game-themed mods for GTA 5, following the show’s cultural impact since 2021. Early versions suffered from incomplete scripting, poor AI, and lack of polish. However, recent mods have introduced stable gameplay mechanics, configurable timers, accurate detection logic, and thematic NPC behavior that better match both the TV series’ tone and GTA’s style. Some mods feature fenced arenas, custom spectator cameras, and announcers, providing a more immersive multiplayer experience. This evolution exemplifies how modding communities progressively refine projects to create more enduring and engaging content.

Updated Star Wars Mod “TIE Fighter: Total Conversion” Gains New Features

The “TIE Fighter: Total Conversion” mod for the classic game X-Wing Alliance recently received a major update. This mod reimagines the 1994 TIE Fighter game with improvements and is now available in separate installers for “Classic” and “Reimagined” versions. The update adds stability enhancements via side process executables and upgrades the in-game menu areas with high-definition art and animations. The developer noted this milestone marks nearly a year of work, with further updates planned including cooperative multiplayer and additional refinements. The mod continues to be regarded as one of the most impressive fan projects in the Star Wars gaming community.
