Batman: Arkham Origins Gets a Graphics Mod That Feels Like a Remaster

A newly updated mod for Batman: Arkham Origins called the "Arkham Origins Advanced Graphics mod" brings substantial visual improvements that make the game resemble a remaster. The mod increases the snow particles and their deformation, enhances the lighting for a wintry atmosphere, and upgrades textures, including a higher resolution Gotham skyline and Batman’s suit sourced from other Arkham titles. Shadows see reduced artifacts, and reflections receive notable upgrades. However, cutscenes remain capped at 720p due to file format limitations. This mod requires at least one DLC installed due to the game’s configuration. It offers fans a way to revisit the 2013 game with enhanced visuals without waiting for an official remaster.

Death Stranding 2 Surpasses Two Million Sales With PC Release

The PC version of *Death Stranding 2: On the Beach* sold 425,000 copies in its first week, contributing to a total exceeding two million copies across PC and PlayStation 5. This marks it as one of the fastest-to-PC console exclusives, reaching the platform nine months after initial PS5 launch. Interestingly, the PC launch coincided with increased PS5 sales following a discount, suggesting renewed interest in the game overall. Despite Sony’s reported retreat from broad PC releases for single-player prestige games, this milestone showcases the potential impact of timed PC ports. The game continues to garner discussion for its narrative themes and unique gameplay.

Star Wars Zero Company Director on Adding Story and Style to Turn-Based Tactics

Greg Foertsch, creative director of the upcoming turn-based tactics game *Star Wars: Zero Company*, emphasizes that tactics games do not need to sacrifice story, visuals, or control elegance for gameplay depth. He argues against the notion that the genre must settle for minimal graphics or sparse narrative, advocating instead for immersive storytelling and polished presentation that complement strategic gameplay. Drawing on his experience from *XCOM* and *Marvel’s Midnight Suns*, Foertsch believes players can have both depth and production value without compromise. His approach challenges existing genre stereotypes and may influence future tactics game design emphasizing both gameplay and narrative richness.

Epic Games Layoffs Affect Terminally Ill Programmer; CEO Responds

Mike Prinke, a longtime technical writer at Epic Games, was among the 1,000-plus employees laid off recently. His termination comes while he is undergoing treatment for terminal brain cancer, a circumstance publicly disclosed by his wife. The layoff affected his employer-provided health and life insurance, creating difficulties for treatment and financial stability. Epic CEO Tim Sweeney acknowledged the situation on social media, stating the company is contacting the family to resolve insurance issues. He apologized for not anticipating the hardship. This case highlights the personal risks of layoffs in industries where health coverage is tied to employment and raises questions about protections for vulnerable employees.

Crimson Desert Patch Adds Mounts and Tweaks Stealing Mechanics

The latest update to *Crimson Desert* brings five new mounts obtainable through in-game achievements, enriching player traversal options. The patch also introduces a notable change to the game's crime system: players only lose contribution points (a reputation metric) for stealing if an NPC witnesses the act. Other improvements include refined movement controls, added chests, relocated storage furniture, and visual asset replacements removing previously spotted AI-generated artwork.
