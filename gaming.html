Zero Parades Shows Promising Moments Amid Challenging Legacy

ZA/UM, the studio behind the acclaimed RPG Disco Elysium, demonstrated some encouraging elements from their new game Zero Parades at GDC. Tasked with creating a follow-up that balances Disco Elysium’s distinctive verbose style with a fresh spy thriller tone, Zero Parades offers flashes of the original’s wit. Notable moments include a tense yet humor-filled intercom interaction and a clever sequence involving a locked door keypad, where failure leads to amusing attempts rather than dead ends. While the tone still feels uncertain — veering between seriousness and sarcasm — these scenarios suggest ZA/UM aims to preserve the narrative creativity that made Disco Elysium stand out.

Seed MMO Aims for Persistent Characters and Player-Run Societies

Klang Games, led by former EVE Online developers, presented updates on Seed, an ambitious MMO mixing elements of RimWorld, The Sims, and Dwarf Fortress. Its key feature is that player characters, called “Seedlings,” continue to live and act within the persistent world even when players are offline. Societies formed by groups of players govern resources, laws, and commerce, creating layered social dynamics reminiscent of EVE’s political intrigue. The game also targets players who enjoy caring for individual characters and homes, offering a blend of deep simulation and community-driven gameplay. Seed remains in closed beta with a gradual player rollout, and a mobile app is planned to let players manage Seedlings remotely.

Shadowstone Begins Surprise Public Playtest

Secret Door and Dreamhaven launched a public playtest for Shadowstone, a tactics roguelike set in the world of Sunderfolk, during the Future Games Show. The playtest invites players to explore run-based solo or co-op matches, focusing on adaptive skill cards and randomized ruins. Progress will not carry into the full release, which is expected later this year, and participants are encouraged to share their experiences publicly as no NDA is in place. The playtest is by sign-up only and intends to gather feedback as development continues toward early access.

Industry Veteran Rob Pardo Warns Against Layoffs in GDC Keynote

In his keynote at this year’s Game Developers Conference, former Blizzard chief creative officer Rob Pardo emphasized the value of development teams over individual game projects. Reflecting on his career working on titles like Starcraft and Warcraft 3, Pardo highlighted that game successes depend on the team’s ability to navigate unforeseen challenges. He cautioned executives against layoffs following successes, arguing that preserving the original team is the best way to replicate achievements. Pardo’s message comes amid ongoing industry layoffs even after major releases, underscoring tension between business decisions and creative continuity.

Path of Exile’s Mirage League Revamps Currency Drops and Atlas Gameplay

Grinding Gear Games launched the Mirage league for Path of Exile alongside patch 3.28, introducing significant changes to currency drop rates and atlas endgame progression. Currency now drops more frequently from enemies, making crafting and economy management easier for players throughout the campaign. The league’s new “mirage” mechanic allows running portions of maps multiple times with special rewards, enabling strategies like double boss fights and resource farming. The atlas system replaces specific maps with tiered tokens and region-based modifiers, adding a more intentional exploration structure. These changes mark the most substantial economic overhaul in years and aim to enhance the game’s late-stage play.
