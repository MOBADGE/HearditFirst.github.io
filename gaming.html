This decision came amid concerns about repeat violence similar to clashes in Amsterdam the previous year.

It raises broader questions about integrating AI technology in law enforcement and the importance of human oversight.

Arc Raiders Discards Auction House to Preserve Gameplay Focus

Embark Studios, developer of the third-person shooter Arc Raiders, revealed plans to avoid implementing an auction house-style trading system similar to that of Escape From Tarkov. Design lead Virgil Watkins explained that during development, introducing an auction house shifted gameplay focus to currency management rather than exploration and item discovery.

Instead, Arc Raiders emphasizes manual, in-person trading within matches and crafting systems, limiting direct purchases from vendors to encourage crafting and item scavenging. Watkins mentioned potential future improvements to the trading interface that would maintain player interaction, such as direct exchanges rather than item dropping. This decision reflects a conscious effort to preserve gameplay depth and player engagement over convenience.

Quarantine Zone: Contraband and Special Survivor Features

Quarantine Zone: The Last Check, a recent simulation game where players screen survivors for infection and contraband at a post-apocalyptic checkpoint, has detailed its contraband system. Players must confiscate weapons, drugs, and hazardous items carried by survivors while allowing safe items like food and toiletries. Some contraband, such as zombie body parts, cannot be removed by the player and require sending infected survivors to liquidation.

In addition, players must check for internally hidden contraband using in-game scanning tools, with a character named Charlie handling extractions. The game features various survivor cameos, including content creators depicted as survivors with special appearances and voice acting. These elements add variety and easter eggs to the otherwise methodical gameplay centered on inspection and quarantine.
