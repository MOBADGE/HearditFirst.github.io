Sonic Racing: CrossWorlds Review Highlights Online Issues and Deep Customization

Sonic Racing: CrossWorlds, released in September 2025, presents a robust kart racing experience with extensive vehicle customization and inventive track design. Developed by Sonic Team and published by SEGA, the game includes a roster of familiar Sonic characters plus lesser-known ones. Its tracks offer multiple paths, vertical elements, and mid-race portals shifting players to different course sections, encouraging experimentation.

Despite strong offline modes and customization options‚Äîfive vehicle types and a gadget system allowing tailored playstyles‚Äîthe game struggles significantly with online competitive play. Players frequently encounter connection errors that boot them to the lobby, and item balancing issues promote sandbagging strategies that detract from skill-based competition. The price point of $70 was criticized, considering its limited online stability and content. DLC characters add variety but lack voice acting, weakening character interaction.

For players prioritizing offline racing and customization, the game offers considerable depth. However, for those focused on online competition, current issues may diminish enjoyment until further patches address stability and balance.

Sony Inzone H9 II Review: Comfort and Noise Cancelling Balanced by High Price

Sony's Inzone H9 II gaming headset, priced at $350, offers a lightweight design (260 grams without mic) and effective active noise cancelling (ANC). Its build prioritizes comfort over premium materials, with a thin headband and soft foam earcups suited for extended wear. The 30 mm carbon composite drivers provide a warm, clear sound profile with customizable EQ through Sony‚Äôs software, though it does not achieve audiophile-level quality.

The removable cardioid condenser microphone performs well for light streaming but is susceptible to plosives. Battery life is about 30 hours with ANC off, which is shorter than some competitors. Reviewers note that while the headset delivers solid noise cancelling and comfort, the price may be hard to justify compared to higher-end or better-equipped alternatives available for less.
