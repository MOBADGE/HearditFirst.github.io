Goblin Sushi: A New Cooking Simulator with a Goblin Twist

Indie developer Old Cake Factory is launching *Goblin Sushi*, an upcoming cooking simulator set in a fantasy dungeon environment. Players control a goblin chef preparing sushi dishes for other sushi-loving goblins. The game involves combining ingredients like rice, seaweed, fish, and even cave creatures to serve a continuous stream of customers, including unique characters like two-headed goblins and dogmen. Timely service is crucial, as customers grow impatient if dishes are late or if poorly made items (including comedic "pile of poo" dishes) appear. Meanwhile, players must also manage rent payments to a demanding goblin landlord. *Goblin Sushi* aims for a balance between simple gameplay and a quirky aesthetic. It will enter early access on February 9, with a playable demo currently available on Steam.
