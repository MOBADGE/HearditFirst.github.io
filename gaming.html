Blizzard Faces Backlash Over Abundance Event Changes in World of Warcraft

Blizzard is encountering difficulties balancing the Abundance world event in World of Warcraft: Midnight, which rewards players with Unalloyed Abundance currency important for crafting. Players found success in large raid groups to maximize gains, but these groups caused severe server lag. In response, Blizzard appears to have disabled the ability to split Abundance among large groups, limiting the maximum currency earned per run. This change makes hitting the weekly cap more stressful and favors early access players, who remain ahead.

The event encourages players to complete eight runs per week, reflecting a grind-heavy approach resembling past WoW systems. However, the recent nerfs have frustrated profession-focused players who previously enjoyed the chaotic group dynamics. Blizzard’s struggle to satisfy both casual and hardcore players highlights ongoing challenges around rewarding crafting and endgame content in WoW.

Windows 11 Bug Locks Some Samsung PC Users Out of Their C: Drives

A significant issue affecting Windows 11 users on Samsung Galaxy Book 4 and certain desktop PCs running versions 24H2 and 25H2 has emerged, where users receive an “Access denied” error when trying to access their C: drives. This problem stems from conflicts introduced by the Samsung Galaxy Connect app and affects basic file access, running applications, and even system utilities.

Microsoft and Samsung have temporarily removed the offending app from the Microsoft Store and reverted it to an earlier stable version. Recovery options remain limited for affected users as both companies continue investigating fixes. While the issue is isolated to Samsung devices, it raises concerns about system stability for a large Windows 11 user base currently over one billion worldwide.

Bethesda Downplays Starfield’s Reception Ahead of Upcoming News

Bethesda sought to temper expectations following lukewarm reception of Starfield. After the game’s composer suggested it would become "legendary" over time, the studio quipped that Todd Howard’s only "visionary power" relates to his skills at EA College Football 26. Bethesda promises additional updates next week but cautions that the forthcoming patch won't fundamentally change the experience for players who did not initially connect with the game.

The next update reportedly aims to introduce meta changes to space travel and gameplay systems rather than act as a major overhaul. This measured communication reiterates that Starfield’s development focus is on steady improvements for fans rather than a dramatic reinvention.

FBI Seeks Information on Malware Hidden in Steam Games

The FBI has called for reports from victims who downloaded malware concealed within several Steam games, including BlockBlasters, Chemia, and PirateFI, active between May 2024 and January 2026. The malware campaigns involved malicious files that compromised users’ computers and, in some cases, led to financial losses.

Affected users can report their experiences via email or a dedicated FBI form to assist ongoing investigations. The FBI also cautions users to be wary of online investment advice and urges skepticism toward any requests for personal or financial information. Past incidents, such as PirateFi and BlockBlasters, demonstrate how malware disguised as legitimate games can exploit Steam’s platform to reach large user bases.

Resident Evil Requiem Hits Six Million Sales Quickly

Capcom announced that Resident Evil Requiem has sold over six million units worldwide, marking the fastest in series history to reach this milestone. The game has enjoyed overwhelming positive reviews and high concurrent player counts on Steam since launch.

Capcom plans ongoing support including new features like a photo mode and minigames coming in May, and early work has started on downloadable content that may take longer to develop. The announcement coincides with the approaching 30th anniversary of the Resident Evil series, with other related events and collaborations planned for 2026, offering fans continued engagement with the franchise.
