**Fallout: New Vegas' Mr. House Reflects Changing Views of Tech Titans**

With the second season of the Fallout TV series focusing on New Vegas, lead writer of the original 2010 game, John Gonzalez, shared insights into one of its key characters, Mr. House. Originally inspired by early 20th-century entrepreneur Howard Hughes, Mr. House is a tech magnate who governs New Vegas via a supercomputer. Gonzalez noted that his perception of Mr. House has evolved, particularly observing the rise of Silicon Valley figures who aspire to technological immortality. Unlike these modern figures, Mr. House was fictionalized with the ability to almost perfectly predict the timing of the Great War. This reflection connects the game's themes with ongoing real-world discourse about the influence and intentions of technology leaders.

**Roblox Ban in Russia Leads to Uncommon Public Protests**

A ban on Roblox in Russia has sparked rare public demonstrations, notably in Tomsk where protesters gathered despite cold weather and risks of state reprisal. The ban, justified by Russian authorities on grounds of inappropriate content and alleged promotion of "LGBT propaganda," has drawn criticism and debate about censorship and children's access to digital platforms. Roblox is popular in Russia, making the ban a significant disruption for many users. While authorities argue the ban protects minors, critics point to Roblox's ongoing challenges with child safety and online moderation, issues the company has publicly addressed with various measures. The protests and thousands of letters from children opposing the ban indicate notable grassroots resistance, despite likely limited influence on official policy.

**Fallout Season 2 Premiere Moves Up Slightly**

The first episode of Fallout Season 2 will arrive marginally earlier than initially announced, now set for December 16 at 6 p.m. PT instead of just after midnight on December 17. Hosted with a thematic reveal on the Las Vegas Sphere, the earlier release gives viewers a small advance on the anticipated season. This timing adjustment may cater to audience convenience but does not signify other changes in the show's schedule.

**Resident Evil Requiem Highlights Leon Kennedy's Expanded Role**

Developers of Resident Evil Requiem have confirmed that fan-favorite character Leon Kennedy will return, with his role designed to be more demanding than ever before. The game's director, Akifumi Nakanishi, described Leon as a composed, empathetic figure who combines maturity with wit. This marks Leon's first appearance as a main protagonist since Resident Evil 6, showcasing a more seasoned version of the character. Gameplay alternates between Leon and a new character, Grace, blending horror and action elements to create narrative and pacing contrasts. The developers emphasize their intent to maintain a unified story experience while expanding Leon's combat capabilities and character depth. This inclusion reinforces Leon's continuing importance within the Resident Evil franchise.

Ceasing SATA production reflects Samsung's shift away from entry-level consumer storage solutions toward higher-performance products and AI-related markets.
