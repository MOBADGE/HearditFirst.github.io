New Flexible Fibre Chips Could Influence Future Gaming Hardware

Scientists at Fudan University have developed flexible, fibre-integrated circuits (FICs) as thin as a human hair that can house up to 100,000 transistors per centimetre. These chips show remarkable durability, capable of withstanding extreme physical stress, including crushing under a 15.6-ton container truck. While current technology limits integration density, future advances in photolithography may allow these chips to rival classical CPU capacities. The resilience and thinness of these fibres suggest potential applications in wearable or embedded computing and even brain-computer interfaces. Although practical use in gaming hardware remains distant, this technology represents a step toward more adaptable and durable electronics.

By adopting shared input frameworks and kernel improvements, the collective seeks to make Linux a more accessible platform for gaming. This initiative may encourage increased support from game developers and distributors by simplifying the Linux gaming landscape.
