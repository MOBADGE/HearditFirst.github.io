Computer History Museum Launches Digital Collection Portal

The Computer History Museum near Mountain View, California, has introduced a digital portal that allows users worldwide to explore its extensive collection of computing artifacts online. This new portal includes high-resolution images of retro hardware such as early Silicon Graphics three-button mice and the first-ever Lisp processor megachip. Visitors can browse through curated selections, stories from the collection, and a "discovery wall" featuring a broad variety of nostalgic computing items. For those interested in the history of technology and gaming hardware, this digital access offers a convenient way to engage with significant artifacts without needing to visit the museum in person.

**Why it matters:** The launch makes historical computing hardware more accessible to a global audience, preserving and sharing the origins of modern computing and gaming technology. It also provides a resource for enthusiasts and researchers who may not have the means to travel but want to study these early systems.
