Arc Raiders Flashpoint Update Faces Player Backlash

Embark Studios is set to release the Flashpoint update for Arc Raiders on March 31. The update promises new content including a new map condition, arc threat, player project, and updates to the Scrappy character. A recent teaser revealed that the Shredder enemy will be migrating from the Stella Montis map to other areas like Blue Gates. This move aims to escalate the PvE threat, continuing Embark’s previous design intentions.

However, many players have expressed disappointment, feeling that simply moving existing enemies to new maps does not constitute substantial new content. Some see the Shredders replacing the previous arc threat, the Arc, leading to frustration over the update’s direction. While Embark has a history of regular updates and balance changes, including weather effects and matchmaking improvements, a segment of the player base expected more significant additions. The community will be watching closely to see if the update includes a genuinely new arc or if they will need to wait for the more expansive Riven Tides update planned for next month.

Intel Launches Arc Pro B70 GPU Focused on AI, Not Gaming

Intel has officially launched the Arc Pro B70 graphics card, featuring the Big Battlemage GPU with 32 Xe cores (codenamed G31) and 32 GB of VRAM, priced at $949. Despite earlier speculation that this chip would form the basis of a mainstream gaming card comparable to Nvidia’s RTX 5070, Intel positions the Arc Pro B70 primarily for AI inference workloads rather than consumer gaming.

The card’s large VRAM capacity makes it attractive for running large AI models locally, offering more memory and a lower price point than comparable Nvidia professional GPUs like the RTX Pro 4000 Blackwell. The Arc Pro B70’s gaming performance is not the focus, and driver support for gaming is likely limited or nonexistent at this stage. Intel also released the Arc Pro B65, with the existing G21 GPU and 32 GB of VRAM, targeting a lower price bracket. The launch reflects Intel’s pivot to capitalize on AI market demands, while the long-anticipated gaming variant of Big Battlemage remains uncertain.

Asus Responds to FCC Router Import Ban, Issues Security Patch

Following the US FCC’s recent inclusion of foreign-made consumer routers on its Covered List—effectively restricting their sale without special approvals—Taiwanese router manufacturer Asus issued a statement affirming confidence in its product security and supply chain integrity. Asus stated that the ban does not affect existing devices, software updates, or customer support.

Coincidentally, Asus released a major security patch addressing CVE-2025-15101, a high-severity vulnerability that could allow authenticated users to execute system commands through unexpected methods. The timing highlights ongoing challenges in router security, though regular patching indicates Asus’s commitment to maintenance. The situation reflects broader concerns as other manufacturers have faced botnets and major vulnerabilities in recent years. Users are advised to keep their networking hardware updated to minimize risks.

Trump Appoints Tech CEOs to Science and Technology Advisory Committee

President Trump has appointed a new membership lineup for the President’s Council of Advisors on Science and Technology (PCAST), featuring a notable number of business leaders from the technology sector. Among the 13 members are Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang, AMD CEO Lisa Su, and Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg, alongside venture capitalists and industry executives.

The council, reestablished under Trump’s administration, is tasked with advising on scientific research, technology policy, innovation, national security, and economic topics. The emphasis on industry figures has drawn discussion due to a relative scarcity of academics and researchers, but supporters argue that CEOs bring practical insight into technology’s policy implications. The inclusion of leaders from major AI and hardware companies signals an intent to focus on emerging technologies like AI, quantum computing, and biotechnology.
