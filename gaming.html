Affordable RTS Bundle Featuring Homeworld and More

A new Humble Bundle sale offers the Homeworld Remastered Collection, Homeworld: Deserts of Kharak, and eight other real-time strategy (RTS) games for $8. Both main Homeworld titles have recently been discounted on Steam, but this bundle also includes additional classics like Railroad Tycoon 2 Platinum and Freedom Force vs. The Third Reich. The deal lasts until April 24. This is significant for strategy enthusiasts who want to explore a range of well-regarded RTS games—some decades old—without investing heavily, especially as RTS titles tend to age well due to gameplay focus over graphics.

Skyblivion Development Optimism Despite Risks of Delay

The lead developer of Skyblivion, a massive fan project remaking The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion in Skyrim's engine, shared cautious optimism about meeting the 2026 release target. Despite a recent call for additional modders to assist primarily with polish work, the project lead, Rebelzize, reassured fans that the team has made key progress, including completing and QA-checking all quest locations and overcoming major issues with the Imperial City area. While acknowledging the inherent risks of setbacks, he emphasized the effort’s dedication and urged patience from the community. This update matters because Skyblivion remains a highly anticipated mod that could extend the life of Skyrim’s engine and bring a classic RPG to modern standards.

Google AI Struggles with Basic Feed Customization

Google’s new AI-powered “Tailor your feed” feature in its Discover service is failing to effectively block unwanted content sources. Users attempting to exclude links from certain sites, like X.com (formerly Twitter), report that the AI offers only partial reduction of such content rather than complete removal. This issue highlights the challenges and limitations of AI systems in handling straightforward user commands and raises questions about algorithmic control over information filtering. It also reflects broader concerns about large tech companies prioritizing certain content providers despite user preferences, and the difficulties in balancing AI complexity with simple operational tasks.

The Gaming Historian Ends YouTube Career with Nintendo Court Documents

YouTuber and video game historian Norman Caruso, known as The Gaming Historian, announced his departure from video production after over 15 years. Citing creative burnout following his extensive documentary on The Oregon Trail, Caruso said he no longer feels motivated to create new videos. As a parting gift, he shared scanned court documents from a landmark 1982 Universal Studios vs. Nintendo trademark case involving Donkey Kong, now freely accessible on the Internet Archive. This close marks the end of a notable era for video game scholarship on YouTube, while providing valuable archival materials for researchers and fans interested in gaming history.

Samson Aims to Complement, Not Replace, Grand Theft Auto

Liquid Swords studio co-founder Christofer Sundberg discussed their upcoming open-world crime brawler, Samson, positioning it as a smaller-scale alternative to the Grand Theft Auto series rather than a direct competitor. While acknowledging similarities to GTA 4 in setting and gameplay, Sundberg emphasized that Samson aims to offer shorter, more focused experiences suitable for players who want a break from large, time-intensive open-world games. Samson will launch on April 8 after downsizing its scope following significant layoffs. This approach might fill a niche for players seeking crime-action games without the scope or intensity typical of AAA GTA titles.

House Flipper Free to Keep on Steam Until April 6

House Flipper, a home renovation simulation game, is currently free to claim and keep on Steam until April 6 during the ongoing House and Home Fest sale. The base game involves cleaning, remodeling, and selling homes, and is supported by numerous DLC packs expanding gameplay. A remastered version is planned for later in 2026 with discount incentives for current owners depending on their DLC purchases. This temporary free offering provides an accessible opportunity for players interested in simulation and renovation games to try House Flipper ahead of its remaster.
