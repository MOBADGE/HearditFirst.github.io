Death Stranding 2 Now Runs Better on Steam Deck After Patch

Death Stranding 2, which launched on PC last month, initially performed poorly on Valve’s Steam Deck handheld, with low and inconsistent frame rates that made gameplay difficult. However, a recent 1.2 patch from port developer Nixxes has significantly improved performance on the Steam Deck. Players can now expect smoother frame rates generally between 30 and 35 fps in open-world sections and up to 50 fps indoors, though battery life remains limited to around 90 minutes of play. This update shows that even demanding, big-budget games can be optimized to run respectably on handheld hardware when given proper support.

Playing Dungeons & Dragons in Zero Gravity Is Possible

Rabea Rogge, the first German woman in space, demonstrated that it is indeed possible to play Dungeons & Dragons—or at least roll dice—in zero gravity during her mission aboard the SpaceX Fram2 polar orbit flight. She showed a simple technique for rolling a D20 die by flicking it upward to spin and then catching it in her hand, enabling an easy way to check dice results without gravity. While complex dice rolls remain tricky, this confirms that tabletop roleplaying games can adapt to unconventional environments like space, adding an unusual footnote to RPG culture.

Mods Spark Interest in Survival Elements for Crimson Desert

Players of the action RPG Crimson Desert have begun experimenting with mods that introduce survival mechanics such as hunger and food poisoning, sparking hope for a potential full survival conversion. Some existing mods impose debuffs for skipping meals or eating spoiled food, enhancing the game's cooking and gathering systems. These changes highlight untapped survival gameplay possibilities within Crimson Desert’s open world. While official mod support from developer Pearl Abyss is currently lacking, the modding community’s interest suggests that fans want more depth in resource management and camping features.

King Legacy Codes for April Offer Free In-Game Rewards

Roblox game King Legacy is offering an updated list of redeemable codes that grant players free Gems, Gold Keys, experience bonuses, and other items. Among the active codes are “DinoxLive” and “Peodiz” for 100,000 in-game currency each, as well as several codes offering double experience boosts for limited time periods. Players need to access the in-game shop menu to enter codes. As some codes require membership in the Sea King Games group or game progression, players should check code requirements before redeeming. These codes are useful for progressing faster in King Legacy’s combat and exploration content.

Kerbal Space Program Sees Player Surge Amid Artemis 2 Moon Mission

Kerbal Space Program has reached its highest concurrent player count on Steam in over ten years, a spike attributed largely to NASA’s Artemis 2 mission orbiting the moon. The renewed interest has brought both returning and new players to the spaceflight simulator, with many inspired by the real-world mission to recreate lunar flights in-game. Although the numbers are not enough to place KSP among Steam's top 100 concurrent players, the increase is notable for an 11-year-old title. Kerbal Space Program 2 has also seen a smaller player count boost around the same time.

Bungie Addresses Increasing Cheating Issues in Marathon Ranked Mode

Bungie has acknowledged growing complaints over cheating in Marathon’s newly launched Ranked competitive mode, where abuses are undermining the player experience. The studio reaffirmed its zero-tolerance policy on cheating and said it is actively banning offenders while improving detection tools. These enhancements are already partly live, with more updates planned. Bungie is also working to make it easier for players to report cheating and toxic behavior in-game and via its website. The developer emphasized that anti-cheat measures will be an ongoing focus to protect competitive integrity.
