Esoteric Ebb Highlights the Power of Cleric Class in RPG Design

Christoffer Bodegård, developer of the 2026 RPG *Esoteric Ebb*, shared insights into why the game's protagonist is a Cleric—a class often undervalued by players seeking stealth or rogue archetypes. Bodegård explained that Clerics are “just overpowered” in D&D 5th Edition rules, combining melee, healing, and spells with versatile armor options. This jack-of-all-trades nature allowed the game to explore broad narrative themes such as religion and masculinity through the Cleric’s complex relationship with a god named Urth. Although players can embrace other classes during play, the Cleric remains central to *Esoteric Ebb*’s design and storytelling.

Windrose Surpasses One Million Sales Quickly, Faces Multiplayer Challenges

*Windrose*, a pirate-themed survival crafting game from Kraken Express, achieved over one million copies sold in just six days and peaked at 200,000 concurrent Steam players. The developers acknowledged the strong player support while also requesting community assistance to resolve ongoing issues with online cooperative multiplayer functionality. Despite these early technical challenges, the game’s naval combat has been well received as a straightforward but engaging experience reminiscent of *Assassin’s Creed: Black Flag*. The rapid sales highlight player interest in survival crafting games with nautical elements, although long-term success will depend on addressing networking stability.

Minecraft ‘Create Aeronautics’ Mod Allows Building Functional Vehicles

The new *Create Aeronautics* mod expands upon Minecraft’s contraption-building Create mod by adding tools for designing and piloting complex vehicles. Players can now build flying machines like propeller planes and hot air balloons, powered boats, offroad terrestrial vehicles with engines and suspensions, and large mechanical equipment such as combine harvesters. The mod integrates physics simulation to make vehicle components interact realistically and requires the Create mod plus the Sable physics mod. This addition significantly broadens Minecraft’s possibilities for player creativity in mechanical and vehicular design.

Dark Souls 2 Cut Sewer Map Unearthed, Offers Insight into Game Development

A YouTuber and fan of FromSoftware, Doneda, has restored and explored an enormous cut sewer map from *Dark Souls 2* that was removed during the game’s mid-development reboot. The partially textured, enemy-free level shows a large interconnected area with design elements reminiscent of The Gutter and the series’ typical challenging environments. Unique points include a tavern area—an uncommon “safe space” for the series—and a potential boss arena with unclear layout intentions. This discovery sheds light on uncertainties and transitions in FromSoftware’s design process during *Dark Souls 2*, a title often cited as divisive but influential in the series’ evolution.

Dave Oshry of New Blood Interactive Shares Personal Gaming Habits

Dave Oshry, CEO of New Blood Interactive, discussed his long-term engagement with PC games in an interview. His highest Steam playtime is in *Fallout 76* (over 488 hours), which he plays primarily as a single-player experience for exploration and cryptid hunting despite its multiplayer focus. Oshry also praised classic RPGs like *Baldur’s Gate* and *Torment*, and highlighted a modded “Definitive Edition” of *Vice City* as a game he frequently returns to. Notably, Oshry said he will never uninstall the original *Doom*, emphasizing its enduring appeal and active modding community. He also recommended the search utility “Everything” by Voidtools for efficient file management on PCs.

Airborne Empire Officially Launches with Major Content Update and Discount

The sequel to *Airborne Kingdom*, called *Airborne Empire*, has officially exited early access with a version 1.0 update. The update introduces a new snowy region named Kingsfell featuring additional stories, quests, and environmental challenges. Players will encounter new characters, lost civilizations, and a concluding battle against a pirate queen. In addition, new structures, upgrades, and customization options expand city-building possibilities. A significant feature is the jet engine, enabling cities to perform bursts of speed useful in combat or evasion. To celebrate launch, the game is available at 50% off until May 1, with the original *Airborne Kingdom* also discounted.
