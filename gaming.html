Bandcamp Bans AI-Generated Music Content

Music platform Bandcamp announced a strict new policy banning music and audio that are "generated wholly or in substantial part by AI." Additionally, Bandcamp prohibits AI tools used to impersonate other artists or styles, reinforcing their existing rules against impersonation and intellectual property infringement. This move follows a rise in low-quality AI-generated music acts, such as The Velvet Sundown, which amassed hundreds of thousands of monthly listeners despite being entirely AI-created.

The Bandcamp policy aims to maintain a human-centric music marketplace, addressing concerns about AI floodwaters potentially diluting the platform. This decision highlights ongoing tensions in content platforms grappling with AI's growing role.

Hytale Early Access Launches With Room to Grow

Hypixel Studios released Hytale into early access, bringing their Minecraft-like adventure sandbox to players after a turbulent development period that included cancellation by Riot Games last summer. The early access version showcases many core gameplay systems such as crafting, combat, exploration, and base-building, demonstrating significant work but also exhibiting many unfinished features and placeholders.

Players can explore a procedurally generated blocky world filled with varied biomes and creatures, craft tools and weapons, and engage with NPCs. While the game lacks a campaign and some survival mechanics found in similar titles, its early systems function well and set a foundation for future development. The launch not only marks a comeback for a once-canceled project but also secures funding to support further development over the coming years.

This restructuring follows years of heavy financial losses exceeding $50 billion without producing a mainstream hit product in VR or metaverse spaces.

The company is shifting its investment away from fully immersive VR headsets and the broader metaverse toward AI-powered wearables and phone features. While VR hardware development will continue at a reduced pace, Meta‚Äôs new focus aims to make the division more financially sustainable. Games Workshop, known for Warhammer, has stated it prohibits the use of generative AI in its products and design processes. CEO Kevin Rountree described the company's approach as "very cautious," emphasizing that AI-generated content is not allowed internally or in external competitions, such as the Golden Demon painting contests. This follows a prior incident where AI-generated art was used without authorization.

Despite some senior managers exploring AI technology, Games Workshop is focusing on expanding its human creative workforce rather than adopting AI tools.
