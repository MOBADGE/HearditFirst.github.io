Former Marathon Art Director Reflects on Pre-Release Challenges

Joseph Cross, who served as art director on Bungie's upcoming extraction shooter Marathon, shared his thoughts following his departure from the studio in December 2025. Marathon has faced several issues including delayed release dates, criticism following playtests, and controversy over stolen art assets. Cross expressed pride in the visual work his team accomplished, emphasizing that while he had no control over game design or mechanics, he stands by the art direction. He acknowledged the intense pressure of launching a major game amid mixed public reception and the uncertainty of the industry, especially in light of the rapid failure of another Sony-associated game, Concord. Stop Killing Games Petition Surpasses EU Signature Threshold

The Stop Killing Games initiative, a European Citizens' Initiative aimed at curbing the shuttering of online games and ensuring player access post-shutdown, has had over 1.29 million verified signatures out of 1.44 million collected. This surpasses the minimum one million signatures required for formal consideration by the European Commission. The petition demands that game publishers provide end-of-life support options such as fan servers, rather than abruptly cutting players off from purchased content. Although it has faced resistance from some in the industry, the petition's wide support reflects ongoing concern among gamers about game preservation and continued access. The initiative now moves to the next stage where EU politicians will review the proposal.

Doom Now Runs on PineBuds Pro Earbuds with Remote Play

Sydney developer Arin Sarkisian has achieved a novel technical feat by porting Doom to run on PineBuds Pro, wireless earbuds notable for their open-source firmware and surprisingly capable CPU. The game runs at about 18 frames per second after significant optimizations. Since the earbuds lack displays, Sarkisian streams the gameplay to viewers via a website where anyone can take turns controlling the earbuds remotely through their browsers. This project continues the long tradition of the iconic shooter being adapted to unconventional platforms, showcasing both programmer ingenuity and the enduring appeal of Doom.

GTA Roleplay Sees Growth in LSPD Police Academy Recruitment

The player-driven law enforcement role in GTA Online roleplay servers is undergoing a structured revival. Servers such as Eclipse RP have developed detailed recruitment and training pipelines for players wishing to join the fictional Los Santos Police Department (LSPD). Prospective officers complete applications, interviews, and a police academy covering realistic duties like radio protocol and report writing. After probationary patrols, players can gain full officer status. The increased complexity marks a shift toward more serious roleplay, blending game mechanics with real-world procedures. This trend highlights the evolving social aspects of GTA roleplay and the commitment of players seeking immersive experiences.

Banjo-Kazooie Receives Native PC Port by Modders

Modders led by Wiseguy have released Banjo: Recompiled, a native PC port of the original Banjo-Kazooie, previously only available on Nintendo 64 and Xbox consoles. The port supports Windows, Linux, and Mac, offering improvements such as ultrawide aspect ratio support, higher framerates, and customizable controls. Features also include quality-of-life enhancements like retaining collected notes between level exits, taken from the Rare Replay version. The port comes with a built-in mod menu compatible with several popular mods, including Zelda-themed and HD texture packs. Releases like this demonstrate the dedication of the community to preserving classic games while improving accessibility beyond official channels.
