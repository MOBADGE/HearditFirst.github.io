Crimson Desert: A Slow-Paced Open World Experience

Pearl Abyss’s newly launched open-world RPG *Crimson Desert* has drawn significant attention, reaching nearly 240,000 concurrent players on Steam at peak. A recent 75-hour playthrough suggests that this game thrives when approached slowly rather than rushed through. Players are encouraged to explore its vast environments, engage in side content, build community in their camp, and enjoy exploration over simply advancing the main story.

The game has a large number of systems and activities that benefit from a measured pace. Some special gameplay elements are locked until later progression, which discourages hastening to the endgame. Players who take time to gather crafting materials, discover hidden locations, gamble, and customize their camp report a more rewarding experience.

Launch Issues with Pre-Order and Deluxe Edition Bonuses

Despite a strong player base, *Crimson Desert*’s launch has not been without issues. Some players on Steam have reported a bug that prevents them from accessing pre-order and deluxe edition rewards through the in-game supply box. Pearl Abyss has acknowledged the problem and is actively working on a fix.

These bonuses include exclusive gear such as the Khaled Shield and special horse armor sets. While it’s unclear how much these bonuses impact early gameplay, their inaccessibility has been a notable inconvenience for those who purchased these editions. The developer’s prompt response indicates a focus on addressing post-launch problems during this early adoption period.

Fallout: New Vegas Available for 99 Cents During Steam Sale

Steam’s Spring sale features *Fallout: New Vegas* at the low price of 99 cents, making it an attractive purchase for RPG fans. Widely regarded as one of the strongest entries in the *Fallout* series—due largely to Obsidian Entertainment’s development rather than Bethesda—*New Vegas* offers a unique blend of impactful player choice and narrative humor.

The sale provides an affordable opportunity to experience the game with or without its DLC, which is bundled in the ultimate edition at a discounted price. The timing coincides with the release of the second season of the *Fallout* TV show, which revisits locations from *New Vegas*, potentially increasing interest among newcomers.

Insights from Ultima’s Robert Garriott on the PC Game Market from 1989

A recently digitized recording from the 1989 Computer Game Developers Conference reveals that Robert Garriott, a key figure behind the *Ultima* series, expressed concerns over the rapidly expanding PC game market even back then. He attributed declining sales per title partially to "product proliferation"—the market being flooded with too many games.

Garriott also noted consumer confusion due to mixed quality, underscoring the persistent industry challenge of balancing quantity with quality. His advice focused on creating unique, high-quality titles that could not be replicated on game consoles. The issues and advice from Garriott’s 37-year-old talk remain surprisingly relevant to today’s gaming landscape.

Hoarder: A Dark and Atmospheric Cleaning Simulator from a Darkwood Co-Creator

One of the co-creators of the psychological horror game *Darkwood* is developing *Hoarder*, a cleaning simulator with a dark, unsettling twist. Players declutter and excavate a dilapidated house filled with junk, gradually descending into an eerie basement that defies conventional geometry.

While beginning as a straightforward job simulator with clear objectives, the game progressively strips away guidance and reveals a more mysterious, possibly horror-tinged experience. *Hoarder* does not have a release date yet but is available for wishlisting on Steam. It may appeal to players interested in atmospheric games blending mundane tasks with unsettling narrative elements.

Retro Rewind: A 1990s Video Rental Store Simulator

*Retro Rewind* offers a job simulation focused on running a 1990s-style video rental store. The game quickly immerses players by allowing immediate stock ordering, customer interactions, and store management. It simulates daily retail tasks such as reshelving tapes, managing snack machines, and handling reservations.

The setting reconnects with a specific era before digital streaming, including the social awkwardness of renting adult videos. Players seeking a hands-on, busywork-oriented simulation may find this game engaging. The developer’s faithful recreation of this niche business environment offers a different take on the job sim genre.
