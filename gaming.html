**Industry Reflection at Game Developers Choice Awards**

At the recent Game Developers Choice Awards, composer Austin Wintory performed an unreleased piece from a canceled project he described as his "dream job of all time." The game was to be developed by Humanoid Origin, a studio founded by former BioWare boss Casey Hudson, which closed in 2024 without releasing a title. Wintory’s performance served as both a tribute and a subtle commentary on the realities facing game developers in today's environment. The ceremony also included an In Memoriam segment honoring notable industry figures such as Rebecca Heineman, Julian Lefay, and Vince Zampella, reminding attendees of the rich history and personal losses within the game development community.

---

**ZA/UM’s Zero Parades Shows Moments of Creativity**

The upcoming RPG Zero Parades by ZA/UM, successor to the acclaimed Disco Elysium, presented a demo at this week’s Game Developers Conference. The game aims to differentiate itself tonally from its predecessor while maintaining a similar verbose writing style. Highlights include comedic and impactful interactions with game mechanics such as an intercom system and lock combinations. These moments demonstrate an effort to blend humor and player choice with spy-thriller gameplay. While the overall tone remains uncertain, these elements suggest ZA/UM is exploring new directions while retaining some narrative depth from their previous work.

---

**Klang Games’ Seed MMO Progresses with Persistent Character Simulation**

Klang Games, founded by former EVE Online developers, showcased updates on Seed, an MMO featuring persistent characters that continue to act in the world even when players are offline. The game merges detailed simulation aspects similar to RimWorld and The Sims with player-run societies managing resources, laws, and economies. A recent closed beta revealed dynamic societal politics, including a bread market dispute leading to governmental change. Seed also plans a mobile app to monitor and manage characters remotely. The title remains in closed beta with a wider launch date yet to be announced, appealing primarily to players interested in complex social and economic simulation.

---

**Shadowstone Launches Public Playtest Ahead of Early Access Release**

Secret Door and Dreamhaven have begun a public playtest for Shadowstone, a tactics roguelike set in the same universe as Sunderfolk. The playtest allows up to four players to experience randomized runs focusing on skill cards, equipment, and powerups, both solo and cooperatively. Progress will not carry over to early access, which is expected sometime in 2026, although no specific date has been announced. Interested players are encouraged to sign up on Steam for a chance to participate, providing the developers with valuable feedback before the official release.

---

**Rob Pardo’s GDC Keynote Highlights Value of Stable Development Teams**

At the 2026 Game Developers Conference, industry veteran Rob Pardo addressed the ongoing trend of layoffs within game studios, urging executives to reconsider such moves. Drawing from his extensive career at Blizzard and recent work at Bonfire, Pardo emphasized that maintaining experienced development teams is crucial for replicating past successes. He argued that the unpredictable nature of game development means that the team behind a successful title is a more valuable asset than the game itself and advised business leaders to support and nurture these teams rather than dismantle them after a hit.
