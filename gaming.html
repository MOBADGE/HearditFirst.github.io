Monster Hunter Stories 3: How to Defeat Invasive Monsters

Monster Hunter Stories 3 features a key gameplay element involving "Invasive Monsters," which are enhanced versions of common monsters that inhabit the dens of rare endangered species. These foes possess unique abilities and require specific strategies to repel rather than directly slay. For example, the Invasive Yian Garuga can be driven off by breaking its legs, while the Invasive Plesioth requires using fire weapons to break its head and prevent a devastating attack. Other monsters, such as the Invasive Shogun Ceanataur, demand defensive boosts to survive lethal strikes, and the Invasive Nerscylla can only be damaged with timed gunlance attacks.

This mechanic encourages players to engage with the world thoughtfully, focusing on tactical approaches tailored to each monster’s new abilities. Successfully repelling Invasive Monsters allows players to secure eggs of endangered species, which are vital for progressing and expanding their roster of monsties.

Locations of Endangered Species in Monster Hunter Stories 3

Alongside tips for the Invasive Monsters, knowledge of where to find endangered species is crucial. The game spreads nine such species across three main regions: Azuria, Canalta Timberland, and Serathis. Each endangered species’ den is protected by an Invasive Monster. For instance, the Rathian is found at the eastern edge of Azuria, guarded by the Invasive Yian Garuga, while the Barioth in Serathis is protected by an Invasive Khezu that uses paralysis attacks.

Players must traverse diverse terrains — from waterfalls and caves to mountaintops reached by gliding with flying monsties — reinforcing exploration as a core element of gameplay. Locating these species and defeating their invasive guardians is essential for collecting rare eggs, which enrich the gameplay with unique creatures and abilities.

Darktide Introduces New Outdoor Extraction-Style Mode

The sci-fi shooter Darktide is adding a new mode called "expeditions" in its upcoming Beyond the Hive update, releasing March 17. Unlike the game’s usual claustrophobic environments, this mode takes players to open outdoor areas on the planet Atoma Prime. The gameplay focuses on scavenging valuable Tech-Remnants while managing threats, including roaming enemies and environmental hazards like radiation.

Players have limited time before radiation poisoning becomes lethal, introducing a risk-versus-reward dynamic: if time expires and they die, all gathered loot is lost. The mode incorporates considerable randomization in map layouts and enemy spawns, promoting varied tactical playstyles. This shift diversifies the game’s experience by expanding its settings and pacing.

The Simpsons’ Executive Producer Comments on Hit & Run Revival Possibility

Matt Selman, executive producer of The Simpsons, discussed the possibility of reviving the 2003 game The Simpsons: Hit & Run in a recent interview. Although no official plans are underway, Selman’s response of “never say never” signals some openness to meeting fan demand for a remaster or sequel. He praised the original game’s storytelling quality, considering it a standout among Simpsons-related titles.

While this doesn't confirm development, it offers some hope to fans hoping for easier access or new content related to this cult classic. Interest remains high, as the game is often cited as one of the best licensed games based on a TV show.

Samson’s Latest Trailer Leaves Some Doubts Ahead of April Release

Samson, an upcoming open-city game by the studio Liquid Swords led by Just Cause creator Cristofer Sundberg, aims for a focused and action-packed urban crime experience. Set to release April 8, its recent gameplay trailer showcased car chases and hand-to-hand combat but raised concerns about the quality of its melee mechanics and world liveliness.

While the driving mechanics appear solid and the game's aesthetic stands out with a gritty industrial city flair, the combat sequences seem repetitive and mechanically shallow. The sparse environment population also calls into question how vibrant the game world will feel. The developer is targeting a tighter gameplay loop with daily objectives and resource management, distinguishing it from larger open-world games, but the trailer’s execution has left some potential players unconvinced.
