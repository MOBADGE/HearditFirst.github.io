Shadowstone Begins Public Playtest Ahead of 2026 Early Access Launch Secret Door and Dreamhaven have launched a surprise public playtest for their tactics roguelike, Shadowstone, during the Future Games Show Spring Showcase. Shadowstone is a run-based spinoff set in the world of Sunderfolk but designed for mouse-and-keyboard play rather than smartphone controls. The playtest, which supports solo or up to four-player multiplayer, allows participants to try the game ahead of its early access release planned later in 2026. Progress made in the playtest won’t carry over to the full game, and there is no non-disclosure agreement, encouraging players to stream and discuss their experience. Interested players can sign up on Steam for a chance to participate. The staggered rollout reflects the developers’ intent to gather feedback and build interest ahead of the official early access launch.

Rob Pardo Urges Industry Leaders to Rethink Layoffs at GDC Keynote Veteran game designer Rob Pardo, speaking at this year’s Game Developers Conference, highlighted the importance of retaining experienced development teams following successful game releases. Reflecting on his career, including work on titles like Warcraft 3 and founding Bonfire Studios, Pardo cautioned executives not to lay off key staff after a hit game’s launch. He stressed that the value of a well-functioning developer team often exceeds that of the game product itself, due to the team's ability to manage the inherent unpredictability of game development. Pardo’s message comes amid ongoing industry practices of laying off developers even after major successes, pointing to a disconnect between business decisions and sustaining long-term creative talent.

Path of Exile’s Mirage League Tweaks Economy and Gameplay Grinding Gear Games has introduced the Mirage league in Path of Exile’s 3.28 update, marking a significant adjustment to the game’s currency drop rates for the first time in years. Currency items now make up a larger portion of enemy drops, aiming to balance the economy and reduce reliance on trading or external farming. Mirage introduces a new mechanic where players can replay small parts of zones for additional rewards and “wishes,” which grant beneficial effects or items. The league also features an overhaul of the atlas system, shifting from specific map drops to tiered map tokens usable more flexibly across regions. These changes provide a more streamlined and intentional endgame progression. Early impressions suggest Mirage could be one of the more impactful league updates in recent years.

Dave the Diver Announces June DLC “In the Jungle” with Expanded Gameplay Mintrocket revealed that Dave the Diver’s next expansion, In the Jungle, will launch on June 18. The DLC takes the fishing RPG’s setting to a new village called Utara beside a freshwater lake, introducing a more detailed day-night cycle and real-time task management reminiscent of Stardew Valley. Alongside core diving and fishing activities, players will engage in relationship-building with villagers, new minigames, and exploration facilitated by a multi-functional weapon called the Jungle Gun. The DLC replaces the more linear restaurant environment from the original game with an open isometric space serving as a social hub. Originally planned for 2025, the expansion was delayed but promises about 10 hours of new content.

Marathon’s Latest Update May Have Increased PvP Intensity Through Sound Changes After a recent patch in the competitive FPS Marathon, players have reported a notable increase in the frequency of fights and third-party engagements. The update extended the audible range of gunfire and explosions, making combat actions more detectable across the game’s relatively small maps. This change has led to complaints that matches now feel like constant ambushes, particularly in solo queue where stealth and tactical positioning had previously been more viable. Some community members have called for the sound adjustments to be reversed or tweaked to reduce the unintended increase in encounter density. Developers have not provided detailed explanations for the sound range change, but the feedback suggests it may have altered gameplay dynamics more than intended.
