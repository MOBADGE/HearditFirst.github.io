Stormgate Ends Online Multiplayer Support

Stormgate, a real-time strategy game often compared to StarCraft, will be discontinuing its online multiplayer support at the end of April. This decision follows Hathora, the company providing Stormgate's server infrastructure, being acquired by an AI firm that has announced it will shut down its gaming services by May 5. Although Frost Giant Studios, the game's developer, plans to release a patch enabling offline play, online modes will no longer be available unless a new server partner is found.

This change is significant because Stormgate's online player base had dwindled over recent months, with concurrent player counts rarely surpassing 30. Despite strong initial funding and community interest, the game struggled to sustain a large audience post-release. Offline play will preserve the campaign and solo modes, but the multiplayer aspect, central to the game's appeal, is effectively on hold pending future developments.

Intel May Drop Traditional E-Cores in Upcoming Low-Power Laptop CPUs

Leaked specifications suggest Intel’s upcoming Core 300 mobile processors, targeting thin and affordable laptops, will omit the usual efficiency-optimized E-cores. Instead, these chips will feature a hybrid architecture with two high-performance P-cores and four low-power LPE cores, continuing to use Darkmont low-power cores introduced in Panther Lake processors.

All models in this series share a 35W turbo thermal design power (TDP), aiming at efficiency over gaming performance. Notably, these chips include up to two Intel Xe3 graphics cores, which is less than some competing mobile and handheld gaming processors. The shift away from standard E-cores could streamline the design for this tier but likely means these upcoming CPUs will not target higher-end gaming laptops.

Introducing Billy, the Unluckiest Pickpocket in Crimson Desert

In the open-world RPG Crimson Desert, a recurring character named Billy the Pickpocket has garnered attention for repeatedly getting caught and arrested. Billy roams the town of Hernand attempting to steal from players, but he is easily caught and can become a bounty target early in the game.

This informal “first introduction” to the bounty hunting system is designed to encourage players to engage with the feature naturally. Despite his thievery, Billy’s thefts are minimal, often seen as a minor nuisance rather than a major threat. Players are encouraged to consider letting him off as his small crimes may stem from hardship rather than malice. Billy’s presence adds charm and depth to the game world’s social dynamics.

How to Obtain the Canto Blueprint in Arc Raiders

Arc Raiders players looking to craft the Canto submachine gun blueprint will need to focus on looting First Wave Raider Caches during matches with the Hurricane map condition active. These “hurricane caches” are small tin containers buried around the map’s edges, identifiable by a faint beeping sound, though this may be hard to hear due to high winds.

The Canto blueprint is a rare drop and tends to be harder to acquire than some legendary blueprints like the Dolabra. Crafting the weapon requires Gunsmith level 3 and a mix of mechanical components, magnets, and gun parts. Despite its small magazine and noticeable recoil, the Canto offers competitive close-range damage, making it a desirable option for players who can secure the blueprint.

Oracle Lays Off Around 10,000 Employees Amid AI Industry Shifts

Oracle has reportedly laid off roughly 10,000 staff members, including senior engineers and technical specialists, marking a notable reduction in workforce. An insider clarified that the layoffs were not performance-related. The technology giant has been heavily investing in AI projects, including a substantial $300 billion deal with OpenAI to supply datacenter infrastructure.

This personnel reduction comes amid broader speculation about challenges in the AI sector’s growth trajectory. While Oracle continues to invest in hardware and AI chip development, the layoffs indicate operational adjustments to align with shifting market conditions. The event reflects the ongoing volatility in tech industries centered around AI innovation.
