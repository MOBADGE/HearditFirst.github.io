**Industry Analyst Highlights Decline in Gaming Attention Across Key Markets**

Analyst Matthew Ball released a report indicating that gaming faces challenges retaining player attention in major markets including the US, UK, Japan, and others. Since the COVID-19 pandemic, the proportion of people identifying as regular gamers has decreased in several countries, with user engagement flatlining or declining in terms of consumer spending on PC and console games. Ball attributes this trend to the rise of alternate digital entertainments such as short-form video, AI applications, and subscription services competing for users' time. The report suggests a compounding issue for the games industry, where a shrinking player base may increase monetization pressures on remaining players, potentially affecting overall game quality and market growth.
