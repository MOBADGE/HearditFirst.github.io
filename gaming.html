**Bravely Default Flying Fairy HD Remaster Arrives on Steam Without Announcement**

Square Enix released a high-definition remaster of the 2012 RPG *Bravely Default Flying Fairy* on Steam today without prior notice. The remastered version includes updated graphics, a redesigned user interface, an option to fast-forward cutscenes, and maintains the original's turn-based "Brave and Default" battle system. It also adds two new minigame types and is verified for the Steam Deck handheld. The game is discounted by 20% until March 26, priced at $32/£25.59/€32. This release expands the game's availability beyond Nintendo platforms, potentially reaching a wider PC audience and removing a previously noted exclusive game advantage for the upcoming Nintendo Switch 2.

**Meaning Machine Reports High Player Enjoyment of AI-Powered NPCs**

Developer Meaning Machine shared results from a study involving 68 players who tested their game *Dead Meat*, which features generative AI-powered NPCs delivering reactive dialogue based on real-time gameplay. According to the study conducted with the University of Bristol, 95% of participants found playing with AI NPCs enjoyable, and 97% found it rewarding. Additionally, 75% felt the game allowed meaningful choices and self-expression. While the full research paper is pending publication later this year, these findings contribute to ongoing discussions about AI integration in games and player reception, indicating that AI-driven content can enhance interactivity without diminishing creativity when applied thoughtfully.

**Developer of Indie Deckbuilder *All Will Rise* Joins No Games For Genocide Boycott**

Speculative Agency, developer of the political deckbuilding RPG *All Will Rise*, announced its support for the No Games For Genocide campaign, which protests Microsoft's association with the Israeli Defense Force amid allegations of human rights violations. The studio plans to return funding it previously received from Microsoft’s developer acceleration program to align with the boycott. This decision comes despite risks including losing Xbox platform access and facing funding challenges. The developer encourages others in the industry to consider joining the boycott, emphasizing the importance of conscience over commercial success. The game’s release platform strategy may change, and the studio is seeking alternate funding sources, including a successful Kickstarter campaign.

**World of Warcraft’s Questing System Shaped by Non-MMO Playtest Feedback**

Jeff Kaplan, former WoW quest designer, revealed in an interview that the game’s questing system was significantly redesigned after early playtests with Blizzard staff unfamiliar with MMORPGs. Initially modeled closely on EverQuest’s more grind-focused questing, testers quickly ran out of quests and expected a continuous flow. This feedback caused a major shift toward the quest-driven leveling progression that guided players step-by-step through the experience, contributing to WoW’s broad accessibility and success. The approach—“design along the path of least resistance”—became a core principle for Blizzard’s game design philosophy.

**Subnautica 2 Reveals New Base Building System with Greater Flexibility**

Developer Unknown Worlds shared a developer vlog showcasing the “brand new” base building system for *Subnautica 2*. Moving away from fixed building pieces used in previous entries, the new system employs procedural generation allowing players to create more sculptural and customized structures. Features include window placement in varied shapes, color customization, lighting options, and internal cosmetic choices. Multiplayer functionality will be optional for players wishing to share their bases. While a release date for early access was not provided, the update indicates a strengthened focus on player creativity and base customization for the sequel.

**PNY GeForce RTX 5070 Ti 16 GB OC Graphics Card Review**

The PNY RTX 5070 Ti OC is evaluated as a solid mid-to-high tier option within Nvidia’s 50 series, balancing performance with a price around $1,000/£840. It features 8,960 CUDA cores, 16 GB of GDDR7 memory running at 28 Gbps, and a triple-fan triple-slot cooler design. Benchmarks show performance competitive with comparable RTX 5070 Ti cards and close to AMD’s RX 9070 XT, which often offers better value. The PNY card runs at higher average clock speeds but also exhibits higher power draw and peak temperatures, reaching approximately 71°C under load and 350 watts peak power consumption. Buyers should weigh cost against performance, especially considering the cheaper RX 9070 XT alternative.
