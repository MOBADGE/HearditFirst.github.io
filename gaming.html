**Resident Evil Requiem Reveals Leon Kennedy Is Married**

In the latest "Resident Evil Requiem" cutscenes and concept art, players noticed Leon Kennedy sporting a wedding ring, revealing the long-speculated character development that Leon is married. This detail has sparked wide fan debate regarding the identity of his spouse, with theories mostly clustering around two main candidates: Claire Redfield and Ada Wong. Fans supporting Claire point to her compatible personality and recent story interactions hinting at closer ties, while Ada supporters highlight the established dynamic and screen time shared with Leon. The specific spouse remains unconfirmed by Capcom, with some speculation fueled by a theory that players will see a matching ring on Chris Redfield during upcoming DLC. This revelation adds a new layer to Leon's character narrative and could influence future storylines in the Resident Evil series.

**Memory Prices Poised for Further Increases, Impacting PC Market**

South Korean sources report that major DRAM manufacturers Samsung and SK Hynix have notified customers of another planned DRAM price hike for the second quarter of 2026. The move comes amid persistent supply constraints driven by high AI-related demand, limiting production capacity to approximately 60%. Industry insiders suggest that smaller clients are expected to accept significant price increases to secure supply, with some potentially facing price rises exceeding double their previous contracts. These increases will likely push PC component and device prices higher, reinforcing recent Gartner forecasts that anticipate a 130% surge in DRAM and SSD prices by year’s end. The increased costs are projected to raise PC prices by 17% and contribute to a 10.4% decline in worldwide PC shipments in 2026, indicating continued strain on budget-friendly PC options.

**Linux Gaming: ScopeBuddy Tool Enhances HDR Support**

A notable development for Linux gamers has emerged with the adoption of ScopeBuddy, a tool designed to simplify the use of the Gamescope micro-compositor. Previously, running games in HDR on Linux systems, such as with openSUSE Tumbleweed, posed challenges, with users facing washed-out visuals or incompatibility issues. ScopeBuddy addresses these by automatically detecting monitor specifications including resolution, HDR status, and VRR capability, streamlining game launch configurations with a straightforward command. This advancement has lowered barriers for Linux users aiming to replace Windows for gaming, marking progress in Linux's compatibility and user experience despite the tool itself having been available for some time. It represents an incremental step toward broader HDR readiness on Linux platforms.

**UK Collector’s Sega Dev Kits Seized Amid Legal Dispute**

Darius Khan, a UK-based game collector, was raided by police after acquiring approximately £10,000 worth of hardware, including Sega development kits, during a disposal of equipment from Sega’s UK office. The equipment, initially intended for e-waste processing, was purchased from a subcontractor without Sega’s direct involvement. Following online listings of some items, law enforcement seized the dev kits and prototype cartridges under suspicions of theft. Despite no formal charges at this time, the case highlights the complexities surrounding the preservation of video game history, corporate intellectual property rights, and e-waste disposal practices. The situation underscores challenges collectors face in legally acquiring and safeguarding retro and development hardware.

**Microsoft’s Copilot Discord Server Restricts Use of “Microslop” Nickname**

Microsoft has implemented a ban on the term "Microslop"—a pejorative nickname for its Copilot AI—within its official Copilot Discord server. The restriction extends to variations such as "Microsl0p," leading the company to limit posting and access permissions for users circumventing the filter. After a period of moderated access, posting capabilities appear restored, with community members continuing to reference the term or related alternatives informally. This incident reflects ongoing tensions between corporate brand management and community discourse in online platforms, illustrating the difficulties companies face in controlling derogatory or mocking language among users.

**Battlefield 6’s Javelin Anti-Cheat System Clarified**

Battlefield Studios has provided further insight into the operation of Battlefield 6’s kernel-level anti-cheat system, Javelin. The studio explains that the system assesses cheating through confirmed bans alongside suspicions based on multiple detection signals, resulting in a metric known as match infection rate (MIR). MIR includes both detected and suspected cheating cases to estimate the proportion of affected matches. This methodology accounts for increases in the MIR as detection improves, such as during January 2026. Javelin
