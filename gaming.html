**MSI Updates Raider and Crosshair Gaming Laptops to Reduce Fan Noise and Improve Portability** MSI has announced refreshed versions of its Raider and Crosshair gaming laptops with a focus on reducing fan noise, a common issue with portable gaming PCs. The company redesigned the Raider's motherboard to a more compact layout, allowing for larger fans that operate at lower speeds to maintain airflow while keeping noise levels under 50 dBA during gaming. Additionally, MSI optimized the fan curve to avoid sudden fan speed spikes that can be distracting. This redesign also enables a slimmer chassis depth for easier transport, addressing previous complaints about the bulkiness of MSI's gaming laptops. If MSI maintains performance levels alongside these improvements, the new Raider and Crosshair models could be more practical choices in the competitive gaming laptop market.

**New York Mayor Zohran Mamdani Uses Mario Kart Metaphor to Explain Philanthropy and Corporate Greed** At a recent press conference focused on the Child Care Action Fund—a campaign aiming to provide universal child care in New York City—Mayor Zohran Mamdani used an extended Mario Kart analogy to describe government and philanthropy’s roles. He likened the government to Yoshi, philanthropy to the golden mushroom that boosts a racer’s speed, and corporate greed to Bowser, the main antagonist. Mamdani’s use of a gaming metaphor highlights how charitable efforts complement governmental support to overcome systemic challenges like corporate greed. This public engagement also connects to ongoing efforts to encourage New York’s wealthy residents to contribute more, amid tax hikes on high-value properties that have sparked complaints.

**Xenosaga Series Makes PC Debut With Port of 2004 Mobile-Only Game** The Xenosaga JRPG series is appearing on PC for the first time, but not in the way fans might expect. Instead of porting the well-known PlayStation 2 trilogy, publisher G-Mode is releasing Xenosaga: Pied Piper, a little-known 2004 mobile game originally exclusive to Vodafone phones in Japan. This port keeps the game’s original design without modern quality-of-life improvements and does not include an English translation. While it preserves a piece of Xenosaga history and provides PC players access to a rare title, the mobile game’s dated interface and language barrier may limit its appeal. Given that Nintendo now owns Monolith Soft, the future of the classic Xenosaga trilogy on PC remains uncertain.

**Breaking Blue and Red Crystal Barriers in Pragmata: What Players Need to Know** In Capcom’s sci-fi shooter Pragmata, players encounter impassable blue and red crystal barriers blocking progression and side areas. Blue crystals, called filament masses, require unlocking the Lim Eraser power, which is obtained late in Sector 2: Mass Production Array after completing specific story objectives. Red crystals, known as dead filament, appear later and need a different ability called Cleanse, unlocked in Sector 5: Experimental Pragmatics. These mechanics necessitate backtracking to previously inaccessible areas to collect additional rewards and upgrades. Understanding when and how to break these barriers is key for thorough exploration and progression.

**Recommended Weapons and Loadout Strategy for Pragmata** Pragmata’s weapon system classifies guns into primary (infinite ammo with recharge), attack, tactical, and defensive units, with the latter three being consumable and disappearing once out of ammo. Players start with the basic Grip pistol but gain access to a variety of weapons throughout the game. Recommended attack weapons include the Shockwave Gun and Charge Piercer for their damage and utility. Tactical weapons like Sticky Bombs and Stasis Net provide crowd control, useful for managing enemy swarms. The Decoy Generator stands out as a defensive option, drawing enemy attention away from the player. Players can upgrade weapons, but must manage resources carefully due to the cost and limited ammo. Optimizing loadouts with these choices can improve survivability and combat effectiveness.
