Europa Universalis 5 Campaign Reveals Series Challenges

A longtime Europa Universalis 4 (EU4) player shared mixed feelings after completing a 93-hour campaign in the recently released Europa Universalis 5 (EU5). While EU5 offers a more complex and intricate internal management system compared to EU4’s streamlined "map painting," it lacks the distinctive national flavors that made EU4 engaging. The reviewer noted that factions in EU5 feel too similar, leading to repetitive gameplay, and frequent bugs further disrupted the experience. Unlike EU4’s expansive imperial conquests, EU5 emphasizes micromanaging economy and rebellions over territorial domination. The lack of mission trees and national ideas also contributed to a sense of depth without distinct identity. For veterans, these design choices detract from the sense of purpose that made EU4 compelling.

Castlevania: Belmont's Curse Embraces Exploration without "Metroidvania" Label

Konami and developer Evil Empire have been careful to avoid calling the upcoming Castlevania: Belmont’s Curse a "metroidvania," even as it clearly draws inspiration from the genre. In a behind-the-scenes reveal, the developers described the game as a "2D action adventure world" with intricate level design focused on exploration and verticality, directly referencing Castlevania: Symphony of the Night as a key influence. The game features RPG-lite elements such as character leveling but will not include roguelike mechanics typical of Evil Empire’s previous work. This measured positioning aims to appeal to fans of the classic style while navigating trademark sensitivities around the metroidvania term. User-generated tags on Steam already classify it alongside similar titles.

Graveyard Keeper 2 Announced, Original Now Free on Steam

Developer Lazy Bear Games announced Graveyard Keeper 2, which will launch later this year. The sequel expands on the original’s dark farming sim formula by adding automation, base building, and a tower defense element as players raise an undead army to defend their town from zombies. The original Graveyard Keeper game has gained new attention due to its free availability on Steam until April 13. Players unfamiliar with the series can now try the original, which features resource crafting from grim materials like human body parts. The sequel’s trailer showcases familiar survival and management gameplay alongside new automated production and combat systems.

Last Flag: Imagine Dragons Brothers Release Capture-The-Flag Shooter

Dan and Mac Reynolds, known for their work with Imagine Dragons, are releasing their first game, Last Flag, a multiplayer capture-the-flag shooter. The brothers emphasized focus on pure CTF gameplay with a unique mechanic allowing players to hide their own team’s flag, adding a strategic element to matches. The game aims to deliver a stand-alone, balanced experience built from the ground up around flag capturing and hiding. A 22-minute mini-documentary explores the game’s development, soundtrack creation, and the brothers’ long-standing ambition to make games. Last Flag launches April 14 on Steam and Epic Games Store, with a discount period and plans for post-release updates.

Road to Vostok Early Access Secures Long-Term Funding Quickly

Road to Vostok, a hardcore survival shooter developed by solo Finnish developer Antti Leinonen, has seen strong commercial success immediately after its early access launch on Steam. According to Leinonen, day-one sales have secured enough funds to support the game’s development for years to come. The game ranks high on Steam’s top sellers list in several regions and holds a Very Positive player review rating with appreciation for its realistic and deliberate combat mechanics. Planned updates include AI improvements, quests, and new maps, with a tentative full release timeline of two to four years. The success provides financial stability for ongoing development.

Gunzilla CEO Responds to Allegations of Delayed Payments to Staff

Gunzilla Games’ CEO Vlad Korolev has responded to public allegations that the company has failed to pay some employees and contractors for months. Korolev denied delaying payments to full-time official employees beyond one week but acknowledged that some contractors may have experienced delayed or scheduled payments due to cash flow management. Several former employees publicly shared their experiences of nonpayment and contract disputes, contradicting Korolev’s statements. The studio, known for NFT battle royale Off the Grid, is currently facing criticism over its workplace culture and financial practices. Korolev framed many complaints as coming from detractors aiming to damage the company’s reputation, but further transparency and resolution remain to be seen.
