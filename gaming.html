Sony Inzone H9 II Gaming Headset Reviewed: Comfortable but Pricey

Sony's new Inzone H9 II headset, priced at $350, offers a remarkably lightweight design at just 260 grams without the microphone, making extended wear comfortable. The headset features 30 mm carbon composite drivers, active noise cancellation (ANC), and 30 hours of battery life with ANC off. The noise cancelling is notably effective, creating a quiet personal space ideal for gaming or work. Sound quality emphasizes clarity with a warm low-mid range, and an EQ allows user customization. The removable cardioid condenser microphone delivers clear, acceptable vocal capture, suitable for streaming or chatting.

However, the headset's build feels less premium compared to similarly priced audiophile options like the Audeze Maxwell. The lightweight and minimalist construction has trade-offs in perceived durability and the plushness of materials. Additionally, the battery life is shorter than some competitors. At full price, the Inzone H9 II is a good but not exceptional choice, with better audio-focused headsets available for less. Discounts could make it a more attractive option for those prioritizing comfort and noise cancellation.

Asus and MSI Clarify RTX 5070 Ti Graphics Card Not End-of-Life

Following reports suggesting Asus had listed its Nvidia GeForce RTX 5070 Ti GPU as end-of-life (EoL) due to supply issues, both Asus and MSI have issued statements denying these claims. Asus clarified that the RTX 5070 Ti and RTX 5060 Ti 16 GB models have not been discontinued or marked as EoL, explaining current availability problems stem from memory shortages impacting production rather than an intentional product phase-out. MSI echoed this, confirming no plans to stop supporting these GPUs.

While supply constraints and shifts in manufacturing priorities remain challenges, the cards are still officially supported. Gamers seeking these models should expect ongoing stock fluctuations but no immediate discontinuation from major partners.
