## Counter-Strike Player Receives 10-Year Ban for Onstage Assault

At the CAGGTUS Leipzig tournament, German Counter-Strike 2 player MAUschine was banned for 10 years after physically striking his opponent, Fabian "Spidergum" Salomon, during the awards ceremony. Video footage shows MAUschine punching Spidergum, whose glasses flew off, before casually continuing to greet other players. The incident reportedly stemmed from Spidergum mockingly repeating MAUschine’s catchphrase after a match. Tournament organizers, Dach CS Masters, condemned the violence and reported the case to the Esports Integrity Commission. This ban highlights the zero-tolerance stance esports organizations maintain regarding physical altercations, emphasizing sportsmanship and player safety in competitive gaming.

## RuneScape Faces Emergency Rollback Due to Rune Duplication Bug

Jagex implemented an emergency rollback for RuneScape following a recent update that inadvertently led to players possessing excessive amounts of runes. The update increased rune pouch capacity drastically, but a bug caused players to duplicate billions, sometimes over a trillion, runes per person. This sudden inflation threatened to destabilize the in-game economy. Jagex decided to revert all progression to pre-update status to contain the issue quickly and preserve the game’s economic integrity. While players expressed frustration over resetting progress, the community generally agreed the rollback was necessary to prevent long-term damage.

## Mass Effect TV Series Writer Denies Being Asked to Rewrite for Non-Gamers

Daniel Casey, writer and executive producer for the upcoming Mass Effect TV series from Amazon MGM Studios, publicly denied reports that he was instructed to rewrite the script to appeal more to non-gamers. Casey expressed surprise at the claim and said no such directive had been given to him. The series, which is still early in production stages, is notable for not adapting the original game trilogy’s story. This clarification sheds light on the ongoing debate about how adaptations balance accessibility for new audiences with faithfulness to existing fans.

## World of Tanks: Heat Offers a Faster-Paced Take on Tank Combat in Closed Beta

Wargaming’s new spinoff, World of Tanks: Heat, is currently in closed beta and presents a quicker, more action-oriented hero shooter style compared to the traditional World of Tanks gameplay. Matches are small-scale 5v5 with shorter durations and abilities similar to hero shooters, combined with detailed tank damage modeling. The game emphasizes positioning and timing rather than stealth or long-range artillery play. While still in beta, this approach aims to provide a more accessible and dynamic experience. The beta remains open for a few more days for interested players to try.

## Armored Core 6’s Mech Building Community Showcases Creative Builds

Despite no news of an official expansion or sequel to Armored Core 6, the game’s fan community remains active, particularly around mech customization. YouTuber Thryfe recently gained attention with a detailed “Sailor Moon” inspired mech build that creatively repurposes parts and color schemes from the game’s components. The build demonstrates both aesthetic ambition and functional suitability for PvP. The ongoing enthusiasm for mech building reflects Armored Core 6’s strength in customization and its lasting appeal among players who enjoy intricate vehicle design.

## Crusader Kings 3’s Upcoming Expansions to Introduce Playable Popes and Merchant Republics

Paradox Interactive announced two major paid expansions for Crusader Kings 3 arriving in the latter half of 2026 as part of Chapter Five. The "By God Alone" expansion will overhaul religious gameplay with new systems focused on Christian institutions, rites, and playable theocracies, including the ability to play as the Pope. A second expansion, "Silk & Silver," will introduce merchant republics and trading company mechanics, allowing players to establish trade routes and compete for economic and political power. Alongside these, a free update adds new event chains, aging mechanics, and a Kingdom Come: Deliverance collaboration. These expansions continue Paradox’s trend of deepening CK3’s simulation complexity and offering new playstyles.
