**Gaming Platform Updates: New Blood Doubles Down on GOG Support**

Dave Oshry, CEO of New Blood Interactive, has reaffirmed his studio’s commitment to the games storefront GOG following recent concerns about its viability. GOG, known for offering DRM-free classic games, has struggled to define its place alongside dominant platforms like Steam. In 2021, the service faced financial losses under its then-parent company CD Projekt, prompting a strategic refocus on classic titles and strict adherence to its DRM-free philosophy.

Oshry previously criticized GOG for not providing enough incentive for players to choose it over Steam. However, he now plans deeper support by aligning New Blood’s sales and releases—including anniversary sales and new titles like *Dungeons of Dusk* and *Tenebrous Somnia*—to launch simultaneously on both platforms. Additionally, demos and one-click mod installs of the *Dusk HD* remaster are available on GOG, mirroring Steam Workshop functionality.

GOG’s renewed independence and community-backed preservation efforts may benefit from partnerships like this. For users interested in DRM-free games, this marks a commitment to platform parity and ongoing classic game availability.

**Resident Evil Requiem Now Playable in Virtual Reality**

A new mod enables players to experience *Resident Evil Requiem* in VR, allowing control of FBI character Grace Ashcroft through immersive first-person perspective. While the base game offers first-person and third-person views, this mod intensifies the horror experience by placing players directly in Grace’s shoes.

Players must remove their headsets for some puzzle segments involving computer terminals, but otherwise the mod provides a near-full VR adaptation. The mod requires additional software components such as ViGEmBus and REFramework to function. This adaptation may appeal to fans comfortable with VR and horror, though it is considered a niche experience given the intense nature of the game.

**Player-Run Servers Bring Marvel Heroes Back Online**

Nearly nine years after its shutdown, the MMO action RPG *Marvel Heroes* has returned via player-run servers. The original game, which combined Diablo-like gameplay with Marvel Universe characters, closed in late 2017 after its publisher Gazillion Entertainment lost its Marvel license.

Community developers have released a server emulator at version 1.0, with the first active server—Project Tahiti—currently hosting over a thousand players. The emulator also supports a single-player mode for offline play. Players interested can find resources online to set up their own servers or connect to existing ones.

This revival allows fans to revisit the game’s cooperative action RPG style and preserves a piece of MMO history that official support had ended.

**Slay the Spire 2 Balance Patch Receives Mixed Reactions**

Development studio Mega Crit addressed player concern following the first balance patch for *Slay the Spire 2*. The patch notably changed a popular card, “Prepared,” significantly altering its cost and effects, which sparked widespread negative reviews on Steam including a review bomb.

Mega Crit explained that this patch is an initial step in a series of ongoing adjustments over the next one to two years during early access. The developer emphasized that changes are experimental, not final, and that feedback from players using the in-game reporting tool is particularly helpful.

The response highlights the challenges developers face balancing community expectations with long-term design goals in live-service early access titles.

**AI-Generated Music Fraud Results in Guilty Plea**

Michael Smith, a man from North Carolina, pled guilty to generating thousands of AI-created songs and artificially inflating their stream counts with bot accounts to collect over $8 million in fraudulent royalties. The U.S. Attorney’s Office announced the charge of conspiracy to commit wire fraud.

Smith’s operation involved billions of bot-generated streams across the fake AI music tracks. This case underscores growing concerns over AI misuse in digital media and royalty systems, where small payments can sum to large illicit profits when automated at scale.

Sentencing is scheduled for July, and authorities are pursuing forfeiture of the illegally obtained funds.

**AI Art Controversy in Crimson Desert Prompts Developer Apology**

Pearl Abyss acknowledged player reports identifying AI-generated assets in the *Crimson Desert* game, stating such visuals were placeholders created during early development with experimental tools. The studio apologized for lack of transparency and assured that these AI-generated elements would be replaced with final artwork to meet quality standards.
