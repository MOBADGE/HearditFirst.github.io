Indie Pass Launch Faces Criticism Over Revenue Model and Discoverability

A new subscription service called Indie Pass is set to launch on April 13, offering access to a curated selection of 70 indie games for $6.99 per month. Operated by the publisher indie.io, the service aims to improve discoverability for lesser-known indie titles by sharing revenue with developers based on the amount of time players spend in their games. Despite this approach targeting the challenge of crowded game markets, Indie Pass has seen a largely negative reaction from both developers and players.

Critics highlight issues with the compensation model, arguing that basing payouts on playtime might discourage games that don’t encourage extended looping gameplay. Others point out that indie games are generally affordable and prefer direct purchases to support developers more transparently. Additionally, some users raised concerns about adding yet another game launcher and the inherent drawbacks of not owning games outright. Industry figures such as 3D Realms’ co-founder have called such services harmful to developers. Indie Pass’s director of growth, Jess Mitchell, defended the initiative by emphasizing the difficulty indie developers face in gaining visibility amid the flood of new releases. However, it remains uncertain if enough developers or players will engage with the platform given the skepticism expressed so far.

ScummVM Expands Support for Obscure FMV and Adventure Games

ScummVM, a widely used tool that enables classic PC games to run on modern systems, has recently added support for eight additional titles. Among these are the FMV shooters Crime Patrol and Crime Patrol 2: Drug Wars, known for their campy acting and distinctive ‘90s full-motion video style. Also added is Necronomicon: The Dawning of Darkness, a Lovecraftian point-and-click adventure game that has received mixed receptions, including a low score from a YouTube reviewer citing its poor quality.

While many players focus on well-regarded classics, ScummVM offers a way to experience more obscure and sometimes flawed games that contribute to gaming history. The project’s continued expansion keeps older, less accessible games playable across platforms, supporting preservation and curiosity-driven gameplay. ScummVM maintains a wiki listing where players can find these titles, some available for purchase on sites like GOG.

AI Startup Tesana Promotes Mass Participation in Game Development through Generative AI

Tesana, a startup showcased at the recent Game Developers Conference, offers an AI-driven game creation platform that translates natural language prompts into playable game code. Leveraging proprietary technology alongside existing AI tools for generating assets, Tesana aims to make game development accessible to a broader population beyond skilled programmers and designers.

With around 10,000 paying users in its early weeks, co-founder Johannes Vermandois envisions a future where millions can create games simply through text input. He suggests such tools could foster new game genres and improve overall quality by increasing creative experimentation. Nonetheless, some observers remain cautious, pointing out that the loss of detailed control and the potential for repetitive outputs could challenge the idea that AI-generated games will significantly elevate the medium. Vermandois acknowledges these concerns but remains optimistic that creative vision will retain value alongside AI-assisted production.

Slay the Spire 2 Beta Introduces End-of-Run Badges and Gameplay Adjustments

In the latest beta update for Slay the Spire 2, developer Mega Crit has added a system of end-of-run badges designed to highlight notable achievements or unique occurrences within individual playthroughs. These badges function similarly to achievements and will later be integrated into run histories and statistical screens. The update also includes multiple gameplay tweaks, balance changes to cards and enemies, and new visual effects.

This patch follows a previous balance update that prompted significant player backlash, resulting in many negative reviews. While the current update continues to receive mixed reactions, the amount of negative feedback has decreased substantially. Mega Crit encourages beta participants to provide feedback as the studio aims to refine scoring and run distinctiveness. The full patch notes detail changes to map generation, card mechanics, enemy behavior, and bug fixes across single-player and multiplayer modes.
