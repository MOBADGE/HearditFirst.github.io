**Soulmask Leaves Early Access with Free Egypt-Themed DLC**

The tribal survival game Soulmask has officially exited early access with a major update described by developer CampFire Studio as a "complete reinvention." The launch includes a new DLC called *Shifting Sands*, which introduces an Ancient Egypt-themed map roughly the size of the base game's Mayan jungle setting. This expansion adds 10 new ruins, six new bosses, and 325 new talents for tribesmen. Players can also build new vessels, including wooden boats and Falcon-class airships, with even larger airships planned for future updates.

CampFire Studio is offering the DLC free for one month to all owners of the full game, meaning anyone who purchases Soulmask before May will receive the expansion at no extra cost. Alongside the DLC, the 1.0 release adds three distinct game modes—traditional survival, tribe development, and a warrior-focused action RPG mode—plus new tribe development mechanics and an improved onboarding system. Soulmask is currently discounted by 10% until April 23.

**Marvel Rivals Expanding Beyond Shooter Gameplay**

Guangyun Chen, creative director of Marvel Rivals at NetEase, announced plans to broaden the game beyond its standard 6v6 player-versus-player shooter experience. The update, themed around the upcoming *Avengers: Doomsday* movie releasing December 18, 2026, will introduce new PvE modes and additional content inspired by the Marvel Cinematic Universe’s Infinity Saga.

While specific gameplay details are limited, the expansion aims to enhance Marvel Rivals into what Chen described as a "comprehensive Marvel 'moving anime' experience." The game previously experimented with PvE through its Marvel Zombies mode, but this new content is expected to be more substantial. Chen indicated that co-op boss fights and other collaborative modes might be included.

**Wizards of the Coast Streams Classic 1980s D&D Cartoon**

Wizards of the Coast has begun uploading episodes of the 1980s Dungeons & Dragons animated series to YouTube. Released originally in the mid-1980s, the show consists of 27 episodes blending fantasy adventure with the era’s distinctive animation style, produced by Marvel Productions and Toei Animation.

The cartoon is notable for featuring voice actors like Peter Cullen and Frank Welker, known for their roles in various classic 1980s cartoons. The episodes provide a nostalgic look at early tabletop RPG crossover efforts and include recognizable creatures from Dungeons & Dragons lore. New episodes are uploaded every Friday in blocks of two or three.

**Developer of 1000xResist to Return to FMV for Next Game**

Sunset Visitor, the studio behind the narrative game 1000xResist, plans to incorporate full-motion video (FMV) once again in their upcoming title *Prove You're Human*. Last year’s game unexpectedly included a video sequence sourced from documentary footage, a decision made “out of desperation,” according to creative director Remy Siu.

For their next project, which features a player controlling a digital copy interacting with an AI, the studio sees FMV as a useful storytelling tool. This approach aims to highlight the separation between the physical and virtual worlds within the narrative. FMV, which had a contentious reputation in the past, has seen renewed interest in indie games for its potential in narrative delivery.

**Fighting Game YouTuber Offers $10,000 Bounty for Rollback Netcode in UMvC3**

Maximillian Christiansen, known as Maximilian Dood, has offered a $10,000 bounty to anyone able to implement rollback netcode in *Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3* for Steam. Currently, the game uses delay-based netcode, which can cause input lag and affect the online experience negatively.

Rollback netcode predicts player inputs to reduce latency and is regarded as superior for fighting games, where timing precision is critical. Christiansen has supported the modding community for Marvel vs. Capcom games through financial contributions and believes an improved netcode would benefit the game's longevity and online play. He cited the success of the Super Smash Bros. Melee community’s netcode improvements as inspiration.
