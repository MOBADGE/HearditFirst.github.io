Warhammer World Expands to the US

Games Workshop announced that Warhammer World, its well-known hobby store and gaming destination originally based in the UK, will open a new location near Washington, DC in late 2027. This venue will be a flagship space celebrating the Warhammer tabletop games, including Warhammer Fantasy, Warhammer 40,000, and Age of Sigmar. While it won't be an exact replication of the original Nottingham site, the US version promises to maintain high quality and immersive experiences across a hobby store, social space, and themed restaurant. The expansion reflects Warhammer's growing fanbase in North America and aims to provide a dedicated physical hub for enthusiasts outside the UK.

Epic Games CEO Responds to Controversy Over AI-Generated Content on X

Epic Games CEO Tim Sweeney addressed recent calls by US lawmakers to ban the social media platform X (formerly Twitter) and its AI chat tool Grok over their capability to generate sexually explicit images of minors. Sweeney described these political demands as attempts by "gatekeepers" to censor opponents under the guise of regulating AI. He acknowledged that AI technologies have flaws but criticized selective targeting driven by political motives. The controversy arises from reports that Grok can produce synthetic child sexual abuse material (CSAM) from user images, prompting outrage and calls for stricter controls. Despite moving some AI image features behind a paywall, X has yet to fully address these concerns. Critics highlight the ethical tensions between free speech, platform responsibility, and content moderation in emerging AI applications.
