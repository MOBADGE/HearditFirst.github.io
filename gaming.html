**Star Wars Zero Company: Studio Highlights Risk-Taking and Complex Narrative**

Bit Reactor, the new studio behind the upcoming Star Wars tactics game *Zero Company*, has praised Lucasfilm and Respawn for supporting an unusual project in the franchise. Greg Foertsch, Bit Reactor’s founder, noted that turn-based tactics games are a niche genre and it is rare for big companies to back a new studio tackling such a format. The involvement of familiar Star Wars characters is expected to help draw attention, but Foertsch emphasized the importance of the faith shown by Lucasfilm and Respawn in enabling the team to innovate within this setting.

Narrative director Aaron Contreras explained that *Zero Company* aims to move away from typical RPG tropes of a heroic protagonist quickly bonding with a team. Instead, the game focuses on managing a team with distinct personalities and conflicts, reflecting the ambiguous moral landscape of the Clone Wars era. The game’s bond system allows squad members to develop relationships that influence gameplay, yet disagreements and friction are intentionally woven into the story to reflect leadership challenges.

**Blockchain Game Storefront Robot Cache Reportedly Closing, Impacting Users’ Access**

Reports have emerged that Robot Cache, a digital game storefront integrating blockchain technology and allowing users to resell games, is shutting down. According to leaked emails shared by users, the platform plans to close within 30 days, after which purchased games will no longer be playable even if fully downloaded. This is due to the deactivation of the platform’s DRM verification system.

Founded by Brian Fargo in 2018, Robot Cache attempted to differentiate itself from competitors like Steam by emphasizing blockchain functionality and user-to-user sales. Despite early partnerships and a vision to raise significant funding, the platform struggled commercially, generating minimal revenue in recent years and undergoing extended periods of silence and minimal staff activity.

If confirmed, Robot Cache's closure underscores the risks associated with DRM-reliant and blockchain-dependent game services, raising concerns about preservation and ownership for users.

**Two Renowned Composers Collaborated on Neverwinter Nights 2: Mask of the Betrayer Soundtrack**

The expansion *Mask of the Betrayer* for *Neverwinter Nights 2* featured a notable soundtrack collaboration between composer Alexander Brandon and the audio duo Womb Music (Rik Schaffer and Margaret Tang). Though both the base game and expansion have long been considered underrated RPGs, the soundtrack stands out for its mature, atmospheric tone supporting the game’s darker narrative.

Brandon, known for his work on *Deus Ex* and *Unreal*, praised the cooperative dynamic in crafting themes that added depth and tension to the expansion’s setting, particularly the city of Mulsantir. Womb Music, also celebrated for their work on *Vampire: The Masquerade – Bloodlines*, helped shape a soundscape balancing mystery, foreboding, and emotional resonance that contributed significantly to the game’s mood.

**Umamusume Cosplayers Organize Real-Life Races Globally**

Fans of the anime-style horse racing game *Umamusume: Pretty Derby* have taken their enthusiasm beyond cosplay events, organizing actual races at horse tracks worldwide. These events feature participants dressed as their favorite characters competing in running races, emulating the game’s core theme.

Locations reported include tracks in Thailand, Malaysia, the United States, and Peru, with events largely organized by local fan groups rather than officially sanctioned by the game's publisher, Cygames. While these races lack formal prizes or showcases, they demonstrate a dedicated fanbase investing time and effort into bringing the game's spirit into physical activity.

**Crimson Desert Remains a Compelling but Confusing Large-Scale Game After Extensive Play**

After over 150 hours with *Crimson Desert*, senior writer Sean Martin reflects on the game’s sprawling size and complex design, which defy easy classification. Pearl Abyss’s title blends elements of action, RPG, and MMO worlds but does not neatly fit any of those molds, partly due to design changes made since launch in response to player feedback.

Although the story and main character may feel conventional, the game offers a huge open world with many side activities, mounts, and systems that encourage exploration and gradual immersion. While bugs and control issues persist, *Crimson Desert* continues to attract players with its scale and ambience despite its identity challenges.
