Portal 2: Community Edition Launches Open Beta

A new modded version of Portal 2 called Portal 2: Community Edition (CE) has entered open beta. Unlike a remake, this community-maintained build enhances the original game by introducing upgraded lighting, volumetrics, detailed textures, expanded scripting, and custom campaign support with a built-in UI. It is based on Strata Source, a fork of the CS:GO engine, and aims to extend modding possibilities far beyond the original limitations. So far, modders have released custom maps and assets, including a campaign menu that supports maps from other games like Half-Life 2. The developers are working on an original campaign for CE, which could bring fresh content alongside these technical improvements. This release matters because Portal 2 has a long-standing, active modding scene, and CE may renew interest by allowing creators to build more sophisticated and visually distinct levels.

MechDoomer: Rebooted Adds 28 Pilotable Mechs to Doom

After two years in development, the MechDoomer: Rebooted mod for Doom 1 and 2 has been released in its 1.0 version. The mod adds 28 different mechs across light to assault classes, letting players pilot giant robots within the classic shooter. It features mech-style movement, a revamped UI, and customizable weapon slots. Notably, players can exit their mech at will, allowing the mod to coexist with many other Doom mods, including the popular Halo mod. This mod alters the game’s perspective, adding occasional third-person views as seen in the trailer, and changes the gameplay pace. The significance lies in how it reshapes Doom’s gameplay with a completely different approach, offering both nostalgia and novelty to longtime fans.

AOC to Update Gaming Monitor OSD Interface

AOC has acknowledged widespread criticism of its gaming monitors' on-screen display (OSD) software and is rolling out a redesigned version for all new and upcoming models. The company admits the OSD was a frequent negative point in reviews and sought to simplify the interface. The new OSD provides a two-tier system: a basic overview with common settings on the first screen and advanced, granular options accessible through a secondary menu. It also uses color coding to differentiate between AOC’s product lines for clearer navigation. This matters because a user-friendly OSD is crucial for gamers adjusting settings on the fly, especially with monitors supporting complex features like dual-mode refresh rates. Improving the OSD could enhance the overall user experience and reduce frustration.

BAFTA Withdraws Trailer for The Quiet Things Over Sensitive Themes

Silver Script Games’ founder Alyx Jones had a trailer for the indie game The Quiet Things pulled from the BAFTA awards event hours before its scheduled debut. The trailer had been revised previously to remove content flagged by BAFTA as potentially triggering, but the organization still decided not to show it, citing insufficient time to add audience warnings. The Quiet Things deals with childhood trauma and abuse, and the decision has sparked debate about content sensitivity and artistic expression in games. BAFTA stated the choice was made with guest wellbeing in mind and does not reflect opposition to games handling difficult subjects. This incident highlights ongoing challenges around trauma representation in games and the balance between content warnings and creative freedom.

GTA: Carcer City Mod Brings Manhunt Setting into San Andreas

A total conversion mod for Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas called GTA: Carcer City has been released as a demo after 13 years in development. It replaces the original San Andreas map with Carcer City, the dark urban environment from 2003’s Manhunt, which shares the same fictional universe. The mod includes a new storyline set in 2001, aiming for a darker, more immersive tone than traditional GTA titles. The city draws visual inspiration from Rust Belt cities like Detroit and Cleveland. Players can explore known locations from Manhunt as part of the mod. Although still incomplete, this mod offers fans a chance to experience a different take on Rockstar’s universe. It is noteworthy for its scale and ambition in modding a well-known open world.
