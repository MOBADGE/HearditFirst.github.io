Minecraft Introduces the Sulfur Cube Mob in Upcoming Chaos Cubed Update

Minecraft announced its second major update for 2026, titled Chaos Cubed, featuring the new Sulfur Cave biome and a unique mob called the Sulfur Cube. This mob stands out with its ability to consume blocks and adopt their attributes, influencing its movement and behavior. For example, feeding it ice causes it to slide, while wooden blocks make it act like a ball suitable for minigames. Although the update lacks a confirmed release date, it is expected around mid-2026, likely summer. The Sulfur Cube’s gameplay potential lies in player experimentation, as Mojang has only revealed a few examples, leaving many details such as taming and drops undisclosed.

Nvidia App Beta Update Adds DLSS 4.5 and Auto Shader Compilation

Nvidia released a beta update for its Nvidia App that adds support for DLSS 4.5 Dynamic Multi Frame Generation (DMFG), enhancing frame rate performance by generating up to six AI-assisted frames per original frame for RTX 50-series GPUs. The feature can be globally enabled or customized per game, syncing with monitor refresh rates. The update also includes a new DLSS model for RTX 40- and 50-series cards aimed at improved image stability with UI elements.

Additionally, a new Auto Shader Compilation feature is introduced in beta. This tool rebuilds the DirectX 12 shader cache during system idle times following driver updates to reduce stuttering and lengthy cache rebuilds. Users must opt into the beta and have the latest Game Ready Driver to test these features.

Amazon’s Fallout Series Reaches 100 Million Viewers Across Two Seasons

Amazon reports that its Fallout series on Prime Video has been viewed by 100 million people to date, combining viewership from the first season’s 65 million and the second season’s 83 million (partial views counting anyone who started watching). This makes Fallout one of Amazon's most successful launches. The second season’s release boosted interest in the first, contributing to the milestone. Production for a third season is slated to begin this summer and will feature a new location not previously explored in the game series.

Steam’s House and Home Sale Highlights Cozy Game Recommendations

Steam is hosting the House and Home Fest sale from now until April 6, showcasing a selection of cozy games focused on building, organizing, and home care themes. Some notable picks include:

- *Creature Kitchen*: A six-hour cooking puzzle game with charming critters and unique snack-making mechanics. - *PowerWash Simulator 2*: An updated sequel featuring improved cleaning tools and cooperative gameplay. - *Hozy*: A neighborhood fixer-upper sim involving cleaning and remodeling buildings room-by-room. - *Unpacking*: A 2D home decor sim emphasizing puzzle-like placement of unpacked items. - *My Little Life*: An idler game where players manage tiny characters’ needs and careers.

The sale offers demos for some games and discounted prices, catering to players interested in relaxing, home-themed experiences.

Reviews for Super Mario Galaxy Movie Are Mostly Negative

The recently released Super Mario Galaxy Movie has garnered predominantly negative reviews from critics. Major outlets like The Guardian and The New York Times criticize the film as lacking depth, originality, and meaningful content, describing it as flat or formulaic. Even some positive reviews acknowledge its shortcomings but note that the movie maintains a fast pace that may keep viewers engaged temporarily. Overall, the adaptation appears to prioritize commercial appeal over storytelling or artistic merit, disappointing many reviewers.

Dark Souls Beaten at Level 1 Without Hits, Dodges, or Ranged Weapons

A player known as Zeldasouls has completed a remarkable Dark Souls run adhering to stringent self-imposed rules: no leveling up, no ranged attacks (including magic and consumables), no dodge rolls or special evasive moves, and no taking damage from enemies. This hitless playthrough, spanning over two years of effort, is considered one of the toughest challenge runs in the series. The run requires exceptionally cautious, methodical play through all bosses and areas, providing a slow but impressive demonstration of skill and strategy for dedicated Dark Souls fans.
