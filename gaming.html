**Samson Aims to Offer a Smaller Alternative to Grand Theft Auto**

Liquid Swords’ upcoming open-world crime brawler, *Samson*, draws frequent comparisons to Rockstar’s *Grand Theft Auto* series due to its setting and themes. However, co-founder Christofer Sundberg emphasized that *Samson* is intended as a more compact experience, not a competitor aiming to replicate *GTA’s* scale or cultural phenomenon. Scheduled for release on April 8, the game follows a low-level criminal navigating a city filled with crime and cars. Sundberg envisions *Samson* filling a space for players seeking a shorter, more focused action experience, akin to classic 90-minute action movies, rather than the sprawling epics that modern open-world games often are. This distinction addresses a potential niche for players who want a break from large-scale GTA titles without stepping away from the genre altogether.

**House Flipper Available Free on Steam for a Limited Time**

Simulation fans have the opportunity to claim *House Flipper*, a home renovation simulator, free to keep on Steam until April 6. Players engage in renovating rundown homes by cleaning, repairing, and remodeling properties to sell at a profit. The game has a variety of DLC packs that expand content with new furniture, pets, and environments, and the remastered version is set to launch later this year with discounts available for owners of the original. While its detailed focus on mundane tasks may not appeal to everyone, the free offer provides a low-barrier entry for those interested in this popular cozy simulation subgenre.

**Echo Point Nova’s April Update Adds New Game+ and Movement Options**

Greylock Studio announced a substantial April update for their indie open-world FPS *Echo Point Nova*, including a New Game+ mode and three new movement mechanics: rope grappling (similar to Spider-Man’s swinging), sprint with wallrunning, and a jetpack. The New Game+ mode will allow players to carry over selected unlocks and customize elements like randomized weapon pickups and enemy spawns, increasing replayability. These additions are designed to enrich player mobility and tactical options, expanding on the game’s signature high-speed action and vertical combat. The update is expected later this month and aims to enhance the game’s core “movement and shooting” loop.

**State of Decay 3 Announces Alpha Tests for May**

After a lengthy development period since its 2020 announcement, *State of Decay 3* from Undead Labs is moving forward with alpha playtests scheduled for May. The upcoming tests will focus on four-player cooperative play, new base-building features, resource management strategies, and combat mechanics amid a zombie outbreak. This announcement signals a transition from extended silence to active community engagement and testing, suggesting more concrete progress on the survival shooter. Players interested in early access can register on the official website, with Undead Labs promising multiple playtest opportunities throughout the year.

**Rust Introduces Water Wheels Harnessing Hydro and Human Power**

Facepunch Studios released the Spring Clean update for *Rust*, adding water wheels as a new source of renewable energy alongside existing wind and solar options. These wheels can generate power placed in moving water, but also serve as large treadmills that players—or forcibly restrained ones—can use to produce energy. This feature introduces new tactical possibilities, including the ability to compel handcuffed players to power devices, reflecting the game’s harsh environment. Additional update content includes an armored ladder hatch for base defense, colored buttons for easier use, an overfishing mechanic, and seasonal Easter events with themed cosmetics.

**Take-Two Lays Off Part of AI Team Including Head of AI Amid Mixed Public Messaging**

Take-Two Interactive has laid off an unspecified number of employees from its artificial intelligence team, including its Head of Artificial Intelligence, Luke Dicken. Dicken’s departure comes less than two years after his January 2025 hire and follows public comments from Take-Two’s CEO Strauss Zelnick that expressed cautious optimism about generative AI’s role in game development. Dicken’s LinkedIn notes his leadership in integrating modern AI techniques like large language models and diffusion models into Take-Two’s workflows. The layoffs highlight ongoing uncertainties within the gaming industry's approach to AI, despite earlier statements about embracing AI technologies. The move contrasts with other recent corporate interest and investment in AI, reflecting a sector still adjusting to these tools.
