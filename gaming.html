Older Call of Duty Titles Coming to Game Pass in 2026

Xbox leadership, under new head Asha Sharma, announced key changes to the Game Pass subscription service. While the price of Game Pass will decrease, newly released Call of Duty games will no longer be available on the service at launch. Instead, they will arrive approximately one year after release, coinciding with the release of the next Call of Duty installment.

This shift marks the end of a two-year period where Xbox provided its most profitable franchise to subscribers immediately. Activision reportedly pushed for the change due to the impact of Game Pass availability on sales of games like Black Ops 6 and 7. However, Activision plans to add more legacy Call of Duty games to Game Pass within the year. Currently, only recent titles from 2017 onwards are included. The addition of older classics such as Modern Warfare 2 and Black Ops could appeal to longtime fans, given their continued sales at high prices on other platforms.

Google Cloud Executive Proposes AI as a Solution for Game Industry Challenges

Jack Buser, Google Cloud’s global director for games, discussed the challenges facing the video game industry, including rising development costs, declining profits, game cancellations, and ongoing layoffs. He noted revenue growth was limited to regions like China and platforms like Roblox, leaving other developers struggling.

Buser highlights artificial intelligence as a key tool to transform the industry by accelerating processes in marketing, analytics, and development. He suggests AI can shorten development cycles and reduce costs, enabling smaller studios to compete more effectively with larger companies. However, he acknowledges that AI will not directly alleviate employment concerns, framing it instead as a means to "right-size" business models. This term hints at further workforce reductions, raising questions about AI's long-term impact on game developers.

Hideo Kojima Visits Valve HQ, Shoots Photos with Half-Life Props

Renowned game creator Hideo Kojima recently shared images from his visit to Valve’s headquarters in Seattle. Among the photos are Kojima posing with iconic Half-Life gravity guns and other Valve memorabilia, including portal guns and a custom flag held by the Kojima Productions mascot.

While Kojima did not provide a specific reason for the visit, the images appear more as a friendly nod to Valve's influence rather than indicating any formal collaboration. Historically, Kojima has praised Valve’s Half-Life series for its gameplay and atmosphere, acknowledging its influence on his own work, particularly Metal Gear Solid 2.

Fallout Co-Creator Emphasizes Non-Linear Game Design

Tim Cain, co-creator of Fallout, recently outlined his principles for level design, emphasizing the importance of not assuming players will interact with game elements in a specific order. He criticizes design approaches that force players into predetermined sequences, such as mandatory conversations with NPC guards.

Cain encourages designers to create open, flexible environments that accommodate various player behaviors. His philosophy supports non-linearity to enrich player experience, avoiding restrictions that can inhibit exploration or alternate strategies. This approach has influenced the design of many role-playing games that value emergent gameplay over rigid scripting.

Fallout: New Vegas Setting Considered for New Orleans, Source Code Issues Raised

Chris Avellone, writer for Fallout: New Vegas, revealed in an interview that an early concept considered setting the game in New Orleans, inspired by the unique atmosphere and post-apocalyptic themes from the comic series Grendel. He expressed enthusiasm for this setting as it offered a distinct flavor while fitting the Fallout universe.

Avellone also mentioned that Bethesda reportedly does not have the source code for New Vegas, complicating potential remasters or official sequels. This has contributed to ongoing uncertainty about Fallout’s future. He speculates that the planned Fallout 5 might be set in San Francisco, aligning with deleted content and Bethesda's previous requests during New Vegas development.
