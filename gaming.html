Muri: Wildwoods Demo Offers a New Take on Virtual Cleaning

A demo for *Muri: Wildwoods* has caught attention for its engaging blend of adventure and cleaning gameplay reminiscent of *PowerWash Simulator*. Players control Pelle, a mouse-like creature called a Muri, tasked with cleaning a mysterious island covered in a black "corruption." The cleaning mechanic revives the environment and its wildlife, including charming creatures known as Shruppies. Unlike traditional cleaning sims, this game visually represents progress by showing the world springing back to life as areas are cleansed. The demo, available via Steam Next Fest, offers about an hour of gameplay and has received praise for its immersive and calming experience. This marks a unique approach within adventure games, emphasizing environmental restoration through interactive cleanliness.

Direct3D Update Brings Developer-Centric Enhancements

Microsoft has updated Direct3D 12 to include Shader Model 6.9, adding features such as opacity micromaps and shader execution reordering (SER). These enhancements improve efficiency in ray tracing, particularly for scenes with many transparent or complex shaders. Although these updates hold little immediate significance for gamers, developers will benefit from standardized access to GPU capabilities found in Nvidia's RTX 40 and 50-series cards. The changes facilitate better performance optimization in high-fidelity graphics, helping future games run smoother. This update signals continued refinement in graphics APIs to support advanced rendering techniques without altering gamers‚Äô day-to-day experiences.
