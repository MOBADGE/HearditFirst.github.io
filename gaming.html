**Project Zomboid Removes Steam Mods Containing Malicious Code**

The developers of Project Zomboid recently identified and banned 14 Steam Workshop mods after discovering they contained "heavily obfuscated code" that created malicious files outside the game’s directory. These mods, mostly related to soundtracks from various games and shows, had been installed on between 500 and 2,200 devices, primarily affecting the game's current Build 42 testing branch. The Indie Stone, Project Zomboid's developer, recommends users who installed these mods take comprehensive security measures beyond simply uninstalling them. This incident highlights ongoing risks associated with modding communities, especially on popular platforms like Steam Workshop, emphasizing the need for vigilance both from developers and players.

**Hazelight Studios Celebrates 50 Million Game Sales**

Hazelight Studios, known for cooperative action-adventure games, announced it has sold over 50 million copies across its three titles: A Way Out (13 million), It Takes Two (30 million), and Split Fiction (7 million). While A Way Out has the longest market presence among them, Split Fiction’s sales are notable given its recent release last year. Hazelight credits consistent fan support for this achievement and has confirmed it is developing a fourth game, although details remain under wraps. This milestone underscores the studio’s continued influence in the co-op game genre and the market's positive reception to cooperative storytelling experiences.

**World of Warcraft Player Recreates Counter-Strike’s Dust 2 Map**

A World of Warcraft player has creatively reconstructed the iconic Dust 2 map from Counter-Strike using the game’s player housing tools. The build features detailed elements like a wooden car made from shelves, doormats, and fruit plates, impressing viewers with its accuracy despite the limitations of WoW’s housing system. The player shared instructions for others on European servers to visit the map, which is hidden inside their in-game house. Blizzard’s official WoW account responded with humor, reflecting community appreciation for such fan creations. This project showcases the versatility of player housing and the community’s passion for cross-game creativity.

**Songs of Glimmerwick Demo Offers Ethical Wizard School Life Simulation**

Eastshade Studios released a playable demo for Songs of Glimmerwick, a wizard school life simulation game with a focus on inclusive and ethical worldbuilding. Players assume the role of a new student cultivating magical gardens and learning music-based magic at the Etchery university. Set in a society recovering from a period of magical repression, the game addresses themes like access to education and civil disobedience while maintaining a charming, storybook art style. While still in development, the demo reveals a thoughtfully crafted experience with voice acting and an emphasis on community values. Eastshade aims to launch the full game later this year, continuing a trend of indie titles exploring nuanced fantasy settings.

**Overwatch’s New Hero Sierra Sparks Debate Over Auto-Aim Ability**

The recent trailer for Overwatch’s upcoming hero Sierra revealed a controversial ability: firing a dart that marks an enemy, causing the player’s subsequent shots to curve toward the target. This auto-aiming mechanic is drawing comparisons to past abilities disliked by the player base for reducing the skill needed to aim. Sierra’s kit also includes a grappling hook and an area bombardment ultimate, rounding out a versatile playstyle. While Blizzard likely balanced her abilities before release, Sierra may face bans in competitive play similar to previous contentious heroes. Her arrival on April 14 will be closely watched to see how the community responds to this aiming-assist feature.
