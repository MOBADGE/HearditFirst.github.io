OpenAI Ends Sora Video Generation Service; Disney Withdraws Investment

OpenAI has announced the discontinuation of Sora, its AI-powered video generation app, less than six months after the launch of Sora 2. The tool, known for creating viral AI-generated clips such as one featuring OpenAI CEO Sam Altman in a humorous scenario, faced criticism for enabling copyright infringements by easily generating videos using protected content without permission. Following demands from Japanese studios to stop using their intellectual property, OpenAI tightened its approach but ultimately decided to exit the video generation space. As a result, video features are also being removed from ChatGPT, with OpenAI shifting focus towards productivity tools combining ChatGPT, Codex, and Atlas browser.

Disney had previously agreed to a $1 billion investment in OpenAI that would see its characters licensed for Sora-generated content. However, following Sora’s closure, Disney has pulled out of the deal. A Disney spokesperson expressed respect for OpenAI’s decision and reaffirmed the company’s commitment to exploring AI integrations that respect intellectual property and creator rights.

Marathon Review: Bungie's Extraction Shooter Revival

Bungie's new extraction shooter, *Marathon*, released earlier this month, is receiving positive reception after several weeks of player experience. The game reboots Bungie's original *Marathon* FPS series with an emphasis on high-stakes loot gathering and PvP combat, set on a sci-fi world called Tau Ceti IV. Players engage in rapid contracts with intense firefights and a strong progression system tied to weapon variants and upgrades.

The reviewer notes *Marathon* excels in delivering satisfying gunplay reminiscent of Bungie’s strengths, while also innovating on extraction shooter mechanics by featuring smaller maps with dense, tactical zones fostering multiple encounters. The game integrates lore through extensive in-game logs and environmental storytelling, enriching its sci-fi setting without a traditional campaign. While some UI and balance issues remain, Bungie’s ongoing support and roadmap suggest continued improvement. Overall, *Marathon* stands as a noteworthy addition to multiplayer shooters this year.

Slay the Spire 2 Sets Records for Deckbuilder Genre

*Slay the Spire 2*, which launched recently in early access, is establishing itself as the best-performing deckbuilding game ever, according to analyst firm Newzoo. The game reached a peak of 575,000 concurrent users on Steam within days of launch—an unprecedented number far exceeding previous highs set by similar games like the original *Slay the Spire* and *Balatro*. Sales figures show 3 million copies sold in the first week, generating approximately $75 million in gross revenue.

Newzoo attributes this record performance partly to the strong anticipation built up over seven years since the original, which had no sequel or spin-offs to divide its player base. The sequel maintains a solo-player focus that appeals to dedicated fans. Developer Mega Crit's reputation for consumer-friendly development practices, avoiding aggressive monetization, also contributed to the positive reception.

Call of Duty Veteran’s New Studio Dark Outlaw Shuts Down

Sony has confirmed the closure of Dark Outlaw Games, a studio founded by former *Call of Duty* director Jason Blundell just a year ago. The studio was reportedly in early development of an unannounced project when it was shut down, leading to layoffs estimated at around 50 employees. This follows the recent closure of Sony’s Bluepoint Games, highlighting ongoing restructuring and cost-cutting efforts at Sony Interactive Entertainment.

Blundell’s career in *Call of Duty* was marked by leadership roles on several *Black Ops* titles, but recent industry turbulence and studio consolidations have affected even well-known veterans. Sony cited strategic adjustments to support long-term sustainability and thanked impacted workers in its statement.
