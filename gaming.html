MSI MEG X870E Godlike X Edition Review: A Limited, Feature-Rich Motherboard

MSI released the MEG X870E Godlike X Edition motherboard as a limited edition to mark the 10th anniversary of its Godlike series. Only 1,000 units are available through select retailers in the US and UK, making it largely inaccessible to most consumers. This board builds upon the already well-equipped standard MEG X870E Godlike model with only minor differences, such as a special M.2 SSD heatsink featuring an embedded display and a USB-powered showcase stand, alongside some aesthetic updates.

The motherboard supports the AMD AM5 socket and X870E chipset, compatible with Ryzen 7000 to 9000 series CPUs, and offers extensive connectivity‚Äî15 USB ports on the rear panel alone, multiple M.2 slots, and high-end networking options including 10G LAN and Wi-Fi 7. It features two polychromatic displays for system monitoring and a large variety of headers that appeal to enthusiasts who want comprehensive control over fans, lighting, and power.

Performance testing showed that while the Godlike X Edition is stable and feature-rich, it does not deliver superior gaming or productivity performance compared to much cheaper alternatives. Thermal performance was average, with unusually high reported VRM temperatures that may be sensor-placement related. The board requires some manual tuning in UEFI for those seeking maximum CPU and memory speeds.

Overall, this motherboard exemplifies high-end excess and connectivity without providing standout performance gains over other AM5 boards. Due to its limited availability and high price ($1300), it is mainly suited to collectors or specialists needing extreme I/O capacities.

[Full review on PC Gamer](https://www.pcgamer.com/hardware/motherboards/msi-meg-x870e-godlike-x-edition-review/)
