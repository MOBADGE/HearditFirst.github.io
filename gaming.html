Dice Rolling in Space: Roleplaying Games in Zero Gravity

Astronaut Rabea Rogge, the first German woman in space, recently demonstrated rolling a twenty-sided die (D20) aboard the SpaceX Fram2 mission in polar orbit. Rogge experimented with methods to control dice rolls in zero gravity and found that flicking the die to spin it and catching it by hand worked best. This informal test showed that tabletop roleplaying games like Dungeons & Dragons can be played in space, although rolling multiple dice simultaneously remains challenging. While not a significant technical milestone, Rogge’s experiment highlights the everyday moments and hobbies possible even in orbit.

Mods Point Toward Potential Full Survival Conversion in Crimson Desert

Recent mods for the action RPG Crimson Desert have introduced hunger systems and food-related consequences such as poisoning and debuffs, sparking interest in a possible full survival game mode. The current game features cooking and eating mechanics, but these mods make food consumption critical to character survival. Players have expressed a desire for more survival features like camping systems, portable beds, item degradation, and realistic environmental effects. While the developer Pearl Abyss has no current plans for deep mod support to enable these features, the modding community’s efforts hint at the potential to expand Crimson Desert’s gameplay into the survival genre.

King Legacy Codes for April 2026

Surge in Kerbal Space Program Players Linked to Artemis 2 Mission

Kerbal Space Program has experienced its highest concurrent player count on Steam in over a decade, coinciding with NASA’s Artemis 2 lunar flyby mission. Fans and newcomers alike appear inspired by renewed public interest in space exploration to launch or return to the spaceflight simulator. Social media discussions reveal that many players are attempting to recreate the Artemis mission within the game. While the boost may decrease after the mission conclusion, it highlights how real-world space events can influence engagement in space-themed games. Kerbal Space Program 2 also saw a smaller increase in players but remains less popular overall.

Bungie Addresses Cheating Concerns in Marathon’s Competitive Mode

Bungie has acknowledged increasing frustration over cheating in Marathon’s newly launched Ranked competitive mode. Reports of cheating have become widespread enough that some community members say the mode is “ruined.” Bungie reiterated its zero-tolerance policy on cheating, confirming ongoing bans of offenders and enhancements to detection technologies. The studio also plans improvements to player reporting tools, potential feedback about action taken, and moderation features to manage toxicity and stream sniping. Bungie encourages continued player reports as part of efforts to maintain fairness within the competitive multiplayer environment.

Passing of Double Dragon Creator Yoshihisa Kishimoto

Yoshihisa Kishimoto, creator of influential beat-em-up series Double Dragon and Kunio-kun, passed away at age 64. Kishimoto’s early work in the 1980s helped establish key elements of the beat-em-up genre with titles like Renegade and Double Dragon, the latter becoming a worldwide arcade success. His career included contributions to various arcade and console games, and in later years, consulting roles on sequels and indie revivals of his series. Kishimoto’s legacy remains in the popularity and continued influence of his game designs, which combined street-level action with cooperative gameplay. His death marks the loss of a notable figure in video game history.
