Bully Online Mod Shuts Down Shortly After Launch

Bully Online, a fan-made multiplayer mod for Rockstar's classic game *Bully*, has been shut down just one month after its release. The mod offered new multiplayer features and minigames but was initially funded through donations, raising legal concerns. The mod's lead developer stated the shutdown was unplanned and suggested it was likely due to pressure from Rockstar or Take-Two Interactive. The entire project, including source code and servers, has been taken offline permanently. This case highlights the ongoing challenges fan projects face when using intellectual property owned by major companies, even though Rockstar has previously embraced mods under controlled circumstances.

Hytale and Minecraft: Understanding the Differences

With Hytale recently launching into early access, comparisons with *Minecraft* are inevitable. Both games share core features like procedurally generated worlds, crafting, and a block-based aesthetic. However, Hytale is much earlier in development and not yet feature complete. Notably, Hytale aims to combine sandbox gameplay with role-playing elements, including structured campaigns and quests, which are currently missing. Its combat system includes special moves and ultimates, contrasting *Minecraft*'s simpler mechanics. Crafting in Hytale is menu-based and features progression through upgraded stations, differing from Minecraft's grid-based crafting. While Hytale offers a fresh take, the game is still evolving and lacks several anticipated features.

Cygames Issues Apology Amid AI Subsidiary Backlash

Japanese developer Cygames, known for the *Umamusume* franchise, announced the creation of Cygames AI Studio, focusing on AI-assisted content creation. The announcement sparked strong negative reactions among fans, particularly those of *Umamusume*. Following widespread criticism, Cygames issued an apology, acknowledging concerns about generative AI and promising transparency about its use in products. The company clarified that no generative AI art was currently used in their releases and pledged prior notice before implementation. However, job postings related to generative AI remain active, which has tempered fan confidence. This incident reflects the growing tensions in the gaming community around AI integration and the balance between innovation and preserving creative integrity.

Valheim Player Builds Dollar Generals to Frustrate Friends

In *Valheim*, player Greg The Sorcerer has been deliberately building Dollar General convenience stores across a shared server, much to the annoyance of fellow players. Starting with one fluorescent-lit store, Greg expanded with multiple Dollar Generals and even a Waffle House, complete with a skeleton employee. Friends have expressed frustration with these incongruous modern buildings disrupting the Viking-themed game environment. While it is a humorous example of player antics, it underscores the social dynamics and freedom within sandbox multiplayer games, where in-game behavior can affect group harmony.

Corsair Air 5400 PC Case Review

Corsair has released the Air 5400, a successor to its well-regarded Air 540 chassis. The new case features a triple-chamber design separating the main components, cable management, and radiator cooling to improve airflow and thermal performance. It supports up to E-ATX motherboards and includes options for RGB fans. Hinged tempered glass panels allow easy access to components, enhancing usability for builders. During testing, the case maintained low CPU and GPU temperatures, partly due to its innovative airflow ducts. Overall, the Air 5400 integrates modern features into a familiar form, appealing to enthusiasts who appreciated its predecessor.
