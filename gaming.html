Woman "Marries" Cardboard Kazuma Kiryu at Like a Dragon 20th Anniversary Event

At a recent Sega event celebrating the 20th anniversary of the Like a Dragon (Yakuza) series in Tokyo, a woman known as lala_nyan0617 on X symbolically "married" a cardboard cutout of Kazuma Kiryu, the series' protagonist. Wearing a wedding dress, she posted photos expressing happiness about "meeting" Kiryu, pledging support to him. The event, titled "Weddings, Funerals, and Other Ceremonies Exhibition," runs through December 22 and offers fans opportunities to enact symbolic ceremonies reflecting themes of life and drama found in the game series. Though not a real wedding, such displays underscore the deep connections fans feel to game characters. Another instance of the exhibit is planned for Osaka in February 2026.

Codex Mortis: A Roguelite Created Entirely by AI

Developer Crunchfest3 has released a demo for Codex Mortis, a roguelike game built entirely through generative AI tools, claiming it as the "world's first fully playable game created 100% through AI." The game was constructed without a traditional engine, using TypeScript and libraries like PIXI.js, with AI systems like Claude Code and ChatGPT generating much of the code, art, and animations. The game is available in demo form on Steam and plays as a bullet-hell style game reminiscent of Vampire Survivors, though the art and design show typical AI-generated roughness. While previous AI-assisted games exist, Codex Mortis' release on Steam highlights ongoing experimentation with AI-driven game development, even if it raises questions about quality and originality.

Fate of the Old Republic Director Commits to Release Before 2030

Casey Hudson, co-founder of Arcanaut Studios and director of the upcoming Star Wars RPG Fate of the Old Republic (FotOR), has publicly promised that the game will launch before 2030. This statement responds to skepticism following reports that Arcanaut was only founded in 2025, making a 2030 release optimistic for a AAA title. Hudson emphasized on Twitter that the development cycle would be about four and a half years, expressing personal motivation tied to his age. While preproduction is likely underway, completing a large-scale RPG within this timeframe will be challenging given the typical scope of modern game development. Nonetheless, the comment provides a tentative timeline for fans awaiting the next major Star Wars RPG.

French President Emmanuel Macron Praises Clair Obscur After Game Awards Success

Following Clair Obscur: Expedition 33's win of nine awards at The Game Awards, including Game of the Year, French President Emmanuel Macron publicly congratulated its developer Sandfall Interactive. Macron called the achievement a historic first for a French title and a source of pride for Montpellier and France. His message marked the second public acknowledgment this year, highlighting the increased recognition of video games as significant cultural works. Clair Obscur's success has elevated Sandfall Interactive's status in the RPG genre and signaled growing national attention to French game development on the global stage.

Prolific Voice Actor Jim Ward Passes Away at 66

Jim Ward, a veteran voice actor known for roles in numerous games including Knights of the Old Republic, Fallout: New Vegas, Grim Fandango, and Ratchet & Clank, has died at 66 due to complications from Alzheimer's disease. Ward's career spanned decades, voicing characters such as Dr. Klein in Fallout: New Vegas and Trask Ulgo in KOTOR. He was noted for his versatility and contribution to early fully voiced RPGs. Fans and colleagues have expressed condolences and memories of his work, marking the loss of a respected figure in the gaming voice acting community.
