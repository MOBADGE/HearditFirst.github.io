Arc Raiders: Quest Walkthroughs and Blueprint Drop Surge

Two quests from the recent Cold Snap update in *Arc Raiders* have garnered attention today, alongside notable reports from the player community about in-game loot.

**Deciphering the Data Quest** This quest involves interacting with terminals in two buildings within the Spaceport area: Fuel Control and the Arrival Building. Players start by using the Magnetic Decryptor on a terminal in the Fuel Control Building, located near the Launch Towers and Rocket Assembly. After the first terminal breaks, the objective shifts to finding a second terminal on the top floor of the Arrival Building, tucked between rows of machines. The quest does not require extraction, allowing players to use a free loadout. Successfully interacting with both terminals completes the quest.

Players need to carefully manage the key to avoid getting stuck. Upon completion, players receive rewards including a new variation of the Warden outfit.

**Blueprint Drop Rates Increase Post-Update** Following the Cold Snap update, players have reported a significant increase in rare blueprint drops, especially on maps affected by the Cold Snap condition. Several users posted images showing backpacks filled predominantly with blueprints. While this abundance is welcomed by many, some players expressed concerns that constant access to blueprints may reduce their sense of rarity and the game's challenge. Embark Studios has not issued an official statement regarding these changes yet, so it is unclear if this is a temporary bonus or an unintended bug.

Hardware Scam Highlights Risks Amid Rising Prices

A new scam targeting PC hardware shoppers has surfaced, illustrating risks in the current tech market.

**DDR5 Memory Replacement Scam** A user known as BravoNorris purchased multiple kits of DDR5 memory from Amazon Spain, only to find that one sealed box contained DDR2 sticks with deceptive stickers mimicking DDR5 heat spreaders. The fake package also included a small metal plate to mask weight differences. This suggests return fraud, where genuine products are replaced with counterfeit components and resold as new.

**Broader Context** This scam emerges in the midst of ongoing supply issues and high prices in the memory market, dubbed the "memory apocalypse." Similar fraudulent activities have been seen with graphics card box swaps and scalping during hardware shortages. Experts recommend recording detailed unboxing videos to provide evidence if disputes arise.
