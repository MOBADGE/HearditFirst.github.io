Zero Parades Shows Promising Flashes Beyond Disco Elysium Style

At this year's Game Developers Conference, RPG developer ZA/UM showcased a longer demo of Zero Parades, their follow-up to the acclaimed Disco Elysium. The game takes a different tone as a spy thriller but retains the original’s verbose style and dialogue-driven gameplay. Notable moments include a witty exchange at a locked intercom, showcasing unexpected comedy and player choice shaping the protagonist, spy Hershel. Another scene involved a failed skill check at a keypad that humorously extended the interaction with multiple dialogue tries rather than ending abruptly.

These instances suggest that while Zero Parades moves away from the original’s amnesiac lead and grim tone, it aims to maintain the distinctive narrative depth and creative problem-solving. The game’s tone is still settling, but ZA/UM appears to be finding new ways to deliver engaging, text-based moments reflecting their previous success.

Seed: An MMO Where Your Character Lives On Offline

Klang Games, founded by former EVE Online developers, presented Seed, an MMO with persistent characters that continue their lives even when players are offline. At GDC, the team introduced a trailer emphasizing society simulation aspects influenced by games like RimWorld, Dwarf Fortress, and The Sims. Players join societies that can range from large cyberpunk cities to small villages, shaping laws, economies, and culture collectively.

Players can manage multiple characters ("Seedlings") living in different societies, allowing diverse playstyles. An example from the closed beta involved a bread market dispute influencing societal leadership through taxation and elections—an echo of EVE Online's player-driven politics. Seed also offers a mobile app to monitor and interact with characters remotely, utilizing generative AI for conversations, though this feature’s appeal is uncertain. The project remains in closed beta with no firm launch date.

Shadowstone Begins Public Playtest Ahead of 2026 Early Access

Secret Door and Dreamhaven launched a surprise public playtest for Shadowstone, a tactics roguelike spinoff of Sunderfolk, during the Future Games Show Spring Showcase. The playtest is open until early access, planned for later in 2026 though no exact date is set. Shadowstone features 1-4 player matches focusing on escape runs through randomized ruins, with evolving skill cards and equipment affecting strategies.

Unlike Sunderfolk, which uses smartphones as controllers, Shadowstone employs a standard PC interface, streamlining some RPG elements for shorter run-based gameplay. Progress in the playtest will not carry over to the full release, and players are encouraged to stream and discuss their experiences to help the developers showcase the game.

Industry Veteran Rob Pardo Urges Stability Amid Layoffs

In his keynote at GDC, Rob Pardo, former Blizzard chief creative officer, emphasized the value of development teams over individual game successes. Citing his experience on landmark titles like Starcraft and Warcraft 3, he warned against the industry trend of laying off successful developers post-release, highlighting that teams are essential for replicating success amid the unpredictability of game development.

Pardo shared insights on how iterative development and learning from missteps underlie hit games, urging executives to "treasure" their teams and provide autonomy to maintain player care. His message adds to ongoing discussions about workplace stability in an industry that has recently seen significant layoffs even after commercially successful launches.

Path of Exile’s Mirage League Rebalances Economy and Endgame

Grinding Gear Games released the Mirage league for Path of Exile, introducing significant adjustments to currency drop rates—the largest since a 2019 overhaul. The changes increase the frequency of currency drops directly from enemies, improving player ability to craft and trade without extensive farming or shopping.

Central to the league is the mirage mechanic, allowing players to replay portions of levels with selectable "wishes" granting various rewards. This includes running popular map modifiers twice, effectively doubling boss fights and loot opportunities. Additionally, the atlas endgame has been reworked to use tiered map tokens instead of specific maps, aimed at enhancing player control and progression feel. Early impressions highlight smoother crafting access and richer endgame variety.
