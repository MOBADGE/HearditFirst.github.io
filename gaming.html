World of Warcraft's Midnight Expansion Faces Class Balance Challenges

World of Warcraft’s recent Midnight expansion has made significant changes to its combat system by limiting the use of addons that previously helped players manage ability rotations. While the change was necessary to streamline gameplay, some classes are struggling with the new balance. Players report that certain specialisations, especially those with burst damage windows, feel weaker outside their short peak periods.

This issue is reminiscent of a long-standing problem in Final Fantasy 14, where the combat revolves around a rigid two-minute burst cycle. In FF14, this design can make classes feel inactive or underpowered outside their burst windows, which complicates encounter design beyond traditional boss fights. World of Warcraft’s Midnight appears to be heading in a similar direction, which may reduce fun and engagement during world content where these burst windows are less relevant.

For WoW players who spend a lot of time questing or exploring, this imbalance could become more noticeable. Blizzard’s development approach is generally more responsive than Square Enix’s, so any negative feedback might lead to quicker adjustments moving forward.

Skyrim Modpack 'Curseadelica' Delivers a Surreal, Crash-Prone Experience

A new massive modpack for Skyrim, named Curseadelica and weighing in at 154 GB, offers a radically transformed version of the game that mixes surreal visuals, bizarre NPCs, and an overwhelming number of quests. Its creator intended to create a “feast for the eyes,” but many players, including one tester on Linux, found it to be more of an acid trip with frequent crashes.

The modpack replaces most of Skyrim’s content with psychedelic changes, new races, and questlines that often defy easy understanding. Although installation is straightforward with mod manager tools, performance is unstable, and many users have experienced multiple crashes. Despite this, the mod demonstrates both the potential and limits of Skyrim as a modding platform and highlights how far the modding community can push a classic game.

This serves as a reminder that while ambitious mod projects can greatly expand a game’s life, they can also compromise stability, requiring players to weigh enjoyment against technical risks.

Heroes of Science and Fiction Launches as a Sci-Fi Alternative to Heroes of Might and Magic

For fans of the classic turn-based strategy series Heroes of Might and Magic waiting for the new installment, a fresh alternative has just reached full release on Steam. Heroes of Science and Fiction adopts the familiar gameplay but places it in a sci-fi setting, where players control factions battling for control of star systems.

The game features five factions, each with unique units and abilities, plus four campaigns and skirmish maps. It supports multiplayer modes including online, LAN, and hot seat. After 18 months in early access, the game reached version 1.0 with additional content, AI improvements, and achievements. A launch discount is currently available, encouraging strategy players to explore this space-themed reinterpretation.

This release exemplifies how genre favorites can be reinvented in new settings while maintaining core mechanics, giving players fresh yet familiar experiences during gaps between major franchise releases.

Death Wish 2.0 Mod Expands Classic FPS Blood with New Maps and Episode

The cult classic first-person shooter Blood has received an extensive update to one of its best fan-made mods, Death Wish. The 2.0 version features overhauled or completely new maps, an additional fourth episode integrated into the storyline, new cutscenes, and a custom soundtrack.

Death Wish 2.0 now includes 48 maps in total, surpassing the original game’s count. The fourth episode ranges from ancient tombs to futuristic museums, broadening the mod’s scope. Death Wish remains available as a free update for owners of Nightdive’s Blood: Refreshed Supply, or as a standalone download.

This update highlights the enduring appeal of retro shooters and the creativity of modding communities that continue to expand and refine classic games decades after their release.
