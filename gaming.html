Logitech Launches New Gaming Mouse Technology with the G Pro X2 Superstrike

Logitech introduced the G Pro X2 Superstrike, a gaming mouse featuring a new analog click technology termed HITS (Haptic Inductive Trigger System). This system enables adjustable actuation points and rapid trigger responses by using electromagnetic induction to detect precise button presses rather than the common Hall effect sensors found in most gaming peripherals. The result is significantly reduced click latency, which can improve performance in shooter games by shortening the delay between input and action.

Unlike mechanical or optical switches, the Superstrike‚Äôs inductive sensing uses coils and a moving metal plate to measure button travel with great accuracy over a very short distance (under 1 mm). The technology also incorporates haptic feedback motors to simulate the tactile click feel, a feature Logitech developed closely with professional gamers to ensure a satisfying user experience. Early testing shows the Superstrike can reduce click latency by 15 to 30 milliseconds compared to traditional mice. Logitech prioritized bringing this innovation to market quickly after a one-year development cycle, aiming to set a new industry standard.
