Fortnite Chapter 7 Season 2 Launch and Updates

Fortnite Chapter 7 Season 2, titled "Showdown," will begin on March 19, after a two-week delay from its originally planned early March launch. The season is set to introduce significant, though typical, changes such as a refreshed loot pool, new movement items including a group-use feature, and a "new-old meta loot spot" that activates mid-game. The storyline centers around the mission to save The Foundation, a character played by Dwayne Johnson, who is currently frozen in ice and held captive by the Ice King. This plotline revisits elements from Chapter 1 Season 7 where a similar scenario unfolded. Despite fan expectations, the current season will not end with a major live event, but smaller "story moments" have taken place recently.

Alongside gameplay updates, the battle pass includes collaborations with Bugs Bunny and reportedly an Overwatch set of skins, though the latter lacks confirmation in official key art. Other rumored collaborations include Toy Story-themed items, but these are not confirmed for the battle pass. Notably, Epic Games is raising the price of V-Bucks, Fortnite's in-game currency, and reducing the amount given in the battle pass and Fortnite Crew subscriptions. The delay in Season 2's launch is believed to be connected to ongoing technical issues experienced since January.

Cyberpunk Trading Card Game Kickstarter Surpasses $3 Million

WeirdCo, a tabletop game developer, launched a Kickstarter for Cyberpunk TCG, a new trading card game based on CD Projekt Red's Cyberpunk universe. The campaign swiftly surpassed its $100,000 funding goal, raising over $3.6 million within an hour and unlocking multiple stretch goals. Cyberpunk TCG involves players competing by collecting "Gigs" through dice mechanics and card battles involving characters from Cyberpunk 2077. The rapid funding indicates strong community interest in expanding Cyberpunk content into physical tabletop formats.

Nvidia Announces DLSS 5 with AI-Driven Lighting, Receives Mixed Feedback

Nvidia revealed DLSS 5, the next iteration of its Deep Learning Super Sampling technology. The update introduces a neural rendering model that enhances lighting and material properties on a per-pixel basis for games, aiming to deliver photorealistic visuals. Demonstrations have included enhancements to character models in titles like Resident Evil Requiem and Starfield. However, the reception among the gaming community has been notably critical. Some players feel the lighting alterations significantly change character appearances, sometimes in ways that make them look less authentic or overly "beautified." Nvidia compares this advancement to the introduction of programmable shaders in the early 2000s, signaling a major technological shift.

Starfield Arrives on PlayStation 5 with Major DLC and Updates

Bethesda announced that Starfield will launch on PlayStation 5 on April 7. Simultaneously, the "Terran Armada" and "Free Lanes" updates, described as the largest post-launch content and improvements to date, will release across all platforms. This brings the game to what Bethesda calls its most complete version, addressing player feedback and expanding content. The PS5 launch offers an opportunity for a broader audience to access Bethesda’s space RPG.

March 2026 Game Pass Additions and Departures

Xbox Game Pass continues to add diverse titles for March 2026. Recent additions include DreamWorks Gabby’s Dollhouse: Ready to Party for younger players, and upcoming releases such as Disco Elysium (March 19), Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth (March 24), and Resident Evil 7: Biohazard (March 31). Older titles like Absolum and South of Midnight are also joining the service, while some games will be leaving in the coming weeks. Early April will see Clair Obscur Expedition 33 and Final Fantasy IV added to Game Pass Premium and other tiers. These additions reflect Game Pass’s ongoing strategy to provide a wide range of genres and appeal to different player demographics.

Take-Two CEO Comments on In-Game Advertising in Full-Price Games

Strauss Zelnick, CEO of Take-Two Interactive, the parent company of the Grand Theft Auto series, spoke against the inclusion of in-game advertisements in full-priced games. He stated that ads are acceptable in free-to-play models due to their business structure but would be unfair in games sold at premium prices of $70 or $80.
