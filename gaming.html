The Simpsons Hit & Run Revival: A Possibility, Not a Promise

Matt Selman, executive producer of *The Simpsons*, expressed cautious optimism about a potential revival of the 2007 cult-favorite game *The Simpsons: Hit & Run*. In an interview, Selman acknowledged the unexpected enduring popularity of the game, which offers open-world exploration and racing through Springfield. While no formal plans have been announced, his "never say never" stance leaves room for fans hoping for a remaster or rerelease. Selman, however, prefers the original 2007 release, praising its storytelling quality. The revival of this title could give new audiences access to what many consider the best Simpsons game, though any developments remain uncertain.

Concerns Over Samson’s Upcoming Launch After Mixed Trailer Reception

*Samson*, an open-city game from Liquid Swords led by *Just Cause* creator Cristofer Sundberg, is set to launch on April 8 but has drawn mixed reactions following its latest gameplay trailer. The game features a debt-driven protagonist navigating a gritty industrial city under time constraints and action point limits designed to create tension. While the driving mechanics look promising, brawling combat appears repetitive and underdeveloped. Sparse population and mission variety have also raised questions. Despite these reservations, the game’s unique premise and aesthetic set it apart from recent open-world titles seeking a tighter, more manageable experience than massive offerings like *Grand Theft Auto 6*. The launch will reveal if *Samson* can deliver a satisfying gameplay loop.

Total Chaos Remake Revisits a Classic Doom Mod with Atmosphere but Struggles in Combat

Sam Prebble’s *Total Chaos* remake updates a celebrated *Doom 2* mod into a standalone premium survival horror FPS using Unreal Engine 5. The game excels in crafting a bleak and tense atmosphere within the industrial, maze-like town of Fort Oasis, blending psychological horror with complex level design and puzzles. However, its melee combat and resource balance have been critiqued as less satisfying, with combat feeling light and over-reliant on plentiful resources. The addition of a new game plus mode offers a more challenging experience, hinting at the game’s potential when stripped of excess. Overall, while *Total Chaos* pays homage to its mod roots and stands out as a narrative-driven horror project, it falls short of the innovative heights of Prebble’s previous work, *Turbo Overkill*.

Embark Studios Moves Away from AI Voices in Arc Raiders, but AI Still Tools for Production

Embark Studios’ CEO Patrick Söderlund confirmed the studio is replacing a significant amount of AI-generated dialogue in *Arc Raiders* with recordings by professional voice actors. This decision comes amid criticism of AI voices for their lack of emotional depth and realism. Söderlund clarified that AI remains a valuable internal tool for testing and iteration, not a replacement for human performances. The move reflects ongoing industry debates around AI’s role in game development, balancing efficiency gains against quality and ethical considerations. However, Embark does not intend to eliminate AI usage entirely, highlighting its utility in the production process despite its current limits in matching human actors.

Dead Space Franchise Sale Offers Significant Discounts but Future Sequels Unlikely

EA has launched a franchise sale on *Dead Space* games, featuring up to 85% off, notably including the 2023 *Dead Space* remake priced at $9. The remake updates the original with enhanced visuals, new mechanics, and full voice work. The original sequels, *Dead Space 2* and *3*, are also available at reduced prices, although *Dead Space 3* is generally less well-regarded. Despite strong sales and recognized quality, prospects for a *Dead Space 2* remake or a new installment seem minimal. EA reportedly rejected a pitch for *Dead Space 4* in 2024 and has shown a preference for investing in sports franchises and other priorities. The sale may attract new players but is unlikely to influence EA’s franchise strategy significantly.
