Echo Point Nova Expands with New Game+ and Movement Modes

Indie developer Greylock Studio will release a significant update for their acclaimed open-world FPS *Echo Point Nova* later this month. The update introduces a New Game+ mode that allows players to carry over specific unlocks and customize their replay experience, including options for randomizing weapon pickups and enemy spawns. Additionally, the game will receive three new movement modes: a jetpack, wallrun-enabled sprint, and a rope grapple mechanic reminiscent of Spider-Man’s swinging. These additions offer more varied ways to navigate the game’s sky islands, enriching player mobility and strategic options. This update continues the game’s emphasis on fluid, high-speed combat and movement, an aspect praised since the title’s release last year.

State of Decay 3 Announces Alpha Tests for May

*State of Decay 3*, the long-anticipated zombie survival sequel from Undead Labs, has confirmed alpha testing sessions scheduled for May. This marks the first substantial activity since the game’s announcement in 2020 and a brief teaser in 2024. The alpha will focus on four-player co-op, new base-building mechanics, resource management, and combat enhancements. Fans interested in early access can register at the official playtest website, with further testing opportunities promised throughout the year. This development signals that the project is progressing beyond extended silence and could provide concrete insights into its gameplay and direction ahead of launch.

Rust’s Spring Update Adds Water Wheels and New Base Defenses

The latest patch for the survival game *Rust* introduces water wheels as a new source of hydroelectric power. Players can place water wheels in rivers or oceans to generate electricity, supplementing the game’s existing wind and solar power options. Unusually, these wheels can also be powered by players running inside them, including forcing handcuffed players into this “hamster wheel” mechanic. The update also introduces an armoured ladder hatch to improve base protection, coloured buttons for better control clarity, and an overfishing system to maintain the balance of marine resources. Seasonal content for Easter includes an egg hunt and themed cosmetics. These changes expand base-building strategies and add new tactical elements for players.

HoYoVerse Opens Beta Signups for Petit Planet

HoYoVerse has begun accepting registrations for a closed beta test of *Petit Planet*, a forthcoming cozy galactic life simulation. The beta, titled the Stardrift Test, starts on April 21 and will add new activities, NPC neighbors, and improvements to the game’s central hub, the Galactic Bazaar. Players manage their own planets, engaging in farming, fishing, cooking, and social interaction within the Starsea universe. Although the release date remains unannounced, insider information suggests the game is nearing completion. This beta will serve as an important milestone for refining gameplay and gathering player feedback.

Raven Software Co-founder Brian Raffel Retires After 36 Years

Brian Raffel, co-founder and longtime leader of Raven Software, announced his retirement after a 36-year career in the games industry. Raffel helped establish Raven in 1990 and guided it through its acquisition by Activision. Under his leadership, the studio produced influential shooters like *Heretic*, *Hexen*, and the *Jedi Knight* series, before shifting focus to contributions on *Call of Duty* titles including *Warzone* and several Black Ops campaigns. Raffel’s departure marks the end of an era for the studio, which has been a notable presence in FPS development for decades. Industry peers have acknowledged his role in shaping the company and maintaining its relevance over multiple generations of games.

Bungie Details Upcoming Buffs for Marathon’s Recon Shell

Bungie has shared details on changes coming to the Recon Shell in their extraction shooter *Marathon*, addressing concerns about the character’s relative weakness. The April 14 update will improve Recon’s Echo Pulse ability by distinguishing between AI enemies and human players, and reducing its visibility to foes to prevent easy counterplay. Additionally, the tracker drone that assists Recon will be “more aggressive,” able to switch targets if blocked, reducing frustrating moments when it becomes stuck on terrain. These adjustments aim to make Recon more competitive within the game’s roster and improve overall player experience for those who favor the intelligence-based playstyle. Further balance changes to other characters will be announced soon.
