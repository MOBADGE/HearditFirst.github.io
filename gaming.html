Updated: April 16, 2026

Moves of the Diamond Hand Enters Early Access Indie developer Cosmo D has launched *Moves of the Diamond Hand* in early access, presenting a unique RPG that combines adventure, politics, and dice-based skill checks. Set in the surreal noir setting of Off-Peak City, the game mixes open-ended narrative quests with a tactile dice-rolling mechanic reminiscent of tabletop games. Players pursue multiple storylines, including joining an eccentric circus and navigating a contentious mayoral election involving clones and political rivals. The game features a light stealth system and a complex urban environment that rewards skill development across cooking, deception, music, and more. While early access status means some quests can be incomplete or contain bugs, the game shows promise as a distinct, narrative-rich RPG for players who appreciate exploration and story-driven gameplay. A full release is targeted for spring 2027.

Pickmos Removed From Steam Amid Publisher Intervention Pickmos, formerly known as Pickmon, has been pulled from Steam following controversy over its striking similarities to Pokémon and other popular games. Developed by PocketGame and published by NetworkGo, Pickmos drew criticism for its close imitation of *Palworld*, Pokémon, and even *The Legend of Zelda*. In response, publisher NetworkGo announced official intervention, signaling that development will be supervised to address player concerns and avoid legal issues. The developer confirmed the game is being revised and will return only after publisher approval. The situation highlights ongoing challenges in the industry regarding intellectual property and imitation games. Whether Pickmos will reappear remains unclear.

Phasmophobia Hosts Alan Wake Crossover Event in May *Phasmophobia*, the cooperative ghost-hunting game, will feature a limited-time crossover event with *Alan Wake 2* starting May 12 through June 2. This marks the first collaboration event for *Phasmophobia,* introducing a new "presence" based on the supernatural elements of the Alan Wake universe. Players will encounter a new type of mimic entity that alters investigations in unique ways. The event will add themed in-game rewards but details remain scarce. Developer Kinetic Games noted the event is a one-time occurrence but left open the possibility of future collaborations depending on player reception. The crossover combines narrative elements from both games, expanding the atmosphere and content in *Phasmophobia*.

Call of Duty Movie Scheduled for June 2028 Activision and Paramount have confirmed the release date for the *Call of Duty* movie as June 30, 2028. The film’s development includes involvement from writer Taylor Sheridan and director Peter Berg. While details about the plot remain limited, it is expected to follow a style consistent with military action films, focusing on intense conflict and espionage themes. The announcement formalizes the timeline for the highly anticipated adaptation of the popular shooter franchise but places the release two years in the future.

Lana Del Rey Releases 007 First Light Theme Song, Mixed Reception Lana Del Rey has released the theme song “First Light” for the upcoming *007 First Light* video game. The track aims to fit within the James Bond musical tradition but has received a lukewarm response, with critics citing a lack of impact and originality compared to classic Bond themes. The music includes typical elements like orchestral strings and brass, but many find it generally uninspired. This reception contrasts with Del Rey’s usual acclaim and highlights the subjective nature of Bond theme songs, which often divide audiences.

Metro 2039 Revealed With Darker Tone and Voiced Protagonist The new *Metro 2039* game, the first non-VR installment since 2019, was unveiled at the Xbox First Look event. The storyline centers around a character called "The Stranger," a voiced protagonist navigating a war-torn Moscow Metro controlled by a Nazi faction named Novoreich, led by an ominous figure called the Fuhrer. The narrative addresses themes of propaganda, tyranny, and the cost of freedom, influenced by current global events. The game promises a darker tone and a handcrafted single-player experience with high-end graphics. It is developed by 4A Games in collaboration with original author Dmitry Glukhovsky and is scheduled for release this winter.
