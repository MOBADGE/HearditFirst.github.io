**Arc Raiders Nerfs the Il Toro Shotgun in Update 1.20.0** The recent 1.20.0 update for Arc Raiders brings significant changes to the Il Toro shotgun, a weapon well-known for its dominant performance in close quarters, especially on the Stella Montis map. Embark, the developer, reduced the shotgun's pellet damage, fire rate, and accuracy while increasing reload times and damage fall-off at range. This adjustment aims to better align the green-quality Il Toro with its intended power level and address community feedback on its overwhelming strength in PvP encounters. Additionally, the update cuts the sell price of Energy Clips to prevent exploitative crafting loops that previously allowed easy in-game currency gains. These changes will likely reshape Arc Raiders’ weapon meta and economic balance, with further tuning expected based on player response.

**Ark: Survival Ascended Incorporates Pirate Elements from Atlas in New DLC** Studio Wildcard is preparing a new expansion called Tides of Fortune for Ark: Survival Ascended, which introduces large pirate ships and networked ocean physics inspired by their earlier title Atlas. Although Atlas struggled with a punishing game loop and technical issues, its sailing mechanics and ocean interactions received praise. The Tides of Fortune DLC seeks to bring these elements into Ark in a more accessible form, allowing players to build and customize ships, engage in naval exploration, and transport dinosaurs on board. The revamped ocean physics promise improvements over Ark’s traditionally static water, featuring waves, wakes, and realistic lighting effects. This integration represents an effort to enrich Ark’s gameplay with fresh mechanics while leveraging lessons from Atlas.

**Comprehensive Guide to Monster Mutations in Monster Hunter Stories 3** Monster Hunter Stories 3 offers an ecosystem-based mutation system enabling players to obtain rare, stronger monster subspecies by restoring habitats and meeting specific environmental conditions. The article details how to unlock each mutation, such as the Pink Rathian, Dreadqueen Rathian, Ivory Lagiacrus, Hellblade Glavenus, and others, by raising Ecosystem Ranks and introducing particular combinations of monsters. Mutations improve stats and abilities, providing strategic benefits though not mandatory for progressing through the story. Understanding these requirements helps players plan which monsters to raise and release to trigger desired mutations, enhancing team composition and gameplay depth.

**Overwatch Director Reflects on 5v5 Versus 6v6 Format Controversy and Communication** Aaron Keller, Overwatch’s game director, addressed the long-running debate over the game’s shift from six-player to five-player teams in Overwatch 2. The change, implemented in 2022, was highly controversial and led to community dissatisfaction. Keller acknowledged that the development team delayed engaging with player concerns for about a year, hoping the debate would subside. When Blizzard finally tested and reintroduced a 6v6 mode in 2024, it became the second most popular mode but didn’t fully resolve player division. Keller emphasized that the delay in communication cost player trust and stressed the importance of listening and adapting to community feedback in future live service development. The talk highlighted ongoing challenges in balancing innovation with core game identities.

**Former Blizzard Designer Jeff Kaplan Shares Views on Game Criticism and WoW Development** Jeff Kaplan, previously a vice president and game designer at Blizzard, recently revealed insights from a 10-hour stream for his new indie studio, including his frustration with online negativity toward games players never engaged with. He criticized the prevalence of unproductive outrage and called on people to refrain from dismissive comments about games they have not played. Kaplan also shared historical context from early World of Warcraft development, saying he had advocated for a first-person perspective rather than the eventual third-person style. His reflections provide a candid perspective on game design decisions and community discourse, emphasizing constructive engagement and creative freedom.

**Bungie Tests Experimental Duos Mode in Marathon for Player Feedback** Bungie is introducing a temporary “experimental” duos mode in Marathon starting March 18, lasting roughly two weeks. This new queue, limited to the Perimeter map, responds to community interest in a dedicated two-player matchmaking option. The implementation is provisional, with Bungie aiming to observe how changes in squad sizes affect gameplay dynamics, map flow, and matchmaking wait times before committing to broader integration. The move reflects an iterative approach to enhancing multiplayer options, recognizing that player count shifts impact both game balance and social experiences.
