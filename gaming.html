Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous Offers Extensive Character Customization

Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous features an unusually large number of subclasses for players to choose from—over 230 spread across 26 base classes and several prestige classes. This makes it one of the most complex RPGs in terms of character building, appealing notably to players who enjoy deep customization and experiment with “buildcrafting.” Not all subclasses are equally useful, but some, such as the Ranger with its rideable griffon mount from level one, stand out for their power and uniqueness. Mounted combat is a strategic choice in this challenging game, allowing players to protect their fragile characters by focusing attacks on their mounts.

This depth of choice can also be daunting, especially for players who like to restart their campaigns repeatedly in search of the perfect build. The game’s complexity and difficulty offer a distinct experience compared to other modern RPGs, highlighting a niche for players seeking detailed tactical options and systems faithful to Pathfinder’s tabletop roots.

Star Wars Zero Company Director Discusses Resurgence of Classic PC Genres

Greg Foertsch, creative director of the upcoming Star Wars Zero Company, shared his perspective on why several old-school PC genres—including real-time strategy, turn-based tactics, and CRPGs—have made a comeback. He attributes a long lull in their popularity partly to the challenges these genres faced transitioning from PC to consoles during the early 2000s. Limitations in interface design and camera technology made it difficult to adapt complex, isometric strategy games to couch play on TVs.

Foertsch suggests that advances in user experience and camera control are allowing these genres to become more accessible on consoles, leading to wider acceptance and innovation. He points out that recent games in these genres are not only returning but also pushing forward with new ideas, and cross-platform releases like Baldur's Gate 3 are helping bring these traditionally PC-centric genres to broader audiences.

Owlcat Clarifies Use of Generative AI in The Expanse: Osiris Reborn Development

Owlcat Games confirmed that while generative AI is being used during early development stages of their upcoming RPG The Expanse: Osiris Reborn, it will not be responsible for any final game assets. The studio specified AI is employed for prototyping, placeholders, and “vision coordination” to speed up internal processes, but all final art and writing will be entirely human-made.

This approach reflects a cautious use of AI tools amid ongoing industry debate. Despite the studio's assurances, some players remain skeptical about AI’s involvement, highlighting continuing concerns over the ethical use of AI in game development and the impact on creative roles.

Mechabellum’s Strategy Gameplay Emphasizes Player Interaction

Mechabellum, a lesser-known 1v1 autobattler, has received new content and attention as its latest season launches. The game stands out through its emphasis on interaction and adaptation during matches rather than memorization or strict adherence to meta strategies. Players place units and respond to each other in a dynamic back-and-forth, creating a “conversation” that shapes each game’s flow.

Matches are short and conceptually clear, making it accessible for players with limited time while still offering strategic depth. This focus on responsive decision-making rather than fixed build orders differentiates Mechabellum in a genre often dominated by rigid optimization.

Elder Scrolls: Blades to Shut Down After Eight Years

Bethesda’s mobile and console Elder Scrolls spinoff, The Elder Scrolls: Blades, will shut down on June 30, after an eight-year run. The developers have marked down all in-game store items to a minimal price and provided players with free currency bundles to experience remaining content before closure. The game never launched on PC and was generally considered a modest mobile title with numerous in-app purchases.

Blades’ shutdown follows the discontinuation of another Elder Scrolls spin-off, Legends, indicating Bethesda is scaling back on smaller online projects. The closure highlights the difficulty of maintaining long-term live-service titles on mobile platforms and the limited preservation of such games compared to traditional PC and console releases.
