League of Legends Login Outage Caused by Expired SSL Certificate

On January 5, millions of *League of Legends* players worldwide faced hours-long login issues due to an expired SSL certificate in the game client. Riot Games had failed to renew the certificate, which is crucial for establishing secure encrypted connections. The game's login process stalled because the client's Windows system refused connections with the expired certificate.

This incident closely mirrors a similar problem Riot experienced exactly ten years ago, showing a lapse in maintaining this critical security component. Riot responded by releasing a client update to extend the certificate's validity until 2125, resolving the issue. The event exposed how a single overlooked technical detail can disrupt access to one of the world‚Äôs most popular multiplayer games, affecting millions simultaneously.

Sandisk Rebrands Popular SSDs Under the 'Optimus' Label

Sandisk has begun rebranding some of its widely used solid state drives, previously sold under the WD (Western Digital) name, under a new "Optimus" brand. This change follows the separation of Sandisk from WD, which is now focusing on hard drives, while Sandisk operates independently on SSDs.

The new lineup includes multiple tiers: Sandisk Optimus, Optimus GX, and Optimus GX Pro, with familiar drives like the WD_Black SN7100 rebranded as Optimus GX 7100. Despite the name change, the hardware and performance remain unchanged. These branding adjustments will roll out during 2026, aiming to build Sandisk's distinct identity in the SSD market. However, the move may cause some short-term confusion due to the loss of WD's well-established branding.
