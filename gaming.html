Budget Alternatives to 2026’s Major Game Releases

A recent PC Gamer article explores less expensive indie and niche games as alternatives to some of the biggest AAA titles coming out in 2026. The writer highlights games like *Samson* and *The Precinct* as substitutes for open-world crime games like *Grand Theft Auto 6*. Other examples include *Wreckreation* for fans of *Forza Horizon 6* and narrative-driven horror games like *Fear the Spotlight* as budget options compared to *Resident Evil Requiem*. This approach may appeal to gamers looking to avoid the increasingly steep costs of AAA releases and associated hardware requirements.

Survey Reveals Most PC Gamers Keep Their PC on the Desk

In a recent reader survey conducted by PC Gamer, 37% of respondents reported that their gaming PC is located beside them on their desk—considered the “correct” answer. Close behind, 34% admit to keeping their PCs on the floor. Other common PC placements include being on a stage attached to the desk (12%) and next to the TV (7%). These results provide a glimpse into gaming setups, highlighting that a significant portion of players treat their machines as an accessible part of their workspace, while many others prioritize out-of-the-way or space-saving locations.

Microsoft’s Project Helix Chip to Power Third-Party Xbox Machines, Not Available Separately

Leaks indicate Microsoft's upcoming next-generation Xbox console, powered by the AMD-engineered Project Helix chip, will serve as the foundation for third-party devices made by companies such as Asus and MSI. However, the Helix chip itself will not be sold to consumers as a standalone component. This preserves Microsoft's control over hardware configuration and distribution, differentiating Project Helix from off-the-shelf AMD chips. For PC gaming enthusiasts, this means no budget-friendly standalone Helix gaming rigs are expected, although OEM machines based on the chip could provide alternatives to official Xbox consoles.

Pragmata’s Path Tracing Sparks Debate but No Evidence of GPU Sales Conspiracy

Capcom’s sci-fi shooter *Pragmata* offers both ray tracing and a more demanding path tracing mode. Some users on Reddit and elsewhere speculated that stark visual differences between the two might be a deliberate attempt to push gamers toward investing in higher-end GPUs capable of handling path tracing. Testing shows that path tracing produces more defined shadows and lighting effects, especially in dim scenes, but the ray-traced mode still looks visually solid. The game's performance at 4K max settings maintains smooth framerates with ray tracing on, while path tracing significantly reduces performance. The analysis concludes that these differences are most likely technical rather than conspiratorial, reflecting a choice between graphical fidelity and performance.

Kiki Wolfkill Leaves Microsoft After Nearly 30 Years

Kiki Wolfkill, a longtime Microsoft veteran credited with contributions to franchises including *Halo*, *Fable*, *Mass Effect*, and *Forza Motorsport*, has departed the company as of April 17, 2026. Wolfkill held leadership roles at 343 Industries and later became head of Xbox Film and TV, overseeing projects such as the *Halo* TV series. She posted a message expressing gratitude for her time at Microsoft and excitement about future endeavors outside the company. Wolfkill’s departure marks the exit of a significant figure involved in Microsoft’s gaming and media initiatives.

Fallout: New Vegas Writer Details Bethesda’s Harsh Feedback to Obsidian

Chris Avellone, a key writer for *Fallout: New Vegas*, revealed that after the game’s release, Bethesda subjected Obsidian Entertainment to a detailed internal critique, including a PowerPoint presentation highlighting perceived shortcomings of the studio. Avellone noted that Bethesda used review scores to justify not continuing collaborations with Obsidian on DLCs, despite the studio’s efforts to deliver a distinct survival horror tone in *Dead Money*. While Avellone’s remarks come amid personal controversies, his account provides insight into some of the tensions behind the scenes during the production and post-release phases of the highly regarded RPG.
