**Timberborn Officially Releases With New Automation Features** The city-builder Timberborn has exited early access with its 1.0 release, adding significant new content including a system of automation reminiscent of Factorio. Developed by Mechanistry, the update introduces twenty new buildings functioning as logic nodes to enable programming of structures to operate independently. This includes general components like levers and sensors, as well as specialized elements such as detonators useful for signaling. Players can now automate tasks like closing floodgates in response to environmental hazards or pausing production when resources run low, adding depth to the city management experience. Since its initial alpha, Timberborn has steadily expanded with features like water pumps, ziplines, and robotic beavers. The game maintains a very positive reception, holding a 95% rating on Steam. A launch discount is currently available until March 26. *Why it matters:* This release marks a maturation of Timberborn into a more complex and versatile city-builder. The automation system opens up new gameplay possibilities, appealing both to fans of meticulous management and those who appreciate technical challenges.

**Project Shadowglass Trailer Highlights Deep Influence from Thief Series** Starhelm Studios released a new trailer for Project Shadowglass, a stealth-focused fantasy heist game, emphasizing its heavy inspiration from Looking Glass’s Thief: The Dark Project. The game features pixelated yet fully 3D graphics that combine nostalgic aesthetics with modern freedom of movement. The trailer showcased stealth mechanics such as rooftop navigation, blackjack knockouts, and water arrows used to extinguish lights. Combat is deliberately described as "authentically sloppy," aligning with the stealth gameplay ethos. The game also promises mission planning phases, long-term consequences for player actions, and prison escape scenarios if caught. Although still in early development with no set release date, a demo is planned for later this year. *Why it matters:* Project Shadowglass attempts to revive and build upon classic stealth gameplay using modern technology and design approaches. If successful, it could fill a niche for fans of tactical stealth games longing for complex, immersive heists.

**Deep Rock Galactic: Survivor Announces 'Heavy Duty' Expansion with New Class** The roguelike action shooter Deep Rock Galactic: Survivor is set to receive an expansion titled Heavy Duty on April 30. This update introduces the Demolisher, the first new class added to the series, piloting a vehicle called the Rock Dozer that can flatten terrain and enemies alike. Players will have access to three class mods focusing on mining output, mobility, or machinery efficiency. Additionally, the expansion will add 10 new weapons usable by all classes, featuring elemental and automated gadget types. The developers have also mentioned two significant unannounced features yet to be revealed. The base game is currently discounted on Steam until March 18. *Why it matters:* Adding a new class and substantial content signals ongoing support for Deep Rock Galactic: Survivor, expanding its gameplay variety and player options. The introduction of a vehicle-centric class could shift strategies and attract players seeking fresh combat dynamics.

**Monster Hunter Stories 3 Guides: How to Defeat Invasive Monsters and Find Endangered Species** Capcom’s Monster Hunter Stories 3 features powerful variants known as Invasive Monsters that guard the dens of rare Endangered Species. These monsters require specific tactics rather than standard combat approaches to drive them away and claim valuable eggs. For example, players must break an Invasive Yian Garuga's legs or exploit the Invasive Plesioth’s fire weakness to break its head. The guide details strategies for all nine Invasive Monsters and maps their locations alongside their respective Endangered Species within the game’s world. Successfully dealing with these monsters is crucial to obtaining some of the strongest companions. *Why it matters:* Understanding the mechanics and locations of Invasive Monsters and Endangered Species is key to progressing and enhancing the player’s roster in Monster Hunter Stories 3. These guides provide clarity on challenging encounters that otherwise could be frustrating or confusing.
