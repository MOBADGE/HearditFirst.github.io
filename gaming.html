Fighting Game YouTuber Offers $10,000 Bounty to Improve Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3 Netcode

Fighting game content creator Maximillian Christiansen, known online as Maximilian Dood, has announced a $10,000 bounty for anyone able to develop rollback netcode for *Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3* (UMvC3). The game currently relies on delay-based netcode, which can result in sluggish and unresponsive online matches. Rollback netcode, on the other hand, predicts inputs to reduce latency and improve the online experience in precision-dependent fighting games.

Christiansen has a history of supporting fan mods, having invested nearly $30,000 into a mod for *Marvel vs. Capcom Infinite*. He expressed hope that the fighting game community could eventually move beyond stopgap solutions like Parsec—a remote play software commonly used by UMvC3 players—and implement modern netcode akin to what communities around classic games like *Super Smash Bros. Melee* have achieved.

Achieving rollback netcode for UMvC3 would be significant, as it could revitalize the online player base by enabling smoother, less delayed matches in a classic but still popular fighter.

Hasbro’s Star Wars Action Figure Pack Featuring Kreia Sells Out Quickly

Hasbro recently launched preorders for a Black Series two-pack action figure set exclusively available on Amazon. The pack includes Darth Nihilus, a well-known Sith from *Knights of the Old Republic 2*, and for the first time, an official figure of Kreia (Darth Traya), the game’s complex antagonist.

Kreia’s figure captures her distinctive, confrontational personality and comes with three telekinetically floating lightsabers represented by clear plastic stands. Although the figure sold out quickly, it has been praised for its detail and design, generating enthusiasm among fans of the cult classic RPG.

The swift sell-out suggests strong demand for collectibles tied to lesser-represented characters in the Star Wars universe, particularly from the *Knights of the Old Republic* series.

Former Collaborator Shares Wild Anecdotes About Late Dead or Alive Creator Tomonobu Itagaki

An interview with former collaborators of Tomonobu Itagaki, the late creator of the *Dead or Alive* and rebooted *Ninja Gaiden* series, reveals some dramatic episodes from his life. One notable story involves Itagaki being held at gunpoint by the Los Angeles Police Department during a pre-E3 event after a loud incident at a hotel.

Despite these incidents, Itagaki was known for a volatile but charismatic persona. He passed away in October 2025, leaving behind a legacy of challenging game design and a controversial public image. The anecdotes offer insight into the high-energy, tumultuous life of a prominent figure in fighting and action gaming.

Rockstar Games Faces Ransom Threat After Security Breach

Rockstar Games has confirmed a data breach involving its outsourced Snowflake cloud storage system, reportedly accessed via credentials linked to a third-party analytics company, Anodot. The hacker group ShinyHunters claims to have held the data and threatened to leak it unless Rockstar pays a ransom by April 14, 2026.

Rockstar indicated that only a limited amount of non-material company information was accessed, and there is no impact on players or ongoing development. This marks the second major security incident related to the studio’s upcoming *Grand Theft Auto 6*, following a previous high-profile leak in 2022.

The breach highlights broader cybersecurity risks tied to third-party services in the gaming industry and underscores ongoing challenges for studios protecting sensitive data.

New Proton Experimental Build Improves Capcom Classics on Steam Deck and Linux

Valve's Proton Experimental branch has received updates that improve compatibility and performance of several older Capcom games on Steam Deck and Linux systems. Titles updated include original *Resident Evil*, *Resident Evil 2*, *Dino Crisis*, and *Dino Crisis 2*.

While these games have recently become available on Steam, native compatibility on Steam Deck has been limited. The Proton patch promotes these games closer to “playable” status, though some issues remain—for example, *Dino Crisis 2*’s full-motion video cutscenes still require community fixes.

This build allows fans of vintage survival horror to enjoy classic experiences on portable hardware and Linux, adding more options for retro gaming on modern platforms.
