River City Ransom: Underground Delisting from Steam

River City Ransom: Underground, a sequel to the classic NES beat 'em up, is currently being delisted from Steam due to licensing issues. The game remains available for purchase at $3 until January 5, but the exact removal date is unclear, and screenshots and trailers have already been removed from its Steam page. Owners will retain access and can continue playing as usual. Although Underground is less popular than spinoffs like *River City Girls*, it holds a mostly positive player rating. The delisting is a reminder to acquire the title soon for those interested in its beat 'em up style and online multiplayer, which faces uncertain future support post-delisting.

World of Warcraft Player Housing Sees Creative Builds

Since the recent introduction of player housing in *World of Warcraft*, players have widely showcased their creative constructions. A notable example is Reddit user Top_Syllabub6619, who has built multiple Star Wars-inspired structures within this system, including an AT-AT walker, R2-D2 house, Millennium Falcon, and Imperial Destroyer. These builds stand out for their scale and visual accuracy despite limitations of the in-game objects. This activity reflects both strong engagement with the new housing features and the broader trend of players using game tools to recreate popular cultural elements.

Trine Classic Collection Free on Epic Today with Crossplay Update

The *Trine Classic Collection*, which includes the first four entries in the *Trine* puzzle-platformer series, is free on the Epic Games Store for one day only, until December 31. Each game has also been updated to improve crossplay functionality between Steam and Epic players, allowing for smoother co-op experiences across platforms. The *Trine* series is recognized for its lighthearted gameplay involving three distinct characters working together to solve puzzles. This offer provides around two dozen hours of content and underscores the continued popularity of accessible co-op platformers.

Arc Raiders Tops Steam Sales Over Christmas Ahead of Battlefield 6

According to SteamDB data, *Arc Raiders* was the best-selling game on Steam during the week of Christmas, with *Battlefield 6* closely following. Both shooters emphasize a less stressful, more casual approach compared to traditional competitive shooters. *Arc Raiders* offers a more approachable extraction shooter experience, while *Battlefield 6* avoids ranked competitive modes. This shift in player preference toward accessible multiplayer shooters marks a notable trend in the genre. Friendslop Games Highlight 2025 Shift Away from Graphics Priority

The gaming year of 2025 saw a marked shift away from high-fidelity graphics toward gameplay-focused cooperative games, a subgenre pejoratively dubbed "friendslop." Games such as *Peak*, *REPO*, *Phasmophobia*, and *Lethal Company* found success with simple mechanics, accessible controls, and art styles designed for lower-end hardware. This emphasis on accessible yet engaging gameplay contrasts with the plateau in graphics performance improvements and costly innovations like ray tracing. Even major releases such as *Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2* and *Battlefield 6* prioritized performance and playability over graphical extremes, reflecting changing player values and potential impacts on future game development prioritization.
