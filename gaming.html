Disposable Vapes Become Synth Ocarinas in DIY Project

A group called Paperbagteam has developed a DIY project that transforms disposable vapes into small synthesizer instruments inspired by the ocarina. The project involves repurposing vape components such as the battery, charging circuit, and sensor, combining them with 3D printed parts and additional electronics like speakers and capacitors. The result is a playable synth that activates by inhaling, similarly to using a vape. This initiative also includes plans for a wireless model that can interface with computers or musical instruments.

This project highlights issues around the rising e-waste from disposable vapes, with over 11 million units sold monthly. By diverting components into creative open-source hardware, it offers a novel approach to recycling tech that otherwise contributes to pollution. Such efforts underline how discarded consumer products can be reimagined in sustainable and educational ways.

Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2 Sells 5 Million Copies Since Release

Developer Warhorse Studios announced that Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2 (KCD2) has sold five million copies since its launch last year. This marks a significant milestone, reflecting a strong reception and growing player base. For comparison, the original Kingdom Come sold about two million copies in its first year and eight million overall by late 2024.

The success of KCD2 is attributed by Warhorse co-founder Martin Klima to the game's mature design, which respects players by offering a less hand-holding experience. The game has received critical acclaim, with several outlets naming it game of the year for 2025. Its sales figures underline the continued demand for thoughtful, older-school RPGs.

Blades in the Dark '68 Expansion Shifts to Psychedelic 60s Sci-Fi Setting

The tabletop role-playing game Blades in the Dark is releasing an expansion titled "Blades '68" that reimagines its traditionally gothic heist setting into a 1960s-inspired psychedelic sci-fi dystopia. Drawing inspiration from games like Deathloop and Disco Elysium, it introduces elements like "plasmovision" TV and an artificial, colorful sky maintained by a controversial power source drawn from human bodies.

This setting retains core Blades themes like ghostly threats but introduces new classes and factions, offering a fresh experience within the established system. The change demonstrates a trend in tabletop RPGs to explore different tones and themes while preserving mechanical familiarity. The Kickstarter launch has seen rapid support, showing interest in innovative expansions of known properties.
