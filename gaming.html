Mirror’s Edge: A Look Back at Its Innovative Free-Running Gameplay

PC Gamer revisited the 2009 release Mirror’s Edge, a first-person game centered on Faith, a courier navigating a dystopian cityscape through parkour-style movement. The game’s core appeal lies in its smooth, immersive traversal mechanics and bright, streamlined urban environment. Reviewer Graham Smith praised the detailed animations and physics that make the parkour feel tangible and rewarding. However, Smith noted that the game’s combat elements—mainly disarming and fighting police enemies—interrupt the otherwise fluid movement, causing frustration. While the combat is intentionally minimalistic to emphasize evasion, it detracts from the pure free-running experience. The story is concise, about six hours, and the game lacks multiplayer but offers Time Trial and Speed Run modes that focus on movement skills without enemies. Overall, Mirror’s Edge demonstrates the challenges of blending innovative movement with traditional gameplay mechanics.

Create Aeronautics Mod Expands Minecraft’s Vehicle Building

A new mod for Minecraft, Create Aeronautics, builds upon the existing Create mod to enable players to construct fully operational vehicles such as planes, helicopters, offroad vehicles, combine harvesters, and airships. The mod adds new blocks, redstone components, and mechanics like propeller bearings, burners for hot air balloons, and suspension systems. It also integrates with the Sable mod, which introduces physics interactions between block groups. This extension significantly increases the complexity and variety of mechanical creations in Minecraft, allowing for more dynamic exploration and transportation options. Players interested in expanding their engineering within Minecraft can download Create Aeronautics, provided they have the Create and Sable mods installed.

Marathon’s WSTR Shotgun Faces Second Nerf in Balance Update

Bungie plans to further reduce the power of the WSTR shotgun in its game Marathon, citing concerns that it overshadows other short-range weapon options. Previously weakened by a range reduction, the shotgun remains dominant in close combat. From the upcoming update on Tuesday, it will require more shots to down opponents equipped with blue shields or stronger, enhancing counterplay opportunities. The weapon will retain two-shot kills against players with weaker, green shields to remain viable in early-game encounters. This change is part of Bungie’s ongoing efforts to refine game balance and encourage diverse tactics among players. The developer has shown willingness to adjust the meta aggressively, evidenced by recent experiments with loadouts and new items like Mercy Kits.

Turtle WoW Classic Server to Shut Down Following Legal Ruling

After a legal battle, the private World of Warcraft server Turtle WoW announced it will shut down on May 14, following an injunction won by Blizzard. The server, which offered a “Classic Plus” experience with additional raids, zones, and races based on WoW’s pre-expansion era, received a cease and desist order last week as part of a settlement. Developer Torta expressed gratitude toward the community for eight years of support. The closure signals the difficulties fan-run servers face despite their popularity, highlighting the ongoing legal tensions between game companies and unofficial projects. Turtle WoW’s shutdown recalls similar cases like Nostalrius and underscores Blizzard’s cautious approach to fan servers despite community demand for expanded classic content.
