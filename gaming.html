Nvidia Plans Significant GPU Production Cuts in 2026 Due to VRAM Shortages

Nvidia reportedly intends to reduce the production of its gaming GPUs by up to 40% in the first half of 2026 due to limited availability of high-density GDDR7 VRAM modules. The reduced supply particularly affects models with 16GB VRAM variants like the RTX 5060 Ti and RTX 5070 Ti, which require more memory chips. The company appears to be prioritizing supply of newer RTX 50 Super models that use higher capacity 3GB VRAM modules, complicating inventory management. Despite the cuts, an oversupply of GPUs is unlikely as demand for desktops and laptops is also slowing due to the broader DRAM supply crisis. Consumers may see constrained availability or price fluctuations especially for high-end graphics cards next year.
