**2025: A Mixed Year for PC Game Performance and Development**

A recent review of PC game development in 2025 highlighted significant disparities in technical quality among new releases. Three games stood out positively, exemplifying strong performance and optimization:

- *Battlefield 6* delivered solid frame rates and stability by foregoing advanced rendering techniques like ray tracing, focusing instead on performance and scale across hardware generations. - *Kingdom Come: Deliverance 2* impressed with its use of CryEngine, balancing detailed open-world visuals with smooth gameplay even on mid-range hardware. - *Doom: The Dark Ages* managed respectable performance with hardware ray tracing enabled, though it demanded relatively newer GPUs to run efficiently.

Conversely, several titles struggled technically:

- *Monster Hunter Wilds* launched with poor optimization, often requiring upscaling and frame generation to reach playable frame rates, despite unimpressive graphics quality. - *Borderlands 4* suffered from performance issues even on high-end hardware attributed to development choices rather than the underlying engine. - *Gears of War: Reloaded,* despite being a remaster of an older title, delivered subpar performance on handheld devices due to inefficient CPU usage.

Overall, while some games showed advancements in development quality and performance scalability, many others failed to meet expectations, reflecting ongoing challenges in PC game optimization.
