Star Wars Zero Company and the State of Realtime-with-Pause Combat

Bit Reactor’s upcoming tactical RPG *Star Wars Zero Company* offers an evolution of the turn-based genre, combining XCOM-style squad combat with RPG elements reminiscent of *Mass Effect*. Lead designer James Brawley recently emphasized in an interview that realtime-with-pause (RTWP) combat, a hybrid of real-time and turn-based play, is not obsolete. While the recent trend favors turn-based games due to innovations in pacing and presentation, Brawley expects RTWP to see a revival with future innovations. This perspective highlights a broader debate about combat systems, as turn-based tactics continue to attract players through improved immersion and accessibility, challenging longtime perceptions of the genre as slow or cumbersome.

RPG Maker’s Visual Overhaul Inspired by Octopath Traveler

RPG Maker, a longstanding tool used by many indie developers, is undergoing its first major visual overhaul. The update introduces a 2.5D aesthetic similar to that of *Octopath Traveler* and other Square Enix titles. This change aims to move beyond RPG Maker’s traditional 16-bit style, potentially broadening its creative possibilities and appeal. While the new style features grid-based movement rather than free-range, the improved lighting and shadow effects represent a significant graphical upgrade. RPG Maker’s accessible asset creation process remains intact, making this update important for developers seeking more modern visuals without losing the simplicity of the tool.

Dimension 20 Partners with White Wolf for Vampire: The Masquerade Series

The actual-play show *Dimension 20* is launching a new season titled *City Council of Darkness* in collaboration with White Wolf, the creators of *Vampire: The Masquerade*. This marks the first time *Dimension 20* has worked directly with an official publisher on an actual-play series. The story is set in Purpee, Oregon, a small town hosting vampire representatives in an unusual setting that challenges typical vampire tropes centered around urban environments. The season will feature the core *Dimension 20* cast and run for 14 episodes on the subscription service Dropout starting April 8. This collaboration expands the visibility of *Vampire: The Masquerade* in the online tabletop community and reflects the growing popularity of actual-play content.

Pro World of Warcraft Player Disconnected Mid-Raid Due to Expired Subscription

A professional *World of Warcraft* player competing in the high-profile world first race for the *Midnight* expansion was unexpectedly disconnected mid-boss fight because their game subscription expired. The incident, streamed live during the raid, revealed that the player had purchased a WoW token but forgotten to activate it. Although embarrassing, the situation is reportedly common in this competitive scene, where multiple accounts must be managed, sometimes leading to oversights. The player’s guild maintained focus and eventually resumed their attempt. This episode underscores the logistical challenges faced by professional MMO players in managing multiple accounts alongside intense gameplay.

Valve Veteran Criticizes Epic Games Over Layoffs and Company Direction

Chet Faliszek, a former Valve writer noted for his work on iconic titles like *Half-Life 2* and *Portal*, publicly criticized Epic Games and CEO Tim Sweeney following the layoffs of over 1,000 employees at Epic. Faliszek questioned the company’s approach to employee ownership and morale, contrasting it with Valve’s culture where developers reportedly felt a strong sense of agency and pride. He expressed concern about the impact of such layoffs on creative passion and company loyalty, suggesting that repeated cuts risk disengaging talent. This criticism highlights ongoing tensions in the industry between business decisions and nurturing developer creativity and job security, especially amidst changing market conditions.

Neuralink Patient Demonstrates Hands-Free World of Warcraft Raiding

A participant in Neuralink’s brain implant trial shared progress after 100 days of using the device, demonstrating the ability to play *World of Warcraft* using only thought control. British Army veteran Jon Noble described the surgical implantation process and rapid learning curve involved in controlling a computer cursor and ultimately raiding in WoW without physical input devices. This represents a significant technological advancement in brain-computer interfaces and shows potential for increased independence for people with paralysis. While still experimental and limited to a small number of users, Neuralink’s adaptability across different software interfaces signals important possibilities for both gaming accessibility and broader assistive technologies.
