RPG Fans Highlight D&D Inaccuracies in Stranger Things Season 5

Josh Sawyer, design director at Obsidian, has publicly noted several inaccuracies in the depiction of Dungeons & Dragons rules in the latest season of *Stranger Things*. Fans of tabletop RPGs pointed out that the show blends editions of D&D inaccurately‚Äîusing terminology and spell effects from 5th Edition while claiming to play 1st Edition. Examples include the Cleric class mistaken for having access to the spell Dimension Door, and the use of the Sorcerer class name, not introduced until 3rd Edition. Sawyer argues these errors detract from the show's authenticity as a period piece set in the 1980s and suggest the writers referenced newer materials rather than original rulesets. While some defend the mistakes as reflective of how young players might adapt or misremember rules, Sawyer counters that the characters' seriousness about the game makes such errors less plausible. The discussion underscores the depth of engagement RPG communities have with game history and rules.
