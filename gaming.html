High on Life 2 Review: A Different Tone with Familiar Humor

High on Life 2 launches as a single-player first-person shooter from Squanch Games, priced at $60. Following the departure of co-founder Justin Roiland amid controversy, the sequel adopts a notably calmer and less cynical tone, while retaining its characteristic dark humor and fourth-wall breaks. The game follows the Outlaw, a bounty hunter turned folk hero, as they target corrupt pharmaceutical executives in a sci-fi setting where humans are literally turned into drugs.

The game features creative level designs that often deviate from typical shooting sequences, such as a murder mystery on a cruise ship. Despite ambitious and unique level moments, the combat system feels underwhelming with limited weapon depth and unremarkable enemy variety. Movement mechanics, including a skateboard that replaces traditional sprinting, add a fresh element but lack meaningful progression or thrill.

Technically, the game shows signs of instability, with bugs affecting movement and dialogue scripting, and requires high-end hardware to run smoothly. The use of Unreal Engine 5's lighting system reportedly impacts gameplay clarity. Overall, High on Life 2 offers a fun, if uneven, single-player FPS experience with a more genuine tone than its predecessor.
