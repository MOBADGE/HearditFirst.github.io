**PC Games Revenue Forecast to Surpass Consoles by 2028**

Market research firm Newzoo has reported a significant shift in the gaming industry, predicting that PC game revenue will outgrow console software sales by 2028. After trailing console sales for over a decade, PC revenue growth outperformed consoles in 2025, driven mainly by a growing player base in regions like China and increasing adoption among younger generations such as Gen Z and Gen Alpha. Newzoo forecasts a 6.6% annual growth rate for PC gaming revenue between 2025 and 2028, compared to 4.4% for consoles, with the PC player base expected to surpass 1 billion by 2028.

However, Newzoo’s forecast comes with caveats. Much of the growth in China benefits domestic games, posing challenges for international developers. Additionally, an ongoing memory and storage supply crisis, exacerbated by increased demands from AI-related manufacturing, threatens to impact the PC hardware market and possibly the wider consumer electronics sector. This supply constraint could affect the gaming industry’s growth projections through 2028.

**Apple’s MacBook Neo Chip Outperforms x86 CPUs in Single-Core Benchmarks**

Benchmarks of Apple’s new MacBook Neo, which incorporates the A18 Pro iPhone chip, reveal that it leads all current x86 PC processors in single-core performance as measured by Cinebench 2024. Scoring 147 points, the MacBook Neo surpasses desktop CPUs like AMD’s Ryzen 9 9950X and Intel’s Core Ultra 9 285HX, which scored 139 and 135 points respectively.

This performance is notable given the MacBook Neo’s budget pricing and the fact that the chip is derived from Apple’s M4 silicon architecture, offering high instructions-per-clock efficiency. While this benchmark highlights Apple’s advancement in CPU core design, the chip’s peak performance is limited by power and thermal conditions, particularly in mobile devices like the iPhone. The results illustrate Apple’s ongoing competitive positioning in computing hardware.

**Embark’s Arc Raiders Pivoted to Extraction Shooter After Development Challenges**

The development of the game Arc Raiders underwent substantial changes before its current form as an extraction shooter. Originally intended to be a PvE-focused game with varying described identities—from battle royale to co-op Souls-like—the project experienced internal conflicts about its direction.

According to production director Caio Braga, the team frequently tested competing gameplay features, sometimes with contradictory results, leading to inconsistent player experiences. After several years and a large team, the project was downsized and reoriented around an extraction shooter model, leveraging Embark’s previous PvP expertise. This pivot delivered a more focused game balancing accessibility and depth, which contributed to its current reception in the market.

**Memory and Storage Prices Expected to Remain High Until Late 2027**

Market research from Counterpoint Research indicates that the ongoing global shortage and price surge in memory (RAM) and storage components will persist through at least the second half of 2027. Prices for server-grade DDR5 modules, mobile LPDDR5X, and laptop DDR4 modules have seen significant quarterly increases, sometimes exceeding 150%.

The demand from AI infrastructure and hyperscale data centers continues unabated, limiting relief for consumer products. Although production capacity for DRAM and NAND is forecasted to grow over 25% this year, it will not be sufficient to counteract tight supply in the near term. Memory suppliers are focusing on maximizing profits from commodity memory products rather than high-end specialized chips, further impacting pricing trends.

**Sony’s Pullback from PC Releases May Be Driven by Competition with Valve**

Sony’s recent decision to reduce its efforts in bringing console exclusives to PC has prompted speculation about the underlying causes. Peter Dalton, head of technology at Bluepoint Games, suggests that Sony may be concerned not about Microsoft’s expanding Xbox-PC ecosystem, but about Valve’s growing presence through devices like the Steam Deck and Steam Machine.

Dalton posits that Valve could create a Steam-based console experience that offers both the ease of use found in consoles and access to the extensive PC game library. This hybrid model could challenge traditional consoles, potentially reshaping the competitive landscape. Sony’s strategy shift reflects this uncertainty, highlighting the evolving dynamics in game distribution and hardware ecosystems.
