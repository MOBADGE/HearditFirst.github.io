Far Cry 2 Review Reflects on a Pioneering FPS from 2008

PC Gamer revisited Ubisoft's Far Cry 2, originally released in 2008, highlighting its innovative approach to open-world first-person shooters. The review emphasizes the game's unique narrative system, where player choices significantly influence the storyline and character fates, providing a more dynamic experience than traditional scripted games. The atmospheric African setting and environmental effects like realistic fire propagation contribute to immersion, while the game maintains a consistent first-person perspective without intrusive interface breaks.

Despite these strengths, the review notes some weaknesses: the supporting characters (referred to as "buddies") are underutilized, vehicle combat can feel clunky, and mission variety dwindles during story-heavy segments. The game's world-building also lacks depth, with limited cultural detail outside the immediate conflict. Nonetheless, Far Cry 2's compelling combat mechanics and emergent storytelling mark it as a significant title that raised expectations for future shooters.

Anno 117: Pax Romana’s Volcanic DLC Arrives April 23

Ubisoft announced the first DLC for city-builder Anno 117: Pax Romana, titled *Prophecies of Ash*. Launching on April 23, this expansion introduces the largest island in the series, Cinis, dominated by a massive volcano. The island offers new resources including obsidian and features explosive volcanic hazards like eruptions and volcanic winters that impact agriculture and city management.

The DLC adds new gameplay elements such as a temple to Vulcan that enhances mine productivity and fire safety, reflecting the volcanic environment. While Anno 117 has faced criticism over an incomplete campaign and use of AI-generated art (now removed), it achieved the series' most successful launch to date. This DLC aims to broaden the game’s strategic diversity and challenge players with natural disaster management in a historical setting.

The Planet Crafter 2.0 Update Enhances Terraforming Survival

The survival and terraforming game The Planet Crafter has received a major 2.0 update by developer Miju Games. This update adds a new, undisclosed biome on the main planet, visual improvements such as enhanced skyboxes, terrain, vegetation, and water shaders, plus new craftable items to enrich player bases, including lockers, vending machines, and showers.

A notable feature includes the evolution of terraformed moons visible in the planetary sky, reflecting player progress. Additionally, a jetpack upgrade for the rover facilitates navigation of difficult terrain. Coinciding with the update, the game is offered at a 50% price reduction on Steam until April 20, potentially attracting new players to its unique blend of survival and large-scale planetary engineering.

Soulmask Exits Early Access with Free Ancient Egypt DLC

Soulmask, a tribal survival game, officially left early access alongside the release of a substantial Ancient Egypt-themed DLC called *Shifting Sands*. Developer CampFire Studio is offering this DLC free for one month to all owners of the full version. The expansion introduces a new, equally large map featuring deserts, deltas, new ruins, dungeons, bosses, and over 300 new talents for tribe members.

The base game also saw a major overhaul with version 1.0, adding three distinct game modes—survival, tribe development, and action RPG-style warrior mode—plus improved onboarding for new players. Soulmask is currently discounted by 10% until April 23, making it an appealing time to try or continue the game with its expanded content.
