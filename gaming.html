Starfield’s Terran Armada Story DLC Revealed

Bethesda has announced the upcoming Terran Armada story DLC for Starfield, scheduled for release on April 7 alongside the Free Lanes update. The DLC introduces a new faction, the Terran Armada, who claim to be the true inheritors of Earth's legacy and field an army of robots. Players will encounter hostile robots during exploration, but will also be able to recruit a morally ambiguous robot companion described as neither good nor evil. Additionally, players can recruit a ModelG mini-bot that acts more like a customizable pet.

Terran Armada adds gameplay elements such as incursions—dynamic events reflecting the faction’s invasion progress that can interrupt travel and disable ship systems. The DLC also includes new ships, ship parts, real-world styled gear like machine guns, and more outpost decorations, including a fully furnished outpost that can be deployed on any planet. The DLC will cost $10 but is included for free with the premium upgrade.

This expansion broadens Starfield’s narrative scope and gameplay options, providing new challenges and customization opportunities, which could enhance player engagement with the game’s universe.

Death Stranding 2 Leak Occurs Before Official Launch

A pre-release version of Death Stranding 2 leaked online two days ahead of its official Steam launch on March 19. The leak reportedly involved unencrypted game files accidentally made available on Steam, allowing the full 113GB game to be downloaded and distributed on piracy sites. While Steam's DRM is in place, it is easier to circumvent than other protection methods, and the leaked build notably lacks Russian voiceovers due to Steam's separate language packaging system.

Pirated copies of the game will not have access to its significant online features, such as shared player infrastructure. The leak serves as a reminder of the ongoing challenges game publishers face in securing digital launches, especially for high-profile titles.

Subnautica 2 Early Access Set for May Amid Legal Dispute

Subnautica 2 will enter early access in May, as confirmed by Krafton and Unknown Worlds following a prolonged legal dispute and leadership turmoil. Krafton acknowledged the team’s recent development progress and milestone reviews, signaling readiness for early access despite disagreeing with recent court rulings that require reinstating former Unknown Worlds CEO Ted Gill.

Unknown Worlds’ current leadership expressed optimism about collaboration with Gill to ensure a smooth transition and successful launch. The court's decision also extends a $250 million performance bonus deadline tied to the game's launch to at least September 2026. The announcement ends months of uncertainty surrounding the studio and the game’s release timeline.

Take-Two CEO Declines to Advise New Microsoft Gaming Head

Strauss Zelnick, CEO of Take-Two Interactive, has publicly declined to offer advice to Asha Sharma, the new CEO of Microsoft Gaming. Sharma, who succeeded Phil Spencer in the role, has a background outside of gaming, leading some to question her appointment. Zelnick praised Sharma as a highly accomplished executive and emphasized his focus on managing his own company.

Zelnick shared his past experience transitioning from entertainment into gaming leadership and highlighted his focus on balancing creative and business efforts. He stressed the importance of producing successful games, noting Take-Two’s reputation as a consistent hit maker and its decision to move away from indie-focused publishing to concentrate on major titles.

Remedy Ends Support for FBC: Firebreak with Final Update

Remedy Entertainment has announced that support for its co-op shooter FBC: Firebreak will end following the recent "Open House" update. This last update introduced five new maps inspired by Remedy’s Control, an overhauled perk system for better character builds, and various gameplay improvements.

The base price of the game has been permanently reduced, and a new cross-platform Friend’s Pass allows owners to invite friends to join matches. Remedy confirmed that while no new content updates are planned, ongoing gameplay patches will be provided as needed. The company expressed gratitude to the community and assured that the game will remain playable for years despite a smaller player base.
