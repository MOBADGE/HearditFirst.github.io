Notepad++ Website Hijacked in 2025 Cyberattack

The open-source text editor Notepad++ suffered a significant security breach between mid-2025 and late 2025. Attackers compromised a shared hosting server used by Notepad++, redirecting update traffic to malicious servers. Importantly, the breach occurred at the hosting provider level, not through vulnerabilities in the Notepad++ code itself. Independent security researchers, including cybersecurity firm Rapid7, attribute the attack to the Chinese state-sponsored Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) group called Lotus Blossom, known for targeted espionage. The attackers used a sophisticated backdoor named "Chrysalis," employing complex methods to evade detection. Notepad++‚Äôs development team has since switched to a more secure hosting provider.
