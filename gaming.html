US Patent Office Moves to Revoke Nintendo's Pokémon Battling Patent

The United States Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO) has issued a nonfinal decision to reject all claims of Nintendo’s controversial US patent 12,403,397, which covers a specific system for battling summoned characters in games. Originally granted in September 2025 amidst Nintendo’s ongoing legal battles with Palworld developer Pocketpair, the patent faced strong criticism for potentially limiting creative game design. Legal experts described its initial approval as a failure of the patent system.

The patent does not cover the broad concept of battling summoned monsters but claims a highly specific mechanic involving summoning sub-characters to designated locations to engage in automated battles. After further examination and reexamination initiated by the USPTO Director, the office concluded that Nintendo’s claims were too similar to prior art, including other video game patents from Konami, Bandai Namco, and even Nintendo's own earlier applications. Nintendo now has the option to contest the decision before a final ruling is made. This development highlights ongoing tensions around intellectual property rights in the gaming industry and the challenges in defining patentable innovations in game mechanics.

s&box, the Successor to Garry's Mod, Launching on Steam April 28

Facepunch Studios announced that s&box, the long-awaited follow-up to Garry’s Mod, will launch on Steam on April 28. The announcement was understated, appearing first as a Steam page update rather than a traditional marketing campaign. s&box is a sandbox game platform built on a modified Source 2 engine that allows users to create and release their own standalone games on Steam without paying royalties to Facepunch, thanks to a licensing deal with Valve.

While the studio is still finalizing licensing details, s&box aims to foster creativity and offer users freedom akin to what Garry’s Mod provided. Pricing is estimated to be between $10 and $20. This launch marks a significant moment for established game creation platforms and reflects a trend toward user-generated content with minimal developer overhead.

Black Ops 7’s April Fools’ Map ‘GRWM’ Is Real and Extremely Small

Treyarch’s April Fools’ joke for Call of Duty: Black Ops 7 turned out to be genuine: a new playlist called GRWM features the smallest map in Call of Duty history, set inside a single walk-in closet. The map supports an 8-player free-for-all with frantic combat that leaves players no safe spots, encouraging constant engagement.

Though the concept began as a joke referencing “Get Ready With Me” beauty videos, it now serves as a high-intensity mode that quickly rewards players with XP and kills. The map may only be available during April Fool’s Day, but it notably deviates from typical small maps by disallowing camping strategies, instead emphasizing nonstop action.

Shinji Mikami’s New Studio Acquired by Korean Publisher Shift Up

Industry veteran Shinji Mikami, known for his founding roles at PlatinumGames, Tango Gameworks, and now Unbound, has had his latest studio acquired by Korean publisher Shift Up. The acquisition comes shortly after a stealth reveal of Unbound’s first project, which appears to be a horror-themed game featuring gothic and Egyptian motifs as well as creature elements.

Shift Up’s CEO expressed optimism about the partnership enhancing their global development capabilities, while Mikami noted the creative synergy with the publisher and his renewed full-time involvement in a large-scale game project. This move signals a notable investment in Mikami’s creative direction after his departure from Tango Gameworks in 2023.

Marathon’s Outpost Map Adjusted for Higher Difficulty and Better Rewards

Bungie updated Marathon’s popular Outpost map in patch 1.0.5.2, making the central Pinwheel loot location more challenging to access but also more rewarding. The patch reinstates a previously disabled entry, increases security requirements to obtain the valuable red security cards, and adds enemy waves during loot extraction.

Loot quality has been significantly improved, offering higher-tier items such as Cryo Archive keys and Prestige implants. However, solo players have raised concerns that the increased difficulty favors group play, as navigating the new enemy presence alone is more demanding. This update reflects Bungie’s ongoing balancing efforts to ensure risk-reward gameplay remains engaging but fair.
