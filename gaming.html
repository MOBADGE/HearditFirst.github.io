Crimson Desert Recommended to Play with a Controller

Crimson Desert, despite its large and ambitious world, has been criticized for clunky and awkward controls on PC. The game features numerous inputs and complex combinations, which lead to frustrating experiences when played with mouse and keyboard—for example, executing the Blinding Flash move during the introduction. PC Gamer suggests using a controller instead, as it offers a more manageable and comfortable control scheme by condensing actions onto fewer buttons.

While the controller layout can cause some accidental inputs, it provides quicker access to key actions like healing, which are otherwise awkwardly bound to less accessible keyboard keys. However, keyboard controls have the advantage of full key rebinding options, unlike the default in-game controller setup. Overall, the recommendation is to play Crimson Desert with a controller if possible, as it improves usability in this complex action RPG.

CD Projekt Gives Update on Project Hadar

CD Projekt revealed new information about Project Hadar, an entirely new intellectual property in early development, during its latest earnings report. The studio said work is progressing dynamically, with the team having established the foundational elements and core pillars of the project’s world and gameplay. Multiple prototypes are being created and tested using Unreal Engine.

Project Hadar represents CD Projekt’s first original IP distinct from The Witcher and Cyberpunk franchises. The company has been tight-lipped about Hadar since its announcement in 2022, making this update significant even if details remain sparse. Currently, the studio’s biggest teams remain focused on The Witcher 4 and Cyberpunk 2, but Hadar’s ongoing development signals long-term plans to diversify their portfolio.

Caves of Qud Co-Creator Discusses Untapped Computing Potential in Games

Brian Bucklew, co-creator of the complex roguelike Caves of Qud, discussed how contemporary games underutilize modern CPU and network capabilities. While graphics demands have increased GPU usage, many games still run simple simulations that do not fully exploit multi-core processors or high bandwidth internet connections.

Bucklew argues that most games continue to use gameplay designs from roughly 2005, limited by simulating straightforward world states on few cores. Caves of Qud uses more CPU-intensive procedural generation and systemic simulations than typical titles, but even it leaves substantial compute power unused. Bucklew’s future projects aim to create games that saturate modern CPU cores, potentially evolving strategy and simulation gameplay, though such games might require high-end hardware.

Marathon Puzzle Solved, Launching Cryo Archive Map on Weekends

The Marathon community recently solved a challenging alternate reality game (ARG) puzzle related to the Cryo Archive map. Bungie confirmed that this long-anticipated fourth map will launch on March 20 alongside a new ranked mode. Cryo Archive is described as a difficult, raid-style challenge with dense puzzles, layered mechanics, and seven vaults containing exclusive loot.

Notably, Bungie will restrict access to this map to weekends only, similar to Destiny 2’s Trials of Osiris mode. This limitation may affect accessibility but appears planned to align with the map’s high difficulty and design focus. The community’s successful puzzle completion has set the stage for players to explore this new content starting tomorrow.

World of Warcraft Players Resume Long-Running Puzzle Hunt

Players of World of Warcraft’s Midnight expansion have reignited a year-old secret puzzle first introduced in previous content. The puzzle involves activating twelve orbs positioned on pedestals forming a clock, originally solved up to the ninth orb to unlock a motorcycle mount. The recent discovery of the tenth orb’s activation confirms that Blizzard intends for this puzzle to continue over multiple expansions.

The new step was uncovered during a separate questline involving a mysterious cabal and cryptic items, indicating that the puzzle’s solution is multi-layered and time-gated. The community is currently deciphering variable numbers associated with the new orb’s activation, suggesting an evolving challenge with unknown rewards beyond the original mount.

Capcom Credits PC for Continued Profit Growth

Capcom reported its 12th consecutive year of increased operating profit, with PC sales playing a crucial role in this growth. The fiscal year 2025 data shows that approximately half of Capcom’s game sales come from PC platforms, a slight dip from previous years where PC accounted for about 60 percent but still indicating a strong focus.
