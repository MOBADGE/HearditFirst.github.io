Fallout: New Vegas PS3 Mod Now Playable on PC

A modder has ported a faithful recreation of the Fallout: New Vegas PS3 "Greatest Hits" Ultimate Edition—a version known for its lower performance and quirks—into the PC version of the game. Originally created as a whimsical in-game weapon, this PS3 box mod adds an unusual twist: when equipped alongside the Wild Wasteland perk, it can instantly kill enemies on hit. The mod serves as both a nostalgic reminder of the PS3 port’s flaws and a playful addition to the PC version. While largely a novelty, it highlights the enduring affection for New Vegas, despite the technical issues of its PS3 release.

Writer Reflects on PS5 Experience, Uncertain About PS6

Jess Kinghorn, a hardware writer, shared her personal perspective on buying a PlayStation 5 at launch and her reluctance to purchase the next PlayStation console. Factors influencing her stance include the PS5’s increasing price (now about $650/£570 for the base model), ongoing hardware shortages, and the broader economic effects on component costs. She also notes a shift in her gaming preferences toward PC and smaller indie titles rather than the large, expensive AAA exclusives PlayStation is known for. With reports suggesting that Sony might delay the PS6 launch to 2028 or 2029 due to AI-driven demand for memory chips, the console’s future looks uncertain for some longtime fans.

MindsEye: A Flawed But Somehow Enjoyable Sci-Fi Shooter

MindsEye, a recent game by Build a Rocket Boy, has garnered attention for its chaotic and often glitchy gameplay. Despite numerous flaws—a lack of meaningful NPC reactions, awkward story developments, and technical issues—the reviewer found it oddly entertaining over a 20-hour playthrough. The game’s story touches on themes like corporate surveillance, but it ultimately fails to provide satisfying conclusions or compelling characters. Its mix of violence and eccentric narrative moments offers a disorganized experience that some might find "so bad it's good." However, the high $60 price tag feels steep for the kind of rough-around-the-edges product it is.

Final Fantasy 14’s New Dungeon Praised, But Rewards Draw Criticism

The latest variant dungeon in Final Fantasy 14, The Merchant’s Tale, has been well received for its design quality, fight mechanics, and aesthetics. It offers a fresh challenge for midcore players with a new “advanced” difficulty. However, community and critics have expressed dissatisfaction with the rewards structure. Unlike expectations for dungeons to provide meaningful gear upgrades, rewards are mainly cosmetic or low-value crafting items, with no significant gear drops until raid content is released much later. This has raised concerns that good design work is being undermined by a lack of compelling incentives, potentially affecting player engagement in the longer term.

Nightdive Studios Remasters Obscure 3DO Shooter PO’ed

Nightdive Studios recently released a remaster of PO’ed, a 1995 FPS originally on the 3DO console, now for PC. The game is noted for its unusual mix of humor, a chef protagonist, and early six-degrees-of-freedom movement with a jetpack. Despite these novel features for its time, PO’ed suffers from poor level design, slippery controls, and controversial enemy choices involving crude humor. While the remaster preserves the original’s rough qualities for historical interest, it remains largely unplayable by modern standards. The effort is viewed more as a preservation exercise than an overlooked classic.

Indie Pass Subscription Service Fails to Impress

A new subscription service called Indie Pass, offering access to a curated catalog of indie games for $6.99 a month, has launched with mixed reception. The model compensates developers based on the time players spend in their games, a system that critics argue could discourage short, well-designed experiences in favor of padded content to maximize engagement. Additionally, players and industry figures question the value of another subscription and digital launcher, given that indie games are usually affordable and owning them outright remains preferable. Indie Pass faces challenges in convincing both developers and customers to adopt its platform amid skepticism.
