**Midnight Ramen Shop Demo Offers Relaxed Restaurant Management but Lacks Consequence**

*Midnight Ramen Shop* is a restaurant management simulator that aims for a calmer pace by removing customer interaction stress. Players prepare ramen bowls according to custom orders submitted through a shutter system, focusing on crafting without face-to-face encounters. The demo highlights a simple and accessible gameplay loop of assembling dishes with specific tools and ingredients.

However, the pace remains slow with limited customer flow and few meaningful upgrades, leading to a lack of progression or challenge. An unusual observation during the demo was that customers often paid full price regardless of order accuracy, reducing the incentive for careful preparation. While this absence of negative consequences may appeal to players seeking relaxation, it diminishes the gameplay depth and realism. The game may benefit from refining these mechanics before full release to balance its laid-back approach with player engagement.

**Micron Highlights Importance of GPU Memory for Gaming Despite Exiting Consumer Market**

Memory manufacturer Micron published a blog emphasizing the growing role of GPU memory capacity in improving gaming performance and enabling next-generation visual effects. The post notes that increases in graphical fidelity, AI-driven rendering, and expansive game worlds drive demand for larger video memory pools, citing upcoming GDDR7 technology supporting up to 96 GB of VRAM.

This message draws attention due to Micron's recent decision to exit the consumer memory market and refocus on AI data center products. While the technical rationale behind the need for more GPU memory is valid, the company's pivot away from gaming hardware creates a disconnect for PC gamers. Further complicating the landscape, Nvidia reportedly plans to reduce production of some mid-range 16 GB GPUs, highlighting industry supply challenges. Micron's post underscores important trends but also reflects tensions between industry priorities and gamer expectations.
