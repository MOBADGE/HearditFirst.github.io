YouTuber Creates PC Side Panel with 15 Noctua Fans Reducing Temperatures by 20°C

YouTuber Major Hardware, known for hardware experimentation, recently built a unique PC side panel called the "Superdome," which integrates 15 Noctua 120 mm fans. Initially estimated to cost over $500, the project gained support from Noctua, which supplied fans and 3D printing materials to closely match their look. The panel was designed as a dome fitting a Lian Li O11 Dynamic XL case and was 3D-printed to house all fans.

Despite complicated cable management and minor fan connection issues, the Superdome proved effective in cooling. It operates quietly, consuming 27.6 watts and lowers CPU temperatures in games like Battlefield 6 from around 86°C to about 67°C, a reduction of nearly 20°C. While lacking dust filters and a particularly efficient setup, it shows potential as a strong cooling solution. Major Hardware has shared the blueprints online for anyone interested in building their own.

Steelseries Rival Pro Mini Wireless Gaming Mouse Leaked on Reddit

An unreleased Steelseries gaming mouse, the Rival Pro Mini, has appeared in an unboxing shared by a Reddit user. This mouse notably uses an ultra-wideband (UWB) wireless connection instead of the common 2.4 GHz band. UWB may reduce wireless interference but typically has a shorter range. The mouse weighs 49 grams, features an 8,000 Hz polling rate, and an "Infinity Power System" with rechargeable, swappable batteries, similar to Steelseries’ Arctis Nova Pro headphones.

Images suggest a design akin to the Steelseries Prime Wireless Pro family with some cuts to create a smaller, potentially more affordable model. The box label acknowledges that the design is tentative. This leak indicates Steelseries is exploring advanced wireless tech for gaming mice, following Elecom's UWB mouse released last year.

Crimson Desert Patch Adds QoL Improvements Including Mounted Teleportation and New Ability

Pearl Abyss released patch 1.03.00 for Crimson Desert introducing several quality-of-life changes. The update allows players to fast travel while mounted on horses, falling, swimming, or climbing — a significant convenience improvement over previous restrictions. Other gameplay updates include an improved lock-on system during boss fights and a new ability called Focused Aerial Roll, which provides a boost while flying.

Additional fixes address usability for some puzzles, quest completion issues, NPC replacements, and UI improvements such as clearer faction quest icons and better item management. The patch also adds support for Intel Arc GPUs and new video settings. The changes address previous player feedback and help smooth out some of the game's quirks without altering core gameplay.

Nvidia Details How AI Accelerates GPU Design

At Nvidia’s GTC event, chief scientist Bill Dally shared insights into how the company uses artificial intelligence to speed up GPU development. Nvidia employs reinforcement learning-based programs like NVCell to automate adapting its large library of logic gate cells to new semiconductor process nodes. This previously manual task requiring extensive human effort now completes overnight on a GPU, often producing better designs.

AI also aids in design verification and optimizing chip components such as carry-lookahead adders, delivering improvements over human efforts. Furthermore, Nvidia fine-tuned a proprietary large language model, ChipNeMo, on internal design documents to assist junior engineers in understanding GPU architecture. This blend of AI tools helps Nvidia shorten design cycles and improve chip efficiency.

Overwatch Season 2 "Summit" Launches April 14 with New Hero and Gameplay Updates

Blizzard’s Overwatch Season 2, titled "Summit," begins April 14. The season introduces a new DPS hero, Sierra, equipped with abilities including an automatic Helix Rifle, a tracking shot that marks enemies, an Anchor Drone with grappling capability, and an ultimate drone that carpet bombs an area. Sierra also features perks that enhance her abilities in various ways.

Beyond new content, the update improves several existing hero abilities with new perks, returns the post-match accolades system redesigned to allow integrated voice chat, and revamps the competitive map voting system to favor majority choices and add a “random map” option. A new battle pass and store bundles accompany the release. The season continues Overwatch’s shift to a year-long story arc format.
