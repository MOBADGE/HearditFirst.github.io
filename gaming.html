Super Meat Boy 3D Fails to Capture Original's Distinctive Style

Super Meat Boy 3D, the latest entry in the well-regarded platformer series, has been released with mixed reception. While the core gameplay mechanics such as Meat Boy’s jump dynamics, wall running, and dashing remain intact and offer precise controls, the game struggles with its presentation. Critics point out that unlike the original's unique 2D pixel art style—marked by a blend of cartoonish violence and grim humor—the 3D iteration adopts a generic look common in many modern platformers. The absence of Edmund McMillen, co-creator of the original, is cited as a likely reason for the shift away from the series’ signature “vibe.”

The new game’s level design is described as lacking coherence and thematic clarity, appearing more like user-created maps designed for difficulty rather than rich world-building. This affects player engagement, as the eerie, apocalyptic atmosphere of the original is replaced by bright, somewhat cluttered environments that don’t evoke the same emotional impact. Despite these shortcomings, the gameplay remains competent, though certain levels can lean heavily on trial and error. This release demonstrates the challenges of translating a beloved 2D platformer identity into 3D without losing its core appeal.

World of Warcraft Players Attempt Early Raid Boss Kill Thwarted by Blizzard

Ahead of the official launch of World of Warcraft’s Midnight’s March of Quel'Danas raid, some players attempted to defeat an unreleased boss named L'ura prematurely. Using creative exploits involving damage-over-time behavior, resurrection sickness mechanics, and even sacrificing low-level creatures combined with a bugged attack, players devised a method to reduce the boss’s health significantly.

However, Blizzard had anticipated such attempts and implemented an invulnerability safeguard on L'ura, making it impossible to kill before the scheduled raid opening. While the players managed to inflict more damage than the boss’s apparent health, the game’s systems prevented the kill from registering. Although this closed the door on early completion, players found other ways to apply their discoveries in PvP and other content.

This incident highlights the ongoing cat-and-mouse dynamic between MMO developers and their communities, where exploits are quickly met with countermeasures to preserve the intended content progression schedule.

Eidos Montreal Faces Major Layoffs and Leadership Change

Eidos Montreal has announced another round of layoffs amounting to 124 employees across production and support roles. Alongside this workforce reduction, longtime studio head David Anfossi has departed the company. His role included key leadership on franchises such as Deus Ex and Shadow of the Tomb Raider.

This move follows a series of layoffs since early 2024 and the reported cancellation of several projects, signaling a strategic shift as the studio focuses on co-development contributions to games like Obsidian’s Grounded 2 and Playground Games’ Fable reboot. The studio’s acknowledgment of changing project needs and resource concentration suggests a restructuring to align with new priorities.

The cuts at Eidos Montreal contribute to a broader industry trend of layoffs across notable studios in recent months, reflecting ongoing economic pressures affecting game development sectors globally.

Inzoi Studio Reflects on Early Access Challenges

Hyungjun “Kjun” Kim, head of Inzoi Studio and developer of the Sims-like life simulation Inzoi, has expressed some regrets over the decision to release the game in early access. Despite over two decades of experience in game development, Kim found that creating a life sim is more complex than anticipated, especially when competing with well-established series like The Sims.

Inzoi sold over a million copies at launch but has seen a decline in concurrent players on Steam. Some criticisms focus on the game's lack of deep simulation features and social interaction polish, issues common in early access releases. Kim emphasized that early access was necessary due to the inability to fully test the game internally, effectively making players testers at purchase, a trade-off he finds unfortunate for users.

The studio plans continued development with new content roadmaps, improved mod support, and user-generated content to enhance the game’s longevity and community engagement.

Peak’s Update Introduces Extensive Customization Options

The adventure title Peak has launched its "Play It Your Way Update," offering players a wide array of customizable settings for their climbing expeditions. Features include toggling environmental hazards such as fall damage, hunger, and hostile creatures, as well as options to control item availability like removing or limiting blowguns.
