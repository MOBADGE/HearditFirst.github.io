Arc Raiders Quest Guides: Clamoring for Attention and Dust on the Wires

Arc Raiders players received a couple of new quest walkthroughs with the recent Flashpoint update. The quest **Clamoring for Attention** involves using various noise-making devices to test how machines respond to sound. Completing it requires collecting three Wires and one Battery to repair or power devices in three different Blue Gate locations: the Warehouse Complex siren on the roof (requires three Wires), a boombox in the Village area (requires a Battery), and sounding a bus horn at Checkpoint (no additional items required). Players should be cautious, as activating these noisy devices can attract enemies or other players. The quest rewards include Lure Grenades, Noisemakers, and Tagging Grenades, tools useful for distracting Arc machines.

Meanwhile, the **Dust on the Wires** quest directs players to investigate a missing scout patrol near Spaceport. Players must visit Field Depots, interact with terminals, and locate patrol traces near the Maintenance Hangar by overturned trolleys to recover and deliver a Scout Patrol Note. The quest involves several stages with recommended exploration routes and highlights the importance of carrying a safe pocket to protect quest items. Both quests add depth to Arc Raiders’ evolving mission structure, encouraging exploration and resource management.

Crimson Desert Voice Actor Details Challenges in Narrative Cohesion

Alec Newman, the voice actor for Kliff, the protagonist of Crimson Desert, revealed that the game’s story faced significant development hurdles. In a recent podcast, he shared that recording sessions spanned nearly five years, beginning with what he thought was only a demo phase. Newman explained that the narrative shifted multiple times, with evolving character backgrounds and plot points that weren’t always clear, making it difficult to deliver a cohesive performance.

He emphasized the importance of storytelling in games and pushed for clearer character motivations, noting that some key emotional moments tying Kliff to his comrades were underwritten. This caught attention especially since the game received mixed feedback regarding its story pacing and clarity. The revelations provide context for the narrative critiques voiced by players and reviewers, explaining the disjointed feeling some experienced despite strong individual segments.

TSMC’s Chip Manufacturing Capacity Fully Booked Until 2028

Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC), the largest global chip foundry, is reportedly sold out through 2028, including its advanced N2 node used in GPUs and CPUs by major companies like Nvidia, Apple, AMD, and Intel. South Korean reports indicate that even TSMC’s planned Arizona fab, scheduled for production by 2030, has pre-sold its entire capacity.

The surge in demand is partly due to new customers from the AI sector such as Google and Amazon, in addition to established players. With only TSMC and Samsung capable of 2nm chip manufacturing, the strain on supply may push customers toward competitors like Samsung, which has recently signed deals with Tesla and Nvidia. This shortage underscores ongoing challenges in semiconductor supply chains, which can affect the release and pricing of future gaming hardware.

The Slay the Spire Board Game Expands with Mod-Inspired Downfall DLC

Contention Games has launched a Kickstarter for a new expansion to the **Slay the Spire** board game titled **Downfall**, inspired by one of the PC game's most popular mods of the same name. Unlike the base game, which faithfully recreates the original’s roguelike card gameplay, this expansion allows players to assume the roles of iconic enemies like the Slime Boss, Hexaghost, and the Guardian, as well as a mod-specific hero called the Hermit.

Downfall adds new enemies, bosses, relics, potions, and cards, offering fresh gameplay perspectives. Pricing ranges from $84 for the expansion alone to $268 for a bundle including the base game’s collector edition. The expansion is notable for drawing its content from fan-created material, a relatively unusual approach in board game adaptations of video games. The campaign runs until early April, with future retail availability expected.

Super Meat Boy 3D attempts to translate the high-precision platforming of the acclaimed 2D original into three dimensions but falls short in visual and thematic impact.
