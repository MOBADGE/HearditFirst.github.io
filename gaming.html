Memory Prices Drive Chinese DIY Market Toward DDR3 Motherboards

Due to sharply rising prices for DDR5 and DDR4 memory modules, the Chinese PC DIY market is seeing a surprising shift back to DDR3 motherboards. Recent reports from TrendForce highlight a near 10% increase in DDR4 prices in just one week, with DDR5 memory also in short supply. This price spike is linked to AI industry demand consuming a large proportion of memory wafers, cautious inventory releases by suppliers, and upcoming Lunar New Year fab shutdowns. With newer memory costly and supplies tight, some buyers are opting for older generation hardware compatible with DDR3 memory, such as Intel 6th to 9th Gen CPUs. While DDR3 is outdated, it still supports minimum specs for certain recent games, like *Arc Raiders*, making it a cost-effective option amid the current memory shortage.
