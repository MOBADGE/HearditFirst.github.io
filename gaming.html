**Marathon Update: New Map, Overpowered Shotgun, and Community ARG**

Bungie's extraction shooter Marathon continues to evolve as players explore its third map, Outpost, which unlocks at level 12. Outpost offers a more compact but highly fortified environment, surrounded by walls and patrols, with the addition of a persistent environmental hazard that drains health in exposed areas. This design encourages tactical play with emphasis on verticality and cover, drawing comparisons to Bungie's earlier titles. The gameplay experience also highlights subtle features such as the sound of doors opening and closing, which influence stealth strategies.

A standout weapon in ongoing player discussions is Marathon’s double-barrel shotgun, noted for its high damage output that can down full-health opponents with a single close-range blast. While its rarity and costly ammo somewhat balance this power, its prominence is reshaping player tactics in PvP.

Additionally, the community’s lore and puzzle engagement have ramped up with the discovery of active terminals across the Perimeter map. These terminals broadcast cryptic audio logs from Durandal, the AI villain from classic Marathon games, hinting at the upcoming Cryo Archive map. Bungie has acknowledged this development by linking to a related website, encouraging players to delve into the unfolding alternate reality game (ARG) elements integrated into Marathon, adding layers of exploration beyond standard gameplay.

**Artist Credit Restored After Bungie’s Use of Stolen Art**

The controversy around artist Antireal’s work being used without permission in Bungie’s projects, including Marathon and Destiny 2, has reached a resolution. Bungie admitted the unauthorized use was committed by a former employee and conducted a review of assets. Antireal confirmed the issue was settled to her satisfaction and has since been officially credited as a visual design consultant in Marathon. This acknowledgment, although subtle, holds significance in the context of professional recognition within the games industry.

However, Antireal reported continued unauthorized use of her artwork, notably on an official Xbox controller, indicating ongoing challenges surrounding intellectual property rights for artists working with major studios.

**Stardew Valley’s Upcoming NPC Sandy: Schedule and Gifting**

Ahead of the anticipated Stardew Valley 1.7 update, which will introduce Sandy as a marriage candidate, players can begin building friendship with her at the Oasis in the Calico Desert. Sandy maintains a predictable schedule, predominantly at her shop, and appears in specific events such as the Desert Festival. Players are advised on gift preferences to maximize relationship points, with certain flowers like Sweet Pea and Crocus being her favored items, while others such as Holly are disliked. Sandy’s introduction broadens the social and narrative depth of Stardew Valley, encouraging exploration beyond Pelican Town.

**Valve’s Steam Machine Faces Possible Delay Amid Hardware Shortages**

Valve’s planned launch of its new lineup, including the Steam Machine living room PC, Steam Controller, and Steam Frame VR headset, is encountering uncertainty due to widespread shortages in memory and storage components. Initially slated for release in the first half of 2026, Valve now acknowledges supply constraints and escalating costs may postpone shipping into 2027. The pricing challenge is notable since Valve aims to keep costs competitive without subsidizing hardware like traditional consoles. This situation underscores ongoing supply chain difficulties affecting the broader tech and gaming markets.

**Diablo 2 Expansion Revives Decades-Old Easter Egg for Loot Farming**

With the Reign of the Warlock expansion, Diablo 2 players have found new value in a long-standing easter egg known as the "souls dance," involving leaving wisps alive to trigger a hidden 19-hour magic find buff. This mechanic now complements changes in the late-game loot farming approach, notably improving drop rates for high-tier items from new "herald" enemies in terrorized zones. The development demonstrates how older game content can gain fresh relevance post-expansion, impacting player strategies and the game's longevity in the loot-driven RPG genre.

**The Legend of Khiimori Gains Praise for Horse Realism**

Early impressions of The Legend of Khiimori horse sim highlight its detailed and realistic depiction of equine behavior and genetics, with comparisons drawn to Red Dead Redemption 2’s acclaimed horse mechanics. While still in early access and experiencing some bugs and missing features, the game’s blend of historical Mongolian setting and deep horse training systems has generated positive response among horse enthusiasts. The developer’s commitment to patching issues and expanding the experience signals potential growth in this niche simulation space
