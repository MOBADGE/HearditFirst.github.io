Epic Games Layoffs Impact Fortnite Development

Epic Games recently laid off over 1,000 employees, reducing its workforce by about 20%, leaving around 4,000 staff. Fortnite producer Robby Williams addressed the community, asking for patience as the remaining teams "pick up the pieces" and adapt to the changes. He acknowledged the difficulty ahead and said the impacts on Fortnite throughout 2026 and beyond are unclear. This substantial staff cut follows CEO Tim Sweeney’s comment that Fortnite faces challenges delivering consistent quality with each season. A company-wide meeting is planned to clarify future plans. The scale of layoffs suggests a challenging period for Fortnite’s development and support.

Horizon Chase Turbo to Be Delisted in June

The arcade racer Horizon Chase Turbo and its mobile predecessor will be removed from digital stores starting June 1. Existing owners will still be able to download them, but new purchases will end. The games are notable for lacking licensed vehicles and online multiplayer, relying solely on arcade-style single-player racing. The reason behind the delisting is unclear, but it comes shortly after Epic Games acquired Horizon Chase developer Aquiris and during Epic’s recent layoffs. Meanwhile, Horizon Chase 2 remains available on platforms including Epic Games Store and Apple Arcade.

Caves of Qud Comes to Nintendo Switch

The roguelike RPG Caves of Qud has been successfully ported to Nintendo Switch, an unexpected move given the game’s complexity and reliance on keyboard controls. Co-creator Brian Bucklew said the port was driven by a desire to "solve impossible problems." The game underwent a UI overhaul in 2024 to better support gamepad controls, which helped its transition to Switch. The port has sold well, even reaching a top-five position in combined physical and digital sales rankings. The developers are now exploring a mobile port, aiming to adapt controls and UI for touchscreens and portrait mode.

1047 Games Announces New Movement Shooter Project

1047 Games, the studio behind Splitgate, revealed it is developing a new movement-based shooter inspired by games like Titanfall and Call of Duty: Black Ops 3. CEO Ian Proulx emphasized the studio’s ambition to become multi-game focused. This announcement comes after Splitgate 2’s troubled launch and subsequent reversion to beta status, along with staff layoffs. The new game aims to recapture fans of fast-paced shooters with parkour or mobility mechanics. 1047 plans to start playtesting soon and is inviting interested players to sign up for early access.

World of Warcraft Guild Disqualified for Exploiting New Raid

In the newly released Midnight raid for World of Warcraft, the guild RAoV QA Strikes Back claimed a world first kill using exploits. They instantly dealt nearly 300 million damage to the boss Chimaerus while using low-level gear, prompting Blizzard to disqualify and remove them from the official leaderboard. This guild previously exploited bosses for fame and was banned in a prior expansion. Meanwhile, legitimate raiders have yet to defeat Chimaerus, with many focusing on the main Voidspire raid instead. Blizzard’s move underscores ongoing enforcement against cheating in competitive content.

Stardew Valley Player Misled by AI, Brews Hundreds of Useless Rice Juice

A Stardew Valley player shared a cautionary tale after following incorrect advice from a Google AI summary about making vinegar. The player milled unmilled rice directly in a keg to produce vinegar but ended up with 136 bottles of "rice juice," a less valuable item. The error stemmed from the AI incorrectly stating that unmilled rice can be used for vinegar production, which contradicts official game mechanics requiring milled rice. The incident highlights the risks of relying on AI-generated gaming tips without verification and the importance of consulting dedicated game wikis or communities.
