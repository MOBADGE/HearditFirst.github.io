**New Retro Adventure: Witchhunter.exe Blends Witch Trials and Glitch Horror**

Witchhunter.exe is an upcoming retro-style adventure game that casts players as a witch hunter in a Salem-inspired setting. The game incorporates a text-based interrogation mechanic where you must determine which women are allied with the Devil. An unusual element is the Devil’s presence as invasive pop-up boxes, introducing a glitch-horror motif reminiscent of games like Pony Island. Combat involves timely word selection to avoid attacks, adding a layer of tension to interactions.

This title stands out for its stylistic homage to classic horror films and old-school RPG interfaces, offering a fresh take on choose-your-own-adventure gameplay within a simulated "haunted" operating system. Witchhunter.exe is still in development with a beta playtest forthcoming on Steam, potentially appealing to players interested in narrative-driven and horror-themed indie games.

**Five New Steam Releases You Might Have Missed**

Several new games have launched on Steam recently, providing a range of genres and styles:

- *Mama’s Sleeping Angels* is a multiplayer dream-world shooter blending surrealism with cooperative gameplay and cursed object powers.

- *Lucid Blocks* offers a procedurally generated voxel world akin to Minecraft but with an emphasis on liminal and dreamlike environments, including cities and abandoned structures.

- *The State of Nowhere* is a resource management game with moral decisions where players allocate food under a totalitarian regime, featuring multiple endings.

- *Thysiastery* is a pixel-art, turn-based dungeon crawler where players navigate a shifting labyrinth to escape, with numerous skills and party members.

- *Pluto* combines roguelike dungeon crawling with deckbuilding mechanics, featuring eight characters and elemental synergies on a quest that mixes dark humor and fantasy.

These games showcase the diversity in indie and niche titles released on Steam, offering options for players seeking new experiences in cooperative, survival, or strategy genres.

**Controversial Patch for Tomb Raider I-III Remastered Sparks Backlash**

The remastered collection of the first three Tomb Raider games recently received a free update adding a new challenge mode and ten new character outfits. However, the updated outfits have been widely criticized by the community for their low visual quality. Several users on Steam reported technical issues including broken audio, AI problems, and lost save files after the patch.

Original lead artist Giovanni Lucca confirmed on social media that he and the original development team were not involved in this update. There have been unconfirmed rumors regarding the use of generative AI in creating the new assets.

This patch follows last year’s controversy when unauthorized AI-generated voice lines were removed from the remasters. The situation highlights ongoing concerns about quality control and AI’s role in game asset production, especially in legacy game updates.

**Inside the PC of Abubakar Salim, Actor and Game Studio Founder**

Abubakar Salim, known for his roles in *Assassin’s Creed: Origins* and *Raised by Wolves*, as well as founder of developer Surgent Studios, shared an overview of his PC gaming habits. He cites early influences like *Age of Empires* and *World of Warcraft* and currently alternates between games such as *No Rest for the Wicked* and *Dave the Diver*, enjoying their progression systems and gameplay loops.

His oldest installed game is *Warcraft 3*, which holds nostalgic value due to multiplayer experiences with friends. The game he has logged the most hours in is *Garry’s Mod*, used extensively for roleplaying.

Salim’s desktop is reportedly cluttered with many icons, a situation he finds amusing but impractical. Outside gaming software, he relies heavily on Final Draft for scriptwriting, tying his creative projects together. His insights reveal a balance between gaming, acting, and game development in his daily routine.
