Europol’s Operation PowerOFF Disrupts DDoS-for-Hire Services

Europol, in cooperation with 21 countries, has made significant progress against criminal infrastructure facilitating distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks. During a coordinated action week starting April 13, the operation took down 53 domains offering illegal "booter" services, issued 25 search warrants, and arrested four individuals. Warning notices were sent to 75,000 users linked to these platforms.

The crackdown targeted services that allowed users with limited technical knowledge to launch disruptive attacks against online services such as marketplaces and telecommunications providers. Europol emphasized the varied motivations behind attacks, including financial extortion and ideological purposes. With key infrastructure dismantled and over 3 million criminal user accounts exposed, Operation PowerOFF is now entering a prevention phase. Efforts include removing related URLs from search results and targeting young people with educational messages to discourage cybercrime.

This development is important as DDoS attacks continue to disrupt gaming services, with notable incidents earlier this year affecting titles like Arc Raiders and Final Fantasy XIV Online. Europol’s action represents ongoing international collaboration to reduce these threats at the source.

Pragmata Surpasses One Million Copies Sold Shortly After Release

Capcom has announced that its new third-person sci-fi action game *Pragmata* sold over one million copies within two days following its April 17 launch. The game, developed mainly by younger Capcom staff and delayed three times over four years, was initially revealed in 2020 with a planned 2022 release.

*Pragmata* combines elements of third-person shooting and hacking gameplay set in a dystopian moon-based New York. Despite its prolonged development, early reception is positive, including an 87 score from PC Gamer and strong demo interest exceeding two million downloads. The swift sales milestone highlights solid market interest for this unique, albeit niche, sci-fi title.

This success signals that even games with complex development and unconventional premises can find a receptive audience. It also establishes a foundation for potential future additions or sequels.

Windrose Outperforms Pragmata on Steam Amid Strong Pirate Game Demand

Released April 14 into early access, *Windrose*, a pirate-themed survival game, has sold over one million copies in six days and currently holds over 220,000 concurrent players on Steam. It ranks as the platform’s second highest-earning game after *Counter-Strike 2*, surpassing *Pragmata* in concurrent user numbers despite the latter’s broader console availability.

The performance underscores the continued appetite for pirate-related games within the PC gaming community. Unlike other recent pirate titles, *Windrose* has gained considerable traction, suggesting the genre retains strong commercial potential. For the week, the top three Steam revenue generators were *Counter-Strike 2*, *Windrose*, and *Pragmata*.

These figures provide insight into market trends, favoring multiplayer survival and accessible adventure experiences over more niche, story-driven sci-fi shooters.

RuneScape: Dragonwilds Introduces Fishing Skill Tomorrow

The upcoming update to *RuneScape: Dragonwilds* will add the long-missed fishing skill to the game on April 21. The absence of fishing since the game’s launch had disappointed some players, given its traditional importance in the *RuneScape* franchise.

The new skill includes typical fishing mechanics like rod and net use around ponds and pools, plus a feature to create mounted trophies from prized catches. Additionally, players can use magic spells to fish, providing alternative methods of gathering. The fishing skill will have a level cap of 99, with a special cape awarded upon maxing out.

Reintroducing fishing restores a key component of the classic *RuneScape* experience and expands activities available in the survival crafting world of Dragonwilds.

CPU Shortage Remains a Challenge as Industry Awaits Intel’s 18A Chips

A recent report highlights that the global CPU shortage is more severe for processors than for memory chips, affecting various AMD and Intel models. Enterprise PC builders relying on these chips face constraints despite high prices, with shortages ongoing since 2025.
