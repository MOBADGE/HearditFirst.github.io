Pinball Map Website Catalogs Over 51,000 Public Pinball Machines Worldwide

For fans of physical pinball, a comprehensive online resource called Pinball Map has expanded to cover more than 51,000 public machines at over 12,000 locations globally. The crowd-sourced platform, dating back to 2008, lets users find nearby pinball machines in bars, arcades, museums, and other public venues. The site also offers free Android and iOS apps for on-the-go access. Users can contribute new machine locations and submit high scores, fostering a community around these vintage and modern machines. Given the rarity of tactile gaming experiences in an increasingly digital world, Pinball Map serves as a practical tool for players looking to engage with physical arcade entertainment.

Talystro: A Roguelike Deckbuilder That Demands Numerical Precision

The upcoming game Talystro introduces a unique twist on roguelike deckbuilders by focusing on precision in numerical attacks rather than simply dealing the highest damage. Players control a mouse character battling enemies represented by target numbers, requiring them to craft attack values that exactly match these numbers using dice and cards that modify results. The gameplay balances mathematical problem-solving with resource management, as only a limited number of dice and card slots are available each turn. This demands careful planning and strategic sequencing to defeat waves of enemies. Talystro features a retro cartoon art style, and although still in early development with some interface roughness, it promises a fresh take on turn-based combat. A public demo is expected soon on Steam.
