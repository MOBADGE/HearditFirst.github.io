Fallout Mod Maker Calls for Creation Engine Upgrade

Dean Carter, lead developer of the Fallout: London mod for Fallout 4, has expressed concerns over Bethesda's continued use of the aging Creation Engine. Originating from the 2006 engine behind Oblivion, the engine is seen as limiting innovations such as smoother loading, better optimization, and gameplay features like drivable vehicles or metro systems. Carter highlighted that late-stage development constraints led to performance and loading issues in Fallout: London, suggesting that Bethesda should consider either upgrading or replacing the engine for future projects like Fallout 5. Nvidia Faces Lawsuit Over Use of Pirated Books for AI Training

An amended class-action lawsuit filed in California alleges that Nvidia knowingly used pirated books from illegal sources, including a repository called Anna's Archive, to train its large language models. Internal communications reportedly show Nvidia staff exploring the use of this 'shadow library' despite its legal risks. Nvidia previously defended the use of similar datasets under fair use, arguing that AI learns statistical correlations rather than copying material. This case is part of an ongoing legal debate surrounding AI training data, with previous suits against other companies producing mixed rulings. The outcome could have implications for how AI companies source training data going forward.
