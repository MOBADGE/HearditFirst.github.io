**Sega Reports High Praise but Disappointing Sales**

Sega Sammy shared its financial results for 2025, revealing a puzzling disconnect between positive reception and actual sales. Despite releasing several critically well-received games — including Virtua Fighter 5: R.E.V.O., Like a Dragon: Pirate Yakuza in Hawaii, and Atlus’ Metaphor: ReFantazio — the company noted stagnant sales figures. Sonic Racing: CrossWorlds sold about one million units, falling short of expectations, and issues with Football Manager 26 also impacted performance. Sega attributes these sales challenges partly to weaknesses in marketing and sales strategies and is reforming its global publishing approach to improve revenue. On a positive note, character licensing and amusement machines showed record earnings, underscoring strengths outside direct game sales. However, Rovio, acquired by Sega in 2023, continues to underperform, leading to a $200 million write-off.

**US Court Rules Continued Use Can Imply Consent to Terms of Service**

A recent US court decision concerning Bluetooth tracker company Tile, owned by Life360, ruled that by continuing to use a device or service after terms of service updates, users implicitly consent to the new terms. This ruling arose from a class-action lawsuit alleging Tile’s inadequate anti-stalking safeguards. Plaintiffs argued they had not agreed to updated terms related to arbitration, but the court found that ongoing app use after notification — even if emails landed in spam folders or went unnoticed — constituted acceptance. The decision highlights ongoing challenges users face with digital agreements and privacy protections, especially given Bluetooth trackers’ potential risks, including data leaks identified in past research.

**LG Offers High-Refresh 1440p OLED Gaming Monitor Bundle**

LG’s latest announcement targets PC gamers who prefer 27-inch 1440p monitors. The company is selling its new UltraGear 27GX790B-B OLED monitor, featuring a 540 Hz refresh rate and 335 nits brightness, at the standard price while including a 27-inch 200 Hz IPS panel (27G610A-B) as a free bonus. The OLED panel offers enhanced response time and HDR certification improvements compared to previous models. Although the bundled IPS model retails separately for about £199, the deal mainly highlights the growing interest in OLED technology for gaming and the resilience of the 1440p 27-inch format amid competition from larger and wider screens.

**Anthropic Launches AI-Powered Claude Code Review Tool**

Anthropic unveiled Claude Code Review, a new AI tool that automates source code examination to identify bugs and issues. Operating on-site without requiring plugins, the system employs parallel searches, verification to filter false positives, and severity ranking. While the tool doesn't approve code changes, it aims to reduce bottlenecks in manual code review processes. Early feedback from industry users notes that the new tool offers deeper analysis at a higher computational cost compared to existing AI integrations. The launch reflects ongoing integration of AI in software development, though it raises questions about cost-effectiveness and the balance between automation and human oversight.

**Crimson Desert’s Updated PC Requirements Include Handheld Gaming PCs**

Developer Pearl Abyss revealed detailed PC requirements for the upcoming game Crimson Desert. Minimum specs are relatively modest with older six-core CPUs and mid-range GPUs required for 1080p gameplay at 30 fps. For 4K at 60 fps on Ultra settings, newer mid-tier processors and recent high-end GPUs like Nvidia’s RTX 5070 Ti suffice. Notably, Pearl Abyss listed system requirements specifically for handheld gaming PCs, including the Asus ROG Xbox Ally and its Xbox Ally X variant, treating them akin to consoles with preset performance modes. This is a distinctive approach given these devices run Windows, not console OSes. Overall, the specifications suggest wide accessibility across current PC hardware tiers, balancing graphical fidelity against performance.
