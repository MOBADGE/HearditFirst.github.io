Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles Crossover in Magic: The Gathering

The upcoming Magic: The Gathering set will feature a Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (TMNT) crossover, bringing New York City back as the setting. Unlike past Universes Beyond expansions, the TMNT set will include a pre-constructed Commander deck inspired by the TMNT video games, featuring cards named after gaming concepts like Game Over and High Score, with pixel art enhancing the arcade nostalgia. Alongside this, a Turtle Team-Up box will allow up to four players to cooperate in boss battles, aimed at helping new players learn Magic through varying difficulty levels. The set also introduces new mechanics: "Sneak," which lets players play cards during an opponent's blocker phase, and "Disappear," which triggers effects when cards leave the battlefield. The "Alliance" mechanic, previously seen in the Avatar: The Last Airbender set, returns to emphasize teamwork. Cards featuring artwork by TMNT co-creator Kevin Eastman will also be included. The TMNT Magic set will release in Magic Arena on March 3, with paper versions available on March 6 and prerelease events starting February 27.

YouTube Outage Resolved

YouTube experienced a significant outage impacting its recommendation system earlier today. Many users encountered "something went wrong" errors when browsing, with DownDetector reporting over 340,000 problem reports at its peak. The problem affected multiple YouTube surfaces, including the homepage, YouTube app, Music, and Kids versions, causing videos not to appear properly. YouTube acknowledged the issue and confirmed that the homepage was restored but a complete fix was still underway. This disruption highlights the dependency many users have on stable video platform operations across devices, especially as services increasingly integrate recommendations into their user experience.
