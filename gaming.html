**Resident Evil Requiem PC Requirements Offer Accessibility**

Capcom has shared the recommended PC specifications for the upcoming survival horror game Resident Evil Requiem, set to release on February 27. The requirements are notably modest, making the game accessible on older or less powerful hardware. The minimum graphics card required is a GeForce GTX 1660 or Radeon RX 5500 XT, both released over six years ago. Recommended specs suggest a GeForce RTX 2060 Super or Radeon RX 6600, graphics cards from 2019 and later. This means that players do not need to invest heavily in new hardware, which is significant given the rising costs of PC components. The moderate specs continue a recent trend among AAA PC game releases aiming for broader accessibility.

**Rust Developer Views AI as a Development Tool**

Alistair McFarlane, COO of Facepunch, the studio behind Rust, has expressed a positive perspective on the use of artificial intelligence in game development. Speaking to Sky News, McFarlane described AI as "another tool" that can improve efficiency and allow developers to focus more on creative aspects, despite acknowledging its disruptive nature. This stance aligns with industry trends, as reports show that over half of Japanese game developers now use AI in some capacity. Companies like Sony emphasize AI as a support tool rather than a replacement for human creativity, while Nintendo remains cautious about rapid adoption. Facepunch's approach highlights a balanced view of AI's potential benefits and challenges.

**Amazon‚Äôs King of Meat to Shut Down After Less Than a Year**

Amazon Game Studios and Glowmade announced that King of Meat, their online multiplayer dungeon-crawler released in October 2025, will cease operations on April 9, 2026. Despite a substantial marketing effort at launch including prominent streamers, player numbers declined steeply, with Steam data showing low double-digit concurrent players in recent weeks. The developers cited difficulty in attracting the expected audience as the main reason for the shutdown. The game will remain accessible until the servers close, and refunds will be processed through respective digital storefronts. This closure adds to the growing list of live-service games that struggle to retain players shortly after launch.

**Xbox Assures No Immediate Studio Changes After Leadership Shift**

Matt Booty, newly promoted to Executive Vice President and Chief Content Officer at Xbox, addressed concerns regarding organizational stability following recent leadership changes. In a memo, Booty stated that no structural changes are planned for the nearly 40 studios under Xbox Game Studios. This clarification comes amid some apprehension that the new Xbox CEO, Asha Sharma, known for her AI expertise, might influence studio operations or lead to layoffs. Xbox studios have recently faced layoffs and project cancellations, notably in mid-2025, including the closure of The Initiative. For now, Microsoft appears to be maintaining the current setup as it navigates the leadership transition.

**Bungie Commits to Permanent Bans for Marathon Cheaters**

Ahead of the official launch of Marathon, Bungie has announced a stringent anti-cheating policy. Players caught cheating will receive permanent bans without second chances, emphasizing fairness in the competitive environment. Bungie acknowledges that no detection system is flawless and has established an appeals process for disputed cases. The developer has implemented measures like the "Fog of War" system to limit players' map awareness, reducing common cheats such as wall hacks and loot reveals. This proactive stance aims to promote integrity and enjoyable gameplay from the outset of Marathon's release.
