**Disco Elysium's Extensive Writing Challenges Branching Narrative Software**

A recent documentary episode on the making of Disco Elysium revealed that the game's rich and densely written narrative caused technical challenges during development. The writing team used Articy, a tool designed for branching storylines, but found that Disco Elysium's sheer volume of dialogue and text overwhelmed the software. Writer Helen Hindpere noted that unlike previous projects where the issue was insufficient writing, here the difficulty was managing too much content, leading to slowdowns and occasional crashes in Articy. This highlights the challenges of creating deeply layered narrative games within existing tooling limits, underscoring how ambitious writing can strain development resources.

**Ecco the Dolphin Reboot Details Emerge Ahead of April Reveal**

New information has surfaced about the upcoming Ecco the Dolphin reboot and remasters, scheduled for an official reveal in April 2026. The new game is in development by California-based studio A&R Atelier, with original Ecco producer Ed Annunziata serving as chief creative officer. The team reportedly includes some of the original creators alongside new members. While specifics remain sparse, the confirmation of multiple Ecco projects suggests renewed focus on the franchise. Given the original's legacy as a challenging and atmospheric Sega Genesis title, this revival may attract interest from both nostalgic players and new audiences when more details are released.

**Hytale's Refund Policy Offers More Flexibility Than Steam**

Hytale, the recently launched sandbox adventure, offers a refund policy that differs from Steam's standard terms. While Steam typically restricts refunds to under two hours of playtime within 14 days of purchase, Hytale extends the allowable playtime to four hours within the same two-week window. This change responds to player feedback that longer play sessions may be needed to evaluate complex games properly. However, some payment methods for Hytale purchases do not qualify for refunds, so customers are advised to verify eligibility before buying. This more generous policy may serve as a useful model for similar games.

**God of War TV Series Casts Ryan Hurst as Kratos**

The Prime Video adaptation of God of War has cast Ryan Hurst in the lead role of Kratos. Hurst is known for previous roles in Sons of Anarchy and The Walking Dead, as well as providing the voice for Thor in God of War: Ragnarok. The casting marks a departure from Christopher Judge, the voice actor for Kratos in the games, although Judge is reportedly not involved in the live-action series. The project underwent significant changes recently, including a showrunner and writing team reboot, now led by Ronald Moore. Production remains in early stages with no further casting details yet announced.
