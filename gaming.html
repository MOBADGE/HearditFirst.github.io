Slay the Spire 2 Explores New Game Modes and Ending Development

Mega Crit co-founder Casey Yano revealed that the team behind *Slay the Spire 2* is considering three new game modes to expand player interaction beyond the current offerings. These include a competitive mode for more intense player-versus-player action, a condensed or faster mode for those with limited time, and a multiplayer or social interaction mode based on existing systems. While specifics remain vague, these ideas reflect the team's experimentation during early access, which is expected to continue for another year or two. Yano cautioned that not all concepts may materialize, as some ideas are dropped during development if they dilute the core experience.

Regarding the game's final ending, Yano confirmed plans for a more elaborate and significant conclusion than the current placeholder, which involves a battle with a figure known as The Architect. The ending has yet to be finalized and will be shaped partly by player feedback during early access. The development focus for now remains on alternative acts, characters, and game modes, with the ending likely appearing closer to the full release.

**Why it matters:** This insight highlights Mega Crit’s open, iterative approach, aiming to refine *Slay the Spire 2*’s design responsively. New modes and a polished ending could enhance replayability and narrative depth in one of the most successful deckbuilders.

Samson Carves a Unique Path in Open World Crime Genre Amid Downsizing

Swedish developer Liquid Swords’ upcoming open-world crime game *Samson*, releasing April 8 on Steam and Epic Games Store, stands out with an unusual day-by-day structure. Players manage a protagonist’s escalating debt by choosing daily activities that consume finite action points, balancing story missions and illicit work. This roguelike-adjacent design emerged after the studio was forced to cut half of its team due to financial pressures.

Originally planned as a larger action RPG—with gun combat included—*Samson* shifted focus exclusively to hand-to-hand combat, deepening melee mechanics while emphasizing higher difficulty and strategic escapes in a gritty city environment. The developers compare the game’s vibe to classic action movies rather than the sprawling, gun-heavy Grand Theft Auto series, aiming for a tighter, more intense experience.

**Why it matters:** *Samson* reflects how real-world constraints can lead to innovative gameplay design. Its focus on melee combat and emergent challenges sets it apart in a crowded genre, offering a distinct alternative to open-world crime games.

Piranha Games Cuts 30% of Staff Amid Ongoing MechWarrior Challenges

Piranha Games, known for *MechWarrior 5: Mercenaries*, confirmed layoffs affecting about 30% of their workforce following an earlier round last year. The studio’s CEO Russ Bullock acknowledged the difficult decision but emphasized ongoing development, including a DLC planned for May and another product targeted for release this fall. Narrative director Chris Lowrey expressed uncertainty about the franchise’s long-term future but affirmed continued support as long as possible.

The layoffs followed disappointing sales of *MechWarrior 5: Clans*, despite positive reviews, and reflect broader industry challenges for niche single-purchase games. The company faces revenue pressures amid changes in audience reach and market dynamics.

**Why it matters:** These reductions underscore the volatility even established studios face, particularly with games relying on single-purchase models and smaller fanbases. The situation raises questions about the sustainability of long-term support for traditional MechWarrior titles.

Bungie Announces Upcoming Balance Changes for Marathon

Marathon director Joe Ziegler outlined several upcoming balance adjustments aimed at addressing gameplay imbalances. Planned changes include nerfing the excessively powerful knife damage and reducing the dominance of bubble shields and thermal scopes. Sniper weapons will see a reduction in overall effectiveness, while Vandal and Recon player classes are set for buffs to better compete.

Additionally, Bungie will introduce new items to improve the experience for solo players and provide more forgiving options in multiplayer encounters. These adjustments will roll out across two forthcoming patches, with further content updates to follow based on community feedback.

**Why it matters:** This proactive balancing effort aims to enhance competitive fairness and tactical variety in *Marathon*, demonstrating attention to player experience and responsiveness to community input.
