Austin Wintory Performs Unreleased Music from Canceled Game at Game Developers Choice Awards

During Thursday’s Game Developers Choice Awards, celebrated composer Austin Wintory played a previously unheard piece from a canceled project he described as the “dream job of all time.” The project was tied to Humanoid Origin, a studio led by former Mass Effect director Casey Hudson, which closed in 2024 without releasing a game. Wintory viewed the live performance as a personal and public way to cope with the cancellation, allowing the music to reach an audience despite the game’s demise. This moment underscored ongoing challenges in the industry, where several high-profile projects, including Perfect Dark and Rare’s Everwild, have recently been canceled or shuttered. The awards show also paid tribute to notable industry figures who passed away in the last year, including Rebecca Heineman, Julian Lefay, and Vince Zampella.

Zero Parades Demo Highlights Potential of Disco Elysium Successor

A demo of Zero Parades, the forthcoming RPG from ZA/UM and successor to Disco Elysium, revealed moments of humor and inventive narrative that suggest the game retains some of the original’s distinctive style. Players control Hershel, a disgraced spy navigating a city rife with intrigue. Two notable sequences—an intercom negotiation and a locked door keypad puzzle—combine problem-solving with comedic writing and meaningful character choices. While Zero Parades aims for a different tone, perhaps more sarcastic and less introspective than Disco Elysium, the demo preserved the original’s penchant for dialogue-driven gameplay and narrative surprises. The game continues to attract attention for how it balances innovation with the legacy of its predecessor.

Former EVE Online Developers Show New MMO with Persistent Characters

Klang Games, founded by former EVE Online developers, showcased Seed, an MMO featuring persistent player characters called "Seedlings" that continue their lives even when the player is offline. Seed blends deep simulation features, societal governance, and individual character stories, drawing inspiration from titles like RimWorld, The Sims, and Dwarf Fortress. Players can join or build societies with complex social and economic systems, which range from sprawling cities to small communities. The game’s closed beta includes emergent political drama reminiscent of EVE Online’s player-driven scenarios. Additionally, Klang plans a mobile companion app to allow players to manage their Seedlings remotely. Seed is still in closed beta, with wider access yet to be announced.

Shadowstone Begins Public Playtest Ahead of Early Access Release

Shadowstone, a tactical roguelike developed by Secret Door and Dreamhaven set in the Sunderfolk universe, has launched a surprise public playtest concurrent with the Future Games Show Spring Showcase. The playtest allows up to four players to experience initial content in solo or multiplayer modes, though progress will not carry over to the full early access release planned later in 2026. Shadowstone simplifies some RPG elements from Sunderfolk, adopting a mouse-and-keyboard control scheme and focusing on "runs" where players navigate randomized ruins using adaptable skill cards and equipment. The open nature of the playtest, with no NDA, invites community participation and feedback ahead of its official launch.

Industry Veteran Rob Pardo Urges Caution on Layoffs in GDC Keynote

At this year’s Game Developers Conference, former Blizzard chief creative officer Rob Pardo delivered the keynote address emphasizing the value of development teams over individual game titles. Drawing on decades of experience, including work on Starcraft and Warcraft 3, Pardo highlighted the unpredictable nature of game development and the importance of nurturing teams that have achieved success rather than disbanding them. He criticized the industry trend of laying off staff even after a commercially successful launch, noting that replacing seasoned teams risks losing hard-earned institutional knowledge. Pardo advised executives to respect and maintain the teams behind enduring games to foster sustained quality and player care.
