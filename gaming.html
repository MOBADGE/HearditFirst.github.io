**Star Wars Zero Company: Developers Praise Uncommon Risk-Taking on Turn-Based Tactics**

Greg Foertsch, founder of Bit Reactor, the studio behind Star Wars Zero Company, recently expressed his gratitude toward Lucasfilm and Respawn Entertainment for supporting their niche turn-based tactics project within the Star Wars universe. In an interview, Foertsch emphasized that this kind of risk-taking—backing a lesser-known genre with a high-profile IP—is increasingly rare in a market that often favors safer, derivative game designs. Despite the challenges, Star Wars Zero Company benefits from this unusual support, aiming to provide fresh strategic gameplay while leveraging a beloved franchise. The project’s success could signal a renewed willingness for big publishers to experiment beyond conventional genres.

**Narrative Direction in Zero Company Focuses on Team Dynamics and Moral Ambiguity**

Aaron Contreras, narrative director for Star Wars Zero Company, shared insights about the game’s story approach, distinguishing it from typical RPG tropes. Instead of centering on a single heroic figure, the game focuses on leadership over a diverse team of operators with conflicting personalities and viewpoints. Set during the morally complex Clone Wars era, the narrative explores shades of grey rather than the straightforward good versus evil seen in later Star Wars eras. The in-game bond system reflects this by fostering real friction and varied relationships among squadmates, challenging players to balance interpersonal tensions alongside tactical decisions. This grounding in team leadership aims to deepen player engagement in both story and gameplay.

**Robot Cache Storefront Reportedly Closing, Leaving Purchased Games Inaccessible**

A blockchain-powered PC game storefront, Robot Cache, appears to be shutting down, according to emails shared by users online. The store, founded by inXile’s Brian Fargo and launched in 2018, allowed users to resell digital games, leveraging blockchain as part of its infrastructure. However, the company cited insufficient user adoption and sales to maintain operations. Crucially, the closure means customers will lose access to their purchased games since the platform’s verification systems will go offline, effectively rendering the games unplayable even if downloaded. This situation highlights ongoing concerns around digital rights management and ownership in online storefronts, particularly those reliant on persistent verification servers.

**Composer Alexander Brandon Reflects on the Underrated Neverwinter Nights 2: Mask of the Betrayer Soundtrack**

In a feature celebrating game music, Alexander Brandon, known for work on Deus Ex and Unreal, discussed his collaboration on the soundtrack for Neverwinter Nights 2: Mask of the Betrayer (MotB). The expansion, noted for its mature storytelling and atmosphere, received contributions from Brandon alongside audio duo Rik Schaffer and Margaret Tang of Womb Music. Though MotB did not achieve widespread fame, its soundtrack blends dark fantasy tones with familiar D&D motifs, helping to establish an immersive setting unique in the Forgotten Realms series. Brandon recalled integrating his work with the existing themes and praised the creative leadership during development, underscoring the value of soundtrack composition in elevating narrative-driven RPG experiences.

**Tarn Adams on Gaming Interests: Factorio, Terraforming Games, and Dwarf Fortress**

Tarn Adams, co-creator of Dwarf Fortress, shared his current gaming interests in a recent interview. While best known for the complex fantasy colony sim that celebrated its 20th anniversary this year, Adams revealed a fascination with terraforming games like The Planet Crafter and incremental titles such as Terraformental. He discussed the limitations of Dwarf Fortress in accommodating science-fiction themes, which partly explains his exploration of related but distinct games. Notably, Adams has amassed over 1,400 hours in Factorio, highlighting his enjoyment of factory simulation and modding communities. His comments shed light on the varied tastes and ongoing creative passions behind one of PC gaming’s seminal indie projects.
