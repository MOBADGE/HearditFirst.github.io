**Rust Adds Hydro Power with Water Wheels in Spring Clean Update** Facepunch Studios has released the Spring Clean update for Rust, introducing water wheels as a new way to generate renewable energy. Unlike previous additions like wind turbines and solar panels, these water wheels can be placed in rivers or oceans to harness hydro power. Notably, the wheels can also be powered manually by players, including forcing handcuffed players to operate them, continuing Rust's harsh player interaction mechanics. Additional features in the update include the armored ladder hatch to better protect vertical base access, colored buttons for clearer operation, an overfishing mechanic to prevent reliance on abundant sea resources, and a streamlined technology unlock system. The update also brings back Rust’s Easter Egg Hunt event with themed rewards and costumes. Outside the game, Facepunch mentioned ongoing discussions about a potential Rust movie adaptation, though no immediate progress is expected.

**Petit Planet Beta Signups Open for Cozy Space Life Sim** HoYoVerse has announced signups are open for the upcoming closed beta of Petit Planet, a galactic life simulation game scheduled to start on April 21. Players manage their own planets within the Starsea universe, engaging in activities like farming, fishing, decorating, socializing, and exploring the Galactic Bazaar. The game emphasizes building relationships with “Neighbors” and shaping planetary environments through a vitality system called Luca. The forthcoming Stardrift Test will include new characters and expanded activities to enhance gameplay variety. While there is no confirmed release date yet, leaks indicate the game is in its final development stages, suggesting a public launch may follow soon after the beta.

**Brian Raffel Retires After 36 Years at Raven Software** Brian Raffel, co-founder and longtime executive of Raven Software, has retired after over three decades in the video game industry. Starting with the company’s early RPGs and moving into first-person shooters, Raffel helped establish Raven’s reputation through titles like Heretic, Hexen, and the Jedi Knight series. In recent years, Raven Software focused primarily on the Call of Duty franchise, contributing notably to Warzone and several Black Ops campaigns. Raffel’s leadership spanned Raven’s acquisition by Activision and the studio’s evolution within the FPS space. Industry figures, including Doom co-creator John Romero, have acknowledged Raffel’s role in sustaining the studio's success. Raven continues to work on Call of Duty projects with shared leadership following Raffel’s departure.

**Bungie Details Upcoming Recon Shell Improvements in Marathon** Bungie has provided an early look at planned changes to the Recon character class in their extraction shooter Marathon, addressing feedback that the Shell is underpowered. The April 14 update will enhance Recon’s Echo Pulse ability by allowing it to differentiate between AI-controlled enemies and human players, improving situational awareness. The pulse will also become less noticeable to opponents, making it harder to detect. Additionally, changes to Recon’s tracker drone will make it more aggressive by allowing it to switch targets if it cannot reach the current one, aiming to reduce frustrating drone behavior. These adjustments seek to balance Recon relative to other characters, with further game changes expected in the near future.

**Take-Two Lays Off Part of AI Team, Including Head of AI** Take-Two Interactive appears to have reduced the size of its artificial intelligence department, including the departure of Luke Dicken, the company’s Head of AI. Dicken joined Take-Two in early 2025 after leading generative AI initiatives at Zynga, and has confirmed leaving along with some members of his team, though specifics were not disclosed. This move contrasts with CEO Strauss Zelnick's recent statements about actively embracing generative AI across the firm’s studios. Take-Two’s public stance on AI has been mixed, with cautious comments in 2025 giving way to more optimistic remarks in 2026. The layoffs come amid broader uncertainties in the AI industry, including other companies scaling back investments in related technology.

**Reflections on Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2019 as a Last High Point** A recent revisit of Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2019 highlights its continued strengths seven years after release. Praised for its visual fidelity, sound design, and grounded gameplay, this installment is seen as a benchmark for quality in the franchise. Its balance of realistic presentation with arcade shooter elements set a standard that subsequent entries, especially those following Warzone’s integration, have struggled to meet.
