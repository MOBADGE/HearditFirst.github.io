Bungie Nerfs Marathon’s Dominant Shotgun Again

Bungie announced a second nerf to the WSTR shotgun in Marathon due to its overwhelming dominance in short-range combat. Previously reduced in range, the weapon still outperforms other options in close encounters. The upcoming update, rolling out Tuesday, will prevent the WSTR from downing players with blue shields or stronger in just two shots, requiring a reload instead. However, it remains effective against those with green shields, preserving its utility in early game areas. The change aims to encourage weapon diversity and counterplay, moving away from the WSTR overshadowing other secondary weapons. Bungie continues to actively adjust Marathon’s meta to balance gameplay as the community experiments with new content and mechanics.

Longstanding Elder Scrolls Mystery About Redguard Uncovered

Nearly 28 years after the release of The Elder Scrolls Adventures: Redguard (1998), the mystery of what lies beneath female NPCs’ skirts using the game’s free camera has finally been solved. The Redguard Unity project, which is porting the game to the Unity engine for increased stability and mod support, revealed a humorous Easter egg: a smiley face. Rather than hidden character models, Bethesda developers had included this as a cheeky response to potential upskirting attempts by players. The discovery confirms the game’s quirky and playful side, while the ongoing Unity port is expected to improve performance, add modern controls, and allow for easier modding of this classic but often crash-prone title.

Owlcat Shows New Warhammer 40K Companions for Rogue Trader and Dark Heresy

Owlcat Games revealed two new companions linked to their Warhammer 40K RPGs. For Rogue Trader, the Infinite Museion DLC introduces Eogann Februs, a tech-priest character. Although another tech-priest already exists in the main game, this addition expands the companion roster before the DLC's release. Separately, the playable alpha for the upcoming Dark Heresy RPG features Haymar Devos, a guardsman from the Catachan jungle world known for its deadly environment and over-the-top action-hero archetypes. The Dark Heresy alpha has received positive feedback, with the game still in active development ahead of a "coming soon" launch. These additions highlight Owlcat’s ongoing efforts to enrich character diversity in their licensed RPG universe.

Philips Brings 5K Dual-Mode Monitor to Western Markets

Philips announced that its Evnia 27M2D5901A, a 27-inch 5K dual-mode gaming monitor, will launch in Europe in July for €799. The monitor can switch between 5120x2880 resolution at 165 Hz and 2560x1440 resolution at 330 Hz. This offers users flexibility between high-fidelity visuals for immersive games and ultra-high refresh rates for competitive play. The white-colored panel also features Philips Ambilight, which synchronizes LED lighting with gameplay, alongside USB-C power delivery and Matter compatibility for smart home integration. While 5K gaming remains niche and demanding on hardware, this monitor offers a novel combination of resolution and refresh rate in a single package, though there is currently no announcement of a US release.

Monitor Manufacturer AOC Addresses OSD Complaints and Encourages Upgrading

At a press event, AOC acknowledged that its on-screen display (OSD) software received significant criticism and announced an all-new interface for its current and upcoming monitors. The updated OSD offers two layers of control—a simple quick-adjust panel for common settings and a deeper menu for detailed customization. The redesign aims to improve usability, especially important as AOC expands its lineup of dual-mode monitors capable of switching resolutions and refresh rates. The company also suggested upgrading to OLED panels could be a more impactful improvement than investing in expensive GPUs or memory at current market prices. While this perspective reflects AOC’s business interests, it underscores the continuing evolution of display technology in gaming setups.
