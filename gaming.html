Valve Attributes Steam Deck OLED Scarcity to Ongoing Memory Crisis

Valve has confirmed that shortages of the Steam Deck OLED model in some regions, including the United States, are due to industry-wide memory and storage shortages. These component shortages have affected multiple sectors of consumer electronics, contributing to product scarcity and price increases.

The situation is linked to surging demand from AI-related industries, which has strained supplies of DRAM and NAND flash memory. Valve also indicated that these shortages are impacting its upcoming hardware releases, such as the Steam Machine and Steam Frame, delaying pricing and release announcements.

This acknowledgment aligns Valve with other major companies like Sony and Nintendo, who are also facing challenges related to memory supply and considering release delays or price adjustments. The ongoing crisis underlines how broader semiconductor supply issues continue to impact gaming hardware availability.
