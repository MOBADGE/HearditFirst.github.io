Future Games Show Spring Showcase Returns This Thursday

The Future Games Show Spring Showcase is set for March 12, airing from 1 pm PT / 4 pm ET / 8 pm GMT, coinciding with the Game Developers’ Conference in San Francisco. Hosted by Devora Wilde and Shai Matheson, the approximately 90-minute event will feature over 40 games, blending big-budget titles and indie projects. Highlights include survival mystery "Hello Sunshine," sci-fi horror "Directive 8020" by Supermassive, and the unique ghost-fighting renovation game "There Are No Ghosts At The Grand." Viewers can watch the show across multiple streaming platforms, including Twitch, YouTube, Steam, and others, with over 250 co-streamers participating worldwide. This broad availability and diverse lineup make it a convenient way for audiences to discover upcoming games and industry trends.

Ballistic Moon Studio Officially Closes

Ballistic Moon, the studio responsible for the 2024 remake of "Until Dawn," has formally dissolved as of February 2, 2026. The closure follows significant staff layoffs last year, with most employees departing before the remake’s release in October 2024. Although the remake received positive reviews for its horror atmosphere, Ballistic Moon struggled to sustain operations, reportedly seeking funding before ultimately shutting down. The official dissolution comes after a largely quiet wind-down period. This development marks the end of Ballistic Moon’s brief involvement in the franchise, while Supermassive, the original series developer, continues work on related projects despite its own recent layoffs.

Resident Evil 2 VR Mod Demonstrates Immersive Potential — But at a Cost

A fan-made virtual reality mod for the "Resident Evil 2 Remake" was recently demonstrated using the Virtuix Omni One, an omni-directional VR treadmill setup. This full-body VR experience includes peripherals like a chest harness and haptic recoil accessories, significantly enhancing immersion and atmospheric tension. However, the hardware required to replicate this setup remains costly, with the Omni One treadmill priced above $3,000 and additional accessories costing hundreds more. Physical space requirements and potential motion sickness present further barriers to wide adoption. While community mods like this push VR’s boundaries in gaming, the expense and logistics currently limit accessibility for most players.

Anthropic Sues US Government Over Blacklisting Amid AI Safeguards Dispute

Anthropic, an AI developer, has filed a lawsuit against the US government following its blacklisting tied to a refusal to remove safeguards preventing AI use in autonomous weapons and mass surveillance. The company alleges retaliation after government pressure led to cancellation of its contract, with threats to designate Anthropic a national security risk. Supporting Anthropic's case, both OpenAI and Google have signed an amicus brief warning that the government's actions could harm US competitiveness in AI technology. The lawsuit challenges the government’s invocation of national security and seeks to protect established ethical limits on AI applications. A hearing may occur shortly, with potential implications for AI governance and industry-government relations.

Marathon’s Protect/Destroy 4 Quest Guide

Players tackling the Protect/Destroy 4 quest in "Marathon" will find it challenging due to complex objectives spread across Outpost map locations and a strict 20-minute time limit. The quest requires interacting with three key objectives: downloading the shipping manifest terminal in the Processing building, scanning four tagged containers in the Airfield area, and hacking a terminal inside Flight Control’s data archive. Awareness of enemy activity and environmental hazards like the Heat Cascade is critical, and players must successfully extract alive to complete the mission. This guide helps clarify the locations and order of objectives, providing useful navigation tips to improve completion chances.

Resident Evil Requiem Prepares Additional Story Content and Features

Following a strong launch and steady player engagement, the director of "Resident Evil Requiem," Koshi Nakanishi, confirmed new story content is in development. While the studio recently released a patch addressing bugs and performance, upcoming additions include a photo mode, a minigame planned for May, and longer-term plans for enriching the game’s narrative. Specific details on the extra story content remain undisclosed, but the development team aims to deepen the game’s world and continue supporting the title post-launch. The director also expressed gratitude to the player community and pledged ongoing updates.
