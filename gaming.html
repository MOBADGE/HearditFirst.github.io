**WCW’s Expensive Attempt to Imitate Mortal Kombat Ends Quickly**

In the 1990s, World Championship Wrestling (WCW) invested heavily in an ice-themed wrestling character named Glacier, clearly inspired by Mortal Kombat’s Sub-Zero. WCW spent around $400,000 on Glacier’s elaborate entrance effects and $35,000 on the costume design, aiming to capitalize on Mortal Kombat’s popularity. However, Midway, Mortal Kombat’s rights holder, threatened legal action, and WCW ceased using the gimmick shortly after its debut. The character’s expensive production and derivative nature failed to gain traction, especially amid the emergence of the more authentic New World Order storyline. Glacier’s situation highlights the costly and flawed creative decisions WCW made during its peak years, fueled by financial backing from billionaire Ted Turner. The incident also underscores the risks of intellectual property infringement in entertainment.

**Stardew Valley Prepares Clint as New Marriage Candidate**

Stardew Valley players can start building relationships with Clint, the town’s blacksmith, ahead of his official introduction as a marriage candidate in the upcoming 1.7 update. Clint’s daily routine involves working at the Smithy and visiting the Saloon in the evenings, with predictable timing throughout the seasons. He favors gifting precious gems and smelted bars, while disliking unrefined ores and flowers. Players can improve their friendship by interacting with Clint and giving him preferred gifts, unlocking new recipes and heart events. Clint’s addition to Stardew Valley’s marriage pool expands the relationship options and adds more depth to Pelican Town’s social content.

**Roblox Introduces ‘Roblox Plus’ Subscription and Creator Incentives**

Roblox has revealed Roblox Plus, a new subscription set to replace Roblox Premium on April 30. Roblox Plus offers subscribers escalating discounts on Robux purchases (starting at 10%, increasing to 20% after three months), free access to private servers (including those that usually charge fees), and no transaction fees for Robux transfers between players aged 18 and over. Unlike Premium, Roblox Plus excludes a monthly Robux stipend and bonus on Robux reloads, which could increase costs for some users. To compensate creators, Roblox will pay up to 100 Robux per subscriber who spends sufficient time in paid private servers and bonuses for creators who successfully encourage players to subscribe. The initiative signifies an effort by Roblox to grow its subscription base while engaging creators financially, though the changes carry potential downsides for some users.

**US Government Targets Gamers to Address Air Traffic Controller Shortage**

The US Department of Transportation has launched a recruitment campaign aimed at gamers for air traffic controller positions, using gaming imagery and messaging to attract young adults. The campaign highlights the career’s challenges and salary potential, noting an average pay of $155,000 after three years. This initiative responds to a significant shortage in certified controllers, worsened by recent government shutdowns that led to unpaid staff and increased early retirements. While the job demands high responsibility and carries notable stress, the government’s approach reflects a novel method of workforce outreach by leveraging the skills and interests of gamers. The campaign underscores ongoing staffing issues within the Federal Aviation Administration.

**Pete Hines Reflects on Leaving Bethesda Amid Company Changes**

Pete Hines, Bethesda Softworks’ longtime marketing and PR figure, spoke about his 2023 departure after nearly 25 years with the company. Hines described leaving reluctantly because he felt powerless to prevent mismanagement and damage at Bethesda following its acquisition by Microsoft. He expressed admiration for Bethesda’s development complexity and open-world ambition but lamented the studio’s decline in efficiency and treatment under new ownership. Despite frustrations, Hines praised Todd Howard and the Bethesda team for their creative output and enduring respect within the industry. His comments provide insight into the internal challenges Bethesda faced during its transition period.
