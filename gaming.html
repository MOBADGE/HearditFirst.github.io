Pragmata’s Path Tracing vs Ray Tracing Debate

Capcom’s sci-fi game *Pragmata* has sparked discussion over its graphics options, specifically the difference between ray tracing and path tracing. Some players suspect that the game’s path tracing option is deliberately made to look significantly better than standard ray tracing, possibly to push GPU sales or due to “lazy” development. Independent tests show that path tracing does provide cleaner shadows, more defined lighting, and better reflections, which can improve visual fidelity, especially in shadow-heavy scenes. However, path tracing is notably more demanding on graphics cards, dropping frame rates considerably compared to ray tracing. The consensus is that ray tracing still offers good visuals at a much smoother performance, making path tracing more suited for high-end setups. This debate highlights the ongoing challenges in balancing cutting-edge graphics with practical performance in modern games.

Kiki Wolfkill Departs Microsoft After Nearly 30 Years

Kiki Wolfkill, a long-serving Microsoft veteran known for her extensive work across franchises like *Halo*, *Fable*, and *Mass Effect*, has left the company as of April 17, 2026. Wolfkill notably led Xbox’s Film and TV division, overseeing productions such as the *Halo* TV series. In a LinkedIn post, she described her departure as both difficult and exciting, signaling plans for new ventures. Her exit marks a significant shift at Xbox, as she was a key figure in extending game properties into other media. Wolfkill’s departure may lead to changes in how Xbox approaches transmedia projects in the future.

Fallout: New Vegas Developer on Bethesda’s Criticism

Chris Avellone, writer for *Fallout: New Vegas*, recently revealed in an interview that Bethesda once made Obsidian Entertainment attend a presentation detailing all the ways it allegedly underperformed on the game and its DLCs. Avellone expressed that this experience was demoralizing, especially given the game’s critical acclaim and lasting fan appreciation. He noted that Bethesda's dissatisfaction was tied to sales and review scores, which influenced their decision to not continue working with Obsidian on Fallout-related projects. Avellone also discussed the divisive nature of the *Dead Money* DLC, explaining its survival horror approach was a departure that didn’t sit well with part of the fanbase. This insight sheds light on the sometimes tense relationship between developers and publishers, and the challenges of creative departures within established franchises.

Preview of The Expanse: Osiris Reborn Shows Progress in Cinematic Action

Owlcat Games’ upcoming RPG *The Expanse: Osiris Reborn* presents a shift from the studio’s usual isometric style to third-person cinematic action inspired by series like *Mass Effect*. A hands-on preview focusing on an early-game mission demonstrated competent third-person shooting mechanics with cover-based combat and gadgets that add tactical variety. The mission features navigating a besieged space station, including a zero-gravity section that offers novel visual and gameplay elements. While the RPG narrative and character development aspects remain to be seen, the combat phase shows promise in delivering engaging action. The game is scheduled for full release in spring 2027 and aims to blend action and roleplaying elements in a sci-fi setting.

Peter Molyneux Voices Caution About AI Use in Game Development

Peter Molyneux, known for ambitious promises in his game career, recently expressed a cautious stance on the use of AI in game development during a BBC interview. He stated that AI technology is currently not of sufficient quality for extensive use in games and emphasized the need for careful safeguards to prevent misuse. Despite acknowledging potential disruption caused by AI, Molyneux highlighted human adaptability to societal changes. His comments contrast with some industry trends of widespread AI experimentation, reflecting reservations about the current state and ethical concerns surrounding AI in creative fields. This perspective adds to ongoing discussions about the role of AI in game design and production.
