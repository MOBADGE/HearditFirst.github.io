Shadowstone Opens Surprise Public Playtest

Shadowstone, a new tactics roguelike from Secret Door and Dreamhaven, has launched a surprise public playtest announced during the Future Games Show Spring Showcase. The game is a spin-off set in the Sunderfolk universe, shifting from smartphone controls to a more traditional mouse-and-keyboard setup. Players can test the game solo or in multiplayer groups of up to four, experiencing randomized ruin runs that require adaptive strategy through skill cards, equipment, and powerups. Progress won’t carry over to the eventual early access release, which is planned for sometime in 2026, though no precise date has been given. Interested players can sign up on Steam for a chance to join the playtest.

This early access to gameplay aims to gather player feedback and build community interest. It’s notable that the developers are allowing open discussion and streaming without NDAs, which can help visibility ahead of release.

Industry Veteran Rob Pardo Urges Companies to Preserve Development Teams

At the Game Developers Conference keynote, former Blizzard chief creative officer Rob Pardo delivered a message to industry executives about the value of retaining game development teams. Drawing from his experience working on titles like Starcraft and Warcraft 3, Pardo emphasized that the unpredictability and collaborative problem-solving involved in making successful games mean the team behind the game is more important than the game itself.

His comments come amid an ongoing trend of layoffs in the industry, including on successful projects such as Battlefield 6, where teams have been cut shortly after a major launch. Pardo argued that keeping experienced developers on board is essential for sustaining a game's success and that replacing them risks losing hard-earned expertise.

Path of Exile Launches Mirage League with Economy and Map Overhaul

Grinding Gear Games’ Path of Exile introduced its new Mirage league in update 3.28, which includes significant changes to drop rates and the endgame map system. Currency items now drop more frequently from enemies, potentially increasing player ability to craft and trade. A new mirage mechanic lets players revisit portions of maps with special rewards and allows doubling up on league mechanics like Ritual or Harvest, improving farming efficiency.

The update also replaces specific map drops with tiered tokens, improving player control over their atlas progression. Modifiers called astrolabes enhance groups of maps, adding strategic variety. This combination of economy enhancements, new mechanics, and reworked map progression alters the gameplay loop for both new and veteran players.

Tony Hoare, Inventor of Quicksort Algorithm, Passes at 92

Tony Hoare, a pioneering computer scientist famed for creating the Quicksort sorting algorithm and contributing foundational work to programming language theory and concurrency, has died aged 92. Invented in 1959 after a small wager, Quicksort remains one of the fastest sorting algorithms used in computer science. Hoare was also co-designer of ALGOL W and developed Hoare logic, a formal system for proving program correctness.

Throughout his career, Hoare advocated for simplicity in software design. He received the Turing Award in 1980 and was knighted in 2000 for his contributions to education and computer science. His work has influenced both academic research and practical programming widely.

Marathon’s Recent Patch Changes Match Dynamics

Marathon players have noticed a significant rise in the frequency and intensity of combat following a recent patch that increased the hearing range of gunfire and explosions. This change has led to more frequent third-party engagements in matches, as noise now draws players from greater distances, especially on the game’s relatively small maps.

Community feedback on the change is mixed but generally concerned that the increased sound range reduces opportunities for stealth or isolated play, making solo matches particularly challenging. The developers have not elaborated on the reasoning behind this adjustment, but speculation suggests it aims to increase player encounters. Some players hope for a reversal if the change proves detrimental to gameplay balance.
