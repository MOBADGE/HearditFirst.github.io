Valve Veteran Criticizes Epic Games’ Layoffs and Leadership

Former Valve writer Chet Faliszek publicly criticized Epic Games and CEO Tim Sweeney following the company's recent layoffs affecting over 1,000 employees. Faliszek questioned the motivation for employees to work hard when the company lays off staff despite reportedly making significant revenue. He contrasted Epic with Valve, which he says fostered a strong sense of ownership and pride among employees, partly due to its private company structure. Faliszek suggested that Epic’s focus has shifted away from creating multiple games to concentrating on Fortnite, and criticized decisions like raising V-buck prices amid layoffs. These comments highlight concerns about employee morale and company culture in the industry following waves of downsizing.

Neuralink User Demonstrates Hands-Free World of Warcraft Gameplay

Jon Noble, a British Army veteran and participant in Neuralink’s brain implant trial, shared progress 100 days after receiving the device installed in his motor cortex. Noble demonstrated playing World of Warcraft purely through thought control, without a mouse or keyboard. Initially using a Mac interface to control cursor movement, he quickly adapted to the technology and began raiding in WoW with mental commands alone. This achievement marks a significant step in brain-computer interfaces for people with severe paralysis, showing enhanced digital interaction capabilities. Although still in early trials with limited participants, Neuralink’s progress suggests potential for greater independence through mind-controlled computing devices beyond gaming.

Star Wars Zero Company: A Clone Wars Tactics Title with RPG Elements

Bit Reactor’s Star Wars Zero Company, expected to release sometime in 2026, offers a turn-based tactics experience set in the Clone Wars era. The game blends well-known XCOM-style combat with RPG features including third-person exploration of the home base and an overarching narrative. Players lead a mercenary squad comprising both authored story characters and custom operatives, each with unique abilities and faction ties. The game emphasizes strategic combat, permadeath, and rich squad interactions, while offering varied missions on a galaxy map. Published by EA and developed by a team with many former XCOM veterans, Zero Company aims to merge tactical gameplay with immersive storytelling in the Star Wars universe.

Stranger Than Heaven Explores Yakuza’s World Through Multiple Time Periods

Ryu Ga Gotoku’s new project, Stranger Than Heaven, was showcased during the Xbox Partner Preview and is confirmed to take place across five distinct eras between 1915 and 1965. The game revisits Kamurocho, the series’ iconic setting, decades before previous Yakuza titles, alongside other cities like Osaka. Unlike past entries that often leaned into quirky or over-the-top narratives, Stranger Than Heaven appears to adopt a more grounded tone without returning characters. The gameplay will retain the series’ signature close-quarters combat. The time-spanning setting might provide fresh environments and stories, with a fuller presentation planned for May 6.

Stunlock Studios Ends V Rising Updates, Announces New Vampire Project

Stunlock Studios announced that V Rising will no longer receive content updates as the game’s story arc around Dracula is considered complete. The studio is now focusing on a new game set in the same vampire universe, described as “the odyssey of the Vampire continues.” Although still early in development, the new project promises a deeper and more dangerous world while building on knowledge gained from V Rising’s development. Stunlock confirmed there will be no official modding support for V Rising due to technical limitations but suggested that future projects may include such features. Ongoing balance patches will continue for the existing game.
