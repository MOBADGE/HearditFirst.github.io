Crimson Desert Launch Faces Pre-Order Item Bug

Crimson Desert launched on March 20, 2026, with a peak of over 239,000 concurrent Steam players, marking a solid start for the game. However, some players who pre-ordered or purchased the deluxe edition are encountering a bug that prevents access to their special in-game items. These bonuses, which include shields, horse armor, and a full Kairos armor set for the protagonist, currently do not appear in the game's supply box as expected.

Developer Pearl Abyss has acknowledged the issue and is investigating a solution. They apologized for the inconvenience and promised an update soon. While the impact of the missing items on gameplay remains unclear—whether purely cosmetic or offering in-game advantages—the inability of players to access these rewards is a notable glitch at a major launch. Overall player reception is mixed, with some reviews noting the game's large, complex open world and busywork-heavy gameplay.

Fallout: New Vegas Available for 99 Cents in Steam Spring Sale

Steam’s ongoing Spring Sale includes a significant discount on Fallout: New Vegas, one of the most praised entries in the Fallout series, currently priced at just 99 cents. Originally developed by Obsidian, New Vegas is often regarded as superior in narrative complexity and player choice to Bethesda’s Fallout 3 and Fallout 4.

The game’s surge in popularity is also linked to recent renewed interest spurred by the second season of the Fallout TV show. The sale includes the Ultimate Edition for around five dollars, which bundles all DLC content. For new players and fans returning to the title, PC Gamer recommends mods to modernize graphics and gameplay, though the base game remains enjoyable "out of the box."

Insights from 1989: Robert Garriott on Market Saturation in PC Gaming

A newly surfaced recording from the 1989 Computer Game Developers Conference reveals Robert Garriott, co-founder of Origin Systems and brother of Ultima creator Richard Garriott, addressing challenges faced by the PC gaming industry over three decades ago. He highlighted concerns about an oversaturated market, with new game releases increasing faster than overall sales, leading to reduced sales per title.

Garriott also discussed competition from Nintendo consoles impacting PC game sales, but he emphasized that deeper role-playing and strategy games should have been shielded from that effect. His call for increased quality and unique PC-focused products reflects ongoing challenges in the gaming industry regarding discoverability and product differentiation. The concerns he voiced in 1989 still resonate today as digital storefronts feature thousands of new releases monthly.

Hoarder: A New Cleaning Simulator from a Darkwood Co-Creator

One of the creators of the acclaimed survival horror game Darkwood is developing a new title called Hoarder, which blends cleaning simulation with horror elements. The game tasks players with decluttering an old hoarder’s house, slowly uncovering dark mysteries as the gameplay shifts away from straightforward tasks toward a disturbing exploration of a strange basement.

Hoards features atmospheric tension and minimal guidance, with developers promising that no one will come to aid the player once deeper challenges emerge. While not yet dated for release, Hoarder has drawn interest for its combination of mundane mechanics and unsettling themes, continuing a trend of experimental indie titles mixing horror and simulation.

Seven Deadly Sins: Origin Shares New Redeemable Codes for Rewards

Netmarble's anime-inspired gacha RPG Seven Deadly Sins: Origin currently offers active redeem codes granting in-game benefits such as hero draw tickets, gold, and enhancement stones. These codes are accessible either through the in-game menu or Netmarble’s official website once players complete initial tutorial quests.

Players are advised to redeem codes promptly, especially those with unclear expiration dates, to avoid missing out on free rewards that can assist in game progression. The game blends the popular anime’s characters and story themes with gacha mechanics, continuing to engage fans with regular content updates and promotional codes.
