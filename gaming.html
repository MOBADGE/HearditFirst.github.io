Hazelight Studios Celebrates Over 50 Million Game Sales in Under a Decade Hazelight Studios, recognized for its cooperative action-adventure games, announced that its three titles combined—*A Way Out*, *It Takes Two*, and *Split Fiction*—have surpassed 50 million copies sold worldwide. *It Takes Two* received an 80% review score in 2021, while *Split Fiction* raised acclaim with an 87% score in 2025. Interestingly, *A Way Out*, despite mixed reception, remains their best-selling game over time, with 13 million copies sold across eight years, compared to 7 million copies of *Split Fiction* in just one year. The milestone underscores the studio’s steady growth and success in the cooperative game genre. Hazelight is currently developing a new title, continuing its focus on innovative cooperative gameplay.

ChainStaff Explores Fresh Directions in Retro-Inspired Shooters Indie developer Mommy’s Best Games released *ChainStaff*, a psychedelic alien shooter that draws inspiration from lesser-known corners of classic gaming history rather than the usual retro influences like *Zelda* or *Mega Man*. The game’s surreal visuals and imaginative designs evoke the style of 1990s Amiga titles such as *Shadow of the Beast*. Its gameplay combines old-school run-and-gun elements with modern twists, including a versatile grappling weapon. This release highlights how developers can revisit retro aesthetics while offering novel experiences, helping to diversify the currently crowded field of retro-styled indie games.

Excel's 1900 Leap Year Bug Remains Due to Legacy Compatibility Microsoft confirmed that the longstanding issue where Excel incorrectly treats 1900 as a leap year will not be corrected to maintain compatibility with Lotus 1-2-3, Excel's influential predecessor. Fixing this flaw would disrupt date calculations and break compatibility across numerous existing spreadsheets and software that rely on the same serial date system. As a result, Excel continues to present incorrect weekday values for dates before March 1, 1900, though this affects very few users. This decision illustrates how deep-rooted legacy decisions persist in modern software to avoid widespread disruption.

GTA 6’s User-Generated Content Expected to Create New Opportunities YouTuber HipHopGamer, citing industry sources, suggests that *Grand Theft Auto 6* will feature a significant user-generated content (UGC) platform, potentially allowing players to earn substantial income. This aligns with Rockstar and Take-Two's recent integration of modding platforms like FiveM into an official marketplace. The expectation is that *GTA 6* will extend beyond a traditional game to become an ecosystem supporting player-created content, especially on PC. While concrete details remain unconfirmed, this approach could influence how open-world games engage communities and monetize user creations.

Songs of Glimmerwick Offers a New Ethically-Minded Wizard School Life Sim The life simulation game *Songs of Glimmerwick* has released a playable demo ahead of its planned 2026 launch. Developed over seven years by Eastshade Studios using proceeds from their prior game, it combines gardening, music-based magic, and social storytelling in a setting emphasizing egalitarianism and resistance to past oppressive regimes. The game features a charming pseudo-2D art style, voice acting, and a narrative that addresses themes of education access and political backlash. It stands out for its conscious approach to the wizard school trope, offering an alternative to recent entries in the genre burdened with problematic associations.

Overwatch’s New Hero Sierra May Stir Debate With Assisted Aiming Ability Blizzard revealed a new *Overwatch* hero, Sierra, whose trailer demonstrates an ability that marks enemies with a dart, causing her shots to curve and seek targets. This auto-aim mechanic is unusual in the game’s competitive shooter context and may influence how players perceive fairness and balance. Sierra also has mobility options and an ultimate that involves carpet bombing. Players may debate whether the targeting assistance is acceptable, as similar abilities in other shooters often generate controversy. Sierra will be available starting April 14, giving the community time to assess her impact on gameplay.
