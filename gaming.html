**AA Batteries Fail to Power Gaming PCs Practically**

Tech creator ScuffedBits has once again demonstrated that AA batteries are not a realistic power source for gaming PCs. Using a pack of 64 AA batteries—including carbon, alkaline, and rechargeable types—ScuffedBits managed to power a gaming PC for about 33 minutes. During this time, they played several games and completed CPU stress tests, marking a significant improvement over their first attempt, which lasted only long enough to finish Minesweeper. Despite some technical tweaks like added capacitors to stabilize voltage, the experiment remains impractical for daily use. This highlights the considerable power demands of modern gaming hardware, especially as AI systems increasingly strain energy resources and data centres struggle with electricity supply. For now, AA batteries remain a novelty rather than a solution.

**YouTuber’s 15 Mini-Fan PC Cooling Setup Matches Standard Fan Performance**

YouTuber Major Hardware replaced a conventional Noctua 110mm fan in a PC with 15 miniature 30mm fans in an experiment dubbed “The Fanhattan Project.” Although the custom fan assembly produced surprisingly strong airflow, it also generated significant noise—about 73 decibels, comparable to a dishwasher. Performance-wise, cooling tests using an Intel Core i7 7700K processor showed only a marginal difference: the custom fan kept CPU temperatures around 69 °C, virtually the same as the standard fan. The project reveals that while novel cooling approaches can match conventional solutions, they may come with trade-offs like increased noise and complexity. It serves as an interesting DIY case study rather than a practical upgrade.

**Final Fantasy 14 Solo Challenge Runner Faces New Limits, Reveals Backup Plan**

RathGames, known for the challenging “Solo Only” Final Fantasy 14 run—completing content intended for groups entirely alone—has encountered a significant obstacle in the Shadowbringers expansion. The penultimate boss, Hades, requires multi-player cooperation for certain mechanics that pose a damage-dealing challenge solo players cannot easily overcome. After over 1,000 hours of effort, RathGames revealed he had secretly prepared a backup strategy involving multiboxing: controlling two characters at once, both adhering to the solo-only rules. This secret plan took 233 days to prepare and involved extensive planning to coordinate moves across characters. The feat underlines the depth and complexity of community-driven challenge runs, as well as the creative lengths players will go to push game boundaries within self-imposed constraints.

**Bungie Developer Clarifies Many Original Halo Creators Still Work There**

In response to claims that Bungie no longer represents the original creators behind the Halo franchise, Elliott Gray—a UI designer who worked on Bungie's Marathon series—listed around 40 developers from the Halo era still employed at Bungie. This counters narratives suggesting most original Halo talent has left the company. Additionally, former Destiny executive Mark Noseworthy emphasized that Bungie’s culture and technology legacy continue through knowledge passed across staff changes. This discourse affirms Bungie’s claim as the studio behind Halo, reflecting ongoing continuity despite personnel changes over the years.

**Intel Discloses Multiple UEFI Security Vulnerabilities Requiring Local Access**

Intel has published information about several security vulnerabilities affecting the UEFI firmware on many of its products. The most severe issues allow local attackers to execute code with elevated privileges after improper input validation. While the vulnerabilities rate as high severity (CVSS up to 8.7), they all require local system access, which limits the attack surface. Intel and motherboard manufacturers are rolling out mitigation updates, though many users may not see fixes immediately. Users are advised to update firmware when available but need not be overly concerned if their system is secure from local breaches, as remote exploitation is not possible with these flaws.

**Windows 11 Samsung PCs Experience Major Boot Drive Access Issue**

Some users of Samsung laptops and desktops running Windows 11 (versions 24H2 and 25H2) have been locked out of their system drives, encountering “C:\ is not accessible – Access denied” errors. The problem appears linked to the Samsung Galaxy Connect application, affecting permissions and sometimes blocking users from elevating privileges, uninstalling updates, or logging system actions.
