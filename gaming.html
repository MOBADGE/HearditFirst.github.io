**PC Gamer Hardware Awards 2025: Top Picks and Insights**

The PC Gamer Hardware Awards for 2025 highlight the most notable PC gaming hardware reviewed this year. Despite the high cost of many components amid ongoing "RAMpocalypse" pricing challenges, several products stood out for performance, design, and value.

- **Best Gaming Laptop:** Razer Blade 16 took the top spot with its slim design, efficient cooling, and the ability to deliver strong gaming and work performance both plugged in and on battery.

- **Best Gaming Monitor:** The KTC H27T22C-3 offered an impressive 1440p experience at a budget price, featuring a 210 Hz refresh rate and good color accuracy, though it lacks USB ports and has limited ergonomic adjustments.

- **Best Handheld Gaming PC:** Lenovo Legion Go S SteamOS impressed with solid performance, comfortable design, and a more console-like experience through SteamOS, despite some limitations with game compatibility and trackpad functionality.

- **Best SSD:** The WD Black SN8100 2 TB NVMe SSD stood out as the fastest PCIe 5.0 drive on the market, with high sustained read/write speeds and good thermal management.

- **Best Gaming CPU:** AMD Ryzen 9 9950X3D delivered top-tier gaming and content creation performance at a premium price, representing the latest in AMD's 3D V-cache technology.

- **Best Motherboard:** Gigabyte‚Äôs X870 Aorus Elite Wi-Fi Ice combined aesthetics, functionality with multiple PCIe 5.0 and USB ports, and strong value for the AM5 platform.

- **Best Gaming Keyboard:** Be Quiet! Light Mount was praised for being the quietest keyboard tested by PC Gamer, with silent tactile switches, effective dampening, vibrant RGB, and useful extra features like a knob and macro keys.

- **Best Gaming Mouse:** Corsair Sabre V2 Pro Ultralight impressed as an ultra-light 36g mouse aimed at competitive FPS players, focusing strictly on performance without extra features.

- **Best Gaming Headset:** Razer BlackShark V3 provided strong wireless audio performance, comfort, and a low-latency mode suited for competitive gaming.

- **Best Gaming Microphone:** Amazon Basics USB condenser mic surprised with sound quality exceeding expectations for its sub-$30 price, offering a simple and durable option for entry-level streamers and content creators.

- **Best Graphics Card:** AMD Radeon RX 9070 XT provided a competitive alternative to Nvidia‚Äôs offerings with better price-to-performance, solid ray tracing and AI features, and improved upscaling though initial launch prices were inflated.

- **Wildcard Award:** The Razer Wolverine V3 Pro 8K controller was honored for its high polling rate (8000 Hz), Hall effect thumbsticks and triggers, and robust build, though its price is notably high.

**Additional Noteworthy Hardware**

Among other highly rated gear, the Arctic Freezer 36 CPU cooler impressed as an affordable, quiet, and effective air cooler under $50, highlighting some value in budget-priced hardware this year. Conversely, some hardware disappointed: among the lowest review scores were the Logitech G PowerPlay 2 mouse pad and the OneXPlayer X1 Air handheld PC, both criticized for their high price despite weak functionality.

A PC Gamer hardware editor shared a personal endorsement of the 8BitDo Ultimate 2C wireless controller, purchased independently despite access to more premium review units. At around $30, it offers remappable buttons, Hall effect triggers to reduce stick drift, and a compact design reminiscent of the Nintendo Switch Pro controller, fitting smaller hands well.

While budget plastic construction is evident, performance and comfort for casual gaming were excellent. The controller‚Äôs distinct purple color also matched the editor's existing hardware, illustrating how aesthetics remain an important factor alongside technical features for many users.
