Linux Gaming Progress and Remaining Challenges

2025 has been noted as a good year for Linux gaming, primarily due to improvements in SteamOS and Proton, the compatibility layer translating Windows games for Linux. Valve's SteamOS opening to third-party handhelds like the Lenovo Legion Go S helped boost Linux's viability for gaming, with notable advantages in performance and system control.

However, Linux still faces important hurdles preventing many users from switching away from Windows. Key among these is the widespread use of kernel-level anti-cheat systems in many popular multiplayer games, which are not supported on Linux. These anti-cheat programs operate at deep system levels incompatible with the open and modifiable Linux kernel architecture. This makes some modern multiplayer titles unplayable on Linux, limiting adoption among competitive gamers. Additionally, driver support and system stability issues persist on many devices, as seen in some users‚Äô experiences with SteamOS on non-Steam Deck hardware. This landscape suggests Linux gaming will likely grow but not fully replace Windows gaming in the near term.
