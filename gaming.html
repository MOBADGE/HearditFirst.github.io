The Gaming Historian Ends YouTube Career, Shares Rare Nintendo Court Documents

Norman Caruso, known as The Gaming Historian on YouTube, announced he is stepping away from creating documentary videos after over 15 years and more than 1 million subscribers. He attributed the decision to burnout following his last major project on *The Oregon Trail*, saying the motivation to maintain video quality had faded. As a final gift to fans and researchers, Caruso has uploaded a large collection of scanned court documents related to a 1982 legal case where Universal Studios sued Nintendo over *Donkey Kong*. These files, now available on the Internet Archive, reveal interesting development trivia, such as alternate names considered for *Donkey Kong*. Caruso will continue podcast work with his wife, marking the end of an influential era in game history documentation.

Samson Developer Sees Room for Smaller Open-World Crime Games

The studio behind *Samson*, Liquid Swords, comments on comparisons between their upcoming open-world brawler and the *Grand Theft Auto* series. Christofer Sundberg, co-founder of Liquid Swords and Avalanche Studios, acknowledges *GTA*’s cultural impact but states their goal is not to replicate Rockstar’s large-scale games. Instead, *Samson* aims to provide a shorter, leaner action experience suitable for players wanting a break from epic titles like *GTA 6*. Despite downsizing and layoffs last year, the game is scheduled to release on April 8. This approach reflects a niche for crime games with more focused scope offering quick play sessions.

House Flipper Temporarily Free on Steam During Home Fest

*House Flipper*, a home renovation simulation game, is available to claim for free on Steam until April 6 as part of the platform’s House and Home Fest sale. Players can clean, remodel, and flip homes in a mechanic-driven, slower-paced gameplay loop that has attracted a dedicated audience. The game’s various DLC expansions, which add new furniture and environments, are also discounted. A remastered edition of *House Flipper* is planned for later this year, with existing owners receiving discounts based on their purchased content. Offering the base game at no cost invites new players to try the niche renovation sim ahead of the updated release.

Echo Point Nova Adds New Movement Modes and New Game+ This Month

Greylock Studio’s indie FPS *Echo Point Nova* will receive a significant update later in April introducing a New Game+ mode and three new movement types. The New Game+ allows players to carry selected unlocks into fresh playthroughs with options to randomize enemy spawns and weapon pickups. The new movement modes include a jetpack, wallrunning sprint, and a rope grapple akin to Spider-Man’s swinging mechanics, expanding on the original hoverboard and zipline traversal. These additions aim to enhance mobility and replayability, maintaining the game’s emphasis on fast-paced, fluid gunplay.

State of Decay 3 Announces Alpha Tests for May

Undead Labs has confirmed that alpha playtests for *State of Decay 3* will begin in May, inviting players to register for a chance at early access. This marks the first major public step for the zombie survival shooter since its announcement in 2020. The alpha will support four-player co-op and feature new base-building and combat mechanics. Co-creator Brant Fitzgerald described it as a fresh take while retaining core scavenging gameplay. With a long development cycle and limited updates since its reveal, these tests represent a key milestone toward the game’s eventual release.

Take-Two Lays Off Head of AI and Part of AI Team Amid Mixed Signals on AI Use

Take-Two Interactive has reportedly laid off its Head of Artificial Intelligence, Luke Dicken, along with some members of its AI division, just months after publicly embracing generative AI technologies. Dicken joined the company in early 2025 to lead AI strategy following his work at Zynga. While CEO Strauss Zelnick previously acknowledged ongoing AI pilots across Take-Two’s studios, this move signals possible reevaluation of AI integration plans. The layoffs reflect wider industry challenges around generative AI, with companies adjusting strategies amid technical and commercial uncertainties. The impact on upcoming projects like *Grand Theft Auto 6* remains unclear.
