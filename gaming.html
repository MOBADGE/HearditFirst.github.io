Slay the Spire 2 Exploring New Game Modes and Ending Improvements

Mega Crit co-founder Casey Yano shared plans for Slay the Spire 2’s development during early access, which could span another one to two years. The team is experimenting with three new game mode concepts intended to offer different interactions not found in the current game. These include a competitive mode for players seeking direct contests, a shorter experience mode for those with limited time, and a multiplayer or social interaction mode leveraging existing game systems. However, Yano cautioned that not all ideas may reach players if they dilute the core experience.

Regarding the game’s ending, currently represented by an incomplete cutscene with a figure called The Architect, Mega Crit intends to develop a more elaborate and serious finale. The final version aims to respond to player feedback and theorycrafting, with potential multiple endings or twists. This update is expected to arrive later in development after more content expansions and alternative acts are implemented.

Samson: Open-World Crime Game’s Unique Development Journey

Samson, an open-world crime game launching on April 8, 2026, took an unusual turn mid-development due to financial and staffing challenges. The developer Liquid Swords had to downscale from a larger action RPG vision, laying off half the team about a year ago. This led to a focus on a roguelike-style daily gameplay loop where players manage a debt each morning through a limited number of action points to perform missions, fights, and getaways.

The resulting game emphasizes close-quarters combat and strategic planning over typical open-world shooting mechanics. Gun combat was removed, making melee fights and environmental tactics central. This structure aligns well with the oppressive setting of the decaying city of Tyndalston and distinguishes Samson from other crime games often compared to Grand Theft Auto. Despite the challenges, the team avoided crunch and prioritized effective work hours, aiming to release a polished core experience while leaving more elaborate features for potential sequels or post-launch.

Piranha Games Lays Off 30% of Staff Amid MechWarrior Uncertainty

MechWarrior developer Piranha Games has confirmed layoffs affecting roughly 30% of its workforce. This follows a previous round of cuts a little over a year ago, linked to disappointing sales of MechWarrior 5: Clans, despite positive reviews. CEO Russ Bullock clarified that planned DLC content will still launch in May 2026, and the studio is working on another MechWarrior project targeting a fall release.

However, narrative director Chris Lowrey expressed uncertainty about the franchise’s longevity given ongoing industry and financial pressures. He noted that supporting a single-purchase title with five years of post-launch content is exceptional but difficult to sustain. The layoffs reflect broader challenges in the gaming industry, including shifting market demands and the difficulty of expanding MechWarrior’s audience.

Bungie Reveals Planned Balance Changes for Marathon

Marathon game director Joe Ziegler previewed upcoming gameplay balance tweaks planned for the weeks ahead. Bungie aims to address “outliers” in combat effectiveness identified through player feedback and internal observation. Key changes will include nerfs to knife damage—currently considered too strong—as well as adjustments reducing the dominance of bubble shields and thermal scopes.

Conversely, some runner shells like Vandal and Recon are set to receive buffs to better compete with other classes. The update also hints at two secret new items designed to provide more balanced options for solo players and crew-based gameplay during downed states. The changes will be rolled out over two patches, with additional improvements on the way after that.

OpenGOAL Team Completes Native PC Ports of Jak & Daxter Trilogy

The OpenGOAL team announced completion of a native PC port of Jak 3, concluding their multi-year project to port the entire Jak & Daxter trilogy to PC. This effort uses the Game Oriented Assembly Lisp language developed by Naughty Dog specifically for the series. The ports require original game discs but deliver improved performance and modding capabilities beyond traditional emulators or original hardware.

The project enhances accessibility to these classic titles, widely regarded for their distinctive aesthetic and gameplay. While the team has not confirmed plans to decompile the spinoff Jak X: Combat Racing, the continuation of this preservation work is notable among dedicated fans and preservationists.
