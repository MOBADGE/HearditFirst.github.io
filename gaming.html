OpenAI Introduces Age Prediction for ChatGPT Safety Features

OpenAI is rolling out a new age verification program for ChatGPT, similar to recently introduced measures by Discord. The system predicts a user's age by analyzing their usage patterns, such as the topics discussed and the times of activity. If the AI cannot confidently verify age based on behavior alone, users can opt to verify through a third-party service, Persona, which confirms adult status without OpenAI retaining personal ID information.

This update aims to enable an upcoming "adult mode" for ChatGPT while maintaining content safety for younger users. Users who do not verify their age will encounter stricter content filters limiting graphic violence, risky viral challenges, and certain role-playing content. The move highlights growing concerns about online privacy and digital age checks, as the technology relies heavily on existing user data to assess age.

Exploring Alternatives as Discord Age Verification Tightens

With Discord set to enforce age verification globally from March, including facial scans or ID uploads for those unverified, some users are seeking alternatives that preserve privacy. A recent feature tested three free alternatives: TeamSpeak, GameVox, and Signal.

- **TeamSpeak** offers large voice servers but requires paid subscriptions for personal servers. - **GameVox** is free and feature-rich but has some instability and strict text filtering. - **Signal** stands out for ease of use, end-to-end encryption, and consistent video performance, though it lacks game streaming and requires a phone number.

This shift reflects growing privacy concerns around age verification methods and user data handling in communication platforms popular among gamers.
