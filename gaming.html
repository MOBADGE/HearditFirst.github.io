## Bungie Clarifies Marathon Battle Pass and Monetization Model

Bungie has confirmed key details about the upcoming shooter Marathon’s post-launch plans ahead of its release. Notably, Marathon’s battle passes will not expire, allowing players to purchase previous seasons at any time. This approach aims to avoid the typical "fear of missing out" (FOMO) issues seen in some live-service games. Additionally, Bungie reiterated that gameplay content such as new maps, “Runners,” and weapons will be free for all players, with paid battle passes offering only cosmetic items. The game’s economy will use just two currencies: Silk, earned by playing to progress through battle passes, and Lux, a premium currency for store purchases. This restrained design contrasts with the more complicated and pay-to-win criticisms experienced by Bungie’s Destiny franchise.

## Final Fantasy 11 Experiences Unusual Growth After 23 Years

The nearly quarter-century-old MMORPG Final Fantasy 11 is undergoing a resurgence in player activity so intense that its busiest server, Odin, has had to halt new character creation and transfers temporarily. Game director Yoji Fujito announced the measures to address server congestion directly on the official forums. With around 87,700 active players overall and approximately 13,000 on Odin alone, Final Fantasy 11’s population remains notably healthy for an MMO of its age. This renewal reflects a broader interest in classic MMORPG experiences as newer games struggle to maintain long-term communities.

## How to Access the Rookery Cache in World of Warcraft: Midnight

World of Warcraft: Midnight features several new collectible treasures, one of which is the Rookery Cache located in Silvermoon City atop the Sunwing Rookery tower. To open the cache, players must purchase Tasty Meat from the nearby NPC vendor Farstrider Aerieminder and place it for a mischievous chick in the area. Feeding the chick drops a key needed to unlock the cache, which rewards players with a Sunwing Hatchling pet and increases Silvermoon Court reputation by 50 points. This treasure is part of the Treasures of the Eversong Woods achievement, encouraging players to explore hidden rewards and collect unique companions.

## Nvidia Releases Hotfix Driver Addressing Voltage and Resident Evil Requiem Issues

Nvidia has issued a hotfix driver (version 595.76) intended to resolve GPU voltage capping problems and improve performance with path tracing in Resident Evil Requiem. Earlier drivers reportedly caused frame rate drops and hardware issues, particularly for users running overclocked GPUs. The hotfix aims to stabilize GPU voltage boosting and eliminate white glowing artifacts seen in the game. Nvidia cautions that hotfix drivers undergo reduced quality assurance and recommends waiting for the next certified driver release if uncertain. Nonetheless, this update may be relevant for players experiencing performance problems with the latest Final Fantasy game.

## Copyright Battles Resolved for Obscure Japanese Game Cookie’s Bustle

The Video Game History Foundation (VGHF) has successfully pushed back against repeated copyright takedown attempts by a company named Graceware, restoring access to materials documenting the 1999 Japanese point-and-click adventure Cookie’s Bustle. Due to Graceware’s aggressive use of DMCA notices, many Let’s Play videos, screenshots, and fan content related to this cult classic had been removed from major platforms for years. VGHF’s legal challenge highlighted questionable copyright claims and ultimately led to the cessation of automated takedowns by Ukie, the organization handling notices on Graceware’s behalf. The preservation of Cookie’s Bustle exemplifies ongoing efforts to maintain access to obscure and orphaned games that might otherwise vanish from public view.

## US Government Considers Forcing Tencent to Divest US Gaming Assets

Reports indicate that the US government is debating whether Chinese conglomerate Tencent should be compelled to sell its holdings in US and Finnish game companies over national security concerns. Tencent owns or holds significant stakes in major Western studios including Riot Games, Epic Games, Larian Studios, and Ubisoft. Officials worry about potential access to personal data of American users under Tencent’s control. This review follows earlier scrutiny beginning in 2020 and is set against a broader context of geopolitical tensions and technology sector regulation. A forced divestment could cause disruption within the industry, potentially leading to private equity acquisitions and further consolidation. The situation remains fluid as discussions continue at the cabinet level.
