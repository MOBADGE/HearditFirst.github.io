Marvel Heroes Revived Through Player-Run Servers

Nearly eight years after its shutdown in late 2017, the cult MMO action RPG *Marvel Heroes* has returned in a new form. Players and enthusiasts have brought the game back online using a server emulator that recently reached version 1.0. The first player-operated server, Project Tahiti, is active and attracting over a thousand players at a time. While the official servers closed when Marvel ended its partnership with developer Gazillion Entertainment, this revival allows fans to experience the game again, either online with others or solo through a self-hosted option. This emergence of player-run servers highlights the dedication of communities to preserving and continuing beloved titles beyond their commercial lifespan.

Man Admits to AI-Generated Music Streaming Fraud

Michael Smith, a man from North Carolina, pleaded guilty to conspiracy to commit wire fraud after using artificial intelligence to generate thousands of songs and employing bots to stream them billions of times. This manipulated streaming inflated royalty earnings, resulting in over $8 million in fraudulent payouts. The Department of Justice revealed massive scale and complexity behind the scheme, noting that although the music and listeners were fake, the financial damage was real. Smith is awaiting sentencing in July and has agreed to forfeit the illicit earnings. This case underscores the complexities and emerging challenges of AI in digital content, particularly regarding ethical use and fraud prevention in music streaming.

Slay the Spire 2 Developer Addresses Community Backlash to First Balance Patch

Mega Crit, the developer of *Slay the Spire 2*, responded to significant community backlash following the release of the game’s first balance pass on its beta branch. The update notably altered a popular zero-cost card, which angered many players, leading to thousands of negative Steam reviews, some seemingly influenced by region-specific factors. Mega Crit clarified that the patch represents an early step in a longer, iterative balancing process expected to extend over the next one to two years, with no changes guaranteed as permanent. The developer encouraged players to provide feedback through in-game reporting tools, emphasizing data and design philosophy as bases for adjustments. This exchange illustrates typical growing pains as developers refine live-service titles post-launch.

Developer of Half Sword Responds to Mixed Early Access Reception

The medieval dueling simulator *Half Sword* has seen a mixed reception since launching in Early Access, due largely to differences from its previously acclaimed tech demo. Players found the combat system in the alpha to be more difficult and less intuitive than anticipated, resulting in frustration. The development team explained that the demo showcased an older version of the game, and significant changes to combat and animation systems have since been implemented. They clarified that the final game will differ from both the demo and current alpha, with ongoing adjustments planned based on player feedback and development progress. The team also addressed concerns over promotional materials showing features not yet available, noting these were meant as future teasers. This situation highlights challenges developers face when transitioning from demo to full early access.

Peglin Adds New Act and Final Boss Nearly Two Years After Launch

Nearly two years after its 1.0 release, the fantasy pachinko roguelike *Peglin* has received a significant update that introduces a fourth act and a final boss encounter. Developer Red Nexus Games announced this addition, which brings new enemies and optional challenges designed to extend the game’s progression and replayability. Along with fresh music and relics, the update also improves load times and frame rates. Peglin blends pachinko mechanics with roguelike elements, distinguishing itself within indie gaming circles. This expansion demonstrates ongoing support and content development for smaller titles well after their initial release, maintaining player engagement over time.

Dragon Quest X to Receive AI-Powered Companion, Remains Japan-Exclusive

Square Enix is collaborating with Google to add an AI-driven companion called Chatty Slimey to the Japan-exclusive MMO *Dragon Quest X*. This conversational AI can interact with players using voice, initiate dialogues based on in-game events, and provide a semblance of companionship during grinding or solo play. A beta test is underway with applications open through late March. While the integration of AI chatbots in MMOs aims to enrich the experience for solo players, past attempts have revealed risks around manipulation and maintaining genuine engagement. The project reflects the growing interest in using AI to address loneliness and enhance player interaction in online games, even as broader adoption remains limited outside Japan.
