AMD Launches Ryzen 9 9950X3D2 Dual Edition with Expanded Cache for Creators and Developers

AMD has announced the Ryzen 9 9950X3D2 Dual Edition, a new processor launching on April 22 that features 3D V-Cache on both of its eight-core chiplets—delivering a total of 208 MB of L3 cache, the largest ever for a Ryzen CPU. While it maintains 16 cores like its predecessor (the Ryzen 9 9950X3D), it has a higher power draw at 200 W TDP. AMD positions this chip as a hybrid for users who need both strong creative and productivity performance as well as gaming capabilities. The new CPU offers a 5–10% improvement in creative workloads such as software builds, game engine compiles, and 3D rendering compared to the 9950X3D, though specific gaming performance gains are not detailed. The design targets professionals who want a single processor to handle both game development tasks and gaming without major compromises.

Star Wars Zero Company Embraces Permadeath for Narrative and Gameplay Impact

Developers at Bit Reactor have confirmed that their upcoming tactical game, Star Wars Zero Company, integrates permadeath for its story-centric squadmates, a choice that initially caused internal debate. Narrative lead Aaron Contreras revealed that while some feared permadeath would overly limit key characters' story roles, the team concluded that it ultimately strengthened the Star Wars storytelling experience by reflecting the franchise’s themes of loss. The game safeguards certain characters during their initial story introduction but leaves them vulnerable afterward, with permadeath becoming a permanent consequence. This approach also balances the narrative-driven squad with customizable recruits. The use of permadeath aims to deliver meaningful consequences in combat and enhance immersion, staying true to Star Wars’ tone and encouraging emotional investment.

Dying Light: The Beast’s Restored Land Mode Introduces Persistent Zombie Clearance with Resource Scarcity

Techland has released a free update called Restored Land for Dying Light: The Beast, adding a mode where zombies killed remain dead permanently, changing the dynamic of the game world. Unlike the standard mode where enemies and loot respawn, Restored Land restricts loot to one-time availability per location, reducing resource abundance and increasing survival difficulty. Players must carefully manage supplies such as food and flashlight batteries. An optional One Life setting further raises stakes by deleting the save file upon death. However, cars will continue to respawn to aid navigation. This mode emphasizes permanent impact from player actions and introduces a more strategic and cautious gameplay experience.

World of Warcraft: Midnight Expansion Review Highlights Ambition with Some Rough Edges

The latest expansion for World of Warcraft, Midnight, is seen as a significant and ambitious release that revisits and extensively redesigns classic zones like Silvermoon and Eversong Woods. The expansion brings a mix of nostalgia and new content, including revamped player housing and an overhauled class system designed to function without popular combat addons. While the narrative offers interesting political intrigue and well-crafted side quests, some storytelling elements feel over-explained and underdeveloped. New features like the Prey system inject added challenge but currently lack depth, and certain class specializations have faced balance and design issues. Despite these flaws, the expansion delivers substantial improvements in world design and gameplay systems, with the potential for refinement as updates arrive.

Senator Bernie Sanders Proposes US Moratorium on AI Data Center Construction

US Senator Bernie Sanders, alongside Representative Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez, has introduced the AI Data Center Moratorium Act, a bill calling for an immediate pause on new data center construction in the US. The proposed moratorium intends to provide lawmakers time to establish regulations addressing AI's environmental, economic, and societal impacts, including misinformation, energy consumption, and worker displacement. Sanders cited the rapid and profound changes brought by AI and robotics, emphasizing the need for democratic oversight to ensure benefits are equitably shared and negative effects mitigated. The bill also seeks to prevent AI hardware exports to countries lacking similar protections. Despite these aims, the legislation faces significant political hurdles in a government largely supportive of AI investment, making its passage uncertain.
