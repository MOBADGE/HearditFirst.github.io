Sprawl Zero Aims to Define a New Generation of FPS

The indie developer MAETH has announced *Sprawl Zero*, a first-person shooter inspired by early 2000s classics like *Halo: Combat Evolved*, *FEAR*, and *Half-Life 2*. Positioned as a departure from the fast-paced, twitch-heavy "boomer shooter" style, *Sprawl Zero* introduces gameplay that balances speed with a stronger sense of character weight and physicality. Players control a cybernetically enhanced soldier named Five, tasked with confronting a techno-religious faction. The game features time and gravity manipulation mechanics, along with enemy AI designed to coordinate tactics such as flanking and grenade use. Drawing on *Half-Life 2* for its environmental and weaponry influences, including gravity gloves and helicopter enemies, *Sprawl Zero* seeks to capture the feel of the early console FPS era. Although no release date has been set, the original *Sprawl* is currently discounted as a primer.
