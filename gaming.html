Fortnite Chapter 7 Updates Enhance Weapon Balance and Gameplay Flexibility

Epic Games has recently made significant adjustments to Fortnite's Chapter 7 loot pool, resulting in what some describe as the most balanced and flexible selection of weapons in recent memory. Early in the chapter, the Deadeye Assault Rifle dominated gameplay, overshadowing other rifles and rendering SMGs nearly irrelevant. However, subsequent buffs to weapons like the Holo and Flex SMGs have revitalized close-quarters combat options, with increased damage and magazine capacity making them more competitive. The addition of mobility-enhancing items such as zero-gravity Flowberry and Mist Grenades complements the faster-paced playstyle these weapons promote.

The introduction of a new red-dot rifle, the MK-seven, offers a viable alternative to the Deadeye AR, contributing to a more diverse meta. These changes allow more varied loadouts, enabling players to carry combinations like sniper rifles alongside SMGs and mobility tools. While the Lock On Assault Rifle remains controversial due to its auto-aim capability, overall weapon variety now offers multiple solid choices, closing the gap between top-tier and previously underused weapons.

Renegade X Fan-Made Spiritual Successor to Command & Conquer: Renegade Reaches Version 1.0

After nearly two decades of development and 12 years of beta testing, Totem Arts has officially released version 1.0 of Renegade X, a fan-made spiritual successor to the 2002 first-person shooter Command & Conquer: Renegade. Originally created to fill the gap left by the cancellation of Renegade 2, Renegade X utilizes Unreal Engine 3 to deliver large-scale, team-based battles with vehicle combat, superweapons, and extensive multiplayer modes with bot support.

The 1.0 update includes bug fixes, balance adjustments, and a new map called CnC-City, adapted from the original Renegade. The game supports full online functionality and offers a single-player campaign titled Black Dawn from earlier releases. Totem Arts is also developing Firestorm, a reimagining of Command & Conquer: Tiberian Sun presented as another ambitious first-person shooter experience.
