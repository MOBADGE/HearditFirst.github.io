New Companions Revealed for Warhammer 40K RPGs by Owlcat

Owlcat Games has unveiled two new companion characters for its Warhammer 40,000 RPG titles. For *Rogue Trader*, the upcoming *The Infinite Museion* DLC will introduce Eogann Februs, a tech-priest companion. While the addition is consistent with the existing Adeptus Mechanicus presence in the game, some players anticipated more exotic character types given the Warhammer 40K universe's diversity.

Additionally, Owlcat shared a trailer for *Dark Heresy*, currently in playable alpha, revealing Haymar Devos, a Catachan guardsman. The character represents a classic death world soldier archetype, broadening the party options and reinforcing the game's connection to Warhammer lore. This update is part of Owlcat's gradual content roll-out, targeting improvements and expansions after the initially problematic launch of *Rogue Trader*. Both the DLC and *Dark Heresy* remain unpriced and undated beyond "coming soon" on Steam.

AOC Highlights Monitors as a Practical Upgrade Amid Rising GPU and Memory Costs

During a press event in Croatia, monitor manufacturer AOC suggested that upgrading gaming monitors may currently offer better value than purchasing new GPUs or computer memory. This stance responds to ongoing memory shortages that have pushed GPU prices higher, making other upgrades less accessible.

AOC’s European commercial leader, Cesar Reyes Acosta, pointed to advances in monitor technology and price-to-performance improvements over the past five years as evidence that monitors evade some inflation pressures. For example, modern 27-inch 1440p, 260 Hz monitors are retailing at prices comparable to older, less capable 24-inch 165 Hz models.

The company also introduced new OLED monitors employing fifth-generation QD-OLED and fourth-generation W-OLED technology, with models priced between €899 and €999. These offer higher HDR standards and refreshed designs, aiming to attract gamers despite the high price point. Philips, AOC’s sister brand, released a 5K dual-mode gaming monitor capable of switching between 5K resolution at 165 Hz and 1440p at 330 Hz for €799, targeting users who want both visual fidelity and responsiveness.

Philips Launches Dual-Mode 5K Gaming Monitor in Europe with Ambilight Support

Philips has announced the Western release of its Evnia 27M2D5901A, a 27-inch 5K gaming monitor featuring dual-mode capability. This allows users to switch between a 5120x2880 resolution at 165 Hz and 2560x1440 resolution at 330 Hz, tailoring display performance to different game types.

Notably, the monitor includes Philips' Ambilight lighting system, which synchronizes with in-game events and can integrate with smart home lighting. It also supports 65W USB Type-C charging, adding convenience for connected devices. Priced at €799 and available in July, the monitor competes with other 5K displays such as Asus' ROG Strix XG27JCG, offering slightly higher brightness and a distinct all-white design.

While 5K gaming remains niche due to hardware demands, the flexible resolution modes make the monitor a versatile choice for players without top-tier rigs. Philips currently has no plans to release the model in the U.S.

Turtle WoW Private Server Announces Shutdown Following Legal Injunction

Turtle WoW, a popular World of Warcraft private server that provided a "Classic Plus" experience with additional content beyond the original game, announced it will shut down after Blizzard secured a legal injunction.

The developer team revealed that all servers will close on May 14, with social media and forums shutting down by October 16. This follows a federal judge’s ruling in Blizzard’s favor, ending an eight-year run for the modded server. Turtle WoW had sought a fan-server licensing agreement from Blizzard, but efforts did not yield results.

The closure reflects continued tension between game publishers and private server communities. Unlike officially sanctioned fan servers for titles like *EverQuest* and *City of Heroes*, unauthorized servers remain vulnerable to legal action despite their role in preserving older game experiences.
