Ivy Road Studio Shuts Down Amid Funding Challenges

Ivy Road, the developer behind the cozy tea shop adventure game *Wanderstop*, announced it will be closing its doors on March 31. The studio’s closure comes after unsuccessful attempts to secure funding for a new project titled *Engine Angel*. Despite the setback, *Wanderstop* will remain available for purchase on current platforms, and a new development related to the game will be unveiled in collaboration with Annapurna Interactive.

This news highlights ongoing difficulties smaller studios face in securing financing in today’s market. Ivy Road’s experience underscores the challenges for creative teams trying to navigate funding gaps in the current industry landscape.

ZA/UM Takes New Direction with Zero Parades

The developers of *Zero Parades*, the upcoming RPG from ZA/UM, revealed they intentionally moved away from cop-centered narratives after their previous game, *Disco Elysium*. Lead writer Siim Sinamäe explained that shifting cultural attitudes toward police influenced their decision. Instead, the team chose to explore a spy thriller angle, providing a fresh genre while still emphasizing strong writing.

This conscious pivot reflects how developers are responding to social and political climates in their storytelling. It also shows a desire to innovate within narrative-driven RPGs while remaining mindful of broader cultural contexts.

Frictional Games Bundle Offers Classic Horror Titles for $15

A collection of all major games by horror specialist Frictional Games—including *Penumbra*, *Amnesia* titles, and *SOMA*—is currently available through Humble Bundle for $15. The bundle packages several highly regarded entries in the survival horror genre, appealing to both new players and longtime fans. The offer runs until April 1, 2026, and includes Steam keys that must be redeemed by April 1, 2027.

For players interested in exploring landmark horror games at a low cost, this bundle represents an accessible opportunity. The inclusion of multiple titles spanning the developer’s history provides a comprehensive look at their evolution.

Path of Exile Discord Server Leader Banned, Impacting Item Economy

Jenebu, the leader of the prominent Path of Exile trading Discord server The Forbidden Trove, had his in-game account banned. The ban has caused concern among the community because Jenebu’s account held many rare and irreplaceable items, used in a widely trusted item duplication service known as “mirror service.” His appeal to Grinding Gear Games to restore the items was publicly shared but has not resulted in reversal.

This development could accelerate the decline of third-party trading communities within *Path of Exile*, particularly as the official game tools now support asynchronous player trading. It also illustrates tensions between unofficial player-led economies and developer policies.

Deus Ex: Human Revolution’s Soundtrack Shift Explained by Original Composer

Alexander Brandon, composer of the original *Deus Ex* soundtrack, discussed his limited involvement with the 2011 prequel *Human Revolution*. Although given a chance to contribute a track, his compositions were ultimately not used, as the new development team at Eidos Montreal sought to establish their own musical identity. Brandon expressed understanding of the decision but also some disappointment over being sidelined for subsequent spinoff titles.

The insight sheds light on creative shifts that occur when legacy franchises change hands or directions, and how such changes affect long-term contributors. It also reflects challenges experienced by game composers in maintaining roles amid industry changes.

PUBG: Blindspot Early Access Ends After Under Two Months

The tactical top-down shooter *PUBG: Blindspot* will end its early access phase on March 30 due to insufficient player numbers. Developer Arc Team cited difficulty sustaining the experience and moved to discontinue active development. The game struggled with low concurrent player counts, impacting matchmaking times and overall viability.

*PUBG: Blindspot’s* closure highlights the difficulties faced by new multiplayer games, especially free-to-play titles dependent on active communities. It also reflects broader financial pressures within companies like Krafton, which has recently faced profit declines and organizational shifts.
