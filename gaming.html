Banquet For Fools: A Demanding, Note-Taking RPG Experience

Banquet for Fools is a recently released RPG emphasizing player involvement through manual note-taking and exploration. Players are dropped into a complex world with scarce guidance, compelling them to record information personally as the game offers no quest logs or automatic markers. This design demands active engagement, encouraging deeper investment in the game’s social structures and environment. Combat combines real-time action with tactical pauses, fostering a sense of survival in a hostile wilderness. The game’s unorthodox approach may frustrate some with its opacity and lack of hand-holding, but it rewards persistence and careful observation. As an indie title priced at $20, it offers a unique, slow-paced RPG experience distinct from more conventional entries in the genre.

Revisiting Mars War: Logs, a Gritty AA RPG for 99 Cents

Mars War: Logs, a 2013 RPG from Spiders, has been brought back into attention due to its availability on sale for just 99 cents on Steam. While not considered a polished or standout game, it provides an intriguing mix of a choice-driven narrative and a bleak sci-fi setting on a post-apocalyptic Mars. Combat remains functional but unremarkable, with some distinctive mechanics like sand-throwing to stun enemies. The game’s appeal lies in its atmospheric storytelling and rough edges typical of "AA" RPGs, making it a low-risk trial for fans looking for lesser-known RPGs. It runs well on portable systems like the Steam Deck, adding to its accessibility during the ongoing Steam Spring sale.

Sand: Raiders of Sophie Release Date Uncertainty Continues

Sand: Raiders of Sophie, a multiplayer PvPvE extraction shooter scheduled for release in March 2026, has yet to receive a specific launch date despite the month nearing its end. The developers and community managers have addressed fan inquiries, explaining that final development details are still being resolved, and a firm date cannot be announced without certainty. This has led to speculation among players about a possible delay, though no official confirmation has been made. Sand aims to blend base-building, vehicular combat, and extraction gameplay on an alien planet, and while the lack of precise timing may disappoint eager fans, the developers emphasize the need for caution before committing to a release date.

Lost Wiki: Kozlovka Blends Mystery and Minimalism in a $5 Puzzle Game

Lost Wiki: Kozlovka is a narrative-driven mystery game where players investigate a small Eastern European town through a series of Wikipedia-like database entries. Players piece together timelines, family trees, and reports to uncover secrets spanning generations, particularly surrounding a strange tree known as Draken Oak. The game focuses on thorough reading and deduction rather than complex puzzles or red herrings, making its mystery relatively straightforward. Its interface and storytelling emphasize both nostalgia and the nature of information control. Priced at $5 on Steam, the game offers a compact and focused investigative experience for fans of narrative puzzles.

Players Call for Mount Domestication Changes in Crimson Desert

Since the launch of Crimson Desert, players have expressed dissatisfaction that several exotic mounts—such as bears, dodo-like birds, and dragons—can only be ridden temporarily rather than kept permanently. Although these unusual mounts can be acquired briefly, they revert to hostility once dismounted, and stables will not accept them as permanent companions. This limitation contrasts with earlier marketing footage implying broader mount options. Players argue that in this single-player game, allowing permanent use of these mounts would not unbalance gameplay but increase enjoyment. It remains to be seen if developer Pearl Abyss will adjust this mechanic in response to community feedback.

Capcom Clarifies Its Use of Artificial Intelligence in Game Development

Capcom has stated it will not incorporate AI-generated assets directly into its game content but intends to leverage generative AI to improve efficiency in development workflows. This announcement came during a recent investor briefing, clarifying the company’s stance amid wider industry discussions about AI’s role in creative processes. Capcom is evaluating potential applications of generative AI in areas such as graphics, sound, and programming but draws a line at including AI-created assets in final games. The statement aims to balance technological adoption with maintaining artistic control, addressing concerns over AI’s impact on game quality and originality.
