Former Valve Economist Yanis Varoufakis Faces Trial Over Drug Admission

Yanis Varoufakis, a former Valve economist turned Greek parliamentarian, will stand trial on charges of promoting drug use after admitting during a podcast that he took ecstasy once, 36 years ago. The statement was part of an interview framed as a futuristic discussion aimed at younger audiences and intended to illustrate the consequences of drug use.

Varoufakis clarified that his comment was a factual admission in response to a direct question and that he did not promote drugs. His political party, MeRA25, criticized the charges, arguing that the legal action signals a justice system intolerant of dissent.

The trial has attracted attention because of Varoufakis's unique career path spanning both economics in the gaming industry and political office, as well as the implications for free expression in discussing past drug use.
