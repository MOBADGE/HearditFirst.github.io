League of Legends Introduces WASD Controls Amid Mixed Reactions

Riot Games has added WASD keyboard controls to League of Legends for the first time in its 16-year history, aiming to improve accessibility for new players familiar with the format. Released on December 3, the alternative control scheme applies to selected game modes and intends to ease the learning curve by offering a familiar input method alongside traditional mouse-driven controls.

However, the rollout has faced criticism from the community. Initial concerns about balance disruption have shifted, with many players now reporting that WASD controls feel clunky and less efficient, particularly for advanced techniques like animation cancelling used to maximize attack speed. Riot implemented an artificial attack speed penalty for WASD users to avoid unbalancing gameplay, especially affecting fast-attack champions. Despite these issues, Riot plans to monitor usage data and player feedback over the next month before drawing final conclusions, with the feature not yet available in ranked matches.

Early Look: Everything is Crab, a Spore-Inspired Roguelike

Everything is Crab, an early-access roguelike from Odd Dreams Digital, offers a fresh take on evolution-themed gameplay reminiscent of Spore. Players start as simple blob creatures and evolve into varied forms by acquiring traits that affect combat, movement, and survival strategies within a dynamic ecosystem. The game emphasizes experimentation with both predatory and herbivorous play styles.

Critics of the early build note that combat pacing and dodge mechanics need refinement, as current dodge cooldowns make avoiding attacks feel challenging. Nevertheless, the emergent gameplay of carving out survival niches through genetic traits stands out, making the game a promising addition to the roguelike genre ahead of its planned summer 2026 release.
