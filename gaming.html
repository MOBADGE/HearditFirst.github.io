European Commission Age Verification App Demonstrated Vulnerabilities

Security researchers quickly bypassed the demo version of the European Commission's new age verification app designed for use across EU online services. The app, which is intended to verify users’ ages without repeatedly sharing personal data, was hacked in under two minutes by UK security consultant Paul Moore. By manipulating a configuration file on the Android demo app, Moore showed it was possible to reset and sidestep the app’s six-digit PIN, effectively granting unauthorized access to verified credentials.

Although the European Commission confirmed these vulnerabilities exist only in the demo version and stated the final release will address such issues, concerns remain due to the sensitive personal information involved and the app’s €4 million development cost. The episode underscores ongoing challenges in creating secure, user-friendly age verification systems that protect privacy without introducing new risks.

Netflix’s Live-Action Gundam Film Casts Jason Isaacs

Netflix has announced Jason Isaacs among the cast for its upcoming live-action adaptation of the Gundam franchise, alongside Sydney Sweeney and Noah Centineo. The film will depict rival mech pilots engaged in conflict across Earth and space colonies. Isaacs’ involvement has drawn attention given his acclaimed performances in other projects.

While the cast announcement has piqued interest, there remains uncertainty about the mech designs and overall film direction, important factors for fans of the lengthy Gundam series. The film recently began production with no release date yet announced. For many, existing Gundam anime series remain the preferred way to experience the franchise’s stories.

Assassin’s Creed Black Flag Remake Trailer Leaks Before Official Reveal

Ubisoft’s remake of Assassin’s Creed Black Flag, dubbed "Black Flag Resynced," suffered yet another leak ahead of its official reveal scheduled for April 23. A short clip surfaced on social media, showing protagonist Edward Kenway alongside Blackbeard in naval combat sequences. The footage highlights improved character models and lighting compared to the original.

This remake has been highly anticipated but also heavily leaked, to the point the publisher has joked about it being “gaming’s worst-kept secret.” The early trailer leak continues that trend, but the official showcase is expected to provide fuller details, confirming enhancements and features planned for the updated version of the 2013 pirate adventure.

Windows Task Manager CPU Usage Calculations Explained

The longstanding confusion over inaccurate CPU usage readings in Windows Task Manager received clarification from its original creator, Dave Plummer. In a recent video, Plummer explained that the application uses averaged data over time intervals rather than real-time instantaneous usage to produce its CPU utilization figure. This method can cause temporary spikes in processor activity to appear diluted or even missed depending on refresh timing.

Microsoft is rolling out fixes in preview Windows builds aiming for more consistent CPU workload reporting based on standard industry metrics. Plummer emphasized that Task Manager prioritizes simplicity and general usefulness over perfect precision, a compromise given modern CPUs’ complex dynamic frequency scaling and multi-core designs.

Cybersecurity Expert Pleads Guilty to Ransomware Conspiracy

A former cybersecurity professional has pleaded guilty to conspiring to deploy ransomware with two co-conspirators. Between April and November 2023, the trio used the BlackCat/ALPHV ransomware strain to attack victims, extorting at least $1.2 million in Bitcoin. One defendant, Angelo Martino, exploited his position as a ransomware negotiator to supply attackers with insider information on victim insurance policies, maximizing ransom demands.

Law enforcement has seized assets valued at $10 million from Martino, including vehicles and luxury items bought with illicit proceeds. The guilty pleas highlight ongoing challenges in addressing cybercrime even from individuals with knowledge and previous roles intended to counter such threats.

Crimson Desert Prepares Major Update with Inventory and Visual Improvements

Pearl Abyss announced an upcoming update for its action RPG Crimson Desert, promising several quality-of-life improvements and visual enhancements. Scheduled for release this week, the patch will introduce preset control schemes for keyboard/mouse and controllers, difficulty settings, and categorized inventory tabs separating food, wardrobe, gatherables, and collection items. These changes aim to address longstanding player concerns about cumbersome inventory management.

Additionally, the update will improve distant scenery quality for more realistic vistas, albeit increasing download size. The developer plans to roll out new gameplay features over coming months, seeking to refine player experience in the open-world environment.
