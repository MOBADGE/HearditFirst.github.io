March 31, 2026

Conflict and Security in the Middle East

Reports have emerged that Iran is using children in security roles during the ongoing war, with witnesses stating that an 11-year-old was killed while manning a checkpoint in Tehran during an air strike. This raises serious concerns about the involvement of minors in conflict zones and the humanitarian impact of the war.

Meanwhile, Israel has announced plans to maintain control over a portion of southern Lebanon after the war with Hezbollah ends. Defence Minister Israel Katz also indicated that houses in Lebanese villages near the Israeli border will be demolished. This move could affect stability in the region and prolong tensions between Israel and Lebanon.

In addition, two Indonesian UN peacekeepers were killed by a roadside explosion in Lebanon. This came a day after another Indonesian peacekeeper was also killed, highlighting the dangers faced by peacekeeping forces in the area.

Kidnapping and Security Incidents

A US journalist, Shelly Kittleson, has been kidnapped in Baghdad. The US State Department reported that a suspect connected to the abduction has ties to Kataib Hezbollah, a militia group backed by Iran. This incident adds to security concerns in Iraq and highlights the risks faced by journalists in conflict areas.

Legal and Political Developments

A judge temporarily halted the construction of a ballroom at the White House associated with Donald Trump. The ruling was based on the opinion that Trump is a "steward" of the White House rather than the owner. This legal decision could delay or alter construction plans related to the presidential residence.

In a separate development, US Army pilots who flew helicopters near the home of musician Kid Rock had their suspension lifted. President Donald Trump commented on the incident by saying, "They like Kid Rock. I like Kid Rock." This resolves the disciplinary action involving the pilots.

Business and Economy

Tech company Oracle announced significant job cuts, with thousands of employees reportedly losing their jobs. Oracle is one of the world's largest technology firms, and such a large reduction in workforce may indicate shifts in the tech industry and broader economic challenges.

Other Notable News

In Italy, a tragedy surrounding a Christmas meal has turned into a murder investigation after tests suggested ricin poisoning as the cause of death of two women. Police have yet to identify a suspect in the case.

China has banned the practice of storing cremated remains in so-called "bone ash apartments," where mourners keep ashes instead of burying them in cemeteries. This move reflects government efforts to regulate funeral and mourning customs.

Finally, golfer Tiger Woods announced he will step away from the sport to seek treatment. This comes after his arrest and charge for driving under the influence following a car accident in Florida, where he clipped a truck and rolled his vehicle.
