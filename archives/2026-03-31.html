Heard It First – Daily Digest

March 31, 2026

Middle East Conflict and Security

Reports from Tehran indicate that children are being used in security roles amid ongoing conflict, with an 11-year-old reportedly killed while manning a checkpoint during an airstrike. In southern Lebanon, Israel’s Defence Minister Israel Katz said Israel plans to maintain control over certain border areas after the war with Hezbollah ends. Additionally, houses in Lebanese villages near the Israeli border are set to be demolished.

Meanwhile, violence in Lebanon has also impacted United Nations peacekeepers. Two Indonesian UN peacekeepers were killed by a roadside explosion, following the death of another Indonesian peacekeeper in the country just the day before. In Iraq, US journalist Shelly Kittleson was kidnapped in Baghdad. The US State Department said a suspect linked to the Iran-backed militia group Kataib Hezbollah is involved in her abduction.

Political and Legal Developments in the US

A judge appointed by Republicans has temporarily halted construction of a ballroom at the White House linked to Donald Trump’s ongoing projects. The ruling emphasized that Trump is a "steward" rather than the owner of the White House, impacting his ability to make alterations to the historic building.

Technology and Employment

Oracle, one of the world's largest technology companies, has announced large-scale job cuts described as "significant." While precise details are not available, thousands of employees are believed to have lost their jobs. This move reflects ongoing changes in the tech industry’s workforce and may have broad implications for the sector.

International Incidents and Crime

In Italy, what initially appeared to be a tragic Christmas meal involving the death of two women has turned into a murder investigation. Tests suggest the presence of ricin, a deadly poison, although no suspect has yet been identified.

In Baghdad, the kidnapping of US journalist Shelly Kittleson also raises concerns about security and militia activity connected to Iran-backed groups, highlighting ongoing risks for foreigners in conflict zones.

Wildlife and Public Safety

In Germany, a woman was bitten by a wolf on a busy shopping street in Hamburg. This incident is reportedly the first wolf attack on a human in Germany since wolves began reestablishing their population decades ago. Authorities and the public are likely to monitor this development closely given its unusual nature.

Other Developments

In China, authorities have banned the storage of cremated remains in "bone ash apartments," which are spaces set aside for housing ashes instead of using traditional cemeteries. This move reflects efforts to regulate mourning and burial practices within the country.

In sports news, golf champion Tiger Woods announced he will step away from competition to seek treatment following a car crash in Florida. He was arrested and charged with driving under the influence after the incident, marking a significant moment in his personal and professional life.
