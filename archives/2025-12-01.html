Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 01, 2025

Ukraine Conflict and Diplomacy

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky has identified control over Ukraine's territory as the "most difficult" issue ahead of upcoming talks. This comes as US envoy Steve Witkoff, accompanied by Jared Kushner, a former adviser to Donald Trump, plans to meet Russian leader Vladimir Putin on Tuesday. The meetings could have implications for the ongoing conflict and efforts to negotiate its resolution.

US Legal and Political Developments

An appeals court has disqualified Alina Habba, known as former US President Donald Trump's personal lawyer, from serving as the US Attorney for New Jersey. This decision impacts Trump's team amid ongoing legal and political scrutiny. In a separate US legal case, Joaquín Guzmán López, son of convicted drug lord El Chapo, has pleaded guilty to drug trafficking charges. His plea followed a US government agreement not to seek the death penalty.

Central American Election Update

In Honduras, the presidential election results remain extremely close, with right-wing candidate Nasry Asfura narrowly leading former TV host Salvador Nasralla by only 515 votes, according to the country's electoral authority. The race is described as a "technical tie," underscoring the tense and uncertain political environment in the country as the final results are awaited.

Natural Disasters in Southeast Asia and South America

Indonesia continues to cope with severe flooding after a cyclone struck the region, pushing the confirmed death toll past 500. Thousands have been injured, and around 500 people remain missing. Meanwhile, in Peru's central Ucayali region, a deadly landslide struck two boats along a river, and rescue efforts are being hampered by strong currents. Many people are reported missing, and authorities are working to locate them.

Violence and Unrest in Africa and Georgia

In Nigeria, attacks have escalated with the abduction of a pastor and a new bride during raids on a church in central Nigeria and a wedding party in the mostly Muslim northern part of the country. These incidents highlight ongoing security challenges in the region. Additionally, in Georgia, anti-government protesters are believed to have been sprayed with camite, a toxic chemical used during World War One, based on an investigation by the BBC. This raises concerns about the treatment of demonstrators during recent unrest.

Notable Death in Cameroon

The death of opposition figure Anicet Ekane, aged 74, in detention has caused shock in Cameroon. His family was reportedly given no clear explanation, with his wife only presented with his body. The circumstances surrounding his death remain unclear and have drawn attention to issues of detention and human rights in the country.
