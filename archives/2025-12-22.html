Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 22, 2025

US-Greenland Relations

A new envoy appointed by Donald Trump has stated his goal is to make Greenland part of the United States. This ambition has drawn a warning from Denmark to respect Greenland's sovereignty. Greenland itself has emphasized that it will independently decide its own future. The situation underscores ongoing tensions regarding influence and control in the Arctic region, which could have implications for international diplomacy and regional stability.

International Conflicts and Ceasefire Talks

Thailand and Cambodia are set to hold ceasefire discussions after recent clashes that have resulted in at least 41 deaths and displaced nearly one million people on both sides of the border. These talks aim to ease tensions and address the humanitarian impacts of the conflict. Meanwhile, in Moscow, Lieutenant General Fanil Sarvarov was killed by a car bomb. He is the third military official to die in bomb attacks in the Russian capital over the past year, signaling ongoing security challenges in the city.

Legal and Security Developments

In France, a staff member from the Élysée Palace, Thomas M, is due to stand trial accused of stealing valuable silver and porcelain used during state banquets. The case shines a light on security within important government buildings. In Australia, following a deadly shooting in Bondi, police have released documents alleging the gunmen planned the attack meticulously over several months. This has led the government to fast-track new gun control and protest laws, though civil liberties advocates warn these measures may harm democracy and unfairly target gun owners.

US Political and Economic News

US lawmakers from both parties are threatening legal action against the Attorney General over the handling of files related to Jeffrey Epstein. The lawmakers argue that the Department of Justice failed to fulfill legal responsibilities regarding transparency. In energy policy, the US is pursuing a third oil tanker linked to Venezuela following President Trump's order to block sanctioned oil tankers entering and leaving the country. This move is part of broader US efforts to enforce sanctions on Venezuela’s oil sector.

Markets and Diplomacy

Gold and silver prices have reached record highs as investors seek safe assets amid geopolitical tensions and expectations of further US interest rate cuts. On the diplomatic front, despite no major breakthroughs, peace talks between the US and Ukraine in Miami were described as productive by Donald Trump’s special envoy Steve Witkoff and the top Ukrainian negotiator. These talks address ongoing conflict issues and offer hope for progress in negotiations.
