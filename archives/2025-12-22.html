Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 22, 2025

Global Politics and Security

Tensions are rising internationally on several fronts. In the Arctic, former US president Donald Trump stated that the US "has to have" Greenland, naming a special envoy to pursue this goal. Denmark has warned the US to respect Greenland’s sovereignty, while Greenland has emphasized it will independently decide its future.

In Eastern Europe, Russia has intensified attacks on the Ukrainian region of Odesa. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky warned that these strikes aim to block Ukraine’s access to maritime logistics, a critical lifeline for trade and supplies amid ongoing conflict.

Southeast Asia is also facing instability as Thailand and Cambodia prepare to hold ceasefire talks. The conflict along their border has resulted in at least 41 deaths and displaced nearly one million people. Officials from both countries are moving toward negotiation after prolonged violence.

War and Humanitarian Issues

In Gaza, one-year-old Siwar Ashour, who was evacuated to Jordan for medical treatment during the recent conflict, has returned to a hospital in Gaza. The BBC followed Siwar’s story as part of ongoing coverage of the humanitarian impact of the war.

Meanwhile, concerns are growing about the future of aid organizations in Gaza. New Israeli regulations require international NGOs to register by December 31, or they face closure within Israel. Though the Israeli government assures that aid delivery will continue, many NGOs fear the rules could jeopardize their operations and thus humanitarian support to Gaza.

Crime and Justice

In Russia’s capital, Moscow, Lt Gen Fanil Sarvarov was killed by a car bomb. This marks the third military official murdered in such attacks in the city over the past year, highlighting ongoing security challenges there.

In Ecuador, four soldiers have been sentenced to 34 years in prison for the forced disappearance and subsequent mutilation and burning of four boys. The case has drawn attention to military abuses and accountability in the country.

Also in France, a staff member of the Élysée Palace, identified as Thomas M, is set to stand trial for stealing solid silver and porcelain tableware used during state banquets. The theft has raised questions about security within the presidential residence.

Economy and Markets

Precious metal markets are seeing significant activity as gold and silver hit record prices. Investors are turning to these safe-haven assets amid heightened geopolitical tensions and forecasts of more US interest rate cuts. This trend reflects broader concerns about economic stability and global uncertainties.

Local News

In Spain, the town of La Bañeza, which suffered severe wildfire damage this summer, won €468 million in the country’s Christmas lottery. With a population of about 11,000, this large prize could bring significant relief and opportunities for rebuilding to the affected community.
