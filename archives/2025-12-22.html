Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 22, 2025

Security and Violence Updates

Police have released documents alleging that the suspects involved in the recent Bondi shooting planned the attack meticulously over several months. This information sheds light on the preparation behind the deadly incident, prompting calls for changes in gun laws in Australia. However, proposals to fast-track new gun and protest laws have faced criticism from civil liberties groups who argue these measures could harm democracy and unfairly target gun owners.

In Russia, Fanil Sarvarov, the head of the armed forces' operational training department, was killed by a car bomb in Moscow. Russian officials have confirmed his death but have not provided detailed information about the investigation or motives, which raises concerns about security within the capital.

International Conflict and Diplomacy

Efforts to resolve ongoing conflicts continue with some developments. The United States and Ukraine described recent talks in Miami as productive, although no major breakthroughs were achieved. The discussions involved Donald Trump’s special envoy, Steve Witkoff, alongside Ukraine’s top negotiator, focusing on current peace plans for the region.

Separately, Thailand and Cambodia plan to hold ceasefire talks after a violent dispute that has claimed at least 41 lives and displaced nearly one million people on both sides. Officials emphasized the humanitarian impact and the urgency of reaching a peaceful resolution.

Legal and Political Developments in the US

In the United States, bipartisan concerns have been raised about the Department of Justice’s handling of documents related to Jeffrey Epstein. A Republican and a Democrat who have championed transparency threatened legal action against the Attorney General, accusing the department of not fulfilling its legal obligations to release the files.

Additionally, the US government is actively pursuing a third oil tanker linked to Venezuela, following President Donald Trump’s recent announcement of a blockade on sanctioned vessels entering and leaving the country. This move signals continued pressure on Venezuela amid ongoing political and economic tensions.

Economy and Market Movements

The price of gold has surged to a record high above $4,400 per ounce. Investors are turning to precious metals as a safe haven amid ongoing geopolitical tensions and expectations of further interest rate cuts by the US Federal Reserve. This trend highlights the cautious approach of global markets in uncertain times.

Middle East Settlement Expansion

Israel has approved the construction of 19 new settlements in the occupied West Bank. Far-right Finance Minister Bezalel Smotrich stated the decision aims to prevent the establishment of a Palestinian state. This move is likely to deepen tensions in the region, as settlement expansion has been a contentious issue in Israeli-Palestinian relations.
