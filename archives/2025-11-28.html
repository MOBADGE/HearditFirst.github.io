Heard It First – Daily Digest

November 28, 2025

Ukraine Political Developments

Andriy Yermak, who has served as the chief of staff to the Ukrainian president during the full-scale war with Russia, resigned following an anti-corruption raid on his home. The details of the allegations have not been fully disclosed, but this marks a significant change in Ukraine's presidential administration amid ongoing conflict. The resignation could impact the political handling of Ukraine's war efforts and governance.

Middle East Conflict Update

An Israeli military raid in southern Syria’s village of Beit Jinn resulted in the deaths of thirteen people, marking the deadliest Israeli strike in the region in recent months. Israel stated the operation targeted militants, while Syria’s foreign ministry condemned the attack as a “war crime.” This incident may further heighten tensions between Israel and Syria.

US Migration and Security Issues

The US president announced a pause on migration from unspecified “third-world countries” and pledged to remove anyone deemed “not a net asset” to the country. This statement comes shortly after a shooting in Washington DC allegedly carried out by an Afghan national led members of the Afghan diaspora in the US to urge the administration against a tougher stance toward the community. These developments suggest a potential shift in US immigration policies and continuing debates over national security and refugee issues.

Hong Kong Fire Investigation

Authorities in Hong Kong arrested eight additional individuals in connection with a devastating fire that occurred on Wednesday, which killed at least 128 people. Of those, 89 bodies remain unidentified, and 79 people are still injured. The arrests indicate a continued investigation into the causes and liability related to the tragedy, highlighting concerns over fire safety in the city.

Science and Society Highlights

Scientists have detected lightning on Mars for the first time, based on 28 hours of electric crackle recordings gathered over two Martian years. This discovery advances our understanding of Martian weather and atmospheric phenomena.

Meanwhile, in Japan, a Tokyo court ruled that the country’s ban on same-sex marriage is constitutional. This decision contrasts with other judicial rulings in Japan that had generated hope for marriage equality, and it may influence future legal challenges and social debates in the country.

Other Notable News

- In South Africa, the daughter of a former president resigned amid allegations that she deceived South Africans into fighting for Russia in the Ukraine conflict. This was highlighted by a South African who recounted the brutal realities of war. - In Tunisia, dozens of opposition figures received prison sentences after being accused of attempting a coup. Human rights groups have criticized the trial as politically motivated. - Three nuns in an Alpine convent, who claimed they were forcibly removed and placed in a care home, were given the option to remain at the convent if they agree to stop using social media, indicating ongoing tensions within religious communities about modern communication technology.
