Heard It First – Daily Digest

November 28, 2025

Ukraine Leadership Change

Andriy Yermak, the chief of staff to the Ukrainian president throughout the ongoing conflict with Russia, has resigned following a raid by Ukrainian anti-corruption authorities on his home. Yermak, aged 54, played a significant role during Russia's full-scale war against Ukraine. His departure comes amid growing concerns about corruption within Ukraine's political circles and may have implications for the president's administration and the country's anti-corruption efforts.

Legal Actions After Hong Kong Fire

Authorities have arrested eight more individuals in connection with a deadly fire in Hong Kong that occurred earlier this week. The blaze claimed at least 128 lives, with 89 bodies yet to be identified and 79 people still injured. The arrests indicate ongoing investigations into the causes and possible negligence surrounding the incident. The scale of the tragedy has raised questions about fire safety standards and emergency response in the city.

U.S. Migration Policy and Afghan Community Response

The U.S. president announced a pause on migration from unspecified "third-world countries," pledging to remove immigrants deemed "not a net asset" to the country. This announcement follows a shooting in Washington DC allegedly carried out by an Afghan national. In response, Afghans in the United States, many of whom left Afghanistan due to conflict, have publicly urged the U.S. administration not to adopt a tougher stance on migration, emphasizing the importance of fairness and inclusion.

Conflict and Allegations in the Middle East

An Israeli military raid in southern Syria's village of Beit Jinn resulted in the deaths of thirteen people, marking the deadliest Israeli operation in the area in months. Israel stated its target was militants, but Syria's foreign ministry condemned the raid as a "war crime." Separately, a United Nations panel has reported that Israel operates a "de facto policy of torture" in its detention centers, citing detailed accounts from human rights organizations. Israel has not officially responded to the recent UN findings but has previously denied torture allegations.

Developments in South Africa and Religious Communities

In South Africa, the daughter of a former president resigned amid allegations that she deceived South Africans into fighting for Russia. This resignation coincides with a South African recruited to fight in Ukraine sharing harrowing experiences of war. Meanwhile, three nuns who had left their Alpine convent but were placed in a care home against their will have been told they can remain in the convent if they agree to leave social media. This situation highlights tensions involving religious life and personal freedoms in that community.

Science and Legal News

Researchers have, for the first time, detected lightning on Mars. They recorded electric crackling sounds over 28 hours spanning two Martian years, confirming new atmospheric phenomena on the planet. In Japan, a Tokyo court ruled that the country’s ban on same-sex marriage is constitutional. This decision contrasts with previous court rulings around Japan that had offered hope for marriage equality, signaling ongoing debates about LGBTQ+ rights in the country.
