International Trade and Diplomacy

Tensions between the United States and the European Union have increased over tariffs related to Greenland. US President Donald Trump has stated that he will “100%” carry out his threat to impose tariffs, prompting a firm response from the EU’s foreign policy chief, Kaja Kallas. She said the EU does not want to pick a fight but will stand its ground to protect its interests. Trump’s former chief economic adviser downplayed the situation, describing the President’s actions as a negotiating tactic rather than a final stance.

Meanwhile, analysis from Europe highlights that transatlantic relations, while tested and damaged, are not broken. The European approach is shifting from a softer diplomacy to a more united and resolute front to communicate effectively with the Trump administration.

Transportation Tragedies

Spain is in mourning following a catastrophic high-speed train collision that occurred in the southern part of the country on Sunday. At least 40 people died in what has been described as the worst train crash in Spain in more than ten years. The Spanish government has declared three days of national mourning to honor the victims.

In the United States, heavy snow in Michigan’s Ottawa County led to a massive crash involving more than 100 vehicles, including jack-knifed trucks scattered across the motorway. Such incidents highlight the ongoing challenges of winter weather for transportation safety.

Environmental and Wildlife Incidents

Australia’s waterways have recently been the site of concern after four shark attacks in 48 hours, including one injury to a surfer. Authorities are urging the public to avoid entering the water, especially following recent rainfall, which may affect shark movement and behavior.

In a lighter story from San Francisco, the city bid farewell to Claude, its beloved albino alligator. Claude garnered millions of admirers for his unique albinism, and to honor him, California has named a street after the reptile, marking a fond remembrance within the local community.

Cultural Heritage

Nigeria has marked the passing of Kasali Akangbe Ogun, a master wood carver from a family tradition dedicated to preserving local carving techniques. Following a brief illness, Ogun was laid to rest, and his death underscores the importance of cultural preservation and the continuation of craftsmanship in the region.
