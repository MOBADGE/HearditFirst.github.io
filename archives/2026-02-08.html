Heard It First – Daily Digest

February 08, 2026

Asia Election Results

In Japan, the ruling coalition led by Prime Minister Takaichi is expected to win a snap election by a large margin. They are projected to secure two-thirds of the seats in the House of Representatives, solidifying their parliamentary dominance. Meanwhile, in Thailand, the conservative ruling party has claimed an unexpected victory. Despite polls forecasting a win for the reformist People's Party, the conservatives came out well ahead of their rivals.

Legal and Crime Updates

Russia has named three suspects in the shooting of a general. The alleged gunman, Lyubomir Korba, reportedly fled to the United Arab Emirates after the attack. In France, a murder trial faces complications because twin brothers share the same DNA. DNA evidence recovered from a weapon cannot conclusively identify which brother was involved in the crime. Additionally, in New Zealand, Brenton Tarrant, who is serving a life sentence for the 2019 mosque shootings, is seeking to overturn his guilty plea.

Human Rights News

Narges Mohammadi, an Iranian Nobel laureate, has been given an additional prison sentence. She was arrested in December after making what officials called "provocative remarks" at a memorial event, where she was reportedly beaten. Her sentencing highlights ongoing issues surrounding political expression and human rights in Iran.

Sports

American skier Lindsey Vonn suffered a crash during the women's downhill competition in Cortina. Competing with a torn ACL, Vonn fractured her left leg and has since undergone surgery. This injury casts uncertainty on her future in skiing, especially as it may have occurred during what could be her final Olympic event.

Other News

In Portugal, António José Seguro, a centre-left candidate, is on track for a strong presidential victory. With most votes counted, he is set to defeat far-right rival André Ventura by a wide margin. In the United States, news anchor Savannah Guthrie has made a public plea for the safe return of her 84-year-old mother, Nancy Guthrie, who disappeared from her home in Tucson, Arizona, last weekend. Finally, influential Ghanaian musician Ebo Taylor has died at the age of 90. Taylor was known for blending Ghanaian music with funk, creating works that have been sampled globally.
