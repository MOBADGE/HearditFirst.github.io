Heard It First – Daily Digest

February 08, 2026

Global Politics

In Japan, the ruling coalition led by Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi is on track for a major election victory, according to an exit poll. The anticipated landslide win could solidify the coalition’s position and influence Japan’s political direction in the near future.

Meanwhile, Thailand is holding elections after a period of political instability caused by the collapse of several coalition governments. Early results indicate conservatives are leading, and Prime Minister Prayut Chan-o-cha has claimed victory. The election outcome will shape whether the country pursues significant changes or maintains its current course.

Crime and Investigation

Russian authorities have identified three suspects connected to the shooting of a general. Among them is Lyubomir Korba, who reportedly fled to the United Arab Emirates after the attack. Details about motives or further leads have not been disclosed.

In South Africa, Wiandre Pretorius, a figure linked to a police corruption investigation, was found dead. He had survived an assassination attempt the previous week but reportedly died by a self-inflicted gunshot wound. The case adds to ongoing concerns about corruption and violence within law enforcement.

In France, a murder trial faces complications due to the involvement of twin brothers who share identical DNA. Police have retrieved DNA from a weapon, but cannot determine which twin was responsible for the crime, posing challenges for the legal process.

Missing Person and Public Appeals

In the United States, news anchor Savannah Guthrie has made a public appeal for help in finding her mother, Nancy Guthrie, 84, who disappeared from her home in Tucson, Arizona, during the night last weekend. She emphasized a willingness to pay for information leading to her mother's safe return.

Sports

American skier Lindsey Vonn suffered a crash during the women's downhill event in Cortina while competing with a torn ACL. The incident occurred in what may be her final Olympic event, marking a difficult and emotional moment for the celebrated athlete.

Culture and Society

Ghana has lost a significant cultural figure with the death of Ebo Taylor at age 90. Taylor was a guitarist, composer, and bandleader known for blending Ghanaian music traditions with funk, influencing musicians worldwide with his work.

In the United States, Brad Arnold, the lead singer of rock band 3 Doors Down, died at age 47. He had revealed a diagnosis of stage 4 cancer in May 2025. His passing marks a loss for the music community and fans of the band.

Public Order

In Italy, authorities arrested at least six protesters in Milan following clashes during demonstrations. These events come a week after violence during a different protest in Turin, highlighting continued civil unrest and law enforcement challenges in the lead-up to the upcoming Olympic Games.
