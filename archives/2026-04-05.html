Animal Hero Honored in Cambodia

Cambodia has unveiled a statue to honor a remarkable African giant pouched rat known for its work sniffing out landmines. This rat was credited with detecting more than 100 landmines over its lifetime, helping to make areas safer for people. The statue serves as a tribute to the animal’s vital role in humanitarian efforts and raises awareness about ongoing dangers from unexploded mines in the region.

Small Town Rejects Detention Center Plan

In the United States, a small farming town has rejected plans by Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) to build a new detention center locally. While residents support the national immigration policies of the current administration, they expressed strong opposition to hosting a detention facility in their community. The decision reflects concerns about the impact such a center could have on the town’s character and resources. This setback could influence ICE’s future strategies for expanding detention infrastructure.
