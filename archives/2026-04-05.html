US Airman Rescued from Iran

A US airman who went missing after the F-15 fighter jet he was aboard was shot down over southern Iran has been located and rescued. The recovery was confirmed by former US President Donald Trump. Both the United States and Iran had conducted separate search efforts to find the missing crew member. The airman sustained injuries but is reported to be safe following the rescue. This development ends a tense search operation and may influence ongoing US-Iran relations.

Pope Leo XIV's First Easter Message

Pope Leo XIV delivered his first Easter Sunday address to thousands of worshippers gathered in St Peter's Square. In his message, he called on global leaders to commit to peace. This public appeal highlights the Pope's focus on international harmony and sets the tone for his leadership moving forward. His message comes at a time of various global tensions and could be a rallying call for diplomatic efforts worldwide.

Cambodia Honors Heroic Landmine-Sniffing Rat

Cambodia has unveiled a statue to honor a famous African giant pouched rat known for sniffing out more than 100 landmines during its lifetime. The statue recognizes the critical role this rat played in making affected areas safer for local communities. The tribute also highlights broader efforts to address the dangers of landmines in regions affected by past conflicts.

Community Pushes Back on Detention Centre Plan

In a small farming town, residents have expressed strong opposition to plans by the U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) agency to build a detention centre nearby. While the community supports the broader immigration agenda of the current administration, they do not want the facility in their local area. This local resistance showcases the challenges government agencies face when proposing infrastructure projects that impact small communities directly.
