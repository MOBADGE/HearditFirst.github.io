Royal Family Statement

Norway’s Crown Princess Mette Marit has publicly spoken out about her connection to the late sex offender Jeffrey Epstein. In an interview with Norwegian TV, she said she felt "manipulated and deceived" by Epstein and expressed regret, saying she wished she had never met him. This marks a rare public acknowledgement from the royal family regarding the personal impact of the Epstein scandal.

Political and Legal Developments

In Tunisia, Saadia Mosbah, a leading anti-racism activist who advocated for migrants, has been sentenced to eight years in prison. Her activism intensified following statements by President Kais Saied labeling migrants as a demographic threat. This development has significant implications for human rights and migrant protections in the country.

Meanwhile, Hungarian leader Viktor Orbán is facing accusations of disloyalty and blackmail over his recent veto of a loan intended for Ukraine. Orbán stated that funds would only be released once a pipeline carrying Russian oil through Ukraine to Hungary is repaired. This dispute highlights ongoing political disagreements within Europe over support for Ukraine during the conflict with Russia.
