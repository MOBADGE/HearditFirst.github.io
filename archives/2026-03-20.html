Middle East Tensions and Military Strikes

New analysis reveals that Iranian strikes on military bases used by the US caused approximately $800 million in damage. These largely followed early retaliatory actions by Iran after a joint US-Israeli operation. Meanwhile, Israel conducted air strikes on Syrian weapons sites and government infrastructure, which Syria condemned as a violation of its sovereignty. Adding to regional tensions, former US president Donald Trump compared US strikes on Iran to Japan’s surprise attack on Pearl Harbor in 1941 during a meeting with Japan’s prime minister.

Changes in International Oil Markets

The US Treasury has lifted sanctions on some Iranian oil, a move expected to introduce around 140 million additional barrels to global markets amid rising gas prices. This change could help ease some energy cost pressures worldwide. At the same time, a disagreement between Ukraine and Hungary over the extent of damage to an oil hub—a site bombed by Russia—has halted oil flow from the region and threatens an EU loan. The dispute centers on differing assessments of the damage's severity.

Legal and Political Developments

She had campaigned for migrants’ rights after statements by President Kais Saied suggesting migrants posed a demographic threat.

This rise in prejudice has affected the community during an important religious period.
