March 13, 2026

Military and Security Incidents

A US refuelling aircraft crashed over western Iraq, killing all six crew members on board, the US Central Command confirmed. Meanwhile, Kurdish fighters based in Iraq reported coming under drone attacks from Tehran as they prepare to return to confront the Iranian regime. In Lebanon, an airstrike has killed a family member of a suspect involved in a synagogue attack in Michigan. The FBI revealed the suspect had large quantities of fireworks and petrol in his vehicle at the time of the incident.

Ukraine and International Sanctions

Ukraine’s president criticized the US decision to ease sanctions on Russia, stating it does not help efforts to achieve peace. Ukrainian officials and their allies worry that reducing sanctions might prolong the conflict between the two countries.

China’s Political Landscape

Chinese leader Xi Jinping’s anti-corruption campaign, now 14 years old, continues with ongoing purges within the military. Critics argue that these actions are part of Xi’s broader strategy to enforce absolute loyalty and tighten control over the military forces.

Middle East Conflict and Sport

The ongoing war in the Middle East has led to the planned cancellation of the Bahrain and Saudi Arabia Grands Prix scheduled for next month. The decision underscores the regional instability impacting international events.

Cuba-US Relations

Cuba’s president publicly confirmed negotiations with the United States amid the country’s struggle with an oil blockade. As part of a goodwill gesture, Cuba also announced the release of 51 prisoners. These developments suggest potential easing of tensions between the two nations.

Social and Cultural News

In India, the Supreme Court rejected a petition supporting menstrual leave for women, a subject with divided opinions nationally. Though some Indian states and private companies already offer such leave, the court expressed concerns about women facing discrimination in employment.

In music news, a guitar used by David Gilmour on Pink Floyd’s iconic album "Dark Side of the Moon" sold for a record-breaking $14.6 million, reflecting the lasting cultural impact of the band.
