Heard It First – Daily Digest

March 13, 2026

Military and Security Incidents

A US Air Force refuelling plane crashed over western Iraq, resulting in the deaths of all six crew members onboard, according to the US Central Command. Meanwhile, in Iraq, Kurdish fighters who have been exiled say they face ongoing drone attacks from Iran and are preparing to confront the Iranian regime. In another military-related development, the bodies of 84 Iranian sailors killed on 4 March when the Iranian warship Iris Dena was sunk by a US submarine near Sri Lanka are to be repatriated to Iran.

Ukraine and International Relations

Ukraine's president expressed concern over the United States’ decision to ease sanctions on Russia, saying the move "certainly does not help achieve peace." This reflects broader anxiety among Ukraine and its allies that reducing sanctions could prolong the ongoing conflict with Russia. The situation underscores the fragile nature of diplomatic and economic policies aimed at ending the war.

Regional Conflicts and Events

Tensions in the Middle East continue to affect global events beyond the battlefield. The Bahrain and Saudi Arabia Grands Prix, scheduled for next month, have been cancelled due to the ongoing war in the region. This cancellation highlights how conflict can disrupt major international sporting events and has economic and cultural implications for the host countries and audiences worldwide.

Domestic Security and Legal Issues

In the United States, the FBI is investigating an attack at a synagogue in Michigan as a "targeted act of violence against the Jewish community." The governor of Michigan described the attack as "hate, plain and simple," though a clear motive has not yet been established. Separately, the sheriff involved in the case of Nancy Guthrie, who was kidnapped 41 days ago and is the mother of TV presenter Savannah Guthrie, indicated that investigators believe they understand the kidnapper’s motive.

Social and Cultural Developments

India’s highest court has rejected a petition seeking menstrual leave for women, a subject that has been highly debated within the country. While some states and private companies already offer this leave, the court’s decision highlights ongoing divisions about workplace policies related to women’s health.

International Diplomacy and Humanitarian Gestures

Cuba’s president confirmed that negotiations with the United States are taking place amid the country’s challenges caused by an ongoing oil blockade. In a goodwill gesture connected to the talks, Cuba announced it will release 51 prisoners. This move could signal a potential easing of tensions and a step toward improved relations between the two countries.

Arts and Collectibles

In cultural news, a guitar used by David Gilmour of Pink Floyd sold for a record-breaking $14.6 million. The instrument was played on some of the band’s most famous albums, including "Dark Side of the Moon," highlighting the enduring legacy and value of iconic music memorabilia.
