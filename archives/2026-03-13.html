International Military Incidents

A US refuelling plane crashed over western Iraq, killing four crew members. The US Central Command confirmed that rescue efforts are ongoing in the region. This incident comes amid heightened tensions in the Gulf, where sailors have reported the frequent presence of drones, cruise missiles, and fighter jets, underscoring the volatile security situation at sea.

In a related development, the bodies of 84 Iranian sailors killed after their warship, the Iris Dena, was sunk on 4 March by a US submarine near Sri Lanka are set to be repatriated. The event has drawn attention to the risks faced by naval forces operating in contested waters.

Political Developments in France

France is holding local elections that serve as a test of the country’s deeply divided electorate. The most closely watched race is for the mayorship of Paris, which has been held by left-wing politicians for 25 years. The right-wing parties are hoping to gain control of the capital, which could shift the political landscape ahead of future national elections.

Cultural Repatriation Efforts

France has returned a sacred "talking drum" to Ivory Coast. This action is part of a broader initiative that began in 2017 to return cultural artefacts taken during colonial rule to African nations. These moves aim to address historical injustices and improve cultural ties between France and former colonies.

Legal and Social Issues in India

India’s top court has rejected a petition seeking menstrual leave for women. The debate over menstrual leave has been ongoing in the country, with some states and private companies already offering such benefits. Opponents argue that granting menstrual leave could affect women’s employment opportunities, reflecting the complex social attitudes toward gender and work policies.

Miscellaneous News

In the United States, the FBI is investigating a vehicle ramming incident at a synagogue in Michigan as a targeted act of violence against the Jewish community. The suspect died at the scene.

Meanwhile, Qantas airline has agreed to pay $74 million in refunds related to Covid-19 travel voucher policies. Many customers received travel credits instead of cash for flights cancelled during the pandemic, leading to legal action over compensation practices.
