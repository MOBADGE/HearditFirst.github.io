March 13, 2026

Global Politics and Security

China's anti-corruption campaign under Xi Jinping continues, with millions of officials investigated so far. Critics argue the ongoing purges aim to secure absolute loyalty and control over the military, highlighting the leadership's focus on consolidating power.

In the Middle East, tensions remain high as Kurdish fighters exiled in Iraq face drone attacks from Tehran. These fighters say they are ready to return home despite the risks, signaling ongoing conflict and instability in the region. Meanwhile, Bahrain and Saudi Arabia have cancelled their Formula 1 Grands Prix scheduled for next month due to the war in the Middle East, showing how regional conflicts are impacting international events.

Military Incidents

A US refuelling aircraft crashed over western Iraq, resulting in the deaths of all six crew members on board, according to the US Central Command. Details about the cause remain unclear but the loss marks another tragic event amid complex military operations in the region.

Crime and Justice

In the United States, authorities continue investigating the kidnapping of Nancy Guthrie, the 84-year-old mother of TV presenter Savannah Guthrie. The incident, which occurred 41 days ago, remains unresolved, but the local sheriff says there are insights into the kidnapper’s motive.

Separately, the FBI reported that the suspect in the recent Michigan synagogue attack had large amounts of fireworks and petrol in his vehicle, which caught fire after he rammed the building. The suspect’s family was reportedly killed in a Lebanese airstrike, adding a tragic dimension to the case.

International Relations

Cuba has confirmed diplomatic talks with the United States amid challenges caused by an ongoing oil blockade. During a national broadcast, Cuba’s president also announced the release of 51 prisoners as a gesture of goodwill, signaling a potential thaw in relations.

Meanwhile, Ukraine's president criticized recent US decisions to ease sanctions on Russia, stating that they "certainly do not help achieve peace." This reflects concerns among Ukraine and its allies that loosening sanctions may prolong the ongoing conflict with Russia.

Society and Culture

India’s Supreme Court rejected a petition that sought to introduce menstrual leave, a controversial topic for many years. While some Indian states and private companies currently offer such leave, the court expressed concern that official menstrual leave policies might negatively impact women's employment opportunities.

In lighter news, a guitar played by Pink Floyd’s David Gilmour sold at auction for a record-breaking $14.6 million. The instrument was used on some of the band's most famous albums, including "The Dark Side of the Moon," underscoring its cultural and historic significance.
