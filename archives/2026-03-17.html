Iran's Leadership Faces Uncertainty After Larijani's Death

Ali Larijani, a highly experienced and influential figure in Iran's political landscape, has died. Larijani was regarded as a key policymaker within the Islamic Republic. His passing deepens an ongoing crisis at the core of Iran's leadership, raising questions about future political stability and the direction of policy decisions in the country.

Tragic Air Strike on Kabul Rehabilitation Centre

A recent air strike, reportedly carried out by Pakistan, hit a drug rehabilitation centre in Kabul while patients were having dinner. The exact death toll is unclear but is feared to be in the hundreds. Forensic sources indicated that some bodies were badly damaged, making identification difficult. This incident marks a significant humanitarian concern and may increase tensions in the region.

Chile Begins Construction of Border Barrier

Chile’s new president, José Antonio Kast, has initiated the building of a border barrier shortly after assuming office. The barrier includes a ditch described as a "milestone" by Kast. This move signals the administration’s approach to managing border security early in its term, potentially impacting migration and border policies in the region.

Italy Warns of Danger from Drifting Russian Tanker

Italy, along with eight other EU countries, has called on the European Commission to act regarding the Russian tanker Arctic Metagaz, which is drifting uncontrollably in the Mediterranean Sea. Authorities warn that the tanker poses a significant risk of explosion, raising safety and environmental concerns for Mediterranean countries.

Water Supply Cut in Moldova Following Oil Spill

Moldova has cut its water supply and declared a 15-day state of alert in the Dniester river basin after an oil spill was reportedly caused by a Russian strike in Ukraine. The measure is a preventive step to protect residents and manage the environmental impact. This event highlights the spillover effects of military conflicts on neighboring countries' infrastructure and resources.

US Attorney General Summoned to Congress in Epstein Case

The US Attorney General, Bondi, has been formally summoned to testify before Congress regarding the Jeffrey Epstein case. This summons follows accusations from a Republican lawmaker alleging a "cover-up" by the Department of Justice related to the release of Epstein files. This development continues to draw attention to ongoing investigations and oversight related to the case.
