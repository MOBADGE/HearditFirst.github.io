March 17, 2026

Global Security and Conflict

Joe Kent, the director of the U.S. National Counterterrorism Center, resigned in protest over the ongoing war with Iran. He criticized President Donald Trump for starting the conflict, attributing it to pressure from Israel and its lobbying groups in America. Kent urged Trump to “reverse course,” signaling significant dissent within U.S. counterterrorism leadership about the war’s origins and handling.

Meanwhile, violence continues to escalate in Afghanistan. An air strike by Pakistan hit a drug rehabilitation center in Kabul during dinner, killing at least 100 people according to forensic sources. The death toll remains uncertain, with some bodies badly disfigured. This attack has caused considerable distress and raised further concerns about civilian casualties in the region.

Middle East Tensions

Iran suffered another blow as Ali Larijani, a senior security official, was killed in an air strike confirmed by state television. Another high-ranking figure, Gholamreza Soleimani, was also reported dead in the same strike. These deaths may impact Iran’s security dynamics and regional stability, potentially escalating tensions in the Middle East.

European and Latin American Affairs

In Europe, Italy has expressed growing alarm over the Arctic Metagaz, a drifting Russian tanker in the Mediterranean Sea that poses a risk of explosion at any moment. Italy, along with eight other European Union countries, is pressing the European Commission to take urgent action to prevent possible environmental disaster.

In Latin America, Chile’s newly inaugurated president, José Antonio Kast, has begun constructing a border barrier shortly after taking office. The barrier, described as a ditch, marks a significant step in his administration’s approach to border control and immigration policies.

In Mexico, King Felipe of Spain has reignited historic debate by acknowledging abuses during the conquest of Mexico 500 years ago. His remarks have helped ease some tensions in Spain-Mexico relations, but also reopened discussions about the long-term impact of colonialism.

U.S. Legal and Political Developments

U.S. Attorney General Bondi has been formally summoned to appear before Congress in connection with the Jeffrey Epstein case. The summons follows allegations from a Republican lawmaker accusing the Justice Department of attempting to cover up information related to the case. This development may lead to increased congressional scrutiny of the department’s handling of sensitive files.

Environmental and Humanitarian Concerns

Moldova has cut water supplies as a precaution following an oil spill in the Dniester river basin, which officials blame on a Russian strike in Ukraine. To respond to the contamination threat, the country has declared a 15-day state of alert in the affected area. This situation underscores the growing environmental risks tied to the conflict in Ukraine.

Crime and Justice

In Utah, Kouri Richins was found guilty of poisoning her husband with a fentanyl-laced drink in March 2022. Richins had previously written a book about grief, and the case has attracted attention due to its tragic and unusual circumstances. The verdict closes one chapter of this criminal case but highlights ongoing issues related to drug-related poisonings.
