March 17, 2026

International Tensions and Conflict

Tensions remain high in the Middle East following recent developments. Israel has conducted an air strike that it says killed Ali Larijani, an Iranian security chief, along with the head of Iran's paramilitary Basij force. However, these deaths have not yet been officially confirmed by Iranian authorities. Meanwhile, the United States faces internal friction as Joe Kent, the director of the National Counterterrorism Center, resigned over the current conflict with Iran. Kent criticized former President Trump, accusing him of starting the war due to pressure from Israel and its American lobby, and urged a reversal of this course.

Regional Violence in South Asia

A deadly air strike in Kabul, Afghanistan, killed at least 100 people at a drug rehabilitation centre. Forensic reports indicate some victims were injured beyond recognition, emphasizing the attack's severity. The incident adds to ongoing instability in the region and raises concerns about civilian casualties amid military actions.

Domestic Developments in the United States

In Utah, a jury found Kouri Richins guilty of poisoning her husband using a fentanyl-laced drink in March 2022. Richins had previously written a book about grief, making the case particularly notable. Separately, severe weather is impacting large parts of the United States. The west coast is preparing for an early spring heatwave described as "dangerous," while other regions face extreme temperature swings that include bitter cold.

Environmental Concerns in Eastern Europe

Moldova has cut its water supply and declared a 15-day state of alert in the Dniester river basin after an oil spill linked to a Russian strike in Ukraine. This precautionary measure highlights the ongoing environmental risks posed by the regional conflict and its effects on neighbouring countries’ resources.

Politics and Social Issues

In Zambia, reports have emerged that aspiring female politicians were asked for sexual favors, which activists warn could set back progress in closing the country's gender representation gap. In Chile, newly inaugurated President José Antonio Kast began construction of a border barrier within his first week in office, calling it a "milestone" in addressing border issues.

In U.S. politics, former President Trump drew criticism from the National Center for Learning Disabilities after mocking California Governor Gavin Newsom's dyslexia. Trump said presidents "should not have learning disabilities," remarks that were described as disturbing by disability advocates.

Culture and Entertainment

On a lighter note, actor Michael B Jordan celebrated his recent Oscar win for Best Actor by joining a select group of celebrities who commemorate their victories with an In-N-Out burger, highlighting a popular tradition in the entertainment world.
