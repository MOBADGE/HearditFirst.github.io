March 17, 2026

International Conflicts and Security

Tensions remain high in several regions around the world. In northeast Nigeria, at least 23 people were killed in multiple suspected suicide bombings on Monday evening in Maiduguri, one of the city's busiest areas. Meanwhile, an airstrike in Kabul, Afghanistan, on a rehabilitation centre resulted in at least 100 deaths. Forensic sources indicate some of the victims were severely disfigured. Pakistan has been blamed for this strike, though details remain emerging.

The escalating war in Iran has also complicated U.S. foreign policy efforts. Former U.S. President Donald Trump is seeking to delay a planned meeting with Chinese leader Xi Jinping amidst these tensions, signaling the impact of the conflict on diplomatic engagements.

Domestic News: Legal Cases and Social Issues

In Utah, Kouri Richins was found guilty of poisoning her husband in March 2022 with a fentanyl-laced drink, according to a jury verdict. This case has attracted attention due to the nature of the crime and the use of a potent opioid in the poisoning.

In immigration-related news, Leqaa Kordia, a protester from Columbia University, was released after spending a year in immigration custody. Officials report she overstayed her student visa, while her lawyer contends she was targeted because of her pro-Palestinian activism.

Remarks on Learning Disabilities

Donald Trump sparked controversy after making comments that presidents "should not have learning disabilities," while mocking California Governor Gavin Newsom’s dyslexia. The National Center for Learning Disabilities responded, stating they were "disturbed" by his remarks. These comments have reignited discussions about disability stigma and leadership qualifications.

Energy and Environmental Measures in Asia and the Caribbean

Several countries are adopting new measures to cope with fuel shortages. Sri Lanka announced it will observe Wednesdays off as part of ongoing efforts to conserve fuel, a step seen alongside similar actions by other Asian countries heavily reliant on Gulf oil.

Cuba continues to experience severe energy issues, with millions of residents currently without electricity after the island’s power grid collapsed. Chronic fuel shortages, worsened by a U.S. blockade restricting oil shipments, remain at the root of the crisis.

Technology and Innovation

The artificial intelligence company Anthropic is actively seeking to hire a weapons expert. The firm’s goal is to prevent the "catastrophic misuse" of its AI systems, highlighting ongoing concerns about the safety and ethical use of increasingly powerful technology.

Cultural Moments

On a lighter note, actor Michael B. Jordan celebrated his Best Actor Oscar win with a visit to In-N-Out burger, joining a unique tradition among some celebrities who mark their Academy Award victories with a fast-food meal.
