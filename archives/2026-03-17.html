March 17, 2026

International Security and Conflict

An air strike by Pakistan on a drug rehabilitation centre in Kabul has killed at least 100 people. Forensic sources reported that some bodies were severely mutilated, highlighting the strike's devastating impact. In north-east Nigeria, suspected suicide bombings struck busy areas of Maiduguri, resulting in at least 23 deaths. These attacks underline ongoing instability and violence in both regions.

Meanwhile, the war in Iran continues to dominate global attention and is reportedly overshadowing other US foreign policy goals. Against this backdrop, former US President Donald Trump has requested a delay in his planned meeting with Xi Jinping in China, reflecting shifting diplomatic priorities amid the conflict’s escalation.

Political Remarks and Reactions

Donald Trump drew criticism for comments about learning disabilities during remarks targeting Gavin Newsom, who has dyslexia. Trump stated that presidents "should not have learning disabilities." The National Center for Learning Disabilities expressed that it was "disturbed" by these remarks, highlighting concerns about stigma toward disabilities in public discourse.

Human Rights and Immigration

Leqaa Kordia, a protester from Columbia University, was released after spending a year in immigration custody. Federal officials indicated she had overstayed her student visa, but her lawyer argued that her detention was linked to her pro-Palestinian activism. This case brings attention to immigration enforcement and political activism intersecting in the United States.

Energy and Infrastructure

Sri Lanka announced it will observe Wednesdays as a day off to conserve fuel, part of a broader trend in Asian countries that depend heavily on Gulf oil. This follows several energy conservation measures responding to tightening fuel supplies. In Cuba, millions remain without electricity following a collapse of the power grid. Chronic fuel shortages, worsened by a US blockade restricting oil shipments, have undermined Cuba’s energy infrastructure and daily life.

Technology and Safety

The artificial intelligence company Anthropic is seeking to hire a weapons expert to help prevent “catastrophic misuse” of its AI systems. This move signals growing concerns in the tech industry about managing the risks associated with advanced AI technologies and ensuring they are not used for harmful purposes.

Culture

In lighter news, actor Michael B Jordan celebrated his recent Oscar win for Best Actor by enjoying a burger at In-N-Out, joining a select group of celebrities who mark their Academy Award victories with the popular fast-food meal.
