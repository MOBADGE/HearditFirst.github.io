February 05, 2026

International Relations and Security

Chinese leader Xi Jinping spoke with Donald Trump, stressing that the US must be cautious when supplying arms to Taiwan. This discussion occurs amid a recent series of visits by Western leaders to China, signaling complex diplomatic sensitivities in the region. Meanwhile, the New Start nuclear arms reduction treaty between the US and Russia has expired. This treaty had capped the number of strategic nuclear warheads both nations could deploy, raising concerns about a potential new arms race.

US Efforts on Global Minerals and Military Deployment

The US has launched an initiative to address China's dominance over critical minerals essential to various industries and technologies. Representatives from over 50 countries attended the event, highlighting the international scope of the effort. In Nigeria, escalating violence was reported as gunmen killed dozens in attacks on local Muslims who refused to join a jihadist group. The US military has confirmed a deployment to Nigeria in response to the security situation.

Domestic Incidents in Australia

In Australia, a man has been charged with terrorism following an attack involving a homemade bomb thrown at a protest crowd rallying on Australia Day. The attack targeted Aboriginal Australians. Separately, the suspect in the disappearance of four-year-old Gus Lamont in the remote Australian outback has been identified. Gus was last seen nearly five months ago outside his home on a sheep station.

Healthcare and Social Issues

A coroner reported that the doctor who treated Joel Cauchi—the individual responsible for the Bondi Junction mass killing—failed to act on warnings that Cauchi was relapsing into schizophrenia. The doctor has been referred to the health regulator for further review. Additionally, a hospital in Gaza is struggling to provide care for newborn babies due to insufficient aid, despite some increase in supplies since a ceasefire began three months ago. The UN has said that the aid remains far below what is needed.

Personal and Human Interest Stories

US news anchor Savannah Guthrie made a public plea for the return of her 84-year-old mother, Nancy Guthrie, who was abducted and is currently without vital medication needed for her survival. This appeal has drawn attention to personal security and humanitarian concerns amidst broader global issues.
