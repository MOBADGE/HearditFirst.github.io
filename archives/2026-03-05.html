Maritime Incident Near Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka's navy reports that around 140 people are missing after a military vessel sank off the southern coast of the country. The incident involves an Iranian warship, and a US submarine is said to have been involved in sinking the vessel in the Indian Ocean. The situation is developing, and rescue efforts are likely underway as authorities try to account for those missing.

China's Economic Growth Target Lowered

China has set its lowest economic growth target since 1991, marking a further slowdown in its economic ambitions. This new target is the first reduction since it was lowered to "around 5%" in 2023. The decision reflects ongoing challenges in the country's economic environment and signals cautious expectations for growth in the year ahead.

US Trade Court Orders Tariff Refunds

A US trade court has enabled businesses to receive refunds related to tariffs that the Supreme Court struck down last month. This ruling represents a setback for the Trump administration's tariff policies. It may affect companies that had paid these tariffs and could lead to broader implications for US trade policy and relations with trading partners.

US Soldiers Killed in Middle East Conflict

The Pentagon has identified six US soldiers who died after a drone strike targeted a command center in Port Shuaiba, Kuwait. The drone managed to evade air defenses before hitting the facility on Sunday. This incident highlights ongoing tensions and the dangers faced by military personnel in the region.

Conditions in Venezuelan Prisons

Recently released prisoners in Venezuela have shared details about the conditions inside the country's detention centers. They describe harsh treatment and an elaborate system for smuggling messages out of jail, using items like dirty laundry and chocolate bars. These accounts shed light on the challenges faced by inmates and the broader issues within the Venezuelan prison system.

Ongoing Conflict in Sudan

The conflict in Sudan continues with both sides having reasons to persist in fighting. A steady supply of weapons and a history of prolonged wars contribute to the difficulty in resolving the violence. This ongoing instability undermines prospects for peace and affects the lives of many people in the region.
