Space Exploration

The Artemis II crew is preparing to return to Earth following their mission to the Moon. The four astronauts said they are bringing back "all the good stuff" from their discoveries, including many new pictures and stories. Ahead of their expected splashdown on Saturday, the BBC's Science Editor spoke with the crew about their experiences. The mission marks another milestone in deep space exploration and sets the stage for future Artemis missions.

Mining and Environmental Policy

Argentina has passed a new bill that loosens protection of its glaciers. The legislation will make it easier for provincial governments to approve mining projects in glacier regions. This change raises concerns about the potential environmental impact on important natural reserves, as well as broader implications for water security and climate resilience in the region.

Human Interest and Rescue Efforts

A trapped gold miner was rescued from a flooded tunnel in Mexico after 14 days. The 42-year-old was found standing in waist-high water by rescuers, highlighting a successful but challenging rescue operation. This event underscores the risks faced by miners and the ongoing importance of emergency response capabilities in hazardous working conditions.

Notable Death

Doug Allan, an award-winning wildlife cameraman and photographer, has died in Nepal. Known for his work on acclaimed nature series such as Blue Planet alongside Sir David Attenborough, Allan won eight Emmy Awards. His pioneering contributions to wildlife filmmaking have left a lasting legacy in environmental storytelling and conservation awareness.

Energy and Economy

Petrol and diesel prices have risen again, with motoring groups warning drivers there is unlikely to be a significant drop in costs in the near future. This increase comes amid growing concerns over ceasefire prospects in conflict areas affecting global fuel markets. Rising fuel prices may impact household budgets and transportation costs going forward.
