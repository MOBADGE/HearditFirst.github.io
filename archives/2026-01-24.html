Deadly Natural Disasters in Indonesia and New Zealand

Tragic landslides have struck Southeast Asia and the Pacific. In Indonesia's West Java village, seven people have died, and rescue teams continue searching for 83 missing individuals after the landslide. Meanwhile, in New Zealand, rescue efforts for six missing persons following a landslide have been called off as police do not expect to find survivors. These disasters underline the ongoing risks of natural hazards in vulnerable regions and the challenges faced in recovery operations.

This reflects growing community resistance to immigration enforcement actions and highlights the social tensions involved.
