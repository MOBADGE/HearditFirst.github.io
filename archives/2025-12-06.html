Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 06, 2025

Conflict and Violence

Russia carried out attacks on Ukraine, prompting condemnation from France's president, who announced a forthcoming meeting in London with leaders from Ukraine, Britain, and Germany to discuss the situation. Meanwhile, deadly violence erupted along the Pakistan-Afghanistan border, breaking a fragile ceasefire in place since October, with both countries blaming each other for starting the fighting.

In Sudan's South Kordofan region, drone strikes hit a town, resulting in at least 50 deaths, including in an attack reported at a kindergarten. South Africa also experienced tragedy when gunmen attacked a hostel near Pretoria, killing 11 people, among them a three-year-old child.

Humanitarian Concerns in Migration

A report highlights the dangers faced by single women migrating to Europe in search of better lives. Esther, one woman who fled abuse in Nigeria, recounted experiences of sexual violence during her journey, underlining the risks migrants encounter.

Natural Disasters

A wildfire swept through a coastal area north of Sydney, Australia, damaging multiple homes and forcing residents to confront significant losses. Those affected described losing everything as they battled the fast-moving fire.

Significant Events and Memorials

In the occupied West Bank, Bethlehem's public Christmas tree was lit for the first time in two years, marking a brief return to public festive celebrations that had been canceled due to the ongoing conflict in Gaza.

Health Policy Changes in the United States

A US health panel voted to end the recommendation for hepatitis B vaccination for all newborns. Former President Trump praised the decision and has called for further reviews and potential changes to childhood vaccination policies by the health department.

Notable Death

Frank Gehry, the acclaimed architect known for his distinctive use of jagged angles and industrial materials, passed away at the age of 96. His work left a significant impact on modern architecture worldwide.

Ongoing Recovery

Andrew Wolfe, a National Guardsman who was shot in Washington DC, remains in acute care with a head wound. His family reports he is slowly healing but will require further treatment in the coming weeks.
