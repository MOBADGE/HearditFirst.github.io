International Politics: Myanmar Election Criticized Amid Conflict

The recent general election in Myanmar, held under military-backed authorities, has been widely described as a "sham." Numerous political parties were banned from participating, and voting did not take place in approximately half of the country. The nation remains deeply affected by ongoing civil war and widespread fear, casting doubt on the election’s legitimacy and the future of democratic processes in Myanmar.

United States: Minneapolis Shooting of Nurse Sparks Protests

Alex Pretti, a 37-year-old intensive care nurse in Minneapolis, was fatally shot by federal agents in an incident that has stirred protests and public outcry. Pretti was known as an avid outdoorsman with a passion for mountain biking. Federal officials claim the shooting was in self-defense, while Pretti’s parents have strongly denied this, calling the official accounts "sickening lies." Footage obtained by bystanders captured moments leading up to the shooting. Demonstrations near the site saw protesters chanting Pretti’s name, highlighting tensions over federal law enforcement actions in the city.

Notable Death: Sir Mark Tully, Renowned BBC India Correspondent, Passes Away

Sir Mark Tully, famously regarded as the BBC’s "voice of India," has died at the age of 90. During a distinguished career spanning several decades, he reported on many key moments in India’s history, becoming a respected figure in journalism. His death marks the loss of a prominent voice in international news coverage concerning India.

Adventurous Feat: Alex Honnold Climbs Taiwanese Skyscraper Without Safety Equipment

Alex Honnold has achieved a notable climbing milestone by scaling a 101-floor skyscraper in Taiwan without using any safety gear. This feat marks the first time anyone has climbed the building alone without protection, underlining Honnold’s reputation as one of the most daring climbers in the world.

Social Media Controversy: Cosmetic Doctor Apologizes for Critiquing Singer’s Appearance

Dr. Zayn Khalid Majeed, a cosmetic doctor, has issued an apology after publicly criticizing singer Troye Sivan’s looks on TikTok. The singer responded critically to the doctor’s comments, and Majeed stated he aims to produce more positive content moving forward. This exchange has sparked conversations about online conduct and the impact of public criticism on social media.

Global Reflections: Challenges for Middle Powers in a Changing World Order

Analysts are observing that the global political landscape is shifting toward a pre-World War II order, bringing challenges for "middle powers"—countries with notable but not dominant global influence. Issues such as economic stagnation, growing inequality, and declining trust in democratic institutions pose serious hurdles. These factors contribute to a "rupture" in the post-WWII international system, suggesting that political figures like Donald Trump may reflect deeper systemic changes rather than isolated causes.
