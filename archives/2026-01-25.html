Global Order and Middle Powers

Experts note the world is gradually moving toward a new order reminiscent of the period before World War II. This shift is accompanied by growing economic stagnation and rising inequality, which have eroded trust in democratic institutions. Some analysts suggest that disruptive political figures, such as Donald Trump, may be symptoms rather than causes of this broader "rupture" from the post-World War II international system. This evolving landscape presents significant challenges, especially for "middle powers"—countries that are influential but not dominant—who must now navigate a more unstable global environment.
