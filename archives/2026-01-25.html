US Federal Agents’ Shooting of Alex Pretti in Minneapolis

This month, Alex Pretti, a 37-year-old nurse and US citizen, was fatally shot by federal agents in Minneapolis. Government officials stated the shooting happened in self-defense. However, Pretti’s parents have strongly rejected this account, calling the agents’ statements “sickening lies.” Public anger has been evident as protests have taken place near the shooting scene, with demonstrators chanting Pretti’s name. This incident marks the second fatal shooting by federal agents in the city within the same month. Video footage capturing moments around the shooting has circulated, bringing renewed attention and calls for clarity about the circumstances of Pretti's death.

Remarkable Climb by Alex Honnold in Taiwan

In a notable feat of urban climbing, Alex Honnold has become the first person to scale a 101-floor skyscraper in Taiwan without the use of safety gear. This solo ascent marks a significant achievement in the world of free climbing and has drawn attention for its combination of physical skill and daring. Honnold’s climb pushes the boundaries of what is possible in urban climbing and continues to inspire interest in extreme sports and adventure activities.
