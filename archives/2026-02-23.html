Violence in Mexico After Death of Drug Lord

Mexico has deployed thousands of soldiers to control a surge in violence following the death of a powerful drug cartel leader. The drug lord was killed after being captured by special forces, which has triggered unrest across multiple regions. The government’s response aims to stabilize affected areas and prevent further escalation of criminal activities.

Major Snowstorm Hits the US Northeast

A severe snowstorm has swept through the northeastern United States, breaking snowfall records and disrupting daily life. The storm forced the cancellation of more than 5,000 flights, affecting millions of travelers and causing widespread transportation delays. Recovery efforts are ongoing as communities work to clear roads and restore normal operations.

US Embassy in Beirut Partially Evacuated

The US State Department has ordered the partial evacuation of its embassy in Beirut, Lebanon. Non-essential staff were instructed to leave following a security review amid increasing tensions with Iran. This move highlights growing concerns about safety at the diplomatic post and may impact US diplomatic engagement in the region.

Political Changes in the Netherlands

Rob Jetten has become the youngest-ever prime minister of the Netherlands at age 38. He took office following a narrow victory in the recent October elections. His leadership marks a generational shift in Dutch politics, and his government faces the task of addressing pressing national issues.

Security Incident at Trump's Residence

At the time of the incident, Trump was in Washington, DC. Authorities continue to investigate the man's motives and how he gained access to the secure area.
