Middle East Ceasefire Brings Temporary Calm

A 10-day ceasefire between Israel and Lebanon has begun, providing a pause after weeks of conflict. Iran-backed Hezbollah has expressed support for the truce, and negotiations involving the US and Iran are ongoing. The ceasefire brings relief to Lebanon, where people are celebrating the halt in fighting. However, experts caution that maintaining this ceasefire could be challenging, as deeper obstacles to peace remain unresolved.

Oil Prices Drop Amidst Strait of Hormuz Opening

Oil prices saw a significant decline after Iran announced that the Strait of Hormuz, a critical shipping route, is fully open for commercial vessels for the duration of the ceasefire. Brent crude oil prices plunged by 10%, reflecting eased concerns about supply disruptions in the region. This development may influence global oil markets by stabilizing supply routes that are vital for international trade.
