US Suspends Afghan Immigration Requests After National Guard Shooting

The US government has paused immigration requests from Afghan nationals following a recent shooting involving the National Guard in Washington. The alleged shooter is an Afghan citizen who arrived in the US in September 2021. This development reflects heightened concerns over security amid ongoing immigration and resettlement efforts involving Afghans.

Tensions Persist in South Lebanon One Year After Ceasefire

A year after a ceasefire was established in southern Lebanon, peace remains elusive as Israel continues strikes in the region. Israeli forces say these attacks target Hezbollah's attempts to recover militarily from the previous conflict. The ongoing violence suggests that stability in the area remains fragile despite ceasefire efforts.

Pope Leo is making his first foreign visit six months into his papacy, traveling to Turkey. Military Seizes Power in Guinea-Bissau During Election Wait

The military in Guinea-Bissau has taken control of the government and detained the president just as the country awaited the results of a recent election held on Sunday. This takeover marks a serious setback for the West African nation’s political stability and democratic process, casting uncertainty over its immediate future.
