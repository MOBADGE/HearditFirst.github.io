November 27, 2025

Fire in Hong Kong Tower Blocks

A major fire has engulfed tower blocks in Hong Kong, resulting in at least 55 deaths and leaving hundreds more reported missing. Authorities have arrested three people in connection with the fire and launched a corruption investigation. This blaze is reported as the worst in Hong Kong in 70 years. The incident raises concerns about building safety and regulatory oversight in the city.

US Suspends Afghan Immigration Requests

The United States has suspended immigration requests for Afghans following a shooting involving the National Guard in Washington. The alleged gunman is an Afghan national who entered the US in September 2021. This suspension comes amidst increased scrutiny over immigration and security policies related to Afghan nationals.

France Reinstates Limited Military Service

France will bring back a form of military service next year with 3,000 volunteers signing up. President Emmanuel Macron emphasized that preparedness is crucial to avoiding danger, indicating a focus on national security. The renewed service aims to bolster the country’s defense capabilities while involving young volunteers in military training.

Ongoing Conflict in South Lebanon

A year after a ceasefire agreement, peace remains elusive in southern Lebanon. Israel continues to conduct strikes targeting Hezbollah, which it says is rebuilding its military capacity following recent conflict. The ongoing tensions highlight persistent instability in the region despite diplomatic efforts to maintain peace.

Venezuela Bans Six Major Airlines

Venezuela has banned six major airlines after the companies stopped operating flights to the country. This decision follows warnings from the US about heightened military activity, which likely contributed to the airlines' withdrawal. The ban reflects rising tensions between Venezuela and the US and could affect international travel and commerce involving Venezuela.

Soldiers Take Control in Guinea-Bissau

Soldiers in Guinea-Bissau have seized power and detained the president as the country awaited election results from a recent vote. The military takeover interrupts the political process at a critical time and introduces uncertainty about the nation’s future governance and stability.

Pope Leo’s First Foreign Visit to Turkey

Pope Leo has arrived in Turkey for his first foreign trip since becoming pope. His activities include meeting with leaders of various Christian traditions in Nicaea and celebrating Mass in Beirut. This visit is significant for fostering religious dialogue and strengthening relationships among different Christian communities.
