Nepal's Former Prime Minister Arrested Over Deadly Protest Crackdown

Nepal’s former prime minister, KP Sharma Oli, has been arrested in connection with the violent crackdown on protests last year. The unrest led to the ousting of Oli's government and resulted in over 70 deaths. The arrest marks a significant development in addressing accountability for the deadly events. It remains to be seen how this case will progress and what impact it may have on Nepal’s political landscape.

United Nations Votes on Addressing Historical Slavery

The United Nations has voted on a resolution recognizing slavery as "the gravest crime against humanity." The motion highlights demands from African and Caribbean nations seeking compensation from countries that benefited historically from slavery. However, arranging reparations is expected to be complex and politically sensitive. This vote reflects ongoing international discussions on historical injustices and their modern-day consequences.

Tiger Woods Charged with Driving Under Influence

Golfer Tiger Woods was arrested and charged with driving under the influence following a car accident in Florida. Woods, known for his record 15 major championship wins, was released on bail after the arrest. This incident adds to public scrutiny of the sports figure and raises questions about his legal situation in the coming weeks.

U.S. House to Vote on Department of Homeland Security Funding Amid Airport Delays

The U.S. House of Representatives is set to vote on funding for the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) as travel disruptions continue across the country. Passengers are facing long security lines at airports due to the ongoing funding impasse. The upcoming vote is unlikely to resolve the funding deadlock immediately, meaning travel delays and security issues may persist for some time.

BBC Russian Services Face New Challenges After 80 Years

BBC Russian has a long history of resilience, having endured decades of broadcast jamming during the Cold War. Now, its website is blocked, yet millions of readers continue to access its content. Despite these new restrictions, BBC Russian’s ability to reach audiences highlights ongoing efforts to provide independent news in restrictive environments.

Displacement Crisis in Lebanon

In Lebanon, more than one million people have been displaced from their homes, according to recent reports. BBC correspondent Hugo Bachega spoke with families in Beirut who described losing their homes amid the crisis. The situation underscores severe humanitarian challenges in Lebanon and raises concerns about the country’s stability and the well-being of its displaced population.
