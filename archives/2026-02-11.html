Cyclone Devastates Madagascar’s Port City

A powerful cyclone has killed at least 31 people as it swept through a key port area in Madagascar. The country's disaster management officials describe severe damage, including uprooted trees, downed power poles, and destruction to about ninety percent of roofs in the affected area. Roads remain inaccessible, complicating rescue and recovery efforts. The scale of the damage underscores the vulnerability of the region to natural disasters and the ongoing need for disaster preparedness and aid.
