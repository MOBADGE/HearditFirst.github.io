February 11, 2026

Violence in Canada

Nine people have died and 25 others were injured in a mass shooting that took place at a school and a home in a rural area of western Canada. Authorities are continuing investigations to learn more about the incident. This tragic event has deeply impacted the small community involved, highlighting ongoing concerns about public safety in rural regions.

Cyclone Devastates Madagascar

A powerful cyclone struck Madagascar, causing extensive damage in the port area. At least 20 people have lost their lives. The island's disaster management officials reported widespread destruction, including uprooted trees, downed power poles, and about 90% of roofs torn off in the affected region. Roads are currently inaccessible, complicating relief efforts and recovery operations in the aftermath.

Developments in International Politics

Israel's prime minister plans to meet with former U.S. President Donald Trump to discuss the critical stage of talks concerning Iran's nuclear program. The leader aims to advocate for a deal that not only addresses nuclear issues but also limits Iran’s ballistic missile capabilities and support for armed groups. This diplomatic engagement may shape the future of Middle Eastern security policies.

Conflict and Humanitarian Impact in Ukraine

In northeastern Ukraine near Kharkiv, Russian strikes resulted in the deaths of four people, including three toddlers and a 34-year-old man. The bodies were recovered from the rubble of a destroyed house, emphasizing the continued human cost of the ongoing conflict in the region.

Legal and Cultural Updates

Swiss bar owner Jacques Moretti has been questioned by lawyers investigating a New Year's Day fire in Crans-Montana that killed 41 people. The inquiry seeks to determine the circumstances leading to the disaster.

In the United States, lawmakers are calling for a member of the British Royal Family, Andrew, to provide testimony concerning police investigations into files linked to Jeffrey Epstein. This follows statements from the King pledging the Royal Family's support for the inquiries.

On the cultural front, pop singer Britney Spears sold the rights to her entire music catalogue to independent publisher Primary Wave for approximately $200 million. The deal was finalized on December 30 and represents a significant business move for the artist.

U.S. Airspace Reopens Near Texas Border

The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) has reopened the airspace over El Paso, Texas, after closing it for more than a week due to security concerns following a reported drone incursion. Flights have now resumed, signaling an easing of restrictions in the area.
