Croatia Reinstates Military Conscription

Croatia has resumed compulsory military service, with hundreds of teenagers reporting for duty. The training program will be comprehensive, covering traditional military skills alongside modern capabilities such as drone control, cyberwarfare techniques, and defensive countermeasures. This marks a significant development in Croatia’s defence policy, likely reflecting regional security considerations and a focus on preparing young people for contemporary military challenges.

Political Developments in Guinea

In Guinea, opposition leader Cellou Dalein Diallo has called for "direct resistance" following the dissolution of 40 political parties. Diallo accused the country's leader, who came to power through a junta and later became president, of attempting to eliminate political opposition. This political crackdown raises concerns about democratic governance and stability in Guinea, and it may lead to increased tensions or unrest.

Canada Heads to By-Elections

Canada is holding three by-elections that could allow the Liberal government to secure a majority in parliament, where they currently hold 169 seats—just three short of a majority. Winning these elections would strengthen the government’s ability to pass legislation and govern more decisively. Observers will be watching these contests closely as indicators of public support ahead of the next general election.

International Incident: US Embassy Explosion Suspect in Norway

Norwegian police have released images of a suspect linked to an explosion outside the US embassy in Oslo, which occurred on Sunday. Investigations suggest the blast was caused by an improvised explosive device. Authorities continue to investigate the incident, which raises security concerns for diplomatic missions and may influence counterterrorism efforts in the region.
