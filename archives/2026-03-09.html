Tragic Accident in US Classroom Prank

A US teacher, Jason Hughes, died in a car accident that followed a classroom prank involving toilet paper. This tragedy highlights the unforeseen consequences that can arise from seemingly harmless acts.

G7 Addresses Rising Oil Prices

Finance ministers from the G7 countries and representatives from the International Energy Agency (IEA) met to discuss measures to stabilize energy supplies amid oil prices exceeding $100 per barrel. The discussions reflect growing concerns over global energy security and economic stability. The outcome may influence future energy policies and market conditions worldwide.

Croatia Reintroduces Military Conscription

Croatia has reinstated conscription, with hundreds of teenagers reporting for duty. The training program encompasses a wide range of skills, including traditional military tactics, drone operations, and cyberwarfare techniques and countermeasures. This move signals Croatia's response to evolving security challenges and its focus on modernizing defence capabilities.

Political Tensions in Guinea

Opposition leader Cellou Dalein Diallo has called for 'direct resistance' after the dissolution of 40 political parties by the country's government. Diallo accused the president, a former junta leader, of attempting to eliminate political opposition. This development underscores ongoing political instability and challenges to democratic processes in Guinea.

Investigation Continues After Oslo Embassy Explosion

Norwegian police have released images of a suspect in connection with the explosion near the US embassy in Oslo on Sunday. The authorities have stated that the blast was caused by an improvised explosive device. The investigation is ongoing as security concerns remain high in the area.

International Concerns Over Iran Women's Football Team

Australia has been urged to protect the Iranian women's football team as they prepare to return home. The team has faced criticism from some in Iran for not showing the expected salute during the national anthem, with some labelling them "wartime traitors." There are fears for their safety given the domestic backlash against the players.
