Global Energy Concerns and G7 Response

Finance ministers from the G7 countries, along with the International Energy Agency (IEA), met to discuss strategies to stabilise oil prices, which have recently climbed above $100 per barrel. The group's focus is on taking “necessary measures” to support steady energy supplies amid ongoing market volatility. The outcome of these discussions could influence global energy costs and economic stability in the coming months.

Military Training Resumes in Croatia

Croatia has reinstated conscription, calling hundreds of teenagers to participate in military training programs. The training aims to equip recruits with a wide range of skills, from traditional military tactics to modern areas like drone operation, cyberwarfare, and countermeasures. This move reflects a broader focus on preparing for diverse security challenges in a changing geopolitical environment.

Political Developments in Canada and Guinea

In Canada, three by-elections have been announced by the Liberal government. Winning these elections could provide Prime Minister Carney’s party with a parliamentary majority, currently just out of reach with 169 seats. Meanwhile, in Guinea, opposition leader Cellou Dalein Diallo has called for “direct resistance” after the dissolution of 40 political parties by the country’s president, accusing the leader of eliminating political competition. These events indicate shifting political dynamics in both countries.

Concerns for their safety have increased amid such tensions.

Regional Conflict and Diplomatic Moves

This development adds context to the unfolding conflict in the region and may influence diplomatic relations between these countries.
