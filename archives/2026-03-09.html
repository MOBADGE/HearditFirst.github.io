March 09, 2026

International Security and Conflict

An incident in Oslo involving an explosion near the US embassy is under investigation by Norwegian police. Officials say the blast was caused by an improvised device. Meanwhile, tensions in the Middle East remain high as India revealed it had offered sanctuary to an Iranian ship shortly before the United States sank it. This occurred at the start of a war between the US, Israel, and Iran.

In Guinea, political instability continues as the opposition leader, Cellou Dalein Diallo, called for "direct resistance" after the ruling government dissolved 40 opposition parties. Diallo accused the country's leader, who transitioned from junta head to president, of removing political threats ahead of elections.

Energy and Economy

Finance ministers from G7 countries met with the International Energy Agency to discuss stabilising oil prices, which have recently risen above $100 a barrel. The ministers committed to taking "necessary measures" to ensure steady energy supplies. This move reflects concerns about the global impact of volatile energy prices on economies and consumers.

Politics and Elections

In Canada, Prime Minister Justin Carney has called three by-elections that could give his Liberal Party a majority in parliament. Currently, the government holds 169 seats, just three short of a majority. The upcoming elections will be an important test of Carney's mandate and may shape the government's legislative agenda.

Society and Safety

A tragic accident occurred after a school prank involving toilet paper went wrong, resulting in the death of US teacher Jason Hughes. His family has publicly stated they do not want the students involved to be prosecuted, highlighting the unintended consequences of what began as a practical joke.

Also, in New Zealand, two teenagers were arrested after accidentally donating a bag containing cannabis and cash to a charity shop; the knapsack was found by the staff, prompting the arrests.

Military and Conscription

Croatia has reinstated conscription, with hundreds of teenagers reporting for duty. The training program will cover traditional military skills as well as modern areas such as drone operation, cyberwarfare techniques, and countermeasures. This reflects a broader trend of bolstering national defense capabilities in response to emerging security challenges.

Human Rights and Sports

Concerns are rising for the safety of the Iranian women's football team as they prepare to return home from Australia. The team faced criticism from some in Iran after not saluting during their national anthem, with one critic labeling them "wartime traitors." Advocates have urged Australia to offer protection, highlighting ongoing issues around freedom of expression and women's rights in Iran.
