February 18, 2026

International Diplomacy

Peace talks between Ukraine and Russia concluded after just two hours on their second day, with no breakthroughs reached. The brief session highlights continuing difficulties in negotiations aimed at resolving the ongoing conflict. The outcome suggests that further talks may be needed but progress remains uncertain.

Security and Unrest

In Iran, a security official reportedly fired on a crowd gathered at a cemetery to commemorate victims of the government's crackdown on protesters. This incident reflects ongoing tensions within the country related to the government's handling of dissent.

Meanwhile, in France, eleven people have been detained in connection with the killing of far-right student activist Quentin Deranque. The arrests come amid increased pressure on far-left groups suspected of involvement, pointing to heightened political tensions in the region.

Accidents and Rescue Efforts

Tragedy struck in Nigeria when a suspected carbon-monoxide leak resulted in the deaths of many miners just before sunrise as they completed their shifts. Witnesses reported the incident, underscoring continuing safety risks in mining operations.

Separately, in California, rescuers continue searching for nine skiers missing after an avalanche. Authorities warn that ongoing heavy snowfall is expected, which could hamper rescue efforts in the coming days as weather conditions remain hazardous.

Technology and Social Issues

Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg appeared in court to testify in a trial concerning social media addiction. The questioning focused on whether Instagram usage causes harm to children, reflecting growing legal and societal scrutiny of social media platforms and their impact on youth mental health.

Miscellaneous

Turkey’s president publicly rejected Israel’s recognition of Somaliland during an official visit to Ethiopia, warning that the move could destabilize an already volatile region.

In the United States, Air Force One and other government aircraft are set to receive a new paint job featuring colors preferred during the Trump administration, updating the iconic presidential fleet’s appearance.

On a lighter note, 71-year-old dancer Denise Sides was selected from hundreds of applicants to star in a Taylor Swift music video for the song "Opalite," highlighting opportunities across ages in the entertainment industry.

In Madrid, police arrested a 20-year-old accused of defrauding a luxury hotel by booking rooms for just one cent each, resulting in losses exceeding €20,000. Authorities linked the scam to manipulation of an online payment system.
