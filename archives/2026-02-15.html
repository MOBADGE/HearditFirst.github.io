Iran Signals Willingness to Negotiate Nuclear Deal

Iran’s deputy foreign minister, Majid Takht-Ravanchi, said Iran is ready to discuss compromises to finalize a nuclear deal. Speaking to the BBC in Tehran, he indicated that the responsibility now lies with the United States to show it wants to reach an agreement. This statement suggests potential progress in sensitive international negotiations.

Violence in Gaza Claims Eleven Lives

Israeli airstrikes in Gaza killed at least eleven people, according to rescuers. The Palestinian Red Crescent reported that six people died in a strike targeting a tent encampment in northern Gaza. The ongoing violence contributes to the region’s instability and raises concerns about civilian casualties.

UK Says Russian Opposition Leader Was Poisoned

The UK Foreign Office stated that Russian opposition figure Alexei Navalny was killed using a toxin derived from dart frogs. Officials said there is no innocent explanation for the presence of this toxin in samples taken from Navalny’s body. This accusation escalates tensions related to allegations of poisoning in Russia.

Global Protests Show Solidarity with Iran Demonstrators

Hundreds of thousands of people worldwide joined protests supporting those demonstrating in Iran. Large crowds gathered in cities such as Munich, Los Angeles, and Toronto, with additional demonstrations in Tel Aviv and Lisbon. These global rallies reflect widespread concern and solidarity with the situation unfolding inside Iran.
