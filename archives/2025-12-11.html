Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 11, 2025

Latin America Tensions Rise

In Venezuela, the U.S. government escalated its pressure campaign by seizing an oil tanker believed to be connected to Caracas. Venezuela condemned the seizure as an "act of piracy," signaling heightened tensions between the two countries. Meanwhile, María Corina Machado, a Venezuelan Nobel laureate who has faced threats from her government, spoke publicly about her secret trip to Oslo despite knowing the risks after months in hiding. These developments highlight the ongoing political struggle and unrest in Venezuela.

Human Rights Concerns in Australia and Myanmar

New data from Australia shows a troubling increase in Indigenous deaths in custody. Between July last year and June this year, 33 of the 113 deaths were Indigenous people—the highest level since 1980. This raises ongoing concerns about the treatment of Indigenous Australians within the justice system.

In Myanmar, a military air strike hit a hospital, killing more than 30 people. The military junta has intensified air bombardments as part of its efforts to regain control from ethnic armies, further worsening the country's humanitarian crisis.

International Legal and Social Issues

In Thailand, two brothers linked to a deadly fire at a nightclub in Goa, India, were detained after fleeing to Phuket. This case is likely to draw attention to issues of accountability and cross-border cooperation in criminal investigations.

Separately, in South Korea, a series of celebrity scandals has reignited debates about the country’s "cancel culture," as three prominent entertainers have recently faced public backlash. This ongoing conversation touches on social norms and the impact of public opinion on careers.

Economic and Political Developments

Mexico announced plans to impose tariffs of up to 50% on goods from China and other countries, starting January 1. The tariffs will affect products including cars, clothing, and appliances and reflect Mexico's efforts to protect its domestic industries amidst global trade tensions.

Portugal experienced its first general strike in 12 years, significantly disrupting daily life. The strike expresses widespread opposition to labour reforms proposed by Prime Minister Luís Montenegro’s government, which appears to have underestimated the strength of the protests.

Immigration and Workplace Issues

In the U.S., Donald Trump launched a new $1 million "gold card" immigration visa program. These special visas will be awarded to individuals who can demonstrate a "substantial benefit" to the country, representing a new approach to selective immigration.

In Japan, a prominent shogi (chess) player publicly criticized the national association for pregnancy-related biases in its rules. The current policies force players to choose between pursuing their careers and having children, sparking discussion about workplace equality and support for women in professional sports.
