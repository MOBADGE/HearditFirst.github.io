Tensions Rise as US Issues Deadline to Iran

The US president has set a deadline for Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, a vital waterway for global energy shipments. If Iran does not comply by Tuesday, the president has threatened to take military action against the country "in one night." This escalation reflects the strategic importance of the Strait and the high stakes involved in keeping it accessible. Diplomatic talks have shown little progress as the deadline approaches, putting the US president in a delicate political position.
