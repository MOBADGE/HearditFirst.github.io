Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 29, 2025

International Conflicts and Tensions

2025 has been marked by several major conflicts and rising geopolitical tensions. Russia accused Ukraine of a drone attack on a presidential residence, which Ukraine’s President Zelensky denied, calling the claim "typical Russian lies." Meanwhile, Thailand has accused Cambodia of violating a recently signed ceasefire agreement that took effect on Saturday at noon local time. The situation highlights ongoing instability in multiple regions around the world. Journalist John Simpson reflected on the many wars this year, noting the unprecedented global impact of these conflicts.

Middle East Developments

Former US President Donald Trump met with Benjamin Netanyahu in Florida to discuss urgent Middle East issues, including Iran, Lebanon, Syria, and Gaza. The meeting also aimed to support the plan for a second phase of the Gaza ceasefire in January, a priority for the Trump administration. Additionally, Israel’s recent recognition of Somaliland as an independent state has drawn attention, as Somaliland seeks wider international recognition. This move has sparked debate over its timing and potential motivations.

Humanitarian and Environmental Issues

The United States has pledged $2 billion in humanitarian aid, a sum welcomed by the United Nations, though it follows prior significant cuts in US funding for global humanitarian operations. In environmental news, Nepal announced it will scrap its Mount Everest waste deposit scheme. The policy required climbers to pay a $4,000 deposit, refundable only if they brought down 8kg of waste. The government called the scheme a failure, pointing to ongoing challenges in protecting the mountain’s environment.

Regional Security and Military Activity in Asia

China has conducted military drills around Taiwan as a warning to what it terms "separatist forces." These exercises occurred shortly after the US announced a major weapons sale to Taiwan, underscoring tensions between Beijing, Taipei, and Washington. In Syria, refugees living in Turkey are reconsidering their options regarding returning home following the fall of Assad’s government, though many remain cautious given ongoing uncertainties and challenges.

Community Response to Violence

In Australia, the actions of Ahmed al Ahmed, a shop owner in Bondi, drew praise for preventing a gunman from harming innocent people. Authorities credited him with saving many lives during the incident, highlighting the impact individuals can have in moments of crisis.
