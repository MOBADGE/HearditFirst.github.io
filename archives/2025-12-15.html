Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 15, 2025

Mass Shooting in Bondi

A tragic mass shooting took place during Bondi’s Hanukkah festival, resulting in multiple deaths. Among the victims were two rabbis, a Holocaust survivor, and a 10-year-old girl. Eyewitness accounts and video footage describe how the celebratory event turned into a scene of horror within minutes. This incident marks the worst mass shooting in Australia in nearly three decades, raising concerns about public safety at community gatherings.

Terror Plot Prevented in Southern California

Authorities in southern California announced the foiling of a New Year’s Eve terror plot. Four individuals were arrested and face conspiracy charges after being caught planning to test explosives in the desert. The swift action by federal agents stopped a potentially dangerous attack, highlighting ongoing vigilance around holiday periods and public events.

High-Profile Conviction in Hong Kong

Jimmy Lai, a 78-year-old pro-democracy tycoon and UK citizen, was convicted in a national security trial in Hong Kong. He was found guilty of lobbying foreign governments to impose sanctions on Hong Kong and China. This case is part of a broader crackdown on dissent in Hong Kong, underscoring continued tensions over political freedoms and international relations.

Political Developments in Chile and Ukraine

Chile has elected José Antonio Kast as its next president. Kast is widely regarded as the most right-wing leader since the dictatorship of Augusto Pinochet in the 1970s and 80s. This shift in leadership suggests a move toward more conservative policies in the country.

Meanwhile, US officials reported progress in discussions with Ukraine regarding security guarantees. However, key issues about Ukraine’s sovereign territory remain unresolved. The talks in Berlin signal ongoing diplomatic efforts amid continuing conflict, but the details on how to preserve Ukraine’s sovereignty have yet to be clarified.

Natural Disasters and Other News

Severe flash floods in the Moroccan city of Safi have affected at least 70 homes, causing significant damage in the old city center. Emergency response efforts are underway as authorities assess the impact.

In China, Iranian Nobel laureate Narges Mohammadi was reportedly taken to hospital following a violent arrest by plainclothes agents. Her family said she was beaten on the head and neck during the incident.

In other news, the India tour of famous footballer Lionel Messi ended smoothly after an initially chaotic start. In Kolkata, frustrated fans damaged a stadium when they were unable to see him, but subsequent events were peaceful.

Personal Tragedy in Los Angeles

Nick Reiner was arrested in connection with the deaths of director Rob Reiner and his wife. The couple was found dead in their home, and authorities have taken their son into custody. Further details are awaited as investigations continue.
