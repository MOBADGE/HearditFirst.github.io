December 15, 2025

Australia Mass Shooting at Bondi Hanukkah Festival

A joyous Hanukkah festival in Bondi, Australia, turned tragic when a mass shooting occurred, marking the worst such incident in almost 30 years in the country. Videos and eyewitness accounts reveal the rapid shift from celebration to horror within minutes. Authorities have confirmed that among the victims were two rabbis, a Holocaust survivor, and a 10-year-old girl. This attack has deeply shocked the nation and raised concerns about public safety at community events.

Security Guarantees for Ukraine

US officials have announced "strong" security guarantees for Ukraine during talks in Berlin. However, there is little clarity on how Ukraine’s sovereignty and territorial issues will be resolved. The discussions reflect ongoing international efforts to support Ukraine amid conflict, but key details remain unsettled, indicating that delicate diplomatic negotiations are still needed.

Hong Kong Trial and Conviction

Jimmy Lai, a pro-democracy prominent businessman in Hong Kong and UK citizen, has been convicted in a high-profile national security trial. The 78-year-old was found guilty of lobbying foreign governments to impose sanctions on Hong Kong and China. The verdict highlights continuing tensions over political freedoms and the implementation of national security laws in the region.

Iranian Nobel Laureate Hospitalized After Arrest

Narges Mohammadi, an Iranian Nobel laureate and human rights activist, was taken to hospital following a violent arrest. Her family said she suffered head and neck injuries inflicted by plainclothes agents during her detention in Mashhad. This development underscores ongoing concerns about the treatment of activists in Iran.

Deadly Floods in Morocco

Severe flash floods have caused significant damage in Morocco’s Safi city. At least 70 homes in the old city centre were inundated. The floods have resulted in fatalities and widespread disruption to the local community, with rescue efforts continuing amid challenging conditions.

Violence in Rhode Island and Syria

In the United States, two people have died and nine were injured in a shooting at Brown University, Rhode Island. Authorities are actively searching for the gunman involved in Saturday's attack. Separately, the US military reported that three Americans, including two soldiers and a civilian interpreter, were killed by an Islamic State gunman in Syria, illustrating ongoing security challenges in multiple regions.
