April 10, 2026

International Relations

US President Donald Trump criticized Iran for its management of the Strait of Hormuz, stating it was "not the agreement we have" and that Iran was "doing a very poor job" with the crucial waterway. Meanwhile, Israel and Lebanon are set to begin peace talks, signaling potential progress in their long-standing tensions. In another notable diplomatic development, Cheng Li-wun, leader of Taiwan’s Kuomintang party, visited Beijing. Cheng is the first sitting Kuomintang leader to meet Chinese President Xi Jinping in a decade, marking a rare engagement amid complex cross-strait relations.

Conflict and Security

Tensions continue in the occupied West Bank as a Palestinian was shot dead during an attack by Israeli settlers on a village. This incident comes as former Israeli security officials expressed concern about "government sponsored Jewish terrorism" spiraling out of control. On the Russia-Ukraine front, a temporary development offers a brief pause in conflict: Russian President Vladimir Putin announced an Orthodox Easter truce effective from Saturday afternoon through Easter Sunday, halting hostilities during this religious observance.

Legal and Human Rights News

Prince Harry faces a defamation lawsuit filed by Sentebale, the charity he co-founded. The dispute follows his departure from the organization last year amid disagreements over its management. In the UK, Melania Trump responded to rumors linking her to Jeffrey Epstein, denying any connection and condemning the claims as "mean-spirited attempts to defame" her reputation. Separately, in Sweden, a 61-year-old man has gone on trial accused of coercing his wife into having sex with 120 men. He denies using threats and drugs as alleged by the prosecution.

Crime and Justice

In East Africa, popular Tanzanian musician Matonya has been charged with rape in Kenya. He denies the allegations but has been released on bail and required to surrender his passport. In Europe, German authorities arrested Marla-Svenja Liebich, described as a transgender far-right extremist formerly associated with the neo-Nazi group Blood and Honour. Her arrest in the Czech Republic raises questions about ongoing far-right extremist activity across the region.

Science and Environment

Researchers studying chimpanzee communities in Uganda's Ngogo area report a violent split within what was once a close-knit group. A "civil war" among the chimpanzees has erupted over the past eight years, offering insight into the social dynamics and conflicts within wild primate populations. This research may have implications for understanding similar patterns of social behavior and territorial disputes in other animal communities.
