April 10, 2026

International Relations

US President Donald Trump criticized Iran's management of the Strait of Hormuz, a key oil shipping route, saying it is "not the agreement we have" and that Iran is "doing a very poor job" with the waterway. This comment comes amid efforts to ease tensions in the Middle East, as Israel and Lebanon have begun peace talks.

In China, Cheng Li-wun, the current leader of Taiwan’s Kuomintang party, visited Beijing. This is the first visit by a sitting Kuomintang leader to China in ten years and could signal potential shifts in cross-strait relations.

Russia-Ukraine Truce

Russian President Vladimir Putin announced a truce with Ukraine for Orthodox Easter. The ceasefire will start Saturday afternoon and continue through Easter Sunday. This temporary pause in fighting offers a brief respite in the ongoing conflict.

US Domestic News

The White House has instructed its staff not to place bets on prediction markets, especially those involving wagers on conflicts. These gambling platforms have grown in popularity recently, raising concerns over the ethics of betting on geopolitical events.

Since October, the US has admitted 4,499 refugees, with all but three coming from South Africa. President Trump has prioritized refugees from the Afrikaner community, a white minority group he says is facing persecution. However, South African officials dispute this characterization.

Social Media Regulation in India

The Indian government has proposed new rules to regulate news and political posts on social media platforms. The regulations would extend government oversight to include content shared by podcasters and influencers, increasing official control over online political discourse.

Cultural News

Afrika Bambaataa, a pioneering hip-hop artist from the Bronx known for the 1980s global hit "Planet Rock," has died aged 68. Bambaataa is credited with helping to shape the hip-hop genre and culture worldwide.

Other Notable News

In the Czech Republic, German authorities have arrested Marla-Svenja Liebich, a transgender former far-right extremist linked to the neo-Nazi group Blood and Honour. This arrest draws attention to ongoing concerns about extremist networks in Europe.

Summary

Today’s news covers significant diplomatic events including US-Iran tensions and Taiwan-China engagement, a temporary Easter ceasefire in Ukraine, and domestic US and Indian policy shifts focused on refugees and social media regulation. The music world marks the loss of hip-hop icon Afrika Bambaataa, while European security forces continue efforts to disrupt extremist activities.
