Heard It First – Daily Digest

April 10, 2026

International Relations

US President Donald Trump criticized Iran's management of the Strait of Hormuz, describing it as “not the agreement we have” and saying Iran is “doing a very poor job” with the strategic waterway. Meanwhile, peace talks between Israel and Lebanon are set to begin, potentially opening a new chapter in regional diplomacy. In a related development, Taiwan’s opposition leader, Cheng Li-wun, visited Beijing for talks with Xi Jinping, marking the first visit by a Kuomintang leader to China in a decade.

Conflict and Ceasefire

Russia and Ukraine have agreed to a truce for Orthodox Easter, lasting from Saturday afternoon through Easter Sunday. The announcement came from Vladimir Putin, signaling a temporary cessation of hostilities during the religious holiday. This ceasefire may provide a brief respite for civilians affected by the ongoing conflict.

US Domestic and Refugee Policy

Since October, the US has admitted 4,499 refugees, with all but three coming from South Africa. Priority has been given to Afrikaners, a white minority group whom President Trump says are facing persecution. This characterization has drawn objections from South Africa. Additionally, White House staff have been instructed not to place bets on prediction markets, which have gained popularity for wagering on conflicts and other events.

Social Media and Media Regulation

India is proposing new rules to regulate news and political content on social media platforms. The proposed changes would expand government authority over posts shared by podcasters and influencers, aiming to tighten control of news-related materials online. This move could significantly affect the landscape of digital media and political discourse in the country.

Other News

Melania Trump denied any connection to Jeffrey Epstein, calling rumors that Epstein introduced her to Donald Trump “mean-spirited attempts to defame my reputation.” In law enforcement news, a German transgender far-right extremist, Marla-Svenja Liebich, previously linked to the neo-Nazi group Blood and Honour, was arrested in the Czech Republic. In the arts, hip-hop pioneer Afrika Bambaataa, known for the influential 1980s hit "Planet Rock," has died at the age of 68.
