Heard It First – Daily Digest

April 10, 2026

International Relations

The US president criticized Iran's management of the Strait of Hormuz, saying it is "not the agreement we have" and accusing Tehran of "doing a very poor job" with the important waterway. Meanwhile, efforts toward peace continue in the Middle East as Israel and Lebanon prepare to begin peace talks, signaling a potential easing of regional tensions.

In China, Cheng Li-wun, the leader of Taiwan’s Kuomintang party, visited Beijing. This marks the first time in a decade that a sitting opposition leader from Taiwan has met with Chinese officials, potentially influencing cross-strait relations.

Conflict and Security

Russia and Ukraine have agreed to a truce over Orthodox Easter, announced by Vladimir Putin. The ceasefire is set to last from Saturday afternoon through Easter Sunday, offering a temporary pause in the ongoing conflict.

In a separate security development, German transgender individual Marla-Svenja Liebich was arrested in the Czech Republic. Reports indicate a past association with the neo-Nazi group Blood and Honour, raising concerns about far-right extremism.

Social Media and Information

India has proposed new rules to regulate social media posts related to news and politics. The changes would give the government increased authority over content shared by podcasters and influencers, potentially affecting how political information is disseminated online.

In the United States, White House staff have been instructed not to place bets on prediction markets, which are gambling platforms that have seen increased activity, including wagers on conflicts. This step reflects concerns over ethical implications and the growing popularity of such markets.

Humanitarian and Refugee Issues

Since October, the US has admitted 4,499 refugees, with all but three coming from South Africa. The majority belong to the Afrikaner community, a white minority group described by the US president as facing persecution. However, the South African government disputes this characterization.

Culture and Society

Hip-hop pioneer Afrika Bambaataa has died at the age of 68. Born in the Bronx, he was influential in shaping the hip-hop genre in the 1980s with his global hit "Planet Rock," leaving a lasting legacy in music and culture.

Additionally, Melania Trump has denied any links to Jeffrey Epstein, calling rumors that Epstein introduced her to Donald Trump "mean-spirited attempts to defame" her reputation. She also urged support for hearings that would allow survivors to share their experiences.
