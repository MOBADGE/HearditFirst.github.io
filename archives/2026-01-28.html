US Immigration Operations Under Fire in Minnesota

The recent killing of Alex Pretti in Minnesota has intensified calls to end the immigration enforcement operation led by Donald Trump in the state. In response, Trump stated that the government would "de-escalate" its activities there. The incident has sparked public outrage as two citizens were killed by federal agents during the crackdown. Some Republican lawmakers have started questioning the approach amid growing backlash. This development highlights tensions over immigration enforcement policy and its local impact, with further political debate expected as officials weigh next steps.

Attack on Minnesota Congresswoman Ilhan Omar

Minnesota congresswoman Ilhan Omar was attacked with an unknown liquid during a public event but was not injured. Despite the incident, she continued her speech. The circumstances of the attack remain unclear, but it reflects heightened security concerns for public figures amid political tensions in the region.

Drone Strike Kills Five on Ukrainian Passenger Train

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky condemned a Russian drone strike that targeted a packed passenger train in north-eastern Ukraine. The attack resulted in five deaths, according to officials. The incident underscores ongoing conflict-related violence in Ukraine, with civilian transportation remaining vulnerable to attacks. International attention is likely to focus on the humanitarian impact as the situation develops.

Iran Protests Met with Brutal Crackdown

Young Iranians told the BBC about the harsh repression they witnessed during recent protests, including the deaths of friends and others at the hands of security forces. The crackdown on demonstrations earlier this month has drawn international concern for human rights violations. The testimonies illustrate the severe domestic unrest and the challenges faced by protesters seeking political change in Iran.

Resignations and Reforms in Malaysian Football

The entire executive committee of the Football Association of Malaysia resigned following a scandal involving several foreign-born players. This move is seen as an effort to allow for organizational reform. The situation points to ongoing governance issues in Malaysian sports administration and the demand for greater transparency and accountability.
