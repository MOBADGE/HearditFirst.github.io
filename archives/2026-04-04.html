April 04, 2026

Conflict and Security

A Russian drone attack struck a busy market in a southern Ukrainian town on Saturday morning, killing five people and injuring 21 others. This attack is part of a broader escalation that Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky says Russia chose to launch during the Easter period rather than agree to a ceasefire. During this surge, hundreds of drones and missiles were reportedly launched, resulting in six civilian deaths and 40 injuries.

In Iran, efforts continue to locate a missing US crew member after a harrowing incident. A former US marine told the BBC that the main priority for the rescue team is to find signs of life amid what is described as a dangerous search operation.

Space Exploration

NASA's Artemis II mission is now halfway to the Moon. The crew aboard the Orion capsule took a spectacular image of Earth during the journey. The photograph was captured by the mission commander, Reid Wiseman. This mission marks another step in NASA's ongoing efforts to explore beyond Earth orbit and return humans to the lunar surface.

Domestic Politics and Public Projects

Former US Speaker Nancy Pelosi has criticized a proposal by Donald Trump to spend $152 million to reopen the Alcatraz prison. Pelosi described the plan as a “stupid notion” and said it would waste taxpayers' money. The proposal has sparked debate over the use of public funds for this controversial project.

Social Unrest and Protests

In Peru, a pregame rally for the football match between Alianza Lima and Universitario de Deportes turned violent, leaving one person dead and dozens injured. Hundreds of fans had gathered before the match when the unrest occurred, highlighting tensions surrounding the event.

In Nigeria, individuals who were evicted to make way for the construction of the country’s capital have accused the government of not fulfilling promises made to them. These residents are calling for recognition and a voice in matters concerning their land, indicating ongoing challenges related to displacement and development.

Economy and Policy

Senegal has banned ministers from traveling abroad in response to rising oil prices that have nearly doubled the government’s budgeted cost for fuel. The move aims to ease financial pressure on the country's stretched public finances.

In Canada, a large-scale gun control effort recently banned 2,500 models of so-called "assault-style" firearms. However, a related buy-back program to reduce the number of such guns in private hands has met with limited success, falling short of its goals by about half. This highlights the challenges the government faces in successfully implementing its gun control policies.

Culture and History

A new exhibition traces the transformation of Bombay from a fort city into a bustling metropolis. The display uses paintings, photographs, and prints collected over centuries to showcase the city’s development and cultural changes. This exhibition offers insight into the historical evolution of one of India’s largest urban centers.
