Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 31, 2025

Transportation and Travel

Eurostar high-speed train services through the Channel Tunnel have returned to normal after a major disruption. Though operations resumed, the rail firm has warned passengers that last-minute delays and cancellations might still occur and advised travelers to check before their journeys. Meanwhile, a serious train crash occurred on the single-track line to Machu Picchu, resulting in the death of the train driver and injuring at least 40 passengers. Emergency responses are ongoing as safety concerns around the popular route are likely to be reviewed.

International Conflict and Diplomacy

Thailand has released 18 Cambodian soldiers who had been detained since July as part of a new ceasefire agreement following weeks of deadly clashes along the Thai-Cambodia border. This development signals a potential easing of tensions between the two countries after prolonged conflict in the disputed border area. In Guinea, the leader of the military junta has won the presidency in a controversial election, securing a seven-year mandate. The election outcome was widely anticipated despite criticism, and the region is watching how this will affect Guinea’s political stability.

Health and Personal Stories

Several notable individuals are facing serious health issues. Former Australian cricket batter Damien Martyn, aged 54, is in an induced coma in a Queensland hospital after being diagnosed with meningitis. In Brazil, former far-right politician Jair Bolsonaro has undergone a third medical procedure for persistent hiccups that began over the weekend, according to his wife. Additionally, Tatiana Schlossberg, granddaughter of John F. Kennedy, passed away at age 35 after publicly sharing her terminal cancer diagnosis last month.

Human Rights and Legal Concerns

Jimmy Lai, a Hong Kong tycoon imprisoned for his pro-democracy activism, is reportedly experiencing severe health problems while in custody. His daughter told the BBC that his teeth are rotting and his fingernails are falling off, raising concerns about his treatment in prison and broader issues surrounding political detainees in Hong Kong.

Significant Public Events

Hundreds of thousands of people gathered in Dhaka to mourn the death of Khaleda Zia, Bangladesh’s former prime minister, who passed away at the age of 80. A state funeral was held to honor her life and political legacy, highlighting her impact on the country’s history and ongoing political scene.

United States Military Actions

US President Donald Trump announced that the United States struck a "big facility" related to alleged Venezuelan drug boats. He did not confirm media reports suggesting the explosion was carried out via a CIA drone strike. This action reflects ongoing US efforts to combat drug trafficking routes linked to Venezuela, with potential diplomatic repercussions in the region.
