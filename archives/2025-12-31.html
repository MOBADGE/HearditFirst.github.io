Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 31, 2025

New Year's Message of Peace in Sydney

As thousands gathered to watch the annual New Year's Eve fireworks, the words "peace" and "unity" were projected onto the Sydney Harbour Bridge. This display came in response to a recent terror attack at Bondi, signaling a message of reconciliation and hope as the city entered the new year.

Rising Tensions Between Russia and Ukraine

The European Union's top diplomat publicly rejected recent Russian claims that Ukraine had attacked government sites. This statement followed the release of video footage by Russia, which it said showed a downed Ukrainian drone targeting one of Vladimir Putin's residences. The dispute underscores ongoing tensions and conflicting narratives in the region.

Train Crash Near Machu Picchu

A train accident on the single-track line to Machu Picchu resulted in the death of the train driver and injuries to at least 40 passengers. The crash highlights concerns about safety on one of Peru's most popular tourist routes.

Humanitarian Aid Controversy in Gaza

Israel announced plans to bar 37 international aid groups from operating, citing new registration rules for NGOs. This move has drawn criticism from ten countries, including the UK and EU, who described the restrictions as "restrictive" and "unacceptable," warning that it would severely impact humanitarian efforts in Gaza.

Regional Travel Restrictions and Border Developments

Mali and Burkina Faso imposed travel bans on US citizens in response to US entry restrictions on their nationals. Meanwhile, Thailand released 18 Cambodian soldiers detained since July, part of a new ceasefire deal following recent clashes along the Thai-Cambodia border. These developments reflect ongoing diplomatic and security challenges in different parts of the world.

Other Notable Updates

- Eurostar services through the Channel Tunnel have returned to normal after major disruptions, though some last-minute delays and cancellations remain possible.

- A widely watched health concern emerged as the daughter of Jimmy Lai, a jailed Hong Kong businessman, reported that his health is deteriorating, including issues such as rotting teeth and falling fingernails.

- In Bangladesh, huge crowds gathered in Dhaka to mourn Khaleda Zia, the country’s first female prime minister, who passed away at the age of 80. Her state funeral drew a large outpouring of respect and remembrance.
