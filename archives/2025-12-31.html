Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 31, 2025

International Conflicts and Security

Tensions remain high between Russia and Ukraine as Russia released a video claiming to show a downed Ukrainian drone targeting one of Vladimir Putin's residences. However, the European Union's top diplomat dismissed these claims, rejecting the narrative of a Ukrainian attack on Russian government sites. Meanwhile, Finnish police detained 14 crew members on a ship traveling from St Petersburg to Haifa, suspecting the vessel of sabotage against an undersea cable, underlining ongoing concerns over infrastructure security in the region.

Political Unrest and Human Rights

Unrest continues in Iran, where protesters tried to break into the governor's office in the city of Fasa, with verified video showing a crowd forcibly entering the building. In Hong Kong, Jimmy Lai's daughter has spoken out about his deteriorating health while imprisoned, stating that his teeth are rotting and fingernails are falling off. These developments highlight ongoing challenges related to political dissent and human rights in both countries.

Economic Changes and Currency Updates

Bulgaria officially joined the eurozone, adopting the euro as its currency. This move has divided public opinion; some see it as a significant step forward for the country’s integration into Europe, while others worry it might result in economic stagnation. In China, the government has altered the tax policy on birth control and childcare starting January 1, removing a 13% sales tax from childcare services while introducing it on contraceptives. This measure aims to encourage higher birth rates amid demographic concerns.

Humanitarian Concerns

Israel announced it will bar 37 international aid organizations, prompting criticism from ten countries including the UK and the EU. These nations described the new registration requirements for NGOs as overly restrictive and "unacceptable," warning of severe impacts on humanitarian efforts in Gaza. This move raises significant issues regarding access to aid for populations in conflict zones.

Major Accidents and Public Responses

A tragic train crash near Machu Picchu resulted in the death of the train driver and injuries to at least 40 passengers. The incident on the single-track route to the historic site is under investigation. Meanwhile, Sydney marked New Year's Eve by projecting messages of "peace" and "unity" onto the Harbour Bridge, signaling an effort to promote harmony following a recent attack at Bondi Beach. Additionally, Bangladesh held a large state funeral in Dhaka for Khaleda Zia, the nation's first female prime minister, who passed away earlier this week at age 80. Her death has drawn huge crowds paying their respects.
