Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 31, 2025

Travel and Transport

Eurostar continues to experience delays, with some passengers reportedly stuck onboard trains overnight. The company has announced plans to operate all of its services on Wednesday, but travelers should still expect potential delays and cancellations as the issues persist. Meanwhile, in Peru, a train crash on the single-track line to Machu Picchu resulted in the death of the train driver and injuries to at least 40 passengers. Emergency services responded to the accident, which has raised concerns about safety along this popular tourist route.

International Relations and Conflict

Tensions along the Thai-Cambodia border have eased following weeks of violent clashes. As part of a new ceasefire agreement, Thailand has released 18 Cambodian soldiers who had been detained since July. This move may signal a step toward more stable relations between the neighboring countries. Additionally, in Guinea, the leader of the military junta has won a controversial presidential election, securing a seven-year term that was widely anticipated. The outcome has drawn international attention to the country's political future.

Health and Human Interest

The daughter of Jimmy Lai, a jailed Hong Kong tycoon, has shared concerns about her father’s deteriorating health while in prison, mentioning issues such as rotting teeth and falling fingernails. In Australia, former cricketer Damien Martyn, who played 67 Test matches, has been placed in an induced coma at a Queensland hospital after being diagnosed with meningitis. These reports highlight ongoing health struggles faced by prominent individuals.

Notable Deaths

Several well-known figures have passed away recently. Tatiana Schlossberg, granddaughter of John F Kennedy, has died at the age of 35, after publicly sharing her terminal cancer diagnosis just last month. Actor Isiah Whitlock Jr., known for his memorable lines on the television series The Wire and Veep, also died at age 71. Reflecting on the year, high-profile deaths included Pope Francis, musician Ozzy Osbourne, and actor Diane Keaton.

U.S. Military Activity

The U.S. president reported hitting a "big facility" linked to alleged Venezuelan drug boats but did not confirm media reports suggesting a CIA drone strike was responsible for an explosion at the site. This statement adds a new dimension to U.S. efforts targeting drug trafficking in the region, with further details expected to emerge.
