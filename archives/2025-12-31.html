Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 31, 2025

International Conflicts and Diplomacy

Tensions continue between Russia and Ukraine amid recent claims and denials about attacks. Russia released video footage it says shows a Ukrainian drone being shot down near one of Vladimir Putin's residences. The European Union's top diplomat has dismissed these Russian assertions, denying Ukraine targeted government sites. This exchange highlights ongoing conflict narratives between the two countries.

In the Middle East, Israel has announced it will bar 37 international aid groups from operating under new registration rules. The decision is drawing criticism from the United Kingdom, the European Union, and eight other countries, who describe the restrictions as "unacceptable" and warn of serious consequences for humanitarian aid delivery in Gaza. The situation raises concerns over access to aid in the region.

Social Unrest and Health

Protests in Iran are ongoing, with crowds recently breaking into the governor’s office gate in the city of Fasa. This act is part of wider unrest in the country, signaling continued domestic challenges to the government.

Meanwhile, in Hong Kong, the daughter of imprisoned businessman Jimmy Lai has expressed worry about her father’s deteriorating health. She reported that his teeth are rotting and his fingernails are falling off, bringing attention to prison conditions and the wellbeing of detainees.

Economic Developments

Bulgaria has officially adopted the euro as its currency, a decision that is dividing public opinion. Supporters view it as an important step forward economically and politically, while opponents are concerned it may lead to economic stagnation. The move marks a significant change for the country’s financial system.

In China, new tax and childcare policies are being introduced to influence population growth. From January 1, contraceptives will be subject to a 13% sales tax, while childcare services will be exempted from such taxes. This approach aims to address declining birth rates by adjusting financial incentives.

Public Safety and Incidents

A tragic accident occurred near the historic site of Machu Picchu where a train crash resulted in the death of the train driver and left at least 40 people injured. The crash on the single-track railway raises questions about safety measures on popular tourist routes.

Community and Commemoration

In Sydney, the New Year’s Eve celebrations featured a strong message of peace and unity. The words "peace" and "unity" were projected onto the Sydney Harbour Bridge as thousands gathered for the annual fireworks display. This message comes shortly after a recent attack at Bondi, reflecting community efforts to promote harmony.

In Bangladesh, huge crowds assembled in the capital Dhaka to mourn Khaleda Zia, the country's first female prime minister, who passed away recently at the age of 80. The state funeral attracted a large outpouring of grief and respect, underscoring Zia’s significance in the nation's political history.

Security Measures

Finnish authorities detained a ship suspected of sabotaging an undersea cable. The vessel was traveling from St Petersburg in Russia to the Israeli port of Haifa when Finnish police seized it and arrested 14 crew members. The incident highlights ongoing concerns about infrastructure security and geopolitical tensions.
