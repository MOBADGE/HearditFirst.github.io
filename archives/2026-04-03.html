Space Exploration

The Artemis II mission crew captured a striking photograph of Earth from aboard the Orion capsule as they journey toward the Moon. The image was taken by Reid Wiseman, the mission commander. This visual marks an important moment in the ongoing spaceflight, highlighting the progress of human exploration beyond Earth's orbit.

Politics and Governance

In Burkina Faso, Capt Ibrahim Traoré, who took control of the country in 2023, stated that democracy is not a suitable system for the nation and urged to "forget" about democratic rule. This stance signals a continued shift away from democratic governance in Burkina Faso under military leadership.

Meanwhile, in Cuba, more than 2,000 prisoners are set to be released amid mounting pressure from the United States. US President Trump has expressed a desire to change Cuba's leadership and has imposed an oil embargo on the country, intensifying bilateral tensions.

Security and Legal Developments

France’s highest courts overturned a ban imposed by the Paris police on a four-day Muslim gathering. Authorities had argued the event posed a security threat as it might be targeted by terrorism, but the courts ruled against the ban, allowing the event to proceed.

In the United States, former Attorney General Pam Bondi was removed by President Trump. Her tenure was notably marked by controversy surrounding her handling of files related to Jeffrey Epstein, drawing significant public attention.

International Trade and Cybersecurity

A French-owned ship passed through the Strait of Hormuz, apparently the first major European-owned vessel to do so since recent regional conflicts began. This passage may reflect changes in maritime activity and international trade routes in a tense geopolitical environment.

In Italy, the Uffizi Gallery confirmed it was targeted in a cyber-attack. While hackers reportedly accessed IT systems, the gallery assured that none of the artworks were compromised, suggesting that the cultural heritage remains secure despite the technological breach.

Social and Cultural News

In the music world, rapper Gucci Mane was reportedly kidnapped and robbed by fellow artist Pooh Shiesty, according to prosecutors. The incident involved forcing Gucci Mane to sign documents to release Pooh Shiesty from a record contract, highlighting criminal activity within the entertainment industry.

International Deportations

The United States deported eight individuals described as being "of African origin" to Uganda. The Uganda Law Society criticized the deportations as illegal and "dehumanising," raising human rights concerns over immigration and deportation practices.
