Change in Australian Political Leadership

Australia's Liberal Party has replaced its first woman leader, Sussan Ley, after just nine months. Ley's leadership faced poor polling numbers, and Angus Taylor took over the role amid a challenging political environment. Ley’s brief tenure raised discussions about the difficulties women face in Australian politics, with some analysts citing the concept of the "glass cliff," where women leaders are often appointed during crises and face challenging conditions.
