March 02, 2026

Middle East Conflict Escalates

Israeli forces have conducted strikes in Lebanon following rocket fire from the group Hezbollah. Lebanese officials report that 52 people were killed in the Israeli attacks, while there have been no reported casualties in Israel. This violence is part of a broader conflict involving Iran, as the US and Israel continue to carry out strikes inside Iran. Residents there describe frequent explosions disrupting daily life, with attacks ongoing for a third consecutive day. The intensifying conflict raises concerns about further regional instability.

Rising Global Energy Prices

The escalating conflict in the Middle East has contributed to a jump in oil and gas prices globally. Experts warn that if the fighting continues for an extended period, energy prices could rise even further. This could impact economies worldwide as the cost of fuel and energy-dependent goods may increase.

Nuclear Policies in Europe and Asia

In Europe, France has announced plans to strengthen its nuclear arsenal and extend its nuclear deterrence to eight other countries in the region. French leader Emmanuel Macron stressed that Paris will retain exclusive control over any decision to use nuclear weapons. Meanwhile, in Asia, India and Canada have reached a significant agreement to supply India with uranium. This deal is part of a broader reset in relations between the two countries, unveiled during a meeting between Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi and Canadian official Mark Carney.

Conflict and Violence in Africa

South Sudan has suffered a sudden and deadly attack that killed at least 169 people. Peacekeepers in the area are currently sheltering about 1,000 civilians near their base and providing emergency medical care. The incident highlights ongoing security challenges in the region.

Political Shifts in Nepal

Nepal is preparing for an important election that many hope will bring change after a year of protests. Notably, a popular rap artist is emerging as a strong contender against a former prime minister, signaling potential shifts in the country’s political landscape driven by younger voters known as Generation Z.

Other Notable News

In the US, Texas authorities have identified the victims of a recent shooting in an Austin bar. The incident resulted in the deaths of two victims and the suspected gunman, with 14 others injured. The FBI has indicated possible terrorism links in the case. On a cultural note, a long-lost Rembrandt painting from 1633 has been rediscovered after being missing since 1961. Meanwhile, the comedy show Saturday Night Live has faced criticism from a Tourette's syndrome charity for a sketch they called hurtful, amid ongoing discussions about representation and sensitivity in entertainment.
