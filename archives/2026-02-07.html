U.S. Political Developments

U.S. President Donald Trump removed a video he posted that included a racist clip portraying President Barack Obama and former First Lady Michelle Obama as apes. Trump said he "didn't make a mistake" and claimed he had only seen the beginning of the video before it was shared online.

Additionally, Trump signed an executive order threatening to impose tariffs on countries that engage in trade with Iran. While the order did not set a specific tariff rate, it mentioned 25% as a possible example. This move signals a toughening stance on international trade related to Iran.

Ongoing Search in Arizona

Authorities continue to investigate the disappearance of Nancy Guthrie, the 84-year-old mother of NBC News presenter Savannah Guthrie. Nancy Guthrie is believed to have been abducted from her home in Tucson, Arizona, nearly a week ago. Police are currently examining new leads in the case as the search efforts continue.

Global Security Incident in Pakistan

A deadly attack occurred in Islamabad, Pakistan, targeting a mosque during Friday prayers. More than 30 people were killed and around 170 injured in the blast, according to officials. The attack highlights ongoing security challenges in the country’s capital.

Cultural Spotlight: Bad Bunny and the Super Bowl

Puerto Rican music star Bad Bunny is preparing to perform at the Super Bowl halftime show, which has recently become more politically and culturally polarized. The widely anticipated performance raises questions about how he might address or respond to his critics on this very large global platform.

Community Achievement in India

Anke Gowda, a retired factory worker from India, has built a library containing more than two million books. The library is accessible to everyone and Gowda was recently honored with an award from the Indian government in recognition of this achievement. This story highlights the impact of community efforts to promote literacy and education.
