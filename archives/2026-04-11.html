Hungarian Election: A Potential Shift in Leadership

Hungarians are voting on whether to end Viktor Orbán's 16-year tenure as prime minister. Most opinion polls currently favour Péter Magyar, who leads a grassroots political party challenging Orbán. Despite being seen as the front-runner, Orbán has expressed a defiant stance as election day approaches. The outcome could reshape Hungary’s political landscape after nearly two decades under Orbán’s rule.

Artemis Moon Mission: Astronauts Return After Historic Voyage

Four astronauts from the Artemis mission have safely returned to Earth after a nine-day journey that took them farther from home than any humans in history. The crew splashed down in the Pacific Ocean following their trip around the Moon. Their mission marks a significant milestone in space exploration and sets the stage for future lunar missions and potential long-term human presence beyond Earth.

California Governor Candidate Faces Abuse Investigation

A high-profile Democrat running for governor in California is being investigated by prosecutors over abuse allegations. The candidate has strongly denied the accusations. The probe adds a new dimension to an already competitive race for one of the most influential positions in US state politics.

Violent Incident at New York City’s Grand Central Terminal

A man identifying himself as "Lucifer" attacked people at Grand Central Terminal in New York City, slashing three individuals. The New York Police Department fatally shot the assailant after he ignored around 20 police commands to disarm. Authorities responded swiftly to prevent further harm, and investigations are ongoing.

Irish Fuel Protests Cause Significant Disruptions

Fuel protests in the Republic of Ireland have entered their fifth day, causing widespread disruption. Hundreds of petrol stations have run dry due to continued blockades and demonstrations. In response, the Irish cabinet is convening to discuss the situation and seek solutions to the ongoing fuel shortages impacting daily life across the country.
