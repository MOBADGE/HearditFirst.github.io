Migrant Pushbacks in Greece

Reports reveal that since at least 2020, Greek police have been using masked migrants to forcibly push other migrants back across the border. This tactic involves recruiting migrants, described as mercenaries, to assist in the illegal pushbacks, which bypass official asylum procedures. The practice raises concerns about human rights violations and the treatment of refugees and migrants trying to enter Greece. The ongoing use of such methods highlights challenges in managing migration and border security in Europe.

Legal Action in South Korea

A 90-year-old woman in South Korea has been sentenced to one year in jail for laundering money linked to her son's drug-related activities. Her son is currently imprisoned in Cambodia, and she was found guilty of handling the proceeds of his criminal actions. This case underscores efforts by South Korean authorities to crack down on financial crimes connected to drug trafficking, even when the parties involved are elderly family members.

Philippine President Addresses Health Rumors

The president of the Philippines, Bongbong Marcos, has publicly responded to rumors about his health by inviting critics to join him in exercise. He demonstrated his fitness with star jumps, challenging anyone who doubts his physical condition to meet him in the gym. This public display aims to assure the public and counter speculation about his ability to fulfill his duties. The incident reflects the role of personal health perceptions in political leadership.
