Migration and Border Control in Greece

New reports reveal that Greek police have been using masked migrants to help forcibly push other migrants back across the border. This practice, which reportedly involves mercenaries recruited since at least 2020, has been described as illegal "pushbacks." These operations raise concerns about the treatment of migrants and compliance with international laws protecting refugees and asylum seekers. The situation highlights ongoing tensions along Greece’s borders and the challenges European countries face in managing migration flows. The use of migrants in enforcement roles could lead to further scrutiny and questions about the ethics and legality of these border control measures.

Philippine President Addresses Health Rumors

The President of the Philippines responded to rumors about his health by publicly demonstrating his physical fitness. He invited critics to join him in the gym, where he performed star jumps to showcase his condition. This move was aimed at dispelling concerns about his ability to carry out his duties effectively. The president’s demonstration serves to reassure the public and political observers of his well-being amid ongoing speculation. How this will impact political discourse in the Philippines remains to be seen, but it highlights the role public appearances play in managing political reputations.
