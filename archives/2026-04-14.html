Migrant Pushbacks in Greece

Greek police have been using masked migrants to forcibly push other migrants back across the border, according to a BBC report. These recruits, described as mercenaries, have been involved in illegal migrant pushbacks since at least 2020. This practice raises significant human rights concerns and questions about the methods used in border control. The use of migrants themselves to carry out these operations complicates the situation and may impact Greece’s international standing and its approach to migration management going forward.

Violence in Turkey High School

In south-east Turkey, a former student opened fire at a high school, injuring sixteen people, including students and teachers. This incident has drawn attention to issues of school security and violence in the region. Authorities are likely to investigate the motives behind the attack and consider measures to prevent such incidents in the future, highlighting ongoing concerns about public safety in educational institutions.

Investigation into Haiti Stampede Deaths

Seven people, including police officers, have been arrested as part of an investigation into a deadly stampede at the Laferrière Citadel in Haiti that resulted in 25 deaths. The event occurred on Saturday, and authorities are looking into what caused the deadly crowd crush. This investigation could lead to accountability for the tragedy and may prompt changes in how public safety is managed during large gatherings in Haiti.

Sentencing in South Korea for Money Laundering

A 90-year-old woman in South Korea has been sentenced to one year in jail for laundering drug money linked to her son, who is currently imprisoned in Cambodia. The case highlights ongoing efforts by authorities to tackle financial crimes related to drug trafficking. The sentence demonstrates a firm stance on money laundering regardless of the perpetrator’s age, which may serve as a warning to others involved in similar activities.

Philippine President Responds to Health Rumors

The president of the Philippines, Bongbong Marcos, addressed rumors about his ill health by publicly challenging critics to join him in the gym. He performed star jumps to showcase his fitness and dispel doubts regarding his physical condition. This response aims to reassure the public and critics about his ability to fulfill his duties, potentially stabilizing perceptions of his leadership.
