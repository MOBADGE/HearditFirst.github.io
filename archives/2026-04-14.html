Migration and Border Issues in Greece

New reporting has revealed that Greek police have recruited masked migrants since at least 2020 to force other migrants back across the border. These pushbacks are described as illegal, involving migrants acting on behalf of authorities to physically prevent others from entering Greece. This practice raises concerns about human rights and the treatment of migrants attempting to seek asylum or better opportunities in Europe. The use of migrants in this role complicates accountability and adds to the debate on how European countries manage migration and border security.

Detention of French Woman in the US

Marie-Thérèse, an 86-year-old French pensioner, is currently being held by US Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) in Louisiana. She moved to the United States hoping to reunite with a long-lost love. Her son has expressed worry about her frail health while she remains detained. This case highlights the challenges faced by older immigrants and raises questions about immigration enforcement and humanitarian considerations for detainees, especially elderly and vulnerable individuals.

School Shooting Incident in Turkey

A shooting at a high school in south-eastern Turkey resulted in 16 people injured, including students and teachers. The attacker was identified as a former student of the school. This incident has likely increased concerns about school safety in the country and may lead to calls for strengthened security measures or programs to address violence and mental health among students. Details on the motivation behind the shooting have not been disclosed.

Ceasefire Ahead of Pope’s Visit in Cameroon

Separatist groups in Cameroon announced a pause in their fighting as the Pope prepares to visit the country. The groups said their decision showed responsibility, restraint, and respect for human dignity. This temporary ceasefire could offer a brief period of calm in a region affected by conflict and might facilitate the Pope’s efforts to promote peace and reconciliation during his visit. The move signals the potential influence of international and religious figures in conflict zones.

Investigation into Deadly Haiti Stampede

Authorities in Haiti have arrested seven people, including police officers, following a deadly stampede at Laferrière Citadel on Saturday that killed 25 people. Investigators are looking into the circumstances that led to the tragedy. The involvement of law enforcement officers among the arrested indicates potential failures in crowd control or security management. The investigation is ongoing, and it may result in safety reforms to prevent future incidents during large gatherings or events.

Legal Case in South Korea

A 90-year-old South Korean woman has been sentenced to one year in jail for laundering drug money for her son, who is currently imprisoned in Cambodia. This case illustrates South Korea's efforts to crack down on financial crimes linked to drug trafficking and highlights how family members can be implicated in illegal activities. The sentence reflects the judiciary’s stance on money laundering regardless of the defendant’s age.
