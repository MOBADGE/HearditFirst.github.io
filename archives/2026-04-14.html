Migrant Pushbacks in Greece

Greek police have been using masked migrants to forcibly push other migrants back across the border, according to a recent report by the BBC. This practice involves mercenaries who have been recruited since at least 2020 to carry out illegal migrant "pushbacks." The use of migrants themselves in these operations raises serious human rights and legal concerns. The situation highlights ongoing tensions and challenges in managing migration flows on Greece’s borders and raises questions about the methods used by authorities to control crossings.

Deadly Stampede Investigation in Haiti

Authorities in Haiti have arrested seven individuals, including police officers, as part of an investigation into a deadly stampede that resulted in 25 deaths at Laferrière Citadel last Saturday. The exact causes of the tragedy remain under investigation, but the involvement of law enforcement officers in the arrests suggests complications in crowd control or security measures during the incident. The investigation will seek to clarify what led to the stampede and ensure accountability.

Sentencing in South Korea for Money Laundering

In South Korea, a 90-year-old woman has been sentenced to one year in jail for laundering drug money on behalf of her son. The son is currently imprisoned in Cambodia. This case underlines the international reach of money laundering related to drug crimes and shows how family members can become involved in legal proceedings tied to criminal activities. The sentence reflects efforts by South Korean authorities to crack down on financial crimes connected to drugs.

Philippine President Addresses Health Rumours

The President of the Philippines, Bongbong Marcos, has responded to rumours about his health by publicly demonstrating his physical fitness. He invited critics to join him in the gym and performed star jumps on camera to show his capabilities. This response aims to reassure the public and counter any doubts about his ability to carry out official duties effectively. The demonstration illustrates how public figures sometimes address personal health speculation through direct, visible actions.
