Migrant Pushbacks in Greece

New reports reveal that Greek authorities have been involved in using masked migrants to forcibly push other migrants back across the border. According to the BBC, mercenaries have been recruited since at least 2020 to carry out these illegal pushbacks. This practice raises serious concerns about human rights and the treatment of migrants attempting to enter Greece. The use of migrants to enforce border controls adds a complex layer to an already fraught issue of migration and border security in the region. The situation may draw further attention from international human rights organizations and could impact Greece’s relations with its neighbors and European partners.

Sentencing in South Korea for Money Laundering

In South Korea, a 90-year-old woman has been sentenced to one year in jail for laundering money on behalf of her son. Her son is currently imprisoned in Cambodia for drug-related offenses. This case highlights the ongoing efforts by South Korean authorities to combat drug trafficking and related financial crimes, even when those involved are family members. The sentencing underscores a strict legal stance on money laundering and may serve as a deterrent in similar cases involving cross-border criminal activity.

Philippine President Addresses Health Rumors

Philippine President Bongbong Marcos has publicly challenged rumors about his health by inviting critics to join him for physical exercise. In a show of fitness, he performed star jumps and encouraged others to test their own fitness levels alongside him. This move aims to counter doubts about his physical condition and reaffirm his capability to lead. By addressing the rumors directly, he seeks to maintain public confidence and support during his tenure. The president’s approach reflects an emphasis on transparency and personal demonstration in managing public perceptions.
