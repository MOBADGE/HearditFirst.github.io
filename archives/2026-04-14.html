Middle East Tensions

Hamas has rejected a plan to disarm in Gaza, according to a Palestinian official. The armed group has said it will not engage in further talks until Israel fulfills its commitments. This development highlights ongoing challenges in achieving peace and stability in the region. The stalemate could prolong tensions and complicate future diplomatic efforts.

US and International Relations

China criticized the US naval blockade of Iranian ports, calling it irresponsible and dangerous. As the largest buyer of Iranian oil, China argued the blockade undermines a fragile ceasefire. This statement signals growing international concern about US actions in the region and potential impacts on global diplomacy and security.

In Hungary, the US vice-president defended his support for the country's leader, calling him a "great guy" who did a "very good job" despite a recent landslide defeat. The vice-president expressed confidence in working with the new Hungarian leader, Péter Magyar, indicating a willingness to maintain diplomatic engagement amid political changes.

Legal and Social Issues in the US

Another woman has accused US politician Eric Swalwell of drugging and raping her in 2018. The accuser, Lonna Drewes, alleges the incident occurred after he drugged her drink in a hotel room. This accusation adds to ongoing scrutiny and could impact Swalwell's public standing and career.

In Louisiana, an 86-year-old French woman named Marie-Thérèse is being held by the US Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) after moving to the United States to reunite with a long-lost love. Her son expressed concern for her frail health while she remains in detention, raising questions about immigration enforcement and humanitarian considerations.

Crime and Safety

In Turkey's south-east, sixteen people were injured after a former student opened fire at a high school. Among the injured were students and teachers. This incident has brought attention to school safety and gun violence in the region.

Other Notable News

The retrial concerning the death of Argentine football legend Diego Maradona has begun. The trial focuses on members of Maradona's medical team accused of providing poor care. The first trial collapsed after a judge permitted cameras in the courtroom, which had been controversial.

Separatist groups in Cameroon announced a pause in fighting ahead of an upcoming visit by the Pope. The decision aims to demonstrate responsibility, restraint, and respect for human dignity during the visit, offering a rare moment of calm in an otherwise conflict-affected region.

Finally, in Zanzibar, the partner of US influencer Ashly Robinson, known online as Ashlee Jenae, is speaking to police as a witness following her death. Authorities reported that she died after attempting to take her own life.
