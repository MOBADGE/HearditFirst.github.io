January 30, 2026

International Concerns Over UN Funding

António Guterres, the UN secretary general, has warned that the United Nations could face an "imminent financial collapse" if member countries continue to delay their fee payments. The organisation may run out of money as soon as July, which could impact its operations worldwide. This situation highlights ongoing challenges in funding international bodies and may prompt urgent calls for member states to meet their financial obligations.

US Federal Reserve Leadership Change

Former US president Donald Trump has chosen economist Kevin Warsh to lead the Federal Reserve. Warsh, who is 55 years old, had been considered for the Fed chair position during Trump's first term. His appointment will be closely watched by economists and markets, as the Federal Reserve plays a crucial role in US monetary policy, affecting inflation, interest rates, and economic growth.

Tribal Integration Deal in Syria

The Syrian government has reached an agreement with Kurdish-led forces to slowly integrate these forces and their administration into the Syrian state. This deal marks a significant step toward resolving tensions and could influence the political and security landscape in the region, potentially leading to increased stability over time.

Conflicts and Legal Developments

Several notable legal and political incidents occurred recently:

- Luigi Mangione, 27, will not face the death penalty if convicted, after a judge dismissed two federal counts against him. However, he still faces state murder charges.

- Former CNN host Don Lemon was arrested during an anti-ICE protest at a church in Minnesota. Lemon said he was covering the event as a journalist.

- Mamadou Hawa Gassama, a Malian lawmaker, was jailed in Ivory Coast for insulting the country’s president Alassane Ouattara by calling him a "tyrant" and "an enemy of Mali".

In addition, diplomatic tensions escalated when South Africa and Israel expelled each other’s envoys. South Africa accused Israel’s envoy of insulting President Ramaphosa, while Israel called the expulsion baseless.

Ukraine and Russia Developments

Ukraine experienced a relatively quiet night amid ongoing conflict. Donald Trump stated that Russian President Vladimir Putin will not attack Ukrainian cities. It was reported that the US president requested Russia pause strikes on Kyiv until 1 February, citing cold weather as a reason. This temporary pause could affect the conflict’s dynamics in the short term but uncertainty remains about future escalations.

Myanmar Election Violence and Legal Actions in the US

The UN reported that at least 170 people were killed in air strikes during Myanmar's recent election period, with more than 400 strikes recorded by credible sources. The violence has drawn international criticism and raises concerns about the election’s fairness and security.

In the US, Donald Trump and his sons have filed a lawsuit against the IRS and Treasury Department seeking $10 billion in damages. The suit follows leaked tax information concerning Trump and his family that was published in the media, raising questions about privacy and government transparency.
