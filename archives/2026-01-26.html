US Winter Storm Causes Power Outages and Fatalities

A major winter storm has swept across the United States, leading to widespread power outages and fatalities. At least seven people have died as nearly 800,000 homes and businesses lost electricity. The storm has prompted nearly half of US states to declare emergencies, with official warnings about "life threatening conditions" that could impact around 180 million people. Authorities continue to respond to the challenging weather that is affecting much of the country’s infrastructure and daily life.

Minneapolis Faces Tensions After Federal Shootings

Minneapolis is grappling with community anger and concern following two recent deadly shootings involving federal agents. The latest incident involved an immigration agent fatally shooting a man named Alex Pretti. The US president acknowledged the event and said his administration is "reviewing everything" related to the case. Residents describe the situation as "horrifying," expressing frustration over the violence and its impact on the city’s atmosphere. The events highlight ongoing tensions between local communities and federal law enforcement.

Philippines Ferry Disaster Leaves Dozens Missing

Tragedy struck in the Philippines when a ferry carrying about 300 passengers sank, resulting in at least 18 deaths. The local coast guard is actively searching for 24 individuals still reported missing following the accident involving the MV Trisha Kerstin. Rescue operations continue amid efforts to understand the cause of the sinking and to ensure the safety of other vessels in the area.

Japan Returns Pandas to China as Diplomatic Relations Worsen

Japan has returned twin panda cubs to China, marking the first time since 1972 that Japan will have no pandas in its zoos. The departure of the cubs comes amid a downturn in diplomatic relations between the two countries. The pandas have been a popular attraction and symbol of goodwill. This move may reflect broader geopolitical shifts affecting cultural and diplomatic ties in the region.

Gold Prices Reach Record High Amid Uncertainty

Gold prices have surged past $5,000 per ounce for the first time ever, continuing a historic rally. Investors often turn to gold as a "safe-haven" asset during periods of uncertainty and low interest rates. This milestone underscores current economic concerns and may influence investment strategies globally as markets navigate ongoing volatility.

Japan’s Self-Defense Forces Address Military Sexual Abuse Case

In a landmark case in Japan, former soldier Rina Gonoi has reached settlements related to sexual assault she suffered while serving in the country's Self-Defense Forces. The case has drawn attention to issues of abuse within the military and may encourage further scrutiny and reforms to protect service members from harassment and assault.
