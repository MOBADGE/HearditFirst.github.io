Violence and Security Concerns

Social Media Regulation in France

France is advancing plans to ban children under 15 from using social media. Prime Minister Sébastien Lecornu has announced a fast-track legislative process to have the policy enacted by September. This move, endorsed by President Emmanuel Macron, reflects growing concerns about young people's exposure to social media.

Investigations into Online Content

The European Union has launched an investigation into Elon Musk's social media platform, X, regarding the display of manipulated sexually explicit images. The EU will assess whether such deepfake content has been shown to users within its member countries. This inquiry underscores global efforts to regulate AI-driven misinformation and explicit media on digital platforms.

International Relations and Cultural Moments

Japan has returned twin panda cubs to China, marking the first time since 1972 that the country will have no pandas. The return comes amid worsening diplomatic relations between Japan and China. The departure was marked by emotional farewells, highlighting the cultural significance of the animals.

In India, tributes have been paid to Sir Mark Tully, a respected BBC broadcaster who lived in Delhi after his career with the BBC. His cremation was held recently, reflecting his enduring connection to the country and its people.
