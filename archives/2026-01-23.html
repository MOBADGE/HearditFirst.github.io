Global Politics

US President Donald Trump made headlines with two significant developments related to international relations. He sparked controversy by claiming that NATO troops "stayed a little back" and avoided the front line during the Afghanistan war. This remark drew anger as it appeared to question the commitment of allied forces during a major conflict. Additionally, Trump withdrew an invitation for Canada to join the so-called "Board of Peace." No explicit reason was given for this decision, but it follows comments from Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney warning of a "rupture" in the US-led global order. These actions could signal increasing tensions between the US and its allies, raising questions about the future of longstanding partnerships.

This event highlights ongoing issues with violence and instability in parts of Nigeria, drawing attention to the urgent need for protective measures for vulnerable communities.
