Global Security and International Relations

Tensions have risen with two recent developments involving former US president Donald Trump. Trump claimed that NATO troops "stayed a little back" during the war in Afghanistan, a statement described as disappointing by a government minister. This comment touches on ongoing debates about allied contributions in past conflicts. Additionally, Trump has withdrawn Canada's invitation to a forum referred to as the "Board of Peace," without providing a specific reason. This move follows comments by Canada's Prime Minister Mark Carney warning of a "rupture" in the US-led global order, highlighting strains in international cooperation.

In another international security matter, Egisto Ott, a former intelligence officer in Austria, is on trial accused of spying for Russia. The 63-year-old denies the charges of handing over sensitive information to Russian agents. This case unfolds amid broader concerns over espionage and intelligence leaks in Europe.

Nigeria Church Abduction

A violent attack in a Nigerian village has left around 160 people missing after gunmen raided three churches. One victim described the chaotic and bloody escape amid the raid. This incident adds to the ongoing security challenges faced by communities in the region, where such abductions and attacks have become tragically frequent. The fate of the missing people remains uncertain as authorities respond to the crisis.
