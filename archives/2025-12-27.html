Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 27, 2025

Conflict in Ukraine

A sustained 10-hour missile and drone attack struck Kyiv, Ukraine’s capital, resulting in two deaths and 32 injuries, according to local officials. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky commented that the raid indicated Russia’s unwillingness to pursue peace. The attack underscores ongoing tensions and violence in the region, with potential implications for further instability and diplomatic efforts.

Ceasefire in Southeast Asia

After weeks of violent clashes between Thailand and Cambodia, a ceasefire has been established. The fighting had displaced nearly one million people following the resumption of hostilities earlier this month. This truce represents a critical step toward reducing tensions and addressing the humanitarian crisis caused by the conflict.

Severe Weather Disrupts Travel in New York

A major winter storm in New York led to widespread travel disruptions, with hundreds of flights canceled and over 3,000 delayed. The snowstorm has caused significant chaos for travelers and highlights the challenges of winter weather management in major transport hubs.

Security and Counterterrorism Developments

Authorities in Italy have arrested nine people accused of raising approximately €7 million (£6 million) over two years for the militant group Hamas. This case marks a significant law enforcement action against alleged funding of terrorism in Europe.

Meanwhile, the United States conducted air strikes against Islamic State targets in Nigeria. Former US President Donald Trump said he ordered the attacks as "a Christmas present," a statement that Nigerian officials have contested. These strikes demonstrate ongoing international efforts against extremist groups.

Accidents and Natural Events

Tragic accidents have marked the day in several countries. In central Greece, four hikers—three men and one woman—were found dead after an avalanche. The search began following their disappearance on Christmas Day.

In Japan, a massive 50-vehicle pile-up on a central highway resulted in two deaths and 26 injuries. Such large-scale traffic incidents highlight road safety concerns during hazardous conditions.

On a lighter note, a meteor was visible streaking across the night sky over Japan’s Mount Fuji, offering an unusual natural spectacle to onlookers.

Upcoming Elections in Africa

Two African countries, Guinea and the Central African Republic, recently held elections involving notable figures: a general and a mathematician. Both leaders seek to consolidate power following the polls, a development that could influence political dynamics in the region. The outcomes may impact stability and governance in their respective countries.
