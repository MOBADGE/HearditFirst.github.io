Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 27, 2025

Conflict in Ukraine

Kyiv, the capital of Ukraine, was hit by a prolonged 10-hour missile and drone attack, resulting in two deaths and injuring 32 people, according to local officials. Ukraine’s leader expressed that this assault demonstrates that Russia is not seeking peace. The attack highlights ongoing tensions and violence in the region, intensifying concerns about the future stability and security in Ukraine.

Ceasefire Between Thailand and Cambodia

After weeks of deadly clashes that displaced nearly one million people, Thailand and Cambodia have agreed to a ceasefire. The renewed fighting began earlier this month following a breakdown of a previous truce. This latest ceasefire offers hope for easing tensions and addressing humanitarian needs caused by the conflict between the neighboring countries.

Severe Winter Weather Disrupts New York

A winter storm has caused major travel disruptions in New York, with hundreds of flights cancelled and more than 3,000 delayed. The heavy snowfall has contributed to chaotic conditions across the city and surrounding areas, affecting holiday travel and daily commutes. Authorities and travelers are continuing to monitor the weather for further impacts.

Arrests in Italy Linked to Hamas Funding

Italian police have arrested nine individuals accused of raising around €7 million over two years to support Hamas, a militant group. The arrests follow an investigation into fundraising activities, aiming to curb financial support for the group’s operations. This case underscores ongoing concerns about the financing of militant organizations in Europe.

Natural Disasters and Accidents

In multiple incidents around the world, severe weather and accidents have led to fatalities and injuries. In Greece, four hikers—three men and one woman—were found dead after an avalanche in central regions during a search operation launched on Christmas Day. In central Japan, a 50-vehicle pile-up on a highway has left two people dead and 26 injured. These events highlight the dangers posed by severe winter conditions and the importance of safety measures.

African Elections Highlight Power Struggles

Elections recently held in Guinea and the Central African Republic have set the stage for leaders aiming to consolidate power. Among the contenders are a general and a mathematician, each representing different pathways to leadership in these countries. The outcomes of these elections could shape political dynamics and governance in the region moving forward.
