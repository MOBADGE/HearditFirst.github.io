Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 23, 2025

Conflict in Ukraine

Russia launched a large-scale missile and drone attack across multiple Ukrainian cities, resulting in three deaths. The strikes have caused widespread power outages in several regions, affecting the delivery of essential services. This escalation in the conflict marks a significant intensification of military actions, with potential implications for civilian safety and infrastructure stability in Ukraine.

US Political and Military Developments

The US president confirmed plans to retain or sell oil seized from tankers intercepted off Venezuela’s coast. This move follows recent actions by US authorities involving these vessels. Additionally, the president announced intentions to restart American shipbuilding programs by developing a new "Golden Fleet" of battleships named after himself. This initiative aims to enhance naval strength amid concerns about China's growing maritime capabilities.

Tragic Accidents and Disasters

A plane crash in Texas killed five people, including a child burns victim being transferred from Mexico to the US by a charity. In Laos, a ferry sank on the Mekong River, leading to panic as tourists scrambled to safety. At least one local woman and two children are feared dead, underscoring ongoing safety challenges in river transportation.

Technology and Security

Amazon reported blocking around 1,800 job applications from suspected North Korean agents attempting to secure remote IT positions using stolen or fake identities. This highlights continued cybersecurity threats facing large companies from state-sponsored actors.

Vince Zampella, co-creator of the popular Call of Duty video game series, died in a car crash in Los Angeles when his vehicle caught fire. His passing marks a loss for the video gaming community and industry.

Health and Media Updates

The US regulator approved the first pill form of the Wegovy weight-loss drug, moving beyond the existing injection method. This development could increase accessibility and convenience for people seeking medical weight management.

In Israel, parliament passed a bill extending temporary powers to close foreign broadcasters deemed a security risk. These measures were initially introduced during the Gaza war and now have been broadened, reflecting ongoing concerns about media influence and national security during conflict periods.

Human Interest

Jack Hibbert, an officer who was shot in the head and shoulder during an attack in Bondi, has been released from hospital and is home for Christmas. His family described his recovery and return as a "miracle," emphasizing his resilience after a serious injury.
