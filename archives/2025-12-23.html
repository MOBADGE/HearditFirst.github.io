December 23, 2025

International Security and Conflicts

Libya’s army chief, General Mohammed Ali Ahmed al-Haddad, died in a plane crash in Turkey after a jet lost contact shortly after taking off from Ankara. The crash also involved four other people on board. Meanwhile, in eastern Ukraine, Russian forces captured the town of Siversk. This advance brings them closer to seizing the last Ukrainian-controlled cities in the Donetsk region’s “fortress belt,” intensifying the conflict there.

In central Nigeria, 28 Muslim travellers, including women and children, were abducted by armed gunmen while en route to a religious festival. This incident adds to ongoing security concerns in the region.

Human Rights and Justice

United Nations experts have called on Iran to halt the execution of Zahra Tabari, a 67-year-old woman activist sentenced to death after a brief trial reportedly lasting less than ten minutes. Her family has highlighted the rapid proceedings, raising concerns about due process and human rights protections.

US Politics and Economy

Newly released documents from the US House Committee on Oversight and Reform include an email sent to Ghislaine Maxwell from an individual identified only as “A,” requesting “inappropriate friends,” adding further detail to the ongoing public examination of Jeffrey Epstein’s network. Despite this, conservative political figures and influencers, including supporters of President Donald Trump, have remained notably quiet following the release. Political analysts suggest the developments could still pose risks for Trump politically.

In economic news, the US economy expanded at its fastest rate in two years, driven by strong consumer spending. This growth exceeded expectations and reflects robust economic activity in the latter part of the year.

Additionally, the US president stated that the government plans to keep or sell oil seized from Venezuela and intends to retain tankers intercepted near Venezuelan waters. This stance continues the administration’s approach to Venezuelan assets amid ongoing tensions.

Regional Relations and Public Order

Tensions between India and Bangladesh have escalated following recent deadly protests in Bangladesh. These events have further strained the already fragile relations between the two neighboring countries, indicating possible challenges ahead for diplomatic engagement and regional stability.

Human Interest

In Australia, police officer Jack Hibbert has returned home for Christmas after surviving gunshot wounds to the head and shoulder during an attack in Bondi. His family described his recovery and homecoming as “a miracle,” underscoring the personal resilience behind public safety challenges.
