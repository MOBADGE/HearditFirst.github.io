Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 23, 2025

Public Safety and Rescue News

Jack Hibbert, a police officer who was shot in the head and shoulder during an attack in Bondi, has returned home for Christmas. His family described his recovery and homecoming as a "miracle," underscoring the seriousness of his injuries and the progress in his recovery. In Laos, a ferry sank on the Mekong River, sparking panic and screams for help among passengers. Rescue efforts saved many tourists, but a local woman and two children are feared dead following the disaster.

International Conflict Update

Russia has intensified its attacks on the Ukrainian region of Odesa, a key area for maritime trade. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky warned that these strikes are part of Moscow’s attempt to block Ukraine’s access to crucial maritime logistics, which could further impact the country’s ability to trade and receive supplies amid ongoing tensions.

US Policy and Security Developments

The US is taking further actions related to oil shipments connected to Venezuela. The president announced the pursuit of a third oil tanker linked to the country, following a recent order to block sanctioned tankers from entering or leaving Venezuelan ports. In a separate announcement, the US president unveiled plans to boost American naval capacity through a new "Golden Fleet" of battleships, which includes naming some ships after himself. This initiative aims to revive American shipbuilding to compete with China’s naval presence.

In cybersecurity news, Amazon disclosed that it blocked 1,800 job applications from individuals suspected of being North Korean agents. These applicants attempted to secure remote IT positions by using stolen or fake identities, highlighting ongoing cybersecurity risks and attempts at infiltration through employment channels.

Health and Technology Innovation

The US regulator has approved the first pill form of Wegovy, a weight-loss drug previously available only as an injection. This approval represents a significant shift in obesity treatment options, making the drug more accessible and easier to use for patients seeking medical weight loss solutions.

Meanwhile, efforts continue to find environmentally friendly ways to manage the large cooling demands of new data centers. Innovative approaches, including the use of showers and baths, are being explored to keep technology cool while addressing energy efficiency challenges.

Cultural and Miscellaneous

Vince Zampella, a co-creator of the popular video game Call of Duty, tragically died in a car crash in Los Angeles. The incident involved his vehicle catching fire after the crash, marking the loss of a significant figure in the gaming industry.

Lastly, political interest remains in the recent release of Epstein-related files. While conservative groups have been notably quiet, the documents could still pose political risks for the US president and the "MAGA" movement, indicating ongoing public and political scrutiny.
