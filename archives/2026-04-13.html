Hungary’s Political Shift

Viktor Orbán’s 16-year tenure as Hungary’s leader has come to an end after voters elected Péter Magyar, a 45-year-old former party insider, by a large margin. Magyar successfully persuaded a majority of Hungarians to support a change in leadership, marking a significant political shift in the country. This result has been welcomed broadly across Europe, as Orbán was seen as a close ally of Vladimir Putin, and his departure is viewed as a setback for Moscow’s influence inside the European Union.

Developments in Latin America and Canada

In Peru, voting to elect a new president and members of Congress has extended into a second day. This delay comes after technical and logistical difficulties disrupted the election process. Meanwhile, in Canada, Prime Minister Carney is close to securing a majority government, pending the outcome of three by-elections. One additional seat could strengthen Carney’s hold on power after a number of Conservative lawmakers left their party.

Tensions and Human Rights in Iran

Iran has carried out the highest number of executions seen in decades, according to reports from human rights organizations. This increase follows widespread anti-government protests earlier this year. Observers warn that many more executions could take place in the coming months amid ongoing unrest and government crackdowns.
