April 13, 2026

Global Politics

Hungary's next prime minister, Péter Magyar, said that if Russian President Vladimir Putin called, he would tell him to stop the war in Ukraine. However, Magyar said he would not initiate such a call himself. Russia has expressed interest in maintaining "pragmatic" relations with Hungary amid the ongoing conflict.

In Peru, the election process to choose a new president and members of Congress has extended into a second day due to significant technical and logistical problems with ballot delivery. Voters continued casting ballots on Monday as authorities worked through the disruptions.

The Philippines has accused Chinese fishermen of dumping cyanide in the South China Sea, claiming the poison is used to kill fish and deprive stationed troops of food. China rejected the allegations, labeling them a "farce," highlighting ongoing tensions in the region.

Middle East and Africa

BBC correspondent Hugo Bachega reported from Nabatieh, Lebanon, where paramedics are responding after Israeli strikes. The city, once vibrant, is now largely deserted as people flee the violence. This coverage highlights the human impact of recent military actions in the region.

Pope Leo XIV became the first pope to visit Algeria, marking the start of a major tour of Africa. An aide said the pontiff aims to draw global attention to Africa, a continent seen as crucial to the future of the Catholic Church.

In Iran, human rights organisations have reported that executions reached the highest numbers in decades following widespread anti-government protests earlier this year. The warnings suggest that even more executions could occur as the government continues to crack down on dissent.

Europe

The wife of Spain’s prime minister, Begoña Gómez, has been charged with corruption after a two-year investigation. She is accused of using her relationship with Pedro Sánchez to advance her private career.

Italian Prime Minister condemned former US President Donald Trump for his criticism of Pope Leo XIV, calling the remarks "unacceptable." Despite close ties, Trump has refused to apologise to the pontiff, whom he described as "very weak."

In Nigeria, former oil minister Diezani Alison-Madueke denied allegations of corruption. She is accused of living a luxurious lifestyle in the UK in properties reportedly financed and refurbished by figures within the oil industry.

Entertainment and Society

Pop singer Britney Spears voluntarily entered a rehabilitation facility following her recent arrest for driving under the influence. This step comes just three weeks before her upcoming court date, as she seeks treatment.
