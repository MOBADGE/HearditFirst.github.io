Hungary Sees Shift in Leadership

Viktor Orbán's 16-year tenure as Hungary's leader has come to an end after a decisive election victory by Péter Magyar, a 45-year-old former insider of Orbán's party. Magyar managed to win the support of a majority of Hungarian voters, signaling a significant political change in the country. This outcome could lead to new directions in Hungary's policies and its role in European affairs.

Ongoing Violence and Tensions

In Israel, a recent poll reveals that while many citizens feel weary from ongoing conflict, most oppose calls for a ceasefire with Iran. The respondents expressed doubt that Hezbollah or Iran had been significantly weakened by recent Israeli and US military actions. This persistent stance may affect how the situation in the region develops in the near future.

Meanwhile, Iran continues to experience a high rate of executions, reportedly the highest in decades. Human rights organizations caution that this number may rise following widespread anti-government protests earlier this year. The situation points to ongoing internal unrest and government crackdowns in the country.

Italian-US Relations Tested Over Pope Criticism

Despite being close allies, Italy's prime minister has publicly condemned US president Donald Trump for his criticism of Pope Leo XIV, calling it "unacceptable." Trump has declined to apologize to the pope, whom he described as "very weak." This disagreement highlights a rare moment of tension between the two allies, which could have diplomatic implications.

Tragedy and Challenges in Sports and Politics

In Ghana, a football team bus was attacked by armed assailants identified as masked gunmen. The violence resulted in the death of a team member. This tragic event raises concerns about security for athletes and public safety in the region.

Peru's presidential and congressional elections have faced logistical challenges, causing voting to extend into a second day. Problems with ballot deliveries have disrupted the process, potentially impacting the election's outcome and public confidence in the electoral system.

In Canada, Prime Minister Carney might soon secure a Liberal majority government. With votes being counted in three by-elections, one more seat is needed to strengthen his position after several members of the Conservative Party switched allegiance. This development could affect the political landscape and governance in Canada.

Entertainment and Legal Matters

Famous singer Britney Spears has voluntarily entered a rehabilitation facility, ahead of a court appearance related to a driving under the influence arrest. This move may shape her legal and personal trajectory in the coming weeks.

In India, beloved singer Asha Bhosle was cremated with full state honours following her death at the age of 92. Mourners gathered to sing one of her hits, marking the end of an era for Indian music fans.
