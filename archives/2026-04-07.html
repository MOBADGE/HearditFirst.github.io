International Relations

US Vice-President JD Vance has arrived in Hungary to show support for the re-election campaign of Hungarian leader Viktor Orban. This visit highlights ongoing White House backing for Orban ahead of Hungary’s elections. The trip may influence diplomatic ties between the US and Hungary and signals Washington’s interest in the region’s political landscape.

Meanwhile, in East Asia, Taiwan’s opposition leader Cheng Li-wun has made her first visit to China since 2016. Invited by Chinese President Xi Jinping, Cheng expressed hope to act as a “bridge for peace” between Taiwan and China. This visit could affect cross-strait relations, which remain sensitive and closely watched internationally.

Security and Conflict

In Nigeria, there is dispute over claims made by the army following an Easter-day attack. The Nigerian army reported rescuing 31 people and recovering the bodies of five civilians killed by gunmen at the scene. However, local witnesses have questioned the army’s account. This incident underscores ongoing security challenges in parts of Nigeria and raises concerns about the accuracy of official reports.

Technology and Innovation

The global competition in artificial intelligence continues with China and the US each currently leading in different aspects of AI development. Both countries are working to avoid allowing the other to dominate this rapidly evolving field. Experts note that the dynamics of this competition may change as new technologies emerge, affecting economic and geopolitical power balances in the years ahead.
