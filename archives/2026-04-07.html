April 07, 2026

International Incidents and Security

A shooting at the Israeli consulate in Istanbul resulted in one gunman killed and two people injured. The consulate has been empty for over two years, with no Israeli diplomats currently in Turkey. Meanwhile, the World Health Organization (WHO) has suspended medical evacuations in Gaza after Israeli troops killed a contractor driving a vehicle they said posed an immediate threat.

Political Developments

In Zimbabwe, a draft law proposed by the ruling party would eliminate direct presidential elections, sparking controversy and disagreement among the population. In France, a French couple detained in Iran for over three years—Cecile Kohler and Jacques Paris—have now been allowed to leave the country, according to statements from the French president.

Legal Updates

Adriana Rivas, a Chilean woman accused of kidnappings during the Pinochet era, has lost her extradition battle in Australia, where she had been living since the 1970s. In Australia, Ben Roberts-Smith, the country’s most-decorated living soldier, has been charged with alleged war crimes. He denies the allegations and previously lost a defamation case related to similar accusations.

Transportation Accident

A train traveling at 99 mph collided with a lorry in northern France, killing the train driver. Authorities have taken the lorry driver into custody as investigations continue.

Culture and Entertainment

The Wireless Festival in the UK has been cancelled after Kanye West, who was scheduled to headline in July, was blocked from entering the country. This followed criticism over West’s past antisemitic remarks.

Space Exploration

NASA’s Artemis II mission has proceeded nearly flawlessly so far, prompting discussion about whether the test flight demonstrates readiness to send humans back to the lunar surface. This mission marks a key step in efforts to return astronauts to the Moon.
