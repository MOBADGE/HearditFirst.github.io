Heard It First – Daily Digest

April 07, 2026

International Incidents

A shooting occurred at the Israeli consulate in Istanbul, Turkey, resulting in one gunman killed and two others injured. The consulate has been empty for over two years, as no Israeli diplomats are currently in Turkey. Meanwhile, the World Health Organization (WHO) has suspended medical evacuations from Gaza after Israeli troops killed a contractor associated with the operations. The Israeli military stated that troops fired at the vehicle the contractor was driving because they perceived it as an immediate threat.

Political Developments

In Budapest, US Vice-President JD Vance publicly supported Hungarian leader Viktor Orbán's re-election bid during a campaign visit. Vance also criticized the European Union in his remarks. In Vietnam, To Lam has consolidated his power further by being unanimously voted president while already serving as the communist party chief, marking an unusual concentration of authority in the country’s leadership.

Legal and Crime News

Adriana Rivas, a Chilean woman accused of kidnappings dating back to the Pinochet era, has lost her extradition battle in Australia. Rivas had moved to Australia in the 1970s and worked there as a nanny and cleaner. In Australia, Ben Roberts-Smith, the country's most-decorated living soldier, has been charged with alleged war crimes. Roberts-Smith denies all wrongdoing and previously lost a significant defamation case related to accusations of murder during his military service.

Transportation Accident

A fatal accident took place in northern France when a train traveling at 99 mph collided with a lorry, killing the train driver. Authorities have taken the lorry driver into custody as investigations continue into the cause of the crash.

Other News

NASA released the first images from its Moon fly-by mission, showing an "Earthrise" view and a solar eclipse witnessed by the astronauts. In the Bahamas, police are searching for a US woman who reportedly fell off a boat and was swept out to sea on Saturday night; her husband reported the incident to authorities. In Florida, rapper Offset was hospitalized after being shot; he is currently in stable condition and two people have been detained in connection with the shooting.
