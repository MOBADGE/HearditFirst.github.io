US-Hungary Relations

JD Vance, a US vice-president, is visiting Hungary to support Prime Minister Viktor Orbán’s bid for re-election. This visit highlights continued White House backing for Orbán, who has been a controversial figure in European politics. The trip signals strengthened ties between the US and Hungary ahead of the upcoming election, potentially influencing Hungary's political landscape and its relations with both Europe and the US in the near future.

Taiwan-China Engagement

Cheng Li-wun, a Taiwan opposition leader, has made her first visit to China since 2016 after accepting an invitation from Chinese President Xi Jinping. Cheng expressed hopes to serve as a "bridge for peace" between the two sides, which have been marked by tension and political isolation. This visit could open new channels for dialogue, though broader cross-strait relations remain complex and closely watched internationally.

AI Competition Between China and the US

China and the US are engaged in a high-stakes competition in artificial intelligence, with each nation leading in different aspects of AI development. China is noted for excelling in one area of the AI race, while the US leads in another, and both are determined to prevent the other from gaining supremacy. The rivalry is dynamic and may evolve as new technologies and strategies emerge, underscoring the strategic importance of AI in global power balance and innovation.
