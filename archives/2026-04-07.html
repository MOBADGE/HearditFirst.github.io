Heard It First – Daily Digest

April 07, 2026

Entertainment and Events

The Wireless Festival in the UK has been cancelled following controversy over Kanye West, who was scheduled to headline the event in July. West drew criticism due to his past antisemitic comments, and he was subsequently blocked from entering the UK. Organizers decided to cancel the festival entirely as a result.

Space Exploration

NASA’s Artemis II mission, currently in progress, has been nearly flawless so far. The mission is a critical test flight aiming to demonstrate readiness for sending humans back to the Moon’s surface. Experts are assessing whether the success of Artemis II signals that NASA is prepared to carry out a crewed lunar landing in the near future.

International Affairs

A French couple, Cecile Kohler and Jacques Paris, have been allowed to leave Iran after spending more than three years in jail. Their release was confirmed by the French president, and the couple is now on their way home. This development marks the end of a prolonged and sensitive diplomatic situation.

Meanwhile, Zimbabwe faces internal conflict after its ruling party proposed a draft law that would eliminate presidential elections. This move has sparked significant disagreement among Zimbabweans, as it would remove citizens’ ability to elect their president, raising concerns about democratic governance in the country.

Security and Conflict

In Istanbul, a shooting incident at the Israeli consulate left one gunman dead and two others injured. The consulate has been empty of Israeli diplomats for two and a half years, and no Israeli diplomats are currently in Turkey. The circumstances surrounding the attack and its motivations remain under investigation.

Separately, the World Health Organization (WHO) has suspended medical evacuations from Gaza after an Israeli military incident in which troops shot a vehicle driven by a Palestinian contractor. Israeli forces stated that the vehicle was perceived as an “immediate threat,” resulting in the contractor’s death.

Legal and Crime News

In France, one person died after a train traveling at 99 mph collided with a lorry in northern France. The train driver was fatally injured during the accident, and the lorry driver has been taken into custody for questioning.

In another legal case, Adriana Rivas, a Chilean woman accused of kidnappings during the Pinochet era, lost an extradition battle in Australia. Rivas had relocated to Australia in the 1970s and worked as a nanny and cleaner before facing charges related to the historical cases.

In Australia, Ben Roberts-Smith, the country’s most-decorated living soldier, has been charged over alleged war crimes. Roberts-Smith denies all wrongdoing. He had previously lost a major defamation case connected to alleged murders, and these new legal proceedings add to ongoing scrutiny of his military record.
