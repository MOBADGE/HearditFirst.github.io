Heard It First – Daily Digest

March 04, 2026

Global Politics

Spain’s prime minister Pedro Sánchez responded firmly to US President Donald Trump’s threat to sever trade relations with Spain. Sánchez rejected the threat, emphasizing a commitment to peace with the phrase “no to war.” This exchange reflects ongoing tensions between the two countries amid trade disagreements. Meanwhile, a Greek court upheld verdicts against Neo-Nazi party Golden Dawn, reaffirming the criminal organisation conviction of its founder Nikos Mihaloliakos and several officials. This ruling comes nearly six years after the original trial and highlights continued efforts to hold extremist groups accountable.

Security and Intelligence

In the UK, the Metropolitan Police have identified three men suspected of assisting Chinese intelligence services. An MP, whose husband was among those arrested, stated she has seen no evidence to suggest he has broken any laws. This development points to heightened concerns about foreign espionage activities on British soil and ongoing investigations targeting possible intelligence offenses.

Trade and Economy

The US Treasury has announced plans to increase global tariffs on imports, with Scott Bessent indicating the hike will likely occur within the week. This move will raise tariffs from the current 10% rate, signaling a potential shift toward more protectionist trade policies. The increase may affect international trade dynamics and could prompt reactions from US trading partners.

Middle East and Africa

Israel has instructed civilians in large parts of southern Lebanon to evacuate amid retaliatory actions against Hezbollah rocket attacks. Many displaced residents are seeking shelter in tents, parks, and vehicles, highlighting the ongoing security tensions in the region. In response to the escalating Middle East conflict, Nigeria’s Christian Pilgrimage Commission has temporarily halted pilgrimages to the Holy Land to prioritise the safety and comfort of Nigerian pilgrims.

Meanwhile, South Africa mourns the loss of Mosiuoa Lekota, a former defence minister and Robben Island prisoner who was incarcerated alongside Nelson Mandela during apartheid. His death marks the passing of a significant figure in the country’s struggle for freedom.

Health and Safety

In Nigeria, several doctors have been suspended following the death of author Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie’s 21-month-old son in January due to complications during medical procedures at a private Lagos hospital. The suspensions reflect scrutiny over medical care standards.

In Japan, an eight-year-old Australian girl died in a snowmobile accident at a ski resort. This tragedy marks the fourth Australian death at Japanese ski resorts this year, raising concerns about tourist safety in winter sports areas.

Technology and Privacy

TikTok has announced it will not implement end-to-end encryption for direct messages, unlike platforms such as WhatsApp and Messenger. The company cited user safety risks as the primary reason for this decision. This stance adds to ongoing debates about privacy, security, and the responsibilities of social media platforms in protecting user communications.
