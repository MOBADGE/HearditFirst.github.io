Heard It First – Daily Digest

March 04, 2026

Middle East Conflict Escalates

Iran has postponed the funeral of Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei, originally planned as a three-day ceremony in Tehran, to allow for further preparations. This comes amid ongoing US and Israeli military bombardments in the region. In related developments, the US military reportedly sank an Iranian warship in the Indian Ocean, with Sri Lankan authorities fearing around 140 people are missing after the vessel went down near their coast. Meanwhile, Israel has instructed civilians in large parts of southern Lebanon to evacuate ahead of planned retaliatory attacks following rocket fire from Hezbollah. Many displaced residents are taking shelter in tents, parks, and vehicles, highlighting the rising tensions and humanitarian impact in the area.

Diplomatic and Military Developments

In the US political arena, a Senate vote to restrict President Donald Trump’s war powers regarding Iran failed to pass. The proposed measure, which would have required congressional approval before further military action, was blocked largely along party lines. Additionally, Spain’s Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez strongly rejected President Trump’s threat to sever trade relations with Spain, emphasizing a preference for peace over conflict.

Global Maritime Incidents

Tensions are also rising on the Mediterranean Sea where a tanker, the Arctic Metagaz, sank between Libya and Malta after being struck by explosions and fire. Russian officials have blamed Ukrainian naval drones for the incident. These maritime events reflect the broader instability affecting seas critical for global trade and energy supplies.

Energy and Infrastructure Challenges in Cuba

Cuba is experiencing widespread power outages amid a deepening fuel crisis. The shortages have been worsened by a US oil blockade following the seizure of Venezuela’s President Nicolás Maduro. This energy crisis is leading to increased hardship for millions of Cubans, affecting daily life and the island’s economy.

Notable Death in South Africa

Mosiuoa Lekota, a long-serving former defence minister in South Africa and former prisoner on Robben Island alongside Nelson Mandela, has died. His passing marks the loss of a significant figure in South Africa’s political and liberation history.

Legal Rulings on Extremism in Greece

A Greek court has upheld the verdict that found the neo-Nazi Golden Dawn party and its founder Nikos Mihaloliakos guilty of operating a criminal organization. This decision comes almost six years after the original trial, reinforcing legal actions against far-right extremism in the country.
