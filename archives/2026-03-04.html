Heard It First – Daily Digest

March 04, 2026

International Relations

Spain’s prime minister Pedro Sánchez has responded firmly to a threat from US President Donald Trump to cut trade ties with Spain. Sánchez rejected the idea of trade severance, emphasizing a commitment to peace and diplomacy, saying “no to war.” This exchange highlights ongoing tensions in international trade relations and suggests that Spain is seeking to maintain positive economic engagement despite political pressure from the US.

Security and Legal Affairs

In the UK, three men have been arrested on suspicion of spying for China. The Metropolitan Police named them as suspects believed to be assisting Chinese intelligence services. This arrest underlines increasing concerns about foreign espionage activities within the UK.

Meanwhile, in Greece, a court has upheld a verdict finding the neo-Nazi party Golden Dawn a criminal organization. The party's founder, Nikos Mihaloliakos, along with other officials, were again found guilty nearly six years after the initial trial. This ruling reinforces efforts to combat extremist political groups in Europe.

Crime and Law Enforcement

The United States and Ecuador have launched a joint operation targeting drug trafficking networks. The US describes some of the targeted groups as designated terrorist organizations operating in Ecuador. This collaboration indicates a continued focus on combating narcotics flow and associated security threats in South America.

In South Korea, a woman and several doctors were found guilty of the murder of a newborn baby. The case has sparked public attention due to the country’s lack of regulation about how late into pregnancy an abortion can be performed, raising questions about legal and ethical frameworks relating to reproductive rights.

Science and Health

A new study sheds light on changes in pregnant women’s brains, finding that grey matter is shed during pregnancy to prepare for motherhood. Scientists say this challenges the simplistic “baby brain” stereotype by showing that these brain changes are an important part of adapting to parenting.

Technology and Privacy

TikTok has announced it will not implement end-to-end encryption for direct messages on its platform, citing concerns that such privacy measures could put users at risk. This sets TikTok apart from rivals like WhatsApp and Messenger, which already offer this feature. The decision is part of ongoing debates about balancing user privacy with safety.

Accidents and Deaths Abroad

An eight-year-old Australian girl died in a snowmobile accident at a Japanese ski resort. This is the fourth Australian fatality at Japanese ski areas this year, drawing attention to safety concerns for international visitors engaging in winter sports.

Obituaries

Mosiuoa Lekota, a former South African defence minister and prisoner on Robben Island alongside Nelson Mandela, has died. Lekota was a prominent figure in South Africa’s history, and his passing marks the loss of a significant figure in the country’s struggle and governance.
