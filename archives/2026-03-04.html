March 04, 2026

Middle East Tensions and Conflicts

Iran has postponed the funeral of its supreme leader as US and Israeli military actions continue in the region. Officials in Tehran say more time is needed to prepare for the three-day ceremony where the leader will lie in state. Meanwhile, Israel has instructed civilians in large parts of southern Lebanon to evacuate ahead of retaliatory strikes following a rocket attack by Hezbollah. Many residents are seeking shelter in tents, parks, and cars amid the ongoing violence.

In related news, the Pentagon has identified four of six US soldiers killed in recent conflict involving Iran. The deaths occurred after a drone bypassed air defenses and struck a command center in Kuwait. The US Senate is scheduled to vote on a measure that would limit presidential war powers by requiring congressional approval for continued military operations, though the measure is not expected to pass.

International Trade and Economic Relations

Trade tensions are rising as the United States signals it is likely to raise global tariffs this week from the current 10% level. Scott Bessent of the US Treasury indicated increased tariffs could be implemented imminently, suggesting a more protectionist trade stance.

Spain’s prime minister, Pedro Sánchez, responded firmly to US President Donald Trump’s threat to end trade relations with Spain. Sánchez condemned the threat and stated a clear opposition to war, emphasizing the importance of maintaining peaceful and cooperative international relations.

Maritime Incident in the Mediterranean

A tanker named Arctic Metagaz sank in the Mediterranean Sea between Libya and Malta after an explosion and fire, according to Libyan officials. Russia has attributed the incident to Ukrainian naval drone activity. The sinking of the vessel adds to ongoing regional security concerns in the Mediterranean maritime routes.

South Africa and Notable Figures

Mosiuoa Lekota, a former defence minister of South Africa and a former prisoner alongside Nelson Mandela on Robben Island, has died. Lekota was notable for his long service in the country's defence sector and his involvement in South Africa’s political history.

Legal Developments in Greece

A Greek court has reaffirmed its verdict that the neo-Nazi political party Golden Dawn operates as a criminal organization. The ruling again found founder Nikos Mihaloliakos and other party members guilty, nearly six years after the original trial. This decision maintains legal pressure on the far-right group and underscores Greece’s stance against extremist political movements.

Health Sector Controversy in Nigeria

In Nigeria, several doctors were suspended following the death of the 21-month-old son of acclaimed author Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie. The child died in January due to complications from medical procedures at a private hospital in Lagos. The suspensions reflect ongoing concerns about healthcare standards and medical oversight in the country.
