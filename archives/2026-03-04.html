March 04, 2026

Middle East Conflict Escalates

Tensions and violence continue in the Middle East amid ongoing clashes. Iran has postponed the funeral for its supreme leader, citing the need for more preparations for the planned three-day ceremony in Tehran. Meanwhile, hostilities persist in the region: a U.S. submarine reportedly sank an Iranian warship in the Indian Ocean, resulting in about 140 people feared missing according to Sri Lanka's navy.

In southern Lebanon, Israel has ordered civilians to leave large areas ahead of planned attacks, following rocket strikes from Hezbollah. Many displaced people are seeking shelter in tents, parks, and cars. The Pentagon has identified four of six U.S. soldiers killed in a drone attack on a command center in Kuwait linked to the conflict. The ongoing violence underscores the fragile and volatile situation, with potential impacts on regional stability.

U.S. Politics and Military Developments

The U.S. Senate is set to vote on a measure to limit the executive branch’s war powers regarding the Iran conflict. The proposed legislation would require Congressional approval for any continuation of military operations and mandate the withdrawal of forces otherwise. However, the measure is not expected to pass.

Additionally, the U.S. Treasury has announced plans to raise global tariffs, likely later this week, from the current rate of 10%. This move signals a continuation of protectionist economic policies that may affect international trade dynamics.

International Trade Disputes

Tensions in trade relations have surfaced between the United States and Spain. Spain's Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez responded firmly to threats from U.S. President Donald Trump to cut trade ties with Spain. Sánchez's rebuttal emphasized opposition to conflict and highlighted the importance of maintaining cooperative economic relations.

Maritime Incidents in the Mediterranean

A Russian-linked tanker, the Arctic Metagaz, sank between Libya and Malta after explosions and a fire, Libyan officials report. Russia blames Ukrainian naval drones for the incident, marking another episode of maritime tensions amid the ongoing conflict in Eastern Europe and related regions.

Notable Death in South Africa and Other News

Mosiuoa Lekota, South Africa’s long-serving former defence minister and a former Robben Island prisoner alongside Nelson Mandela, has died. His passing marks the loss of a significant figure in South Africa’s political and liberation history.

In Nigeria, doctors at a private hospital in Lagos have been suspended following the death of the 21-month-old son of author Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie. The child died from complications during medical procedures earlier this year, prompting the suspensions as investigations continue.
