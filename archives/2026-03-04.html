March 04, 2026

International Trade and Diplomacy

Spain's prime minister, Pedro Sánchez, responded firmly to a threat from US President Donald Trump to sever trade relations between the two countries. Sánchez rejected the idea of escalating tensions, emphasizing a stance of "no to war." The dispute comes amid broader concerns about international trade policies as the US government, represented by Treasury official Scott Bessent, has indicated that higher tariffs are likely to be announced soon. The US plans to increase its global tariff on imports from the current 10%, though no specific timeline beyond "likely sometime this week" was given.

Middle East Tensions

Tensions remain high in the Middle East following a Hezbollah rocket attack. In response, Israel has asked civilians in large parts of southern Lebanon to evacuate, leading to significant displacement. Many civilians are seeking temporary shelter in tents, parks, and vehicles as preparations continue for possible Israeli retaliatory actions.

Meanwhile, the conflict has had ripple effects on international travel. Nigeria has suspended Christian pilgrimages to the Holy Land to ensure the "safety and comfort" of its pilgrims amid ongoing violence and instability in the region.

Additionally, the US military confirmed the deaths of four of six American soldiers killed in a recent conflict related to the region. These soldiers died after a drone attack successfully bypassed air defenses and struck a command center in Kuwait.

US Domestic Politics and Military Affairs

The US Senate is preparing to vote on a measure concerning the president's war powers in relation to the ongoing conflict involving Iran. The proposed legislation would require any continued deployment of US forces to receive congressional approval, effectively mandating a withdrawal of troops until such approval. However, the measure is not expected to pass.

In coordination with Ecuador, US forces have launched a joint operation targeting drug trafficking networks. The operation focuses on combatting organizations designated as terrorists within Ecuador.

Legal Developments in Greece

A Greek court has upheld a ruling declaring the neo-Nazi Golden Dawn party a criminal organization. The party's founder, Nikos Mihaloliakos, along with other officials, was found guilty once again, nearly six years after the original trial. This decision continues Greece's legal efforts against the far-right group, which has been associated with violence and extremist activities.

Notable Death in South Africa

Mosiuoa Lekota, a longtime former defence minister and former prisoner on Robben Island alongside Nelson Mandela, has died. His death marks the passing of a significant figure in South Africa's history, known for his roles in government and the anti-apartheid movement, linking directly to the country's struggle for freedom.
