Global Politics

Spain's prime minister, Pedro Sánchez, has responded firmly to a recent threat made by US President Donald Trump to end trade with Spain. Sánchez called for peace and said "no to war," underscoring the importance of continuing diplomatic and economic ties between the countries. This exchange reflects ongoing tensions but also signals Spain's intent to maintain open dialogue despite pressure.

Meanwhile, in Greece, a court has upheld the conviction of the far-right party Golden Dawn as a criminal organization. The party's founder, Nikos Mihaloliakos, along with other officials, were reaffirmed guilty almost six years after the original trial. This decision highlights Greece’s long-term legal efforts to address extremist political groups.

Security and Law Enforcement

In the UK, three men have been arrested on suspicion of spying for China. The Metropolitan Police allege the men assisted Chinese intelligence services, raising concerns about foreign espionage activities on British soil. The investigation is ongoing and authorities have not released the men’s names.

In South Korea, a woman and several doctors have been found guilty of murder in the case of a newborn baby. The situation has drawn significant public attention, occurring in a country without clear regulations on how late in pregnancy abortions can be performed. This verdict may prompt discussion on legal standards and medical ethics in South Korea.

International Cooperation on Crime

The United States and Ecuador have launched a joint operation targeting drug trafficking in Ecuador. Washington described the groups involved as designated terrorist organizations, emphasizing the priority given to combating illicit drug networks. This collaboration could influence regional security and drug control efforts.

Society and Science

A new study on pregnant women’s brains challenges the stereotype of "baby brain." Researchers found that pregnant women’s brains actually shed grey matter, a change thought to prepare them for motherhood. This scientific insight encourages a shift in how society understands cognitive changes during pregnancy.

In Japan, a tragic accident has occurred at a ski resort where an eight-year-old Australian girl was killed in a snowmobile incident. This marks the fourth Australian fatality at Japanese ski resorts this year, highlighting safety concerns for international visitors.

Technology and Privacy

TikTok has announced it will not implement end-to-end encryption for its direct messages, unlike platforms such as WhatsApp and Messenger. The company argues that this decision is to reduce risks for users, suggesting that encryption could make it harder to prevent harmful or illegal content. This stance adds to ongoing debates about privacy, security, and content moderation on social media platforms.
