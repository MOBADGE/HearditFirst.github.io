March 22, 2026

Middle East Conflict Escalates

Tensions in the Middle East have risen sharply after recent violent events. In the occupied West Bank, Israeli settlers have attacked Palestinian villages following the death of 18-year-old settler Yehuda Sherman, who was reportedly hit by a vehicle driven by a Palestinian while on his quad bike. These attacks have targeted both people and properties, intensifying conflict in the region.

Meanwhile, Israel is expanding its ground and air attacks against Hezbollah in Lebanon. This escalation has raised fears within Lebanon that Israel might be preparing for a large-scale ground invasion. Adding to the instability, Iranian missiles landed in towns near an Israeli nuclear site, injuring around 180 people. Israel is currently investigating how these ballistic missiles penetrated its air defenses, which are considered sophisticated.

Political Developments in France

In France, recent local elections have resulted in the Socialist Party retaining control of major cities such as Paris and Marseille. This outcome is seen as a boost for mainstream political groups. However, the nationalist right gained victories in places including Nice and other towns, reflecting a divisive political landscape across the country.

Kenya Grants Amnesty to Citizens Fighting Abroad

Kenyans who have been fighting illegally for Russia in Ukraine are set to receive amnesty. It is currently illegal for Kenyan citizens to serve in foreign armies, with penalties that could include up to ten years of imprisonment. This move to pardon fighters may affect ongoing concerns about Kenyans’ involvement in foreign conflicts.

Cuba Faces Another National Blackout

Cuba has experienced its second nationwide power blackout in just one week, highlighting the country’s ongoing struggles. The repeated failure of the power grid underscores Cuba’s hardship under a fuel blockade imposed by the United States. This situation continues to impact daily life and infrastructure across the island.

Humanitarian and Domestic Concerns

In Sudan, a drone strike on a key hospital during Eid has led to tragic loss of life. According to the World Health Organization, 64 people were killed, including 13 children, two nurses, and a doctor. This attack adds to the humanitarian crisis in the region.

On the US domestic front, travelers have faced long delays at airports due to a partial government shutdown. Transportation Security Administration (TSA) agents have not been paid, leading former US President Donald Trump to announce that Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) agents will assist with airport security during the disruption.

Finally, in Hawaii, storms have caused an estimated $1 billion in damage through flooding and rainfall. Governor Josh Green noted that the full extent of damage to roads and infrastructure is still being assessed, pointing to ongoing recovery challenges.

Ongoing Missing Person Search

A nationwide search remains underway in the United States for Nancy Guthrie, the 84-year-old mother of television anchor Savannah Guthrie. Despite seven weeks of efforts, no suspects have been identified. Nancy’s family has publicly renewed their appeal for information, emphasizing their continuing distress over her disappearance.
