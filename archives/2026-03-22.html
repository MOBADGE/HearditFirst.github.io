March 22, 2026

Middle East Tensions Rise

Israeli settlers attacked Palestinian villages in the occupied West Bank following the death of an 18-year-old settler, Yehuda Sherman, who was reportedly hit by a vehicle driven by a Palestinian while riding a quad bike. This incident has escalated violence, targeting both people and property in the area.

Meanwhile, Israel is increasing military operations against Hezbollah in Lebanon by expanding ground and air attacks. There are growing concerns in Lebanon about a possible large-scale Israeli ground invasion. In a related development, Israel is investigating how ballistic missiles, fired by Iran and injuring 180 people near an Israeli nuclear site, managed to penetrate the country’s advanced air defenses.

Kenya Grants Amnesty to Fighters in Ukraine

Kenya has announced that it will grant amnesty to its citizens who have been fighting illegally for Russia in Ukraine. Serving in foreign armies is against Kenyan law and can lead to prison sentences of up to ten years. This move may affect how Kenya addresses its nationals involved in the conflict abroad.

Cuba Faces Repeated Power Blackouts

Cuba experienced a national blackout for the second time in a week. The power grid failure highlights the severe difficulties facing the country, which continues to struggle under a US-imposed fuel blockade. The outages are adding to Cuba’s ongoing economic and infrastructure challenges.

Airport Security Disruptions in the US

The ongoing partial shutdown of the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) in the United States has caused significant travel delays. TSA agents, who have been working without pay, are overwhelmed, resulting in hours-long waits at airports nationwide. In response, former US President Donald Trump stated that Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) agents will assist with airport security operations during the shutdown.

Natural Disasters and Humanitarian Tragedies

Severe storms and flooding in Hawaii have caused an estimated $1 billion in damage, according to Governor Josh Green. While the full extent of the impact on roads and infrastructure is still being assessed, the destruction has already strained local resources.

In Sudan, a drone strike during Eid targeted a key hospital and killed 64 people, including 13 children, two nurses, and a doctor. The World Health Organization reported the attack, marking a devastating blow to healthcare services amid ongoing conflict.

Political Developments in France

Paris is holding mayoral elections that could end the Socialist party’s 25-year control of the French capital. The race is closely contested between Emmanuel Grégoire, the Socialist candidate, and Rachida Dati, who represents the right. The election outcome will shape the city’s political direction in the coming years.
