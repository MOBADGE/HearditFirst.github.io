Tensions Rise Over Iran Protests

The death toll from protests in Iran has reportedly exceeded 2,400, reflecting ongoing unrest. Relatives of an arrested protester have told BBC Persian that he is due to be executed soon, raising international concern. In response, Donald Trump has vowed to take "very strong action" should Iran carry out executions of protesters. The situation remains volatile, with potential repercussions for regional stability and international relations depending on Iran’s next steps.

Deadly Crane Collapse in Thailand

At least 32 people were killed and 64 injured when a crane collapsed onto a passenger train in Thailand. The injured include very young and elderly individuals, highlighting the broad impact of the accident. Authorities are investigating the incident, which may lead to increased scrutiny of construction and safety regulations in the country.

Arctic Summit to Influence Greenland’s Future

A significant summit focused on the Arctic will take place on Wednesday, hosted by US Vice President JD Vance. The meeting will include Danish and Greenlandic foreign ministers, aiming to discuss the future of the Arctic region. For Greenlanders, the outcome is particularly important as it could shape their environmental and economic landscape, emphasizing the growing global attention on Arctic affairs.

China Posts Record Trade Surplus

China has announced a record trade surplus of $1.19 trillion, the largest ever reported by the country. This comes despite tariffs imposed by Donald Trump during his presidency, with China successfully diversifying its trade partners. The milestone indicates China's continuing resilience and growing influence in global trade markets, potentially affecting future international economic strategies.

Political Pressures in Uganda’s Election

Tensions are high in Uganda following concerns over the upcoming election results. The country's election chief has reported receiving threats amid uncertainties surrounding vote declarations. This period has been further complicated by statements from a presidential assistant suggesting that opposition candidate Bobi Wine might not be declared president even if he wins, raising questions about the transparency and fairness of the electoral process.

Diplomatic Developments and Humanitarian News

Two East Asian leaders from Japan and South Korea ended their summit with a unique cultural moment, performing a K-pop drum duet featuring popular music.
