Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 30, 2025

Conflict and International Relations

Russia's military losses in Ukraine have risen sharply, according to records compiled by the BBC counting Russian war casualties. This increase comes amid ongoing conflict and diplomatic efforts, with the United States pushing for a peace deal. Meanwhile, in the Middle East, the US president recently discussed Gaza ceasefire efforts with Israeli leader Netanyahu, expressing hope to quickly reach the next phase of the ceasefire. He also issued a strong warning to Hamas, stating the group would face severe consequences if it did not disarm. Additionally, Saudi Arabia has urged the United Arab Emirates to withdraw from Yemen following a strike on a separatist-controlled port. The UAE denied allegations from Saudi Arabia that it supplied weapons to separatists or encouraged military offensives against the Yemeni government.

Regional Security and Law Enforcement

Turkey conducted nationwide raids resulting in the detention of 357 people suspected of belonging to the Islamic State (IS). This action occurred shortly after a siege in the town of Yalova that left three police officers dead. The arrests are part of broader counter-terrorism efforts amid continuing security concerns in the region.

Transportation Disruptions in Europe

Eurostar has canceled all train services departing from London due to a power outage affecting the Channel Tunnel. This electrical issue also forced the suspension of Le Shuttle services between Folkestone in the UK and Calais in France. The disruption impacts thousands of travelers and highlights vulnerabilities in critical cross-channel transport infrastructure.

Notable Deaths and Cultural Updates

Bangladesh's first female prime minister, Khaleda Zia, has died at 80. She was a prominent figure in the country's politics for decades and known for her longstanding rivalry with former prime minister Sheikh Hasina, a struggle that shaped Bangladesh’s political landscape. In France, a petition seeking a national tribute for film star Brigitte Bardot, who passed away recently, has gathered over 23,000 signatures, reflecting her cultural significance.

Entertainment and Sports News

South Korean K-pop group NewJeans has disbanded following a public dispute with their record label, Ador. The label confirmed that member Danielle Marsh was dropped, and it remains uncertain if member Minji will return. In sports, British heavyweight boxer Anthony Joshua was involved in a car crash in Nigeria that left two members of his team dead. Joshua sustained minor injuries in the accident, which has affected his team and fans worldwide.
