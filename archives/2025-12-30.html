Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 30, 2025

Middle East Tensions and Diplomacy

The US president held talks with Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu, expressing hope to advance quickly to the next phase of a Gaza ceasefire. He also warned Hamas that it would face severe repercussions if it did not disarm. Meanwhile, the United Arab Emirates announced plans to withdraw from Yemen following a Saudi airstrike on a port held by separatists. The UAE and Saudi Arabia, previously allies in the conflict against the Houthi movement, have seen growing tensions due to infighting between rival groups they support.

Iran Protests Over Currency Collapse

Protests have spread across Iran for a third consecutive day after the Iranian rial plummeted to a historic low against the US dollar. Shopkeepers in Tehran initiated the demonstrations, highlighting public frustration with the deteriorating economic conditions. The protests have rapidly expanded to other areas, reflecting widespread concern over inflation and economic instability in the country.

Russia-Ukraine Conflict Developments

The conflict in Ukraine continues to take a heavy toll, with Russian military losses increasing at an unprecedented rate. The BBC has documented a steep rise in reported deaths among Russian soldiers. Efforts to negotiate a peace deal face significant challenges, notably disagreements over territorial control and the future of Europe’s largest nuclear power plant, currently occupied by Russian forces. These issues remain key obstacles to reaching an agreement.

Security and Crime in Europe

In Germany, authorities reported a major bank heist in the city of Gelsenkirchen, where thieves exploited the quiet post-Christmas period to break into a high-street branch and steal approximately €30 million. Meanwhile, Turkey conducted nationwide raids resulting in the detention of 357 individuals suspected of links to the Islamic State group. These arrests came just a day after a siege in Yalova led to the deaths of three police officers.

Transportation Disruptions in Europe

Eurostar resumed services following recent power issues in the Channel Tunnel but warned of ongoing delays. Passengers have reported being stranded and forced to seek alternative routes amid the disruption. The continued problems highlight challenges in maintaining cross-Channel transport reliability during peak travel periods.

Notable Deaths and National Tributes

Bangladesh’s first female prime minister, Khaleda Zia, passed away at the age of 80. She was a defining figure in the country's politics, with a long-standing rivalry with former prime minister Sheikh Hasina. In France, a petition calling for a national tribute to film star Brigitte Bardot, who also died recently, has gained over 23,000 signatures, showing the public’s interest in honoring her legacy.
