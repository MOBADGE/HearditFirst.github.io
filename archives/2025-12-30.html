Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 30, 2025

Conflict and Security Updates

Russia's military losses in Ukraine have increased at an unprecedented rate, as documented by the BBC’s ongoing count of Russian war casualties. This rise comes amid ongoing international efforts, including from the United States, to push for a peace deal in the region.

In the Middle East, the US president expressed hope to advance quickly to the next phase of a ceasefire in Gaza after discussions with Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu. However, he also issued a stern warning to Hamas, stating the group would face severe consequences if it refused to disarm.

Ukraine denied Russian accusations that it launched a drone attack on a presidential residence, describing the claims as "typical Russian lies." These tensions continue to complicate the conflict narrative.

US Foreign Policy and Military Actions

The US president announced that American forces targeted a “big facility” linked to alleged Venezuelan drug boats, though he did not confirm media reports suggesting the strike involved a CIA drone. This operation underscores ongoing US efforts to counter drug trafficking.

Separately, the US president has downplayed recent Chinese military drills around Taiwan, which followed a US announcement of an $11 billion arms sale to the island. The move highlights continuing strategic tensions in the Indo-Pacific region.

Tragedy in Sports

British heavyweight boxer Anthony Joshua was involved in a car crash in Nigeria that resulted in the deaths of two of his team members, who were also close friends. Joshua sustained minor injuries. The incident has affected the boxing community and raised concerns about road safety.

Political Farewell in Bangladesh

Khaleda Zia, Bangladesh’s first female prime minister, has died at the age of 80. Her political rivalry with fellow former prime minister Sheikh Hasina was a defining feature of Bangladeshi politics for decades. Zia’s passing marks the end of an era in the country’s political landscape.

Environmental Concerns

Researchers have warned that great white sharks face possible extinction in the Mediterranean Sea. Factors including overfishing and illegal fishing are contributing to the decline of these sharks and other species, raising alarms about marine biodiversity loss in the region.

Cultural Note

Cecilia Giménez, known internationally for her controversial restoration attempt of the "Ecce Homo" fresco, which became famous as the "Monkey Christ" painting, has died. Her story became a symbol of unexpected viral fame connected to local cultural heritage.

Crime Investigation

Australian police have confirmed that the gunmen responsible for the Bondi attack, which resulted in 15 deaths, acted alone and did not receive training in the Philippines despite spending a month there prior to the incident. This detail contributes to ongoing investigations into the motives and planning behind the attack.
