Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 30, 2025

Global Conflict and Diplomacy

Recent reports show a sharp increase in Russian military losses in Ukraine, with obituaries for Russian soldiers rising faster than before. This development comes as the United States pushes for a peace deal to end the ongoing conflict. Meanwhile, in the Middle East, the US president has expressed hope of quickly advancing to phase two of a ceasefire in Gaza after discussions with Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu. However, he also issued a stern warning that Hamas would face severe consequences if it refuses to disarm. In Yemen, Saudi Arabia has supported a call for the United Arab Emirates to withdraw from the country following a strike on a separatist-controlled port. The UAE has denied accusations of supplying weapons to separatists or encouraging attacks on the Yemeni government.

Political Developments in Africa and Asia

In West Africa, Guinea’s junta chief is leading in a disputed presidential election. The government has restricted access to social media platforms such as TikTok, YouTube, and Facebook as the country awaits final election results. In Turkey, authorities have detained 357 suspects linked to the Islamic State during raids nationwide. These arrests followed a deadly siege in Yalova that resulted in the deaths of three police officers. In South Asia, former prime minister Khaleda Zia of Bangladesh has died at the age of 80. Zia was a prominent political figure whose long-standing rivalry with another former prime minister shaped the nation's politics for decades.

Transport and Public Safety

Eurostar train services through the Channel Tunnel faced major disruption after a power supply problem led to a suspension of services. Although trains will resume running, passengers should expect severe delays and potential cancellations as the situation is resolved. In Nigeria, British heavyweight boxer Anthony Joshua was involved in a serious car accident that tragically killed two members of his team. Joshua himself suffered only minor injuries. The incident has raised concerns about road safety and the wellbeing of athletes abroad.

Culture and Entertainment

The French right-wing has initiated a campaign for a national tribute to film star Brigitte Bardot, who passed away recently. The petition in her honor has gathered over 23,000 signatures, reflecting her enduring cultural impact in France. In South Korea’s music industry, K-pop band member Danielle Marsh faces a $30 million lawsuit amid a dispute with her record label. The label, Ador, has dropped Marsh from the band NewJeans, while the status of another member, Minji, remains uncertain. This high-profile legal battle highlights ongoing tensions in the entertainment business.
