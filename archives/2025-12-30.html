Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 30, 2025

Conflict and Diplomacy in Ukraine and Gaza

Russian military losses in Ukraine have increased sharply, according to a BBC count of soldiers' obituaries, reflecting intensified fighting. Meanwhile, the US is pushing for a peace deal in the region amid ongoing conflict. There have also been reports from Russia accusing Ukraine of attacking President Putin's residence with drones, which Ukraine denies, calling the claims false.

In the Middle East, the US president expressed hope to quickly reach the next phase of a ceasefire in Gaza after talks with Israeli leader Netanyahu. He warned that Hamas would face severe consequences if it failed to disarm, underscoring the fragile situation as efforts continue to halt violence.

Developments in the Middle East: Saudi Arabia and Yemen

Tensions remain high in Yemen as Saudi Arabia urged the United Arab Emirates to withdraw from the country following a strike on a separatist-held port. Saudi Arabia also accused the UAE of supplying weapons to separatists to push offensives against Yemen’s government. The UAE denied these allegations, highlighting the complex and strained relationships influencing the ongoing conflict in Yemen.

Security Actions in Turkey

Turkey conducted nationwide raids resulting in the detention of 357 people suspected of membership in the Islamic State (IS). The operation followed a deadly siege in Yalova where three police officers were killed. This surge in security measures reflects Turkey's ongoing efforts to combat extremist groups within its borders.

Tragedy for British Boxer Anthony Joshua

British heavyweight boxer Anthony Joshua was involved in a car crash in Nigeria that resulted in the deaths of two of his team members, who were also close friends. Joshua sustained minor injuries. This incident is a significant personal tragedy for the athlete and his team.

Transport Disruption in Europe

All Eurostar trains departing from London were canceled due to a power outage affecting the Channel Tunnel. Additionally, the Le Shuttle service between Folkestone and Calais was suspended because of the same power supply issue. This disruption is likely to impact many travelers crossing between the UK and continental Europe.

Cultural and Political News

Brigitte Bardot, a celebrated film star, passed away recently, triggering a wave of tributes. A petition calling for a national homage attracted over 23,000 signatures, indicating Bardot’s lasting cultural impact in France.

In Bangladesh, Khaleda Zia, the country's first female prime minister, died at the age of 80. Her long-standing rivalry with former prime minister Sheikh Hasina played a defining role in the nation’s political landscape over several decades.

Entertainment Industry Dispute

In the K-pop world, a member of the band NewJeans faces a $30 million lawsuit connected to a dispute with record label Ador. The label has dropped Danielle Marsh from the band, while the future of other members remains unclear, illustrating ongoing tensions in the music industry.
