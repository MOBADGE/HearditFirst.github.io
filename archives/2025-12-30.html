Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 30, 2025

Middle East Tensions and Political Developments

The US president has expressed hope to quickly advance to the next phase of a ceasefire in Gaza, following talks with Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu. He also issued a stern warning to Hamas, stating the group would face severe consequences if it did not disarm. Meanwhile, Saudi Arabia has joined calls for the United Arab Emirates to withdraw from Yemen after a strike targeted a separatist-controlled port. The UAE denied allegations from Saudi Arabia that it supplied weapons to separatists and encouraged attacks against the Yemeni government.

Iran Faces Protests Amid Economic Strain

In Iran, protests have expanded beyond Tehran after the national currency, the rial, fell to a record low against the US dollar. The demonstrations began with shopkeepers reacting to the currency's sharp decline and have since spread more widely. This ongoing unrest highlights economic difficulties faced by Iranians and could signal increasing public dissatisfaction with financial conditions.

Russia-Ukraine Conflict Sees Rising Casualties and Diplomatic Challenges

Casualty reports reveal a rapid increase in Russian soldiers' deaths in Ukraine, according to a detailed count by the BBC. This escalation comes as the US continues to advocate for a peace deal between Russia and Ukraine. However, significant obstacles remain, particularly disagreements over territorial control and the status of Europe’s largest nuclear plant, which is currently held by Russian forces. These issues remain critical sticking points that could delay or prevent an agreement.

Security Operations in Turkey and Recent Attacks

Turkey has detained 357 individuals suspected of links to the Islamic State group in nationwide raids. The crackdown followed a deadly siege in the town of Yalova, where three police officers were killed. These events underscore ongoing security challenges and efforts by Turkish authorities to curb militant activity within its borders.

Significant Events in Europe

In Germany, police are investigating a high-value bank robbery in Gelsenkirchen, where thieves reportedly used the post-Christmas quiet period to break into a branch and steal approximately €30 million. Additionally, transportation disruptions continue in the UK after a power outage in the Channel Tunnel suspended Eurostar services. Some trains have resumed but with severe delays and possible cancellations expected.

Notable Deaths and National Tributes

Bangladesh mourns the passing of Khaleda Zia, the country’s first female prime minister, at the age of 80. Her long-standing political rivalry with another former prime minister shaped much of the nation's recent political history. In France, a national tribute is being proposed for film star Brigitte Bardot, who died recently. A petition has gathered over 23,000 signatures in support of honoring her memory.
