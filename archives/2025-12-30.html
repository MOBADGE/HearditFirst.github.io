Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 30, 2025

Global Unrest and Protests

Iran is experiencing widespread protests for the third consecutive day after its currency, the rial, hit a record low against the US dollar. These protests began with shopkeepers in Tehran and quickly spread. The currency’s drastic drop has raised economic fears among ordinary Iranians, contributing to growing dissatisfaction with the government’s handling of economic conditions.

Middle East Conflict and Diplomacy

US President Donald Trump has spoken about progressing quickly to a second phase of a Gaza ceasefire after discussions with Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu. He also issued a strong warning to Hamas, saying the group will face severe consequences if it does not disarm. Meanwhile, Israel has barred 37 aid groups from operating in Gaza, a move criticised by foreign ministers from 10 countries including the UK, who labelled the restrictions as "restrictive" and "unacceptable." These developments highlight ongoing tensions and challenges in efforts to manage the Gaza situation.

In Yemen, the United Arab Emirates (UAE) announced it would withdraw from the country following a Saudi airstrike on a port held by separatists. Although the UAE and Saudi Arabia have been allies in the conflict against the Houthi movement, infighting between factions they support has caused a strain on their relationship.

Russia-Ukraine Conflict and Peace Talks

Reports show a steep rise in Russian military casualties in Ukraine, with the BBC documenting an increase in Russian soldiers’ obituaries. This surge in losses comes as international efforts to negotiate a peace deal continue. However, key issues threaten to derail progress, primarily disputes over territory and the future of Europe’s largest nuclear plant, currently occupied by Russian forces. These challenges underscore the complexity and fragility of negotiating an end to the conflict.

Security and Crime Developments

In Germany, police in Gelsenkirchen are investigating a high-value bank robbery in which thieves used the quiet period after Christmas to break into a branch and steal €30 million. The heist highlights ongoing concerns about sophisticated criminal activity during holiday downtimes.

In Turkey, security forces conducted nationwide raids that led to the detention of 357 suspected members of the Islamic State group. These operations followed a violent siege in Yalova, where three police officers were killed, underscoring continued efforts to combat terrorism within the country.

Notable Deaths and Cultural Tributes

Bangladesh’s first female prime minister, Khaleda Zia, died at the age of 80. Zia’s decades-long political rivalry with another former prime minister, Sheikh Hasina, deeply shaped the country’s political landscape over the years.

In France, a petition calling for a national tribute to film star Brigitte Bardot has gained over 23,000 signatures since her death on Sunday. The proposed homage reflects Bardot’s enduring cultural impact.
