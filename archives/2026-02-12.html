Environment and Climate Policy

The White House has revoked a longstanding ruling that classified greenhouse gases as harmful to public health. This move marks what officials are calling the largest deregulation in U.S. history. Environmental experts, however, warn that rolling back this regulation could have costly consequences for Americans, potentially affecting efforts to address climate change and public health risks associated with pollution.

This development may lead to a reassessment of immigration enforcement strategies amid concerns about community impact and public safety.

Natural Disaster in Madagascar

A severe storm has devastated the port city of Toamasina in Madagascar, destroying most of the infrastructure and affecting over half the population. The storm is moving towards Mozambique, raising concerns about further damage in the region. Recovery efforts will be crucial in the coming weeks to support the affected communities.
