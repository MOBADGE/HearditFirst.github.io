US Politics and Economy

Donald Trump has publicly threatened to fire Jerome Powell, the chair of the Federal Reserve, if he does not step down by May. This marks a new escalation in their ongoing dispute. Meanwhile, Trump is also involved in a public disagreement with Pope Francis, which is causing some conservative Catholics in the US to side with the Pope, potentially costing Trump support within that community.

Middle East Conflict

Tensions continue in the Middle East despite recent diplomatic talks. Days after unusual direct negotiations in the US between Israel and Lebanon, violence resumed with Israeli strikes targeting southern Lebanon and Hezbollah firing rockets back at Israel. Meanwhile, a prominent Palestinian prisoner, Marwan Barghouti, is at the center of controversy after his family reported he was assaulted three times in prison in one month. The Israeli Prison Service denies these claims, calling them unfounded.

Turkey School Shootings

Turkey is grappling with tragedy after a second school shooting in just two days. The latest attack resulted in the deaths of eight students and one teacher, according to the country’s Interior Minister, Mustafa Cifci. The incidents have shocked the nation and raised concerns over school safety.

International Incidents

In Asia, South Korea jailed an American YouTuber named Johnny Somali for causing a public nuisance. The case drew attention after he kissed a statue symbolizing World War Two sex slaves, sparking outrage. In Africa, Roelf Meyer, a former apartheid-era negotiator known for helping end white-minority rule in South Africa, was appointed as the country’s new ambassador to the United States.

Other Developments

In Hungary, Péter Magyar, the politician who recently ended Viktor Orbán’s 16-year rule, is urging for a swift handover of power and wants the new parliament to meet in early May. In Kenya, a Chinese national named Zhang Kequn was sentenced to one year in prison for attempting to smuggle over 2,000 live ants out of the country. Lastly, Sri Lanka has repatriated 238 Iranian sailors who were stranded after a US torpedo attack on the Iranian vessel Iris Dena, an incident that resulted in over 100 deaths.
