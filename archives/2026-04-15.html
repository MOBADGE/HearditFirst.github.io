U.S. Politics: Trump’s Public Disputes and Fed Chair Threat

Donald Trump is openly clashing with two significant figures ahead of the 2026 U.S. elections. He is engaged in a public dispute with the American pontiff, a conflict that prominent conservative Catholics say is causing Trump to lose valuable support within this key group. Meanwhile, Trump has also threatened to fire Jerome Powell, the Federal Reserve chair, if Powell does not step down by May. This threat marks a notable escalation in their ongoing disagreement, raising questions about future Federal Reserve leadership and economic policy stability.

Tragic School Shooting in Turkey

Turkey has suffered a devastating second school shooting within two days. Eight students and one teacher were killed in the latest attack, according to Interior Minister Mustafa Cifci. The incident highlights growing concerns about safety in Turkish educational institutions and raises urgent calls for measures to protect students and staff.

Conflict-Related Violence in the Middle East

Tensions remain high in the Middle East with multiple violent incidents reported. Three paramedics were killed in successive strikes by Israel in Lebanon, leading Lebanon’s government to condemn the killings as a "flagrant crime." In addition, Marwan Barghouti, a prominent Palestinian prisoner, has reportedly been assaulted three times in one month, according to his family. However, the Israeli Prison Service denies claims of physical violence against him, describing the allegations as "false and baseless."

Legal and Crime Updates

Several legal developments have captured attention globally. In Kenya, a Chinese national named Zhang Kequn received a one-year prison sentence for trying to smuggle over 2,000 live ants out of the country. Meanwhile, in the U.S., Stefan Pildes, the organiser of a charity pub crawl called SantaCon, has been charged with stealing $1 million of the $2.7 million raised, allegedly spending much of it on personal expenses.

International and Regional Developments

Other international news includes Sri Lanka repatriating 238 Iranian sailors who were stranded after a U.S. torpedo attack on the Iranian vessel Iris Dena. This attack had previously claimed 104 lives, and the survivors were among those sent home. In Hungary, Péter Magyar, who ended Viktor Orbán’s 16-year rule, has called for a swift handover of power and for the new parliament to meet in early May.

Additionally, in South Korea, an American YouTuber named Johnny Somali has been jailed for causing a public nuisance after kissing a statue symbolizing World War Two sex slaves, an act that sparked public outrage.
