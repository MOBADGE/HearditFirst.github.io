February 24, 2026

Conflict in Ukraine

Four Russian soldiers spoke to the BBC about harsh conditions they are facing in the war in Ukraine. Two soldiers described witnessing fellow troops being executed on the orders of commanders after refusing to follow certain orders. This report highlights internal discipline measures apparently enforced through deadly force, shedding light on the severe and brutal realities inside the Russian military during the conflict.

Weather Disruptions in the US

A powerful snowstorm has struck the US East Coast, dumping over a foot of snow in several regions. The heavy snowfall caused widespread disruptions, including more than 5,000 flight cancellations, power outages, and transportation difficulties. Recovery efforts are ongoing as people contend with lingering impacts on travel and daily life.

Security in Mexico

Mexican authorities are searching for 23 inmates who escaped from a jail in Puerto Vallarta. Armed assailants reportedly rammed a prison gate to facilitate the breakout. This incident forms part of broader violence affecting the region and raises concerns about security and law enforcement challenges in Mexico.

Missing Person Case in the US

Nancy Guthrie, an NBC host, has been reported missing. Her family is offering a $1 million reward to bring her home. Guthrie’s daughter, fighting back tears, said the family is aware she "may be lost" but continues to seek answers. The case has attracted attention, and the reward aims to encourage public assistance in locating her.

Health and Celebrity News

Celebrity doctor and health influencer Peter Attia has stepped down from his role at CBS. His representatives said this decision was taken to avoid his controversial association with the late sex offender Jeffrey Epstein becoming a distraction. This move follows public scrutiny over Attia's past links.

Meanwhile, actor Robert Carradine, known for his role in "Lizzie McGuire" and "Revenge of the Nerds," has died at age 71. His family revealed he had struggled with bipolar disorder for nearly 20 years and took his own life. The announcement has sparked conversations around mental health awareness.

Other Global Headlines

In Ghana, the main airport has been renamed, removing the name of a coup leader to better reflect the country’s democratic values. Supporters praised the change as a positive step away from authoritarian legacies.

In Russia, officials confirmed a suicide bombing at a Moscow rail hub that killed a traffic police officer and the attacker. The incident adds to ongoing security concerns in the city.

Hong Kong lodged a "strong protest" after Panama’s Supreme Court canceled the contracts of a Hong Kong-based company managing some canal ports, effectively transferring control to Panama. This development reflects ongoing tensions regarding the management of critical international trade infrastructure.

In Thailand, 72 tigers have died from an illness at a tourist park. Authorities suspect canine distemper virus but have not yet identified the source, and an investigation is underway to determine how the outbreak began.
