Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 05, 2025

International Energy and Trade Cooperation

Russia has signaled its readiness to provide "uninterrupted" fuel supplies to India. This commitment comes amid the Russian Prime Minister's visit to India, during which deals were signed in civil nuclear energy and critical minerals sectors. The agreements highlight ongoing efforts to strengthen economic and energy ties between the two countries, potentially impacting global energy markets and India's resource security.

Military Developments in Europe

Germany has voted to introduce a voluntary military service program for 18-year-olds. This initiative forms part of Germany’s broader plan to develop Europe's strongest conventional army. The move marks a shift in Germany’s military approach and may influence defense policies across Europe amid evolving security concerns.

US Supreme Court to Review Birthright Citizenship Case

The US Supreme Court has agreed to hear a case challenging birthright citizenship. This follows the rejection of Donald Trump’s executive order aimed at ending automatic citizenship for those born in the United States by lower courts. The Court’s decision to take up the case signals potential significant changes in US immigration policy depending on the outcome.

Justice and Human Rights in Eritrea

Thirteen Eritreans, including former Olympian cyclist Zeragaber Gebrehiwot who competed in the 1980 Moscow Games, have been released after spending 18 years in prison without trial. Relatives have confirmed their release from a notorious jail, highlighting ongoing issues related to human rights and legal processes in Eritrea.

Major Media Industry Acquisition

Netflix has announced a $72 billion acquisition of Warner Bros' film and streaming businesses. This deal will transfer ownership of major franchises such as Harry Potter and Game of Thrones to Netflix. The acquisition is a significant development in the entertainment industry, likely to reshape global streaming offerings and content availability.

Additional News Highlights

- Namibia lost its bid to overturn a ban on the trade of rhino horns. The country had accumulated large stockpiles of rhino horn after pioneering the practice of removing horns to deter poachers. This decision affects wildlife conservation efforts and trade regulations. - In India, Yangchen Lachungpa was arrested on charges linked to trafficking tiger parts. The woman was wanted by Interpol for allegedly creating trafficking pathways out of India, raising concerns about wildlife trafficking networks. - India is facing worsening travel disruptions as its largest airline, IndiGo, cancels hundreds of flights. The airline expects to return to full operational normalcy only by 10 February, affecting travelers across the country. - Frank Gehry, the acclaimed architect known for his distinctive use of jagged angles and industrial materials, has died aged 96, marking the loss of a major figure in modern architecture. - Former US President Donald Trump praised a peace deal between the Democratic Republic of Congo and Rwanda, calling it "historic." He expressed hope that the agreement will encourage increased American investment in the region, which is rich in natural resources.
