Transport and Safety

Greece is beginning a trial involving 36 individuals connected to the deadliest train accident in the country’s history, where two trains collided in 2023, killing 57 people. The trial is expected to last several years and will focus on accountability and safety failures. This case highlights ongoing concerns about railway safety and infrastructure management.

This outage underscores the challenges of integrating autonomous vehicles into urban transportation systems and may prompt further scrutiny on the reliability and safety of such technology.
