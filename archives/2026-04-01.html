India Launches Massive Census Counting Over a Billion People

India has begun its highly ambitious two-phase census, aiming to count more than a billion people across the country. The operation involves three million officials who will collect data from every individual in India. This census is one of the world's largest and most complex population exercises, and its results will provide crucial information for planning and policymaking in the years ahead.

Health Concerns Rise Amid Chiang Mai’s Severe Air Pollution

The city of Chiang Mai in Thailand is currently experiencing its peak fire season, resulting in severe air pollution levels. The smog has made Chiang Mai one of the most polluted cities globally at this time, raising health concerns among residents. There have been reports from locals about children suffering from nosebleeds and other pollution-related health issues. The situation highlights the ongoing environmental challenges faced by the region during this season.

Megan Thee Stallion Hospitalized During New York Show

American rapper Megan Thee Stallion was taken to hospital after feeling very ill during a performance of Moulin Rouge in New York. Her representatives confirmed the sudden illness midway through the show, although no further details were released about her condition. The incident disrupted the concert and has drawn attention to the artist’s health.

US Army Helicopter Pilots Suspended and Quickly Reinstated

Two US Army military helicopter crews were suspended after flying near the home of singer Kid Rock. However, this suspension was lifted within hours when the Defence Secretary, Pete Hegseth, announced there would be no investigation into the incident. The quick reversal suggests that the flights did not warrant further official action, though details about the flights themselves remain limited.

Oracle Announces Significant Job Cuts

Oracle, one of the world’s largest technology companies, has carried out substantial job reductions. Thousands of employees are believed to have lost their jobs as part of this round of cuts. The scale of these layoffs reflects ongoing shifts within the technology sector, with major firms adjusting their workforces amid changing market conditions.

Anticipation Builds on Florida’s Space Coast Ahead of Artemis II Launch

Excitement is growing around the Kennedy Space Centre in Florida as crowds gather to witness the upcoming Artemis II mission, a historic lunar launch. Locals and visitors alike have expressed enthusiasm for the event, which is seen as a major milestone in space exploration. The launch will be closely watched as it marks an important step in NASA’s plans to return humans to the moon.
