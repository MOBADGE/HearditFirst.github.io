March 11, 2026

Middle East Tensions and Conflict

The ongoing conflict between the US, Israel, and Iran has intensified in recent days. The US president has called for an uprising in Iran, raising concerns about the challenges of starting and ending wars, reflecting on lessons from the Gulf War in 1991. Meanwhile, young people in Iran are coping with a US-Israeli bombing campaign by sheltering at home and avoiding public spaces due to missile attacks.

The conflict's regional impact extends to the West Bank, where settler violence has surged since the war began. The United Nations reports that six Palestinians have been killed in attacks by settlers, prompting the European Union and the UK to demand that Israel take steps to stop this escalation.

Global Energy Response

In response to rising oil prices triggered by the US-Israel war with Iran, the Group of Seven (G7) nations have agreed to release a record amount of emergency oil reserves. This measure aims to stabilize the global energy market and counter the economic repercussions of the conflict, highlighting the interconnected nature of geopolitical tensions and energy security.

Security Concerns in Norway

Norwegian authorities arrested three brothers in connection with an explosion at the US embassy in Oslo. Police are investigating whether a foreign state actor may have been involved in the incident, underscoring ongoing concerns around diplomatic security and international relations within Europe.

Diplomatic Disputes in South Africa

Leo Brent Bozell, the new US ambassador to South Africa, was summoned by officials after making remarks on a dispute linked to South Africa’s apartheid history. He has since apologized for these comments, illustrating the sensitivity of diplomatic relations and the importance of careful communication in international affairs.

Conflict-Related Incidents in Africa and Eastern Europe

In the Democratic Republic of Congo, a French aid worker was among three people killed in drone strikes on a residential building in the rebel-held city of Goma. This tragic event highlights the dangers faced by expatriates and humanitarian workers in conflict zones.

Separately, Ukraine has announced it successfully targeted a Russian plant producing missile components. Russia condemned the attack, alleging civilian casualties and accusing Britain of involvement, which points to ongoing clashes and international tensions in the region.

Other News

In Australia, one member of an Iranian women's football delegation who had previously been granted asylum reversed her decision after discussions with teammates. This development sheds light on the personal and political complexities faced by asylum seekers.

In Switzerland, officials reported no evidence of terrorism in a recent bus fire. Police noted that the incident likely involved a man with psychological issues who set himself on fire. Authorities continue to investigate but currently view the cause as non-criminal.
