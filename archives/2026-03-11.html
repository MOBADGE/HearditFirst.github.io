March 11, 2026

Middle East Conflict and International Responses

The ongoing conflict involving the US, Israel, and Iran continues to cause significant tension and violence. The US president has called for an uprising in Iran, though historical lessons, such as the experience in Iraq in 1991, highlight the difficulties in managing the aftermath of such interventions. Meanwhile, Iranians are coping with daily life amid US-Israeli bombing campaigns by staying home and limiting outdoor activity as streets remain near empty.

In the West Bank, violence by Israeli settlers has surged since the start of Israel's war, resulting in the deaths of six Palestinians, according to the United Nations. Both the European Union and the United Kingdom have demanded that Israel take action to stop these attacks.

Global Oil Market and Economic Measures

In response to rising oil prices linked to the US-Israel conflict with Iran, the G7 group of nations has agreed to a record release of emergency oil reserves. This measure aims to ease the sharp price increases that have put pressure on global energy markets and economies.

Security Concerns in Europe and Africa

In Norway, authorities have arrested three brothers following an explosion at the US embassy in Oslo. Investigations are ongoing to determine if a foreign state actor was involved in this incident.

In the Democratic Republic of Congo, drone strikes in the rebel-held city of Goma have killed three people, including a French aid worker. The strikes hit a residential building known to be frequently occupied by expatriates and aid workers, raising concerns over civilian safety in conflict zones.

Conflict Developments in Ukraine and Russia

Ukraine reports having struck a plant in Russia associated with missile components. Russia has pushed back against these claims, stating that the attack impacted civilians and suggesting it involved British assistance, which underscores the ongoing tensions and accusations between the countries involved.

Other Notable Updates

In Australia, a member of an Iranian women's football delegation who had been granted asylum has reversed her decision after discussions with teammates. In Switzerland, officials have found no evidence linking a recent bus fire to terrorism, attributing the incident to a man with psychological issues.

In South Africa, Bellarmine Mugabe, son of former leader Robert Mugabe, has dropped his bail request and plans to negotiate a plea deal related to charges including attempted murder, following the family’s loss of power.
