March 11, 2026

Middle East Conflict and Regional Impact

Tensions and violence continue following the start of the US-Israel war with Iran. The president of the United States has called for an uprising in Iran, but observers caution about the complex aftermath, recalling difficulties faced during the Iraq conflict in 1991. Meanwhile, a US-Israeli bombing campaign persists, prompting residents in Iran to stay mostly indoors and avoid public spaces due to ongoing missile attacks.

In the West Bank, there has been a surge in violence by Israeli settlers, resulting in the deaths of six Palestinians since the war began. The European Union and the United Kingdom have publicly called on Israel to halt this increase in settler attacks, highlighting concerns over the escalation amid the broader regional conflict.

Global Oil Market Response

In reaction to a sharp rise in oil prices triggered by the war between the US, Israel, and Iran, G7 countries have agreed to a record release of emergency oil reserves. This move aims to stabilize energy markets and mitigate economic impacts worldwide. Coordinated international efforts underscore the significance of the crisis for the global economy and energy security.

Security and Violence Abroad

Norway is investigating an explosion at the US embassy in Oslo, which led to the arrest of three brothers. Authorities are probing the possibility of involvement by a foreign state actor. Additionally, in the Democratic Republic of Congo, drone strikes have targeted a residential building in the rebel-held city of Goma, resulting in the deaths of three people, including a French aid worker. These incidents reflect growing concerns over security threats and the risks faced by diplomatic personnel and aid workers.

Developments in Ukraine and Russia

Ukraine has reported an attack on a Russian facility that produces missile components, asserting this as a strategic strike. Russia has condemned the attack, claiming it caused civilian casualties and accusing the United Kingdom of being complicit. This episode highlights continuing hostilities and international dimensions in the conflict between Ukraine and Russia.

Other Notable Updates

In Australia, one member of an Iranian women’s football delegation has withdrawn her previously granted asylum after consulting with teammates, according to authorities. In Switzerland, police concluded there is no evidence that a recent bus fire was terrorist-related, attributing the event to a man experiencing psychological difficulties who set himself ablaze.

In South Africa, Bellarmine Mugabe, son of former president Robert Mugabe, has abandoned a request for bail and will instead negotiate a plea agreement following charges including attempted murder. This development marks ongoing legal challenges for the Mugabe family after losing political power.
