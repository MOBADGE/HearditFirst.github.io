Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 28, 2025

Politics and International Relations

Myanmar held a widely criticised election on Sunday, which international observers have labelled a "sham." The vote took place while the military junta intensified its crackdown on dissent, leading critics to say the process aims primarily to solidify the junta's grip on power. Meanwhile, in the United States, talks are underway between Volodymyr Zelensky and Donald Trump in Florida. The discussions are expected to focus on contentious issues such as territorial concessions and security guarantees. Zelensky has expressed a belief that Russia "doesn't want peace," highlighting the ongoing tensions surrounding the conflict.

Security and Crime

Italian authorities arrested nine people accused of raising roughly €7 million (£6 million) over two years to support Hamas, a militant group. The investigation underscores continued concerns over financing of extremist organisations in Europe.

Human Rights and Legal Issues

Iran is experiencing a significant rise in executions, with the Iran Human Rights group confirming at least 1,500 deaths by early December. The number of executions is projected to double this year, marking a serious escalation in capital punishment practices. This development signals continuing challenges for human rights within the country.

Natural Disasters and Environment

A major storm swept through Nordic countries including Sweden, Finland, and Norway, resulting in three fatalities and widespread power outages affecting thousands of homes. In Italy, Mount Etna, Europe’s most active volcano, erupted while skiers were on its slopes. Scientists reported increased volcanic activity this week, with constant ash emissions from multiple craters. Both events highlight ongoing risks from natural phenomena in different regions.

Arts and Culture

Two notable figures from the world of cinema are in the spotlight today. Brigitte Bardot, credited with revolutionising French cinema with her unique blend of charm and sensuality in the 1950s, is being remembered for her impact on film history. In Iran, the film community mourns the passing of Bahram Beyzai, a giant in Iranian cinema who died at age 87. His film "Bashu, the Little Stranger" was recently restored and honored at the Venice Film Festival, reflecting his enduring influence.

Social and Economic Issues

In the United States, increased fears of deportation due to immigration crackdowns have led Honduran immigrants to send record amounts of money back to their families in Honduras. This surge in remittances offers insight into how immigration policy is impacting migrant communities and their home countries economically.

Transportation Incident

The Coral Adventurer cruise ship is under investigation after hitting a reef shortly after leaving a passenger behind on a remote island, where she was later found dead. The incident raises questions about safety and responsibility in expedition cruise operations.
