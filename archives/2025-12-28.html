Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 28, 2025

Notable Deaths in Cinema

Two prominent figures in cinema have died recently. Brigitte Bardot, the French actress known as a symbol of sexual liberation in the 1950s, passed away at age 91. Bardot's later years were marked by controversy due to homophobic and racist remarks she made. Meanwhile, Iranian cinema giant Bahram Beyzai died aged 87. Beyzai was celebrated for his influential work, including the film "Bashu, the Little Stranger," which received an award at this year's Venice Film Festival following its restoration.

Political Developments in Myanmar and Italy

Myanmar held an election that drew widespread criticism and has been described by observers as a "sham." The vote took place amid a renewed crackdown on dissent and is seen as a move by the military junta to consolidate its power. In Italy, nine people were arrested for allegedly raising around €7 million (£6 million) over more than two years for Hamas, a militant group. Authorities are investigating the financial activities behind these fundraising efforts.

Security and Violence Incidents

In Suriname, nine people, including five children, were stabbed to death. Police detained a male suspect after shooting him in the leg, and two other individuals sustained serious injuries in the attack. This tragic event has raised concerns about safety and violence in the area.

Weather Impact in Northern Europe

A major storm swept across Nordic countries, particularly affecting Sweden, Finland, and Norway. The storm caused considerable disruption, including leaving thousands of homes without power. In Sweden, the storm resulted in three deaths. Recovery efforts are ongoing as communities deal with the aftermath.

Unique Cultural Event in Spain

In the Spanish town of Ibi, the annual festival "Els Enfarinats" took place, featuring a large-scale mock battle with flour, eggs, and fireworks. The town was covered in flour and eggshells during this traditional and lively event, which attracts participants and spectators interested in this unique cultural celebration.

Health Update on Jair Bolsonaro

Jair Bolsonaro, the 70-year-old former Brazilian leader, underwent medical treatment for hiccups that had persisted for nine months. His wife noted the spasms required attention during a hospital stay related to hernia surgery. This update provides insight into his current health status.
