Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 28, 2025

Myanmar Election Amid Conflict and Criticism

Polling closed on the first day of Myanmar's widely criticised election, described by observers as a "sham" vote aimed at entrenching the military junta's power. The election is taking place amid a renewed crackdown on dissent and ongoing civil war conditions. Voters interviewed at polling stations spoke of a "climate of fear," reflecting the tense and unstable environment surrounding the election. The situation raises concern about the legitimacy and fairness of the electoral process during ongoing violence and repression.

Ukraine-Russia Negotiations Expected in Florida

Ukrainian leadership has said Russia "doesn't want peace" ahead of scheduled discussions in Florida with former U.S. President Donald Trump. The two are expected to talk about possible territorial concessions and security guarantees, key issues in finding a resolution to the conflict between Ukraine and Russia. The meeting signals ongoing international diplomatic efforts to address the war, though skepticism remains about Russia's willingness to compromise.

Severe Winter Storms Impact Nordic Countries and New York

A major storm has swept across parts of northern Europe and the United States. In Sweden, two people died as the storm caused widespread disruption, with thousands of homes losing power across Finland, Sweden, and Norway. Meanwhile, New York experienced heavy snowfall that led to hundreds of flight cancellations and over 3,000 delays, severely disrupting travel and daily life. The harsh winter weather continues to pose challenges for emergency services and transportation networks in affected regions.

Mount Etna Erupts Amid Skiing Season

Mount Etna in Italy, a volcano known for frequent activity, has erupted again. Volcanologists report that volcanic activity has intensified this week, with the craters continuously emitting ash. The eruption coincided with skiers on the slopes nearby, highlighting how the natural event is unfolding during the winter tourist season. Authorities continue to monitor the volcano closely for any significant changes that might affect safety in the region.

Maritime Incident Under Investigation

The Coral Adventurer cruise ship is under investigation after hitting a reef during its first trip since a controversial incident in which a passenger, Suzanne Rees, was left behind on a remote island and later found dead. The inquiry aims to determine accountability for the incident and prevent similar tragedies in the future. This case has drawn attention to cruise ship safety and passenger welfare in remote areas.

Surging Remittances from Hondurans in the U.S.

Remittances sent back to Honduras from Hondurans living in the United States have reached record levels. This increase is linked to fears of deportation amid strict immigration enforcement policies implemented during the Trump administration. The flow of money is crucial for many families in Honduras, supporting households and the local economy despite the uncertainty faced by migrants abroad.
