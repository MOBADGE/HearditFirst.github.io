Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 28, 2025

International Elections and Political Developments

Polling has concluded on the first day of Myanmar's much-criticized election, described by observers as a "sham." The vote comes amid a renewed crackdown on dissent and is widely seen as a move to solidify the military junta's hold on power. The election's legitimacy remains in doubt internationally, and the political situation in Myanmar is likely to remain tense.

Tragic Violence in Suriname

A stabbing incident in Suriname resulted in the deaths of nine people, including five children. The police detained a male suspect after shooting him in the leg. Two other individuals were seriously injured in the attack. This tragic event marks a significant moment of violence in the country, raising concerns about security and public safety.

Escalation of Executions in Iran

Human rights monitoring group Iran Human Rights (IHR) reports that executions in Iran have approximately doubled in 2025. At least 1,500 executions have been verified as of early December. This increase highlights ongoing and intensifying human rights concerns within Iran.

Security Actions in Europe

Italian police have arrested nine individuals accused of raising around €7 million (£6 million) for Hamas over more than two years. The arrests reflect continuing efforts in Europe to disrupt financial networks tied to militant groups and raise questions about security and intelligence cooperation on the continent.

Severe Weather Hits Nordic Region

A major storm swept across parts of Finland, Sweden, and Norway, resulting in three deaths and leaving thousands of homes without power. The storm caused widespread disruption across the Nordic countries, emphasizing the continuing vulnerabilities of infrastructure to extreme weather events in the region.

Cultural Events and Notable Deaths

In Spain, the town of Ibi held its annual "Els Enfarinats" festival, an energetic event marked by participants throwing flour, eggs, and fireworks in a mock battle. The colorful and messy tradition is a popular cultural celebration attracting many locals.

In France, Brigitte Bardot, a symbol of sexual liberation in the 1950s and a notable figure in cinema history, has died at the age of 91. Bardot was celebrated for revolutionizing French cinema with her blend of charm and sensuality, though her later years were marked by controversy due to homophobic and racist remarks. Her legacy remains complex, reflecting both cultural impact and personal controversies.

Health Update on Brazilian Politician

Jair Bolsonaro, the former Brazilian president, is undergoing medical treatment for persistent hiccups that lasted nine months. The 70-year-old was already hospitalized for hernia surgery when these spasms were addressed. The health concerns have attracted media attention given Bolsonaro's public profile.
