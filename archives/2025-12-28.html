Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 28, 2025

Culture and Arts

French cinema lost one of its iconic figures as actress Brigitte Bardot passed away at the age of 91. Bardot became a symbol of sexual liberation during the 1950s, captivating audiences with her unique mix of charm and sensuality. However, her later years were marked by controversy due to her homophobic and racist remarks. Meanwhile, Iranian filmmaker Bahram Beyzai, noted for his influential work in Iranian cinema, also died at 87. His film *Bashu, the Little Stranger* was recently celebrated with an award at the Venice Film Festival after restoration.

Politics and Conflict

Myanmar held elections amid widespread criticism, with observers branding the vote a "sham." The elections coincided with a crackdown on dissent, leading many to believe the process is aimed at solidifying the military junta's control. In diplomatic developments, Ukrainian leader Volodymyr Zelensky accused Russia of not wanting peace ahead of a key meeting in Florida with Donald Trump. They are expected to discuss potential territorial concessions and guarantees for Ukraine's security.

Security and Crime

Italian authorities arrested nine people accused of raising nearly €7 million over two years for the militant group Hamas. This operation highlights ongoing concerns about the funding of extremist groups in Europe.

Environment and Disasters

A powerful storm swept across Nordic countries, causing three deaths in Sweden and leaving thousands of homes in Finland, Sweden, and Norway without power. Meanwhile, Mount Etna in Italy continued its volcanic activity, with eruptions and continuous ash emissions observed as skiers enjoyed the slopes nearby. Scientists reported heightened volcanic activity throughout the week.

Human Rights

The Iran Human Rights group released a report indicating that executions in Iran have roughly doubled in 2025. The group has verified at least 1,500 executions up to the start of December, underscoring reports of harsh judicial measures in the country.

Travel and Safety

The cruise ship Coral Adventurer is under investigation following an incident where a passenger, Suzanne Rees, was left behind on a remote island and later found dead. Shortly after, the ship hit a reef on its return trip, raising further questions about safety protocols on board.
