December 28, 2025

Notable Deaths in Cinema

Two renowned figures in international cinema passed away recently. French actress Brigitte Bardot, who was a major symbol of sexual liberation during the 1950s, died at the age of 91. In her later years, she became known for making controversial homophobic and racist comments. Meanwhile, Bahram Beyzai, an influential Iranian cinema figure, died aged 87. His legacy includes the film *Bashu, the Little Stranger*, which was recently honored with an award at the Venice Film Festival following its restoration.

Myanmar Election Draws Criticism

Polls closed in Myanmar, marking the first day of elections that have faced widespread criticism. Observers described the vote as a "sham," viewing it as a move by the ruling military junta to strengthen its hold on power. The election took place alongside a renewed crackdown on dissent, raising concerns about the state of democracy and civil liberties in the country.

Storm Causes Fatalities and Power Outages in Nordic Region

A powerful storm swept across parts of Sweden, Finland, and Norway, causing significant disruption. Three people died in Sweden as a result of the storm, and thousands of homes across the Nordic countries lost power. Emergency services continue to respond to the storm’s aftermath as communities work to restore normal conditions.

Violent Incident in Suriname

In Suriname, a tragic stabbing attack resulted in the deaths of nine people, including five children. Police detained a male suspect, who was shot in the leg during the arrest. Two other individuals are reported to be seriously injured. Authorities are investigating the circumstances surrounding the attack.

Security Operation in Italy Targets Fundraising for Militant Group

Italian police arrested nine people accused of raising approximately €7 million (£6 million) over more than two years for the militant group Hamas. The operation highlights ongoing international concerns about funding for extremist organizations. Officials are continuing their investigation into the network and its activities.
