February 20, 2026

International Relations and Security

Former U.S. President Donald Trump is considering a limited military strike on Iran, according to recent statements. This threat comes shortly after he gave Iran approximately ten days to agree to a deal aimed at curbing its nuclear program. Meanwhile, the U.S. Supreme Court has issued a ruling that limits the authority to impose tariffs, which weakens Trump’s ability to manage international trade issues if he pursues a second term. These developments may impact U.S. foreign policy and international negotiations going forward.

Space Exploration

NASA aims to send humans back around the Moon in early March, targeting the Artemis II mission launch after completing a successful "wet dress rehearsal," a key pre-launch test. This mission represents a significant step in lunar exploration and could pave the way for further human space travel beyond Earth’s orbit.

Legal and Crime News

In Cameroon, three soldiers have been sentenced to prison for the killing of 21 civilians in the country’s Anglophone region. The prison sentences are rare in this conflict-ridden area, marking an unusual instance of accountability.

In Austria, a climber has been found guilty following the death of his girlfriend from hypothermia during a mountain trip on Grossglockner in January 2025. The case highlights risks associated with extreme mountain climbing.

In France, a soldier died after being shot in the head during a “game” with fellow soldiers, which involves attempting to disarm one another. Authorities are investigating the incident as an accident linked to military practices.

Human Rights and Social Issues

In Venezuela, opposition politician Juan Pablo Guanipa has been released after nearly nine months in prison, following the passage of an amnesty law. His release could affect political dynamics within the country.

In India, a mother and her infant were tragically burnt to death after a mob accused the woman of practising witchcraft. The incident draws attention to ongoing issues related to superstition and mob violence in certain communities.

Other News

Quentin Griffiths, co-founder of the online fashion retailer Asos, has died after falling from an apartment block in Thailand. Griffiths had left Asos in 2005 but remained a significant shareholder.

In Italy’s city of Catania, authorities reported that a dog was purposely trained to illegally dump rubbish. The city condemned these actions, emphasizing that creative methods should not be used as excuses for antisocial behavior.
