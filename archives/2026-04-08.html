April 08, 2026

Middle East Ceasefire and Ongoing Conflict

A two-week ceasefire between the US and Iran was announced after more than a month of coordinated attacks by the US and Israel on Iran. This provisional truce may change global perceptions of the US but comes with a high cost. Despite the ceasefire announcement, Israel carried out a large wave of air strikes in Lebanon, targeting areas including the southern suburbs of Beirut, southern Lebanon, and the eastern Bekaa Valley. These attacks occurred just hours after the US-Iran ceasefire was declared, showing the fragile nature of peace efforts in the region.

Economic Impact of the Ceasefire

Following the announcement of the US-Iran ceasefire plan, oil prices fell sharply by more than 15%. However, crude prices remain significantly higher than before the conflict began. Stock markets responded positively, with shares jumping on the news, reflecting cautious optimism about the potential easing of tensions in the Middle East and its effects on global energy markets.

Political Updates from the United States

In US political news, Republican Clay Fuller, who is supported by Donald Trump, won the election to fill the remaining term of Marjorie Taylor Greene. Fuller will serve until January 2027, continuing representation for the district Greene previously held.

Security and Legal Developments in Nigeria and Mexico

Nigeria has begun a mass trial of 500 terrorism suspects, a notable development considering that prosecutions for terror-related attacks in the country have been rare, with many held without trial. Meanwhile, in the United States, Érick Valencia Salazar, co-founder of the Jalisco New Generation drug cartel, pleaded guilty. He was among 29 alleged drug lords extradited from Mexico, highlighting ongoing efforts to combat cross-border drug trafficking.

Other International News

Germany has suspended a little-known military requirement that men under 45 obtain approval for long stays abroad—a regulation that had largely gone unnoticed until recently. In Cameroon, reports confirm that military contractors have died while participating in the Russia-Ukraine war, although the Cameroonian government typically does not comment on such involvement.

Lastly, Greece announced it will ban social media use for children under 15 starting next year. This follows similar restrictions imposed by other European countries, including France and Spain, aimed at protecting young people online.
