US and Iran Agree on Two-Week Ceasefire

The United States and Iran have agreed to a provisional two-week ceasefire following more than a month of coordinated attacks by the US and Israel on Iran. This temporary truce marks a significant pause in hostilities and may change how the global community views the US. The deal offers an opportunity to avoid further escalation, though it comes with complex political costs. Details around how the ceasefire will be maintained and whether it will lead to longer-term peace remain to be seen.

Pakistan's Role in Facilitating the Ceasefire

Pakistan played a key part in helping negotiate the fragile ceasefire between the US and Iran. The country has a long-standing relationship with Iran, which helped establish trust for the agreement. While the deal was not guaranteed, Pakistan’s involvement appears to have been crucial in bridging divides and encouraging dialogue during this uncertain period.

Impact on Oil Markets

Following the ceasefire announcement, oil prices fell sharply by up to 15%. This drop reflects eased fears over the security of the Strait of Hormuz, a vital route for global oil shipments that was threatened during the conflict. Although prices fell significantly, they remain higher than before the war began, indicating that markets remain cautious about future developments.

Political Shift: Clay Fuller Wins Election

In US domestic politics, Republican Clay Fuller, backed by Donald Trump, won the election to replace Marjorie Taylor Greene. Fuller will serve the remainder of Greene’s term, which ends in January 2027. This change may influence political dynamics moving forward, as the new representative takes office amid ongoing international and national challenges.

US Journalist Released from Captivity in Iraq

US journalist Shelly Kittleson has been released after being kidnapped in Iraq. US officials are now focused on ensuring her safe departure from the country. Her release provides relief amid regional instability and highlights ongoing risks journalists face when reporting from conflict zones.
