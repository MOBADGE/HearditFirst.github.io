Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 26, 2025

Peace Talks and Conflict in Eastern Europe and the Middle East

The Ukrainian leader announced plans to meet with the US president in Florida on Sunday to discuss ways to end the war with Russia. The Ukrainian leader expressed optimism that significant decisions could be made before the new year. Meanwhile, in Syria, at least eight people were killed in a blast at a mosque during Friday prayers in Homs. The explosion targeted a predominantly Alawite area, a community that has faced violence following the fall of the former Syrian president.

In Israel, two people were killed in a suspected attack involving a car ramming and stabbing. Police said the attacker came from the occupied West Bank and committed the violent acts several miles apart. Additionally, Israel officially recognized Somaliland as an independent state. The move drew criticism from Somalia, Egypt, Turkey, and Djibouti, who warned it sets a dangerous precedent in the region.

Military Actions and Security Concerns

The United States launched airstrikes against the Islamic State in Nigeria. This action followed the US president’s prior accusations that the Nigerian government had failed to protect Christians from jihadist violence. This military intervention represents ongoing US involvement against extremist groups beyond its borders.

In Southeast Asia, fighting continues along the Cambodia-Thailand border. Despite ongoing ceasefire talks, Thailand bombed areas near the border, leading Cambodia to accuse Thailand of “indiscriminate attacks.” Negotiators are working to resolve weeks of renewed conflict in the area.

Legal and Political Developments

In Malaysia, former prime minister Najib Razak received a 15-year prison sentence for his involvement in a large-scale state funds scandal linked to the sovereign wealth fund 1MDB. His conviction is part of a broader effort to tackle corruption in the country.

In the United States, a federal judge blocked the detention of Imran Ahmed, a British social media campaigner accused of attempting to pressure companies into censoring content. Ahmed was among five individuals facing these charges, highlighting legal debates related to free speech and social media regulation.

Environmental Warnings in the United States

Following heavy rain, California is facing the risk of floods and mudslides due to saturated soil conditions. Although downpours have eased, forecasters warn that even moderate additional rainfall could trigger dangerous flood and mudslide events in affected areas, underlining ongoing concerns about weather-related hazards in the region.

Cultural Disputes and International Controversies

A case involving a top Russian archaeologist wanted by Ukraine has sparked debate around the role of museums and scholars in occupied Crimea. The situation raises questions about cultural heritage, national identity, and the intersection between academia and political conflict.
