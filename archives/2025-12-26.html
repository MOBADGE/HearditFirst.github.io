Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 26, 2025

Ukraine and US Discussing Possible Progress on War

Ukraine’s leader, Volodymyr Zelensky, said he is scheduled to meet with US President Donald Trump in Florida on Sunday. Zelensky expressed hope that their talks could lead to important decisions on ending the ongoing conflict with Russia before the new year. This meeting comes amid continuing tensions and efforts to find a resolution to the war.

US Military Action Against Islamic State in Nigeria

The US has launched strikes targeting Islamic State militants in Nigeria. President Trump has previously criticized the Nigerian government for not doing enough to protect Christian communities from jihadist attacks. These strikes represent ongoing US involvement in combatting extremist groups in the region.

Violence Across the Middle East and Israel

In Syria, a blast at a mosque in Homs during Friday prayers killed at least eight people. The mosque is located in a mainly Alawite area, a group that has experienced violence since the fall of former Syrian leader Bashar al-Assad. Meanwhile, in Israel, two people were killed in an incident involving a suspected Palestinian attacker from the West Bank. The attacker reportedly ran over one person and stabbed another several miles away, according to police reports.

Tensions and Talks on the Thai-Cambodian Border

Despite ongoing ceasefire negotiations aiming to end renewed fighting, Thailand bombed areas along the Cambodian border. Cambodia condemned these actions as "indiscriminate attacks." Negotiators are meeting for a third day, seeking to bring stability to the region after weeks of clashes.

International Recognition and Legal Developments

Israel has recognized Somaliland as an independent state, a move that was strongly condemned by Somalia, Egypt, Turkey, and Djibouti. These countries warn that the recognition could set a risky precedent. In Malaysia, former Prime Minister Najib Razak was sentenced to 15 years in prison following his conviction related to a massive scandal involving state sovereign wealth fund 1MDB.

Legal Rulings in the United States

A US judge blocked the detention of Imran Ahmed, a British social media campaigner who was among five individuals accused of trying to pressure US companies to censor speech. This ruling highlights ongoing debates around free speech and online regulation.

Heritage and Politics in Crimea

A debate has arisen regarding a prominent Russian archaeologist wanted for trial in Ukraine over excavations in occupied Crimea. The case underscores the complicated relationships between museums, scholars, and state authorities in contested territories.
