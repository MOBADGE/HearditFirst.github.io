Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 26, 2025

International Diplomacy and Peace Efforts

Ukraine’s leader, Volodymyr Zelensky, is scheduled to meet with US President Donald Trump in Florida this Sunday to discuss ways to end the ongoing war with Russia. Zelensky expressed optimism that the talks could lead to significant progress before the new year. This meeting highlights continued international efforts to find a resolution to the conflict in Ukraine.

Security and Conflict Updates

Multiple violent incidents occurred today across different regions. In Syria, a blast at a mosque in Homs killed at least eight people. The attack targeted an Alawite area, a community that has experienced violence since the fall of former President Bashar al-Assad.

In Israel, two people were killed in a suspected ramming and knife attack. Police reported that the attacker, from the occupied West Bank, ran over at least one individual and stabbed a second victim several miles away.

Meanwhile, fighting continues along the Cambodia-Thailand border. Thailand has bombed areas near the border during ongoing ceasefire talks. Cambodia accused Thailand of indiscriminate attacks as negotiators work to end weeks of renewed fighting.

US Military Action

The United States launched strikes against the Islamic State group in Nigeria. This action follows accusations from President Trump that the Nigerian government has failed to protect Christians from jihadist attacks. The strikes illustrate the US’s continued involvement in counterterrorism efforts abroad.

Political and Legal News

In Malaysia, former Prime Minister Najib Razak was sentenced to 15 years in jail. He was convicted for his involvement in the multi-billion-dollar 1MDB state fund scandal, a significant corruption case.

In the United States, a judge blocked the detention of British social media campaigner Imran Ahmed. Ahmed was one of five people accused of trying to pressure US tech firms into censoring speech, raising questions about free speech and legal limits in digital spaces.

International Relations

Israel recognized Somaliland as an independent state, a move announced by Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu. This decision was met with strong criticism from Somalia, Egypt, Turkey, and Djibouti, who warn that it could set a dangerous precedent in the region. The recognition adds a new dimension to ongoing debates about sovereignty and statehood in the Horn of Africa.

Cultural and Legal Controversies

A case involving a top Russian archaeologist facing potential trial for excavations in Crimea has sparked debate about the relationship between museums and the Russian state. The scholar is wanted by Ukraine, drawing attention to complex issues around cultural heritage and political conflict in the region.
