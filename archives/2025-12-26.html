Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 26, 2025

International Security and Conflict

The United States has launched military strikes against the Islamic State group in Nigeria. This action comes amid ongoing concerns about violence by jihadist groups in the region. Former President Trump had previously criticized the Nigerian government for not adequately protecting Christians from such attacks, highlighting longstanding tensions and security challenges in the country.

In Syria, a blast at a mosque in Homs on Friday resulted in at least eight deaths. The mosque is located in an area predominantly inhabited by Alawites, a minority sect that has faced violence and crackdowns since the fall of former-President Bashar al-Assad. This attack underscores the continuing instability and sectarian strife in parts of Syria.

Meanwhile, in Israel, two people were killed in an incident involving a suspected Palestinian attacker from the West Bank. Authorities say the attacker struck one person with a vehicle and then stabbed another victim. The incident reflects ongoing tensions and violence in the region.

Border Conflict in Southeast Asia

Negotiations to end weeks of renewed fighting continue between Cambodia and Thailand, as ceasefire talks enter their third day. Despite the talks, Thailand carried out bombings in the border area. Cambodia has condemned these as "indiscriminate attacks," indicating the fragility of peace efforts in the region and the risk of further escalation if talks fail.

Diplomatic Developments

Israel has officially recognized Somaliland as an independent state, a decision announced by Israeli leader Netanyahu. This move has drawn strong condemnation from Somalia, Egypt, Turkey, and Djibouti, who argue it sets a dangerous precedent. The recognition is likely to affect diplomatic relations and regional politics in the Horn of Africa.

Legal and Judicial News

In Malaysia, former Prime Minister Najib Razak was sentenced to 15 years in jail for his involvement in the 1MDB scandal, a major corruption case involving the country's sovereign wealth fund. This marks a significant development in Malaysia’s efforts to address corruption at high levels of government.

In the United States, a judge has blocked the detention of British social media campaigner Imran Ahmed. Ahmed was one of five individuals accused of trying to pressure US companies into censoring free speech. The ruling reflects the complexities around legal actions involving online activism and free expression.

Environmental Concerns in the US

California is experiencing relief from heavy downpours, but forecasters warn that the risk of floods and mudslides remains. The ground is heavily saturated, and even moderate additional rainfall could trigger dangerous conditions. Residents and authorities continue to monitor weather closely to prevent further damage and casualties.

Culture and Heritage Dispute

A case involving a prominent Russian archaeologist wanted in Ukraine has sparked debate over the interaction between museums and the Russian state. The archaeologist’s work in occupied Crimea has raised questions about cultural heritage, national sovereignty, and accountability amid ongoing geopolitical tensions in the region.
