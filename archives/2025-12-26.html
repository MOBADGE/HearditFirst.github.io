Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 26, 2025

Ukraine and US Discussing War Resolution

The Ukrainian leader is set to meet with the US president in Florida on Sunday for talks aimed at ending the ongoing conflict with Russia. The Ukrainian leader expressed optimism, saying that "a lot can be decided before the new year." This meeting could be a critical step toward diplomatic efforts to resolve the war, with potential implications for regional stability and international relations.

US Military Action in Nigeria

The US has launched strikes against Islamic State targets in Nigeria. This military intervention comes amid concerns about jihadist violence in the region. The US president has previously criticized the Nigerian government for not adequately protecting Christian communities from extremist attacks. These strikes indicate an escalation in US involvement aimed at combating terrorism in West Africa.

Violence in Syria and Israel

A blast at a mosque in Homs, Syria, during Friday prayers has killed at least eight people. The explosion targeted a predominantly Alawite area, a sect that has experienced violence since the fall of the former Syrian leader. Meanwhile, in Israel, two people were killed in a suspected Palestinian attack involving a vehicle ramming and a stabbing. The attacker, reportedly from the occupied West Bank, carried out assaults in separate locations. Both incidents highlight ongoing tensions and violence in the region.

Border Conflict and Ceasefire Talks in Southeast Asia

Fighting continues along the Thailand-Cambodia border as ceasefire negotiations enter their third day. Despite ongoing talks, Cambodia has accused Thailand of conducting "indiscriminate attacks" in the border area. The renewed clashes have raised concerns about the stability of the region and the prospects for a lasting peace agreement.

Legal and Cultural Developments

In Malaysia, the former prime minister Najib Razak has been sentenced to 15 years in prison for his involvement in a massive scandal concerning the state investment fund 1MDB. This verdict marks a significant moment in addressing corruption in the country. In the US, a judge blocked the detention of a British social media campaigner, Imran Ahmed, who was accused along with others of pressuring companies to censor free speech. Additionally, a debate has emerged around a Russian archaeologist wanted by Ukraine over excavations in occupied Crimea, raising questions about the intersection of cultural heritage and political conflict.
