Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 26, 2025

International Diplomacy and Conflict

Ukrainian leader Volodymyr Zelensky plans to meet with US President Donald Trump in Florida this Sunday to discuss efforts to end the ongoing war with Russia. Zelensky expressed optimism that "a lot can be decided before the new year," signaling potential progress in peace talks that could impact the conflict's trajectory.

Meanwhile, the United States has launched military strikes against Islamic State targets in Nigeria. This move follows President Trump's earlier accusations that the Nigerian government has not adequately protected its Christian population from jihadist violence. The strikes indicate an ongoing US commitment to counterterrorism efforts in the region.

In the Middle East, a blast at a mosque during Friday prayers in Homs, Syria, resulted in at least eight deaths. The explosion targeted a predominantly Alawite neighborhood, a group that has experienced violence since the fall of former-President Bashar al-Assad. This incident underscores continuing tensions and sectarian violence in Syria.

Regional Violence and Security Incidents

In Israel, two people were killed in an attack involving a vehicle ramming and a stabbing. The attacker is reported to be from the occupied West Bank. Police say the assailant ran over at least one person and stabbed another several miles away, highlighting ongoing security challenges in the area.

Along the border between Thailand and Cambodia, hostilities persisted despite ongoing ceasefire talks. Thailand conducted bombing raids in Cambodian border areas, which Cambodia condemned as "indiscriminate attacks." Negotiators are meeting for a third day to try to resolve weeks of renewed fighting, but violence continues to complicate peace efforts.

Political Developments and Legal Cases

Israel has officially recognized Somaliland as an independent state, according to Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu. This decision has prompted condemnation from Somalia, Egypt, Turkey, and Djibouti, who warn it sets a dangerous precedent. The recognition reflects shifting alliances and regional tensions in the Horn of Africa.

In Malaysia, former Prime Minister Najib Razak was sentenced to 15 years in prison for his role in a major corruption scandal involving the sovereign wealth fund 1MDB. His conviction is a significant development in the fight against corruption in Malaysia and may have lasting political repercussions.

Legal and Social Issues

In the United States, a judge blocked the detention of British social media campaigner Imran Ahmed, one of five people accused of attempting to pressure US companies into censoring content. This ruling reflects ongoing legal debates over free speech and the role of social media in public discourse.

Separately, a debate has emerged over whether a prominent Russian archaeologist should face trial for conducting excavations in Crimea, a region occupied by Russia but claimed by Ukraine. The case raises complex questions about the intersection of cultural heritage, museum work, and geopolitical conflict.

Weather and Environmental Concerns

California saw a reduction in heavy rain after recent downpours, but officials continue to warn that the risk of floods and mudslides remains high. Saturated ground means that even small amounts of additional rainfall could trigger dangerous conditions. Residents are advised to remain vigilant as the weather and recovery efforts continue.
