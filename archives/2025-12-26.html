Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 26, 2025

Ukraine and US Discuss Peace Prospects

The leader of Ukraine is scheduled to meet with US President Donald Trump in Florida this Sunday. The Ukrainian leader expressed optimism, suggesting that significant decisions on ending the war with Russia could be made before the new year. This meeting comes amid ongoing conflict and diplomatic efforts to find a resolution.

US Military Action Against Islamic State in Nigeria

The United States has launched strikes targeting Islamic State militants in Nigeria. President Trump has criticized the Nigerian government for what he sees as a failure to protect Christians from jihadist violence. This military move is part of broader efforts to counter extremist groups in the region.

Violence in Syria and Israel

A blast at a mosque in the Syrian city of Homs killed at least eight people. The explosion occurred during Friday prayers in an area predominantly inhabited by Alawites, a community that has faced violence and crackdowns since the fall of Bashar al-Assad’s presidency.

In Israel, two people died in an incident involving a suspected Palestinian attacker from the West Bank. Police report that the attacker first ran over a victim with a vehicle and then stabbed another several miles away. The incident highlights ongoing tensions in the region.

Border Conflict and Ceasefire Talks Between Thailand and Cambodia

Fighting continues on the border between Thailand and Cambodia despite ongoing ceasefire negotiations. Cambodia has accused Thailand of carrying out indiscriminate bombings in the disputed area. Negotiators are meeting for a third consecutive day, seeking to end weeks of renewed violence but so far have not reached an agreement.

Legal and Cultural Developments

In Malaysia, former Prime Minister Najib Razak was sentenced to 15 years in prison for his involvement in a multibillion-dollar scandal linked to the 1MDB sovereign wealth fund. His conviction marks a significant development related to corruption investigations in the region.

Meanwhile, a US judge blocked the detention of British social media campaigner Imran Ahmed, who was among five people accused of pressuring US companies to censor free speech. The decision reflects ongoing debates about regulation and freedom online.

In Russia, a case involving a prominent archaeologist wanted by Ukraine has sparked discussion about the relationship between museums and the state. The scholar’s work in occupied Crimea has raised questions about cultural heritage and politics in contested areas.
