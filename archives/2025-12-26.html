Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 26, 2025

Diplomacy and Conflict

Ukrainian leader Volodymyr Zelensky plans to meet US President Donald Trump in Florida this Sunday. Zelensky said the meeting could result in significant decisions on ending the war with Russia before the new year. This meeting comes amid ongoing international efforts to address the conflict in Ukraine.

In Nigeria, the US launched airstrikes targeting the Islamic State, responding to ongoing jihadist threats. President Trump has criticized the Nigerian government for not adequately protecting Christians from attacks. The strikes mark an escalation in US efforts to combat extremist groups in the region.

Meanwhile, tensions in the Middle East and Southeast Asia remain high. A blast during Friday prayers at a mosque in Homs, Syria, killed at least eight people. The attack targeted a predominantly Alawite area, a sect frequently caught in conflict since the fall of former-President Bashar al-Assad.

In Israel, two people were killed in a suspected attack involving a ramming and stabbing. Police said the attacker came from the occupied West Bank and targeted victims miles apart.

On the border between Thailand and Cambodia, fighting persists. Thailand bombed a Cambodian border area as ceasefire negotiations entered their third day. Cambodia accused Thailand of "indiscriminate attacks," underlining the fragility of the peace talks.

International Recognition and Political Justice

Israel has formally recognized Somaliland as an independent state, according to Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu. This move has been strongly condemned by Somalia, Egypt, Turkey, and Djibouti, who warn that it sets a dangerous precedent in international relations.

In Malaysia, former Prime Minister Najib Razak was sentenced to 15 years in jail. He was convicted for his involvement in a massive scandal related to the country's sovereign wealth fund, 1MDB. The ruling is a significant development in Malaysia’s ongoing efforts to address corruption at high levels.

Legal and Social Issues

In the United States, a judge blocked the detention of Imran Ahmed, a British social media campaigner. Ahmed was among five people accused of trying to pressure US companies into censoring free speech online. The case raises questions about balancing free speech with regulation on social media.

In Russia, a debate has emerged around whether a top archaeologist should face trial for excavating in Crimea, a region occupied by Russia but claimed by Ukraine. The case has sparked wider discussions on the intersection of cultural heritage institutions and the Russian state.

Weather and Environment

California’s recent heavy rainfall has started to ease, but authorities warn that risks remain. With the ground saturated, even moderate additional rain could cause flooding and mudslides in vulnerable areas. Residents and officials are staying alert to the potential for further damage in the coming days.
