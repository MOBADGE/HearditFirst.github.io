Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 26, 2025

Diplomacy and Conflict

Ukrainian leader Volodymyr Zelensky plans to meet US President Donald Trump in Florida on Sunday to discuss ways to end the war with Russia. Zelensky expressed optimism that significant decisions could be made before the new year, signaling a potential shift in diplomatic efforts amid ongoing conflict.

Meanwhile, violence continues in several regions. In Israel, a suspected Palestinian attacker from the occupied West Bank killed two people in a ramming and stabbing incident. The attacker reportedly ran over at least one individual and then stabbed another several miles away, highlighting ongoing security concerns.

Military Actions and Security

The United States launched military strikes targeting Islamic State militants in Nigeria. President Trump had previously criticized the Nigerian government for failing to protect Christian communities from jihadist violence. These strikes represent an active US effort to counter terrorism in the region.

In Syria, a blast at a mosque in the city of Homs during Friday prayers killed at least eight people. The explosion targeted a predominantly Alawite area, a group that has faced violence since the fall of former-President Bashar al-Assad.

Southeast Asia Tensions

Thailand conducted bombing raids on border areas in Cambodia, drawing accusations from Cambodia of "indiscriminate attacks." These military actions come as ceasefire talks between the two countries continue for a third day. The ongoing negotiations aim to resolve weeks of renewed fighting along their shared border.

Legal Developments

Former Malaysian Prime Minister Najib Razak received a 15-year prison sentence over his involvement in a major scandal linked to the sovereign wealth fund 1MDB. The conviction stems from the billion-dollar corruption case that has drawn global attention.

In the United States, a judge blocked the detention of British social media campaigner Imran Ahmed, who was one of five accused of attempting to pressure US companies into censoring speech. The ruling paused a contentious debate about free speech, censorship, and the authority of social media firms.

Cultural and Legal Questions in Crimea

A dispute has arisen over a Russian archaeologist wanted by Ukraine for conducting digs in the occupied peninsula of Crimea. The case has ignited discussion about how museums and cultural institutions relate to the Russian government, reflecting broader tensions over Crimea’s status and heritage.
