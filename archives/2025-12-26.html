Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 26, 2025

International Security

The United States has carried out airstrikes targeting Islamic State militants in Nigeria. This is part of an ongoing effort to counter jihadist groups in the region. Previously, President Donald Trump criticized the Nigerian government for not adequately protecting Christian communities from such militant attacks. The airstrikes mark a continuation of US involvement in combating extremist groups abroad, underscoring concerns about terrorism in West Africa.

Ukraine Peace Talks

Ukraine’s leader, Volodymyr Zelensky, expressed optimism following discussions with US envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner about new peace proposals. Zelensky highlighted “new ideas” that emerged from the talks, suggesting potential progress in efforts to resolve the conflict affecting his country. The outcome of these discussions could influence future diplomatic approaches to achieving stability in Ukraine.

Israeli Incident

In Israel, two people were killed in a suspected attack involving a ramming and stabbing incident. Police report that the attacker, described as being from the occupied West Bank, first ran over at least one individual and then stabbed another victim several miles away. This event is part of ongoing tensions and violence in the region.

Southeast Asia Border Conflict

Thailand has carried out bombing operations near the Cambodian border amid ceasefire negotiations. Cambodia has accused Thailand of conducting “indiscriminate attacks” while both sides are engaged in talks for a third day to end renewed fighting that has lasted for weeks. The continued fighting and diplomatic efforts highlight ongoing challenges in resolving border disputes in Southeast Asia.

Malaysian Political Corruption Case

Former Malaysian Prime Minister Najib Razak was found guilty of involvement in a major scandal linked to the country’s sovereign wealth fund, 1MDB. The conviction relates to the misappropriation of billions of dollars from the fund, a case that has had significant implications for Malaysia’s political and financial sectors. This ruling may affect Najib’s political future and set a precedent for anti-corruption efforts in the region.

Legal Developments in the US

In the United States, a judge has blocked the detention of British social media campaigner Imran Ahmed. Ahmed was one of five individuals accused of attempting to pressure US companies into censoring free speech. The judge’s decision suggests a cautious approach to this case, which raises questions about the limits of activism and free expression in the digital age.

Cultural Dispute Over Crimea

A debate has arisen over whether a prominent Russian archaeologist should face trial for conducting excavations in Crimea, territory that is internationally disputed. The scholar is wanted by Ukraine, and the case has sparked discussions about the role of museums and cultural institutions in relation to state policies, particularly in contested regions. This situation illustrates the complex intersection of archaeology, politics, and national sovereignty.
