Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 13, 2025

Conflict and Security

Three Americans, including two soldiers and a civilian interpreter, were killed in Syria during an attack carried out by an IS gunman, according to the US Central Command. In Gaza, the Israeli military targeted Raed Saad, a senior commander in Hamas' Qassam Brigades, killing him in an airstrike. Meanwhile, fighting along the Cambodia-Thailand border has prompted Cambodia to shut border crossings, despite US President Donald Trump stating that both countries had agreed to a ceasefire.

Ukraine Situation

Ukraine continues to face serious challenges from Russian attacks. Overnight strikes on the energy grid have left about one million households without power. At the same time, a series of attacks on medical supply warehouses has destroyed stock essential to most pharmacies across the country. These developments come as US envoy Steve Witkoff prepares for further discussions with Ukraine's president about ongoing support and crisis management.

International Relations and Diplomacy

In a significant diplomatic move, Belarus released 123 prisoners, including opposition activist Maria Kolesnikova, following an agreement with the United States. This deal has also led to the US lifting sanctions against Belarus, signaling a potential thaw in relations that may impact regional politics.

Culture and Society

In India, football star Lionel Messi, who plays for Inter Miami and the Argentina national team, has been on a tour through several cities including Kolkata, Hyderabad, Mumbai, and Delhi. During one event, some fans became unruly, throwing chairs and bottles, highlighting tensions amid what was meant to be a celebratory occasion. In Nigeria, Lagos is hosting a street art festival, transforming the city into an "open-air gallery" that showcases its growing arts scene, creativity, and nightlife.

Unusual News

In Virginia, a raccoon nicknamed the "trashed panda" is suspected of breaking into a karate equipment store and stealing snacks from a Department of Motor Vehicles location. The raccoon had previously been reported for being drunk, adding an unusual twist to local animal encounters.

Additional Notes

New photographs from Jeffrey Epstein’s estate have been shared with the US House Oversight Committee, showing figures such as Donald Trump, Prince Andrew, and Bill Clinton. The photos do not imply any wrongdoing but are part of a large collection the committee is reviewing.
