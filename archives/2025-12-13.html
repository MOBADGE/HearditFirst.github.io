Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 13, 2025

Conflict and Security

Three Americans, including two US soldiers and a civilian interpreter, were killed in an attack by an Islamic State gunman in Syria, according to the US Central Command. Meanwhile, Israel conducted an airstrike killing Raed Saad, a senior commander in Hamas’ Qassam Brigades. These incidents reflect ongoing tensions and conflicts in the Middle East and Syria.

In Ukraine, Russian attacks have severely damaged the country’s energy grid, leaving around one million households without power. The strikes come as US envoy Steve Witkoff prepares for further talks with Ukraine’s president, indicating ongoing diplomatic efforts amid the crisis. Additionally, Russian strikes have targeted medical warehouses supplying most of Ukraine’s pharmacies, impacting health supplies across the nation.

International Diplomacy and Developments

Belarus has released 123 prisoners, including opposition activist Maria Kolesnikova, as part of a deal with the United States. This development coincides with the US lifting sanctions on Belarus, signaling a possible easing of tensions between the two countries.

Despite reports of a ceasefire agreement announced by US President Donald Trump, fighting continues between Cambodia and Thailand, leading Cambodia to shut border crossings with Thailand. The situation remains fragile, with ongoing conflict despite diplomatic statements.

Culture and Society

In India, an event featuring Lionel Messi, the forward for Inter Miami and Argentina, encountered disruption as some angry fans threw chairs and bottles. Messi’s tour, which includes cities like Kolkata, Hyderabad, Mumbai, and Delhi, has drawn significant attention but also some unrest.

In Lagos, Nigeria, a new street art festival is transforming the city into an "open-air gallery." Known for its vibrant arts scene and nightlife, Lagos is showcasing street art more prominently through this festival, highlighting a growing appreciation for diverse forms of creativity.

Unusual News

In Virginia, a raccoon suspected of being drunk has allegedly broken into a karate equipment shop and stolen snacks from the Department of Motor Vehicles. This unusual incident adds a lighter note amidst the more serious news of the day.

Notable Document Releases

New photos from Jeffrey Epstein’s estate have been shared with the House Oversight Committee. The images, which include figures such as Donald Trump, Prince Andrew, and Bill Clinton, do not imply any wrongdoing but are part of a broader release of materials related to Epstein.
