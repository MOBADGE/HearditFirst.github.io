Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 13, 2025

International Diplomacy and Prisoner Release

Belarus has freed 123 prisoners, including opposition activist Maria Kolesnikova, following a new agreement with the United States. This step appears to be linked to the US lifting some sanctions on Belarus. The release may signal a thaw in relations and could lead to further diplomatic engagement between the two countries, though details of the broader deal have not been fully disclosed.

Ongoing Conflict in Ukraine

Ukraine continues to face significant challenges as Russia has launched overnight attacks targeting the country’s energy infrastructure. These strikes have left around one million households without power. In addition, Russian forces have destroyed warehouses supplying most of Ukraine’s pharmacies, deepening the country’s humanitarian and medical supply crisis. US envoy Steve Witkoff is preparing to hold further talks with Ukraine’s president amid these escalating attacks, indicating ongoing international efforts to address the conflict.

Middle East Violence

In Gaza, Israeli forces targeted a senior Hamas official in an airstrike that resulted in the deaths of four people and injuries to others nearby, according to Gaza’s Hamas-run civil defense agency. This strike continues the cycle of violence in the region, raising concerns about further escalation and its impact on civilians in Gaza.

Southeast Asia Border Tensions

Despite a claimed ceasefire agreement announced by US President Donald Trump, Cambodia has shut down border crossings with Thailand amid continued fighting. The closure suggests that hostilities remain unresolved, and the situation on the border is still volatile. This ongoing conflict complicates efforts toward lasting peace in the region.

Notable Cultural and Social Events

In a lighter yet unusual story, a raccoon in Virginia suspected of breaking into a karate shop and the Department of Motor Vehicles has attracted attention for its antics, including reportedly consuming alcohol. Meanwhile, in India, soccer star Lionel Messi faced a disruptive event during a public appearance when angry fans threw chairs and bottles, showing the unpredictable nature of large sports events.

In Nigeria, Lagos is hosting a street art festival that is transforming the city into an "open-air gallery." While the city is already famous for its lively arts and nightlife, this event is introducing a new dimension by spotlighting street art, offering a fresh cultural experience for residents and visitors alike.

Political Images from Epstein Estate

New photos recently made public from Jeffrey Epstein’s estate show figures including Donald Trump, Andrew, and Bill Clinton. The images form part of a collection obtained by the US House Oversight Committee. Importantly, these photos do not imply any wrongdoing by those pictured. The release reflects ongoing interest and investigations related to Epstein’s connections.
