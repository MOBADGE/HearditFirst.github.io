Heard It First – Daily Digest

March 30, 2026

Global Politics

Spain has closed its airspace to US aircraft involved in the conflict in Iran and denied the US access to two jointly run military bases in Andalusia. This move limits US military operations from Spanish territory amid rising tensions related to the Iran war. Meanwhile, Ukraine’s president says allied countries have asked him to scale back attacks on Russian energy infrastructure. The request comes as energy prices soar due to the conflict in Iran, reflecting concerns about the wider economic impact of military actions.

In Southeast Asia, Myanmar’s military leader Min Aung Hlaing has been nominated as president. Sanctioned by Western countries, he is now expected to assume the presidency, consolidating the military’s control over the country.

Energy and Trade

A Russian oil tanker has reached Cuban waters after former US President Donald Trump said he had “no problem” with the shipment. The tanker’s arrival comes amid a loosening of the blockade on Russian oil exports to Cuba. This development highlights ongoing shifts in international relations and sanctions policies affecting energy trade.

The conflict in Iran is also influencing global energy markets indirectly, as indicated by Ukraine’s allies expressing concern about high energy prices spurred by attacks on Russian infrastructure.

Migration and Refugees

In Germany, many Syrian refugees are expected to return to Syria within the next three years, according to statements by government officials. This position aligns with the German government’s tougher stance on refugees and immigrants amid growing support for the anti-immigration AfD party. The situation reflects ongoing debates in Europe about the future of refugee populations and immigration policy.

Incidents and Legal News

Australian police shot and killed Dezi Freeman, a fugitive who had been in hiding for seven months. Freeman was wanted in connection with the fatal shooting of two police officers in August. His death marks the end of a lengthy manhunt.

In the UK, Cardiff City’s £104 million damages claim over the death of footballer Emiliano Sala has been dismissed. The club now faces a bill of approximately £400,000 in costs related to the case.

Culture and Entertainment

The Barbie Dream Fest event in Florida is issuing refunds after fans complained about the experience. Attendees criticized the event for a dull grey backdrop and a so-called “swag bag” that contained only a plastic pouch with a Barbie-branded hand sanitiser despite ticket prices up to $450.

On a lighter note, Kris Jenner’s image has become a symbol of good luck on Chinese social media, with hundreds of thousands of posts shared in recent days as users hope for prosperity. This trend shows the unexpected cultural reach of celebrity figures in global online communities.

Corporate News

Air Canada’s CEO Michael Rousseau announced plans to retire following criticism over his condolence video after a fatal collision at LaGuardia airport. The message was delivered only in English, drawing calls for his resignation amid concerns about language inclusivity in Canada’s bilingual context.
