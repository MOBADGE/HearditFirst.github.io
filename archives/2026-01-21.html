January 21, 2026

EU-US Relations

Tensions between the European Union and the United States have increased recently. The EU has suspended approval of a US trade deal amid conflicts tied to Donald Trump's interest in acquiring Greenland. This dispute has added strain to transatlantic relations as European leaders prepare for an emergency EU meeting in Brussels on Thursday to address the situation. Trump's comments and actions are reportedly causing frustration among many European officials, indicating that the dialogue between the EU and US may become more complicated in the near future.

Donald Trump’s Activities and Reactions

Donald Trump delivered a speech at the Davos economic forum that received mixed reactions from attendees, according to journalist Faisal Islam. In other developments, seven additional Muslim-majority countries accepted an invitation from Trump to join his new organisation called the "Board of Peace." These countries join existing members including Albania, Bahrain, Hungary, Morocco, and Israel. The expansion of this group suggests that Trump continues to pursue international initiatives despite facing criticism in other areas.

Middle East Conflict and Humanitarian Issues

In Gaza, violence continued with an Israeli strike killing three journalists, as reported by first responders. Alongside this, eight Palestinians were killed by Israeli artillery and gunfire on the same day. In the occupied West Bank, Israeli authorities have postponed the demolition of a football pitch used by Palestinian children in Bethlehem following an international campaign, showing the impact of global advocacy on local decisions.

US and Middle East Security

The United States has announced plans to transfer Islamic State prisoners from Syria to Iraq. This move comes as Syrian government forces regain control of northeastern areas once dominated by Kurdish-led forces. The transfer highlights the shifting power dynamics in Syria and the ongoing efforts to manage security threats related to the Islamic State.

Science and Energy

A significant archaeological discovery was made on the Indonesian island of Sulawesi, where researchers found the world’s oldest known cave painting: a red claw hand stencil. This find could reshape understanding of the timeline of human creativity and artistic expression.

Meanwhile, Japan has restarted a reactor at the world’s largest nuclear power plant nearly 15 years after the Fukushima disaster. The restart signals Japan’s cautious steps toward rebuilding its nuclear energy capacity while still grappling with the memories and consequences of the meltdown.

United States Judicial Developments

The US Supreme Court expressed skepticism regarding Donald Trump’s firing of Federal Reserve Governor Lisa Cook. One conservative justice warned that the administration’s interpretation of the law could undermine the independence of the Federal Reserve. This ongoing legal scrutiny may influence future actions involving the central bank’s governance.
