Heard It First – Daily Digest

January 21, 2026

Middle East Conflict and Developments

An Israeli strike in Gaza resulted in the deaths of three journalists, according to first responders. Additionally, eight Palestinians were killed by Israeli artillery and gunfire across Gaza on the same day, with medics reporting the casualties. In a related development, Israel has postponed the demolition of a Palestinian children's football pitch in Bethlehem following an international campaign. This delay signals a response to public pressure concerning the treatment of Palestinian communities in the occupied West Bank.

Meanwhile, in Syria and Iraq, the United States announced plans to transfer Islamic State prisoners from Syria to Iraq. This move comes as Syrian government forces take control of territories in the northeast, which had previously been under Kurdish-led forces. This transfer could impact the management of captured militants and regional security dynamics.

International Peace Initiatives

Israel has agreed to join former US President Donald Trump's newly formed Board of Peace. This organisation has also attracted countries including Albania, Bahrain, Hungary, Morocco, and the United Arab Emirates. The initiative aims to promote peace, though details on the board's activities and impact remain limited.

Human Rights in Iran

Photos recently leaked to the BBC reveal the faces of hundreds of people killed during a government crackdown on protests in Iran. These images were shown to families attempting to identify their loved ones at a mortuary in Tehran. The crackdown has drawn international attention to the human rights situation in the country.

Transportation Safety in Spain

Spain experienced a second deadly rail crash within days. The incident resulted in at least 37 people being injured, with five in serious condition. This follows a collision between two trains in Andalusia just days earlier, raising concerns about railway safety and response measures.

Energy and Historical Discoveries

Japan restarted a reactor at the world’s largest nuclear plant, nearly 15 years after the Fukushima meltdown. The decision reflects Japan’s ongoing efforts to manage energy resources while addressing lingering public concerns tied to the disaster.

In the field of archaeology, researchers announced the discovery of the world's oldest known cave painting on Indonesia's Sulawesi island. The stencilled outline of a red claw hand could rewrite current timelines regarding the origins of human creativity. This finding offers new insights into early artistic expression by ancient peoples.

Legal and Cultural Notes

In Japan, a man received a life sentence for the killing of former Prime Minister Shinzo Abe. The assassination was a shocking event in a country known for very low gun crime rates.

Separately, court documents revealed personal text messages from actress Blake Lively, in which she called director Justin Baldoni "a clown." The messages emerged during a legal proceeding involving the two entertainment figures.
