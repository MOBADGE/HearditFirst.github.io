Iran Conflict and Leadership Crisis

The ongoing conflict involving Iran continues to escalate, with significant impacts at home and abroad. Joe Kent, director of the US National Counterterrorism Center, resigned recently, criticizing President Donald Trump for initiating the war under pressure from Israel and its American allies. His resignation underscores growing tensions within US policy circles about the conflict's direction.

In Iran, the death of Ali Larijani, a key and experienced political figure, has added to the instability at the heart of the country's leadership. Larijani was known for his influential role in shaping policy, and his passing deepens the existing crisis amid the conflict.

Meanwhile, ordinary Iranians face harsh realities as they live between US-Israeli air strikes and a hardening Iranian government seeking to maintain control. Tehran residents describe an atmosphere of fear and repression, highlighting the human toll of this ongoing war.

Impact on India’s Energy Supplies

India is feeling the effects of the Iran conflict on its energy resources. Currently, the country relies heavily on liquefied petroleum gas (LPG), but disruptions linked to the war have caused uncertainty in supply and pricing. There is growing concern that India's piped natural gas network could soon face similar challenges if tensions persist, potentially affecting millions of consumers who depend on this fuel source for heating and cooking.

Advances in Affordable Weight-Loss Medications

On a different note, India is poised to make a significant contribution to global health through the introduction of cheaper weight-loss medications. With the patent for semaglutide expiring on March 20, Indian manufacturers are set to launch about 50 generic versions. This development could make effective obesity treatments more accessible worldwide, potentially reshaping efforts to address the global obesity epidemic by lowering costs and expanding availability.
