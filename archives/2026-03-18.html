Conflict and Tensions in Iran

Residents in Tehran are living under intense pressure due to ongoing air strikes by US and Israeli forces. At the same time, Iran's government is enforcing strict repression as it seeks to reassert control amid this conflict. This dual threat is creating a climate of fear and uncertainty for ordinary Iranians, who feel caught between external attacks and internal crackdowns.

Leadership Crisis in Iran

The death of Ali Larijani, a key and experienced figure within Iran's political system, has deepened existing uncertainties at the center of the country's leadership. Larijani was viewed as an influential policymaker, and his passing may affect the stability and decision-making processes in the Islamic Republic during these turbulent times.

US Counterterrorism Official Resigns

Joe Kent, the director of the US National Counterterrorism Center, has resigned, citing disagreement with the US government's approach to the Iran conflict. Kent criticized former President Trump for initiating the war under influence from Israel and its supporters in the US. He has called for a reversal of this policy, highlighting internal divisions within US security agencies about the handling of the Iran situation.

Changes in the Global Obesity Fight

India is poised to expand access to affordable weight-loss drugs as its patent on semaglutide expires on March 20. This change will allow about 50 new generic versions to enter the market, potentially lowering costs and enabling wider use. This development could significantly impact the global fight against obesity by making effective treatments more accessible.

Energy Concerns for India

India’s energy sector is facing challenges linked to the conflict in Iran. While liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) users have already experienced disruptions, there are growing concerns that India’s piped natural gas network may also face supply squeezes. How this situation evolves could influence energy availability and prices across the country.
