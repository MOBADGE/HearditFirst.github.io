Iran Conflict and Its Impact on Civilians

Residents of Tehran are experiencing severe hardship amid ongoing air strikes by the US and Israel, alongside increased repression by the Iranian government. People describe a climate of fear and uncertainty as the regime works to strengthen its control. This tense situation highlights the serious effects of the conflict on ordinary Iranians caught between external attacks and internal crackdowns.

Death of Iranian Policy Maker Ali Larijani

Ali Larijani, a long-standing influential figure in Iran’s political sphere, has died. His passing is expected to deepen the challenges within Iran’s leadership, as he was considered an experienced and key policymaker. The loss may complicate efforts to manage the evolving crisis inside the country, though further developments regarding the leadership response remain to be seen.

US Counterterrorism Resignation Over Iran War

Joe Kent, director of the US National Counterterrorism Center, has resigned from his role. He criticized President Donald Trump’s decision to initiate the war against Iran, attributing it to pressure from Israel and related lobbying groups in the United States. Kent called for a reversal of this policy, signaling dissent within US security circles about the current approach to the Iran conflict.

Expansion of India’s Weight-Loss Drug Market

India’s semaglutide patent is set to expire on March 20, allowing approximately 50 new branded generic versions to enter the market. This development is expected to make semaglutide-based weight-loss treatments more affordable and accessible, potentially transforming the global fight against obesity by providing cheaper medication options worldwide.

Risks to India’s Energy Supply Amid Iran War

The conflict in Iran has already impacted users of liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) in India, causing supply concerns. There is now growing apprehension about potential shortages in India’s piped gas network if tensions and disruptions continue. This situation underscores the wider economic and energy security implications of the Iran war for countries dependent on regional energy supplies.
