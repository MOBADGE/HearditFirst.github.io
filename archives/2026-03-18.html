Conflict and Repression in Iran

Residents of Tehran are experiencing severe fear and hardship amid ongoing US-Israeli air strikes and the Iranian government's efforts to reassert control. The combination of external military action and internal repression is creating a tense and dangerous atmosphere in the capital. Civilians find themselves caught between foreign attacks and a regime focused on maintaining power, which has deep human consequences and raises concerns about stability in the region.

Political Turmoil Following Death of Ali Larijani

Iran faces a leadership crisis after the death of Ali Larijani, a figure considered one of the country’s most experienced and influential policy makers. Larijani's passing leaves a significant gap at the center of Iran's political landscape, complicating the already fraught situation as the country deals with internal and external pressures. How Iran navigates this leadership vacuum could affect its domestic governance and international relations going forward.

US Leadership Changes and Criticism Over Iran Conflict

Joe Kent, director of the US National Counterterrorism Center, has resigned amid the ongoing conflict with Iran. He criticized former President Trump for initiating the war, alleging it was driven by pressure from Israel and its American supporters. Kent is calling for a reversal of US policy, reflecting a notable dissent within the US government on the handling and escalation of the Iran conflict. This development could signal shifting attitudes toward the war strategy in Washington.

Shift in Global Weight-Loss Drug Market

India is poised to reshape the global fight against obesity with the imminent patent expiration of semaglutide on March 20. This event will allow around 50 branded generic versions of the drug to enter the market, potentially lowering costs and increasing accessibility worldwide. This move could have significant impacts on obesity treatment options, especially in lower- and middle-income countries, by making an effective medication more widely available.

Possible Impact of Iran Conflict on India’s Energy Supply

The ongoing war in Iran is affecting energy supplies beyond the immediate region, with India already seeing disruptions in liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) supplies. Now, concerns are rising about potential shortages in India’s piped gas network. As India relies on imports linked to Iran, further constraints could disrupt household and industrial energy consumption, underscoring the wider economic fallout from the conflict in the Middle East.
