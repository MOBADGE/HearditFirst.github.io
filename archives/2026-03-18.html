March 18, 2026

Conflict and Repression in Iran

Residents of Tehran are facing increasing hardship as Iran experiences ongoing air strikes by the US and Israel. At the same time, the Iranian government is intensifying repression to maintain control amid the conflict. People in the capital describe a sense of dread caught between external military attacks and internal crackdowns. This combination has created an atmosphere of fear and uncertainty for many Iranians as the regime seeks to reassert its power.

Leadership Changes in Iran

The recent death of Ali Larijani, a long-standing influential figure in Iran’s political landscape, adds to the uncertainty. Larijani was regarded as one of the Islamic Republic’s most experienced policy makers. His passing is seen as deepening the leadership crisis within the country’s ruling system during a time of conflict and internal strain. How this will affect Iran’s political direction remains to be seen.

US Political Developments

In the United States, Joe Kent, director of the National Counterterrorism Center, resigned in protest over the ongoing conflict with Iran. Kent criticized former President Trump, accusing him of initiating the war under pressure from Israel and its advocates in the US. His resignation highlights internal dissent within the US government regarding the handling of the Iran conflict.

Separately, a US judge has ordered the Trump administration to reinstate funding and staffing for Voice of America, an international broadcaster. The channel had seen 85% of its workforce laid off after Trump accused it of biased reporting. The ruling may lead to the reopening and revitalization of the broadcaster, which serves as a key source of news for international audiences.

Impact on India’s Energy and Health Sectors

India is facing economic ripple effects due to the war in Iran. Many users of liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) have already experienced disruptions, raising concerns about possible shortages in piped gas supplies, which serve millions of households. The conflict’s impact on energy imports could strain India’s domestic fuel availability going forward.

On a more positive note, India is poised to significantly influence the global fight against obesity. The patent for the weight-loss drug semaglutide is expiring on March 20, allowing around 50 new generic versions to enter the market. These more affordable drugs could make effective treatments more accessible worldwide and reshape approaches to managing obesity.
