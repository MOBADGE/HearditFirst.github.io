Social Media Platforms Held Liable for Addiction

After a five-week trial, Meta and Google have been found liable in a landmark case regarding the addictive nature of their social media platforms. The verdict marks a significant moment in ongoing efforts to address how social media impacts users’ mental health and behavior. The outcome could influence future regulations and how these companies design their platforms to reduce addictive features.

UN Recognises Slavery as a Grave Crime and Calls for Reparations

The United Nations has passed a landmark resolution declaring slavery the "gravest crime against humanity." The resolution calls for an official apology and contributions to a reparations fund, though it does not specify the amount to be paid. This recognition is a historic step toward addressing past injustices and could lead to further international efforts aimed at reconciliation and justice related to slavery’s legacy.

U.S. Political Shift: Democrats Win Key Florida State Seat

Democrat Emily Gregory is projected to have flipped a legislative seat in Florida that includes former US President Donald Trump’s Mar-a-Lago home. This seat had previously been won by a Republican candidate by a wide margin of 19 percentage points in 2024. The result may indicate changing political dynamics in the state and could have implications for future elections both locally and nationally.

Human Remains of Historical Figure Rediscovered in the Netherlands

The remains believed to be those of d’Artagnan, a famed musketeer immortalized in Alexandre Dumas’s novels, have been discovered under a Dutch church. D’Artagnan died during the Siege of Maastricht in 1673. The find sheds new light on historical events of the 17th century and highlights ongoing interest in uncovering tangible links to famous figures from history.

Philippines Declares Energy Emergency Amid Oil Concerns

The Philippines has declared an energy emergency as it faces supply issues. The country’s president announced plans to procure one million barrels of oil to bolster current reserves. This move aims to stabilize the energy situation and ensure a steady flow of oil amidst regional and global market pressures that could affect prices and availability in the coming months.
