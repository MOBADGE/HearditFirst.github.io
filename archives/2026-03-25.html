Justice and Accountability

Five women who were abused by Jeffrey Epstein shared their experiences with BBC Newsnight, describing the profound and lasting impact of their ordeal. The survivors spoke about the fear and control Epstein exerted during his abuses. Politics in Denmark and the Philippines

In Denmark, the ruling party has won the most votes in the latest election but failed to secure a clear majority. This marks the party’s weakest performance since 1903 and begins a period of challenging negotiations to form a new government. Meanwhile, in the Philippines, Vice President Sara Duterte has started impeachment hearings amid concerns over her potential presidential bid in 2028. The political process is intensifying, reflecting broader tensions ahead of upcoming elections.

Economy and Energy Concerns

Larry Fink, a leading figure in finance, warned that if oil prices reach $150 and remain high for a sustained period, the world could face a global recession. He emphasized that such a rise would have significant effects on the global economy, underlining the broader risks tied to energy markets and economic stability.

Media and Technology Developments

OpenAI has ended its partnership with Disney and shut down the Sora video-making app, less than two years after its launch. The app, which used artificial intelligence to create videos, had caused disruptions in the media industry. This move signals a shift in how AI-based media tools are being developed and managed.

Social and Cultural News

The South Korean boy band BTS attracted a global audience of 18.4 million viewers for their comeback concert on Netflix. This livestream was their first performance together since October 2022, showcasing the group's continued international popularity.

In Australia, journalists at the national broadcaster went on strike for the first time in 20 years. The 24-hour walkout was in response to a pay rise offer that did not match inflation rates, highlighting ongoing labor disputes in the media sector.

Education and Youth Policy

Two years after the Netherlands introduced a ban on mobile phones in schools, the government is considering further measures to restrict social media use among children under 16. This initiative reflects efforts to improve young people's focus and well-being in educational settings by limiting digital distractions.
