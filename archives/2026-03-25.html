March 25, 2026

Legal and Social Media News

A landmark US court ruling found Meta and YouTube liable in a case involving social media addiction. The plaintiff, a woman, was awarded $3 million in damages. This verdict could influence many other pending cases across the United States, as it sets a precedent for holding social media companies accountable for harm caused by their platforms.

Global Human Rights

The United Nations passed a historic resolution recognising the enslavement of Africans as the "gravest crime against humanity." The resolution calls for formal apologies and contributions to a reparations fund, although the amount for reparations was not specified. This recognition marks a significant step in addressing the lasting impacts of slavery worldwide.

US Political Developments

In a notable political shift, Democrat Emily Gregory is projected to have won a Florida state legislative seat that includes the area of the former US President Donald Trump's Mar-a-Lago home. This district was strongly Republican in 2024, with the Republican candidate winning by 19 percentage points. This flip signals changing political dynamics in the state.

Meanwhile, US Congressman Thomas Massie commented on the recent release of documents related to the late financier Jeffrey Epstein. Responding to survivors’ interviews, he stated that "men need to be perp-walked," emphasizing the importance of holding perpetrators publicly accountable for abuse.

Philippines Energy Emergency

The Philippines declared an energy emergency amid concerns about oil supplies. President Marcos promised to secure an additional one million barrels of oil to boost the country’s stockpiles. This move aims to stabilize energy availability and prevent shortages in the near future.

Human Interest and Cultural News

Savannah Guthrie, a US television presenter, gave her first interview since the disappearance of her mother. She described the emotional toll, sharing how terrifying thoughts often wake her at night as she tries to imagine what happened.

In a historical discovery, the remains believed to be those of the musketeer d’Artagnan were found beneath a Dutch church. D’Artagnan, killed in 1673 during the Siege of Maastricht, was later immortalised in Alexandre Dumas’s famous stories.

In entertainment, talk show host Stephen Colbert, a known Tolkien fan, announced he will write a new film adaptation based on an early part of the first Lord of the Rings novel. This project adds a fresh artistic dimension to the enduring legacy of Tolkien’s works.

International Election News

Denmark’s Prime Minister faces challenges in forming a new government after the ruling party recorded its weakest election result since 1903. Although the party won the most votes, it lacks a clear majority, leading to complex coalition negotiations. This outcome may affect Denmark’s political stability in the coming months.

Tragic Discovery in Kenya

Authorities in Kenya uncovered a mass grave containing 32 bodies, mostly infants and children. Among them are 25 children believed to have come from local hospitals and mortuaries. This grim finding raises serious concerns about the handling and safety of vulnerable populations in the region.
