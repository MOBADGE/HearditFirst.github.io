Social Media Liability Case

A US court has held Meta and YouTube liable in a significant social media addiction lawsuit. A woman was awarded $6 million in damages after claiming harm linked to the platforms. This verdict could set a precedent for hundreds of similar cases across the country, potentially affecting how social media companies address user wellbeing and addiction concerns moving forward.

US-Iran Peace Talks

Indirect communications between the United States and Iran are ongoing amid efforts to negotiate peace. Despite these existing channels, experts suggest that a formal agreement remains distant. The talks highlight complex geopolitical dynamics and continued attempts to reduce tensions, though substantial progress has yet to be seen.

UN Resolution on African Enslavement

The United Nations has adopted a landmark resolution recognizing the enslavement of Africans as one of the "gravest crimes against humanity." The resolution also calls for an official apology and advocates for contributions to a reparations fund, although it does not specify the amount or detailed implementation steps. This development marks a significant acknowledgment on the international stage of historical injustices.

Philippines Energy Emergency

The Philippines has declared an energy emergency in response to supply concerns. The President announced plans to procure one million barrels of oil to supplement the country's current reserves. This move aims to stabilize energy availability and prevent shortages, addressing pressing needs in the nation’s energy sector.

Discovery of D’Artagnan’s Remains

Archaeologists believe they have uncovered the remains of d’Artagnan, the famous musketeer, beneath a church in the Netherlands. D’Artagnan died during the 1673 Siege of Maastricht and was later immortalized in Alexandre Dumas’s novels. This discovery provides a historical connection to the well-known literary figure and offers new insights into 17th-century European history.

Kenya Mass Grave Found

Authorities have discovered a mass grave in Kenya containing 32 bodies, most of whom are infants. Among the deceased, 25 children are believed to have come from local hospitals and mortuaries. The discovery raises urgent questions about healthcare, child mortality, and potential investigations into how these deaths occurred.
