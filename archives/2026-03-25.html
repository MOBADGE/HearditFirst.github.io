March 25, 2026

Legal and Social Media Developments

Meta and Google have been found liable in a landmark trial assessing the addictive nature of social media platforms. The five-week trial concluded with a verdict holding the companies responsible, marking a significant moment in legal approaches to social media’s impact on users. This ruling may influence future regulations and how tech companies design their platforms to address addiction concerns.

International Human Rights

The United Nations has passed a resolution recognising slavery as the "gravest crime against humanity." This historic decision calls for an official apology and the creation of a reparations fund, though it does not specify the amount to be contributed. This resolution underscores ongoing global efforts to address the legacy of slavery and promote justice for affected communities.

Political Shifts in the United States

In Florida, Democrats have flipped a state legislative seat that includes the location of the Mar-a-Lago estate. Emily Gregory’s projected victory is notable as the district was won by a Republican candidate by 19 percentage points in 2024. This result could signal changing political dynamics in the region ahead of future elections.

Human Interest and Crime

Stephen Colbert, known as a talk show host and Tolkien enthusiast, will write a new film adaptation of an early part of The Lord of the Rings novel. Separately, US congressman Thomas Massie commented on the release of files related to financier Jeffrey Epstein, responding to survivors of abuse by saying “Men need to be perp-walked,” advocating for public accountability.

Meanwhile, US television presenter Savannah Guthrie has spoken publicly for the first time since her mother’s disappearance, describing ongoing distress and fearful thoughts about what might have happened.

International News and Discoveries

In the Philippines, the government has declared an energy emergency amid concerns about oil supplies. President Marcos promised a “flow of oil,” with plans to procure one million barrels to bolster the current stock. This move aims to stabilize the country’s energy situation.

In Europe, there is news from the Netherlands that the remains of d’Artagnan, a 17th-century musketeer made famous by Alexandre Dumas’ stories, are believed to have been discovered beneath a Dutch church, a finding of historical significance.

In Denmark, the Prime Minister's party faced its weakest election performance since 1903, despite winning the most votes. The party, in power since 2019, now faces tough negotiations to form a new government.

Emerging Humanitarian Concerns

A mass grave containing 32 bodies, mostly infants, has been found in Kenya. Many of the victims are believed to have come from local hospitals and mortuaries, raising urgent questions about healthcare practices and oversight. This discovery highlights critical issues related to child welfare and the management of medical facilities in the region.
