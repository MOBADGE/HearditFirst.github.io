Social Media Addiction Trial

A US court has held Meta and YouTube liable in a landmark case concerning social media addiction. A woman was awarded $6 million in damages after a trial that could affect many similar cases nationwide. The verdict addresses the responsibility of major platforms for the mental health impacts linked to their services, setting a significant legal precedent.

US-Iran Peace Talks

Indirect communications continue between the United States and Iran through undisclosed channels. Despite ongoing contacts, a peace agreement remains distant. Both sides have complex demands and concerns, suggesting that negotiations will require sustained diplomacy before any meaningful deal can be reached.

UN Recognition of Enslavement as a Crime

The United Nations passed a resolution recognizing the enslavement of Africans as "the gravest crime against humanity." The resolution urges an official apology and calls for contributions to a reparations fund, though it does not specify financial amounts. This move highlights growing international efforts to address historic injustices related to the transatlantic slave trade.

Philippines Energy Emergency and Oil Supply

The Philippines has declared an energy emergency due to supply concerns. The President promised to secure the flow of oil by procuring an additional one million barrels to boost the country's reserves. This action aims to stabilize energy availability amid rising demand and potential supply disruptions.

Discovery of D'Artagnan's Remains

Archaeologists believe they have found the remains of the musketeer d'Artagnan beneath a Dutch church. D'Artagnan was killed during the Siege of Maastricht in 1673 and is famous from the novels of Alexandre Dumas. This discovery provides historical insight into a figure celebrated in literature and military history.

Human Rights Concern in Kenya

Authorities discovered a mass grave in Kenya containing 32 bodies, mostly infants. Among the remains, 25 children appeared to have originated from local hospitals and mortuaries. This finding raises serious questions about the circumstances surrounding these deaths and the need for thorough investigations.
