Social Media Liability Ruling

Meta and YouTube have been found liable in a notable social media addiction trial in the US. A woman was awarded $6 million, marking a significant legal decision that could influence hundreds of similar cases across the country. This ruling draws attention to the potential consequences platforms may face over the impact of their services on users' mental health.

United Nations Recognizes Enslavement as Crime Against Humanity

The UN has passed a resolution formally recognizing the enslavement of Africans as one of the "gravest crimes against humanity." The resolution calls for an official apology and contributions to a reparations fund, although it does not specify the amount needed. This move highlights ongoing global efforts to address historical injustices and their lasting effects.

Political Shift in Florida

Emily Gregory, a Democrat, has won a legislative seat in Florida that includes the area around Mar-a-Lago, the residence of Donald Trump. This victory is notable because the district was won by a Republican candidate by a 19-point margin in the 2024 elections. The result may signal shifts in voter sentiment and could impact future state-level politics.

Energy Emergency in the Philippines

The Philippines has declared an energy emergency amid concerns over supply. President Marcos announced plans to procure an additional one million barrels of oil to bolster the country's existing reserves. This step aims to ensure a steady energy supply during a challenging period, reflecting broader global issues related to energy security.

Discovery of Mass Grave in Kenya

Authorities in Kenya have uncovered a mass grave containing 32 bodies, including 25 children, many believed to have been transferred from local hospitals and mortuaries. The discovery has raised urgent questions about the circumstances surrounding the deaths and the handling of remains, prompting calls for investigation.

Cultural Discovery: Musketeer d'Artagnan’s Remains

Archaeologists have likely found the remains of the famous musketeer d'Artagnan beneath a church in the Netherlands. D'Artagnan died during the Siege of Maastricht in 1673 and was later immortalized in Alexandre Dumas’s novels. This discovery provides a tangible link to a historical figure celebrated in literature.
