February 17, 2026

Civil Rights Legacy

Jesse Jackson, a prominent US civil rights activist, passed away at the age of 84. His life spanned decades of advocacy for social justice and equality. Jackson's work had a lasting impact on civil rights movements in the United States, and his passing invites reflection on the progress and ongoing challenges in this area.

Violence at Rhode Island High School Hockey Game

Tragedy struck at a high school hockey game in Pawtucket, Rhode Island, where a shooting left two people dead and three others injured. Authorities have identified a suspect in connection with the shooting. This incident highlights ongoing concerns about gun violence in public spaces and the safety of community events.

Ukraine Addresses Population Decline

Facing a significant population decline, Ukraine has introduced a law that funds troops wishing to freeze their eggs or sperm. This measure aims to support the country's demographic challenges by preserving the future reproductive potential of its military personnel. The policy reflects broader concerns about population demographics amid ongoing conflict.

US Military Narco-Trafficking Operations

The US military reported 11 deaths in multiple strikes against alleged drug trafficking boats. Since September, more than 130 people have been killed in these operations, which target maritime narcotics smuggling. These actions underscore continued efforts by the US to combat illegal drug trade through coordinated military interventions.

Political Changes in South Africa

John Steenhuisen announced that he will not seek re-election as leader of South Africa's Democratic Alliance, the country's second-largest political party. This decision opens the field for new leadership and could shape the party's future direction and strategy ahead of upcoming elections.

Ongoing International Negotiations and Developments

In Geneva, a third round of US-brokered talks between Russia and Ukraine began, aiming to negotiate an end to the ongoing conflict. Expectations are low for a breakthrough as strikes continue. Meanwhile, Iran stated that "guiding principles" have been agreed with the US at nuclear talks, although additional work remains before a deal can be finalized. The US has not yet commented on Iran’s statement. In another diplomatic development, France released a suspected Russian tanker after a payment of several million euros, reflecting the complex economic and political tensions involving Russia.

US Military Assistance in Nigeria

US soldiers have arrived in Nigeria to assist in the fight against Islamist militants. The troops landed in Bauchi state but will not engage in combat operations. Their presence signifies continued US support for regional security efforts in West Africa.

Additional News

In Zambia, the family of former President Lungu dismissed allegations that he was poisoned. The president’s body remains in a South African morgue following his death, with ongoing discussions about the circumstances surrounding his passing.
