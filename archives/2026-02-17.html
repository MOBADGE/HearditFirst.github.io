US Civil Rights Leader Jesse Jackson Dies at 84

Jesse Jackson, a prominent American civil rights activist, passed away at the age of 84. He was widely recognized as a transformative figure and an agent of change throughout his life. Tributes have come from various politicians who acknowledged his significant contributions to civil rights and social justice in the United States. Jackson's decades-long activism included leadership roles and efforts to promote equality, and his legacy is being remembered and reflected upon.

Russia-Ukraine Peace Talks Begin in Geneva

Peace negotiations between Russia and Ukraine started in Geneva, but after the first day, hopes for a quick resolution remain low. The talks are set to continue, though the chances for an immediate breakthrough to end the ongoing conflict appear slim. Observers see the discussions as a first step, with difficult negotiations expected ahead as both parties work toward possible resolutions.

US Military Strikes on Alleged Drug Trafficking Boats

The US military has conducted multiple strikes targeting boats suspected of involvement in drug trafficking. These operations have resulted in at least eleven deaths in recent strikes alone, contributing to a total of more than 130 people killed since September in efforts to disrupt narco-trafficking activities. The strikes reflect continued US military activity aimed at combating drug trafficking in affected regions.

Political Changes in South Africa's Democratic Alliance

John Steenhuisen, the leader of South Africa's second-largest political party, the Democratic Alliance (DA), announced he will not seek re-election. His decision opens the field for new leadership candidates and marks a significant moment for the party as it prepares for its next leadership phase. This change could influence the party’s future direction and strategy ahead of upcoming elections.
