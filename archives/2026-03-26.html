Sports Policy Change for Olympics

The International Olympic Committee announced that starting with the 2028 Games, the women's category in Olympic sports will be limited exclusively to biological females. This marks a significant shift in eligibility criteria that could affect athletes who do not fall within these biological parameters. The change is expected to raise discussions on the future of inclusivity and fairness in women's sports competitions.

Landmark Social Media Addiction Ruling

A recent court verdict addressing social media addiction could shape the future of how platforms operate. BBC technology editor Zoe Kleinman described this ruling as potentially the "beginning of the end" for social media as it is currently known. The decision may force major tech companies to reconsider design and regulation to address concerns over addictive features and their societal impacts.

EU-US Trade Deal Receives Conditional Approval

The European Parliament has given conditional approval to a new trade agreement with the United States. Under the deal, the EU would reduce tariffs on US goods to zero, while the US would maintain a 15% tariff on imports from the EU. This move aims to enhance trade between the two blocs, with further negotiations expected to clarify the details and address outstanding conditions.

Legal Sentence for Tariq Ramadan

Tariq Ramadan, an Islamic scholar and former Oxford professor, has been sentenced to 18 years in prison by a French court on rape charges. Ramadan denied the allegations, and his legal team stated he was too ill to attend the trial. The ruling concludes a high-profile case with significant public attention.

Zimbabwe Nationals Killed in Foreign Conflict

Zimbabwe has reported that 15 of its nationals have died after being recruited to fight for Russia. The government is currently in talks with Russian authorities to repatriate the bodies and to bring back any surviving Zimbabweans. This situation highlights ongoing international concerns related to foreign fighters and geopolitical conflicts.

India Faces Protests Over Transgender Rights Bill

In India, a newly proposed bill that seeks to amend the legal recognition of transgender people and restrict their right to self-identify has sparked public protests. Critics of the bill argue that the changes could undermine transgender rights, prompting calls for more inclusive legislation and respect for self-identification.
