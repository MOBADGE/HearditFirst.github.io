Transatlantic Relations

The US secretary of state has emphasized the importance of the alliance between the United States and Europe despite recent tensions. High-Profile Legal and Political Issues

In France, Gisèle Pelicot spoke at length about her experience in the country’s largest rape trial. She described feeling overwhelmed by the horror but stated she does not harbor anger, focusing instead on healing and making constructive choices moving forward.

Public Health Controversy

The World Health Organization has criticized a planned US-funded vaccine trial in Guinea-Bissau. The trial proposed giving some newborns an established hepatitis B treatment while withholding it from others, a practice the WHO has called unethical. This criticism raises important questions about medical ethics and equity in clinical research in developing countries.

Crime and Security Updates

In the US, police carried out a search near the home of Nancy Guthrie in Tucson, Arizona, following her abduction almost two weeks ago. No arrests have been made so far, and investigations are ongoing.

In Rio de Janeiro, police took an unusual approach to combat phone theft during the Carnival festival by dressing in costumes. Their efforts resulted in two arrests after a drone spotted a woman stealing a phone from an attendee, illustrating innovative law enforcement tactics during large public events.

Cultural Events and Legal Proceedings
