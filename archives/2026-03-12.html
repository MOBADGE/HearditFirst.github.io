Iran: Police Chief Issues Warning Amid Protests

Iran's police chief has issued a strong warning to citizens amid ongoing unrest. He stated that anyone protesting "at the enemy's request" would be treated as an enemy by security forces. This reflects heightened government concerns about public demonstrations and may signal a tougher crackdown on dissent. How this will affect Iran's internal stability and public response remains to be seen.

China Enforces New Language Law for Ethnic Minorities

China has approved a law mandating that ethnic minority children must learn Mandarin from before kindergarten through the end of high school. The law is framed as promoting "ethnic unity," but it requires minority-language education to be aligned with Mandarin instruction. This policy could significantly impact cultural and linguistic diversity in China’s minority communities.

War in Ukraine Influences Hungarian Election

With elections approaching in Hungary, war in Ukraine features prominently in the campaign. Prime Minister Viktor Orbán has targeted Ukraine's president as a key adversary, intertwining the conflict with domestic political rhetoric. The war’s role in the election highlights regional tensions and could shape Hungary’s future stance on the conflict.

Floods and Landslides in Ethiopia Result in Over 60 Deaths

Severe floods and landslides in Ethiopia have claimed more than 60 lives. Meteorologists link the increasing intensity of storms in the region partly to global warming. This disaster underscores the growing challenges that climate change and extreme weather events pose to vulnerable communities.
