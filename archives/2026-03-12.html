March 12, 2026

Middle East Tensions Rise

A statement attributed to Mojtaba Khamenei, identified as Iran's new supreme leader, has warned that Iran intends to block the Strait of Hormuz. The message also indicated Iran will continue its attacks targeting US military bases in the region. This development comes amid ongoing conflict in the Gulf, where the United Arab Emirates reports it has faced more than 1,800 missile and drone strikes since the war began. The UAE has called on Iran to halt these strikes, highlighting the continued threats to regional stability and global energy routes.

Nepal Election Results

In Nepal, the Rastriya Swatatantra Party, led by rapper-politician Balendra Shah, has won a large majority in recent elections. The campaign focused heavily on issues like corruption, resonating with voters seeking change. This result may lead to shifts in Nepal’s political priorities and governance, with the new majority positioned to implement its agenda.

Legal and Human Rights Issues

The Israeli military has dropped charges against soldiers accused of abusing a Palestinian detainee at Sde Teiman military prison. The decision was justified by the military's top lawyer citing "exceptional circumstances," though details on those circumstances were not provided. In Italy, there is a case where parents are accused of hiding their young daughter's HIV status and neglecting her medical care, including missing vaccinations. The child reportedly did not see a doctor until she was six years old.

Laws and Social Developments in China and France

China has passed a new law promoting "ethnic unity" by requiring all minority children to be taught Mandarin from before kindergarten through high school. This policy aims to unify linguistic education but may have significant cultural impacts on China's minority communities.

Meanwhile, in France, a recent audit revealed a large-scale fraud involving fake car dealerships manipulating vehicle registration data. This "ghost car" scandal allowed approximately one million illegal vehicles to be driven on the roads, raising concerns about road safety and regulatory oversight.

Other Notable News

Brazilian women have told the BBC that Jeffrey Epstein used a modelling agent who operated businesses to recruit girls and facilitate their travel to the US. This adds to ongoing investigations into Epstein’s recruitment methods.

In Russia, 19 individuals have been jailed in connection with the deadly 2024 attack at the Crocus City Concert hall near Moscow, where gunmen killed 149 people and injured over 500.

Finally, René Redzepi, head chef of the Danish restaurant Noma, has resigned following allegations from former employees that he created a toxic work environment. Redzepi had previously issued an apology in response to these accusations.
