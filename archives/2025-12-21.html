December 21, 2025

Memorial Held for Bondi Beach Attack Victims

A minute's silence was observed to honor the victims of the recent Bondi Beach attack in Australia. The memorial event saw the arrival of Australian Prime Minister Anthony Albanese, who was met with booing from some attendees. The remembrance underscores ongoing community grief and tensions related to the incident.

Record Number of Executions in Saudi Arabia Draws Criticism

Human rights groups have condemned Saudi Arabia for carrying out a record number of executions this year. According to the UK-based campaign group Reprieve, at least 347 people have been executed in 2025. This increase has raised international concerns about human rights practices in the country.

Israel Approves 19 New Settlements in West Bank

Israel has approved the construction of 19 new settlements in the occupied West Bank. Far-right Finance Minister Bezalel Smotrich stated this step aims to block the creation of a Palestinian state. The move is likely to intensify tensions in the region and complicate peace efforts.

US Seizes Second Oil Tanker off Venezuela

The United States has seized a second oil tanker near Venezuela's coast. This action follows an order from US President Donald Trump to enforce a "blockade" on oil tankers subject to sanctions entering or leaving Venezuela. The seizure highlights ongoing tensions between the US and Venezuela over the country's oil exports.

Violence in South Africa Leaves Nine Dead

A gun attack at a tavern in Bekkersdal, South Africa, resulted in nine deaths and left ten others injured. Reports indicate that the assailants opened fire and continued shooting indiscriminately. The incident adds to concerns about violence and security in the area.
