Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 21, 2025

International Conflict and Settlements

Israel has approved the construction of 19 new settlements in the West Bank. This decision comes as part of a broader effort to prevent the establishment of a Palestinian state, according to statements made by Finance Minister Bezalel Smotrich. The move is likely to intensify existing tensions in the region, as settlement expansion has been a long-standing and contentious issue affecting peace prospects.

Human Rights and Legal Developments

Rights groups have condemned the record number of executions in Saudi Arabia this year, with at least 347 people reportedly put to death. The figures come from the campaign group Reprieve, which has called attention to the rising use of the death penalty in the kingdom.

In Pakistan, former Prime Minister Imran Khan and his wife have been given additional jail sentences in a state gift fraud case. Khan, who is already imprisoned, has announced plans to appeal the verdict. This development adds to ongoing political and legal challenges faced by the former leader.

Violence and Security Incidents

South Africa experienced a tragic shooting when gunmen attacked a tavern in Bekkersdal, killing nine people and injuring ten more. The attackers reportedly fired randomly before fleeing. This incident underscores ongoing concerns about violent crime and community safety in parts of the country.

Environmental and Wildlife Concerns

In India’s north-eastern state of Assam, seven elephants were killed after being struck by an express train while crossing the tracks. The collision occurred early Saturday and raises issues about wildlife protection and the impact of infrastructure on animal habitats in the region.

Cultural and Social Notes

Comedian Bowen Yang, a notable cast member of Saturday Night Live who also hosts a podcast and appears in the Wicked film series, has announced he is leaving the show. Yang’s departure marks a change for the popular television program as it enters a new phase.

Memorial and Public Reaction

A minute’s silence was held in Australia to honor the victims of an attack at Bondi Beach. The event was attended by Prime Minister Anthony Albanese, who was booed upon arrival by some members of the public. The memorial reflects ongoing national attention to the incident and public sentiments around it.
