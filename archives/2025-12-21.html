Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 21, 2025

Global Politics

Israel has approved plans for 19 new settlements in the occupied West Bank. Far-right Finance Minister Bezalel Smotrich stated that the move aims to block the establishment of a Palestinian state. This development is likely to increase tensions in the ongoing Israeli-Palestinian conflict. In related news, a grassroots group of families of hostages held in Gaza has gained significant influence in Israel and internationally as their efforts continue, with only one hostage still held.

Meanwhile, in Pakistan, former Prime Minister Imran Khan and his wife have been given additional jail sentences related to a state gift fraud case. Khan, who is already incarcerated, has announced plans to appeal the verdict.

Human Rights and Justice

Human rights groups have condemned the record number of executions carried out this year in Saudi Arabia. According to the UK-based campaign group Reprieve, at least 347 people have been executed in 2025. The high number of deaths has drawn criticism from international observers concerned about the kingdom’s use of the death penalty.

In the United States, survivors and lawmakers have expressed nervousness and skepticism regarding the recent release of documents related to Jeffrey Epstein. Lawmakers who advocated for making these files public criticized the initial release as incomplete, raising concerns over transparency and the full disclosure of information.

Crimes and Violence

A tragic attack occurred in Bekkersdal, South Africa, where gunmen opened fire in a tavern, killing nine people and injuring ten others. The perpetrators reportedly continued to shoot randomly, leaving the community devastated.

In Australia, a minute’s silence was held to remember the victims of an attack at Bondi Beach. The memorial event was marked by controversy as Australian Prime Minister Anthony Albanese was booed upon arrival, reflecting ongoing public tensions connected to the incident.

Environment and Accidents

In northeastern India, an express train struck and killed seven elephants crossing the railway tracks in Assam. This incident has raised concerns about wildlife protection and the need for measures to prevent future collisions between trains and animals.

Business and International Relations

The United States has seized a second oil tanker off Venezuela’s coast. This action follows an order by US President Donald Trump to enforce a "blockade" on sanctioned oil tankers entering and exiting Venezuela. The move reflects ongoing pressures related to US-Venezuela relations and efforts to restrict oil exports amid political tensions.

Culture and Entertainment

Comedian Bowen Yang announced his departure from the television show Saturday Night Live. Yang has been one of the show’s most prominent cast members and continues to host a podcast and appear in the film series Wicked. His exit marks a significant change for the show as it moves forward into the next season.
