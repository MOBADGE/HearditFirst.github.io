January 08, 2026

US Immigration Incident in Minneapolis

A US immigration enforcement agent fatally shot a woman in Minneapolis. Former US President Trump said the woman was attempting to run over Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) agents, but local leaders have disputed this account. This incident has sparked debate about the actions and conduct of ICE agents.

Return to School in Gaza

Children in Gaza have returned to school after years without formal education, according to UNICEF. The education system has faced significant challenges, as most schools in the territory were damaged or destroyed during the ongoing conflict. The return to class represents an important step toward rebuilding education infrastructure amid difficult circumstances.

Protests and Violence in Iran

Protests in Iran have spread to new areas, with violent clashes reported. Semi-official Iranian media reported that two police officers were killed in a town in western Iran. Videos from the region show security forces using guns and tear gas against demonstrators. The continued unrest highlights ongoing tensions between the government and protestors.

US Withdraws from Climate Treaty and Other Groups

The US has withdrawn from a key international climate treaty along with dozens of other organizations. The White House stated that these entities "no longer serve American interests." This move signals a shift in US engagement with global environmental and multilateral efforts, potentially affecting future international cooperation on climate issues.

Power Outages in Ukraine Due to Russian Attacks

Russian attacks have caused widespread blackouts in two southeastern Ukrainian regions, Dnipropetrovsk and Zaporizhzhia. Officials reported that these areas were nearly entirely without power. The attacks highlight the ongoing conflict’s impact on civilian infrastructure and the daily lives of residents.

Extreme Heat and Fire Risk in Australia

Australia is experiencing a heatwave, with officials warning of a "catastrophic" fire risk. Northern Victoria will have the highest fire danger level set, indicating severe conditions that could lead to widespread fires. Authorities are likely to increase preparedness and response measures to address this heightened threat.

Australian Inquiry into Bondi Shooting

Australian Prime Minister Anthony Albanese has announced a royal commission into a shooting incident at Bondi Beach. He had previously resisted calls for such an inquiry despite pressure from the Jewish community. The commission aims to provide a thorough investigation into the circumstances and implications of the shooting.

International Legal Action: Cambodia Extradites Scam Suspect

Cambodia has extradited Chen Zhi, an alleged mastermind behind a large-scale scam, to China. Chen is accused of using his business as a front for fraud operations targeting people worldwide. This extradition marks a significant step in addressing international fraud networks.

Reality TV Star Runs for Los Angeles Mayor

Spencer Pratt, known from reality TV, has announced his candidacy for mayor of Los Angeles. His announcement comes on the anniversary of a wildfire that destroyed his home in the Pacific Palisades area. His bid is considered a longshot but adds a notable name to the mayoral race.

Upcoming Meeting Between US and Colombian Leaders

Former US President Trump said he will meet with Colombian President Gustavo Petro at the White House "in the near future." He called a recent call with Petro a "Great Honor." This meeting is planned shortly after Trump mentioned that a military operation in Colombia "sounds good," indicating potential discussions on security and cooperation.
