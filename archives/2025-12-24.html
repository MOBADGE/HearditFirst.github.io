Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 24, 2025

Ukraine Peace Plan and Border Tensions

Ukrainian leader Zelensky has proposed new ideas aiming to de-escalate conflict in the eastern Donbas region. One key component under discussion is the creation of a "free economic zone" intended to ease tensions. Meanwhile, Ukraine reports that some villagers near the Russia-Ukraine border, particularly in the Sumy region, have been abducted and taken to Russia. Efforts to evacuate residents have faced resistance, with many choosing to remain despite the risks. These developments highlight ongoing security and humanitarian challenges in the region.

Security Incidents in Moscow and Pennsylvania

In Moscow, two police officers were killed after approaching a "suspicious" individual who detonated a device. Authorities are investigating the incident, which underscores concerns about urban security. Separately, in Pennsylvania, two people died following twin explosions and a fire at a nursing home. Officials are examining whether a gas leak caused the blasts, which also resulted in a partial building collapse. Both incidents are under active investigation.

International Disputes and Legal Actions

Tensions remain high between Venezuela and the United States after the seizure of two Venezuelan oil tankers by US authorities. Venezuela accused the US of "extortion," prompting an emergency meeting of the United Nations Security Council to address the dispute. In another legal development, Brazil’s Supreme Court authorized former president Bolsonaro to leave prison temporarily for a hernia surgery scheduled on Christmas Day. This decision reflects ongoing political and judicial issues in Brazil.

Immigration and Visa Restrictions

Australia announced it will deport a British man charged with displaying Nazi symbols, with the immigration minister describing him as an unwelcome "guest." Additionally, the US government under the Trump administration has refused entry visas to five social media campaigners who advocate for tech regulation. These moves highlight ongoing debates around immigration and the regulation of digital platforms in different countries.

Notable Death and Document Releases

A military plane crash in Turkey resulted in the death of Libya’s army chief, Mohammed Ali Ahmed al-Haddad, and four others. The jet lost contact shortly after taking off from Ankara, and the circumstances are under investigation. In the United States, the Department of Justice released the largest set of documents related to Jeffrey Epstein to date, including messages referencing Donald Trump and possible co-conspirators. This release may have implications for ongoing discussions about Epstein’s network.
