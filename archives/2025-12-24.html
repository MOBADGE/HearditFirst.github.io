Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 24, 2025

Ukraine Peace Plan and Border Situation

Ukraine’s leader, Zelensky, has proposed a new peace plan focused on creating demilitarised zones, particularly addressing the conflict in the eastern Donbas region. One option being considered is establishing a "free economic zone" there, which could affect the region’s economic and governance arrangements. Meanwhile, near Ukraine’s border with Russia, officials have attempted to evacuate residents amid ongoing tensions. However, many villagers in the Sumy region have declined to leave despite the risks, and reports indicate that some people have been abducted and taken into Russia.

Security Incidents in Moscow and Pennsylvania

In Moscow, an explosion occurred after police approached a suspicious person, resulting in the deaths of two officers. Authorities are investigating the incident, which raises concerns about public safety in the Russian capital. Separately, in Pennsylvania, two explosions and a fire at a nursing home have caused a partial collapse of the building and led to two deaths. Initial investigations are focusing on a possible gas leak as the cause of the blasts.

International Disputes and Diplomatic Moves

Tensions between Venezuela and the United States have escalated following the US seizure of two Venezuelan oil tankers. Venezuela has condemned the action as "extortion," prompting an emergency meeting of the United Nations Security Council to address the dispute. Meanwhile, in Australia, a British man charged with displaying Nazi symbols is set to be deported, with the immigration minister calling him an unwelcome "guest." Additionally, five social media campaigners from the UK, known for advocating tech regulation, have been denied US visas under the Trump administration’s policy.

Notable Legal and Political Developments

Brazil’s former president Bolsonaro has been permitted by the country’s Supreme Court to leave prison temporarily for a hernia surgery scheduled on Christmas Day. This decision allows him to seek medical treatment outside custody. In Turkey, the Libyan army chief Mohammed Ali Ahmed al-Haddad and four others died in a plane crash after losing contact with air traffic control shortly after takeoff from Ankara. The crash marks a significant loss for Libya’s military leadership.

Epstein Files Release

The Justice Department released the largest batch of documents related to the Jeffrey Epstein investigation to date. The files include new details and mention several notable individuals, including former US President Trump. The documents shed light on complex connections and highlight ongoing legal and public interest in the Epstein case.
