Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 24, 2025

Ukraine Peace Plan

Ukrainian leader Zelensky has proposed a new approach to the conflict in eastern Ukraine's Donbas region. He suggested the establishment of a "free economic zone" there as part of a peace plan. This would involve creating demilitarised zones to reduce tensions and foster economic cooperation. The proposal marks a potential step toward resolving one of the key issues in the ongoing conflict. How this plan will be received by all parties remains to be seen.

Justice Department Review of Epstein Documents

US authorities have uncovered an additional one million documents that could relate to the case involving Jeffrey Epstein. The Department of Justice has stated that the process of reviewing this material is ongoing. This development means the full public release of Epstein-related documents will be delayed by several more weeks. The extra documents may provide further insight into the case, but the extended review underscores the complexity involved.

Honduran Election Results Contested

A candidate supported by former US President Trump, Nasry Asfura, has narrowly won the presidential election in Honduras. However, the opposition has raised concerns over potential manipulation in the vote count and has not clearly accepted the results. These disputes may lead to political instability or delays in confirming the election outcome, depending on how electoral authorities and political actors respond.

Legal Appeal Filed by Sean 'Diddy' Combs

Sean "Diddy" Combs has filed an appeal requesting immediate release from prison. He claims his sentence was incorrectly determined and has also asked for his conviction to be overturned. The legal process will assess these claims, which could affect the timeline of his imprisonment and any related legal matters.

Algeria Passes Law on Colonisation

Algerian lawmakers have unanimously approved a new law that declares France's colonisation of Algeria a crime. The legislation calls for a formal apology and reparations from France, reflecting ongoing tensions over the colonial past. This law may impact diplomatic relations between Algeria and France moving forward.

Other Notable Developments

- In South Africa, authorities arrested eleven people following a mass shooting at a tavern. Police investigations suggest the violence may be connected to conflicts over illegal mining territories.

- Two police officers were killed in Moscow when a device exploded after they approached a suspicious individual. The incident is under investigation.

- Venezuela has accused the US of "extortion" after its seizure of two oil tankers. The issue prompted an emergency meeting of the UN Security Council, highlighting international tensions over the case.

- In the US, five social media campaigners, including UK nationals who advocate for tech regulation, were denied visas by the Trump administration.

- In Washington, a Democratic lawmaker, Rep Joyce Beatty, has filed a lawsuit seeking to remove Donald Trump's name from the Kennedy Center. She argues that a name change requires Congressional approval, which has not been obtained.
