Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 24, 2025

Ukraine Peace Plan and Border Tensions

Ukraine’s leader, Volodymyr Zelensky, has proposed a new peace plan focused on creating demilitarized zones in the eastern Donbas region. Part of the plan includes the possibility of establishing a "free economic zone" there, aiming to reduce military conflict and foster economic activity. Meanwhile, in areas near the Russian border, Ukraine continues evacuation efforts amid ongoing conflict, but many civilians in the Sumy region are choosing to stay despite the risks. Reports indicate some border villagers have been abducted and taken to Russia, heightening concerns about civilian safety.

Violence in Moscow

A tragic incident occurred in Moscow when an explosion killed two police officers. The explosion happened after officers approached a "suspicious" individual, according to authorities. Details about the suspect or the motivation behind the blast have not been released, but the event underscores ongoing security challenges in the Russian capital.

International Disputes over Oil and Diplomacy

Tensions between Venezuela and the United States escalated following the US seizure of two Venezuelan oil tankers. Caracas has accused the US of “extortion” and called for an emergency meeting of the United Nations Security Council to discuss the issue. The situation reflects ongoing disputes related to sanctions and oil exports between the two countries.

Legal and Political Updates

In Brazil, the Supreme Court has authorized former president Jair Bolsonaro to leave prison temporarily for a hernia operation scheduled on Christmas Day. Meanwhile, in Libya, a significant loss occurred with the death of the army chief, General Mohammed Ali Ahmed al-Haddad, in a plane crash near Ankara, Turkey. The crash also involved four others, with signal loss reported shortly after takeoff.

Social Media and Legal Actions Abroad

The US government has denied visas to five individuals, including UK-based social media campaigners advocating for technology regulation. This move was taken by the Trump administration and represents ongoing contention over tech oversight. Separately, Australia is preparing to deport a British man charged with displaying Nazi symbols, describing him as an unwelcome "guest" in the country.

Public Safety Incidents in the US

In Pennsylvania, two people died following twin explosions and a fire at a nursing home. Authorities are investigating whether a gas leak caused the blasts, which led to a partial collapse of the building. The incident has raised concerns about safety and emergency response in care facilities.
