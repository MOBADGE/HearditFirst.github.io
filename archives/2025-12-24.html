Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 24, 2025

Ukraine Peace Proposal

Ukrainian leader Volodymyr Zelensky has put forward a new peace plan aimed at reducing military tensions in the eastern Donbas region. One key element of the proposal is establishing a "free economic zone" in the area, which could provide a special status focused on economic development rather than conflict. This move indicates an effort to find a diplomatic solution to a long-running and violent conflict, but further negotiations and details will be needed to see how this plan might be implemented.

Security Incidents and Investigations

Two separate violent incidents raised concerns about safety and security. In Moscow, two police officers were killed after they approached a suspicious individual, who then detonated a device. Authorities are investigating the circumstances surrounding the attack.

In Pennsylvania, twin explosions and a fire at a nursing home resulted in two deaths and caused part of the building to collapse. Officials are looking into whether a gas leak may have caused the blasts, as investigations continue.

Additionally, Libya’s army chief, Mohammed Ali Ahmed al-Haddad, died in a plane crash near Ankara, Turkey, after signals were lost from the jet carrying him and four others.

International Diplomacy and Disputes

Tensions between Venezuela and the United States escalated after the U.S. seized two Venezuelan oil tankers. In response, Venezuela accused the U.S. of "extortion," and the issue prompted an emergency meeting of the United Nations Security Council to address the dispute.

In a notable move by Algeria, lawmakers unanimously passed a law declaring France’s colonization of Algeria a crime. The new law calls for an official apology and reparations from France, reflecting long-standing grievances over colonial history.

Legal and Political Developments

Brazil’s Supreme Court permitted former president Jair Bolsonaro to leave prison temporarily for a hernia operation scheduled on Christmas Day. Bolsonaro had been serving a prison sentence prior to the surgery.

Meanwhile, the U.S. administration banned five social media campaigners, including activists calling for tighter tech regulation, from entering the country. This decision followed restrictions imposed during the Trump administration.

Other News

Australia announced plans to deport a British man charged with displaying Nazi symbols, with the immigration minister describing him as an unwelcome "guest" in the country.

Also, the U.S. Department of Justice released the largest batch of documents related to the Jeffrey Epstein case yet. Among the files were notable messages and several mentions of President Donald Trump, illuminating new details as investigations continue.
