Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 24, 2025

Ukraine Peace Proposal

Ukraine’s leader, Zelensky, has put forward a new peace plan focusing on the eastern Donbas region. A key part of the proposal includes the possibility of creating a "free economic zone" to help ease tensions. The plan also involves establishing demilitarised zones. This approach aims to address ongoing conflict in the region and could represent a step toward negotiation and reducing hostilities, though much remains uncertain about how this will be received by all parties involved.

Developments in the Epstein Case

US Department of Justice officials have found approximately one million additional documents that may relate to the Epstein case. This discovery means the full release of the documents, which has been anticipated to shed more light on the matter, will likely be delayed by a few weeks. The ongoing review underscores the complexity and scale of the investigation, with legal teams continuing to sort through the large volume of material.

Honduran Election Results

In Honduras, a candidate supported by former US President Trump, Asfura, has won a very close election. However, his opponents have raised concerns about the vote count, alleging manipulation. It remains unclear whether these challengers will accept the official results, potentially affecting the country’s political stability as the situation develops.

Legal Actions Involving Celebrities and Public Figures

Sean "Diddy" Combs has filed an appeal requesting immediate release from prison. He claims his sentence was improperly determined and seeks to have his conviction overturned. This legal move is a significant step in his case, which has drawn public attention.

Meanwhile, in the United States, Democratic lawmaker Rep Joyce Beatty is suing to reverse the renaming of the Kennedy Center to include Trump’s name. She argues the change was made without the necessary approval from Congress and therefore is illegal. Beatty also serves on the center’s board, highlighting a dispute over procedural and political aspects of the renaming.

International Incidents and Tensions

Several serious incidents and political tensions have been reported this week. In South Africa, eleven people have been arrested following a mass shooting at a tavern. Preliminary investigations suggest the violence may be linked to turf wars over illegal mining activities.

In Moscow, two police officers were killed when a device exploded after officers approached an individual deemed suspicious. Authorities have not released further details but are emphasizing the dangers faced by law enforcement in the region.

Separately, Venezuela has accused the United States of "extortion" after the US seized two Venezuelan oil tankers. This action prompted an emergency meeting of the United Nations Security Council, highlighting ongoing international disputes over the control and ownership of resources.

Other Notable Developments

In Algeria, lawmakers unanimously passed a law declaring France’s colonisation a crime. The legislation demands an official apology and reparations from France, reopening a historical debate and potentially affecting relations between the two countries.

Additionally, in the US, the Trump administration has denied visas to five social media campaigners who advocate for technology regulation. This move reflects ongoing tensions over tech policy and political influence on immigration controls.
