Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 24, 2025

Ukraine Peace Plan Developments

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky proposed new ideas as part of a peace plan focusing on the conflict in eastern Ukraine's Donbas region. One option he highlighted involves creating a "free economic zone" in parts of Donbas. This plan aims to reduce military tensions by moving towards demilitarised zones, suggesting a potential pathway for easing the longstanding conflict in the area. The proposal is a step toward seeking peaceful solutions, although the details and reception by other parties are yet to unfold.

Justice and Legal News

The U.S. Department of Justice announced the discovery of an additional one million documents potentially related to the Jeffrey Epstein case. This significant find may delay the full public release of the documents by a few more weeks as authorities continue reviewing the materials. Meanwhile, music artist Sean "Diddy" Combs filed an appeal requesting immediate release from prison. He argues his sentence was improperly decided and is also seeking to have his conviction overturned.

International Tensions and Reactions

Algeria's lawmakers unanimously passed a law declaring France's colonial rule a crime. The legislation demands an official apology and reparations from France for its colonial actions, signaling a strong stance on historical grievances. At the United Nations, Venezuela accused the United States of "extortion" after the U.S. seized two Venezuelan oil tankers. This accusation prompted an emergency meeting of the UN Security Council to discuss the incident, highlighting growing tensions around energy resources and international sanctions.

Security Incidents and Crime

In South Africa, eleven people were arrested following a mass shooting at a tavern. Police investigations suggest the motive may be linked to turf wars involving illegal mining operations, a serious issue that affects security in certain regions of the country. In Moscow, two police officers were killed when a device detonated after they approached a suspicious individual. Russian authorities reported the explosion, underscoring ongoing challenges related to security and public safety in the capital.

Other Notable Updates

In the United States, five social media campaigners who advocate for stronger tech regulation were denied visas under measures enacted by the Trump administration. This move reflects ongoing tensions regarding digital policy and international travel. Additionally, Libya's army chief, General Mohammed Ali Ahmed al-Haddad, died in an air crash in Turkey. The jet lost signal shortly after taking off from Ankara with four others on board, marking a significant loss for Libya's military leadership.
