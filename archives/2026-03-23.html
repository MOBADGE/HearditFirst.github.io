Heard It First – Daily Digest

March 23, 2026

Global Politics and Security

The US president reported that "very good and productive" talks have been held with the goal of ending an ongoing war, leading to a drop in oil prices and a rebound in stock markets. Meanwhile, in Hong Kong, new national security rules now give the police the authority to demand phone passwords. Those who refuse to comply could face up to one year in jail and a fine of HK$100,000 (about $12,700). In Kenya, Raphael Tuju, a former foreign minister, has been found safe after concerns about his possible abduction had sparked alarm.

Environment and Climate

The UN's weather agency has issued a stark warning that Earth's climate is being pushed beyond its limits, describing current conditions as more out of balance than at any other time in recorded history. This warning comes as the El Niño weather pattern is anticipated to develop, which may further impact global weather systems and climate stability.

Public Safety and Social Issues

In Nigeria, police arrested 15 people following the circulation of viral videos alleging sexual assaults at a festival. The videos provoked widespread public outrage, with the hashtag #StopRapingWomen trending on social media platforms. These developments highlight ongoing concerns about safety and accountability in public spaces.

Industry and Culture

Shares of the agency managing the popular music group BTS fell after the turnout for their recent comeback concert was significantly lower than expected. Approximately 104,000 fans attended, less than half of the anticipated 260,000. In a related note on influential figures in media, Leonid Radvinsky, known for becoming a billionaire through his investment in the site OnlyFans, passed away at age 43.

Notable Deaths

France's former Socialist Prime Minister Lionel Jospin has died at the age of 88. Jospin led the government from 1997 to 2002 and is remembered for introducing the 35-hour working week, a significant labor reform.

Accidents and Industrial Actions

A tragic accident at New York's LaGuardia Airport resulted in the deaths of two pilots after a passenger plane collided with a fire truck. Additionally, the Australian Broadcasting Corporation's staff have planned a 24-hour strike, the first in 20 years, which is expected to disrupt live TV and radio broadcasts across the country.
