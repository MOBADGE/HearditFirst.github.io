Hong Kong’s New Security Measures on Mobile Devices

Under new national security rules, Hong Kong police now have the authority to demand phone passwords from individuals suspected of certain offences. Those refusing to comply face penalties of up to one year in jail and fines reaching HK$100,000 ($12,700). The policy change reflects the region's evolving approach to security and privacy, and may impact civil liberties debates.

Kenyan Authorities Investigate Missing Former Official

Kenyan police are looking into the alleged disappearance of Raphael Tuju, a former foreign minister. His absence has led to speculation and claims of possible abduction. The investigation is ongoing, and developments will be closely watched due to the political and social implications surrounding the case.

Australian Broadcaster ABC Faces First Strike in Two Decades

Staff at Australia's national broadcaster, ABC, have announced a 24-hour strike, the first in 20 years. The strike is expected to disrupt live television and radio programming across the country. The industrial action points to underlying workplace issues within the broadcaster, and further negotiations may be necessary to resolve the dispute.
