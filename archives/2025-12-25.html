Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 25, 2025

Global Conflicts and Security

Pope Leo delivered his first Christmas address at the Vatican, calling for "courage" to help end the ongoing war in Ukraine. He also expressed concern about the condition of Palestinians during his message to worshippers. Meanwhile, in Nigeria, a bomb blast at a mosque in Maiduguri during evening prayers killed five people and injured 35 others, highlighting continuing security challenges in the region. In Turkey, authorities arrested 115 suspects linked to the Islamic State who were allegedly planning attacks around New Year's. Firearms were seized, and investigations continue to trace further suspects.

Political Developments

Tarique Rahman, a prominent political figure in Bangladesh who has been living in exile in London since 2008, returned to Bangladesh ahead of crucial national elections. His arrival comes 17 years after he left the country, potentially influencing the upcoming vote. In Honduras, a tightly contested election concluded with a candidate supported by former U.S. President Donald Trump declared the winner. However, his rival Salvador Nasralla disputed the result but urged supporters to remain calm as the political situation develops.

Community and Cultural Events

Christmas celebrations were underway worldwide, with various unique traditions illuminating the holiday season. For example, waterskiing Santas entertained crowds, and lavish meals featuring large cuts of meat were part of the festivities from London to Bethlehem. Additionally, Norad marked its 70th year of Santa Claus tracking by enlisting help from Donald Trump, who answered children's calls on Christmas Eve, adding a special element to the annual event.

Crime and Law Enforcement

In Texas, police are investigating an incident where two robbers forcibly removed an ATM from inside a store in White Settlement. Authorities have released video footage and are actively searching for the suspects to bring them to justice. This event further underlines ongoing concerns about property crime in the area.
