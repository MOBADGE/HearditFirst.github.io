Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 25, 2025

Global Politics

Pope Leo called for courage to end the ongoing war in Ukraine during his Christmas address at the Vatican. He also expressed concern about the situation of Palestinians, highlighting the need for peace and compassion in the region. In Bangladesh, Tarique Rahman, who has been living in exile in London since 2008, returned to the country ahead of crucial elections. His arrival could influence the political landscape as these elections approach.

Security and Conflicts

In Turkey, authorities detained 115 suspects linked to the Islamic State who were allegedly planning attacks around New Year's celebrations. Firearms were seized, and investigations continue to locate additional suspects. Meanwhile, in Nigeria, a bomb exploded in a mosque in the city of Maiduguri during evening prayers. The attack killed five people and injured 35 others, raising concerns about ongoing violence in the area.

Elections and Political Developments

Honduras saw a closely contested election in which a candidate supported by Donald Trump was declared the winner. However, the rival candidate, Salvador Nasralla, rejected the election results while urging his supporters to remain calm. This political tension could impact the country's stability in the coming months.

Holiday Celebrations

Christmas festivities took place worldwide with various unique local traditions. Scenes included waterskiing Santas and large festive meals, showing the holiday's cultural diversity from London to Bethlehem. Additionally, President Donald Trump participated in the 70th anniversary of the North American Aerospace Defense Command's (Norad) Santa tracking event, engaging with children who called in to follow Santa’s journey on Christmas Eve.

Crime

In Texas, an incident was captured on video where two robbers forcibly removed an ATM from inside a store in White Settlement. Police are actively searching for the suspects involved in this armed robbery as investigations continue.
