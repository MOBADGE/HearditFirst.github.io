Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 25, 2025

Ukraine Peace Talks

Ukraine's leader expressed optimism following discussions with US envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner about a new plan for peace. The talks highlighted "new ideas" aimed at resolving the ongoing conflict involving Ukraine. While details of the proposed plan have not been fully disclosed, this development signals continued diplomatic efforts to seek a resolution to the war. The reception from Ukraine's leadership suggests potential momentum, though it remains to be seen how other parties involved will respond.

Severe Weather in California

Heavy rainstorms have severely affected California during the Christmas holiday, causing flash floods and mudslides. As of Christmas night, three people have died due to the extreme weather conditions. The storms have disrupted busy holiday travel and posed safety risks to residents and visitors in the region. Authorities continue to monitor the situation, and further weather-related impacts may occur as the storm system persists.

Global Security Concerns: Turkey and Nigeria

In Turkey, authorities detained 115 suspects linked to the Islamic State who were reportedly planning attacks over the New Year's period. Firearms were seized, and ongoing efforts aim to identify additional suspects connected to the planned attacks.

Meanwhile, in Nigeria, a bomb exploded in a mosque in Maiduguri during evening prayers, killing five people and injuring 35 others. This attack underscores ongoing security challenges in the region and highlights the risks civilians face amid continuing violence.

Political Developments in Latin America and South Asia

In Honduras, a candidate backed by Donald Trump narrowly won a contested election. The runner-up, Salvador Nasralla, rejected the results but called for calm among his supporters. The political tension following the election outcome points to potential instability or further disputes in the country’s political landscape.

In Bangladesh, Tarique Rahman, a key political figure, returned from 17 years of exile in London just weeks before crucial elections. His return could significantly impact the country's political dynamics as parties prepare for the upcoming vote.

Notable Figures and Events

Former Brazilian President Jair Bolsonaro underwent a successful surgery for a hernia while serving a 27-year prison sentence related to a coup plot following the 2022 election.

Additionally, the Pope delivered his first Christmas address, urging courage to end the war in Ukraine. He also expressed concern about the situation faced by Palestinians, emphasizing the need for peace and compassion in these troubled times.

Finally, on a lighter note, Christmas celebrations continued worldwide, from waterskiing Santas in London to special services in Bethlehem, showcasing diverse cultural ways of marking the holiday season.
