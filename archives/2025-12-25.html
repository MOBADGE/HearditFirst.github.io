Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 25, 2025

Ukraine Peace Talks

Ukraine's leader, Volodymyr Zelensky, expressed optimism after meeting with US envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner to discuss a plan aimed at peace. Zelensky described the talks as introducing "new ideas," signaling hope for progress in ending the ongoing conflict. The nature of the proposed peace plan and next steps have not been detailed, but Zelensky's positive outlook suggests there may be further diplomatic engagement ahead.

Severe Weather in California

Heavy rainstorms have caused serious disruptions in California during the Christmas holiday period. The storms have resulted in flash flooding and mudslides that complicated travel and led to the deaths of three people by Christmas night. Emergency services remain on alert as the region deals with the aftermath. The weather highlights the continuing challenges of managing natural disasters amid busy holiday travel seasons.

Security Actions in Turkey

Turkish authorities have detained 115 suspects believed to be linked to the Islamic State (IS) who were allegedly planning attacks for New Year’s celebrations. Firearms were confiscated during the operation, and investigations are ongoing to identify further suspects. This action aims to prevent potential terrorist activities during a high-risk period for attacks.

Political Developments in South America and Asia

In Honduras, a candidate endorsed by former US President Donald Trump narrowly won a tightly contested election. His opponent, Salvador Nasralla, has refused to accept the result but has called on supporters to remain calm. This election result could affect Honduras’ political direction in the near term.

Meanwhile, Tarique Rahman, a leading figure in Bangladesh’s political scene who has been living in exile in London since 2008, returned to the country ahead of crucial elections. His arrival marks a significant moment in Bangladeshi politics as the election approaches, potentially influencing the country's political landscape.

Other Notable Events

In Nigeria’s city of Maiduguri, a bomb exploded inside a mosque during evening prayers, killing five people and injuring 35 others.

Police are investigating the attack, which has raised concerns about ongoing security issues in the region.

In Brazil, former President Jair Bolsonaro underwent successful hernia surgery.

Bolsonaro is currently serving a 27-year prison sentence related to allegations of plotting a coup following the 2022 presidential election.

In Washington, a Democratic lawmaker, Rep.

Joyce Beatty, who also serves on the board of the Kennedy Center, filed a lawsuit seeking to block the removal of President Donald Trump’s name from the cultural institution.

Beatty argues the name change is illegal without congressional approval.

Finally, Christmas celebrations continued worldwide, featuring unique festive traditions such as waterskiing Santas in London and special services in Bethlehem, reflecting diverse ways people marked the holiday season.
