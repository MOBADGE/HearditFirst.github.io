Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 25, 2025

International Diplomacy and Peace Efforts

Ukraine's leader expressed optimism following discussions with US envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner about a peace plan. He hailed the talks for bringing "new ideas" on achieving peace amid the ongoing conflict. Meanwhile, Pope Leo called for "courage" to end the war in Ukraine during his first Christmas address at the Vatican. The pope also used his speech to highlight concerns about the situation facing Palestinians, emphasizing the need for compassion and peace in the region.

Natural Disasters in the United States

Heavy rainstorms in California caused significant disruptions over the Christmas period. The storms resulted in flash flooding and mudslides, leading to the deaths of three people. These severe weather conditions came during a busy time of holiday travel, compounding the risks and challenges for residents and travelers in the affected areas.

Security and Political Developments

Turkish authorities detained 115 suspects linked to the Islamic State who were allegedly planning attacks around New Year's celebrations. The operation included the seizure of firearms, and officials continue to investigate and track additional suspects. In Nigeria, a bomb explosion in a crowded mosque in Maiduguri killed five people and injured around 35 during evening prayers, underscoring ongoing security concerns in the region.

Political Changes and Elections

In Bangladesh, Tarique Rahman, a front-runner in the upcoming elections, returned from 17 years in exile in London. His arrival came just weeks ahead of pivotal voting, setting the stage for a significant political moment in the country. In Honduras, a candidate backed by Donald Trump narrowly won in a closely contested election. However, the rival candidate Salvador Nasralla rejected the result but urged supporters to stay calm to avoid unrest.

Health and Legal News

Former Brazilian President Jair Bolsonaro underwent successful hernia surgery. He is currently serving a 27-year prison sentence for his role in plotting a coup attempt after losing the 2022 election. In the United States, Democratic Representative Joyce Beatty sued to remove Donald Trump's name from the Kennedy Center. Beatty, who is on the center’s board, contends that the name change was illegal as it requires congressional approval.

Christmas Celebrations Worldwide

Festive activities marked Christmas across the globe with various cultural traditions on display. From waterskiing Santas in some places to giant cuts of meat shared in others, communities celebrated the holiday with services and gatherings, creating a spirited and diverse expression of holiday cheer.
