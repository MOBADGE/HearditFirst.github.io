Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 25, 2025

Global Politics

Tarique Rahman, a prominent political figure in Bangladesh who has lived in exile in London since 2008, returned to the country just weeks ahead of critical elections. His return could influence the political landscape as parties prepare for a pivotal vote.

In Honduras, a closely contested election resulted in a victory for a candidate supported by Donald Trump. However, his opponent, Salvador Nasralla, has not accepted the election result but has called on his supporters to remain calm, signaling potential tensions ahead.

Security and Conflict

Turkey arrested 115 individuals suspected of being involved with the Islamic State and planning attacks around New Year's celebrations. Firearms were confiscated, and authorities continue efforts to identify other possible suspects.

In Nigeria, a bomb exploded inside a busy mosque in Maiduguri during evening prayers, killing five people and injuring 35 others. The attack highlights ongoing security challenges in the region.

Religious and Cultural Observances

Pope Leo delivered his first Christmas address at the Vatican, urging courage to end the ongoing war in Ukraine. He also expressed concern over the situation of Palestinians, appealing for peace and compassion during the holiday season.

Around the world, Christmas festivities continued with unique and varied traditions, from waterskiing Santas to large communal meals, as communities celebrated the holiday in diverse ways.

Community and Local News

In Texas, police are investigating a robbery caught on video, which showed suspects forcibly removing an ATM from inside a store. The White Settlement Police Department is actively searching for the two individuals involved.

Meanwhile, in a lighter holiday event, former U.S. President Donald Trump later joined efforts to track Santa Claus’s Christmas Eve journey, participating in the 70th anniversary of the North American Aerospace Defense Command (NORAD) Santa tracker.
