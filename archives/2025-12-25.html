Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 25, 2025

Global Peace and Conflict

Pope Leo delivered his first Christmas address from the Vatican, urging "courage" to bring an end to the war in Ukraine. He also expressed concern for the condition of Palestinians, highlighting their plight during the holiday message. The pope’s call for peace extended broadly to conflicts worldwide, emphasizing the need for reconciliation and hope during the festive season.

In Nigeria, a bomb exploded in a crowded mosque during evening prayers in Maiduguri, killing five people and injuring 35 others. The attack adds to the ongoing security challenges facing the region and raises concerns about the safety of religious sites.

Security and Counterterrorism in Turkey

Turkish authorities detained 115 suspects believed to be planning attacks over the New Year period. Firearms were seized in the operation, and authorities continue efforts to identify and apprehend additional suspects. This move reflects ongoing counterterrorism efforts in Turkey ahead of a potentially volatile period.

Political Developments in South and Central America

In Honduras, a Trump-backed candidate narrowly won the presidential election amid a disputed result. His opponent, Salvador Nasralla, has refused to accept the outcome but has called for his supporters to remain calm to avoid unrest. The close result underscores political tensions in the country as it prepares for a new administration.

In Brazil, former President Jair Bolsonaro underwent successful surgery for a hernia. Bolsonaro is currently serving a 27-year prison sentence for allegedly plotting a coup after losing the 2022 presidential election. His health situation is being closely monitored given his legal and political status.

Bangladesh's Upcoming Elections

Tarique Rahman, a key opposition figure who has been living in exile in London since 2008, returned to Bangladesh weeks before crucial elections. His return may influence the political landscape in Bangladesh during what is expected to be a closely watched electoral process.

Christmas Celebrations and Cultural Moments

Across the globe, Christmas festivities took many forms, from services and traditional meals to unique events such as waterskiing Santas. London and Bethlehem, among other cities, marked the holiday with ceremonies and celebrations that highlight the diverse ways people observe Christmas.

Additionally, President Donald Trump took part in a lighthearted moment by helping NORAD track Santa’s journey on Christmas Eve, marking the 70th anniversary of the event. This gesture added a festive note to the day’s events.

Domestic U.S. Legal and Cultural Issues

In the United States, Representative Joyce Beatty filed a lawsuit aimed at removing President Donald Trump’s name from the Kennedy Center. Beatty argues that the name change was done illegally, as it requires Congressional approval. This legal challenge may prompt further debate about the process and symbolism involved in naming public institutions.
