Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 25, 2025

Global Politics

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky expressed optimism after discussions with US envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner regarding new ideas for peace efforts. The talks suggest ongoing international engagement aimed at resolving the conflict in Ukraine. Meanwhile, Pope Leo delivered his first Christmas address, calling for courage to end the war in Ukraine and expressing concern about the situation in Palestine, highlighting the ongoing humanitarian issues in these regions.

In Bangladesh, Tarique Rahman, a key political figure who has been living in exile in London for 17 years, returned to the country ahead of crucial upcoming elections. His arrival marks a significant moment in Bangladesh’s political landscape. Additionally, the Honduran presidential election concluded with a narrow victory for a candidate backed by Donald Trump. The runner-up, Salvador Nasralla, has refused to accept the result but urged supporters to remain peaceful, reflecting tensions in the election’s aftermath.

Natural Disasters and Climate Impact

Heavy rainstorms struck California during the Christmas holiday, resulting in three deaths and causing flash floods and mudslides. The storms have posed challenges for travelers and emergency responders during one of the busiest travel periods of the year.

In Indonesia, frustration is growing among residents of Sumatra after severe floods devastated the region weeks ago. Many affected people still lack access to essentials such as clean water and food, leading some to raise white flags as a plea for aid. The situation highlights ongoing difficulties in disaster response and relief efforts in vulnerable areas.

Security Concerns in Turkey

Turkish authorities detained 115 suspects linked to the Islamic State (IS) who were reportedly planning attacks around the New Year’s period. Firearms were confiscated during the crackdown, and investigations are continuing to identify additional suspected members. This operation reflects ongoing security challenges related to terrorism in the region.

Health and Legal News

Former Brazilian President Jair Bolsonaro underwent surgery for a hernia, which was reported as successful. Bolsonaro is currently serving a 27-year prison sentence for allegedly plotting a coup after losing the 2022 presidential election. His health and legal status remain topics of national interest in Brazil.

Environment and Energy

India’s rapid expansion of solar power is facing a significant challenge related to waste management. A recent study projects that by 2047, the country could produce over 11 million tonnes of solar waste if recycling efforts are not greatly improved. This issue raises questions about the environmental sustainability of India’s growing renewable energy sector and the need for better waste handling policies.

Christmas Around the World

Festivities and unique holiday traditions are in full swing globally. From waterskiing Santas in some locations to large communal meals in others, Christmas celebrations continue to bring cheer despite various regional challenges. Worship services and seasonal events were held in places such as London and Bethlehem, reflecting the diverse ways people observe the holiday.
