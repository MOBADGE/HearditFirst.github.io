Heard It First – Daily Digest

January 07, 2026

International Politics

The United States is reportedly considering various options to acquire Greenland, including potentially using military means, according to the White House. This comes amid opposition from Denmark, which governs Greenland, and a strong rallying of support among European allies against the move. The situation highlights ongoing tensions over Arctic territory and resources.

In Yemen, the situation remains tense as the presidency has accused Aidarous al-Zubaidi, leader of the UAE-backed Southern Transitional Council, of treason. The council’s leader was expelled after failing to attend talks in Riyadh. Around the same time, Saudi Arabia has launched strikes targeting forces in the region, underscoring the ongoing conflicts and power struggles within Yemen.

Latin America Developments

The U.S. continues to increase its involvement in Venezuela, with former U.S. President Donald Trump announcing that Venezuela will be "turning over" up to 50 million barrels of oil to the U.S. He also stated that American oil companies could begin operations in the country within 18 months. This follows the recent seizure of Venezuelan leader Nicolás Maduro by U.S. forces, an event that has left many Venezuelans uncertain about what will come next.

Meanwhile, opposition leader María Corina Machado, a Nobel laureate, has stated that her coalition should take leadership in Venezuela. However, Trump publicly dismissed Machado, claiming she lacks local support and respect, adding complexity to the political landscape in the country.

Security and Legal Matters

In Burkina Faso, the military junta reported it thwarted a plot to assassinate Captain Traoré. The country’s security minister accused neighboring Ivory Coast of financing this plan. The foiled plot raises concerns about regional security and diplomatic tensions in West Africa.

In legal news from Nigeria, Abubakar Malami, the country’s former justice minister and a significant figure in the administration of former President Buhari, has been granted bail in a $6 million corruption case. The legal proceedings highlight the ongoing challenges Nigeria faces regarding corruption and governance.

Public Safety and Accidents

Tragedy struck in Crans-Montana, Switzerland, when a New Year’s Eve fire at a ski bar claimed 40 lives and injured 116 people. Families of the victims have expressed shock upon learning that the bar had reportedly not been inspected for years before the fire. This tragedy has raised questions about safety regulations and enforcement in the area.

Security Breaches and Espionage

Japan’s nuclear agency is investigating the loss of a phone containing confidential data at a Shanghai airport. Authorities have not confirmed whether the data was leaked. This incident highlights ongoing concerns about the security of sensitive information during international travel.

Separately, Aldrich Ames, a former CIA agent who was convicted of spying for the Soviet Union and compromising over 100 operations, has died at the age of 84. Ames’ betrayal is considered one of the most damaging in U.S. intelligence history.
