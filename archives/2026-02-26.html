Danish Election Called with Greenland Issue in Focus

Denmark's prime minister has called a snap election, with the future of Greenland expected to be a key topic in the campaign. This comes amid reports that Donald Trump has expressed interest in annexing Greenland. The issue is likely to shape political debates in Denmark as parties outline their positions ahead of the vote. The election outcome may influence Denmark’s approach to Greenland's status and international relations around the Arctic region.

US and Iran Seek to Avoid Conflict Through Talks

Indirect negotiations between the US and Iran are taking place in Geneva in an effort to prevent further conflict. These talks are considered a last-ditch attempt to reach a nuclear agreement, although the likelihood of success remains uncertain. The discussions underscore ongoing international concerns about nuclear proliferation and efforts to stabilize relations in the Middle East.

Spyware Scandal in Greece Leads to Convictions

Four individuals have been convicted in connection with a major spyware scandal in Greece, known locally as "Greece’s Watergate." The surveillance software called Predator was reportedly used to target 87 people, raising concerns about privacy and government overreach. The convictions mark a significant step in addressing abuses related to this scandal.

Controversy Over Incident Involving Palestinian Boy

A video has emerged showing Israeli soldiers standing near a 14-year-old Palestinian boy who was bleeding after being shot. The scene lasted at least 45 minutes during which Palestinian ambulances were blocked from reaching him. This incident has drawn attention to the ongoing conflict and humanitarian issues in the region.

Cuba Reports Armed Infiltration Attempt

Cuban authorities say they fired on a US-registered speedboat carrying Cuban nationals living in the United States. The Cuban government described the event as an attempted "armed infiltration." Details about the passengers and the incident highlight continuing tensions and security concerns related to Cuba’s borders.
