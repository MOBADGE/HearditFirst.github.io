Heard It First – Daily Digest

February 26, 2026

International Diplomacy

Indirect talks between the United States and Iran aimed at reviving a nuclear agreement recently concluded in Geneva. A mediator described the discussions as having made "significant progress," though the future of a formal deal remains uncertain. These negotiations are seen as a final effort to prevent escalating tensions over Iran's nuclear program.

Meanwhile, Russia and Ukraine have exchanged the bodies of over 1,000 soldiers. This development came as Ukraine's leading negotiator met with US peace envoys in Geneva, signaling ongoing diplomatic efforts to address the conflict between the two countries.

Security and Conflict

Along the Afghanistan-Pakistan border, violence continues with recent attacks launched by the Afghan Taliban. Pakistan reported that two of its soldiers were killed, and both sides claim they imposed heavy casualties on each other. This exchange highlights the persistent instability and conflict in the region.

In Ukraine-related news, Kenya has charged an individual accused of recruiting young men to fight for Russia in its war against Ukraine. Reports indicate that about 1,000 Kenyans have been recruited to participate in this conflict.

Regional Politics

Denmark is preparing for a snap election with the issue of Greenland expected to be a key topic. The campaign is likely to focus on Greenland due to external interest, notably former US President Donald Trump's intention to annex the territory. The election's outcome could influence Denmark’s approach to Greenland's status and its international relations.

Human Rights and Legal Matters

A disturbing video emerged showing Israeli soldiers shooting a Palestinian boy who then bled to death as ambulances were blocked from reaching him for at least 45 minutes. The boy was 14 years old, raising concerns about human rights and the rules of engagement in the Israeli-Palestinian conflict.

In the United States, at least ten FBI agents involved in an investigation into former US President Donald Trump's handling of classified documents have been fired. Their dismissal follows scrutiny over the probe that took place after Trump's first term.

Other News

Cuba has reported that a group of people shot on a US-registered speedboat were Cuban nationals residing in the US and accused them of planning an "armed infiltration." Details on the incident remain limited.

In Germany, an 82-year-old man died after being crushed inside a car at a scrapyard in Munich. Police noted the car was scheduled for scrapping and suggested the man entered the vehicle unnoticed before the accident occurred.

Finally, the head of the World Economic Forum resigned following a review of his communications with Jeffrey Epstein. The official acknowledged contact but stated he was unaware of Epstein’s criminal history. This resignation occurs amid ongoing discussions about accountability and associations with Epstein.
