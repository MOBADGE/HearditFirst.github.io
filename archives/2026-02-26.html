Heard It First – Daily Digest

February 26, 2026

International Diplomacy and Security

Indirect talks between the US and Iran have begun in Geneva in an effort to prevent further conflict and possibly revive a nuclear agreement. Although these negotiations are described as a last-ditch effort, the outcome remains uncertain. Separately, North Korea's Kim Jong Un stated that his country could "get along" with the US if it accepts Pyongyang's nuclear weapons as permanent.

Meanwhile, Cuba reported shooting dead four people on a US-registered speedboat. The Cuban government claimed the passengers, Cuban nationals living in the US, intended to carry out a "terrorist" infiltration. In Ukraine-related news, Hungary’s prime minister criticized Kyiv for what he calls an "oil blockade" after a critical pipeline closure following Russian strikes. This accusation followed Hungary's veto of an EU loan to Kyiv.

Human Rights and Conflict

A troubling video has emerged showing Israeli soldiers shooting a 14-year-old Palestinian boy and then blocking ambulances, leaving him bleeding for over 45 minutes. This incident has raised serious concerns about the conduct of Israeli forces and implications for humanitarian treatment in conflict zones.

New evidence has also come to light regarding the killings of 19 people during protests in Kathmandu, Nepal, last September. The BBC report revealed police orders that led to the deaths of protesters, some of whom were shot while wearing school uniforms, highlighting serious issues around law enforcement responses to public demonstrations.

Domestic Legal and Political Matters

Hong Kong tycoon Jimmy Lai won an appeal overturning a fraud conviction. However, he remains detained due to a separate national security case, indicating ongoing legal challenges for the prominent business figure.

In Nigeria, an inquest will be held into the death of the toddler of internationally known writer Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie. Adichie has accused the Euracare hospital of negligence, an allegation the hospital denies. This development puts a spotlight on healthcare standards and accountability in Nigeria.

Crime and Justice Updates

A man dubbed the "suitcase murderer" by the media was deported from Bali to the US after serving 11 years in jail abroad. The 33-year-old will still face federal charges in the US and is scheduled to appear in court soon, underscoring the ongoing legal proceedings in this case.

Public Figures and Apologies

In Australia, the prime minister issued an apology after referring to Grace Tame, a child sex abuse survivor and advocate, as "difficult." Tame responded by calling the term a "misogynist's code for a woman who won't comply," sparking discussions about language and respect in public discourse.

These stories reflect ongoing challenges in diplomacy, human rights, justice, and leadership across the globe today. The outcomes of negotiations and legal processes may have significant implications for peace, accountability, and governance in the months ahead.
