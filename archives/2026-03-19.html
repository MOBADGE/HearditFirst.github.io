Middle East Tensions and US-Israel Relations

Recent missile attacks on gas fields have intensified focus on relations between the US, Israel, and Iran. Former US President Trump posted on social media suggesting alignment with Israel over responses to these attacks, although the full extent of US-Israeli coordination remains unclear. Meanwhile, Trump criticized Japan during a meeting with its prime minister, citing Japan's lack of support in managing security issues in the Strait of Hormuz, a key strategic waterway near Iran. This dynamic comes amid ongoing concerns about possible conflict involving Iran.

Conflict and Diplomacy in Africa

Tensions between the Democratic Republic of Congo (DR Congo) and Rwanda persist despite a peace agreement signed in December. Following talks in the United States, both countries have agreed to ease tensions, marking a potential step toward resolving ongoing conflict in the region. In Central Africa, Chad has issued a strong warning to Sudan after a drone strike killed 17 mourners. Chad's president ordered the military to remain on high alert and announced a complete border closure, signaling heightened regional instability.
