January 16, 2026

Global Peace Initiative in Gaza

A new "Board of Peace" has been formed to address the ongoing conflict in Gaza. The White House announced that the board's founding executive members include former UK prime minister Tony Blair and former US secretary of state Marco Rubio. This initiative signals a coordinated international effort to find solutions for peace in the region. The involvement of high-profile former officials suggests an emphasis on diplomatic and strategic approaches to easing tensions. How this board will influence the situation on the ground remains to be seen.

Protests and Unrest in Iran

Reza Pahlavi, an opposition figure living in the US and former crown prince, has called on the international community to intensify pressure on Iran's Revolutionary Guards leadership. His appeal comes amid continuing unrest in Iran, where protesters face harsh crackdowns. BBC interviews with Iranians near the Iraq border describe violent clashes, including people being hit in the face by pellets. The Iranian government has also imposed widespread internet blackouts to hinder the flow of information. These developments indicate escalating tensions and a deepening crisis in Iran.

Rising Tensions Over Greenland

US President Donald Trump has threatened to impose new tariffs on countries opposing the United States' proposals related to Greenland. This statement was made while a bipartisan group of US lawmakers was visiting Denmark to ease tensions concerning Greenland's strategic importance. The threat highlights complex international negotiations that involve territorial interests and trade policies. The outcome of these discussions could have implications for transatlantic relations and economic exchanges.

Election Developments in Uganda and Venezuela

In Uganda, President Museveni appears headed toward re-election with about 75% of votes counted so far. His main rival has questioned the legitimacy of the results, suggesting possible disputes ahead. Meanwhile, in Venezuela, opposition leader María Corina Machado has stated she intends to lead the country "when the right time comes" following the US seizure of its president. This statement underscores ongoing political uncertainty in Venezuela.

US-Venezuela and China-Canada Relations

The CIA director recently met for two hours with Venezuela's new leader in Caracas. Discussions focused on economic opportunities and preventing Venezuela from becoming a base for US adversaries. This meeting signals ongoing US involvement in Venezuela's political future. Separately, China and Canada announced relief on tariffs after a significant meeting between China's Xi Jinping and Canada's Mark Carney. The agreement centers on trade in canola oil and electric cars, reflecting improving ties after years of strained relations.

Legal and Military Affairs

Myanmar has begun its defense in a landmark genocide case at the UN court, contesting claims brought by The Gambia regarding atrocities against the Muslim-minority Rohingya population. Myanmar argues insufficient evidence has been provided. In South Africa, the defense minister has ordered an investigation into allegations that the military defied orders by allowing Iran to participate in naval drills. The US has accused South Africa's military of becoming too close to Iran, which could affect regional security and alliances.
