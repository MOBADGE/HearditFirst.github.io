Satellite images analyzed by BBC Verify reveal extensive damage in southern Lebanon following Israeli demolitions. Since March 2, more than 1,400 buildings have been destroyed in the region. Legal Developments in International Training Case

Daniel Duggan, a former US Marine pilot, has lost his appeal against extradition from Australia. Duggan was arrested in 2022 over allegations that he illegally trained the Chinese military while in South Africa. The court's decision to proceed with extradition emphasizes ongoing legal scrutiny related to military training activities and international security concerns. The next steps will likely involve Duggan facing charges in the requesting jurisdiction.

US Political Tensions and Federal Reserve

Donald Trump has publicly threatened to fire Jerome Powell, the chair of the US Federal Reserve if he does not resign by May. This marks a significant escalation in a dispute between Trump and Powell, reflecting ongoing tensions over monetary policy and leadership at the Federal Reserve. Powell’s role in managing the US economy's direction means this conflict could have broader implications for financial markets and policy stability in the months ahead.
