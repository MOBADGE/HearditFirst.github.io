Global Politics

Pope Francis criticised leaders he called "tyrants" for spending billions on wars, remarks that come after a public dispute with US President Donald Trump. Trump had earlier labelled the Pope "weak on crime," escalating tensions between the two figures. The Pope’s comments highlight ongoing global concerns about military spending and conflict.

In South Africa, opposition figure Julius Malema was sentenced to five years in prison for firing a gun. He is currently appealing the decision, so he will not enter prison immediately. Meanwhile, in a separate incident, South African police arrested activist Keba Seba over his alleged support for a coup plot in Benin. Seba is known for opposing French influence in Africa and supporting West African military leaders.

Middle East and Conflict Zones

Satellite images analysed by BBC Verify reveal extensive Israeli demolitions in Lebanese villages, showing over 1,400 buildings destroyed since early March. This assessment provides a clear picture of the widespread destruction in the area amid ongoing tensions.

At the same time, the conflict involving Iran continues to affect global economies, though China reported faster-than-expected economic growth during this period, defying concerns that the war would undermine regional markets.

Security and Law Enforcement

Turkish authorities detained 162 people in connection to online posts following two recent school shootings. One shooting at a high school on Tuesday injured at least 16 people, and a separate shooting on Wednesday resulted in nine deaths. The detentions are part of a broader crackdown as Turkish officials respond to rising violence and online commentary related to these tragedies.

In the United States, former Brazilian spy chief Alexandre Ramagem, a known ally of former President Jair Bolsonaro, was briefly detained by immigration agents in Orlando, Florida, but was later released. The reasons behind the detention have not been detailed.

Economy and Energy

An unprecedented fire broke out at a major Australian oil refinery, raising concerns about the country’s petrol supplies amid a tight global market. The incident adds to existing fears about fuel shortages and could have wider economic implications if repairs or production disruptions are prolonged.

Sports and Tragedies

The football world mourns the death of former Arsenal goalkeeper Alex Manninger, who died at 48 after his car was hit by a train. Manninger’s passing marks a tragic loss for fans and the sport, coming unexpectedly and prompting an outpouring of tributes.
