Heard It First – Daily Digest

December 12, 2025

Middle East Peace Efforts

Efforts to advance a peace plan in Gaza, backed by former US President Donald Trump, are struggling to make progress. Washington faces major challenges in convincing both Israel and Hamas to move forward with the plan's second stage. Meanwhile, the people of Gaza continue to endure difficult conditions, and delays in diplomacy are prolonging their suffering. The situation remains complex, with both sides facing tough choices about the next steps.

Political Developments in the US and Australia

In Indiana, some Republican lawmakers have defied Trump by rejecting a new voting map. Those involved in the vote have reported receiving death threats and intimidation, highlighting deep political tensions around electoral processes.

Meanwhile, Reddit has launched a legal challenge in Australia's High Court against a new policy banning social media use by children. Reddit argues that this policy threatens Australians' privacy and political rights. The case could have significant implications for social media regulation and digital rights in Australia.

Legal and Criminal News

Matt Wright, a well-known crocodile expert and former Netflix star, has been jailed after being found guilty of evidence tampering related to a fatal helicopter crash in 2022. He was convicted of lying to the police during the investigation.

In a separate case, Do Kwon, involved in the collapse of a $40 billion stablecoin, has been sentenced after pleading guilty to conspiracy to defraud and wire fraud. Kwon apologised for the impact of his actions, which caused significant disruption in the cryptocurrency market.

International Security

Around 200 West African soldiers are currently stationed in Benin to help stabilize the country after a failed coup attempt. This is the first official confirmation of the size of the foreign military force deployed to assist with security and restore order following the mutiny.

Cultural Protests and Entertainment

The French role-playing game Clair Obscur achieved major recognition at The Game Awards, winning nine prizes, including Game of the Year, as well as awards for music and performance. Its success marks a significant accomplishment in the gaming industry.

In the cultural arena, Swiss singer Nemo, a recent Eurovision winner, has announced plans to return the trophy in protest of Israel's participation in the contest. Nemo sees a conflict between Israel’s involvement and Eurovision’s values, sparking discussion about the event's political dimensions.

Technology and Regulation

In the United States, President Donald Trump has signed an executive order blocking states from enforcing their own artificial intelligence regulations. This move aims to create a uniform policy approach but has been met with criticism, including a strong response from California’s governor Gavin Newsom. The debate over AI governance and state versus federal control is expected to continue.
