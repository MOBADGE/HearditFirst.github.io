Legal Accountability for Historical Events

A Nigerian court ordered the United Kingdom to pay £420 million in compensation over the killing of miners during a strike in 1949. The colonial police, composed of Nigerians and Europeans, shot dead workers who were protesting for better working conditions. This ruling is a significant moment in addressing colonial-era human rights abuses.
