South Asia Tragedy: Deadly Blast at Islamabad Mosque

A large explosion during Friday prayers in a mosque in Islamabad, Pakistan, killed more than 30 people and injured almost 170 others. Authorities are investigating the incident, which has added to concerns about security and stability in the region.

Technology and Privacy: TikTok Faces EU Pressure Over Design

The European Union has demanded that TikTok change aspects of its app’s design that are described as “addictive,” warning that failure to comply could lead to substantial fines. TikTok has strongly denied these accusations and said it plans to challenge the EU's claims. The dispute reflects growing scrutiny of social media platforms and their influence on user behavior.

Global Relations: Canada and France to Open Consulates in Greenland

Canada and France are preparing to open consulates in Greenland. Although these plans were made before former US President Trump publicly expressed interest in acquiring the Arctic island, the move is widely viewed as a sign of support for the Greenlandic people. This development underscores the increasing geopolitical importance of the Arctic region.

Human Interest and Safety: Fisherman Killed by Crocodile in Zambia

In Zambia, a fisherman who fled from a herd of elephants sought refuge in a river stream but was tragically killed by a crocodile. The incident raises awareness about the dangers people face living and working near wildlife habitats. It also illustrates the complex interactions between humans and animals in rural areas.
